Vaccinium youngii

Vaccinium youngii, commonly known as the young's blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are typically harvested in late summer. These berries are not only edible but also serve as an important food source for local wildlife.

Its foliage is dense and evergreen, providing year-round habitat and ornamental value in gardens.
