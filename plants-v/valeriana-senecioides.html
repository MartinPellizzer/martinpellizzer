Valeriana senecioides

Valeriana senecioides, commonly known as stiffleaf valerian, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Valeriana senecioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
