Vriesea mourae

Vriesea mourae, commonly known as the Moura's Vriesea, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful inflorescence that often displays shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features a rosette of elongated, waxy leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. Its flowering period typically occurs in the summer months, and it thrives in bright, indirect light.

Vriesea mourae is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
