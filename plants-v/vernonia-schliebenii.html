Vernonia schliebenii

Vernonia schliebenii, commonly known as the Schlieben's ironweed, is a perennial shrub native to the highlands of East Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy species in arid environments.

Vernonia schliebenii is also noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and supporting local biodiversity.
