Viola pedata f. lineariloba

Viola pedata f. lineariloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly hairy surface. The flowers are usually violet or purple, with five petals and a distinctive spur at the base. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique foliage and floral characteristics.
