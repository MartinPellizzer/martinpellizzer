Viola micrantha

Viola micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 10-30 centimeters, featuring heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are usually purple or violet in color, with five petals and a distinctive spur. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and along stream banks.

Viola micrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
