Verbascum turcicum

Verbascum turcicum, commonly known as Turkish mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in the summer, which are clustered in a loose, upright spike. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Verbascum turcicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties.
