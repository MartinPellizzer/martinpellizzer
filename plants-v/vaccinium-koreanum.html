Vaccinium koreanum

Vaccinium koreanum, commonly known as the Korean blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Korea.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers and dark blue to black berries. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are leathery and glossy, and the plant is often found in forest understories.

Vaccinium koreanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its berries, which are used in traditional Korean cuisine.
