Verbesina encelioides subsp. exauriculata

Verbesina encelioides subsp.

exauriculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the central and eastern United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring lanceolate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in dense cylindrical inflorescences, and bloom in late summer to early fall.

The species is noted for its robust growth and adaptability to various soil types, making it a hardy ornamental plant.
