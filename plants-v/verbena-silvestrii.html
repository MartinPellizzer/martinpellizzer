Verbena silvestrii

Verbena silvestrii, commonly known as the wild verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically blue to purple in color and bloom during the summer months. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for gardens.

It is also known for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
