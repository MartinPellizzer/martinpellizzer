Vieraea

Vieraea, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid regions. The plants are characterized by their woody stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. Vieraea species are known for their hard, durable wood, which has been traditionally used for construction and tool-making.
