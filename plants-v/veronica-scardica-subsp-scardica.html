Veronica scardica subsp. scardica

Veronica scardica subsp.

scardica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved speedwell, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.

The plant features slender, upright stems and small, blue or violet flowers arranged in spikes. Its leaves are narrow and often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Veronica scardica subsp.

scardica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
