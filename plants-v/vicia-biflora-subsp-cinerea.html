Vicia biflora subsp. cinerea

Vicia biflora subsp.

cinerea, commonly known as the hairy vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, slender stems and trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

This subspecies is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and use in ecological restoration.
