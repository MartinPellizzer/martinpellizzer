Verbascum nigrum var. glabrescens

Verbascum nigrum var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth mullein, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features tall, erect stems covered with soft, velvety hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, branched inflorescences. Its leaves are large, lance-shaped, and covered with a dense layer of fine, woolly hairs.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
