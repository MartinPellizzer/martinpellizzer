Vernonia valenzuelana

Vernonia valenzuelana, commonly known as the velvetleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces dense clusters of yellow to orange flower heads, which are typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
