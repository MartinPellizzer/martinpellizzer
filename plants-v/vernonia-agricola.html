Vernonia agricola

Vernonia agricola, commonly known as the agricola ironweed, is a tall, upright perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant produces showy purple to pinkish flower clusters that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped, rough to the touch, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Vernonia agricola is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in full sun and well-drained soils.
