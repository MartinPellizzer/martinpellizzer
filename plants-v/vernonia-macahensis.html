Vernonia macahensis

Vernonia macahensis, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands, and it thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense, spherical clusters.

Vernonia macahensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by some indigenous communities.
