Vernonia hebeclada

Vernonia hebeclada, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and forest edges. Its foliage is narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and it produces clusters of blue to purple flowers in late summer.

Vernonia hebeclada is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
