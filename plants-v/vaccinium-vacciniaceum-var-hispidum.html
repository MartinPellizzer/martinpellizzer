Vaccinium vacciniaceum var. hispidum

Vaccinium vacciniaceum var. hispidum, commonly known as the rough huckleberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, or pubescent, stems and leaves, which give it its specific epithet "hispidum.". The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark blue or black when mature. These berries are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicines.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forested areas.
