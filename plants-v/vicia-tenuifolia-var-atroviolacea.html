Vicia tenuifolia var. atroviolacea

Vicia tenuifolia var. atroviolacea, commonly known as the dark violet vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, trailing stems with small, trifoliate leaves that are typically dark green. The flowers are usually purple or violet, with a distinctive spurred petal, and bloom in early summer. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
