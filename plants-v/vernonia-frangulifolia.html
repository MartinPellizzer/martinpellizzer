Vernonia frangulifolia

Vernonia frangulifolia, commonly known as the frangipani bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, the Americas, and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant is also valued for its ability to provide nectar for bees and other pollinators.
