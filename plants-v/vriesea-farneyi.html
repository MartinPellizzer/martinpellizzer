Vriesea farneyi

Vriesea farneyi, commonly known as the Farney's bromeliad, is a species native to Brazil.

It is a striking epiphytic plant characterized by its vibrant, spathaceous inflorescence that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that collect water, supporting its epiphytic lifestyle. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive red or purple throat.

Vriesea farneyi thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
