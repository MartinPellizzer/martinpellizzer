Viola dissecta var. chaerophylloides

Viola dissecta var. chaerophylloides, commonly known as the dissected violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, deeply lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Violaceae family.
