Viola calcarata var. rosularis

Viola calcarata var. rosularis, commonly known as the rock violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with fleshy, often hairy leaves that are adapted to rocky and dry environments. The plant produces clusters of violet-blue or purple flowers with a distinctive hooded structure, typical of the Violaceae family. Its leaves are usually arranged in a rosette, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
