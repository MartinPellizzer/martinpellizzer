Vernonia malaissei

Vernonia malaissei, commonly known as the Malayan ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with slender, hairy stems and narrow leaves. Its inflorescence consists of yellow disc flowers surrounded by pale green to brown ray flowers.

Vernonia malaissei is often found in open woodlands and forest margins, thriving in well-drained soils.
