Valeriana beddomei

Valeriana beddomei, commonly known as the Malayan valerian, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Malaysia and Indonesia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth, where it thrives in humid and temperate climates. Valeriana beddomei is also valued in traditional medicine for its calming effects.
