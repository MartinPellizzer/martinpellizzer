Vanilla aromatica var. lutescens

Vanilla aromatica var. lutescens, commonly known as the yellow vanilla orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellowish-orange flowers. The plant produces fragrant, pendulous flowers that are often used in perfumery and aromatherapy. Its unique coloration and aroma make it a sought-after ornamental plant.

However, it is less commonly cultivated for vanilla flavoring compared to its more famous relative, Vanilla planifolia.
