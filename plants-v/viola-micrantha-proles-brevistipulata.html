Viola micrantha proles brevistipulata

Viola micrantha proles brevistipulata, commonly known as the short-stipuled violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves with short, hairy stipules. The plant produces clusters of delicate, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
