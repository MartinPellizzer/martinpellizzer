Vagnera bicolor

Vagnera bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking bi-colored flowers, which typically feature a contrasting pattern of white and purple. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Vagnera bicolor is a climbing plant that can reach significant lengths, making it suitable for trellises or vertical gardens.
