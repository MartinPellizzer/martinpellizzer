Vaccinium cyclopense

Vaccinium cyclopense, commonly known as the cyclopense blueberry, is a species of shrub native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark blue to purple in color, similar to other blueberry species. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in cloud forests at high elevations.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered plant species.
