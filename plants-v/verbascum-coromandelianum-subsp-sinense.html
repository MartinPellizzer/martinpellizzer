Verbascum coromandelianum subsp. sinense

Verbascum coromandelianum subsp.

sinense, commonly known as the Chinese mullein, is a perennial herb native to China. It features large, fuzzy, ovate leaves that are often covered in soft, silky hairs.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its medicinal properties have been traditionally used in Chinese herbal medicine.
