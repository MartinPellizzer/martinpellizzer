Viburnum willeanum

Viburnum willeanum, commonly known as the white viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green above and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. Viburnum willeanum thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
