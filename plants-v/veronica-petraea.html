Veronica petraea

Veronica petraea, commonly known as the rock speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and alpine regions of Europe.

It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, often hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in crevices or on limestone outcrops.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments.
