Viola yezoensis var. takahasii

Viola yezoensis var. takahasii, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to gardens and natural habitats.
