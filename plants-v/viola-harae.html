Viola harae

Viola harae, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Viola harae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
