Viola tatjanae

Viola tatjanae, commonly known as the Tatjana violet, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Viola tatjanae is considered a species of conservation concern.
