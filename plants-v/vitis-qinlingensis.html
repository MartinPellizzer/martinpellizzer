Vitis qinlingensis

Vitis qinlingensis, commonly known as the Qinling grapevine, is a rare and endemic species native to the Qinling Mountains in China.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its climbing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that transition into berries, which are typically dark purple when mature. Vitis qinlingensis is valued for its ornamental potential and has been studied for its potential in horticulture and conservation efforts.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
