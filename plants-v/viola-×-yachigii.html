Viola × yachigii

Viola × yachigii, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is also valued for its ability to naturalize and form dense mats, making it a popular choice for ground cover.
