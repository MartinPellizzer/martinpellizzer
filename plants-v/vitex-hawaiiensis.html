Vitex hawaiiensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas and dry forests. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom throughout the year. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture, while its bark is smooth and grayish.
