Vaccinium ramonii

Vaccinium ramonii, commonly known as the Ramon huckleberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the cloud forests of Central America, particularly in countries like Costa Rica and Panama. This evergreen shrub typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces small, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires a humid, shaded environment.

Its botanical features include alternate, elliptical leaves and bell-shaped, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the spring.
