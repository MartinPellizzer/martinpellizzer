Vernonia vidalii

Vernonia vidalii, commonly known as the Vidal's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is lance-shaped with a rough texture, and it produces clusters of blue to purple flowers.

Vernonia vidalii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
