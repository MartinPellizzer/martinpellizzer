Varasia pseudoaquatica

Varasia pseudoaquatica, commonly known as the false water plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or floating, and it produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in aquatic environments.

Varasia pseudoaquatica plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
