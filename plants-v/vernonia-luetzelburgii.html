Vernonia luetzelburgii

Vernonia luetzelburgii, commonly known as the Luetzelburg's ironweed, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Vernonia luetzelburgii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
