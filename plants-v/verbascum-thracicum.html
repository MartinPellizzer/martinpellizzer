Verbascum thracicum

Verbascum thracicum, commonly known as the Thrace mullein, is a perennial herb native to southeastern Europe.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, which give it a distinctive appearance.

Verbascum thracicum is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
