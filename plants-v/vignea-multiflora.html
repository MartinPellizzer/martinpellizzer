Vignea multiflora

Vignea multiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Vignea multiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its beautiful blooms.
