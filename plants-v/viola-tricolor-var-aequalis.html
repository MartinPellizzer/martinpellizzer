Viola tricolor var. aequalis

Viola tricolor var. aequalis, commonly known as the violet tricolor, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three heart-shaped leaflets, and produces clusters of small, colorful flowers in shades of purple, white, and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-20 cm and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is native to Europe and is often cultivated as a garden ornamental due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.

The flowers are also used in traditional herbal medicine for their mild sedative properties.
