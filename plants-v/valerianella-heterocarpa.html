Valerianella heterocarpa

Valerianella heterocarpa, commonly known as the common valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the early summer months. Its leaves are ovate to lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Valerianella heterocarpa is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its calming effects.
