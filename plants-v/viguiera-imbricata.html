Viguiera imbricata

Viguiera imbricata, commonly known as Mexican sunflower, is a perennial plant native to Mexico and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. Viguiera imbricata thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens.

It is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
