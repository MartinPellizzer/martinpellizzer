Viola odorata subsp. beraudii

Viola odorata subsp.

beraudii, commonly known as the sweet violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers have five petals, with the lower petal often featuring a distinctive yellow or white spot.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller flowers and more compact growth habit compared to other viola species.
