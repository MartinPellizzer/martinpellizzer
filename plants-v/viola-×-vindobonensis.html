Viola × vindobonensis

Viola × vindobonensis, commonly known as the Vienna violet, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Viola tricolor and Viola verecunda.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful flowers that typically display combinations of purple, blue, and white. The plant is native to Central Europe and thrives in moist, shady environments. Its heart-shaped leaves are dark green and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Viola × vindobonensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in gardens.
