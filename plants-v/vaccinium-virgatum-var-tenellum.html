Vaccinium virgatum var. tenellum

Vaccinium virgatum var. tenellum, commonly known as the lowbush blueberry, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. The plant produces small, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and commonly used in culinary applications. Its leaves are elliptical, dark green, and have a slightly serrated edge.

This variety is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its fruit yield.
