Valeriana javanica

Valeriana javanica, commonly known as Javan valerian, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

Valeriana javanica is valued both for its ecological role and its historical use in herbal remedies.
