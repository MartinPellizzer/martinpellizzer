Viguiera brittonii

Viguiera brittonii, commonly known as desert mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. It produces showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall.

Viguiera brittonii is drought-tolerant and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
