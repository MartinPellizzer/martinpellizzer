Vachellia gerrardi var. gerrardi

Vachellia gerrardi var. gerrardi, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It features bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, slender leaflets. The plant produces yellow to orange floral spikes that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. Its woody pods contain seeds that are dispersed by wind or animals.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.
