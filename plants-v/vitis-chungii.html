Vitis chungii

Vitis chungii, commonly known as the Chinese grape or Chung's grape, is a species of flowering vine native to China.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into grape-like berries. Vitis chungii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject for study in both horticulture and botany.
