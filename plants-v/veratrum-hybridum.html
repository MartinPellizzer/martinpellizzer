Veratrum hybridum

Veratrum hybridum, commonly known as the hybrid white hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, stout stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, often covered in a whitish, waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of pale green to white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are large, broad, and typically have a wavy or serrated margin.

Veratrum hybridum contains various toxic alkaloids, making it a plant with significant medicinal and cautionary botanical importance.
