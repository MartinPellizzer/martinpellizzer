Vermeulenia caspia

Vermeulenia caspia, commonly known as the Caspian lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the regions around the Caspian Sea.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found near coastal areas or in wetlands. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, pink or purple flowers during the summer months. Its flowers are clustered in umbels and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Vermeulenia caspia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
