Viola canina var. adunca

Viola canina var. adunca, commonly known as the bent-leaved violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often curled or folded, giving it its common name. Its flowers are usually violet-blue in color, with five petals and a distinctive spur at the back.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
