Viola chinensis var. transitica

Viola chinensis var. transitica, commonly known as the Chinese violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that typically range in color from violet to white. Native to China, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. The flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
