Verbascum × insignitum

Verbascum × insignitum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a loose panicle. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
