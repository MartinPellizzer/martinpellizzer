Vaccinium parvibracteum

Vaccinium parvibracteum, commonly known as the small-bract huckleberry, is a species of shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The leaves are evergreen, narrow, and have a leathery texture, contributing to its adaptability in harsh environments.
