Viguiera mollis

Viguiera mollis, commonly known as Mexican heather, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features gray-green, woolly foliage and produces showy yellow to orange composite flowers in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Viguiera mollis is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
