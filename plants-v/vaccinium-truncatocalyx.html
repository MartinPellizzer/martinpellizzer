Vaccinium truncatocalyx

Vaccinium truncatocalyx, commonly known as the short-calyx blueberry, is a species of shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a small, dark blue berry that is edible and contains a high concentration of antioxidants.

This species thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or swampy habitats.
