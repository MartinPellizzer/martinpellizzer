Vitis reticulata

Vitis reticulata, commonly known as the Chinese grape or reticulated grape, is a deciduous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets and its clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to dark purple as they mature. Vitis reticulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
