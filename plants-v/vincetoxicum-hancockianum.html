Vincetoxicum hancockianum

Vincetoxicum hancockianum, commonly known as the Hancock's glorybower, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features opposite, simple, ovate leaves with serrated margins and produces bright orange or red berries in the fall. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and relatively low-maintenance plant for landscaping purposes.
