Valeriana nemorensis

Valeriana nemorensis, commonly known as the wood betony, is a perennial herb native to the forests of Europe.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Valeriana nemorensis is known for its aromatic foliage and is often found in shaded, moist woodlands.

It has been historically used in traditional medicine for its calming properties.
