Veronica miqueliana f. takedana

Veronica miqueliana f. takedana, commonly known as the Takeda's speedwell, is a hybrid form of the species Veronica miqueliana.

It is a low-growing perennial plant native to Japan, characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and covered in a fine layer of down.

This cultivar is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
