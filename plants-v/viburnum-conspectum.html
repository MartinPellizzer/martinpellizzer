Viburnum conspectum

Viburnum conspectum, commonly known as the white-fruited snowball viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features showy, spherical clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant produces dark blue, waxy berries that persist into winter, providing food for birds. Viburnum conspectum typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 feet and thrives in partial to full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
