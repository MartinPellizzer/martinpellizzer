Vitis gigantea

Vitis gigantea, commonly known as the giant grapevine, is a vigorous climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple when mature. Vitis gigantea is known for its rapid growth and ability to form extensive thickets.

It is often found in moist, wooded areas and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
