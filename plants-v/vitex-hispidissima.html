Vitex hispidissima

Vitex hispidissima, commonly known as the velvetwood or hibiscus tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, grayish bark and opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of purple or pink, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, brown seeds.

Vitex hispidissima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
