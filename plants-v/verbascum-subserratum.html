Verbascum subserratum

Verbascum subserratum, commonly known as the narrowleaf mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in soft, velvety hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Verbascum subserratum is also valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for respiratory ailments.
