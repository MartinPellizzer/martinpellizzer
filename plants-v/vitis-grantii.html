Vitis grantii

Vitis grantii, commonly known as the Grant's grape, is a species of flowering vine native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and the production of small, dark blue to black berries. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Vitis grantii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
