Vaccinium gilvum

Vaccinium gilvum, commonly known as the yellow huckleberry, is a species of shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, yellow berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are typically dark green and oval-shaped, with a slightly waxy texture.
