Vitis acris

Vitis acris, commonly known as the slender grapevine, is a perennial vine native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in the spring, followed by clusters of small, edible berries. Vitis acris is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
