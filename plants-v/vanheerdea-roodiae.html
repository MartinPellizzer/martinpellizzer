Vanheerdea roodiae

Vanheerdea roodiae, commonly known as the Red Mountain orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species native to the arid regions of Namibia.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers, which bloom during the rainy season. The plant typically grows on rocky outcrops and has adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils. Its roots form a symbiotic relationship with mycorrhizal fungi, aiding in nutrient absorption.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
