Valeriana faberi

Valeriana faberi, commonly known as the Chinese valerian, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, adorned with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Valeriana faberi is valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional medicine.
