Vachellia siamensis

Vachellia siamensis, commonly known as the Siam acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers that attract pollinators. Its bark is often fissured and may exude a milky sap when damaged.

Vachellia siamensis is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in disturbed or dry soils.
