Viola tanaitica

Viola tanaitica, commonly known as the Tanaitic violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus, specifically in countries like Georgia and Turkey. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces delicate purple flowers with five petals. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
