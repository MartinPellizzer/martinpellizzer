Valeriana altaica

Valeriana altaica, commonly known as the Siberian valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a robust root system and erect, branching stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, aromatic scent, which is characteristic of the Valerianaceae family.

Valeriana altaica is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its calming effects.
