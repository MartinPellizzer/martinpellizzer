Viola pectinata

Viola pectinata, commonly known as the pectinata violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces clusters of violet-blue flowers with yellow centers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola pectinata is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits in native gardens.
