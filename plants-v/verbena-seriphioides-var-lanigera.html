Verbena seriphioides var. lanigera

Verbena seriphioides var. lanigera, commonly known as the woolly verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for gardens. Its compact growth habit and long blooming season make it a popular ornamental plant.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
