Vesalea floribunda

Vesalea floribunda, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Vesalea floribunda prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements contribute to its appeal among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
