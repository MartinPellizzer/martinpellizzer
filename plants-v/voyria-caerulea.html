Voyria caerulea

Voyria caerulea, commonly known as the blue star, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its star-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are opposite and covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and low maintenance requirements.
