Vicia angustifolia var. lutescens

Vicia angustifolia var. lutescens, commonly known as yellow vetch, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow or orange pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and as a nitrogen-fixing plant in agricultural settings.
