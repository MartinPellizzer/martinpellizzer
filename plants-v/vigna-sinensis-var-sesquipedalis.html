Vigna sinensis var. sesquipedalis

Vigna sinensis var. sesquipedalis, commonly known as the giant cowpea, is a tropical legume characterized by its large, showy flowers and elongated, edible pods.

It is native to Africa and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. The plant can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring pinnately compound leaves and erect stems. Its seeds are typically white or pale yellow and are used in various culinary applications.

The species is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
