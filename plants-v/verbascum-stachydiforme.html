Verbascum stachydiforme

Verbascum stachydiforme, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers in clusters at the top of the stems, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Verbascum stachydiforme is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
