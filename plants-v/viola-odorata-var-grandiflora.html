Viola odorata var. grandiflora

Viola odorata var. grandiflora, commonly known as the sweet violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces fragrant, violet-blue flowers with yellow markings. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, often found in woodland areas. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery and medicine.
