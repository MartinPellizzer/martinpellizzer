Vinticena lavanalensis

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored leaves that range from red to orange, which are often used in traditional medicine by local communities. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom during the wet season, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Due to habitat loss, Vinticena lavanalensis is classified as endangered, making conservation efforts critical for its survival.
