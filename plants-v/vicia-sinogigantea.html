Vicia sinogigantea

Vicia sinogigantea, commonly known as the Chinese broadleaf vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, bushy growth habit and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers, which are typical of many legumes and attract pollinators such as bees. Its seeds are enclosed in pods that mature into dry, dehiscent fruits.

Vicia sinogigantea is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and potential ecological benefits.
