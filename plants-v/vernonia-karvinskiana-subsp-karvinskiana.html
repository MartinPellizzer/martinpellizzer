Vernonia karvinskiana subsp. karvinskiana

Vernonia karvinskiana subsp.

karvinskiana, commonly known as the Karvinskian ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flower heads.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or swampy areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while the flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
