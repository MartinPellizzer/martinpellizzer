Vagaria legionariorum var. gattefossei

Vagaria legionariorum var. gattefossei, commonly known as the Gattefosse's sea holly, is a halophytic plant adapted to grow in saline environments.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, blue or purple flowers. The plant has a compact growth habit with a rosette of succulent, fleshy leaves that store water. Its flowering stems are often covered in fine hairs, providing protection against harsh coastal conditions.

This variety is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in coastal dunes and salt marshes.
