Valeriana sylvatica

Valeriana sylvatica, commonly known as wood valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and has a creeping root system that can spread widely. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a strong, somewhat unpleasant odor when crushed.

Valeriana sylvatica is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used for its calming effects.
