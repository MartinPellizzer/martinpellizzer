Veronica longifolia

Veronica longifolia, commonly known as slender speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and thrives in partial shade to full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Veronica longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden settings for its delicate appearance.
