Viola verecunda f. divaricata

Viola verecunda f. divaricata, commonly known as the creeping violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit, with slender stems that often root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across the ground. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a hairy surface and a slightly serrated margin. Its flowers are typically violet or purple, with five petals and a distinctive hooded structure.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
