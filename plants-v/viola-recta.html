Viola recta

Viola recta, commonly known as the early purple orchid, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and hairy.

Viola recta is often found in clusters, with each flower having five petals arranged in a distinctive, butterfly-like pattern.
