Vernonia spinulosa

Vernonia spinulosa, commonly known as the spiked ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and wetlands. The plant features tall, erect stems covered with stiff, bristly hairs. Its showy, purple to pink flowers appear in dense, conical clusters at the top of the plant.

Vernonia spinulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
