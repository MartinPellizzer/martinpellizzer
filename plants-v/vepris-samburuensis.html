Vepris samburuensis

Vepris samburuensis, commonly known as the Samburu bitter ash, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically green when mature, and the bark is often used in traditional medicine.
