Valeriana paniculata

Valeriana paniculata, commonly known as the panicled valerian, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in panicles. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Valeriana paniculata is also valued for its medicinal properties, with its roots used in traditional herbal remedies.
