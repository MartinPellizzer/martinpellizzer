Verbena paraguariensis

Verbena paraguariensis, commonly known as yerba mate, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its erect stems and small, tubular flowers. The plant is cultivated for its leaves, which are used to prepare a traditional caffeinated beverage. Its leaves contain caffeine, saponins, and various other bioactive compounds.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
