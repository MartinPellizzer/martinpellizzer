Verticordia ovalifolia

Verticordia ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval leaf bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular subject for botanists and gardeners alike.
