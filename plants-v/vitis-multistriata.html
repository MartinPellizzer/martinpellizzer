Vitis multistriata

Vitis multistriata, commonly known as the multistriate grapevine, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often featuring distinctive white or yellow veins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in the spring, followed by clusters of edible berries that range in color from green to black. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Vitis multistriata is also valued for its historical use in traditional Chinese medicine.
