Viola pseudograeca

Viola pseudograeca, commonly known as the Greek violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 10-20 centimeters, featuring heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers with five petals, often in shades of purple or blue. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Viola pseudograeca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings for its delicate beauty.
