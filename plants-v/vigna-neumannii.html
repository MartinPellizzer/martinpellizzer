Vigna neumannii

Vigna neumannii, commonly known as the Neumann's bean, is a species of legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, which are typically heart-shaped. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Vigna neumannii is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and can be used in various culinary preparations.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability and ecological importance in soil enrichment.
