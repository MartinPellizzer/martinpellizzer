Viburnum pyramidatum

Viburnum pyramidatum, commonly known as the Chinese viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are clustered in umbels, often displaying white or pink hues. The plant produces red berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and floral display.

Viburnum pyramidatum is also valued for its adaptability to partial shade and its ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions.
