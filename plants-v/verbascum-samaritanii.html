Verbascum samaritanii

Verbascum samaritanii, commonly known as the Samaritan's candle.

It is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its tall, sparsely haired stems and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Verbascum samaritanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
