Vaccinium vacillans var. columbianum

Vaccinium vacillans var. columbianum, commonly known as the Columbian blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It produces small, edible berries that range in color from blue to black, depending on the maturity of the fruit. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires full to partial sunlight for optimal growth. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a glossy surface and a dark green color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
