Viola uniflora var. kareliniana

Viola uniflora var. kareliniana, commonly known as the Karelin's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive dark green coloration. The plant produces a single, showy violet flower with five petals, typically in shades of purple or blue. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
