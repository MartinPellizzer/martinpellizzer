Vilfa pungens

Vilfa pungens, commonly known as the spiked violet, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Violaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, spiky leaves and clusters of small, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or stream sides. Its leaves are often hairy and have a slightly serrated edge, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Vilfa pungens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
