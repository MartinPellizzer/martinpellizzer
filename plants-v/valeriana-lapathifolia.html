Valeriana lapathifolia

Valeriana lapathifolia, commonly known as the lesser valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils, which contribute to its historical use in herbal medicine.

Valeriana lapathifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
