Vernonia patentissima

Vernonia patentissima, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its most striking feature is its clusters of bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer to early fall.

Vernonia patentissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
