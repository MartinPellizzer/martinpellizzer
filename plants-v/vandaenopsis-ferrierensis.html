Vandaenopsis ferrierensis

Vandaenopsis ferrierensis, commonly known as the Ferrier's vanda, is a rare and delicate orchid native to the high altitudes of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, white to pale lavender flowers. The plant typically grows epiphytically on trees, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence.

Due to its limited habitat and threats from deforestation, it is classified as critically endangered by the IUCN.
