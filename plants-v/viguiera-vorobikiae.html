Viguiera vorobikiae

Viguiera vorobikiae, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, purple-pink composite flower heads. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, tubular florets that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for native gardens and ecological restoration projects.

Viguiera vorobikiae is also valued for its ornamental appeal and role in supporting local wildlife.
