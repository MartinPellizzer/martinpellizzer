Viscum sexangulatum

Viscum sexangulatum, commonly known as the six-angled mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of various host trees.

It belongs to the family Viscaceae and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and gelatinous, sticky berries. The plant lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its stems, which are embedded in the host's bark. It is native to regions in Europe and is often found in temperate climates.

Its unique growth habit and ecological role make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
