Vandellia saginiformis

Vandellia saginiformis, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It typically grows in rocky or coastal environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. The plant features a compact rosette of fleshy, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid conditions. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and often clustered in a spike, with a unique structure that resembles a miniature orchid.

Vandellia saginiformis is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient and specialized species within its habitat.
