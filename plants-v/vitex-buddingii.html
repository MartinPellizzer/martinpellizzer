Vitex buddingii

Vitex buddingii, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of blue to purple, mist-like flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Vitex buddingii is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
