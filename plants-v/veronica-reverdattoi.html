Veronica reverdattoi

Veronica reverdattoi, commonly known as the Sicilian speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal areas and limestone habitats. The plant features small, blue to purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from spring to summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its drought-resistant characteristics.

Veronica reverdattoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
