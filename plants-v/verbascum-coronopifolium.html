Verbascum coronopifolium

Verbascum coronopifolium, commonly known as the crownleaf mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in soft hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in a spike-like inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
