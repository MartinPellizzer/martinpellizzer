Verbena ribifolia

Verbena ribifolia, commonly known as redstem verbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that are often tinged with red or purple hues. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Verbena ribifolia is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
