Vernonia ganevii

Vernonia ganevii, commonly known as the Ganev's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across open habitats. Vernonia ganevii thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and disturbed areas.

Its foliage is typically gray-green, with hairy surfaces that help reduce water loss in arid environments.
