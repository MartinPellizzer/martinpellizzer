Verbascum blattaria var. crenatum

Verbascum blattaria var. crenatum, commonly known as the crested goosefoot, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and tall, erect flowering stems that bear yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its hairy leaves and distinctive flower clusters make it easily identifiable.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
