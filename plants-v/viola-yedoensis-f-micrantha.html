Viola yedoensis f. micrantha

Viola yedoensis f. micrantha, commonly known as the Japanese violet or Yedo violet, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves and produces delicate, violet-blue flowers with yellow markings. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-15 cm, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a bedding plant. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.

This cultivar is noted for its compact growth habit and is often used in horticultural displays.
