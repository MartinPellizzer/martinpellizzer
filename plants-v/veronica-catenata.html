Veronica catenata

Veronica catenata, commonly known as the chainflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Plantaginaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while the stems are usually prostrate or ascending.

Veronica catenata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting a variety of pollinators.
