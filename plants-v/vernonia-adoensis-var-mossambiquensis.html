Vernonia adoensis var. mossambiquensis

Vernonia adoensis var. mossambiquensis, commonly known as the Mozambique Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in regions with distinct wet and dry seasons. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and often branched.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential ecological and medicinal uses.
