Viola tricolor subsp. hortensis

Viola tricolor subsp.

hortensis, commonly known as the heartsease or violet, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, colorful flowers that typically range from purple to white and yellow.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or garden beds. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a decorative plant.
