Vigna crinita

Vigna crinita, commonly known as the hairy vigna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a legume belonging to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are encased in hairy, elongated pods, which contain edible seeds used in traditional cuisines.

Vigna crinita is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in agricultural systems.
