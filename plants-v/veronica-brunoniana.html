Veronica brunoniana

Veronica brunoniana, commonly known as the blueweed or blue grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, sky-blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Veronica brunoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
