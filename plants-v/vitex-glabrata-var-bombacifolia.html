Vitex glabrata var. bombacifolia

Vitex glabrata var. bombacifolia, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, flame-colored flowers that appear in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its trunk is often buttressed at the base. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species in warm climates.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its propagation.
