Vitex rotundifolia f. albescens

Vitex rotundifolia f. albescens, commonly known as the white-flowered chaste tree, is a compact, ornamental shrub characterized by its rounded, aromatic leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is native to the Mediterranean region, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its compact growth habit and striking white floral display make it a popular choice for garden landscapes and container planting.
