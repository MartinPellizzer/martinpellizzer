Vatairea paraensis

Vatairea paraensis, commonly known as the Brazilian wisteria, is a woody vine native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces striking purple to violet flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots contain compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

Vatairea paraensis is often found growing along riverbanks and in humid, shaded environments.
