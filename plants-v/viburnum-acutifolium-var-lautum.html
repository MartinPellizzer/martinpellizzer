Viburnum acutifolium var. lautum

Viburnum acutifolium var. lautum, commonly known as the mountain cranberry bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in spring, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits in the fall. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem with thorn-like protrusions and a preference for well-drained, acidic soils.

It is often found in rocky or open woodland habitats.
