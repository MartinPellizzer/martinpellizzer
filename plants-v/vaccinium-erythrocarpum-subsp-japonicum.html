Vaccinium erythrocarpum subsp. japonicum

Vaccinium erythrocarpum subsp.

japonicum, commonly known as the Japanese huckleberry, is a shrub native to eastern Asia. It produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in traditional cuisine.

The plant typically grows in acidic soils and thrives in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a leathery texture and a dark green color.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
