Viviania rosea

Viviania rosea, commonly known as the roseleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, rose-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a distinct reddish hue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Viviania rosea is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, making it an important component of riparian ecosystems.
