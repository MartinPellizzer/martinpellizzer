Viola odorata var. floribunda

Viola odorata var. floribunda, commonly known as the sweet violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces fragrant, violet-blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade.

Its flowers are popular in gardens for their ornamental value and historical use in perfumery.
