Vriesea paradoxa

Vriesea paradoxa, commonly known as the "Paradox Vriesea," is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, brightly colored inflorescence that can reach up to 3 meters in height. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that collect water, forming a central reservoir. Its flowers, which bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow, are highly ornamental and attract pollinators.

Vriesea paradoxa thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
