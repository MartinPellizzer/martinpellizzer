Vitex nlonakensis

Vitex nlonakensis, commonly known as the African blueberry, is a shrub native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small, blue to purple berries. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tubular flowers that range in color from white to violet. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

The berries are edible and have been traditionally used in local cuisines and herbal remedies.
