Vigna pseudotriloba

Vigna pseudotriloba, commonly known as the three-lobed cowpea, is a tropical legume native to Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with three rounded lobes and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces elongated, slender pods that contain several small, edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional seeds.

Vigna pseudotriloba is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting subsequent crops.
