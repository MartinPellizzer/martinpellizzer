Vernonia ischnophylla

Vernonia ischnophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
