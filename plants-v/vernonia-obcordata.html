Vernonia obcordata

Vernonia obcordata, commonly known as the obcordate ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Vernonia obcordata typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
