Viola cornuta

Viola cornuta, commonly known as the horned violets, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horn-shaped petals that give it its common name. Native to regions of Europe and western Asia, this plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Viola cornuta produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple and violet, often with yellow markings.

The plant's leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
