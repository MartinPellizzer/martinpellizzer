Viola canina var. major

Viola canina var. major, commonly known as the greater dog violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodlands and meadows. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces violet flowers with a distinctive yellow center. It blooms from April to June and is known for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
