Vernonia leandrii

Vernonia leandrii, commonly known as the Leander's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is usually lance-shaped with a rough texture, and it is often found in savannas and woodland edges.

Vernonia leandrii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
