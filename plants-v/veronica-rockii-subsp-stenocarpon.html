Veronica rockii subsp. stenocarpon

Veronica rockii subsp.

stenocarpon, commonly known as the narrow-fruit speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrow, elongated fruit capsules, which are a key identifying feature. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
