Vitex bracteata

Vitex bracteata, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range from red to orange, often appearing in clusters. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are usually gland-dotted. Its bark is smooth and greyish, and it produces woody, dry fruits that contain seeds.

Vitex bracteata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to warm, sunny climates.
