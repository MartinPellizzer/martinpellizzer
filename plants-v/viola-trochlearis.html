Viola trochlearis

Viola trochlearis, commonly known as the heartleaf violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. Viola trochlearis thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
