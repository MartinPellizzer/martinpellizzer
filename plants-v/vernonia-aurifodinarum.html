Vernonia aurifodinarum

Vernonia aurifodinarum, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant yellow flower heads that resemble a crown. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are clustered in dense, radiating arrays at the top of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The genus Vernonia is part of the sunflower family, Asteraceae, and includes many species with similar structural and ecological traits.
