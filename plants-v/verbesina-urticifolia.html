Verbesina urticifolia

Verbesina urticifolia, commonly known as beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant has hairy stems and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive, slightly prickly texture.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
