Vilfa vinzentii

Vilfa vinzentii, commonly known as the Vincent's violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in soft, velvety hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple and violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Vilfa vinzentii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value and hardy nature make it a sought-after plant among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
