Vigna membranaceoides

Vigna membranaceoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinct membranous texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into cylindrical pods containing several edible seeds. It is well adapted to drought conditions and thrives in sandy or loamy soils.

Vigna membranaceoides is often cultivated for its nutritious seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines.
