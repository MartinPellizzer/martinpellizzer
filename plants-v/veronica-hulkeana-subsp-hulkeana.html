Veronica hulkeana subsp. hulkeana

Veronica hulkeana subsp.

hulkeana, commonly known as Hulke's speedwell, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America. It features upright, branched stems that bear opposite leaves, which are ovate to lanceolate in shape and have a hairy texture.

The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in rock gardens and alpine collections.
