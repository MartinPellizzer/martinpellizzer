Viola cornuta var. micrantha

Viola cornuta var. micrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, violet-blue flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 inches and prefers moist, well-drained soils in partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it is often found in woodland settings.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
