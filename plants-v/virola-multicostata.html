Virola multicostata

Virola multicostata, commonly known as the Amazonian ironwood, is a large evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, dark bark and dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a glossy appearance.

Virola multicostata plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
