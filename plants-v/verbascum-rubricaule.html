Verbascum rubricaule

Verbascum rubricaule, commonly known as the red-hair flax or red-hair mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and fuzzy, gray-green leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.
