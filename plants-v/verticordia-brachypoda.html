Verticordia brachypoda

Verticordia brachypoda, commonly known as the short-stemmed featherflower, is a striking member of the family Scrophulariaceae native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery inflorescences that bloom in shades of pink and red, typically from late summer to early autumn. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to the dry, Mediterranean climate of its native region.

Due to its unique beauty and ecological significance, it is a popular subject in both horticulture and botanical study.
