Voacanga cornuta

Voacanga cornuta, commonly known as the African horned cucumber, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive horn-shaped fruits and hairy, compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has a strong, often unpleasant odor. Its roots and seeds contain psychoactive alkaloids, making it significant in traditional medicine and ethnobotanical practices.

The botanical features of Voacanga cornuta make it an intriguing subject for both scientific study and cultural exploration.
