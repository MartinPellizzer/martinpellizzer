Viola silvatica

Viola silvatica, commonly known as the forest violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in shady, moist woodland habitats, where it thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple, violet, and white. Viola silvatica is part of the Violaceae family, known for its ornamental value and ecological importance.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and contributes to the biodiversity of forest ecosystems.
