Valeriana montana var. incisa

Valeriana montana var. incisa, commonly known as the serrated valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features upright, branched stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or alpine habitats.

Valeriana montana var. incisa is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
