Verbascum decurrens var. thapsonigrum

Verbascum decurrens var. thapsonigrum, commonly known as the dark thapsus mullein, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, covered in dense, velvety gray-green foliage. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in clustered inflorescences, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
