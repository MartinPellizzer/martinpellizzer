Vaccinium onimense

Vaccinium onimense, commonly known as the Japanese huckleberry, is a species of shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional Japanese cuisine. Its leaves are evergreen, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Vaccinium onimense is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its culinary uses.
