Viburnum setigerum var. sulcatum

Viburnum setigerum var. sulcatum, commonly known as the glossy buckeye, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers in the spring, which are followed by distinctive, spiny, orange-red fruits. Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and unique fruiting habit.

It prefers partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a versatile addition to gardens.
