Valeriana seminuda

Valeriana seminuda, commonly known as the slender valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant has aromatic leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. Valeriana seminuda is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported calming properties.
