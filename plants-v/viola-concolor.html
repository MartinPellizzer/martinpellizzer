Viola concolor

Viola concolor, commonly known as the purple violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola concolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
