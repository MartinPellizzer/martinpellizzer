Volkameria antirrhinoides

Volkameria antirrhinoides, commonly known as the Mexican flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling the shape of a flame. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, woody fruits that contain numerous seeds. Volkameria antirrhinoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display and adaptability to warm climates.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.
