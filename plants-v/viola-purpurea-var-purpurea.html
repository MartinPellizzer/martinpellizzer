Viola purpurea var. purpurea

Viola purpurea var. purpurea, commonly known as the common violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
