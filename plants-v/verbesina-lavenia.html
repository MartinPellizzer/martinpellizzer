Verbesina lavenia

Verbesina lavenia, commonly known as golden beggar's-ticks, is a herbaceous plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its fruit is a dry, three-lobed capsule that contains several small seeds.

Verbesina lavenia is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in full sun.
