Viguiera szyszylowiczii

Viguiera szyszylowiczii, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, composite flower heads. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Viguiera szyszylowiczii thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping in arid regions.

Its foliage is usually gray-green, with hairy surfaces that help reduce water loss.
