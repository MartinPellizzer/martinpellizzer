Vernonia ituriensis var. occidentalis

Vernonia ituriensis var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to several meters in height, characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow to orange flower heads. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
