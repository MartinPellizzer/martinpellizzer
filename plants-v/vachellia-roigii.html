Vachellia roigii

Vachellia roigii, commonly known as the Socotra thorn tree, is a small, spiny shrub native to the Arabian Peninsula and parts of East Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is adapted to arid environments, featuring small, waxy leaves to reduce water loss. Its unique appearance, including its thorny stems and yellow flowers, makes it a distinctive species in its habitat.

Vachellia roigii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
