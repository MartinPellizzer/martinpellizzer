Vicia calcarata var. cossoniana

Vicia calcarata var. cossoniana, commonly known as the Cosson's vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in dry, open habitats. The plant features slender, erect stems with trifoliate leaves composed of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are small, pea-like, and usually appear in clusters, ranging in color from pale yellow to reddish-purple.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
