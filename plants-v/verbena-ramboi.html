Verbena ramboi

Verbena ramboi, commonly known as the Brazilian verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, with slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Verbena ramboi is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
