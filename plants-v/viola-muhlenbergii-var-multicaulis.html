Viola muhlenbergii var. multicaulis

Viola muhlenbergii var. multicaulis, commonly known as the Muhlenberg's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often hairy, with a distinctive violet or purple flower that blooms in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shady woodlands and thrives in well-drained soil. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
