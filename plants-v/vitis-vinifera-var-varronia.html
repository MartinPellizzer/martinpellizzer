Vitis vinifera var. varronia

Vitis vinifera var. varronia, commonly known as the Muscat of Alexandria, is a grapevine variety native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in aromatic compounds. The plant is known for its high sugar content and distinct muscat flavor, making it popular in winemaking. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soils and requires a long growing season.

This variety is also valued for its resistance to certain diseases and its adaptability to different viticultural practices.
