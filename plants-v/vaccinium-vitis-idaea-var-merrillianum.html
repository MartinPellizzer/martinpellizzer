Vaccinium vitis-idaea var. merrillianum

Vaccinium vitis-idaea var. merrillianum, commonly known as the dwarf cranberry shrub, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to boreal and subarctic regions.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a waxy surface, and it thrives in acidic, well-drained soils.

This variety is notable for its hardiness and ability to colonize rocky or barren areas.
