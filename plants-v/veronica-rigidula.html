Veronica rigidula

Veronica rigidula, commonly known as rigid speedwell, is a small flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, slender stems and pairs of opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or coastal environments.

Veronica rigidula is typically short-lived, with a lifespan of one to two years.
