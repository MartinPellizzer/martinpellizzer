Viola × lanciformis

Viola × lanciformis, commonly known as the heartleaf viola, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, colorful flowers that typically range from purple to yellow. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Viola × lanciformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
