Viola diffusa subsp. apoensis

Viola diffusa subsp.

apoensis, commonly known as the Sierra Madre violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of violet or purple. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to its natural habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
