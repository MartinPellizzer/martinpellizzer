Vernonia procera

Vernonia procera, commonly known as tall ironweed, is a tall, erect herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows up to 6 feet in height, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its sturdy stem and rough texture make it a hardy species often found in moist to wet soils.

Vernonia procera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for pollinators.
