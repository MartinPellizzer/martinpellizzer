Verbesina occidentalis

Verbesina occidentalis, commonly known as yellow ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its flowers are arranged in panicles. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open woods and along roadsides.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.
