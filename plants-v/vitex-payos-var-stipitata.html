Vitex payos var. stipitata

Vitex payos var. stipitata, commonly known as the Payos vitex, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in regions of Peru and Ecuador.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range from white to purple, which bloom during the dry season. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule containing several seeds.

Vitex payos var. stipitata is often used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
