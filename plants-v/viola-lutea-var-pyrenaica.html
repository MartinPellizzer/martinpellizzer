Viola lutea var. pyrenaica

Viola lutea var. pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean yellow violet, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula and southern France.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with a distinctive violet throat. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
