Viola excisa

Viola excisa, commonly known as the heartleaf violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically velvety and dark green, with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from violet to white, often with a yellow center. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Viola excisa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
