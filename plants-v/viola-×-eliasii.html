Viola × eliasii

Viola × eliasii, commonly known as the Elias's violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in woodland habitats with moist, well-drained soil. It is a perennial herb that spreads through rhizomes, forming dense mats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in natural settings.
