Vernonia moaensis

Vernonia moaensis, commonly known as the Moa's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are usually opposite and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, while its flowers are yellowish-green and arranged in clusters.

Vernonia moaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
