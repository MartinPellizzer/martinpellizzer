Vernonia bequaertii

Vernonia bequaertii, commonly known as the KwaZulu-Natal vernonia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is known for its yellow to orange-colored flowers. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Vernonia bequaertii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
