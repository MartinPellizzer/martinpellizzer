Vitiphoenix polyclada

Vitiphoenix polyclada, commonly known as the African silver tree, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a small, slow-growing tree with a distinctive silver-gray trunk and feathery, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, red, fleshy fruits. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
