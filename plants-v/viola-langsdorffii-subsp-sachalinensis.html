Viola langsdorffii subsp. sachalinensis

Viola langsdorffii subsp.

sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Far East, including parts of Russia and Japan. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a serrated edge and produces delicate violet flowers with a distinctive yellow center. It is characterized by its short stature and ability to thrive in cool, temperate climates.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
