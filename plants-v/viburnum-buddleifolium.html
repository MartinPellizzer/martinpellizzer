Viburnum buddleifolium

Viburnum buddleifolium, commonly known as the Chinese snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in late spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits in the fall. Viburnum buddleifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its seasonal interest.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.
