Viola belophylla

Viola belophylla, commonly known as the white violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and western Asia, typically growing in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, showy flowers that are usually white or pale violet in color. Viola belophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

It is also noted for its ability to thrive in cool climates and its preference for well-drained soil.
