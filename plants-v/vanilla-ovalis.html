Vanilla ovalis

Vanilla ovalis, commonly known as the oval vanilla, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, curved pods, which are the source of its name. Vanilla ovalis is a climbing vine that requires support for growth and thrives in warm, humid environments.

Its botanical features make it a unique and valuable species within the orchid family.
