Viola × vadutiensis

Viola × vadutiensis, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a species resulting from the cross between Viola lutea and Viola tricolor.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 10–20 cm, featuring heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of purple, blue, and white, often with a yellow or white eye. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is widely grown in temperate regions.
