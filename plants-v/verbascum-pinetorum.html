Verbascum pinetorum

Verbascum pinetorum, commonly known as the pine wood mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a cylindrical shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and rocky areas.

Verbascum pinetorum is a perennial plant that is both ornamental and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
