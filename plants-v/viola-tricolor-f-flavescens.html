Viola tricolor f. flavescens

Viola tricolor f. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow violet, is a hybrid form of the common violet.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which contrast with the typical purple and white petals of the species. The plant is a low-growing perennial that thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators.
