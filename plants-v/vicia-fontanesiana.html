Vicia fontanesiana

Vicia fontanesiana, commonly known as the Fontanesia vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink or purple. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind.

Vicia fontanesiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in marginal environments.
