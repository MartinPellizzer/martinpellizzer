Viburnum costaricanum

Viburnum costaricanum, commonly known as the Costa Rican viburnum, is a shrub native to Central America, particularly Costa Rica.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. Viburnum costaricanum typically grows in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
