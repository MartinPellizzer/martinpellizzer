Valeriana locusta

Valeriana locusta, commonly known as the lesser valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink, blooming in late summer. Valeriana locusta is often found in moist, shady habitats such as woodland edges and streambanks.

It is known for its aromatic properties and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
