Vitis ficifolia var. lobata

Vitis ficifolia var. lobata, commonly known as the Chinese wine grape or the broadleaf grape, is a species native to East Asia.

It is a climbing vine with compound leaves that have lobed margins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by clusters of round, edible berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional use in winemaking.

The leaves and fruits are used in various medicinal applications in traditional Chinese medicine.
