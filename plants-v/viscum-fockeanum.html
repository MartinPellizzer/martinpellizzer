Viscum fockeanum

Viscum fockeanum, commonly known as the Focke's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus Acacia.

It lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its stems, which are covered in a dense, spongy tissue. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are often clustered together, followed by small, round berries that contain the seeds. Viscum fockeanum is native to southern Africa and is often found in coastal and inland regions where its host trees thrive.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can impact the health of its host, but it also plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for birds and other animals.
