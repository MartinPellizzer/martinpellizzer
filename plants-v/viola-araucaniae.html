Viola araucaniae

Viola araucaniae, commonly known as the Araucanian violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern South America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.

Viola araucaniae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
