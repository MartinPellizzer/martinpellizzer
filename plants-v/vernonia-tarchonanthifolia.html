Vernonia tarchonanthifolia

Vernonia tarchonanthifolia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in moist soils.
