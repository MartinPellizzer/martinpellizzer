Vitis barbata f. triloba

Vitis barbata f. triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed grapevine, is a unique variant of the grapevine species Vitis barbata.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. This plant is native to regions of Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in temperate climates. The vines produce small, often sour berries that are not typically cultivated for wine or food.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural and ecological studies.
