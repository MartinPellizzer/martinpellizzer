Vernonia muhiensis

Vernonia muhiensis, commonly known as the Muhiensis ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flower clusters that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Vernonia muhiensis typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun.

Its hardy nature makes it an interesting subject for botanical study and potential horticultural use.
