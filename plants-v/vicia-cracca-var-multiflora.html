Vicia cracca var. multiflora

Vicia cracca var. multiflora, commonly known as the tall yellow vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of yellow pea-like flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its fibrous root system makes it a hardy and adaptable species.
