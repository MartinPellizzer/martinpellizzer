Vaccinium longetubulosum

Vaccinium longetubulosum, commonly known as the long-tubular huckleberry, is a species of shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits, which are typically blue-black when ripe, are edible and have a tart flavor.

This species thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and woodlands.
