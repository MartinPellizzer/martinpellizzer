Valeriana hunzikeri

Valeriana hunzikeri, commonly known as the Hunziker's valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It belongs to the Valerianaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often hairy leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its roots contain essential oils and alkaloids, which have been traditionally used in herbal medicine.

Valeriana hunzikeri is also valued for its ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
