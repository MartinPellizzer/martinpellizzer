Vernonia scorpioides var. subtomentosa

Vernonia scorpioides var. subtomentosa, commonly known as the silky Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are densely covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage and prolonged flowering period.
