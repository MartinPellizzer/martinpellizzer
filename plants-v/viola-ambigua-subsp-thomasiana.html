Viola ambigua subsp. thomasiana

Viola ambigua subsp.

thomasiana, commonly known as the Thomasiana violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States. It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple and white.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in native plant communities.
