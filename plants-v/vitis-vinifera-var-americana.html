Vitis vinifera var. americana

Vitis vinifera var. americana, commonly known as the American grapevine, is a wild variety of grapevine native to North America.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. The plant produces small, sour tasting berries that are typically used for winemaking or as forage for wildlife. It has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, greenish-white flowers.

This variety is often used in viticulture research due to its genetic diversity and adaptability.
