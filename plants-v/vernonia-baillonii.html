Vernonia baillonii

Vernonia baillonii, commonly known as the Bailloni's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodlands, and it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from pale yellow to deep orange.

Vernonia baillonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
