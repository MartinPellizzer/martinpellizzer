Vitex triflora var. ovata

Vitex triflora var. ovata, commonly known as the broadleaf chaste tree, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features opposite, ovate to broadly ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, often in shades of purple or white, which bloom in the spring or early summer. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
