Viola lutea var. sudetica

Viola lutea var. sudetica, commonly known as the Sudetic yellow violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and along stream banks, preferring well-drained, loamy soils. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces delicate yellow flowers with purple markings. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, and the plant is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.

Viola lutea var. sudetica is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
