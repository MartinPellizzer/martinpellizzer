Valeriana pinnata

Valeriana pinnata, commonly known as American valerian, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves that are pinnately divided, giving it a delicate, lacey appearance. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Valeriana pinnata is often found in the eastern United States and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits.
