Vernonia noveboracensis

Vernonia noveboracensis, commonly known as New York ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and clusters of purple to pinkish-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and grayish-green in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in meadows and open woodlands.

Vernonia noveboracensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
