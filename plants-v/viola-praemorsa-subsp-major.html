Viola praemorsa subsp. major

Viola praemorsa subsp.

major, commonly known as the large heartleaf violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet coloration.

The plant produces showy violet-purple flowers with five petals, often with a dark purple throat. It is characterized by its robust growth and is part of the Violaceae family.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in native plant gardens and ecological studies.
