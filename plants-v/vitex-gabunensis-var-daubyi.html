Vitex gabunensis var. daubyi

Vitex gabunensis var. daubyi, commonly known as the Gabon vitex, is a shrub or small tree native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the verbena family (Verbenaceae) and is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, which bloom during the dry season. It is often found in moist to wet forest understories and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
