Veronica archboldii subsp. patulifera

Veronica archboldii subsp.

patulifera, commonly known as the Bigleaf Speedwell, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and open habitats, such as grasslands and scrublands. It is notable for its upright growth habit and showy inflorescences.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
