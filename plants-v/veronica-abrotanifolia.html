Veronica abrotanifolia

Veronica abrotanifolia, commonly known as common speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces clusters of small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
