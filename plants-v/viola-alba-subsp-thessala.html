Viola alba subsp. thessala

Viola alba subsp.

thessala, commonly known as the Balkan violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and forest understories.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, pale violet or white flowers. Its flowers are often bilaterally symmetrical, with five petals, and are arranged in racemes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
