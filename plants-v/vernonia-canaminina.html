Vernonia canaminina

Vernonia canaminina, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring lance-shaped leaves with rough, hairy surfaces. Its showy purple to blue flowers bloom in cylindrical clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Vernonia canaminina is often found in dry to moist grasslands and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soils.
