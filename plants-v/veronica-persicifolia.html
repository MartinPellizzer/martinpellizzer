Veronica persicifolia

Veronica persicifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf speedwell, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are heart-shaped, which gives the plant its common name, and they are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
