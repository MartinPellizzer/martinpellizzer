Vicia cornigera

Vicia cornigera, commonly known as the mountain vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, often found in mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive hairy texture and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. Its root system has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

Vicia cornigera is adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitats.
