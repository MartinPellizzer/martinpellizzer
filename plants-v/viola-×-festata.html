Viola × festata

Viola × festata, commonly known as the common heartleaf violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant violet or purple flowers. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and meadows. The flowers typically bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
