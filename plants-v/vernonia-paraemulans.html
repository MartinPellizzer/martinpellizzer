Vernonia paraemulans

Vernonia paraemulans, commonly known as the Paraemulan ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow to orange in color.

Vernonia paraemulans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
