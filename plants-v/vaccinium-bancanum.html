Vaccinium bancanum

Vaccinium bancanum, commonly known as the black huckleberry, is a species of shrub native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as blueberries and cranberries. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in forested areas. Its small, dark purple to black berries are edible and often used in local cuisines or for making jams and wines.

The plant's botanical features include alternate, elliptic leaves and a compact, bushy growth habit.
