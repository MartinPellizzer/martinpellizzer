Valdesia rosea

Valdesia rosea, commonly known as the rose-like valdesia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, pinkish-red petals that resemble those of roses, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky soils, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Valdesia rosea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
