Verbena drummondii

Verbena drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches, featuring slender stems and small, tubular flowers. The flowers are usually blue to violet in color, with five petals, and bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
