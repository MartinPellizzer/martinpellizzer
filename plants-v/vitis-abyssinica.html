Vitis abyssinica

Vitis abyssinica, commonly known as Abyssinian grape, is a climbing vine native to Ethiopia and parts of East Africa.

It produces small, dark purple berries that are typically consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its vigorous growth habit. Its botanical features include tendrils for climbing and a woody vine structure.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments within its native range.
