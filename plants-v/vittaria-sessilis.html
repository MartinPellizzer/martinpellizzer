Vittaria sessilis

Vittaria sessilis, commonly known as the small-leaf fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Vittariaceae and is characterized by its compact, dense growth habit. The plant has small, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually less than 2 cm in length. Its fronds are typically dark green and glossy, with a delicate, lace-like appearance.

Vittaria sessilis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or rocks in the understory of rainforests.
