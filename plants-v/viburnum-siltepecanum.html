Viburnum siltepecanum

Viburnum siltepecanum, commonly known as the Mexican hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and small mammals. Viburnum siltepecanum typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in suitable climates.
