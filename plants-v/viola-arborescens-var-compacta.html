Viola arborescens var. compacta

Viola arborescens var. compacta, commonly known as the compact violet, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves and produces clusters of violet or purple flowers with yellow centers. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 inches, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, and it is known for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
