Vernonia inuloides

Vernonia inuloides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
