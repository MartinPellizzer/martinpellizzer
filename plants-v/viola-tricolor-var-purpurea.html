Viola tricolor var. purpurea

Viola tricolor var. purpurea, commonly known as the heartsease or violet, is a flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, typically with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The flowers are usually purple or violet, with a distinctive pattern of three petals, and are borne on slender stems. This perennial species thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or gardens.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in herbal medicine.
