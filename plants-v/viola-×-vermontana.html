Viola × vermontana

Viola × vermontana, commonly known as the Vermont violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shady woodland areas and thrives in well-drained soil. It is native to the northeastern United States and is often found in New England states.

Viola × vermontana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
