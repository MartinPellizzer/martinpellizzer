Valeriana bractescens

Valeriana bractescens, commonly known as sweet valerian, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, square stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Valeriana bractescens is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in herbal remedies for its calming effects.
