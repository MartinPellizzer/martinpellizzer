Viola × interjecta

Viola × interjecta, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and prefers moist, well-drained soil. It is native to Europe and has been introduced to various parts of the world.

Viola × interjecta is often found in woodland areas, meadows, and along stream banks.
