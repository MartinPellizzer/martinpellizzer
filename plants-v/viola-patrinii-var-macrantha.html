Viola patrinii var. macrantha

Viola patrinii var. macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces showy violet-blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a food source for various pollinators and its ornamental value in native gardens.
