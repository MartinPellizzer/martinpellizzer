Viola stewardiana

Viola stewardiana, commonly known as the Stewart's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the forest floor.

Viola stewardiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
