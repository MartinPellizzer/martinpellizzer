Verbascum sinuatum subsp. gaillardotii

Verbascum sinuatum subsp.

gaillardotii, commonly known as the Gaillardot's mullein, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in a loose spike. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
