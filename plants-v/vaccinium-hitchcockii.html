Vaccinium hitchcockii

Vaccinium hitchcockii, commonly known as Hitchcock's blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and heathers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Vaccinium hitchcockii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
