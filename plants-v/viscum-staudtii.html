Viscum staudtii

Viscum staudtii, commonly known as the Staudt's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It belongs to the Viscaceae family and is native to parts of Africa, particularly South Africa. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces white or pale yellow berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for certain birds and insects.

Its parasitic nature allows it to obtain water and nutrients from its host, while still performing photosynthesis.
