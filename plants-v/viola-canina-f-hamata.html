Viola canina f. hamata

Viola canina f. hamata, commonly known as the hairy violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive hairy leaves and violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a serrated edge, and the flowers are usually purple or violet in color.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
