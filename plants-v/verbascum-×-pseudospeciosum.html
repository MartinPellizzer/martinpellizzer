Verbascum × pseudospeciosum

Verbascum × pseudospeciosum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Verbascum blattaria and Verbascum speciosum.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance and ease of care.
