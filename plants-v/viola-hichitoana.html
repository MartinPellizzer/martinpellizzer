Viola hichitoana

Viola hichitoana, commonly known as the Hichitoan violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky crevices. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the landscape.

Viola hichitoana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
