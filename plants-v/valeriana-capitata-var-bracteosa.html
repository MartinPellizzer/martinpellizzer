Valeriana capitata var. bracteosa

Valeriana capitata var. bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteate valerian, is a flowering plant native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive, aromatic scent, and its root system is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy perennial in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental gardening and herbal applications.
