Viburnum retusum

Viburnum retusum, commonly known as roughleaf viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and features clusters of white, fragrant flowers in the spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for birds. Its leaves are oval with a rough, textured surface, giving it its common name.

Viburnum retusum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
