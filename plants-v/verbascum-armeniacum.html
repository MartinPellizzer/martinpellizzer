Verbascum armeniacum

Verbascum armeniacum, commonly known as Armenian mullein, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in soft, woolly hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a terminal spike. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Verbascum armeniacum is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
