Veratrum flavum

Veratrum flavum, commonly known as the yellow hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States and eastern Canada.

It features tall, smooth, cylindrical stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, often adorned with yellowish-green flowers in late summer. The plant has large, palmately lobed leaves that are typically dark green and have a waxy texture. It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous if ingested.
