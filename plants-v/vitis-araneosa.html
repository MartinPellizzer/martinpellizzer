Vitis araneosa

Vitis araneosa, commonly known as the wild grape or sand grape, is a perennial vine native to the United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when mature. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

Vitis araneosa is adapted to sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
