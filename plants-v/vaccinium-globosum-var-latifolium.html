Vaccinium globosum var. latifolium

Vaccinium globosum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf blueberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. Its fruit, a dark blue berry, is edible and often used in culinary applications.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
