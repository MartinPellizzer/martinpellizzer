Veronica trichadena

Veronica trichadena, commonly known as the mountain veronica, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Veronica trichadena is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
