Verbascum graciliflorum

Verbascum graciliflorum, commonly known as slenderflower mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that are often used medicinally. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
