Vicoa cernua

Vicoa cernua, commonly known as the hanging violets, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes mountains.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine regions.

Vicoa cernua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
