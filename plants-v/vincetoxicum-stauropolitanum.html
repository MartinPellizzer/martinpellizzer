Vincetoxicum stauropolitanum

Vincetoxicum stauropolitanum, commonly known as the Stauropotent vine, is a climbing plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a deep green color and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical structure includes tendrils for climbing and a woody stem that supports its growth.
