Vernonia lobbii

Vernonia lobbii, commonly known as the Kenyan ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the dry season. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in tropical and subtropical regions.

Vernonia lobbii is also valued for its hard, durable wood and is used in traditional construction and as a source of charcoal.
