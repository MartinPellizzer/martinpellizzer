Viola maculata f. chillanensis

Viola maculata f. chillanensis, commonly known as the Chilean violet, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple and white flowers, which bloom in early spring. The leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, adding to its visual appeal. This variety is often cultivated for its beauty in gardens and as a houseplant.

It prefers cool, moist conditions and is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice among plant enthusiasts.
