Vriesea alta

Vriesea alta, commonly known as the tall bromeliad, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and large, often colorful foliage that can reach several feet in height. The plant produces a striking inflorescence with bright, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, or yellow. Its rosette-like structure holds water, which helps it thrive in humid environments.

Vriesea alta is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its visual appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
