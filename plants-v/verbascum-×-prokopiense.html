Verbascum × prokopiense

Verbascum × prokopiense, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant belonging to the Scrophulariaceae family.

It is a hybrid between Verbascum blattaria and Verbascum blattartii, characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Verbascum × prokopiense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
