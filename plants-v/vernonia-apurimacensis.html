Vernonia apurimacensis

Vernonia apurimacensis, commonly known as the Apurimac Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of Peru.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its conical flower heads. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and shrublands, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from yellow to orange in color.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
