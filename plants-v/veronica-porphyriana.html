Veronica porphyriana

Veronica porphyriana, commonly known as the purple speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

The plant's ornamental value comes from its delicate flowers and compact growth habit.
