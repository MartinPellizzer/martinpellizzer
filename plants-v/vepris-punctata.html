Vepris punctata

Vepris punctata, commonly known as the African rosewood or yellowwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by winged fruits, typical of the Rutaceae family. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and fragrance, making it important in traditional crafts and local economies.

The plant also has medicinal uses in some African cultures, where various parts are used to treat ailments.
