Verbascum thapsus var. thapsiforme

Verbascum thapsus var. thapsiforme, commonly known as Great Mullein, is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers in clustered inflorescences, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its anti-inflammatory and expectorant effects.
