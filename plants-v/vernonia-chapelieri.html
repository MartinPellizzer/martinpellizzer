Vernonia chapelieri

Vernonia chapelieri, commonly known as the Cape York Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or inland regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Vernonia chapelieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
