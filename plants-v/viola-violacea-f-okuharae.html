Viola violacea f. okuharae

Viola violacea f. okuharae, commonly known as the Okuhara violet, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its small, violet-blue flowers with yellow centers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its flowers bloom in early spring.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
