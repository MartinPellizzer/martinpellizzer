Vernonia solorzanoana

Vernonia solorzanoana, commonly known as the Solorzano ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as cloud forests, and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow to orange in color.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
