Viscum fischeri

Viscum fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of various host trees.

It belongs to the family Viscaceae and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. The plant lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its stems, which are embedded into the host's bark. Its small, white or pale green leaves are arranged in a spiral pattern, and it produces small, yellowish flowers followed by berries.

Viscum fischeri plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for birds and other wildlife.
