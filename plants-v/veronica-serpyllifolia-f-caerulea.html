Veronica serpyllifolia f. caerulea

Veronica serpyllifolia f. caerulea, commonly known as blue speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, blue or sky-blue flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to June. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
