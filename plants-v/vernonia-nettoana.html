Vernonia nettoana

Vernonia nettoana, commonly known as the Brazilian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in open woodlands or grasslands. It produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
