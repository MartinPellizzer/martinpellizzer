Verbascum cheiranthifolium var. cheiranthifolium

Verbascum cheiranthifolium var. cheiranthifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mullein, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, gray-green, woolly leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers in clusters at the top of the stem, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
