Veronica cuneifolia subsp. atlantica

Veronica cuneifolia subsp.

atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic bentweed, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Iberian Peninsula. It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, often prostrate stems that are covered in fine, soft hairs.

The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, typically arranged in a basal rosette, and are characterized by their blue-green coloration. It produces small, blue-violet flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months.

This species is adapted to sandy or rocky coastal soils and is often found in dune environments.
