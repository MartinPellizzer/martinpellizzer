Veronica spicata var. major

Veronica spicata var. major, commonly known as the tall speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and spikes of blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.

Its historical use in traditional medicine and as a symbol of purity adds to its cultural significance.
