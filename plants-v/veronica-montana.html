Veronica montana

Veronica montana, commonly known as mountain speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features upright stems with opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and clustered in dense, terminal sprays, adding a delicate ornamental value.

Veronica montana is often used in gardens for its hardiness and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
