Verbascum sinaiticum

Verbascum sinaiticum, commonly known as the Sinai mullein, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, showy yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are broad and fuzzy, often used traditionally for their soothing properties.
