Volutaria lippii subsp. lippii

Volutaria lippii subsp.

lippii, commonly known as the prairie verbena, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open prairies and dry soils.

The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in spikes during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It plays an important role in pollinator habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
