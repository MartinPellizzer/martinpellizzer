Valerianoides sanguinea

Valerianoides sanguinea, commonly known as red valerian, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. Valerianoides sanguinea is often found in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its mild sedative properties.
