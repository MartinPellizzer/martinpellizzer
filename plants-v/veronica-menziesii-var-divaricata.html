Veronica menziesii var. divaricata

Veronica menziesii var. divaricata, commonly known as the Sierra veronica, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in mountainous regions. The plant features slender, branched stems and produces small, blue to purple flowers in clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
