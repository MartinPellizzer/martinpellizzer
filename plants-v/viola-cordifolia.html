Viola cordifolia

Viola cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and velvety in texture. The plant produces delicate flowers that range in color from purple to white, often with a distinctive yellow eye. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Viola cordifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
