Viola rubella var. angustifolia

Viola rubella var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet-blue coloration and produces small, delicate flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for centuries.
