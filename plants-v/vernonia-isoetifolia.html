Vernonia isoetifolia

Vernonia isoetifolia, commonly known as the sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with five petals. It is adapted to salt spray and can tolerate periodic flooding, making it a hardy coastal species.

The plant plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for small animals and insects.
