Vincetoxicum rutenbergianum

Vincetoxicum rutenbergianum, commonly known as the Rutenberg's devil's ivy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.
