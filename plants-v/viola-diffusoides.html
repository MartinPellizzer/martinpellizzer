Viola diffusoides

Viola diffusoides, commonly known as the creeping violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically less than 5 cm in length. The plant produces small, violet-blue flowers with five petals, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along streambanks.

Viola diffusoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
