Vigna gracilicaulis

Vigna gracilicaulis, commonly known as slender cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with small, elliptical leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated, cylindrical pods containing edible seeds. Vigna gracilicaulis is often grown for its nutrient-rich seeds, which are a good source of protein and dietary fiber.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate for optimal growth.
