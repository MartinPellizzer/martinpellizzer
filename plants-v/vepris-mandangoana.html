Vepris mandangoana

Vepris mandangoana, commonly known as the African wild pepper, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible and have a peppery taste. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is commonly found in forest understories.
