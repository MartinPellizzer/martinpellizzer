Viola anodonta

Viola anodonta, commonly known as the heartleaf violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple or violet flowers. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant's flowers typically bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its foliage is often used in herbal remedies, though it is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal.
