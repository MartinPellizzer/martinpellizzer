Viola acanthophylla

Viola acanthophylla, commonly known as the spiked violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight. Its unique feature is the presence of spines on the leaf stalks, which give it its common name.

Viola acanthophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
