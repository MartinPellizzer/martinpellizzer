Verbena parvula f. albiflora

Verbena parvula f. albiflora, commonly known as the white vervain, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a compact, herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 12 to 18 inches. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and slightly serrated. Its most distinctive characteristic is its clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The flowers are arranged in spikes and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.
