Vaccinium pubicarpum

Vaccinium pubicarpum, commonly known as the pubescent blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other berry-producing plants like cranberries and huckleberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark blue or purple in color. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and covered in fine pubescence, giving it its common name.

Vaccinium pubicarpum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swamps.
