Valeriana isophylla

Valeriana isophylla, commonly known as the broad-leaved valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often pinkish or white flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Valeriana isophylla thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

It is valued for its medicinal properties, though it is less commonly cultivated than other valerian species.
