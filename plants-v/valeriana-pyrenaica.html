Valeriana pyrenaica

Valeriana pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southwestern Europe.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and emit a strong, sometimes disagreeable fragrance.

Valeriana pyrenaica is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its calming effects.
