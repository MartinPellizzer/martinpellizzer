Verbascum latisepalum

Verbascum latisepalum, commonly known as the broad-sepal mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of soft, velvety hairs.

The broad-sepal mullein is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
