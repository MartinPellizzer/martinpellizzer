Vaughania dionaeifolia

Vaughania dionaeifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a prominent yellow center. Vaughania dionaeifolia thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

It is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
