Viola lactea var. stricta

Viola lactea var. stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, pale green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The flowers are typically violet or white, with five petals and a distinctive yellow or greenish throat. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade, often found in woodland areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
