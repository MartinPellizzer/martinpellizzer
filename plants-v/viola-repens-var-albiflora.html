Viola repens var. albiflora

Viola repens var. albiflora, commonly known as the white violets, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and meadows.

Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
