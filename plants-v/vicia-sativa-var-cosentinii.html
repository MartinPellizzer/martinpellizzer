Vicia sativa var. cosentinii

Vicia sativa var. cosentinii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf vetch, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe.

It features slender, erect stems with trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or marginal habitats.

This variety is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
