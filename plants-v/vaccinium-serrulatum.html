Vaccinium serrulatum

Vaccinium serrulatum, commonly known as the serrated blueberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color. Its leaves are alternate, oval-shaped, and have serrated edges, giving it its common name.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.
