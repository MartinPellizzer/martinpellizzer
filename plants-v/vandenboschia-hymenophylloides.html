Vandenboschia hymenophylloides

Vandenboschia hymenophylloides, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers during the winter months, which are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
