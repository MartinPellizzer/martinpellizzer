Veronica spuria var. schraderi

Veronica spuria var. schraderi, commonly known as the Schrader's speedwell, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender, hairy stems and small, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged opposite along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
