Vernonia aurantiaca

Vernonia aurantiaca, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
