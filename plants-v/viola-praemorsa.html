Viola praemorsa

Viola praemorsa, commonly known as the death violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers bloom in early spring, producing a range of colors from pale purple to deep violet.

Viola praemorsa is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
