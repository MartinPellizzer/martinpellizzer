Verbascum × kerneri

Verbascum × kerneri, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes species known for their fuzzy, woolly leaves and showy yellow or orange flowers. The hybrid form combines traits from its parent species, Verbascum blattaria and Verbascum thapsus, resulting in a plant with robust stems and large, soft leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
