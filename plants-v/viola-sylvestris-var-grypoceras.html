Viola sylvestris var. grypoceras

Viola sylvestris var. grypoceras, commonly known as the purple violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in woodland habitats and thrives in partial shade. It produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
