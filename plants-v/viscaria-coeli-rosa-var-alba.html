Viscaria coeli-rosa var. alba

Viscaria coeli-rosa var. alba, commonly known as the white star of Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its delicate, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its historical significance includes use in religious symbolism and traditional herbal medicine.
