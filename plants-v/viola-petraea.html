Viola petraea

Viola petraea, commonly known as the rock violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained environments. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are violet-blue in color, with five petals, and bloom in early spring.

Viola petraea is known for its hardiness and ability to withstand harsh, alpine conditions.
