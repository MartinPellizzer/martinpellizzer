Vaccinium serratum var. leucobotrys

Vaccinium serratum var. leucobotrys, commonly known as the white-fruited huckleberry, is a variant of the huckleberry plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, acidic soils, often in forested areas. The plant produces small, white to pale blue berries that are distinct from the typical red or black berries of other Vaccinium species. Its leaves are elliptical with serrated edges, and the plant is known for its ornamental value as well as its ecological importance.

It plays a role in supporting local wildlife and is sometimes cultivated for its unique fruit characteristics.
