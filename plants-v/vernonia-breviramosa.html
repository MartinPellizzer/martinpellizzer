Vernonia breviramosa

Vernonia breviramosa, commonly known as the short-stemmed ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright, slender stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and forest edges. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Vernonia breviramosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
