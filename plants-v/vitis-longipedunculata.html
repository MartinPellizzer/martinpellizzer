Vitis longipedunculata

Vitis longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-peduncled grapevine, is a species native to eastern North America.

It is a woody vine that typically grows along streambanks and in moist woodlands. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges and can reach up to 10 inches in length.

The fruit is a small, round grape that is typically dark purple when mature.
