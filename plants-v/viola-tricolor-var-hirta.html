Viola tricolor var. hirta

Viola tricolor var. hirta, commonly known as the hairy violet, is a flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically hairy, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of violet, purple, and white, often with a yellow center. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
