Vachellia nilotica subsp. adstringens

Vachellia nilotica subsp.

adstringens, commonly known as the African bristlecone acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa. It is characterized by its straight, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves that are gland-dotted.

The plant produces yellow flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, lens-shaped seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and adaptability to poor soils.

The subspecies is notable for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of tannins.
