Verbascum thapsiforme subsp. densiflorum

Verbascum thapsiforme subsp.

densiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower mullein, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are broad and velvety, with a soft, fuzzy texture that helps reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
