Veronica peloponnesiaca

Veronica peloponnesiaca, commonly known as the Peloponnese speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, blue to purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Veronica peloponnesiaca thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
