Vernonia philippinensis

Vernonia philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 4 meters tall, characterized by its rough, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, which bloom during the dry season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges or disturbed areas.

Vernonia philippinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
