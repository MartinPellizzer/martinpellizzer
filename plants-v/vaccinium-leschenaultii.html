Vaccinium leschenaultii

Vaccinium leschenaultii, commonly known as the dwarf blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in local cuisine. Its foliage is dense and leathery, providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Vaccinium leschenaultii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
