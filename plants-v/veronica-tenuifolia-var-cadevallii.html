Veronica tenuifolia var. cadevallii

Veronica tenuifolia var. cadevallii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a low-growing perennial that forms dense mats, often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to xeriscaped gardens due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
