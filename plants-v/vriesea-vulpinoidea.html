Vriesea vulpinoidea

Vriesea vulpinoidea, commonly known as the fox-tail bromeliad, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, spathaceous inflorescence that resembles a fox's tail. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that collect rainwater, creating a small reservoir that supports various microorganisms. Its flowers are typically red or orange, adding a bold contrast to its foliage.

Vriesea vulpinoidea is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its adaptability and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.
