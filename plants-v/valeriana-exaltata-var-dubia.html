Valeriana exaltata var. dubia

Valeriana exaltata var. dubia, commonly known as the purple valerian, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant purple or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple hue on the underside. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, often found in moist woodland areas.

Its aromatic foliage and showy flowers make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
