Veronica densifolia

Veronica densifolia, commonly known as denseleaf speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features slender, upright stems covered in dense clusters of small, blue-to-purple flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
