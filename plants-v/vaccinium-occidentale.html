Vaccinium occidentale

Vaccinium occidentale, commonly known as the western huckleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, tart berries that are typically dark blue to black in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The leaves are oval-shaped with a leathery texture, and the plant is valued for both its ecological role and edible fruits.
