Vernonia macranthele

Vernonia macranthele, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Vernonia macranthele is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
