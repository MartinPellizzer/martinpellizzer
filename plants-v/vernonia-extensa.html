Vernonia extensa

Vernonia extensa, commonly known as the broadleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine, hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers that bloom from late summer into early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough, slightly hairy surface.

Vernonia extensa is often found in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and riverbanks.
