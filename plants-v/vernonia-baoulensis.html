Vernonia baoulensis

Vernonia baoulensis, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its bark and leaves contain compounds used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Vernonia baoulensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
