Valeriana pleurota

Valeriana pleurota, commonly known as the narrowleaf valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Caprifoliaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive aromatic scent.

Valeriana pleurota is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported calming properties.
