Venidium × fastulaceum

Venidium × fastulaceum, commonly known as the hybrid boxwood, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is a hybrid between Venidium corymbosum and Venidium fastulans, both of which are members of the family Apiaceae. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. It produces small, white flowers in umbels, which are followed by small, dark berries.

Venidium × fastulaceum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
