Vanda coerulea f. luwangalba

Vanda coerulea f. luwangalba, commonly known as the Blue Vanda or Luwangalba Vanda, is a rare and striking orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to violet flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns of white and purple. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surfaces of trees or rocks without being parasitic. Its leaves are typically broad and fleshy, adapted to store moisture in its natural habitat.

This variety is highly valued in horticulture for its unique coloration and ornamental appeal.
