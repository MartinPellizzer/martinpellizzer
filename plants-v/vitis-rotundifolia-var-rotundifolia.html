Vitis rotundifolia var. rotundifolia

Vitis rotundifolia var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the sand grape, is a deciduous vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, round leaves and clusters of small, dark blue berries. The plant thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks. Its botanical features include tendrils for climbing and a robust root system that helps stabilize the soil.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
