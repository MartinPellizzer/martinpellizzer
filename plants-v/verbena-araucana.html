Verbena araucana

Verbena araucana, commonly known as the Andean verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Verbena araucana is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
