Viola chaerophylloides f. viridis

Viola chaerophylloides f. viridis, commonly known as the green-leaved violet, is a variant of the viola genus characterized by its distinctive foliage.

This plant is native to certain regions of Europe and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are typically a vibrant green, which sets it apart from other viola species. The flowers are usually violet or purple in color, with five petals arranged symmetrically.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in cool climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
