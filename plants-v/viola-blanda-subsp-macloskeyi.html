Viola blanda subsp. macloskeyi

Viola blanda subsp.

macloskeyi, commonly known as the Macloskey's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States. It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of purple and violet, often with a yellow or white eye. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is known for its delicate appearance and preference for partial to full shade.

It is a member of the Violaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities in native gardens.
