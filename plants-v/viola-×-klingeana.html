Viola × klingeana

Viola × klingeana, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a species resulting from the cross between Viola lutea and Viola tricolor.

It is a low-growing perennial plant with heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet, yellow, and white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding vibrant color to the landscape.

Viola × klingeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
