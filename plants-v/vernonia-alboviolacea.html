Vernonia alboviolacea

Vernonia alboviolacea, commonly known as the white violet vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Vernonia alboviolacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
