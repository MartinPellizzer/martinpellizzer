Vignea muricata

Vignea muricata, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. Vignea muricata is typically found in moist, sunny environments such as forests and disturbed soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
