Vicia tenuifolia subsp. atroviolacea

Vicia tenuifolia subsp.

atroviolacea, commonly known as the dark violet vetch, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, pea-like flowers.

The plant produces trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are typically violet or purple in color. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands, wastelands, and along roadsides.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
