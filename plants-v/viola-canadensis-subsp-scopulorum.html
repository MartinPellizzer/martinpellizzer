Viola canadensis subsp. scopulorum

Viola canadensis subsp.

scopulorum, commonly known as the rock violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and violet to purple flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, such as stream banks and woodland edges. It is a member of the Violaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller, more compact growth form compared to other viola species.
