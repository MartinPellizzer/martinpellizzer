Vernonia cana

Vernonia cana, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its most distinctive feature is the blue to purple mist-like appearance of its flower heads, which consist of numerous tiny disc florets.

Vernonia cana thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
