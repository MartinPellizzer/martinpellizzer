Verbascum × submesopotamicum

Verbascum × submesopotamicum, commonly known as the Mesopotamian mullein, is a hybrid plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family, characterized by its large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional remedies.
