Vitex arborea

Vitex arborea, commonly known as the chaste tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, lavender-blue flowers in summer, which are followed by distinctive, woody, bead-like fruits. Vitex arborea is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping.

It is also known for its historical use in herbal medicine.
