Verbascum agrimonioides

Verbascum agrimonioides, commonly known as the agrimony goosefoot, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Verbascum agrimonioides is also valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
