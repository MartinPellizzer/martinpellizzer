Verbascum nudatum

Verbascum nudatum, commonly known as the naked-stem mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Verbascum nudatum is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its anti-inflammatory and soothing effects.
