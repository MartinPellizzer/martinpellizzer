Viola reichii

Viola reichii, commonly known as the Reich's violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola reichii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
