Viola fedtschenkoana

Viola fedtschenkoana, commonly known as the Mongolian violet, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Mongolia and northern China.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and pale purple to white flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and is of particular interest to botanists and conservationists.

Its unique morphology and ecological significance make it a subject of ongoing botanical research.
