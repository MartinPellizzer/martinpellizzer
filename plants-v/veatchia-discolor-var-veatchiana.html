Veatchia discolor var. veatchiana

Veatchia discolor var. veatchiana, commonly known as the Veatch's pandanus, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, paddle-shaped leaves that are often a striking green or yellowish-green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences at the base of the leaves. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires ample sunlight to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
