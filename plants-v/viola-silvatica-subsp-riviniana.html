Viola silvatica subsp. riviniana

Viola silvatica subsp.

riviniana, commonly known as the mountain violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and rocky slopes, preferring cool and humid environments.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of violet or blue. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
