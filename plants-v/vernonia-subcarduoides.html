Vernonia subcarduoides

Vernonia subcarduoides, commonly known as the carduoides ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, spiny foliage that resembles a cactus, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in compact clusters, which bloom during the dry season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.

Vernonia subcarduoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
