Vignea omiana

Vignea omiana, commonly known as the golden vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant's leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a smooth, glossy surface. Vignea omiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and lush foliage.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
