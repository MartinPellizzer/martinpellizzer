Valerianella locusta

Valerianella locusta, commonly known as common mouse-ear or mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing annual plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, rounded leaves with a hairy texture and produces tiny white or pinkish flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in gardens, lawns, and along paths. It is a member of the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and has a short life cycle, completing its growth within a single growing season.

Valerianella locusta is also known for its edible leaves, which are used in salads and herbal teas.
