Vernonia greggii var. palmeri

Vernonia greggii var. palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and dense clusters of yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its fibrous root system helps it thrive in well-drained soils, and it is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Palmer's ironweed is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and resilience.
