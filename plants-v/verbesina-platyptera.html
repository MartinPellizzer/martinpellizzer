Verbesina platyptera

Verbesina platyptera, commonly known as the broadleaf beggarlice, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 1 meter tall, featuring alternate, pinnately compound leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers in clusters, which bloom from late summer to early autumn. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it thrives in disturbed soils, often found in fields and along roadsides.

The plant has a hairy stem and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
