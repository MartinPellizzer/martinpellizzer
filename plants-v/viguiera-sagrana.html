Viguiera sagrana

Viguiera sagrana, commonly known as sacred bluebells, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring.

Viguiera sagrana is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
