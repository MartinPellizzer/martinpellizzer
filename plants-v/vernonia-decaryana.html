Vernonia decaryana

Vernonia decaryana, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight. Its showy inflorescences attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Vernonia decaryana is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
