Vanda longitepala

Vanda longitepala, commonly known as the long-tongued vanda, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender petals and a distinctive, elongated labellum that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow with a contrasting purple or brown lip.

Vanda longitepala is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
