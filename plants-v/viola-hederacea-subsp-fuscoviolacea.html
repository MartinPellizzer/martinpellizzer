Viola hederacea subsp. fuscoviolacea

Viola hederacea subsp.

fuscoviolacea, commonly known as the purple viola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in woodland and shaded areas, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purplish tinge and produces violet-blue flowers with yellow markings. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is noted for its compact growth habit and ornamental value in garden settings.
