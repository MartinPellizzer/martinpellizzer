Viola × neumanniana

Viola × neumanniana, commonly known as the Viola × neumanniana, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and prefers moist, well-drained soil in partial shade. It is native to Europe and has been cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of climates, making it a popular choice for gardeners.
