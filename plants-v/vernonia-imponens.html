Vernonia imponens

Vernonia imponens, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies, and it thrives in full sun. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
