Viguiera corymbosa

Viguiera corymbosa, commonly known as Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces showy, purple to pink flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. Viguiera corymbosa is a perennial herb with hairy, lance-shaped leaves and a robust root system.
