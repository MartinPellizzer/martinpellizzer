Vitex pierrei

Vitex pierrei, commonly known as the mountain vitex, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive, aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or dry environments.

Vitex pierrei is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
