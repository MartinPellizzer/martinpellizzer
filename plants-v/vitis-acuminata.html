Vitis acuminata

Vitis acuminata, commonly known as the sharpleaf grapevine, is a deciduous vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers in the spring, followed by dark purple to black berries in the fall. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a pointed apex.

Vitis acuminata is often found in moist, woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
