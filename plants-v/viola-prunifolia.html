Viola prunifolia

Viola prunifolia, commonly known as the cherry-leaved violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture and a distinctive reddish-purple underside. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, with five petals and a spurred lower petal. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Viola prunifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
