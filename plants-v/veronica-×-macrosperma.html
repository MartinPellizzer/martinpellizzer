Veronica × macrosperma

Veronica × macrosperma, commonly known as the common speedwell, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically reaches a height of 10 to 30 centimeters and has opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
