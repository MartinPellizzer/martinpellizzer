Vernonia accedens

Vernonia accedens, commonly known as the golden ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in fine, grayish hairs. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

The showy yellow flowers appear in clusters at the tips of the stems, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
