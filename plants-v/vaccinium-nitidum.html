Vaccinium nitidum

Vaccinium nitidum, commonly known as the glossy-leaf cranberry shrub, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and azaleas. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, glossy, dark green leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, red, edible berries that are similar in taste to cranberries.

This plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
