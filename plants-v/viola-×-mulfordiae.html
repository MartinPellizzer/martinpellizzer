Viola × mulfordiae

Viola × mulfordiae, commonly known as the Mulford's violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, heart-shaped flowers that range in color from violet to white. The plant typically produces two to three pairs of oval, hairy leaves with a deep green hue. Native to the eastern United States, it thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is often grown in gardens and conservation areas.
