Valeriana coleophylla

Valeriana coleophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved valerian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Valerianaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Valeriana coleophylla is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically for its mild aromatic properties.
