Vernonia goetzeana

Vernonia goetzeana, commonly known as the Goetze's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as swamps and riverbanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small, blue to purple, and arranged in dense clusters.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
