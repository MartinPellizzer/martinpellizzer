Veronica barkeri

Veronica barkeri, commonly known as Barker's speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring, typically arranged in clusters along the stems. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is well adapted to dry conditions and is relatively drought-tolerant.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
