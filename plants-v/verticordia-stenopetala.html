Verticordia stenopetala

Verticordia stenopetala, commonly known as the narrow-petaled emu bush, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, urn-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with contrasting yellow centers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and is adapted to sandy soils and dry conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique floral structure and ecological significance in its native habitat.
