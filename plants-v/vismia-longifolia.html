Vismia longifolia

Vismia longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf vismia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, South America, and Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
