Viola humillima

Viola humillima, commonly known as the dwarf violet, is a small flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, with heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple or violet flowers. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in moist, shady woodland areas. Viola humillima is a perennial species that thrives in cool climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
