Viola persicariifolia

Viola persicariifolia, commonly known as the peach-leaved violet, is a flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive peach-colored underside, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, delicate violet flowers that are typically purple or blue in color. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, it thrives in moist, shady environments.

Viola persicariifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to bloom in early spring.
