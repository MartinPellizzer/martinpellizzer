Vernonia prenanthoides

Vernonia prenanthoides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower clusters that appear in late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense, showy inflorescences. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Vernonia prenanthoides is a popular choice for native gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
