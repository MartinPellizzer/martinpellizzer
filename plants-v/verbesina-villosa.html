Verbesina villosa

Verbesina villosa, commonly known as rough hibiscus, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, yellow-orange flowers with five petals, typically blooming from summer to fall. The plant has hairy, rough-textured stems and leaves that are often lobed or serrated. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open woods or along streambanks.
