Verbascum varians var. pilosior

Verbascum varians var. pilosior, commonly known as the hairy flax or woolly mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in a cylindrical inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This variety is notable for its distinctive appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
