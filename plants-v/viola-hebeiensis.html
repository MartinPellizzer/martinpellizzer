Viola hebeiensis

Viola hebeiensis, commonly known as the Hebei violet, is a flowering plant native to northern China.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola hebeiensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
