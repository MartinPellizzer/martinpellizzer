Vidalasia fusca

Vidalasia fusca, commonly known as the red-leafed nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, oval-shaped leaves that turn reddish-purple in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by orange-red berries. Vidalasia fusca is often found in moist, woodsy areas and thrives in partial to full shade.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in native gardens.
