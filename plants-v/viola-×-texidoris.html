Viola × texidoris

Viola × texidoris, commonly known as the Texas violet, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Viola sororia and Viola pedata.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to the southern United States, particularly Texas and Oklahoma. The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces vibrant violet or purple flowers with yellow markings. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Viola × texidoris is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
