Vignea phaeoleuca

Vignea phaeoleuca, commonly known as the white-flowered morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth. Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover.

Vignea phaeoleuca is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
