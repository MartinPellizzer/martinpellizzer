Vernonia platycephala

Vernonia platycephala, commonly known as the broad-headed ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. It produces showy, violet-blue flower heads that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
