Vaccinium vietnamense

Vaccinium vietnamense, commonly known as Vietnamese huckleberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are usually pink or white, and it produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential use in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
