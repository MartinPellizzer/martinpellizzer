Vitex coursii

Vitex coursii, commonly known as African bluebean, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Vitex coursii is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber and fuelwood in its native regions.
