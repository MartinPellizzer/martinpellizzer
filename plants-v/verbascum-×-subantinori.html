Verbascum × subantinori

Verbascum × subantinori, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
