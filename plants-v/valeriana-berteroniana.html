Valeriana berteroniana

Valeriana berteroniana, commonly known as the Mexican valerian, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, producing small, clustered white or pinkish flowers.

Valeriana berteroniana is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its calming effects.
