Veronica pasquorum var. pubescens

Veronica pasquorum var. pubescens, commonly known as the Downy Speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence, giving it its varietal name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or meadows.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
