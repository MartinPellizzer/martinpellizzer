Vernonia cocleana

Vernonia cocleana, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Vernonia cocleana is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
