Valeriana longiflora var. paui

Valeriana longiflora var. paui, commonly known as the long-flowered valerian, is a perennial herb native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features a rhizomatous root system and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers arranged in terminal spikes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its aromatic foliage and medicinal properties have made it of interest in traditional herbal medicine.
