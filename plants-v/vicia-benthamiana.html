Vicia benthamiana

Vicia benthamiana, commonly known as the Bentham vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and slender, trailing stems. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Vicia benthamiana is also valued for its role in ecological studies due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
