Valeriana dahurica

Valeriana dahurica, commonly known as Greater Valerian, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features a stout, upright stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically pink or white, during the summer months. Its root system is extensively branched and contains the main medicinal compounds.

Valeriana dahurica is valued in traditional medicine for its sedative and calming properties.
