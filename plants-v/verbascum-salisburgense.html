Verbascum salisburgense

Verbascum salisburgense, commonly known as the Salzburg mullein, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes several species of flowering plants known for their fuzzy leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in clusters at the ends of stems.

Verbascum salisburgense is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
