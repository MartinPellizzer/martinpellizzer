Valeriana quadridentata

Valeriana quadridentata, commonly known as the four-toothed valerian, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. Valeriana quadridentata thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its calming properties.
