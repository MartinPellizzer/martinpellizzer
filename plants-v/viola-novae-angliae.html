Viola novae-angliae

Viola novae-angliae, commonly known as the New England violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the northeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet-tinged underside and produces delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 inches and thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

The species is also noted for its ability to naturalize and spread through rhizomes.
