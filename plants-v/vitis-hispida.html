Vitis hispida

Vitis hispida, commonly known as the bristly grapevine, is a woody vine native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, grape-like berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are alternate and have a rough texture, with serrated edges.

Vitis hispida is a member of the Vitaceae family and is known for its adaptability to harsh climates.
