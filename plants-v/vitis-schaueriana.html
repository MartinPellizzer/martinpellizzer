Vitis schaueriana

Vitis schaueriana, commonly known as Schauer's grapevine, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, dark blue or black berries that are typically not eaten by humans but are used by wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Vitis schaueriana is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
