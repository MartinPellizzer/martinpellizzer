Viola odorata subsp. cyanea

Viola odorata subsp.

cyanea, commonly known as the sweet violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in woodlands and meadows. It is known for its fragrant flowers, which release a sweet scent, and its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soil.

This subspecies is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its historical use in herbal medicine.
