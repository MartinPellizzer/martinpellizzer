Veronicastrum japonicum f. albiflorum

Veronicastrum japonicum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the whiteflower false spirea, is a cultivar of the flowering plant native to Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically medium green and provide an attractive backdrop to the flowers. It is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is often used in landscape design for its compact size and extended blooming period.
