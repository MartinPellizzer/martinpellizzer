Viola × domburgensis

Viola × domburgensis, commonly known as the Domburg violet, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of delicate, five-petaled flowers that come in shades of violet, purple, and white. Native to the Netherlands, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value. It prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens or shaded borders.

The species is notable for its resilience and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
