Vernonia angusta

Vernonia angusta, commonly known as narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust growth habit. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Vernonia angusta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
