Viola pomelii

Viola pomelii, commonly known as the Chinese violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and woodlands. Its flowers bloom in early spring, producing a fragrant scent that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola pomelii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
