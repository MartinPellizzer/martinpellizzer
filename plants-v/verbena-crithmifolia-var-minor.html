Verbena crithmifolia var. minor

Verbena crithmifolia var. minor, commonly known as lesser sea verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Its fragrant foliage and showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
