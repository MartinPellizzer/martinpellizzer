Vinicia

Vinicia, commonly known as the evergreen vine, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Violaceae.

These plants are native to various regions in the Americas, particularly in tropical and subtropical areas. Vinicia species are characterized by their woody stems, opposite leaves, and showy, often violet or purple flowers. They can grow as climbers or shrubs, depending on the species.

Some species are valued for their ornamental beauty and are used in horticulture for their vibrant foliage and flowering displays.
