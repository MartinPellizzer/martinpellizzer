Valeriana punctata subsp. punctata

Valeriana punctata subsp.

punctata, commonly known as the spotted valerian, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It is a herbaceous perennial with upright, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

The species is characterized by its distinctive spotted leaves and its aromatic, sometimes fragrant, foliage.
