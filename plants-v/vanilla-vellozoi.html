Vanilla vellozoi

Vanilla vellozoi, commonly known as the Mexican vanilla or vanilla orchid, is a climbing vine native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a unique, aromatic pod known as a vanilla bean, which is used in culinary and perfumery applications. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and often have a waxy texture, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for climbing.

Vanilla vellozoi thrives in warm, humid climates and requires specific conditions for successful cultivation.
