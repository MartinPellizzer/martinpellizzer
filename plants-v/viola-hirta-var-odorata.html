Viola hirta var. odorata

Viola hirta var. odorata, commonly known as the sweet violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces delicate violet flowers with a fragrant scent. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and moist meadows, thriving in partial shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a touch of color to the landscape.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery and traditional medicine.
