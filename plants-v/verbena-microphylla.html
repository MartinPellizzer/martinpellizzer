Verbena microphylla

Verbena microphylla, commonly known as sticky verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces showy clusters of blue, violet, or purple flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, sticky hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Verbena microphylla is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
