Veronica spuria var. maxima

Veronica spuria var. maxima, commonly known as the Great Spurflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in sunny, well-drained environments. The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, blue to purple in color, and are clustered in dense spikes, making it a striking addition to gardens.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in marginal conditions.
