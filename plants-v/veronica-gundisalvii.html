Veronica gundisalvii

Veronica gundisalvii, commonly known as the Alpine speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically has narrow, hairy leaves and forms dense mats, making it well adapted to rocky and alpine environments. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in meadows and slopes at high elevations.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
