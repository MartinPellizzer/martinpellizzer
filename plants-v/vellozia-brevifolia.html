Vellozia brevifolia

Vellozia brevifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved vellozia, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette growth habit and fleshy, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces striking, cone-like inflorescences that bear small, white or pale yellow flowers. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal rainfall.

Due to its specialized morphology and habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
