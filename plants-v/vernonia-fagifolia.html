Vernonia fagifolia

Vernonia fagifolia, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, hard wood, which is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves that are lanceolate in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by bees and other pollinators.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional remedies.
