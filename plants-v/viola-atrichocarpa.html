Viola atrichocarpa

Viola atrichocarpa, commonly known as the woolly violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and small, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is often found in clusters along the ground.

Viola atrichocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
