Veronica anagallis-aquatica var. anagallidiformis

Veronica anagallis-aquatica var. anagallidiformis, commonly known as the water speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, blue or violet flowers clustered in groups of four, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. The plant has slender, branching stems that often grow near water bodies such as ponds and wet meadows. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs.

This variety is closely related to other species in the Veronica genus, sharing similar morphological traits and ecological preferences.
