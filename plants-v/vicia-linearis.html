Vicia linearis

Vicia linearis, commonly known as slender vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are typically dispersed by wind. Vicia linearis is native to Europe and western Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, benefiting the surrounding soil.
