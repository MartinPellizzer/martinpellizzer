Vernonia pectoralis

Vernonia pectoralis, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. Its most distinctive feature is the bright yellow flower head, which consists of numerous small florets arranged in a disk shape.

Vernonia pectoralis is often found in moist, sunny habitats such as prairies and woodland edges.
