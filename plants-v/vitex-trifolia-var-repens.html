Vitex trifolia var. repens

Vitex trifolia var. repens, commonly known as creeping chaste tree, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, prostrate stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across the ground. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of purple or blue, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge, often with a hairy texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
