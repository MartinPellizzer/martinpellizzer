Vinca minor f. argenteovariegata

Vinca minor f. argenteovariegata, commonly known as silverleaf periwinkle, is a compact, evergreen groundcover plant native to Europe.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are predominantly dark green with distinctive silvery-white variegation. The plant produces delicate blue to purple flowers in late spring, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in partial to full shade and is highly tolerant of moist, well-drained soils.

Its ornamental appeal makes it a popular choice for woodland gardens and shaded landscapes.
