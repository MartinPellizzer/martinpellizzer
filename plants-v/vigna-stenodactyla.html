Vigna stenodactyla

Vigna stenodactyla, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being elliptical and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. Vigna stenodactyla is valued for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines.

It also plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial in sustainable agriculture.
