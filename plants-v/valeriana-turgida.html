Valeriana turgida

Valeriana turgida, commonly known as the swollen valerian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, fragrant, and often purple or pinkish flowers. The plant has hairy stems and oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. Valeriana turgida is known for its medicinal properties, particularly for its calming effects, and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.
