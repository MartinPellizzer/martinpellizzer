Verbena roemeriana

Verbena roemeriana, commonly known as the Mexican verbena, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically blue or purple, and they bloom throughout the growing season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for gardens in warm climates.

Its ornamental value and ability to attract wildlife make it a popular choice among horticulturists.
