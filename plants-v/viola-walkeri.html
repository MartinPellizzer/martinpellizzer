Viola walkeri

Viola walkeri, commonly known as the purple viola, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady woodlands and thrives in well-drained soil. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the forest floor.

Viola walkeri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
