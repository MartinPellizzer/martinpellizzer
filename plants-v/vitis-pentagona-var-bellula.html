Vitis pentagona var. bellula

Vitis pentagona var. bellula, commonly known as the bell-shaped grapevine, is a unique variant of the grapevine species Vitis pentagona.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers, which are typically pink to purple in color. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, fertile soils. It produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and vineyards.
