Viola patrinii var. subsagittata

Viola patrinii var. subsagittata, commonly known as the sagittate violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Its scientific classification includes the genus Viola, which encompasses a wide variety of flowering plants with showy blooms.
