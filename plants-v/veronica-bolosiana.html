Veronica bolosiana

Veronica bolosiana, commonly known as the Bolosian speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and opposite, with a hairy texture.

Veronica bolosiana is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.
