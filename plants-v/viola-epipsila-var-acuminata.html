Viola epipsila var. acuminata

Viola epipsila var. acuminata, commonly known as the slender violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its flowers are violet-blue in color, with five petals and a distinctive spur at the back.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
