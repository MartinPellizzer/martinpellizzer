Vitis apyrena

Vitis apyrena, commonly known as the blackcurrant, is a deciduous vine native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, lobed leaves and produces small, aggregate fruits that are rich in antioxidants. The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is often cultivated for its edible berries, which are used in jams, wines, and other culinary applications.

Vitis apyrena is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
