Veronica austriaca var. teucrioides

Veronica austriaca var. teucrioides, commonly known as the Austrian speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of small, blue to violet flowers with five petals. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
