Vicia venosa subsp. venosa

Vicia venosa subsp.

venosa, commonly known as the common vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant produces elongated pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

As a member of the Fabaceae family, it has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, benefiting soil fertility.
