Vachellia × standleyi

Vachellia × standleyi, commonly known as the African burr acacia, is a hybrid leguminous shrub native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by woody pods containing lens-shaped seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to harsh climates.

Vachellia × standleyi plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
