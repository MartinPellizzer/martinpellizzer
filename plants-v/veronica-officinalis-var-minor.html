Veronica officinalis var. minor

Veronica officinalis var. minor, commonly known as lesser speedwell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in clusters along the stems. The plant has oval, opposite leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical name reflects its historical use in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
