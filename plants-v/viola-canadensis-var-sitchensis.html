Viola canadensis var. sitchensis

Viola canadensis var. sitchensis, commonly known as the Sitka violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a dark green upper surface and a lighter, sometimes hairy underside. The plant produces clusters of violet-blue or purple flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

This variety is notable for its adaptability and ornamental value in gardens and natural habitats.
