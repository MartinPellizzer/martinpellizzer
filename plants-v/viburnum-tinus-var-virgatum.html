Viburnum tinus var. virgatum

Viburnum tinus var. virgatum, commonly known as the Variegated Laurustinus, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, oval, dark green leaves with white or yellow variegation, adding visual interest throughout the year. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by dark blue berries that attract birds. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is well-suited for hedges or foundation plantings.

Its dense growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice in ornamental gardening.
