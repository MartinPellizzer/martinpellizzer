Vernonia lejolyana

Vernonia lejolyana, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture.

Vernonia lejolyana is often found in open woods, meadows, and along roadsides, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
