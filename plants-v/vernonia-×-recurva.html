Vernonia × recurva

Vernonia × recurva, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a hybrid plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its soft, hairy stems and clusters of small, blue-gray flowers. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil. Its foliage is often covered in fine, silvery-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Vernonia × recurva is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
