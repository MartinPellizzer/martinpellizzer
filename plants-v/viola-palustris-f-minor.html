Viola palustris f. minor

Viola palustris f. minor, commonly known as the lesser marsh violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist soils along stream banks, marshes, and other wetland environments. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in association with other moisture-loving plants.

Due to its delicate appearance and ecological significance, it is a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
