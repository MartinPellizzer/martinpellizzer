Vernonia salicifolia var. baadii

Vernonia salicifolia var. baadii, commonly known as the Baadi vernonia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa, particularly Kenya and Tanzania.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and is known for its tall, slender stems and clusters of yellow to orange flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
