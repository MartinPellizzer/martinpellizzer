Verbascum persicum

Verbascum persicum, commonly known as Persian mullein, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

Verbascum persicum is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for respiratory and skin ailments.
