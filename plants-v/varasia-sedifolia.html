Varasia sedifolia

Varasia sedifolia, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a succulent species native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features fleshy, silver-gray leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps reduce water loss in its dry habitat. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are covered in a fine layer of trichomes, giving them a distinctive silvery appearance.

Varasia sedifolia is well adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient choice for xeriscaping.
