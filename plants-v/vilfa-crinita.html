Vilfa crinita

Vilfa crinita, commonly known as the curly hair grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curly, hair-like leaves that give it its common name. Native to certain regions of South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color.

Vilfa crinita is often cultivated as an ornamental grass due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
