Viola tayemonii

Viola tayemonii, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a species of flowering plant in the violaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its delicate, violet-colored flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It has small, lanceolate leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.

Viola tayemonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
