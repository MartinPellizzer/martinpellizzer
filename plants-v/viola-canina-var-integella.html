Viola canina var. integella

Viola canina var. integella, commonly known as the English violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces violet-blue flowers with yellow centers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate appearance and early spring blooming habit.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
