Viguiera cordifolia var. latisquama

Viguiera cordifolia var. latisquama, commonly known as the broad-leafed ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central and southern United States.

It features upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 2 to 3 meters tall. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for native gardens.
