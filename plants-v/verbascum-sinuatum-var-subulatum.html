Verbascum sinuatum var. subulatum

Verbascum sinuatum var. subulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mullein, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are narrow and pointed. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in the summer, which are clustered in a cylindrical spike. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
