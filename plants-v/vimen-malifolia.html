Vimen malifolia

Vimen malifolia, commonly known as the sour orange tree, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by round, orange-colored fruits. The fruit is known for its high acidity and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
