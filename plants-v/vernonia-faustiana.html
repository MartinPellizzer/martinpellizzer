Vernonia faustiana

Vernonia faustiana, commonly known as the golden ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its bright yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Vernonia faustiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in herbal medicine.
