Vaccinium acuminatissimum f. sumatranum

Vaccinium acuminatissimum f. sumatranum, commonly known as the Sumatran huckleberry, is a botanical variant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and slightly pubescent. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, followed by edible berries that are typically red or black when mature. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in local cuisines or traditional medicine.

Its ornamental value and ecological significance make it an important species in its native habitat.
