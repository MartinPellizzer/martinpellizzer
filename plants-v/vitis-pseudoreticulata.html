Vitis pseudoreticulata

Vitis pseudoreticulata, commonly known as the Chinese grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to regions of East Asia, including China, Korea, and Japan. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and greenish-white.

Vitis pseudoreticulata produces small, round berries that are usually dark blue or black in color.
