Vitis serratifolia

Vitis serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated grapevine, is a woody vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when mature. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Vitis serratifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
