Viguiera neocronquistii

Viguiera neocronquistii, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

Its showy, pinkish-purple flowers bloom in late summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viguiera neocronquistii is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
