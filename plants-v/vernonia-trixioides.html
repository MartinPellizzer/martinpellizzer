Vernonia trixioides

Vernonia trixioides, commonly known as the golden ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Vernonia trixioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
