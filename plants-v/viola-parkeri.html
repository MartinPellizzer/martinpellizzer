Viola parkeri

Viola parkeri, commonly known as the Sierra viola, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and small, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in mountainous regions. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of five-petaled flowers.

Viola parkeri is often found in open woodlands and alpine meadows, where it thrives in cool, moist environments.
