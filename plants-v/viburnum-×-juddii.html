Viburnum × juddii

Viburnum × juddii, commonly known as the Canadian arrowwood, is a hybrid shrub native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 feet and features clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring. The plant produces bright red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a serrated margin, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

Viburnum × juddii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
