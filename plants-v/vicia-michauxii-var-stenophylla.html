Vicia michauxii var. stenophylla

Vicia michauxii var. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf vetch, is a perennial legume native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, compound leaves and small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and along stream banks. It produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for various insects and wildlife.
