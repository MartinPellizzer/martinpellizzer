Vahea tomentosa

Vahea tomentosa, commonly known as the velvet tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, hairy leaves that give it a velvety appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its bark is rough and fibrous, often used in traditional medicine.

Vahea tomentosa thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for both its ornamental and medicinal properties.
