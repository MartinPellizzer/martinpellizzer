Viola lineariifolia

Viola lineariifolia, commonly known as the linear-leaf violet, is a species of flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are elongated and have a distinctively linear appearance. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Viola lineariifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
