Vernonia volkameriifolia var. siamica

Vernonia volkameriifolia var. siamica, commonly known as the Siam Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking, conical inflorescences that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
