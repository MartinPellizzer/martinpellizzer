Valeriana officinalis var. montana

Valeriana officinalis var. montana, commonly known as mountain valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clustered inflorescences, typically blooming in late summer. Its roots and rhizomes are the primary parts used for medicinal purposes, containing compounds like valepotriates and iridoids.

This variety is valued for its calming effects and is often used in traditional medicine for treating anxiety and sleep disorders.
