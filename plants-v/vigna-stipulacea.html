Vigna stipulacea

Vigna stipulacea, commonly known as the stipule bean, is a species of legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and trifoliate leaves with distinctive stipules. The plant produces small, pale green pods that contain edible seeds, which are used in local cuisines. Vigna stipulacea thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
