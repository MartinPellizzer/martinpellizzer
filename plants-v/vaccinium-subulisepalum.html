Vaccinium subulisepalum

Vaccinium subulisepalum, commonly known as the slender-leaved blueberry, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink to white in color. Its fruit is a small, dark blue berry that is edible and often used in local culinary traditions.

The plant's foliage is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs.
