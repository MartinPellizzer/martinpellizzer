Verbascum thyrsoideum

Verbascum thyrsoideum, commonly known as the tall hoary thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its gray-green, woolly leaves and tall, branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in dense clusters, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands.

Verbascum thyrsoideum is a perennial species that is both ornamental and ecologically valuable, supporting various pollinators.
