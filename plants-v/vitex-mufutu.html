Vitex mufutu

Vitex mufutu, commonly known as the African blueberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small, blue or purple berries. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Vitex mufutu is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
