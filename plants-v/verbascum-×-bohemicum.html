Verbascum × bohemicum

Verbascum × bohemicum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant belonging to the Scrophulariaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in soft, velvety hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
