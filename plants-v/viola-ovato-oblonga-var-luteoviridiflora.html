Viola ovato-oblonga var. luteoviridiflora

Viola ovato-oblonga var. luteoviridiflora, commonly known as the yellow-green violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia.

It features heart-shaped to oval leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly hairy texture. The flowers are distinctive, with a yellowish-green corolla that has a prominent violet throat, giving it its common name. It blooms in early spring, often in shaded woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique coloration.
