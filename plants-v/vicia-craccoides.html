Vicia craccoides

Vicia craccoides, commonly known as field vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense spikes. Its pods contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

Vicia craccoides is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant in certain environments.
