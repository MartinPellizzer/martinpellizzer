Viola riviniana var. nemorosa

Viola riviniana var. nemorosa, commonly known as the wood violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist woodland habitats, where it thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet or purple underside, and it produces small, delicate flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.

The species is also known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, forming dense colonies in suitable environments.
