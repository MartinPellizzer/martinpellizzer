Vicia sativa subsp. macrocarpa

Vicia sativa subsp.

macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-seeded vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and long, slender pods that contain large, kidney-shaped seeds.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas. It has a shallow root system and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it valuable for soil enrichment.

Its flowers are typically pale purple or blue, adding ornamental value to the landscape.
