Vesicaria utriculata subsp. bulgarica

Vesicaria utriculata subsp.

bulgarica, commonly known as the Bulgarian bladder wort, is a rare aquatic plant characterized by its inflated, bladder-like structures that aid in buoyancy. It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams.

The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged. Its unique morphology helps it thrive in nutrient-poor waters by trapping oxygen and nutrients.

This subspecies is of particular interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and specialized ecological adaptations.
