Verbesina calciphila

Verbesina calciphila, commonly known as the limestone false sunflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central and southern United States.

It thrives in calcareous soils, showcasing its adaptation to alkaline environments. The plant features upright stems with alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy yellow flowers, which resemble those of the sunflower, bloom in late summer.

Verbesina calciphila is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
