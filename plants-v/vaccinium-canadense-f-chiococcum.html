Vaccinium canadense f. chiococcum

Vaccinium canadense f. chiococcum, commonly known as the red stemmed blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, deciduous leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, acidic berries that are typically dark blue to black in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of swamps.

This form is distinguished by its reddish stems and is a valuable species for wildlife and ecological restoration projects.
