Verbascum rupestre

Verbascum rupestre, commonly known as woolly mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, silver-green leaves and tall spikes of yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats. It is a perennial herb that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height.

Its leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their mild laxative and anti-inflammatory properties.
