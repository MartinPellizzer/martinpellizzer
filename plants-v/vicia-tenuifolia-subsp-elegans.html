Vicia tenuifolia subsp. elegans

Vicia tenuifolia subsp.

elegans, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are pinnate with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale purple to white in color, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, woodland edges, and disturbed sites.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
