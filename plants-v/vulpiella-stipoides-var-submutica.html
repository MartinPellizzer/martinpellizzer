Vulpiella stipoides var. submutica

Vulpiella stipoides var. submutica, commonly known as the rough-leaved flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky outcrops.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
