Verbascum renzii

Verbascum renzii, commonly known as the Kashmir mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Verbascum renzii is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
