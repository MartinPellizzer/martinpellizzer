Veronica glabrifolia

Veronica glabrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Veronica glabrifolia is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
