Vaccinium ardisioides

Vaccinium ardisioides, commonly known as the purple highbush blueberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically violet to dark purple in color, with a tart flavor. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

This species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its potential as a fruit crop.
