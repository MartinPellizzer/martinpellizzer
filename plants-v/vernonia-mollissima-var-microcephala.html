Vernonia mollissima var. microcephala

Vernonia mollissima var. microcephala, commonly known as the narrow-headed ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a branching stem that often lies prostrate or semi-upright.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
