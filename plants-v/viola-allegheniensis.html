Viola allegheniensis

Viola allegheniensis, commonly known as the Allegheny violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola allegheniensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in native plant gardens.
