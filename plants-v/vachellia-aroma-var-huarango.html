Vachellia aroma var. huarango

Vachellia aroma var. huarango, commonly known as the Huarango tree, is a leguminous plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its deep taproot system, which allows it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The tree produces small, yellow flowers and flat, bipinnate leaves that help reduce water loss. It plays a crucial role in soil stabilization and is often used in reforestation efforts.

Its wood is durable and has been historically used by indigenous communities for construction and tool-making.
