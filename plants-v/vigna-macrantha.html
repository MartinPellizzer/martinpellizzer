Vigna macrantha

Vigna macrantha, commonly known as the giant cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in various cuisines. Vigna macrantha is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it a valuable crop for soil enrichment.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
