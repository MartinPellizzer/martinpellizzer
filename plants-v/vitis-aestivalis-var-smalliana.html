Vitis aestivalis var. smalliana

Vitis aestivalis var. smalliana, commonly known as the smallleaf muscadine, is a vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and produces small, round, black grapes that are typically harvested in the fall. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates. Its berries are often used in local cuisines and traditional remedies.

The vine is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
