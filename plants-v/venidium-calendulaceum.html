Venidium calendulaceum

Venidium calendulaceum, commonly known as the yellow rocket, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard family plants. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and pinnately divided leaves. It typically grows in moist soils along stream banks and in wetlands.

Venidium calendulaceum is often found in disturbed habitats and can spread rapidly, making it both a weed and a valuable early successional plant.
