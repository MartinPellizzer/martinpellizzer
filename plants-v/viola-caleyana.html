Viola caleyana

Viola caleyana, commonly known as the Hawaiian violet, is a flowering plant native to the island of Maui in Hawaii.

It is a member of the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, violet-colored flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Viola caleyana is considered a rare and endangered species due to habitat loss and invasive species.
