Vignea × gerhardtii

Vignea × gerhardtii, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems, oval-shaped leaves, and small, yellowish-green flowers. Its berries, which are typically red when mature, contain toxic compounds and are not edible.

Vignea × gerhardtii is often found in disturbed habitats and can be invasive in certain regions.
