Vicia laevigata

Vicia laevigata, commonly known as the smooth vetch, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Vicia laevigata is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in agricultural settings.
