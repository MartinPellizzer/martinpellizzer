Vernonia capensis

Vernonia capensis, commonly known as the Cape ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions. Its wood is hard and dense, historically used for tool handles and construction.

The plant also has medicinal uses in traditional African medicine.
