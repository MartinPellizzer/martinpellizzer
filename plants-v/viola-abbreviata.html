Viola abbreviata

Viola abbreviata, commonly known as the dwarf violet, is a small flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Viola abbreviata has a short stem and produces small, showy blossoms that bloom in early spring.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable species in native plant gardens.
