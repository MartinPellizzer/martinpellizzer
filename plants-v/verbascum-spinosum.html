Verbascum spinosum

Verbascum spinosum, commonly known as the spiny mullein, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in a spike-like inflorescence. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as hillsides and wastelands.

Verbascum spinosum is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its soothing effects on respiratory ailments.
