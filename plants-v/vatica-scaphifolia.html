Vatica scaphifolia

Vatica scaphifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, hard wood and lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ecological and commercial significance. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are followed by woody capsules containing seeds.
