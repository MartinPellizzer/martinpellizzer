Viola palustris subsp. epipsila

Viola palustris subsp.

epipsila, commonly known as the marsh violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America. It typically grows in moist soils along stream banks, marshes, and other wet areas.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet or purple underside, and its flowers are usually pale violet or white with a dark purple throat. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
