Vicia cassubica var. rigida

Vicia cassubica var. rigida, commonly known as the rigid vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Vicia cassubica var. rigida is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil enrichment.
