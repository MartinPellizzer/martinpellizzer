Viola tripartita var. glaberrima

Viola tripartita var. glaberrima, commonly known as the glabrous violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has a sprawling growth habit. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

The species is notable for its smooth, hairless stems and leaves, which distinguish it from other viola varieties.
