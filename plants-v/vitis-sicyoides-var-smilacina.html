Vitis sicyoides var. smilacina

Vitis sicyoides var. smilacina, commonly known as the creeping grape or smilacina grape, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped, palmate leaves that are often lobed and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy berries. Its stems are woody and can twine around supports, while its roots are fibrous and adaptive to moist, well-drained soils.

This species is often found in shaded, humid environments such as forests and along stream banks.
