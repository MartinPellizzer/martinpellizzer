Vulpia fibrosa

Vulpia fibrosa, commonly known as slender vulpia, is a species of grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils such as roadsides, fields, and waste areas. The plant has slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and often have a fibrous texture, contributing to its common name.

Vulpia fibrosa is an annual plant that plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
