Vitex pubescens var. ptilota

Vitex pubescens var. ptilota, commonly known as the silky flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink and violet, blooming in summer. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in soft, silvery hairs, giving the plant its "silky" appearance. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental in tropical and subtropical gardens.

Its rapid growth and ability to tolerate coastal conditions further enhance its horticultural value.
