Veronica × andersonii

Veronica × andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's speedwell, is a hybrid plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the plantain family, Plantaginaceae, and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
