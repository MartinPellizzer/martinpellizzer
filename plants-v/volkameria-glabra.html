Volkameria glabra

Volkameria glabra, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree with smooth, grayish bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces large, fragrant, white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
