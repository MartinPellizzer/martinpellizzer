Viola hirta subsp. ausonensis

Viola hirta subsp.

ausonensis, commonly known as the Appalachian violet, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center. It is part of the Violaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit.

The subspecies is distinguished by its larger flowers and more robust foliage compared to other viola species.
