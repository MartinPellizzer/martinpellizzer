Verbena trachea

Verbena trachea, commonly known as the common verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of blue, purple, and red, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Verbena trachea is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value and hardy nature contribute to its widespread cultivation in gardens across temperate climates.
