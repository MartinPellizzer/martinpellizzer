Valeriana multifida

Valeriana multifida, commonly known as the forked valerian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Valeriana multifida is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported calming effects.
