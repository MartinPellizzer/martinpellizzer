Veronica schmidtiana subsp. bandaiana

Veronica schmidtiana subsp.

bandaiana, commonly known as the Banda veronica, is a flowering plant native to the island of Banda Neira in Indonesia. It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is typically found in shaded, moist forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
