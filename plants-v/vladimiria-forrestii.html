Vladimiria forrestii

Vladimiria forrestii, commonly known as the Chinese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding to its seasonal appeal. It is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and parks.

Vladimiria forrestii prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for a variety of landscapes.
