Veronica hispidula

Veronica hispidula, commonly known as hairy speedwell, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. Its small blue or violet flowers bloom in clusters during the spring and summer.

Veronica hispidula is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to support pollinators.
