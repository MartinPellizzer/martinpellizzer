Valerianella c

Valerianella c, commonly known as garden sorrel, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy and have a tangy, sour taste due to the presence of oxalic acid. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Valerianella c is often grown as a decorative and edible plant in gardens.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.
