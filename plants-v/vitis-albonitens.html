Vitis albonitens

Vitis albonitens, commonly known as the white-veined grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and Georgia. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with prominent white veins and small, clustered green flowers. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Vitis albonitens is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils.
