Vernonia samfyana

Vernonia samfyana, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is known for its dark green, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Vernonia samfyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
