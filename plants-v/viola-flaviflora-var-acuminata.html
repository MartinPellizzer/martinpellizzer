Viola flaviflora var. acuminata

Viola flaviflora var. acuminata, commonly known as the slender yellow violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers with distinctive purple markings. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
