Velleia discophora

Velleia discophora, commonly known as the small-leaf velleia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often covered in fine hairs.
