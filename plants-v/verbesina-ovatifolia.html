Verbesina ovatifolia

Verbesina ovatifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf beggar-tick, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its ovate, dark green leaves and produces showy, yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant is often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as wetlands and along stream banks. It has hairy stems and is known for its ability to thrive in wet conditions.

The species is also noted for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
