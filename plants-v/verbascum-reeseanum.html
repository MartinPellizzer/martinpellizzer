Verbascum reeseanum

Verbascum reeseanum, commonly known as the Armenian mullein, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in soft, velvety hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Verbascum reeseanum is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat respiratory ailments.
