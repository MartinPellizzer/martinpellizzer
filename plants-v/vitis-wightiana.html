Vitis wightiana

Vitis wightiana, commonly known as the Wight's grapevine, is a woody vine native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by clusters of edible, sweet berries. It thrives in temperate to subtropical climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
