Veronica grosseserrata

Veronica grosseserrata, commonly known as the roughleaf speedwell, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features upright stems that bear opposite pairs of lance-shaped leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

Veronica grosseserrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
