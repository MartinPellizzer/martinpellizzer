Valeriana moyanoi

Valeriana moyanoi, commonly known as the Armenian valerian, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or woodland soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its root system is known for containing compounds that have been traditionally used in herbal medicine.
