Viola lobata subsp. integrifolia

Viola lobata subsp.

integrifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a smooth, uninterrupted margin, giving it its subspecies name "integrifolia.". Its flowers are usually purple or violet, with five petals and a distinctive beak-like spur.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
