Vepris drummondii

Vepris drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's lime, is a shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces yellowish-green fruits that are edible and have a citrus-like flavor. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Vepris drummondii thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.
