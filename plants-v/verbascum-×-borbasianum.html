Verbascum × borbasianum

Verbascum × borbasianum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant belonging to the Scrophulariaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are typically gray-green in color, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is native to regions of southern Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Verbascum × borbasianum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
