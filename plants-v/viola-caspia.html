Viola caspia

Viola caspia, commonly known as the Caspian violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces violet-blue flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and forest edges. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola caspia is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
