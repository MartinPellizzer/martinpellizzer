Verbesina pterophora

Verbesina pterophora, commonly known as the winged beggar-tick, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy yellow flowers. The plant has distinctive winged stems and leaves with a rough, hairy texture. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along streambanks.

Verbesina pterophora is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its anti-inflammatory effects.
