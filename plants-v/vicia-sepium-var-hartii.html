Vicia sepium var. hartii

Vicia sepium var. hartii, commonly known as Hart's hairy vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility.

Its adaptability makes it a useful plant for ecological restoration and erosion control.
