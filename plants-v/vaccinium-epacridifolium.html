Vaccinium epacridifolium

Vaccinium epacridifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved huckleberry, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as blueberries and cranberries. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and it produces small, edible berries that are often used in culinary applications.

This species thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.
