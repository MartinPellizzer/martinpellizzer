Viola × cracoviensis

Viola × cracoviensis, commonly known as the Cracow violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant is native to Central Europe and is often found in woodland habitats. It prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for shaded garden beds.

Viola × cracoviensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
