Viola tricolor var. cantiana

Viola tricolor var. cantiana, commonly known as the three-color violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces showy flowers in shades of violet, white, and yellow. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for garden borders and woodland gardens. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
