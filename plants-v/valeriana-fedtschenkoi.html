Valeriana fedtschenkoi

Valeriana fedtschenkoi, commonly known as the Far Eastern valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes and roots are used in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.
