Viguiera retifolia

Viguiera retifolia, commonly known as Mexican heather, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, silver-gray foliage that is aromatic and often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Viguiera retifolia is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
