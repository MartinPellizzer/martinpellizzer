Verbena russellii

Verbena russellii, commonly known as Russell's verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender stems and opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy. The plant produces showy clusters of blue to purple flowers, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies adds ecological value to gardens.
