Viola odorata subsp. maderensis

Viola odorata subsp.

maderensis, commonly known as the Mader's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, violet-colored flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly larger leaves and more robust stems compared to other viola species.
