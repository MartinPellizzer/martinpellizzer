Viburnum rafinesqueanum var. affine

Viburnum rafinesqueanum var. affine, commonly known as the Allegheny blackhaw, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, which are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue, edible berries in the fall. Its bark is characterized by deep furrows and a reddish-brown color, which becomes more pronounced with age.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
