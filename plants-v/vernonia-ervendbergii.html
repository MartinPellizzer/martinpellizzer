Vernonia ervendbergii

Vernonia ervendbergii, commonly known as the Karoo Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. It features upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves, and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
