Vepris africana

Vepris africana, commonly known as African rosewood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its durability and fragrance. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange or red fruits containing winged seeds.

The tree is often found in moist forests and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
