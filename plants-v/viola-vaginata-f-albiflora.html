Viola vaginata f. albiflora

Viola vaginata f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered violet, is a flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and small, lanceolate leaves.

The flowers bloom in early spring and are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.
