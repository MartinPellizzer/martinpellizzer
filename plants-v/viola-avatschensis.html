Viola avatschensis

Viola avatschensis, commonly known as the Avatscha violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and small, colorful flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and cool climates. Its flowers, which range from purple to blue, bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to its natural environment.

Viola avatschensis is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
