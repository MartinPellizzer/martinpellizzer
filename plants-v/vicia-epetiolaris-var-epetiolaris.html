Vicia epetiolaris var. epetiolaris

Vicia epetiolaris var. epetiolaris, commonly known as the narrow-leaf vetch, is a perennial legume native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its seeds are enclosed in pods that mature in late summer, contributing to its role in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in sustainable landscaping.
