Viola × hyrcanica

Viola × hyrcanica, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Viola lutea and Viola wittrockii, combining traits from both parents. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, violet-colored flowers. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Viola × hyrcanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
