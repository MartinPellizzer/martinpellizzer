Veronicastrum rubellum

Veronicastrum rubellum, commonly known as redstem verbena or redstem false verbena, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features tall, erect stems that are typically reddish in color, especially when mature. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
