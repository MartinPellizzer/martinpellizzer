Viola xanthopetala

Viola xanthopetala, commonly known as the yellow violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy yellow flowers with a distinctive purple throat. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
