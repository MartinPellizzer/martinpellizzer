Vernonia andohii

Vernonia andohii, commonly known as the Madagascar ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust growth habit. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its foliage is usually gray-green, with rough, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss.

Vernonia andohii produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that bloom in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value in botanical gardens.
