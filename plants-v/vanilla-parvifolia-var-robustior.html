Vanilla parvifolia var. robustior

Vanilla parvifolia var. robustior, commonly known as the robust vanilla, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It produces fragrant, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically pollinated by specific bee species. The plant's most notable feature is its dark brown to black, aromatic pods, which are used to make vanilla extract. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and partial shade.

The variety is valued for its strong vanilla aroma and is often cultivated for commercial use.
