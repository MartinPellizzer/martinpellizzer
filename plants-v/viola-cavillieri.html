Viola cavillieri

Viola cavillieri, commonly known as the Italian violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or woodland habitats and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola cavillieri is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
