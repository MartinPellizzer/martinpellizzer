Vallisneria octandra

Vallisneria octandra, commonly known as narrow-leaf waterweed, is a flowering aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface, with the lower portion submerged. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that develop underwater, followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. Vallisneria octandra is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and lakes.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to water organisms.
