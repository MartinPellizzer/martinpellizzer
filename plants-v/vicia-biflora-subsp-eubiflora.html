Vicia biflora subsp. eubiflora

Vicia biflora subsp.

eubiflora, commonly known as the European vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, often forming dense mats. It is a perennial species that produces small, lens-shaped pods containing several seeds. Vicia biflora subsp.

eubiflora is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant in certain habitats.
