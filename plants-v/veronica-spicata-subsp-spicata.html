Veronica spicata subsp. spicata

Veronica spicata subsp.

spicata, commonly known as upright speedwell, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, cylindrical flower spikes that are often used in gardens for their ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This subspecies is valued for its hardiness and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
