Verbascum henriquesii var. ramosum

Verbascum henriquesii var. ramosum, commonly known as the hairy mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in dense, soft hairs that give it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and also covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
