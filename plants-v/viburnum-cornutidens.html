Viburnum cornutidens

Viburnum cornutidens, commonly known as the horned viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are clustered in flat-topped umbels. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue berries in the fall. Viburnum cornutidens is valued for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.
