Viola eizanensis

Viola eizanensis, commonly known as the Eizan violet, is a rare species of flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and small, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and mountainous regions. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a subtle touch of color to its natural habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
