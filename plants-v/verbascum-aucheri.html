Verbascum aucheri

Verbascum aucheri, commonly known as Woolly Mullein, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Verbascum aucheri is also valued for its medicinal properties and historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
