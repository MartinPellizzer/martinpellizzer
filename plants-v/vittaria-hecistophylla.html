Vittaria hecistophylla

Vittaria hecistophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and have a distinctive, feathery appearance with a pale underside.

Vittaria hecistophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
