Vaccinium dunalianum var. calycinum

Vaccinium dunalianum var. calycinum, commonly known as the dwarf blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the coastal regions of the Pacific Northwest.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, rich in antioxidants and nutrients. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires ample sunlight for optimal growth. It is often found in moist, sandy or rocky habitats along the coastline.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for both ecological and culinary purposes.
