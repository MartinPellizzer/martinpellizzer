Vicia sativa var. serotina

Vicia sativa var. serotina, commonly known as late vetch, is a flowering plant species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are typically dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

This variety is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural settings.
