Veronica longiracemosa

Veronica longiracemosa, commonly known as the long raceme speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a hairy texture.

Veronica longiracemosa is often found in rocky or gravelly soils in open habitats such as roadsides and woodland edges.
