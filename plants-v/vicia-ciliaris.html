Vicia ciliaris

Vicia ciliaris, commonly known as the woolly vetch, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its trifoliate leaves consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with hairy surfaces.

Vicia ciliaris is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
