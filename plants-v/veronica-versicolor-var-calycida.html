Veronica versicolor var. calycida

Veronica versicolor var. calycida, commonly known as the California speedwell, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
