Viola × tourbillonensis

Viola × tourbillonensis, commonly known as the whirlwind violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from purple to blue. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers often have a distinctive downward-facing orientation. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

Viola × tourbillonensis is a popular choice for rock gardens and as a bedding plant due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
