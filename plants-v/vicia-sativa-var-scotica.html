Vicia sativa var. scotica

Vicia sativa var. scotica, commonly known as Scottish vetch, is a perennial legume native to the British Isles.

It features slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.
