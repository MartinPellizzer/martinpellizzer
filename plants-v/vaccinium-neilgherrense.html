Vaccinium neilgherrense

Vaccinium neilgherrense, commonly known as the Nilgiri blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the high-altitude regions of the Western Ghats in India.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acidic soil-loving plants. The plant produces small, oval berries that are typically blue or purple in color when mature. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.

It thrives in cool, moist climates and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.
