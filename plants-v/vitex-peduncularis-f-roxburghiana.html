Vitex peduncularis f. roxburghiana

Vitex peduncularis f. roxburghiana, commonly known as the Roxburgh's chaste tree, is a botanical variant of the chaste tree species.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of fragrant, blue-violet flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy flowers and autumn foliage, which display vibrant hues of red and orange.
