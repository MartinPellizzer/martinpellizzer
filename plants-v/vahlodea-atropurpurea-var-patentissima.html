Vahlodea atropurpurea var. patentissima

Vahlodea atropurpurea var. patentissima, commonly known as the purple vahlodea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are lanceolate and often tinged with purple, while its stems are erect and hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive species within the Apiaceae family.
