Vernonia erythrophiloides

Vernonia erythrophiloides, commonly known as the red-leaved ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs and have a reddish tinge when mature, giving it its common name.

The flowers are small, yellowish to brownish, and are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.
