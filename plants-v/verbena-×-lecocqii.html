Verbena × lecocqii

Verbena × lecocqii, commonly known as the hybrid verbena, is a flowering plant belonging to the Verbenaceae family.

It is a hybrid between Verbena rigida and Verbena bonariensis, resulting in a plant that exhibits characteristics of both parents. The plant is known for its clusters of small, brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of blue, purple, and pink. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Verbena × lecocqii prefers full sun and well-drained soil, making it a versatile choice for various garden settings.
