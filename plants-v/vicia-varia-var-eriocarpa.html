Vicia varia var. eriocarpa

Vicia varia var. eriocarpa, commonly known as the woolly horsegram, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces cylindrical pods that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky habitats.

Vicia varia var. eriocarpa is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in agricultural settings.
