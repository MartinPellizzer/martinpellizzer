Vernonia confusa

Vernonia confusa, commonly known as the rough vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to brownish yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Vernonia confusa is often found in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and roadsides.

Its fibrous roots and drought tolerance make it a hardy species adapted to various environmental conditions.
