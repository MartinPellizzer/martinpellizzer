Veronica longifolia var. linneana

Veronica longifolia var. linneana, commonly known as the Linnean speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, arranged oppositely along the stem. This variety is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
