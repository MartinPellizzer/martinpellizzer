Vicia flaccida

Vicia flaccida, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. Vicia flaccida is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including meadows and waste areas.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility through its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
