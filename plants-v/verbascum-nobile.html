Verbascum nobile

Verbascum nobile, commonly known as the noble mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Verbascum nobile is a perennial plant that is both ornamental and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
