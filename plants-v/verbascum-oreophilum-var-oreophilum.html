Verbascum oreophilum var. oreophilum

Verbascum oreophilum var. oreophilum, commonly known as the alpine mullein, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a loose, elongated inflorescence. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and dry slopes.

Verbascum oreophilum var. oreophilum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
