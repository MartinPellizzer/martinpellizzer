Vriesea bleheri

Vriesea bleheri, commonly known as the rainbow bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that resemble petals, often displaying shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features a central rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that collect water, providing a habitat for small organisms. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, emerging from the leaf axils.

Vriesea bleheri thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity to flourish.
