Veronica hederifolia var. lucorum

Veronica hederifolia var. lucorum, commonly known as the creeping speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, sky-blue flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for ground cover in garden settings.
