Vicia mathewsii var. lessonii

Vicia mathewsii var. lessonii, commonly known as the Lesson's vetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Vicia mathewsii var. lessonii plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
