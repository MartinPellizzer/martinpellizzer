Viburnum lantana var. europaeum

Viburnum lantana var. europaeum, commonly known as European arrowwood, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe.

It typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters, featuring dense, upright branches and opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green above and pale underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in late spring to early summer, followed by bright red berries in the fall.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its structure and seasonal interest.
