Vernonia desiliens

Vernonia desiliens, commonly known as the slender ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks, thriving in moist, sandy soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from yellow to orange in color.

Vernonia desiliens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
