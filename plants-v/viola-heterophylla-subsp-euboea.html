Viola heterophylla subsp. euboea

Viola heterophylla subsp.

euboea, commonly known as the Euboean violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in woodland or open habitats.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces violet flowers with five petals, usually in shades of purple or blue. It is characterized by its variable leaf morphology, which can range from ovate to trifoliate.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
