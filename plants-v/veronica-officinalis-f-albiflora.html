Veronica officinalis f. albiflora

Veronica officinalis f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered speedwell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces white or pale blue flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
