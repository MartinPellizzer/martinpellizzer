Vaccinium summifaucis

Vaccinium summifaucis, commonly known as the highbush blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other acid-loving plants like heathers and rhododendrons. The plant is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and clusters of small, elliptical leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants.

Vaccinium summifaucis thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental and culinary value.
