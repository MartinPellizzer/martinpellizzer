Vachellia pringlei

Vachellia pringlei, commonly known as the African bristlenut, is a leguminous shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate, and its yellowish to orange-pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and it has a deep root system that helps it survive in drought conditions.

Vachellia pringlei is also known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable for soil enrichment in degraded landscapes.
