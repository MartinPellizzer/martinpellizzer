Vincetoxicum atrum

Vincetoxicum atrum, commonly known as black nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring upright stems and alternate, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or purple, which bloom in late summer. Its berries, which are typically black when mature, are toxic to humans and animals.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other toxic plants like belladonna and tomato.
