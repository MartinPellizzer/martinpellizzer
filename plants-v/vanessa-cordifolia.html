Vanessa cordifolia

Vanessa cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces a unique fruit that is often used in traditional medicine and culinary practices. Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and greenhouses.

Vanessa cordifolia thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.
