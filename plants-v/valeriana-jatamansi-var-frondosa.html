Valeriana jatamansi var. frondosa

Valeriana jatamansi var. frondosa, commonly known as Himalayan valerian, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It features a stout, woody root system and lanceolate, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and has traditional medicinal uses in Ayurvedic and Tibetan medicine.
