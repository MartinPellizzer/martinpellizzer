Viscum nervosum

Viscum nervosum, commonly known as the silky mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in the Mediterranean region.

It has small, leathery leaves that are usually covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are typically inconspicuous and followed by small, fleshy berries. Viscum nervosum relies on its host for water and nutrients, while it provides shelter and food for certain insects and birds.

Its life cycle involves both vegetative growth and seed dispersal by birds that consume its berries.
