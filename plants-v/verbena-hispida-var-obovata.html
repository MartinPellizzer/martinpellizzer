Verbena hispida var. obovata

Verbena hispida var. obovata, commonly known as the rough verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and clusters of vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and wildlife gardens.
