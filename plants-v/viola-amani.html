Viola amani

Viola amani, commonly known as the Amani violet, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Viola amani is known for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Its flowers bloom in spring, adding a splash of color to its natural habitat.
