Vincetoxicum officinale var. petrophilum

Vincetoxicum officinale var. petrophilum, commonly known as the rock-loving devil's bit.

This variant is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions, characterized by its trailing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. It produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained conditions.
