Veronica cilicica

Veronica cilicica, commonly known as the Cilician speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Veronica cilicica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, early spring blooms.
