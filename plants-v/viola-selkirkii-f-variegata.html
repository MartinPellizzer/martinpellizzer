Viola selkirkii f. variegata

Viola selkirkii f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated violet, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with striking variegation of white and green, enhancing its ornamental appeal. The plant produces delicate, spurred flowers in shades of violet or purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its foliage and flowers add a subtle, elegant touch to shaded landscapes.
