Verticordia minutiflora

Verticordia minutiflora, commonly known as the small-flowered feather liver, is a striking member of the Myrtaceae family native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, feathery inflorescences that bear small, urn-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
