Vaccinium meyenianum

Vaccinium meyenianum, commonly known as the red huckleberry, is a species of shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisine. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions.

The plant plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity.
