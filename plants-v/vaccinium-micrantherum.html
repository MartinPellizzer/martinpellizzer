Vaccinium micrantherum

Vaccinium micrantherum, commonly known as the dwarf blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by edible berries that are dark blue when ripe. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
