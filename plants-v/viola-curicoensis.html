Viola curicoensis

Viola curicoensis, commonly known as the Curico violet, is a species of flowering plant native to Chile.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or along stream banks. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Viola curicoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
