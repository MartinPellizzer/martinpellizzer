Vitis bororensis

Vitis bororensis, commonly known as the Bororensis grapevine, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a vigorous vine that produces small, dark berries with a distinctive flavor profile. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential in viticulture.

However, it is less commonly cultivated compared to other grape species due to its specific environmental requirements.
