Veronica hederifolia var. praestabilis

Veronica hederifolia var. praestabilis, commonly known as the creeping speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shady garden settings.

Its attractive appearance makes it a popular choice for ground cover in horticulture.
