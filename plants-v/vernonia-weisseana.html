Vernonia weisseana

Vernonia weisseana, commonly known as the white vernonia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its striking white to pale yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Vernonia weisseana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
