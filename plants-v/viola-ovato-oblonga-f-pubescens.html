Viola ovato-oblonga f. pubescens

Viola ovato-oblonga f. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent heartleaf violet, is a flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its ovate-oblong leaves that are densely covered with soft, glandular hairs, giving it a pubescent appearance. The flowers are typically violet or purple, with five petals, and are borne on short stems. This variety is native to parts of East Asia, including China and Japan, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a hardy perennial in suitable climates.
