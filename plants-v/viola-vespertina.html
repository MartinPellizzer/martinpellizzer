Viola vespertina

Viola vespertina, commonly known as the evening violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and shaded gardens, preferring moist, well-drained soil. Its flowers emit a subtle fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola vespertina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
