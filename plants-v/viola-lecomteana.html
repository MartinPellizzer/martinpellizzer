Viola lecomteana

Viola lecomteana, commonly known as Lecomte's violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of violet flowers with yellow centers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas and thrives in well-drained soil. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola lecomteana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
