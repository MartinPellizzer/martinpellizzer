Viola mandshurica subsp. nagasakiensis

Viola mandshurica subsp.

nagasakiensis, commonly known as the Nagasaki violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan. It typically grows in forested areas and along stream banks, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces violet-blue or purple flowers in early spring. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.

The subspecies is distinguished by its compact growth habit and unique floral characteristics compared to other viola species.
