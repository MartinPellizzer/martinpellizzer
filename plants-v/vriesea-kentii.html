Vriesea kentii

Vriesea kentii, commonly known as the Kent's Vriesea, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescence that resembles a pine cone. The plant features a rosette of sword-shaped, evergreen leaves that can grow up to 30 inches in length. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Vriesea kentii is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its vibrant appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
