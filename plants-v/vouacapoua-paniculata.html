Vouacapoua paniculata

Vouacapoua paniculata, commonly known as the yellow tamarind tree, is a tropical species native to South America.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, featuring a dense canopy and smooth, greyish-brown bark. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
