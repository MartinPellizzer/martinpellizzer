Veprecella riparia

Veprecella riparia, commonly known as the riparian water lily, is a aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters such as ponds and streams. The plant has broad, floating leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to prevent water loss. Its flowers are white or pale pink, with a distinct central boss of stamens.

Veprecella riparia plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
