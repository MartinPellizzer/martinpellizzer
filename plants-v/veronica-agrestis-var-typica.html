Veronica agrestis var. typica

Veronica agrestis var. typica, commonly known as the common speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a perennial herb with erect, branched stems that often grow in moist meadows and woodland edges. The plant features small, blue or violet flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Veronica agrestis var. typica is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
