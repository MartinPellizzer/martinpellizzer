Vitex wittrockiana

Vitex wittrockiana, commonly known as the common Vitex or chaste tree, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of purple, pink, or white, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its adaptability and aesthetic appeal contribute to its widespread cultivation in temperate climates.
