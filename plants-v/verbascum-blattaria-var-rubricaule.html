Verbascum blattaria var. rubricaule

Verbascum blattaria var. rubricaule, commonly known as the red-hairy mullein, is a striking plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem covered in dense, soft, reddish-hairy foliage. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its unique appearance and ecological benefits make it a valuable addition to botanical collections and native plant gardens.
