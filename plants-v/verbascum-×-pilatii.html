Verbascum × pilatii

Verbascum × pilatii, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes species known for their large, showy flowers and fuzzy leaves. The hybrid is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
