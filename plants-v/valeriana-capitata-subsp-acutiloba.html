Valeriana capitata subsp. acutiloba

Valeriana capitata subsp.

acutiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved valerian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia and the Americas. It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers.

The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture. This species is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open habitats.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
