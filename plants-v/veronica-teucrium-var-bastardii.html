Veronica teucrium var. bastardii

Veronica teucrium var. bastardii, commonly known as bastard speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has opposite, linear to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or shaded areas.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
