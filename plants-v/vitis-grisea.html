Vitis grisea

Vitis grisea, commonly known as the gray grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the family Vitaceae.

It is native to North America, particularly found in the eastern United States. The plant is characterized by its woody vines, heart-shaped leaves, and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in the spring. Vitis grisea produces small, round berries that are typically green when mature.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can also be used in the production of certain types of wine.
