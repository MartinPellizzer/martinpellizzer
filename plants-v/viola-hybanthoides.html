Viola hybanthoides

Viola hybanthoides, commonly known as the yellow violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola hybanthoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
