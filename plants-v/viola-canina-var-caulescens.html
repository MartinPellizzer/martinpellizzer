Viola canina var. caulescens

Viola canina var. caulescens, commonly known as the creeping violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow beard on the lower lip, which is a characteristic feature of the Viola genus. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its flowers bloom in spring, and the plant is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate appearance.
