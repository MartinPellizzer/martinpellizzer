Viola arborescens var. latifolia

Viola arborescens var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf woodsorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, broad leaves with a distinctive yellowish or white underside, and produces small, showy flowers in shades of purple or violet. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has a fibrous root system. It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
