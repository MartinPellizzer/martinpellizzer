Vernonia corymbulosa

Vernonia corymbulosa, commonly known as the clustered ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its dense, showy clusters of yellow to orange-red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and swampy areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough, hairy texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
