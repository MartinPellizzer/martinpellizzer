Virola fosteri

Virola fosteri, commonly known as the Amazonian ironwood, is a large evergreen tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. The tree produces distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by large, fleshy fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife. Its leaves are broad and glossy, contributing to its dense canopy and providing shade in the undergrowth.

Virola fosteri plays a vital role in the ecological balance of its habitat, supporting biodiversity and maintaining forest structure.
