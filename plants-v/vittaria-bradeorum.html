Vittaria bradeorum

Vittaria bradeorum, commonly known as the Brazilian fern, is a species of fern native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the family Vittariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often on rocks or tree trunks. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Vittaria bradeorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its habitat.
