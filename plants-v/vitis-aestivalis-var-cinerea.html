Vitis aestivalis var. cinerea

Vitis aestivalis var. cinerea, commonly known as the silverleaf muscadine, is a variety of the muscadine grape native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-gray pubescence on the leaves, which gives it its common name. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a deep green upper surface. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are usually dark purple to black when mature.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some use in local winemaking.
