Vicia leavenworthii var. occidentalis

Vicia leavenworthii var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western vetch, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is a perennial legume that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features trifoliate leaves with small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several small, rounded seeds.

This species is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
