Verbascum × amaliense

Verbascum × amaliense, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes various species of flowering plants known for their fuzzy, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its robust growth and long flowering period.
