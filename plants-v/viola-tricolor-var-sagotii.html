Viola tricolor var. sagotii

Viola tricolor var. sagotii, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a compact, low-growing perennial species native to Japan.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive dark green upper surface and a lighter, often hairy underside. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of violet, white, and yellow, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact size and delicate floral display.
