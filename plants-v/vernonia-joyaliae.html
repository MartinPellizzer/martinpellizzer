Vernonia joyaliae

Vernonia joyaliae, commonly known as the flame violet, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in dense clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Vernonia joyaliae is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique appearance.
