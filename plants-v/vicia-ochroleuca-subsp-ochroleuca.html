Vicia ochroleuca subsp. ochroleuca

Vicia ochroleuca subsp.

ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow vetch, is a perennial legume native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets.

The flowers are typically yellow or pale yellow, arranged in loose, elongated clusters. The plant produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds.

It is often found in grasslands, disturbed soils, and along roadsides, and is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability.
