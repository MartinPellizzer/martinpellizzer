Viola delphinantha subsp. kosaninii

Viola delphinantha subsp.

kosaninii, commonly known as the Kosanin's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, particularly Russia and China. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves and delicate, violet-blue flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the species is noted for its ornamental value in gardens.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly smaller flowers and more compact growth habit compared to other viola species.
