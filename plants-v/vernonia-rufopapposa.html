Vernonia rufopapposa

Vernonia rufopapposa, commonly known as the red pappus Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, yellow to orange flower heads that attract pollinators. Its bracts are reddish and papery, giving it its common name.

Vernonia rufopapposa is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodlands.
