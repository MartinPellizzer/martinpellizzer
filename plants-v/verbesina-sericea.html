Verbesina sericea

Verbesina sericea, commonly known as silky beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early fall. Its leaves are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and along streambanks.
