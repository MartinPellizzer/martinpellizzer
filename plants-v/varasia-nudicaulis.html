Varasia nudicaulis

Varasia nudicaulis, commonly known as the naked-stemmed varasia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairless stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
