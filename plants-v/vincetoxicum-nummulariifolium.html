Vincetoxicum nummulariifolium

Vincetoxicum nummulariifolium, commonly known as the penny leaf elder, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with ovate to round leaflets that give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the spring. Its berries are red and toxic, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.

It is often found in moist woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade.
