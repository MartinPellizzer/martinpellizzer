Vachellia bravoensis

Vachellia bravoensis, commonly known as the Bravo acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of northern Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny branches and small, bipinnate leaves that reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces yellow, globular inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seeds are encased in a hard, woody pod, which opens to release the seeds when mature.

Vachellia bravoensis plays a vital role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat in its native ecosystems.
