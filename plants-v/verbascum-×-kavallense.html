Verbascum × kavallense

Verbascum × kavallense, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes several species of flowering plants known for their fuzzy, velvety leaves. The hybrid is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
