Valeriana himalayana

Valeriana himalayana, commonly known as Himalayan valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Himalayas.

It typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky slopes, thriving in cool and temperate climates. The plant features erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes, and are usually pink or white in color.

The roots of Valeriana himalayana are often harvested for their aromatic properties and traditional medicinal uses.
