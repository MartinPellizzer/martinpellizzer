Viola patagonica

Viola patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian violet, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Viola patagonica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
