Verbascum blattaria f. albiflorum

Verbascum blattaria f. albiflorum, commonly known as the whiteflower mullein, is a variant of the common mullein plant.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
