Vella aspera

Vella aspera, commonly known as the rough vella, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showing a high degree of drought tolerance. Its flowers bloom in the spring and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

Vella aspera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
