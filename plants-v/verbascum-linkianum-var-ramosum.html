Verbascum linkianum var. ramosum

Verbascum linkianum var. ramosum, commonly known as the Armenian mullein, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a raceme at the top of the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
