Verbascum parsana

Verbascum parsana, commonly known as the Armenian mullein, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Verbascum parsana is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
