Viburnum tinoides var. venezuelense

Viburnum tinoides var. venezuelense, commonly known as the Venezuelan arrowwood, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It features opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are typically dark green above and lighter beneath. The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
