Verbena urticifolia f. simplex

Verbena urticifolia f. simplex, commonly known as the smoothleaf verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are simple and often hairy, with a slightly rough texture.

This variety is distinguished by its smooth, less pubescent leaves compared to other forms of Verbena urticifolia.
