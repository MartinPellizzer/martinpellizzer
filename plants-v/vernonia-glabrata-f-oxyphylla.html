Vernonia glabrata f. oxyphylla

Vernonia glabrata f. oxyphylla, commonly known as the silky vernonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant is distinguished by its narrow, silvery-green leaves and small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique foliage and delicate flowers make it a valuable addition to both native and cultivated landscapes.
