Vernonia proclivis

Vernonia proclivis, commonly known as the leaning ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as swamps and marshes, and thrives in moist, sandy soils. It produces striking yellow to orange flower heads that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and the plant can reach heights of up to 2 meters.
