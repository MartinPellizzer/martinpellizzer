Verbascum agastachyum

Verbascum agastachyum, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in dense, soft, woolly hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are large, broad, and also covered in a fuzzy texture, contributing to its overall ornamental value.

Verbascum agastachyum is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
