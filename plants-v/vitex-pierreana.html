Vitex pierreana

Vitex pierreana, commonly known as the African bluecurls, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its aromatic leaves and striking blue-violet flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Vitex pierreana is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
