Viola × neoburgensis

Viola × neoburgensis, commonly known as the Viola of Vienna, is a hybrid species in the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is native to Central Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is known for its adaptability and is used in horticulture for garden landscaping.
