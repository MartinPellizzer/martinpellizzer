Viola tenuis var. geralensis

Viola tenuis var. geralensis, commonly known as the Geralense violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Brazil.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of violet and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique and sought-after species in horticultural collections.
