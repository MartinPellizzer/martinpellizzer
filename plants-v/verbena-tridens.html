Verbena tridens

Verbena tridens, commonly known as coastal verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The flowers are typically blue to violet in color, with five petals and a distinctive central tube. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found growing along coastal areas and in dry, sandy habitats.

Verbena tridens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
