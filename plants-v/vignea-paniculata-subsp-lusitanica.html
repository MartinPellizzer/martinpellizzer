Vignea paniculata subsp. lusitanica

Vignea paniculata subsp.

lusitanica, commonly known as the Iberian vetch, is a leguminous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
