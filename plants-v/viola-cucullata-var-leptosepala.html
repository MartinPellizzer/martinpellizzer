Viola cucullata var. leptosepala

Viola cucullata var. leptosepala, commonly known as the slender-sepaled violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive violet or purple coloration. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of violet, blue, or white, typically blooming in early spring. Its sepals are narrow and elongated, giving it its specific epithet "leptosepala.".

It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded, rich soils.
