Vitex discolor

Vitex discolor, commonly known as the Chinese vitex or fragrant vitex, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white, pink, or purple, which bloom in the summer. Its fruit is a dry, two-valved capsule containing several small, dark seeds.

Vitex discolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
