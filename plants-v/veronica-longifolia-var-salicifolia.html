Veronica longifolia var. salicifolia

Veronica longifolia var. salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaved speedwell, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, willow-like leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens for its delicate appearance.
