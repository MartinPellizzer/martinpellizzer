Viburnum pauciflorum

Viburnum pauciflorum, commonly known as the few-flowered viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and features opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits in the fall. Viburnum pauciflorum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
