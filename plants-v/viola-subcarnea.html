Viola subcarnea

Viola subcarnea, commonly known as the pale violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically pale green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from pale purple to white, often with a darker throat. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Viola subcarnea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
