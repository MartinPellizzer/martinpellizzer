Vernonia caboverdeana

Vernonia caboverdeana, commonly known as the Cape Verde ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the island of Santiago in the Cape Verde archipelago.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered with fine hairs, and it has a fibrous root system.

Vernonia caboverdeana is valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.
