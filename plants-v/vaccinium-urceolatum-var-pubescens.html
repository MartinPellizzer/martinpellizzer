Vaccinium urceolatum var. pubescens

Vaccinium urceolatum var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent huckleberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or purple in color. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and potential culinary uses.
