Viola toroensis

Viola toroensis, commonly known as the showy viola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers with yellow markings, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Viola toroensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
