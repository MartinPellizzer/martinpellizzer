Veronica serpyllifolia

Veronica serpyllifolia, commonly known as common speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environments, making it a hardy species.

Botanically, it belongs to the Plantaginaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar characteristics.
