Verbascum phlomoides var. macranthum

Verbascum phlomoides var. macranthum, commonly known as the large-flowered mullein, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in soft, velvety gray-green leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in dense spikes, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
