Vaccinium claoxylon

Vaccinium claoxylon, commonly known as the New Zealand cranberry, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of bright red berries. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions. Its berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them popular in local cuisine and traditional uses.

The plant also plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
