Vernonia grandifolia

Vernonia grandifolia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features showy blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

Vernonia grandifolia is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
