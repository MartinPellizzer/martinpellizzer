Vaccinium kunstleri

Vaccinium kunstleri, commonly known as the Kunstler's blueberry, is a species of shrub native to the coastal regions of southern South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like heathers and blueberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, similar to other Vaccinium species. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
