Vicia sativa var. peregrina

Vicia sativa var. peregrina, commonly known as the wandering vetch, is a leguminous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces cylindrical pods containing several seeds, which are typically small and dark in color. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in waste areas.

This variety is known for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
