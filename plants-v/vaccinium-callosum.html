Vaccinium callosum

Vaccinium callosum, commonly known as the smooth blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, which are rich in antioxidants and flavor. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Vaccinium callosum is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
