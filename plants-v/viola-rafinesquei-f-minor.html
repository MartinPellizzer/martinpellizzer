Viola rafinesquei f. minor

Viola rafinesquei f. minor, commonly known as the dwarf violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and streambanks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
