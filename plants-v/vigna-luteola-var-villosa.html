Vigna luteola var. villosa

Vigna luteola var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its botanical variant name "villosa.". The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into cylindrical, edible pods containing seeds. It is often cultivated for its nutritional value, as the seeds are rich in protein and can be used in various culinary preparations.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
