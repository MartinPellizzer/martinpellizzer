Veronica × sekkauensis

Veronica × sekkauensis, commonly known as the hybrid speedwell, is a perennial plant native to the British Isles.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and produces small, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or shaded gardens.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy and attractive flowering plant.
