Vantanea occidentalis

Vantanea occidentalis, commonly known as the western vantanea, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects.

Vantanea occidentalis is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological contributions in its native environment.
