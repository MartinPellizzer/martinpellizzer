Vernonia spirei

Vernonia spirei, commonly known as the spirea vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage is usually gray-green, and it produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Vernonia spirei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
