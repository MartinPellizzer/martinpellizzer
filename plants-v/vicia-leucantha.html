Vicia leucantha

Vicia leucantha, commonly known as the white vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of Europe and western Asia, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, trailing stems with small, trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are typically white or pale purple, arranged in small clusters, and it produces lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds.

Vicia leucantha is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration efforts.
