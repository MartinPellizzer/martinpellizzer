Veronica saturejoides subsp. olympica

Veronica saturejoides subsp.

olympica, commonly known as Olympic speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is a low-growing perennial with lance-shaped leaves and clusters of blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight. It is often found in meadows, stream banks, and forest edges.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
