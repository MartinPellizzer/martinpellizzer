Viola pachyrhizonia

Viola pachyrhizonia, commonly known as the broad-leaved violet, is a flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features heart-shaped, broad leaves and produces small, delicate purple or violet flowers. Its botanical structure includes a short stem, hairy foliage, and a root system adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Viola pachyrhizonia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
