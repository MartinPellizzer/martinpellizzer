Vaccinium caracasanum f. acuminatum

Vaccinium caracasanum f. acuminatum, commonly known as the sharp-pointed huckleberry, is a botanical variant of the Vaccinium genus, which includes various species of blueberries and cranberries.

This particular form is characterized by its elongated, acutely pointed leaves and small, dark blue berries that ripen in the autumn. It is native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere, particularly in parts of Europe and Asia. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or heathland environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
