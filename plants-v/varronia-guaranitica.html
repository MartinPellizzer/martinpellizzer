Varronia guaranitica

Varronia guaranitica, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in spikes. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are orange-red when mature. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and riverbanks.

Varronia guaranitica is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in medicine.
