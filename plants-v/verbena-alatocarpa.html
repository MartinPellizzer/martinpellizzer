Verbena alatocarpa

Verbena alatocarpa, commonly known as the hairy verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Verbena alatocarpa is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
