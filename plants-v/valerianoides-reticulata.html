Valerianoides reticulata

Valerianoides reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined valerian, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Valerianaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has slender, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinct net-like venation pattern. Native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Valerianoides reticulata is often found in woodlands and meadows, where it plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
