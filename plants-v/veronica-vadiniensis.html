Veronica vadiniensis

Veronica vadiniensis, commonly known as the Vadiniensis Speedwell, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit with basal leaves and spikes of small blue or purple flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
