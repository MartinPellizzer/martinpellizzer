Vitis commersonii

Vitis commersonii, commonly known as the Commerson's grape, is a deciduous vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to dark purple as they mature. It is often found growing in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities.

Vitis commersonii is also notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in local ecosystems.
