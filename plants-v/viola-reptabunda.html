Viola reptabunda

Viola reptabunda, commonly known as the creeping violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly violet hue. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from pale purple to deep violet, often with a distinctive yellow eye-like mark. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Viola reptabunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
