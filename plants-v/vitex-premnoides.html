Vitex premnoides

Vitex premnoides, commonly known as the Indian Vitex or velvetwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy beneath. The plant produces panicle-like inflorescences with showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink or purple. Its fruits are small, woody drupes that contain a single seed.

Vitex premnoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of timber.
