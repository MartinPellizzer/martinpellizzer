Vernonia leprosa

Vernonia leprosa, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the United States and Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and dense clusters of yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Vernonia leprosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
