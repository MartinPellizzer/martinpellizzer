Vitis wrayi

Vitis wrayi, commonly known as Wray's grape, is a rare species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly found in Georgia and South Carolina. The plant is characterized by its tendrils, heart-shaped leaves, and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are berries that mature to a dark purple color, typically appearing in late summer.

Vitis wrayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens due to its rarity and unique appearance.
