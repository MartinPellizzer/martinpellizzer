Vigna vexillata var. macrosperma

Vigna vexillata var. macrosperma, commonly known as the large-seeded cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its seeds are typically large, oval, and have a smooth, shiny surface.

This variety is valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its nutritional content, making it an important crop in many parts of the world.
