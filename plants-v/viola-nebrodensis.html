Viola nebrodensis

Viola nebrodensis, commonly known as the Albanian violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, five-petaled flowers that range in color from purple to white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Viola nebrodensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
