Vignea muhlenbergii

Vignea muhlenbergii, commonly known as the Muhlenberg's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Vignea muhlenbergii thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in disturbed soils along roadsides and in open woodlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
