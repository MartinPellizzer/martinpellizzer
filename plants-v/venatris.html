Venatris

Venatris, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, triangular seed pods. Venatris is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is often considered an invasive species in some areas due to its aggressive spreading habit.
