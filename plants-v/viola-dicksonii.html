Viola dicksonii

Viola dicksonii, commonly known as the Dickson's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Chile.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers are usually violet or purple in color, with a distinctive pattern of darker markings.

Viola dicksonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
