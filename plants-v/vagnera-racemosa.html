Vagnera racemosa

Vagnera racemosa, commonly known as the Brazilian broom, is a shrub native to South America.

It features slender, branching stems covered in small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. These flowers are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits containing numerous seeds.

Vagnera racemosa is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
