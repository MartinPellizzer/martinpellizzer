Veronica anagallis-aquatica var. macra

Veronica anagallis-aquatica var. macra, commonly known as the water speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wet meadows, stream banks, and other moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions. The plant features small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting biodiversity in wetland habitats.
