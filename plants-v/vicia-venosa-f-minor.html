Vicia venosa f. minor

Vicia venosa f. minor, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. Vicia venosa f. minor is a perennial herb that is well adapted to temperate climates and is often found in regions with well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
