Vellozia bahiana

Vellozia bahiana, commonly known as the Brazilian hairpin fern, is a unique plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Velloziaceae and is characterized by its rosette-like arrangement of leaves. The plant has a succulent, stemless structure with thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. Its leaves are often covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Vellozia bahiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in dry gardens.
