Viola sylvestris var. barbata

Viola sylvestris var. barbata, commonly known as the hairy violet, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are often adorned with distinctive markings and bloom in early spring.

The variety is distinguished by its hairy stems and leaves, which provide it with a unique appearance.
