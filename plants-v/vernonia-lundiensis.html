Vernonia lundiensis

Vernonia lundiensis, commonly known as the Lundi's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with purple-tipped bracts.

Vernonia lundiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
