Vachellia horrida subsp. benadirensis

Vachellia horrida subsp.

benadirensis, commonly known as the Benadire acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, thorny foliage and yellow flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant has small, leathery leaves that help reduce water loss in dry conditions. It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for wildlife. Vachellia horrida subsp.

benadirensis is also valued for its hard wood and traditional uses in local communities.
