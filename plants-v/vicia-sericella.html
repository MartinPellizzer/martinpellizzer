Vicia sericella

Vicia sericella, commonly known as the silver vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces small, flat pods containing several seeds, which are typically dispersed by wind. Native to Europe and parts of western Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Vicia sericella is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
