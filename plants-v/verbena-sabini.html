Verbena sabini

Verbena sabini, commonly known as scarlet verbena, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy clusters of tubular, brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Verbena sabini is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
