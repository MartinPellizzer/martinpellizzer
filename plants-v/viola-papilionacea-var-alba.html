Viola papilionacea var. alba

Viola papilionacea var. alba, commonly known as the white violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, hairy leaves and showy, white or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or stream banks. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with five petals arranged in a distinctive "papilionaceous" pattern, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both garden use and traditional medicinal purposes.
