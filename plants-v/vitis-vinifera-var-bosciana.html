Vitis vinifera var. bosciana

Vitis vinifera var. bosciana, commonly known as the Bosciana grape, is a lesser-known variety of grape native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, dark blue berries that are typically used for making jams and wines. The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It has a moderate growth rate and is relatively resistant to pests and diseases.

Its botanical features include compound leaves with five to seven leaflets and tendrils that aid in climbing.
