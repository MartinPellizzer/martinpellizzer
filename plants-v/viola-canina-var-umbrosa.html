Viola canina var. umbrosa

Viola canina var. umbrosa, commonly known as the shady violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a hairy texture and produces violet-blue flowers with yellow markings. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and meadows. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
