Vernonia senescens

Vernonia senescens, commonly known as the red vervain, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences composed of small, violet to reddish-purple flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Vernonia senescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
