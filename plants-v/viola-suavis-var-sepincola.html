Viola suavis var. sepincola

Viola suavis var. sepincola, commonly known as the sweet violet's coastal variant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive violet hue and produces clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, often found in rocky or coastal habitats. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery and traditional medicine.
