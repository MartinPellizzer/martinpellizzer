Vangueria rufescens subsp. angustiloba

Vangueria rufescens subsp.

angustiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved vangueria, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
