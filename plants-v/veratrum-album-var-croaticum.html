Veratrum album var. croaticum

Veratrum album var. croaticum, commonly known as the Croatian white hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, straight stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, bearing large, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The roots and leaves contain toxic alkaloids, making it a plant with both medicinal and hazardous properties.
