Veronicastrum villosulum var. parviflorum

Veronicastrum villosulum var. parviflorum, commonly known as the slender blueweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that often reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in cylindrical spikes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, hairy texture, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
