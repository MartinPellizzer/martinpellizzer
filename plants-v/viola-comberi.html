Viola comberi

Viola comberi, commonly known as the Comber's violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the landscape.

Viola comberi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
