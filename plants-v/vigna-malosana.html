Vigna malosana

Vigna malosana, commonly known as the Malosan bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and elongated pods. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces small, yellow flowers. Its seeds are edible and are often used in traditional cuisines.

Vigna malosana is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting agricultural systems.
