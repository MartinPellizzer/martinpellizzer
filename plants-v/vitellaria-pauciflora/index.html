Vitellaria Pauciflora

Shea tree, scientifically known as Vitellaria pauciflora, is a vital species native to the savannas of Africa. This tree is renowned for its economic and ecological importance, particularly in regions like Mali, Burkina Faso, and Ghana. Its fruit produces a valuable butter, which is widely used in cosmetics, food, and traditional medicine. The shea tree also plays a crucial role in soil conservation and supports local communities through sustainable harvesting. Despite its significance, it faces threats from deforestation and climate change, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
