Vaccinium × tescorum

Vaccinium × tescorum, commonly known as the crossblue huckleberry, is a hybrid evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is a natural cross between Vaccinium ovatum and Vaccinium philadelphicum, resulting in a plant with unique botanical characteristics. The species features dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green with a bluish tint, and it produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The berries are usually blue or black, with a tart flavor, and are often used in culinary applications.

This plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is commonly found in coastal and mountainous regions.
