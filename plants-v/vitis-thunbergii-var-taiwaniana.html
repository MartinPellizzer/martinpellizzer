Vitis thunbergii var. taiwaniana

Vitis thunbergii var. taiwaniana, commonly known as the Taiwan foxglove vine, is a climbing vine native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale green flowers. The plant produces small, round, yellowish berries that ripen in the fall. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
