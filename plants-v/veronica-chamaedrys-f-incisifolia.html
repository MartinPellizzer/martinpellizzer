Veronica chamaedrys f. incisifolia

Veronica chamaedrys f. incisifolia, commonly known as the creeping speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The foliage is characterized by its finely toothed, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This plant is also valued for its ability to spread and form dense mats, which help suppress weeds and prevent soil erosion.
