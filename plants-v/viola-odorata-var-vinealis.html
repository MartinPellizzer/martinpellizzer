Viola odorata var. vinealis

Viola odorata var. vinealis, commonly known as sweet violet vine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive violet hue and produces clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, often twining around supports, and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in herbal medicine.
