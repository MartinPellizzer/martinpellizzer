Verbesina parrasana

Verbesina parrasana, commonly known as the Mexican beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The plant is known for its ability to spread rapidly and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
