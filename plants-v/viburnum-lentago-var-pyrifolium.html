Viburnum lentago var. pyrifolium

Viburnum lentago var. pyrifolium, commonly known as the Pennsylvania crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically medium to dark green above and lighter green below. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem with thornless branches and a dense, rounded form.

It is often found in moist woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
