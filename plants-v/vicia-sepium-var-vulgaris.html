Vicia sepium var. vulgaris

Vicia sepium var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common hop-crown.

This plant is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by slender, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. The plant is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in hedgerows.

It has a fibrous root system and is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
