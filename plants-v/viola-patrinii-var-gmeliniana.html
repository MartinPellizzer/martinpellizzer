Viola patrinii var. gmeliniana

Viola patrinii var. gmeliniana, commonly known as the Gmelin's violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, hairy leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
