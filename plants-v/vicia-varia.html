Vicia varia

Vicia varia, commonly known as the variable vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to various environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Vicia varia is known for its variable leaf morphology, which contributes to its common name and makes it a subject of botanical interest.
