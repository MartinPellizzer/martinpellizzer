Vaccaria vulgaris

Vaccaria vulgaris, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing annual plant characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and clusters of tiny white flowers.

The plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, fields, and along roadsides. The leaves are often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance.
