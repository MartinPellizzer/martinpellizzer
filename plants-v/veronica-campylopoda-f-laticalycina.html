Veronica campylopoda f. laticalycina

Veronica campylopoda f. laticalycina, commonly known as the broad-leaved speedwell, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, oval-shaped leaves and produces clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream sides. It is native to regions of Europe and western Asia.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
