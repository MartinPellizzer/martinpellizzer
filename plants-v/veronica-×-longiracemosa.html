Veronica × longiracemosa

Veronica × longiracemosa, commonly known as the long raceme speedwell, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
