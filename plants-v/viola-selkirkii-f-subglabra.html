Viola selkirkii f. subglabra

Viola selkirkii f. subglabra, commonly known as the smooth-leaved violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are typically smooth and hairless, distinguishing it from other viola species. The plant produces delicate purple or violet flowers with five petals, often with a white or pale yellow throat. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Violaceae family.
