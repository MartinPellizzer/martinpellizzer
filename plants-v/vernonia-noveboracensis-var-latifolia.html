Vernonia noveboracensis var. latifolia

Vernonia noveboracensis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, cone-shaped flower heads that range in color from purple to pink, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to native plant gardens.

Its foliage and flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
