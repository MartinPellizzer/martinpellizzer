Viguiera asperrima

Viguiera asperrima, commonly known as the roughleaf coneflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and showy, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping. It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

Viguiera asperrima is also known for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
