Viola hirta subsp. foudrasii

Viola hirta subsp.

foudrasii, commonly known as the Algerian violet, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

The plant produces violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow throat, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
