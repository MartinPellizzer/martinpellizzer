Varasia infelix

Varasia infelix, commonly known as the unfortunate plant, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and small, pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Its botanical name reflects its historical association with misfortune in traditional folklore.

Varasia infelix is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in some traditional medicinal practices.
