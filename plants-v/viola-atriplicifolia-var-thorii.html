Viola atriplicifolia var. thorii

Viola atriplicifolia var. thorii, commonly known as the purple violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple underside, and its flowers are typically violet or purple, with five petals. The plant prefers moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rich, loamy soils.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
