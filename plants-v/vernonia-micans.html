Vernonia micans

Vernonia micans, commonly known as the silverleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and conical flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs that give it a distinctive appearance.

Vernonia micans is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local pollinators.
