Viola philippica

Viola philippica, commonly known as the Philippine violet, is a species of flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or mountainous regions. It has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy blooms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola philippica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
