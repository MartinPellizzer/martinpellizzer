Viola patrinii var. acuminata

Viola patrinii var. acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces delicate violet flowers with purple petals and yellow markings. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

The species is also noted for its ability to tolerate partial shade and its resilience in challenging climates.
