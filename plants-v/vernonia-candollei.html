Vernonia candollei

Vernonia candollei, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of bright yellow flower heads. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower structure. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia candollei is often found in open woodlands and prairies, thriving in well-drained soils.
