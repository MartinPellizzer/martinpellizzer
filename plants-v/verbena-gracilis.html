Verbena gracilis

Verbena gracilis, commonly known as slender verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, sunny environments such as wetlands and prairies. The plant features slender, erect stems and produces clusters of small, vibrant blue or purple flowers. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Verbena gracilis is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
