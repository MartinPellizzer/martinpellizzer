Verbena intermedia f. albiflora

Verbena intermedia f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered verbena, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems covered in narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, showy white or pale blue flowers during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its drought tolerance and long blooming period add ornamental value to garden landscapes.
