Verbena moricolor

Verbena moricolor, commonly known as the rainbow verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It produces vibrant, showy flowers in a range of colors, including red, orange, yellow, and purple. The plant is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
