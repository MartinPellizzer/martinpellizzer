Vicia bijuga

Vicia bijuga, commonly known as the narrow-leaf vetch, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, climbing stems and small, trifoliate leaves with a distinctive narrow shape. Its flowers are typically pale yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in clusters.

Vicia bijuga is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in ecological restoration efforts.
