Vaccinium lucidum var. pumilum

Vaccinium lucidum var. pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the northeastern United States.

It typically reaches heights of less than 30 centimeters, with small, oval leaves that are dark green and glossy on top. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which develop into edible, blue-black berries. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in culinary applications.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
