Viola praemorsa var. venosa

Viola praemorsa var. venosa, commonly known as the spotted violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with distinctive purple or brown markings. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of violet, blue, or white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
