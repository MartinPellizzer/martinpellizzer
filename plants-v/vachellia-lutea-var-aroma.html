Vachellia lutea var. aroma

Vachellia lutea var. aroma, commonly known as sweet acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, branching form and small, feathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that emit a sweet, aromatic fragrance, especially in the evening. Its seed pods are slender and woody, containing several angular seeds.

Vachellia lutea var. aroma is adapted to drought conditions and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of firewood.
