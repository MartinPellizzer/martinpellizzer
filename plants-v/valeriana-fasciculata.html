Valeriana fasciculata

Valeriana fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered valerian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Valeriana fasciculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its calming properties.
