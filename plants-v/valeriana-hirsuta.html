Valeriana hirsuta

Valeriana hirsuta, commonly known as hairy valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late summer. Valeriana hirsuta is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its calming effects.
