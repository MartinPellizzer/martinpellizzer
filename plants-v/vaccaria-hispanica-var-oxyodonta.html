Vaccaria hispanica var. oxyodonta

Vaccaria hispanica var. oxyodonta, commonly known as the hairy sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to August. The plant has a compact rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves that help it retain moisture in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it an ornamental and drought-tolerant species well adapted to Mediterranean climates.
