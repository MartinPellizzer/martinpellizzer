Verbena officinalis var. eremicola

Verbena officinalis var. eremicola, commonly known as the desert verbena, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that typically range in color from blue to purple. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Verbena officinalis var. eremicola is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in traditional medicine.
