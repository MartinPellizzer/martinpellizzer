Verapazia barbaeriana

Verapazia barbaeriana, commonly known as the Mexican orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the highlands of Mexico.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a single spike of flowers during the spring season.

Due to its limited habitat and threats from habitat destruction, it is considered a vulnerable species.
