Vernonia tavoyana

Vernonia tavoyana, commonly known as the Tavoyan ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, yellow to orange-red tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Vernonia tavoyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
