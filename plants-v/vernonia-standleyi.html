Vernonia standleyi

Vernonia standleyi, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and along riverbanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in terminal panicles.

Vernonia standleyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
