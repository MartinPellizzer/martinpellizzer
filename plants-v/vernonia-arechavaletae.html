Vernonia arechavaletae

Vernonia arechavaletae, commonly known as the Arechavaleta's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of individual flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is known for its tall, sturdy stems and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer, are usually a striking yellow or orange, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
