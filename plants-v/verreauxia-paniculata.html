Verreauxia paniculata

Verreauxia paniculata, commonly known as the African snowball, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring a rugged, woody stem and dense, rounded clusters of white flowers. The plant has pinnate leaves composed of several small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The flowers bloom in spring and summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds.
