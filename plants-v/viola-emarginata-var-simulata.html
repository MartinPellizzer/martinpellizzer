Viola emarginata var. simulata

Viola emarginata var. simulata, commonly known as the mimic violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, delicate flowers that bloom in shades of purple and violet. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers have five petals, with the lower petal often featuring a distinctive notch, which gives it its scientific name.

The species is notable for its ability to mimic other violets, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
