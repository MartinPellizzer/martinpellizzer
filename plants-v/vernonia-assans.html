Vernonia assans

Vernonia assans, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant has gray-green, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive white or silver appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Vernonia assans is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
