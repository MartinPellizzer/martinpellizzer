Veronica albanica

Veronica albanica, commonly known as the Albanian speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy areas and is adapted to temperate climates. It has lance-shaped leaves and is often found in mountainous regions.

Veronica albanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
