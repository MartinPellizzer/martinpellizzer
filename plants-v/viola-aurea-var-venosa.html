Viola aurea var. venosa

Viola aurea var. venosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically deep green with a distinctive bluish tinge. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of yellow and purple, often with a darker throat. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
