Vatica beccariana

Vatica beccariana, commonly known as the Beccarian nut.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

The species is valued for its timber and has ecological importance in its native habitats.
