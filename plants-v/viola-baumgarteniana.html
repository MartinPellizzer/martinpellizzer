Viola baumgarteniana

Viola baumgarteniana, commonly known as the Baumann's violet, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a touch of color to the landscape during the colder months.

Viola baumgarteniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
