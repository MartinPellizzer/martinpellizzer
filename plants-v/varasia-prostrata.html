Varasia prostrata

Varasia prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate varasia, is a low-growing flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically forms a dense mat of foliage close to the ground, with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
