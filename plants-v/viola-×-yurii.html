Viola × yurii

Viola × yurii, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a hybrid species belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments, such as woodland areas or gardens. It has a compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Viola × yurii is known for its ability to thrive in cool climates and its preference for well-drained soil.
