Vicia magellanica var. magellanica

Vicia magellanica var. magellanica, commonly known as the Magellan vetch, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial legume that typically grows in grasslands and disturbed areas. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters. Its pods contain several seeds, and it has a robust root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

Vicia magellanica var. magellanica is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a forage plant.
