Villaresia chilensis

Villaresia chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean bellflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the Andes mountains of Chile.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in the study of Andean flora.
