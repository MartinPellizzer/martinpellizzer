Vigna trichocarpa

Vigna trichocarpa, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, trifoliate leaves, and small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant is well adapted to warm climates and is often grown as a forage crop or for its nutritious seeds. Its flowers are typically pale yellow, and it has a shallow root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

Vigna trichocarpa is also valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
