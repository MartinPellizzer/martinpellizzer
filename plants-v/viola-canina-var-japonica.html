Viola canina var. japonica

Viola canina var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese pansy, is a cultivar of the viola genus native to Japan.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of violet, yellow, and white. The plant thrives in cool climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for container gardening.

This variety is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
