Veronica serpyllifolia var. integerrima

Veronica serpyllifolia var. integerrima, commonly known as the common speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant has slender, spreading stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in rocky areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and adaptable species suitable for various garden environments.
