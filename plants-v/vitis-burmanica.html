Vitis burmanica

Vitis burmanica, commonly known as the Burmese grape, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Myanmar, Thailand, and Vietnam. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that help it ascend supports. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy surface, while the flowers are small and greenish-white.

The fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to dark blue when mature, containing several seeds.
