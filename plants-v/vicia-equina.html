Vicia equina

Vicia equina, commonly known as the common vetch, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its seed pods are hairy and contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Vicia equina is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
