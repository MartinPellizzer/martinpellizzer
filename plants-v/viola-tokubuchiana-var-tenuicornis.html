Viola tokubuchiana var. tenuicornis

Viola tokubuchiana var. tenuicornis, commonly known as the slender-horned violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers bloom in early spring, often with a subtle fragrance.

The variety is distinguished by its elongated, slender calyx lobes, which give it its specific epithet.
