Viola calcarata var. bertolonii

Viola calcarata var. bertolonii, commonly known as the Bertoloni's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Italy.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a distinctive white or pale green underside. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, often with a yellow or white throat. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cool, moist environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
