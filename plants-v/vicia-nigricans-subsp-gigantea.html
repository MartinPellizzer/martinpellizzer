Vicia nigricans subsp. gigantea

Vicia nigricans subsp.

gigantea, commonly known as the giant black vetch, is a tall, leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being ovate and hairy.

The plant produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep purple. Its pods are elongated and contain several seeds, which are typically dispersed by wind.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing ability in agricultural settings.
