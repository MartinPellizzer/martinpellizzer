Vernonia bolivarensis

Vernonia bolivarensis, commonly known as the Bolivian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Bolivia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its foliage is usually gray-green with a rough texture, and it produces small, yellowish to brownish composite flowers.

Vernonia bolivarensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
