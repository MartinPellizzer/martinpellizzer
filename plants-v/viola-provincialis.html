Viola provincialis

Viola provincialis, commonly known as the provincial violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola provincialis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
