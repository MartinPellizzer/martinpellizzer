Verbascum nitidulum

Verbascum nitidulum, commonly known as the smooth mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, woolly leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats like grasslands and woodland edges.

Verbascum nitidulum is also valued for its medicinal properties, historically used to treat respiratory ailments.
