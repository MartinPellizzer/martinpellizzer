Vicia fauriei var. unijuga

Vicia fauriei var. unijuga, commonly known as the single-flowered vetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish in color. Its trifoliate leaves consist of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Vicia fauriei var. unijuga is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
