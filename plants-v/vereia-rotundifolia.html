Vereia rotundifolia

Vereia rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leafed vereia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, giving it a compact appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale blue flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to woodland gardens and wetland habitats.
