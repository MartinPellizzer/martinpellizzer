Veronica confusa

Veronica confusa, commonly known as the confused speedwell, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. It has opposite, linear leaves and produces capsules as its fruit.

Veronica confusa is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate.
