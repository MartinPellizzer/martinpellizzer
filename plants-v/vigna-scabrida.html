Vigna scabrida

Vigna scabrida, commonly known as the rough cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. Vigna scabrida is adapted to poor soils and can thrive in arid conditions, making it a resilient crop in many parts of the world.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
