Vernonia brachystephana

Vernonia brachystephana, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed ironweed, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue-gray flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Vernonia brachystephana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
