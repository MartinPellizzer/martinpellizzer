Verbesina lundellii

Verbesina lundellii, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Verbesina lundellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
