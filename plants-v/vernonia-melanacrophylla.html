Vernonia melanacrophylla

Vernonia melanacrophylla, commonly known as the black-stemmed ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow to brownish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and grasslands.

Vernonia melanacrophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
