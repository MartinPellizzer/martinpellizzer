Viola turritella

Viola turritella, commonly known as the turban violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, woodland habitats. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet or purple coloration. Its flowers are typically violet or blue, with a spurred petal at the back.

Viola turritella is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
