Veronica serpyllifolia subsp. apennina

Veronica serpyllifolia subsp.

apennina, commonly known as the Apennine speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe. It features small, blue to violet flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from spring to early summer.

The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine white pubescence. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
