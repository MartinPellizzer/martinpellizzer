Viola dimorphophylla

Viola dimorphophylla, commonly known as the fairy lantern, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns resembling lanterns. The plant produces two types of leaves—dorsal and ventral—distinguishing it from other viola species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release its seeds, aiding in its propagation.
