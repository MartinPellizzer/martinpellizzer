Vicia minuta

Vicia minuta, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and slender, trailing stems. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Vicia minuta is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in dry, open habitats.

It has a shallow root system and is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
