Valeriana ciliata

Valeriana ciliata, commonly known as the hairy valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caprifoliaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces, which give it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or shady areas.

Valeriana ciliata is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its calming effects.
