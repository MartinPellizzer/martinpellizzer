Viola × contempta subsp. lactea

Viola × contempta subsp.

lactea, commonly known as the milk viola, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family. It is characterized by its distinctive white to pale pink flowers with a prominent yellow eye, which are typically borne on slender, erect stems.

The leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, downy layer, giving the plant a soft, velvety appearance. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, showy blooms.
