Viola vaginata f. purpureocalcarata

Viola vaginata f. purpureocalcarata, commonly known as the purple-hooded violet, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive violet flowers with a hooded calyx that turns purplish-red, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its flowers bloom in early spring.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden settings for its vibrant color and unique floral structure.
