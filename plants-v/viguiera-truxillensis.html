Viguiera truxillensis

Viguiera truxillensis, commonly known as the Truxillense ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caribbean, specifically Cuba.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically showy blooms. The plant features upright stems covered in stiff, hairy foliage, and its flowers are typically purple or pink, arranged in dense clusters. Viguiera truxillensis is adapted to grow in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or disturbed soils.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a habitat for small invertebrates.
