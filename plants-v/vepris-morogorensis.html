Vepris morogorensis

Vepris morogorensis, commonly known as the East African lauraceae, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as cinnamon and camphor. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by small, spherical berries.

The bark and leaves of Vepris morogorensis contain essential oils and have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
