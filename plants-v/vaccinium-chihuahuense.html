Vaccinium chihuahuense

Vaccinium chihuahuense, commonly known as the Chihuahua blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Chihuahuan Desert in northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
