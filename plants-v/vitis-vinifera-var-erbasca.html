Vitis vinifera var. erbasca

Vitis vinifera var. erbasca, commonly known as the erbasca grape, is a lesser-known variety of the common grapevine.

It is characterized by its small, dark berries and is typically found in the Mediterranean region. The plant has a robust vine structure with deep green, lobed leaves. It is often used in local winemaking due to its unique flavor profile.

However, it is not as widely cultivated as other Vitis vinifera varieties.
