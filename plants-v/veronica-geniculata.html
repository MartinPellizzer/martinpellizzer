Veronica geniculata

Veronica geniculata, commonly known as the bent-leaved speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically opposite and often have a distinctive bend or twist, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
