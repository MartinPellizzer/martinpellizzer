Verbascum battandieri

Verbascum battandieri, commonly known as the Armenian mullein.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region, characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, elongated inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or semi-arid environments.

Verbascum battandieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
