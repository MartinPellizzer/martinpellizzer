Veronica beccabunga var. alpina

Veronica beccabunga var. alpina, commonly known as the alpine speedwell, is a flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. It has a compact, low-growing habit with slender stems and opposite leaves.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh conditions.
