Vernonia filipendula

Vernonia filipendula, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and fluffy, lavender-pink flower heads that appear in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower structure. Vernonia filipendula thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits to pollinators.
