Vicia basilei

Vicia basilei, commonly known as the Jerusalem bean, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in Mediterranean regions. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually purple or pink in color. Its seed pods contain several edible seeds, which have been used in traditional cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
