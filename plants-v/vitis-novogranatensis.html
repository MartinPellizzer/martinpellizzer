Vitis novogranatensis

Vitis novogranatensis, commonly known as the Colombian grapevine, is a species native to South America, particularly Colombia and Ecuador.

It is a woody vine characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when mature. It is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for cultivation, it is sometimes grown in botanical gardens and for its unique fruit characteristics.
