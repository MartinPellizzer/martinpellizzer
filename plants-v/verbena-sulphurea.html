Verbena sulphurea

Verbena sulphurea, commonly known as sulphur verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
