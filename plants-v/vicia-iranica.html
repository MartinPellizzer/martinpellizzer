Vicia iranica

Vicia iranica, commonly known as Iranian vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Vicia iranica is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable for ecological restoration.

It is also occasionally cultivated as a forage crop for livestock.
