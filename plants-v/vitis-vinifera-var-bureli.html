Vitis vinifera var. bureli

Vitis vinifera var. bureli, commonly known as Bureli grape, is a lesser-known variety of grape native to France.

It is characterized by its small, dark purple berries that are typically used in regional winemaking. The plant exhibits typical vitis vinifera traits, including compound leaves and tendrils for climbing. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils.

Due to its limited cultivation, it remains a niche variety in viticulture.
