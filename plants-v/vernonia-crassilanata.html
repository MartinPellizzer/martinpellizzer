Vernonia crassilanata

Vernonia crassilanata, commonly known as the broad-leaved ironweed, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine. Vernonia crassilanata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value has also led to its cultivation in gardens for its vibrant foliage and floral display.
