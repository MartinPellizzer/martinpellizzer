Vitis ficifolia

Vitis ficifolia, commonly known as the Chinese wine grape or wild grapevine, is a woody vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Vitis ficifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine.
