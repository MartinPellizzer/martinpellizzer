Veronica teucrium subsp. crinita

Veronica teucrium subsp.

crinita, commonly known as the curly veronica, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit.

The plant produces clusters of small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
