Veronica macrocalyx

Veronica macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered speedwell, is a herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features upright, branched stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of blue or purple flowers with five petals, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to support pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
