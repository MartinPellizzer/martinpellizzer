Veitchia subdisticha

Veitchia subdisticha, commonly known as the golden veitchia, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that are often bright green with a metallic sheen. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that emerge from a tall, branched inflorescence. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular in tropical gardens and as a houseplant.
