Vignantha vulgaris

Vignantha vulgaris, commonly known as the common wild vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its fruit is a hairy pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Vignantha vulgaris is often found in disturbed areas and can be invasive in certain ecosystems.
