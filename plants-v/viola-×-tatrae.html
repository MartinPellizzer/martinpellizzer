Viola × tatrae

Viola × tatrae, commonly known as the Tatra violet, is a hybrid species native to the Tatra Mountains of Central Europe.

It is a perennial herb with heart-shaped, dark green leaves and violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its flowers have five petals, with the lower petal being spurred, a characteristic feature of the Violaceae family.

Viola × tatrae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
