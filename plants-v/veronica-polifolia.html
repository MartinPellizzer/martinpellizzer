Veronica polifolia

Veronica polifolia, commonly known as the many-branched speedwell, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in small, opposite leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of blue or violet flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Veronica polifolia is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and along streambanks.
