Valeriana organensis

Valeriana organensis, commonly known as the Organ Mountains valerian, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, herbaceous stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
