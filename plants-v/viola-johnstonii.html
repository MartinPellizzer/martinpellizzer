Viola johnstonii

Viola johnstonii, commonly known as Johnston's violet, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. It has small, rounded leaves and produces flowers with five petals, often with a distinctive yellow center.

Viola johnstonii is a perennial herb that thrives in cool, temperate climates.
