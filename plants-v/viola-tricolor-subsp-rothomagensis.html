Viola tricolor subsp. rothomagensis

Viola tricolor subsp.

rothomagensis, commonly known as the Rothomagens violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped lobes.

The plant produces showy flowers in shades of purple, blue, and white, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
