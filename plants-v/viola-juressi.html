Viola juressi

Viola juressi, commonly known as the Jurenski violet, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola juressi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
