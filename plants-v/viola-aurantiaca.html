Viola aurantiaca

Viola aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, where it thrives in woodland and grassland habitats. The plant produces showy, orange-red flowers with five petals, often featuring a distinctive violet center. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are hairy and erect.

Viola aurantiaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
