Verbena caracasana

Verbena caracasana, commonly known as the Brazilian verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy flowers, which range in color from blue to purple, bloom in terminal clusters.

Verbena caracasana is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun.
