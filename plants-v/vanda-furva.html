Vanda furva

Vanda furva, commonly known as the black vanda, is a epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth. Vanda furva is known for its ability to climb and attach itself to trees or other structures.

Its flowers bloom throughout the year, making it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
