Vincentia hildebrandtii

Vincentia hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's vining plant, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems that twine around supports. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Native to South America, it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.
