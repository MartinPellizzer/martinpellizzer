Viola fulcrata

Viola fulcrata, commonly known as the rock violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the eastern United States.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in sunny, well-drained habitats. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive wavy margin and produces delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple and violet. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, alpine environments.

Viola fulcrata is a member of the Violaceae family, which includes other violets and related species.
