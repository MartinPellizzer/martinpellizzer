Viola kitaibeliana subsp. trimestris

Viola kitaibeliana subsp.

trimestris, commonly known as the three-leaved violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia, particularly China. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which consist of three heart-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, often with yellow markings. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or forested areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
