Veratrilla

Veratrilla, commonly known as the crown imperial, is a flowering plant belonging to the Liliaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that grow in dense, towering clusters at the top of long, slender stems. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing vivid red, orange, or yellow flowers that resemble a crown. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight.

Veratrilla is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and has also been used in traditional medicine.
