Vulpia myuros subsp. megalura

Vulpia myuros subsp.

megalura, commonly known as the broad-leaved vulpia, is a grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with a bluish hue.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and agricultural fields.

Its botanical features make it an important component of grassland ecosystems.
