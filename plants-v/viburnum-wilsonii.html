Viburnum wilsonii

Viburnum wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Viburnum wilsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to wildlife.
