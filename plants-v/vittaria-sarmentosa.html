Vittaria sarmentosa

Vittaria sarmentosa, commonly known as the creeping fern, is a tropical epiphytic plant belonging to the family Vittariaceae.

It is characterized by its small, delicate fronds that grow horizontally along the surface of trees or rocks. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in sori, a feature typical of ferns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Vittaria sarmentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
