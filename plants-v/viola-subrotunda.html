Viola subrotunda

Viola subrotunda, commonly known as the heartleaf violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces violet flowers that range in color from pale purple to deep blue, depending on the variety. Its flowers are usually borne on long, slender stems and bloom in early spring.

Viola subrotunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
