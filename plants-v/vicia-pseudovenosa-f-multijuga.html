Vicia pseudovenosa f. multijuga

Vicia pseudovenosa f. multijuga, commonly known as the multi-seeded vetch, is a variant of the vetch plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features compound leaves with numerous small, oval leaflets and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its seed pods are distinctive, often containing multiple seeds, which is reflected in its cultivar name.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
