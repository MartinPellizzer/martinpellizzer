Viguiera anchusifolia var. genuina

Viguiera anchusifolia var. genuina, commonly known as Mexican ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear clusters of small, pink to purple flower heads, which bloom in late summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves with serrated edges, giving it a distinctive, coarse appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value in native gardens.
