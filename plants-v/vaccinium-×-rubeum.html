Vaccinium × rubeum

Vaccinium × rubeum, commonly known as the common cranberry, is a hybrid species within the Ericaceae family.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub that produces small, red, berry-like fruits. The plant thrives in acidic, peat-rich soils and is typically found in wetland environments. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a leathery texture, and the flowers are pink or white.

The berries are used in culinary applications and are also valued for their nutritional content.
