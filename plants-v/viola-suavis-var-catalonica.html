Viola suavis var. catalonica

Viola suavis var. catalonica, commonly known as the Catalonian violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and forest edges. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
