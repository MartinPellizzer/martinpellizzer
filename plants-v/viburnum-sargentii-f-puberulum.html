Viburnum sargentii f. puberulum

Viburnum sargentii f. puberulum, commonly known as the Sargent viburnum, is a compact shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by dark red berries in the fall. The plant has glossy, ovate leaves that remain green throughout the growing season. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.

Viburnum sargentii f. puberulum is relatively low maintenance and resistant to pests and diseases.
