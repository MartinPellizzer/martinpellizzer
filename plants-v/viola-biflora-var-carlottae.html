Viola biflora var. carlottae

Viola biflora var. carlottae, commonly known as the Carlotta violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of violet-blue flowers with yellow centers, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
