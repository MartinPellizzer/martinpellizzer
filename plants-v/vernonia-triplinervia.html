Vernonia triplinervia

Vernonia triplinervia, commonly known as the three-veined ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, often hairy stems. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped with three prominent veins, giving it its scientific name.

The flowers are yellow to orange in color, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom during the dry season.
