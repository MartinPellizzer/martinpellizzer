Viola borealis

Viola borealis, commonly known as the Arctic violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and is adapted to cold climates. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, violet or purple flowers. It is often found in tundra and alpine environments where it thrives in low temperatures.

Viola borealis plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain herbivores.
