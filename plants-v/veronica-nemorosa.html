Veronica nemorosa

Veronica nemorosa, commonly known as wood speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, blue-to-purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Veronica nemorosa is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
