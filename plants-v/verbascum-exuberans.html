Verbascum exuberans

Verbascum exuberans, commonly known as the showy mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Verbascum exuberans is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
