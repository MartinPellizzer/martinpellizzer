Vernonia costata

Vernonia costata, commonly known as the coast ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along coastal areas and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture.

Vernonia costata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
