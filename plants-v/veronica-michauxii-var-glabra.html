Veronica michauxii var. glabra

Veronica michauxii var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth veronica, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, smooth hairs, giving the plant its "glabra" variety name. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
