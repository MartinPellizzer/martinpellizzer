Vaccinium nagurae

Vaccinium nagurae, commonly known as the Nagura huckleberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is typically found in moist, acidic soils in mountainous regions. The plant produces small, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are evergreen, with a leathery texture and dark green color.

Vaccinium nagurae plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food for wildlife and contributing to forest biodiversity.
