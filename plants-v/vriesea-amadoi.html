Vriesea amadoi

Vriesea amadoi, commonly known as the Amado's Vriesea, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant, spathiform inflorescence that often displays a mix of red, orange, and yellow hues. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that collect rainwater, providing a habitat for small organisms. Its flowers bloom in late summer or early autumn, adding dramatic visual interest to the landscape.

Vriesea amadoi is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its adaptability to moderate light and humidity conditions.
