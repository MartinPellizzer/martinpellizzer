Vulpia geniculata var. dasyantha

Vulpia geniculata var. dasyantha, commonly known as the tufted barnyard grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in various ecosystems.
