Viguiera rhombifolia

Viguiera rhombifolia, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has heart-shaped to rhomboidal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for xeriscaping.

Viguiera rhombifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
