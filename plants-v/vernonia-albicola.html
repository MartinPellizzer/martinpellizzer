Vernonia albicola

Vernonia albicola, commonly known as white ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its pale yellow to white flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
