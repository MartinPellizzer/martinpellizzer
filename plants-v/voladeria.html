Voladeria

Voladeria, commonly known as the flying grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its slender, grass-like structure.

It features long, narrow leaves and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that allow it to spread easily across open areas. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides or in fields, and thrives in temperate climates. Its lightweight seeds, encased in a feathery pappus, enable it to be carried by the wind over long distances.

Due to its adaptability, Voladeria is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
