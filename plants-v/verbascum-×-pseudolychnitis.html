Verbascum × pseudolychnitis

Verbascum × pseudolychnitis, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their large, showy flowers. The hybrid exhibits characteristics of both its parent species, Verbascum lychnitis and Verbascum pseudolychnis, resulting in a unique combination of traits. It produces tall, hairy stems that bear clusters of yellow or orange flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
