Verbesina semiserrata

Verbesina semiserrata, commonly known as the serrated sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves with serrated edges, giving it its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open fields and along roadsides.

The genus Verbesina is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
