Vicia narbonensis f. serratifolia

Vicia narbonensis f. serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated vetch, is a variant of the common vetch.

It is a leguminous plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes. It is typically found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of climates.

This ferny annual is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing abilities.
