Viburnum cylindricum var. crassifolium

Viburnum cylindricum var. crassifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, ovate, dark green leaves that are thick and leathery, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscape gardens and natural habitats.
