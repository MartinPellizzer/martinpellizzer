Viburnum nudum var. grandifolium

Viburnum nudum var. grandifolium, commonly known as the fragrant winterhaw, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, which are typically dark green and glossy on top. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by red, berry-like drupes in the fall. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
