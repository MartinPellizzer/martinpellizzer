Virea pyrenaica

Virea pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean virea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in rocky and limestone soils, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to withstand cold and harsh environmental conditions.

Virea pyrenaica is an important component of the local flora, contributing to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
