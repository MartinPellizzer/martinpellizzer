Vaccinium hangchouense

Vaccinium hangchouense, commonly known as the Hangchow blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark blue to black in color, containing a high concentration of antioxidants. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas with cool, moist climates.

Due to its ornamental value and potential nutritional benefits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and research settings.
