Veronica spicata var. australis

Veronica spicata var. australis, commonly known as the Australian spiky speedwell, is a perennial plant native to Australia.

It features upright, spiky flower clusters that bloom in shades of blue and purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and extended blooming period make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
