Virgulus patens var. phlogifolius

Virgulus patens var. phlogifolius, commonly known as the flameleaf maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking, lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in early spring, which are followed by winged seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
