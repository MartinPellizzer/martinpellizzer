Vulpia ciliata

Vulpia ciliata, commonly known as the hairy vulpia, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a low-growing, annual plant that typically forms dense mats on disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with fine hairs along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets.

Vulpia ciliata is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in poor soil conditions.
