Vitex parviflora

Vitex parviflora, commonly known as the smallflower chaste tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that are clustered in racemes, typically blooming in the summer months. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that split open to release seeds.

Vitex parviflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
