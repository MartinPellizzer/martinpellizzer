Vahlia somalensis subsp. somalensis

Vahlia somalensis subsp.

somalensis, commonly known as the Somali vahlia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Somalia. It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant is adapted to dry and hot climates, with a deep root system to access groundwater. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its resilience, it plays a role in stabilizing desert soils and supporting local biodiversity.
