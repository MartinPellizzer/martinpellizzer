Veronica cygnorum

Veronica cygnorum, commonly known as the swanflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Veronica cygnorum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
