Viguiera excelsa var. pachycephala

Viguiera excelsa var. pachycephala, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, spiky, purple to pink flowers. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are hairy and arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The botanical features of this plant make it an attractive addition to native gardens and wildlife habitats.
