Vernonia gaudichaudiana

Vernonia gaudichaudiana, commonly known as the Hawaiian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, upright growth habit. The plant features gray-green, hairy leaves and produces showy, violet-blue flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. It thrives in coastal and dry forest habitats, often growing on rocky soils.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
