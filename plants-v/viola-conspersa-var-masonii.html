Viola conspersa var. masonii

Viola conspersa var. masonii, commonly known as the Mason's violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of violet-blue flowers with yellow centers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodlands or stream banks, preferring partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the forest floor.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
