Valeriana excelsa subsp. transiens

Valeriana excelsa subsp.

transiens, commonly known as the Transylvanian valerian, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It features a robust rhizomatous root system and produces tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive, slightly hairy texture and emits a strong, aromatic scent. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and are typically pink or white in color.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
