Viola tricolor proles royanensis

Viola tricolor proles royanensis, commonly known as the Royan violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, typically with a deep green upper surface and a lighter underside. The flowers are usually violet or purple, with three petals and a distinctive yellow center. Native to the Royan region in France, it thrives in moist, shady environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant colors and delicate appearance.
