Vandenboschia dilatata

Vandenboschia dilatata, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has thick, leathery leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, providing a dense, architectural form. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Vandenboschia dilatata is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
