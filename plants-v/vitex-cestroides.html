Vitex cestroides

Vitex cestroides, commonly known as the African blue mistflower, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with a slightly hairy surface. The plant is typically found in open woodlands and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
