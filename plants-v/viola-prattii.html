Viola prattii

Viola prattii, commonly known as the Pratte's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has heart-shaped, velvety leaves. The plant produces delicate, five-petaled flowers that range in color from violet to blue, often with a yellow throat. Viola prattii is part of the Violaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
