Vallaris maingayi

Vallaris maingayi, commonly known as the African sweet pea, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators make it a popular choice in horticulture.
