Viburnum toronis

Viburnum toronis, commonly known as the Japanese snowball.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Japan, characterized by its clusters of white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not particularly palatable. Viburnum toronis typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters and thrives in partial shade to full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
