Verbascum laciniatum

Verbascum laciniatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Verbascum laciniatum is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
