Verbascum trapifolium

Verbascum trapifolium, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in a spike at the top of the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Verbascum trapifolium is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically for its soothing effects.
