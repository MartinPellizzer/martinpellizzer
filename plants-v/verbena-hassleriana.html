Verbena hassleriana

Verbena hassleriana, commonly known as the Mexican verbena, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant purple or pink flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 3 feet. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Verbena hassleriana thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
