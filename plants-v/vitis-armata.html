Vitis armata

Vitis armata, commonly known as the Japanese grapevine, is a deciduous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, greenish-white flowers in the spring. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and can grow up to several meters in length. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red or black when ripe.

Vitis armata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
