Viola crassa var. tayemonii

Viola crassa var. tayemonii, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow throat. The plant typically grows to a height of 10-15 cm and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.

The species is closely related to other viola species and is valued for its hardiness and adaptability.
