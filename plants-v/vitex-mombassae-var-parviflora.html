Vitex mombassae var. parviflora

Vitex mombassae var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered chaste tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of purple or blue, which bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are dry, capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

Vitex mombassae var. parviflora is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
