Vogelia

Vogelia, commonly known as the "sugar cane plant," is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its upright, branching structure and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces edible, starchy roots that have been traditionally used for food and medicine. Vogelia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
