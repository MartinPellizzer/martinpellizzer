Vallisneria neotropicalis

Vallisneria neotropicalis, commonly known as the giant vallisneria, is a species of aquatic plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant typically thrives in slow-moving or still freshwater environments, such as lakes and ponds. Vallisneria neotropicalis reproduces through the production of small, floating fruits that contain multiple seeds.

Its presence often indicates good water quality and is a common species in aquariums and natural waterways.
