Viola domestica f. alba

Viola domestica f. alba, commonly known as the white violets, is a flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, broad leaves and clusters of small, showy white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and gardens. It is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
