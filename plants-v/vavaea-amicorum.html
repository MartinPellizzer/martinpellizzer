Vavaea amicorum

Vavaea amicorum, commonly known as the friendship vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its cultural significance in traditional medicine.
