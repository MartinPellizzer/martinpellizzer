Vignea × boenninghausiana

Vignea × boenninghausiana, commonly known as the hybrid cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are rich in protein. Vignea × boenninghausiana is valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.

It is often cultivated as a forage crop and for its nutritional value in human diets.
