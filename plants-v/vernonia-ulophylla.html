Vernonia ulophylla

Vernonia ulophylla, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is known for its hard, dense wood, which is highly valued for construction and tool-making. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Vernonia ulophylla is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
