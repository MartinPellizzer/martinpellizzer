Vitis leeoides

Vitis leeoides, commonly known as the Chinese wine grape, is a species of flowering vine native to China.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its tendrils and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into clusters of berries. These berries are typically dark purple when mature and are used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Vitis leeoides is valued for its ornamental qualities and its historical use in winemaking.
