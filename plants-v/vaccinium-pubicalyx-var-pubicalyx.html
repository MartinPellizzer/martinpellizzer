Vaccinium pubicalyx var. pubicalyx

Vaccinium pubicalyx var. pubicalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved huckleberry, is a species of shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are densely covered with short, glandular hairs. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, dark blue berries. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local wildlife.
