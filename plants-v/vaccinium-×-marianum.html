Vaccinium × marianum

Vaccinium × marianum, commonly known as the blueberry bush, is a hybrid species within the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which give way to the characteristic blue-black berries. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are widely cultivated for both commercial and culinary purposes.

Its growth habit is shrub-like, with evergreen foliage that provides ornamental value throughout the year.
