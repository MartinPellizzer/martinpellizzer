Vochysia elliptica

Vochysia elliptica, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or angel's trumpet, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that contain seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Vochysia elliptica thrives in warm, humid climates and is adapted to well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and visually appealing plant in both natural and cultivated settings.
