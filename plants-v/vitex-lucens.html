Vitex lucens

Vitex lucens, commonly known as the shining verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Vitex lucens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
