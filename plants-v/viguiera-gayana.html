Viguiera gayana

Viguiera gayana, commonly known as Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its vibrant orange to red flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viguiera gayana is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
