Veronica macrostachya subsp. macrostachya

Veronica macrostachya subsp.

macrostachya, commonly known as the large-stemmed speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of blue to violet flowers.

The plant produces small, oval leaves that are usually arranged oppositely along the stem. It blooms from late winter to early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
