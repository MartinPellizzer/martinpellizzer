Vernonia mossambicensis

Vernonia mossambicensis, commonly known as the Mozambique Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its foliage is lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers appear in dense clusters.

Vernonia mossambicensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
