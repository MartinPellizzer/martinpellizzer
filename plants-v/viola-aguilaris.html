Viola aguilaris

Viola aguilaris, commonly known as the Mexican viola, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Viola aguilaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
