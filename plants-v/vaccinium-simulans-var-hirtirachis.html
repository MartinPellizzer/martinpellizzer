Vaccinium simulans var. hirtirachis

Vaccinium simulans var. hirtirachis, commonly known as the hairy whortleberry, is a variant of the whortleberry plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, acidic soils, often found in forest understories. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are characterized by their hairy texture, which gives the variety its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
