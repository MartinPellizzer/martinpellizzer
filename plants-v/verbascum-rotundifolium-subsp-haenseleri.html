Verbascum rotundifolium subsp. haenseleri

Verbascum rotundifolium subsp.

haenseleri, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are rounded at the base.

The plant produces tall, upright stems topped with clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
