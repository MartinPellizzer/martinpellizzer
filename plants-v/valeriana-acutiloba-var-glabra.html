Valeriana acutiloba var. glabra

Valeriana acutiloba var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale pink, during the summer months. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its sedative properties.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shady areas.
