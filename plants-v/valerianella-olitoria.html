Valerianella olitoria

Valerianella olitoria, commonly known as garden sorrel, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are heart-shaped and slightly serrated. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Valerianella olitoria is often grown as a leafy vegetable, valued for its tangy, slightly sour flavor.

It thrives in cool climates and is commonly used in salads and as a garnish.
