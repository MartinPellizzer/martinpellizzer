Vernonia leucocalyx

Vernonia leucocalyx, commonly known as the white-veined ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in fine, silver-gray hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and prominent veins, contributing to its ornamental value.

Vernonia leucocalyx produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
