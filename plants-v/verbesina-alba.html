Verbesina alba

Verbesina alba, commonly known as white beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves with a rough texture, and it produces small, spiny seeds. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Verbesina alba is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
