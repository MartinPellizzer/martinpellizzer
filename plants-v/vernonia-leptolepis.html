Vernonia leptolepis

Vernonia leptolepis, commonly known as narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of blue to purple flowers.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a valuable nectar source for pollinators.
