Vicia canariensis

Vicia canariensis, commonly known as the Canary vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Canary Islands.

It features slender, upright stems with trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Vicia canariensis is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
