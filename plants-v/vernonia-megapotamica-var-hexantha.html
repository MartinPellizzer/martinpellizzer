Vernonia megapotamica var. hexantha

Vernonia megapotamica var. hexantha, commonly known as the Brazilian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, yellow-orange flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, bract-like structures that surround the flower clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
