Verbascum szovitsianum var. szovitsianum

Verbascum szovitsianum var. szovitsianum, commonly known as the Szovits' mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, velvety leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in the summer, which are clustered in dense, candle-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in gardens and natural landscapes.
