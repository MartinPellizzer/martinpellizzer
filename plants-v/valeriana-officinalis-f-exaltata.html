Valeriana officinalis f. exaltata

Valeriana officinalis f. exaltata, commonly known as the tall valerian, is a variant of the common valerian, characterized by its increased height and more robust growth.

It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically growing in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes. Its root system is aromatic and contains volatile oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the production of essential oils used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.
