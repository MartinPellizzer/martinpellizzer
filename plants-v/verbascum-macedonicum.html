Verbascum macedonicum

Verbascum macedonicum, commonly known as the Macedonian mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in soft, woolly hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are large, broad, and lobed, often with a fuzzy texture.

Verbascum macedonicum is adapted to rocky and dry habitats, showcasing resilience in arid environments.
