Vitex sebesiae

Vitex sebesiae, commonly known as the African blueberry or blueberry bush, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often pubescent. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, which are followed by blue-black berries. Vitex sebesiae thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
