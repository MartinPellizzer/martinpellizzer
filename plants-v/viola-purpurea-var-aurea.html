Viola purpurea var. aurea

Viola purpurea var. aurea, commonly known as the golden violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, violet-pink flowers with a yellow center. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-15 centimeters and prefers moist, well-drained soil in partial to full shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in woodland environments.
