Viola alba var. scotophylla

Viola alba var. scotophylla, commonly known as the white violet or moon violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped, pale green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy, white or pale lavender flowers that bloom in early spring. It prefers shady, moist environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
