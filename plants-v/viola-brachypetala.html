Viola brachypetala

Viola brachypetala, commonly known as the short-petalled violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of purple and violet, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Viola brachypetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
