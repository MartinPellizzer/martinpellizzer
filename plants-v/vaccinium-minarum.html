Vaccinium minarum

Vaccinium minarum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blueberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like heathers and cranberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, rich in antioxidants and nutrients. It prefers acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight.

Vaccinium minarum is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
