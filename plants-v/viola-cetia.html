Viola cetia

Viola cetia, commonly known as the Cetian violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola cetia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
