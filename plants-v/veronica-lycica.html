Veronica lycica

Veronica lycica, commonly known as the Cyprus speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its delicate blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny environments. It has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, showy blooms.

Veronica lycica is often used in rock gardens and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
