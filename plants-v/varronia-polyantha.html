Varronia polyantha

Varronia polyantha, commonly known as the purple trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, with a rounded crown and smooth, grayish bark. The plant is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, blooming in clusters during the rainy season. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Varronia polyantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
