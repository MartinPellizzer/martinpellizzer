Vernonia gracilis

Vernonia gracilis, commonly known as slender ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers contribute to its ornamental value.

Vernonia gracilis is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
