Verbena tetragonocalyx

Verbena tetragonocalyx, commonly known as the square-stemmed verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Verbena tetragonocalyx is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
