Vernonia microcephala

Vernonia microcephala, commonly known as the small-headed ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodlands. It is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Vernonia microcephala is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
