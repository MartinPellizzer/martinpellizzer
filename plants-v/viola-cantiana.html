Viola cantiana

Viola cantiana, commonly known as the common violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and along the edges of forests, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Viola cantiana is an herbaceous perennial that blooms in early spring, often producing showy flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
