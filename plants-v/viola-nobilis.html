Viola nobilis

Viola nobilis, commonly known as the heartsease, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola nobilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
