Veronicastrum cerasifolium

Veronicastrum cerasifolium, commonly known as redstem verbena, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features tall, slender stems that are often tinged with red, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
