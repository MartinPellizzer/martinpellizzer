Viola silvatica var. pygmaea

Viola silvatica var. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf violet, is a compact, low-growing perennial herb native to the temperate forests of Europe.

It typically reaches heights of only a few centimeters, with heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are dark green in color. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of violet or purple, often with a distinctive yellowish throat. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
