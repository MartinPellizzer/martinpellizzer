Vitis coccinea

Vitis coccinea, commonly known as the redcurrant grape, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It produces small, bright red berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary applications. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.

Vitis coccinea is also a host plant for several insect species, contributing to local biodiversity.
