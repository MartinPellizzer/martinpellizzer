Viola takedana

Viola takedana, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a delicate perennial flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, colorful flowers that typically bloom in shades of violet, purple, and white. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or shaded gardens. Viola takedana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
