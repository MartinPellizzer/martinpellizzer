Verbena kuhlmannii

Verbena kuhlmannii, commonly known as the Kuhlmann's verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in shades of blue, purple, and white, with five petals that are often adorned with a distinctive throat color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Verbena kuhlmannii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
