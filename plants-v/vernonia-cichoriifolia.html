Vernonia cichoriifolia

Vernonia cichoriifolia, commonly known as the Jerusalem artichoke, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, rough, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is known for its edible tuberous roots, which are rich in inulin and used in various culinary applications.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
