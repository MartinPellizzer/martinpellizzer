Vidalia navesii

Vidalia navesii, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright orange to red tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Vidalia navesii thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, easy-to-grow ornamental plants.
