Verbascum italicum

Verbascum italicum, commonly known as Italian mullein, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, hairy stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, velvety leaves that are gray-green in color. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in the summer, which are clustered in a loose panicle. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry environments.

Verbascum italicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically for its medicinal properties.
