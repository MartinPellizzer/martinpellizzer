Virola carinata var. gracilis

Virola carinata var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender wax tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elongated, elliptical leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by distinctive, woody fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local cultures.
