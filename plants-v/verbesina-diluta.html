Verbesina diluta

Verbesina diluta, commonly known as yellow woodflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Malvaceae, which includes other plants like hibiscus and cotton.
