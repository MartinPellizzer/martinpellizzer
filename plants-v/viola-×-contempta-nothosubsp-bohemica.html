Viola × contempta nothosubsp. bohemica

Viola × contempta nothosubsp.

bohemica, commonly known as the Bohemian violets, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in the Czech Republic and surrounding regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
