Viola patrinii f. toyokoroensis

Viola patrinii f. toyokoroensis, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple and white, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland plantings.
