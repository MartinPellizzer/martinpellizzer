Viola hirta proles thomasiana

Viola hirta proles thomasiana, commonly known as the Thomasiana violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces small, showy flowers in shades of violet or purple. Its flowers are borne on short pedicels and are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
