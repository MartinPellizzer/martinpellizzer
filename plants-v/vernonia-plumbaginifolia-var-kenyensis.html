Vernonia plumbaginifolia var. kenyensis

Vernonia plumbaginifolia var. kenyensis, commonly known as Kenyan ironweed, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flower heads. The plant produces narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a suitable candidate for xeriscaping.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
