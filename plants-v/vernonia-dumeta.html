Vernonia dumeta

Vernonia dumeta, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its cylindrical, dark green leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while its flowers are small and yellowish-green, appearing in late summer.

Vernonia dumeta is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along the edges of streams.
