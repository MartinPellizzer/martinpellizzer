Vernonia fimbrillata

Vernonia fimbrillata, commonly known as the fringed ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy purple or blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, hair-like structures, while its flowers are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

Vernonia fimbrillata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
