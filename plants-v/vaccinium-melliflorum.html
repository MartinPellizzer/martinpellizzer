Vaccinium melliflorum

Vaccinium melliflorum, commonly known as the sweet blueberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale blue flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and vitamins, particularly vitamin C. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Vaccinium melliflorum is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
