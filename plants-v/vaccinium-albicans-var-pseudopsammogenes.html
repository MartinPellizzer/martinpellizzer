Vaccinium albicans var. pseudopsammogenes

Vaccinium albicans var. pseudopsammogenes, commonly known as the coastal blueberry, is a shrub native to sandy coastal regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, oval, dark blue to black berries that are typically covered in a whitish bloom. The plant has small, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to salt spray and wind exposure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
