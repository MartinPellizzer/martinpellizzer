Viguiera strigosa

Viguiera strigosa, commonly known as Mexican heath.

It is a perennial flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in fine, hair-like structures. Its small, clustered flowers are typically pink or purple, blooming in late summer to early fall.

Viguiera strigosa is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
