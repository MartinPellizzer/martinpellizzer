Viola szetschwanensis var. nudicaulis

Viola szetschwanensis var. nudicaulis, commonly known as the naked-stem violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to China.

It features delicate, heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairless, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces small, violet-blue flowers with five petals, often borne on slender stems. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and shade gardens.
