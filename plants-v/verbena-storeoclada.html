Verbena storeoclada

Verbena storeoclada, commonly known as the golden verbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Verbena storeoclada is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
