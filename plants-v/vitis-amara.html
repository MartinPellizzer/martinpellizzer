Vitis amara

Vitis amara, commonly known as poison ivy vine, is a woody vine native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a distinctive reddish or purple underside.

Vitis amara is known for containing toxic compounds, particularly urushiol, which can cause skin irritation in humans.
