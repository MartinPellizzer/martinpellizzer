Vitex teloravina

Vitex teloravina, commonly known as the Teloravina chaste tree, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its fruit is a dry, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Vitex teloravina is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to the unique ecological conditions of its native environment.
