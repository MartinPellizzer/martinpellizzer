Viola tenuicornis subsp. ircutiana

Viola tenuicornis subsp.

ircutiana, commonly known as the Iberian violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The plant produces clusters of violet-blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded woodland areas and is characterized by its delicate appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
