Viola chamaedrys

Viola chamaedrys, commonly known as the barrenwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white down. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in woodland habitats and prefers partial shade with well-drained soil.

Viola chamaedrys is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
