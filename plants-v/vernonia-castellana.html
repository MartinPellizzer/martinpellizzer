Vernonia castellana

Vernonia castellana, commonly known as the Castle Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in late summer, adding vibrant color to the landscape.

Vernonia castellana is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
