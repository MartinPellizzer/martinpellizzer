Verbena glabrata var. glabrata

Verbena glabrata var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a slightly rough texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

This species is known for its long blooming period, often extending from late spring through early autumn.
