Viburnum obovatum var. punicifolium

Viburnum obovatum var. punicifolium, commonly known as the red-leaf viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, broad, ovate leaves that turn a vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in early spring, which are followed by dark blue, edible berries. Its foliage and berries provide food for various birds and wildlife.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its seasonal color changes.
