Vigna matengoana

Vigna matengoana, commonly known as the Matengo bean, is a leguminous plant native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, typical of many species in the Vigna genus. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several round, edible seeds. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its nutritious seeds.

Vigna matengoana is valued for its adaptability to poor soils and its importance in local agriculture.
