Vimen silesiaca

Vimen silesiaca, commonly known as the Silesian willow, is a species of flowering plant in the Salicaceae family.

It is native to Central and Eastern Europe, particularly found in moist, temperate regions. The plant is characterized by its slender, often drooping branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, catkin-like flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color.

Vimen silesiaca is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in wetland environments.
