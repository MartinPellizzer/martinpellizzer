Viola herzogii

Viola herzogii, commonly known as the Herzog's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a deep green color and a hairy texture, and its flowers are typically violet or purple, with five petals. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
