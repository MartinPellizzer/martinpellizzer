Viola canadensis var. pubens

Viola canadensis var. pubens, commonly known as the pubescent violet, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces violet flowers with five petals, often with a distinctive yellow or white spot at the base. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding color to the landscape during the cooler months.
