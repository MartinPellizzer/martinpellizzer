Verbesina bridgesii

Verbesina bridgesii, commonly known as the Bridges' beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats like wetlands and along stream banks.

Verbesina bridgesii is often found in disturbed soils and can spread through its fibrous root system and seeds.
