Vernonia tarijensis

Vernonia tarijensis, commonly known as the Tarijensis ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Bolivia and Peru.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia tarijensis is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
