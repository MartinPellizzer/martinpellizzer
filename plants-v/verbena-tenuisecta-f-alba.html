Verbena tenuisecta f. alba

Verbena tenuisecta f. alba, commonly known as the white verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its delicate appearance and easy cultivation make it a valued ornamental plant.
