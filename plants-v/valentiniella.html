Valentiniella

Valentiniella, commonly known as the "false hellebore," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Polygonaceae.

It consists of several species, most notably Valentiniella ciliata, which is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. The plant is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Valentiniella is often found in moist, calcareous soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
