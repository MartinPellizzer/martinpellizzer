Vitex triflora var. hirsuta

Vitex triflora var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy chaste tree, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of blue or purple, which bloom in the summer. Its fruit is a dry, three-lobed capsule that releases seeds when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
