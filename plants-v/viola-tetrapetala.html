Viola tetrapetala

Viola tetrapetala, commonly known as the four-petaled violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive four-petaled flowers, which are typically purple or violet in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and thrives in moist, shaded environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Viola tetrapetala is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
