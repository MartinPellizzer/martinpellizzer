Viola × grubovii

Viola × grubovii, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Viola tricolor and Viola lutea, resulting in a plant with diverse morphological characteristics. The species typically features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple, violet, and white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Viola × grubovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
