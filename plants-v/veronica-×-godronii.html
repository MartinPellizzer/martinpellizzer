Veronica × godronii

Veronica × godronii, commonly known as Godron's speedwell, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of blue or violet flowers, which bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
