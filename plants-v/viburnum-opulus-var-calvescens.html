Viburnum opulus var. calvescens

Viburnum opulus var. calvescens, commonly known as the narrowleaf snowball, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with elongated, narrow leaflets that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of white, fluffy flowers in early spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to wildlife.
