Ventilago madraspatana var. madraspatana

Ventilago madraspatana var. madraspatana, commonly known as the Madras banyan, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, pendulous flowers. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
