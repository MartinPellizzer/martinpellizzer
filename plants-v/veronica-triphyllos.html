Veronica triphyllos

Veronica triphyllos, commonly known as the three-leaved speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It features small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to August. The plant has a compact growth habit with three pairs of opposite leaves, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and rockeries.
