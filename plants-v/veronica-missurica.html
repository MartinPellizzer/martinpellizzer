Veronica missurica

Veronica missurica, commonly known as blueweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems that are often reddish in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small, blue to violet, and arranged in spike-like clusters.

Veronica missurica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
