Vitex maranhana

Vitex maranhana, commonly known as Brazilian lilac, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, opposite leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds, typical of the Vitex genus. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed soils and open areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm regions.
