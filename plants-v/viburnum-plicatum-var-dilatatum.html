Viburnum plicatum var. dilatatum

Viburnum plicatum var. dilatatum, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its uniquely folded, white flowers that resemble bells, which appear in early spring. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red or purple in the fall. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
