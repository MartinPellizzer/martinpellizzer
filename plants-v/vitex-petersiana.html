Vitex petersiana

Vitex petersiana, commonly known as the African blueberry or wild lilac, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often woody and branched. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Vitex petersiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
