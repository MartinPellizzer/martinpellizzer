Verbascum kurdicum

Verbascum kurdicum, commonly known as the Kurdish mullein, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are broad and covered in a dense layer of soft, woolly hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Verbascum kurdicum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
