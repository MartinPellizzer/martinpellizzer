Viola gomphopetala

Viola gomphopetala, commonly known as the Tibetan violet, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, showy flowers in shades of purple and violet. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its alpine habitat. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola gomphopetala is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
