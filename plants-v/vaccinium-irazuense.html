Vaccinium irazuense

Vaccinium irazuense, commonly known as the Irazu huckleberry, is a shrub native to the highlands of Costa Rica.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes blueberries and cranberries, and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark blue or black when mature. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are often consumed fresh or used in local culinary traditions.

Vaccinium irazuense thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is adapted to the cool, moist climates of its native habitat.
