Viola palmata f. albiflora

Viola palmata f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered heartleaf violet, is a rare and striking variant of the heartleaf violet.

This plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and pale to white flowers, which contrast sharply with its dark green foliage. It is native to specific regions in North America, typically found in moist, shaded woodlands. The flowers are usually small, with five petals, and bloom in early spring.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is considered a botanical curiosity and often sought after by plant enthusiasts and botanists.
