Vaccinium viscifolium var. viscifolium

Vaccinium viscifolium var. viscifolium, commonly known as the black huckleberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and commonly used in local cuisines. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in coniferous forest understories. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a leathery texture and a distinctive aromatic scent.

The species is valued for both its ecological role and its edible fruits.
