Vachellia farnesiana var. farnesiana

Vachellia farnesiana var. farnesiana, commonly known as the sweet acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorned stems and bipinnate leaves that are feathery in appearance. The plant produces yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods that split open when mature.

The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates.
