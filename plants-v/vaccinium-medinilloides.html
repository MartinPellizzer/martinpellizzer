Vaccinium medinilloides

Vaccinium medinilloides, commonly known as the Medinillo violet, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes blueberries and heathers, and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in the spring. Its berries, though not widely cultivated, are edible and have a tart flavor.

Vaccinium medinilloides thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.
