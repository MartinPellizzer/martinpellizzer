Vachellia lutea var. moniliformis

Vachellia lutea var. moniliformis, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and bipinnate leaves that are divided into numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, cylindrical spikes, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to poor soils and drought conditions.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and for its hard, durable wood.
