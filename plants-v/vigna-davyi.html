Vigna davyi

Vigna davyi, commonly known as the African burr bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, which are typically heart-shaped. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Vigna davyi is adapted to arid environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological studies and agricultural use in regions with limited resources.
