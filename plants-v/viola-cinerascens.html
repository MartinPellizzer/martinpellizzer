Viola cinerascens

Viola cinerascens, commonly known as the cinerascens violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has heart-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow throat, which bloom in early spring. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is often found in shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
