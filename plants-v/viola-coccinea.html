Viola coccinea

Viola coccinea, commonly known as the scarlet violet, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red petals with a contrasting yellow center, which are typical of the Violaceae family. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soil. Viola coccinea is a perennial herb that blooms in spring and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, native flora.
