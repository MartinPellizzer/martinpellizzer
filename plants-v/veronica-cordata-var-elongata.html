Veronica cordata var. elongata

Veronica cordata var. elongata, commonly known as narrowleaf speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite pairs of lance-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
