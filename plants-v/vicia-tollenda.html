Vicia tollenda

Vicia tollenda, commonly known as the Tolenda vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial legume that typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its pods contain several seeds, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

Vicia tollenda is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
