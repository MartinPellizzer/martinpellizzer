Valeriana kassarica

Valeriana kassarica, commonly known as the Kassarica valerian, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Valerianaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, violet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, spicy scent, while its roots contain essential oils used in traditional medicine.

Valeriana kassarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal remedies.
