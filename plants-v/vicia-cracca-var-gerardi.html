Vicia cracca var. gerardi

Vicia cracca var. gerardi, commonly known as the red clover, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and trifoliate leaves with a distinctive red or purple coloration. The plant produces small, pink or purple pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.

Vicia cracca var. gerardi is valued for its nitrogen-fixing abilities and historical use in agriculture.
