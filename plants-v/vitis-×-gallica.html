Vitis × gallica

Vitis × gallica, commonly known as the French grapevine, is a hybrid species within the Vitaceae family.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and the production of small, often sweet berries. The plant typically features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and tendrils that aid in climbing. Its flowers are small and greenish, appearing in clusters, and it is cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

Vitis × gallica is well-suited to temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
