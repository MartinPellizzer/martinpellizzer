Verbesina serrata var. pringlei

Verbesina serrata var. pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's tickseed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and serrated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for arid landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
