Vignantha

Vignantha, commonly known as the Indian bean or wild legume, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces pods that contain several edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. Vignantha thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

It plays an important role in traditional agriculture and is used in various culinary and medicinal applications.
