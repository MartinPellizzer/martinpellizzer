Vangueria esculenta var. glabra

Vangueria esculenta var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth vangueria, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are smooth and often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It is adapted to arid environments and can store water in its stems for survival.

The species is also valued for its edible fruits, which are used in local cuisines.
