Veronica americana f. albiflora

Veronica americana f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered speedwell, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is distinguished by its pale, almost white flowers, which set it apart from other forms of Veronica americana.
