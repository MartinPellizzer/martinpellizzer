Veronica × divergens

Veronica × divergens, commonly known as the common speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm, featuring upright stems covered in fine, hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers with five petals, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Veronica × divergens thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
