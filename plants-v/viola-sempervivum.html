Viola sempervivum

Viola sempervivum, commonly known as the common violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, fuzzy layer that helps retain moisture.

Viola sempervivum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
