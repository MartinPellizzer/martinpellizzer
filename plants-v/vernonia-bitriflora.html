Vernonia bitriflora

Vernonia bitriflora, commonly known as the two-flowered ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its stem is covered in fine, hairy hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and disturbed sites.

Vernonia bitriflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
