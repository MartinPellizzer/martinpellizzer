Vachellia reficiens subsp. misera

Vachellia reficiens subsp.

misera, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, bipinnate leaves that are typically reduced to leaflets.

The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which are followed by flat, leathery pods. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
