Vaccinium bartlettii

Vaccinium bartlettii, commonly known as Bartlett's blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, similar to other species in the Vaccinium genus. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a glossy surface and dark green color, while its flowers are bell-shaped and white or pale pink.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
