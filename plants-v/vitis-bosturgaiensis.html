Vitis bosturgaiensis

Vitis bosturgaiensis, commonly known as the Bosturghani grapevine, is a species native to Central Asia.

It is a climbing vine belonging to the Vitaceae family, characterized by its tendrils and compound leaves. The plant produces small, clustered berries that are typically green when immature and turn red or black when ripe. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for cultivation, it is occasionally grown in gardens and for its unique fruit characteristics.
