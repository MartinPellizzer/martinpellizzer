Vittadinia megacephala

Vittadinia megacephala, commonly known as the broad-leaved vittadinia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. Its flowers are arranged in a distinctive, rounded inflorescence, which is a key identifying feature.

Vittadinia megacephala is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
