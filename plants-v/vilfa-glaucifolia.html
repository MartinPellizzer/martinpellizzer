Vilfa glaucifolia

Vilfa glaucifolia, commonly known as the blueleaf vilfa, is a perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy, bluish coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure, making it a hardy species adapted to harsh mountain environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
