Verbascum × kavinae

Verbascum × kavinae, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant belonging to the genus Verbascum in the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are typical of the mullein genus. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, terminal spikes. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Verbascum × kavinae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used historically for its medicinal properties.
