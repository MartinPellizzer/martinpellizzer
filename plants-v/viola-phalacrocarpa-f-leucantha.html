Viola phalacrocarpa f. leucantha

Viola phalacrocarpa f. leucantha, commonly known as the white viola, is a rare and striking cultivar of the violet family.

It is characterized by its pale white to cream-colored petals, which contrast with its deep green foliage. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments, often in woodland or shaded areas. It produces small, delicate flowers that bloom in early spring.

This form is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique coloration.
