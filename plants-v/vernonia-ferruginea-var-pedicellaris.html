Vernonia ferruginea var. pedicellaris

Vernonia ferruginea var. pedicellaris, commonly known as the rusty Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters called capitula. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
