Verbascum shiqricum

Verbascum shiqricum, commonly known as the Shiqri mullein, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

Verbascum shiqricum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
