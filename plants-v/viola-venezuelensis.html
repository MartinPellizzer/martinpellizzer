Viola venezuelensis

Viola venezuelensis, commonly known as the Venezuelan violet, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Venezuela.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers bloom in spring and are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola venezuelensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and has limited cultivation outside its native habitat.
