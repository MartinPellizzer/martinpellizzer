Vernonia ambigua

Vernonia ambigua, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its most striking feature is the clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall.

Vernonia ambigua is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
