Viola tenuicornis subsp. baicalensis

Viola tenuicornis subsp.

baicalensis, commonly known as the Baikal violet, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow throat.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and floral characteristics.
