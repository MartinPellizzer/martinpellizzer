Vicia sativa var. bacla

Vicia sativa var. bacla, commonly known as the common vetch, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces pods containing small, edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Vicia sativa var. bacla is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes.
