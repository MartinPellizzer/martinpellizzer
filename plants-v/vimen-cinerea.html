Vimen cinerea

Vimen cinerea, commonly known as the silver willow, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its grayish-brown bark and long, slender leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody capsules containing seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers or in wetlands.

Vimen cinerea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
