Viscum liquidambaricola

Viscum liquidambaricola, commonly known as the liquidambaricola mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of liquidambar trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while also producing its own food through photosynthesis using the limited light it receives. The plant forms a dense, leafy growth that can significantly affect the health of its host by draining resources. Its small, white or yellowish flowers appear in the spring, followed by berries that are often consumed by birds.

Viscum liquidambaricola plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and contributing to the ecological balance of its habitat.
