Viola tricolor var. sudetica

Viola tricolor var. sudetica, commonly known as the Sudetic violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of violet, white, and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It has hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge.

The flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
