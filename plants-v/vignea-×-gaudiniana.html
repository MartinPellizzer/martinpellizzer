Vignea × gaudiniana

Vignea × gaudiniana, commonly known as the hybrid vigna, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, which are typically heart-shaped. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits. Vignea × gaudiniana is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial in soil enrichment and sustainable agriculture practices.
