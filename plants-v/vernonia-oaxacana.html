Vernonia oaxacana

Vernonia oaxacana, commonly known as the Oaxaca ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Vernonia oaxacana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
