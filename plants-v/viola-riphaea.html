Viola riphaea

Viola riphaea, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapting to cold and harsh environments.

Viola riphaea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic alpine conditions.
