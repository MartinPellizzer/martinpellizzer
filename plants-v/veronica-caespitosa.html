Veronica caespitosa

Veronica caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, blue or violet flowers in clusters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Veronica caespitosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden landscapes.
