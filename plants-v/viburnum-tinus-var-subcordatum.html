Viburnum tinus var. subcordatum

Viburnum tinus var. subcordatum, commonly known as the Persian boxwood, is a compact evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, dark green foliage.

It features small, white, clustered flowers that appear in early spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in hedges or as a specimen plant.

Its slow growth rate makes it suitable for formal garden designs.
