Viola woroschilovii

Viola woroschilovii, commonly known as the Woroschilov's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
