Vochysia assua

Vochysia assua, commonly known as the golden glory tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, palmate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. These flowers are followed by oval, woody fruits containing edible seeds.

Vochysia assua is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
