Vochysia punctata

Vochysia punctata, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its fragrant blooms.
