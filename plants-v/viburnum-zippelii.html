Viburnum zippelii

Viburnum zippelii, commonly known as Zippel's viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and features opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers in late spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. Viburnum zippelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also noted for its resistance to common diseases and pests.
