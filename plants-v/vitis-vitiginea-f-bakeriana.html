Vitis vitiginea f. bakeriana

Vitis vitiginea f. bakeriana, commonly known as the Baker's wild grape, is a variant of the wild grapevine native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically followed by clusters of black, tart berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it is notable for its adaptability and role in local ecosystems.
