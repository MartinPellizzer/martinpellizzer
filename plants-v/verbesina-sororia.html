Verbesina sororia

Verbesina sororia, commonly known as the yellow beggar-ticks, is an annual flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves with a rough texture, and its seeds are encased in bristly pappi that aid in wind dispersal. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along stream banks.

The species is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
