Viola eugeniae subsp. eugeniae

Viola eugeniae subsp.

eugeniae, commonly known as the Eugenia violet, is a species native to southern Africa. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils.

The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center. It blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
