Verbesina lanata

Verbesina lanata, commonly known as the woolly beggar-tick, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from summer to fall. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along stream banks.

Verbesina lanata is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
