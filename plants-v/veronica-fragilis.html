Veronica fragilis

Veronica fragilis, commonly known as slender speedwell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture.

Veronica fragilis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
