Vitis parkeri

Vitis parkeri, commonly known as the Parker's grape, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly found in Florida and Georgia. The plant is characterized by its climbing vines, heart-shaped leaves, and small, greenish-white flowers. Its berries are typically small and turn from green to red as they mature.

Vitis parkeri is often grown for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and unique appearance.
