Viburnum sibiricum

Viburnum sibiricum, commonly known as Siberian cranberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, red berries that ripen in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It produces fragrant, white flowers in panicles during early spring.

Viburnum sibiricum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist, acidic soils.
