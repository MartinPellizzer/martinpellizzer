Veronica serotina

Veronica serotina, commonly known as late Veronica, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and spikes of blue or purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, hence its common name. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Veronica serotina is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
