Virola crebrinervia

Virola crebrinervia, commonly known as the Amazonian ironwood tree, is a large evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, dark bark and large, elliptical leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, spiky fruits containing edible seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood and has traditional uses in local medicine.

The species plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
