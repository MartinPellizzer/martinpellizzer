Vernonia malabarica

Vernonia malabarica, commonly known as Malabar ironweed, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of India and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and along stream banks.

Vernonia malabarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
