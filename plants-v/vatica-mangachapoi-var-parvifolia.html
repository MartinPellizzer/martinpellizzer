Vatica mangachapoi var. parvifolia

Vatica mangachapoi var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, hard wood and elliptical leaves. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and is valued for its durable timber. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are often yellow or greenish in color.
