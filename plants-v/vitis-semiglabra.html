Vitis semiglabra

Vitis semiglabra, commonly known as the smoothleaf grapevine, is a woody vine native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into clusters of round, edible berries. Vitis semiglabra is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
