Vigna bosseri

Vigna bosseri, commonly known as the African wing bean, is a leguminous plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds. Vigna bosseri is drought-tolerant and thrives in warm, tropical climates.

It is valued for its nutritional content and is often grown as a food crop in regions where it is adapted.
