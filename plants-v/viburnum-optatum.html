Viburnum optatum

Viburnum optatum, commonly known as the snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that appear in early spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and hairy underneath. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Viburnum optatum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and wildlife support.
