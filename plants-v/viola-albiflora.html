Viola albiflora

Viola albiflora, commonly known as the white violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, delicate flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Viola albiflora is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat respiratory and digestive ailments.
