Viola brevistipulata subsp. hidakana

Viola brevistipulata subsp.

hidakana, commonly known as the Hidakana Violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky areas.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and small, delicate flowers that range in color from pale purple to white. Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
