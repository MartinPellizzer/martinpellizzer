Viola × raunsiensis

Viola × raunsiensis, commonly known as the Swedish violet, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Viola lutea and Viola tricolor.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe, particularly Sweden and neighboring regions. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and clusters of violet or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable environments.
