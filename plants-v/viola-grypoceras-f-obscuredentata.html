Viola grypoceras f. obscuredentata

Viola grypoceras f. obscuredentata, commonly known as the obscure-leaved viola, is a rare and lesser-known botanical variant within the Viola genus.

It is characterized by its unique foliage, which features densely packed, dark green leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of violet and white, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in shaded woodland areas.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
