Verbascum hybridum

Verbascum hybridum, commonly known as the common mullein, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features tall, hairy stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, velvety leaves that are broad and heart-shaped. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in clusters at the top of the stems, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves and flowers contain various bioactive compounds, making it useful in traditional medicine.

Verbascum hybridum is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
