Vaccinium uliginosum var. nanum

Vaccinium uliginosum var. nanum, commonly known as dwarf blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to moist, acidic environments such as bogs and heathlands.

It produces small, dark blue berries that are rich in antioxidants and commonly used in culinary applications. The plant has small, oval leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained, nutrient-poor soil.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form dense ground cover.
