Vigna desmodioides

Vigna desmodioides, commonly known as the sticky vetch, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into elongated, pod-like fruits. It has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, making it useful in soil enrichment.

Vigna desmodioides is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain environments.
