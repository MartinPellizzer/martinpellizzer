Vernonia inanis

Vernonia inanis, commonly known as the slender ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands, swamps, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

Vernonia inanis is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
