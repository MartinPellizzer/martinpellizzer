Vaccinium darrowii

Vaccinium darrowii, commonly known as the Darrow huckleberry, is a species of shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark blue or black in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The plant has both ornamental and ecological value, supporting local wildlife and providing food for birds and mammals.
