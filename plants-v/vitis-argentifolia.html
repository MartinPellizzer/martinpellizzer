Vitis argentifolia

Vitis argentifolia, commonly known as silverleaf grapevine, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in the spring, followed by edible berries that ripen to a dark purple color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Vitis argentifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
