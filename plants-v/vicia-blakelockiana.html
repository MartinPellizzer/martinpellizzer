Vicia blakelockiana

Vicia blakelockiana, commonly known as Blakelock's vetch, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial shade. It has a trailing growth habit and produces small, elliptical pods that contain several seeds.

Vicia blakelockiana is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
