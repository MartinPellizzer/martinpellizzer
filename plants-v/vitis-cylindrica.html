Vitis cylindrica

Vitis cylindrica, commonly known as the cylindrical grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its climbing habit and cylindrical, often hairy stems. The leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and typically have a rough texture. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by berries that turn from green to yellow or red as they mature.

This plant is often found in moist woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
