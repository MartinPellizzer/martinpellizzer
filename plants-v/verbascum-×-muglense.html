Verbascum × muglense

Verbascum × muglense, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is a hybrid between Verbascum blattaria and Verbascum sinaiticum, characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It is native to the Middle East and parts of North Africa, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

Verbascum × muglense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
