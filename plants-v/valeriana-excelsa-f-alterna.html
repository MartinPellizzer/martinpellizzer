Valeriana excelsa f. alterna

Valeriana excelsa f. alterna, commonly known as the alternate valerian, is a variant of the valerian plant native to parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or woodland areas. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a serrated margin, while its roots are aromatic and contain essential oils.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
