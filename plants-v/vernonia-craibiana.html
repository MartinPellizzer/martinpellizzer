Vernonia craibiana

Vernonia craibiana, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces striking blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. Vernonia craibiana is often found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and forest edges.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-resistant, native plants.
