Vangueria subbiflora

Vangueria subbiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved vangueria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture, adapting to the dry climate.

Vangueria subbiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
