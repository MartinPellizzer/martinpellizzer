Veronica tenuifolia var. oscensis

Veronica tenuifolia var. oscensis, commonly known as the slender speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

The species is also known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, forming dense mats.
