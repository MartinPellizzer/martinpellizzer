Verbascum purpureum

Verbascum purpureum, commonly known as purple mullein, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands.

Verbascum purpureum is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its soothing effects.
