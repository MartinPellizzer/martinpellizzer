Viburnum tomentosum var. rotundifolium

Viburnum tomentosum var. rotundifolium, commonly known as the roseleaf snowball, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are ovate to round in shape, with a velvety, grayish underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

This variety is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators and its adaptability to various soil types.
