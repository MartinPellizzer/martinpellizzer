Veronica × rotundata

Veronica × rotundata, commonly known as roundleaf speedwell, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are rounded and often covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
