Vignea bertolonii

Vignea bertolonii, commonly known as the Bertoloni's vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural studies.
