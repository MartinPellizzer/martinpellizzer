Viola pentadactyla

Viola pentadactyla, commonly known as the five-finger violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with five distinct lobes, giving it its common name. The plant produces delicate violet flowers that range in color from purple to blue, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in shaded areas.

Viola pentadactyla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
