Vernonia congesta

Vernonia congesta, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is a tall, herbaceous perennial that can grow up to 2 meters in height, characterized by its dense, spiky inflorescences. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in compact clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia congesta is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in full sun and well-drained soils.
