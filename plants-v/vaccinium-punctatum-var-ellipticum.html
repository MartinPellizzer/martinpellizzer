Vaccinium punctatum var. ellipticum

Vaccinium punctatum var. ellipticum, commonly known as the elliptic bilberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by edible, dark blue berries. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in boreal forests and heathlands.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
