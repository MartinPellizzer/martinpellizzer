Vittaria linearifolia

Vittaria linearifolia, commonly known as the slender fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Vittariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or branches in the understory of rainforests. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a finely divided structure, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Vittaria linearifolia is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities and ability to thrive in controlled indoor environments.
