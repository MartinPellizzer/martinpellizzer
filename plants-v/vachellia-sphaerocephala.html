Vachellia sphaerocephala

Vachellia sphaerocephala, commonly known as the knob thorn, is a shrub or small tree native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive knobby, thorn-covered stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by long, flat pods containing seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species in regions with limited rainfall.

Vachellia sphaerocephala is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of browse for livestock.
