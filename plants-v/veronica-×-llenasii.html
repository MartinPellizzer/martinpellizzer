Veronica × llenasii

Veronica × llenasii, commonly known as the hybrid speedwell, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse blue to purple flowers. The plant typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from late spring to early autumn.
