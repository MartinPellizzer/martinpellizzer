Viola variegata f. ircutiana

Viola variegata f. ircutiana, commonly known as the variegated violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, featuring a mix of green and white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple, pink, or white, typically blooming in early spring. Native to temperate regions of Europe, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation and botanical collections.
