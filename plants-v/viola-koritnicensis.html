Viola koritnicensis

Viola koritnicensis, commonly known as the Koritnic viola, is a species of flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 15 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces violet-colored flowers with five petals, often with a distinctive dark purple or black spot near the base. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of downy hair.

Viola koritnicensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
