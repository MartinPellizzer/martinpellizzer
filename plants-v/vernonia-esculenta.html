Vernonia esculenta

Vernonia esculenta, commonly known as wild ironweed, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features tall, slender stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and often covered with a fine white bloom.

Vernonia esculenta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
