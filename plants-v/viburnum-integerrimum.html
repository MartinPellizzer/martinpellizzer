Viburnum integerrimum

Viburnum integerrimum, commonly known as American beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of showy, violet-pink berries that appear in late summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces fragrant, white flowers in panicles during early spring.

Viburnum integerrimum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its vibrant fruit and attractive foliage.
