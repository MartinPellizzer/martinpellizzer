Viola × indivisa

Viola × indivisa, commonly known as the common pansy, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant flowers and compact growth habit.

Viola × indivisa is native to Europe and has been widely introduced to other regions for garden use.
