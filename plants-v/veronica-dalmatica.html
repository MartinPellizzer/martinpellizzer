Veronica dalmatica

Veronica dalmatica, commonly known as the Dalmatian speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Veronica dalmatica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
