Vernonia syringifolia

Vernonia syringifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and upright growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow to brown flower heads that appear in late summer to early fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and disturbed sites.

Vernonia syringifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to support local pollinators.
