Verbascum × dervichorum

Verbascum × dervichorum, commonly known as the Whirling Dance Plant, is a hybrid species of the genus Verbascum, characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowing robes.

It is native to the arid regions of central Asia and is known for its unique growth habit, where the stems twist and coil as the plant matures. The plant typically produces showy yellow or orange flowers that emerge from the base of the bracts, creating a dramatic visual effect. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.

Due to its unusual appearance and cultural significance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
