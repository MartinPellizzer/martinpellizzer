Vicia unijuga var. angustifolia

Vicia unijuga var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf vetch, is a perennial legume native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellowish-green pea-like flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in meadows and along roadsides. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for both horticultural and environmental purposes.
