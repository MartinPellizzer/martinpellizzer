Vernonia pentacantha

Vernonia pentacantha, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its grayish bark and compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by spherical, woody fruits. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

The plant has traditional uses in medicine and is also valued for its hard, durable wood.
