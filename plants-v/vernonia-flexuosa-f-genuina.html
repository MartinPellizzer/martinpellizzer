Vernonia flexuosa f. genuina

Vernonia flexuosa f. genuina, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, dark violet to purple flowers. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in wetland ecosystems.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
