Vaccinium motosukeanum

Vaccinium motosukeanum, commonly known as the Japanese huckleberry, is a species of dwarf shrub belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bright red berries. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers are typically pink or white, and it produces edible fruits that are appreciated in local cuisine.

The plant plays a role in supporting local wildlife and has cultural significance in Japanese horticulture.
