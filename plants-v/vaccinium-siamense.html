Vaccinium siamense

Vaccinium siamense, commonly known as Siam huckleberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes blueberries and cranberries, and is characterized by its acidic soil preferences. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by berries that range in color from red to black. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a waxy surface, typical of many Ericaceae species.

Vaccinium siamense is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
