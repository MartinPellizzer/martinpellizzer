Viola umbrosa

Viola umbrosa, commonly known as the shadow violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces delicate, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive yellow center. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Viola umbrosa is also known for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
