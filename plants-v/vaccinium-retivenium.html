Vaccinium retivenium

Vaccinium retivenium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved huckleberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. Its flowers are bell-shaped, pink to white, and appear in clusters, followed by edible, dark blue berries.

Vaccinium retivenium thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, temperate climates.
