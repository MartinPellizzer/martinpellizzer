Viguiera tenuis var. alba

Viguiera tenuis var. alba, commonly known as the whiteleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy choice for gardens and natural landscapes.

Its botanical name reflects its slender stems and white-flowered variant, distinguishing it from other members of the Asteraceae family.
