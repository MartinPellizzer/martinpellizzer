Verbesina crassicaulis

Verbesina crassicaulis, commonly known as the broad-stemmed beggar-tick, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaves. The plant features rough, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often found in moist, open habitats like meadows and along stream banks.
