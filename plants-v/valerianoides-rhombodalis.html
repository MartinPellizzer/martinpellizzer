Valerianoides rhombodalis

Valerianoides rhombodalis, commonly known as the diamondleaf valerian, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Valerianaceae and is characterized by its rhomboid-shaped leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
