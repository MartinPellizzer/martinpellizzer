Viola hladnikii

Viola hladnikii, commonly known as the Hladnik's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or woodland soils and prefers partial shade to full sun. Viola hladnikii is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

It also plays a role in supporting local pollinators due to its nectar-rich blossoms.
