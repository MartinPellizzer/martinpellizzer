Vicia tomentosa

Vicia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, often silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Vicia tomentosa is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

It has a shallow root system and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
