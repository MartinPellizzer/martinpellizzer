Vigna antillana

Vigna antillana, commonly known as the Caribbean cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems that twine around supports. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Vigna antillana is valued for its nutritional content and is often cultivated for both food and forage purposes.
