Vangueria oligacantha

Vangueria oligacantha, commonly known as the rock fig or African rock fig, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, pale green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels. The plant is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it drought-resistant and resilient. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that is edible and often consumed by both humans and animals.

Vangueria oligacantha plays an important role in local ecosystems and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
