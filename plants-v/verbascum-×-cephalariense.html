Verbascum × cephalariense

Verbascum × cephalariense, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Scrophulariaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in dense, soft grayish-white hairs. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
