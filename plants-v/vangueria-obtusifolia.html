Vangueria obtusifolia

Vangueria obtusifolia, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature.

Vangueria obtusifolia is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in dry climates.
