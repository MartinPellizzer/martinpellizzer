Veronica teucrium var. subfloccosotomentosa

Veronica teucrium var. subfloccosotomentosa, commonly known as the woolly speedwell, is a flowering plant in the family Plantaginaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, woolly layer of hairs, which helps reduce water loss.
