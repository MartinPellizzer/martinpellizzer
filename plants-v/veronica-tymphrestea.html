Veronica tymphrestea

Veronica tymphrestea, commonly known as the woolly speedwell, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Veronica tymphrestea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
