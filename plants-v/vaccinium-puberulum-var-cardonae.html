Vaccinium puberulum var. cardonae

Vaccinium puberulum var. cardonae, commonly known as the Cardonae blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It produces small, dark blue berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers a humid, temperate climate. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its potential as a food source in its native habitat.
