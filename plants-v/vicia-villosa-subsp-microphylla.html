Vicia villosa subsp. microphylla

Vicia villosa subsp.

microphylla, commonly known as the hairy vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and western Asia. It features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple.

The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods containing several elliptical seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in agriculture as a cover crop and for soil nitrogen fixation.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
