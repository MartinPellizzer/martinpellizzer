Viola sepincola subsp. adriatica

Viola sepincola subsp.

adriatica, commonly known as the Adriatic violet, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is a member of the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and violet-blue flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has small, hairy stems and produces seed pods that split open to release tiny seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
