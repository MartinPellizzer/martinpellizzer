Vaccinium oxycoccos var. intermedium

Vaccinium oxycoccos var. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate cranberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, glossy, dark green leaves and red or orange berries. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, bogs, and heathlands. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.

The berries are edible but are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
