Vitis intricata

Vitis intricata, commonly known as the prickly pear cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, paddle-shaped stems that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers in the spring, followed by edible, fleshy fruits known as tunas. Its botanical features include a robust root system and the ability to photosynthesize through its stems.

Vitis intricata plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various desert animals.
