Veronica bellardii

Veronica bellardii, commonly known as the Bellard's speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Veronica bellardii is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
