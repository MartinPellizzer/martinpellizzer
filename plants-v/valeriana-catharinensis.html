Valeriana catharinensis

Valeriana catharinensis, commonly known as the Brazilian valerian, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Valerianaceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clustered, small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and riverbanks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a distinct aromatic scent.

Valeriana catharinensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its calming effects.
