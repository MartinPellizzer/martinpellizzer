Vaccinium exiguum

Vaccinium exiguum, commonly known as the dwarf blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like heather and cranberry. The plant produces small, acidic berries that are typically consumed by wildlife and birds. Its leaves are elliptical, dark green, and covered in fine, white hairs.

Vaccinium exiguum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in boreal and subarctic environments.
