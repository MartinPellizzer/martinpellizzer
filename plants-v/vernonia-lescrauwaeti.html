Vernonia lescrauwaeti

Vernonia lescrauwaeti, commonly known as the Lescrau's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats and is known for its hardy nature. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia lescrauwaeti is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
