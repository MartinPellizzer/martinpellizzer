Veronica virgata

Veronica virgata, commonly known as the slender speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often branched.

Veronica virgata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
