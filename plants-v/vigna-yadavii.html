Vigna yadavii

Vigna yadavii, commonly known as the Indian dwarf bean, is a leguminous plant native to India.

It is characterized by its small, climbing vines and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. Vigna yadavii is often grown for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients.

It thrives in warm climates and is adapted to well-drained soils.
