Veronica austriaca var. dentata

Veronica austriaca var. dentata, commonly known as the Austrian speedwell, is a perennial plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features basal rosettes of lance-shaped, toothed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers with five petals, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along streambanks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
