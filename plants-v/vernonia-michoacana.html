Vernonia michoacana

Vernonia michoacana, commonly known as Michoacan ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Vernonia michoacana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
