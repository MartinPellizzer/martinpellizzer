Vernonia triflora

Vernonia triflora, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Vernonia triflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to support local pollinators.
