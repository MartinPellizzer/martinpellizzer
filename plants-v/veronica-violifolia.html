Veronica violifolia

Veronica violifolia, commonly known as the blueweed or violetleaf speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and opposite, often with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
