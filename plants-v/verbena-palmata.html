Verbena palmata

Verbena palmata, commonly known as the Florida verbena or sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. The plant has palmately arranged leaves, which give it its common name, and it thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. It is often found in coastal dunes and dry, open habitats.

Verbena palmata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
