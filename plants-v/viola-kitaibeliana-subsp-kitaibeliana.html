Viola kitaibeliana subsp. kitaibeliana

Viola kitaibeliana subsp.

kitaibeliana, commonly known as the Kitaibel's violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia. It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green and covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of violet, blue, or white, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
