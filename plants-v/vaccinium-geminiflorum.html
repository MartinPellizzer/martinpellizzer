Vaccinium geminiflorum

Vaccinium geminiflorum, commonly known as the twinflower blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in early spring, often in clusters of two, giving it its common name. The plant produces edible berries that are small, tart, and typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local wildlife.
