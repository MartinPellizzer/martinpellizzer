Vernonia dictyophlebia

Vernonia dictyophlebia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in wetlands and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are typically blue to purple in color.

Vernonia dictyophlebia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
