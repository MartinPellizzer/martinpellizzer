Viola latistipula

Viola latistipula, commonly known as the broad-tipped violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet-blue flower. The plant has a short, hairy stem that supports its five-petaled flowers, which bloom in early spring. Its botanical characteristics include a taproot system and small, kidney-shaped leaves.

Viola latistipula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
