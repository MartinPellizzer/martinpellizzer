Viola linariifolia

Viola linariifolia, commonly known as the Linaria viola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves with a velvety texture, and its flowers are typically violet or purple, with five petals arranged in a spurred shape. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, often growing in rocky or alpine habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
