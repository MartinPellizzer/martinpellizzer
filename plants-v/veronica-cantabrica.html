Veronica cantabrica

Veronica cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Veronica cantabrica thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
