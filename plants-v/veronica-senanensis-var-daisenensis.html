Veronica senanensis var. daisenensis

Veronica senanensis var. daisenensis, commonly known as the Daisen Veronica, is a perennial flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate foliage and prolonged flowering period.
