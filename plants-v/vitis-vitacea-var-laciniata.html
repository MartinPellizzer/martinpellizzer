Vitis vitacea var. laciniata

Vitis vitacea var. laciniata, commonly known as the cutleaf muscadine vine, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, deeply lobed leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by clusters of edible, dark purple to black grapes. Its vines are known for their vigorous growth and ability to thrive in partial shade.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and for its fruit, which has a musky flavor.
