Veronica chamaedrys var. pulcherrima

Veronica chamaedrys var. pulcherrima, commonly known as the sweet everlasting or sweet germander, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, blue-to-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has silver-green, needle-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its flowers are known for their long-lasting nature, retaining their color and shape even when dried.
