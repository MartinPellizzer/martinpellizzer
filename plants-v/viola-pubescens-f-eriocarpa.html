Viola pubescens f. eriocarpa

Viola pubescens f. eriocarpa, commonly known as the downy violet, is a botanical variant of the common violet.

It is characterized by its dense, pubescent foliage and flowers that typically range in color from purple to white. The plant belongs to the Violaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
