Vicia galegifolia

Vicia galegifolia, commonly known as the yellow vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green color and produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of yellow and green. Its root system forms symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enriching the soil.

Vicia galegifolia is valued for its ecological role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
