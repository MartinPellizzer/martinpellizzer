Vernonia angustifolia var. mohrii

Vernonia angustifolia var. mohrii, commonly known as Mohri's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
