Vicia arvernensis

Vicia arvernensis, commonly known as the Arvernian vetch, is a flowering plant species native to Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. Vicia arvernensis thrives in moist, grassy habitats and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of ecological systems in its native range.
