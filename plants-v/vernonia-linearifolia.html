Vernonia linearifolia

Vernonia linearifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Vernonia linearifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
