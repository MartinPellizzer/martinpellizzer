Vaccinium longibracteatum

Vaccinium longibracteatum, commonly known as the longibracteatum blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, similar to other Vaccinium species. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

This plant is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its potential as a fruit crop.
