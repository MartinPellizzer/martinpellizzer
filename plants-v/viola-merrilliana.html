Viola merrilliana

Viola merrilliana, commonly known as the Hawaiian violet, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers have five petals with a distinctive dark purple center.

Viola merrilliana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also an important part of Hawaii's native flora.
