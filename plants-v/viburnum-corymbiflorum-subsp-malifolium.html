Viburnum corymbiflorum subsp. malifolium

Viburnum corymbiflorum subsp.

malifolium, commonly known as the Malifolium viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa. It features compound leaves with a broad, ovate shape and a distinctive, fragrant white flower cluster that appears in late winter to early spring.

The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
