Vincetoxicum hirundinaria var. timbal-lagravei

Vincetoxicum hirundinaria var. timbal-lagravei, commonly known as the Timbal-Lagrave's goatweed, is a variant of the common goatweed species.

It is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. The plant features slender, branched stems that often grow along the ground, and its leaves are opposite, with a heart-shaped base. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds.

This variant is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
