Verbena macrodonta

Verbena macrodonta, commonly known as the largeflower verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom from summer into fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity in arid landscapes.
