Viola suavis var. adriatica

Viola suavis var. adriatica, commonly known as the Adriatic violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its charming appearance.
