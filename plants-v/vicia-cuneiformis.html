Vicia cuneiformis

Vicia cuneiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf vetch, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Europe and western Asia, often found in grassy areas and along roadsides. The plant has slender, trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes. Its seed pods are slender and contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Vicia cuneiformis is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects.
