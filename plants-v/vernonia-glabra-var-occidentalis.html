Vernonia glabra var. occidentalis

Vernonia glabra var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
