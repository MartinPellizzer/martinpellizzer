Viscaria neglecta

Viscaria neglecta, commonly known as the common chickweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The flowers are small, white or pink, and typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in lawns, gardens, and along pathways.

This plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
