Verbascum × obornyi

Verbascum × obornyi, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Verbascum blattaria and Verbascum blattifolium.

It is a tall, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils. The plant features large, velvety, gray-green leaves that are broad and lance-shaped. It produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a spike-like inflorescence.

Verbascum × obornyi is native to the Mediterranean region and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
