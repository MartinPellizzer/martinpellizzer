Verbascum phoeniceum subsp. flavidum

Verbascum phoeniceum subsp.

flavidum, commonly known as the yellow flax or yellow mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, yellowish-green leaves.

The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and resilient species.

Its fibrous stems and leaves have historically been used for textile production and medicinal purposes.
