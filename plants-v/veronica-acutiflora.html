Veronica acutiflora

Veronica acutiflora, commonly known as the sharpflower speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that bear small, blue to violet flowers with five petals, typically blooming from June to August. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and prolonged flowering period.
