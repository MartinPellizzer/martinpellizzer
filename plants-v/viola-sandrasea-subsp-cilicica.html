Viola sandrasea subsp. cilicica

Viola sandrasea subsp.

cilicica, commonly known as the Cilician violet, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in woodland or scrub habitats.

The plant produces violet-blue flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
