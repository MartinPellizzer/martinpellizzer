Vicia olbiensis

Vicia olbiensis, commonly known as the Olbiensis vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Vicia olbiensis is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed habitats.
