Vachellia natalitia

Vachellia natalitia, commonly known as Natal umbrella thorn, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, woody stems and compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by long, narrow pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to poor soil conditions.

Vachellia natalitia is often used in traditional medicine and for soil stabilization in degraded landscapes.
