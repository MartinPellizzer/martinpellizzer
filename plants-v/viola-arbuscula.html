Viola arbuscula

Viola arbuscula, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb with a low-growing habit, featuring heart-shaped leaves and showy violet flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing flowers in shades of purple, violet, and white. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and forested areas.

Viola arbuscula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
