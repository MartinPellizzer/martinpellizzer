Vernonia tropophila

Vernonia tropophila, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of yellow to orange tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands, where it thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia tropophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
