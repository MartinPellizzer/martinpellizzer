Viburnum pubinerve f. calvescens

Viburnum pubinerve f. calvescens, commonly known as the velvetleaf viburnum, is a shrub characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and showy, fragrant flowers.

It belongs to the honeysuckle family (Caprifoliaceae) and is native to eastern North America. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a soft, velvety texture on both sides. It produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to a range of soil conditions.
