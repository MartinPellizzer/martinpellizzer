Vismia caparosa

Vismia caparosa, commonly known as the African ebony, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and glossy, elliptical leaves that are often clustered at the ends of the branches. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its hard, heavy wood used in furniture and musical instruments.
