Viola × brauniae

Viola × brauniae, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a member of the Violaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental, heart-shaped leaves and showy flowers.

It is a hybrid species resulting from crosses between Viola tricolor and Viola × wittrockiana, which gives it a diverse range of color variations. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and thrives in partial shade with moist, well-drained soil. Its flowers, which range from purple to white, bloom in spring and are often used in gardens for their aesthetic appeal.

Viola × brauniae is valued for its hardiness and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
