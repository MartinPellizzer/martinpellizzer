Vinticena celle

Vinticena celle, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, hanging clusters of yellow-orange flowers that resemble small bells. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement along the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Vinticena celle is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
