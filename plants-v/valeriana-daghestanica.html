Valeriana daghestanica

Valeriana daghestanica, commonly known as the Caucasian valerian, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features a stout, upright stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters. Its roots are rich in volatile oils and are used in traditional medicine for their sedative properties.

Valeriana daghestanica thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.
