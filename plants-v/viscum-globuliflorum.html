Viscum globuliflorum

Viscum globuliflorum, commonly known as the globular flower mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that relies on host trees for water and nutrients.

It belongs to the Viscaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are thick and leathery, adapted to reduce water loss in its host environment.

Viscum globuliflorum is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives on a variety of host trees.
