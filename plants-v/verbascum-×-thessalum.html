Verbascum × thessalum

Verbascum × thessalum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes several species of flowering plants known for their fuzzy leaves and showy yellow or orange flowers. The hybrid form combines characteristics from its parent species, Verbascum blattaria and Verbascum thapsus, resulting in a plant with robust growth and attractive foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
