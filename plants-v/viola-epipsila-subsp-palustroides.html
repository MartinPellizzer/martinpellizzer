Viola epipsila subsp. palustroides

Viola epipsila subsp.

palustroides, commonly known as the marsh violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as bogs, marshes, and along stream banks.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet or purple coloration, and its flowers are usually pale purple or white with dark veins. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining wetland biodiversity.
