Viburnum × hizenense

Viburnum × hizenense, commonly known as the Japanese snowball viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its clusters of white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
