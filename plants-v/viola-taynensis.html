Viola taynensis

Viola taynensis, commonly known as the Tayan violet, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States, particularly found in the Appalachian region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of small, delicate purple flowers. Viola taynensis belongs to the Violaceae family, which includes other violets and related species.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically significant component of its native ecosystem.
