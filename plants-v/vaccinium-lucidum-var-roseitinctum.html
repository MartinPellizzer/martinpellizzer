Vaccinium lucidum var. roseitinctum

Vaccinium lucidum var. roseitinctum, commonly known as the rose-tinctured huckleberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and rhododendrons. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits, which are typically red or purple, are edible and often used in jams and culinary applications.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
