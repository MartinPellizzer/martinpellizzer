Vaccinium montis-ericae

Vaccinium montis-ericae, commonly known as the mountain heather, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and azaleas. The plant typically features small, evergreen leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Vaccinium montis-ericae thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and rocky areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of alpine and subalpine ecosystems.
