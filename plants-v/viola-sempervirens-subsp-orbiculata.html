Viola sempervirens subsp. orbiculata

Viola sempervirens subsp.

orbiculata, commonly known as the roundleaf violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy violet or purple flowers with a distinctive yellow center.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and ornamental value in gardens and natural habitats.
