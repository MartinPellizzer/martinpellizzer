Vaccinium elliptifolium

Vaccinium elliptifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and produces small, edible blue berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist environments. It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting pollinators.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
