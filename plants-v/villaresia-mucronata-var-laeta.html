Villaresia mucronata var. laeta

Villaresia mucronata var. laeta, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb and linear leaves, typically growing in shaded, humid environments. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often with a distinct yellow lip. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered threatened and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
