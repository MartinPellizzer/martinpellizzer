Vitis pentagona var. honanensis

Vitis pentagona var. honanensis, commonly known as the Honan grapevine, is a lesser-known species within the Vitaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its distinctive five-angled stem and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered berries that are typically green when mature. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.

However, its fruit is not widely cultivated for commercial purposes.
