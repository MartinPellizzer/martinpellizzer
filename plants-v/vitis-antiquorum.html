Vitis antiquorum

Vitis antiquorum, commonly known as the ancient grapevine, is a rare and ancient species of vine native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that develop into berries, which are typically dark purple in color. Vitis antiquorum is valued for its historical significance and potential use in viticulture and horticulture.

Due to its limited distribution and rarity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
