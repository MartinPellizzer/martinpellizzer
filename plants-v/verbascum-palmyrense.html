Verbascum palmyrense

Verbascum palmyrense, commonly known as the Palmyrene mullein, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their large, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are large, fuzzy, and gray-green in color, while its flowers are yellow and clustered in a terminal spike.

It is often found in areas with low precipitation and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
