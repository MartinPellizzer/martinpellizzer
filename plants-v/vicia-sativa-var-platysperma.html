Vicia sativa var. platysperma

Vicia sativa var. platysperma, commonly known as broad bean or fava bean, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow pea-like flowers that develop into pods containing several large, flat seeds. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein.

This variety is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
