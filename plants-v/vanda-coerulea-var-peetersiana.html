Vanda coerulea var. peetersiana

Vanda coerulea var. peetersiana, commonly known as the Peeters' orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has long, strap-like leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. It thrives in humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beauty in both indoor and greenhouse settings.
