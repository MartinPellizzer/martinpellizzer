Vicia baborensis

Vicia baborensis, commonly known as the Baborensis vetch, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Vicia baborensis is often found in grasslands, along roadsides, and in disturbed soils, where it plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain insect species.
