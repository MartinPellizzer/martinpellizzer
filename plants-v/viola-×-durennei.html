Viola × durennei

Viola × durennei, commonly known as the velvetleaf viola, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Its hybrid origin contributes to its adaptability and resilience in various soil conditions.
