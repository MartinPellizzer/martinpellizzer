Veronica opaca var. affinis

Veronica opaca var. affinis, commonly known as the narrowleaf speedwell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems covered with small, opposite leaves that are typically lance-shaped and hairy. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers, each with five petals, that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or meadow habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
