Vignea pulla

Vignea pulla, commonly known as the small morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, often opening in the morning. Vignea pulla thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
