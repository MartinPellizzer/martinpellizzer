Verbascum alpigenum

Verbascum alpigenum, commonly known as the alpine mullein, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a loose panicle. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Verbascum alpigenum is a perennial species that is adapted to cold climates and can tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
