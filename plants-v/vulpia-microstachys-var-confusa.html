Vulpia microstachys var. confusa

Vulpia microstachys var. confusa, commonly known as the narrow-stalked veldtgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils, exhibiting a low, tufted growth habit. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green coloration.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
