Viola duclouxii

Viola duclouxii, commonly known as the Ducloux violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight. Viola duclouxii is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

Its flowers are usually purple or violet in color, with five petals and a distinctive pattern of markings.
