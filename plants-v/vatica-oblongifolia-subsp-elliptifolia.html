Vatica oblongifolia subsp. elliptifolia

Vatica oblongifolia subsp.

elliptifolia, commonly known as the elliptic vatica, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the branches.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill evergreen forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
