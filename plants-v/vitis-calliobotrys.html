Vitis calliobotrys

Vitis calliobotrys, commonly known as the hairy grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to parts of Central and South America, where it typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that help it ascend supports. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are small and greenish-white, and the fruit is a small, round berry that ripens to a dark purple color.
