Vernonia betonicifolia

Vernonia betonicifolia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blue or purple blooms. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-gray hue.

Vernonia betonicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
