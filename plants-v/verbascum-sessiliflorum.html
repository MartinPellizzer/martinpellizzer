Verbascum sessiliflorum

Verbascum sessiliflorum, commonly known as mullein, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in a terminal spike. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Mullein is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for respiratory and skin ailments.
