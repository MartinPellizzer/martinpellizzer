Viola regina

Viola regina, commonly known as the royal viola, is a flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, where it thrives in woodland and meadow habitats. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. Viola regina is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
