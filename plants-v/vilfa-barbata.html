Vilfa barbata

Vilfa barbata, commonly known as the hairy veldt grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and can thrive in a variety of soil types. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered at the base of the plant.

Vilfa barbata is valued for its role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock.
