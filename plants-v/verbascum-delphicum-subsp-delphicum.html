Verbascum delphicum subsp. delphicum

Verbascum delphicum subsp.

delphicum, commonly known as the Delphic mullein, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, velvety leaves that are often covered in soft, fuzzy hairs.

The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in late summer, which are clustered in loose, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential soothing properties.
