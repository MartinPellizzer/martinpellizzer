Vilfa xerampelina

Vilfa xerampelina, commonly known as the hybrid vilfa, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with shades of green and yellow. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white and pale pink. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and limited natural range, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
