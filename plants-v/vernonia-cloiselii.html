Vernonia cloiselii

Vernonia cloiselii, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, shrub-like species that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense heads, which are often visited by bees and other pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, and the plant is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in arid conditions.

Vernonia cloiselii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
