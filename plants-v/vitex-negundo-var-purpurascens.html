Vitex negundo var. purpurascens

Vitex negundo var. purpurascens, commonly known as purple-leaf vitex, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that turn a striking purple or reddish hue in the autumn. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale blue flowers in the summer, which are followed by smooth, dark blue-black berries. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it suitable for hedging and landscape design.
