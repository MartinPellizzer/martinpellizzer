Vriesea ventricosa

Vriesea ventricosa, commonly known as the pineapple bromeliad, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored and covered in a waxy layer. The plant produces a striking inflorescence with a tall, spiky flower that can reach several feet in height. Its leaves are thick and succulent, adapted to store water in its native arid environments.

Vriesea ventricosa is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
