Verbascum × collinum subsp. semialbum

Verbascum × collinum subsp.

semialbum, commonly known as the white mullein, is a hybrid plant belonging to the genus Verbascum in the family Scrophulariaceae. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are often covered in soft hairs.

The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats like roadsides and meadows.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
