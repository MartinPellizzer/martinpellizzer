Viola tricolor proles arvensis

Viola tricolor proles arvensis, commonly known as the common heartsease, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy violet, yellow, and white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It has a fibrous root system and can spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural fields.

The species is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in cool, temperate climates.
