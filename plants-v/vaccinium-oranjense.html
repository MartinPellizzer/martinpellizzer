Vaccinium oranjense

Vaccinium oranjense, commonly known as the Orange honeysuckle, is a shrub native to the Cape Province of South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Vaccinium oranjense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators.
