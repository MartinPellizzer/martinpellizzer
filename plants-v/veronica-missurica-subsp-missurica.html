Veronica missurica subsp. missurica

Veronica missurica subsp.

missurica, commonly known as the Missourian speedwell, is a perennial herb native to the central United States. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies, meadows, and woodland edges.

The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces blue to purple flowers in clusters. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in sandy or loamy soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological restoration projects.
