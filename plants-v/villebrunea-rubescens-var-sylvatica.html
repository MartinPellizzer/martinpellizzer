Villebrunea rubescens var. sylvatica

Villebrunea rubescens var. sylvatica, commonly known as the red-stemmed sylvatic vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems that turn reddish-brown with age, and its heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, followed by yellowish-orange fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
