Veronica vandewateri

Veronica vandewateri, commonly known as Vandewater's speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the plantain family, Plantaginaceae, and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environments.
