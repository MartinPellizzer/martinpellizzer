Verbena tristachya

Verbena tristachya, commonly known as the slender verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in shades of blue, purple, and white, producing a profusion of flowers throughout the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.

Verbena tristachya is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
