Vandopsis

Vandopsis, commonly known as the lace fern, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Vandopsis.

These plants are characterized by their delicate, fern-like fronds and are native to tropical regions of Africa. They typically grow as epiphytes, attaching themselves to trees and other structures in their natural habitat. Vandopsis species often have small, clustered flowers that are inconspicuous compared to their foliage.

The genus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in greenhouses or as houseplants.
