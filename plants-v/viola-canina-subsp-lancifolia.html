Viola canina subsp. lancifolia

Viola canina subsp.

lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features heart-shaped, lanceolate leaves with a distinctive violet-blue flower that blooms in early spring. Its flowers have five petals, with the lower petal often bearing a yellow or white mark.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
