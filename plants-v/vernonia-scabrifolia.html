Vernonia scabrifolia

Vernonia scabrifolia, commonly known as roughleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and narrow, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces conical flower heads that range in color from yellow to brown, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. Vernonia scabrifolia is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodland edges and wetlands.

Its ecological value includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
