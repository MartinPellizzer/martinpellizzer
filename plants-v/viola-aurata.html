Viola aurata

Viola aurata, commonly known as the aurate violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple and white. Viola aurata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and blooming habit.

It is also valued for its ability to naturalize in suitable conditions, making it a popular choice for gardeners.
