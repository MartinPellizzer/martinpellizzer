Vidoricum motleyanum

Vidoricum motleyanum, commonly known as the shimmerleaf vine, is a climbing plant native to the humid rainforests of the Equatorial region.

It is characterized by its waxy, iridescent leaves that display a range of colors depending on light exposure. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are pollinated by both insects and birds. Its unique ability to absorb moisture from the air makes it highly adaptable to shaded environments.

The fruit, a small, fleshy capsule, contains seeds that are dispersed by animals attracted to its mild scent.
