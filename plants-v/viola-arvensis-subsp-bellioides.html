Viola arvensis subsp. bellioides

Viola arvensis subsp.

bellioides, commonly known as the bell-shaped violet, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces showy violet flowers with a distinctive bell-like shape. Its flowers are usually purple or blue, with a yellow throat, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
