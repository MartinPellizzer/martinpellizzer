Viola sheltonii var. biternata

Viola sheltonii var. biternata, commonly known as the Shelton's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive violet hue and produces clusters of delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple and blue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
