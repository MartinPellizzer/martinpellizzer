Viola dissecta var. incisa

Viola dissecta var. incisa, commonly known as the dissected violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and toothed, giving it a delicate, fern-like appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of purple, violet, and white, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported calming and healing properties.
