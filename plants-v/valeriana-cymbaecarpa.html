Valeriana cymbaecarpa

Valeriana cymbaecarpa, commonly known as the mountain valerian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Valerianaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, preferring well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a strong, spicy scent.

Valeriana cymbaecarpa is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its calming effects.
