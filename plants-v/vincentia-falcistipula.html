Vincentia falcistipula

Vincentia falcistipula, commonly known as the narrow-tipped vining plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with narrow, pointed leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which are typical of the legume family. It is native to tropical regions of South America and is often found in humid, shaded environments.

The seeds are enclosed in elongated pods, which split open to release the seeds upon maturity.
