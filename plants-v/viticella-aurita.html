Viticella aurita

Viticella aurita, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, round, green fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Viticella aurita is a climbing vine that can grow up to 15 meters in length.
