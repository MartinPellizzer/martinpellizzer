Verbesina agricolarum

Verbesina agricolarum, commonly known as the agricola false sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while the stem is hairy and upright.
