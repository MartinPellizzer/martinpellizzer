Veronica latifolia var. urticifolia

Veronica latifolia var. urticifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and slightly serrated. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers with five petals, typical of the Plantaginaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and naturalized areas.
