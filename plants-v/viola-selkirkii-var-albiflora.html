Viola selkirkii var. albiflora

Viola selkirkii var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of small, white or pale lavender flowers. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
