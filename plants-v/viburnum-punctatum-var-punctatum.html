Viburnum punctatum var. punctatum

Viburnum punctatum var. punctatum, commonly known as the spotted viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue, edible berries. Its foliage turns a striking red or orange in the fall, adding seasonal interest to the landscape.

Viburnum punctatum var. punctatum is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
