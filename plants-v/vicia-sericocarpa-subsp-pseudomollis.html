Vicia sericocarpa subsp. pseudomollis

Vicia sericocarpa subsp.

pseudomollis, commonly known as the silky vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae. It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant produces pods that contain several small, rounded seeds, and its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets. It is typically found in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
