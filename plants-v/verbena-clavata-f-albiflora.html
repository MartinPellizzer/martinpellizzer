Verbena clavata f. albiflora

Verbena clavata f. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a compact shrub with slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
