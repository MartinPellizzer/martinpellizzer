Vernonia remotiflora var. tricholepis

Vernonia remotiflora var. tricholepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy surface that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
