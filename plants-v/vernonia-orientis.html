Vernonia orientis

Vernonia orientis, commonly known as the oriental ironweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring upright stems covered in fine hairs. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

The flowers are small, yellowish-green, and arranged in dense, spherical clusters, which attract various pollinators.
