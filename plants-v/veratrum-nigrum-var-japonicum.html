Veratrum nigrum var. japonicum

Veratrum nigrum var. japonicum, commonly known as Japanese white hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, stout stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, often covered with a white, waxy coating. The plant produces large, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are large, broad, and typically have a wavy or notched margin.

This species contains toxic alkaloids and is part of the Melanthiaceae family, known for its medicinal and poisonous properties.
