Valeriana dacica

Valeriana dacica, commonly known as Romanian valerian, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Europe.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often reddish or purple flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are hairy and slightly serrated. Its rhizomatous root system is used in traditional medicine for its sedative and calming properties.

Valeriana dacica thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.
