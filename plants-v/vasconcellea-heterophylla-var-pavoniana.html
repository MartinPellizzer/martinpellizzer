Vasconcellea heterophylla var. pavoniana

Vasconcellea heterophylla var. pavoniana, commonly known as the Pavoniana waxplant, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, featuring a woody stem and dense foliage. The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a waxy coating that gives the plant its common name. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually yellow or white, which bloom in clusters.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
