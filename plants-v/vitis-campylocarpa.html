Vitis campylocarpa

Vitis campylocarpa, commonly known as the creeping grape or swamp grape, is a species of vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into fleshy, red or purple berries. It thrives in moist, swampy areas and is often found along riverbanks and in wet woodlands.

Vitis campylocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing food for wildlife.
