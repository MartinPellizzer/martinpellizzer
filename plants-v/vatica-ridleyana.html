Vatica ridleyana

Vatica ridleyana, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color.

The tree is also notable for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
