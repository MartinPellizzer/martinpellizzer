Viola hillii

Viola hillii, commonly known as the Hill's violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central America, particularly in countries like Guatemala and Honduras. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, with five petals arranged symmetrically. Viola hillii thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of soft, fuzzy hairs.
