Vernonia stoechadifolia

Vernonia stoechadifolia, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and swampy areas. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
