Vernonia bipontini

Vernonia bipontini, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its conical flower heads. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from yellow to orange.

Vernonia bipontini is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
