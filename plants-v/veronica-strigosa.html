Veronica strigosa

Veronica strigosa, commonly known as slender speedwell, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along roadsides. Veronica strigosa is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

It is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
