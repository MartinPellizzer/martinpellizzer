Viola × salvatoriana

Viola × salvatoriana, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is a cross between Viola tricolor and Viola verecunda, resulting in a plant with distinctive, often bicolor flowers. The foliage is typically heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and the flowers can range in color from purple to white and yellow. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

This plant is also valued for its ability to naturalize and spread in suitable conditions.
