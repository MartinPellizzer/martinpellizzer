Viola maculata f. typica

Viola maculata f. typica, commonly known as the common violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and violet or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to Europe, it thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and gardens. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Its ornamental value and ability to naturalize make it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
