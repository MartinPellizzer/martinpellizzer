Veronica diffusa

Veronica diffusa, commonly known as common speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, blue or violet flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Veronica diffusa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
