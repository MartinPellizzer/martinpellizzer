Viola provostii

Viola provostii, commonly known as the Provost's violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves and produces showy, violet-blue flowers with yellow markings. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
