Vaccinium molle

Vaccinium molle, commonly known as the sweet blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It produces small, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet, tart flavor. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and prefers acidic, well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as for its fruit.

Vaccinium molle thrives in partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
