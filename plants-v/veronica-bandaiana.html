Veronica bandaiana

Veronica bandaiana, commonly known as the Banda vervain, is a flowering plant native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Veronica bandaiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
