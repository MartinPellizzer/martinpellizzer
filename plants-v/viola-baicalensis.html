Viola baicalensis

Viola baicalensis, commonly known as the Fringed Gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas and thrives in cool, moist climates. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers have five petals with distinctive fringed edges.

Viola baicalensis is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its traditional medicinal uses.
