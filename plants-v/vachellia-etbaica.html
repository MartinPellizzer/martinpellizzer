Vachellia etbaica

Vachellia etbaica, commonly known as the Ethiopian acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, featuring deep root systems and a thick, waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for local crafts and construction.
