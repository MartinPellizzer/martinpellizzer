Verbena globulifera

Verbena globulifera, commonly known as the globular verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is typically found in rocky or sandy soils. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Verbena globulifera is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
