Vigna stenocarpa

Vigna stenocarpa, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, with small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein. Vigna stenocarpa has a fibrous root system that helps in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its foliage and flower structure.
