Venatris salicifolius

Venatris salicifolius, commonly known as the willow-leaf venation plant, is a species characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, resembling those of willow trees, with a prominent venation pattern that gives it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments, such as along stream banks or in woodland areas. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes, often blooming in early spring.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
