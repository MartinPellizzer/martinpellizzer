Veronica × czemalensis

Veronica × czemalensis, commonly known as the Czemalensky speedwell, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with dense clusters of blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated as a ornamental ground cover. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a range of morphological variations in its natural habitat.
