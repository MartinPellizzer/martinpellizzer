Vernonia huilensis

Vernonia huilensis, commonly known as Huilens Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or grassy areas, and is adapted to cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from yellow to orange in color.

Vernonia huilensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
