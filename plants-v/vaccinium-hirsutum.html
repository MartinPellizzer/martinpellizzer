Vaccinium hirsutum

Vaccinium hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy blueberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color. Its leaves are elliptical and covered with fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it its common name.

Vaccinium hirsutum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
