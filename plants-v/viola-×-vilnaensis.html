Viola × vilnaensis

Viola × vilnaensis, commonly known as the Vilna violet, is a hybrid species of the genus Viola.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to eastern Europe, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and gardens. The flowers typically have five petals, with the lower petal being prominently marked.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in horticulture for its beauty and adaptability.
