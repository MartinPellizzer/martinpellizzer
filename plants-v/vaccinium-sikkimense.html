Vaccinium sikkimense

Vaccinium sikkimense, commonly known as the Sikkim blueberry, is a species of shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces bell-shaped, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. Its fruits are edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, with a tart flavor.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments at higher elevations.
