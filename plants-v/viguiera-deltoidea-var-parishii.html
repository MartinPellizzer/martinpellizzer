Viguiera deltoidea var. parishii

Viguiera deltoidea var. parishii, commonly known as the Parish's golden aster, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herbaceous species belonging to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes, and it has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its inflorescence consists of clustered flowers with ray florets, and it is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
