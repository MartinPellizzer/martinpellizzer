Viola stagnina var. schultzii

Viola stagnina var. schultzii, commonly known as the marsh violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wet meadows, marshes, and stream banks. Its flowers have five petals and are often accompanied by yellowish-green sepals.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
