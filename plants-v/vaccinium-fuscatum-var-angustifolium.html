Vaccinium fuscatum var. angustifolium

Vaccinium fuscatum var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved huckleberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by dark blue berries that are edible. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, providing a dense canopy that offers habitat for various wildlife species.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
