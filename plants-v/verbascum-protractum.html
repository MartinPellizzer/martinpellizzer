Verbascum protractum

Verbascum protractum, commonly known as the protracted mullein, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, erect stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, covered in soft, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers in clusters at the top of the stems, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are large, lance-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine, woolly hairs.

Verbascum protractum is often found in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to thrive in full sun.
