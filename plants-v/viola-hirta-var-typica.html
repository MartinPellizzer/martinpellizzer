Viola hirta var. typica

Viola hirta var. typica, commonly known as the hairy violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and grassy slopes. The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces small, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.

Viola hirta var. typica is also known for its ability to thrive in cool climates and its role in supporting pollinators.
