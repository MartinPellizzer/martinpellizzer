Veronica nanella

Veronica nanella, commonly known as the slender speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Veronica nanella is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its compact growth habit.
