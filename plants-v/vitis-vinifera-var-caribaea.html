Vitis vinifera var. caribaea

Vitis vinifera var. caribaea, commonly known as the Caribbean grape, is a lesser-known cultivar of the common grapevine.

It is native to the Caribbean region and is characterized by its vigorous growth and adaptability to tropical climates. The plant produces small, sweet berries that are often used in local cuisines and traditional beverages. Botanically, it exhibits a complex root system and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

Its foliage is typically dark green and glossy, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
