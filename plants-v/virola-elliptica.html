Virola elliptica

Virola elliptica, commonly known as the Amazonian ironwood, is a large evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its thick, dark bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing numerous seeds. Virola elliptica is valued for its hard, durable wood and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of aromatic resins.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species in its native environment.
