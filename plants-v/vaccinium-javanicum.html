Vaccinium javanicum

Vaccinium javanicum, commonly known as the Java huckleberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acidic-loving plants. The plant is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and produces edible, dark blue to black berries. It typically grows in acidic soils and thrives in shaded, moist environments.

The berries are used in local cuisines and have traditional medicinal uses in some regions.
