Vernonia oligantha

Vernonia oligantha, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the United States, particularly found in the southeastern region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves, and it produces small, pale yellow to brownish flowers that bloom in late summer.

Vernonia oligantha is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
