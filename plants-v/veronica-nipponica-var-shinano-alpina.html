Veronica nipponica var. shinano-alpina

Veronica nipponica var. shinano-alpina, commonly known as the Shinano Alpine Speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it is known for its hardiness in cold climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
