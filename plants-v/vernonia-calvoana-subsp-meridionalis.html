Vernonia calvoana subsp. meridionalis

Vernonia calvoana subsp.

meridionalis, commonly known as the southern ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southern United States. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodlands.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. It is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
