Veronica × erecta

Veronica × erecta, commonly known as the upright speedwell, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is typically found in moist, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.

Veronica × erecta is a perennial that thrives in full sun to partial shade.
