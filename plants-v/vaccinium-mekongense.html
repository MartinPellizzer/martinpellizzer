Vaccinium mekongense

Vaccinium mekongense, commonly known as the Mekong blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, similar to other Vaccinium species. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas with high humidity.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and less-studied plant in botanical research.
