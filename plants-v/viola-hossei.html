Viola hossei

Viola hossei, commonly known as the Hossi's violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are usually violet or purple in color, with five petals and a distinctive hooded structure.

Viola hossei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
