Vitex havilandii

Vitex havilandii, commonly known as the Haviland's vitex, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant produces showy, fragrant purple or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture with a serrated margin.

Vitex havilandii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
