Vaccinium pterocarpum

Vaccinium pterocarpum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved huckleberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and leathery in texture. Its flowers are bell-shaped and pink to white, and it produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible.

This plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streamside areas.
