Valeriana vaginata

Valeriana vaginata, commonly known as the velvetleaf valerian, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Valeriana vaginata is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported calming properties.
