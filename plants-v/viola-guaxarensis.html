Viola guaxarensis

Viola guaxarensis, commonly known as the Guaxarensis Violet, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant purple petals. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing clusters of small, showy flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to habitat loss, Viola guaxarensis is considered endangered and is of particular interest to botanists and conservationists.
