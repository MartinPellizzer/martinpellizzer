Veronica donetzica

Veronica donetzica, commonly known as the Donetz speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Veronica donetzica is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
