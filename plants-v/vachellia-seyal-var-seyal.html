Vachellia seyal var. seyal

Vachellia seyal var. seyal, commonly known as the Sickle Tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and yellow or orange flowers that appear in summer. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species for dry climates. Its bark and wood have been traditionally used for various purposes, including construction and medicinal applications.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for wildlife.
