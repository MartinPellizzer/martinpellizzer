Vesicaria mendocina

Vesicaria mendocina, commonly known as the Mendo bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to California.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers and its inflated, bladder-like seed pods. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Vesicaria mendocina is an annual herb that plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
