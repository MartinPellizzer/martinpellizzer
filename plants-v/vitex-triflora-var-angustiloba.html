Vitex triflora var. angustiloba

Vitex triflora var. angustiloba, commonly known as narrowleaf chaste tree, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically purple or blue in color, blooming in the summer months. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that split open to release seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
