Viola grayi

Viola grayi, commonly known as Gray's violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple and white. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola grayi is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in native plant gardens.
