Viola tricolor var. bicolor

Viola tricolor var. bicolor, commonly known as the common heartsease, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three heart-shaped leaflets, and its showy, five-petaled flowers that typically display a combination of purple, yellow, and white. The flowers bloom in early spring and are often found in meadows, woodland edges, and gardens. This species is part of the Violaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners and naturalists alike.
