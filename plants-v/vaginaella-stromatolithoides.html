Vaginaella stromatolithoides

Vaginaella stromatolithoides, commonly known as the stromatolite-forming algae, is a type of cyanobacterium that plays a significant role in the formation of stromatolites.

It thrives in shallow, saline environments such as coastal lagoons and tidal flats. This organism is characterized by its ability to produce calcium carbonate deposits, which contribute to the structural development of stromatolites. Its growth patterns are influenced by factors such as light intensity, water salinity, and temperature.

Vaginaella stromatolithoides is an important indicator species for ancient and modern microbial communities in marine ecosystems.
