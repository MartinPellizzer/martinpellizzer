Viola trichosepala

Viola trichosepala, commonly known as the hairy-sepaled violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a distinctive hairy texture on the underside. The plant produces delicate, violet-colored flowers with five petals, often with a white or pale violet throat. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories or rocky habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique and ornamental addition to botanical gardens and native plant collections.
