Vitis balansana

Vitis balansana, commonly known as Balansan grape, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by small, round berries that are typically edible.

Vitis balansana is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential in horticulture and traditional medicine.
