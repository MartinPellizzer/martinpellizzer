Valeriana laciniosa

Valeriana laciniosa, commonly known as the mountain valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It features a creeping rhizome and produces clusters of small, fragrant, pink to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Valeriana laciniosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported sedative properties.
