Vicia sativa var. minor

Vicia sativa var. minor, commonly known as lesser vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial legume that produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces slender, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

This plant is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
