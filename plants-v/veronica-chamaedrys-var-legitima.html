Veronica chamaedrys var. legitima

Veronica chamaedrys var. legitima, commonly known as the rock speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This hardy species is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its compact growth habit and ornamental value.
