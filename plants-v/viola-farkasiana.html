Viola farkasiana

Viola farkasiana, commonly known as the Hungarian violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It produces delicate, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive dark purple throat and yellow markings. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-15 centimeters, with heart-shaped, dark green leaves. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial shade.

Viola farkasiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
