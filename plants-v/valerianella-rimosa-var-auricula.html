Valerianella rimosa var. auricula

Valerianella rimosa var. auricula, commonly known as the golden toothed salad or auricula valerianella, is a low-growing annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces tiny, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, tooth-like leaf margins, which give it its common name. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in meadows and woodland areas.

Valerianella rimosa var. auricula is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in culinary and medicinal applications.
