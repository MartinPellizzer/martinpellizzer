Verbascum numidicum

Verbascum numidicum, commonly known as the Algerian mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Verbascum numidicum is also valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its soothing effects.
