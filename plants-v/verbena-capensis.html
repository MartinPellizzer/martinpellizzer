Verbena capensis

Verbena capensis, commonly known as the common verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces vibrant clusters of blue, purple, or pink flowers that bloom throughout the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Verbena capensis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
