Viola beckwithii var. trinervata

Viola beckwithii var. trinervata, commonly known as the Sierra violet, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of violet-blue flowers in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which give it its scientific name.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
