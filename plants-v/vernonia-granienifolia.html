Vernonia granienifolia

Vernonia granienifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fluffy clusters of yellow or brown flowers, which are typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in warm climates.

Vernonia granienifolia is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
