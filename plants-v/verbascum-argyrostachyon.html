Verbascum argyrostachyon

Verbascum argyrostachyon, commonly known as silverleaf mullein, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in dense white or silver hairs. The plant produces showy clusters of yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
