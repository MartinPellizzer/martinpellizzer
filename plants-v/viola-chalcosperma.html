Viola chalcosperma

Viola chalcosperma, commonly known as the copperleaf violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky and sandy soils. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from violet to white, often with a distinctive yellow or greenish hue on the lower petals. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Viola chalcosperma is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
