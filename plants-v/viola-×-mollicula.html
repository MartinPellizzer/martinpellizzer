Viola × mollicula

Viola × mollicula, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. It is native to Europe and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

Viola × mollicula is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for centuries in gardens.
