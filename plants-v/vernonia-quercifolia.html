Vernonia quercifolia

Vernonia quercifolia, commonly known as the oak-leaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its palmate, oak-like leaves and its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of small, blue-violet flowers. Vernonia quercifolia typically grows in moist, woodland areas and is often found in partial shade.
