Verbena lasiostachys f. albiflora

Verbena lasiostachys f. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower verbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
