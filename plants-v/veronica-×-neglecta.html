Veronica × neglecta

Veronica × neglecta, commonly known as the common speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It features small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its hardy nature and ability to spread via runners make it a resilient and easy-to-maintain ground cover.
