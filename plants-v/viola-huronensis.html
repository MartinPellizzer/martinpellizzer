Viola huronensis

Viola huronensis, commonly known as Huron violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Great Lakes region of North America.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of violet-blue flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soil.

Viola huronensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
