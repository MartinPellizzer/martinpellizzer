Veronica decumbens

Veronica decumbens, commonly known as the prostrate speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has prostrate stems that spread along the ground, often forming dense mats. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
