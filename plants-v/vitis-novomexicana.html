Vitis novomexicana

Vitis novomexicana, commonly known as New Mexico grape, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to the southwestern United States, particularly New Mexico, and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

The fruit is a small, sweet, and edible berry, typically harvested in the fall.
