Velaea vestita

Velaea vestita, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the variety. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Velaea vestita is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
