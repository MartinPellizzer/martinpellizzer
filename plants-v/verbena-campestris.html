Verbena campestris

Verbena campestris, commonly known as common verbena, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It produces spikes of vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom throughout the summer months. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower meadows.

Verbena campestris is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
