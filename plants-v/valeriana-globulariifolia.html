Valeriana globulariifolia

Valeriana globulariifolia, commonly known as the globular leaf valerian, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rounded leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in dense clusters. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or stream banks.

Valeriana globulariifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
