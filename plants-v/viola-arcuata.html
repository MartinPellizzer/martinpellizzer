Viola arcuata

Viola arcuata, commonly known as the meadow violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple underside and produces delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple and white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers bloom from spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola arcuata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
