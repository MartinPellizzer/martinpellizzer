Viola × teresiana

Viola × teresiana, commonly known as the heartleaf viola, is a hybrid species belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including purple, violet, and white. Native to Europe, this plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a bedding plant. It prefers cool, moist conditions and thrives in partial shade, making it suitable for temperate climates.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, facilitating its propagation.
