Vincetoxicum sublanceolatum var. dickinsii

Vincetoxicum sublanceolatum var. dickinsii, commonly known as the Dickinsii Milkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
