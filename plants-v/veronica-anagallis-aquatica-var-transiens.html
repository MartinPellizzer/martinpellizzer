Veronica anagallis-aquatica var. transiens

Veronica anagallis-aquatica var. transiens, commonly known as the water speedwell, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in ponds, ditches, and stream edges. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to aquatic environments and its role in supporting local insect populations.
