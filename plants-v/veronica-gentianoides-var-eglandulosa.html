Veronica gentianoides var. eglandulosa

Veronica gentianoides var. eglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers with five petals, typical of the Veronica genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
