Vicia bifolia

Vicia bifolia, commonly known as the twinleaf vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf structure, featuring two pairs of leaflets that give it its common name. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Vicia bifolia is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and woodland edges.

It has a fibrous root system and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
