Vallisneria spiralis

Vallisneria spiralis, commonly known as waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Asia.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length, which help it thrive in slow-moving or still waters. The plant reproduces through stolons, which are horizontal stems that produce new shoots at their nodes. Vallisneria spiralis plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen for aquatic life.

It is also widely used in aquascaping due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
