Verbascum majale var. bicolor

Verbascum majale var. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for gardens and natural habitats.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and easily identifiable species within the Scrophulariaceae family.
