Verbena pulchella f. latilobata

Verbena pulchella f. latilobata, commonly known as the broad-lobed verbena, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves with distinctive broad lobes, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces spikes of vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
