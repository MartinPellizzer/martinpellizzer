Vicia dentata

Vicia dentata, commonly known as the toothed vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in dry, rocky soils and open habitats. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are compound, with a serrated or toothed edge, giving it its common name.

Vicia dentata is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
