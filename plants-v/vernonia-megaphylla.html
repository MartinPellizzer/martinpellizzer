Vernonia megaphylla

Vernonia megaphylla, commonly known as the giant ironweed, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.

Its botanical features make it an important species for pollinators and ecological restoration projects.
