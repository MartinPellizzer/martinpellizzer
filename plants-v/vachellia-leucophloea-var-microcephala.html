Vachellia leucophloea var. microcephala

Vachellia leucophloea var. microcephala, commonly known as the small-headed silver thorn, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, thorned stems and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces yellow flowers in terminal clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to semi-arid and arid environments.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fodder for livestock.
