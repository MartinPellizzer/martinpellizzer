Valerianella radiata f. demareei

Valerianella radiata f. demareei, commonly known as the "Italian parsley" or "scurvy grass," is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, bright green, feather-like leaves that are often used as a culinary herb. The plant produces tiny white or pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Valerianella radiata f. demareei is valued for its aromatic leaves and historical use in traditional medicine.
