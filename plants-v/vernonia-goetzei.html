Vernonia goetzei

Vernonia goetzei, commonly known as the Goetze's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple composite flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with rough textures and is often found in moist, disturbed soils. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Vernonia goetzei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
