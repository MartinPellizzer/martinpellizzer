Valeriana scandens var. candolleana

Valeriana scandens var. candolleana, commonly known as the climbing valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, climbing stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, often twining around supports. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and covered with fine hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its calming properties.
