Veronica strictissima

Veronica strictissima, commonly known as the upright speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in dense spikes.

Veronica strictissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
