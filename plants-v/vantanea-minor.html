Vantanea minor

Vantanea minor, commonly known as the lesser vantanea, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense foliage of dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Vantanea minor thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
