Viburnum luzonicum var. luzonicum

Viburnum luzonicum var. luzonicum, commonly known as the Luzon viburnum, is a species native to the Philippines.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces dark blue, waxy berries that are edible and often used in local cuisine. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn vibrant shades of red in the fall.

Viburnum luzonicum var. luzonicum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
