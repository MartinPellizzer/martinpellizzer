Vitex zeyheri var. brevipes

Vitex zeyheri var. brevipes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved chaste tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It features opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, pale blue to purple flowers in the summer months. These flowers are followed by small, woody, egg-shaped fruits that turn from green to brown as they mature.

It is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
