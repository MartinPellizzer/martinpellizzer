Vitex iraquensis

Vitex iraquensis, commonly known as the Persian lilac, is a shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its fragrant foliage and attractive blooms.

Vitex iraquensis is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
