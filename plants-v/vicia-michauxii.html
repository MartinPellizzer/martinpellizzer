Vicia michauxii

Vicia michauxii, commonly known as Michaux's vetch, is a perennial legume native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has a fibrous root system. Its seed pods are slender and contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Vicia michauxii plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it ecologically significant in forest ecosystems.
