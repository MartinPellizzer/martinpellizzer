Viola grisebachiana

Viola grisebachiana, commonly known as the gray viol or Grisebach's violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is native to regions of southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in grassland and rocky habitats. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with five petals, typically in shades of purple or blue. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Viola grisebachiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
