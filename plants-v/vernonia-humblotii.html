Vernonia humblotii

Vernonia humblotii, commonly known as the Humblot's ironweed, is a perennial shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to the island's unique ecological conditions.

Due to its restricted range and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
