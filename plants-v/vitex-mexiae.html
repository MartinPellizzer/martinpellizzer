Vitex mexiae

Vitex mexiae, commonly known as the Mexican chaste tree, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to violet. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Vitex mexiae is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
