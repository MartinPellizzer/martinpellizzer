Viola × awagatakensis

Viola × awagatakensis, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Viola walteri and Viola praetorii.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. Native to Japan, it thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded woodland areas. The plant produces showy flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in temperate climates.
