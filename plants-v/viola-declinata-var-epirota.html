Viola declinata var. epirota

Viola declinata var. epirota, commonly known as the Epirus violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the undergrowth.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
