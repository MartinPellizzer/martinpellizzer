Vochysia elliptica var. firma

Vochysia elliptica var. firma, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree is valued for its hard, dense wood, which is highly sought after in musical instrument manufacturing. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and wood quality, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes in tropical regions.
