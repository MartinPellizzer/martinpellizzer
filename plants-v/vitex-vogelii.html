Vitex vogelii

Vitex vogelii, commonly known as the African blueberry or velvetleaf vitex, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in summer. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule containing several seeds.

Vitex vogelii is often found in disturbed habitats and can be invasive in some regions.
