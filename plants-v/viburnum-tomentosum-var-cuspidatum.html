Viburnum tomentosum var. cuspidatum

Viburnum tomentosum var. cuspidatum, commonly known as the arrowleaf viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins, often tinged with red or purple in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue, edible berries. It thrives in partial to full shade and is valued for its ornamental appeal and wildlife value.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for small gardens and woodland settings.
