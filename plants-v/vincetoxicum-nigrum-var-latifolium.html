Vincetoxicum nigrum var. latifolium

Vincetoxicum nigrum var. latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf black nightshade, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, dark purple to black flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically dark blue to black in color, which are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its toxicity, it is considered a weed in some regions and requires caution when handling.
