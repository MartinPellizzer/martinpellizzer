Viola skanderbegii

Viola skanderbegii, commonly known as the Skanderbeg violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas and thrives in well-drained soil. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

Due to habitat loss, it is also considered a species of conservation concern in some regions.
