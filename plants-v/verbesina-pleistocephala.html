Verbesina pleistocephala

Verbesina pleistocephala, commonly known as the giant beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, spiny fruits that contain seeds dispersed by animals. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticulture.
