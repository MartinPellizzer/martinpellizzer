Valeriana parviflora

Valeriana parviflora, commonly known as sweet valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, small white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and have a distinctive, somewhat hairy texture.
