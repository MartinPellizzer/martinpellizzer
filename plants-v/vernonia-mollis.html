Vernonia mollis

Vernonia mollis, commonly known as silky Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow to orange tubular flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Vernonia mollis thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
