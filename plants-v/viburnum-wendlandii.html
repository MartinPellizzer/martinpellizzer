Viburnum wendlandii

Viburnum wendlandii, commonly known as the Wendland viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant white flowers that appear in late spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its seasonal color changes and attractive form.
