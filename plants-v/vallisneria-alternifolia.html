Vallisneria alternifolia

Vallisneria alternifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Asia.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that alternate along its stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically borne in clusters near the water surface. Vallisneria alternifolia plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen for various aquatic organisms.

It is often used in aquariums due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its aesthetic appeal.
