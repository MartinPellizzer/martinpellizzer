Veronica monticola

Veronica monticola, commonly known as the mountain veronica, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Veronica monticola is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
