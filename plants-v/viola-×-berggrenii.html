Viola × berggrenii

Viola × berggrenii, commonly known as the Berggren's violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. This plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are usually violet or purple, with five petals and a distinctive dark purple or black spot at the base of the lower petal.

Viola × berggrenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
