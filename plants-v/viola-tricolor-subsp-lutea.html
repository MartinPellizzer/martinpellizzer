Viola tricolor subsp. lutea

Viola tricolor subsp.

lutea, commonly known as the yellow violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The flowers are typically violet or purple with yellow markings, though the subspecies lutea has more prominent yellow hues. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens.
