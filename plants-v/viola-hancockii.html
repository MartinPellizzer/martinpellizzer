Viola hancockii

Viola hancockii, commonly known as the Hancock's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola hancockii is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
