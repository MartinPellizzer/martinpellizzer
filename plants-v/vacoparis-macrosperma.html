Vacoparis macrosperma

Vacoparis macrosperma, commonly known as the Chilean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of Chile.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and grows as a herbaceous perennial. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Solanaceae family.
