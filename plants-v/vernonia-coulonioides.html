Vernonia coulonioides

Vernonia coulonioides, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows in dry, rocky soils. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are often visited by bees and other pollinators. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay, making it useful in construction and furniture-making.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, contributing to its ecological importance in its native habitat.
