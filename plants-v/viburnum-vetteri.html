Viburnum vetteri

Viburnum vetteri, commonly known as the Vetter's viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and features opposite, ovate leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the autumn. Viburnum vetteri thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract wildlife.
