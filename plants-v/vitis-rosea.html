Vitis rosea

Vitis rosea, commonly known as the pink grapevine, is a climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish tinge. The plant produces small, pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round berries. Vitis rosea is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and flowers.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for gardens and woodland settings.
