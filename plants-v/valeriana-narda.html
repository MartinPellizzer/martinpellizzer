Valeriana narda

Valeriana narda, commonly known as the narrowleaf valerian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Valerianaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grassy areas and woodland edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in spikes. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly rough texture.

Valeriana narda is known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used for its calming effects.
