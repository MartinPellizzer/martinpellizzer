Veronica schmidtiana f. candida

Veronica schmidtiana f. candida, commonly known as the white-flowered speedwell, is a compact, low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, narrow leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
