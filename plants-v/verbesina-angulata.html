Verbesina angulata

Verbesina angulata, commonly known as angular beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that grows up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring hairy stems and alternate leaves with a distinctive angular shape. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from summer to fall. Its seeds are small, wind-dispersed, and have a tuft of hairs at the end, aiding in their spread.

Verbesina angulata is often found in disturbed soils and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal use.
