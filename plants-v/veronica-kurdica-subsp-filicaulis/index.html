Veronica Kurdica Subsp. Filicaulis

Veronica kurdica subsp. filicaulis is a rare and delicate plant native to the mountainous regions of the Eastern Mediterranean. It is characterized by its slender stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that give it a unique appearance. This subspecies thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, blue-violet flowers bloom in early spring, adding a touch of color to the landscape. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
