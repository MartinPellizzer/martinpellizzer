Vernonia sublanata

Vernonia sublanata, commonly known as the silky vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft appearance. Its yellow flower heads consist of many small florets arranged in a disc shape, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Vernonia sublanata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woods and along stream banks.
