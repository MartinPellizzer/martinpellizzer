Verbena bipinnatifida var. gooddingii

Verbena bipinnatifida var. gooddingii, commonly known as Goodding's verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The leaves are finely divided, giving the plant a delicate, lace-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
