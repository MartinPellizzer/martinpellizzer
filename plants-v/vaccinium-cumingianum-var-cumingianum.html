Vaccinium cumingianum var. cumingianum

Vaccinium cumingianum var. cumingianum, commonly known as the Cuming's blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark blue or black when mature, similar to other blueberry species. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for cultivation, it is of interest to both horticulturists and botanists.
