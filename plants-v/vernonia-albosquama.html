Vernonia albosquama

Vernonia albosquama, commonly known as white-haired ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The showy yellow flowers appear in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
