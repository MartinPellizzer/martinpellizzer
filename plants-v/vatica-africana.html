Vatica africana

Vatica africana, commonly known as African ash, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Vatica africana is valued for its hard, durable wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
