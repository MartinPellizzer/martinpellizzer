Vernonia albifolia

Vernonia albifolia, commonly known as the white ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy plant suitable for gardens and natural habitats.

Vernonia albifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
