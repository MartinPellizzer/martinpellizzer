Viburnum roraimense

Viburnum roraimense, commonly known as the Roraima viburnum, is a species native to the northern regions of South America, particularly Venezuela and Brazil.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical forests. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and are typically white or pale pink, blooming in the spring.

The fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that attracts various birds and small mammals, contributing to its ecological significance.
