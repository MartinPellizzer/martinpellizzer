Valerianoides integrifolia

Valerianoides integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf valerian, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Valerianaceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its medicinal properties. Its roots and leaves contain compounds that have been traditionally used for their calming effects.

Valerianoides integrifolia is often found in chaparral and desert scrub environments.
