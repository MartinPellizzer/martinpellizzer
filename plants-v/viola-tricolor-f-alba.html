Viola tricolor f. alba

Viola tricolor f. alba, commonly known as the white pansy, is a cultivar of the common violet, characterized by its predominantly white petals with hints of pale yellow and lavender.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces clusters of small, delicate flowers. It thrives in cool climates and prefers well-drained soil, making it suitable for shady garden areas.

The white pansy is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
