Veronica serpyllifolia var. neomexicana

Veronica serpyllifolia var. neomexicana, commonly known as New Mexico speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is typically found in rocky or sandy soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a desirable addition to native plant gardens.
