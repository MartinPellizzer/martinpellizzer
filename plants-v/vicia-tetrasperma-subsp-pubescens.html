Vicia tetrasperma subsp. pubescens

Vicia tetrasperma subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the hairy vetch, is a herbaceous perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, hairy stipules and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet.

The plant produces small, hairy pods containing several elliptical seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

As a legume, it has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
