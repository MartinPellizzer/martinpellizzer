Vernonia sericea var. purpurascens

Vernonia sericea var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright stems covered in soft, silver-gray hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces showy, cone-shaped flower heads that range in color from purple to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
