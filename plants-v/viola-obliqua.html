Viola obliqua

Viola obliqua, commonly known as the zigzag violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive zigzag pattern along the petioles. The plant produces clusters of violet-blue flowers with yellow centers, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Viola obliqua is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
