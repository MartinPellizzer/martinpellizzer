Viola ligustina

Viola ligustina, commonly known as the Italian violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of western Asia. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and produces delicate purple or violet flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Viola ligustina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
