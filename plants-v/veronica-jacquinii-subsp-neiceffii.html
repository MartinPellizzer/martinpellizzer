Veronica jacquinii subsp. neiceffii

Veronica jacquinii subsp.

neiceffii, commonly known as the Neiceff's speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
