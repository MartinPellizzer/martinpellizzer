Vitex hirsutissima

Vitex hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy chaste tree, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, hairy leaves and clusters of fragrant, blue-violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Vitex hirsutissima is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
