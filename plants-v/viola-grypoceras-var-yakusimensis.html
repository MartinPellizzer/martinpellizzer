Viola grypoceras var. yakusimensis

Viola grypoceras var. yakusimensis, commonly known as the Yakutsk violet, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the Russian Far East.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and pale purple flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with rich, well-drained soil. Its botanical features include a hairy stem and showy, five-petaled blooms that bloom in early spring.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
