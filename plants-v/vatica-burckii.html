Vatica burckii

Vatica burckii, commonly known as the black ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a waxy texture. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody capsules containing its seeds.

Vatica burckii is typically found in lowland and hill forest areas, where it plays an important ecological role.
