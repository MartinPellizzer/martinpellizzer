Viola × insolita

Viola × insolita, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. It is native to Europe and has been cultivated for its ornamental value.

Viola × insolita is often found in gardens and woodland areas where it spreads through rhizomes and self-seeding.
