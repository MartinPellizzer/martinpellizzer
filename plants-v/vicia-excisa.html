Vicia excisa

Vicia excisa, commonly known as the cutleaf vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early summer. Vicia excisa is often found in grassy areas, disturbed soils, and along roadsides.

It has a fibrous root system and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
