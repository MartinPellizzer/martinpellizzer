Verbena mahonetii

Verbena mahonetii, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, brightly colored flowers, usually in shades of blue, purple, or white, which bloom throughout the summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils.

Verbena mahonetii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
