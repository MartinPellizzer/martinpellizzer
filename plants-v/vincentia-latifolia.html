Vincentia latifolia

Vincentia latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf vining plant, is a tropical vine native to regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped, broad leaves that are often prominently veined and have a glossy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that are typically followed by elongated, bean-like pods. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in shaded areas.
