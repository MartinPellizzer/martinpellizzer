Virgulus ericoides subsp. pansus

Virgulus ericoides subsp.

pansus, commonly known as the broad-leaved heath, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, often with a slight waxy coating.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
