Vriesea aurea

Vriesea aurea, commonly known as the golden vriesea, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to the tropical regions of South America.

This plant is characterized by its vibrant, sword-shaped leaves that form a rosette, creating a lush, green backdrop for its showy inflorescence. The flowers, which typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange, are clustered in a spadix surrounded by colorful bracts, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant. Vriesea aurea thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

It is popular in indoor gardening due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in humid environments.
