Veronica gibbsii

Veronica gibbsii, commonly known as Gibbs' speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.

It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
