Verbesina cabrerae

Verbesina cabrerae, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in dry habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The flowers are arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters and attract various pollinators including bees and butterflies.
