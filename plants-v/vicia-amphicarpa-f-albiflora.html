Vicia amphicarpa f. albiflora

Vicia amphicarpa f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow pea-like flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a narrow, two-valved pod that contains several elliptical seeds.

This variety is often found in disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing ability.
