Vatica granulata subsp. granulata

Vatica granulata subsp.

granulata, commonly known as the granulated ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength.

The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and has compound leaves with opposite arrangement. Its flowers are small and yellowish-green, often clustered in inflorescences, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
