Valeriana ajanensis subsp. ajanensis

Valeriana ajanensis subsp.

ajanensis, commonly known as the Ajan Valley valerian, is a perennial herb native to the highlands of Ethiopia. It is characterized by its aromatic, often hairy leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or rocky slopes. Its roots are valued in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs.
