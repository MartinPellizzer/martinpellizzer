Veronica topiaria

Veronica topiaria, commonly known as the stonecrop veronica, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mound-like growth habit. The plant features small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically fleshy and succulent, adapted to arid environments.

Veronica topiaria is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
