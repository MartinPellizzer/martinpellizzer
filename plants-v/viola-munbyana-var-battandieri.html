Viola munbyana var. battandieri

Viola munbyana var. battandieri, commonly known as the Munby's violet, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a deep green color and is covered in dense, woolly hairs. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow throat, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
