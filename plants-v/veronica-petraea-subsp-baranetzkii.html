Veronica petraea subsp. baranetzkii

Veronica petraea subsp.

baranetzkii, commonly known as the Baranetski's speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, dark green leaves and clusters of blue or violet flowers.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
