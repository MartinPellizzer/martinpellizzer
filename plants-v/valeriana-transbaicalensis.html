Valeriana transbaicalensis

Valeriana transbaicalensis, commonly known as the Transbaikal valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems, dark green leaves, and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces oval, dark blue to purple berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Valeriana transbaicalensis is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its calming effects.
