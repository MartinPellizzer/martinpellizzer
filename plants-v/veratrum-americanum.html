Veratrum americanum

Veratrum americanum, commonly known as American aconite, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, covered with white to greenish-yellow, waxy flowers. The plant has large, broad leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth texture. Its roots are thick and fleshy, containing toxic alkaloids such as veratridine and cevadine.

Due to its toxicity, it is not suitable for cultivation in gardens and requires caution during handling.
