Viola odorata var. jucunda

Viola odorata var. jucunda, commonly known as the sweet violet, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant is known for its strong, sweet scent.

Viola odorata var. jucunda is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has a long history of use in herbal medicine.
