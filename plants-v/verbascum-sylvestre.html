Verbascum sylvestre

Verbascum sylvestre, commonly known as wild mullein, is a biennial plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a spike-like inflorescence. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, fields, and woodland edges.

Verbascum sylvestre has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties, particularly for respiratory ailments.
