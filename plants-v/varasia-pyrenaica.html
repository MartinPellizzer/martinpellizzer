Varasia pyrenaica

Varasia pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees mountain range.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are blue to violet in color, arranged in terminal racemes, and bloom in late summer.

Varasia pyrenaica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens and wildflower meadows.
