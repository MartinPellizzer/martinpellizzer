Vaccinium damingshanense

Vaccinium damingshanense, commonly known as the Daming Mountain blueberry, is a species native to China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, similar to other vaccinium species. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at higher elevations.

This plant is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique ecological niche and potential for cultivation.
