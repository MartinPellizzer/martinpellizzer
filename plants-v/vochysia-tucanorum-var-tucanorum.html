Vochysia tucanorum var. tucanorum

Vochysia tucanorum var. tucanorum, commonly known as the tucan tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and dense, rounded crown. The leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptic in shape, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters, and is followed by woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often found in humid, lowland tropical forests.
