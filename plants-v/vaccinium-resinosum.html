Vaccinium resinosum

Vaccinium resinosum, commonly known as the resinous blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of the Pacific Northwest.

It produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a glossy surface and a reddish tint during the growing season.

Vaccinium resinosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing food for wildlife and supporting local biodiversity.
