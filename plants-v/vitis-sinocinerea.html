Vitis sinocinerea

Vitis sinocinerea, commonly known as the Chinese silver vine, is a climbing vine native to China.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that give way to dark purple berries. It is adapted to temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
