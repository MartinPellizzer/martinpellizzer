Viola tricolor var. nemausensis

Viola tricolor var. nemausensis, commonly known as the French violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of purple, white, and yellow flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and meadows. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
