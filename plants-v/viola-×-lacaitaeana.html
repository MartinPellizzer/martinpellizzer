Viola × lacaitaeana

Viola × lacaitaeana, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a species resulting from the cross between Viola tricolor and Viola verecunda.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant with heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that typically come in shades of purple, blue, and white. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or gardens. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in horticulture.
