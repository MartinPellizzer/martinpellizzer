Verbascum × ambracicum

Verbascum × ambracicum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes several species of flowering plants known for their large, showy flowers. The hybrid is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
