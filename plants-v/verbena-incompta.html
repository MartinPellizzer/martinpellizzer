Verbena incompta

Verbena incompta, commonly known as the Mexican verbena, is a perennial flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in shades of blue, purple, and white, and its foliage is often covered in fine, hairy hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Verbena incompta is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
