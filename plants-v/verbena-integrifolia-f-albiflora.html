Verbena integrifolia f. albiflora

Verbena integrifolia f. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower verbena, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern California.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse display of white or pale pink flowers. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with entire margins, and the plant typically thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
