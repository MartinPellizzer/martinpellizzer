Vachellia caurina

Vachellia caurina, commonly known as the silver acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny branches and small, yellow flowers that appear in dense, cylindrical clusters. The leaves are typically bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of tiny, feather-like leaflets. The plant produces long, flat pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of savanna ecosystems.
