Vigna appendiculata

Vigna appendiculata, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a climbing leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves with small, hairy stipules and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical pods. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species. Its seeds are edible and are often used in various cuisines, while the plant also serves as a forage crop for livestock.

Vigna appendiculata plays an important role in agricultural systems due to its nitrogen-fixing capabilities and adaptability.
