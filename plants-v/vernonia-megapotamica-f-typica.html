Vernonia megapotamica f. typica

Vernonia megapotamica f. typica, commonly known as the Brazilian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, yellowish-green flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
