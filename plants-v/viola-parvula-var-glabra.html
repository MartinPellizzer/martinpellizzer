Viola parvula var. glabra

Viola parvula var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth violet, is a small herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are smooth and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces delicate violet flowers with five petals, typically in shades of purple or blue. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
