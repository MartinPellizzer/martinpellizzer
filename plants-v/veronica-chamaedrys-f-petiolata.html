Veronica chamaedrys f. petiolata

Veronica chamaedrys f. petiolata, commonly known as the creeping speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has rounded, evergreen leaves that are slightly toothed and arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as ground cover.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
