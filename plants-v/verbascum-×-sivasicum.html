Verbascum × sivasicum

Verbascum × sivasicum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Verbascum blattaria and Verbascum sinaiticum.

It is a tall, biennial or perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia. The plant features large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are characteristic of the mullein genus. Its flowering stems produce showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties.
