Vitis subrhomboidea

Vitis subrhomboidea, commonly known as the Chinese grapevine, is a woody vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is known for its ability to climb using tendrils and thrives in temperate climates. Its berries are typically small and can be used in traditional medicine.

Vitis subrhomboidea is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
