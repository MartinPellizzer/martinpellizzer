Viola × ryoniae

Viola × ryoniae, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is a hybrid between Viola tricolor and Viola wittrockii, exhibiting a variety of flower colors including purple, blue, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm, with heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

Viola × ryoniae is known for its ability to naturalize and spread through self-seeding, making it a popular choice for ground cover in shaded areas.
