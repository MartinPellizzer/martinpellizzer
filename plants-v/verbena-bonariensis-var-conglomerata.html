Verbena bonariensis var. conglomerata

Verbena bonariensis var. conglomerata, commonly known as the clustered verbena, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant produces showy inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a silver-gray underside that adds visual interest.

It prefers full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.
