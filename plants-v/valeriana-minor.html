Valeriana minor

Valeriana minor, commonly known as lesser valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

Valeriana minor prefers well-drained soils and can thrive in partial shade to full sun.
