Viburnum corymbosum

Viburnum corymbosum, commonly known as the American cranberrybush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pink, arranged in flat-topped clusters. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Viburnum corymbosum thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and wetlands.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
