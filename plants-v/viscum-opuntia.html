Viscum opuntia

Viscum opuntia, commonly known as the barbed fig, is a unique plant that combines features of both cacti and mistletoes.

It is a hemiparasitic plant, meaning it partially relies on host plants for water and nutrients while also performing photosynthesis. The plant has a woody stem covered in spines, typical of cacti, but it also produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals. Its seeds are dispersed by animals that eat the fruits, aiding in its spread.

Viscum opuntia is often found in arid regions, where it adapts to harsh environmental conditions.
