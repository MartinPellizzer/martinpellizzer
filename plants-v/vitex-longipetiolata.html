Vitex longipetiolata

Vitex longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled vitex, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in panicles, typically in shades of white or pale purple. Its fruit is a woody drupe that splits open to release seeds.

Vitex longipetiolata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
