Veratrum shuehshanarum

Veratrum shuehshanarum, commonly known as Shuehshan larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a food source for certain wildlife and its use in traditional medicinal practices.
