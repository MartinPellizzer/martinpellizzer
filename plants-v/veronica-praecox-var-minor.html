Veronica praecox var. minor

Veronica praecox var. minor, commonly known as early speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, blue or violet flowers that bloom early in the spring, typically from April to June. The plant has slender, branching stems covered with fine hairs, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
