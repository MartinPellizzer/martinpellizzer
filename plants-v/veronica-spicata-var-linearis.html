Veronica spicata var. linearis

Veronica spicata var. linearis, commonly known as the slender speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikes of blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. It is often used in garden landscapes for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate.

This variety is a hardy perennial that thrives in temperate climates.
