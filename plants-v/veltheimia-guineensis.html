Veltheimia guineensis

Veltheimia guineensis, commonly known as the Guinean velvet fern, is a striking evergreen perennial native to South Africa.

It features elegant, arching leaves that resemble ferns, with a velvety texture and a deep green color. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded borders.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
