Vicia ludoviciana var. texana

Vicia ludoviciana var. texana, commonly known as the Texas vetch, is a perennial legume native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
