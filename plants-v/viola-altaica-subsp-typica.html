Viola altaica subsp. typica

Viola altaica subsp.

typica, commonly known as the Siberian violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and Central Asia. It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, showy flowers in shades of purple and violet.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, often growing in rocky or alpine environments. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the landscape during the short growing season.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
