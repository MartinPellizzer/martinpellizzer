Viola polychroma

Viola polychroma, commonly known as the multicolored violet, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its strikingly colorful flowers that display a variety of hues, including purple, blue, and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help to reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.

Viola polychroma is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
