Verbascum boerhavii var. knochei

Verbascum boerhavii var. knochei, commonly known as the Knoche's mullein, is a variant of the mullein plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
