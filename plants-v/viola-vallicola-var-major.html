Viola vallicola var. major

Viola vallicola var. major, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and cool, moist conditions. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces showy, violet-colored flowers with five petals. It is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
