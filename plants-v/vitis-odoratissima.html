Vitis odoratissima

Vitis odoratissima, commonly known as the fragrant grapevine, is a species native to China.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves with a distinct aromatic scent. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are highly fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its berries are typically small and yellow, with a sweet, musky flavor.

Vitis odoratissima is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional Chinese medicine.
