Viguiera palmeri var. rzedowskii

Viguiera palmeri var. rzedowskii, commonly known as the Rzedowskii's golden coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange ray flowers and dark brown central disk, which attract various pollinators. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, providing some drought tolerance.

The species is valued in xeriscaping due to its ornamental appeal and low water requirements.
