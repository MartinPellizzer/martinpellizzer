Vaccinium dictyoneuron var. koebrense

Vaccinium dictyoneuron var. koebrense, commonly known as the coastal blueberry, is a variant of the blueberry plant native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in humid, saline environments and is adapted to withstand coastal winds and salt spray.

Its botanical features include alternate, elliptical leaves and white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring.
