Vernonia glabra

Vernonia glabra, commonly known as the smooth ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as swamps and stream banks, and can reach heights of up to six feet. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and clustered in dense, spherical heads.

Vernonia glabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
