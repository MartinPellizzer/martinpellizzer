Vitex vestita f. millsii

Vitex vestita f. millsii, commonly known as the Mexican chaste tree, is a compact shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate flowers and attractive foliage.
