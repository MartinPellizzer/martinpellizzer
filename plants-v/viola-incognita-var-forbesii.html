Viola incognita var. forbesii

Viola incognita var. forbesii, commonly known as the Forbes' violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy violet flowers with a distinctive purple-blue hue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady woodland environments and is often found in rich, humusy soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ecological benefits.
