Vaccinium nigrum

Vaccinium nigrum, commonly known as black huckleberry, is a species of shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a leathery texture and are typically evergreen.

Vaccinium nigrum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
