Viola pyrenaica var. maroccana

Viola pyrenaica var. maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan violet, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of North Africa.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, often with a white or yellow throat. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
