Veronica grandiflora var. minor

Veronica grandiflora var. minor, commonly known as the lesser blueweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly serrated, often with a grayish-green hue.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
