Veronica × joannis-wagneri

Veronica × joannis-wagneri, commonly known as the hybrid speedwell, is a perennial plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate flowers and adaptability to various garden conditions.
