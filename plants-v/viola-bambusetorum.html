Viola bambusetorum

Viola bambusetorum, commonly known as the bamboo violet, is a unique hybrid plant that combines features of violas and bamboo.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems that resemble bamboo, while its leaves are heart-shaped and resemble those of traditional violas. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of violet and white, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden areas.

Due to its ornamental value and unusual appearance, it is often cultivated in specialty gardens and botanical collections.
