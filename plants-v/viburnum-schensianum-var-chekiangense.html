Viburnum schensianum var. chekiangense

Viburnum schensianum var. chekiangense, commonly known as the Chinese snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern China.

It features clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that appear in late spring, followed by red berries in the autumn. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Viburnum schensianum var. chekiangense prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of growing conditions.
