Viola odorata proles barcinonensis

Viola odorata proles barcinonensis, commonly known as the Barcinoensis Violet, is a rare and distinctive variant of the common violet.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking violet-blue flowers with a fragrant aroma. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing small, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. Native to the Iberian Peninsula, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and shaded gardens.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a botanical curiosity and is often cultivated by enthusiasts.
