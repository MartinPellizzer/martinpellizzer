Vavaea pachyphylla

Vavaea pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a splash of color to its desert habitat. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

Vavaea pachyphylla is well adapted to extreme temperatures and low precipitation, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
