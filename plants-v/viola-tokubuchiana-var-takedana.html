Viola tokubuchiana var. takedana

Viola tokubuchiana var. takedana, commonly known as the Takeda violet, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and vivid violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to its natural habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
