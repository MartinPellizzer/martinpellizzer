Vauquelinia californica subsp. sonorensis

Vauquelinia californica subsp.

sonorensis, commonly known as the Sonoran vauquelinia, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
