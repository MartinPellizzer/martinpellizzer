Valeriana utkinii

Valeriana utkinii, commonly known as the Utkin's valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces distinctive, aromatic roots that are often used in traditional medicine for their calming properties. Valeriana utkinii thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of medicinal plants and ecological biodiversity.
