Vatairea trialata

Vatairea trialata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or red sanders, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy green upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is also cultivated for horticultural and pharmaceutical purposes.
