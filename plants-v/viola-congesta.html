Viola congesta

Viola congesta, commonly known as the clustered violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers, which range in color from purple to white, bloom in early spring.

Viola congesta is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
