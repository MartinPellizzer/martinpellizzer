Verbesina arnottii subvar. bupthalmifolia

Verbesina arnottii subvar. bupthalmifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and pointed, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open woods and along roadsides.
