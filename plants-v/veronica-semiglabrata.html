Veronica semiglabrata

Veronica semiglabrata, commonly known as the smooth veronica, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly rough texture.

Veronica semiglabrata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
