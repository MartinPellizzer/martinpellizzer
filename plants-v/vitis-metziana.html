Vitis metziana

Vitis metziana, commonly known as the Chinese grapevine, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically blue-black in color when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Vitis metziana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both horticultural and medicinal purposes.
