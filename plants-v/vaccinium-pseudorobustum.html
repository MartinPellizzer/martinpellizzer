Vaccinium pseudorobustum

Vaccinium pseudorobustum, commonly known as the dwarf huckleberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in mountainous or coastal regions. Its small, dark blue berries are edible and are sometimes used in local cuisine or for making jams.

The leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a dense canopy that supports various wildlife species.
