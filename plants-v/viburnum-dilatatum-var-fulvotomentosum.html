Viburnum dilatatum var. fulvotomentosum

Viburnum dilatatum var. fulvotomentosum, commonly known as the Florida holly or swamp viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, with a distinctive hairy underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that persist into winter. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and it often grows in wet or moist soils along streambanks and swamps.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
