Vernonia cajamarcensis

Vernonia cajamarcensis, commonly known as the Peruvian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, yellowish to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Vernonia cajamarcensis is valued for its ornamental potential and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
