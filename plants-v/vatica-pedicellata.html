Vatica pedicellata

Vatica pedicellata, commonly known as the slender oil tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds. It is valued for its oil-rich seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of biofuel.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
