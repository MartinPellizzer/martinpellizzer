Vitis vinifera var. thunbergii

Vitis vinifera var. thunbergii, commonly known as the Japanese grapevine, is a variety native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and deep green, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, sweet, and aromatic berries that are often used in traditional Chinese and Japanese medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal.

The berries are also used in the production of wines and herbal remedies.
