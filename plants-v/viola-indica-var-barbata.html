Viola indica var. barbata

Viola indica var. barbata, commonly known as the hairy violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive hairy leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained soil. Its foliage is often covered in fine, soft hairs, which give it a fuzzy appearance.

Viola indica var. barbata is valued both for its ornamental beauty and traditional medicinal uses in local herbal remedies.
