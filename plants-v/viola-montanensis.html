Viola montanensis

Viola montanensis, commonly known as the mountain violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, hairy leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. It has a compact growth habit and produces small, delicate blooms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola montanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
