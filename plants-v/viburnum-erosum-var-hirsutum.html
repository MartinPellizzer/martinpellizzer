Viburnum erosum var. hirsutum

Viburnum erosum var. hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy arrowwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it its common name, and it produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers in the spring. The plant's small, dark blue berries are edible and attract various wildlife. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Viburnum erosum var. hirsutum is also valued for its ability to tolerate a variety of soil conditions.
