Vitis flexuosa var. wallichii

Vitis flexuosa var. wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan grapevine, is a climbing vine native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible berries that range in color from green to black. It thrives in temperate to cold climates and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
