Viola mauritii

Viola mauritii, commonly known as the Mauritius violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the island of Mauritius.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple and white. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

Viola mauritii is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
