Verbascum × edirnense

Verbascum × edirnense, commonly known as the Edirnense mullein, is a hybrid plant belonging to the genus Verbascum in the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Native to regions of Central Asia, it thrives in dry, well-drained soils and full sun.

Its medicinal properties are attributed to the presence of various bioactive compounds, including mucilage and flavonoids.
