Viola persicifolia var. rothiana

Viola persicifolia var. rothiana, commonly known as the Roth's violets, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and produces clusters of small, delicate violet flowers with purple petals. The plant typically blooms in early spring, adding a splash of color to its natural habitat. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
