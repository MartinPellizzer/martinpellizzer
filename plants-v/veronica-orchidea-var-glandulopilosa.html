Veronica orchidea var. glandulopilosa

Veronica orchidea var. glandulopilosa, commonly known as the glandular-pilose veronica, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodland areas or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine, glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable humidity and light conditions.
