Valeriana officinalis var. minor

Valeriana officinalis var. minor, commonly known as lesser valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The roots of lesser valerian contain volatile oils and are traditionally used in herbal medicine for their sedative and calming properties.
