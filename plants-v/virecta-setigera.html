Virecta setigera

Virecta setigera, commonly known as the slender spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, arching stems that are often covered with small, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Virecta setigera thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract wildlife.
