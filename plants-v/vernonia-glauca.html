Vernonia glauca

Vernonia glauca, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, blue-gray flower heads. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, preferring moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving them a distinctive appearance.

The flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
