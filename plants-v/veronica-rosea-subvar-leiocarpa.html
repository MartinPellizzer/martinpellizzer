Veronica rosea subvar. leiocarpa

Veronica rosea subvar. leiocarpa, commonly known as the smooth-leaved speedwell, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of small, blue-to-purple flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth, glabrous surface, contributing to its common name. It prefers well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, often found in meadows and rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
