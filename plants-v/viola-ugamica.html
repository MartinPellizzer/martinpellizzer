Viola ugamica

Viola ugamica, commonly known as the Armenian violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola ugamica is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
