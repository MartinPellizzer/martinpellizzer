Verapazia

Verapazia, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Verapazia is also known for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and its use in traditional medicine.
