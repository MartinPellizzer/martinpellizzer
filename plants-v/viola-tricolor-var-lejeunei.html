Viola tricolor var. lejeunei

Viola tricolor var. lejeunei, commonly known as the Dutch violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of violet, yellow, and white, depending on the variety. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
