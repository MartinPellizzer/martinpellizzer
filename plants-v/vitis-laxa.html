Vitis laxa

Vitis laxa, commonly known as the loose grapevine, is a woody vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and tendrils that aid in its growth along supports. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible berries that are typically dark blue to black in color. Vitis laxa is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
