Vernonia missurica var. austroriparia

Vernonia missurica var. austroriparia, commonly known as the eastern ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, erect stem and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, dark purple to brown flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open woods and along stream banks.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it valuable in ecological restoration projects.
