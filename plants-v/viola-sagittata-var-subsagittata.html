Viola sagittata var. subsagittata

Viola sagittata var. subsagittata, commonly known as the arrowleaf violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, arrow-like leaves with a distinctive dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces violet flowers that range from purple to blue, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade, often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native plant gardening.
