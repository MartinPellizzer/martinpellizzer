Velarum supinum

Velarum supinum, commonly known as the creeping thornplant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with trailing stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Velarum supinum is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
