Veronica hybrida subvar. albiflora

Veronica hybrida subvar. albiflora, commonly known as the white speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically reaches a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features slender, hairy stems. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for garden cultivation.
