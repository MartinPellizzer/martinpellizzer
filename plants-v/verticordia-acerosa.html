Verticordia acerosa

Verticordia acerosa, commonly known as the hard-leaved devil's-bit, is an evergreen shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and striking, pinkish-red flower spikes that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to full sun exposure. Its flowers are often clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, with individual flowers having three narrow, recurved petals.

Verticordia acerosa is a popular species in native gardens due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
