Valerianella multidentata var. oscensis

Valerianella multidentata var. oscensis, commonly known as the Corsican buttonweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces tiny white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Apiaceae family, known for its aromatic foliage and ecological significance.
