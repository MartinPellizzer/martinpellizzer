Veronica kermanica

Veronica kermanica, commonly known as Kerman's speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer.

Veronica kermanica is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
