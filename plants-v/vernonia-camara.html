Vernonia camara

Vernonia camara, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower clusters that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture.

Vernonia camara is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
