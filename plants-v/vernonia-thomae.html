Vernonia thomae

Vernonia thomae, commonly known as Thomae's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the United States, particularly found in the southeastern region.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, violet-blue flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and forest edges, where it thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often arranged alternately along the stem.

Vernonia thomae is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
