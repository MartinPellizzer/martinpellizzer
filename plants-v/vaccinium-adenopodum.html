Vaccinium adenopodum

Vaccinium adenopodum, commonly known as the creeping blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or black in color, and it thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. It is often found in bogs, wetlands, and other moist environments.

Its adaptability to harsh conditions makes it an important component of its native ecosystems.
