Vernonia appariciana

Vernonia appariciana, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Vernonia appariciana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
