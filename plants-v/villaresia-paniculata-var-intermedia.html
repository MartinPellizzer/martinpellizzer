Villaresia paniculata var. intermedia

Villaresia paniculata var. intermedia, commonly known as the Brazilian senna, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is part of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm regions.
