Viola silvatica f. macrantha

Viola silvatica f. macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered wood violet, is a botanical variant of the wood violet species.

It is characterized by its larger, showy flowers compared to the typical form, with petals that are often a vibrant violet or purple color. The plant typically grows in woodland areas, thriving in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a soft, downy texture.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden settings for its visual interest.
