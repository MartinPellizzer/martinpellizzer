Vernonia vauthieriana var. angustifolia

Vernonia vauthieriana var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, dark purple to brownish-red flower heads that bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
