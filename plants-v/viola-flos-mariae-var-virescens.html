Viola flos-mariae var. virescens

Viola flos-mariae var. virescens, commonly known as the green-flowered violet, is a variant of the viola genus characterized by its unique foliage and floral coloration.

This plant typically features heart-shaped leaves with a dark green hue and velvety texture. The flowers are notable for their striking greenish-white petals, which stand out against the foliage. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is native to temperate regions and is well-suited for shaded garden settings.
