Valeriana vaga

Valeriana vaga, commonly known as wandering valerian, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slight hairy texture.

Valeriana vaga is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.
