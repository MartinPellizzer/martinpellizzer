Vernonia ottonis

Vernonia ottonis, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads that resemble mist, which bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft, velvety texture.

Vernonia ottonis is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
