Vernonia umbellifera

Vernonia umbellifera, commonly known as the umbrella Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and wetlands. It produces showy, yellow-orange flower heads that are arranged in a loose, umbrella-like shape, giving it its common name.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
