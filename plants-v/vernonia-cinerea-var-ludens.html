Vernonia cinerea var. ludens

Vernonia cinerea var. ludens, commonly known as silverleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in USDA hardiness zones 6 through 9. Its foliage is covered in fine, white hairs, which give it a distinctive, almost frosty appearance.

The plant is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits to pollinators.
