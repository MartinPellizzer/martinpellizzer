Vernonia bipontini var. bipontini

Vernonia bipontini var. bipontini, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, spiky, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open woods and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
