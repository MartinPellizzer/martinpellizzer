Viola × krascheninnikoviorum

Viola × krascheninnikoviorum, commonly known as the velvetleaf viola, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of showy, pansy-like flowers that range in color from purple to white. The plant is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in cool climates and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its foliage and flowers are also used in traditional herbal remedies for their mild medicinal properties.
