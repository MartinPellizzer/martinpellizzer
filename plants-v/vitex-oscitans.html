Vitex oscitans

Vitex oscitans, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 15 feet. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Vitex oscitans is well adapted to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
