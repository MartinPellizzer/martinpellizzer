Vitis amoena

Vitis amoena, commonly known as the sweet white grapevine, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, sweet, white grapes that are edible and often used in jams or wines. Vitis amoena thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is an important species in the Vitaceae family and has historical significance in both horticulture and botany.
