Vernonia camporum

Vernonia camporum, commonly known as the plains Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the central and western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open prairies and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with purple centers.

Vernonia camporum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
