Viola planifolia

Viola planifolia, commonly known as the flatleaf violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and broad. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are usually purple or violet in color, with five petals arranged symmetrically. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, it thrives in moist, temperate environments.

Viola planifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
