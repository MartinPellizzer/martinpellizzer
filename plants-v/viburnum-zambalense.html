Viburnum zambalense

Viburnum zambalense, commonly known as the Zambal viburnum, is a species native to the Philippines.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that attracts birds and other wildlife.

Viburnum zambalense is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
