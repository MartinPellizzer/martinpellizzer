Vernonia polyanthes var. tijucana

Vernonia polyanthes var. tijucana, commonly known as the Tijuana ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and dense clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, oblong seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread in suitable environments.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance.
