Vincetoxicum koi

Vincetoxicum koi, commonly known as the Japanese angelica tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a serrated edge, and produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters. The plant is known for its ornamental value, with showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Vincetoxicum koi is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
