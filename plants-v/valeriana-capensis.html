Valeriana capensis

Valeriana capensis, commonly known as the Cape valerian, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in summer. Its roots are valued in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

Valeriana capensis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
