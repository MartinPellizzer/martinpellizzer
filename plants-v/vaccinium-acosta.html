Vaccinium acosta

Vaccinium acosta, commonly known as Acosta's blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many familiar plants such as blueberries and cranberries. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories. Its small, leathery leaves are dark green and glossy, and it produces clusters of white flowers in the spring.

The berries, which are usually blue or purple, are edible and have a tart flavor.
