Viola × basilensis

Viola × basilensis, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant is native to Europe and has been widely cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile garden plant.

Viola × basilensis is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of climates and soil conditions.
