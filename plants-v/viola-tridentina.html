Viola tridentina

Viola tridentina, commonly known as the three-toothed violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with three distinct tooth-like lobes, giving it its common name. The plant produces delicate purple or violet flowers with a distinctive yellow eye in the center. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Viola tridentina is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
