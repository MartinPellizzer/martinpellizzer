Vernonia heptantha

Vernonia heptantha, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its clusters of small, sky-blue to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands, prairies, and forest edges. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Vernonia heptantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
