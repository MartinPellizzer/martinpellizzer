Vandenboschia capillacea

Vandenboschia capillacea, commonly known as the Cape hairveld, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage that forms a low-growing mat. The plant has small, needle-like leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss. It produces yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months.

Vandenboschia capillacea is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
