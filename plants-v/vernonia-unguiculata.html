Vernonia unguiculata

Vernonia unguiculata, commonly known as Guinea grass, is a flowering plant native to Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow to brown. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
