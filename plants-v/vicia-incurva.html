Vicia incurva

Vicia incurva, commonly known as the bent vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its creeping growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its seed pods are slender and curved, typical of the genus Vicia, and contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Vicia incurva is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats including grasslands and woodland edges.
