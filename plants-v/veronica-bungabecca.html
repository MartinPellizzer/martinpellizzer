Veronica bungabecca

Veronica bungabecca, commonly known as the Bungabega veronica, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine pubescence.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
