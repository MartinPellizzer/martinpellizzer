Verbena hassleriana var. glandulosa

Verbena hassleriana var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that range from blue to purple. The leaves are typically narrow and covered with glandular hairs, which give the plant its specific variety name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
