Valeriana glomerulosa

Valeriana glomerulosa, commonly known as clustered valerian, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Valeriana glomerulosa is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its roots are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported sedative properties.
