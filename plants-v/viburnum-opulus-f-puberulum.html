Viburnum opulus f. puberulum

Viburnum opulus f. puberulum, commonly known as the hairy elderberry, is a cultivar of the European elderberry.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and showy white flowers that appear in flat-topped clusters. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but should be consumed in moderation. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
