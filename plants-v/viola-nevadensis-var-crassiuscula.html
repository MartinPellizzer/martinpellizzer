Viola nevadensis var. crassiuscula

Viola nevadensis var. crassiuscula, commonly known as the Sierra violet, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces violet-blue flowers in early spring. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
