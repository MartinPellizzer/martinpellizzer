Vanhouttea

Vanhouttea, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical regions of Africa.

These plants are characterized by their vibrant, showy flowers that come in a wide range of colors, including purple, pink, white, and blue. The leaves are typically velvety and dark green, often with a waxy texture. Vanhouttea species are popular houseplants due to their ability to thrive in low-light conditions and their long blooming period.

They are also valued for their ornamental appeal and ease of care.
