Vaccinium xerampelinum

Vaccinium xerampelinum, commonly known as the lowbush blueberry, is a species of shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically harvested in the summer months. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary uses, it is cultivated in various regions for both landscaping and commercial purposes.
