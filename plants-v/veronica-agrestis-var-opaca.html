Veronica agrestis var. opaca

Veronica agrestis var. opaca, commonly known as the opaca speedwell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and forms dense mats, often found in moist, shaded habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and adaptable species for ecological and horticultural use.
