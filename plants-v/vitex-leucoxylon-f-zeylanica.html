Vitex leucoxylon f. zeylanica

Vitex leucoxylon f. zeylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon vitex, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, opposite leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.

The species is also valued for its hard, durable wood in local crafts and construction.
