Vernonia jalcana

Vernonia jalcana, commonly known as the desert ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has silver-gray foliage, which helps it conserve water in arid environments. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The genus Vernonia is part of the sunflower family, Asteraceae, and includes many species with similar floral structures.
