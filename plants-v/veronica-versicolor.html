Veronica versicolor

Veronica versicolor, commonly known as the blueweed or common speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer.

Veronica versicolor is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
