Vigna linearis

Vigna linearis, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by elongated, slender pods containing edible seeds. Vigna linearis is valued for its nutritional content and is often grown as a food crop.

It also has potential uses in soil improvement due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
