Vernonia obscura

Vernonia obscura, commonly known as the dark vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its conical inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its dark green leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its small yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Vernonia obscura is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
