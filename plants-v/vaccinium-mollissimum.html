Vaccinium mollissimum

Vaccinium mollissimum, commonly known as the trailing blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in early spring. Its berries, which are typically blue or purple, are edible and contain high levels of antioxidants.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in acidic, well-drained soils.
