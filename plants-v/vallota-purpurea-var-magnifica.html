Vallota purpurea var. magnifica

Vallota purpurea var. magnifica, commonly known as the purple vallota, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

This variant is characterized by its vibrant purple foliage and showy, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun exposure. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking colorful, low-maintenance plants.

Native to tropical regions, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
