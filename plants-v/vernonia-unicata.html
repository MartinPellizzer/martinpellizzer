Vernonia unicata

Vernonia unicata, commonly known as the slender ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, often hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in wetland environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence, while its flowers are small and yellowish-green, arranged in dense clusters.

Vernonia unicata is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential use in ornamental horticulture.
