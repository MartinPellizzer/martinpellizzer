Villarsia aquatica

Villarsia aquatica, commonly known as the water villarsia, is a rare aquatic plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant has slender, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface, with roots submerged in the substrate. Its flowers are small and yellow, often clustered in dense inflorescences near the water's edge.
