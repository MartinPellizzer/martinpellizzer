Verbascum scamandri

Verbascum scamandri, commonly known as the Crimean mullein, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a loose, cylindrical shape. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Verbascum scamandri is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its soothing effects on respiratory conditions.
