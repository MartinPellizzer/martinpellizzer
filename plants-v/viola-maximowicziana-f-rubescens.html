Viola maximowicziana f. rubescens

Viola maximowicziana f. rubescens, commonly known as the crimson violet, is a flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking violet to crimson flowers with a distinctive yellow throat, which bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hairy texture.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and delicate appearance.
