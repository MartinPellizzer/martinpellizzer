Vernonia auyantepuiensis

Vernonia auyantepuiensis, commonly known as the Angel's Trumpet of the Amazon, is a striking plant native to the rainforests of Venezuela and Brazil.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing along riverbanks and in dense undergrowth.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a notable species in the study of Amazonian flora.
