Vaccinium myrsinites

Vaccinium myrsinites, commonly known as sheep laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the northern regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, which bloom in the spring. Its berries are bright red and contain seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.

Vaccinium myrsinites is often found in moist, acidic soils and can thrive in shaded environments.
