Vernonia tovarensis

Vernonia tovarensis, commonly known as Tovarensis ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces small, blue to purple flower heads, which are typically arranged in dense, conical clusters. Vernonia tovarensis is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its striking appearance and resilience.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for gardens in temperate climates.
