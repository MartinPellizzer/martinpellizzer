Valeriana alternifolia subsp. stubendorfii

Valeriana alternifolia subsp.

stubendorfii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
