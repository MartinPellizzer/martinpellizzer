Vernonia tonkinensis

Vernonia tonkinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its dense, upright growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow to orange-red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and often covered in fine hairs.

Vernonia tonkinensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
