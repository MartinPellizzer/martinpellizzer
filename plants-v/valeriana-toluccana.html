Valeriana toluccana

Valeriana toluccana, commonly known as Toluca valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Valerianaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, slightly medicinal scent. The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and typically range in color from white to pale pink.

Valeriana toluccana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
