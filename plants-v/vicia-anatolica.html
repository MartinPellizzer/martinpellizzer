Vicia anatolica

Vicia anatolica, commonly known as Anatolian vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Turkey.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Vicia anatolica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
