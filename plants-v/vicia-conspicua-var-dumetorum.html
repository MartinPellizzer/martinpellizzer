Vicia conspicua var. dumetorum

Vicia conspicua var. dumetorum, commonly known as the Alpine vetch, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb with slender, climbing stems that often form dense mats. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late spring. Its trifoliate leaves are compound, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

This species is adapted to rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
