Veronica cymbalariifolia var. fallacina

Veronica cymbalariifolia var. fallacina, commonly known as the purple veronica, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing shades of violet and blue. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
