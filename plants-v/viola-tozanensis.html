Viola tozanensis

Viola tozanensis, commonly known as the Tozan violet, is a species of flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodland areas and mountainous regions. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the landscape.

Viola tozanensis is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its role in local ecosystems.
