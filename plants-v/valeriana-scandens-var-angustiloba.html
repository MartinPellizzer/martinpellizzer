Valeriana scandens var. angustiloba

Valeriana scandens var. angustiloba, commonly known as narrowleaf valerian, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, ascending stems and small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its root system is known for containing volatile oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties.
