Veronica evenosa

Veronica evenosa, commonly known as the common speedwell, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Plantaginaceae.

It is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of western Asia, often found in grassy areas and along roadsides. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Veronica evenosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
