Verbascum dentatum

Verbascum dentatum, commonly known as the toothed mullein, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are often lobed or toothed. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

Verbascum dentatum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its soothing effects on respiratory ailments.
