Viola riviniana subsp. minor

Viola riviniana subsp.

minor, commonly known as the lesser violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple underside and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple and violet. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its short, creeping stems.

The species is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
