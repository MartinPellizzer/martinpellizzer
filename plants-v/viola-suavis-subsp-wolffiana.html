Viola suavis subsp. wolffiana

Viola suavis subsp.

wolffiana, commonly known as the sweet violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers.

Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this perennial herb thrives in moist, shady environments. The plant's flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
