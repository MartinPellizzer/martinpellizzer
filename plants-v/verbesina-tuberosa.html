Verbesina tuberosa

Verbesina tuberosa, commonly known as the yellow beggar-ticks, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves that are often lobed or heart-shaped. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from summer to fall. Its roots are tuberous, giving it the species name *tuberosa*, and are used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in moist soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
