Vanda arcuata

Vanda arcuata, commonly known as the waxy vanda, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and large, broad leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Vanda arcuata is popular among orchid enthusiasts due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
