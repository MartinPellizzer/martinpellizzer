Vasconcellea aurantiaca

Vasconcellea aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange passionfruit vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, woody stems and compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that are followed by inflated, orange-colored fruits resembling small balloons. The fruits are edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
