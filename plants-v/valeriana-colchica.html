Valeriana colchica

Valeriana colchica, commonly known as the Crimean valerian, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus and parts of southern Europe.

It features a robust rhizomatous root system and produces tall, branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant has dark green, alternate leaves that are lanceolate in shape and emit a strong, spicy aroma. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences.

Valeriana colchica is known for its medicinal properties, particularly for its calming effects and use in treating nervous disorders.
