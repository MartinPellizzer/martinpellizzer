Vitex phaseolifolia

Vitex phaseolifolia, commonly known as the coffeebean vitex, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters. The plant belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its fruit is a dry capsule that contains several small, bean-like seeds.

Vitex phaseolifolia is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun.
