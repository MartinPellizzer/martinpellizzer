Veronica denkichiana var. canescens

Veronica denkichiana var. canescens, commonly known as the woolly speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are covered in a fine layer of white, woolly hair, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
