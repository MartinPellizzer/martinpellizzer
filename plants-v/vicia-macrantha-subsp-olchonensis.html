Vicia macrantha subsp. olchonensis

Vicia macrantha subsp.

olchonensis, commonly known as the Mongolian vetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets.

The plant produces clusters of elongated, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale pink to white. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes.

Its seeds are small and oval, typically dispersed by wind, contributing to its ecological role in soil stabilization.
