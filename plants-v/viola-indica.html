Viola indica

Viola indica, commonly known as the common violet, is a flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 10-30 cm and thrives in cool, moist environments. Viola indica is native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

It is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and along stream banks.
