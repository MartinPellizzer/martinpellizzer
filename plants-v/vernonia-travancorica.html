Vernonia travancorica

Vernonia travancorica, commonly known as the Travancore Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forested areas. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters, which bloom during the monsoon season. Its leaves are elliptical, with a rough texture and a distinct aromatic scent.

Vernonia travancorica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
