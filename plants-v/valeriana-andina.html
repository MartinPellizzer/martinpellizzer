Valeriana andina

Valeriana andina, commonly known as Andean valerian, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has aromatic leaves and stems, which contain essential oils and compounds with sedative properties. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude regions.

Valeriana andina is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its traditional use in herbal medicine.
