Veronica bucharica subsp. ramosissima

Veronica bucharica subsp.

ramosissima, commonly known as the Armenian speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, blue to purple flowers in clusters.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is a perennial species that thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens in suitable climates.
