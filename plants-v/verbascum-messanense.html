Verbascum messanense

Verbascum messanense, commonly known as the Messina mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

Verbascum messanense is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
