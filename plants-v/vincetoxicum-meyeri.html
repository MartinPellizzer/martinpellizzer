Vincetoxicum meyeri

Vincetoxicum meyeri, commonly known as the Meyer's false bindweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Vincetoxicum meyeri is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in partial to full shade.
