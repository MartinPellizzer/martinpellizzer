Verbascum elegans

Verbascum elegans, commonly known as the silky mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in soft, velvety hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are large, lance-shaped, and covered in fine downy hairs.

Verbascum elegans is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in herbal medicine.
