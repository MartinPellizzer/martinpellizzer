Villarsia submersa

Villarsia submersa, commonly known as the submerged villarsia, is a rare aquatic plant native to certain freshwater habitats in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are adapted for life in submerged environments. The plant typically grows in slow-moving or still waters, such as ponds and marshes. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often floating on the water surface.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
