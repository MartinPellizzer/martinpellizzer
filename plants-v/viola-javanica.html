Viola javanica

Viola javanica, commonly known as the Java violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, five-petaled flowers. The flowers typically range in color from violet to white and are often adorned with dark purple markings. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
