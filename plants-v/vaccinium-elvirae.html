Vaccinium elvirae

Vaccinium elvirae, commonly known as the Elvirae blueberry, is a species of shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark blue or purple in color. These berries are rich in antioxidants and have a tart, slightly sweet flavor.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires a cool climate for optimal growth.
