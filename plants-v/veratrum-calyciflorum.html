Veratrum calyciflorum

Veratrum calyciflorum, commonly known as the Western white hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, stout stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, topped with large, showy white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has large, palmately lobed leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
