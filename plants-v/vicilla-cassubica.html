Vicilla cassubica

Vicilla cassubica, commonly known as the Cassubian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. It has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is part of the Campanulaceae family.

Vicilla cassubica is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
