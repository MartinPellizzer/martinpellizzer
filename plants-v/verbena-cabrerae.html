Verbena cabrerae

Verbena cabrerae, commonly known as the Cape verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its blue or violet flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in coastal environments. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Verbena cabrerae is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
