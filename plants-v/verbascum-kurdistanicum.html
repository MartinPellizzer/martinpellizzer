Verbascum kurdistanicum

Verbascum kurdistanicum, commonly known as the Kurdish mullein, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and northern Iraq.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are broad and velvety, which helps reduce water loss in dry climates.

Verbascum kurdistanicum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
