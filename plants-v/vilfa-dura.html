Vilfa dura

Vilfa dura, commonly known as the hardy violet, is a perennial plant native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Vilfa dura is well adapted to moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and shaded gardens.
