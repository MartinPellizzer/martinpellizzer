Vitis vinifera var. bauhinii

Vitis vinifera var. bauhinii, commonly known as the Muscat of Alexandria, is a grapevine species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic berries that are typically green to yellow in color. The plant is known for its vigorous growth and is often cultivated for its wine-making potential. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a distinctively serrated margin.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping.
