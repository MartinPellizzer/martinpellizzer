Viola pogonantha

Viola pogonantha, commonly known as the bearded violet, is a species of flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces violet-blue flowers with a yellow center, typically blooming in spring. It prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Viola pogonantha is a perennial herb that plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
