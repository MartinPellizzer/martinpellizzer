Vietsenia rotundifolia

Vietsenia rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leafed vietsia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its humid habitat. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for ornamental gardening.

Due to its unique foliage and delicate blooms, it is occasionally cultivated for its aesthetic value in tropical gardens.
