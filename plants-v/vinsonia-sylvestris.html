Vinsonia sylvestris

Vinsonia sylvestris, commonly known as the forest vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense woodlands. Its stems are woody and can grow quite long, allowing it to climb trees and other structures.

The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
