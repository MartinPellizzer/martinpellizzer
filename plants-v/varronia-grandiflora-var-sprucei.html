Varronia grandiflora var. sprucei

Varronia grandiflora var. sprucei, commonly known as the Spruce's varronia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture with a distinct aroma.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
