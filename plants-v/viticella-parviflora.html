Viticella parviflora

Viticella parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered ivy, is a climbing plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly velvety texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the late spring to early summer. It thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Viticella parviflora is also valued for its ability to climb walls and trellises, making it a popular choice for vertical gardening.
