Valerianoides strigosa

Valerianoides strigosa, commonly known as the rough valerian, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Valerianaceae and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in the spring. Valerianoides strigosa is often found in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its root system is known for containing volatile oils, which contribute to its aromatic properties.
