Valeriana nasturtiifolia

Valeriana nasturtiifolia, commonly known as the fragrant valerian, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, pinkish-purple flowers and pinnately compound leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Valeriana nasturtiifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
