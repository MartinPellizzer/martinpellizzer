Verbascum longifolium subsp. pannosum

Verbascum longifolium subsp.

pannosum, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length.

The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Its hairy foliage and robust growth make it a distinctive and hardy species within the genus Verbascum.
