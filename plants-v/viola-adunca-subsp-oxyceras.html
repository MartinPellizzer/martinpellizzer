Viola adunca subsp. oxyceras

Viola adunca subsp.

oxyceras, commonly known as the narrowleaf violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive serrated edge and produces small, violet-colored flowers with five petals. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, creeping growth habit.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrow leaves and slightly elongated petals compared to other viola species.
