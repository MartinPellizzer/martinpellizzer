Viguiera bicolor

Viguiera bicolor, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers and dark green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Viguiera bicolor thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy perennial in USDA hardiness zones 7 through 10.

Its striking appearance and ecological benefits make it a popular choice for gardeners and native plant enthusiasts.
