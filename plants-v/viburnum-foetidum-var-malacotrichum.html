Viburnum foetidum var. malacotrichum

Viburnum foetidum var. malacotrichum, commonly known as the sweet viburnum, is a flowering shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. Its fruit is a dark blue to black drupe, often containing one or two seeds.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.
