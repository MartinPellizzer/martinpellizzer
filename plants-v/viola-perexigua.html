Viola perexigua

Viola perexigua, commonly known as the dwarf violet, is a small perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces delicate, violet-colored flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, making it one of the smallest species in the viola genus. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a subtle touch of color to the forest floor.
