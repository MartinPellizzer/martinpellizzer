Viola teydea

Viola teydea, commonly known as the Turkish violet, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Viola teydea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
