Vaccinium henryi var. henryi

Vaccinium henryi var. henryi, commonly known as the Henry's blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark blue berries that are typically edible. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It has lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental appeal and as a source of wild berries.
