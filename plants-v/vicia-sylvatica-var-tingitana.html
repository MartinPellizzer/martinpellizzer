Vicia sylvatica var. tingitana

Vicia sylvatica var. tingitana, commonly known as the Canary vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its seeds are enclosed in slender, hairy pods that mature to a brownish color.

This species is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience in challenging conditions.
