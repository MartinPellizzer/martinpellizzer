Viola dehnhardtii

Viola dehnhardtii, commonly known as the Dehnhardt's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central Europe.

It typically grows to a height of about 10-20 centimeters and features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces showy violet-blue flowers with five petals, often marked with distinctive patterns. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Viola dehnhardtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
