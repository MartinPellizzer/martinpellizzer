Vachellia mbuluensis

Vachellia mbuluensis, commonly known as the Kalahari thornveld acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny branches and bipinnate leaves that are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, cylindrical clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods containing flattened seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to poor soil conditions.

Vachellia mbuluensis plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various desert wildlife.
