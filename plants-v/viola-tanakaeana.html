Viola tanakaeana

Viola tanakaeana, commonly known as the Tanaka's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streambanks. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with five petals, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Viola tanakaeana is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
