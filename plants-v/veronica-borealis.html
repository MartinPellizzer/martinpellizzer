Veronica borealis

Veronica borealis, commonly known as the Arctic Speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Arctic and subarctic regions.

It features small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to cold, harsh environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in tundra and rocky habitats.

Veronica borealis plays a role in supporting local pollinators in its native ecosystems.
