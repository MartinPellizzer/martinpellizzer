Vitis heyneana var. glabra

Vitis heyneana var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth grapevines, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round berries that range in color from green to red. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
