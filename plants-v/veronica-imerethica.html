Veronica imerethica

Veronica imerethica, commonly known as the alpine speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Veronica imerethica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic alpine conditions.
