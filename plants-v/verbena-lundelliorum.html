Verbena lundelliorum

Verbena lundelliorum, commonly known as the Lundell's verbena, is a flowering plant belonging to the Verbenaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant purple or blue flowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, narrow, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Verbena lundelliorum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
