Vigna hookeri

Vigna hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated, slender pods. The plant is characterized by its robust root system and ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, enhancing soil fertility. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Vigna hookeri is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
