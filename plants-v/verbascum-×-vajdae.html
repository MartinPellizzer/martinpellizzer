Verbascum × vajdae

Verbascum × vajdae, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a loose spike at the top of the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rocky or arid landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
