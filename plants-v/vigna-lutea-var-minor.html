Vigna lutea var. minor

Vigna lutea var. minor, commonly known as small cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish pods and trifoliate leaves. The plant is typically annual, growing to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white, and it produces small, round seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines.

The plant is valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
