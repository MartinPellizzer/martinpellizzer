Viticella heterophylla var. tenera

Viticella heterophylla var. tenera, commonly known as the velvetleaf grape.

This plant is a variant of the viticella grapevine, characterized by its distinctive velvety, waxy leaves that give it its common name. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a deep green color with a textured surface.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
