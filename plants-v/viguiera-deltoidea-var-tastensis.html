Viguiera deltoidea var. tastensis

Viguiera deltoidea var. tastensis, commonly known as the Tastens ghostflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves and produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to desert environments.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a valuable ornamental plant in xeriscaping.
