Viguiera ovatifolia

Viguiera ovatifolia, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows as a tall, erect perennial with lance-shaped leaves and produces showy purple to pink flowers in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for garden landscapes.

Viguiera ovatifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
