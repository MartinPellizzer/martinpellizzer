Viola silvatica var. imberbis

Viola silvatica var. imberbis, commonly known as the hairless wood violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to woodland areas in Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15-30 cm, with hairy stems that become hairless as the plant matures. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest floors or along stream banks.

The flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
