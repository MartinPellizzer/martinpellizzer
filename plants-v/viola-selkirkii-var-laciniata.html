Viola selkirkii var. laciniata

Viola selkirkii var. laciniata, commonly known as the Selkirk fringed violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive notches and lobes, giving it a fringed appearance. The flowers are typically violet or purple, with five petals and a spurred petal at the back. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
