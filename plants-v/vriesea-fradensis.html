Vriesea fradensis

Vriesea fradensis, commonly known as the Fradensian Vriesea, is a species of bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescence that can range from red to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often form a rosette shape to collect rainwater.

Vriesea fradensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in greenhouses and indoor gardens.
