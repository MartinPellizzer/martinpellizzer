Vaccinium acuminatissimum f. borneense

Vaccinium acuminatissimum f. borneense, commonly known as the Bornean huckleberry, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Borneo.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bright red berries. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in lowland and hill forests. Its berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them a valuable food source for local wildlife.

The species is also of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
