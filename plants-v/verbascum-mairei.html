Verbascum mairei

Verbascum mairei, commonly known as the Maire's mullein, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes many species of flowering plants commonly referred to as mulleins. The plant is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in clusters at the top of the plant.

Verbascum mairei is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is adapted to grow in dry, sunny conditions.
