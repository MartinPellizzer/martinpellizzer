Viola adunca subsp. radicosa

Viola adunca subsp.

radicosa, commonly known as the hairy violet, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces small, violet-colored flowers with five petals. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive pubescent foliage.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
