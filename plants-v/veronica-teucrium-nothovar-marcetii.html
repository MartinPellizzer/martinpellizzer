Veronica teucrium nothovar. marcetii

Veronica teucrium nothovar. marcetii, commonly known as the Alpina Veronica, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft, velvety texture. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice among gardeners seeking hardy, ornamental plants.
