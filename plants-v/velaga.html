Velaga

Velaga, commonly known as *Erythroxylum velaga*, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, green, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Velaga is often found in humid, lowland areas and is adapted to thrive in shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject for ecological and horticultural study.
