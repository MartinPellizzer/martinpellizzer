Veitchia smithii

Veitchia smithii, commonly known as the giant swamp taro, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to three meters in length, making it a dramatic addition to any landscape. The plant produces striking orange-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the early morning. It thrives in wet, swampy environments and requires consistent moisture to flourish.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
