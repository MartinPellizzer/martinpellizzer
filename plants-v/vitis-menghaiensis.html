Vitis menghaiensis

Vitis menghaiensis, commonly known as Menghai wild grape, is a species native to China, particularly found in the Yunnan Province.

It is a climbing vine that produces small, dark blue to black berries. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with five to seven leaflets and its robust, woody stems. It thrives in subtropical climates and is often found in humid, mountainous regions.

The berries are used in local traditional medicine and have potential for cultivation as a fruit crop.
