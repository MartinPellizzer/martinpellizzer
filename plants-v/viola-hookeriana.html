Viola hookeriana

Viola hookeriana, commonly known as Hooker's violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to the Andes Mountains of South America, specifically in regions of Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
