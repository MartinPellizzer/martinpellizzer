Vincentia brideliifolia

Vincentia brideliifolia, commonly known as the Brideliifolia vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate, and its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
