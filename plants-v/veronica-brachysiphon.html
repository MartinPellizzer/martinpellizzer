Veronica brachysiphon

Veronica brachysiphon, commonly known as narrow-leaved speedwell, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant features small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Veronica brachysiphon is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
