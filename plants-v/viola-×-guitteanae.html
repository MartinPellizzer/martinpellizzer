Viola × guitteanae

Viola × guitteanae, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a member of the Violaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often violet or purple flowers.

This hybrid plant typically features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers may have distinctive patterns or markings. It is a perennial herb that thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in woodland or shaded gardens. The plant is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its vibrant blooms and foliage.

Its hybrid nature results from crosses between different species of violas, contributing to its genetic diversity and adaptability.
