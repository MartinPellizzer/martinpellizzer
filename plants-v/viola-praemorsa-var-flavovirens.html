Viola praemorsa var. flavovirens

Viola praemorsa var. flavovirens, commonly known as the yellow-green violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and dark green veins. The flowers are typically violet-blue with yellow centers, blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
