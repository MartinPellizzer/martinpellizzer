Vigna vexillata var. sepiaria

Vigna vexillata var. sepiaria, commonly known as the black-eyed bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with a distinctive dark green color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated, cylindrical pods containing several kidney-shaped seeds. Its seeds are typically black or dark brown with a pale stripe, and the plant is valued for its nutritional content and adaptability to various soil conditions.

It is also used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop in some regions.
