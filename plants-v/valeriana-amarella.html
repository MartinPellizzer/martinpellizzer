Valeriana amarella

Valeriana amarella, commonly known as sweet valerian, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, often reddish or purple flowers and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies in moist, shady environments.

Valeriana amarella is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its calming effects.
