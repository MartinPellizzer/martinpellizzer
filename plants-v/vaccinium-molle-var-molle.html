Vaccinium molle var. molle

Vaccinium molle var. molle, commonly known as the lowbush blueberry, is a species native to North America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils in forested areas. The plant produces small, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a dark green color.

The plant is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and wildlife.
