Verbascum bicolor

Verbascum bicolor, commonly known as the velvetleaf mullein, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in soft, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

Verbascum bicolor thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for gardens and wild landscapes.
