Viola canina var. rupestris

Viola canina var. rupestris, commonly known as the rock violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and limestone habitats in Europe.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a deep green color and prominent veins. The plant produces violet-blue flowers with five petals, often with a distinctive yellow throat, during late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
