Valeriana effusa var. fiebrigii

Valeriana effusa var. fiebrigii, commonly known as the feverfew valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a robust root system and erect, branched stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters called cymes. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
