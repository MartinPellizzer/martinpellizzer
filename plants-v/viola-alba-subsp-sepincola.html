Viola alba subsp. sepincola

Viola alba subsp.

sepincola, commonly known as the Sepincola violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pale violet to white flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
