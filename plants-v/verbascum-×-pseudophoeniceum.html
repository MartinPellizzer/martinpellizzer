Verbascum × pseudophoeniceum

Verbascum × pseudophoeniceum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant belonging to the Scrophulariaceae family.

It is a hybrid between Verbascum phoeniceum and Verbascum blattaria, resulting in a plant with distinctive fuzzy, velvety leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, well-drained soils and is native to regions of Europe and parts of Asia. It produces tall spikes of yellow flowers in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is often grown in gardens and landscapes.
