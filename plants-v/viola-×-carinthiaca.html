Viola × carinthiaca

Viola × carinthiaca, commonly known as the Viennese violet, is a hybrid perennial plant native to Central Europe.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, violet-blue flowers with yellow centers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or shaded garden settings. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom in early spring.

Viola × carinthiaca is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its use in both garden landscaping and traditional herbal medicine.
