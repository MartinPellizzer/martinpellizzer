Vigna fischeri

Vigna fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Vigna fischeri is valued for its nutritional content, including protein-rich seeds, and is often cultivated for both food and forage.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adaptable to various climatic conditions.
