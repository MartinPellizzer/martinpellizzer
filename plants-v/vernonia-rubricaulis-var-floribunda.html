Vernonia rubricaulis var. floribunda

Vernonia rubricaulis var. floribunda, commonly known as the scarlet false heather, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant red bracts that surround small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a reddish tint on the underside of the leaves.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its striking color and drought tolerance.
