Velaea dissecta

Velaea dissecta, commonly known as the dissected morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories.

Velaea dissecta is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
