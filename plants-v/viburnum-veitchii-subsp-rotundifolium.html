Viburnum veitchii subsp. rotundifolium

Viburnum veitchii subsp.

rotundifolium, commonly known as the roundleaf viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically rounded at the base.

The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
