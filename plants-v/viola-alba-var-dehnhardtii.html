Viola alba var. dehnhardtii

Viola alba var. dehnhardtii, commonly known as the Dehnhardt's white violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green with a slight pubescence. The plant produces small, showy white or pale violet flowers with a distinctive yellow throat, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
