Vinca difformis subsp. difformis

Vinca difformis subsp.

difformis, commonly known as the common periwinkle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is a low-growing evergreen shrub with glossy, dark green foliage and clusters of blue to purple flowers.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its adaptability and persistent foliage make it a valuable plant in landscaping and horticulture.
