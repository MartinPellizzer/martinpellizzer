Vaccinium spicigelum

Vaccinium spicigelum, commonly known as the spiky blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by edible berries that are rich in antioxidants. Its leaves are dark green and leathery, and it thrives in acidic, well-drained soils.

Vaccinium spicigelum is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its culinary uses.
