Valeriana sylvatica var. uliginosa

Valeriana sylvatica var. uliginosa, commonly known as bog valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in northern Europe and Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that are often reddish in color and bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive aromatic scent. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its roots are known for their medicinal properties, containing compounds such as valerenic acid.

This variety is adapted to moist, acidic soils and is often found in peat bogs and marshy areas.
