Valeriana auricula

Valeriana auricula, commonly known as the auricled valerian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, auriculate (ear-shaped) leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer. Valeriana auricula thrives in woodland and shady environments, preferring moist, well-drained soil.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and has a long history in traditional medicine.
