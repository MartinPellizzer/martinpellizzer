Verbena chiricahensis

Verbena chiricahensis, commonly known as Chiricahua verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces showy clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
