Vitis vinifera var. luneli

Vitis vinifera var. luneli, commonly known as Luneli grape, is a rare and ancient variety originating from the Languedoc region of France.

It is characterized by its small, dark-colored berries and is typically used in the production of fortified wines. The plant exhibits moderate vigor and is well-suited to temperate climates with sufficient sunlight. It has a long growing season and requires careful management to achieve optimal ripening.

Due to its historical significance and unique flavor profile, it is considered a valuable heritage grape variety.
