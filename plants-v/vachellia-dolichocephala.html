Vachellia dolichocephala

Vachellia dolichocephala, commonly known as the slender thorn tree, is a leguminous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its long, slender, thorned stems and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, followed by elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important component of savanna ecosystems.

Vachellia dolichocephala is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a source of browse for livestock.
