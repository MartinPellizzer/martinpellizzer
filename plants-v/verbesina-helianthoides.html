Verbesina helianthoides

Verbesina helianthoides, commonly known as the sunflower coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like yellow-orange flowers with a prominent central disk, blooming from late summer to early fall. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its bright flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
