Valeriana sylvestris

Valeriana sylvestris, commonly known as wood valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its rhizomatous root system is known for containing volatile oils and alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Valeriana sylvestris is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and along stream banks.
