Verbascum × decalvans subsp. decalvans

Verbascum × decalvans subsp.

decalvans, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in dense, soft, woolly hairs.

The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are large, lance-shaped, and covered in a similar fuzzy coating.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
