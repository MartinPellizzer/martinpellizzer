Vitis arcuata

Vitis arcuata, commonly known as the wisteria vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and clusters of fragrant, blue-purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are alternate and have a serrated margin. Its fruits are small, dry berries that contain seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Vitis arcuata is typically found in moist, wooded areas and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
