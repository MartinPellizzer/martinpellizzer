Vernonia aurea

Vernonia aurea, commonly known as golden ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. Its foliage is typically green and lance-shaped, with some varieties exhibiting a reddish tinge.

Vernonia aurea is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
