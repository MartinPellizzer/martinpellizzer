Vignea stellulata

Vignea stellulata, commonly known as the starry morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces vines that can climb or trail along the ground. Its flowers typically bloom in the early morning, hence its common name, and come in shades of pink, purple, and white.

Vignea stellulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
