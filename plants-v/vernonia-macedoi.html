Vernonia macedoi

Vernonia macedoi, commonly known as the Macedonian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with erect, branching stems covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while the flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters.

Vernonia macedoi is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
