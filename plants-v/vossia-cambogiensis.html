Vossia cambogiensis

Vossia cambogiensis, commonly known as the Cambodian grass, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in wetland or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Due to its ecological importance and potential medicinal uses, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
