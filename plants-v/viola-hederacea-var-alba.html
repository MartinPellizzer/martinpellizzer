Viola hederacea var. alba

Viola hederacea var. alba, commonly known as the white violet, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, showy white or pale violet flowers. Its flowers have five petals, with the lower petal often bearing a distinctive yellow or orange spot.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
