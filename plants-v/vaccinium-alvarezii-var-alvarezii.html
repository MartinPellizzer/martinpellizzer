Vaccinium alvarezii var. alvarezii

Vaccinium alvarezii var. alvarezii, commonly known as the Alvarez blueberry, is a species native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude areas, often in cloud forests or paramo ecosystems. The plant produces small, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are evergreen, with a leathery texture and a deep green color.

The species is valued for both its ecological role and its potential as a crop for local communities.
