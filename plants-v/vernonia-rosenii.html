Vernonia rosenii

Vernonia rosenii, commonly known as the Rosen's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

Vernonia rosenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
