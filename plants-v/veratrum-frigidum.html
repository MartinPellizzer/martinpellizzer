Veratrum frigidum

Veratrum frigidum, commonly known as the Canadian snakeroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, stout stems and large, compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or greenish-white flowers in late summer. Its roots and rhizomes contain toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous if ingested.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands.
