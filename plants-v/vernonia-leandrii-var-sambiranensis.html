Vernonia leandrii var. sambiranensis

Vernonia leandrii var. sambiranensis, commonly known as the Sambiranensis Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry or seasonally wet habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
