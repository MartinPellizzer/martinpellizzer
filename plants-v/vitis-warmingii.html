Vitis warmingii

Vitis warmingii, commonly known as the Chinese fox grape, is a species of flowering vine native to China.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into clusters of berries, which are typically dark purple when mature. Vitis warmingii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
