Viola sepincola subsp. glabrescens

Viola sepincola subsp.

glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a smooth, glabrous surface, and its flowers are usually violet or purple in color. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
