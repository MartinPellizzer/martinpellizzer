Verbascum × godronii

Verbascum × godronii, commonly known as the Godron's mullein, is a hybrid plant belonging to the genus Verbascum in the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated both for its beauty and potential health benefits.
