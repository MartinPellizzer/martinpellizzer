Veronicastrum sibiricum var. australe

Veronicastrum sibiricum var. australe, commonly known as the Siberian blueweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that produce spikes of blue to purple flowers in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
