Vaccinium rotundifolium

Vaccinium rotundifolium, commonly known as the roundleaf blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its oval, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, which bloom in early spring. Its berries, which are dark blue when mature, are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in jams and culinary applications.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is commonly found in forest understories.
