Veronica teucrium subsp. dubia

Veronica teucrium subsp.

dubia, commonly known as the slender speedwell, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in open woodlands or along stream banks.

The plant features small, blue to violet flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
