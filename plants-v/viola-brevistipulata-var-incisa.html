Viola brevistipulata var. incisa

Viola brevistipulata var. incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive serrated margin, which is characteristic of the varietal form incisa. The plant produces violet-blue flowers with a yellow center, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
