Viguiera nesomii

Viguiera nesomii, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes, thriving in full sun. Its showy yellow to orange flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viguiera nesomii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
