Vernonia chrysophylla

Vernonia chrysophylla, commonly known as the golden-leaf vernonia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the dry season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and savannas.

Vernonia chrysophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
