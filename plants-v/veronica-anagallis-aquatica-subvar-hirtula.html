Veronica anagallis-aquatica subvar. hirtula

Veronica anagallis-aquatica subvar. hirtula, commonly known as the hairy water speedwell, is a perennial herb native to moist, often calcareous habitats.

It features small, blue or violet flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and covered with fine hairs, giving the plant its "hairy" appearance. It thrives in wet meadows, streambanks, and other damp environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
