Viola × bohemica

Viola × bohemica, commonly known as the Bohemian violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant is native to Central and Eastern Europe and thrives in moist, shaded environments. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds.

Viola × bohemica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and woodland settings.
