Veronica officinalis var. albiflora

Veronica officinalis var. albiflora, commonly known as white-flowered speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white down. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
