Viola hispida subsp. rothomagensis

Viola hispida subsp.

rothomagensis, commonly known as the Rothomagens violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly pubescent surface.

The plant produces violet flowers with five petals, often in shades of purple or blue, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
