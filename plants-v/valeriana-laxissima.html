Valeriana laxissima

Valeriana laxissima, commonly known as the slender valerian, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late summer. Valeriana laxissima is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and forest edges.

Its aromatic foliage has been used in traditional medicine for its calming properties.
