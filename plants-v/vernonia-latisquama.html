Vernonia latisquama

Vernonia latisquama, commonly known as the broad-leaved ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a perennial shrub or subshrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. The flowers are usually blue to purple in color, and they bloom during the dry season.

Vernonia latisquama is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
