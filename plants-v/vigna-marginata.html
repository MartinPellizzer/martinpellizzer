Vigna marginata

Vigna marginata, commonly known as the cowpea or southern peas, is a leguminous plant native to Africa and widely cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, trifoliate leaves, and small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate with adequate sunlight. Vigna marginata is valued for its nutritional content, providing protein-rich seeds that are used in various cuisines.

Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil makes it an important crop for sustainable agriculture.
