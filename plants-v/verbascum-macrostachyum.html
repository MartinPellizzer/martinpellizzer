Verbascum macrostachyum

Verbascum macrostachyum, commonly known as the giant mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces large, fuzzy, yellowish-green leaves that are covered in soft, woolly hairs. Its flowers are clustered in dense, showy spikes at the top of the stems, typically blooming in late summer.

Verbascum macrostachyum is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and sandy soils.
