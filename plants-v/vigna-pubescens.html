Vigna pubescens

Vigna pubescens, commonly known as the hairy cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it produces small, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. The plant is often cultivated for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein and essential nutrients. Vigna pubescens thrives in warm climates and is adaptable to various soil types.

Its growth habit makes it a valuable crop for both agricultural and ecological purposes.
