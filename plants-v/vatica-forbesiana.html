Vatica forbesiana

Vatica forbesiana, commonly known as the Forbes' ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, hard wood and broad, leathery leaves. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan. It produces small, pale yellow flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

Vatica forbesiana is valued for its timber and plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
