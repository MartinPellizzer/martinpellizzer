Viola kitaibeliana

Viola kitaibeliana, commonly known as the purple violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a deep green color and produces showy, violet-blue flowers with yellow centers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Viola kitaibeliana is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
