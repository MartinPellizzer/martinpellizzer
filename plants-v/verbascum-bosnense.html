Verbascum bosnense

Verbascum bosnense, commonly known as Bosnian mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its fuzzy, woolly leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Verbascum bosnense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
