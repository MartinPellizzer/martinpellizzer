Vitis vinifera var. normalis

Vitis vinifera var. normalis, commonly known as the common grapevine, is a species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth, producing both male and female flowers on the same plant. The plant's leaves are typically heart-shaped with serrated edges, and it bears clusters of small, green berries that ripen to a deep purple or black color. It is widely cultivated for its fruit, which is used in winemaking and table consumption.

The variety is known for its adaptability to various climates and soils, making it a significant crop in viticulture.
