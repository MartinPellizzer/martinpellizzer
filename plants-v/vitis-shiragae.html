Vitis shiragae

Vitis shiragae, commonly known as the Japanese grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its vigorous growth and trailing vines. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by berries that are typically black when mature. Vitis shiragae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some historical use in traditional Japanese agriculture.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various soil types and climates.
