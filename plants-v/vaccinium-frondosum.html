Vaccinium frondosum

Vaccinium frondosum, commonly known as the creeping blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and rhododendrons. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by edible berries that are typically blue or purple. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, and the plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils.

Vaccinium frondosum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, attractive ground covers.
