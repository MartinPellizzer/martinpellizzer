Vitis moschata

Vitis moschata, commonly known as the sand cherry or sweet gourd, is a species of flowering vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested in the fall. Vitis moschata is often grown for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
