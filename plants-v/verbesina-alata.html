Verbesina alata

Verbesina alata, commonly known as antelope's horn, is a perennial plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers and distinctive, hollow, ridged stems that resemble antlers. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Verbesina alata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
