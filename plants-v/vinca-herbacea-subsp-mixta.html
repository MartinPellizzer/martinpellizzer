Vinca herbacea subsp. mixta

Vinca herbacea subsp.

mixta, commonly known as the common periwinkle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a low-growing, evergreen perennial that produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white.

The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shady garden beds.

The species is also valued for its ability to prevent soil erosion due to its dense, spreading growth habit.
