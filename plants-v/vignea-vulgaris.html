Vignea vulgaris

Vignea vulgaris, commonly known as the common morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces edible seeds, though caution is advised as some species can be toxic. Vignea vulgaris thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value contribute to its widespread cultivation.
