Vernonia luxensis

Vernonia luxensis, commonly known as the Luxemburg Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to two meters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
