Vaccinium mjoebergii

Vaccinium mjoebergii, commonly known as the Mjoeberg blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark blue to black in color, with a tart flavor. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is cultivated in some horticultural gardens.
