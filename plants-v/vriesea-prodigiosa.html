Vriesea prodigiosa

Vriesea prodigiosa, commonly known as the "Queen of the Bromeliads," is a striking tropical plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting inflorescence that can reach up to three feet in height. The plant features a rosette of sword-shaped, glossy leaves that collect water, creating a small pool that supports various microorganisms and small aquatic life. Its flowers, which can be red, orange, or yellow, are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.

Vriesea prodigiosa is a popular houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
