Verbesina subpanduriformis

Verbesina subpanduriformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf beggar-tick, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its fibrous roots and hairy stems contribute to its ability to spread and establish in various wetland habitats.
