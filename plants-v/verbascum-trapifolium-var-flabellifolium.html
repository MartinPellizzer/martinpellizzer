Verbascum trapifolium var. flabellifolium

Verbascum trapifolium var. flabellifolium, commonly known as the flabellifolium mullein, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features tall, hairy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, velvety leaves that are deeply lobed. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in late summer, arranged in clustered inflorescences. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
