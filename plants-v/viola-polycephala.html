Viola polycephala

Viola polycephala, commonly known as the clustered violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to the eastern United States, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has a short, upright stem and produces distinctive, rounded flower clusters.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native garden landscaping.
