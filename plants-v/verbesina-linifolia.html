Verbesina linifolia

Verbesina linifolia, commonly known as slender beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, linear leaves. The plant produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in swamps, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its fibrous roots and stems make it a hardy species that can tolerate periodic flooding.
