Verbena morongii

Verbena morongii, commonly known as the Morong's verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of purple and blue, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its foliage is typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Verbena morongii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
