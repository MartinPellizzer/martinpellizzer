Verbascum gabrieliae

Verbascum gabrieliae, commonly known as Gabriel's mullein, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes several species of flowering plants known for their fuzzy leaves and showy yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it adapts to harsh environmental conditions.
