Vachellia × cedilloi

Vachellia × cedilloi, commonly known as the hybrid acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is adapted to hot, dry climates and is often found in savannas and rocky soils. It produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators like bees and birds.

Vachellia × cedilloi is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in reforestation efforts due to its ability to thrive in challenging environments.
