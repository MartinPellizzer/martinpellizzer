Verbena alata

Verbena alata, commonly known as the winged verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and pairs of opposite leaves that often have a wing-like appearance. The plant produces vibrant blue or purple flowers in spikes, which bloom from summer to autumn. Verbena alata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
