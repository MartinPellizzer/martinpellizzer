Viola mandshurica f. hasegawae

Viola mandshurica f. hasegawae, commonly known as the Hasegawa's violet, is a rare and ornamental species native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant violet to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its flowers are often borne on slender stems, and the species is noted for its delicate appearance and horticultural value.

Botanically, it belongs to the Violaceae family and is closely related to other viola species found in the region.
