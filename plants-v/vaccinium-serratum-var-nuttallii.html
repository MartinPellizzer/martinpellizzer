Vaccinium serratum var. nuttallii

Vaccinium serratum var. nuttallii, commonly known as Nuttall's huckleberry, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and azaleas. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark blue to black in color, with a tart flavor. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
