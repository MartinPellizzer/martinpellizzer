Villaria purpurea

Villaria purpurea, commonly known as purple violets, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Violaceae family, characterized by its showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

The flowers bloom in spring and are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.
