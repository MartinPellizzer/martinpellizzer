Viola nepaulensis

Viola nepaulensis, commonly known as the Nepal violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves and clusters of delicate, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountain meadows. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola nepaulensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
