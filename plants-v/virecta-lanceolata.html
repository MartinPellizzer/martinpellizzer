Virecta lanceolata

Virecta lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf vireya, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and white. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of tropical forests. Virecta lanceolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
