Vatica imbricata

Vatica imbricata, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and can grow up to 40 meters tall. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic in shape and have a smooth surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release its seeds.
