Viburnum sargentii f. flavum

Viburnum sargentii f. flavum, commonly known as the goldenleaf viburnum, is a flowering shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to yellow, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes make it a valuable addition to landscape designs.
