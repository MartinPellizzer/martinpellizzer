Viburnum erosum var. formosanum

Viburnum erosum var. formosanum, commonly known as the Formosan snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Taiwan.

It features small, white, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
