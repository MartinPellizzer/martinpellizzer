Vernonia kwangolana

Vernonia kwangolana, commonly known as Kwangolana ironwood, is a flowering plant native to Africa, specifically found in countries like Kenya and Tanzania.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is notable for its hard, durable wood, which has been traditionally used for construction and tool-making. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, while its flowers are yellow with prominent central disks.

Vernonia kwangolana thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.
