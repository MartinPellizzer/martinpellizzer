Vernonia pseudonatalensis

Vernonia pseudonatalensis, commonly known as the Natal ironwood, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows in coastal and inland forested areas. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a waxy texture.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
