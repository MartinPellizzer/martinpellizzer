Veronicastrum robustum subsp. grandifolium

Veronicastrum robustum subsp.

grandifolium, commonly known as blue mist spirea, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value comes from its showy inflorescences and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
