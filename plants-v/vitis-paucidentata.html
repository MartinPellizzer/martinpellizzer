Vitis paucidentata

Vitis paucidentata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, wooded areas. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically greenish-white and appear in clusters, followed by small, round berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Vitis paucidentata plays an important role in local ecosystems, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
