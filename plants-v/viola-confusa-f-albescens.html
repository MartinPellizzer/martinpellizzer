Viola confusa f. albescens

Viola confusa f. albescens, commonly known as the white viola, is a hybrid form of the viola confusa species.

It is characterized by its pale lavender to white flowers, which contrast with its dark green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant is a perennial herb native to Europe and is often found in woodland habitats. It produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and blooms in early spring.

The white variant is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and conservation efforts.
