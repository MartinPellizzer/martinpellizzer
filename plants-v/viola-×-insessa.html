Viola × insessa

Viola × insessa, commonly known as the evergreen violet, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both annual and perennial violas.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. Viola × insessa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its hardiness and ability to naturalize.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
