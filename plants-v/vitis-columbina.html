Vitis columbina

Vitis columbina, commonly known as the grape ivy, is a vine native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by edible berries that range in color from green to black. Vitis columbina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to climb structures or trellises.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to various garden environments.
