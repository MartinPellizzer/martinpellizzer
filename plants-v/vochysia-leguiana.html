Vochysia leguiana

Vochysia leguiana, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators. The tree has smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
