Vicia hatschbachii

Vicia hatschbachii, commonly known as the Hatschbach vetch, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale blue or white in color. Vicia hatschbachii is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.

Its ecological role includes nitrogen fixation, which enriches the soil in its native habitat.
