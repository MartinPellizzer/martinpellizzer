Viola violacea

Viola violacea, commonly known as the violet violaceae, is a flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant purple to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Viola violacea is native to parts of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
