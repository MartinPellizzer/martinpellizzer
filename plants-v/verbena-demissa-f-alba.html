Verbena demissa f. alba

Verbena demissa f. alba, commonly known as white verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The flowers are typically white or pale blue, with five petals, and bloom throughout the summer months. The plant produces small, oblong seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
