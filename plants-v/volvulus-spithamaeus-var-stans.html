Volvulus spithamaeus var. stans

Volvulus spithamaeus var. stans, commonly known as the creeping marshweed, is a perennial herb native to wetland environments.

It features slender, ascending stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in spikes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.
