Vereia acutiflora

Vereia acutiflora, commonly known as the sharp-flowered vereia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
