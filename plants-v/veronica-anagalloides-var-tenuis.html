Veronica anagalloides var. tenuis

Veronica anagalloides var. tenuis, commonly known as narrowleaf speedwell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has slender, spreading stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in rocky or open habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and resilient plant suitable for various ecological and ornamental purposes.
