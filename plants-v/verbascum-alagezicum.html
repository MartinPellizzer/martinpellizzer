Verbascum alagezicum

Verbascum alagezicum, commonly known as the Armenian mullein, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a loose raceme. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Verbascum alagezicum is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
