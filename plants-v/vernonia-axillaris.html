Vernonia axillaris

Vernonia axillaris, commonly known as the creeping ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters, which bloom during the dry season. Vernonia axillaris is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils or along riverbanks.

Its resilience and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
