Veronica anagallis-aquatica var. brittonii

Veronica anagallis-aquatica var. brittonii, commonly known as the Britton's water speedwell, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, blue to purple flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy coating.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
