Viola × olimpia

Viola × olimpia, commonly known as the Olympic violet, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Viola lutea and Viola verecunda.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 10–20 cm, featuring heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of purple, blue, or white, depending on the cultivar. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or shaded gardens.

Viola × olimpia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in temperate climates.
