Vouapa acaciifolia

Vouapa acaciifolia, commonly known as the Madagascar acacia, is a shrub native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a woody stem with a rough, fissured bark that provides structural support. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in coastal and dry forest regions.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant in soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
