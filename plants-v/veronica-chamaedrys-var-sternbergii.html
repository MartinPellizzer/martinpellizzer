Veronica chamaedrys var. sternbergii

Veronica chamaedrys var. sternbergii, commonly known as the creeping speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a mat-forming habit with spreading stems and small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and ground covers.

Botanically, it belongs to the Plantaginaceae family, which is characterized by its spurred flowers and specialized pollen structures.
