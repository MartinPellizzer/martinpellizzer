Viola lehmannii

Viola lehmannii, commonly known as Lehmann's violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in countries like Argentina and Chile. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, with five petals arranged in a symmetrical pattern. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Viola lehmannii thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
