Vernonia argyrotrichia

Vernonia argyrotrichia, commonly known as silver-haired ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant produces fluffy, white to grayish hairs on its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
