Vigna unguiculata subvar. typica

Vigna unguiculata subvar. typica, commonly known as cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with stipules. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain one to four seeds. It is well adapted to warm climates and is often cultivated for its nutritious seeds.

Its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen makes it a valuable crop in sustainable agricultural systems.
