Vitex triflora var. floribunda

Vitex triflora var. floribunda, commonly known as the fragrant flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces fragrant, clustered flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for coastal and tropical gardens.

The species is often used in ornamental horticulture for its showy blooms and aromatic foliage.
