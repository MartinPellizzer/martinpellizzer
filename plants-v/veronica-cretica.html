Veronica cretica

Veronica cretica, commonly known as upright speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in small, opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy. Its flowers are blue to violet in color, arranged in spikes, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Veronica cretica thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky habitats.
