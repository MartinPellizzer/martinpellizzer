Viola helioscopia

Viola helioscopia, commonly known as the sun peeper, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, showy flowers with five petals, typically in shades of yellow and purple. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers open in response to sunlight.

Viola helioscopia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
