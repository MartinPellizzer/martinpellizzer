Verbascum luteum

Verbascum luteum, commonly known as the yellow mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a cylindrical shape at the top of the stem. Verbascum luteum is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family, also known as the figwort family.

It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in grasslands and rocky soils.
