Vanda undulata

Vanda undulata, commonly known as the wavy vanda, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, fleshy, and often twisted stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and violet, typically blooming during the summer months. Vanda undulata thrives in humid, warm environments with bright indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Its unique growth habit and striking floral display make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
