Vicia lunata var. grandiflora

Vicia lunata var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial legume characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, showy purple to pink flowers. The plant produces slender, hairy pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing ability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
