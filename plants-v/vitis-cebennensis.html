Vitis cebennensis

Vitis cebennensis, commonly known as the Italian grapevine, is a species of flowering vine native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into clusters of berries, which are typically dark purple when mature. Vitis cebennensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in viticulture.

However, it is less commonly cultivated today compared to other grape species.
