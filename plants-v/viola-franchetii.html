Viola franchetii

Viola franchetii, commonly known as the Franchet's violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola franchetii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
