Viola chelmea

Viola chelmea, commonly known as the purple marsh violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetlands and marshy areas in Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy violet flowers with five petals, typically in shades of purple or blue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Viola chelmea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
