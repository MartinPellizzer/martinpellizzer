Vigna tisserantii

Vigna tisserantii, commonly known as the Tisserant's bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or white, and it produces lens-shaped seeds within its pods.

Vigna tisserantii is valued for its nutritional content and is sometimes cultivated for both food and ornamental purposes.
