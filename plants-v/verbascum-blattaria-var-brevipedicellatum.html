Verbascum blattaria var. brevipedicellatum

Verbascum blattaria var. brevipedicellatum, commonly known as the short-stemmed mullein, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves and tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or dry habitats. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued in herbal medicine for its potential respiratory benefits and is also cultivated as an ornamental plant.
