Vernonia arctioides

Vernonia arctioides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads that resemble mist. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland or swampy areas, preferring partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are upright and often branched.

The flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
