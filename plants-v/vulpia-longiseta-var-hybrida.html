Vulpia longiseta var. hybrida

Vulpia longiseta var. hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid false brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, meadows, and stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets.

This variety is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
