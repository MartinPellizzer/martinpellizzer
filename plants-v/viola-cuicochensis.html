Viola cuicochensis

Viola cuicochensis, commonly known as the Cuicocha violet, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as mountain forests and cloud forests. Its flowers bloom in spring and are pollinated by bees and other insects.

Viola cuicochensis is notable for its ornamental value and its role in the local biodiversity of its native habitat.
