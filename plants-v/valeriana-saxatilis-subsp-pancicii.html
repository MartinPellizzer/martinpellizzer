Valeriana saxatilis subsp. pancicii

Valeriana saxatilis subsp.

pancicii, commonly known as the Pancici's valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows in rocky and limestone-rich habitats, often in open woodland or grassland environments.

The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and often hairy, with small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes. It blooms from June to August, producing small, clustered flowers that are attractive to pollinators.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
