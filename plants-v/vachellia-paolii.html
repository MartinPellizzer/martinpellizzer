Vachellia paolii

Vachellia paolii, commonly known as the Socotra candelabra, is a unique leguminous shrub native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its striking, branched structure with long, thin, spines and small, yellow flowers. The plant has adapted to the arid climate of Socotra with deep roots and a waxy leaf coating to reduce water loss. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and dispersed by animals, contributing to its ecological role in the region.

Vachellia paolii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential in ecological restoration.
