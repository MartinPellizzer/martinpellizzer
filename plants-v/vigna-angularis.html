Vigna angularis

Vigna angularis, commonly known as the cowpea or black eye bean, is a leguminous plant native to Africa and widely cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit, trifoliate leaves, and distinctive pods that contain edible seeds. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate with adequate rainfall. Its flowers are typically white or pale purple, and it is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting subsequent crops.

Vigna angularis is valued both for its nutritional content and its role in agricultural systems.
