Verbascum denudatum

Verbascum denudatum, commonly known as the naked mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, fuzzy leaves that are often covered in soft, woolly hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Verbascum denudatum is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
