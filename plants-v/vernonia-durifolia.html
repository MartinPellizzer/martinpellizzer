Vernonia durifolia

Vernonia durifolia, commonly known as the roughleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, dark blue to purple composite flowers. The plant features rough, hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a strong, somewhat unpleasant odor. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Vernonia durifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
