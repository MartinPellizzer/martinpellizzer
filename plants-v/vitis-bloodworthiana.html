Vitis bloodworthiana

Vitis bloodworthiana, commonly known as the Bloodworth's grape, is a species of flowering vine native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into clusters of small, edible berries. Vitis bloodworthiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique foliage.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.
