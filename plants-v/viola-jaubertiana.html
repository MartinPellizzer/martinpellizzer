Viola jaubertiana

Viola jaubertiana, commonly known as the Jaubert's violet, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces small, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and vibrant violet flowers that bloom in early spring. Its flowers are usually violet or purple, with a distinctive pattern of darker veins.

Viola jaubertiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
