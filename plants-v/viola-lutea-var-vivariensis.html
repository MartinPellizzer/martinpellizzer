Viola lutea var. vivariensis

Viola lutea var. vivariensis, commonly known as the yellow violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, yellow flowers with five petals. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in gardens and natural habitats.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in cool, temperate climates.
