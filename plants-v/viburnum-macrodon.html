Viburnum macrodon

Viburnum macrodon, commonly known as the large-flowered viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white, clustered flowers that appear in late spring. The plant produces dark blue, waxy berries that are an important food source for birds. Viburnum macrodon typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 feet and has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.
