Verbascum boerhavii subsp. conocarpum

Verbascum boerhavii subsp.

conocarpum, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a terminal spike. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
