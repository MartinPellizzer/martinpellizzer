Viola sylvestris var. montana

Viola sylvestris var. montana, commonly known as the mountain violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky soils and open habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet or purple coloration, and its flowers are usually violet or blue-violet with yellow markings. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
