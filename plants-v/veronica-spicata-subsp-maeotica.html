Veronica spicata subsp. maeotica

Veronica spicata subsp.

maeotica, commonly known as the Maurotian speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the steppes of southern Europe and parts of Asia. It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its upright growth habit and spikes of small, blue or violet flowers.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is noted for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
