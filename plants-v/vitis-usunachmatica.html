Vitis usunachmatica

Vitis usunachmatica, commonly known as the Usunachmatic grapevine, is a lesser-known species within the Vitaceae family.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and the production of small, often unremarkable berries. The plant typically thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces tendrils for climbing.

Due to its limited distribution and lack of commercial cultivation, it remains a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.
