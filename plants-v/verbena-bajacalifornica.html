Verbena bajacalifornica

Verbena bajacalifornica, commonly known as desert verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to hot, dry conditions. It has small, narrow leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Verbena bajacalifornica is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
