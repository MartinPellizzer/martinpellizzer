Viola nummulariifolia

Viola nummulariifolia, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a small, low-growing perennial plant native to Japan.

It features heart-shaped, silver-green leaves that are densely packed along its stems. The plant produces delicate violet flowers with five petals, typically in shades of purple or blue. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
