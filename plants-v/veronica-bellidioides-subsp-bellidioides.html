Veronica bellidioides subsp. bellidioides

Veronica bellidioides subsp.

bellidioides, commonly known as the bellidium speedwell, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in dry, sunny habitats.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and arranged in clusters, blooming from late spring to early summer.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
