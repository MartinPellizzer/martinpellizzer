Veronica filifolia

Veronica filifolia, commonly known as the common speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Veronica filifolia is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
