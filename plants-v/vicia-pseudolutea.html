Vicia pseudolutea

Vicia pseudolutea, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with small, trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are typically yellow or yellow-green, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Vicia pseudolutea is a perennial plant that plays a role in soil stabilization and can be used in ecological restoration projects.
