Vernonia kayuniana

Vernonia kayuniana, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its dense, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent.

Vernonia kayuniana is adapted to dry and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species often found in savannas and woodland areas.
