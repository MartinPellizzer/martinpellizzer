Vicia sativa var. sallei

Vicia sativa var. sallei, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a leguminous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three small, heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in meadows.

Vicia sativa var. sallei is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil enrichment and as a forage crop.
