Vernonia polyura

Vernonia polyura, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, woodland areas. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. Its botanical name reflects its historical use in traditional medicine.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
