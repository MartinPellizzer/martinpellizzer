Vaccinium luzoniense

Vaccinium luzoniense, commonly known as the Luzon huckleberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes blueberries and cranberries, and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its berries, which are typically red or black when mature, are edible and used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.

Vaccinium luzoniense thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.
