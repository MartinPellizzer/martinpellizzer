Verbascum foemina

Verbascum foemina, commonly known as the female mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides. Its leaves are broad and woolly, and the flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Verbascum foemina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically for its medicinal properties.
