Verbascum insigne

Verbascum insigne, commonly known as the noble mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in soft, velvety hairs. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are large, lance-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine, fuzzy hairs.

Verbascum insigne is often found in dry, rocky soils and is known for its medicinal and ornamental uses.
