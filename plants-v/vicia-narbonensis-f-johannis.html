Vicia narbonensis f. johannis

Vicia narbonensis f. johannis, commonly known as the Johann's vetch, is a variant of the common vetch, characterized by its distinctive floral morphology.

This plant is a perennial legume that typically grows in temperate regions, often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Its flowers are usually violet or purple, arranged in racemose inflorescences, and it produces small, three-lobed pods that contain several seeds. The plant is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

It is also valued in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
