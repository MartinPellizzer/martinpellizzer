Vinca minor var. argenteovariegata

Vinca minor var. argenteovariegata, commonly known as silverleaf periwinkle, is a low-growing evergreen groundcover plant native to Europe.

It features glossy, dark green leaves with striking silver-white variegation, enhancing its ornamental value in gardens. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers in the spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in partial to full shade and is drought-tolerant once established, making it ideal for woodland gardens or shaded borders.

Its dense growth habit helps suppress weeds and stabilize soil, contributing to its popularity in landscaping.
