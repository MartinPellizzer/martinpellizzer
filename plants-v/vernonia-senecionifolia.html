Vernonia senecionifolia

Vernonia senecionifolia, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and small, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture.

Vernonia senecionifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally by Indigenous peoples for various purposes.
