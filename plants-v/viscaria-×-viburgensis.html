Viscaria × viburgensis

Viscaria × viburgensis, commonly known as the hybrid carnation, is a flowering plant belonging to the genus Viscaria in the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Viscaria vulgaris and Viscaria × viburgensis, characterized by its vibrant and showy flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of small, brightly colored petals that range from pink to red, often with a white or pale center. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Viscaria × viburgensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
