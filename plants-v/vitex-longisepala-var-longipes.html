Vitex longisepala var. longipes

Vitex longisepala var. longipes, commonly known as the long-sepaled chaste tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually pubescent on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically lavender to purple in color, blooming in the summer months. Its fruit is a dry, woody capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

This species is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental foliage and traditional medicinal uses.
