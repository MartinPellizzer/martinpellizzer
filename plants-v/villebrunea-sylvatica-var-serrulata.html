Villebrunea sylvatica var. serrulata

Villebrunea sylvatica var. serrulata, commonly known as the serrated sylvatica vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural collections.
