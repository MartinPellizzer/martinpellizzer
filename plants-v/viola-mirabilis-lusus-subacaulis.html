Viola mirabilis lusus subacaulis

Viola mirabilis lusus subacaulis, commonly known as the slender violet, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of Europe.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive dark green hue and a velvety texture. The plant produces small, showy purple flowers with intricate patterns, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and protected species.
