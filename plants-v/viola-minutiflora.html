Viola minutiflora

Viola minutiflora, commonly known as the dwarf violet, is a small flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows to a height of only a few centimeters, with delicate, heart-shaped leaves and tiny, violet-blue flowers. The plant prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in woodlands or rocky habitats. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it is known for its ability to thrive in shaded conditions.

Viola minutiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
