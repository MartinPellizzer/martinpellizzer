Vatica vinosa

Vatica vinosa, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by winged fruits.

Vatica vinosa is often found in lowland rainforests and is known for its ecological and economic significance.
