Valeriana trichomanes

Valeriana trichomanes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in well-drained, moist soils, often found in forested or rocky habitats.

Valeriana trichomanes is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its calming effects.
