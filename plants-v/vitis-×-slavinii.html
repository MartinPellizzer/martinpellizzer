Vitis × slavinii

Vitis × slavinii, commonly known as the hybrid grapevine, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Vitis vinifera and Vitis riparia.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and adaptability to various soil types, making it a resilient plant in diverse environments. The plant produces small, often clustered berries that can vary in color from green to red. Its botanical features include compound leaves with three to five leaflets and tendrils used for climbing.

Vitis × slavinii is primarily cultivated for its potential in breeding programs aimed at improving disease resistance and climate adaptability in grapevines.
