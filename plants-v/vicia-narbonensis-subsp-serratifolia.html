Vicia narbonensis subsp. serratifolia

Vicia narbonensis subsp.

serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers.

The plant produces slender, cylindrical pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and historical use in traditional agriculture.
