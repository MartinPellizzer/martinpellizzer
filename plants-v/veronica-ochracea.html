Veronica ochracea

Veronica ochracea, commonly known as the yellow speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

Veronica ochracea is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
