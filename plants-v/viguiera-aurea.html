Viguiera aurea

Viguiera aurea, commonly known as golden coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its showy yellow blooms appear in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viguiera aurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in arid landscapes.
