Viola ovato-oblonga var. obtusa

Viola ovato-oblonga var. obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leaved violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of East Asia.

It features heart-shaped to ovate-lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive dark green color. The plant produces violet flowers with purple petals that are typically borne on slender stems. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native ornamental plants.
