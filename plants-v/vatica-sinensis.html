Vatica sinensis

Vatica sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Vatica sinensis is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ecological and commercial significance.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both conservation and forestry efforts.
