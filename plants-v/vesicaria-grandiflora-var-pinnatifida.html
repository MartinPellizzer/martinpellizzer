Vesicaria grandiflora var. pinnatifida

Vesicaria grandiflora var. pinnatifida, commonly known as the broad-leaved bladderwort, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its pinnately divided leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is typically found in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or wetlands. It has a shallow root system and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its delicate appearance.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
