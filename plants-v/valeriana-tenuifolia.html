Valeriana tenuifolia

Valeriana tenuifolia, commonly known as slender valerian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has aromatic, hairy leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Valeriana tenuifolia is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its medicinal properties.
