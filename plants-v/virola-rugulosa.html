Virola rugulosa

Virola rugulosa, commonly known as the rough-leaved virola, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves with a rough texture. The tree produces fragrant, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. Its fruits are large, woody capsules that split open to release seeds.

Virola rugulosa is valued for its wood, which is used in traditional crafts and as a source of incense.
