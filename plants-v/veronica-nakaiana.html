Veronica nakaiana

Veronica nakaiana, commonly known as the Mauna Kea veronica, is a flowering plant native to Hawaii.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soil. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in high-altitude environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
