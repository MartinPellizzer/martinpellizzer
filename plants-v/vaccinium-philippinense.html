Vaccinium philippinense

Vaccinium philippinense, commonly known as the Philippine huckleberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or purple in color. It thrives in acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

The leaves are leathery and elliptical, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.
