Verbesina potosina

Verbesina potosina, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its bright orange to yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, hairy, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners in arid climates.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
