Vaccinium lanceolatum f. ellipticum

Vaccinium lanceolatum f. ellipticum, commonly known as the elliptic blueberry, is a variant of the highbush blueberry.

It is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, followed by edible, blue-black berries. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This form is valued for its ornamental qualities and fruit production in suitable climates.
