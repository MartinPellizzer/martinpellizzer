Verbena doniana

Verbena doniana, commonly known as the African verbena, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its woody stem and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptical in shape and have a slightly hairy texture.

Verbena doniana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
