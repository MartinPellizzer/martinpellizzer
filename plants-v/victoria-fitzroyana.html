Victoria fitzroyana

Victoria fitzroyana, commonly known as the Fitzroy water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface. The plant produces pale yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its roots are anchored in the muddy substrate, allowing it to thrive in still or slow-moving water.

Victoria fitzroyana plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various water organisms.
