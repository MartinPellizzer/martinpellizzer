Vincetoxicum propinquum

Vincetoxicum propinquum, commonly known as the American cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, wooded areas and along streambanks. The plant features showy, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and the stems are often covered in fine hairs.

Vincetoxicum propinquum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
