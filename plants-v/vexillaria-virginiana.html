Vexillaria virginiana

Vexillaria virginiana, commonly known as the Virginia flag.

It is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States, characterized by its distinctive red, flag-like flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist, sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are often prostrate or trailing.

Vexillaria virginiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
