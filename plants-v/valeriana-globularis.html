Valeriana globularis

Valeriana globularis, commonly known as globular valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and is often found in moist, woodland habitats. It is part of the Valerianaceae family and is known for its aromatic foliage.

Valeriana globularis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
