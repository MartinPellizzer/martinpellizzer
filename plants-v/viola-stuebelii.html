Viola stuebelii

Viola stuebelii, commonly known as the Stuebel's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the forest floor.

Viola stuebelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
