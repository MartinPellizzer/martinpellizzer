Verticordia subulata

Verticordia subulata, commonly known as the narrowleaf featherflower, is an evergreen shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained sandy soils and full sun, making it well-suited to arid and coastal environments. Its unique floral structure and vibrant color make it a distinctive addition to native gardens.

It is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
