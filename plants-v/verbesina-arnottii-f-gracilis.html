Verbesina arnottii f. gracilis

Verbesina arnottii f. gracilis, commonly known as the slender ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, elongated fruits called achenes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
