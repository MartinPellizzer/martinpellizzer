Viola dactyloides

Viola dactyloides, commonly known as the common violet, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Viola dactyloides is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
