Vangueria verdickii

Vangueria verdickii, commonly known as the red-flowered vangueria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often blooming in spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with prominent veins.

Vangueria verdickii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant in regions with low rainfall.
