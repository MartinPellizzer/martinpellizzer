Veatchia discolor var. pubescens

Veatchia discolor var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy veatchia, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The leaves are often covered in a fine layer of hair, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
