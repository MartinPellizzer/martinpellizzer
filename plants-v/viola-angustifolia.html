Viola angustifolia

Viola angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple or violet. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in cool, temperate climates.

Viola angustifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
