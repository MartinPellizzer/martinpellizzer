Viola chamissoniana subsp. chamissoniana

Viola chamissoniana subsp.

chamissoniana, commonly known as the Chamisson's violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
