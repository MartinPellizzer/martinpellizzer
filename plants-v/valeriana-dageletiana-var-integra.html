Valeriana dageletiana var. integra

Valeriana dageletiana var. integra, commonly known as the Dageletian valerian, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Caucasus region.

It features a robust rhizomatous root system and lanceolate, serrated leaves that are typically dark green and arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in terminal panicles, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
