Veslingia cauliflora

Veslingia cauliflora, commonly known as the cauliflower orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, cauliflower-like inflorescence composed of numerous small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is known for its ability to produce flowers directly on its stem, a phenomenon called cauliflorous growth. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

Veslingia cauliflora is typically found in humid, shaded environments and is prized for its ornamental value in botanical gardens.
