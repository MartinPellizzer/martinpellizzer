Vicia angustifolia var. ramstadina

Vicia angustifolia var. ramstadina, commonly known as the narrow-leaf vetch, is a perennial legume native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, trifoliate leaves with a distinctive narrow shape and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and soil stabilization.
