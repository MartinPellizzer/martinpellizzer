Verbena rhinanthifolia

Verbena rhinanthifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved verbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, bright blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture.

Verbena rhinanthifolia is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
