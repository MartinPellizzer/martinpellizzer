Vanda pseudocaerulescens

Vanda pseudocaerulescens, commonly known as the blue vanda, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a contrasting white or yellow throat. The plant has long, arching stems and broad, leathery leaves that help it thrive in humid, shaded environments. It requires bright indirect light and consistent moisture to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
