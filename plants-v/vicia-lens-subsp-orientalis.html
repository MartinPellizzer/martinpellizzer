Vicia lens subsp. orientalis

Vicia lens subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the oriental field bean, is a leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant produces lens-shaped pods containing several seeds, which are used in traditional agriculture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides.

This subspecies is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in crop rotation systems.
