Viola canina var. frustranea

Viola canina var. frustranea, commonly known as the Italian violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces violet-blue flowers with yellow markings. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in calcareous soils.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
