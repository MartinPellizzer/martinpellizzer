Verbena alpigena

Verbena alpigena, commonly known as alpine verbena, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically blue or violet in color and bloom from late spring to early summer. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
