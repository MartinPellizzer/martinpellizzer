Vesta longifolia

Vesta longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf vesta, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its root system is fibrous and helps it thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Vesta longifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
