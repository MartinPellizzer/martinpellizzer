Vriesea maculata

Vriesea maculata, commonly known as the spotted Vriesea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves with distinctive spots. The plant produces showy inflorescences that resemble a flower spike, often with red, orange, or yellow hues. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Vriesea maculata is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
