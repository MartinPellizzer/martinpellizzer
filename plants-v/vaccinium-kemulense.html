Vaccinium kemulense

Vaccinium kemulense, commonly known as the Kew mountain blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a glossy appearance.

Vaccinium kemulense thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude forests.
