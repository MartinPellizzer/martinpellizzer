Valeriana decussata var. normalis

Valeriana decussata var. normalis, commonly known as the common valerian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, covered with small, opposite leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its roots are known for their medicinal properties, containing compounds such as valerenic acid.

Valeriana decussata var. normalis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
