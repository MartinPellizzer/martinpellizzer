Veronica × myriantha

Veronica × myriantha, commonly known as the common everlasting, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom throughout the summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in floral arrangements.
