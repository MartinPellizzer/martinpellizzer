Vaccinium subfalcatum

Vaccinium subfalcatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a dark green upper surface with a lighter underside.

The small, bell-shaped flowers produce edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color.
