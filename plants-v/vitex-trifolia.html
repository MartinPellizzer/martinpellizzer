Vitex trifolia

Vitex trifolia, commonly known as the three-leaved chaste tree, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its opposite, trifoliate leaves that consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant, blue or purple flowers in summer, which are followed by distinctive, woody, three-lobed fruits. Vitex trifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.
