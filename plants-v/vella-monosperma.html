Vella monosperma

Vella monosperma, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by dark blue to black berries. Vella monosperma is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.

It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.
