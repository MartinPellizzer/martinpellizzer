Vicia sativa var. cordata

Vicia sativa var. cordata, commonly known as heartleaf vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, trailing stems that produce small, pea-like flowers in shades of purple or pink. The leaves are distinctive, with heart-shaped leaflets that give the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

This plant is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment and ecological restoration projects.
