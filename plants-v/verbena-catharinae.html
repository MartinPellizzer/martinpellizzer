Verbena catharinae

Verbena catharinae, commonly known as the Canary Islands verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually purple or violet in color. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is adapted to thrive in sunny, well-drained soils.

Verbena catharinae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
