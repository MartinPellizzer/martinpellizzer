Vochysia revoluta

Vochysia revoluta, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red velvetwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, round fruits that resemble small cherries. Vochysia revoluta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
