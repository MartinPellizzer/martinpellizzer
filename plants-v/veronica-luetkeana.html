Veronica luetkeana

Veronica luetkeana, commonly known as the silver leaf speedwell, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features silver-gray, woolly leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
