Viola steinbachii

Viola steinbachii, commonly known as the Steinbach violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the forest floor.

Viola steinbachii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
