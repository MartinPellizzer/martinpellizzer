Verbena officinalis f. microphylla

Verbena officinalis f. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf vervain, is a compact, herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and spikes of bright blue or purple flowers that bloom from summer to autumn. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and anti-inflammatory properties.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, visually appealing plants.
