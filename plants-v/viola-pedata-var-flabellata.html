Viola pedata var. flabellata

Viola pedata var. flabellata, commonly known as the broad-leaf violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 inches, featuring heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a distinctive wavy margin. The flowers are violet-blue in color, with five petals and a spurred lower petal that resembles a small bell. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
