Viola leucantha

Viola leucantha, commonly known as the white violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves and delicate, white or pale violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady woodlands and thrives in well-drained soil. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola leucantha is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
