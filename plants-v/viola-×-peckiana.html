Viola × peckiana

Viola × peckiana, commonly known as the peck's violet, is a hybrid species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States, where it thrives in woodland and shaded areas. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of fragrant, violet-colored flowers. It is typically found in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Viola × peckiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
