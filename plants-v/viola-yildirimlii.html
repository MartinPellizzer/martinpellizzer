Viola yildirimlii

Viola yildirimlii, commonly known as the Turkish violet, is a species native to Turkey and parts of the Balkans.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial with heart-shaped leaves and distinctive violet flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing small, showy flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Viola yildirimlii prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for both gardeners and botanists.
