Valeriana acutiloba

Valeriana acutiloba, commonly known as sharp-leaved valerian, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its aromatic roots are often used in traditional medicine for their sedative properties.

Valeriana acutiloba thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
