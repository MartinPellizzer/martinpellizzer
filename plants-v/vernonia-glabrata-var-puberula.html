Vernonia glabrata var. puberula

Vernonia glabrata var. puberula, commonly known as the rough vernonia, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, rough-textured leaves and conical, spiky flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in sandy or clay soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to native plant gardens and ecological landscapes.
