Vernonia saltuarii

Vernonia saltuarii, commonly known as the saltbush vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its gray-green, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Vernonia saltuarii is often found growing in sandy or rocky soils, demonstrating its adaptability to poor, dry conditions.

Its resilience makes it a valuable species for ecological restoration in degraded landscapes.
