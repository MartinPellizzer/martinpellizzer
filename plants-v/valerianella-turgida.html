Valerianella turgida

Valerianella turgida, commonly known as water pepper, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and it produces tiny, pale pink to white flowers arranged in clusters. The plant thrives in moist environments such as wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. Its root system is fibrous, and it is often found in areas with high water content.

Valerianella turgida is known for its ability to tolerate wet conditions, making it a common component of riparian vegetation.
