Vicia sabinarum

Vicia sabinarum, commonly known as the Spanish vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant produces cylindrical pods that contain several kidney-shaped seeds. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Vicia sabinarum is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for ecological restoration projects.
