Viscum lophiocladum

Viscum lophiocladum, commonly known as the Chinese mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of various host trees.

It belongs to the Viscaceae family and is native to regions of East Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers and red berries that contain toxic seeds. Its leaves are usually small, oval, and have a leathery texture.

Viscum lophiocladum plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for certain birds and insects.
