Vachellia sutherlandii

Vachellia sutherlandii, commonly known as the Sutherland's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorned stems and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating drought tolerance and adaptability to poor soils.

Vachellia sutherlandii plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
