Verbena × covasii

Verbena × covasii, commonly known as the hybrid verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a cross between Verbena rigida and Verbena bonariensis, resulting in a plant with distinctive characteristics. The species is known for its vibrant purple or blue flowers that bloom in spikes, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Verbena × covasii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
