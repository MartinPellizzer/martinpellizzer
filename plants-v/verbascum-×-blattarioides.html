Verbascum × blattarioides

Verbascum × blattarioides, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the genus Verbascum in the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are covered in soft hairs. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers in the summer, which are clustered in dense, terminal spikes. It is native to regions of southern Europe and is often found in dry, rocky or sandy soils.

Verbascum × blattarioides is cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
