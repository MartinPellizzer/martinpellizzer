Vaccinium irigaense

Vaccinium irigaense, commonly known as the Iriya huckleberry, is a species of shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark blue or black in color. These berries are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.

Vaccinium irigaense thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is typically found in forested areas.
