Viola pseudojaponica f. viridescens

Viola pseudojaponica f. viridescens, commonly known as the green-flowered viola, is a horticultural form of the viola genus characterized by its distinctive greenish-white flowers.

This variant exhibits a unique coloration that sets it apart from the typical purple or violet hues of the species. It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in woodland or shaded garden settings. The foliage is typically heart-shaped with a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its attractive appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
