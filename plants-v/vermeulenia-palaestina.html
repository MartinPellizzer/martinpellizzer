Vermeulenia palaestina

Vermeulenia palaestina, commonly known as the Palestine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and branching.

Vermeulenia palaestina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
