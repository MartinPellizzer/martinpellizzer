Veronica thymoides subsp. pseudocinerea

Veronica thymoides subsp.

pseudocinerea, commonly known as the silver veronica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in dry, sunny environments.

The plant features slender, branching stems covered in dense, silver-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, blue or violet flowers are arranged in spikes and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
