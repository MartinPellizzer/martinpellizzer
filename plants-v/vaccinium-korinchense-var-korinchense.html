Vaccinium korinchense var. korinchense

Vaccinium korinchense var. korinchense, commonly known as the Korinchense blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It produces small, tart berries that are typically harvested in the fall. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a glossy green surface and turn reddish in the fall.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits, making it a popular choice for gardens and small-scale cultivation.
