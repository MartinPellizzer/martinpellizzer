Viburnum aragonense

Viburnum aragonense, commonly known as the Aragon honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
