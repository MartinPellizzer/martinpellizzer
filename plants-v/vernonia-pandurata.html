Vernonia pandurata

Vernonia pandurata, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring upright stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, slightly hairy texture.

Vernonia pandurata is known for its hardy nature and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
