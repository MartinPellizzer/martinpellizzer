Viola paillouxii

Viola paillouxii, commonly known as the Pailloux violet, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern France.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and pale violet to white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens due to its unique appearance.

Due to habitat loss, Viola paillouxii is considered endangered and is protected in several regions.
