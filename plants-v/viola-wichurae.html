Viola wichurae

Viola wichurae, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Viola wichurae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
