Vallisneria triptera

Vallisneria triptera, commonly known as narrow-leaf waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Asia.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by the development of cylindrical, spongy fruits. Vallisneria triptera plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

It is widely used in aquariums and ponds due to its aesthetic appeal and water-cleaning properties.
