Viola japonica var. subaequiloba

Viola japonica var. subaequiloba, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces delicate, violet-blue flowers with five petals, often with a distinctive yellow or white marking at the base. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
