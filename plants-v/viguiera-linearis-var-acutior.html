Viguiera linearis var. acutior

Viguiera linearis var. acutior, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central and southern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 2 to 3 feet tall. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of purple to pink, which bloom from late summer into early fall. Its leaves are linear and pointed, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.
