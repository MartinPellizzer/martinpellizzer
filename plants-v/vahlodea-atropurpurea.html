Vahlodea atropurpurea

Vahlodea atropurpurea, commonly known as purple vahlodea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its striking purple to reddish hues on its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Vahlodea atropurpurea is also noted for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom throughout the summer season.
