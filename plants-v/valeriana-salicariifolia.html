Valeriana salicariifolia

Valeriana salicariifolia, commonly known as water valerian, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in moist or aquatic environments, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth texture and emit a faint, aromatic scent.

Valeriana salicariifolia plays an important role in supporting local biodiversity by providing habitat and nectar for various insects.
