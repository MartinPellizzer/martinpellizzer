Viola × popovae

Viola × popovae, commonly known as the Popov's violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly Kazakhstan and southern Siberia. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm, featuring heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive yellow throat. It thrives in well-drained soils and cooler climates, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is more commonly encountered in its natural wild state.
