Vigna sandwicensis

Vigna sandwicensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian bean, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a small, herbaceous vine that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pods containing edible seeds. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability.

Vigna sandwicensis is of cultural and ecological significance in Hawaii, often found in native forests and used in traditional practices.
