Viola calaminaria var. westfalica

Viola calaminaria var. westfalica, commonly known as the Westfalica Violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, violet-blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in less fertile soils.
