Vernonella kirkii

Vernonella kirkii, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. Vernonella kirkii is known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat various ailments.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.
