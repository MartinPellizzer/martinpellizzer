Veronica hispanica

Veronica hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, blue or violet flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
