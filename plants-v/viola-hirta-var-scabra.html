Viola hirta var. scabra

Viola hirta var. scabra, commonly known as the rough violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a rough texture. Its flowers are usually violet or purple, with five petals and a distinctive spur at the base.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
