Vaccinium erythrocarpum

Vaccinium erythrocarpum, commonly known as the red-fruited heath, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in moist, shady environments. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are usually pink or white, and it produces bright red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
