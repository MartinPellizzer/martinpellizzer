Vernonia ararana

Vernonia ararana, commonly known as the red vervain, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in open woodlands and prairies.

Vernonia ararana is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
