Vignea siccata

Vignea siccata, commonly known as the desert bean, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant is adapted to survive in dry environments, with deep roots that allow it to access groundwater. Its leaves are typically narrow and hairy, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Vignea siccata plays a role in stabilizing soil in desert ecosystems and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
