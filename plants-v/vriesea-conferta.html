Vriesea conferta

Vriesea conferta, commonly known as the velvet leaf bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its broad, dark green leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces striking inflorescences with brightly colored bracts, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which can last for several months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

Vriesea conferta is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
