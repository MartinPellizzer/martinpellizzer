Vitex parviflora var. puberulenta

Vitex parviflora var. puberulenta, commonly known as the silverleaf vitex, is a shrub native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in coastal or semi-arid environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
