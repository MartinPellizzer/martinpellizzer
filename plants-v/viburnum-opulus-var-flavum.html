Viburnum opulus var. flavum

Viburnum opulus var. flavum, commonly known as the yellow arrowwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered white flowers that appear in late spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Viburnum opulus var. flavum is often used in gardens for its seasonal interest and hardy nature.
