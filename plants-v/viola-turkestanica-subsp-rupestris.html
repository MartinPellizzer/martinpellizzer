Viola turkestanica subsp. rupestris

Viola turkestanica subsp.

rupestris, commonly known as the rock viol, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or limestone habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces violet or purple flowers during the spring and early summer. It is often found in alpine meadows and mountainous regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Violaceae family.
