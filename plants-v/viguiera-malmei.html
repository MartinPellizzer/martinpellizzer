Viguiera malmei

Viguiera malmei, commonly known as Mexican ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, purple to pinkish flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces seeds in fluffy, white pappus structures. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a drought-tolerant species suitable for xeriscaping.

Viguiera malmei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
