Viola agrestis

Viola agrestis, commonly known as the field violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

It typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks, preferring partial to full shade. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet or purple coloration, and it produces clusters of small, showy flowers with five petals. Viola agrestis is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to mild frosts.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
