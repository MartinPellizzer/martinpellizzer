Vernonia regis

Vernonia regis, commonly known as the royal ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. Its stems are sturdy and woody, which contribute to its hardy nature.

Vernonia regis is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
