Vigna vexillata var. hirta

Vigna vexillata var. hirta, commonly known as the hairy cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, pale green to yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, cylindrical pods. The seeds are typically small, oval, and can vary in color from white to brown.

It is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are used in various cuisines around the world.
