Vaccinium dendrophilum

Vaccinium dendrophilum, commonly known as the tree cranberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and azaleas. The plant is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and produces clusters of bright red berries that are edible. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The berries are rich in antioxidants and are used both in culinary applications and traditional medicine.
