Verbena hatschbachii

Verbena hatschbachii, commonly known as the Mexican verbena, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Verbena hatschbachii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
