Vitis selloana var. glabra

Vitis selloana var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth grapevine, is a member of the Vitaceae family.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves, which distinguish it from other grapevine varieties. The plant is native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, and thrives in temperate climates. It produces small, clustered berries that are typically green when mature.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in some traditional herbal practices.
