Verbascum kochiiforme

Verbascum kochiiforme, commonly known as the Kashmir mullein, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often covered in soft, woolly hairs that help reduce water loss.

Verbascum kochiiforme is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
