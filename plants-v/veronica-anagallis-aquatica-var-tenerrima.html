Veronica anagallis-aquatica var. tenerrima

Veronica anagallis-aquatica var. tenerrima, commonly known as the water speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems and small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist or wet environments such as marshes, stream banks, and damp woodlands. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly wavy margin.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions.
