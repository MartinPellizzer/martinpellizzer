Vulpia hackelii

Vulpia hackelii, commonly known as the common vulpia, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The leaves are narrow and typically have a distinct midrib, while the flowers are wind-pollinated and arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands, roadsides, and agricultural fields.

Vulpia hackelii is adapted to temperate climates and is relatively drought-tolerant.
