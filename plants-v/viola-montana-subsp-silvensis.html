Viola montana subsp. silvensis

Viola montana subsp.

silvensis, commonly known as the Alpine violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, showy flowers in shades of violet and blue. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and partial shade.
