Veronica velutina

Veronica velutina, commonly known as velvet veronica, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Veronica velutina is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
