Verbascum daenzeri

Verbascum daenzeri, commonly known as the Turkish mullein, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Verbascum daenzeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
