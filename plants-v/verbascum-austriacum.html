Verbascum austriacum

Verbascum austriacum, commonly known as the Austrian mullein, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, woolly leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry meadows and rocky areas.

Verbascum austriacum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
