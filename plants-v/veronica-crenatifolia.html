Veronica crenatifolia

Veronica crenatifolia, commonly known as the scalycup veronica, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of oval, waxy leaves with a scalloped edge. The plant produces clusters of blue or purple flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Veronica crenatifolia is often grown as an ornamental plant in gardens for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
