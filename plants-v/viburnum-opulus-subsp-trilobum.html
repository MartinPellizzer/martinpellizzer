Viburnum opulus subsp. trilobum

Viburnum opulus subsp.

trilobum, commonly known as three-lobed arrowwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America. It features compound leaves with three distinct lobes, giving it its common name, and produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring.

The plant is characterized by its showy, red berries that persist into winter, providing food for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The bark and leaves contain compounds that have been historically used in traditional medicine.
