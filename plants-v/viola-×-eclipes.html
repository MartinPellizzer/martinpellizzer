Viola × eclipes

Viola × eclipes, commonly known as the violet shield.

This hybrid plant is a result of crossbreeding between different viola species, leading to a diverse range of cultivars with varying flower colors and growth habits. It typically features heart-shaped leaves and showy, often violet or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to Europe and has been widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile choice for gardeners.
