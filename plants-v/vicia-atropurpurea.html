Vicia atropurpurea

Vicia atropurpurea, commonly known as purple vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of purple or violet pea-like flowers. The plant has a robust root system that helps prevent soil erosion. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Vicia atropurpurea is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in agricultural and ecological restoration settings.
