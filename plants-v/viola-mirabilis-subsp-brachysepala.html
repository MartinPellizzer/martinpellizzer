Viola mirabilis subsp. brachysepala

Viola mirabilis subsp.

brachysepala, commonly known as the short-sepaled violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-pink flowers that bloom in spring.

The subspecies is distinguished by its shorter sepals compared to other viola species, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
