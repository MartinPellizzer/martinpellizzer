Vernonia poissonii

Vernonia poissonii, commonly known as the Poisson's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and branched.

Vernonia poissonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
