Vitis Aestivalis Var. Bicolor

Bicolor grape, scientifically known as Vitis aestivalis var. bicolor, is a unique variety of native North American vine prized for its striking two-toned appearance. This plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas where it clings to trees and shrubs. Its leaves turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall, adding visual interest to the landscape. The small, sweet berries are not only edible but also attract a variety of wildlife. Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it has become a popular choice for gardeners and conservationists alike.
