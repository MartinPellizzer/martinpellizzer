Veronica alliaria

Veronica alliaria, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, blue or purple flowers and hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its foliage is edible and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
