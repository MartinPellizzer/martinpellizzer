Viola dactylifera

Viola dactylifera, commonly known as the Persian viola, is a species of flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces violet-blue flowers with yellow centers, typically blooming in spring. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and is often found in rocky or dry soils. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
