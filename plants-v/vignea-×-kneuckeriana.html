Vignea × kneuckeriana

Vignea × kneuckeriana, commonly known as the Kneucker's morning glory, is a hybrid species within the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in the early morning. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a distinctive wavy pattern on the petals. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while the vines can grow up to several meters in length.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
