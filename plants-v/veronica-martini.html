Veronica martini

Veronica martini, commonly known as the blueweed or sea veronica, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Veronica martini thrives in coastal areas and sandy soils, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
