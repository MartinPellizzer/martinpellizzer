Verbascum valerianifolium

Verbascum valerianifolium, commonly known as the Valerian-leaved mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves and tall spikes of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in Mediterranean climates and is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.

Its common name reflects the resemblance of its leaves to those of the Valerian plant.
