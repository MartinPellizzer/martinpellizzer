Vincetoxicum parviflorum

Vincetoxicum parviflorum, commonly known as smallflowered ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features upright stems covered in fine, hairy hairs, and it produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, wet meadows, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Vincetoxicum parviflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
