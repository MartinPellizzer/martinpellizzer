Valeriana gracilis

Valeriana gracilis, commonly known as slender valerian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and it produces small, round fruits known as schizocarps.

Valeriana gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential calming properties.
