Viola hancockii var. fangshanensis

Viola hancockii var. fangshanensis, commonly known as the Fangshan viola, is a rare and striking member of the Violaceae family native to China.

This variant is characterized by its distinctive violet-blue petals with intricate patterns and a spurred lip, which are adaptations for attracting specific pollinators. It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in mountainous regions, showcasing its preference for harsh, alpine environments. The plant produces small, heart-shaped leaves and blooms in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
