Vicia americana var. sinensis

Vicia americana var. sinensis, commonly known as Chinese vetch, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces slender, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated as a forage crop due to its high nutritional value for livestock.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil health.
