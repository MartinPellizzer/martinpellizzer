Virgularia mansonii

Virgularia mansonii, commonly known as the Florida morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetlands and swampy areas. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually blue or purple in color. It blooms from spring to early summer and is often found in moist, sunny habitats.

The species is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in waterlogged soils.
