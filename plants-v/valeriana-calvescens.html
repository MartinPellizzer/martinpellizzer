Valeriana calvescens

Valeriana calvescens, commonly known as sweet valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer. Its roots are rich in essential oils and have been traditionally used in herbal medicine.

Valeriana calvescens thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight.
