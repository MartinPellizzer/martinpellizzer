Viola circaeoides

Viola circaeoides, commonly known as the common violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of delicate, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-30 centimeters and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola circaeoides is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and along streambanks.
