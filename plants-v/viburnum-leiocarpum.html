Viburnum leiocarpum

Viburnum leiocarpum, commonly known as the Chinese snowball.

It is a deciduous shrub native to China, characterized by its clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces dark blue, waxy berries that are edible for birds but generally not for humans. Viburnum leiocarpum typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters and prefers well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
