Vernonia digitata

Vernonia digitata, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are opposite in arrangement, and its flowers are typically clustered in terminal panicles. Vernonia digitata is known for its hard, heavy wood, which is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay.

It also has traditional medicinal uses in various African cultures.
