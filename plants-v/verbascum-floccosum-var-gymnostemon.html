Verbascum floccosum var. gymnostemon

Verbascum floccosum var. gymnostemon, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are covered in soft, woolly hairs. The plant produces tall spikes of yellow flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its distinctive appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
