Valeriana officinalis var. mikanii

Valeriana officinalis var. mikanii, commonly known as Japanese valerian, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features upright stems with opposite, ovate leaves that have a distinct, slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters called cymes. Its roots are the primary part used for medicinal purposes, containing essential oils and iridoids.

It is often cultivated for its aromatic properties and traditional use in herbal medicine.
