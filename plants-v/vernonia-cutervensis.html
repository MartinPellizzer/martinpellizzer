Vernonia cutervensis

Vernonia cutervensis, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom from late winter to early spring. Vernonia cutervensis is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
