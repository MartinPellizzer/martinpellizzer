Varasia dshimilensis

Varasia dshimilensis, commonly known as the Dshimilis mountain bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue and violet. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental beauty.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
