Verbena echegarayi

Verbena echegarayi, commonly known as the Echegaray's verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flowers. The plant typically produces small, tubular flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance contribute to its appeal in both garden settings and ecological restoration projects.
