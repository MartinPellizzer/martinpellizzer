Veronica rudolphiana

Veronica rudolphiana, commonly known as the Rudolph's speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine pubescence, while its flowers are small and arranged in racemes.

Veronica rudolphiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
