Villadia painteri

Villadia painteri, commonly known as the painter's violet, is a rare flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet petals that resemble brushstrokes, giving it its common name. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and emits a subtle fragrance during the evening. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and limited habitat, it is considered a valuable species in botanical research and conservation efforts.
