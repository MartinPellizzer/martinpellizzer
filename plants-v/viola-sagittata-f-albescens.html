Viola sagittata f. albescens

Viola sagittata f. albescens, commonly known as the white-veined violet, is a rare and attractive variant of the common violet.

It is characterized by its pale green to white foliage, which contrasts with its violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, spurred flowers that bloom in spring.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
