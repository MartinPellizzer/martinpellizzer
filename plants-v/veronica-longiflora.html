Veronica longiflora

Veronica longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and produces small, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas.

Veronica longiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
