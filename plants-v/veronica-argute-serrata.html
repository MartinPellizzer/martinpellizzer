Veronica argute-serrata

Veronica argute-serrata, commonly known as the serrated speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers with five petals, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
