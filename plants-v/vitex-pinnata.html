Vitex pinnata

Vitex pinnata, commonly known as the Indian lilac or champa, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its opposite, pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces fragrant, clustered flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, which bloom in summer. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that split open to release seeds.

Vitex pinnata is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in medicine and perfumery.
