Vaccinium bracteatum var. formosanum

Vaccinium bracteatum var. formosanum, commonly known as the Formosan huckleberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Taiwan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are white and clustered.

This variety is well adapted to cool, moist environments and thrives in acidic soils.
