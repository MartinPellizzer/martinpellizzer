Vinsonia humilis

Vinsonia humilis, commonly known as the dwarf vinsonia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and appear in clusters during the spring.

Vinsonia humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local insect populations.
