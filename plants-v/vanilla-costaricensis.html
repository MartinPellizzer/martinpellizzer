Vanilla costaricensis

Vanilla costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican vanilla, is a climbing orchid native to Central America.

It produces fragrant, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically pollinated by specific bees. The plant's vines can grow up to several meters long, often climbing trees or shrubs in its natural habitat. Its fruit, a dark purple pod, contains aromatic seeds that are used in culinary applications.

The species is valued for its unique flavor profile and is cultivated in certain regions for vanilla production.
