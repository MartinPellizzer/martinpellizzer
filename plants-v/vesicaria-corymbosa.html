Vesicaria corymbosa

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures that trap aquatic invertebrates for nutrient absorption. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. Its leaves are often submerged, while its flowering stems rise above the water surface.
