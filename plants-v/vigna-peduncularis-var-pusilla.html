Vigna peduncularis var. pusilla

Vigna peduncularis var. pusilla, commonly known as dwarf cowpea, is a small, herbaceous legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 30 centimeters, making it suitable for container gardening or as a ground cover. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and is often cultivated for its nutritional seeds and ornamental value.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it a beneficial companion plant in sustainable agricultural systems.
