Vaccinium stabilipes

Vaccinium stabilipes, commonly known as the stablefoot blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and heathers. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible berries that are dark blue to black in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or rocky areas.

Vaccinium stabilipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
