Vernonia betsimisaraka

Vernonia betsimisaraka, commonly known as the Madagascar ironwood, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its dark, aromatic bark and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the dry season. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a rough texture, while its wood is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay.

Vernonia betsimisaraka plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
