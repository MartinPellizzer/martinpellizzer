Verbascum × siatistense

Verbascum × siatistense, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant belonging to the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is native to regions of Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care.

Verbascum × siatistense thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
