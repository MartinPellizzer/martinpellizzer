Viola riviniana subsp. neglecta

Viola riviniana subsp.

neglecta, commonly known as the creeping violet, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, violet or purple flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread vegetatively.

Its botanical features make it a common species in horticultural settings and natural habitats.
