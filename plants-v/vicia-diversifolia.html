Vicia diversifolia

Vicia diversifolia, commonly known as the variable vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features trifoliate leaves with variable leaflet shapes, contributing to its common name. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in early summer.

Vicia diversifolia is known for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
