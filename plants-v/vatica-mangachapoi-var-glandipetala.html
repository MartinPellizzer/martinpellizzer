Vatica mangachapoi var. glandipetala

Vatica mangachapoi var. glandipetala, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood and can grow up to 40 meters tall. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a smooth surface and a pointed tip. The flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in inflorescences, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its hard, durable timber and is often found in lowland and hill forests.
