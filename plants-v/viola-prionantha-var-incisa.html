Viola prionantha var. incisa

Viola prionantha var. incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, toothed leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow throat, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, rich woodland soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
