Verbascum phlomoides var. thapsiforme

Verbascum phlomoides var. thapsiforme, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features large, gray-green, fuzzy leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of soft, silky hairs. The plant produces tall spikes of yellow flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in rocky or coastal areas.

Its distinctive appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
