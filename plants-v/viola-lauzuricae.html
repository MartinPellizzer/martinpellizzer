Viola lauzuricae

Viola lauzuricae, commonly known as the Lauzurica violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas and has heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers with five petals, often with a distinctive yellow or white spot at the base. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.

Viola lauzuricae is relatively hardy and can thrive in USDA hardiness zones 4 through 7.
