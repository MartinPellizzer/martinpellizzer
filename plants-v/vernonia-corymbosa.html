Vernonia corymbosa

Vernonia corymbosa, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
