Viola arenaria subsp. puberula

Viola arenaria subsp.

puberula, commonly known as the downy violet, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, downy hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. Its flowers are usually violet or purple, with five petals and a distinctive spur at the back.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
