Veronica tzvelevii

Veronica tzvelevii, commonly known as the Tzvelev's speedwell, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Veronica tzvelevii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
