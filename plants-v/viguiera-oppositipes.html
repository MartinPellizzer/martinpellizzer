Viguiera oppositipes

Viguiera oppositipes, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features upright stems covered in dense, grayish-green foliage. Its showy, yellow-orange flower heads appear in late spring to early summer.

Viguiera oppositipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
