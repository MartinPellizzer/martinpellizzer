Vitis angulata

Vitis angulata, commonly known as the angular grapevine, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in the spring, followed by clusters of round, black berries in the fall. Vitis angulata is adapted to moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded environments.

It is a member of the Vitaceae family and has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties.
