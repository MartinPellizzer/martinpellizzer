Vigna donii

Vigna donii, commonly known as the "southern bean" or "wild pigeon pea," is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and erect, branching stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. Vigna donii is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

It is valued for its nutritional content and potential use in sustainable agriculture.
