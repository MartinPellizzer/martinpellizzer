Valantia persica

Valantia persica, commonly known as the Persian rose, is a flowering plant native to the regions of Persia and surrounding areas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. Valantia persica prefers well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice in gardens and floral arrangements.
