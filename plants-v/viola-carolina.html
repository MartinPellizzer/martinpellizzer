Viola carolina

Viola carolina, commonly known as the Carolina violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 inches and features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are dark green in color. The plant produces showy violet or purple flowers with five petals, often with a distinctive yellow or white marking at the base. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Viola carolina is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
