Vernonia tenella

Vernonia tenella, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and prairies. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, dark blue to purple flowers. Its flowers are visited by a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Vernonia tenella is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
