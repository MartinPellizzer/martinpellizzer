Valdesia

Valdesia, commonly known as the "false dandelion," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are native to South America, particularly in regions of Brazil and Argentina. The plants typically have basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. Their flowers are composite, consisting of yellow disk flowers surrounded by white or pale yellow ray florets.

Valdesia species are often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.
