Valeriana neglecta

Valeriana neglecta, commonly known as the lesser valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant, small white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Valeriana neglecta is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat nervous disorders and insomnia.
