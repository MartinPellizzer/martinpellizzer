Verbena paniculata var. pinnatifida

Verbena paniculata var. pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, pinnately divided leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

The species is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
