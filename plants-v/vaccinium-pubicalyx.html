Vaccinium pubicalyx

Vaccinium pubicalyx, commonly known as the woolly blueberry, is a species of shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many other acid-loving plants. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, white pubescence, giving it a woolly appearance. Its flowers are typically pale blue or white, and it produces small, dark purple berries that are edible.
