Viola cyathiformis

Viola cyathiformis, commonly known as the Cape Verde viola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Cape Verde Islands.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers are typically borne on slender stems and are pollinated by insects.

Viola cyathiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
