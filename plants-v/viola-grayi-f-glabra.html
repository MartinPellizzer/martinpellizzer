Viola grayi f. glabra

Viola grayi f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth viola, is a variant of the viola grayi species characterized by its smooth, hairless leaves.

This perennial plant belongs to the Violaceae family and is native to specific regions in North America. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The flowers are usually violet or purple, with a distinctive heart-shaped lower lip.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for horticultural and ecological studies.
