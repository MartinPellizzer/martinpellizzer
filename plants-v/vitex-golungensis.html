Vitex golungensis

Vitex golungensis, commonly known as the African blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the verbena family (Verbenaceae) and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Vitex golungensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
