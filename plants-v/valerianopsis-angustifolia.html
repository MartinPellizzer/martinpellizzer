Valerianopsis angustifolia

Valerianopsis angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf valerian, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Valerianaceae and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or stream banks. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their sedative properties.

Valerianopsis angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
