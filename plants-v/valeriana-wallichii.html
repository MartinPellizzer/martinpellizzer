Valeriana wallichii

Valeriana wallichii, commonly known as Himalayan valerian, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its rhizomes and roots contain compounds that have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their calming effects.

Valeriana wallichii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
