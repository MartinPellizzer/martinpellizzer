Vaccinium reticulatum

Vaccinium reticulatum, commonly known as the redtop blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants. The plant features small, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slightly waxy texture. Its flowers are bell-shaped and typically pink or white, blooming in the spring.

The fruit is a small, round berry that ripens to a deep red color and is edible, though often used in jams and preserves.
