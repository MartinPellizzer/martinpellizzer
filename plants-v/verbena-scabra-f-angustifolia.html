Verbena scabra f. angustifolia

Verbena scabra f. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial with slender, upright stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its drought-resistant nature. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
