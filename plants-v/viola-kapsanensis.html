Viola kapsanensis

Viola kapsanensis, commonly known as the Kapsa violet, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeastern Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola kapsanensis is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
