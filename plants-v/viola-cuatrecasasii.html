Viola cuatrecasasii

Viola cuatrecasasii, commonly known as the Cuatrecasas violet, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and streambanks. It is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

Viola cuatrecasasii is also valued for its role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its native ecosystems.
