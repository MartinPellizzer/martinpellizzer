Veronica peregrina f. xalapensis

Veronica peregrina f. xalapensis, commonly known as the Xalapa speedwell, is a hybrid form of the European Veronica peregrina.

It is a low-growing perennial plant with small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The species is characterized by its slender stems and lance-shaped leaves, which are typically covered in fine hairs. Native to Mexico, it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
