Viola macloskeyi var. pallens

Viola macloskeyi var. pallens, commonly known as the pale viola, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy violet flowers that range in color from pale lavender to white. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is known for its delicate, fragrant blooms.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
