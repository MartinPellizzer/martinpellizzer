Viola howellii

Viola howellii, commonly known as Howell's violet, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Viola howellii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and conservation efforts.
