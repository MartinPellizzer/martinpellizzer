Viola maculata f. buchtieniana

Viola maculata f. buchtieniana, commonly known as the Buchtienian violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and is covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, with a distinctive white or yellowish marking on the petals. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
