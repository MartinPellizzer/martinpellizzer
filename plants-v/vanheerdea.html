Vanheerdea

Vanheerdea, commonly known as the Vanheerde's orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plants are characterized by their intricate, often showy flowers with prominent sepals and petals. They typically grow as epiphytes, attaching themselves to trees in humid, shaded environments.

Vanheerdea species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
