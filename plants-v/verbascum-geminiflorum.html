Verbascum geminiflorum

Verbascum geminiflorum, commonly known as the twinflower mullein, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal medicine. Its leaves are often used to make a soothing herbal tea, while the flowers are sometimes used in floral arrangements.

Verbascum geminiflorum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in arid environments.
