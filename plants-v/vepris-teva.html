Vepris teva

Vepris teva, commonly known as the African birch, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland forest areas.

Due to its unique chemical composition, it has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
