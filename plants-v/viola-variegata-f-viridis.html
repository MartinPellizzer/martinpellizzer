Viola variegata f. viridis

Viola variegata f. viridis, commonly known as the green-leaved viola, is a variant of the common violet characterized by its distinctive foliage.

This plant belongs to the Violaceae family and is native to Europe, particularly in regions such as the British Isles. Its leaves are predominantly green with minimal variegation, contrasting with the more colorful forms of the species. The flowers are typically violet-blue in color, with five petals and a distinctive spur.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
