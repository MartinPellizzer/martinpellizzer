Verbascum thapsus var. elongatum

Verbascum thapsus var. elongatum, commonly known as the tall mullein, is a variant of the common mullein plant.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are densely covered in soft hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow and appear in clusters at the top of the stem.

This variety is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
