Verbascum blattarioides var. lusitanicum

Verbascum blattarioides var. lusitanicum, commonly known as the Lusitanian mullein, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features tall, erect stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, covered in dense, velvety gray-green leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in clusters at the top of the stems, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

The species is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.
