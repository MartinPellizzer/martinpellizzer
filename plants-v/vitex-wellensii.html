Vitex wellensii

Vitex wellensii, commonly known as the African blueberry or wild vitex, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has narrow, opposite leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Vitex wellensii is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
