Viguiera tuberosa f. major

Viguiera tuberosa f. major, commonly known as the Mexican coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It features upright, branching stems that are often hairy, and it produces showy, daisy-like yellow flowers with a prominent central disk. The plant has heart-shaped to ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in native plant gardens.
