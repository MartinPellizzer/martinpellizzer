Vicia rerayensis

Vicia rerayensis, commonly known as the Reray vetch, is a flowering plant species native to certain regions of Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open areas, preferring well-drained soils. Vicia rerayensis is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria, contributing to soil fertility.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional herbal practices.
