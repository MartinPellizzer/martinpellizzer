Vitis × pulchra

Vitis × pulchra, commonly known as the hybrid grapevine, is a species resulting from the crossbreeding of Vitis vinifera and Vitis riparia.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and the production of large, thick-skinned berries. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and showy flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Vitis × pulchra is also valued in viticulture for its potential in breeding programs aimed at improving disease resistance.
