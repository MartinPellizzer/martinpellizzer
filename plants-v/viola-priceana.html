Viola priceana

Viola priceana, commonly known as the Price's violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to parts of North America, particularly the eastern United States. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, with five petals arranged in a symmetrical pattern. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Viola priceana thrives in woodland habitats and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
