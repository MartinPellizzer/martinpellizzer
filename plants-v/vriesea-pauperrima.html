Vriesea pauperrima

Vriesea pauperrima, commonly known as the poor man's vriesea, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant produces striking, long-lasting inflorescences with vibrant orange or red flowers. It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its low maintenance requirements and ornamental appeal.

Vriesea pauperrima thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate watering to maintain its health.
