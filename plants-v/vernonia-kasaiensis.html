Vernonia kasaiensis

Vernonia kasaiensis, commonly known as Kasai ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and forest margins. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellowish-green.

Vernonia kasaiensis is valued for its ornamental potential and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
