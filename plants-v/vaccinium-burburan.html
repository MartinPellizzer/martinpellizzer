Vaccinium burburan

Vaccinium burburan, commonly known as the burr blueberry, is a species of shrub native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark blue or black in color, with a slightly tart flavor. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The shrub is valued for its ornamental appeal and its contribution to local ecosystems.
