Vicia aintabensis

Vicia aintabensis, commonly known as Aintab vetch, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in early summer. Vicia aintabensis is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are an important food source for various wildlife species.
