Veronica kovacsii

Veronica kovacsii, commonly known as the Hungarian speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian region of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Veronica kovacsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
