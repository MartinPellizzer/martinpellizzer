Viguiera purisimae

Viguiera purisimae, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers bloom in late summer and early fall.

Viguiera purisimae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
