Veronica spicata subsp. lanigera

Veronica spicata subsp.

lanigera, commonly known as the woolly speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance.

The plant produces spikes of small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or wet meadows.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
