Viburnum longipedunculatum

Viburnum longipedunculatum, commonly known as the sweet pepper bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by bright red berries that persist into winter. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is adaptable to various soil types.

Viburnum longipedunculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and wildlife value, providing food for birds and other animals.
