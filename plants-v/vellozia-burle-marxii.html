Vellozia burle-marxii

Vellozia burle-marxii, commonly known as the Burle-Marx vellozia, is a striking plant native to Brazil, specifically the state of Rio de Janeiro.

It is a member of the Velloziaceae family and is characterized by its unique, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, adding to its ornamental appeal. Vellozia burle-marxii is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its distinctive appearance and ecological significance make it a notable species in botanical collections.
