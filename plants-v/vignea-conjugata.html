Vignea conjugata

Vignea conjugata, commonly known as the clustered morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, violet to blue flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Vignea conjugata is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of several meters, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
