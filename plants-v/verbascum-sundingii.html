Verbascum sundingii

Verbascum sundingii, commonly known as the Sunda's mullein, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes species known for their showy, woolly leaves and yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Verbascum sundingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
