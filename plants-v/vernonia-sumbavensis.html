Vernonia sumbavensis

Vernonia sumbavensis, commonly known as the Sumbawa ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the island of Sumbawa in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while the flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters.

Vernonia sumbavensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
