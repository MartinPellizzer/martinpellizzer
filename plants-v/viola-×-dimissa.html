Viola × dimissa

Viola × dimissa, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is often found in woodland habitats.

Viola × dimissa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for centuries in gardens and as a wildflower.
