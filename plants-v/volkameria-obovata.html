Volkameria obovata

Volkameria obovata, commonly known as the Mexican buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are broadly ovate and often covered in a fine, white or gray pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or containers.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
