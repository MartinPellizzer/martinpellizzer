Vitis pulcherrima

Vitis pulcherrima, commonly known as the "Beautiful Grapevine," is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a woody vine that produces small, fragrant flowers and is known for its ornamental value. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and can vary in color from deep green to reddish hues.

Vitis pulcherrima is also valued for its potential in horticulture and may have medicinal properties under study.
