Vanilla aromatica var. discolor

Vanilla aromatica var. discolor, commonly known as the fragrant vanilla orchid, is a climbing epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its long, vine-like stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically pollinated by specific bees. Its fruit, a dark purple pod, contains the aromatic seeds used in vanilla extraction.

This variety is distinguished by its variegated leaf patterns and unique floral scent.
