Vernonia martiana

Vernonia martiana, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Vernonia martiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
