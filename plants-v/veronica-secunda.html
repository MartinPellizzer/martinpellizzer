Veronica secunda

Veronica secunda, commonly known as the lesser speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Veronica secunda is often found in grassy areas, along roadsides, and in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in many regions.
