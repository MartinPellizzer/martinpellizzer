Vernonia stictophylla

Vernonia stictophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom during the dry season. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Vernonia stictophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
