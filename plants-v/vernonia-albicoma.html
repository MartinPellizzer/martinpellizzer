Vernonia albicoma

Vernonia albicoma, commonly known as the white cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually gray-green, covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in heads.

Vernonia albicoma is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
