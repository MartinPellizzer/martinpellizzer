Veronica hederifolia var. umbrosa

Veronica hederifolia var. umbrosa, commonly known as the shade-loving speedwell, is a perennial plant native to the forests of Europe.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are slightly hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value lies in its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
