Vanda tricolor var. suavis

Vanda tricolor var. suavis, commonly known as the sweet-scented vanda, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its strikingly colorful flowers, which typically display shades of pink, white, and green. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted for efficient photosynthesis.

This orchid is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique fragrance.
