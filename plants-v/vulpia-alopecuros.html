Vulpia alopecuros

Vulpia alopecuros, commonly known as the bristlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and waste areas. The plant has slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, which helps reduce water loss in dry conditions.

Vulpia alopecuros is often found in soils that are moderately fertile and well-drained.
