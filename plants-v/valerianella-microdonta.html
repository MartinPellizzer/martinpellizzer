Valerianella microdonta

Valerianella microdonta, commonly known as dwarf valerian, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically reaches a height of only a few centimeters, with small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Valerianella microdonta is often used in ornamental gardening for its compact form and delicate appearance.
