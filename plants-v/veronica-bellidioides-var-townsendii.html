Veronica bellidioides var. townsendii

Veronica bellidioides var. townsendii, commonly known as Townsend's speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in a fine white coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and drought-tolerant addition to native plant gardens.
