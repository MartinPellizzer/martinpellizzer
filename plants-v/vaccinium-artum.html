Vaccinium artum

Vaccinium artum, commonly known as the highbush blueberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and are typically harvested in the summer months. Vaccinium artum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
