Viola garganica

Viola garganica, commonly known as the Garganica violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its flowers are usually purple or violet in color, with a distinctive five-petaled structure.

Viola garganica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
