Veronica foliosa var. latifolia

Veronica foliosa var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a broad, glossy surface that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and rocky areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
