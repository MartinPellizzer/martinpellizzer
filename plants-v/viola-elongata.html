Viola elongata

Viola elongata, commonly known as the elongated violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 10-30 centimeters, featuring heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are usually purple or violet in color, with a distinctive pattern of darker markings. Viola elongata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
