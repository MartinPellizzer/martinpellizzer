Veronica linariifolia f. villosula

Veronica linariifolia f. villosula, commonly known as the hairy veronica, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, hairy texture, giving it its common name. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade conditions.

It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, particularly in coastal regions.
