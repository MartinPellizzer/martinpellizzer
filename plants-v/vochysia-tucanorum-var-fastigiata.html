Vochysia tucanorum var. fastigiata

Vochysia tucanorum var. fastigiata, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, columnar growth habit and its showy, orange-yellow inflorescences that bloom in clusters. The plant features compound leaves with elongated, narrow leaflets that give it a distinctive, upright appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological significance.
