Veronica cheesemanii subsp. flabellata

Veronica cheesemanii subsp.

flabellata, commonly known as the flabellate speedwell, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
