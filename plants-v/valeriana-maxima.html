Valeriana maxima

Valeriana maxima, commonly known as the giant valerian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, upright stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, adorned with lance-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its roots are extensively used in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

Valeriana maxima thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens and natural habitats.
