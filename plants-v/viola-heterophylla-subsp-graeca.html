Viola heterophylla subsp. graeca

Viola heterophylla subsp.

graeca, commonly known as the Greek violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple and violet. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology compared to other viola species.
