Viola picta

Viola picta, commonly known as the painted violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple and green mottling, and its flowers are typically violet or purple with yellow markings. The plant grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Viola picta belongs to the Violaceae family, which includes other violets and related species.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
