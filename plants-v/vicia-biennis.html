Vicia biennis

Vicia biennis, commonly known as the two-year vetch, is an annual legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes. The plant has slender, hairy stems and pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in fields.

Vicia biennis is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
