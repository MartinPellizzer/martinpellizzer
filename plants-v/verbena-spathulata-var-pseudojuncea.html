Verbena spathulata var. pseudojuncea

Verbena spathulata var. pseudojuncea, commonly known as the narrow-leaf verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, vividly blue or purple flowers. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. This species thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and long blooming period make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
