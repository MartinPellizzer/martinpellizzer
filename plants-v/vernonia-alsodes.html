Vernonia alsodes

Vernonia alsodes, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow to orange disk flowers surrounded by pale green to brown ray florets. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

Vernonia alsodes is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
