Vitis filiformis

Vitis filiformis, commonly known as the creeping or trailing grapevine, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems that often trail along the ground or climb low structures. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Vitis filiformis is often found in moist, wooded areas and can be used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
