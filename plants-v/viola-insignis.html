Viola insignis

Viola insignis, commonly known as the purple violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is native to the eastern United States, where it thrives in moist, wooded areas. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet hue and produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple and violet. Viola insignis is typically found in shaded environments and prefers well-drained, loamy soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
