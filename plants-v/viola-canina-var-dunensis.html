Viola canina var. dunensis

Viola canina var. dunensis, commonly known as the Scottish violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, woodland soils and thrives in shaded environments. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves and produces violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
