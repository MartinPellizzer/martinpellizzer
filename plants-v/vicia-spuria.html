Vicia spuria

Vicia spuria, commonly known as the common vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and woodland edges. The plant features slender, upright stems with alternate pinnate leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually pink or purple, appearing in racemes during the spring and early summer.

Vicia spuria is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological restoration projects.
