Valeriana plagiostephana

Valeriana plagiostephana, commonly known as the mountain valerian, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It features a robust rhizomatous root system and erect, branching stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite, lanceolate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Valeriana plagiostephana is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its calming effects and use in traditional herbal remedies.
