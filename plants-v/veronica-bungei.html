Veronica bungei

Veronica bungei, commonly known as Bunge's speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white wool, which helps to reduce water loss in arid environments.

Veronica bungei is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
