Vinca libanotica

Vinca libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanese periwinkle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. Its hardy nature allows it to survive in a variety of climates, and it is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Additionally, Vinca libanotica has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
