Vignea bohemica

Vignea bohemica, commonly known as the Bohemian vine, is a climbing plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale yellow or white, followed by elongated pods containing several seeds. Vignea bohemica is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils, where it thrives in partial shade.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
