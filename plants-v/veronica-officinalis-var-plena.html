Veronica officinalis var. plena

Veronica officinalis var. plena, commonly known as the full-flowered speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and wetlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
