Vernonia virgata

Vernonia virgata, commonly known as the silverleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its showy purple to pink flower heads bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Vernonia virgata is often found in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and streambanks.
