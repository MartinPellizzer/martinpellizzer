Vicia benghalensis subsp. benghalensis

Vicia benghalensis subsp.

benghalensis, commonly known as the Bengal vetch, is a herbaceous annual plant native to South and Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its slender stems and trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being ovate to elliptic in shape.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by slender, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

The species is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural contexts.
