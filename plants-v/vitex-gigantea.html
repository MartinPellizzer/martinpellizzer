Vitex gigantea

Vitex gigantea, commonly known as the giant Vitex or the glorybower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, woody stems and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Vitex gigantea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in landscaping due to its hardiness and adaptability.
