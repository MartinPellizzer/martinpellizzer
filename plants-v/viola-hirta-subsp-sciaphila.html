Viola hirta subsp. sciaphila

Viola hirta subsp.

sciaphila, commonly known as the shade viola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rock crevices.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, which helps reduce water loss in its cool, dim habitats. Its flowers are usually violet or purple, with five petals and a distinctive spur at the base.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with partial shade.
