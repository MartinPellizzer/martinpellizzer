Valeriana alternifolia

Valeriana alternifolia, commonly known as the alternate-leaved valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown roots and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale pink or white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, often found in woodland areas.

Valeriana alternifolia is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its calming effects.
