Viola silvatica proles riviniana

Viola silvatica proles riviniana, commonly known as the river valley violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist woodland habitats.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of violet or purple, often with yellow markings. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a common sight in riparian zones.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for pollinators and ecological studies.
