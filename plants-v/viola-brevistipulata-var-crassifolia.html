Viola brevistipulata var. crassifolia

Viola brevistipulata var. crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved violet, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, broad leaves that are typically velvety in texture. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that range in color from violet to white, often with a yellow or orange center. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ability to attract pollinators.
