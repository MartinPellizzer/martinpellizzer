Vernonia oligactoides

Vernonia oligactoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodlands. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and its flowers are typically blue to purple in color.

Vernonia oligactoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
