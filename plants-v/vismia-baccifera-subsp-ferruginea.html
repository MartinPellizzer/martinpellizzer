Vismia baccifera subsp. ferruginea

Vismia baccifera subsp.

ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty vismia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its elongated, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a rusty-colored pubescence.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
