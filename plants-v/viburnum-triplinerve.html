Viburnum triplinerve

Viburnum triplinerve, commonly known as the three-veined viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with three prominent veins, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. Viburnum triplinerve is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
