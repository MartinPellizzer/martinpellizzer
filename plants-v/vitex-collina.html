Vitex collina

Vitex collina, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that produces clusters of small, blue-violet flowers in the summer. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Vitex collina is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
