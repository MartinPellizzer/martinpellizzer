Vaccinium usunoki

Vaccinium usunoki, commonly known as the Japanese huckleberry, is a shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark red or black in color. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in traditional Japanese cuisine.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
