Viola flos-idae

Viola flos-idae, commonly known as the celestial violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers have five petals, with the upper one often being larger and more prominent.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its beauty and fragrance.
