Vicia tenuifolia var. rifana

Vicia tenuifolia var. rifana, commonly known as the narrow-leaf vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, elongated pods containing lens-shaped seeds.

This variety is often found in temperate regions and is noted for its adaptability to poor soil conditions.
