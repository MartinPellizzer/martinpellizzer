Viburnum tomentosum var. parvifolium

Viburnum tomentosum var. parvifolium, commonly known as dwarf sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, ovate leaves that are hairy on both sides, giving it a distinctive grayish appearance. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a suitable choice for garden landscapes.

Its compact size and ornamental value make it a popular plant for erosion control and wildlife habitat.
