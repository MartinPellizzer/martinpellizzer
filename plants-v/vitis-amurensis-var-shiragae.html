Vitis amurensis var. shiragae

Vitis amurensis var. shiragae, commonly known as the Japanese grapevine, is a cold-hardy vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and ability to thrive in temperate climates. The plant produces small, clustered berries that are typically dark purple in color. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

This variety is also valued for its potential in viticulture due to its adaptability and disease resistance.
