Vernonia stenocephala

Vernonia stenocephala, commonly known as the narrow-headed ironweed, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Vernonia stenocephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
