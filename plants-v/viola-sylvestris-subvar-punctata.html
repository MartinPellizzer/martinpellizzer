Viola sylvestris subvar. punctata

Viola sylvestris subvar. punctata, commonly known as the spotted violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple or violet coloration, often marked with spots or streaks. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, with a distinctive pattern of markings. It thrives in woodland and grassland habitats, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
