Viburnum ellipticum

Viburnum ellipticum, commonly known as the American cranberrybush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, elliptic to ovate leaves that are often reddish beneath, turning vibrant red or orange in the fall. The plant produces flat, white to pinkish inflorescences known as cymes, which bloom in early spring. Its fruits are small, dark red berries that are edible and often used in jams and wines.

Viburnum ellipticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
