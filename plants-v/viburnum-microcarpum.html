Viburnum microcarpum

Viburnum microcarpum, commonly known as the dwarf arrowwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by dark red berries in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in partial to full shade.

Viburnum microcarpum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract wildlife.
