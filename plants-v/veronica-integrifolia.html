Veronica integrifolia

Veronica integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf speedwell, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and entire, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open meadows and rocky areas.

Veronica integrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
