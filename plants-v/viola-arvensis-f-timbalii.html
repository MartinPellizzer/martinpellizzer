Viola arvensis f. timbalii

Viola arvensis f. timbalii, commonly known as the Timbalii violet, is a botanical variant of the common field violet.

It is characterized by its distinctive violet-blue flowers with a contrasting yellow center. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

This form is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
