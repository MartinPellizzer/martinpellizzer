Viguiera quinqueremis

Viguiera quinqueremis, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and showy purple to pinkish-pink composite flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with rough textures and produces small, dark brown seeds in its composite flower heads. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy perennial suitable for xeriscaping.

Viguiera quinqueremis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
