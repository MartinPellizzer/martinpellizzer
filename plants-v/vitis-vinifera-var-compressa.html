Vitis vinifera var. compressa

Vitis vinifera var. compressa, commonly known as the compact grapevine, is a lesser-known variety of the common grapevine.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, making it suitable for small spaces or container gardening. This variety typically produces small, thick-skinned berries that are often used in winemaking or as table grapes. Its foliage is dense, with dark green leaves that provide good shade.

The plant is relatively hardy and can thrive in a variety of climates, though it prefers well-drained soil and full sun.
