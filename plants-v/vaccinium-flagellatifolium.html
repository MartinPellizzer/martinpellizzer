Vaccinium flagellatifolium

Vaccinium flagellatifolium, commonly known as the slender-leaved huckleberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and azaleas. The plant is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and small, edible berries that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Vaccinium flagellatifolium plays an important ecological role by providing food for wildlife and contributing to forest biodiversity.
