Virdika

Virdika, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil in full sun. Virdika is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can thrive in USDA hardiness zones 5 through 8.

Its foliage is broad and lance-shaped, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms.
