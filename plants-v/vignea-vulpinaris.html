Vignea vulpinaris

Vignea vulpinaris, commonly known as the foxglove vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It produces striking, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bats. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.

Vignea vulpinaris is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
