Voandzeia geocarpa

Voandzeia geocarpa, commonly known as the African groundnut, is a leguminous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and pinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces cylindrical pods that contain several kidney-shaped seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

Due to its nutritional value, it is an important crop in some parts of sub-Saharan Africa.
