Vernonia polyantha

Vernonia polyantha, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fluffy, blue-gray flower heads that resemble a misty cloud, which bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silver-white hairs, giving them a distinctive appearance.

Vernonia polyantha is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
