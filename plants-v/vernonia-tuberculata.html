Vernonia tuberculata

Vernonia tuberculata, commonly known as the roughleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has rough, hairy leaves that give it its common name, and it typically grows in moist woodlands and along stream banks.

Vernonia tuberculata is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
