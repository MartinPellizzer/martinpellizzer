Verbena cumingii

Verbena cumingii, commonly known as Cuming's verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flowers. The plant typically blooms from summer to fall, producing showy spikes of flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
