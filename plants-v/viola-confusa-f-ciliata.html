Viola confusa f. ciliata

Viola confusa f. ciliata, commonly known as the ciliate viola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features heart-shaped leaves with ciliate (fringed) margins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces violet flowers that range in color from pale blue to deep purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes grown in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
