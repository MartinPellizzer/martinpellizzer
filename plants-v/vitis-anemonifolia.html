Vitis anemonifolia

Vitis anemonifolia, commonly known as the anemoneleaf grape, is a species of flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. Vitis anemonifolia is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important member of the Vitaceae family.
