Viola macrostipulata

Viola macrostipulata, commonly known as the large-leaf violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of East Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins and its violet or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and rocky slopes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for use in gardens and horticulture.
