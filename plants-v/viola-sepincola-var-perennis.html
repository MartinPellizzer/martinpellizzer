Viola sepincola var. perennis

Viola sepincola var. perennis, commonly known as the perennial viola, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation. It is often grown for its ornamental value and can thrive in temperate climates.

The variety perennis is distinguished by its ability to return year after year, unlike some other viola species.
