Viola phalacrocarpa var. ishidoyana

Viola phalacrocarpa var. ishidoyana, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy violet flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers bloom in early spring, producing a delicate pink to purple hue.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
