Veronica linifolia

Veronica linifolia, commonly known as silverleaf speedwell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters along its stems. The plant has slender, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Veronica linifolia is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
