Valeriana excelsa subsp. versifolia

Valeriana excelsa subsp.

versifolia, commonly known as the blue valerian, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and borders. Valeriana excelsa subsp.

versifolia is also valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its calming effects.
