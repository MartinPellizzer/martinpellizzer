Veronica × microcoma

Veronica × microcoma, commonly known as the common speedwell, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and produces clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically has slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
