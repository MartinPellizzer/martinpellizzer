Viola flos-mariae

Viola flos-mariae, commonly known as the Mary's flower, is a delicate perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant violet or purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing clusters of small, showy blossoms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
