Verbena bangiana

Verbena bangiana, commonly known as the tropical verbena, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple or red flowers that bloom in spikes during the summer months. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Verbena bangiana is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
