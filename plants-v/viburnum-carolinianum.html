Viburnum carolinianum

Viburnum carolinianum, commonly known as the Carolina arrowwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding to its seasonal appeal. It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and thrives in partial to full shade.

Viburnum carolinianum is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to support local wildlife.
