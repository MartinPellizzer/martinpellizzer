Veronica grossa

Veronica grossa, commonly known as the common speedwell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and can be found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small but numerous, often appearing in pairs.

Veronica grossa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
