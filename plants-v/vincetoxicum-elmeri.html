Vincetoxicum elmeri

Vincetoxicum elmeri, commonly known as Elmer's beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces striking purple-black berries in the fall, which are a food source for birds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly serrated margin.

Vincetoxicum elmeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
