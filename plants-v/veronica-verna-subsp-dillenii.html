Veronica verna subsp. dillenii

Veronica verna subsp.

dillenii, commonly known as the spring speedwell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May.

The plant has slender, often hairy stems that branch near the base, supporting its delicate foliage. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and covered with fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
