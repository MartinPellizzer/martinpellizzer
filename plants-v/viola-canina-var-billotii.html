Viola canina var. billotii

Viola canina var. billotii, commonly known as the Canary viola, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. It is a perennial herb that produces small, delicate blooms from spring to early summer.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
