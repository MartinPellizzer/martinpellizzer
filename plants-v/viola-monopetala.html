Viola monopetala

Viola monopetala, commonly known as the single-petaled violet, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 10 to 15 centimeters. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, violet-colored flowers with a single petal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its unique floral structure makes it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
