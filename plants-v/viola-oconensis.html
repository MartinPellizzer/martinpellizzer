Viola oconensis

Viola oconensis, commonly known as the Oconee violet, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.

Viola oconensis is often found in the Appalachian region, particularly in Georgia and North Carolina.
