Vitex negundo var. bicolor

Vitex negundo var. bicolor, commonly known as the fragrant ash or white flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, pinnate leaves that are often aromatic when crushed. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of white and purple, which bloom in the summer. Its fruits are small, dry capsules that split open to release seeds.

Vitex negundo var. bicolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil conditions.
