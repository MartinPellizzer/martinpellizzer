Verbascum longirostre

Verbascum longirostre, commonly known as the long-beaked mullein, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are large, velvety, and gray-green in color, often used in traditional medicine.

Verbascum longirostre is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
