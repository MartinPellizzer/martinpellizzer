Verbascum delileanum

Verbascum delileanum, commonly known as the Delile's mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, velvety leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, sunny conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Verbascum delileanum is valued for its medicinal properties and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
