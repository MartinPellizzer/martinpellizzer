Viola villosa var. cordifolia

Viola villosa var. cordifolia, commonly known as the woolly violet, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, or pubescent, stems and heart-shaped leaves, which give it its botanical name. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine.
