Viola heterophylla subsp. splendida

Viola heterophylla subsp.

splendida, commonly known as the Splendid Violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture.

The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
