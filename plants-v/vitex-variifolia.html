Vitex variifolia

Vitex variifolia, commonly known as the variable chaste tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that vary in shape and size along the stem. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of purple or white, which bloom in summer. Its fruit is a dry, three-lobed capsule that contains small, smooth seeds.

Vitex variifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
