Vicia disperma

Vicia disperma, commonly known as the scattered vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous regions. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, and it has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Vicia disperma is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

It is also used in some traditional herbal remedies due to its various bioactive compounds.
