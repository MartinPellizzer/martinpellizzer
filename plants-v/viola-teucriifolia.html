Viola teucriifolia

Viola teucriifolia, commonly known as the creeping violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive purple underside. Its flowers are small, usually violet or blue, and bloom in early spring.

Viola teucriifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
