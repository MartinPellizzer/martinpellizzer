Vignea echinata

Vignea echinata, commonly known as the spiny burr vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiny fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. Vignea echinata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
