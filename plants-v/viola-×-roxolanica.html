Viola × roxolanica

Viola × roxolanica, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a member of the Violaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, often purple or violet flowers.

This hybrid plant is the result of crosses between different species of viola, leading to a wide variety of cultivars with diverse flower colors and forms. It typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are borne in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Viola × roxolanica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a range of garden conditions.
