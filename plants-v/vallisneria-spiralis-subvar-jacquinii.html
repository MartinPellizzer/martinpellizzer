Vallisneria spiralis subvar. jacquinii

Vallisneria spiralis subvar. jacquinii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically narrow and dark green, often reaching lengths of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually found in water, with male flowers floating on the surface and female flowers submerged. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments with soft, nutrient-rich substrates.

This species is often used in aquariums and ponds for its aesthetic appeal and ability to improve water quality.
