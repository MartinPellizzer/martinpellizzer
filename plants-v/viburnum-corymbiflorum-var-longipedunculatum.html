Viburnum corymbiflorum var. longipedunculatum

Viburnum corymbiflorum var. longipedunculatum, commonly known as the African snowball, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy on top and lighter underneath. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, and it produces small, dark blue berries that are edible for birds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
