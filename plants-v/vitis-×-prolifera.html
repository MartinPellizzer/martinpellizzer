Vitis × prolifera

Vitis × prolifera, commonly known as the grapevine, is a hybrid plant species in the Vitaceae family.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and the production of tendrils that help it climb. The plant produces small, often clustered flowers that develop into berries, which can be either edible or used for winemaking. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy surface.

Vitis × prolifera is widely cultivated for its fruit and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
