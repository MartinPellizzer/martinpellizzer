Veronica × kerneri

Veronica × kerneri, commonly known as the common speedwell, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Veronica × kerneri is known for its hardiness and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
