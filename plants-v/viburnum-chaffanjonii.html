Viburnum chaffanjonii

Viburnum chaffanjonii, commonly known as the Chaffanjon viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from its foliage, flowers, and fruit, making it a versatile plant for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
