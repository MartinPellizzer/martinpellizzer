Vaccinium igneum

Vaccinium igneum, commonly known as the flameberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and rhododendrons. The plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly waxy texture. Its flowers are bell-shaped and usually pink or red, adding a striking visual element to its appearance.

The fruit, which is a small, bright red berry, is edible and often used in culinary applications.
