Vernonia geminiflora

Vernonia geminiflora, commonly known as the twin-flower ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia geminiflora is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits to pollinators.
