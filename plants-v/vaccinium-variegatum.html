Vaccinium variegatum

Vaccinium variegatum, commonly known as the variegated huckleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and rhododendrons. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or purple in color, though they may vary in hue depending on the variety. Its leaves are often variegated, featuring white or yellow markings that make it visually distinctive.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
