Verbena mulinoides

Verbena mulinoides, commonly known as the Mexican verbena, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, vibrant blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, four-petaled flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Verbena mulinoides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its colorful blooms and drought tolerance.
