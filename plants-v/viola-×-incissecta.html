Viola × incissecta

Viola × incissecta, commonly known as the cutleaf violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, deeply lobed leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The flowers are typically purple or violet, with five petals and a spurred lower petal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
