Vicia polyphylla

Vicia polyphylla, commonly known as the many-leaved vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial legume that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. Its pods contain several seeds, which are often used as forage for livestock.

Vicia polyphylla plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.
