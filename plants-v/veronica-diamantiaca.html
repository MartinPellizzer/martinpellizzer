Veronica diamantiaca

Veronica diamantiaca, commonly known as the Diamond Plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
