Vitis pterisantha

Vitis pterisantha, commonly known as the Chinese grape or Pterisantha grape, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its climbing habit and tendrils that allow it to grow along supports. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible berries that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value.

Vitis pterisantha is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
