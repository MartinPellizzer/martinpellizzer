Verbascum fruticulosum

Verbascum fruticulosum, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces tall, erect stems that bear clusters of yellow flowers in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Verbascum fruticulosum is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family and is known for its medicinal and ornamental uses.
