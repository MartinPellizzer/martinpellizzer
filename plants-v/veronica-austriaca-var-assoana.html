Veronica austriaca var. assoana

Veronica austriaca var. assoana, commonly known as the Austrian speedwell, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features upright, slender stems that bear opposite pairs of oval, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, blue-to-purple flowers with five petals, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or shaded gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
