Vepris simplicifolia

Vepris simplicifolia, commonly known as African wild tea, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by winged fruits, which are typical of the genus Vepris. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its aromatic properties, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
