Verbesina scaposa

Verbesina scaposa, commonly known as the slender beggar-tick, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy leaves and produces seeds in small, capsule-like structures. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.
