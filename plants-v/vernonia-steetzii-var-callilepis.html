Vernonia steetzii var. callilepis

Vernonia steetzii var. callilepis, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical herbaceous growth habit. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and sandy soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The showy purple to blue flowers appear in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
