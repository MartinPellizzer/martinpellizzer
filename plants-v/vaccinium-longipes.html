Vaccinium longipes

Vaccinium longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed blueberry, is a species of shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes various evergreen shrubs and small trees. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. Its flowers are bell-shaped, typically pink or white, and bloom in the spring.

The plant is valued for its edible berries, which are rich in antioxidants and have a tart, slightly sweet flavor.
