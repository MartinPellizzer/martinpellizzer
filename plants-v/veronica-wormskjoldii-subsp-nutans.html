Veronica wormskjoldii subsp. nutans

Veronica wormskjoldii subsp.

nutans, commonly known as the drooping speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring.

The subspecies nutans is distinguished by its downward-facing flowers, which give it a unique appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
