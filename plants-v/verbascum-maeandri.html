Verbascum maeandri

Verbascum maeandri, commonly known as the Maeandri mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes many species of mullein plants known for their fuzzy, velvety leaves. The plant produces tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of yellow flowers in late summer. Its leaves are large, broad, and covered in a dense layer of soft, woolly hairs.

Verbascum maeandri is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in sunny, well-drained environments.
