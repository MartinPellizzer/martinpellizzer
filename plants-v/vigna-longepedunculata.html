Vigna longepedunculata

Vigna longepedunculata, commonly known as the long peduncle bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated pods that contain small, oval seeds. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with trifoliate leaves and small white or pale pink flowers. Its root system is well-developed, allowing it to thrive in various soil types.

The seeds are often used in local cuisines and can be dried and stored for extended periods.
