Viola sacchalinensis var. alpina

Viola sacchalinensis var. alpina, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple or violet. Its flowers bloom in early spring, often before the leaves are fully developed.

Viola sacchalinensis var. alpina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
