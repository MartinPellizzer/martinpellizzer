Vauquelinia corymbosa subsp. karwinskyi

Vauquelinia corymbosa subsp.

karwinskyi, commonly known as the Karwinsky's vauquelinia, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with leathery, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green in color.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in corymbs, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or woodland habitats.

Its compact growth habit makes it a suitable ornamental plant for gardens and landscapes.
