Vernonia luembensis

Vernonia luembensis, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. Vernonia luembensis is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
