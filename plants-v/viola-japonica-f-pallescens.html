Viola japonica f. pallescens

Viola japonica f. pallescens, commonly known as the pale viola, is a flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its pale green to almost white foliage, which distinguishes it from other viola species. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically lavender to purple in color, with five petals arranged symmetrically. Native to Japan, it thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and subtle floral display.
