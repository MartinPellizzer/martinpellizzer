Vincetoxicum officinale var. micranthum

Vincetoxicum officinale var. micranthum, commonly known as lesser dogbane, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. It is known for its attractive, slender, woody stems that often turn reddish in color.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
