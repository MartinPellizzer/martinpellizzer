Vaccinium mandarinorum var. laetum

Vaccinium mandarinorum var. laetum, commonly known as the Florida huckleberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or orange in color, similar to cranberries but smaller. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or pine flatwoods.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
