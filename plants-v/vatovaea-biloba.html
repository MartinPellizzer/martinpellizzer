Vatovaea biloba

Vatovaea biloba, commonly known as the Madagascar star vine, is a climbing plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped flowers, which are typically white or pale yellow. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are often pubescent, and it produces small, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
