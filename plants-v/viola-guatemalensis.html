Viola guatemalensis

Viola guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is native to Central America, particularly Guatemala, and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and woodlands. Its flowers bloom in early spring and are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola guatemalensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
