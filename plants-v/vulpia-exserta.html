Vulpia exserta

Vulpia exserta, commonly known as the narrowleaf foxtail, is a grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and spikelets that are often tinged with red or purple at maturity. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is often found in temperate climates. Its inflorescence consists of a single spike, which is usually shorter than the leaf blades.

Vulpia exserta is an annual plant that produces small, wind-dispersed seeds, contributing to its widespread distribution.
