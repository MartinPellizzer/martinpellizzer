Veronica transcaucasica

Veronica transcaucasica, commonly known as the Transcaucasian speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in loose inflorescences.

Veronica transcaucasica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
