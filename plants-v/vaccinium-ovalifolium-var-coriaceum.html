Vaccinium ovalifolium var. coriaceum

Vaccinium ovalifolium var. coriaceum, commonly known as the hardleaf blueberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are leathery and dark green, giving it its common name. Its flowers are bell-shaped and white, and it bears edible berries that are typically blue or red in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
