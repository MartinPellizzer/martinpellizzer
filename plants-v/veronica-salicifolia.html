Veronica salicifolia

Veronica salicifolia, commonly known as willowleaf speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, willow-like leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue or violet flowers during the summer months. It typically grows in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream sides.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to gardens and ecological restoration projects.
