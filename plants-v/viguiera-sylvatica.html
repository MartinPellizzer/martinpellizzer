Viguiera sylvatica

Viguiera sylvatica, commonly known as the woodland coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that are typically covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and pink to red petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Viguiera sylvatica thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.
