Viola metajaponica f. barbara

Viola metajaponica f. barbara, commonly known as the Japanese violet or Barbara's violet, is a cultivar of the viola genus native to Japan.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-15 cm and prefers well-drained, moist soil in partial shade. It is a hardy perennial that is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and shady borders.
