Viola aurea subsp. mohavensis

Viola aurea subsp.

mohavensis, commonly known as the Mohave violet, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States. It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers with five petals.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, adapting to harsh desert conditions. It is part of the Violaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
