Veronica ionantha

Veronica ionantha, commonly known as the mountain veronica, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Veronica ionantha thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
