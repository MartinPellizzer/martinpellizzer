Vitex turczaninowii f. puberula

Vitex turczaninowii f. puberula, commonly known as the pubescent vitex, is a shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, blooming in late summer. Its fruits are small, dry capsules that contain numerous seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to arid conditions.
