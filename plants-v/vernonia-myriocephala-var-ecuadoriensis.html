Vernonia myriocephala var. ecuadoriensis

Vernonia myriocephala var. ecuadoriensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, yellowish to reddish bracts. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in high-altitude environments with well-drained soils and is often found in shrublands and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
