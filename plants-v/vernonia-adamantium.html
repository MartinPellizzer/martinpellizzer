Vernonia adamantium

Vernonia adamantium, commonly known as the ironweed, is a striking perennial herb native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Vernonia adamantium is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in full sun and well-drained soils.

It is also valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
