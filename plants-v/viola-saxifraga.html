Viola saxifraga

Viola saxifraga, commonly known as the rock violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or blue-purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola saxifraga is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and early bloom.
