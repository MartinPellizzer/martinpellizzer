Viola yedoensis f. pubescens

Viola yedoensis f. pubescens, commonly known as the Japanese violet or the Edogawa violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces delicate violet flowers with five petals, often in shades of purple or lavender. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forested areas.

Its hirsute (hairy) foliage is a distinguishing characteristic of this particular cultivar.
