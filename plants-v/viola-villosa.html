Viola villosa

Viola villosa, commonly known as the hairy violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces violet-blue or purple flowers with five petals. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive pubescent (hairy) foliage.

Viola villosa is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
