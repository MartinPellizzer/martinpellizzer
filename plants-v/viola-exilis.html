Viola exilis

Viola exilis, commonly known as the slender violet, is a delicate flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of purple and violet. Viola exilis is a perennial species that thrives in cool climates and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking to add a touch of elegance to shaded garden areas.
