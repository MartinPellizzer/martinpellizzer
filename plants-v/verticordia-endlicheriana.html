Verticordia endlicheriana

Verticordia endlicheriana, commonly known as the "Cape York verticordia," is a striking member of the Myrtaceae family native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers, which bloom in spring and are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in heathlands or open woodlands. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
