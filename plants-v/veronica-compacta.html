Veronica compacta

Veronica compacta, commonly known as the dwarf speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to August. The plant has a dense, mat-like growth habit with slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Veronica compacta is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
