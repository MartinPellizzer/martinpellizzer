Viola tricolor subvar. hymettia

Viola tricolor subvar. hymettia, commonly known as the Hymettian violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Hymettus mountain range in Greece.

It produces vibrant violet, white, and yellow flowers in early spring, showcasing its tri-colored petals. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value and ease of cultivation contribute to its popularity among gardeners and botanists alike.
