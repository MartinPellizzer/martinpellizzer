Vatica bantamensis

Vatica bantamensis, commonly known as Bantam ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, rounded crown. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and usually have a waxy surface, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
