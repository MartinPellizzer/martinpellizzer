Vicia malosana

Vicia malosana, commonly known as the Malosan vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, typically found in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its compound leaves consist of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it has slender, hairy stems.

Vicia malosana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
