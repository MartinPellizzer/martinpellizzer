Vicia galilaea var. faboidea

Vicia galilaea var. faboidea, commonly known as the Galilean vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

This species is often found in Mediterranean climates and is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation.
