Vriesea carinata f. intermedia

Vriesea carinata f. intermedia, commonly known as the hybrid Vriesea, is a hybrid cultivar of the Vriesea genus native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated inflorescence that bears bright, colorful bracts resembling flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage consists of broad, glossy, sword-shaped leaves that are dark green with a waxy texture.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and ease of care.
