Viola fraterna

Viola fraterna, commonly known as the brother's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Viola fraterna is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
