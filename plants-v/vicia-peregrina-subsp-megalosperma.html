Vicia peregrina subsp. megalosperma

Vicia peregrina subsp.

megalosperma, commonly known as the giant bean vetch, is a leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves with a deep green color.

The plant produces elongated, pale green pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its nitrogen-fixing ability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
