Veronica spicata f. barrelieri

Veronica spicata f. barrelieri, commonly known as the Barrelier's speedwell, is a compact, low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and suitability in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
