Vernonia meiostephana

Vernonia meiostephana, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with hairy surfaces, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Vernonia meiostephana is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
