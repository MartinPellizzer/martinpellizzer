Valeriana capitata var. hookeri

Valeriana capitata var. hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has aromatic leaves and stems, which contain volatile oils responsible for its distinctive scent. It thrives in well-drained, loamy soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Valeriana capitata var. hookeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its calming properties.
