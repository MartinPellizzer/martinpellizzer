Vaccinium cumingianum

Vaccinium cumingianum, commonly known as the Cuming's blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, similar to other species in the Vaccinium genus. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for cultivation, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
