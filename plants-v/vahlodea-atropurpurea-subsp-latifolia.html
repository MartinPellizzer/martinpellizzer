Vahlodea atropurpurea subsp. latifolia

Vahlodea atropurpurea subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved vahlodea, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America. It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage and floral structure make it a distinctive species within the Polygonaceae family.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
