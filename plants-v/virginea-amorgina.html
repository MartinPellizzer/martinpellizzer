Virginea amorgina

Virginea amorgina, commonly known as the Amorgian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the island of Amorgos in the Aegean Sea.

It typically grows in rocky and coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are blue or violet, bell-shaped, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Virginea amorgina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
