Verbascum × bastardii

Verbascum × bastardii, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Verbascum blattaria and Verbascum blattifolium.

It is a tall, herbaceous plant that can grow up to 2 meters in height, characterized by its large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats such as roadsides and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in the genus Verbascum.
