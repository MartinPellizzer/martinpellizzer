Vaccinium yunnanense var. franchetianum

Vaccinium yunnanense var. franchetianum, commonly known as the Yunnan huckleberry, is a shrub native to southwestern China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of blue-black berries. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers shaded to semi-shaded environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
