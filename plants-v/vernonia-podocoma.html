Vernonia podocoma

Vernonia podocoma, commonly known as the silverleaf vernonia, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs that reflect sunlight. The plant produces small, cone-like flower heads that are typically yellow to brown in color, blooming in late summer to early autumn. Vernonia podocoma is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for arid landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental leaves make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
