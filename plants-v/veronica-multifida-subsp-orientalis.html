Veronica multifida subsp. orientalis

Veronica multifida subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the Eastern Hairy Veronica, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and slightly hairy, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for garden settings.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
