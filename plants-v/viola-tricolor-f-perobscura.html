Viola tricolor f. perobscura

Viola tricolor f. perobscura, commonly known as the violet tricolor, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive three-lobed structure, often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of violet, yellow, and white, typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden settings for its vibrant, colorful blooms.
