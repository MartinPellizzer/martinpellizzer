Verbena grandiflora

Verbena grandiflora, commonly known as the common verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces vibrant blue to violet flowers in late spring and summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Verbena grandiflora is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
