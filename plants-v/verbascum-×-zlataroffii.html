Verbascum × zlataroffii

Verbascum × zlataroffii, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes species known for their large, fuzzy leaves and showy yellow or orange flowers. The hybrid is characterized by its tall, erect stems and broad, velvety foliage that can reach up to 1 meter in height. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique appearance and hardy nature.
