Vincetoxicum himalaicum

Vincetoxicum himalaicum, commonly known as Himalayan false bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its stems are often hairy and can climb or trail along the ground.

Vincetoxicum himalaicum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and floral display.
