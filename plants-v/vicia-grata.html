Vicia grata

Vicia grata, commonly known as the pretty vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. The plant features slender, branching stems with small, compound leaves composed of oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are usually pale blue to purple, arranged in loose clusters, and are attractive to pollinators like bees.

Vicia grata is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
