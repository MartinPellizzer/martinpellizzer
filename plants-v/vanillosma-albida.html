Vanillosma albida

Vanillosma albida, commonly known as the white vanilla orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, white flowers with a subtle vanilla scent. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its leaves are broad and glossy, and it thrives in warm, moist conditions with indirect sunlight.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in greenhouses or botanical gardens.
