Veronica transsilvanica

Veronica transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian speedwell, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and blue to purple flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in dense clusters.

Veronica transsilvanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
