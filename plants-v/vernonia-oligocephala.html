Vernonia oligocephala

Vernonia oligocephala, commonly known as the narrow-headed ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pale yellow to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and sturdy.

Vernonia oligocephala is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
