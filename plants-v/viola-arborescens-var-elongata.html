Viola arborescens var. elongata

Viola arborescens var. elongata, commonly known as the creeping wood violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces showy violet-blue flowers with yellow markings, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
