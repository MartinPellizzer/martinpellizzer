Ventilago velutina

Ventilago velutina, commonly known as the velvet bushwillow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is known for its velvety, hairy foliage, which gives it its common name. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun.

Ventilago velutina is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of forage for livestock.
