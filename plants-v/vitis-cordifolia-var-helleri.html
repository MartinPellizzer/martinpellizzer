Vitis cordifolia var. helleri

Vitis cordifolia var. helleri, commonly known as Heller's heartleaf grape, is a variant of the heartleaf grape vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, sweet berries that are typically blue to black in color. The plant is a deciduous vine that thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight. It is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks, where it contributes to local biodiversity.

The berries are edible and have been used in traditional herbal remedies for their purported health benefits.
