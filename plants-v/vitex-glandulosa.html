Vitex glandulosa

Vitex glandulosa, commonly known as the Mexican chaste tree, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often glandular-dotted, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically lavender or white in color, arranged in terminal clusters. These flowers are followed by small, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

Vitex glandulosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
