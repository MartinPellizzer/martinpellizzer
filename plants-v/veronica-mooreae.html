Veronica mooreae

Veronica mooreae, commonly known as the Moore's speedwell, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
