Verbena hyderabadensis

Verbena hyderabadensis, commonly known as the Hyderabad verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant violet or blue flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous annual or perennial, depending on the climate. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are arranged in dense spikes.

Verbena hyderabadensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
