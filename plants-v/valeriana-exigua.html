Valeriana exigua

Valeriana exigua, commonly known as the narrowleaf valerian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Valeriana exigua is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its calming effects.
