Viola smithiana

Viola smithiana, commonly known as the Smith's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Viola smithiana is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators.
