Viola ucriana

Viola ucriana, commonly known as the Brazilian violet, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and woodland areas. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces violet or purple flowers with five petals. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.

Viola ucriana is also known for its ability to thrive in humid climates and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
