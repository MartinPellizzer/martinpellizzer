Valeriana galeottiana

Valeriana galeottiana, commonly known as the Alpine valerian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, often at elevations above 1,500 meters. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a strong, aromatic scent.

Valeriana galeottiana is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
