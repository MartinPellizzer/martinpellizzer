Vigna reflexopilosa

Vigna reflexopilosa, commonly known as the hairy cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional seeds.

Vigna reflexopilosa is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting subsequent crops.
