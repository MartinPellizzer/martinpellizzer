Vernonia squamulosa

Vernonia squamulosa, commonly known as the silverleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and prairies. Its leaves are covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia squamulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and serves as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
