Vitis-idaea punctata

Vitis-idaea punctata, commonly known as the dwarf grapevine or red berries, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually red or orange in color, especially in the fall. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is commonly found in forested areas.

Vitis-idaea punctata is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to provide habitat for various wildlife species.
