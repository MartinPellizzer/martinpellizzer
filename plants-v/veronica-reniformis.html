Veronica reniformis

Veronica reniformis, commonly known as the kidneyleaf speedwell, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
