Vallisneria michelii

Vallisneria michelii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf waterweed, is a freshwater aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface, with shorter leaves submerged beneath the surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically submerged, followed by the development of elongated, cylindrical fruits. Vallisneria michelii is often found in still or slow-moving waters, such as ponds and lakes, where it forms dense underwater vegetation.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
