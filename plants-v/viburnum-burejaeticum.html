Viburnum burejaeticum

Viburnum burejaeticum, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It typically grows to a height of 2-4 meters and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Viburnum burejaeticum prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile addition to temperate climates.
