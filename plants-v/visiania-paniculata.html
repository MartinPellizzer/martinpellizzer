Visiania paniculata

Visiania paniculata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Erythroxylum paniculatum*, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its showy, red or pink inflorescences that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed, and it plays a role in attracting various bird species.
