Vitis vinifera var. amurensis

Vitis vinifera var. amurensis, commonly known as the Amur grape, is a cold-hardy vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and ability to thrive in temperate to subarctic climates. The plant produces small, tart berries that are often used in local cuisines or for making wine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and provide dense foliage, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

This variety is also valued for its adaptability to poor soil conditions and resistance to certain pests.
