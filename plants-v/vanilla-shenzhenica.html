Vanilla shenzhenica

Vanilla shenzhenica, commonly known as the Chinese vanilla, is a climbing vine native to southern China.

It produces fragrant, dark brown pods that are harvested and processed to make vanilla extract. The plant belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and showy, yellowish-green flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires a trellis or support for its growth.

The pods are typically cured and dried to develop their rich aroma and flavor.
