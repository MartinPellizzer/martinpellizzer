Vernonia brasiliensis

Vernonia brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture.

Vernonia brasiliensis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in horticulture.
