Vasconcellia acutifolia

Vasconcellia acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved vasconcellia, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. Its fruit is a capsule that releases tiny seeds when mature.

Vasconcellia acutifolia is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.
