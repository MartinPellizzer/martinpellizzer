Valeriana jasminoides

Valeriana jasminoides, commonly known as the jasmine valerian, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Valeriana jasminoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant scent.
