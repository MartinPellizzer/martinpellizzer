Vignea pseudoschreberi

Vignea pseudoschreberi, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits.

Vignea pseudoschreberi is often found in lowland rainforests and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan.
