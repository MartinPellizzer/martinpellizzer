Viola tricolor subsp. bellioides

Viola tricolor subsp.

bellioides, commonly known as the heartleaf viola, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

The plant produces small, colorful flowers in shades of violet, yellow, and white, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
