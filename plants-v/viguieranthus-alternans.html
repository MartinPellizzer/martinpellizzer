Viguieranthus alternans

Viguieranthus alternans, commonly known as the zigzag vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, alternating pattern of leaf arrangement, which gives it its scientific name. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
