Vernonia pratensis

Vernonia pratensis, commonly known as meadow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and prairies, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are purple to pink in color.

Vernonia pratensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
