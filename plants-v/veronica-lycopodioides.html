Veronica lycopodioides

Veronica lycopodioides, commonly known as the common speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its low-growing habit with hairy stems and small, blue to purple flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
