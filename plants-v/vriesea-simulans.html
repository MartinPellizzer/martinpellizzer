Vriesea simulans

Vriesea simulans, commonly known as the pink vriesea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescence. The plant features a rosette of broad, waxy leaves that can vary in color from green to bronze. Its flowers, which bloom in shades of pink and white, are borne on a central spike and can last for several months.

Vriesea simulans is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
