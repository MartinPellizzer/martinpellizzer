Vesquella acuminata

Vesquella acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed vesquella, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its roots are fibrous and have been traditionally used in local herbal remedies for their purported medicinal properties.

Vesquella acuminata thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and woodland borders.
