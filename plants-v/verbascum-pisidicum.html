Verbascum pisidicum

Verbascum pisidicum, commonly known as the Turkish mullein, is a perennial plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of soft, woolly hair. The plant produces yellow flowers in the summer, which are clustered in a loose raceme at the top of the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Verbascum pisidicum is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its soothing effects on respiratory ailments.
