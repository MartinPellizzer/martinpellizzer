Vaccinium praeces

Vaccinium praeces, commonly known as the sweet blueberry, is a shrub native to the northern hemisphere.

It produces small, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and vitamins. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. Its leaves are typically dark green and elliptical in shape, while its flowers are white and bell-shaped.

Vaccinium praeces is widely cultivated for both commercial and ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic value and nutritional benefits.
