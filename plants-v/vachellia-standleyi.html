Vachellia standleyi

Vachellia standleyi, commonly known as the red acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorny stems and bipinnate leaves that are arranged in a flat, feathery pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish to orange flowers in spring, followed by long, flat pods that contain edible seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important component of local ecosystems.

Vachellia standleyi is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
