Vitis bioritsensis

Vitis bioritsensis, commonly known as the Bioritsensis grapevine, is a rare and lesser-known species of vine native to specific regions in Central Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family, which includes many economically important grape species. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, compound leaves, and small, clustered flowers that precede the development of its berries. Vitis bioritsensis is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique genetic makeup and potential for cultivating new grape varieties.

However, due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it remains a subject of limited study and conservation concern.
