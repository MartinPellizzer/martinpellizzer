Vaccinium madagascariense

Vaccinium madagascariense, commonly known as the Madagascar huckleberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or purple in color, which are used in local cuisines and traditional medicine. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
