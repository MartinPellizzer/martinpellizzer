Vigna sahyadriana

Vigna sahyadriana, commonly known as the Western Ghats bean, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky hillsides. Vigna sahyadriana is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an important species in the biodiversity of the region.
