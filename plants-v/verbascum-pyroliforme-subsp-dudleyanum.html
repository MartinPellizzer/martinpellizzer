Verbascum pyroliforme subsp. dudleyanum

Verbascum pyroliforme subsp.

dudleyanum, commonly known as Dudley's mullein, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It belongs to the mullein genus, characterized by its fuzzy, woolly leaves and tall flowering stems.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are yellow and clustered in a cylindrical spike, blooming in late summer.

It is often found in Mediterranean climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
