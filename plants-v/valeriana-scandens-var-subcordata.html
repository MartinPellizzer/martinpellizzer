Valeriana scandens var. subcordata

Valeriana scandens var. subcordata, commonly known as the creeping valerian, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, opposite leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows along the ground, forming dense mats and is often found in moist, woodland environments.
