Vulpia octoflora var. hirtella

Vulpia octoflora var. hirtella, commonly known as hairy vulpia, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils such as roadsides, fields, and sandy areas. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single, dense spikelet at the top of the stem, which contains small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The species is characterized by its adaptability to poor soil conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in various ecosystems.
