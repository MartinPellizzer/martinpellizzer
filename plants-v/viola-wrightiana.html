Viola wrightiana

Viola wrightiana, commonly known as Wright's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola wrightiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
