Verbena glutinosa

Verbena glutinosa, commonly known as sticky verbena, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant produces a sticky resin, which gives it its common name and has historically been used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Verbena glutinosa thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
