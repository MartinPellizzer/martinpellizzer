Verbascum thapsus var. australe

Verbascum thapsus var. australe, commonly known as the Australian mullein, is a variant of the common mullein plant.

It is a perennial herb native to southern Australia, characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
