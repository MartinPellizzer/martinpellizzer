Vicia jaubertii

Vicia jaubertii, commonly known as Jaubert's vetch, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters, which are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Vicia jaubertii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
