Vernonia hirsutivena

Vernonia hirsutivena, commonly known as the hairy Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its flowers are usually yellow or orange, arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savanna habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
