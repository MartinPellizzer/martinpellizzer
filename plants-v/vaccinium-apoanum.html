Vaccinium apoanum

Vaccinium apoanum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its small, dark blue berries are edible and often used in local cuisines.
