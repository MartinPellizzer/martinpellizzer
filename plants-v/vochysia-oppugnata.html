Vochysia oppugnata

Vochysia oppugnata, commonly known as the "spiny chandelier" or "candelabra tree," is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive branched, spiny trunk and dense clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces yellow or orange fruits that contain edible seeds, making it valuable in local ecosystems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Vochysia oppugnata is also noted for its ornamental value and use in traditional medicine.
