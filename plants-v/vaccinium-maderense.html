Vaccinium maderense

Vaccinium maderense, commonly known as the Mader's blueberry, is a species of shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of blue-black berries. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces edible fruits that are rich in antioxidants.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
