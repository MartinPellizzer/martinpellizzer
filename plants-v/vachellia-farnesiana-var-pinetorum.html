Vachellia farnesiana var. pinetorum

Vachellia farnesiana var. pinetorum, commonly known as the gum tree or acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and long, slender pods that contain the seeds. The plant is adapted to hot, dry climates and has a deep root system for water absorption. It produces a resinous gum that has been used traditionally for medicinal and industrial purposes.

Its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions makes it an important pioneer species in ecological succession.
