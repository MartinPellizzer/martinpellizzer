Viola × luganensis

Viola × luganensis, commonly known as the Dutch viola, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive blooms and foliage.

Viola × luganensis is native to Europe and is commonly found in gardens and woodland edges.
