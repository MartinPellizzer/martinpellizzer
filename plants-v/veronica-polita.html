Veronica polita

Veronica polita, commonly known as the smooth speedwell, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically smooth and lance-shaped. The plant produces clusters of blue to violet flowers with four petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Veronica polita is a perennial herb that is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to support local pollinators.
