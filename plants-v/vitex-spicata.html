Vitex spicata

Vitex spicata, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that appear in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually gray-green in color and have a fuzzy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Vitex spicata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
