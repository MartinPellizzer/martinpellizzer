Viola cadevallii

Viola cadevallii, commonly known as the Cadevall's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the landscape.

Viola cadevallii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
