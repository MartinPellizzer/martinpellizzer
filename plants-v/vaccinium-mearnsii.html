Vaccinium mearnsii

Vaccinium mearnsii, commonly known as the Australian heath, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in coastal and heathland environments. It produces edible berries that are often used in jams and culinary applications.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
