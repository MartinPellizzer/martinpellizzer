Viola rubella

Viola rubella, commonly known as the red violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Viola rubella is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks, where it forms dense mats of foliage.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
