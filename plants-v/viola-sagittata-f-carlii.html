Viola sagittata f. carlii

Viola sagittata f. carlii, commonly known as the Carlii violet, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces delicate, violet-blue flowers with a yellow center. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 inches and thrives in partial to full shade. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and is often found in woodland habitats.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in gardens.
