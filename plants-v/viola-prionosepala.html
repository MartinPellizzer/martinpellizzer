Viola prionosepala

Viola prionosepala, commonly known as the narrow-sepaled violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its sepals are narrow and distinct, giving it its specific epithet.

Viola prionosepala is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
