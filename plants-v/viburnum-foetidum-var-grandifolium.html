Viburnum foetidum var. grandifolium

Viburnum foetidum var. grandifolium, commonly known as the sweet pepper bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and showy fruit display.
