Vittaria pluridichotoma

Vittaria pluridichotoma, commonly known as the forked moss, is a species of liverwort belonging to the family Pterobryaceae.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves that are typically arranged in two rows along the stem. The plant exhibits a dichotomous branching pattern, which is a key feature in its classification. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on rocks or soil in temperate regions.

Its simple morphology and reproductive structures make it an important subject in the study of bryophyte ecology.
