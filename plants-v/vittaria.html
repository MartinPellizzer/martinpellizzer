Vittaria

Vittaria, commonly known as the "liverworts," is a genus of small, non-vascular plants belonging to the family Vittariaceae.

These plants are typically found in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices, where they thrive in humid conditions. Vittaria species are characterized by their simple, lobed leaves that often form dense mats, and they lack true roots, stems, and vascular tissue. Their reproductive structures, called gametophytes, are usually small and inconspicuous, relying on water for the dispersal of their spores.

Vittaria plays an important ecological role in nutrient cycling and soil stabilization in tropical and subtropical ecosystems.
