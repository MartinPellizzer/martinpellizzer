Verbascum nigrum subsp. alopecuros

Verbascum nigrum subsp.

alopecuros, commonly known as black mullein, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in dense, fuzzy hairs that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and also covered in a thick layer of soft, woolly hairs.

It thrives in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and roadsides.
