Vincetoxicum amplexicaule

Vincetoxicum amplexicaule, commonly known as the creeping false bindweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that clasp the stems, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its berries are typically red or orange and contain seeds that are dispersed by animals.

Vincetoxicum amplexicaule is often considered a weed due to its aggressive growth and ability to spread rapidly in disturbed soils.
