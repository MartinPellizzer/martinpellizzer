Vernonia parvuliceps

Vernonia parvuliceps, commonly known as the small-headed ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its small, blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting various pollinators.

Vernonia parvuliceps is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.
