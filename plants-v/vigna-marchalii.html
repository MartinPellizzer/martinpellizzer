Vigna marchalii

Vigna marchalii, commonly known as the African bean or African cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, producing trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. The plant thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its edible seeds. Vigna marchalii is also valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.

Its botanical features make it an important crop in both traditional and modern agricultural systems.
