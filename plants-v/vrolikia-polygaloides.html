Vrolikia polygaloides

Vrolikia polygaloides, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a slender pod that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

Vrolikia polygaloides is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
