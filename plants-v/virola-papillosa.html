Virola papillosa

Virola papillosa, commonly known as the pink mahogany, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil, Peru, and Colombia.

Its heartwood is highly valued for its rich reddish-brown color and durability, making it a sought-after timber species.

However, due to overharvesting and habitat loss, Virola papillosa is now considered vulnerable in many parts of its range.
