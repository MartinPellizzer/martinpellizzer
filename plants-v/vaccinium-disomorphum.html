Vaccinium disomorphum

Vaccinium disomorphum, commonly known as the dwarf blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are oval-shaped, leathery, and remain green throughout the year.

Vaccinium disomorphum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes.
