Viola sagittata var. hicksii

Viola sagittata var. hicksii, commonly known as the Hick's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet-blue coloration and produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of purple and violet. Its botanical characteristics include a short, fibrous root system and a robust stem that supports its foliage.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
