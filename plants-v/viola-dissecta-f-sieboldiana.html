Viola dissecta f. sieboldiana

Viola dissecta f. sieboldiana, commonly known as the Siebold's violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped, deeply lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of showy violet flowers with distinctive white markings on the petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in woodland gardens.
