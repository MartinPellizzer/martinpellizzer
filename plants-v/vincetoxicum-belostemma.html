Vincetoxicum belostemma

Vincetoxicum belostemma, commonly known as the creeping false bindweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged opposite along the stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Vincetoxicum belostemma is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its aggressive spreading habit.
