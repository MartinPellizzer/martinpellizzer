Vernonia diversifolia f. catatii

Vernonia diversifolia f. catatii, commonly known as the catatii vernonia, is a variant of the Vernonia genus native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. The plant is typically shrub-like, with a compact growth habit and can thrive in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and unique floral structure.

This form is a botanical curiosity within the Vernonia species, showcasing natural variation in plant morphology.
