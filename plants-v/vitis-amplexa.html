Vitis amplexa

Vitis amplexa, commonly known as the climbing grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit, with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into clusters of berries. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Vitis amplexa is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is often found in woodland areas.
