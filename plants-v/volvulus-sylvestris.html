Volvulus sylvestris

Volvulus sylvestris, commonly known as the forest helix, is a unique plant species characterized by its spiral-shaped leaves.

Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant's leaves are often covered in a fine, waxy coating that helps retain moisture. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically in shades of white or pale blue.

Volvulus sylvestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
