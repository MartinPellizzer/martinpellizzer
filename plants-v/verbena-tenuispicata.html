Verbena tenuispicata

Verbena tenuispicata, commonly known as narrow-leaved verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and clusters of small, sky-blue to purple flowers. The plant is part of the Verbenaceae family and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Verbena tenuispicata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
