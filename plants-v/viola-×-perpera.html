Viola × perpera

Viola × perpera, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant is native to Europe and has been widely cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Viola × perpera is often used in gardens for its ability to naturalize and provide early season color.
