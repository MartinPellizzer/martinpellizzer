Vicia vexillaris

Vicia vexillaris, commonly known as the lesser vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white pea-like flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Vicia vexillaris is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
