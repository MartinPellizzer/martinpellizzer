Verbesina elgalloana

Verbesina elgalloana, commonly known as the yellow beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its rough, hairy stems and leaves are adapted to thrive in dry, sunny environments.

Verbesina elgalloana is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
