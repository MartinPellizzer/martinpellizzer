Veronica arvensis var. demissa

Veronica arvensis var. demissa, commonly known as the downy speedwell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, blue to purple flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
