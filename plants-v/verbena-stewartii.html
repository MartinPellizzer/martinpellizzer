Verbena stewartii

Verbena stewartii, commonly known as Stewart's verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically blooms in shades of blue, violet, or purple, with a distinctive tubular corolla that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

Verbena stewartii is drought-tolerant and is often used in xeriscaping due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
