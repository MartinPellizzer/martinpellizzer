Vitis planicaulis

Vitis planicaulis, commonly known as the flat-stemmed grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to parts of eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. The plant is characterized by its flattened, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive hairy underside. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into clusters of edible berries.

Vitis planicaulis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
