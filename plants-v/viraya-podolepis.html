Viraya podolepis

Viraya podolepis, commonly known as the yellow-veined mountain grass, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, sword-shaped leaves with prominent yellow veins, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
