Veronica stenophylla

Veronica stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with opposite, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
