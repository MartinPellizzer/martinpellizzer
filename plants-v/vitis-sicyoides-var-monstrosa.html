Vitis sicyoides var. monstrosa

Vitis sicyoides var. monstrosa, commonly known as the giant grapevine, is a variant of the Sicyoides grapevine native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and large, waxy leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance.

This variety is also valued for its potential in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye.
