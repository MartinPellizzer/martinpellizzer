Varronia calyptrata

Varronia calyptrata, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, giving it its common name. The plant has compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets, and its bark is smooth and often reddish in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental tree in warm climates.

Varronia calyptrata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
