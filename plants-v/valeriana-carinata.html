Valeriana carinata

Valeriana carinata, commonly known as the spiked valerian, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has serrated, lance-shaped leaves and produces distinctive, three-lobed fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly areas.

Valeriana carinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
