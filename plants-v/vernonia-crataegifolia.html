Vernonia crataegifolia

Vernonia crataegifolia, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, shrub-like species that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

Vernonia crataegifolia is valued for its hard, dense wood, which is used in traditional crafts and construction.
