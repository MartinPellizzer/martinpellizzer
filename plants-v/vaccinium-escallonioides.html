Vaccinium escallonioides

Vaccinium escallonioides, commonly known as the Escallonia cranberry, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and heathers. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and produces small, red berries that are edible. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist environments.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
