Veronica beccabunga var. americana

Veronica beccabunga var. americana, commonly known as American speedwell, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and marshes. It has slender, upright stems and opposite leaves that are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
