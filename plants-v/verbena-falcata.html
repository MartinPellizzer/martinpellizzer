Verbena falcata

Verbena falcata, commonly known as narrowleaf verbena, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, arranged in spikes at the ends of the branches. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.

Verbena falcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
