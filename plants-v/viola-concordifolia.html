Viola concordifolia

Viola concordifolia, commonly known as the Concordifolia Violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, facilitating its spread in natural environments.

Viola concordifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
