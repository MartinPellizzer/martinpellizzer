Vitex agnus-castus var. negundoides

Vitex agnus-castus var. negundoides, commonly known as the European hop-hornbeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and smooth, greyish bark that becomes fissured with age. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and structural form.

This variety is closely related to the common vitex and shares similar botanical features.
