Vatica lepidota

Vatica lepidota, commonly known as the glossy ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is typically found in lowland rainforests. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes.

The tree is valued for its durable timber and plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
