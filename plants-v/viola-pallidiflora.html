Viola pallidiflora

Viola pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pale violet or white flowers with five petals, often adorned with a distinctive yellowish throat. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Viola pallidiflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
