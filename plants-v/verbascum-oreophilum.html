Verbascum oreophilum

Verbascum oreophilum, commonly known as the alpine mullein, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of soft, woolly hair. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Verbascum oreophilum is often found in alpine meadows and is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family.
