Viguiera paneroana

Viguiera paneroana, commonly known as Mexican ironweed, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its foliage is hairy and aromatic, with lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color.

Viguiera paneroana produces showy purple to pink flower heads in the fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
