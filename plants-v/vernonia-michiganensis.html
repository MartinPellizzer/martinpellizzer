Vernonia michiganensis

Vernonia michiganensis, commonly known as Michigan ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stature and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Vernonia michiganensis plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
