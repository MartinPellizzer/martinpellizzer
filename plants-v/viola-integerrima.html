Viola integerrima

Viola integerrima, commonly known as the violetleaf violet, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or woodland areas. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola integerrima is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
