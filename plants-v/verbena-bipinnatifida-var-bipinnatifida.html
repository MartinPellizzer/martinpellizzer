Verbena bipinnatifida var. bipinnatifida

Verbena bipinnatifida var. bipinnatifida, commonly known as the purple verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, tubular, violet-blue flowers. The plant has finely divided, feathery leaves that are often gray-green in color, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The species is known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
