Valeriana excelsa var. tripteroides

Valeriana excelsa var. tripteroides, commonly known as the three-winged valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a strong, often disagreeable scent. This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
