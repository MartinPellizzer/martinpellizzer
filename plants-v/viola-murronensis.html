Viola murronensis

Viola murronensis, commonly known as the Murron violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, particularly in the Pyrenees and the Alps. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, with five petals arranged symmetrically. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of pubescence.

Viola murronensis is often found in rocky or grassy habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
