Virgulus ericoides var. pansus

Virgulus ericoides var. pansus, commonly known as the creeping heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the wet heathlands of western Europe.

It features dense, compact foliage with small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or maritime climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in challenging environments.
