Vigna ulugurensis

Vigna ulugurensis, commonly known as the Uluguru bean, is a leguminous plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds used for food. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is adapted to poor soil conditions.

Vigna ulugurensis is valued for its nutritional content and is an important crop in local agriculture.
