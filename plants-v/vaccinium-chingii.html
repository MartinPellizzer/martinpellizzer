Vaccinium chingii

Vaccinium chingii, commonly known as the Chinese huckleberry, is a shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many species of heather and blueberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional Chinese cuisine. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a leathery texture and a glossy appearance.

Vaccinium chingii thrives in acidic soils and is often found in forested areas with high humidity.
