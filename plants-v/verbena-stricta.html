Verbena stricta

Verbena stricta, commonly known as narrowleaf verbena, is an annual plant native to the southern United States.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 3 feet tall. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It produces clusters of small, bright blue or purple flowers that bloom from summer to fall.

This plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
