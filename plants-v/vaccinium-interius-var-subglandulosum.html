Vaccinium interius var. subglandulosum

Vaccinium interius var. subglandulosum, commonly known as the glandular blueberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are elliptical with a glossy upper surface and a hairy underside, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to support local wildlife.
