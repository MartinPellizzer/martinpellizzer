Vulpia alopecuros var. glabrata

Vulpia alopecuros var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth vulpia, is a grass species characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and fine, needle-like leaves.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as sandy soils and disturbed areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are usually hairless, giving it the "smooth" descriptor in its botanical name.

This variety is often found in temperate regions and is an important component of pioneer plant communities.
