Viola × perpensa

Viola × perpensa, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and gardens. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Viola × perpensa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
