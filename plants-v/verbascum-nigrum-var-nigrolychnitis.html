Verbascum nigrum var. nigrolychnitis

Verbascum nigrum var. nigrolychnitis, commonly known as black beard mullein, is a variant of the common mullein plant.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. The plant is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in dense, soft down. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in clusters at the top of the plant.

This variety is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is known for its medicinal and ornamental uses.
