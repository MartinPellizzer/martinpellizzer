Viola tricolor var. monticola

Viola tricolor var. monticola, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Violaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers bloom in early spring and are pollinated by bees and other insects.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
