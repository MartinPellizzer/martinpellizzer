Vigna unguiculata var. dekindtiana

Vigna unguiculata var. dekindtiana, commonly known as the African cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in warm climates and is often grown as a cover crop or for its nutritional value.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, improving soil fertility.
