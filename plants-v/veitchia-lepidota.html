Veitchia lepidota

Veitchia lepidota, commonly known as the velvet bamboo, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that resemble bamboo, covered in a dense, velvety layer of short, stiff hairs. The plant produces large, paddle-shaped leaves that are dark green and often have a metallic sheen. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for tropical and subtropical horticulture.
