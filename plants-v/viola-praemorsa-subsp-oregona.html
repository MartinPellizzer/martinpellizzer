Viola praemorsa subsp. oregona

Viola praemorsa subsp.

oregona, commonly known as the Oregon violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streamside areas.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces delicate violet flowers with five petals. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in moist soils along the Oregon coast.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
