Viguiera montana

Viguiera montana, commonly known as Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces showy, orange to red ray flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Viguiera montana is typically found in dry, sunny habitats like grasslands and rocky soils.

It is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and vibrant floral display.
