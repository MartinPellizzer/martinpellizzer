Veronica brachysepala

Veronica brachysepala, commonly known as the broad-leaf speedwell, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy coating.

Veronica brachysepala is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
