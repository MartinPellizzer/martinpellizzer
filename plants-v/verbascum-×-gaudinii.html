Verbascum × gaudinii

Verbascum × gaudinii, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes various species of mullein known for their fuzzy, velvety leaves. The hybrid is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties, though it is primarily cultivated for ornamental purposes.
