Vernonia corallophila

Vernonia corallophila, commonly known as the coral-branch vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils. Its striking yellow-orange inflorescences are often used in traditional medicine and horticulture.

Vernonia corallophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in supporting local biodiversity.
