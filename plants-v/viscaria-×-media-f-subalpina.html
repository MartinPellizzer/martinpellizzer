Viscaria × media f. subalpina

Viscaria × media f. subalpina, commonly known as the Alpine mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its dense rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is native to alpine regions of Europe. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported soothing properties.
