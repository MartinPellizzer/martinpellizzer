Vangueria loranthifolia subsp. salaensis

Vangueria loranthifolia subsp.

salaensis, commonly known as the African mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and typically grows on the branches of host trees such as Acacia and Combretum.

The plant has small, opposite leaves and produces white or pale yellow flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are edible and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting birdlife and its traditional uses in local medicine.
