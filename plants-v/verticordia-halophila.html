Verticordia halophila

Verticordia halophila, commonly known as the saltbush verticordia, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in saline and coastal environments, demonstrating its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are typically narrow and fleshy, which helps it retain moisture in arid conditions.

Verticordia halophila is also noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for native pollinators.
