Vismia ramuliflora

Vismia ramuliflora, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, often giving it a striking appearance. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. It produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Vismia ramuliflora thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
