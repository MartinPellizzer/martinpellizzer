Viola anomala

Viola anomala, commonly known as the common violet, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet-blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and meadows. It has a short stem with opposite leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Viola anomala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
