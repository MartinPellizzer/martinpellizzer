Viola umbraticola

Viola umbraticola, commonly known as the shade viola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate forests of Europe.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and has heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces delicate violet flowers in shades of purple and blue, typically blooming in spring. Viola umbraticola is well adapted to low light conditions and is often found growing beneath the canopy of deciduous trees.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for woodland gardens and ecological restoration projects.
