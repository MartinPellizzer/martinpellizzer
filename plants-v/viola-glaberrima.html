Viola glaberrima

Viola glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring and are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola glaberrima is known for its smooth, hairless stems and leaves, which give it its common name.
