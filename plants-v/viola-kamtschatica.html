Viola kamtschatica

Viola kamtschatica, commonly known as the Kamchatka violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Viola kamtschatica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
