Vitex seineri

Vitex seineri, commonly known as the narrowleaf chaste tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in clusters, typically in shades of purple or blue, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy flowers and autumn foliage.
