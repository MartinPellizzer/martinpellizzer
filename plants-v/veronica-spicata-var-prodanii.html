Veronica spicata var. prodanii

Veronica spicata var. prodanii, commonly known as the purple loosestrife, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and spikes of blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
