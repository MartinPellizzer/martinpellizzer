Viguiera dentata var. brevipes

Viguiera dentata var. brevipes, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping and native gardens.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
