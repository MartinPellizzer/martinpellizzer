Vernonia araguensis

Vernonia araguensis, commonly known as the Brazilian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Vernonia araguensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
