Viola comollia

Viola comollia, commonly known as the purple heart viola, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly in regions like Virginia and North Carolina. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple-tinged underside and produces showy violet flowers with yellow centers. It thrives in woodland and moist environments, often growing in partial shade.

Viola comollia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
