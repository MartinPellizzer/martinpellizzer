Veronica spicata subsp. incana

Veronica spicata subsp.

incana, commonly known as the hairy loosestrife, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

This perennial plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of garden settings.
