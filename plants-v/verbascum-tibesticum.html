Verbascum tibesticum

Verbascum tibesticum, commonly known as the Tibetan mullein, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves and yellow flowers. The plant thrives in arid and rocky environments, often growing at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its leaves are used traditionally in herbal medicine for their soothing properties.

Verbascum tibesticum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in high-altitude ecosystems.
