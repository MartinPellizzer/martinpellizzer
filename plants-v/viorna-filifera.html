Viorna filifera

Viorna filifera, commonly known as the threadleaf viorna, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, compact formation. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Viorna filifera thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, arid climates.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
