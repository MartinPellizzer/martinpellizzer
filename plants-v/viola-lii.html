Viola lii

Viola lii, commonly known as the slender violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola lii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and foliage.
