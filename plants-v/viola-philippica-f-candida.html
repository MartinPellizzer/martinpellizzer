Viola philippica f. candida

Viola philippica f. candida, commonly known as the white viola, is a flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its pale or white petals, which contrast with its dark green, heart-shaped leaves. Native to the eastern United States, this perennial plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The flowers bloom in early spring, producing a delicate and ornamental display.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and ability to thrive in cool, humid conditions.
