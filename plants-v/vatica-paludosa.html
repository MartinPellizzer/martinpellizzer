Vatica paludosa

Vatica paludosa, commonly known as the swamp ash, is a tropical tree species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often drooping branches and smooth, greyish-brown bark. The plant thrives in wet, swampy environments, often found in lowland and hill forest areas. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptic in shape, with a glossy upper surface.

Vatica paludosa produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody capsules that contain numerous seeds.
