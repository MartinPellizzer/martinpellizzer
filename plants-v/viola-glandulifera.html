Viola glandulifera

Viola glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant is native to regions in Europe and parts of Asia, often growing in woodland areas and meadows. Viola glandulifera typically has glandular hairs on its stems and leaves, which can produce aromatic compounds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
