Verbascum capitis-viridis

Verbascum capitis-viridis, commonly known as the greenhead mullein, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a spike at the top of the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands.

Verbascum capitis-viridis is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its soothing effects.
