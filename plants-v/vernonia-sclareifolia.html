Vernonia sclareifolia

Vernonia sclareifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf ironweed, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces upright spikes of small, blue to purple flowers during the late spring and summer months. It is often found in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
