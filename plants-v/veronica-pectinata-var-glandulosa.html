Veronica pectinata var. glandulosa

Veronica pectinata var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It features basal rosettes of ovate, hairy leaves and produces clusters of blue to purple flowers with five petals. The plant is characterized by its glandular hairs, which give it its specific variety name. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

The flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
