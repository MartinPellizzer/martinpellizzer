Viola egglestonii

Viola egglestonii, commonly known as the Eggleston's violet, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and intricate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to its natural habitat.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Viola egglestonii is considered a vulnerable species.
