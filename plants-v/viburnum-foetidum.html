Viburnum foetidum

Viburnum foetidum, commonly known as the stinkbush or sweet viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five ovate leaflets, and its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pink, appearing in clusters. The plant produces red or black berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage and seasonal color changes.
