Veratrum maximum

Veratrum maximum, commonly known as the Indian cowbane, is a tall, herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features large, broad leaves that are often bluish-green in color and can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces clusters of white to pale yellow flowers in late summer, which are arranged in umbels. Its roots and stems contain toxic alkaloids, making it a plant with both ecological and historical significance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
