Viola arvensis subsp. patens

Viola arvensis subsp.

patens, commonly known as the common violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet or purple coloration. Its flowers are usually purple or blue, with five petals and a spurred lower petal.

It is known for its ornamental value and is often found in gardens and natural habitats.
