Vernonia wetarensis

Vernonia wetarensis, commonly known as the Wetar ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the island of Wetar in Indonesia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its erect, spindly stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Vernonia wetarensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local communities.
