Viola × crassula

Viola × crassula, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, five-petaled flowers that come in shades of purple, violet, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

Viola × crassula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
