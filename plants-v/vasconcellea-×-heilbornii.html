Vasconcellea × heilbornii

Vasconcellea × heilbornii, commonly known as the hybrid banana plant, is a member of the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and elongated, clustered inflorescences.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Vasconcellea species and Musa species, resulting in a plant with a unique combination of traits. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, warm climates with well-drained soil. Its fruit, while not typically cultivated for consumption, is sometimes used in local cuisines.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits genetic variability and is of interest in botanical research and plant breeding programs.
