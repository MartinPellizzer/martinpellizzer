Vittaria lloydiifolia

Vittaria lloydiifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Vittariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves that are often clustered in a rosette formation. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on trees or rocks in the understory of rainforests. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, which helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Vittaria lloydiifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
