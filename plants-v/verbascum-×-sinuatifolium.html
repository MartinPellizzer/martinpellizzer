Verbascum × sinuatifolium

Verbascum × sinuatifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved mullein, is a hybrid plant in the genus Verbascum, native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, velvety, gray-green leaves that are broad and heart-shaped. The plant produces tall, erect stems topped with clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
