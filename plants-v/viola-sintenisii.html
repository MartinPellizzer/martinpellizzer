Viola sintenisii

Viola sintenisii, commonly known as the Sintenis violet, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and small, often purple or violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Viola sintenisii is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and scientific study.
