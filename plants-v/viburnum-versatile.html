Viburnum versatile

Viburnum versatile, commonly known as the common viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the autumn. Viburnum versatile is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in partial to full sun.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
