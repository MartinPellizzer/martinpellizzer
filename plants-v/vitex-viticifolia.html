Vitex viticifolia

Vitex viticifolia, commonly known as the grapevine vitex or blue mistflower, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces showy, fragrant, blue-violet flowers in panicles during the summer months. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.

Vitex viticifolia is also valued for its hardiness and adaptability to a variety of soil types.
