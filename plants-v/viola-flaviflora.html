Viola flaviflora

Viola flaviflora, commonly known as the yellow-flowered violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, showy flowers with yellow petals and a distinctive violet center, blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Viola flaviflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
