Verbena echinata

Verbena echinata, commonly known as the spiked verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 feet. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture.

Verbena echinata is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
