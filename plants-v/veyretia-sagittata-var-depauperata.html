Veyretia sagittata var. depauperata

Veyretia sagittata var. depauperata, commonly known as the poorleaf arrowleaf, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, arrow-shaped leaves that are often reduced in size compared to the typical species. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
