Viola heterophylla subsp. ilvensis

Viola heterophylla subsp.

ilvensis, commonly known as the Ilven's violet, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its variable leaf morphology, with leaves that can be either entire or lobed depending on the habitat.

The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, often with yellow markings. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
