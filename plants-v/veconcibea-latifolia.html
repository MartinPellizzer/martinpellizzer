Veconcibea latifolia

Veconcibea latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf veconcibea, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale yellow in color. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.
