Vaccinium breviflos

Vaccinium breviflos, commonly known as the short-flowered blueberry, is a species of shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other berry-producing plants like cranberries and huckleberries. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white in color. Its leaves are elliptical and slightly hairy, and it thrives in acidic, well-drained soils.

The fruit, which is a small blueberry, is edible and often used in culinary applications.
