Vernonia phyllostachya

Vernonia phyllostachya, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads that resemble mist. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has feathery, gray-green foliage and produces small, white to pale blue ray flowers.

Vernonia phyllostachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
