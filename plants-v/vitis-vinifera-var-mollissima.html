Vitis vinifera var. mollissima

Vitis vinifera var. mollissima, commonly known as the Muscat of Alexandria, is a grapevine species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, dark purple berries with a thick, aromatic skin. The plant is known for its high sugar content and distinctive muscat aroma, making it valuable in winemaking. It is a vigorous grower with tendrils that allow it to climb and spread across supports.

This variety is also appreciated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and flowering habits.
