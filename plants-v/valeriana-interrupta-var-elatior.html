Valeriana interrupta var. elatior

Valeriana interrupta var. elatior, commonly known as the greater valerian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often purple or pink flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its roots contain volatile oils and alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.
