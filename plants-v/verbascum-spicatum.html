Verbascum spicatum

Verbascum spicatum, commonly known as the spiked mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in late summer, which are clustered in elongated spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Verbascum spicatum is also known for its medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
