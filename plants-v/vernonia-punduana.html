Vernonia punduana

Vernonia punduana, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring slender, branching stems. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Vernonia punduana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
