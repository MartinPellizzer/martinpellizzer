Verbena cloverae f. alba

Verbena cloverae f. alba, commonly known as white verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a compact, herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is drought-tolerant and well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
