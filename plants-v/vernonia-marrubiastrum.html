Vernonia marrubiastrum

Vernonia marrubiastrum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Vernonia marrubiastrum is a popular choice for native gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
