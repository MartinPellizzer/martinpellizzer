Veronica graeca

Veronica graeca, commonly known as the Greek speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant features small, blue or purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Veronica graeca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
