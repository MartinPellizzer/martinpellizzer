Viola charlestonensis

Viola charlestonensis, commonly known as the Charleston violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the landscape.

Viola charlestonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
