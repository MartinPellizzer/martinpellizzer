Verbascum corinthiacum

Verbascum corinthiacum, commonly known as the Corinthian mullein, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, hairy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, velvety leaves that are gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in the summer months, which are clustered in a loose spike at the top of the stem. Its leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.

Verbascum corinthiacum is also valued in gardens for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
