Viola niijimensis

Viola niijimensis, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shady, moist environments such as forests and woodland areas. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Viola niijimensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
