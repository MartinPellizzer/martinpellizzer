Vicia hybrida subsp. spuria

Vicia hybrida subsp.

spuria, commonly known as the common vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae. It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from pink to violet.

The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in agricultural settings as a cover crop. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in a variety of soil types. Vicia hybrida subsp.

spuria is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
