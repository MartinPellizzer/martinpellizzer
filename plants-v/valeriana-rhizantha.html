Valeriana rhizantha

Valeriana rhizantha, commonly known as the red valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Valeriana rhizantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its calming properties.
