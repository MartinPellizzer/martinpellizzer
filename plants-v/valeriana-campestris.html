Valeriana campestris

Valeriana campestris, commonly known as field valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a strong, pleasant aroma. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes, and are usually pinkish or reddish in color.

Valeriana campestris is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its calming effects.
