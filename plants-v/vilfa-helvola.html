Vilfa helvola

Vilfa helvola, commonly known as the common tansy, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellow disk flowers that are typically grouped into clusters. Vilfa helvola is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.

It has a long history of use in traditional medicine, though its toxicity requires caution in handling.
