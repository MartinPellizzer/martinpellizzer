Vitis vinifera var. sphaerica

Vitis vinifera var. sphaerica, commonly known as the grapefruit or sour orange, is a citrus plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is typically round or slightly oval, with a thick, colored rind and a segmented interior filled with juicy, acidic pulp. It is widely cultivated for its fruit, which is used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in a variety of climates.
