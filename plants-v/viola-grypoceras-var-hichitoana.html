Viola grypoceras var. hichitoana

Viola grypoceras var. hichitoana, commonly known as the Hichitoan violet, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant violet flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to its natural habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
