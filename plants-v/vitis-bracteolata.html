Vitis bracteolata

Vitis bracteolata, commonly known as the bracteolate grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically followed by berries that range in color from green to red. Vitis bracteolata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and unique fruiting structures.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to various garden environments.
