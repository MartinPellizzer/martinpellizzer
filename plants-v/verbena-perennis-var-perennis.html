Verbena perennis var. perennis

Verbena perennis var. perennis, commonly known as perennial verbena, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing vibrant blue, violet, or purple flowers. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is often used in gardens for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
