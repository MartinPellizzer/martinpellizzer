Vigna harmsiana

Vigna harmsiana, commonly known as the African bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and erect, branching stems. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

Vigna harmsiana is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a beneficial crop in sustainable agriculture.
