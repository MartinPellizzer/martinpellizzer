Vincetoxicum puberulum

Vincetoxicum puberulum, commonly known as the velvet leaf or hairy bindweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain environments.

Vincetoxicum puberulum is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
