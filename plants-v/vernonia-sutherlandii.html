Vernonia sutherlandii

Vernonia sutherlandii, commonly known as the Sutherland's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in open woodland and grassland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom from late summer to early autumn.

Vernonia sutherlandii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
