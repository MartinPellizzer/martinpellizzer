Viscum petiolatum

Viscum petiolatum, commonly known as the petioled mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees.

It lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its leaves, which are modified into small, scale-like structures. The plant produces small, white or pale green berries that are often eaten by birds, which then disperse the seeds. Viscum petiolatum is native to parts of Europe and Asia, where it grows in temperate climates.

Its presence can have both ecological and economic impacts on its host trees.
