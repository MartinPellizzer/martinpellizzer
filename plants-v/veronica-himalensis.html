Veronica himalensis

Veronica himalensis, commonly known as the Himalayan speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Himalayas.

It is a perennial herb with erect stems and small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes at high elevations.

Veronica himalensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
