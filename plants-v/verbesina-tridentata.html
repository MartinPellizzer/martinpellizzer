Verbesina tridentata

Verbesina tridentata, commonly known as the tripartite beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and hairy stems. The plant has trifoliate leaves, which are divided into three distinct leaflets, giving it its common name. It thrives in dry, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Verbesina tridentata is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally by Native American tribes.
