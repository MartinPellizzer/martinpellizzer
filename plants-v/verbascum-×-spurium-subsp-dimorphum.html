Verbascum × spurium subsp. dimorphum

Verbascum × spurium subsp.

dimorphum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a hybrid species within the genus Verbascum, which belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae. It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of soft, woolly hairs.

The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to regions of Europe and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

This subspecies is notable for its variability in leaf morphology and its ability to thrive in a range of environmental conditions.
