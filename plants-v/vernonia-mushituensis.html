Vernonia mushituensis

Vernonia mushituensis, commonly known as the East African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

The flowers are yellow and arranged in dense, conical clusters, making it a visually striking species.
