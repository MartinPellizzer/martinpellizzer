Verbena confinis

Verbena confinis, commonly known as the narrowleaf verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces showy clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Verbena confinis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
