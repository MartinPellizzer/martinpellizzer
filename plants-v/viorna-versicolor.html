Viorna versicolor

Viorna versicolor, commonly known as the variable violet, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers that range in color from purple to white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth, glossy texture.

Viorna versicolor is known for its ability to thrive in humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.
