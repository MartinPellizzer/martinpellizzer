Veronica agrestis subsp. pulchella

Veronica agrestis subsp.

pulchella, commonly known as the meadow veronica, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features slender, upright stems and produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers during the summer months.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
