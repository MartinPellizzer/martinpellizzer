Vriesea subandina

Vriesea subandina, commonly known as the Brazilian pineapple plant, is a striking epiphytic bromeliad native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant, spath-shaped inflorescence that can range in color from yellow to orange and red. The plant features a rosette of broad, waxy, strap-like leaves that are often green with red or yellow markings. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is popular as a decorative houseplant.

Vriesea subandina is known for its long-lasting blooms, which can last several months, making it a favored choice for indoor gardening.
