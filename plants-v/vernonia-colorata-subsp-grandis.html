Vernonia colorata subsp. grandis

Vernonia colorata subsp.

grandis, commonly known as the large-flowered ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States. It features upright, branching stems covered in soft, hairy foliage.

The plant produces showy, dark purple to brown composite flower heads that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly rough texture.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for native gardens and wildlife habitats.
