Vaccinium monteverdense

Vaccinium monteverdense, commonly known as the Monteverde huckleberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the cloud forests of Costa Rica.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes blueberries and cranberries, and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its berries, which are typically red or black when mature, are edible and have a tart flavor.

Vaccinium monteverdense plays an important role in the ecological balance of its habitat, providing food for various wildlife species.
