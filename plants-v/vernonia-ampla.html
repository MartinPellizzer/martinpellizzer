Vernonia ampla

Vernonia ampla, commonly known as the broadleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in wetlands and moist soils, showcasing its adaptability to humid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while the flowers are usually purple or pink in color.

Vernonia ampla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
