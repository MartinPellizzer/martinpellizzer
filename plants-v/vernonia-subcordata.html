Vernonia subcordata

Vernonia subcordata, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its flowering stems are typically branched and bear numerous florets, which are arranged in a cylindrical shape.

Vernonia subcordata is often found in moist, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges.
