Vicia sativa var. melanosperma

Vicia sativa var. melanosperma, commonly known as black vetch, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces dark, elongated seeds that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source for livestock. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

This variety is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
