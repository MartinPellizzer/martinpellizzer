Vernonia abbreviata

Vernonia abbreviata, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, while its stems are covered in fine hairs.

Vernonia abbreviata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
