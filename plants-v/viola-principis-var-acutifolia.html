Viola principis var. acutifolia

Viola principis var. acutifolia, commonly known as the acute-leaved violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive acute tip, and its flowers are typically violet or purple in color, with five petals. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Viola principis var. acutifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
