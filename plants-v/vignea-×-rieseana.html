Vignea × rieseana

Vignea × rieseana, commonly known as the hybrid passionflower, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and striking, showy flowers that often have five or more petals. The plant produces edible berries that are typically orange or red in color and contain a sweet, tangy pulp. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Vignea × rieseana is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.
