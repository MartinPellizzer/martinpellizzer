Viola beckeriana

Viola beckeriana, commonly known as the Becker's violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers with yellow centers, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Viola beckeriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in woodland gardens.
