Vaccinium pipolyi

Vaccinium pipolyi, commonly known as the Pipolyi blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, acidic berries that are typically blue or purple in color, containing high levels of antioxidants. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a glossy surface and are arranged alternately along the stems.

Vaccinium pipolyi thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas with high humidity.
