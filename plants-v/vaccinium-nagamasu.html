Vaccinium nagamasu

Vaccinium nagamasu, commonly known as the Japanese huckleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or purple in color, which are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Vaccinium nagamasu is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
