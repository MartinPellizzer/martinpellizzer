Vicioides striata

Vicioides striata, commonly known as the striped vicia, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with a distinctive striped pattern on the leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Vicioides striata is native to parts of South America and thrives in temperate climates.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and contributes to nitrogen fixation in the soil due to its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
