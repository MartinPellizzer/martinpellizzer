Verbascum latesulcatifolium

Verbascum latesulcatifolium, commonly known as the rugose mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a loose, spike-like inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
