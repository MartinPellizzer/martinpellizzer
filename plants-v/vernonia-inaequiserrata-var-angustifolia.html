Vernonia inaequiserrata var. angustifolia

Vernonia inaequiserrata var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved ironweed, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically narrow and serrated, with a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
