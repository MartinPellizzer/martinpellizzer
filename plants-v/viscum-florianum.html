Viscum florianum

Viscum florianum, commonly known as the Christmas mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It belongs to the family Viscaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant derives some of its nutrients from its host through specialized root structures called haustoria. It produces white or pale yellow berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain birds.

Viscum florianum is widely distributed in Europe and is often associated with holiday traditions due to its ornamental appearance.
