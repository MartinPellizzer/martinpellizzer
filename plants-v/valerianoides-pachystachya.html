Valerianoides pachystachya

Valerianoides pachystachya, commonly known as the tall valerian, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It features a robust root system and upright, hairy stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a strong, aromatic scent.

Valerianoides pachystachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
