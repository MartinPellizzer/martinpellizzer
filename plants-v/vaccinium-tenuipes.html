Vaccinium tenuipes

Vaccinium tenuipes, commonly known as the slender bilberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the boreal forests of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by edible berries that are dark blue to black in color. Its leaves are oval-shaped, leathery, and arranged alternately along the stems.

Vaccinium tenuipes thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, moist environments.
