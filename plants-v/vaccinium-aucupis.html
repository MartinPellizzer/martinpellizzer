Vaccinium aucupis

Vaccinium aucupis, commonly known as the cranberry bush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and heathers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are oval and slightly serrated, and it thrives in acidic, well-drained soils.

Vaccinium aucupis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a habitat for various wildlife species.
