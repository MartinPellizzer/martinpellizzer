Viola canadensis subsp. rydbergii

Viola canadensis subsp.

rydbergii, commonly known as Rydberg's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It typically grows in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces violet or purple flowers with a distinctive yellow center. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy blooms.

This subspecies is often found in the central and eastern United States.
