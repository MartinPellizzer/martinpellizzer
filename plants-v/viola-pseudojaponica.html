Viola pseudojaponica

Viola pseudojaponica, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of violet-blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Viola pseudojaponica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
