Veronica distans

Veronica distans, commonly known as the distant speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically opposite along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.

Veronica distans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
