Vitis sambucina

Vitis sambucina, commonly known as the European grapevine or black grapevine, is a deciduous climbing vine native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It produces small, dark blue to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and have been used in traditional medicine. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and is characterized by its vigorous growth habit. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found along riverbanks and in woodland areas.

Vitis sambucina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in winemaking.
