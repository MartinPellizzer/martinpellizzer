Victoria cruziana f. trickeri

Victoria cruziana f. trickeri, commonly known as the Tricker's water lily, is a rare and striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant's leaves are broad and rounded, often reaching up to 30 centimeters in diameter, and they float on the water's surface. Its botanical features include a thick, spongy stem and a robust root system adapted to aquatic environments.

This cultivar is highly valued in botanical gardens for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
