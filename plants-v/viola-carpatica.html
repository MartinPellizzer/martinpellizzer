Viola carpatica

Viola carpatica, commonly known as the Czech violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is native to Central and Eastern Europe and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and moist soils, preferring partial to full shade. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds.

Viola carpatica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
