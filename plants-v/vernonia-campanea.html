Vernonia campanea

Vernonia campanea, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small blue or purple clouds. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces showy, clustered blooms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Vernonia campanea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to support local wildlife.
