Vinca ellipticifolia

Vinca ellipticifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf vincas, is a perennial flowering plant native to South Africa.

It features upright, branched stems that can grow up to 3 feet tall, adorned with clusters of small, tubular, violet-blue flowers. The leaves are elliptical in shape, glossy, and arranged oppositely along the stems. This plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
