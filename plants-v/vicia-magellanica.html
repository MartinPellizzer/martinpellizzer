Vicia magellanica

Vicia magellanica, commonly known as the Magellan pea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial legume that typically grows in grasslands and open areas. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can be used for forage or soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.

Vicia magellanica is valued for its adaptability and ecological benefits in various habitats.
