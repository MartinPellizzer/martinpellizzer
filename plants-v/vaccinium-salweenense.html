Vaccinium salweenense

Vaccinium salweenense, commonly known as the Salween huckleberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Myanmar, Thailand, and China. The plant is a shrub that typically grows in moist, acidic soils of mountainous areas. Its flowers are small and white or pink, and it produces edible berries that are used in local cuisines.

The plant is valued not only for its culinary uses but also for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
