Valeriana florida

Valeriana florida, commonly known as the flowering valerian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has aromatic leaves that emit a distinctive, sometimes medicinal scent. Valeriana florida belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is known for its adaptability to various soil types.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
