Verbena spicata

Verbena spicata, commonly known as spiked verbena, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically blue to purple in color and bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

It is also known for its ability to reseed itself, ensuring the propagation of its species in suitable habitats.
