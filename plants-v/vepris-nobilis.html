Vepris nobilis

Vepris nobilis, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
