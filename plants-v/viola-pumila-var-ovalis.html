Viola pumila var. ovalis

Viola pumila var. ovalis, commonly known as the oval violet, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are typically oval in shape, giving it its variety name. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are usually violet or purple in color, often with a distinctive yellow throat. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its charming appearance.
