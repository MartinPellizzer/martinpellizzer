Viola tricolor subsp. machadeana

Viola tricolor subsp.

machadeana, commonly known as the Portuguese violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and northern Africa. It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of purple, white, and yellow.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
