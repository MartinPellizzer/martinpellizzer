Veronica lilacina

Veronica lilacina, commonly known as the purple speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, clustered flowers with five petals, typically in shades of purple or blue. The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and slightly hairy, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland margins or along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
