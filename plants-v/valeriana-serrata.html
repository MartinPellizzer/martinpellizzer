Valeriana serrata

Valeriana serrata, commonly known as the serrated valerian, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in the summer months. Valeriana serrata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks.

Its roots are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported sedative and calming properties.
