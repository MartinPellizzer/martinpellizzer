Verbena angustilobata

Verbena angustilobata, commonly known as narrow-lobed verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Verbena angustilobata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
