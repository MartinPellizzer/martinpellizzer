Vernonia buddlejifolia

Vernonia buddlejifolia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flower heads that resemble mist. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while the stems are erect and branching.

Vernonia buddlejifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
