Verbesina neotenoriensis

Verbesina neotenoriensis, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in dry grasslands and disturbed areas.

Verbesina neotenoriensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
