Viticella exilis

Viticella exilis, commonly known as the slender grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by small, round berries. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and ability to climb or trail.

Viticella exilis prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
