Valeriana vulgaris var. intermedia

Valeriana vulgaris var. intermedia, commonly known as sweet valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant, leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

The species is part of the Caprifoliaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like honeysuckle.
