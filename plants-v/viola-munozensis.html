Viola munozensis

Viola munozensis, commonly known as the Munoz's violet, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Viola munozensis belongs to the Violaceae family, which includes other violets and related species.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique adaptations to high-altitude ecosystems and its role in local biodiversity.
