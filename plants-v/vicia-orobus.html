Vicia orobus

Vicia orobus, commonly known as the yellow field bean, is a flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is characterized by its yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces cylindrical pods containing several seeds, which are often used as forage for livestock. Vicia orobus is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.
