Vernonia pobeguinii

Vernonia pobeguinii, commonly known as the golden vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation across open landscapes.

Vernonia pobeguinii is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
