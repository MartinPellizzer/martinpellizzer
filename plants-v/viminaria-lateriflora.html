Viminaria lateriflora

Viminaria lateriflora, commonly known as the woolly stickleaf, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its woody stems and dense, woolly foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are opposite and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Viminaria lateriflora is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy soils.
