Vernonia flotowioides

Vernonia flotowioides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its foliage is often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, mist-like appearance.

The showy blue to violet flowers appear in late summer and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
