Vaccinium almedae

Vaccinium almedae, commonly known as Almeda's blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and heathers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark blue or purple in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

Vaccinium almedae is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
