Vesicaria densiflora

Vesicaria densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures that trap tiny aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in wet, nutrient-poor environments such as bogs and marshes. Its leaves are often submerged, while its flowering stems rise to the surface.

Vesicaria densiflora plays a unique role in its ecosystem by supplementing its nutrient intake through prey capture.
