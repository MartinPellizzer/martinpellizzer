Veronica arvensis var. nana

Veronica arvensis var. nana, commonly known as dwarf speedwell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and is characterized by its compact, mounding growth habit. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
