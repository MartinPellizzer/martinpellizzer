Viola athois

Viola athois, commonly known as the purple marsh violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodland floors and stream sides. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, with five petals and a distinctive violet center. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it has a compact growth habit.

Viola athois is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators in its native ecosystems.
