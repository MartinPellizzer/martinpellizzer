Verbascum phlomoides var. glabrescens

Verbascum phlomoides var. glabrescens, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in soft, white hairs. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear clusters of yellow flowers in the summer months. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its mild laxative properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in rocky or arid landscapes.
