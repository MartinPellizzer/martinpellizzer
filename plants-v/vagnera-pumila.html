Vagnera pumila

Vagnera pumila, commonly known as the woolly veldt grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy, grayish-green foliage. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky outcrops, thriving in well-drained soils. Vagnera pumila produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are usually wind-pollinated.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
