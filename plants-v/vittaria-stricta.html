Vittaria stricta

Vittaria stricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually linear to lanceolate in shape and are arranged in a dense, carpet-like formation.

Vittaria stricta is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value.
