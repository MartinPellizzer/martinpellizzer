Viola tricolor var. agrestis

Viola tricolor var. agrestis, commonly known as the meadow violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, thriving in partial shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
