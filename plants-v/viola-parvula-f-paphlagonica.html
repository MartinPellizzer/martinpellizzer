Viola parvula f. paphlagonica

Viola parvula f. paphlagonica, commonly known as the Paphlagonian violet, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to Turkey.

It is a variant of the small violet, characterized by its pale violet to almost white petals and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a subtle yet striking touch to its natural habitat.

Botanically, it is noted for its compact growth habit and unique floral morphology.
