Veronica paniculata f. angustifolia

Veronica paniculata f. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its delicate appearance makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes and wildflower meadows.

The flowering period usually occurs in late spring to early summer.
