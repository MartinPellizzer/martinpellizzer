Viola sylvestris subsp. rosulata

Viola sylvestris subsp.

rosulata, commonly known as the forest violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in woodland habitats, where it thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces delicate violet flowers with a distinctive yellow center. Its flowers bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge.

The subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
