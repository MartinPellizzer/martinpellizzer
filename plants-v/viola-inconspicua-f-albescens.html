Viola inconspicua f. albescens

Viola inconspicua f. albescens, commonly known as the white-flowered viola, is a rare and delicate flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers, which contrast with its dark green, heart-shaped leaves. Native to specific regions of North America, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its small, rounded leaves are typically covered in a fine layer of hair, aiding in moisture retention.

The plant's unique coloration and limited distribution make it a notable subject in botanical studies.
