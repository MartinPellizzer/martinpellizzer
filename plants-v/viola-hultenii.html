Viola hultenii

Viola hultenii, commonly known as Hulten's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves and clusters of violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Viola hultenii has small, oval-shaped fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of alpine and subalpine habitats.
