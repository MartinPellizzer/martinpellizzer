Valeriana pauciflora

Valeriana pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered valerian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and produces white or pale pink blooms. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a strong, aromatic scent.

Valeriana pauciflora is valued in traditional medicine for its purported calming properties.
