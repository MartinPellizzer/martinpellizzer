Viola epirota

Viola epirota, commonly known as the Epirus violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Viola epirota is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been historically used in traditional herbal medicine.
