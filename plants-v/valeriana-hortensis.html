Valeriana hortensis

Valeriana hortensis, commonly known as garden valerian, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their calming properties.

Valeriana hortensis thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.
