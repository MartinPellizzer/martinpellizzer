Viburnum carlesii

Viburnum carlesii, commonly known as the Koreanspice viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Viburnum carlesii is valued for its attractive foliage, fragrant blooms, and wildlife-friendly berries.
