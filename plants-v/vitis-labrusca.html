Vitis labrusca

Vitis labrusca, commonly known as the fox grape, is a species of flowering vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, dark purple berries. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves in the fall, and thrives in temperate climates. Vitis labrusca is notable for its use in winemaking, particularly in the production of American wines.

Its berries are also used in jams, jellies, and other culinary applications.
