Valeriana polystachya var. normalis

Valeriana polystachya var. normalis, commonly known as the common valerian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features a robust rhizomatous root system and upright, branched stems that bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences, which bloom in summer. Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils, contributing to its use in traditional medicine.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.
