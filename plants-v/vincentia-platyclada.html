Vincentia platyclada

Vincentia platyclada, commonly known as the broad-leafed evergreen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that are typically ovate or elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
