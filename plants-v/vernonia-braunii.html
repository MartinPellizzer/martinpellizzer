Vernonia braunii

Vernonia braunii, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces fluffy, white seed heads that resemble mist, giving it its common name. Vernonia braunii thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist to wet soils.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
