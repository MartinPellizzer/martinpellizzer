Veratrum hoffmeisteri

Veratrum hoffmeisteri, commonly known as Hoffmeister's false hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Veratrum, which is part of the family Melanthiaceae. The plant features tall, sturdy stems with large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are typically arranged in loose clusters and are usually white or pale green, with showy, petal-like bracts.

Hoffmeister's false hellebore is known for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
