Vigna angustifoliolata

Vigna angustifoliolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, trifoliate leaves and small, elongated pods that contain edible seeds. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in warm, well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or white, and it is often cultivated for its nutritional seeds.

Vigna angustifoliolata is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a beneficial crop in sustainable agriculture.
