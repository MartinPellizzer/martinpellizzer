Vitex krukovii

Vitex krukovii, commonly known as the Krukovii chaste tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of purple or blue. Its fruit is a dry, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Vitex krukovii is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.
