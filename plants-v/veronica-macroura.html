Veronica macroura

Veronica macroura, commonly known as the long-stemmed speedwell, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, covered in dense, grayish-white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Veronica macroura is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
