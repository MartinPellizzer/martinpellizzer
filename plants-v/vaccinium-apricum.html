Vaccinium apricum

Vaccinium apricum, commonly known as the cranberry bush, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of bright red berries. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland areas. Its berries are edible and have a tart flavor, commonly used in culinary applications.

The plant also plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, particularly birds and small mammals.
