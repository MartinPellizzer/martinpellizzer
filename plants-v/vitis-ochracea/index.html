Vitis Ochracea

Vitis ochracea is a lesser-known member of the grapevine family native to the eastern united states. this plant is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks where it thrives in shady conditions. its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, fuzzy texture that gives it a unique appearance. while not widely cultivated for fruit, it plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife. its historical use by native american tribes for medicinal purposes adds to its ecological and cultural significance.
