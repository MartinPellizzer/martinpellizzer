Viola elisabethae

Viola elisabethae, commonly known as the Elizabeth violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers that range in color from violet to white. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Viola elisabethae is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in herbal medicine.
