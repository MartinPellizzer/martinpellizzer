Viola lucida

Viola lucida, commonly known as the shining violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and delicate, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in partial to full shade.

Viola lucida is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
