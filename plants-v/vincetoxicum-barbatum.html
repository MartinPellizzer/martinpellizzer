Vincetoxicum barbatum

Vincetoxicum barbatum, commonly known as the hairy elder, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Adoxaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces red berries that are toxic to humans and animals, making it important to handle with care. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular garden plant despite its poisonous properties.
