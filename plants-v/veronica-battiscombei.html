Veronica battiscombei

Veronica battiscombei, commonly known as the Cape veronica, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it blooms from late spring to early autumn.

Veronica battiscombei is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
