Verbascum assurense var. singaricum

Verbascum assurense var. singaricum, commonly known as the Singaric mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the genus Verbascum.
