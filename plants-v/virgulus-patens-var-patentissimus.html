Virgulus patens var. patentissimus

Virgulus patens var. patentissimus, commonly known as the showy goldenweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wet meadows.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a native plant supporting local ecosystems.
