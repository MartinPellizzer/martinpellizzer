Veronica rosea subvar. atrichocarpa

Veronica rosea subvar. atrichocarpa, commonly known as the velvet veronica, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking, lavender-pink flowers that bloom in dense clusters during the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a desirable addition to both cultivated and natural landscapes.
