Verbascum × karaboertlenense

Verbascum × karaboertlenense, commonly known as the hybrid mullein.

This plant is a naturally occurring hybrid between Verbascum blattaria and Verbascum wrightii. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves that resemble those of the common mullein. The flowers are typically yellowish-green to brownish, appearing in clusters at the top of the plant.

It is native to the southwestern United States and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
