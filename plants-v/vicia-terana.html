Vicia terana

Vicia terana, commonly known as the Canary Island vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and erect growth habit. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in spring. Vicia terana thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
