Viola brevistipulata f. parviflora

Viola brevistipulata f. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple and blue. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a touch of color to the forest floor.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
