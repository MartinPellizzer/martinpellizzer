Verbena tomophylla

Verbena tomophylla, commonly known as the pink verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, opposite leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Verbena tomophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
