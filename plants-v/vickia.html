Vickia

Vickia, commonly known as the blue violet, is a flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pentagonal flowers that typically range in color from blue to purple. The plant is native to various regions in the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in moist, woodland environments. Vickia species are often found in meadows, stream banks, and shaded areas where they contribute to the ecological diversity of their habitats.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, playing a vital role in the ecosystem.
