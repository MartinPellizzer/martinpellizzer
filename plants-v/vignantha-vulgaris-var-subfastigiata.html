Vignantha vulgaris var. subfastigiata

Vignantha vulgaris var. subfastigiata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and is often used in traditional agriculture and cuisine.
