Viola × molleri

Viola × molleri, commonly known as the English violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant is native to Europe and has been cultivated for its ornamental value due to its delicate appearance. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or garden settings.

Viola × molleri is also known for its ability to thrive in partial shade, making it a popular choice for shaded gardens.
