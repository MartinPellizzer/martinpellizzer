Venilia vernalis

Venilia vernalis, commonly known as the spring bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often appearing in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Venilia vernalis is often found in woodland edges and meadows, blooming in early spring.
