Vauquelinia heterodon

Vauquelinia heterodon, commonly known as the Texas vauquelinia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and produces small, white to pale pink flowers in spring. The plant has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to rocky and acidic soils. It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.

Vauquelinia heterodon is also valued for its ability to thrive in shaded conditions and its low maintenance requirements.
