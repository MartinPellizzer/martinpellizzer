Vignea vulpina var. crassinervis

Vignea vulpina var. crassinervis, commonly known as the broad-veined morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in the morning and close by midday. The leaves are heart-shaped with prominent, broad veins, giving it its varietal name. The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
