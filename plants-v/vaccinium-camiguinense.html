Vaccinium camiguinense

Vaccinium camiguinense, commonly known as the Camiguin blueberry, is a species of shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, similar to other Vaccinium species. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in montane forests.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
