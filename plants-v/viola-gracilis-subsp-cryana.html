Viola gracilis subsp. cryana

Viola gracilis subsp.

cryana, commonly known as the slender violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features slender, upright stems and heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, delicate flowers with five petals, typically in shades of blue or violet, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
