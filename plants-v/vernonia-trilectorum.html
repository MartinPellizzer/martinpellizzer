Vernonia trilectorum

Vernonia trilectorum, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodland and grassland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Vernonia trilectorum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
