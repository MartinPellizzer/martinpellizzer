Vicia davurica

Vicia davurica, commonly known as the Siberian vetch, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Vicia davurica is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.
