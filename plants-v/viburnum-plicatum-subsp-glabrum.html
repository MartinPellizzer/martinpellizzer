Viburnum plicatum subsp. glabrum

Viburnum plicatum subsp.

glabrum, commonly known as the Japanese snowball viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan. It is characterized by its striking, rounded clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It prefers partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.

Its ornamental value comes from both its foliage and its showy floral displays.
