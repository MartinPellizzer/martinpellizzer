Viola rhodopeia

Viola rhodopeia, commonly known as the Rhodope violet, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or limestone soils. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are violet-blue in color, with a distinctive yellow throat.

Viola rhodopeia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
