Verbena biserrata

Verbena biserrata, commonly known as the Brazilian verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Verbena biserrata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
