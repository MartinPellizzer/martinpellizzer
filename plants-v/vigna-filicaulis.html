Vigna filicaulis

Vigna filicaulis, commonly known as the featherleaf cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds, which are often used as a protein source in various cuisines. Vigna filicaulis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, improving soil fertility.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated as a forage crop or in mixed cropping systems.
