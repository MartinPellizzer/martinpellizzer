Vitis indica

Vitis indica, commonly known as the Indian grape or wild grape, is a vine native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its tendrils, heart-shaped leaves, and small, sour-tasting berries. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing along riverbanks and in moist, shaded areas. Its berries are used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye.

The botanical features of Vitis indica make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
