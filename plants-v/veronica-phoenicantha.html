Veronica phoenicantha

Veronica phoenicantha, commonly known as the flame veronica, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its striking appearance and drought tolerance contribute to its ornamental value in horticulture.
