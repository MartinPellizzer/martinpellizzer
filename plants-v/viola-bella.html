Viola bella

Viola bella, commonly known as the pretty violet, is a flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in moist, shady environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple, blue, and white. Viola bella is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
