Vilfa albicans

Vilfa albicans, commonly known as the silverleaf lily, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and silver-green foliage, which reflect its common name. The plant produces clusters of fragrant blooms during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine, waxy coating that helps reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Vilfa albicans is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its medicinal properties in traditional South American herbal medicine.
