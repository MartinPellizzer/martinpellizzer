Vangueria senegalensis

Vangueria senegalensis, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its latex sap contains milky substances, which have been traditionally used in various cultural and medicinal practices.

Vangueria senegalensis is also valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
