Veronica konantha

Veronica konantha, commonly known as the alpine speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh alpine environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Veronica konantha is often used in rock gardens and alpine arrangements due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
