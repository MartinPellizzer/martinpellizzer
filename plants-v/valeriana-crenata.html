Valeriana crenata

Valeriana crenata, commonly known as creeping valerian, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, fragrant flowers. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. Valeriana crenata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
