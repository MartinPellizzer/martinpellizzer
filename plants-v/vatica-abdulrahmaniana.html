Vatica abdulrahmaniana

Vatica abdulrahmaniana, commonly known as the Abdul Rahman's ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for construction and furniture. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and requires a humid, warm climate with ample rainfall. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in providing habitat for various wildlife species.
