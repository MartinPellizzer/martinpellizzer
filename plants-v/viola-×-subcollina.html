Viola × subcollina

Viola × subcollina, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a perennial flowering plant that belongs to the Violaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Viola sororia and Viola pubescens, resulting in a plant with distinctive botanical features. The leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are typically violet or purple with a yellow center. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
