Viola yezoensis var. dissecta

Viola yezoensis var. dissecta, commonly known as the Japanese violet or Yezo violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped, deeply lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of delicate, violet-blue flowers with yellow centers, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or shaded garden settings.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
