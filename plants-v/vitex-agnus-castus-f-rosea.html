Vitex agnus-castus f. rosea

Vitex agnus-castus f. rosea, commonly known as the roseate chaste tree, is a cultivar of the chaste tree, characterized by its distinctive pink to lavender flower clusters.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region, known for its ornamental value in gardens. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and emits a mild, aromatic scent. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is hardy in USDA zones 5 through 8, making it suitable for temperate climates.
