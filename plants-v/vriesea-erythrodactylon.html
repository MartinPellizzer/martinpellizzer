Vriesea erythrodactylon

Vriesea erythrodactylon, commonly known as the red-fingered vriesea, is a striking bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, red-tipped leaves that form a rosette shape, creating a visually appealing display. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with colorful, long-lasting bracts that add to its ornamental value. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Vriesea erythrodactylon is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
