Veronica myrsinoides

Veronica myrsinoides, commonly known as blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm and features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Veronica myrsinoides is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, thriving in moist to moderately dry conditions.
