Viola saxifraga var. ciliata

Viola saxifraga var. ciliata, commonly known as the hairy violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves with hairy margins. The plant produces small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in cooler climates.
