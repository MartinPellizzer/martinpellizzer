Veronica poljensis

Veronica poljensis, commonly known as the Alpine speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in high-altitude meadows.

Veronica poljensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
