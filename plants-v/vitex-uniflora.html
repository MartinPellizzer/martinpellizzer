Vitex uniflora

Vitex uniflora, commonly known as the chaste tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, fragrant, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its fruit is a dry, woody capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.

Vitex uniflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.
