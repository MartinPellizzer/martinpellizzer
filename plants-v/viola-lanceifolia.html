Viola lanceifolia

Viola lanceifolia, commonly known as the lanceleaf violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive lanceolate shape, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy violet flowers with five petals, typically in shades of purple or blue. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native garden landscapes.
