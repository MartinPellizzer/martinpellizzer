Viola decumbens var. scrotiformis

Viola decumbens var. scrotiformis, commonly known as the scrotiform viola, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive mottled appearance, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers with a spurred petal, typical of the Violaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species for ecological and horticultural study.
