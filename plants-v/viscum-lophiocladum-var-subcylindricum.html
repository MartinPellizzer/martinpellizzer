Viscum lophiocladum var. subcylindricum

Viscum lophiocladum var. subcylindricum, commonly known as the narrow-cylindrical mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It belongs to the family Viscaceae and is characterized by its evergreen, leathery leaves that are typically narrow and cylindrical in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are followed by small, fleshy berries containing seeds. It is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is often found growing on the branches of dipterocarp trees.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can have a significant impact on the health of its host plants.
