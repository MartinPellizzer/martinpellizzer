Veronicastrum virginicum var. purpureum

Veronicastrum virginicum var. purpureum, commonly known as blue mist spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is often a deep green, providing a nice contrast to the delicate flowers.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
