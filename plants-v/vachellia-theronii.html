Vachellia theronii

Vachellia theronii, commonly known as the Karoo thornveld acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorny stems and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact inflorescences, which are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating a high degree of drought tolerance.

Vachellia theronii plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
