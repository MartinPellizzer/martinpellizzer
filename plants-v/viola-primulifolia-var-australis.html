Viola primulifolia var. australis

Viola primulifolia var. australis, commonly known as the Australian violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves and produces delicate, violet-blue flowers with five petals. It has a short flowering period, usually in spring, and relies on insect pollination.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
