Vigna monantha

Vigna monantha, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, which are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. Vigna monantha is valued for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines.

It also plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with Rhizobia bacteria.
