Viola × rolandi-bonapartei

Viola × rolandi-bonapartei, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a species resulting from the cross between Viola lutea and Viola tricolor.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is native to Europe and has been cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to gardens and natural habitats.
