Vernonia suaveolens

Vernonia suaveolens, commonly known as sweet vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow to orange tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and emit a mild, sweet scent, contributing to its common name.

Vernonia suaveolens is often found in moist, open habitats like woodlands and wetlands.
