Vigna angustifolia

Vigna angustifolia, commonly known as small cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, trifoliate leaves and small, elongated pods that contain edible seeds. The plant thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein and essential nutrients. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and improves soil fertility through nitrogen fixation.

Vigna angustifolia is also valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to grow in semi-arid conditions.
