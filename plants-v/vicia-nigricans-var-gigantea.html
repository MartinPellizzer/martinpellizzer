Vicia nigricans var. gigantea

Vicia nigricans var. gigantea, commonly known as the giant black vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. Its trifoliate leaves are compound, with each leaflet being oval-shaped and slightly hairy.

This species is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil enrichment and as a cover crop.
