Vaccinium bracteatum var. chinense

Vaccinium bracteatum var. chinense, commonly known as the Chinese huckleberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark red or black in color, which are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas with partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses in traditional Chinese cuisine.
