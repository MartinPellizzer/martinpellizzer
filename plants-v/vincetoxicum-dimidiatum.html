Vincetoxicum dimidiatum

Vincetoxicum dimidiatum, commonly known as the red bittersweet, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy orange-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but requires caution due to its toxic properties.
