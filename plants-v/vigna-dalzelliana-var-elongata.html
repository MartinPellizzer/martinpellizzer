Vigna dalzelliana var. elongata

Vigna dalzelliana var. elongata, commonly known as the long-seeded cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its elongated, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being ovate and slightly hairy.

The flowers are small, pale yellow, and clustered in racemes, typical of the Fabaceae family.
