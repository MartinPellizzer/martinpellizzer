Veronica kotschyana

Veronica kotschyana, commonly known as the Kashmir violet, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Veronica kotschyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
