Viola nuttallii var. major

Viola nuttallii var. major, commonly known as the largeleaf viola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy violet flowers with five petals, often in shades of purple or blue, during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland areas and streambanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
