Villanova titicacensis

Villanova titicacensis, commonly known as the Titicaca water lily, is a unique aquatic plant native to the Lake Titicaca region in South America.

It is characterized by its large, floating leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter, providing a habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant's flowers are white and fragrant, blooming during the day and closing at night. It plays a vital role in the local ecosystem by contributing to water filtration and supporting biodiversity.

Due to habitat loss and pollution, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
