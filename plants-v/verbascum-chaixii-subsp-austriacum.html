Verbascum chaixii subsp. austriacum

Verbascum chaixii subsp.

austriacum, commonly known as the Austrian mullein, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Alps. It features large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are lance-shaped and can grow up to 30 cm in length.

The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded locations.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
