Vicia californica var. madrensis

Vicia californica var. madrensis, commonly known as the Madrean vetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial legume that typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often found in mountainous regions. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. It has a robust root system that helps stabilize soil and fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological restoration efforts.
