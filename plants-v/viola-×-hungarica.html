Viola × hungarica

Viola × hungarica, commonly known as the Hungarian violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant is native to Central Europe and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors. It produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Viola × hungarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
