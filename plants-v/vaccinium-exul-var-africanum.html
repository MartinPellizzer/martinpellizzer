Vaccinium exul var. africanum

Vaccinium exul var. africanum, commonly known as the African blueberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces edible berries that are typically blue or black in color, similar to other Vaccinium species. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or rocky habitats.

The leaves are evergreen, glossy, and have a slightly serrated edge.
