Vauquelinia corymbosa subsp. latifolia

Vauquelinia corymbosa subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf vauquelinia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is a low-growing, evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and slightly glossy. It produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, round fruits.

This species is well adapted to acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade.
