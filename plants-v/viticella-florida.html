Viticella florida

Viticella florida, commonly known as the flowering currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in jams and jellies. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Viticella florida is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
