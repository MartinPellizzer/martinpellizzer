Vernonia varroniifolia

Vernonia varroniifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slight pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow to brownish-red flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
