Vigna oblongifolia var. parviflora

Vigna oblongifolia var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with small, elliptical leaflets. The flowers are typically pale yellow or white, and the plant produces elongated, narrow pods containing small, edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and is often cultivated for both human consumption and animal feed.
