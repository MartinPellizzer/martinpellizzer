Viburnum semitomentosum

Viburnum semitomentosum, commonly known as the silver-leaf viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of silvery-white hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue, edible berries. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is adapted to a variety of soil types.

Viburnum semitomentosum is valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
