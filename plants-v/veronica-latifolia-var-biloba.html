Veronica latifolia var. biloba

Veronica latifolia var. biloba, commonly known as the broad-leaved speedwell, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically broad and hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
