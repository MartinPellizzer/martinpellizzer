Verbena lilacina

Verbena lilacina, commonly known as the purple verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, five-petaled flowers. The flowers typically range in color from pale purple to deep violet, and they bloom profusely from late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and long blooming period make it an attractive addition to drought-tolerant landscapes.
