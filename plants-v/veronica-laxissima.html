Veronica laxissima

Veronica laxissima, commonly known as the slender speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender stems and small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Veronica laxissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in horticultural settings for its delicate appearance.
