Veronica spicata var. hirsuta

Veronica spicata var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy everlasting, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant features hairy leaves and stems, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value comes from its long-lasting flowers and compact form.
