Vernonia brideliifolia

Vernonia brideliifolia, commonly known as the Brideliifolia ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Vernonia brideliifolia is typically found in open woodlands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
