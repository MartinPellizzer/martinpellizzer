Valeriana alternifolia f. angustifolia

Valeriana alternifolia f. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its aromatic foliage and roots contain compounds used in traditional medicine for their calming properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
