Vernonia megapotamica var. brevifolia

Vernonia megapotamica var. brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and riverbanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense clusters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
