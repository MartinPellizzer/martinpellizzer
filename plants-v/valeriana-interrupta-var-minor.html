Valeriana interrupta var. minor

Valeriana interrupta var. minor, commonly known as lesser valerian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its square, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its root system is often used in traditional medicine for its purported calming properties.
