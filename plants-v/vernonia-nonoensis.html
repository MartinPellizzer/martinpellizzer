Vernonia nonoensis

Vernonia nonoensis, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically showy blooms. The plant is notable for its hard, dense wood, which is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stems.

Vernonia nonoensis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
