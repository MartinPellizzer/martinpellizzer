Vitis salmonea

Vitis salmonea, commonly known as the salmon grape, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to parts of eastern Asia, including China and Korea. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports. Its leaves are alternate and often heart-shaped, with a rough texture.

The flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and appear in clusters, followed by small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange in color.
