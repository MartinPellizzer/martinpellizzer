Verbena sanguinea

Verbena sanguinea, commonly known as scarlet verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red or pink flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often branched.

The flowers are clustered in spikes and bloom from summer to autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
