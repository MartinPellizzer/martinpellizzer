Viola rupestris subsp. puberula

Viola rupestris subsp.

puberula, commonly known as the pubescent rock violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. Its flowers are violet-blue in color, with five petals and a distinctive spur that extends downward.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and beauty.
