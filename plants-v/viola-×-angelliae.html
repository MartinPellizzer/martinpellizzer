Viola × angelliae

Viola × angelliae, commonly known as the Angel's Wings viola, is a hybrid perennial flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–15 cm and thrives in cool, moist environments with partial to full shade. Its flowers are often used in gardens for their ornamental value and can attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola × angelliae is prized for its hardiness and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
