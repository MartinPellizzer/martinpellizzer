Veronica praecox subsp. kavusica

Veronica praecox subsp.

kavusica, commonly known as the early speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom early in the spring.

The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for alpine or rocky garden environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its adaptability in harsh climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
