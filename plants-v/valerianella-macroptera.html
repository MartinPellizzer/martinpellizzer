Valerianella macroptera

Valerianella macroptera, commonly known as the common valerian, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a slightly serrated edge. The plant grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Valerianella macroptera is known for its mild aromatic scent and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
