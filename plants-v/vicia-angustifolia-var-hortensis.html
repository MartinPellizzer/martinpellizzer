Vicia angustifolia var. hortensis

Vicia angustifolia var. hortensis, commonly known as the narrowleaf vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces clusters of pea-like flowers in shades of purple or pink. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening or as a cover crop.

Its growth habit includes trailing or climbing stems, depending on the environmental conditions.
