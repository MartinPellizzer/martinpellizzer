Viola calcarata var. decipiens

Viola calcarata var. decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive violet, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its name, "decipiens," refers to its ability to mimic other plants, potentially misleading herbivores or pollinators.
