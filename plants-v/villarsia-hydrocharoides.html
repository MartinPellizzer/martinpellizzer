Villarsia hydrocharoides

Villarsia hydrocharoides, commonly known as the water lily of the genus Villarsia, is a tropical aquatic plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes. The plant features broad, floating leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive, showy appearance that attracts pollinators like beetles and flies.

The species is notable for its adaptation to aquatic life, with specialized structures that help it thrive in waterlogged conditions.
