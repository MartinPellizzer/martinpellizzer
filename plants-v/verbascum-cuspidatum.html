Verbascum cuspidatum

Verbascum cuspidatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf mullein, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features tall, upright stems covered in soft, velvety leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a terminal spike. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and meadows.
