Viola cephalonica

Viola cephalonica, commonly known as the Greek violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a touch of color to the landscape.

Viola cephalonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
