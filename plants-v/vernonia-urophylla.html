Vernonia urophylla

Vernonia urophylla, commonly known as the woolly ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, woolly leaves and produces spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy, composite flower heads. Vernonia urophylla typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and restoration projects.
