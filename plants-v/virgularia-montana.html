Virgularia montana

Virgularia montana, commonly known as mountain virgularia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes.

Virgularia montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
