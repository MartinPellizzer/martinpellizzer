Vittaria williamsii

Vittaria williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' fern, is a small, epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Vittariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are often covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or branches, deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Vittaria williamsii is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in humid, shaded environments.
