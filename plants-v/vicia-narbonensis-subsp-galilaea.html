Vicia narbonensis subsp. galilaea

Vicia narbonensis subsp.

galilaea, commonly known as the Galilaea vetch, is a flowering plant species native to the Mediterranean region. It is a legume belonging to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a forage plant for livestock.
