Vicia lathyroides

Vicia lathyroides, commonly known as the broad-leaved vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-pink flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several oval, smooth seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Vicia lathyroides has been used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
