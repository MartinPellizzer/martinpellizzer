Valeriana quindiensis

Valeriana quindiensis, commonly known as the Theni Valley valerian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, herbaceous stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a serrated margin, while its roots are thick and fibrous, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Valeriana quindiensis is valued for its potential calming effects and has been used in traditional medicine for its sedative qualities.
