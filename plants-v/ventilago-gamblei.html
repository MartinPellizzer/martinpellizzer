Ventilago gamblei

Ventilago gamblei, commonly known as the Natal wild bean, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its flowers are small and usually white or pale blue, attracting pollinators such as bees.

The seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are often used in traditional medicine.
