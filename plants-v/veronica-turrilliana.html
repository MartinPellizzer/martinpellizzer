Veronica turrilliana

Veronica turrilliana, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of California and Nevada.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, blue to violet flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Veronica turrilliana thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, rocky habitats at higher elevations.
