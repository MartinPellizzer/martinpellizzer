Vaccinium ovalifolium var. shikokianum

Vaccinium ovalifolium var. shikokianum, commonly known as the Shikoku huckleberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, followed by edible berries that are red when mature. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses in traditional Japanese cuisine.
