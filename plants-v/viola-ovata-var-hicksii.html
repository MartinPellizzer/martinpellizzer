Viola ovata var. hicksii

Viola ovata var. hicksii, commonly known as Hicks' violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly found in moist woodlands and along stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces violet-blue flowers with five petals. It typically grows to a height of about 10-15 centimeters and blooms in early spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
