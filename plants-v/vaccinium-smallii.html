Vaccinium smallii

Vaccinium smallii, commonly known as the Small's blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and rhododendrons. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color. Vaccinium smallii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
