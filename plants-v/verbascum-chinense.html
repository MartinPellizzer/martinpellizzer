Verbascum chinense

Verbascum chinense, commonly known as Chinese mullein, is a perennial herb native to China and neighboring regions.

It features tall, hairy stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, velvety leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from summer to autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fibrous roots are valued for their medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and expectorant effects.

Verbascum chinense thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy plant in temperate climates.
