Viburnum japonicum var. boninsimense

Viburnum japonicum var. boninsimense, commonly known as the Bonin holly or Japanese snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Bonin Islands.

It features showy, white, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is relatively low maintenance.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
