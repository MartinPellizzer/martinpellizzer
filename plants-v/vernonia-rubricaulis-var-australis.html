Vernonia rubricaulis var. australis

Vernonia rubricaulis var. australis, commonly known as the Australian ironweed, is a perennial shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
