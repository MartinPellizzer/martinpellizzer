Veronica officinalis var. silvestris

Veronica officinalis var. silvestris, commonly known as wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks.

Botanically, it is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to spread through rhizomes.
