Verbascum phlomoides var. decurrens

Verbascum phlomoides var. decurrens, commonly known as the creeping mullein, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features upright, hairy stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, often branching towards the top. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in a raceme, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, gradually narrowing towards the base, and covered in dense, soft hairs.

This species is often found in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands.
