Vicia sativa subsp. maculata

Vicia sativa subsp.

maculata, commonly known as the red clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often marked with red or purple markings, and its spikes of small, pink or purple flowers.

The plant is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it valuable in agricultural and ecological contexts. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to biodiversity.
