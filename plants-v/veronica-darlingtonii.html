Veronica darlingtonii

Veronica darlingtonii, commonly known as Darlington's speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
