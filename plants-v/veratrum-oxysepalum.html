Veratrum oxysepalum

Veratrum oxysepalum, commonly known as the narrow-sepal veratrum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence composed of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has large, palmately lobed leaves and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and livestock.

Despite its toxicity, it plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
