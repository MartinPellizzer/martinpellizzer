Verbesina oligantha

Verbesina oligantha, commonly known as the slender beggar-tick, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy leaves and produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
