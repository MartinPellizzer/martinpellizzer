Veronica scutellata var. villosa

Veronica scutellata var. villosa, commonly known as hairy veronica, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant features small, blue to purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are characterized by their hairy texture, giving the species its common name.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
