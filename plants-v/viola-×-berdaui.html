Viola × berdaui

Viola × berdaui, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks, where it forms dense colonies.

Viola × berdaui is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
