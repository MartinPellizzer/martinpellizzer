Vicia cretica

Vicia cretica, commonly known as the common vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb that typically reaches heights of 20 to 60 centimeters. The plant features trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, elongated clusters. Its pods are slender, curved, and contain several elliptical seeds.

Vicia cretica is often found in disturbed soils and is commonly used as a forage crop or in ecological restoration projects.
