Vernonia calvoana

Vernonia calvoana, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces showy yellow to orange flower heads that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
