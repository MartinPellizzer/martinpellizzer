Verbascum luteoviride

Verbascum luteoviride, commonly known as the yellow-green mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of yellow-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Verbascum luteoviride is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
