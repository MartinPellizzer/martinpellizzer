Virgulus campestris

Virgulus campestris, commonly known as the field vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are typically dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along fences.

Virgulus campestris is a hardy annual that plays a role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting the surrounding soil ecosystem.
