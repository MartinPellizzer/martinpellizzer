Vernonia commutata

Vernonia commutata, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small blue or purple clouds. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Vernonia commutata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
