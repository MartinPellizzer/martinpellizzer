Viola magniflora

Viola magniflora, commonly known as the magnificent violet, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, butterfly-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing vibrant purple or violet petals with yellow markings. Viola magniflora thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and shaded landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
