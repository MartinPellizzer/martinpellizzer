Viola martia

Viola martia, commonly known as the violet bellflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive bell shape. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Viola martia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
