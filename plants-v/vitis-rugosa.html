Vitis rugosa

Vitis rugosa, commonly known as the Chinese wine grape or sand grape, is a species of vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into berries ranging from green to red or black in color. Vitis rugosa is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which are used in traditional Chinese cuisine and winemaking.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in various climates.
