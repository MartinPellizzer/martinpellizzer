Vaccinium malaccense var. bancanum

Vaccinium malaccense var. bancanum, commonly known as the Malacca blueberry, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical rainforests, preferring humid and shaded environments. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and emit a mild aromatic scent.

The botanical features of this variety make it both ecologically significant and potentially valuable for horticultural and commercial use.
