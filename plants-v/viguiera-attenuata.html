Viguiera attenuata

Viguiera attenuata, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring sturdy, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to cream-colored flowers during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Viguiera attenuata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
