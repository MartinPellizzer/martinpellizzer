Vincetoxicum officinale var. stenolobum

Vincetoxicum officinale var. stenolobum, commonly known as narrow-leafed dogwood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features showy, clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and slightly hairy. It grows in moist, shaded areas and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a woody stem that becomes more pronounced with age.
