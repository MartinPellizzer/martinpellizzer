Verbascum × ramigerum

Verbascum × ramigerum, commonly known as the common mullein, is a perennial herb native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fuzzy, velvety leaves that can grow up to 50 cm in length. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear yellow flowers in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it a popular plant for both ornamental and medicinal uses.
