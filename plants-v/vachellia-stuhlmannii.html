Vachellia stuhlmannii

Vachellia stuhlmannii, commonly known as the knobthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and bipinnate leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in terminal clusters, which are followed by long, bean-like pods. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates.

Vachellia stuhlmannii plays an important role in local ecosystems and is sometimes used for its timber or as a source of browse for livestock.
