Valeriana amphilophis

Valeriana amphilophis, commonly known as the sweet valerian, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences and has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Valeriana amphilophis is often grown in gardens for its pleasant fragrance and ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
