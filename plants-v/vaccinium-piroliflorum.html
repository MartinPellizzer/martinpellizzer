Vaccinium piroliflorum

Vaccinium piroliflorum, commonly known as the Labrador teaberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and have a tart, acidic flavor. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in boreal forest ecosystems.

The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a leathery texture and a glossy appearance.
