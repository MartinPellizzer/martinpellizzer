Veratrum eschscholtzii var. incriminatum

Veratrum eschscholtzii var. incriminatum, commonly known as the yellow hellebore, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features tall, hollow stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often adorned with yellowish-green flowers in late summer. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. Its roots are thick and fleshy, containing toxic alkaloids that have been historically used in medicine.

This variant is distinguished by its unique floral coloration and habitat preferences compared to other veratrum species.
