Vagnera amplexicaulis var. glabra

Vagnera amplexicaulis var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth-leafed vagnera, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, fleshy leaves that wrap around the stems, giving it a unique, almost vine-like appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often blooming in the late spring or early summer. Its leaves are typically smooth and waxy, which helps it retain moisture in its native habitat.

This variety is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
