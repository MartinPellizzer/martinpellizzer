Verbesina curatella

Verbesina curatella, commonly known as the yellow beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are heart-shaped with hairy surfaces, and it has prickly stems that help in seed dispersal.

Verbesina curatella is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
