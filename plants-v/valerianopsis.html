Valerianopsis

Valerianopsis, commonly known as the "button valerian," is a flowering plant belonging to the Caprifoliaceae family.

It is native to regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and thrives in moist, shady environments. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, surrounded by velvety bracts. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slight hairy texture.

Valerianopsis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally used in traditional medicine for its mild sedative properties.
