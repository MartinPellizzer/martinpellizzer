Verbesina luetzelburgii

Verbesina luetzelburgii, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble a crown, giving it its common name. The plant features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Verbesina luetzelburgii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
