Viola sepincola var. tolosana

Viola sepincola var. tolosana, commonly known as the Tolosan violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of violet or purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
