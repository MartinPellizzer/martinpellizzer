Vitis × goliath

Vitis × goliath, commonly known as the giant grapevine, is a hybrid species of the Vitis genus.

It is notable for its exceptionally large size, often reaching heights of over 20 feet. The plant produces large, dark purple berries that are typically used for winemaking. Its vigorous growth habit makes it a popular choice for trellises and arbors.

The leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a deep green color with serrated edges.
