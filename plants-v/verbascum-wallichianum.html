Verbascum wallichianum

Verbascum wallichianum, commonly known as the Himalayan mullein, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South Asia.

It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments and prefers well-drained soils. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by the wind.

Verbascum wallichianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
