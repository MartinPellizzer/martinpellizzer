Verbascum faurei subsp. commixtum

Verbascum faurei subsp.

commixtum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in soft, fuzzy hairs.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations.
