Veratrum viride

Veratrum viride, commonly known as green hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of North America.

It features tall, ribbed stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, covered with large, lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in late summer, which are followed by dark blue berries. It contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in wetland and woodland ecosystems.
