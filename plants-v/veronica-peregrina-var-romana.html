Veronica peregrina var. romana

Veronica peregrina var. romana, commonly known as the Roman speedwell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its historical use in herbal medicine is noted for its potential diuretic and antiseptic properties.
