Valeriana martjanovii

Valeriana martjanovii, commonly known as the Siberian valerian, is a perennial herb native to the cold regions of Asia, particularly Siberia and the Russian Far East.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often pink or white flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered blooms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Valeriana martjanovii is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its calming effects, and is used in traditional herbal remedies.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.
