Verbascum cataonicum

Verbascum cataonicum, commonly known as the Cataonic mullein, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are large, lance-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of soft, fuzzy hairs.

Verbascum cataonicum is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
