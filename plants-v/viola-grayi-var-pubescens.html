Viola grayi var. pubescens

Viola grayi var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent violet, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

This perennial herb features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence, giving it its common name. The flowers are typically violet or purple in color, with five petals and a distinctive spur at the base. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
