Viola tricolor var. meduanensis

Viola tricolor var. meduanensis, commonly known as the Meduan violet, is a variant of the common violet with distinct botanical characteristics.

It features trifoliate leaves composed of three heart-shaped leaflets, typically with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The flowers are usually violet or blue, with five petals arranged in a symmetrical pattern. This variant is native to Central and Eastern Europe and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
