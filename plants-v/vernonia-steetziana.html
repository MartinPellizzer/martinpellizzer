Vernonia steetziana

Vernonia steetziana, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its conical flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as swamps and marshes. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
