Verbascum agrimoniifolium subsp. syriacum

Verbascum agrimoniifolium subsp.

syriacum, commonly known as the Syrian mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features tall, hairy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, lobed leaves that are often grayish-green in color.

The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in clusters during late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable plant in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
