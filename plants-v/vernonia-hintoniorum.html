Vernonia hintoniorum

Vernonia hintoniorum, commonly known as Hinton's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, violet-blue flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as swamp margins and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are arranged in dense, terminal clusters.

Vernonia hintoniorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
