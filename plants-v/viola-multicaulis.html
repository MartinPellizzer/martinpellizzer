Viola multicaulis

Viola multicaulis, commonly known as the clustered violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces violet-blue or purple flowers with five petals, often with a distinctive yellow or white marking at the base. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Viola multicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
