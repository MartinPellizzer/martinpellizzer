Viola albertina

Viola albertina, commonly known as the Albertine violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe, particularly the Alps. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from purple to white, often with distinctive markings. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of downy hair.

Viola albertina thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.
