Veronica orchidea subsp. crassifolia

Veronica orchidea subsp.

crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved veronica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features upright stems that bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
