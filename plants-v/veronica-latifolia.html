Veronica latifolia

Veronica latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Veronica latifolia thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden settings for its delicate appearance.
