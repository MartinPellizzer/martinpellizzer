Verbena canadensis var. ehrenbergii

Verbena canadensis var. ehrenbergii, commonly known as the blue verbena, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender stems and opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it an important plant in ecological gardens.
