Vulneraria sericea

Vulneraria sericea, commonly known as silky vulnerary, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes many well-known mustard plants. The plant is characterized by its delicate, hairy leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Vulneraria sericea is noted for its medicinal properties, particularly its use in traditional herbal remedies for skin wounds and inflammation.
