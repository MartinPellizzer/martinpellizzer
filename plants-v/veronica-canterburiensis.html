Veronica canterburiensis

Veronica canterburiensis, commonly known as Canterbury bell, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence composed of numerous blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and prefers well-drained soils in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
