Vicia hyrcanica subsp. megalodonta

Vicia hyrcanica subsp.

megalodonta, commonly known as the giant hyrcanian vetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its robust, erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with long, slender stipules.

The plant produces dense clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its pods are elongated and contain several oval seeds.

This subspecies is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.
