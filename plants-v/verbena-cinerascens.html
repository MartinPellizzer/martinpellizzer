Verbena cinerascens

Verbena cinerascens, commonly known as the silver verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to white. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine, silvery or grayish pubescence, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
