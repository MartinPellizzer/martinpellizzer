Viburnum plicatum f. tomentosum

Viburnum plicatum f. tomentosum, commonly known as the cloud viburnum, is a compact, deciduous shrub characterized by its unique, cascading foliage.

Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a wavy, pleated margin that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in early spring, which are followed by dark blue, edible berries. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This ornamental plant is valued for its ornate foliage and is often used in landscape design for its aesthetic appeal.
