Vatica spatulata

Vatica spatulata, commonly known as the flat-leaf ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, hard wood and elliptical leaves. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and is valued for its durable timber. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by capsule fruits containing winged seeds.

Vatica spatulata plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various forms of wildlife.
