Vriesea ensiformis var. warmingii

Vriesea ensiformis var. warmingii, commonly known as the Warming's vriesea, is a striking bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, sword-shaped leaves that form a rosette, creating a central water-holding cup. The plant produces vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences with brightly colored bracts, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular among bromeliad enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
