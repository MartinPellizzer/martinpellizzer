Vicoa radiata

Vicoa radiata, commonly known as the golden vicoa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Vicoa radiata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
