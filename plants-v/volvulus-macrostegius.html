Volvulus macrostegius

Volvulus macrostegius, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Volvulus macrostegius is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
