Viola × militaris

Viola × militaris, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and moist meadows, preferring partial shade. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds.

Viola × militaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
