Vaccinium distichum

Vaccinium distichum, commonly known as the dwarf blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in jams and desserts. The plant thrives in acidic, nutrient-poor soils and is typically found in tundra and boreal forest environments. Its leaves are alternate, oval-shaped, and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are bell-shaped and typically pink or white.

Vaccinium distichum plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food for various wildlife species.
