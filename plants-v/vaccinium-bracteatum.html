Vaccinium bracteatum

Vaccinium bracteatum, commonly known as the Florida huckleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It blooms with clusters of white or pale pink flowers in the spring, followed by edible, bright red berries in the fall.

The berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
