Verbascum × hatayense

Verbascum × hatayense, commonly known as the Hatay mullein.

It is a hybrid species native to the eastern Mediterranean region, particularly Turkey and Syria. This plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves. It produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Verbascum × hatayense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun.
