Vernonia baldwinii var. parthenioides

Vernonia baldwinii var. parthenioides, commonly known as the slender ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight pubescence, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
