Vigna monachalis

Vigna monachalis, commonly known as the monk's bean, is a leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, kidney-shaped pods that contain several seeds. Vigna monachalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both its flowers and edible seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.
