Viscum trachycarpum var. lecomtei

Viscum trachycarpum var. lecomtei, commonly known as the Lecomte's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of specific host trees.

It belongs to the Viscaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically consumed by birds, which then disperse the seeds.

It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in forested areas where its host trees thrive.
