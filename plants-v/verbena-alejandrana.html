Verbena alejandrana

Verbena alejandrana, commonly known as Mexican verbena, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are square and branched.

Verbena alejandrana thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
