Vaccinium oblongum

Vaccinium oblongum, commonly known as the narrowleaf blueberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other berry-producing plants. The plant produces small, elliptical berries that are typically blue or purple when mature. These berries are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in culinary applications.

Vaccinium oblongum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.
