Veronica pinnata subsp. nana

Veronica pinnata subsp.

nana, commonly known as the dwarf speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features small, blue to violet flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or shaded areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a compact, hardy ground cover.
