Verbascum × semialbum

Verbascum × semialbum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Verbascum blattaria and Verbascum blattifolium.

It is a tall, biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to southern Europe. The plant features large, velvety, gray-green leaves that are soft to the touch. It produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in late spring to early summer.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
