Vaccinium pugionifolium

Vaccinium pugionifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale pink flowers in the spring. Its fruits are small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or swampy habitats.
