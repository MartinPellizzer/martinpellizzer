Viola philippica subsp. munda

Viola philippica subsp.

munda, commonly known as the Philippine violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Philippines. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky areas.

The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are usually violet or purple, with five petals and a distinctive yellowish throat.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.
