Veronica imperialis

Veronica imperialis, commonly known as the royal blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, sky-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Veronica imperialis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
