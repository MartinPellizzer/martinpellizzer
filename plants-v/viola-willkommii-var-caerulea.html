Viola willkommii var. caerulea

Viola willkommii var. caerulea, commonly known as the blue viol, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves and clusters of striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
