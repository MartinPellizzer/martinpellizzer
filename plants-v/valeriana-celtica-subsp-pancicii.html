Valeriana celtica subsp. pancicii

Valeriana celtica subsp.

pancicii, commonly known as the Celtic valerian, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

Its roots contain compounds that have been traditionally used for their calming properties.
