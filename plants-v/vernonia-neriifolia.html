Vernonia neriifolia

Vernonia neriifolia, commonly known as blackleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust growth habit. The plant features narrow, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its showy purple to pink flower heads bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Vernonia neriifolia is often found in moist, sunny habitats like wetlands and streambanks.
