Viola formosa

Viola formosa, commonly known as the showy viola, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, hairy leaves and vibrant violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its flowers bloom in early spring and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

Viola formosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
