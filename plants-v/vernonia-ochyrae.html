Vernonia ochyrae

Vernonia ochyrae, commonly known as the Hawaiian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the island of Hawaii.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia ochyrae is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its ornamental potential in gardens.
