Viola pichinchensis

Viola pichinchensis, commonly known as the Pichincha violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and cloud forests. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of fragrant, violet-colored flowers. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its showy, butterfly-shaped petals.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
