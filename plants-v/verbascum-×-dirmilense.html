Verbascum × dirmilense

Verbascum × dirmilense, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a flowering plant in the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Verbascum blattaria and Verbascum blattellum, both native to southern Europe. The plant features large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves and tall spikes of yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and ecological benefits.
