Vulpia antucensis

Vulpia antucensis, commonly known as the antuco grass, is a species of annual grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of grassland ecosystems in its native range.
