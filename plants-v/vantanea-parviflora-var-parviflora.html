Vantanea parviflora var. parviflora

Vantanea parviflora var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered vantanea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are clustered in loose inflorescences, adding a delicate ornamental quality.

It is valued in horticulture for its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in humid, partially shaded conditions.
