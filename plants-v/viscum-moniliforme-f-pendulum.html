Viscum moniliforme f. pendulum

Viscum moniliforme f. pendulum, commonly known as the weeping mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees.

It belongs to the family Viscaraceae and is characterized by its drooping, pendulous growth habit. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, white, clustered flowers. It relies on its host for water and some nutrients, while photosynthesis provides some of its own energy.

This unique growth form makes it a striking addition to forest canopies.
