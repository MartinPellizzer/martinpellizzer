Vernonia beckii

Vernonia beckii, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Vernonia beckii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
