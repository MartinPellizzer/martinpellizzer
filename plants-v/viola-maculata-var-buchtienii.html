Viola maculata var. buchtienii

Viola maculata var. buchtienii, commonly known as the Buchtien's violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive purple-tinged underside. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of violet and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
