Valeriana exscapa

Valeriana exscapa, commonly known as the mountain valerian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of small, fragrant white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or wooded areas and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

Valeriana exscapa is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its calming effects.
