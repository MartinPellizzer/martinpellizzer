Viola × ibukiana

Viola × ibukiana, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Viola yedoensis and Viola philippica.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Japan, characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves and vibrant violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15–30 cm and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.

Viola × ibukiana prefers partial shade and well-draining soil, making it a versatile choice for various garden settings.
