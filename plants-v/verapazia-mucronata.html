Verapazia mucronata

Verapazia mucronata, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant orange to red ray florets and yellow disk florets. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Verapazia mucronata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
