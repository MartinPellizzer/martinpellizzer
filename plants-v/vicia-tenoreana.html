Vicia tenoreana

Vicia tenoreana, commonly known as the Tenore's vetch, is a flowering plant species native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Vicia tenoreana is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy species with low water requirements.

Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil makes it ecologically significant in certain ecosystems.
