Veronica austriaca subsp. jacquinii

Veronica austriaca subsp.

jacquinii, commonly known as the alpine speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.

The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, blue to violet, and clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, adding to its ornamental value.

It is valued in horticulture for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and its ability to attract pollinators.
