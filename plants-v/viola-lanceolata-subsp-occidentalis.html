Viola lanceolata subsp. occidentalis

Viola lanceolata subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western heartleaf violet, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features heart-shaped, lanceolate leaves with a distinctive violet or purple bloom. Its flowers are usually spurred and bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
