Vernonia greggii subsp. greggii

Vernonia greggii subsp.

greggii, commonly known as Gregg's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native pollinators.
