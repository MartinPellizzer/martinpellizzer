Veronica speciosa var. brevifolia

Veronica speciosa var. brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features upright stems that bear small, blue to purple flowers clustered in whorls along the stem. The leaves are typically short and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and wildflower meadows.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
