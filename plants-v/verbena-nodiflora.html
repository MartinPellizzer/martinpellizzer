Verbena nodiflora

Verbena nodiflora, commonly known as the creeping verbenas, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, narrow, and slightly hairy leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes, and come in shades of blue, purple, and white.

Verbena nodiflora thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
