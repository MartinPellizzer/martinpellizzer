Viburnum barbigerum

Viburnum barbigerum, commonly known as the Chinese snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features showy, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. Viburnum barbigerum typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 feet and prefers well-drained soil.

Its foliage turns vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall, adding ornamental value to gardens.
