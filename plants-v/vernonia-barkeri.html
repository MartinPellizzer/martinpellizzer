Vernonia barkeri

Vernonia barkeri, commonly known as the Barker's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and dense clusters of small, golden-yellow flowers. The plant produces rough, hairy leaves that are often grayish-green in color, and its stems are typically covered in fine, stiff hairs. Vernonia barkeri is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in suitable climates.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
