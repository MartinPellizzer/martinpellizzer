Vitex secundiflora var. longipes

Vitex secundiflora var. longipes, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a shrub native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dark purple, clustered berries that persist into winter, providing food for wildlife. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces fragrant, blue-violet flowers in the summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy flowers and long-lasting berries.
