Valeriana baltica

Valeriana baltica, commonly known as Baltic valerian, is a perennial herb native to northern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and small, fragrant white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture and emits a strong, spicy aroma. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Valeriana baltica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically for its medicinal properties.
