Vernonia adoensis var. kotschyana

Vernonia adoensis var. kotschyana, commonly known as the Kotschyan ironweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for dry, open habitats. Its foliage is usually gray-green, with hairy surfaces that help reduce water loss.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
