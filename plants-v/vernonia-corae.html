Vernonia corae

Vernonia corae, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Vernonia corae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
