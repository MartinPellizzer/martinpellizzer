Vriesea barclayana

Vriesea barclayana, commonly known as the Barclayana Vriesea, is a striking bromeliad native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant, spathaceous bracts that can range in color from red to orange, often resembling a flower. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that collect rainwater, creating a natural reservoir for its roots. Vriesea barclayana thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
