Verbena villifolia

Verbena villifolia, commonly known as the coastal verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant has slender, hairy stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Verbena villifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
