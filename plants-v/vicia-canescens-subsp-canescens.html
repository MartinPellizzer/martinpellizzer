Vicia canescens subsp. canescens

Vicia canescens subsp.

canescens, commonly known as the hairy vetch, is a leguminous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being oval and slightly hairy.

The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that are followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

This species is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural and ecological restoration contexts.
