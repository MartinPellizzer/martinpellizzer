Volvulus japonicus

Volvulus japonicus, commonly known as the Japanese spiral flower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its unique spiral-shaped inflorescence, which consists of tightly coiled, overlapping bracts. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, with the primary ornamental feature being the striking spiral structure.
