Verbascum × pseudothapsus

Verbascum × pseudothapsus, commonly known as the common mullein, is a hybrid plant in the genus Verbascum, which belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that form a rosette at the base. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a spike-like inflorescence. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions, including North America.

Verbascum × pseudothapsus is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
