Valerianella multidentata subsp. oscensis

Valerianella multidentata subsp.

oscensis, commonly known as the alpine toothed speedwell, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its small, blue to purple flowers and trifoliate leaves with toothed margins.

The plant thrives in rocky and alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It is often found in meadows and grasslands at high elevations. Valerianella multidentata subsp.

oscensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators in its native habitat.
