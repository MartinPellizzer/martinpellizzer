Vitex cofassus f. anomala

Vitex cofassus f. anomala, commonly known as the white vitex or white champa, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves with opposite arrangement, and its stems are typically woody and branching. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several seeds, facilitating its dispersal in natural habitats.
