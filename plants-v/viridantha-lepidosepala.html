Viridantha lepidosepala

Viridantha lepidosepala, commonly known as the velvet-leafed orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often with intricate patterns. Its growth habit is epiphytic, meaning it thrives by growing on other plants without being parasitic.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its aesthetic appeal.
