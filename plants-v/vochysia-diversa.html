Vochysia diversa

Vochysia diversa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or yellow bell, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often consumed by birds and mammals. Vochysia diversa thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
