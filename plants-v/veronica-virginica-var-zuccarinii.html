Veronica virginica var. zuccarinii

Veronica virginica var. zuccarinii, commonly known as the Zuccarinii speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features small, blue to purple flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces seed capsules that split open to release its seeds. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.

This variety is closely related to other members of the Veronica genus, sharing similar botanical characteristics.
