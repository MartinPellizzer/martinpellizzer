Vitex wimberleyi

Vitex wimberleyi, commonly known as Wimberley's vitex, is a flowering plant native to the southcentral United States.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant produces showy, purple or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

Vitex wimberleyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes due to its drought tolerance.
