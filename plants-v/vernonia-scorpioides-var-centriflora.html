Vernonia scorpioides var. centriflora

Vernonia scorpioides var. centriflora, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to orange composite flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
