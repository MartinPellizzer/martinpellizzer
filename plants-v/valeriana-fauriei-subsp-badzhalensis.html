Valeriana fauriei subsp. badzhalensis

Valeriana fauriei subsp.

badzhalensis, commonly known as the Badzhal valerian, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features slender stems and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive aromatic scent. Its flowers are small, clustered in loose spikes, and are usually pink or purple in color.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional remedies.
