Vernonia carnea

Vernonia carnea, commonly known as the pink Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink to lavender-colored flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Vernonia carnea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
