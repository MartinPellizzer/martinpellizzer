Viola minuta subsp. minuta

Viola minuta subsp.

minuta, commonly known as the dwarf violet, is a small, herbaceous perennial native to Europe and western Asia. It typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, with heart-shaped leaves and delicate violet or purple flowers.

The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it is known for its ability to thrive in cool, humid climates.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
