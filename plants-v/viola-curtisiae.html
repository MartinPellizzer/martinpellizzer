Viola curtisiae

Viola curtisiae, commonly known as the African violet, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family, characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is well-adapted to humid climates. Its flowers, which can be purple, blue, or white, bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola curtisiae is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
