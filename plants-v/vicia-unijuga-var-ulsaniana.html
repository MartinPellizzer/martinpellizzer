Vicia unijuga var. ulsaniana

Vicia unijuga var. ulsaniana, commonly known as the Ulsan vetch, is a perennial legume native to parts of East Asia.

It features slender, branching stems that grow from 30 to 60 centimeters in height. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its trifoliate leaves consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with a hairy surface.

This species is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration and agricultural settings.
