Viola lactea

Viola lactea, commonly known as the milk violet, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant produces showy flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, with a distinctive violet hue. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to parts of Europe and Asia.

Viola lactea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
