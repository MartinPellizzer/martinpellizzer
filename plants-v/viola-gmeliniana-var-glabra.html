Viola gmeliniana var. glabra

Viola gmeliniana var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth viol, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces violet-colored flowers with five petals, often with a distinctive dark purple or black spot at the base. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in meadows and rocky slopes.

The smooth viol is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
