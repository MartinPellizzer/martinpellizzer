Viola tricolor var. polychroma

Viola tricolor var. polychroma, commonly known as the "three-color violet," is a herbaceous perennial belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking trifoliate leaves and showy flowers that display three distinct colors—typically purple, yellow, and white—on the same bloom. Native to Europe, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. The flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower displays.
