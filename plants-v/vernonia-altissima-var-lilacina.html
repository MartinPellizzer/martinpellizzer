Vernonia altissima var. lilacina

Vernonia altissima var. lilacina, commonly known as the lilac coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of lavender to pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist to wet soils in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
