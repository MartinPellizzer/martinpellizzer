Valeriana dioica var. hispidula

Valeriana dioica var. hispidula, commonly known as hairy valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along streambanks.

The species is noted for its strong, aromatic scent and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
