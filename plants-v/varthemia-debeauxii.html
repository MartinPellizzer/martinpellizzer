Varthemia debeauxii

Varthemia debeauxii, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or golden gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange, globe-shaped fruits that resemble small tomatoes. The plant produces small, hairy leaves and has a branched stem that grows upright. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The fruit is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor, making it a valued plant in both culinary and ornamental contexts.
