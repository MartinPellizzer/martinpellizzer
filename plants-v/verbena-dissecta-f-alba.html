Verbena dissecta f. alba

Verbena dissecta f. alba, commonly known as the white verbena, is a cultivar of the verbena genus native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, silver-gray leaves and clusters of small, tubular white flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is drought-tolerant and well-suited for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its delicate appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
