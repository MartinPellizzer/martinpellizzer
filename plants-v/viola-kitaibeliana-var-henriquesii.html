Viola kitaibeliana var. henriquesii

Viola kitaibeliana var. henriquesii, commonly known as the Henriques' violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

The species is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
