Viburnum ellipticum var. macrocarpum

Viburnum ellipticum var. macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited arrowwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with ovate to elliptic leaflets that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by showy, red to purple berries in the fall. Its berries are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

The shrub is valued in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
