Viscago paradoxa

Viscago paradoxa, commonly known as the paradoxical mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces white or pale green berries that are often consumed by birds, which aid in seed dispersal. The plant's roots form haustoria that penetrate the host's vascular system to absorb water and nutrients. Viscago paradoxa is notable for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its unique symbiotic relationship with its host.

Its presence can sometimes indicate the health of the ecosystem it inhabits.
