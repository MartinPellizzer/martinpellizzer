Vernonia missionis

Vernonia missionis, commonly known as the Mission blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its blue to violet flowers bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Vernonia missionis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar and habitat for native wildlife.
