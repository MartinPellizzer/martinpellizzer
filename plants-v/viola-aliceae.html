Viola aliceae

Viola aliceae, commonly known as the Alice's violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and thrives in cool climates. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola aliceae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
