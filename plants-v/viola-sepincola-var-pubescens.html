Viola sepincola var. pubescens

Viola sepincola var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers with five petals, often adorned with distinctive markings. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
