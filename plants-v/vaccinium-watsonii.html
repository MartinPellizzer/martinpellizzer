Vaccinium watsonii

Vaccinium watsonii, commonly known as the Watson's blueberry, is a species of shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue or white in color. Its berries are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in local cuisines.

Vaccinium watsonii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and pine flatwoods.
