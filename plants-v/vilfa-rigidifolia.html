Vilfa rigidifolia

Vilfa rigidifolia, commonly known as the rigid-leaved vilfa, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and rigid, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species adapted to harsh alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
