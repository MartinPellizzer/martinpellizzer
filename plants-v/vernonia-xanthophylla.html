Vernonia xanthophylla

Vernonia xanthophylla, commonly known as the golden ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flower heads. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters.

Vernonia xanthophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
