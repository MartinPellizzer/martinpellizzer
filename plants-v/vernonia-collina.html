Vernonia collina

Vernonia collina, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in moist, open habitats such as prairies and wetlands. It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Vernonia collina is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
