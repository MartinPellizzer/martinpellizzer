Valerianella calcarata

Valerianella calcarata, commonly known as the rock valerian, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically features small, pink or white flowers and thrives in rocky or sandy soils. The plant has a creeping habit with hairy stems and is often found in dry, calcareous environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Valerianella calcarata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to stabilize soil in challenging landscapes.
