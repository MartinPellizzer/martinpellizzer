Vincetoxicum bojerianum

Vincetoxicum bojerianum, commonly known as the Persian celandine, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, and its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

The plant contains toxic compounds, so it should be handled with care.
