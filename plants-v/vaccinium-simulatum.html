Vaccinium simulatum

Vaccinium simulatum, commonly known as the common blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, edible berries that range in color from blue to black. Vaccinium simulatum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and heathlands.

It is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its culinary and medicinal uses.
