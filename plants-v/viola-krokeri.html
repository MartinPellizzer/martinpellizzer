Viola krokeri

Viola krokeri, commonly known as the Kroker's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and small, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola krokeri is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
