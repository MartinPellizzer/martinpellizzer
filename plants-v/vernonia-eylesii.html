Vernonia eylesii

Vernonia eylesii, commonly known as the Eyles' ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia eylesii is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
