Verbesina cymosa

Verbesina cymosa, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features bright yellow flower heads that are surrounded by a crown-like ring of bracts, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly rough texture.

Verbesina cymosa blooms from late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
