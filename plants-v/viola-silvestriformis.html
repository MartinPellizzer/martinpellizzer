Viola silvestriformis

Viola silvestriformis, commonly known as the wild violet, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, butterfly-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and along shaded paths, preferring moist, well-drained soil. Its flowers bloom in early spring, ranging in color from purple to white, and are pollinated by bees and other insects.

Viola silvestriformis is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
