Vicia sativa subsp. glabra

Vicia sativa subsp.

glabra, commonly known as the common vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in loose spikes, followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing several seeds. It is a perennial herb that thrives in a variety of soils but prefers well-drained conditions. Vicia sativa subsp.

glabra is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in cultivated fields.
