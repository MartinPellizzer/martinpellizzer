Verbena officinalis subsp. halei

Verbena officinalis subsp.

halei, commonly known as Hale's verbena, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in rocky or dry habitats.

Hale's verbena is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
