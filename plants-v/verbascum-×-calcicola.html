Verbascum × calcicola

Verbascum × calcicola, commonly known as the rock mullein, is a hybrid plant species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas across Europe and parts of North America.
