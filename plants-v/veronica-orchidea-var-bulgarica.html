Veronica orchidea var. bulgarica

Veronica orchidea var. bulgarica, commonly known as the Bulgarian Veronica, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and woodland edges.
