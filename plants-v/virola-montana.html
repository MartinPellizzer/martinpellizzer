Virola montana

Virola montana, commonly known as the Amazonian ironwood, is a large evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the family Myristicaceae and is characterized by its thick, aromatic bark and dense, heavy wood. Virola montana is valued for its hard wood and has cultural significance among indigenous communities.
