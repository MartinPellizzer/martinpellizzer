Vincetoxicum arachnoideum

Vincetoxicum arachnoideum, commonly known as the spider vine, is a perennial climbing plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that appear in late summer. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its rapid growth and invasive tendencies make it a concern in some regions.
