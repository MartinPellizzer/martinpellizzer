Vanilla vanilla

Vanilla vanilla, commonly known as vanilla orchid, is a tropical climbing plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is renowned for producing the vanilla bean, a key ingredient in many cuisines and perfumes. The plant produces fragrant flowers that are hand-pollinated to ensure fruit development. The vanilla bean, harvested when mature, undergoes a curing process that develops its characteristic flavor and aroma.

Botanically, it is a herbaceous vine with heart-shaped leaves and a complex reproductive system that relies on specific pollinators.
