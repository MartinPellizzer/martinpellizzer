Vicia nummularia

Vicia nummularia, commonly known as the silver vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds. Vicia nummularia is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
