Viola viarum f. pilifera

Viola viarum f. pilifera, commonly known as the hairy violet, is a flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The flowers are typically violet or purple in color, with five petals and a distinctive spur at the back. This variant is often found in moist, woodland habitats across temperate regions.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental and ecologically beneficial plants.
