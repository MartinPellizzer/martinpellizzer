Vernonia lyallii

Vernonia lyallii, commonly known as Lyall's ironweed, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal and lowland areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in dense clusters.

Vernonia lyallii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance to native pollinators.
