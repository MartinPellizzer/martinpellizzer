Vigna unguiculata var. sesquipedalis

Vigna unguiculata var. sesquipedalis, commonly known as the large cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves with three leaflets. The flowers are typically pale yellow and are followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing edible seeds. This variety is valued for its high protein content and adaptability to various soil types.

It also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility.
