Vaccinium ingens

Vaccinium ingens, commonly known as the giant cranberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and clusters of small, edible berries that range in color from red to black. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in temperate rainforests. Its berries are used in local cuisine and traditional medicine, valued for their tart flavor and nutritional content.

Vaccinium ingens plays an important ecological role by providing food for wildlife and stabilizing soil in its natural habitat.
