Vernonia annamensis

Vernonia annamensis, commonly known as the Annam vernonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Vernonia annamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
