Viburnum taitoense

Viburnum taitoense, commonly known as the Taito viburnum, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded foliage and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Viburnum taitoense thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
