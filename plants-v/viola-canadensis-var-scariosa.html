Viola canadensis var. scariosa

Viola canadensis var. scariosa, commonly known as the scarious violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating and produces violet or purple flowers in early spring. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its showy, spurred petals.

The variety scariosa is distinguished by its dry, papery texture on the leaves, which gives it its name.
