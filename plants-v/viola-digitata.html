Viola digitata

Viola digitata, commonly known as the five-petaled violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet or purple coloration and produces small, delicate flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-30 centimeters and thrives in moist, shady environments. It is part of the Violaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to woodland areas.
