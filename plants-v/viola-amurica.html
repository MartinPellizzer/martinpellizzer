Viola amurica

Viola amurica, commonly known as the Siberian violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple and white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola amurica is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
