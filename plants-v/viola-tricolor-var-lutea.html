Viola tricolor var. lutea

Viola tricolor var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The flowers of this variety feature a mix of yellow, white, and purple petals, creating a striking visual display. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
