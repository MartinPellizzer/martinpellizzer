Vanda vitellina

Vanda vitellina, commonly known as the golden vanda, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendant stems and showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to yellow and orange. The plant typically produces clusters of blooms during the dry season, and its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air. Vanda vitellina thrives in high light conditions and requires good air circulation for optimal growth.

It is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
