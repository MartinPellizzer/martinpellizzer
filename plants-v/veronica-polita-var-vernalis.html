Veronica polita var. vernalis

Veronica polita var. vernalis, commonly known as the spring speedwell, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or meadows.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
