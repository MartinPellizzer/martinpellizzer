Vangueria crassa

Vangueria crassa, commonly known as the rough vangueria, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils. Its stems are woody and can become branched with age, while its leaves are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Vangueria crassa is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
