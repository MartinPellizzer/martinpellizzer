Ventilago cristata

Ventilago cristata, commonly known as the crown vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by distinctive, bead-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable regions.
