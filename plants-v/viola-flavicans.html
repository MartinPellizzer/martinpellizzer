Viola flavicans

Viola flavicans, commonly known as the yellow violet, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola flavicans is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
