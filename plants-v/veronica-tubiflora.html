Veronica tubiflora

Veronica tubiflora, commonly known as the woolly blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as cliffs and slopes. It has small, lance-shaped leaves covered in fine, fuzzy hairs.

Veronica tubiflora is part of the Plantaginaceae family and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
