Viola perinensis

Viola perinensis, commonly known as the Perin's violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple and violet. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Viola perinensis is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after species in horticulture and botanical gardens.
