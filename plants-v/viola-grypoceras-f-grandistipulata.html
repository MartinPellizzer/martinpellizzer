Viola grypoceras f. grandistipulata

Viola grypoceras f. grandistipulata, commonly known as the large-stippled violet, is a botanical variant of the viola genus characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves with prominent, velvety stipules that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with five petals, typically in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
