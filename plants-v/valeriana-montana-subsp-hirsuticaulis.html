Valeriana montana subsp. hirsuticaulis

Valeriana montana subsp.

hirsuticaulis, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features upright, hairy stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, often branching towards the upper part.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or white, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Valeriana montana subsp.

hirsuticaulis is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its calming effects.
