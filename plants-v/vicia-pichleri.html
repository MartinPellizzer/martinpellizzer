Vicia pichleri

Vicia pichleri, commonly known as the Pichler vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Vicia pichleri is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its natural habitat.
