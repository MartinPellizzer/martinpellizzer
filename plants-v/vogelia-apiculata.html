Vogelia apiculata

Vogelia apiculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bogbean, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet, marshy habitats. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in spikes, blooming from late summer to autumn.

Vogelia apiculata plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
