Viola arenaria subsp. rupestris

Viola arenaria subsp.

rupestris, commonly known as the Alpine violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of violet or blue. It is often found in crevices and on slopes, where it can tolerate harsh climatic conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
