Veronica pusilla var. pusilla

Veronica pusilla var. pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
