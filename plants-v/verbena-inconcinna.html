Verbena inconcinna

Verbena inconcinna, commonly known as the narrowleaf verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value contribute to its use in both garden and ecological restoration settings.
