Vernonia schweinitzii

Vernonia schweinitzii, commonly known as Schweinitz's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of purple to pinkish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as prairies, wetlands, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Vernonia schweinitzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
