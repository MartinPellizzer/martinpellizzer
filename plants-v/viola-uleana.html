Viola uleana

Viola uleana, commonly known as the Italian violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in woodland and grassland habitats. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while the plant prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Viola uleana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
