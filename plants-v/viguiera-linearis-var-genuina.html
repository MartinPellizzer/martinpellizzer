Viguiera linearis var. genuina

Viguiera linearis var. genuina, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a perennial herb native to central Mexico.

It features upright, branching stems that are covered in soft, grayish-green foliage. The plant produces dense clusters of small, pale purple to violet flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
