Vitex excelsa var. petiolata

Vitex excelsa var. petiolata, commonly known as the giant chaste tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually pubescent on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale blue, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in some regions for landscaping purposes.
