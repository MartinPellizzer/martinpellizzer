Valerianella aphanoptera

Valerianella aphanoptera, commonly known as the ghost peppergrass, is a low-growing annual herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, pale green leaves that are nearly invisible against the soil, giving it its common name. The plant produces tiny, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Valerianella aphanoptera is known for its ability to spread rapidly through seeds and is considered a weed in some regions.
