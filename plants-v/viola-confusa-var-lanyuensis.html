Viola confusa var. lanyuensis

Viola confusa var. lanyuensis, commonly known as the Lanyu viola, is a rare and endemic species native to Taiwan.

It is a herbaceous perennial with heart-shaped, dark green leaves and violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories, preferring well-drained, loamy soils. Its flowers have five petals with distinct markings, and it is noted for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native habitat.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
