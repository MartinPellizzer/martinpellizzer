Viburnum erosum var. punctatum

Viburnum erosum var. punctatum, commonly known as the spotted viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often marked with white or silver spots, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue berries in the fall. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and can reach heights of up to 6 feet.

Viburnum erosum var. punctatum is valued for its ornamental foliage and seasonal interest in gardens.
