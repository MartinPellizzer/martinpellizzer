Vernonia barbosae

Vernonia barbosae, commonly known as the Brazilian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, yellowish-green flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Vernonia barbosae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
