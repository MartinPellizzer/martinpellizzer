Verbena lasiostachys var. abramsii

Verbena lasiostachys var. abramsii, commonly known as the Arizona verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of bright blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
