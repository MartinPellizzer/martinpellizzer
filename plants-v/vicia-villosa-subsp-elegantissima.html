Vicia villosa subsp. elegantissima

Vicia villosa subsp.

elegantissima, commonly known as the silky vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with rounded leaflets.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, often in clusters. It thrives in a variety of soils but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in meadows.
