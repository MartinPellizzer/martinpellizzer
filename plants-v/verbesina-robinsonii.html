Verbesina robinsonii

Verbesina robinsonii, commonly known as the Robinson's beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces yellow to orange composite flowers, which are surrounded by pale green bracts. It has hairy stems and leaves with a serrated edge, and it thrives in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies.

Verbesina robinsonii is often found in disturbed soils and is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
