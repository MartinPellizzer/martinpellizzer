Veronica anagallis-aquatica

Veronica anagallis-aquatica, commonly known as water speedwell, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, stream banks, and marshy areas. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Veronica anagallis-aquatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wet conditions.
