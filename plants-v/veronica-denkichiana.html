Veronica denkichiana

Veronica denkichiana, commonly known as the Denkikhian speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing delicate, five-petaled flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Veronica denkichiana is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in harsh, alpine conditions.
