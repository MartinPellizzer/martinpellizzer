Verbascum ptychophyllum

Verbascum ptychophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mullein, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, erect stems covered in dense, velvety gray-green hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in clusters at the ends of the stems, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a fuzzy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
