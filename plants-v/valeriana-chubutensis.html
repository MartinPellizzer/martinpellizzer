Valeriana chubutensis

Valeriana chubutensis, commonly known as the Chubut valerian, is a perennial herb native to the Patagonian region of Argentina.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to the harsh climate of its native environment. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to survive in nutrient-poor soils, and it is often found in open grasslands and shrublands.

Valeriana chubutensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
