Viola rossii f. atropurpurea

Viola rossii f. atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple viola, is a compact perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces striking violet to purple flowers with a distinctive spurred petal. The plant typically blooms in spring, adding a splash of color to woodland gardens or shaded borders. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and moist woodland settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
