Vicia copelandii

Vicia copelandii, commonly known as the Copeland's vetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Vicia copelandii is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for certain insects.
