Valeriana grisebachiana

Valeriana grisebachiana, commonly known as the purple valerian, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits called caryopses, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

Valeriana grisebachiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has mild aromatic properties.
