Veronica dentosa

Veronica dentosa, commonly known as the toothed speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of small, blue or purple flowers with five petals. The leaves are toothed and arranged oppositely along the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
