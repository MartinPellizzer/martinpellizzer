Valeriana villosa

Valeriana villosa, commonly known as hairy valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its creeping rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are opposite and often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Valeriana villosa is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
