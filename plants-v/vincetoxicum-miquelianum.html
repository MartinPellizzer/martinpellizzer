Vincetoxicum miquelianum

Vincetoxicum miquelianum, commonly known as Miquelian beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, violet-blue berries that appear in the fall, making it a notable ornamental plant. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and fruit.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
