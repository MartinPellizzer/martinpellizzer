Vanilla calyculata

Vanilla calyculata, commonly known as the small vanilla orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces fragrant, vanilla-like pods that are used in perfumery and flavoring. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
