Vernonia complanata

Vernonia complanata, commonly known as the flatleaf ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Vernonia complanata plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
