Viburnum × carlcephalum

Viburnum × carlcephalum, commonly known as the Carolina viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.

Viburnum × carlcephalum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract wildlife.
