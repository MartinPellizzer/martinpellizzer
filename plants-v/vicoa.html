Vicoa

Vicoa, commonly known as the "blueberry of the Andes," is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other acid-loving plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart, slightly sweet flavor. Vicoa thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires a cool climate to flourish.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its dense foliage and vibrant autumn foliage.
