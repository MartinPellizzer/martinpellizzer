Valerianella macrocera

Valerianella macrocera, commonly known as the broad-leaved valerian, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of rounded, lobed leaves and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in umbels. The plant is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its aromatic leaves contain compounds that have been historically used in herbal medicine.

Valerianella macrocera is typically perennial, growing to a height of about 30 centimeters.
