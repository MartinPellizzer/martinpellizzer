Viguiera sultepecana

Viguiera sultepecana, commonly known as the Sultepec aster, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with purple markings.

Viguiera sultepecana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
