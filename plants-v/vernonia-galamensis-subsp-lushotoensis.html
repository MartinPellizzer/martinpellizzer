Vernonia galamensis subsp. lushotoensis

Vernonia galamensis subsp.

lushotoensis, commonly known as the Lushoto vernonia, is a flowering plant native to East Africa, particularly Tanzania. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flower heads.

The plant typically grows in open woodland and grassland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in dense, terminal clusters.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental potential and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
