Viburnum trabeculosum

Viburnum trabeculosum, commonly known as the roughleaf viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and a distinctive, rough texture on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. Viburnum trabeculosum is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

It is relatively low maintenance and suitable for native gardens and wildlife habitats.
