Viola bissetii

Viola bissetii, commonly known as the Bisset's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to its natural environment.

Viola bissetii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
