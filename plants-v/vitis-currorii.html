Vitis currorii

Vitis currorii, commonly known as the Curran's grape, is a species of flowering vine native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark blue to purple in color. Vitis currorii is often found in rocky or arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
