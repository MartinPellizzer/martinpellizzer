Vachellia torrei

Vachellia torrei, commonly known as the silver acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, bipinnate leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry climates. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, spherical clusters, which are an important nectar source for various pollinators. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods that split open when mature, dispersing the seeds by wind.

Vachellia torrei is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soils, making it a useful plant in ecological restoration projects.
