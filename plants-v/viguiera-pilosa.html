Viguiera pilosa

Viguiera pilosa, commonly known as Mexican bush sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in soft, grayish hairs, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often fuzzy to the touch. Viguiera pilosa produces showy clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
