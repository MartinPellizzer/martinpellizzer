Viola orphanidis var. pirinica

Viola orphanidis var. pirinica, commonly known as the Pirin violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high altitudes. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.

Due to its restricted distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
