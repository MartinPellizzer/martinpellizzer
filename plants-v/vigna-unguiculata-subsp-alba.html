Vigna unguiculata subsp. alba

Vigna unguiculata subsp.

alba, commonly known as white cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to Africa. It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant produces cylindrical pods that contain several large, edible seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional seeds and forage value.

The leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is well adapted to drought conditions.
