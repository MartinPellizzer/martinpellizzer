Vicia sericocarpa

Vicia sericocarpa, commonly known as the silky vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky and arid environments. The plant features slender, branching stems with small, compound leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to greenish-yellow, arranged in small clusters, and it produces slender, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

Vicia sericocarpa is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant in certain habitats.
