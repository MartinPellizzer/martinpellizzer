Veronica fruticans subsp. cantabrica

Veronica fruticans subsp.

cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian speedwell, is a low-growing, perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features compact, mat-forming growth with small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
