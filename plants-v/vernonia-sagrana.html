Vernonia sagrana

Vernonia sagrana, commonly known as the sacred Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has lance-shaped, hairy leaves. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Vernonia sagrana is also valued for its cultural significance in Hawaiian traditions.
