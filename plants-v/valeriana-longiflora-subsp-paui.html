Valeriana longiflora subsp. paui

Valeriana longiflora subsp.

paui, commonly known as the long-flowered valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It features upright, branching stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its aromatic foliage and medicinal properties have made it of interest in traditional herbal medicine.
