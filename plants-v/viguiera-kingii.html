Viguiera kingii

Viguiera kingii, commonly known as the King's golden coneflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has silvery-green foliage and produces a distinctive, spiky seed head that persists into winter. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

Viguiera kingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in arid landscapes.
