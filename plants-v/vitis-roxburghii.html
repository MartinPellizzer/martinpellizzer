Vitis roxburghii

Vitis roxburghii, commonly known as the Chinese fox grape, is a woody vine native to Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its vigorous growth and climbing habit. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruits are small, round berries that range in color from green to dark purple.

Vitis roxburghii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
