Vittaria confusa

Vittaria confusa, commonly known as the zigzag fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its unique, zigzag-shaped fronds that give it its common name. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive, pleated appearance.

Vittaria confusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
