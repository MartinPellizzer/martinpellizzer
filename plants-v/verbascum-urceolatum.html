Verbascum urceolatum

Verbascum urceolatum, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in a loose, upright spike. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Verbascum urceolatum is also known for its medicinal properties, historically used to treat respiratory ailments.
