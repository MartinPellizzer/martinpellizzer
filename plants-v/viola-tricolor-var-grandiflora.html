Viola tricolor var. grandiflora

Viola tricolor var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of showy violet-blue flowers with yellow and white markings, typically blooming in spring. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and as a cut flower.
