Vernonia galpinii

Vernonia galpinii, commonly known as the Galpin's ironweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy and robust.

Vernonia galpinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
