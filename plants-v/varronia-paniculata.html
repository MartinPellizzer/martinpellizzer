Varronia paniculata

Varronia paniculata, commonly known as the yellow bell or yellow woodbine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, elliptic leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Varronia paniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
