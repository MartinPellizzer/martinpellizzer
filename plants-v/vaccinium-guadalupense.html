Vaccinium guadalupense

Vaccinium guadalupense, commonly known as Guadalupine blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are usually pink or white, and it produces edible berries that are similar in taste to highbush blueberries.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential as a food source for wildlife.
