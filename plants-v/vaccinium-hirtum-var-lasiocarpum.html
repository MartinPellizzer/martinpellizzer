Vaccinium hirtum var. lasiocarpum

Vaccinium hirtum var. lasiocarpum, commonly known as the hairy blueberry, is a variant of the blueberry plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its hairy leaves and small, blue-black berries that are typically edible. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or heathland environments. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces fruit in the summer months.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential culinary uses.
