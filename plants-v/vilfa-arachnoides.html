Vilfa arachnoides

Vilfa arachnoides, commonly known as the spider-tail plant, is a unique species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and clusters of small, spider-like flowers that give it its common name. The plant has a fibrous root system and exhibits a succulent-like structure, allowing it to store water efficiently. Its leaves are typically narrow and pointed, often covered in a fine, hairy texture.

Vilfa arachnoides thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
