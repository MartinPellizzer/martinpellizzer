Valeriana nigricans

Valeriana nigricans, commonly known as black valerian, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, dark berries that are edible but not particularly palatable. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.

Valeriana nigricans is also noted for its historical use in traditional medicine.
