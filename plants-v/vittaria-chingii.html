Vittaria chingii

Vittaria chingii, commonly known as the Chinese sheet moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Vittariaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, creeping growth habit and its ability to form dense, carpet-like mats on shaded, moist surfaces. The plant has elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly curled at the tips, and it produces small, sporophytes that are typically retained on the plant. Vittaria chingii is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and thrives in humid, forested environments.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
