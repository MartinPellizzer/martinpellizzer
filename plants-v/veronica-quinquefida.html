Veronica quinquefida

Veronica quinquefida, commonly known as five-lobed speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Veronica quinquefida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens for its delicate appearance.
