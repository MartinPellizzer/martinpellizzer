Verbesina montana

Verbesina montana, commonly known as the mountain beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open woodlands and rocky areas. The plant features yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The plant is known for its prickly stems and seeds that are dispersed by wind.
