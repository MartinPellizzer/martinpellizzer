Vaccinium laetum

Vaccinium laetum, commonly known as the sweet blueberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet, tart flavor. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial shade to full sun.

Vaccinium laetum is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its culinary uses.
