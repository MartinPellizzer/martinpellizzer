Viola cucullata var. macrotis

Viola cucullata var. macrotis, commonly known as the broadleaf pansy, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It blooms in spring and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The variety macrotis distinguishes it from other viola species by its larger, more prominent flowers.
