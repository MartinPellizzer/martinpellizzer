Viola canina subsp. lactea

Viola canina subsp.

lactea, commonly known as the milk viola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.

The subspecies is distinguished by its milky sap, which is a notable botanical feature.
