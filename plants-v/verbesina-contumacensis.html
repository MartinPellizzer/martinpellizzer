Verbesina contumacensis

Verbesina contumacensis, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in stiff, hairy foliage. Its showy yellow-orange flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Verbesina contumacensis is often found in dry, disturbed habitats like roadsides and rocky areas.
