Viscum perrottetii

Viscum perrottetii, commonly known as the Perrottet's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees.

It belongs to the family Viscaceae and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are often consumed by birds, which then disperse the seeds. Viscum perrottetii is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid environments with ample sunlight.

Its parasitic nature allows it to draw nutrients and water from its host, while still performing photosynthesis to some extent.
