Veronica turkmenorum

Veronica turkmenorum, commonly known as Turkmen Veronica, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Veronica turkmenorum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
