Veratrum album f. lobelianum

Veratrum album f. lobelianum, commonly known as the lobelia-leaved white hellebore, is a variant of the white hellebore plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, erect stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant features large, lance-shaped leaves that are often lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are white and bell-shaped, arranged in loose clusters at the top of the stem.

It is known for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that make it harmful to both humans and animals.
