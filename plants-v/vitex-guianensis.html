Vitex guianensis

Vitex guianensis, commonly known as the Guianese vitex, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, blooming in the summer months. Its fruits are small, dry capsules that split open to release seeds.

Vitex guianensis is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests.
