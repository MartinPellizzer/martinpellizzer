Viola × mixta

Viola × mixta, commonly known as the common garden violet, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Viola tricolor and Viola verecunda.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, often purple or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and thrives in moist, well-drained soil in partial shade. Its flowers are often used in ornamental gardening due to their attractive appearance and ability to naturalize easily.

Viola × mixta is also valued for its medicinal properties, with historical use in treating respiratory and skin conditions.
