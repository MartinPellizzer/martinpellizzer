Veronica acinifolia var. glabrata

Veronica acinifolia var. glabrata, commonly known as the smoothleaf speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features upright stems covered with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers with five petals, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and borders.
