Veronica × austrosibirica

Veronica × austrosibirica, commonly known as the Siberian speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit with lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its hardy nature and ability to withstand cold climates.
