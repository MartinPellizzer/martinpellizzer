Viola kishidae

Viola kishidae, commonly known as the Kishida violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and mountainous regions. Viola kishidae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

Its botanical features include a short stem, hairy foliage, and a preference for well-drained soil.
