Vernonia baadii

Vernonia baadii, commonly known as the Baadi's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its bright yellow to orange flower heads are composed of numerous small florets, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

Vernonia baadii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
