Vaccinium teysmannii

Vaccinium teysmannii, commonly known as the Teyssmann's blueberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines and Indonesia. This evergreen shrub typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments. The plant produces small, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that are typically pink or white in color.

Its berries, which are edible, are usually dark blue or black when mature and are valued for their flavor and nutritional content.
