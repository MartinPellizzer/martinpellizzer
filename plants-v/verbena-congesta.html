Verbena congesta

Verbena congesta, commonly known as the clustered verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 15 inches and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Verbena congesta is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
