Valeriana veronicifolia

Valeriana veronicifolia, commonly known as the blue valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and a slender, branching stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Valeriana veronicifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
