Vexibia alopecuroides

Vexibia alopecuroides, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features soft, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.
