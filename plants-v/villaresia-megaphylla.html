Villaresia megaphylla

Villaresia megaphylla, commonly known as the giant fern, is a striking member of the Blechnaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. This plant is characterized by its large, intricate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The fronds are bi-directionally divided, creating a feather-like appearance with a distinctive, almost lace-like texture.

Its unique morphology makes it a valuable species for both ecological studies and horticultural interest.
