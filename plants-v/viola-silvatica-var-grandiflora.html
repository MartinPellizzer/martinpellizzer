Viola silvatica var. grandiflora

Viola silvatica var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered wood violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It typically grows in woodland habitats, thriving in partial shade and moist, well-drained soils. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces showy violet-blue flowers with yellow markings. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural and garden settings.
