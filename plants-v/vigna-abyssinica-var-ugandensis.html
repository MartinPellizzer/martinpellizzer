Vigna abyssinica var. ugandensis

Vigna abyssinica var. ugandensis, commonly known as the Uganda cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, with small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are typically used as a protein-rich food source. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Vigna abyssinica var. ugandensis is valued for its nutritional content and adaptability to various growing conditions.
