Vernonia eremanthifolia

Vernonia eremanthifolia, commonly known as the clustered ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy perennial suitable for native gardens.

Its botanical structure supports pollinators and contributes to ecological diversity in its habitat.
