Vernonia rugosa

Vernonia rugosa, commonly known as roughleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow to orange-red composite flowers that bloom in late summer. Vernonia rugosa is often found in prairies, open woodlands, and along roadsides.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
