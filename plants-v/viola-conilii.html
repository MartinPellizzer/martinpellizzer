Viola conilii

Viola conilii, commonly known as the Sicilian violet, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to gardens and natural habitats.

Viola conilii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
