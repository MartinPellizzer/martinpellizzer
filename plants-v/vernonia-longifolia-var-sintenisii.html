Vernonia longifolia var. sintenisii

Vernonia longifolia var. sintenisii, commonly known as the longleaf vernonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and floral display.
