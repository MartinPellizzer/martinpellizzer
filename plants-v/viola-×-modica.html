Viola × modica

Viola × modica, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a popular ornamental plant valued for its vibrant, showy flowers.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Viola tricolor and Viola verecunda, resulting in a diverse range of color variations. The plant typically produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and clusters of flowers in shades of purple, violet, and white. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

Viola × modica is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
