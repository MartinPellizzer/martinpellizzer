Valeriana stolonifera subsp. nitida

Valeriana stolonifera subsp.

nitida, commonly known as the silver valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It features upright, branching stems covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture. Valeriana stolonifera subsp.

nitida is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported calming properties.
