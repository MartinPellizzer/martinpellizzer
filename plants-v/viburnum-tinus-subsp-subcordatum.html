Viburnum tinus subsp. subcordatum

Viburnum tinus subsp.

subcordatum, commonly known as the evergreen viburnum, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage that remains throughout the year.

The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in spring, which are followed by bright red berries in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for garden use.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping.
