Vexillabium humilis

Vexillabium humilis, commonly known as the dwarf flag flower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, delicate flowers that resemble tiny flags, typically in shades of white and blue. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is adapted to full sun.

Vexillabium humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
