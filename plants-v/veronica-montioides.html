Veronica montioides

Veronica montioides, commonly known as the mountain speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically features small, blue or violet flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has thin, wiry stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Veronica montioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
