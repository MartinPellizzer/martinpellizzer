Viola × savatieri

Viola × savatieri, commonly known as the common violet or heartleaf violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland areas and gardens, where it spreads through rhizomes and self-seeding.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a shade-tolerant flowering plant.
