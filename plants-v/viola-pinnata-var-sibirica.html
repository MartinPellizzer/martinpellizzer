Viola pinnata var. sibirica

Viola pinnata var. sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a serrated margin and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of violet or blue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
