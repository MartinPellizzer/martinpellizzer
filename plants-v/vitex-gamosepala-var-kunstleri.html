Vitex gamosepala var. kunstleri

Vitex gamosepala var. kunstleri, commonly known as the Kunstler's chaste tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often pubescent on the underside. The flowers are typically clustered in panicles and are usually blue or purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, dry seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
