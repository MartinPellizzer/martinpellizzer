Vernonia junghuhniana

Vernonia junghuhniana, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
