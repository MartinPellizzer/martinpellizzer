Vicia cracca subsp. elegans

Vicia cracca subsp.

elegans, commonly known as the elegant vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae. It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, creeping stems and trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in woodland edges.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for various insects.
