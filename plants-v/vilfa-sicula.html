Vilfa sicula

Vilfa sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian violet, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate purple flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in coastal areas. It has small, hairy stems and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Vilfa sicula is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
