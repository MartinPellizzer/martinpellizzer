Vochysia grandis var. douvillei

Vochysia grandis var. douvillei, commonly known as the Douville's velvetwood, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and produces showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically elliptical and have a leathery texture, while the bark is smooth and often marked with fissures. This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.

It plays an important role in the ecosystems of its native habitats by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
