Viola × napae

Viola × napae, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of delicate, five-petaled flowers that range in color from purple to white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and gardens. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Viola × napae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for centuries in horticulture.
