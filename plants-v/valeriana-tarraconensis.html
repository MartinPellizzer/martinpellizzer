Valeriana tarraconensis

Valeriana tarraconensis, commonly known as the Iberian valerian, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown roots and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Valeriana tarraconensis is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its calming effects.
