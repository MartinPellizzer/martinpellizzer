Veronica crista-galli

Veronica crista-galli, commonly known as the mouse-ear hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense mats. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Veronica crista-galli is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
