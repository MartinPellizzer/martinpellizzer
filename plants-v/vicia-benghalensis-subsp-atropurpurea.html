Vicia benghalensis subsp. atropurpurea

Vicia benghalensis subsp.

atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple vetch, is a perennial legume native to parts of Asia and Africa. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with a distinctive purple hue.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to deep purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
