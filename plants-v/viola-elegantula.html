Viola elegantula

Viola elegantula, commonly known as the elegant violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States, where it thrives in moist, woodland habitats. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple underside and produces showy, violet-blue flowers during the spring. Viola elegantula is a perennial herb that spreads via rhizomes, forming dense colonies in its native environment.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
