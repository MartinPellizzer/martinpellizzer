Vigna vexillata var. davyi

Vigna vexillata var. davyi, commonly known as the African bean or cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellow to green flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it a valuable crop in arid environments.

The seeds are rich in protein and are widely cultivated for food and forage.
