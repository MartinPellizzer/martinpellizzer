Vatica sublacunosa

Vatica sublacunosa, commonly known as the narrow-pored ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood with a high oil content. The tree typically grows in lowland rainforests and can reach heights of up to 30 meters. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a smooth surface, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

The plant is valued for its durable timber and is also studied for its potential ecological and medicinal properties.
