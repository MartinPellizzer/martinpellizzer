Viola palustris proles bourgaei

Viola palustris proles bourgaei, commonly known as the marsh violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of violet-blue flowers with yellow centers. The plant is adapted to moist, acidic soils and often thrives in bogs, marshes, and along stream banks. It is a member of the Violaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and showy blooms.

The species is also noted for its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
