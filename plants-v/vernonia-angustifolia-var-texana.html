Vernonia angustifolia var. texana

Vernonia angustifolia var. texana, commonly known as Texas ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the central and southern United States.

It features erect, branching stems that are typically covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in full sun and well-drained soils.
