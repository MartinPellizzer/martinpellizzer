Verbena montevidensis

Verbena montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevideo verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Uruguay and southern Brazil.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically blue or purple in color, and they bloom throughout the growing season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its use in both horticultural and ecological settings.
