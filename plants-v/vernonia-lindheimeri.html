Vernonia lindheimeri

Vernonia lindheimeri, commonly known as Lindheimer's ironweed, is a perennial shrub native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple composite flowers. The plant produces rough, hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Vernonia lindheimeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
