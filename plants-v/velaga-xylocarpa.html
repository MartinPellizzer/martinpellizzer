Velaga xylocarpa

Velaga xylocarpa, commonly known as the Indian tamarind tree, is a tropical deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The tree produces long, pod-like fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a fibrous, acidic pulp. Its roots are known for their ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

The plant is valued for its timber, fruit, and traditional medicinal uses.
