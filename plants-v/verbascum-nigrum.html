Verbascum nigrum

Verbascum nigrum, commonly known as black mullein, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils.

Black mullein is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its anti-inflammatory and soothing effects.
