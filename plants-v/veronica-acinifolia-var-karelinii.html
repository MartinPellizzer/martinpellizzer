Veronica acinifolia var. karelinii

Veronica acinifolia var. karelinii, commonly known as the Karelin's speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a compact rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
