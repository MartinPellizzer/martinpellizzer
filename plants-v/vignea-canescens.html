Vignea canescens

Vignea canescens, commonly known as the gray vine or desert vine, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit, with slender, hairy stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a distinctive grayish-white color due to dense pubescence.

Vignea canescens is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
