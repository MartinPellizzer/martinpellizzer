Vernonia cordata var. hooveri

Vernonia cordata var. hooveri, commonly known as the Hoover's ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, spiky, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
