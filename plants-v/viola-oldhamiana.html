Viola oldhamiana

Viola oldhamiana, commonly known as the oldham's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of violet-blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Viola oldhamiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
