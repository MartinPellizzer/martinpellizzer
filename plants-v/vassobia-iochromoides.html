Vassobia iochromoides

Vassobia iochromoides, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in shrubland and forest edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are simple and opposite, with a slightly hairy surface.
