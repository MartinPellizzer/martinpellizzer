Viola striatella

Viola striatella, commonly known as the streaked violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or pale green stripe running down the center. The plant produces delicate, violet-blue flowers with five petals, often marked with darker veins. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Viola striatella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
