Viburnum japonicum

Viburnum japonicum, commonly known as Japanese viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits in the fall. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Viburnum japonicum is valued for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
