Vernonia psilophylla var. leiolepis

Vernonia psilophylla var. leiolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in wet conditions.

Its ornamental value is appreciated in wetland restoration projects and native plant gardens.
