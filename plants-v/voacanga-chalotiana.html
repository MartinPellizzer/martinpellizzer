Voacanga chalotiana

Voacanga chalotiana, commonly known as the African ivory tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and West Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with smooth, greyish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by elongated, woody fruits containing kidney-shaped seeds.

Its roots and bark contain alkaloids, making it significant in traditional medicine and pharmacology.
