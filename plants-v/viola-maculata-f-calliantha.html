Viola maculata f. calliantha

Viola maculata f. calliantha, commonly known as the spotted violet, is a hybrid cultivar of the common violet, characterized by its striking bicoloration.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside, often adorned with silver or white markings. The flowers are typically violet with white or cream markings, and they bloom in early spring. This plant thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
