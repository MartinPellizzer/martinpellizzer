Vicia lens subsp. nigricans

Vicia lens subsp.

nigricans, commonly known as the black vetch, is a leguminous plant characterized by its slender, trailing stems and trifoliate leaves. It produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep purple, depending on the variety.

The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural and ecological contexts. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in meadows.

Its seeds are used as forage for livestock and have historical significance in traditional medicine.
