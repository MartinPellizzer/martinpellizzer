Verzinum arboreum

Verzinum arboreum, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a deciduous tree native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by edible, red berries in the fall. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Verzinum arboreum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
