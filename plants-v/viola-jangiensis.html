Viola jangiensis

Viola jangiensis, commonly known as the Jangi violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple and violet. Viola jangiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to both ecological and horticultural contexts.
