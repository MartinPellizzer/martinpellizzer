Viscum demissum var. mahafalense

Viscum demissum var. mahafalense, commonly known as the Mahafal mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in Madagascar.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the limited light it receives. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces tiny, white or yellowish flowers that are often clustered together. Its berries are typically red or yellow and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is notable for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its unique adaptations to its island environment.
