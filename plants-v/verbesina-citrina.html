Verbesina citrina

Verbesina citrina, commonly known as yellow beggar-tick, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and often have a distinctive white or yellowish underside.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
