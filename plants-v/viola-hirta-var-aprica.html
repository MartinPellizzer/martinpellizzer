Viola hirta var. aprica

Viola hirta var. aprica, commonly known as the hairy violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, short hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, with five petals and a distinctive yellow throat. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Viola hirta var. aprica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
