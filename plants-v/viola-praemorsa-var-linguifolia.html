Viola praemorsa var. linguifolia

Viola praemorsa var. linguifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved heartleaf violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are broad and often have a distinct basal rosette structure. The flowers are typically violet or purple, with five petals and a distinctive spurred petal at the back. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
