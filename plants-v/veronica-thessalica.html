Veronica thessalica

Veronica thessalica, commonly known as the Greek speedwell, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Veronica thessalica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
