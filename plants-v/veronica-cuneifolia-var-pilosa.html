Veronica cuneifolia var. pilosa

Veronica cuneifolia var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems covered in fine, soft hairs, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers arranged in spikes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens for its delicate appearance.
