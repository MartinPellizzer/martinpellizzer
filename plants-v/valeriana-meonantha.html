Valeriana meonantha

Valeriana meonantha, commonly known as the creeping valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Valeriana meonantha is often found in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
