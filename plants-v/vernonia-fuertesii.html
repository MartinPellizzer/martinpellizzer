Vernonia fuertesii

Vernonia fuertesii, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture.

Vernonia fuertesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and vibrant floral display.
