Vulpia litardiereana

Vulpia litardiereana, commonly known as the litardierean foxtail, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial plant that typically forms dense mats on dry, sandy soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny seeds. It is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in disturbed or semi-arid habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered an important pioneer species in ecological succession.
