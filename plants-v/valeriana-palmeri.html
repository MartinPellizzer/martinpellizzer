Valeriana palmeri

Valeriana palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's valerian, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments.

Valeriana palmeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in arid gardens.
