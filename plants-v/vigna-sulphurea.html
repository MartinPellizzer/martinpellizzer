Vigna sulphurea

Vigna sulphurea, commonly known as the golden bean or yellow soybean, is a leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow pods and seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowering period is usually during the warm season, and it produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers.

Vigna sulphurea is valued not only for its nutritional content but also for its ornamental appeal due to its striking color.
