Vicia grandiflora subsp. sordida

Vicia grandiflora subsp.

sordida, commonly known as the dark vetch, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep purple. Its seed pods are slender and contain several elliptical seeds.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance in nitrogen fixation and its role in stabilizing soil in degraded areas.
