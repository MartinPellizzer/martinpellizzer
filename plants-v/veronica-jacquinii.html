Veronica jacquinii

Veronica jacquinii, commonly known as the Jacob's ladder, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the plantain family, Plantaginaceae, and is characterized by its cascading clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a stair-step pattern, giving it the common name "Jacob's ladder.".

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
