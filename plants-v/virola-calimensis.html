Virola calimensis

Virola calimensis, commonly known as the pink wood or calimense rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Myristicaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown heartwood that is highly valued for its density and durability. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a spreading crown. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a velvety texture, while its flowers are small and fragrant, typically appearing in clusters.

The plant is also noted for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional medicine.
