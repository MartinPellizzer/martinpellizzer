Vepris occidentalis

Vepris occidentalis, commonly known as West Indian wild laurier, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The fruit, though not commonly consumed, is small and contains a single seed.
