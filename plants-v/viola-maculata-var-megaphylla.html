Viola maculata var. megaphylla

Viola maculata var. megaphylla, commonly known as the large-leaved violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a distinctive white or pale violet blotch on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
