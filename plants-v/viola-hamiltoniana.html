Viola hamiltoniana

Viola hamiltoniana, commonly known as the Hamilton's violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of China, Kazakhstan, and Kyrgyzstan. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are usually violet or purple in color, with five petals and a distinctive spur at the back.

The leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help protect the plant from harsh weather conditions.
