Vernonia chamaedrys

Vernonia chamaedrys, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and fluffy, cloud-like flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its ornamental value comes from its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

Vernonia chamaedrys is often used in native plant gardens for its ecological benefits and aesthetic appeal.
