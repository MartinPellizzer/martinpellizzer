Viola maculicola

Viola maculicola, commonly known as the spotted violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purplish or reddish underside, and its flowers are typically violet or purple with yellow markings. Viola maculicola is a perennial herb that produces small, delicate blossoms in early spring.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
