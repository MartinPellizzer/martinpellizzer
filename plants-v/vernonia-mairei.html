Vernonia mairei

Vernonia mairei, commonly known as the Maire's ironweed, is a perennial shrub native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flower heads. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Vernonia mairei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
