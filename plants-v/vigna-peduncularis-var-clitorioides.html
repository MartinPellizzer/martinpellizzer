Vigna peduncularis var. clitorioides

Vigna peduncularis var. clitorioides, commonly known as the climbing bean or velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that help it ascend supports. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pods containing angular, yellow seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible seeds.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family and is notable for its nitrogen-fixing ability, enriching the soil.
