Vittaria lanceola

Vittaria lanceola, commonly known as the lanceleaf spikemoss, is a small, evergreen fern ally native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Vittariaceae and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in spore capsules at the tips of the stems. Vittaria lanceola typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Its simple morphology and ability to thrive in low-light conditions make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
