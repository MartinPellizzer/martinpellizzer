Villouratea spiciformis

Villouratea spiciformis, commonly known as the spiky leaf vine, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing along forest floors or in dense undergrowth.

Due to its unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
