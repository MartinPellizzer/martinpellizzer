Vaccinium gonzalezii

Vaccinium gonzalezii, commonly known as the Gonzalez huckleberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark blue or black in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for both ecological and potential commercial use.
