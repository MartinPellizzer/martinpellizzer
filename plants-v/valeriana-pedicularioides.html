Valeriana pedicularioides

Valeriana pedicularioides, commonly known as the clustered valerian, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features a sprawling growth habit with slender, aromatic stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and emit a distinctive, spicy fragrance when crushed.

Valeriana pedicularioides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and fragrant blooms.
