Vaccinium corymbosum f. caesariense

Vaccinium corymbosum f. caesariense, commonly known as the blueberry, is a cultivar of the highbush blueberry.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, intensely blue berries. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. It is widely cultivated for its edible fruits, which are rich in antioxidants and commonly used in culinary applications.

This form is valued for its cold hardiness and reliable fruiting in temperate climates.
