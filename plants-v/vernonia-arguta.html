Vernonia arguta

Vernonia arguta, commonly known as the slender ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks, preferring moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters.

Vernonia arguta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
