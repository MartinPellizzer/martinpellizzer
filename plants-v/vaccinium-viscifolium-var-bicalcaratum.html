Vaccinium viscifolium var. bicalcaratum

Vaccinium viscifolium var. bicalcaratum, commonly known as the black alpine bilberry, is a variant of the bilberry species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and northern Asia.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are rich in antioxidants and anthocyanins. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and alpine tundra. Its leaves are lanceolate and leathery, with a dense, compact growth habit.

This variety is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
