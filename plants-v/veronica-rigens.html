Veronica rigens

Veronica rigens, commonly known as the upright speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stems.

Veronica rigens prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardens and naturalized areas.
