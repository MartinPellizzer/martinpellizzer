Vouapa macrophylla var. heudelotiana

Vouapa macrophylla var. heudelotiana, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant has a showy, fragrant flower that blooms at night, attracting specific pollinators such as moths. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the water, while its floating leaves help in oxygenating the aquatic environment.

This variety is notable for its ecological significance in wetland ecosystems.
