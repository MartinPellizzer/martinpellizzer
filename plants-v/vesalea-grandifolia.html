Vesalea grandifolia

Vesalea grandifolia, commonly known as the grandifoliate beauty, is a striking flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in diameter, providing a lush, green backdrop for its showy blooms. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that resemble small bells, often blooming during the wet season. Vesalea grandifolia thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, fertile soil to flourish.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
