Vitis formosana

Vitis formosana, commonly known as the Formosan grapevine, is a deciduous climbing vine native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its tendrils and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by clusters of edible berries. Its botanical features include heart-shaped leaves and a woody stem that can reach several meters in length.

It is often found in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
