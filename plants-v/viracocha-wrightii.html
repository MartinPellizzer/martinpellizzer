Viracocha wrightii

Viracocha wrightii, commonly known as the Wright's giant dandelion, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has a rosette of broad, waxy leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species in botanical studies.
