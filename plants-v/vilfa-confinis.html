Vilfa confinis

Vilfa confinis, commonly known as the coastal spurge, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to saline and windy environments. It plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for small insects.

Vilfa confinis is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
