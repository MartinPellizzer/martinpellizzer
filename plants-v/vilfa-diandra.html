Vilfa diandra

Vilfa diandra, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bright yellow petals and a distinctive, elongated lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are known for their intense fragrance, which attracts specific pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value, Vilfa diandra is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
