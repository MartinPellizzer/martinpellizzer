Vernonia limosa

Vernonia limosa, commonly known as the slender ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as swamps and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and somewhat woody.

Vernonia limosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
