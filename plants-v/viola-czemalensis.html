Viola czemalensis

Viola czemalensis, commonly known as the Czemal's violet, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and cool climates. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Viola czemalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
