Veratrum stamineum var. lasiophyllum

Veratrum stamineum var. lasiophyllum, commonly known as the purple-leaf veratrum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, often adorned with purple or reddish-purple leaves. The plant produces large, showy white or pale yellow flowers in clusters at the top of the stem, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating, and its roots are thick and fleshy.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and floral display.
