Vigna linearis var. latifolia

Vigna linearis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and slender, climbing stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

Its botanical features make it an important crop in sustainable agriculture.
