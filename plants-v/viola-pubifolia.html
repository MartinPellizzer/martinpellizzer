Viola pubifolia

Viola pubifolia, commonly known as the pubescent violet, is a flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces showy violet flowers that range in color from purple to blue, often with yellow markings. Native to temperate regions of Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Viola pubifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
