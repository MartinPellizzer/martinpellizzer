Valantia tricornis

Valantia tricornis, commonly known as the three-horned valantia, is a unique flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tri-lobed leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white and pink. Valantia tricornis thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
