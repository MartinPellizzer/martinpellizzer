Viguiera eriophora

Viguiera eriophora, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flower clusters that bloom from late summer into early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped, gray-green, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Viguiera eriophora is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.
