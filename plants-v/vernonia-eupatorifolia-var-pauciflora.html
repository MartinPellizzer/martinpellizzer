Vernonia eupatorifolia var. pauciflora

Vernonia eupatorifolia var. pauciflora, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silver-white hairs, giving them a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its stems are typically hairy and can grow up to several feet tall, making it a striking addition to gardens.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.
