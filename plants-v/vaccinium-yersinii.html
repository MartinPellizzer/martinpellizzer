Vaccinium yersinii

Vaccinium yersinii, commonly known as the Yersin's blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible berries that are dark blue to black in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, forested environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential culinary uses, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and specialty horticulture settings.
