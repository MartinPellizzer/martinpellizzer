Viguiera decurrens

Viguiera decurrens, commonly known as the Mexican heath or Mexican goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with a woody base and can grow up to 1 meter tall. The plant produces bright yellow to orange flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Viguiera decurrens is often found in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun.
