Vigna coerulea

Vigna coerulea, commonly known as the blue bean or blue gram, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, blue or violet-colored seed pods, which contain edible seeds used in various cuisines. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with trifoliate leaves and small white or purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm climate with adequate sunlight.

Vigna coerulea is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a beneficial crop in sustainable agriculture.
