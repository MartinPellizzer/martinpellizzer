Verbascum carmanicum subsp. carmanicum

Verbascum carmanicum subsp.

carmanicum, commonly known as the Carmarque mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with soft, velvety leaves.

The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in a dense spike at the top of the stem, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
