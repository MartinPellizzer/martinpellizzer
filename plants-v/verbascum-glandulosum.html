Verbascum glandulosum

Verbascum glandulosum, commonly known as the sticky mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are broad, woolly, and often covered in glandular hairs that give the plant its common name. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in rocky areas.

The plant is part of the Scrophulariaceae family and is known for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
