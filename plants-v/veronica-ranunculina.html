Veronica ranunculina

Veronica ranunculina, commonly known as the small bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and typically grows in rocky or limestone soils. The plant features delicate, blue or purple flowers with five petals, and its leaves are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces. It blooms in late spring to early summer and is often found in alpine or woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and habitat specificity, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
