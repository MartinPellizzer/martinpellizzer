Veronica spuria var. angustifolia

Veronica spuria var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, angular stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually lance-shaped. Its flowers are blue to purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky habitats.
