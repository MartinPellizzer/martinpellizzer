Viola biturigensis

Viola biturigensis, commonly known as the mountain violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola biturigensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in woodland gardens and naturalized areas.
