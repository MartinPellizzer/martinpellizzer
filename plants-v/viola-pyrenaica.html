Viola pyrenaica

Viola pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula and parts of southern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and produces violet or purple flowers with a distinctive yellow center. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in alpine meadows and woodland edges.

Viola pyrenaica is also noted for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
