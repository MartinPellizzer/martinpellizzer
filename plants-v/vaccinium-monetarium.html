Vaccinium monetarium

Vaccinium monetarium, commonly known as the silverleaf blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically covered in a fine silvery or blue-gray pubescence. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, and it produces small, acidic berries that are edible but not commonly cultivated for commercial use.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or heathland environments.
