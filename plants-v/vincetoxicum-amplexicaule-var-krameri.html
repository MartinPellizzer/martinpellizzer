Vincetoxicum amplexicaule var. krameri

Vincetoxicum amplexicaule var. krameri, commonly known as the creeping false bindweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along its stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. Its roots are fibrous and can spread aggressively, making it a persistent weed in gardens and natural areas.

The plant contains toxic compounds, which can be harmful to livestock and humans if ingested.
