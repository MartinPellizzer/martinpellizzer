Vanilla seranica

Vanilla seranica, commonly known as the Seranica orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with intricate, spurred petals that are typically violet or purple in color. Vanilla seranica is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is relatively rare in the wild and is often cultivated in controlled environments.
