Vincetoxicum coriaceum

Vincetoxicum coriaceum, commonly known as common agrimony, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems covered in soft, velvety hairs and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough, slightly hairy texture, and often have a distinctive, slightly bitter aroma. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in herbal remedies.
