Verbena sororia

Verbena sororia, commonly known as the slender verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically blue to purple in color and bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
