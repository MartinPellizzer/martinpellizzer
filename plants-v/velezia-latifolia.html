Velezia latifolia

Velezia latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf velezia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Velezia latifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
