Vaccinium urceolatum var. urceolatum

Vaccinium urceolatum var. urceolatum, commonly known as the small blue huckleberry, is a species of shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces edible berries that are typically blue-black in color, which are used in local cuisines and traditional practices. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and coastal regions.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
