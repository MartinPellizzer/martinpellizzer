Vernonia kuntzei

Vernonia kuntzei, commonly known as Kuntze's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive purple or brown center.

Vernonia kuntzei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
