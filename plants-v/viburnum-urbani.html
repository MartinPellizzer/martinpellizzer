Viburnum urbani

Viburnum urbani, commonly known as the urban viburnum, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and features clusters of small, white flowers in the spring. The plant produces dark blue, edible berries that are popular with birds and some wildlife. Viburnum urbani thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to urban environments.
