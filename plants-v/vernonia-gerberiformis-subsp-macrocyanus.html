Vernonia gerberiformis subsp. macrocyanus

Vernonia gerberiformis subsp.

macrocyanus, commonly known as the blue coneflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flower heads, which are surrounded by narrow, grass-like bracts.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
