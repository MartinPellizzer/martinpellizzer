Viola odorata var. oblongata

Viola odorata var. oblongata, commonly known as the sweet violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces fragrant, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in woodland areas or along shady garden paths. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery.

The flowers are typically borne on slender stems and can range in color from deep purple to pale lilac.
