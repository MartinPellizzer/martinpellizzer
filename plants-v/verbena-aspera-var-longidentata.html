Verbena aspera var. longidentata

Verbena aspera var. longidentata, commonly known as the rough verbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
