Valerianella dentata var. macrocarpa

Valerianella dentata var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant toothed sorrel, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are small, white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. The plant produces edible leaves and seeds, which have a tangy, slightly sour flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Valerianella dentata var. macrocarpa is valued both for its culinary uses and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
