Vitis oxyodonta

Vitis oxyodonta, commonly known as the Mexican river grape, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in riparian zones and moist woodlands. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-yellow flowers that develop into edible berries, which are often consumed by wildlife. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Vitis oxyodonta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
