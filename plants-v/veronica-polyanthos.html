Veronica polyanthos

Veronica polyanthos, commonly known as the common speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is a perennial herb with upright, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Veronica polyanthos thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along streambanks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for garden use.
