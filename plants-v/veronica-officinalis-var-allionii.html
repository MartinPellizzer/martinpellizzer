Veronica officinalis var. allionii

Veronica officinalis var. allionii, commonly known as Allion's speedwell, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features upright stems with opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and arranged in clusters, blooming from late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
