Viola × mantziana

Viola × mantziana, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a species resulting from the cross between Viola tricolor and Viola wittrockii.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that come in a variety of colors, including purple, yellow, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and prefers well-drained, fertile soil in partial to full shade.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is known for its ability to thrive in cool, moist environments.
