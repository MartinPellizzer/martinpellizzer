Viridantha secundifolia

Viridantha secundifolia, commonly known as the creeping greenbark vine, is a tropical plant characterized by its trailing growth habit and glossy, dark green leaves.

It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are edible but typically consumed by birds. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant's unique combination of ornamental and medicinal value makes it a notable species in botanical studies.
