Voglera germanica

Voglera germanica, commonly known as the German violet, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm and has heart-shaped, hairy leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Voglera germanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both garden use and traditional herbal purposes.
