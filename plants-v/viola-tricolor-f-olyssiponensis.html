Viola tricolor f. olyssiponensis

Viola tricolor f. olyssiponensis, commonly known as the Olyssiponensis violet, is a compact, low-growing perennial with heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet, white, and yellow flowers.

It is a compact form of the common violet, prized for its ornamental value in gardens. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade. Its foliage is typically dark green, and it produces small, delicate flowers that bloom in early spring.

It is often cultivated as a ground cover or in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
