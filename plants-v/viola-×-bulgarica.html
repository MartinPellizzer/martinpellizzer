Viola × bulgarica

Viola × bulgarica, commonly known as the Bulgarian violet, is a hybrid species belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that typically range in color from purple to violet. Native to the Balkan region, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
