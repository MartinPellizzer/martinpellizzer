Vicia amphicarpa

Vicia amphicarpa, commonly known as the two-seeded vetch, is a species of legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by cylindrical pods containing two seeds. Vicia amphicarpa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
