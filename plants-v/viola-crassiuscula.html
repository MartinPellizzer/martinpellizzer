Viola crassiuscula

Viola crassiuscula, commonly known as the dwarf violet, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of violet or purple. The plant typically grows to a height of only a few inches, making it well-suited for rock gardens or as a ground cover. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Viola crassiuscula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
