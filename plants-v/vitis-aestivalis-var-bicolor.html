Vitis aestivalis var. bicolor

Vitis aestivalis var. bicolor, commonly known as the pink muscat grape, is a cultivar of the grapevine that features distinctive pink to red berries.

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces vigorous vines with compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into its characteristic colored fruit. Its berries are often used in winemaking and have a sweet, aromatic flavor.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities due to its attractive foliage and unique fruit coloration.
