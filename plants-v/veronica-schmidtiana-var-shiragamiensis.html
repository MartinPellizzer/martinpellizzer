Veronica schmidtiana var. shiragamiensis

Veronica schmidtiana var. shiragamiensis, commonly known as the Japanese speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical appeal lies in its delicate appearance and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
