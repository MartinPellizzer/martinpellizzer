Vernonia westermanii

Vernonia westermanii, commonly known as the Westerman's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. It produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
