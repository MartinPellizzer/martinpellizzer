Vernonia depauperata

Vernonia depauperata, commonly known as the poorflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its small, pale yellow to white flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Vernonia depauperata is often found in disturbed sites such as roadsides and dry open areas.
