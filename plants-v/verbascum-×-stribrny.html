Verbascum × stribrny

Verbascum × stribrny, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Verbascum blattaria and Verbascum blattellum.

It is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is sometimes grown in gardens.
