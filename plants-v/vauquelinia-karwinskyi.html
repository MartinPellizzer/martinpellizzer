Vauquelinia karwinskyi

Vauquelinia karwinskyi, commonly known as the Karwinsky's vauquelinia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and leathery, dark green leaves that remain throughout the year. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, which are followed by elliptical, dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Vauquelinia karwinskyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
