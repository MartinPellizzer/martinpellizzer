Viola palmata var. vulgaris

Viola palmata var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is often found in woodlands, meadows, and along stream banks.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.
