Viscum navicellatum

Viscum navicellatum, commonly known as the dwarf mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that grows on various coniferous trees.

It belongs to the Viscaceae family and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It forms dense, shrub-like growths on its host trees, often leading to the death of the host over time.

Viscum navicellatum is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it plays a significant role in forest ecosystems.
