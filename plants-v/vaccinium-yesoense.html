Vaccinium yesoense

Vaccinium yesoense, commonly known as the Yesso blueberry, is a species of shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, similar to other Vaccinium species. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary uses, it is cultivated in certain regions for both horticultural and agricultural purposes.
