Valeriana palustris

Valeriana palustris, commonly known as marsh valerian, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows up to 60 centimeters tall, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters at the top of the stems. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Valeriana palustris is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its calming effects.
