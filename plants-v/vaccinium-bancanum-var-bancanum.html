Vaccinium bancanum var. bancanum

Vaccinium bancanum var. bancanum, commonly known as the black huckleberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black when mature. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
