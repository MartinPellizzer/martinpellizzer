Verbesina densifolia

Verbesina densifolia, commonly known as roughhairs or yellow ironweed, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, hairy stems that are often branched and can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered with fine, rough hairs.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.
