Valeriana langei

Valeriana langei, commonly known as the Lange's valerian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Valerianaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often spicy-smelling foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and prefers cool, temperate climates. Its flowers are small and clustered, often white or pale pink in color, and bloom during the spring and early summer.

Valeriana langei is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for its calming effects.
