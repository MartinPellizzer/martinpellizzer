Viola × heterocarpa

Viola × heterocarpa, commonly known as the heterocarp viola, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, violet or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a common sight in woodland gardens. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for its delicate appearance and hardy nature.
