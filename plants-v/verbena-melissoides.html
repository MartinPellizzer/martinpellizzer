Verbena melissoides

Verbena melissoides, commonly known as honeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are square and erect.

Verbena melissoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
