Vernonia ambolensis

Vernonia ambolensis, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture.

Vernonia ambolensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
