Viola reverchonii

Viola reverchonii, commonly known as the Reverchon's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves and violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, delicate flowers that bloom in early spring, often with a distinctive yellowish-green throat.

Viola reverchonii is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
