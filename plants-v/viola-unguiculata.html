Viola unguiculata

Viola unguiculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is native to regions of Europe, including the British Isles, and is often found in grassy areas and woodland edges. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a distinctive, narrow base.

Viola unguiculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
