Valerianoides acuminata

Valerianoides acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-leaved valerian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Valerianaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with pointed tips, and the flowers are usually white or pale pink.

Valerianoides acuminata is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
