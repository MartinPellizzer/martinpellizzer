Valeriana integrifolia

Valeriana integrifolia, commonly known as the European valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, often purple or pink flowers and its distinctive, pungent scent. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and grows up to 1.5 meters in height. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Valeriana integrifolia is known for its medicinal properties, particularly for its calming effects.
