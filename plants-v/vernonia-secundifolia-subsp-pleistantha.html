Vernonia secundifolia subsp. pleistantha

Vernonia secundifolia subsp.

pleistantha, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellow to orange-red flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
