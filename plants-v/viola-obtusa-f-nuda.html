Viola obtusa f. nuda

Viola obtusa f. nuda, commonly known as the naked viola, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, hairy leaves and showy violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas or along stream banks. Its flowers, which bloom in spring, range in color from pale purple to white, and they are often borne on short, sturdy stems.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
