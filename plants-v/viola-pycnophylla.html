Viola pycnophylla

Viola pycnophylla, commonly known as the dense-leaved violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are densely packed, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of purple and violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Viola pycnophylla is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
