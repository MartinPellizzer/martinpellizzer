Vernonia propinqua var. canescens

Vernonia propinqua var. canescens, commonly known as the grayleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical herbaceous growth habit. The plant features grayish-green, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine pubescence. Its showy purple to pink flower heads appear in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, open woodlands or along stream banks.
