Valeriana turczaninovii

Valeriana turczaninovii, commonly known as the Turczaninov valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

Valeriana turczaninovii is valued both for its ecological role and its potential medicinal applications.
