Vittaria gracilis

Vittaria gracilis, commonly known as the slender fern, is a small, epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It typically grows on trees or rocks, relying on atmospheric moisture for nourishment. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often clustered in a rosette formation. Its fronds are usually light green and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

Vittaria gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
