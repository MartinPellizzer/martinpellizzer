Viola × champlainensis

Viola × champlainensis, commonly known as the Champlain violet, is a hybrid perennial flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and showy, violet-blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade. It produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, dark seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Viola × champlainensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
