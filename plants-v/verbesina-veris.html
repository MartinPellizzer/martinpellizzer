Verbesina veris

Verbesina veris, commonly known as goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in sunny, open areas such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Verbesina veris plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
