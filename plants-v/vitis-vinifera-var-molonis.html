Vitis vinifera var. molonis

Vitis vinifera var. molonis, commonly known as the Molon grape, is a lesser-known variety of grape native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its small, dark purple berries and is typically used for making dessert wines. The plant has a moderate growth habit and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. Its leaves are medium-sized, lobed, and have a glossy appearance.

Due to its limited cultivation, it is not widely recognized in the global wine industry.
