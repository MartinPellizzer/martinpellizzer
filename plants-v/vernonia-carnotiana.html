Vernonia carnotiana

Vernonia carnotiana, commonly known as the scarlet feverfew, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant produces bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Vernonia carnotiana is often cultivated for its vibrant color and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice in native gardens.
