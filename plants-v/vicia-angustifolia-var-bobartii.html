Vicia angustifolia var. bobartii

Vicia angustifolia var. bobartii, commonly known as narrowleaf vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, elongated leaves with a distinctive trifoliate arrangement, and its small, yellow flowers are borne in dense, spike-like clusters. The plant produces slender, twisted pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation in agricultural settings.
