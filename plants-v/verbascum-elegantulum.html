Verbascum elegantulum

Verbascum elegantulum, commonly known as the elegant mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Its common name reflects its graceful appearance and the soft, silky texture of its foliage.
