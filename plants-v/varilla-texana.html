Varilla texana

Varilla texana, commonly known as Texas varilla, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It has a creeping or ascending growth habit and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Varilla texana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
