Verbascum × nothum subsp. subphlomoides

Verbascum × nothum subsp.

subphlomoides, commonly known as the hybrid flaxleaf mullein, is a hybrid plant resulting from crosses between Verbascum species. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, lobed leaves that are often covered in soft, silky hairs.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers in elongated clusters, typical of the Scrophulariaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
