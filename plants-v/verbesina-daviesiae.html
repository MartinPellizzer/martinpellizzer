Verbesina daviesiae

Verbesina daviesiae, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits called cypselae, which are typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
