Verbesina brasiliana

Verbesina brasiliana, commonly known as Brazilian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands. The plant produces showy, golden-yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture.

Verbesina brasiliana is known for its robust growth and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
