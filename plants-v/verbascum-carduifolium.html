Verbascum carduifolium

Verbascum carduifolium, commonly known as the thistleleaf mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Verbascum carduifolium is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes other members known for their medicinal and ornamental uses.
