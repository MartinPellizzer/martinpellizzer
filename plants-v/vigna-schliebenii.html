Vigna schliebenii

Vigna schliebenii, commonly known as the African bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, with small, white to purple flowers. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both food and forage.

Vigna schliebenii plays an important role in agroecosystems due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
