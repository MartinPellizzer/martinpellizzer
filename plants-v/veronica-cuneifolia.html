Veronica cuneifolia

Veronica cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf speedwell, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, sky-blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Veronica cuneifolia is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden landscapes for its delicate appearance.
