Viola flettii

Viola flettii, commonly known as the Flett's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola flettii is often found in California and Oregon, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates.
