Veronica spicata var. urumovii

Veronica spicata var. urumovii, commonly known as the Urumov's speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces spikes of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its delicate floral display.
