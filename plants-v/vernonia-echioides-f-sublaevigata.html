Vernonia echioides f. sublaevigata

Vernonia echioides f. sublaevigata, commonly known as the smooth vernonia, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically smooth or slightly hairy on the upper surface. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its inflorescence is a cylindrical spike, characteristic of the Asteraceae family.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.
