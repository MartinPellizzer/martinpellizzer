Viburnum erosum

Viburnum erosum, commonly known as red-osier dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown stems that become prominent in winter. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn yellow in autumn.

Viburnum erosum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
