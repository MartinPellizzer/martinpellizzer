Veronica ligustrifolia

Veronica ligustrifolia, commonly known as blueweed or narrowleaf speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, blue or violet, and arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Veronica ligustrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens for its delicate appearance.
