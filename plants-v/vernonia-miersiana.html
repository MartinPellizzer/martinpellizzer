Vernonia miersiana

Vernonia miersiana, commonly known as Miers' ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, dark purple to brown flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia miersiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local pollinators.
