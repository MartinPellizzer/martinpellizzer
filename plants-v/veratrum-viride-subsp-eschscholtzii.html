Veratrum viride subsp. eschscholtzii

Veratrum viride subsp.

eschscholtzii, commonly known as the Eschscholtz's green hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It features tall, sturdy stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, crowned with clusters of pale green to yellowish flowers.

The plant has large, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to both animals and humans.
