Vigna Gracilicaulis

Vigna gracilicaulis is a lesser-known legume native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. This plant is characterized by its slender stems and small, clustered leaves that give it a delicate appearance. It produces small, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients. Despite its nutritional value, it remains underutilized in modern agriculture. Its potential as a drought-resistant crop makes it an intriguing subject for further study and cultivation.
