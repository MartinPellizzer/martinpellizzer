Viola × fouilladei

Viola × fouilladei, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Viola lutea and Viola tricolor, resulting in a plant with distinctive violet and yellow petals. The species is native to Europe and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces clusters of showy flowers in spring.

Viola × fouilladei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
