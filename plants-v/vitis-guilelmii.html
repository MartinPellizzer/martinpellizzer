Vitis guilelmii

Vitis guilelmii, commonly known as the Hungarian grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to the Carpathian Basin and is characterized by its climbing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by dark blue to black berries. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.

Vitis guilelmii is also cultivated for its potential in viticulture and ecological restoration projects.
