Viola × cordifolia

Viola × cordifolia, commonly known as the heart-leaf violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, spurred flowers that come in shades of purple, blue, and yellow. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a ground cover.

The plant's botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural and ecological purposes.
