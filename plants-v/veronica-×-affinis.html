Veronica × affinis

Veronica × affinis, commonly known as the common speedwell, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically has narrow, upright leaves and forms dense mats, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, preferring cooler climates.

Veronica × affinis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its hardy and low-maintenance nature.
