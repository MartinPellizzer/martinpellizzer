Vexillabium boreale

Vexillabium boreale, commonly known as the northern bannerflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble small banners, typically in shades of white, yellow, and red. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in tundra and alpine habitats. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to withstand cold temperatures and strong winds.

Vexillabium boreale plays a vital role in pollination and serves as a food source for certain Arctic insects.
