Viola reichenbachiana var. leucantha

Viola reichenbachiana var. leucantha, commonly known as the white viola, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and delicate, pale violet to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens. Its flowers are typically 2-3 inches in diameter, with five petals that are often marked with dark purple veins.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its ability to add a subtle, elegant touch to garden landscapes.
