Vilfa atrovirens

Vilfa atrovirens, commonly known as the black-veined violet, is a flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark purple leaves and violet flowers, which bloom in early spring. Native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins and produces small, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
