Veronica cookiana

Veronica cookiana, commonly known as the Cook's speedwell, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a low-growing habit.

Veronica cookiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
