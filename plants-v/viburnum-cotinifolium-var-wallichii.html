Viburnum cotinifolium var. wallichii

Viburnum cotinifolium var. wallichii, commonly known as the Kashmiri snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features small, white, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits in autumn. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that turn a striking crimson in the fall, adding visual interest to the landscape. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its seasonal color changes and compact growth habit.
