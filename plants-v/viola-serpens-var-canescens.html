Viola serpens var. canescens

Viola serpens var. canescens, commonly known as the downy violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a downy, hairy texture, and its flowers are usually violet or purple in color, with five petals. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
