Valdiviesoa debedoutii

Valdiviesoa debedoutii, commonly known as the Valdivia fern, is a rare and endangered fern species native to the temperate rainforests of southern Chile.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that can reach lengths of up to 1.5 meters. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments with high humidity and partial shade. Its spores are released from small, round sporangia located on the underside of the fronds.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
