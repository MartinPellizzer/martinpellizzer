Veronica galactites

Veronica galactites, commonly known as silverleaf speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It features silver-gray foliage that gives it its common name, and it produces small, blue or purple flowers in clusters. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette growth habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
