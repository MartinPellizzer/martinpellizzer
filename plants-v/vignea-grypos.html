Vignea grypos

Vignea grypos, commonly known as the winged bean, is a leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive winged pods that enclose edible seeds. The plant produces trifoliate leaves and small, white or pink flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is cultivated for its high-protein seeds.

Vignea grypos is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a beneficial crop in sustainable agriculture.
