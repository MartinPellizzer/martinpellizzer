Verbascum atheniense

Verbascum atheniense, commonly known as the Athens mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers in late spring to early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Verbascum atheniense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
