Vernonia karwinskiana

Vernonia karwinskiana, commonly known as Karwinski's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia karwinskiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
