Vitis racemifera

Vitis racemifera, commonly known as the Chinese grapevine, is a deciduous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Vitis racemifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
