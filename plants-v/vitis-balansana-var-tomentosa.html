Vitis balansana var. tomentosa

Vitis balansana var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly grapevine, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both its foliage and fruit.
