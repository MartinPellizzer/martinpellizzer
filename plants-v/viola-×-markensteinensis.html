Viola × markensteinensis

Viola × markensteinensis, commonly known as the violet violet or the hybrid violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable environments.

Viola × markensteinensis is also noted for its adaptability to various climatic conditions, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
