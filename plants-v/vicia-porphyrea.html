Vicia porphyrea

Vicia porphyrea, commonly known as the purple vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, purple-pink flowers that bloom in late spring. Its trifoliate leaves consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces elongated pods that contain several seeds.

Vicia porphyrea is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
