Vaccinium indutum

Vaccinium indutum, commonly known as the dwarf blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and heathers. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by edible berries that are dark blue to black in color. Vaccinium indutum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its compact size and ornamental value make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and wildlife habitats.
