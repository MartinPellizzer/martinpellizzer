Viola dissecta subvar. albida

Viola dissecta subvar. albida, commonly known as the white viola, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves that are finely dissected, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant produces showy, pale yellow to white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained, moist soil and partial to full shade, making it a suitable candidate for woodland gardens.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its charming, dainty flowers.
