Verbascum punalense

Verbascum punalense, commonly known as the Punalus mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes several species of flowering plants known for their fuzzy leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny conditions. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and they bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Verbascum punalense is notable for its adaptability to arid environments and its role in supporting local pollinators.
