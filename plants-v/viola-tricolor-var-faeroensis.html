Viola tricolor var. faeroensis

Viola tricolor var. faeroensis, commonly known as the Faeroese violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Faeroe Islands.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy purple, white, and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. It has small, heart-shaped leaves and produces delicate blooms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native habitat.
