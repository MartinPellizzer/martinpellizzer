Vitis barbata var. jenkensii

Vitis barbata var. jenkensii, commonly known as the Jenkens' wild grape, is a vigorous vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets and small, clustered flowers that produce edible berries. The plant thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of deciduous forests. Its berries are typically dark purple to black in color and have a tart flavor.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
