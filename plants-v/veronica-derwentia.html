Veronica derwentia

Veronica derwentia, commonly known as the Derwent Speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are oval and slightly hairy, with small, blue to violet flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and grassy areas.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a nectar source for pollinators and its historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
