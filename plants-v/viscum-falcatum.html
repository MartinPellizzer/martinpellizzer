Viscum falcatum

Viscum falcatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in the Mediterranean region.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length, and it produces small, white or yellowish flowers. The plant relies on its host for water and some nutrients, while it photosynthesizes to a limited extent. Its berries are usually red or orange, containing sticky seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Viscum falcatum plays an important ecological role in its habitat, supporting various insect and bird species.
