Viburnum bushii

Viburnum bushii, commonly known as the Sierra foothill viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and features clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring. The plant produces dark blue to black berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

Viburnum bushii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and riparian habitats.
