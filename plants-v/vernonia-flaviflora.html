Vernonia flaviflora

Vernonia flaviflora, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found in moist, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

The plant's botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
