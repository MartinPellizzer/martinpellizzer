Vigna ambacensis var. pubigera

Vigna ambacensis var. pubigera, commonly known as the hairy cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It is well adapted to dry conditions and is often cultivated for its edible seeds.

The hairy pubescence on its stems and leaves is a distinguishing feature of this variety.
