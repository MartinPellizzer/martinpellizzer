Vangueria pauciflora

Vangueria pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered vangueria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, usually during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapting to the dry climate.

Vangueria pauciflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
