Viola japonica f. variegata

Viola japonica f. variegata, commonly known as the Japanese violet variegated form, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves with white and green patterns, which add visual interest to gardens. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple and white, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
