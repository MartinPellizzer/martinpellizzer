Viguiera oblongifolia

Viguiera oblongifolia, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in fine, grayish hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. It produces showy, purple to pink flowers in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viguiera oblongifolia is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.
