Viola grypoceras f. kikuzatoi

Viola grypoceras f. kikuzatoi, commonly known as the Kikuzato violet, is a rare and ornamental plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands or rocky crevices. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, soft down, giving it a velvety texture.

The species is noted for its limited natural distribution and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens.
