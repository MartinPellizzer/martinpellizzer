Viola alba subsp. virescens

Viola alba subsp.

virescens, commonly known as the greenish violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive greenish hue, and its flowers are usually pale violet or white with dark purple veins. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
