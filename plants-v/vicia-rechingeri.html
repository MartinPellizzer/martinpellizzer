Vicia rechingeri

Vicia rechingeri, commonly known as the Rechinger's vetch, is a flowering plant species native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. Vicia rechingeri is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
