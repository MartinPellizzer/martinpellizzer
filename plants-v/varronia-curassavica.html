Varronia curassavica

Varronia curassavica, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is a small, deciduous shrub that typically grows up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with a serrated margin.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
