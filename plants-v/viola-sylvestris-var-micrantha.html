Viola sylvestris var. micrantha

Viola sylvestris var. micrantha, commonly known as the lesser violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ecological benefits.
