Vandellia hookeri

Vandellia hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's vandellia, is a unique epiphytic plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding structure. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences with bright red or orange flowers, which attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in water retention and protection from intense sunlight.

Vandellia hookeri is often found growing on trees in humid, shaded environments, where it thrives in the canopy of tropical forests.
