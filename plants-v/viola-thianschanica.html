Viola thianschanica

Viola thianschanica, commonly known as the Tian Shan violet, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple and blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola thianschanica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
