Vicia freyniana

Vicia freyniana, commonly known as the Freyn's vetch, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system helps in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Vicia freyniana is often found in shrublands and rocky areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
