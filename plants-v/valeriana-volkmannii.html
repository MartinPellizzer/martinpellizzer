Valeriana volkmannii

Valeriana volkmannii, commonly known as the Siberian valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a stout, branching stem that can grow up to 1 meter in height, adorned with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically blooming in late summer. Its rhizomatous root system is notable for containing volatile oils and alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Valeriana volkmannii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional use in herbal remedies.
