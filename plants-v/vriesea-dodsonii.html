Vriesea dodsonii

Vriesea dodsonii, commonly known as the Dodson's Vriesea, is a striking bromeliad native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant, spathaceous inflorescence that displays a mix of red, orange, and yellow hues, creating a visually captivating display. The plant features rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves that collect water, forming a central pool that supports various microorganisms. Its flowering period typically occurs during the warmer months, and it thrives in bright, indirect light with high humidity.

Vriesea dodsonii is a popular choice for indoor cultivation due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
