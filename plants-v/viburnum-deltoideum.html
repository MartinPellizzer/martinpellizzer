Viburnum deltoideum

Viburnum deltoideum, commonly known as arrowwood viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the autumn. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Viburnum deltoideum is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food and shelter for wildlife.
