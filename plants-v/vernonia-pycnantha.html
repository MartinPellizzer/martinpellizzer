Vernonia pycnantha

Vernonia pycnantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Vernonia pycnantha is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
