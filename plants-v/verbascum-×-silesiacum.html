Verbascum × silesiacum

Verbascum × silesiacum, commonly known as the Silesian mullein, is a hybrid plant belonging to the genus Verbascum in the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, velvety leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is native to Central Europe and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Verbascum × silesiacum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens.
