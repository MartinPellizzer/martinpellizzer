Vernonia stibaliae

Vernonia stibaliae, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its striking blue to violet flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Vernonia stibaliae is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
