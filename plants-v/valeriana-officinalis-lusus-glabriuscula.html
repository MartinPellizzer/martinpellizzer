Valeriana officinalis lusus glabriuscula

Valeriana officinalis lusus glabriuscula, commonly known as the smooth valerian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its roots are the primary part used in traditional medicine for their calming effects.

Valeriana officinalis lusus glabriuscula is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and moist soils.
