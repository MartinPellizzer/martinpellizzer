Viola hidakana var. ciliata

Viola hidakana var. ciliata, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate violet flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and shaded gardens, preferring moist, well-drained soil. Its flowers bloom in spring, adding a splash of color to the landscape.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
