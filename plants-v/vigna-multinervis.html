Vigna multinervis

Vigna multinervis, commonly known as the slender pigeon pea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with three elliptical leaflets and prominent veins, giving it the species name "multinervis.". The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several round, edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate with adequate sunlight.

Vigna multinervis is often cultivated for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines.
