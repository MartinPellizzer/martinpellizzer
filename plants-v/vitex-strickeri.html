Vitex strickeri

Vitex strickeri, commonly known as Stricker's vitex, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands. The plant produces showy, violet-blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Vitex strickeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
