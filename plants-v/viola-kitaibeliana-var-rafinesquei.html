Viola kitaibeliana var. rafinesquei

Viola kitaibeliana var. rafinesquei, commonly known as the Rafinesque's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blossoms.
