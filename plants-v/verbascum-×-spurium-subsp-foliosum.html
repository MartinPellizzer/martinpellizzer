Verbascum × spurium subsp. foliosum

Verbascum × spurium subsp.

foliosum, commonly known as the leafy mullein, is a hybrid plant belonging to the genus Verbascum in the family Scrophulariaceae. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, velvety, lobed leaves that are covered in a dense layer of soft hairs.

The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in clusters at the top of the stems, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its soothing properties.
