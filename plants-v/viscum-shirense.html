Viscum shirense

Viscum shirense, commonly known as the Japanese mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It belongs to the Viscaceae family and is native to Japan, where it is often found in forested areas. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces white or pale yellow berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its life cycle involves both asexual reproduction through vegetative growth and sexual reproduction via seeds dispersed by birds.

Viscum shirense plays a unique ecological role by contributing to the biodiversity of its host trees and the surrounding ecosystem.
