Viola langsdorffii f. pubescens

Viola langsdorffii f. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, velvety hairs.

It produces showy violet flowers with a distinctive dark purple throat, typically blooming in early spring. The plant is native to southern Africa and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The pubescent foliage gives it a unique texture, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
