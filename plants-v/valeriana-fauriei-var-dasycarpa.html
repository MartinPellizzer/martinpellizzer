Valeriana fauriei var. dasycarpa

Valeriana fauriei var. dasycarpa, commonly known as the dense-fruited valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often reddish, flowering stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to cold climates.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with roots and rhizomes used in traditional medicine for their calming effects.
