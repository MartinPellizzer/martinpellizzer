Villadia ramulosa

Villadia ramulosa, commonly known as the slender vine or creeping vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and slender, flexible stems that can root at the nodes. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
