Viola litoralis var. litvinovii

Viola litoralis var. litvinovii, commonly known as the Litvinov's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the coastal regions of the Black Sea.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shorelines, where it thrives in moist, saline environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, delicate violet flowers in shades of purple and blue. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is also noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
