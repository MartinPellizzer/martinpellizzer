Viola wansanensis

Viola wansanensis, commonly known as the Korean violet, is a flowering plant native to Korea.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in spring, adding a splash of color to its natural habitat.

Viola wansanensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
