Vahlia ramosissima

Vahlia ramosissima, commonly known as the hairy vahlia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, branched growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are usually hairy and oval-shaped, contributing to its common name.

Vahlia ramosissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
