Viola lutea subsp. trinitatis

Viola lutea subsp.

trinitatis, commonly known as the Trinidad violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caribbean island of Trinidad. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and rocky crevices.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, showy flowers in shades of yellow and purple. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is known for its adaptability to humid tropical conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
