Vincetoxicum fuscatum subsp. boissieri

Vincetoxicum fuscatum subsp.

boissieri, commonly known as the Balkan milkvetch, is a perennial shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers.

The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
