Valeriana zapotecana

Valeriana zapotecana, commonly known as the Zapotec valerian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Oaxaca, Mexico.

It belongs to the Valerianaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests. Valeriana zapotecana is valued for its medicinal properties, with traditional uses including the treatment of nervous disorders and insomnia.

However, it is a rare and endangered species, threatened by habitat loss and over-collection.
