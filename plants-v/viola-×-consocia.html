Viola × consocia

Viola × consocia, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Viola tricolor and Viola verecunda.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
