Vilfa murina

Vilfa murina, commonly known as the mouse ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny white flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky areas.

Vilfa murina is often found in moist soils and is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed sites.
