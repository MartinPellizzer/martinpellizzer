Verbesina hypomalaca

Verbesina hypomalaca, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in dry, open habitats.

Verbesina hypomalaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
