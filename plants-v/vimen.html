Vimen

Vimen, commonly known as the sycamore fig, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and smooth, gray bark. The tree produces small, greenish flowers that are followed by edible fig-like fruits. Vimen is often found in moist, tropical or subtropical regions and can grow quite tall, reaching heights of up to 30 meters.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine, and the tree is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
