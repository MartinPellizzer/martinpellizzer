Verbascum pseudonobile

Verbascum pseudonobile, commonly known as the silky mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in soft, fuzzy hairs that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Verbascum pseudonobile is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
