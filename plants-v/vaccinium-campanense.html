Vaccinium campanense

Vaccinium campanense, commonly known as the bell heath, is a perennial shrub native to the northeastern United States and eastern Canada.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and heathers. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing year-round foliage.

Vaccinium campanense thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded woodlands.
