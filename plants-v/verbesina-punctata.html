Verbesina punctata

Verbesina punctata, commonly known as the dotted touch-me-not, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy yellow-orange flowers with distinctive red markings, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that burst open to disperse seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and sunny habitats, often found in fields and along roadsides.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and naturalized areas.
