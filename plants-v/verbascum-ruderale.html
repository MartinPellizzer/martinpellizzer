Verbascum ruderale

Verbascum ruderale, commonly known as rough mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in a loose spike. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Verbascum ruderale is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its soothing effects on respiratory conditions.
