Vernonia buxifolia

Vernonia buxifolia, commonly known as the boxleaf ironweed, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. Vernonia buxifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its compact growth habit.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden settings.
