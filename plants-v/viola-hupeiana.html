Viola hupeiana

Viola hupeiana, commonly known as the Chinese violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil in partial shade. Its flowers are often bi-lobed and have a distinctive violet or purple coloration.

Viola hupeiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
