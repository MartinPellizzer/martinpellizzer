Vicia tenuifolia f. laxiflora

Vicia tenuifolia f. laxiflora, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a herbaceous perennial that features slender, trailing stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces clusters of pea-like flowers, typically in shades of pink or purple, which bloom in early summer. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by bees and other insects, and the plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

This variety is often found in disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
