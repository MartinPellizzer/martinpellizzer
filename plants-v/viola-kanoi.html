Viola kanoi

Viola kanoi, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a flowering plant native to Japan and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in partial shade, preferring moist, well-drained soil. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a touch of color to gardens and natural landscapes.

Viola kanoi is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
