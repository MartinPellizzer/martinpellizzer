Valeriana alpestris

Valeriana alpestris, commonly known as alpine valerian, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in spike-like clusters. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its sedative properties.

Valeriana alpestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
