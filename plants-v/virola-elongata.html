Virola elongata

Virola elongata, commonly known as the long-leaf virola, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Myristicaceae and is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The tree produces fragrant, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, spiny capsules containing numerous seeds. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.

The plant also has traditional uses in folk medicine due to its aromatic properties.
