Vepris Madagascariensis

Vepris madagascariensis is a unique plant native to Madagascar, known for its aromatic leaves and medicinal properties. It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and shares some similarities with the orange tree. This shrub thrives in tropical climates and is often found in humid, shaded areas of the island's forests. Its essential oils are used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects. Despite its importance, it remains relatively understudied in modern scientific research.
