Vitex negundo var. trifoliolata

Vitex negundo var. trifoliolata, commonly known as the three-leaved chaste tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in sunny to partially shaded locations and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and adaptable species within the Vitex genus.
