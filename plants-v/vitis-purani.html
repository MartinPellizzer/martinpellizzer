Vitis purani

Vitis purani, commonly known as the wild grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and the Korean Peninsula. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, tendrils, and heart-shaped leaves. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by edible berries that ripen to a dark purple color.

Vitis purani is often found in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
