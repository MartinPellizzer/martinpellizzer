Vagnera salvinii

Vagnera salvinii, commonly known as the Brazilian violet, is a striking flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant purple to violet flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. Vagnera salvinii is typically grown as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses due to its ornamental value.

It prefers warm, humid conditions and is often propagated through stem cuttings for cultivation.
