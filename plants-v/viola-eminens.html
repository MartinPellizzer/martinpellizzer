Viola eminens

Viola eminens, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Viola eminens is often found at high altitudes, where it adapts to harsh climatic conditions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in alpine botany and a symbol of mountainous flora.
