Viola tricolor subsp. procumbens

Viola tricolor subsp.

procumbens, commonly known as the creeping pansy, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive violet, yellow, and white coloration.

The flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with five petals that often display a striking color contrast. This subspecies is well adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
