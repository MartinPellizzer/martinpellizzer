Viscum crassulae

Viscum crassulae, commonly known as the dwarf mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that primarily grows on the branches of coniferous trees.

It belongs to the Viscaceae family and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and fleshy, berry-like fruits. The plant relies on its host for nutrients and water, often causing significant stress to the host tree. Viscum crassulae is typically found in temperate regions and is known for its unique ecological role in forest ecosystems.

Its presence can influence the growth and health of both the host and surrounding vegetation.
