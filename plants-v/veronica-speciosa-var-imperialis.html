Veronica speciosa var. imperialis

Veronica speciosa var. imperialis, commonly known as the Imperial Speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, showy blossoms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
