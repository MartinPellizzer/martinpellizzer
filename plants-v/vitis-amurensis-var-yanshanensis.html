Vitis amurensis var. yanshanensis

Vitis amurensis var. yanshanensis, commonly known as the Yanshan grapevine, is a cold-hardy vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its robust growth and ability to thrive in temperate to subarctic climates. The plant produces small, clustered flowers and dark purple to black berries that are typically consumed fresh or used in winemaking. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges, and it has a woody stem that can climb or trail along the ground.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential agricultural applications in cooler regions.
