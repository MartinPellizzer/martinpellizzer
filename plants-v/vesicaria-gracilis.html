Vesicaria gracilis

Vesicaria gracilis, commonly known as the slender bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the family Lentibulariaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, branching stems and small, bladder-like structures called bladders, which are used to trap small aquatic organisms. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that are often submerged.

Vesicaria gracilis is a carnivorous plant, relying on its bladders to supplement its nutrient intake in nutrient-poor environments.
