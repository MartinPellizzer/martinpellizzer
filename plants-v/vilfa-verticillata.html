Vilfa verticillata

Vilfa verticillata, commonly known as the whorled filaree, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually pink or white. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
