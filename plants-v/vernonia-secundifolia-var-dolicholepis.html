Vernonia secundifolia var. dolicholepis

Vernonia secundifolia var. dolicholepis, commonly known as the longleaf vernonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping and coastal gardens.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
