Vernonia betsilensis

Vernonia betsilensis, commonly known as the Madagascar ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in shrubland and forest edges, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Vernonia betsilensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its hardy nature.
