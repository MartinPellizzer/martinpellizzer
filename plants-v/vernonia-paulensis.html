Vernonia paulensis

Vernonia paulensis, commonly known as the Brazilian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is typically found in moist, tropical environments and can grow up to several meters in height. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow to orange in color.

Vernonia paulensis is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
