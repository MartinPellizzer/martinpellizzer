Viola brevistipulata f. acuminata

Viola brevistipulata f. acuminata, commonly known as the acute-leaved violet, is a compact perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are shorter and more rounded compared to the typical species. The plant produces showy violet flowers with a distinctive purple-blue hue, often with a yellow throat. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique and sought-after addition to native plant gardens.
