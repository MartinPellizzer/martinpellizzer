Viola × dubia

Viola × dubia, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a perennial flowering plant that belongs to the Violaceae family.

It is a cross between Viola tricolor and Viola wittrockii, resulting in a plant with a wide range of color variations in its flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are bilaterally symmetrical with five petals. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in natural habitats.
