Viguiera seemannii

Viguiera seemannii, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, lavender to pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely cut, gray-green leaves that are covered in a powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Viguiera seemannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
