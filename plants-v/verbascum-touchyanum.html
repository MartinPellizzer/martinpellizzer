Verbascum touchyanum

Verbascum touchyanum, commonly known as the touchyanum mullein, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are fuzzy and gray-green, with a distinctive velvety texture.

Verbascum touchyanum is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh, arid environments.
