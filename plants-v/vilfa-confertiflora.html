Vilfa confertiflora

Vilfa confertiflora, commonly known as the broad-flowered vilfa, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered inflorescences and showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
