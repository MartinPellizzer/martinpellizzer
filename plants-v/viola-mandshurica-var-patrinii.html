Viola mandshurica var. patrinii

Viola mandshurica var. patrinii, commonly known as the Siberian violet, is a flowering plant native to the cold climates of East Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky areas. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
