Vitis scabriuscula

Vitis scabriuscula, commonly known as the rough skinned grapevine, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous climbing vine belonging to the Vitaceae family, characterized by its coarse, rough-textured stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers in the spring, followed by edible berries that range in color from red to purple. It thrives in woodland areas and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Vitis scabriuscula is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
