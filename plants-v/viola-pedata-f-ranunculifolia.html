Viola pedata f. ranunculifolia

Viola pedata f. ranunculifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf violet, is a herbaceous perennial belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, broad leaves that resemble those of a buttercup, giving it its specific epithet "ranunculifolia.". The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple or violet, typically with five petals and a distinctive yellow center. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in garden landscapes for its foliage and floral display.
