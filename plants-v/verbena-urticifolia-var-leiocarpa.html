Verbena urticifolia var. leiocarpa

Verbena urticifolia var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the smoothleaf verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.

Its foliage is generally smooth and less hairy compared to other verbena species, giving it its common name.
