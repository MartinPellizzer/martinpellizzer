Viola × wilczekiana

Viola × wilczekiana, commonly known as the velvetleaf viola, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, pansy-like flowers in shades of purple, violet, and white, often with dark markings. It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in cool, moist environments, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
