Viola cucullata f. thurstonii

Viola cucullata f. thurstonii, commonly known as the Thurston's violet, is a horticultural form of the common violet.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to the eastern United States, this plant thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and the flowers are often borne on slender stems.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
