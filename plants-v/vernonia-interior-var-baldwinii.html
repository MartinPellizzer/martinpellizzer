Vernonia interior var. baldwinii

Vernonia interior var. baldwinii, commonly known as the Baldwin's ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, fluffy purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
