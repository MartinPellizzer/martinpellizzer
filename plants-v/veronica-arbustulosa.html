Veronica arbustulosa

Veronica arbustulosa, commonly known as the bush speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

Veronica arbustulosa is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
