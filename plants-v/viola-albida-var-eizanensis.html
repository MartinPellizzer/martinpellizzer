Viola albida var. eizanensis

Viola albida var. eizanensis, commonly known as the Eizan violet, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its small, pale yellow flowers with a distinctive violet hue. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its overall ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
