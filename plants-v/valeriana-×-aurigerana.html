Valeriana × aurigerana

Valeriana × aurigerana, commonly known as the golden valerian, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Caprifoliaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, oval-shaped berries that are typically reddish when mature. Native to Europe, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
