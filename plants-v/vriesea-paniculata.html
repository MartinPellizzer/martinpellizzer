Vriesea paniculata

Vriesea paniculata, commonly known as the pineapple plant or the bowersia, is a tropical bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored and covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces an inflorescence that resembles a pineapple, with colorful bracts and small white flowers. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Vriesea paniculata is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
