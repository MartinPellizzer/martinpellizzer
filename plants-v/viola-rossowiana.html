Viola rossowiana

Viola rossowiana, commonly known as the Rossow's violet, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories or along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola rossowiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
