Viola stipularis

Viola stipularis, commonly known as the stipular violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It produces small, delicate flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, with five petals and a distinctive spur. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have stipules at the base of the leaf stalks. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Viola stipularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
