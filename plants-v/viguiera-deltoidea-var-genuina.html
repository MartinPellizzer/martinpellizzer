Viguiera deltoidea var. genuina

Viguiera deltoidea var. genuina, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
