Vernonia ikongensis

Vernonia ikongensis, commonly known as the Ikongensis ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers. Vernonia ikongensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar for pollinators and supporting local biodiversity.
