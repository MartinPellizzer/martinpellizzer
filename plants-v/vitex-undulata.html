Vitex undulata

Vitex undulata, commonly known as the zigzag vitex, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that have a distinctive undulating margin. The plant produces small, fragrant, blue to purple flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its fruit is a two-lobed, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Vitex undulata is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
