Vellozia verruculosa

Vellozia verruculosa, commonly known as the woolly vellozia, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Velloziaceae and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting to its harsh environment. The plant produces striking, cone-like inflorescences that bear small, white to pink flowers. Vellozia verruculosa is often found growing on rocky outcrops and in sandy soils, where it thrives in full sun.

Due to its specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
