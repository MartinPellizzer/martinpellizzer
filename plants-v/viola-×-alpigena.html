Viola × alpigena

Viola × alpigena, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, often purple or violet flowers. The plant typically produces heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia, it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Viola × alpigena is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
