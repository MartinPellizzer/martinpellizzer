Verbascum intricatum

Verbascum intricatum, commonly known as the intricate mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are often used in teas to soothe respiratory conditions, while the flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
