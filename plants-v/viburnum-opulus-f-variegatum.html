Viburnum opulus f. variegatum

Viburnum opulus f. variegatum, commonly known as the variegated dogwood, is a decorative shrub characterized by its striking foliage.

Its leaves are typically oval or elliptical, with a white or cream-colored margin that contrasts beautifully with the green center. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal.

Viburnum opulus f. variegatum is relatively low maintenance and suitable for a variety of garden environments.
