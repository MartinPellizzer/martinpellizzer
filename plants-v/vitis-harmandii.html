Vitis harmandii

Vitis harmandii, commonly known as Harmand's grape, is a woody vine native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and canyons.

Vitis harmandii is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
