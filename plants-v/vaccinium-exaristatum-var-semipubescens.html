Vaccinium exaristatum var. semipubescens

Vaccinium exaristatum var. semipubescens, commonly known as the rough cranberry shrub, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It typically thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions. The plant produces small, red, edible berries that are similar in taste to regular cranberries. Its leaves are elliptical, with a rough texture and a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its common name.

It plays an important ecological role by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
