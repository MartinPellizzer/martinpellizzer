Vernonia galamensis

Vernonia galamensis, commonly known as the Galamensian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open woodland and savanna habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense clusters.

Vernonia galamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
