Veronica nevskii

Veronica nevskii, commonly known as the Nevsky speedwell, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Veronica nevskii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
