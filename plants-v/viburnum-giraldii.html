Viburnum giraldii

Viburnum giraldii, commonly known as the Italian viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. Viburnum giraldii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
