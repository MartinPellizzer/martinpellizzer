Verbesina simplicicaulis

Verbesina simplicicaulis, commonly known as the simple-stemmed beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats like wetlands and along stream banks.

The stems are covered in fine hairs, and the plant is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
