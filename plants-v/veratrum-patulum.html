Veratrum patulum

Veratrum patulum, commonly known as the western white hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, adorned with large, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale green to white, bell-shaped flowers in late summer, which are typically borne on long, slender peduncles. Its root system consists of a thick, fleshy caudex surrounded by a network of fibrous roots.

Veratrum patulum contains various toxic alkaloids, making it a plant with both ecological significance and potential medicinal applications.
