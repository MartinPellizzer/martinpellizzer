Vaccinium lobbii

Vaccinium lobbii, commonly known as the Lobb's blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many shrubs and small trees adapted to acidic soils. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and leathery in texture. Its flowers are bell-shaped, white to pink in color, and appear in clusters during the spring.

The fruit, a small blue-black berry, is edible and contains high levels of antioxidants.
