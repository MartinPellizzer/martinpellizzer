Verbesina petzalensis

Verbesina petzalensis, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. The plant produces showy yellow flowers arranged in dense, spherical heads, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Verbesina petzalensis is noted for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
