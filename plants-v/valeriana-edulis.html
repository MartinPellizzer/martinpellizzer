Valeriana edulis

Valeriana edulis, commonly known as edible valerian, is a perennial herb native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its rhizomes and roots are the primary parts used, containing compounds that contribute to its medicinal properties.

Valeriana edulis is valued not only for its culinary uses but also for its calming effects when prepared as a tea or tincture.
