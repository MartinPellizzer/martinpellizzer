Verbascum linkianum var. compositum

Verbascum linkianum var. compositum, commonly known as the composite mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its common name reflects the appearance of its flower cluster, which resembles a composite structure of smaller blooms.
