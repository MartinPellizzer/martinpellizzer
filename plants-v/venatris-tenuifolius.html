Venatris tenuifolius

Venatris tenuifolius, commonly known as the slender-leaf venation plant, is a herbaceous species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, narrow leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Venatris tenuifolius thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forest understories.

Its unique venation patterns on the leaves make it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
