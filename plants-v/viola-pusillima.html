Viola pusillima

Viola pusillima, commonly known as the small violet, is a delicate annual or biennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows to a height of about 5 to 15 centimeters, with heart-shaped leaves and small, violet-colored flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is known for its ability to spread through self-seeding.

Viola pusillima is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
