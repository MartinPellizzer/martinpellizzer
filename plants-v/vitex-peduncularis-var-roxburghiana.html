Vitex peduncularis var. roxburghiana

Vitex peduncularis var. roxburghiana, commonly known as the Indian Vitex or blue bean, is a perennial shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences of blue to violet flowers, which are followed by elongated, bean-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
