Vitex smilacifolia

Vitex smilacifolia, commonly known as the creeping chaste tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a woody vine characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Vitex smilacifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
