Vernonia ramiflora

Vernonia ramiflora, commonly known as the branched ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. Vernonia ramiflora is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
