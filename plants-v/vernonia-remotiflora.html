Vernonia remotiflora

Vernonia remotiflora, commonly known as the remoteflower ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies.

Vernonia remotiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
