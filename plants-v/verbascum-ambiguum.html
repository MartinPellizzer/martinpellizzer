Verbascum ambiguum

Verbascum ambiguum, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves covered in dense, soft hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Verbascum ambiguum is also known for its medicinal properties, historically used to treat respiratory ailments.
