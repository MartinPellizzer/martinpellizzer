Vadia atrovirens

Vadia atrovirens, commonly known as the black-green vadia, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, almost black foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant has small, delicate leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along its stem. It produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or pale yellow in color.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Vadia atrovirens is considered a botanically interesting but endangered species.
