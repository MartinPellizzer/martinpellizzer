Viola besseri

Viola besseri, commonly known as the Bessera violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are typically violet or purple in color, with five petals and a distinctive yellow center. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its beauty in gardens.
