Vochysia rectiflora

Vochysia rectiflora, commonly known as the white tamarillo, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits resembling small tomatoes. Its fruit is often used in culinary applications, while the tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal.

Vochysia rectiflora thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland tropical forests.
