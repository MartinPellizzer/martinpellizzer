Viola brevistipulata var. kishidae

Viola brevistipulata var. kishidae, commonly known as the Kishida's violet, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is a member of the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, purple-pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and mountainous regions. Its foliage is often covered in fine, soft hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
