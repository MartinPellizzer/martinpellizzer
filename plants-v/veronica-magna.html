Veronica magna

Veronica magna, commonly known as the great speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Veronica magna is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for.
