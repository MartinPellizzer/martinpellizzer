Viola timbalii

Viola timbalii, commonly known as the Timbalai violet, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of small, colorful flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers, which can range in color from purple to white, bloom during the spring and summer seasons.

Viola timbalii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
