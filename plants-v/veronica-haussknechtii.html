Veronica haussknechtii

Veronica haussknechtii, commonly known as the desert speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial with small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has succulent leaves and stems, which help it retain moisture in its harsh desert environment. Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters and are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Veronica haussknechtii is well adapted to rocky soils and full sun, making it a hardy species for xeriscaping.
