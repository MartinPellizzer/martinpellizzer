Vachellia zanzibarica var. zanzibarica

Vachellia zanzibarica var. zanzibarica, commonly known as the Zanzibar umbrella tree, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-like crown formed by its long, drooping branches. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils and is often found in coastal forests and dry woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical gardens.
