Vernonia tuerckheimii

Vernonia tuerckheimii, commonly known as the Tuerckheim's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its conical flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from pale yellow to deep orange.

Vernonia tuerckheimii is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
