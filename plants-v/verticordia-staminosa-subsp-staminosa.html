Verticordia staminosa subsp. staminosa

Verticordia staminosa subsp.

staminosa, commonly known as the Red-stemmed Featherflower, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive red stems and feathery, glandular leaves that give it a unique appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a popular species among botanists and gardeners alike.
