Vernonia marguana

Vernonia marguana, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are arranged in dense, cylindrical inflorescences.

Vernonia marguana is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
