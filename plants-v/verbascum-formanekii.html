Verbascum formanekii

Verbascum formanekii, commonly known as the Armenian mullein, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and Turkey.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry environments.

Verbascum formanekii is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
