Viburnum komarovii

Viburnum komarovii, commonly known as the Chinese snowball, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features clusters of white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a striking visual display. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and thrives in partial shade to full sun.

Viburnum komarovii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping for its showy blooms and hardy nature.
