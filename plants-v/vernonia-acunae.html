Vernonia acunae

Vernonia acunae, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale yellow to brown in color, and it is known for its hardy, drought-resistant nature.

Vernonia acunae is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
