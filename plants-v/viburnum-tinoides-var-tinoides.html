Viburnum tinoides var. tinoides

Viburnum tinoides var. tinoides, commonly known as arrowwood viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five ovate leaflets, and its small, white, clustered flowers appear in spring. The plant produces bright red berries in the fall, which are a food source for birds.

It thrives in partial to full shade and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
