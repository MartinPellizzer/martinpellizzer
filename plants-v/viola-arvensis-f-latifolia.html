Viola arvensis f. latifolia

Viola arvensis f. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved field violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, broad leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple or violet, typically with five petals and a yellow center. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common weed in agricultural fields and gardens.
