Vitis sinoternata

Vitis sinoternata, commonly known as the Chinese foxglove vine, is a climbing plant native to China.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its tendrils that help it climb supports. The leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and often have a rough texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
