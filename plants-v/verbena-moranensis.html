Verbena moranensis

Verbena moranensis, commonly known as the Moran's verbena, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, brightly colored flowers. The plant typically blooms in shades of red, purple, and blue, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Verbena moranensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
