Vriesea tessellata var. parisiense

Vriesea tessellata var. parisiense, commonly known as the Parisian Vriesea, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant, strap-like leaves that form a rosette, often displaying intricate patterns of green, yellow, and red. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, colorful flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and orange. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular among bromeliad enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
