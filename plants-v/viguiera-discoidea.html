Viguiera discoidea

Viguiera discoidea, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its bright orange to yellow ray flowers and dark brown disk flowers, which resemble the sun's rays. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Viguiera discoidea is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
