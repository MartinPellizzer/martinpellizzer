Valeriana ovata

Valeriana ovata, commonly known as sweet valerian, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, often pinkish or white flowers and its distinctive, fragrant leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Valeriana ovata is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its use in calming remedies.
