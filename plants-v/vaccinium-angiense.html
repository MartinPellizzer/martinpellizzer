Vaccinium angiense

Vaccinium angiense, commonly known as the Anguilla huckleberry, is a species of shrub native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or purple in color. These berries are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in coastal areas and forest understories.
