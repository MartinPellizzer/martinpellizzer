Viburnum ferrugineum

Viburnum ferrugineum, commonly known as the rusty blackhaw, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by dark blue berries in the fall. Its bark is thick and flaky, often appearing reddish-brown, and it can grow up to 6 meters in height.

Viburnum ferrugineum is often found in moist woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
