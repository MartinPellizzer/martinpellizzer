Viola appendiculata

Viola appendiculata, commonly known as the appendiculate violet, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and delicate violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring and are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola appendiculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
