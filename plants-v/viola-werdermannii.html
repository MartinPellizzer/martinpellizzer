Viola werdermannii

Viola werdermannii, commonly known as the Werdermann's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to its natural habitat.

Viola werdermannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
