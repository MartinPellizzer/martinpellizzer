Vernonia leucocalyx var. acuta

Vernonia leucocalyx var. acuta, commonly known as the acuteleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, blue to purple flower heads. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to moist to wet soil conditions.

This plant is often found in wetlands, prairies, and along the edges of streams.
