Viola turczaninowii

Viola turczaninowii, commonly known as the Turczaninow's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils and sunny to partially shaded habitats. The plant produces violet-blue flowers with five petals, often accompanied by a distinctive yellow spur. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Viola turczaninowii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
