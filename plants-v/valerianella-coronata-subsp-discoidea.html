Valerianella coronata subsp. discoidea

Valerianella coronata subsp.

discoidea, commonly known as "button valerian" or "false sorrel," is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features small, rounded leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive, slightly succulent texture.

The plant produces tiny, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common sight in damp, temperate regions where it contributes to the local flora.
