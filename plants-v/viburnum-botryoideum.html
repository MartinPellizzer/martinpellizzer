Viburnum botryoideum

Viburnum botryoideum, commonly known as the snowball tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring, giving it its common name. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its showy floral display.

Viburnum botryoideum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators and its adaptability to various soil types.
