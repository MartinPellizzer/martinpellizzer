Viola sikkimensis var. debilis

Viola sikkimensis var. debilis, commonly known as the Sikkim violet, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It is a herbaceous perennial with heart-shaped, hairy leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually borne on slender stems and are characterized by their five-petaled structure.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
