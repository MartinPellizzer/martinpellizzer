Veronica spicata subsp. orchidea

Veronica spicata subsp.

orchidea, commonly known as the orchid veronica, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and spikes of small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often gray-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate flowers and compact form, making it a popular choice in horticultural gardens.
