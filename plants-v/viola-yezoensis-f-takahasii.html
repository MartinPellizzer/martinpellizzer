Viola yezoensis f. takahasii

Viola yezoensis f. takahasii, commonly known as the Yezo violet, is a hybrid form of the Japanese violet.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and dark green coloration. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or shaded gardens.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in temperate climates.
