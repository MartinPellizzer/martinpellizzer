Vernonia ignobilis

Vernonia ignobilis, commonly known as the rusty vervain, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with purple veins.

Vernonia ignobilis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
