Veronica elatior

Veronica elatior, commonly known as the tall speedwell, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are opposite along the stems. Its flowers are borne in whorls along the stem, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Veronica elatior thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
