Viola verecunda f. violascens

Viola verecunda f. violascens, commonly known as the violet-leaf viola, is a hybrid plant within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive violet-colored leaves and flowers that range from pale purple to deep violet. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and the flowers are borne on slender stems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
