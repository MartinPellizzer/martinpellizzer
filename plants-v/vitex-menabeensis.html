Vitex menabeensis

Vitex menabeensis, commonly known as the Madagascar glorybower, is a shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the verbena family (Verbenaceae) and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges, and its flowering period typically occurs during the dry season. Vitex menabeensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine by local communities.
