Viola heterophylla subsp. epirota

Viola heterophylla subsp.

epirota, commonly known as the Epirus violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows in moist, shaded woodland habitats and has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces violet-blue flowers with five petals, often with a distinctive yellow spot at the base. It is characterized by its variable leaf morphology, which can range from oval to deeply lobed depending on environmental conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
