Vincetoxicum parvum

Vincetoxicum parvum, commonly known as smallleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist woodlands and along stream banks, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. Its showy purple to pink flowers appear in panicles from late summer to early fall.

Vincetoxicum parvum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
