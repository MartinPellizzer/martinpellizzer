Vitis diffusa

Vitis diffusa, commonly known as Japanese knotweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Vitaceae family.

It is characterized by its bamboo-like stems that can grow up to 10 feet tall and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish tinge. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue to black berries. It is native to East Asia but has become an invasive species in many parts of the world due to its rapid growth and ability to outcompete native vegetation.

Its rhizomatous root system makes it particularly difficult to eradicate.
