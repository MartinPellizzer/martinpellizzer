Viola seoulensis

Viola seoulensis, commonly known as the Seoul violet, is a flowering plant native to South Korea.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its blossoms, which appear in early spring, attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola seoulensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
