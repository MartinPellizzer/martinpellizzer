Vernonia aristeguietae

Vernonia aristeguietae, commonly known as the Sierra Madre ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its distinctive purple to pinkish-blue flowers. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, bract-like structures around its flower heads.

Vernonia aristeguietae is often found in arid regions and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
