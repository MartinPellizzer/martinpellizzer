Valeriana rotundifolia var. tripartita

Valeriana rotundifolia var. tripartita, commonly known as the three-lobed valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous root system and aromatic, lanceolate leaves with a distinctive three-lobed shape. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in dense, rounded clusters called cymes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or alpine habitats.

Valeriana rotundifolia var. tripartita is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its calming effects.
