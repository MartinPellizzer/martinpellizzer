Vallaris dichotoma

Vallaris dichotoma, commonly known as the twin-stemmed vallaris, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces delicate, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Vallaris dichotoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
