Vernonia koelzii

Vernonia koelzii, commonly known as the Koelz's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, purple to red composite flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as coastal areas and dry forests. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often woody and branched.

Vernonia koelzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the unique flora of Hawaii's ecosystems.
