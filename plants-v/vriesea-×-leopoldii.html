Vriesea × leopoldii

Vriesea × leopoldii, commonly known as the leopard plant, is a hybrid bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescence that resembles a leopard's spots, hence its common name. The plant features a rosette of broad, waxy leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Vriesea × leopoldii is popular as a decorative plant due to its vibrant appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
