Verbascum claytonii

Verbascum claytonii, commonly known as Clayton's mullein, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its common name reflects its historical use as a remedy for respiratory ailments.
