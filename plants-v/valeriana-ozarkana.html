Valeriana ozarkana

Valeriana ozarkana, commonly known as Ozark valerian, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, somewhat hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its roots are often harvested for their medicinal properties, which include sedative and calming effects.

Ozark valerian thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
