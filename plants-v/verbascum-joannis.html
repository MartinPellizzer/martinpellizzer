Verbascum joannis

Verbascum joannis, commonly known as the John's mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in soft, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are large, lance-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine down.

Verbascum joannis is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its mild laxative properties.
