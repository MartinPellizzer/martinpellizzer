Viscainoa geniculata

Viscainoa geniculata, commonly known as the creeping broom, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and produces small, yellowish flowers. The plant has slender, branched stems that often root at the nodes, allowing it to spread along the ground. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Viscainoa geniculata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
