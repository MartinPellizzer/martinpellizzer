Viola montana var. lucorum

Viola montana var. lucorum, commonly known as the yellow violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with purple veins, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
