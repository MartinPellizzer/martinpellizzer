Vaccinium sclerophyllum

Vaccinium sclerophyllum, commonly known as the hardleaf blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and heathers. The plant is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisine.

This species is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
