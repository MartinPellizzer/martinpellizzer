Viola × koehleri

Viola × koehleri, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green with a slight pubescence. The plant produces showy, downward-facing flowers in shades of purple, violet, and white, often with intricate patterns on the petals. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Viola × koehleri is a member of the Violaceae family and is known for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
