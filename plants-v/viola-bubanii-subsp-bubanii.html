Viola bubanii subsp. bubanii

Viola bubanii subsp.

bubanii, commonly known as the Balkan violet, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and rocky areas. It has a short, upright stem and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also of interest in botanical studies due to its unique ecological adaptations.
