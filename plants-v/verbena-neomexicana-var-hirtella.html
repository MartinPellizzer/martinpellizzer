Verbena neomexicana var. hirtella

Verbena neomexicana var. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with slender stems and small, opposite leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of showy, five-petaled blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in arid landscapes.
