Viola odorata f. dumetorum

Viola odorata f. dumetorum, commonly known as the sweet violet, is a herbaceous perennial belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. Its flowers emit a sweet, spicy scent, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in herbal medicine.
