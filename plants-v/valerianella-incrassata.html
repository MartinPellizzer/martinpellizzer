Valerianella incrassata

Valerianella incrassata, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing annual plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and it produces tiny, white or pinkish flowers. The plant thrives in moist, cool environments and is often found in gardens, lawns, and along pathways. It is known for its mild, somewhat peppery flavor, making it a popular edible in salads.

Valerianella incrassata is also valued for its ability to suppress weeds and improve soil structure.
