Velleia trinervis var. villosa

Velleia trinervis var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy velleia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, slender stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with three main veins, giving it the species name *trinervis*.

This variety is distinguished by its dense, velvety foliage, which contributes to its common name.
