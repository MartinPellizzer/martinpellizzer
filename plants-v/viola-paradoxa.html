Viola paradoxa

Viola paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet or purple coloration, and its flowers are typically violet or blue with yellow markings. The plant belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy blooms. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Viola paradoxa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
