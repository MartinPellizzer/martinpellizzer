Vernonia elaeagnus

Vernonia elaeagnus, commonly known as silverleaf Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its silvery-gray leaves and showy yellow flowers. The leaves are opposite, narrow, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving them a distinctive appearance. The flowers are clustered in terminal panicles and bloom during the cooler months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
