Vriesea psittacina var. decolor

Vriesea psittacina var. decolor, commonly known as the decolor bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful inflorescence that often displays a unique decoloration pattern. The plant features a rosette of elongated, narrow leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy coating. Its flowers, which bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow, are followed by a long, pendant bract that remains for an extended period.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid, indirect light conditions.
