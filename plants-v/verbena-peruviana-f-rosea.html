Verbena peruviana f. rosea

Verbena peruviana f. rosea, commonly known as the rose verbena, is a flowering plant belonging to the Verbenaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces tubular flowers that are typically arranged in spikes, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. Native to South America, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun conditions.

Its foliage is usually covered in fine, hairy texture, adding to its visual appeal.
