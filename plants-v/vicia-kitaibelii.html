Vicia kitaibelii

Vicia kitaibelii, commonly known as the Kitaibel's vetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in spring. Vicia kitaibelii is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can remain viable in the soil for several years.
