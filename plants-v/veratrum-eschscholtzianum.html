Veratrum eschscholtzianum

Veratrum eschscholtzianum, commonly known as the yellow deathcamas, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Melanthiaceae family and is characterized by its tall, smooth-stemmed structure and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant has large, lance-shaped leaves that are often mistaken for grasses or other wildflowers. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and showy, spadix-like inflorescences.

It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and forest edges.
