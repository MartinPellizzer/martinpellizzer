Viola fimbriatula f. umbelliflora

Viola fimbriatula f. umbelliflora, commonly known as the umbellate violet, is a botanical variant of the common violet.

It is characterized by its distinctive umbellate inflorescence, which consists of a cluster of flowers arranged in a flat-topped or rounded shape. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and deep green coloration. Its flowers are usually violet or purple in hue, with five petals and a distinctive pattern of markings.

This variant is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
