Viburnum wrightii var. minus

Viburnum wrightii var. minus, commonly known as the dwarf wright viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, typically reaching heights of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits in the fall.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
