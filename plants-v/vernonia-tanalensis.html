Vernonia tanalensis

Vernonia tanalensis, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, hard wood, which is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the dry season. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

Vernonia tanalensis is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and arid conditions, making it an important species in ecological restoration efforts.
