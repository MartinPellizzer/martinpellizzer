Vaccinium usunoki var. pubescens

Vaccinium usunoki var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy blueberry, is a variant of the usunoki blueberry native to Japan.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white in color. The berries are edible, with a tart flavor, and are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
