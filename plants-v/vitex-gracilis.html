Vitex gracilis

Vitex gracilis, commonly known as the slender chaste tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant, blue or purple flowers in panicles during the summer months. Its fruit is a dry, two-lobed capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Vitex gracilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
