Viola flavescens

Viola flavescens, commonly known as the yellow violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of about 10-20 centimeters, with heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often a deep green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from yellow to pale orange, usually blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.

Viola flavescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
