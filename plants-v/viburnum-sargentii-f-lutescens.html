Viburnum sargentii f. lutescens

Viburnum sargentii f. lutescens, commonly known as the goldenleaf sargent viburnum, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clustered inflorescences in the spring. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth margin and a glossy, dark green upper surface. In autumn, the foliage turns a striking golden-yellow, adding seasonal interest to the landscape.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental value and adaptability to a variety of soil conditions.
