Vateria siamensis

Vateria siamensis, commonly known as the Siam vateria, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically borne in clusters, followed by woody fruits containing seeds. It is often found in lowland evergreen forests and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.

The species is valued for its timber and has been studied for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
