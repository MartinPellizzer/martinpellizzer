Vitis cinerea var. canescens

Vitis cinerea var. canescens, commonly known as the graybark grape, is a vine native to North America.

It is characterized by its pale to grayish bark and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that give way to berries that range from yellow to red in color. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the eastern United States.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
