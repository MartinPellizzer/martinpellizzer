Viola primulifolia var. acuta

Viola primulifolia var. acuta, commonly known as the acute viola, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive acuminate tip, contributing to its botanical identification. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers with five petals, typical of the Violaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
