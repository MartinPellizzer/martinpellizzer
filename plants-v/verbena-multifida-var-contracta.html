Verbena multifida var. contracta

Verbena multifida var. contracta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. It is known for its compact growth habit and long blooming period, which extends from late spring to early autumn.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
