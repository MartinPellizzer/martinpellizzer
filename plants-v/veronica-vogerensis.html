Veronica vogerensis

Veronica vogerensis, commonly known as the mountain veronica, is a flowering plant native to the high mountain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are borne in dense spikes.

Veronica vogerensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
