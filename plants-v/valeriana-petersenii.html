Valeriana petersenii

Valeriana petersenii, commonly known as Petersen's valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Valerianaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often reddish or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are known for their medicinal properties.

Valeriana petersenii is valued in traditional medicine for its calming effects and is used to treat anxiety and sleep disorders.
