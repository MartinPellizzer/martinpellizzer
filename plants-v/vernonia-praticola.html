Vernonia praticola

Vernonia praticola, commonly known as prairie coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in prairie and open woodland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its yellow-orange flower heads bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Vernonia praticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
