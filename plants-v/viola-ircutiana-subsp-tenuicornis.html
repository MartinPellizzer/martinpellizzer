Viola ircutiana subsp. tenuicornis

Viola ircutiana subsp.

tenuicornis, commonly known as the slender-horned violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet or purple coloration, and its flowers are usually violet or blue with a yellowish throat. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
