Viscum perrieri

Viscum perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Viscaceae and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are often consumed by birds, which then disperse the seeds. Viscum perrieri has a symbiotic relationship with its host, drawing water and nutrients while also providing some ecological benefits.

Its presence is often an indicator of a healthy, diverse forest ecosystem.
