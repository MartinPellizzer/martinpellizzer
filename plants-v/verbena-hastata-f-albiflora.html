Verbena hastata f. albiflora

Verbena hastata f. albiflora, commonly known as the white verbena, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, silky hairs.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens and pollinator habitats.
