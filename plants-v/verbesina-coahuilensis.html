Verbesina coahuilensis

Verbesina coahuilensis, commonly known as the Coahuila goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, semi-desert environments. Its leaves are alternate and often have a rough, hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

Verbesina coahuilensis plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest for its ecological resilience in arid landscapes.
