Viola foucaudii

Viola foucaudii, commonly known as the Cape violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive violet-blue flowers with yellow centers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Viola foucaudii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
