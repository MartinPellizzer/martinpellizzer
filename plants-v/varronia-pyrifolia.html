Varronia pyrifolia

Varronia pyrifolia, commonly known as the flame tree or fire tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often reddish in color, giving it a striking appearance. The tree produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, spiky fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
