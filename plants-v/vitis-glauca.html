Vitis glauca

Vitis glauca, commonly known as prickly vine or sand grape, is a deciduous climbing vine native to North America.

It is characterized by its pale green, waxy leaves and small, yellowish-green berries that mature to a dark blue color. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open areas such as prairies and dunes. Vitis glauca is notable for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions and its role in supporting local wildlife.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
