Vernonia javanica

Vernonia javanica, commonly known as the Java ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows up to 2 meters in height, characterized by its upright stems and dense clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Vernonia javanica is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas, and it has some medicinal uses in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
