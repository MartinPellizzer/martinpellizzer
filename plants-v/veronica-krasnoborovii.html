Veronica krasnoborovii

Veronica krasnoborovii, commonly known as the Krasnoborov's speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb with slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Veronica krasnoborovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
