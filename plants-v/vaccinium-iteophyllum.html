Vaccinium iteophyllum

Vaccinium iteophyllum, commonly known as the red huckleberry, is a species of shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisine. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streamside areas.

Its botanical features include small, alternate leaves and a compact growth habit that contributes to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
