Vaccinium horizontale

Vaccinium horizontale, commonly known as the creeping bilberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is typically found in forest understories or rocky areas. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a waxy surface, and it has a compact growth habit that forms dense mats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting wildlife.
