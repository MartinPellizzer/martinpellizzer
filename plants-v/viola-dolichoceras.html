Viola dolichoceras

Viola dolichoceras, commonly known as the long-beak violet, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers with a distinctive elongated spur. Native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments. The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, and the plant is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking shade-tolerant plants.
