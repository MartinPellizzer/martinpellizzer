Valeriana pallescens

Valeriana pallescens, commonly known as the pale valerian, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a fragrant, somewhat spicy scent.

Valeriana pallescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported calming properties.
