Vernonia subrepanda

Vernonia subrepanda, commonly known as the smooth vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its conical flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Vernonia subrepanda is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
