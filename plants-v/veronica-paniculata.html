Veronica paniculata

Veronica paniculata, commonly known as the panicled speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Veronica paniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
