Veronica glandifera

Veronica glandifera, commonly known as the glandular speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with glandular dots, which give it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Veronica glandifera is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
