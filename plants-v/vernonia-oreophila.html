Vernonia oreophila

Vernonia oreophila, commonly known as the mountain ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage is usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and arranged in dense clusters.

Vernonia oreophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
