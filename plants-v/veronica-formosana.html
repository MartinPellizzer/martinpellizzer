Veronica formosana

Veronica formosana, commonly known as the Taiwan speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Veronica formosana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
