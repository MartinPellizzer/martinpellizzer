Vernonia trichoclada

Vernonia trichoclada, commonly known as the hairy ironweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Vernonia trichoclada is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
