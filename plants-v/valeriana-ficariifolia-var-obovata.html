Valeriana ficariifolia var. obovata

Valeriana ficariifolia var. obovata, commonly known as the broad-leaf valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features aromatic, opposite leaves that are broadly ovate to obovate in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters called cymes. Its root system is fibrous and contains essential oils that contribute to its medicinal properties.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
