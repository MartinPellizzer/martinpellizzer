Viola × savatieri f. detonsa

Viola × savatieri f. detonsa, commonly known as the violet violet or the violet viola, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of delicate, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–20 cm and prefers moist, well-drained soil in partial shade. It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and floral arrangements.
