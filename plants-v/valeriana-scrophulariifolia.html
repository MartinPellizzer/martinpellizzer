Valeriana scrophulariifolia

Valeriana scrophulariifolia, commonly known as the purple valerian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, violet or purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Valeriana scrophulariifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
