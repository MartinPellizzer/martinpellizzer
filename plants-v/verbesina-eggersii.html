Verbesina eggersii

Verbesina eggersii, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features tall, erect stems covered in fine, hairy hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. Its most striking feature is its bright yellow inflorescence, which consists of numerous small, tubular flowers clustered in panicles.

Verbesina eggersii thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy and attractive addition to native gardens.
