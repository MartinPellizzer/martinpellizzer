Vernonia trichantha

Vernonia trichantha, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils.

Vernonia trichantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
