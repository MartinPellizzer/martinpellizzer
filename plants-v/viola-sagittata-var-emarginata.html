Viola sagittata var. emarginata

Viola sagittata var. emarginata, commonly known as the emarginate violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive notched or emarginate base, giving it its botanical name. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
