Veronica umbelliformis

Veronica umbelliformis, commonly known as the clustered speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue-to-purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, sunny environments. It has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to arid conditions.

Veronica umbelliformis is a perennial species that is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
