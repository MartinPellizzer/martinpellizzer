Vitis thunbergii var. cinerea

Vitis thunbergii var. cinerea, commonly known as the gray grapevine, is a deciduous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its grayish-brown bark and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by edible berries that range in color from green to black. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
