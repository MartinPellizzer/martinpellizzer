Veronica scardicoides

Veronica scardicoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas or along riverbanks. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and borne in clusters, adding a delicate ornamental appeal.

It is valued in gardens for its hardiness and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
