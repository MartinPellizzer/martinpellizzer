Vaccinium cubense var. ramonii

Vaccinium cubense var. ramonii, commonly known as the Florida huckleberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or purple in color, which are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and pine flatwoods.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
