Viticella microcalyx

Viticella microcalyx, commonly known as the miniature grapevine, is a compact, ornamental plant belonging to the Vitaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, trailing stems and clusters of tiny, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, round, dark blue berries that are edible but not typically consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for containers or as a ground cover.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is popular in gardens for its aesthetic value.
