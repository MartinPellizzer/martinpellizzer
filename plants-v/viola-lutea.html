Viola lutea

Viola lutea, commonly known as the yellow violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.

Viola lutea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
