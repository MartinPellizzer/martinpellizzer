Vincentia lasiodiscus

Vincentia lasiodiscus, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by pods containing several elliptical seeds. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

Vincentia lasiodiscus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
