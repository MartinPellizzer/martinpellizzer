Vouapa coerulea

Vouapa coerulea, commonly known as the blue-flowered orchid, is a rare and striking plant native to the tropical rainforests of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue petals, which are often speckled with contrasting white or purple markings. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a delicate and fragrant aroma.

Due to its limited habitat and slow growth, Vouapa coerulea is considered endangered and is highly sought after by botanists and conservationists.
