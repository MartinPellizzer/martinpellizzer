Verbena bonariensis f. gracilis

Verbena bonariensis f. gracilis, commonly known as the slender verbena, is a compact, flowering plant native to South America.

It features slender, upright stems and produces clusters of small, vibrant blue or purple flowers. The foliage is lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a delicate appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

This variety is noted for its extended blooming period, adding color to gardens from early summer through fall.
