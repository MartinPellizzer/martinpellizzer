Vriesea friburgensis

Vriesea friburgensis, commonly known as the Friburgensis Bromeliad, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant, spathaceous inflorescence that ranges in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting green or yellow center. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater for the plant's use. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Vriesea friburgensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its low maintenance requirements.
