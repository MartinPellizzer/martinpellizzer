Verbesina schomburgkii subsp. ligulata

Verbesina schomburgkii subsp.

ligulata, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to Central America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, fluffy seeds in its composite flower heads. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
