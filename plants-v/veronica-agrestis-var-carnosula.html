Veronica agrestis var. carnosula

Veronica agrestis var. carnosula, commonly known as the woolly speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice among gardeners.
