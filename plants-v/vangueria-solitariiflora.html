Vangueria solitariiflora

Vangueria solitariiflora, commonly known as the solitary-flowered vangueria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in coastal or inland regions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, which are often borne at the ends of branches. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough, hairy texture.

Vangueria solitariiflora is known for its drought tolerance and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
