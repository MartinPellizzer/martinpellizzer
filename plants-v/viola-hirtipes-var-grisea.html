Viola hirtipes var. grisea

Viola hirtipes var. grisea, commonly known as the gray violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, grayish pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
