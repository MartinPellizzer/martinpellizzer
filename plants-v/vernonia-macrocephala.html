Vernonia macrocephala

Vernonia macrocephala, commonly known as the giant ironweed, is a striking perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stem and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. Its flowers are arranged in dense, spherical clusters at the top of the plant, making it a notable sight in gardens and natural habitats.

Vernonia macrocephala is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
