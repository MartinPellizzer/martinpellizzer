Viola pyrolifolia

Viola pyrolifolia, commonly known as the purple violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green with a slight pubescence. The plant produces clusters of violet-blue flowers with five petals, often adorned with yellow markings. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
