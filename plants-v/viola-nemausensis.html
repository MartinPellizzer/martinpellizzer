Viola nemausensis

Viola nemausensis, commonly known as the French violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces delicate violet flowers with a distinctive yellow center. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its short stature and clustered growth habit.

The flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
