Viola dentata

Viola dentata, commonly known as the toothed violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, with five petals. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Viola dentata is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
