Vitex multidens

Vitex multidens, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in spikes, followed by woody, bead-like fruits. Its botanical features make it suitable for ornamental purposes and traditional uses.

The species is also valued for its potential in medicinal applications.
