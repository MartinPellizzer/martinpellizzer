Vernonia micradenia

Vernonia micradenia, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as swamps and wetlands. It is often found in the Coastal Plain regions of Georgia, Florida, and South Carolina.

Vernonia micradenia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
