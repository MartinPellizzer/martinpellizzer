Vitex ajugiflora

Vitex ajugiflora, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender, often arching stems. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to lavender flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge, often with a silvery underside.

Vitex ajugiflora is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
