Viburnum dilatatum var. macrophyllum

Viburnum dilatatum var. macrophyllum, commonly known as the Florida holly, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptic leaflets, and its flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels. The plant produces dark blue berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form dense thickets.
