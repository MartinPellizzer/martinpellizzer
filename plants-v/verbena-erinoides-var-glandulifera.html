Verbena erinoides var. glandulifera

Verbena erinoides var. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, vibrant blue or purple flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with glandular structures that secrete oils, giving the plant its varietal name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

The plant is also known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
