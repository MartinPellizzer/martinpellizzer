Valeriana punctata subsp. trichophylla

Valeriana punctata subsp.

trichophylla, commonly known as the hairy valerian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions.

The plant has slender stems and small, clustered leaves with a distinctive hairy texture. Its flowers are usually pink or white and bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.

The roots of this species are often used in traditional medicine for their purported calming and sedative properties.
