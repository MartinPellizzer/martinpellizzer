Vitellariopsis

Vitellariopsis, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often feature vibrant yellow or orange hues. The plants typically grow as epiphytes or lithophytes, attaching themselves to trees or rocks in their natural habitat. Vitellariopsis species have slender, wiry stems and are known for their ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Their unique floral structure and ornamental value make them popular among orchid enthusiasts.
