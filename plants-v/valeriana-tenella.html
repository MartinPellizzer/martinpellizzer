Valeriana tenella

Valeriana tenella, commonly known as lesser valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Valeriana tenella is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
