Vaccinium hutchinsonii

Vaccinium hutchinsonii, commonly known as the Hutton's blueberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its small, deciduous leaves and produces clusters of blue-black berries that are edible. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for various wildlife species.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
