Veronica syriaca

Veronica syriaca, commonly known as Syrian speedwell, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial with slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of blue or purple flowers in spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Veronica syriaca thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
