Verbena pogostoma

Verbena pogostoma, commonly known as the mountain verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically produces vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value contribute to its popularity among gardeners.
