Vittaria isoetifolia

Vittaria isoetifolia, commonly known as the water fern, is a small, aquatic fern that thrives in wet, shaded environments.

It has delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant's rhizomes grow along the substrate, anchoring it in place while absorbing nutrients from the surrounding water. Its leaves are usually submerged, with some extending just above the water surface.

Vittaria isoetifolia plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
