Veronica Spicata Var. Urumovii

Veronica spicata var. urumovii is a rare and lesser-known variant of the common speedwell plant. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, this plant thrives in harsh, alpine environments. Its striking blue-purple flowers bloom in dense spikes, making it a unique addition to any botanical collection. Despite its beauty, it remains poorly documented in scientific literature. This article explores its ecological significance and potential horticultural value.
