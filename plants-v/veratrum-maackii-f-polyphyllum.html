Veratrum maackii f. polyphyllum

Veratrum maackii f. polyphyllum, commonly known as the Maack's Aconite, is a variant of the Maack's Veratrum, a member of the Melanthiaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating.

This fern-like plant is known for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
