Visenia mollis

Visenia mollis, commonly known as the Spanish chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and beeches, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring, followed by edible nuts enclosed in spiny husks. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often developing shallow fissures with age.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional medicine.
