Veronica macrocarpa

Veronica macrocarpa, commonly known as the broadleaf speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that bear opposite pairs of oval, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue-to-purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden planting.
