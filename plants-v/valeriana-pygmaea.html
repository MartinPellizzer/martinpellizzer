Valeriana pygmaea

Valeriana pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf valerian, is a small flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and tiny, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of only a few centimeters, with dark green, narrow leaves and pale pink to white blossoms. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
