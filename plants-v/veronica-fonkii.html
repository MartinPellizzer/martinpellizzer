Veronica fonkii

Veronica fonkii, commonly known as the Kashmir violet, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Veronica fonkii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
