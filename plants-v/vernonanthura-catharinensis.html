Vernonanthura catharinensis

Vernonanthura catharinensis, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Vernonanthura catharinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
