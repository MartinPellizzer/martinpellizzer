Veronicastrum sibiricum var. japonicum

Veronicastrum sibiricum var. japonicum, commonly known as Japanese blueweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, adorned with small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for wetland restoration and garden landscaping.
