Vitis oligocarpa

Vitis oligocarpa, commonly known as the Chinese fox grape, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically inedible for humans but can be used in traditional medicine. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets and is characterized by its vigorous growth habit. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shady environments.

Vitis oligocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
