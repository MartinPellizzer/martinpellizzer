Versteegia minor

Versteegia minor, commonly known as the dwarf versteegia, is a small, evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by bright orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and compact size, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, visually appealing plant.
