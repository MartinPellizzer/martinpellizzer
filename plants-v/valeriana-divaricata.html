Valeriana divaricata

Valeriana divaricata, commonly known as sweet valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Valerianaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, creeping stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale pink or white flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late summer. Its rhizomatous root system is often used in traditional medicine for its sedative and calming properties.

Valeriana divaricata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.
