Veronica grandis var. holophylla

Veronica grandis var. holophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental leaves make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
