Verticordia harveyi var. nudipetala

Verticordia harveyi var. nudipetala, commonly known as the Harvey's featherflower, is a species native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, feathery inflorescences that consist of numerous small, glandular-tipped flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in heathland or coastal environments. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to red, are highly attractive to nectar-feeding insects and birds.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
