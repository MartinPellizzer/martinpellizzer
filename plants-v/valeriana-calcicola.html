Valeriana calcicola

Valeriana calcicola, commonly known as the limestone valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It thrives in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is characterized by its strong, aromatic scent.

Valeriana calcicola is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
