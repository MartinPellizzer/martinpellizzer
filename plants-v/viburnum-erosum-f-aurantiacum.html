Viburnum erosum f. aurantiacum

Viburnum erosum f. aurantiacum, commonly known as the goldenleaf viburnum, is a unique horticultural form of the common viburnum.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red foliage, which adds vibrant color to gardens throughout the growing season. The plant typically produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue berries in the fall. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to a variety of garden settings.
