Vitis radiata

Vitis radiata, commonly known as the sand grape or wild grape, is a climbing vine native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces small, sweet berries that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and tendrils that help it climb and spread. Its flowers are typically greenish-white and appear in clusters.

Vitis radiata is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in winemaking.
