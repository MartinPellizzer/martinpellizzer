Viola × murrii

Viola × murrii, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant is native to Europe and has been widely cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Viola × murrii is also known for its ability to naturalize and spread through self-seeding.
