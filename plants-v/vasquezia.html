Vasquezia

Vasquezia, commonly known as the desert lily, is a genus of flowering plants native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

Vasquezia species are typically perennial, with deep root systems that allow them to survive in harsh, dry environments. They play an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
