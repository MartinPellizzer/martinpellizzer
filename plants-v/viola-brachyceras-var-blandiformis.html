Viola brachyceras var. blandiformis

Viola brachyceras var. blandiformis, commonly known as the broadleaf violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its flowers are typically violet or purple, with five petals. The plant thrives in moist, woodland environments and has a fibrous root system.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
