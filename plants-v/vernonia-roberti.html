Vernonia roberti

Vernonia roberti, commonly known as the Robert's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks, thriving in moist, sandy soils. Its showy, conical flower heads are usually a vibrant yellow or orange, attracting various pollinators.

Vernonia roberti is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological benefits and adaptability to aquatic environments.
