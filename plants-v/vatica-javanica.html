Vatica javanica

Vatica javanica, commonly known as the Javan ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, waxy surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Vatica javanica plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
