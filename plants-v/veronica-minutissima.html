Veronica minutissima

Veronica minutissima, commonly known as the tiny speedwell, is a small flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, small blue or violet flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky crevices. It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar growth habits.
