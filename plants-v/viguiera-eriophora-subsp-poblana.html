Viguiera eriophora subsp. poblana

Viguiera eriophora subsp.

poblana, commonly known as Mexican ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central Mexico. It features upright, branching stems covered in dense, stiff hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flower heads that bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and prominent veins.

It is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in arid and semi-arid environments.
