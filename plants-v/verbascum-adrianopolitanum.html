Verbascum adrianopolitanum

Verbascum adrianopolitanum, commonly known as the Adrianopolitan mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes species known for their fuzzy, woolly leaves. The plant produces tall, erect stalks with clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are covered in dense, soft hairs that help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Verbascum adrianopolitanum is often found in rocky soils and dry habitats, making it a resilient and adaptable species.
