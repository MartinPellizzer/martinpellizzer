Viola insularis

Viola insularis, commonly known as the Island Violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 10-20 centimeters, featuring heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of violet, blue, or white, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Viola insularis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
