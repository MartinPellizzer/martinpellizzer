Verbascum × fuscipilum

Verbascum × fuscipilum, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a hybrid plant belonging to the genus Verbascum in the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in a loose spike at the top of the stem. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
