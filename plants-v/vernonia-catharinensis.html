Vernonia catharinensis

Vernonia catharinensis, commonly known as the Brazilian ironweed, is a perennial shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, cylindrical flower clusters that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Vernonia catharinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
