Verbesina aspera

Verbesina aspera, commonly known as rough hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features rough, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its showy yellow flowers, which bloom in summer, are followed by distinctive seed pods that split open to release seeds.

Verbesina aspera is often found in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and streambanks.
