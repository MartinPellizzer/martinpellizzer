Vaccinium stamineum var. melanocarpum

Vaccinium stamineum var. melanocarpum, commonly known as black huckleberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It produces small, dark blue to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a glossy surface and a serrated edge.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting wildlife.
