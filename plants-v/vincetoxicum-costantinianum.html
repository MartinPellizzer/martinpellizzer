Vincetoxicum costantinianum

Vincetoxicum costantinianum, commonly known as Constantine's bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Apocynaceae.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, cream-colored flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are follicles that split open to release seeds, aiding in its spread.

It is native to southern Europe and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
