Vilfa macra

Vilfa macra, commonly known as the tall lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, showy white or pale pink flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a striking addition to alpine gardens.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a food source for pollinators and its ornamental value in horticulture.
