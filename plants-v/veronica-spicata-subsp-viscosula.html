Veronica spicata subsp. viscosula

Veronica spicata subsp.

viscosula, commonly known as sticky veronica, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, sticky resin, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is known for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
