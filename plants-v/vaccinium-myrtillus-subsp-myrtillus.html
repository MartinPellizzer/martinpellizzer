Vaccinium myrtillus subsp. myrtillus

Vaccinium myrtillus subsp.

myrtillus, commonly known as blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northern hemisphere. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers that transition into blue-black berries, which are rich in antioxidants and nutrients.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in boreal and temperate forest ecosystems. Its leaves are oval and dark green, with a leathery texture, and it has a symbiotic relationship with specific mycorrhizal fungi.

The plant is valued both for its edible fruits and its ecological role in supporting wildlife.
