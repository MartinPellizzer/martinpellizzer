Viola tricolor

Viola tricolor, commonly known as the heartsease, is a flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in temperate climates. The plant features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped lobes and produces small, colorful flowers in shades of violet, white, and yellow. Viola tricolor is known for its ability to grow in shady, moist environments, often found in woodland areas or gardens.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
