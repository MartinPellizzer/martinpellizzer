Viridantha plumosa

Viridantha plumosa, commonly known as the silver-feathered fern, is a striking plant characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds.

Native to the humid rainforests of Southeast Asia, it thrives in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are bioluminescent, emitting a soft blue glow at night, a unique adaptation for attracting nocturnal pollinators. The plant's root system is highly efficient at absorbing nutrients from the soil, allowing it to flourish in nutrient-poor conditions.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered a conservation priority in its native habitat.
