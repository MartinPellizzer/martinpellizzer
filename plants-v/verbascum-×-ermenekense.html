Verbascum × ermenekense

Verbascum × ermenekense, commonly known as the Armenian mullein.

It is a hybrid species within the genus Verbascum, characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and Armenia, it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
