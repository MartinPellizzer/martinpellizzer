Viola wettsteinii

Viola wettsteinii, commonly known as the Wettstein's violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, while its flowers are violet-blue with a distinctive yellow center.

Viola wettsteinii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
