Veronica mannii

Veronica mannii, commonly known as the rock violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Veronica mannii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
