Vernonia saligna

Vernonia saligna, commonly known as the slender ironweed, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, dark blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. It has a woody stem that becomes more prominent with age, and its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
