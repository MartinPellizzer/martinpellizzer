Viola saxatilis subsp. polychroma

Viola saxatilis subsp.

polychroma, commonly known as the purple viola, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy violet or purple flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or woodland habitats. It is a member of the Violaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in cool, moist environments.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
