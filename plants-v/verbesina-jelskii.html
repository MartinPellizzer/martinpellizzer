Verbesina jelskii

Verbesina jelskii, commonly known as the Jelskii's beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and semi-arid regions.

Verbesina jelskii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
