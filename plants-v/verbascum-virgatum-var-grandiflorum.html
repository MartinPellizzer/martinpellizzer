Verbascum virgatum var. grandiflorum

Verbascum virgatum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the Great Mullein, is a tall biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that form a rosette at the base. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flower clusters in the summer, which are borne on tall, erect stems. Its flowers are typically grouped in a spike-like inflorescence, and the plant is known for its medicinal and ornamental uses.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
