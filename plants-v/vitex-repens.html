Vitex repens

Vitex repens, commonly known as creeping chaste tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of about 30 cm, with slender, trailing stems that root at the nodes. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture, while the flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and range in color from white to purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed areas and along roadsides.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
