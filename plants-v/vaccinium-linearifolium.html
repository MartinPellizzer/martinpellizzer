Vaccinium linearifolium

Vaccinium linearifolium, commonly known as the slender blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 2 inches long. Its flowers are usually pink or white and bloom in the spring, followed by the development of small, edible berries.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions.
