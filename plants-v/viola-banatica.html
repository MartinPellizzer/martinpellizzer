Viola banatica

Viola banatica, commonly known as the Balkan violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and mountainous regions. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of violet or purple flowers in early spring. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with five petals, and it is part of the Violaceae family.

Viola banatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
