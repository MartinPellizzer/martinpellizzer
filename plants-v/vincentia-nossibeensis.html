Vincentia nossibeensis

Vincentia nossibeensis, commonly known as the African senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are yellow and clustered, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
