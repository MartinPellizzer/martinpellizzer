Veronica japonica

Veronica japonica, commonly known as Japanese speedwell, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem.

Veronica japonica is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ease of cultivation.
