Viola sylvestris var. japonica

Viola sylvestris var. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
