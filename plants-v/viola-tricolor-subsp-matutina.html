Viola tricolor subsp. matutina

Viola tricolor subsp.

matutina, commonly known as the morning violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of violet, white, and yellow.

The plant typically blooms in early spring, hence its common name, and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. It is often found in woodland edges and shaded gardens.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
