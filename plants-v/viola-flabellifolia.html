Viola flabellifolia

Viola flabellifolia, commonly known as the fan-leaved violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and striking violet or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance.

Viola flabellifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
