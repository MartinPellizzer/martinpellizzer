Veronica breviracemosa

Veronica breviracemosa, commonly known as the short-stemmed speedwell, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features upright stems covered in small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem.

Veronica breviracemosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
