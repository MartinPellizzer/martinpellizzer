Vernonia microphylla

Vernonia microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. Its small, narrow leaves are arranged alternately along the stems, and it produces dark purple to black flowers in late summer.

Vernonia microphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
