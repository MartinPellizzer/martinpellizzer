Viola pseudovulcanica

Viola pseudovulcanica, commonly known as the pseudovulcanic violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the volcanic soils of Italy.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained habitats and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers bloom in spring, adding a splash of color to its natural environment.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
