Viola × karakulensis

Viola × karakulensis, commonly known as the Karakul violet, is a hybrid species belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments. The flowers typically have five petals and are often adorned with distinctive markings.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
