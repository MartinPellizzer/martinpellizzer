Vicia coquimbensis

Vicia coquimbensis, commonly known as the Coquimbo vetch, is a flowering plant native to Chile.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and erect, branching stems. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Vicia coquimbensis is adapted to arid environments and is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
