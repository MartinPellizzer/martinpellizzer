Valeriana macropoda

Valeriana macropoda, commonly known as the large-fruited valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Valerianaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer. Its rhizomatous root system is known for containing volatile oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Valeriana macropoda is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional use in herbal remedies.
