Vernonia asteriflora

Vernonia asteriflora, commonly known as the starflower vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the United States, particularly found in the southeastern region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and disturbed sites, preferring well-drained soils. It produces showy, yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive botanical appearance.
