Varasia squarrosa

Varasia squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrosa bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a fine, white powdery coating.

Varasia squarrosa is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
