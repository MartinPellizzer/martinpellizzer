Vernonia angusticeps

Vernonia angusticeps, commonly known as narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its showy purple to pink flower heads bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Vernonia angusticeps is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
