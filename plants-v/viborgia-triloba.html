Viborgia triloba

Viborgia triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, with three prominent lobes on each petal. The plant has a compact growth habit, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Viborgia triloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its delicate, early spring blooms.
