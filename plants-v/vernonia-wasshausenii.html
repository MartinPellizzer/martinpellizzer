Vernonia wasshausenii

Vernonia wasshausenii, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in arid conditions. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The plant is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and attractive floral display.
