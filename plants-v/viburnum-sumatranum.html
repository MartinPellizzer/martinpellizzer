Viburnum sumatranum

Viburnum sumatranum, commonly known as the Sumatran holly, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in inflorescences, blooming in spring.

Viburnum sumatranum produces red berries that are an important food source for various bird species.
