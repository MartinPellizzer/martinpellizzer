Viola clarkiae

Viola clarkiae, commonly known as Clark's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola clarkiae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
