Vernonia neocorymbosa

Vernonia neocorymbosa, commonly known as the Hawaiian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet, with grey-green foliage and showy yellow flowers. Vernonia neocorymbosa plays an important role in the local ecosystem, providing habitat and nectar for native pollinators.
