Verbena officinalis var. minima

Verbena officinalis var. minima, commonly known as narrow-leaved vervain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom from summer to autumn. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
