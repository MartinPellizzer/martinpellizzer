Vitis lonchiphylla

Vitis lonchiphylla, commonly known as the Chinese grapevine, is a climbing vine native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into clusters of berries, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. Vitis lonchiphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.

It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.
