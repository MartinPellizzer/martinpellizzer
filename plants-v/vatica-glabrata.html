Vatica glabrata

Vatica glabrata, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and smooth, greyish bark. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a leathery texture. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

Vatica glabrata is valued for its durable timber and is often found in lowland and hill forest ecosystems.
