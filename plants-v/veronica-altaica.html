Veronica altaica

Veronica altaica, commonly known as the alpine speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help protect it from harsh weather.

Veronica altaica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
