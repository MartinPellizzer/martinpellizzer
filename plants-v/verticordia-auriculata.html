Verticordia auriculata

Verticordia auriculata, commonly known as the golden wattle or the red wattle, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, glandular, and often hairy leaves, which are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces striking clusters of small, yellow to orange-red flowers, which are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
