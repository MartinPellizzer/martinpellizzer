Verbascum carduchorum

Verbascum carduchorum, commonly known as the Armenian mullein.

It is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region, characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Verbascum carduchorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
