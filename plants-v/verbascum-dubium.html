Verbascum dubium

Verbascum dubium, commonly known as the lesser mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, woolly leaves that form a rosette at the base. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a loose panicle. Verbascum dubium thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

It is a perennial species that is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
