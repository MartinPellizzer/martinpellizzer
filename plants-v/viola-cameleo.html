Viola cameleo

Viola cameleo, commonly known as the dwarf violet, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of violet, blue, or white, often with dark markings. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Viola cameleo is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
