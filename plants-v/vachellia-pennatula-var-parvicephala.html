Vachellia pennatula var. parvicephala

Vachellia pennatula var. parvicephala, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular heads. The plant produces small, flat, leathery pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

The leaves are typically bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, thread-like leaflets.
