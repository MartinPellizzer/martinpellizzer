Viola grypoceras var. ovato-oblonga

Viola grypoceras var. ovato-oblonga, commonly known as the broad-leaf violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped to ovate-oblong leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces violet-blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native garden landscaping.
