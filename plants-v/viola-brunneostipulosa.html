Viola brunneostipulosa

Viola brunneostipulosa, commonly known as the brown-stippled violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive brownish or rusty-colored stipules along the petioles. The flowers are typically violet or purple in color, with five petals and a spurred lower petal. This perennial herb is native to regions of eastern North America, where it thrives in woodland and shaded environments.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
