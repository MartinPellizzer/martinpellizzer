Voyria calycina

Voyria calycina, commonly known as the golden cupplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has basal leaves that are palmately lobed, and its stems are covered in fine, hairy pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads.
