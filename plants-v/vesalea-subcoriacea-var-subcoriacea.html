Vesalea subcoriacea var. subcoriacea

Vesalea subcoriacea var. subcoriacea, commonly known as the rough-leaved vesalea, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and leaves that are covered in a dense, rough texture, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for both horticultural and ecological studies.
