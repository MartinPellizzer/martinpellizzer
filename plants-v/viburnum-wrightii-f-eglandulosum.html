Viburnum wrightii f. eglandulosum

Viburnum wrightii f. eglandulosum, commonly known as the silky viburnum, is a variant of the native viburnum species found in the southeastern United States.

This shrub typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and features broad, ovate leaves with a distinctive silvery-white underside. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and appear in spring, followed by showy red berries in the fall. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its seasonal color changes.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to various garden environments.
