Viola cenisia subsp. lapeyrousiana

Viola cenisia subsp.

lapeyrousiana, commonly known as the Lapeyrousian violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cool, moist environments.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of violet and blue. Its blooming period occurs during the spring and early summer, adding ornamental value to its natural habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
