Vernonia desertorum var. macrocephala

Vernonia desertorum var. macrocephala, commonly known as the desert ironweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green to reddish-purple flowers. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are adapted to conserve water in hot, dry environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along rocky outcrops.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinators.
