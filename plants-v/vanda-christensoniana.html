Vanda christensoniana

Vanda christensoniana, commonly known as the Christenson's vanda, is a striking orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, white, and purple. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other structures without deriving nutrients from them. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Vanda christensoniana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
