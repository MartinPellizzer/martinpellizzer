Vicia unijuga var. apoda

Vicia unijuga var. apoda, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a perennial legume native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in early summer. The plant produces trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It is often found in grassy areas, along roadsides, and in disturbed soils.

Vicia unijuga var. apoda is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
