Valeriana ajanensis

Valeriana ajanensis, commonly known as the Ajan valley valerian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, herbaceous stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom during the summer months. Valeriana ajanensis is known for its medicinal properties, particularly for its calming effects, and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its rhizomes and roots are often harvested for their essential oils and bioactive compounds.
