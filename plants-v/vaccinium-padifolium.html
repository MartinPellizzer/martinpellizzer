Vaccinium padifolium

Vaccinium padifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved huckleberry, is a species of shrub native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants such as blueberries and cranberries. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. Its flowers are bell-shaped and usually pink or white, and it produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.

The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in forested areas.
