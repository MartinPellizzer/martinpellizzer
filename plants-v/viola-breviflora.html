Viola breviflora

Viola breviflora, commonly known as the short-flowered violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 10-20 centimeters, featuring heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are usually violet or purple in color, with a distinctive yellow center. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Viola breviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
