Vernonia cumingiana

Vernonia cumingiana, commonly known as the Cuming's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, yellowish to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodland and savanna habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia cumingiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
