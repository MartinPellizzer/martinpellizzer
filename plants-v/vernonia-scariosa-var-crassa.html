Vernonia scariosa var. crassa

Vernonia scariosa var. crassa, commonly known as roughleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, rough-textured leaves and conical flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodlands. It produces small, pale yellow to brown flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its robust growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
