Vandellia cyrtotricha

Vandellia cyrtotricha, commonly known as the glasswurt, is a unique succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, glass-like stems that are covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. Its root system is adapted to store water, allowing it to survive prolonged dry periods.

Due to its striking appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by succulent enthusiasts.
