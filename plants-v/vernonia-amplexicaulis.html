Vernonia amplexicaulis

Vernonia amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are opposite and often clasping, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, attracting various pollinators.
