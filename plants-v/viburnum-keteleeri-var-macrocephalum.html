Viburnum keteleeri var. macrocephalum

Viburnum keteleeri var. macrocephalum, commonly known as the large-headed snowball viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring, creating a striking visual display. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds but not for humans. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, loamy soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
