Vitis suberosa

Vitis suberosa, commonly known as the creeping or wild grapevine, is a perennial vine native to North America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when mature. Vitis suberosa thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
