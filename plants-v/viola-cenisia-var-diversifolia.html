Viola cenisia var. diversifolia

Viola cenisia var. diversifolia, commonly known as the variable violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often variegated with white or silver markings. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of violet, blue, and white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its unique foliage and charming blossoms make it a popular choice for woodland gardens and shaded borders.
