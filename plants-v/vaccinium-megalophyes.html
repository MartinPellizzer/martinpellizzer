Vaccinium megalophyes

Vaccinium megalophyes, commonly known as the large-fruited huckleberry, is a species of shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and are often used in local cuisine. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

The leaves are oval-shaped with a leathery texture and are typically evergreen, providing year-round cover in its habitat.
