Vicia serratifolia

Vicia serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Vicia serratifolia is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in open, sunny habitats.

It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
