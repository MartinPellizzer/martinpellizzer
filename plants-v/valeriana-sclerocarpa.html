Valeriana sclerocarpa

Valeriana sclerocarpa, commonly known as the hard-seeded valerian, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in the summer months. Its roots are thick and fleshy, and the plant is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils.

Valeriana sclerocarpa is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential calming effects.
