Verbascum viminale

Verbascum viminale, commonly known as slender mullein, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy, lobed leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a cylindrical shape at the top of the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Verbascum viminale is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically for its soothing effects on respiratory ailments.
