Viburnum capitatum

Viburnum capitatum, commonly known as the clustered viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels. The plant produces red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Viburnum capitatum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators and support local wildlife.
