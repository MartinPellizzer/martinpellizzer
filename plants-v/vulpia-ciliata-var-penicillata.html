Vulpia ciliata var. penicillata

Vulpia ciliata var. penicillata, commonly known as the hairy veldt grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, hairy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets, which are typically greenish or brownish in color. This plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.

Its ability to tolerate a range of soil conditions makes it an important component of grassland ecosystems.
