Vigna triphylla

Vigna triphylla, commonly known as the three-leaf cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three small, heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain edible seeds, similar to other members of the Vigna genus. It is often grown for its protein-rich seeds, which are used in various cuisines.

Vigna triphylla is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a beneficial crop in sustainable agriculture.
