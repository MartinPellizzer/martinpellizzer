Verbascum haussknechtianum var. haussknechtianum

Verbascum haussknechtianum var. haussknechtianum, commonly known as the Hausknecht's mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, hairy stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, velvety leaves that are gray-green in color. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in a loose spike at the top of the stem. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their mild laxative properties.

The species is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun.
