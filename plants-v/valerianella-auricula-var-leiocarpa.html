Valerianella auricula var. leiocarpa

Valerianella auricula var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the spoonweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant belonging to the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae).

It is characterized by its rosette of spoon-shaped, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a reddish or purplish tinge. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a delicate appearance to its foliage. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Valerianella auricula var. leiocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
