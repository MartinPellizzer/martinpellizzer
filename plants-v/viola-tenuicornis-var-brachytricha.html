Viola tenuicornis var. brachytricha

Viola tenuicornis var. brachytricha, commonly known as the short-horned violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodland habitats, often in rich, loamy soils. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a hairy texture and produces clusters of violet-blue flowers with yellow centers. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is characterized by its short, stout pedicels and is often found in association with other woodland wildflowers.
