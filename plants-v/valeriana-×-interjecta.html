Valeriana × interjecta

Valeriana × interjecta, commonly known as the hybrid valerian, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Caprifoliaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly medicinal scent and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Valeriana × interjecta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
