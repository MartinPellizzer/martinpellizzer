Viburnum

Viburnum, commonly known as hedges or shrubs, is a diverse genus of flowering plants in the family Adoxaceae.

These plants typically have opposite, simple, and often serrated leaves, and they produce clusters of small, fragrant flowers in the spring. They are widely cultivated for their ornamental value, including their attractive foliage and flowering habits.

Viburnum species are adaptable to various soil types and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.
