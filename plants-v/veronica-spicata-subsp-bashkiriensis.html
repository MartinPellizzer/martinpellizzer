Veronica spicata subsp. bashkiriensis

Veronica spicata subsp.

bashkiriensis, commonly known as Bashkir spiked speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia. It features upright, branching stems covered in small, dark green leaves.

The plant produces dense clusters of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
