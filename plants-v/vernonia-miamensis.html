Vernonia miamensis

Vernonia miamensis, commonly known as the Miamian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in wetlands and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to various moisture conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with purple markings.

Vernonia miamensis plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in wetland restoration projects.
