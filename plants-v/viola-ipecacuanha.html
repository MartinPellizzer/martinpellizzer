Viola ipecacuanha

Viola ipecacuanha, commonly known as the Brazilian violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its flowers bloom in spring and are often used in traditional medicine.

The leaves and flowers contain various bioactive compounds, contributing to its medicinal significance.
