Vitex negundo var. philippinensis

Vitex negundo var. philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine vitex or Philippine chaste tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often pubescent on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of purple or white, which bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a dry capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

This species is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to a variety of soil types, making it a resilient plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
