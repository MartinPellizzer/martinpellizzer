Viscum farafanganense

Viscum farafanganense, commonly known as the Farafanga mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant belonging to the Viscaceae family.

It grows on the branches of host trees, primarily in the rainforests of western Africa. The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces white or pinkish flowers that are pollinated by insects. Its berries are red or yellow and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Viscum farafanganense plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
