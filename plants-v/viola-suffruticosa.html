Viola suffruticosa

Viola suffruticosa, commonly known as the creeping yellow violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. The plant features a low-growing, sprawling habit with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or violet, with five petals and a distinctive spur at the back.

Viola suffruticosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
