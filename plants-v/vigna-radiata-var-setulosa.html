Vigna radiata var. setulosa

Vigna radiata var. setulosa, commonly known as the hairy urd bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves, with small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm climates and is cultivated for its nutritional seeds rich in protein and minerals.

This variety is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting subsequent crop growth.
