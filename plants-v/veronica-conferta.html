Veronica conferta

Veronica conferta, commonly known as the dense veronica, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branching stems covered in dense, grayish-white hairs. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and also hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers arranged in spikes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
