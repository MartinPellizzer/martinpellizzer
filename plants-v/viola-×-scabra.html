Viola × scabra

Viola × scabra, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, often purple or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to various parts of the world. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Viola × scabra is also known for its medicinal properties, historically used in traditional remedies.
