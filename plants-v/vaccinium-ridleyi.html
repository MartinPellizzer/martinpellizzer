Vaccinium ridleyi

Vaccinium ridleyi, commonly known as the Malayan huckleberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or purple in color, which are used in local cuisines and traditional medicine. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Vaccinium ridleyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
