Vicia quinquenervia

Vicia quinquenervia, commonly known as the five-veined vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with five distinct leaflets. The flowers are typically small and clustered in racemes, featuring a distinctive pea-like shape with a yellow-and-purple pattern. The plant produces pods that contain several oval, brown seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in temperate regions and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.
