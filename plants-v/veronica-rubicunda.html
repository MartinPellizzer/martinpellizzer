Veronica rubicunda

Veronica rubicunda, commonly known as the red veronica, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically blooms from spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stems.

Veronica rubicunda is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
