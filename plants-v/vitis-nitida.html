Vitis nitida

Vitis nitida, commonly known as the silvervine, is a deciduous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twisting stems and heart-shaped, glossy dark green leaves that often have a silvery underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by small, round, blue-black berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Vitis nitida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and as a climbing plant in shaded areas.
