Viola × redacta

Viola × redacta, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a species resulting from the cross between Viola tricolor and Viola wittrockii.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often multicolored flowers that display shades of purple, violet, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm, with heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are dark green in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or garden beds.

The flowers bloom in early spring and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.
