Vernonia multiflosculosa

Vernonia multiflosculosa, commonly known as the clustered ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy clusters of small, tubular yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Vernonia multiflosculosa is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wetlands.
