Viola tricolor subsp. tricolor

Viola tricolor subsp.

tricolor, commonly known as the common violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped lobes and vibrant flowers that typically display purple, white, and yellow petals.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is often found in woodland areas and along garden borders.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
