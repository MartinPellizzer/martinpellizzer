Valeriana dinorrhiza

Valeriana dinorrhiza, commonly known as the red valerian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has aromatic, somewhat hairy leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its root system is extensive and often used in traditional medicine for its sedative properties.

Valeriana dinorrhiza thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
