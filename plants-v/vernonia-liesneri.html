Vernonia liesneri

Vernonia liesneri, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fluffy, blue-gray flower heads that resemble mist, which appear in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia liesneri is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
