Viburnum japonicum var. fruticosum

Viburnum japonicum var. fruticosum, commonly known as Japanese snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features compound leaves with ovate to elliptic leaflets and produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in late spring. The plant is notable for its bright red, berry-like fruits that persist into winter, providing food for wildlife. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.

Viburnum japonicum var. fruticosum is also valued for its ability to tolerate a range of soil conditions.
