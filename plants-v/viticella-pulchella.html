Viticella pulchella

Viticella pulchella, commonly known as the pink-flowered ivy, is a climbing plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy appearance.

Viticella pulchella is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or walls.
