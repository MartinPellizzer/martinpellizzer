Vicia tenuifolia

Vicia tenuifolia, commonly known as slender vetch, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in late spring. Vicia tenuifolia is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

It has been used historically in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
