Vinsonia propinqua

Vinsonia propinqua, commonly known as the dwarf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually blue or violet in color. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Vinsonia propinqua thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
