Viola flos-mariae var. nivea

Viola flos-mariae var. nivea, commonly known as the Snowy Violet, is a variant of the viola species characterized by its pale white to pale blue flowers.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 10-15 centimeters, with heart-shaped, velvety leaves. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch of color to the landscape.

The species is native to parts of Europe and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
