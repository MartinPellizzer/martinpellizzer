Viola × hirtiformis

Viola × hirtiformis, commonly known as the hairy violet, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Viola tricolor and Viola hirta.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and flowers that typically range from purple to violet in color. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Viola × hirtiformis is a perennial herb that produces heart-shaped leaves and spurred petals, typical of the Violaceae family.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
