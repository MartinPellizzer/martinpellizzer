Vicia cracca subsp. atroviolacea

Vicia cracca subsp.

atroviolacea, commonly known as the dark violet vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated margins.

The flowers are typically purple or violet, arranged in dense, racemose inflorescences. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands, roadsides, and wastelands.

This subspecies is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
