Viola barroetana

Viola barroetana, commonly known as the Barroetana violet, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola barroetana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
