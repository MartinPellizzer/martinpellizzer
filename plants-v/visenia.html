Visenia

Visenia, commonly known as the common ash, is a deciduous tree belonging to the Oleaceae family.

It is characterized by its opposite, simple, and usually ovate to lanceolate leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by winged seeds called samaras. Visenia is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in a variety of soil types.

It is often used in landscaping and has historical significance in traditional medicine.
