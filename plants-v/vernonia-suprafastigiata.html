Vernonia suprafastigiata

Vernonia suprafastigiata, commonly known as the tall ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
