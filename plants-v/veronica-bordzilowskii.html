Veronica bordzilowskii

Veronica bordzilowskii, commonly known as the Armenian speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and blue to purple flowers. The plant features slender stems that often branch near the base, with small, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Veronica bordzilowskii typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
