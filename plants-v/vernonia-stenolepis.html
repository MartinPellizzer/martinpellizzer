Vernonia stenolepis

Vernonia stenolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in dry woodlands and open fields.

Vernonia stenolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
