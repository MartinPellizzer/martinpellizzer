Vitis barbata f. sinuata

Vitis barbata f. sinuata, commonly known as the sinuous grapevine, is a unique variant of the grapevine species Vitis barbata.

It is characterized by its sinuous, twisting growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically has tendrils that curl around supports, and its leaves are often lobed with a serrated edge. It produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers in the spring, followed by edible berries that range in color from green to red.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in horticulture.
