Viguiera microcephala

Viguiera microcephala, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is well adapted to arid environments. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viguiera microcephala is also used in traditional medicine by some indigenous communities.
