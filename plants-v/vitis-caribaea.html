Vitis caribaea

Vitis caribaea, commonly known as the Caribbean grapevine, is a species of vine native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into clusters of berries. These berries are typically edible and have a sweet, musky flavor.

Vitis caribaea is often found in tropical and subtropical environments, growing along riverbanks and in humid forests.
