Vouacapoua humilis

Vouacapoua humilis, commonly known as the dwarf arum, is a herbaceous plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects, particularly flies and beetles. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter.

Vouacapoua humilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and unusual floral structure.
