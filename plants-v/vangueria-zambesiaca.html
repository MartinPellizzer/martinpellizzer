Vangueria zambesiaca

Vangueria zambesiaca, commonly known as the Zambesi wild bean, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Vangueria zambesiaca is also valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional medicinal uses.
