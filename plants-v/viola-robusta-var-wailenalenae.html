Viola robusta var. wailenalenae

Viola robusta var. wailenalenae, commonly known as the Wailenalenae violet, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet petals with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in montane forests. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is of interest to botanists due to its unique floral morphology.
