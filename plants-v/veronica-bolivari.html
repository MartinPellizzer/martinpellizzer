Veronica bolivari

Veronica bolivari, commonly known as Bolivian speedwell, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and arranged in dense spikes.

Veronica bolivari is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
