Veronica hederifolia f. praecox

Veronica hederifolia f. praecox, commonly known as the early common speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, blue to purple flowers. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, shady environments.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to spread readily.
