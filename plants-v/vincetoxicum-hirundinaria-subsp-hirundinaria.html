Vincetoxicum hirundinaria subsp. hirundinaria

Vincetoxicum hirundinaria subsp.

hirundinaria, commonly known as the European adder's tongue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It features tall, slender stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, often with a reddish or purple tinge.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-white with a distinctive red or purple hood. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged alternately along the stem.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
