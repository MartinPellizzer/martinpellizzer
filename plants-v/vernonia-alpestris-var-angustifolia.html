Vernonia alpestris var. angustifolia

Vernonia alpestris var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, meadows, and rocky soils. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to purple composite flowers. It is known for its robust growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Vernonia alpestris var. angustifolia is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
