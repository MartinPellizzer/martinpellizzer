Vinsonia lucida

Vinsonia lucida, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Vinsonia lucida is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
