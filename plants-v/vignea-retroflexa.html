Vignea retroflexa

Vignea retroflexa, commonly known as the creeping wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features alternate, trifoliate leaves composed of three heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Vignea retroflexa is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through runners, making it both a useful ground cover and a potential weed.
