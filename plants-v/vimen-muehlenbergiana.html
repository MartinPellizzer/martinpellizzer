Vimen muehlenbergiana

Vimen muehlenbergiana, commonly known as the white fringe tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves appear. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a smooth, grayish bark. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

The white fringe tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
