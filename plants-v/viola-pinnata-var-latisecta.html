Viola pinnata var. latisecta

Viola pinnata var. latisecta, commonly known as the broad-leafed violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features heart-shaped, broad leaves with a distinctive lobed structure, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of violet or purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
