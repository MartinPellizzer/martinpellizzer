Vicia desertorum

Vicia desertorum, commonly known as the desert vetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow or green pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Vicia desertorum is often found in sandy soils and is resilient to drought conditions.
