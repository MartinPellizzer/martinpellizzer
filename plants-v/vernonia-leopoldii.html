Vernonia leopoldii

Vernonia leopoldii, commonly known as Leopold's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the grasslands of South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, spiky, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in open, sunny habitats. It is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in dry conditions.

Vernonia leopoldii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local pollinators.
