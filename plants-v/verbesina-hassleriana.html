Verbesina hassleriana

Verbesina hassleriana, commonly known as the yellow beggar-ticks, is a perennial herb native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer, and its rough, hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, hairy fruits that contain seeds with tufts of hair, aiding in wind dispersal. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
