Virgulus walteri

Virgulus walteri, commonly known as the Walter's sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to wetland habitats in North America, particularly in the eastern United States. The plant typically grows in dense clusters, with slender, triangular stems and small, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are clustered in spikelets, which are often subtended by bracts.

Virgulus walteri plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
