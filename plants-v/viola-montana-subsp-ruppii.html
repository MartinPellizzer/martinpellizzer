Viola montana subsp. ruppii

Viola montana subsp.

ruppii, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

Botanically, it belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and frost tolerance.
