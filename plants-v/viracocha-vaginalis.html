Viracocha vaginalis

Viracocha vaginalis, commonly known as the sacred mountain orchid, is a rare and ethereal flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pale violet petals and intricate floral structure. The plant thrives in cool, humid microclimates and requires specific soil conditions rich in organic matter. Its blooming period is brief, typically occurring during the late spring to early summer months.

Due to its endangered status, conservation efforts are underway to protect this culturally and botanically significant species.
