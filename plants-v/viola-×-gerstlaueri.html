Viola × gerstlaueri

Viola × gerstlaueri, commonly known as the German violet, is a hybrid species within the Viola genus, characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers.

It typically features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive wavy margin and produces flowers in a range of colors, including purple, violet, and white. The plant is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Viola × gerstlaueri is also valued for its ability to naturalize and spread through self-seeding, contributing to its ecological and horticultural significance.
