Vernonia magnifica

Vernonia magnifica, commonly known as the splendid ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems covered in soft, gray-green foliage. The plant produces showy, fluffy clusters of yellow to orange flower heads, which bloom in late summer to early fall. Vernonia magnifica is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in full sun.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native gardens and pollinator habitats.
