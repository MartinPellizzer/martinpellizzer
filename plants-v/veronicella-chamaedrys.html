Veronicella chamaedrys

Veronicella chamaedrys, commonly known as the creeping vervain, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Veronicaceae and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in clusters from late spring to early summer.

Veronicella chamaedrys is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and blooms.
