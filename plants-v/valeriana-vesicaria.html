Valeriana vesicaria

Valeriana vesicaria, commonly known as blister valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from June to August. Its roots contain volatile oils and have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their sedative properties.

Valeriana vesicaria thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
