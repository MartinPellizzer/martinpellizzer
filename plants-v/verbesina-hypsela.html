Verbesina hypsela

Verbesina hypsela, commonly known as the tall beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and rough, hairy stems. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Verbesina hypsela is often found in moist, disturbed soils along roadsides and in open woodlands.
