Viola grypoceras f. viridans

Viola grypoceras f. viridans, commonly known as the green-leaved violet, is a variant of the viola genus characterized by its distinctive foliage.

This plant typically features heart-shaped leaves that are a vibrant green, providing a striking contrast to its delicate purple flowers. It is native to specific regions in Europe and prefers moist, well-drained soil in partial shade. The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a touch of color to woodland gardens.

Its botanical features make it a sought-after plant for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
