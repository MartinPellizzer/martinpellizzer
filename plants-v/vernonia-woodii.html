Vernonia woodii

Vernonia woodii, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and along streambanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers range from yellow to orange in color.

Vernonia woodii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
