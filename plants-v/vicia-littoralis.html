Vicia littoralis

Vicia littoralis, commonly known as sea pea, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its pods contain several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind.

Vicia littoralis plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and improving soil fertility through nitrogen fixation.
