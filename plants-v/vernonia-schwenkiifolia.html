Vernonia schwenkiifolia

Vernonia schwenkiifolia, commonly known as Schwenk's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, purple to brownish-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for tropical and subtropical gardens.

Vernonia schwenkiifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits to pollinators.
