Vavanga edulis

Vavanga edulis, commonly known as the edible vavanga, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines for their nutty flavor. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with a woody stem and hairy foliage.

Vavanga edulis is valued not only for its nutritional content but also for its role in traditional agriculture and ecological systems.
