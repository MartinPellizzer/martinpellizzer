Vernonia areysiana

Vernonia areysiana, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal and rocky areas. The plant produces small, purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

Vernonia areysiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
