Vigna punctata

Vigna punctata, commonly known as the spotted bean, is a flowering plant species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a legume belonging to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems and small, yellow flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in hairy, elongated pods and are often used as a food source in various cuisines.

Vigna punctata is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important crop in sustainable agriculture.
