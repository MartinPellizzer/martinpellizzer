Verbena lanata

Verbena lanata, commonly known as woolly verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Verbena lanata is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
