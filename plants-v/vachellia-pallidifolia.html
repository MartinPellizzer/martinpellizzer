Vachellia pallidifolia

Vachellia pallidifolia, commonly known as the pale-flowered acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorned stems and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow, fragrant flowers in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in savannas and rocky areas.

Vachellia pallidifolia is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil in degraded landscapes.
