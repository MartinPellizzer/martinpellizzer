Vitis glandulosa

Vitis glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular grapevine, is a woody vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets and small, glandular hairs on the stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by clusters of round, blue-black berries. It is often found in moist, wooded areas and is adapted to a variety of soil types.

Vitis glandulosa is a member of the Vitaceae family, which includes other well-known grapevines.
