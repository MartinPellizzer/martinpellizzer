Vitis modesta

Vitis modesta, commonly known as the Japanese grapevine, is a deciduous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in the spring, followed by clusters of small, edible berries. Vitis modesta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of garden environments.
