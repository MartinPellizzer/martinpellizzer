Vitis congoensis

Vitis congoensis, commonly known as the Congo grape, is a species of flowering vine native to Central and East Africa.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into round, fleshy berries. Its fruits are edible and have a sweet, musky flavor, making it a valuable food source in its native habitat.

Vitis congoensis is also valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
