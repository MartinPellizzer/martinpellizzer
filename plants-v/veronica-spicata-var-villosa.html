Veronica spicata var. villosa

Veronica spicata var. villosa, commonly known as wooly speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces spikes of blue or purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
