Vitis scabicaulis

Vitis scabicaulis, commonly known as the roughleaf grape, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and small, clustered greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, sour-tasting berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. Vitis scabicaulis thrives in moist, woodlands and is often found along stream banks.

It is a member of the Vitaceae family, which includes other well-known grape species.
