Viburnum glomeratum subsp. rotundifolium

Viburnum glomeratum subsp.

rotundifolium, commonly known as the roundleaf snowball viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States. It features showy clusters of white, fragrant flowers that appear in spring, followed by red berries in the fall.

The leaves are oval-shaped and have a rounded base, giving the plant its subspecies name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
