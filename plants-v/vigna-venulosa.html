Vigna venulosa

Vigna venulosa, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that give way to elongated, cylindrical pods. Its seeds are typically small and can be used as a food source.

Vigna venulosa is valued for its nutritional content and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
