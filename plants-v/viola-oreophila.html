Viola oreophila

Viola oreophila, commonly known as the mountain violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple and violet. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola oreophila is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
