Verbascum thapsus subsp. giganteum

Verbascum thapsus subsp.

giganteum, commonly known as the giant mullein, is a tall biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its large, velvety gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, upright spikes. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in open fields.

Its fibrous roots and robust growth make it a resilient species in various environments.
