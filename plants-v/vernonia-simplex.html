Vernonia simplex

Vernonia simplex, commonly known as the slender ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and disturbed soils. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and they bloom in late summer to early fall.

Vernonia simplex is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
