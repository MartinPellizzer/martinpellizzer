Varronia globulifera

Varronia globulifera, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical tree native to Africa and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into round, fleshy fruits resembling small apples. The fruit contains a single large seed surrounded by a white, edible pulp.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional medicine.
