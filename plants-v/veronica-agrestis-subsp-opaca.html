Veronica agrestis subsp. opaca

Veronica agrestis subsp.

opaca, commonly known as the woolly speedwell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features slender, upright stems covered in dense, soft white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance.

The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
