Viguiera corumbensis

Viguiera corumbensis, commonly known as the Brazilian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and dense clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in open areas such as grasslands and savannas, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Viguiera corumbensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
