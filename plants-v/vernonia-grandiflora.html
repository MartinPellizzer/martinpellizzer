Vernonia grandiflora

Vernonia grandiflora, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its rough, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

Vernonia grandiflora is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
