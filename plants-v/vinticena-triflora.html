Vinticena triflora

Vinticena triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered vintic, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Violaceae and is characterized by its striking violet-blue petals and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and thrives in humid climates. Its flowers bloom in clusters of three, giving it its common name, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Vinticena triflora is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
