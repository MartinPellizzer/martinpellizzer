Viola kitaibeliana var. trimestris

Viola kitaibeliana var. trimestris, commonly known as the three-leaved violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being heart-shaped and slightly serrated. The plant produces violet flowers with purple petals and yellow markings, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in woodland habitats and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to native plant gardens.
