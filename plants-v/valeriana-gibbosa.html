Valeriana gibbosa

Valeriana gibbosa, commonly known as the rough valerian, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white. Its root system is fibrous and contains compounds that have been traditionally used in herbal medicine.

Valeriana gibbosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
