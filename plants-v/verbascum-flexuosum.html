Verbascum flexuosum

Verbascum flexuosum, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in dense, soft, grayish-white hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a terminal spike. Its leaves are lance-shaped and also covered in a thick layer of woolly hairs.

Verbascum flexuosum is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to full sun conditions.
