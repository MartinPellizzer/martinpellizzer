Viola × bavarica

Viola × bavarica, commonly known as the German viola, is a hybrid plant in the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–20 centimeters and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and ability to naturalize in gardens.

Viola × bavarica prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens or shaded borders.
