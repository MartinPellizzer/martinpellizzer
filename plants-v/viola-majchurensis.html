Viola majchurensis

Viola majchurensis, commonly known as the Hungarian violet, is a species of flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Viola majchurensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
