Viola lutea subsp. calaminaria

Viola lutea subsp.

calaminaria, commonly known as the Alpine violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine and subalpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with a distinctive violet throat and heart-shaped leaves.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous habitats. It is adapted to cold climates and can tolerate low light conditions.

Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
