Vitis mediterranea

Vitis mediterranea, commonly known as the Mediterranean grapevine, is a species native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a deciduous vine that produces small, clustered berries, which are typically used for winemaking. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens.

The plant plays an important role in both agriculture and local ecosystems.
