Vernonia lantziana

Vernonia lantziana, commonly known as Lantz's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and cylindrical, often reddish-brown stems. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer and early fall.

Vernonia lantziana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for pollinators.
