Vicia sativa subsp. sativa

Vicia sativa subsp.

sativa, commonly known as common vetch, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit, trifoliate leaves, and yellow pea-like flowers.

The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in agriculture as a cover crop and for forage. Vicia sativa subsp.

sativa is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, improving soil fertility.
