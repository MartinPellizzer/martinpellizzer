Vernonia rubricaulis f. squarrosa

Vernonia rubricaulis f. squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrosa ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in dense, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue with a hairy texture.

The showy purple to pink flower heads appear in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
