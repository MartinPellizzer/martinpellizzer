Valerianella

Valerianella, commonly known as garden sorrel, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Valerianella is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often used in ornamental gardening and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
