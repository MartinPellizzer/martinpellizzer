Viola silvatica subsp. insularis

Viola silvatica subsp.

insularis, commonly known as the island violet, is a perennial herb native to certain island regions of the Mediterranean. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is noted for its relatively small size and adaptation to specific island habitats.
