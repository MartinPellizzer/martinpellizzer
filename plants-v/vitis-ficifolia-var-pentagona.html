Vitis ficifolia var. pentagona

Vitis ficifolia var. pentagona, commonly known as the five-angled grapevine, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive five-angled stems and leaves that are often serrated along the edges. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

This vine is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
