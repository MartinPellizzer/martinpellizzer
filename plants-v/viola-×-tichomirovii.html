Viola × tichomirovii

Viola × tichomirovii, commonly known as the Tichomirovii violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and vibrant, bi-colored flowers that typically feature a purple upper lip and a yellow lower lip. This plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in cool, moist environments. It prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a suitable candidate for woodland gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
