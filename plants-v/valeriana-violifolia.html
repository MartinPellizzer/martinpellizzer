Valeriana violifolia

Valeriana violifolia, commonly known as blue valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Valeriana violifolia is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
