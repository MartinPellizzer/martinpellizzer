Viola bicolor

Viola bicolor, commonly known as the common violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in moist, shady environments. Viola bicolor is a perennial herb that often grows in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
