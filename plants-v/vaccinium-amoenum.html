Vaccinium amoenum

Vaccinium amoenum, commonly known as the dwarf blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary applications. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth. Its leaves are lance-shaped and evergreen, providing year-round interest in the landscape.

Vaccinium amoenum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to support local wildlife.
