Vernonia sericea var. angustifolia

Vernonia sericea var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces conical flower heads that range in color from white to pale purple, blooming in late summer to early fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy perennial suitable for native gardens.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and ecologically valuable addition to the landscape.
