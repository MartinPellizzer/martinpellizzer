Vaccinium boreale

Vaccinium boreale, commonly known as the northern blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to arctic and subarctic regions.

It produces small, dark blue berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in local cuisines. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates with long winters. Its leaves are typically narrow and leathery, providing a dense cover in its natural habitat.

Vaccinium boreale plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and small mammals.
