Vernonia brachyscypha

Vernonia brachyscypha, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. Vernonia brachyscypha is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
