Verbascum × godronii var. discolor

Verbascum × godronii var. discolor, commonly known as the white mullein.

This hybrid plant is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety, gray-green leaves. It produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and is often found in rocky or disturbed soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically for its medicinal properties.
