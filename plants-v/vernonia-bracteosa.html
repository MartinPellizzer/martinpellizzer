Vernonia bracteosa

Vernonia bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteosa ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from yellow to orange in color.

Vernonia bracteosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
