Vaccinium glabrescens

Vaccinium glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other berry-producing plants such as cranberries and huckleberries. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partially shaded to sunny conditions. Its small, oval leaves are dark green and glossy, and it produces clusters of bell-shaped white flowers in the spring.

The berries, which are typically blue or purple when mature, are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in culinary applications.
