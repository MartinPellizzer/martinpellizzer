Verbena hastata

Verbena hastata, commonly known as blue verbena, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, tubular blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Verbena hastata is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
