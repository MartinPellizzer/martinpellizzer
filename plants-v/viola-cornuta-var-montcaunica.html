Viola cornuta var. montcaunica

Viola cornuta var. montcaunica, commonly known as the Montcaun horned violets, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, horn-shaped petals that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and it produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of purple and violet.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
