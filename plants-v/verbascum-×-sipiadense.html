Verbascum × sipiadense

Verbascum × sipiadense, commonly known as the hybrid mullein.

It is a perennial plant belonging to the Scrophulariaceae family, characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves and showy yellow flowers. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its soothing properties.

Its hybrid nature results from crosses between Verbascum thapsus and Verbascum blattaria.
