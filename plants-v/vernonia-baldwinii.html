Vernonia baldwinii

Vernonia baldwinii, commonly known as Baldwin's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to violet-colored flowers. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia baldwinii is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides, where it adds a vibrant touch of color during the late summer and early fall.
