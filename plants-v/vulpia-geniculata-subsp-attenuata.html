Vulpia geniculata subsp. attenuata

Vulpia geniculata subsp.

attenuata, commonly known as the slender vulpia, is a grass species characterized by its slender, erect growth habit. It typically features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, across temperate regions. Its flowering period usually occurs in late spring to early summer.

The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types and is relatively drought-tolerant.
