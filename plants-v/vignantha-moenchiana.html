Vignantha moenchiana

Vignantha moenchiana, commonly known as the African hog peanut, is a climbing leguminous plant native to tropical Africa.

It features pinnately compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes. The plant bears pods that contain several elliptical, flattened seeds. It is adapted to grow in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland areas.

Vignantha moenchiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
