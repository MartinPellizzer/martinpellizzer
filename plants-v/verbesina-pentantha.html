Verbesina pentantha

Verbesina pentantha, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, conical flower heads that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Verbesina pentantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
