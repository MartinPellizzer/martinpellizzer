Viola gonzalii

Viola gonzalii, commonly known as the Gonzales violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in countries like Bolivia and Peru. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces delicate, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. Viola gonzalii is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes.

Its ornamental value has made it a sought-after plant in horticulture, though it remains relatively rare in cultivation.
