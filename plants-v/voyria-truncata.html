Voyria truncata

Voyria truncata, commonly known as the roughleaf lily, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves with a rough, textured surface. The plant produces showy, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Voyria truncata is often found in woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
