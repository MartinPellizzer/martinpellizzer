Viola colubrina

Viola colubrina, commonly known as the blue violets, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, showy flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola colubrina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
