Vicia villosa subsp. semicretica

Vicia villosa subsp.

semicretica, commonly known as the hairy vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple.

The plant is characterized by its robust growth habit and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is often used in agricultural settings for soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.

Its seeds are edible and have been traditionally used in various cuisines.
