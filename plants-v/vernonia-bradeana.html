Vernonia bradeana

Vernonia bradeana, commonly known as the Brade's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in wetland areas and is often found in marshes and along the edges of swamps. It produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall.

Vernonia bradeana is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ability to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
