Viburnum tomentosum var. mariesii

Viburnum tomentosum var. mariesii, commonly known as Martha's Vineyard viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant has grayish-white, hairy foliage that becomes more prominent as it matures. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for hedges or as a specimen plant in gardens.
