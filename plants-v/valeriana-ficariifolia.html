Valeriana ficariifolia

Valeriana ficariifolia, commonly known as the leafy valerian, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, small white or pinkish flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural study and conservation efforts.
