Vignea pseudobrizoides

Vignea pseudobrizoides, commonly known as the false brizoid vine, is a climbing plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant has slender, twining stems that can reach several meters in length, and its leaves are pinnately compound with small, ovate leaflets. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

Vignea pseudobrizoides is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments.
