Vigna golungensis

Vigna golungensis, commonly known as the Golung wild bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit, with trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers and is adapted to thrive in dry and semi-arid environments.

It is valued for its potential in sustainable agriculture due to its nitrogen-fixing ability and resilience to harsh conditions.
