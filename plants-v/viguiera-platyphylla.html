Viguiera platyphylla

Viguiera platyphylla, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the United States and Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, purple-pink composite flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping and native gardens.

Viguiera platyphylla is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
