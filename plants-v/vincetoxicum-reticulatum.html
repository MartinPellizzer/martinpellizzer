Vincetoxicum reticulatum

Vincetoxicum reticulatum, commonly known as the net-veined false bindweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the family Apocynaceae.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in late spring to early summer. Due to its aggressive growth habit, it is considered a potential weed in some regions.
