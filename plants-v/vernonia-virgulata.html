Vernonia virgulata

Vernonia virgulata, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows up to 2 feet tall, featuring slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, silky down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Vernonia virgulata is adapted to moist, sunny habitats and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
