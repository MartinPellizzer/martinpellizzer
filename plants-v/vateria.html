Vateria

Vateria, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, floating leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant's flowers are showy, with bright yellow petals that attract pollinators such as beetles and flies. Vateria thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and ponds.

Its extensive root system helps stabilize the soil and prevent erosion in wetland areas.
