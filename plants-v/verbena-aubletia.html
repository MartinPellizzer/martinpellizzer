Verbena aubletia

Verbena aubletia, commonly known as the African verbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of violet, blue, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden use.

Verbena aubletia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
