Valeriana pyricarpa

Valeriana pyricarpa, commonly known as the fireberry valerian, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces distinctive red, berry-like fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, aromatic scent.

Valeriana pyricarpa is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its calming effects.
