Valeriana murrayi

Valeriana murrayi, commonly known as Murray's valerian, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a distinctive, strong scent.

Murray's valerian is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its calming properties.
