Vernonia heringeri

Vernonia heringeri, commonly known as the Heringer's ironweed, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage is dark green with a rough texture, and it produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers in the fall.

Vernonia heringeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
