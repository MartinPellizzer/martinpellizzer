Verbascum tauri

Verbascum tauri, commonly known as the Taurian mullein, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and southern Russia.

It features tall, hairy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, velvety leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves and flowers are rich in mucilage and have been historically used to treat respiratory ailments.

Verbascum tauri thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
