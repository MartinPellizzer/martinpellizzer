Vitis munsoniana

Vitis munsoniana, commonly known as the Munson's grape, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically consumed by wildlife. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Vitis munsoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
