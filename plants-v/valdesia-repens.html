Valdesia repens

Valdesia repens, commonly known as the creeping valdesia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its slender, trailing stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Valdesia repens thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its foliage is lance-shaped with a smooth texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal in gardens.
