Vaccinium besagiense

Vaccinium besagiense, commonly known as the Besaghi blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, rich in antioxidants and nutrients. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist climates.

Its ornamental value, along with its culinary uses, makes it a notable species in both horticulture and local ecosystems.
