Vitis vinifera subsp. sativa

Vitis vinifera subsp.

sativa, commonly known as the common grapevine, is a species native to the temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports.

The plant produces clusters of small, typically green or purple berries that ripen to a range of colors, including red, black, and white. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a serrated margin.

The plant is widely cultivated for its fruit, which is used in winemaking, table consumption, and various culinary applications.
