Vigna lanceolata var. filiformis

Vigna lanceolata var. filiformis, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated pods and small, edible seeds. The plant typically grows as an annual vine, often climbing or sprawling across the ground. Its leaves are trifoliate, with small, ovate leaflets, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

This variety is valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its use in both culinary and ornamental applications.
