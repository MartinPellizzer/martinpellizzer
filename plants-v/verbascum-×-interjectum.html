Verbascum × interjectum

Verbascum × interjectum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in soft, velvety hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Verbascum × interjectum is also valued for its medicinal properties, with historical use in treating respiratory ailments.
