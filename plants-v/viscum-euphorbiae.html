Viscum euphorbiae

Viscum euphorbiae, commonly known as the euphorbia mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of its host, typically euphorbia species.

It lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its stems, which are embedded into the host's bark. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by sticky, berry-like fruits that aid in seed dispersal by birds. Its leaves are alternate and have a waxy texture, and it is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Viscum euphorbiae plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for certain bird species and contributing to the ecological balance of its habitat.
