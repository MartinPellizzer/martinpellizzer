Viola violacea f. rubra

Viola violacea f. rubra, commonly known as the red-flowered violet, is a horticultural form of the common violet.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to reddish-purple flowers, which are typically heart-shaped and bilaterally symmetrical. The plant has ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often pubescent, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This variety is a popular choice for gardeners seeking colorful, hardy plants for shaded areas.
