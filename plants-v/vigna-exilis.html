Vigna exilis

Vigna exilis, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated, cylindrical pods containing edible seeds. Vigna exilis is valued for its nutritional content and is often cultivated for both food and forage.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate for optimal growth.
