Vulpia membranacea var. occidentalis

Vulpia membranacea var. occidentalis, commonly known as western silky grass, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
