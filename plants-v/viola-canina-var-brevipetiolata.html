Viola canina var. brevipetiolata

Viola canina var. brevipetiolata, commonly known as the short-stemmed dog violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a hairy texture and short petioles. The plant produces violet flowers with five petals, typically in shades of purple or blue, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

This variant is distinguished by its shorter petioles compared to the typical variety of Viola canina.
