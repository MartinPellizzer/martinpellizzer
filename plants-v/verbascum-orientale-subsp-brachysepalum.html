Verbascum orientale subsp. brachysepalum

Verbascum orientale subsp.

brachysepalum, commonly known as the short-sepal mullein, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, velvety leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and easy maintenance.
