Vernonia arborescens var. corrientensis

Vernonia arborescens var. corrientensis, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, pale yellow to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
