Verbascum rhiphaeum

Verbascum rhiphaeum, commonly known as the rock mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in soft, woolly hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

Verbascum rhiphaeum is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional medicinal uses.
