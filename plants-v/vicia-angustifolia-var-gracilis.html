Vicia angustifolia var. gracilis

Vicia angustifolia var. gracilis, commonly known as slender vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves composed of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom in clusters. Its root system has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

This variety is often found in disturbed soils and is used in some regions for forage or as a cover crop.
