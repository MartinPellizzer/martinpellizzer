Veratrum polygamum

Veratrum polygamum, commonly known as the western white hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, waxy leaves. The plant produces clusters of greenish-white flowers in late summer, which are followed by egg-shaped berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
