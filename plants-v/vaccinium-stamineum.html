Vaccinium stamineum

Vaccinium stamineum, commonly known as the small whortleberry, is a species of dwarf shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants such as blueberries and rhododendrons. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in boreal and subarctic environments. Its small, dark blue berries are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in local cuisines.

The plant's botanical features include alternate, oval-shaped leaves and showy white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring.
