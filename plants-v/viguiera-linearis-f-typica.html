Viguiera linearis f. typica

Viguiera linearis f. typica, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the central and southern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that are covered in fine, grayish-white hairs. The plant produces showy, purple to pink composite flower heads that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its lance-shaped leaves are typically arranged oppositely along the stem.

Viguiera linearis f. typica is well adapted to full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
