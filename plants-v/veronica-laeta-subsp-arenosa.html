Veronica laeta subsp. arenosa

Veronica laeta subsp.

arenosa, commonly known as the sand veronica, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in coastal or inland dunes. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
