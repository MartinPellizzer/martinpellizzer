Viguiera tenuis f. typica

Viguiera tenuis f. typica, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear clusters of small, bright yellow to orange-red composite flowers. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and ability to support pollinators.
