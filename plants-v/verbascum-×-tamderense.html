Verbascum × tamderense

Verbascum × tamderense, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes various species of mullein known for their fuzzy leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and they bloom in late spring to early summer.

Verbascum × tamderense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
