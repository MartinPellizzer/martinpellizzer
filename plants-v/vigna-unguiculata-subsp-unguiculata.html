Vigna unguiculata subsp. unguiculata

Vigna unguiculata subsp.

unguiculata, commonly known as cowpea, is an annual legume native to Africa. It is characterized by its climbing habit, trifoliate leaves, and pods that contain edible seeds.

The plant produces yellow or white flowers and has a well-developed root system that helps in nitrogen fixation. It thrives in warm climates and is widely cultivated for its nutritional seeds and forage value.

The subspecies is known for its adaptability to various soil types and drought tolerance.
