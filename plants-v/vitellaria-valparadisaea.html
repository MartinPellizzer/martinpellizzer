Vitellaria valparadisaea

Vitellaria valparadisaea, commonly known as shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to the savannas of Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is valued for its economic and ecological importance. The tree produces a fruit with a thick, fatty seed kernel that is the source of shea butter. Its leaves are palmate, and it can grow up to 20 meters tall.

The shea tree plays a crucial role in soil conservation and supports biodiversity in its native habitat.
