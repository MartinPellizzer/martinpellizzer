Vitis zhejiang-adstricta

Vitis zhejiang-adstricta, commonly known as the Zhejiang grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its climbing habit and tendrils that help it ascend supports. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into berries, which are typically dark purple when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for cultivation, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
