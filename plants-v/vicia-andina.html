Vicia andina

Vicia andina, commonly known as the Andean vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is typically found in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, where it thrives in cooler climates. Vicia andina has small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional Andean agriculture.
