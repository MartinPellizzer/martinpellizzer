Veronica spicata var. gracilis

Veronica spicata var. gracilis, commonly known as slender spiked speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that forms dense mats of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikes of small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Its compact growth habit and attractive blooms make it a popular choice for garden use.
