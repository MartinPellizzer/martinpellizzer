Vigna parviflora

Vigna parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant produces elongated, slender pods that contain small, edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate for optimal growth.

Vigna parviflora is often cultivated for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein and essential nutrients.
