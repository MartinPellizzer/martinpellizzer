Verbascum birandianum

Verbascum birandianum, commonly known as the Armenian mullein, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

Verbascum birandianum is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in traditional medicine.
