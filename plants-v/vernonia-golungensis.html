Vernonia golungensis

Vernonia golungensis, commonly known as the East African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow to reddish-brown flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savanna habitats.

Vernonia golungensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some communities.
