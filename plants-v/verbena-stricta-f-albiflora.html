Verbena stricta f. albiflora

Verbena stricta f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered vervain, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are often branched and covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically white or pale blue, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly serrated margin.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant ornamental plants.
