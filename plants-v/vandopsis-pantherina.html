Vandopsis pantherina

Vandopsis pantherina, commonly known as the panther's vanda, is a rare and exotic orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and green. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its botanical features include long, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
