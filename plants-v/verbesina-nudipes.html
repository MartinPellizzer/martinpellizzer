Verbesina nudipes

Verbesina nudipes, commonly known as the nakedstem false sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and large, showy yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, roadsides, and disturbed areas. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a slightly rough texture.

Verbesina nudipes is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
