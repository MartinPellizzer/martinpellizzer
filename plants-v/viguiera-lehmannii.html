Viguiera lehmannii

Viguiera lehmannii, commonly known as Mexican ironweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, purple-pink composite flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, and it produces seeds in a cylindrical, spiky structure called a pappus. Viguiera lehmannii thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping and native gardens.

Its blooming period typically occurs in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
