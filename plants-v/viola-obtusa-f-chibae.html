Viola obtusa f. chibae

Viola obtusa f. chibae, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate violet flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a touch of color to the understory vegetation.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
