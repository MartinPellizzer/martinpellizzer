Veronica fontana

Veronica fontana, commonly known as the blueweed or sweet Veronica, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Plantaginaceae.

It is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of western Asia, often found in moist meadows and woodland edges. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often hairy.

Veronica fontana is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
