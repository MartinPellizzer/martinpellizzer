Vicia faba

Vicia faba, commonly known as the broad bean or fava bean, is a leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces pods containing several angular, flat seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients. Vicia faba has a deep root system that helps fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural rotations.

It is also valued for its ornamental value and historical significance in human agriculture.
