Vernonia lanifera

Vernonia lanifera, commonly known as the woolly ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its ornamental value.

The woolly texture of its foliage gives it a distinctive appearance in gardens and natural settings.
