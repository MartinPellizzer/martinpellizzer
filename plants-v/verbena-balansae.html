Verbena balansae

Verbena balansae, commonly known as the Sierra Madre verbena, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in shades of purple, pink, and white, producing nectar that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or open habitats.

Verbena balansae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
