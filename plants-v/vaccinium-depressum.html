Vaccinium depressum

Vaccinium depressum, commonly known as the lowbush blueberry, is a shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, deciduous leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, rich in antioxidants and used in culinary applications. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, forested areas.

Vaccinium depressum plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for various wildlife species.
