Vallisneria caulescens

Vallisneria caulescens, commonly known as the narrow-leaf waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to the freshwater ecosystems of Southeast Asia.

It features long, ribbon-like leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant reproduces through the production of small, yellowish flowers that emerge above the water surface. Its rhizomes form dense underwater mats, which help stabilize sediments and improve water quality.

Vallisneria caulescens is often used in aquaculture and ornamental pond settings due to its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
