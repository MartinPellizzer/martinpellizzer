Vitex lasiantha

Vitex lasiantha, commonly known as the silky hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and heart-shaped leaves with a soft, silvery underside. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in summer. Vitex lasiantha thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its rapid growth and ability to attract pollinators make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically valuable.
