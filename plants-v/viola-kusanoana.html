Viola kusanoana

Viola kusanoana, commonly known as the Kusano violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to its natural habitat.

Viola kusanoana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
