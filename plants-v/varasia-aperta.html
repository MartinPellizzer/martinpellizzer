Varasia aperta

Varasia aperta, commonly known as the open varasia, is a unique flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has adapted to its harsh environment with a deep root system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers. Its leaves are typically narrow and waxy, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Varasia aperta is often found in rocky outcrops and is a popular subject for botanists and horticulturists due to its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
