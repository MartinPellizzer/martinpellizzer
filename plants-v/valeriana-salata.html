Valeriana salata

Valeriana salata, commonly known as sweet valerian, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces a strong, pleasant fragrance, particularly when the leaves are crushed, due to the presence of volatile oils. Its root system is extensive and contains compounds like valerenic acid, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Valeriana salata is often cultivated for its calming effects and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
