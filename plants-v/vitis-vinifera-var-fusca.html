Vitis vinifera var. fusca

Vitis vinifera var. fusca, commonly known as the Black Muscat, is a variety of grapevine native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically small to medium in size. The plant is known for its aromatic and complex flavor profile, making it popular in both winemaking and culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The leaves are usually lobed and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinct botanical appearance.
