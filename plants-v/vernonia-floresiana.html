Vernonia floresiana

Vernonia floresiana, commonly known as the Hawaiian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a woody stem with a rough texture. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, and it produces small, dry fruits called achenes.

Vernonia floresiana is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in harsh, coastal environments.
