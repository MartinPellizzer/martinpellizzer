Viola × zophodes

Viola × zophodes, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is native to Europe and has been widely cultivated for its ornamental value.

Viola × zophodes is often found in gardens and woodland edges, where it contributes to biodiversity and ecological balance.
