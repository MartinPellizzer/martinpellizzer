Veronica mthiulethica

Veronica mthiulethica, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators.
