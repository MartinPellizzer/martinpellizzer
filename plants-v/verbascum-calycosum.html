Verbascum calycosum

Verbascum calycosum, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in dense, silky white hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a terminal spike. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Verbascum calycosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
