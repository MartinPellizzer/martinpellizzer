Verbena carollata

Verbena carollata, commonly known as Carolina verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial that typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of vibrant blue to purple flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Verbena carollata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
