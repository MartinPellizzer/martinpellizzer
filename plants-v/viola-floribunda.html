Viola floribunda

Viola floribunda, commonly known as the common violet, is a flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and gardens. Viola floribunda is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a versatile garden plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early spring blooming period.
