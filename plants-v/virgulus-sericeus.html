Virgulus sericeus

Virgulus sericeus, commonly known as the silky virgulus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, greenish-yellow inflorescences, which bloom in early summer.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
