Vernonia lutea

Vernonia lutea, commonly known as yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer and early fall.

Vernonia lutea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
