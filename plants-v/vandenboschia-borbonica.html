Vandenboschia borbonica

Vandenboschia borbonica, commonly known as the Bourbon cholla, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in small, spiny segments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. Its adaptations include water storage in its thick stems and reduced leaves to minimize water loss.

Vandenboschia borbonica is often found in rocky or arid environments, thriving in full sun.
