Vanilla heterolopha

Vanilla heterolopha, commonly known as the mountain vanilla, is a climbing vine native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its long, twisting stems and fragrant, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces small, dark brown pods that contain the aromatic vanilla extract used in culinary and perfumery applications. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Vanilla heterolopha thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
