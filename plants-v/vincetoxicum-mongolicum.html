Vincetoxicum mongolicum

Vincetoxicum mongolicum, commonly known as Mongolian snakeroot, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by follicles containing elongated, ribbed seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
