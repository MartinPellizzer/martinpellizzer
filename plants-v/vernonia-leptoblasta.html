Vernonia leptoblasta

Vernonia leptoblasta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, upright growth habit. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Vernonia leptoblasta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
