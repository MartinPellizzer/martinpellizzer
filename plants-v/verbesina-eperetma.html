Verbesina eperetma

Verbesina eperetma, commonly known as the yellow beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along roadsides and in wetlands. The plant features showy yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late summer to early fall. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The plant is known for its hairy stems and seeds that have bristly appendages, aiding in wind dispersal.
