Viola dyris var. calcarea

Viola dyris var. calcarea, commonly known as the limestone violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in calcareous soils, thriving in rocky or limestone-rich environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, and its flowers are usually violet or purple in color. It produces small, spurred flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is often found in open habitats like meadows and woodland edges.
