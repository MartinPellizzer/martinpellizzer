Vitex buchananii var. quadrangula

Vitex buchananii var. quadrangula, commonly known as the African blueberry or four-angled vitex, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, quadrangular (four-sided) leaves and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale blue. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically blue or black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry woodland or savanna habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
