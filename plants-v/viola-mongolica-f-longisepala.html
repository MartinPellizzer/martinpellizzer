Viola mongolica f. longisepala

Viola mongolica f. longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepaled viola, is a flowering plant native to regions of East Asia, including Mongolia and parts of China.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive violet flowers with elongated sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along streambanks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture, while the flowers bloom in early spring.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
