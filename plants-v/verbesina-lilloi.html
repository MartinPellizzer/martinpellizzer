Verbesina lilloi

Verbesina lilloi, commonly known as the Lillo's beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant produces small, hairy fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Verbesina lilloi is often used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
