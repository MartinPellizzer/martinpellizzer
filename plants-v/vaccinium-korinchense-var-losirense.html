Vaccinium korinchense var. losirense

Vaccinium korinchense var. losirense, commonly known as the Losirense blueberry, is a shrub native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, with a tart flavor. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires a cool, humid climate for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its potential use in local food systems.
