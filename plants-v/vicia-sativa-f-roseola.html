Vicia sativa f. roseola

Vicia sativa f. roseola, commonly known as the pink vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink or purple flower clusters, which contrast with its typical green foliage. The plant is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Its trifoliate leaves and pod-like fruits are typical of the genus Vicia.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
