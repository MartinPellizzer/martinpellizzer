Vicia globosa

Vicia globosa, commonly known as the globe vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Vicia globosa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.
