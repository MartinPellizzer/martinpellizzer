Vignidula nikoensis

Vignidula nikoensis, commonly known as the Namibian burr bush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Namibia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with deep root systems that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers. Its small, hairy leaves reduce water loss through transpiration, and the fruits disperse seeds via attachment to animals or clothing.

Vignidula nikoensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife in its native range.
