Vernonia ringoetii

Vernonia ringoetii, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust growth habit. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow to orange in color.

Vernonia ringoetii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
