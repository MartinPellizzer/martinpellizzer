Viola odorata f. caerulea

Viola odorata f. caerulea, commonly known as the blue violet, is a flowering plant characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and striking blue-violet flowers.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is native to Europe, particularly the British Isles. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and gardens. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in herbal medicine.
