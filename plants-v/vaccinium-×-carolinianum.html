Vaccinium × carolinianum

Vaccinium × carolinianum, commonly known as the Carolina blueberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, blue-black berries. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it is valued for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

The fruit is often used in jams, pies, and other preserves due to its sweet-tart flavor.
