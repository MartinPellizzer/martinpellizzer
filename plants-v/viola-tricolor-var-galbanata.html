Viola tricolor var. galbanata

Viola tricolor var. galbanata, commonly known as the violet tricolor, is a variant of the common violet native to Europe.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with trifoliate leaves and showy flowers in shades of purple, blue, and white. The plant typically blooms in early spring and prefers cool, moist environments. It has been historically used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
