Vaccinium whiteanum

Vaccinium whiteanum, commonly known as the Whitean blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

The leaves are evergreen, glossy, and typically have a leathery texture.
