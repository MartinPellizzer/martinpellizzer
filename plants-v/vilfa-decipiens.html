Vilfa decipiens

Vilfa decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive violet, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that resemble those of other violets. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and it produces violet-blue flowers in early spring.

Despite its attractive appearance, it is a rare and endangered species due to habitat loss and over-collection.
