Viola hirtipes

Viola hirtipes, commonly known as the hairy violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly in regions with moist, well-drained soils. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, which gives it its common name. Its flowers are typically violet or purple, with five petals and a distinctive hooded structure.

Viola hirtipes is often found in woodland areas and thrives in partial shade, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
