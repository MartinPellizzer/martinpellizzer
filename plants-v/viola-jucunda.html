Viola jucunda

Viola jucunda, commonly known as the happy violet, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves and delicate, five-petaled flowers that range in color from purple to white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It produces small, round seed pods that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Viola jucunda is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
