Viola declinata var. prolixa

Viola declinata var. prolixa, commonly known as the slender violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically less than 5 cm in length. The plant produces violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow throat, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and butterflies, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
