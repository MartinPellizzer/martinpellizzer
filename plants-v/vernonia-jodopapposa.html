Vernonia jodopapposa

Vernonia jodopapposa, commonly known as Jodopappus ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit. The plant produces showy, purple to pink flower heads that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Vernonia jodopapposa is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
