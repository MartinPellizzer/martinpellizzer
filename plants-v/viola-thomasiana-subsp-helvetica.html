Viola thomasiana subsp. helvetica

Viola thomasiana subsp.

helvetica, commonly known as the Swiss violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and woodlands.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces violet-colored flowers with a distinctive five-lobed structure. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in cool, temperate climates.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
