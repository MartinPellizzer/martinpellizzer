Verbascum infidelium

Verbascum infidelium, commonly known as the common mullein, is a biennial plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a loose, upright spike. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, fields, and waste areas.

Verbascum infidelium has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties, particularly for respiratory ailments.
