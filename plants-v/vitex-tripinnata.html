Vitex tripinnata

Vitex tripinnata, commonly known as the fragrant verbena or white honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The flowers are typically white or purple and bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Vitex tripinnata is known for its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
