Verbascum plicatum

Verbascum plicatum, commonly known as the wrinkled mullein, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in soft, velvety hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Verbascum plicatum is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its soothing effects on respiratory ailments.
