Vaccinium spicatum

Vaccinium spicatum, commonly known as the spiky blueberry, is a shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are alternate, oval-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of white pubescence.

Vaccinium spicatum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in boreal and temperate forest ecosystems.
