Vaccinium montanum

Vaccinium montanum, commonly known as the alpine blueberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale blue flowers. The plant is adapted to cold climates and high altitudes, often growing in rocky or acidic soils. Its fruit, a small, dark blue berry, is edible and contains antioxidants.

Vaccinium montanum plays an important role in alpine ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and contributing to soil stability.
