Vaccinium uliginosum var. alpinum

Vaccinium uliginosum var. alpinum, commonly known as the alpine cranberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It produces small, acidic berries that are typically red or black in color, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous or tundra environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, with a dense, mat-forming growth habit.

This variety is adapted to cold climates and can withstand harsh winter conditions.
