Viguiera bracteata

Viguiera bracteata, commonly known as Mexican blanket flower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant produces small, bract-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance, and it thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Viguiera bracteata is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
