Vicia deflexa

Vicia deflexa, commonly known as the creeping vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, trailing stems with alternate pinnate leaves composed of several small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in clusters.

Vicia deflexa is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration efforts.
