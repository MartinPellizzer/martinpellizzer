Vandellia rivularis

Vandellia rivularis, commonly known as the river vandellia, is a rare and unique flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a short, erect stem and is typically found growing in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in areas with high humidity and consistent water levels.

Due to habitat loss, Vandellia rivularis is considered endangered and is of particular interest to botanists and conservationists.
