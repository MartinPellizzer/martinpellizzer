Vernonia myriocephala

Vernonia myriocephala, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its rough, greyish bark and dense foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

Vernonia myriocephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
