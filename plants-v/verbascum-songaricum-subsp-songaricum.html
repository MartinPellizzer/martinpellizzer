Verbascum songaricum subsp. songaricum

Verbascum songaricum subsp.

songaricum, commonly known as the Songaric mullein, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that are often covered in soft, downy hairs.

The plant produces showy, yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
