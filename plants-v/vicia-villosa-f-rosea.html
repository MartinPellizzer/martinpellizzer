Vicia villosa f. rosea

Vicia villosa f. rosea, commonly known as the pink hairy vetch, is a flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often used as a cover crop due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both ecological and aesthetic benefits.
