Viola tricolor var. orcadensis

Viola tricolor var. orcadensis, commonly known as the Scottish violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the British Isles.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive three-lobed structure and produces clusters of violet, white, and yellow flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

The species is also notable for its role in local ecosystems, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
