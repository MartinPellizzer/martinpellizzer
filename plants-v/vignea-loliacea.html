Vignea loliacea

Vignea loliacea, commonly known as the slender bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. The plant has compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate structures, and its flowers are typically white or pale purple. Vignea loliacea thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate for optimal growth.

It is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
