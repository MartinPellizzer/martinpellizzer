Veratrum maximowiczii

Veratrum maximowiczii, commonly known as the Siberian white hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Melanthiaceae and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant features large, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of white to pale green flowers in early summer. It is known for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that have been studied for potential medicinal uses.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
