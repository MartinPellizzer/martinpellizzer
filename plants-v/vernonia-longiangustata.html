Vernonia longiangustata

Vernonia longiangustata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.

Vernonia longiangustata is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
