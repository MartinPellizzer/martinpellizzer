Veronica macrostemon subsp. luetkeana

Veronica macrostemon subsp.

luetkeana, commonly known as the Luetkeana speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

The plant features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
