Valeriana coreana subsp. badzhalensis

Valeriana coreana subsp.

badzhalensis, commonly known as the Korean valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Korea. It is characterized by its aromatic, cylindrical stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its roots are often harvested for their medicinal properties, which include sedative and calming effects.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a common species in forest understories.
