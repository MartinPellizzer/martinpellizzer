Verbesina mandonii

Verbesina mandonii, commonly known as the Mandon's beggar-ticks, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous roots. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs and produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a fuzzy texture.

Verbesina mandonii is known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
