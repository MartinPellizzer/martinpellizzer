Vincetoxicum hirundinaria subsp. luteolum

Vincetoxicum hirundinaria subsp.

luteolum, commonly known as the yellow elder, is a flowering plant native to the Americas. It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
