Verbascum neglectum

Verbascum neglectum, commonly known as common mullein, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers in the summer, which are clustered in a loose, upright spike. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, fields, and waste areas.

Verbascum neglectum is a perennial plant that is both ornamental and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
