Vernonia longepedunculata

Vernonia longepedunculata, commonly known as the long peduncle ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along riverbanks. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct aromatic scent.

Vernonia longepedunculata is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
