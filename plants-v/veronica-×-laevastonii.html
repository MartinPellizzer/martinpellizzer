Veronica × laevastonii

Veronica × laevastonii, commonly known as the silverleaf speedwell, is a hybrid plant that combines traits from its parent species, Veronica spicata and Veronica × macrantha.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats.

Veronica × laevastonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
