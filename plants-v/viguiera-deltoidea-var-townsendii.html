Viguiera deltoidea var. townsendii

Viguiera deltoidea var. townsendii, commonly known as Townsend's ironweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, dark purple to reddish-pink composite flowers. The plant features gray-green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity in its habitat.
