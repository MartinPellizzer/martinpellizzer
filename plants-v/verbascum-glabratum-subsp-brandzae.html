Verbascum glabratum subsp. brandzae

Verbascum glabratum subsp.

brandzae, commonly known as Brandzae's mullein, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are often covered in soft hairs.

The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in a loose raceme. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance and ecological benefits.
