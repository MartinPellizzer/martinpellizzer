Verbascum pallidiflorum

Verbascum pallidiflorum, commonly known as the paleflower mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes many species known for their soft, fuzzy leaves. It thrives in dry, sunny conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The paleflower mullein is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
