Viola anitae

Viola anitae, commonly known as the Italian violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of delicate, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola anitae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in garden settings.
