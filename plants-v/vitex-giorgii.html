Vitex giorgii

Vitex giorgii, commonly known as the Chinese vitex or sweet vitex, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, which bloom in summer. Its fruits are dry, three-lobed capsules that release tiny seeds when mature.

Vitex giorgii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses in Chinese herbal medicine.
