Viburnum leiocarpum var. punctatum

Viburnum leiocarpum var. punctatum, commonly known as the spotted viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with serrated margins and small, white, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and wildlife. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and seasonal color changes.
