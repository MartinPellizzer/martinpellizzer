Viola tricolor maxima

Viola tricolor maxima, commonly known as the heartleaf violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, colorful flowers that typically range from violet to white and yellow. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for centuries in gardens and botanical collections.
