Veronica cordata

Veronica cordata, commonly known as heartleaf speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal rosettes of dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of blue or violet flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Veronica cordata is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
