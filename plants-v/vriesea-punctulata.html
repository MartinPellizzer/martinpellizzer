Vriesea punctulata

Vriesea punctulata, commonly known as the spotted Vriesea, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, arching inflorescence that bears vibrant, long-lasting flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often adorned with distinctive white or yellow spots. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity to flourish.

Vriesea punctulata is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
