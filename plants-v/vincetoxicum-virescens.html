Vincetoxicum virescens

Vincetoxicum virescens, commonly known as green false bindweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically light to dark green, with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

Vincetoxicum virescens is known for its ornamental value and can be cultivated in gardens, though it may become invasive in certain environments.
