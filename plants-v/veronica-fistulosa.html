Veronica fistulosa

Veronica fistulosa, commonly known as the narrowleaf speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
