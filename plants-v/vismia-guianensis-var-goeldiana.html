Vismia guianensis var. goeldiana

Vismia guianensis var. goeldiana, commonly known as the Goeld's evergreen.

It is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil, Colombia, and Venezuela. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, clustered white flowers followed by bright red berries, which are an important food source for various wildlife.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
