Vicia pseudocracca var. brevipes

Vicia pseudocracca var. brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed vetch, is a perennial legume native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that are typically no more than 30 centimeters in height. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink or purple, which bloom in late spring. Its trifoliate leaves are pinnately compound, with small, rounded leaflets.

This variety is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.
