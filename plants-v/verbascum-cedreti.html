Verbascum cedreti

Verbascum cedreti, commonly known as the Cedreti mullein, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
