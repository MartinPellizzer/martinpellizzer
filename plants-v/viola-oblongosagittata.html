Viola oblongosagittata

Viola oblongosagittata, commonly known as the broadleaf violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy violet flowers with five petals, typically in shades of purple or blue. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological and horticultural studies.
