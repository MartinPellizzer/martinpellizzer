Verbena dissecta f. capitata

Verbena dissecta f. capitata, commonly known as the clustered verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces showy clusters of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a favored plant in horticultural settings.
