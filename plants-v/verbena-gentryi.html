Verbena gentryi

Verbena gentryi, commonly known as Gentry's verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Verbena gentryi is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
