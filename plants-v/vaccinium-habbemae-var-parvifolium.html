Vaccinium habbemae var. parvifolium

Vaccinium habbemae var. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaf blueberry, is a variant of the Vaccinium genus, which includes various species of heath and blueberry plants.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically a deep green in color. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, which bloom in early spring. The berries, which are typically blue or purple when mature, are edible and have a tart flavor.

This variety is often found in acidic, well-drained soils in temperate climates.
