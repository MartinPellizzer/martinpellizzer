Valeriana tangutica

Valeriana tangutica, commonly known as Tangut valerian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its perennial herbaceous growth habit. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.
