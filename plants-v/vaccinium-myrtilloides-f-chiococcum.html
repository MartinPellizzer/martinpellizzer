Vaccinium myrtilloides f. chiococcum

Vaccinium myrtilloides f. chiococcum, commonly known as the dwarf blueberry, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of pale blue to white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically harvested in the fall. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its dense foliage and attractive appearance.
