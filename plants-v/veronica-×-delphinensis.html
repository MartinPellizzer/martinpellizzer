Veronica × delphinensis

Veronica × delphinensis, commonly known as the hybrid speedwell, is a perennial plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable environments.
