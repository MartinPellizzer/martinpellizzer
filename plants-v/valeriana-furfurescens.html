Valeriana furfurescens

Valeriana furfurescens, commonly known as the hairy valerian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Valeriana furfurescens is often found in grasslands, woodland edges, and disturbed sites.
