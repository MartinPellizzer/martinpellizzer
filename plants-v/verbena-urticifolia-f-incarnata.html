Verbena urticifolia f. incarnata

Verbena urticifolia f. incarnata, commonly known as the redstem verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and slender, often hairy stems. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom from late spring to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a distinct, slightly stinging sensation when touched.

This species is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wetlands.
