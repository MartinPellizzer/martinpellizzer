Veronica incanovelutina

Veronica incanovelutina, commonly known as the silverleaf speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial species that is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for xeriscaping in arid regions.
