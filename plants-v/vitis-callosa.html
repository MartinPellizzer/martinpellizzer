Vitis callosa

Vitis callosa, commonly known as the scaly grapevine, is a deciduous woody vine native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive scaly bark and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in the spring, followed by edible berries that ripen to a dark purple color. Vitis callosa is often found in moist woodlands and thrives in partial shade.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
