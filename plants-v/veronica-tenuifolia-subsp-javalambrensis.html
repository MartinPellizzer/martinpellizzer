Veronica tenuifolia subsp. javalambrensis

Veronica tenuifolia subsp.

javalambrensis, commonly known as the Javalambrense Speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Madagascar. It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy texture, and produces small, blue to violet flowers in racemes.

The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly wavy margin. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in shaded or semi-shaded habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
