Verticordia muelleriana

Verticordia muelleriana, commonly known as the Mueller's wand, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, slender, pink or red floral spikes that resemble wands, which are borne on wiry stems. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is found in heathlands and open woodlands. It blooms during the summer months, producing clusters of small, tubular flowers that attract nectar-feeding insects.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a species of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
