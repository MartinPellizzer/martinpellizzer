Viola rotundifolia var. pallens

Viola rotundifolia var. pallens, commonly known as the white violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped, pale green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale lavender in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a subtle beauty to the landscape.
