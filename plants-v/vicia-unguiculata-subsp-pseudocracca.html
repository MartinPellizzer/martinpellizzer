Vicia unguiculata subsp. pseudocracca

Vicia unguiculata subsp.

pseudocracca, commonly known as the narrow-leaf vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae. It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing several seeds. It is typically found in disturbed soils and grasslands, thriving in temperate climates.

This subspecies is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
