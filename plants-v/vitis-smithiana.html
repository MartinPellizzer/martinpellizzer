Vitis smithiana

Vitis smithiana, commonly known as Smith's grapevine, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its tendrils and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by clustered berries that ripen to a dark purple color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Vitis smithiana is often found in rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
