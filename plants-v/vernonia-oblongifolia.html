Vernonia oblongifolia

Vernonia oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Vernonia oblongifolia is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wetlands.
