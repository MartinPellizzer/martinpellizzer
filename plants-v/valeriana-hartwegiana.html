Valeriana hartwegiana

Valeriana hartwegiana, commonly known as Hartweg's valerian, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its root system is often used in traditional medicine for its sedative and calming properties.
