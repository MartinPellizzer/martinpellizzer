Varronia poliophylla

Varronia poliophylla, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, woody fruits containing three to five seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Varronia poliophylla is valued for its timber and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
