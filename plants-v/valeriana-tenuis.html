Valeriana tenuis

Valeriana tenuis, commonly known as slender valerian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.
