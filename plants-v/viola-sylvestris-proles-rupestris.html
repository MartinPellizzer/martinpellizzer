Viola sylvestris proles rupestris

Viola sylvestris proles rupestris, commonly known as the rock violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and alpine regions of Europe.

It typically grows in well-drained soils on limestone or granite substrates, thriving in full sun to partial shade. The plant produces violet-blue or purple flowers with five petals, often with a distinctive yellow or white mark at the base. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapted to retain moisture in harsh environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
