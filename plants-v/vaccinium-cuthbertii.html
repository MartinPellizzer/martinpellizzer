Vaccinium cuthbertii

Vaccinium cuthbertii, commonly known as Cuthbert's blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark blue to black in color. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a leathery texture.

Vaccinium cuthbertii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.
