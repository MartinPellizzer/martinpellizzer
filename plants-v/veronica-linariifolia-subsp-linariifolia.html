Veronica linariifolia subsp. linariifolia

Veronica linariifolia subsp.

linariifolia, commonly known as the slender speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is a perennial herb with erect, branched stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, rocky areas, and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
