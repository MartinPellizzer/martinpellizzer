Viola cucullata var. sororia

Viola cucullata var. sororia, commonly known as the hooded violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive hooded petals that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and it produces clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers in spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
