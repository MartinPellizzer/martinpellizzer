Vernonia fulta f. tomentosa

Vernonia fulta f. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly ironweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its grayish-white, fuzzy foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flower heads that bloom in late summer. It is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in moist, sunny habitats.

The plant's structure includes upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.
