Vaccinium macrocarpon f. eburnea

Vaccinium macrocarpon f. eburnea, commonly known as the black huckleberry, is a variant of the American cranberry plant.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States, typically found in acidic, wet soils. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and dark green, contributing to its ornamental value.

The species is often cultivated for both its edible fruit and its ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
