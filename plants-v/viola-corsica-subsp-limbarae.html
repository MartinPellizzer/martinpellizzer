Viola corsica subsp. limbarae

Viola corsica subsp.

limbarae, commonly known as the Corsican violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region, specifically Corsica. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow throat. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in calcareous soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
