Vincetoxicum izuense f. chloranthus

Vincetoxicum izuense f. chloranthus, commonly known as the Izu green-flowered honeysuckle, is a unique variant of the vincetoxicum species.

It is characterized by its distinct chloranthus foliage, which gives it a striking green hue. The plant is native to Japan, specifically the Izu Islands, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are typically small and yellowish-green, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and subtle floral display.
