Viola atroviolacea

Viola atroviolacea, commonly known as the black viola, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers with dark purple markings, which bloom in early spring. Viola atroviolacea thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
