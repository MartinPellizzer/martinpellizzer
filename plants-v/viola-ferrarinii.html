Viola ferrarinii

Viola ferrarinii, commonly known as the Italian violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe, particularly Italy, and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky areas. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet-blue coloration, and its flowers are typically violet or purple, with five petals. Viola ferrarinii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the genus Viola.
