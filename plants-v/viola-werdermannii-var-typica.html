Viola werdermannii var. typica

Viola werdermannii var. typica, commonly known as the Werdermann's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, violet-blue flowers with five petals. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
