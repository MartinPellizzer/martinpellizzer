Vernonia gnaphalioides

Vernonia gnaphalioides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small blue or purple clouds. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies, and it thrives in full sun. Its feathery leaves and showy inflorescences make it a popular choice for wetland restoration and native gardens.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
