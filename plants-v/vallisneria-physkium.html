Vallisneria physkium

Vallisneria physkium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Southeast Asia.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface, with the lower portion submerged in water. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pollinated by water currents. Vallisneria physkium is often found in still or slow-moving waters, such as ponds and lakes.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
