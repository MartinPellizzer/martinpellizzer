Vernonia antandroy

Vernonia antandroy, commonly known as the Antandroy Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its yellow flowers are clustered in dense, spherical heads, and it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
