Viola arvensis var. tezensis

Viola arvensis var. tezensis, commonly known as the Tezen's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and meadows. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet or purple coloration, and its flowers are usually small, five-petaled, and range in color from pale violet to deep purple. It produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
