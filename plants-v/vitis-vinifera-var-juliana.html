Vitis vinifera var. juliana

Vitis vinifera var. juliana, commonly known as the Juliana grape, is a cultivar of the common grapevine.

It is characterized by its small, dark blue berries that are typically used for making jams and jellies. The plant is a deciduous vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fruit. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor profile and historical significance in European horticulture.
