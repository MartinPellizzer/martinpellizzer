Vernonia ampandrandavensis

Vernonia ampandrandavensis, commonly known as the Madagascar ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, forested areas and is known for its robust, upright stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in dense clusters.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
