Vignea deinbolliana

Vignea deinbolliana, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and gardens.

Vignea deinbolliana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
