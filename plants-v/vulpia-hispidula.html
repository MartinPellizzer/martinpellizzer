Vulpia hispidula

Vulpia hispidula, commonly known as hairy vulpia, is a small annual grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in disturbed soils such as roadsides, fields, and waste areas, demonstrating adaptability to various environmental conditions. The plant has slender, erect stems with rough, hairy leaves that are often reddish in color. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Botanically, it is characterized by its fine, thread-like leaves and the presence of ligules on its blades.
