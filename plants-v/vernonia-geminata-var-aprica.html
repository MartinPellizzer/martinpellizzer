Vernonia geminata var. aprica

Vernonia geminata var. aprica, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy perennial suitable for gardens and natural landscapes.

The yellow ironweed is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
