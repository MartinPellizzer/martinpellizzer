Verutum

Verutum, commonly known as the common mullein, is a biennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in the summer, which are followed by fluffy seed capsules. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in meadows.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
