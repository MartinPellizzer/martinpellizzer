Veprecella rubra

Veprecella rubra, commonly known as the red-stemmed aster, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, reddish stems and produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the fall. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Veprecella rubra is typically found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
