Viola schmidtiana

Viola schmidtiana, commonly known as the Schmidt's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly rough texture. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of purple and violet, often with a distinctive yellow eye-like marking. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Viola schmidtiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with specific soil and climate conditions.
