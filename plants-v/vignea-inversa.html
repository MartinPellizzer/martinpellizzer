Vignea inversa

Vignea inversa, commonly known as the upside-down vining plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive growth pattern.

Its stems exhibit a reverse phototropism, growing downward instead of upward, which sets it apart from typical climbing plants. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface, and its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, producing a subtle fragrance. Vignea inversa thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical interest lies in its unusual morphology and potential adaptations to specific ecological niches.
