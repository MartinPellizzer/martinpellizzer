Vittaria mediosora

Vittaria mediosora, commonly known as the slender fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Vittariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually small and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Vittaria mediosora is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities and ability to thrive in controlled indoor conditions.
