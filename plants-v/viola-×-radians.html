Viola × radians

Viola × radians, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 centimeters and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. It is often found in woodland areas and gardens, where it spreads through rhizomes and self-seeding.

The flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
