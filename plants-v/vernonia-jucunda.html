Vernonia jucunda

Vernonia jucunda, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces fluffy blue or purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Vernonia jucunda is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
