Veronica gracilis var. arguta

Veronica gracilis var. arguta, commonly known as the hairy everlasting, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy leaves and stems, which give it its common name and help reduce water loss in dry conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
