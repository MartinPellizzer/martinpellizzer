Viola hederacea var. sieberiana

Viola hederacea var. sieberiana, commonly known as the Sieber's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of violet, lavender, or white, depending on the variety. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
