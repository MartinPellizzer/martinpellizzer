Verbena porrigens

Verbena porrigens, commonly known as the hairy verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Verbena porrigens is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
