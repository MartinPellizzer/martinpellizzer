Virectaria angustifolia

Virectaria angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf virectaria, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters wide. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical or subtropical forests.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
