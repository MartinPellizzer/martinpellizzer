Vincetoxicum sibiricum var. boreale

Vincetoxicum sibiricum var. boreale, commonly known as the Siberian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern regions of Russia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, and it is adapted to grow in nutrient-poor soils.

It is often found in alpine and tundra environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
