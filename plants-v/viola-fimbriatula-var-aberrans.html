Viola fimbriatula var. aberrans

Viola fimbriatula var. aberrans, commonly known as the variable violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple-tinged underside. The plant produces showy violet flowers with five petals, often marked with dark purple or black veins. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is known for its variability in leaf and flower morphology.

It is often found growing in clusters, forming dense colonies in its natural habitat.
