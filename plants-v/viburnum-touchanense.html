Viburnum touchanense

Viburnum touchanense, commonly known as the Touchan viburnum, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows in humid, lowland forests and is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. Viburnum touchanense has elliptical leaves with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
