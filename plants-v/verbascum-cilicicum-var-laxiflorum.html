Verbascum cilicicum var. laxiflorum

Verbascum cilicicum var. laxiflorum, commonly known as the Cilician mullein, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It features large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in soft hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically in clustered inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or dry garden environments.

This variety is closely related to other mullein species and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical medicinal uses.
