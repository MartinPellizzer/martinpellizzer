Veronica constantinopolitana

Veronica constantinopolitana, commonly known as Constantinople speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Veronica constantinopolitana thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
