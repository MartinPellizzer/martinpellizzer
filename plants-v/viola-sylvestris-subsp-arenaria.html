Viola sylvestris subsp. arenaria

Viola sylvestris subsp.

arenaria, commonly known as the sand violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in open woodland or heathland habitats.

The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow keel. It is part of the Violaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
