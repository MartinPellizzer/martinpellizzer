Verbena platensis f. violacea

Verbena platensis f. violacea, commonly known as the purple verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant purple to violet flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often hairy, while its flowers are arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
