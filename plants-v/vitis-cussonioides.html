Vitis cussonioides

Vitis cussonioides, commonly known as the Chinese grapevine, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe and turn blue-black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.

Vitis cussonioides is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to climb using tendrils.
