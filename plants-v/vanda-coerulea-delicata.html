Vanda coerulea delicata

Vanda coerulea delicata, commonly known as the blue vanda, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue and white flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has long, pendant-like inflorescences and is known for its epiphytic growth habit, attaching itself to trees without harming them. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native arid environment.

Due to its rarity and beauty, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
