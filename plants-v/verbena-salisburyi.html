Verbena salisburyi

Verbena salisburyi, commonly known as the Salisbury verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Verbena salisburyi is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
