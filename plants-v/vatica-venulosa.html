Vatica venulosa

Vatica venulosa, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark brown wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a smooth surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody capsules containing several seeds.

Vatica venulosa is often found in lowland and hill forest ecosystems, where it plays a significant role in the local biodiversity.
