Vaccinium mandarinorum var. austrosinense

Vaccinium mandarinorum var. austrosinense, commonly known as the Australian cranberry bush, is a species of evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bright red berries. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.

The berries are edible and have a tart, cranberry-like flavor.
