Vilobia praetermissa

Vilobia praetermissa, commonly known as the overlooked violet, is a delicate herbaceous plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of pale purple to white flowers. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in shaded, moist environments. The plant has a short growing season and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a subtle yet valuable component of diverse ecosystems.
