Vicia sepium var. alba

Vicia sepium var. alba, commonly known as white field bean, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, calcareous soils, often found in meadows and woodland edges. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow pea-like flowers. Its pods contain several seeds, which are often used as forage for livestock.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its role in nitrogen fixation in the soil.
