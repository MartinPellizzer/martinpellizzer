Vaccinium scandens

Vaccinium scandens, commonly known as trailing blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark blue or black when ripe. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Vaccinium scandens is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form dense ground cover.
