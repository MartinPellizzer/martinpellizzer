Viola arvensis var. deseglisei

Viola arvensis var. deseglisei, commonly known as the Deseglise's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. It is characterized by its distinct varietal features, including a more elongated corolla compared to the typical Viola arvensis.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
