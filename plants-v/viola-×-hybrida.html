Viola × hybrida

Viola × hybrida, commonly known as the common garden violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, often violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Viola × hybrida is also known for its ability to self-pollinate and produce viable seeds.
