Vilfa intermedia

Vilfa intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate vilfa, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually unbranched and somewhat woody at the base.

Vilfa intermedia is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
