Veronica × cantiana

Veronica × cantiana, commonly known as the common speedwell, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a versatile choice for gardens.

This species is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
