Viola × bernardii

Viola × bernardii, commonly known as the velvetleaf viola, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its foliage is often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

Viola × bernardii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
