Vaccinium cereum subsp. cereum

Vaccinium cereum subsp.

cereum, commonly known as the wax bilberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is typically found in boreal and subarctic environments. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a waxy coating, and its flowers are bell-shaped, typical of the Ericaceae family.

The species is notable for its ability to survive in cold climates and its role in supporting local wildlife.
