Verbena ciliata var. pubera

Verbena ciliata var. pubera, commonly known as the hairy verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant features hairy, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
