Veronica nutans

Veronica nutans, commonly known as the drooping speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Veronica nutans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in herbal medicine.
