Vaccinium depressum var. minus

Vaccinium depressum var. minus, commonly known as the low blueberry, is a compact shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. The plant produces small, edible blue berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are dark green and elliptical, with a leathery texture.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its use in local ecosystems and small-scale agriculture.
