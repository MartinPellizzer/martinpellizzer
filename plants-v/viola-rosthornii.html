Viola rosthornii

Viola rosthornii, commonly known as the Rosthorn violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its blooming period occurs in early spring, adding a splash of color to its natural environment.

Viola rosthornii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with specific horticultural requirements.
