Viola pitouchaoensis

Viola pitouchaoensis, commonly known as the Pitouchao violet, is a species of flowering plant in the violaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky areas. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
