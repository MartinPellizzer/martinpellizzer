Viola × albovii

Viola × albovii, commonly known as the white violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, often white or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is native to regions of Europe and has been cultivated for its ornamental value.

Viola × albovii is known for its ability to thrive in garden settings, making it a popular choice for shady gardens and borders.
