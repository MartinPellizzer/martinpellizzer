Villadia recurva

Villadia recurva, commonly known as the zigzag plant, is a unique species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, winding stems that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical forests. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and sometimes have a waxy coating, which helps in reducing water loss.

The flowers, though small, are usually clustered and can be either white or pale yellow, adding to the plant's ornamental value.
