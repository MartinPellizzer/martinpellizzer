Vernonia tinctosetosa

Vernonia tinctosetosa, commonly known as the purpletop vervain, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and ecologically valuable plant for pollinators.
