Vignea festucacea

Vignea festucacea, commonly known as the common vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and is commonly used as a cover crop or forage.

Vignea festucacea has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
