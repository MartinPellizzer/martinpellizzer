Verbesina ceanothifolia

Verbesina ceanothifolia, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that appear in late summer and early fall. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with a silvery underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Verbesina ceanothifolia is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
