Viola alba subsp. alba

Viola alba subsp.

alba, commonly known as the white violet, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate white or pale purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is known for its ornamental value.

The flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.
