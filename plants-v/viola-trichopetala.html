Viola trichopetala

Viola trichopetala, commonly known as the hairy-petaled violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces clusters of violet-blue flowers with five petals. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in wildflower gardens.

Viola trichopetala is also a host plant for certain butterfly species, contributing to local biodiversity.
