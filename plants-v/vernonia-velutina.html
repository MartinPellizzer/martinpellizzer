Vernonia velutina

Vernonia velutina, commonly known as velvetiron, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.

Vernonia velutina is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its role in supporting local pollinators.
