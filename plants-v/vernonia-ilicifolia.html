Vernonia ilicifolia

Vernonia ilicifolia, commonly known as silverleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as swamps and pond edges, and can reach heights of up to six feet. Its leaves are silver-gray in color, covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are usually purple or blue.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
