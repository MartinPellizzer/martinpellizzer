Viola acuminata f. glaberrima

Viola acuminata f. glaberrima, commonly known as the glabrous viola, is a botanical variant of the common violet.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless leaves and stems, distinguishing it from other forms of Viola acuminata. This plant typically features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple or violet. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and role in ecological systems.
