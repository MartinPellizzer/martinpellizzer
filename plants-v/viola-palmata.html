Viola palmata

Viola palmata, commonly known as the palmate violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy violet flowers with five petals, typically in shades of purple or blue. Viola palmata thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
