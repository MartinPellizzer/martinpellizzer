Vitex marquesii

Vitex marquesii, commonly known as the Marquesas chaste tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical Pacific Islands.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows in coastal areas and dry forests. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are usually violet or purple in color, which bloom in clusters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Vitex marquesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic qualities.
