Vinca difformis var. dubia

Vinca difformis var. dubia, commonly known as the spreading Madagascar violet, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is a perennial evergreen shrub characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of vibrant purple or pink flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and flowers.

This variety is known for its robust growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
