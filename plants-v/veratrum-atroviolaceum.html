Veratrum atroviolaceum

Veratrum atroviolaceum, commonly known as the purple-veined aconite, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, with prominent yellow veins. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy coating.

Veratrum atroviolaceum is part of the Melanthiaceae family and is known for its toxic alkaloids, making it a plant of both ecological and medicinal interest.
