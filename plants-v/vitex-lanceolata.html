Vitex lanceolata

Vitex lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf chaste tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Vitex lanceolata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
