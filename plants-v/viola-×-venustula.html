Viola × venustula

Viola × venustula, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, often purple or violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and gardens. It produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Viola × venustula is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for centuries in horticulture.
