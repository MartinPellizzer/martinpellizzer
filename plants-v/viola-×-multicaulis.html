Viola × multicaulis

Viola × multicaulis, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that come in a range of colors, including purple, blue, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is often found in woodland areas and gardens.

Viola × multicaulis is known for its adaptability and is cultivated for its ornamental value.
