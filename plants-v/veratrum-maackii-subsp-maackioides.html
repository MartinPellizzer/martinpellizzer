Veratrum maackii subsp. maackioides

Veratrum maackii subsp.

maackioides, commonly known as the Maack's arrow-leaved veratrum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Russian Far East and northeastern China. It belongs to the Melanthiaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of white to pale blue flowers in late summer, which are followed by dark blue berries.
