Viola lesbiaca

Viola lesbiaca, commonly known as the Lesbia violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in partial shade to full sun. Its flowers bloom in early spring, producing a range of colors from pale purple to deep violet.

Viola lesbiaca is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
