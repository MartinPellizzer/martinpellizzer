Vitex beraviensis f. pilosa

Vitex beraviensis f. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy vitex, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves that are often arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in the summer months. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several small, smooth seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
