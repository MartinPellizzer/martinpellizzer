Vaccinium nuttallii

Vaccinium nuttallii, commonly known as the mountain huckleberry, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark blue to black in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coniferous or mixed forests.
