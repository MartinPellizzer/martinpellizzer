Vitex rehmannii

Vitex rehmannii, commonly known as Chinese vitex, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are a food source for various bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along streambanks.

Vitex rehmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
