Vandenboschia radicans var. naseana

Vandenboschia radicans var. naseana, commonly known as the Naseana ice plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, white flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a valuable addition to drought-tolerant landscapes.
