Vernonia bukamaensis

Vernonia bukamaensis, commonly known as Bukama's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish composite flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the dry season. Vernonia bukamaensis is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
