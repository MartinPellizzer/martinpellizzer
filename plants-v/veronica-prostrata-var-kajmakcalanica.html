Veronica prostrata var. kajmakcalanica

Veronica prostrata var. kajmakcalanica, commonly known as the Kajmakcalan Veronica, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Balkan region.

It features slender, prostrate stems that spread along the ground, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to thrive in rocky or dry soils.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in marginal environments.
