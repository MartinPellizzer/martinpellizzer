Vernonia pygmaea

Vernonia pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of only a few inches, making it a low-growing perennial. Its small, blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
