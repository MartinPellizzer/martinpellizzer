Veronica serpyllifolia subsp. parvifolia

Veronica serpyllifolia subsp.

parvifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically has small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense mats on the ground. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
