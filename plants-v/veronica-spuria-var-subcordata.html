Veronica spuria var. subcordata

Veronica spuria var. subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved speedwell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features slender stems that often form dense mats, with small, opposite leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces clusters of blue to violet flowers with five petals, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in garden settings for their ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
