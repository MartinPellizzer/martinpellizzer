Vernonia luhomeroensis

Vernonia luhomeroensis, commonly known as the Luhomero Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces showy, yellowish-green flowers that bloom during the dry season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.
