Viola odorata var. sepincola

Viola odorata var. sepincola, commonly known as the Italian violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of purple and white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
