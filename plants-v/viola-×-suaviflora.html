Viola × suaviflora

Viola × suaviflora, commonly known as the sweet violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and prefers moist, well-drained soil in partial shade. It is native to Europe and has been cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery.

The flowers emit a fragrant aroma, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
