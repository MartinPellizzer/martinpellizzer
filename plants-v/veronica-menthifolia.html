Veronica menthifolia

Veronica menthifolia, commonly known as silverweed or throatwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely toothed leaves that are often covered in a silvery or grayish coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Veronica menthifolia is a perennial herb that is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
