Viburnum awabuki

Viburnum awabuki, commonly known as Japanese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels. The plant produces dark blue, edible berries that are popular in traditional cuisine. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often used in ornamental landscaping.

Viburnum awabuki is also valued for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
