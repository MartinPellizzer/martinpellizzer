Vandellia cerastoides

Vandellia cerastoides, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. The plant features striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and the plant has a compact, bushy growth habit.

Vandellia cerastoides is well adapted to salt spray and high wind exposure, making it a hardy coastal species.
