Viola rafinesquei

Viola rafinesquei, commonly known as Rafinesque's violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers, which bloom in spring, range in color from pale lavender to deep purple.

Viola rafinesquei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
