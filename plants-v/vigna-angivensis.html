Vigna angivensis

Vigna angivensis, commonly known as the African wing bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and elongated, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds. The plant is typically grown for its high-protein seeds, which are used in various traditional dishes. Vigna angivensis thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It also has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it a beneficial crop for soil fertility.
