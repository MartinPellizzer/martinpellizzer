Verticordia dichroma

Verticordia dichroma, commonly known as the blackleaf stickleback, is a striking member of the family Proteaceae native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, erect, spindly stems and its dark, almost black, waxy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of vivid red or orange flowers, which are highly attractive to nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Due to its striking appearance and limited distribution, it is a sought-after species among botanists and horticulturists.
