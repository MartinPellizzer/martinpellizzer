Viola × kupfferiana

Viola × kupfferiana, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–20 cm and thrives in cool, moist environments with partial shade. Its flowers bloom in spring and are often used in ornamental gardening for their delicate appearance.

Viola × kupfferiana is also valued for its ability to naturalize and form dense mats, making it suitable for ground cover in suitable climates.
