Vaccinium acuminatissimum

Vaccinium acuminatissimum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blueberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to thrive in acidic, well-drained soils.

It is often found in high-altitude environments where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
