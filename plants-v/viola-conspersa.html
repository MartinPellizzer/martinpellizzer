Viola conspersa

Viola conspersa, commonly known as the hairy violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces showy violet-blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

Viola conspersa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
