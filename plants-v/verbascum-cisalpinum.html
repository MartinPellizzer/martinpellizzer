Verbascum cisalpinum

Verbascum cisalpinum, commonly known as the Italian mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Verbascum cisalpinum is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for respiratory ailments.
