Viguiera lanceolata

Viguiera lanceolata, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from white to purple.

Viguiera lanceolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soils.
