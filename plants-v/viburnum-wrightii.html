Viburnum wrightii

Viburnum wrightii, commonly known as the Elliott's viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for wildlife. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often found in moist, wooded areas.

Viburnum wrightii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
