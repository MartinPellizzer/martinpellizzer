Viola spathulata var. latifolia

Viola spathulata var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features heart-shaped, broad leaves that are typically hairy and covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces showy violet flowers with a distinctive white throat and purple petals, often blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
