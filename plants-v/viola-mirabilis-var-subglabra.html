Viola mirabilis var. subglabra

Viola mirabilis var. subglabra, commonly known as the velvetleaf viola, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and showy violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, soft pubescence, contributing to its common name.

The flowers are usually pale violet to nearly white, with a distinctive dark purple throat.
