Vignea × ilseana

Vignea × ilseana, commonly known as the hybrid grapevine, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Vigna sinensis and Vigna luteola.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and climbing habit, often forming dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, curved pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes or as a forage crop.
