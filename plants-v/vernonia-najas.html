Vernonia najas

Vernonia najas, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along the edges of ponds, showcasing its adaptability to moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture, while its flowers are usually blue to purple in color.

Vernonia najas plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
