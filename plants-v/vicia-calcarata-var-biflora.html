Vicia calcarata var. biflora

Vicia calcarata var. biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Vicia calcarata var. biflora is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant in certain habitats.
