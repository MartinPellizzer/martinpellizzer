Vicia tetrasperma

Vicia tetrasperma, commonly known as the four-seeded vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of Europe, Asia, and North Africa, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by cylindrical pods containing four seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a climbing habit with tendrils.

Vicia tetrasperma is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects.
