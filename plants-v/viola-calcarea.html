Viola calcarea

Viola calcarea, commonly known as the limestone violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It thrives in calcareous soils, often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats. The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces delicate, violet-blue flowers with yellow centers. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.

Viola calcarea is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
