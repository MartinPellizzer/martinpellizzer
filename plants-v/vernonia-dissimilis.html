Vernonia dissimilis

Vernonia dissimilis, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow to orange in color.

Vernonia dissimilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
