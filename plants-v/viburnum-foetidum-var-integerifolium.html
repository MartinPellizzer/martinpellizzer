Viburnum foetidum var. integerifolium

Viburnum foetidum var. integerifolium, commonly known as the sweet viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits in the fall. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for small garden spaces or as a foundation plant.

The variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
