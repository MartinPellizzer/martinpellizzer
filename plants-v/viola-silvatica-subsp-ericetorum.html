Viola silvatica subsp. ericetorum

Viola silvatica subsp.

ericetorum, commonly known as the heath violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe. It typically grows in moist, acidic soils in woodland or heathland environments.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and produces violet flowers with purple petals. It is part of the Violaceae family and is often found in shaded, semi-shrubby habitats.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller flowers and more compact growth compared to other viola species.
