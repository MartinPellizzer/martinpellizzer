Valeriana officinalis f. linearifolia

Valeriana officinalis f. linearifolia, commonly known as the linear-leaved valerian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and clusters of small, pale pink or white flowers. The plant belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is known for its aromatic, slightly medicinal scent. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically for its calming properties.
