Vicia qatmensis

Vicia qatmensis, commonly known as the Qatmen vetch, is a flowering plant species native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. Vicia qatmensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

It plays a minor role in local ecosystems and has limited use in agriculture or horticulture.
