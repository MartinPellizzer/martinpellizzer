Vicia Chianschanensis

Vicia chianschanensis is a rare legume native to the mountainous regions of China. This plant is known for its distinctive violet-blue flowers and its ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude environments. It plays an important role in local ecosystems by contributing to soil fertility through nitrogen fixation. Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it faces significant conservation challenges. Researchers are increasingly studying this species to better understand its ecological significance and potential for restoration efforts.
