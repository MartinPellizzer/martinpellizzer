Valeriana pontica

Valeriana pontica, commonly known as the Pontic valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features aromatic, opposite leaves that are typically lanceolate or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, arranged in dense inflorescences. Its roots are fibrous and contain essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Valeriana pontica is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along stream banks.
