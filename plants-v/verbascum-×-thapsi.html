Verbascum × thapsi

Verbascum × thapsi, commonly known as the common mullein, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in soft, velvety gray-green leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a spike-like inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Verbascum × thapsi is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for respiratory ailments.
