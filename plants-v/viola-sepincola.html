Viola sepincola

Viola sepincola, commonly known as the Italian violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Violaceae family and typically grows in woodland and grassland habitats. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple and blue. It is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Viola sepincola is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
