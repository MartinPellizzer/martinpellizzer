Viola longibracteolata

Viola longibracteolata, commonly known as the long-bract violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has a compact growth habit. Its flowers bloom in spring and are often adorned with distinctive bracts that extend beyond the petals.

Viola longibracteolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
