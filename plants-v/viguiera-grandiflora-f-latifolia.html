Viguiera grandiflora f. latifolia

Viguiera grandiflora f. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf Mexican sunflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It features large, showy yellow-orange flowers with prominent dark centers, which bloom from summer to fall. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are broader than the typical variety, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its striking foliage and vibrant blooms make it a valuable addition to native gardens and pollinator habitats.
