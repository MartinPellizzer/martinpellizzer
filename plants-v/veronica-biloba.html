Veronica biloba

Veronica biloba, commonly known as the common speedwell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of western Asia, and it thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, woodlands, and rocky areas. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in the spring. Veronica biloba is often found in moist soils and can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
