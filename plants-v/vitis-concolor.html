Vitis concolor

Vitis concolor, commonly known as the smoothleaf grape, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, wooded areas. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are edible, though typically not as widely cultivated as other grape species.

Vitis concolor plays an important ecological role, providing food for various wildlife species.
