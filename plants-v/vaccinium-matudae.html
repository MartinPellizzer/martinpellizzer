Vaccinium matudae

Vaccinium matudae, commonly known as the Matudae blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Andes mountains in South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark blue or purple in color. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in local cuisines.

Vaccinium matudae thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.
