Veronica pectinata var. villosa

Veronica pectinata var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy speedwell, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it its varietal name "villosa.". The plant produces clusters of small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with a fuzzy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.
