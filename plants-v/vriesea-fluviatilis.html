Vriesea fluviatilis

Vriesea fluviatilis, commonly known as the river vriesea, is a striking bromeliad native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant, strap-like leaves that form a rosette structure, which helps collect water in its natural habitat. The plant produces striking inflorescences with colorful, long-lasting flowers that can range from red to orange and yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found near rivers and streams.

Vriesea fluviatilis is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
