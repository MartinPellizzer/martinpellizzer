Veronica × sessiliflora

Veronica × sessiliflora, commonly known as the common speedwell, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
