Vicia japonica subsp. pallida

Vicia japonica subsp.

pallida, commonly known as Japanese vetch, is a flowering plant native to East Asia. It is a perennial legume that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and forest edges.

The plant features slender, upright stems with trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually pale yellow or white, arranged in drooping clusters.

The subspecies pallida is distinguished by its pale coloration and slightly different morphology compared to other Vicia species.
