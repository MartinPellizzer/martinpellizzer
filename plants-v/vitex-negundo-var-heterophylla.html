Vitex negundo var. heterophylla

Vitex negundo var. heterophylla, commonly known as the Chinese vitex or heterophylla vitex, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that vary in shape along the stem, a feature that gives it its specific epithet "heterophylla.". The plant produces small, fragrant, blue or purple flowers in panicles during the summer months. Its fruits are small, dry, and three-lobed, containing one or two seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
