Viola × matczkasensis

Viola × matczkasensis, commonly known as the Matczkas hybrid violet, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Viola lutea and Viola tricolor, characterized by its vibrant purple, violet, and white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows, preferring well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, edible flowers that are often used in culinary applications.

Viola × matczkasensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
