Verbascum × digitalifolium

Verbascum × digitalifolium, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant that combines features of both Verbascum and Digitalis species.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower landscapes.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a variety of morphological traits that distinguish it from its parent species.
