Vaccinium pernettyoides

Vaccinium pernettyoides, commonly known as the black huckleberry, is a species of shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and azaleas. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are elliptical and have a leathery texture, typical of many ericaceous plants.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions.
