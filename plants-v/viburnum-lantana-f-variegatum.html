Viburnum lantana f. variegatum

Viburnum lantana f. variegatum, commonly known as the variegated arrowwood, is a compact shrub characterized by its striking foliage.

Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with a glossy, dark green upper surface and a lighter, often variegated underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in late spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in partial shade to full sun.

Viburnum lantana f. variegatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
