Viburnum plicatum var. formosanum

Viburnum plicatum var. formosanum, commonly known as the Formosan crossvine, is a deciduous shrub native to Taiwan.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging stems and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces dark blue, waxy berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with serrated edges and a glossy texture.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its ornate appearance.
