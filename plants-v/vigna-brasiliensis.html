Vigna brasiliensis

Vigna brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian bean or Brazilian vigna, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. Vigna brasiliensis is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.

It also has potential agricultural value as a source of edible seeds and for soil enrichment through nitrogen fixation.
