Vernonia guianensis

Vernonia guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in dense clusters.

Vernonia guianensis is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
