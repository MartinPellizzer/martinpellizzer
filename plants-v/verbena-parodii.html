Verbena parodii

Verbena parodii, commonly known as the Mexican verbena, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows up to 3 feet tall, featuring slender stems and clusters of small, five-petaled flowers. The flowers are usually bright purple or blue, with a striking contrast to the green foliage. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
