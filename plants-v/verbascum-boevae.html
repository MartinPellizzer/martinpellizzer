Verbascum boevae

Verbascum boevae, commonly known as the Boevae mullein, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
