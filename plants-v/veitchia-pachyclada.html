Veitchia pachyclada

Veitchia pachyclada, commonly known as the giant cycad, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant produces cycad-like cones, with male and female cones on separate plants, a feature typical of cycads. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
