Vanilla albida

Vanilla albida, commonly known as the white vanilla or white orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, pendulous inflorescences with small, fragrant blossoms that are often used in perfumery and aromatherapy. Vanilla albida is a climbing vine that requires a humid, shaded environment to thrive.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and aromatic properties.
