Vernonia lindbergii

Vernonia lindbergii, commonly known as Lindberg's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, dark purple to reddish-brown flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and often branched.

Vernonia lindbergii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
