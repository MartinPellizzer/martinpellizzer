Vitis auriculata

Vitis auriculata, commonly known as the earleaf grape, is a deciduous vine native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in winemaking. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Vitis auriculata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
