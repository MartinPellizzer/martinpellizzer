Vaccinium polystachyum

Vaccinium polystachyum, commonly known as the purple highbush blueberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green with a slight pubescence. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from white to deep blue as they mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also cultivated for its edible berries.
