Verbascum mughlaeum

Verbascum mughlaeum, commonly known as the Mughlai mullein, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Verbascum mughlaeum is often used in traditional medicine for its soothing properties.
