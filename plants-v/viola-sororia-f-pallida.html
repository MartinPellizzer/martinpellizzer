Viola sororia f. pallida

Viola sororia f. pallida, commonly known as the pale violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces showy, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, rich soils along stream banks and woodland edges. It is a member of the Violaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Its delicate appearance makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native plants.
