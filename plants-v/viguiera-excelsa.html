Viguiera excelsa

Viguiera excelsa, commonly known as Mexican blueweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, sky-blue to lavender-colored flowers. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. Viguiera excelsa is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
