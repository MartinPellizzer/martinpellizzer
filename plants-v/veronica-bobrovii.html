Veronica bobrovii

Veronica bobrovii, commonly known as Bobrov's speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapting to the harsh alpine environment.

Veronica bobrovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
