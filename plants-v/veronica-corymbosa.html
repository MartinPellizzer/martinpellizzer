Veronica corymbosa

Veronica corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered speedwell, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, blue or purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Veronica corymbosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden settings for its delicate appearance.
