Viola × sabuletorum

Viola × sabuletorum, commonly known as the woodland violet, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, hairy leaves and showy violet or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

Viola × sabuletorum is also known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, forming dense colonies in suitable habitats.
