Vitis cordata

Vitis cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf grapevine, is a perennial vine native to North America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and produces small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in the eastern United States. Its stems are woody and can climb or trail along the ground.

Vitis cordata is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine.
