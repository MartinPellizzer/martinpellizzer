Veitchia vitiensis var. parhamiorum

Veitchia vitiensis var. parhamiorum, commonly known as the Vitiensis Veitchia, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and have a smooth texture. The plant produces striking yellow-orange inflorescences, which are often used in traditional Hawaiian ceremonies. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and cultural significance in its native regions.
