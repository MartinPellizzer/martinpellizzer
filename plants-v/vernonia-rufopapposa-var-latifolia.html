Vernonia rufopapposa var. latifolia

Vernonia rufopapposa var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, sparsely hairy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, conical array of bracts that support the tiny florets.

This species is often found in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and wetlands.
