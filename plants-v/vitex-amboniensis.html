Vitex amboniensis

Vitex amboniensis, commonly known as the Madagascar beautyberry, is a shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in spikes, followed by blue to purple berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Vitex amboniensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in its native region.
