Vernonia axilliflora

Vernonia axilliflora, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits. Vernonia axilliflora is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
