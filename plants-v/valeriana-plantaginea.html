Valeriana plantaginea

Valeriana plantaginea, commonly known as the meadow valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

Valeriana plantaginea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
