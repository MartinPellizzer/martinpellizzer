Vitekorchis lepida

Vitekorchis lepida, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features a slender, leafless stem that produces a single, showy golden-yellow flower. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its flowers are characterized by their intricate, spurred structure typical of the Orchidaceae family.

Vitekorchis lepida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
