Veronica sibirica f. latifolia

Veronica sibirica f. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive, textured leaves.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile addition to gardens.
