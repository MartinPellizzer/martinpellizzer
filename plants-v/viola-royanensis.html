Viola royanensis

Viola royanensis, commonly known as the Royal Viol, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant produces showy violet flowers with a distinctive purple hue, often with a yellow or white throat. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a soft, velvety texture.

Viola royanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
