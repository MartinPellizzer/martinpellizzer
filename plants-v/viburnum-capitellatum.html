Viburnum capitellatum

Viburnum capitellatum, commonly known as the clustered honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clustered inflorescences, which bloom in late spring. These are followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds.

Viburnum capitellatum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
