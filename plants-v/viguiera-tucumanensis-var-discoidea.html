Viguiera tucumanensis var. discoidea

Viguiera tucumanensis var. discoidea, commonly known as the Tucuman sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Andean regions of South America.

It features showy, daisy-like yellow flowers with a distinctive central disc, and it thrives in well-drained soils. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its drought tolerance. It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to native plant collections.
