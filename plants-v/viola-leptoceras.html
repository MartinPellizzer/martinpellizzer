Viola leptoceras

Viola leptoceras, commonly known as the slender violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically growing in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet-blue flower that blooms in early spring. Its flowers have five petals, with the lower petal being spurred, a characteristic feature of the genus Viola.

Viola leptoceras is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
