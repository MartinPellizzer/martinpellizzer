Vilfa natalensis

Vilfa natalensis, commonly known as Natal veldt grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in panicles, and it is often used in restoration projects due to its ecological benefits.

Vilfa natalensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small fauna.
