Vigna umbellata var. gracilis

Vigna umbellata var. gracilis, commonly known as slender cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and small, yellow flowers that develop into pods containing edible seeds. The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and is commonly cultivated for both human consumption and animal feed.
