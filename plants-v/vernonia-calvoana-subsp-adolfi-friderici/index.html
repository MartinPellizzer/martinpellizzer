Vernonia Calvoana Subsp. Adolfi-Friderici

Vernonia calvoana subsp. adolfi-friderici is a lesser-known yet fascinating plant species native to parts of Central and South America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other flowering plants. This subspecies is characterized by its unique foliage and striking blooms that often display a range of colors from yellow to orange. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in shaded forest understories or along riverbanks. Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it has gained attention among botanists and horticulturists alike.
