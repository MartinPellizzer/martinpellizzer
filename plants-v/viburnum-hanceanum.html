Viburnum hanceanum

Viburnum hanceanum, commonly known as the Chinese snowball, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring, creating a striking visual display. The plant produces red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a serrated edge, and it typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters.

Viburnum hanceanum thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
