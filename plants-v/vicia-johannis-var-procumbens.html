Vicia johannis var. procumbens

Vicia johannis var. procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate yellow vetch, is a perennial legume characterized by its trailing growth habit and yellow flowers.

It features trifoliate leaves composed of three oval, hairy leaflets. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several elliptical, pale brown seeds. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to fix nitrogen and prevent soil erosion.
