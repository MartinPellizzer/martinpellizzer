Vallisneria nana

Vallisneria nana, commonly known as dwarf waterweed, is a species of aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water's surface or hang submerged. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically submerged, followed by the development of elongated, cylindrical fruits. Vallisneria nana thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often forming dense underwater vegetation.

Its rapid growth and ability to stabilize sediments make it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
