Valeriana laciniata

Valeriana laciniata, commonly known as the cut-leaved valerian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are finely divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Valeriana laciniata is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in well-drained environments.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its calming effects.
