Viola pinnata f. triphylla

Viola pinnata f. triphylla, commonly known as the three-lobed violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, with a distinctive yellow keel. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The botanical characteristics of this plant make it a valuable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
