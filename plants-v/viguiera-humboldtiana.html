Viguiera humboldtiana

Viguiera humboldtiana, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough, hairy texture.

Viguiera humboldtiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
