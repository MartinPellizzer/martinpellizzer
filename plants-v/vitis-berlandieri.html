Vitis berlandieri

Vitis berlandieri, commonly known as the Mexican grape or Desert grape, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit, compound leaves, and small, clustered flowers that develop into edible berries. It is often found growing along rocky outcrops and in disturbed soils.

Vitis berlandieri is also valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes cultivated for its hardy nature and adaptability to harsh conditions.
