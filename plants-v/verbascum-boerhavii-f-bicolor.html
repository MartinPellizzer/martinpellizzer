Verbascum boerhavii f. bicolor

Verbascum boerhavii f. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored mullein, is a flowering plant belonging to the Scrophulariaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils.

This variety is distinguished by its bicolor flowers, which have a distinct color transition from yellow at the base to orange at the tips.
