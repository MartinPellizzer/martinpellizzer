Veronica beccabunga var. aegyptica

Veronica beccabunga var. aegyptica, commonly known as Egyptian speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa.

It features upright, slender stems that bear opposite pairs of oval, dark green leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in clusters during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and hardiness in temperate climates.
