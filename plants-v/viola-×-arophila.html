Viola × arophila

Viola × arophila, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from purple to white. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

Viola × arophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been widely cultivated in gardens.
