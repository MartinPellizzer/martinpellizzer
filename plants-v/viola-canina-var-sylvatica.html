Viola canina var. sylvatica

Viola canina var. sylvatica, commonly known as the woodland violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a deep green color and a distinctive violet-blue flower with a yellow center. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodland habitats, thriving in rich, loamy soils. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
