Vincetoxicum amplexicaule var. castaneum

Vincetoxicum amplexicaule var. castaneum, commonly known as the red bittersweet, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a distinctive red-orange coloration during the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red berries that persist into winter. Its berries are toxic to humans and animals, containing poisonous compounds.

This species is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils, where it thrives in partial shade to full sun.
