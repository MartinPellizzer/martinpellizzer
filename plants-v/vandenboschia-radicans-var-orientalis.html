Vandenboschia radicans var. orientalis

Vandenboschia radicans var. orientalis, commonly known as the oriental sandmat, is a low-growing succulent native to arid regions of Asia.

It features a sprawling, mat-forming growth habit with fleshy, ribbed stems that store water, enabling it to survive in dry environments. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant has a thick, fibrous root system that helps it anchor in sandy soils.

It is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its hardy nature and ornamental appearance.
