Viola llullaillacoensis

Viola llullaillacoensis, commonly known as the Andean Violet, is a striking alpine plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant violet or purple flowers, which bloom in the harsh, cold environments of its native habitat. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to the extreme conditions of high elevation. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help to reduce water loss.

Viola llullaillacoensis is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
