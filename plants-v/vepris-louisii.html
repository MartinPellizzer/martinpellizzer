Vepris louisii

Vepris louisii, commonly known as the West Indian satinwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers followed by yellowish fruits that contain aromatic seeds. Its wood is valued for its fine grain and durability, making it useful in furniture making.
