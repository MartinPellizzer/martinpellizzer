Veronica parviflora

Veronica parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, blue to purple flowers clustered in pairs along the upper parts. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Veronica parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
