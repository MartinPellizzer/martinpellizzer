Vania pulvinata

Vania pulvinata, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant has a slender, branching stem with smooth, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its fruit is a long, slender pod that splits open to release its seeds.

Vania pulvinata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
