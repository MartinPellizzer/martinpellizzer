Vincetoxicum hydrophilum

Vincetoxicum hydrophilum, commonly known as water bindweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams, ditches, and wet woodlands. Its roots can spread aggressively, making it a persistent weed in gardens and natural areas.

Vincetoxicum hydrophilum is also known for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
