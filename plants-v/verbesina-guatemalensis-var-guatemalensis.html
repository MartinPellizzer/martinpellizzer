Verbesina guatemalensis var. guatemalensis

Verbesina guatemalensis var. guatemalensis, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a perennial herb native to Central America.

It features upright, branched stems that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a rough texture.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.
