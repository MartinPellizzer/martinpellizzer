Vaccinium penduliflorum

Vaccinium penduliflorum, commonly known as the hanging-flower blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant features small, bell-shaped flowers that hang in clusters, giving it its common name. Its leaves are typically oval and have a slightly waxy texture.

The berries it produces are edible, though they are not as commonly cultivated as other blueberry species.
