Vitis ficifolia var. thunbergii

Vitis ficifolia var. thunbergii, commonly known as the Japanese grapevine, is a deciduous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by edible berries that turn from green to dark purple as they mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The berries are used in traditional medicine and can also be fermented into alcoholic beverages.
