Vexillabium yakushimense

Vexillabium yakushimense, commonly known as the Yakushima bellflower, is a rare and endemic species native to the subtropical forests of Yakushima Island, Japan.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often near streams or in the undergrowth of evergreen forests. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in a fine, silky pubescence, while its stems are slender and often branched.

Vexillabium yakushimense is of particular interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique adaptations to its specialized habitat.
