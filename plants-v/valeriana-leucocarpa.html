Valeriana leucocarpa

Valeriana leucocarpa, commonly known as white root valerian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces a fibrous, tuberous root system that is often harvested for its medicinal properties. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Valeriana leucocarpa is typically found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
