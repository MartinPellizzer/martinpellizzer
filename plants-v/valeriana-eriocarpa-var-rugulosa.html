Valeriana eriocarpa var. rugulosa

Valeriana eriocarpa var. rugulosa, commonly known as the rough-hairy valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features upright, branching stems covered in dense, rough hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered with a fine layer of hairs.

Valeriana eriocarpa var. rugulosa is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
