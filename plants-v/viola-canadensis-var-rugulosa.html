Viola canadensis var. rugulosa

Viola canadensis var. rugulosa, commonly known as the roughleaf violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shady woodland areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture, which gives it its scientific name. Its flowers are usually violet-blue or purple, with five petals and a distinctive spur.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
