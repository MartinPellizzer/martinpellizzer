Viola hirta var. nipponica

Viola hirta var. nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of violet or purple, often with a white or yellow center. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native ornamental plants.
