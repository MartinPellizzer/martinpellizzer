Veronica propinqua

Veronica propinqua, commonly known as the common speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant typically features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Veronica propinqua is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
