Valeriana interrupta var. interrupta

Valeriana interrupta var. interrupta, commonly known as the interrupted valerian, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features a creeping rhizome and upright, branched stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its aromatic leaves and stems contain essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
