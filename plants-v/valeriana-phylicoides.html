Valeriana phylicoides

Valeriana phylicoides, commonly known as the sweet valerian, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, small white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.
