Vitis × alexandrina

Vitis × alexandrina, commonly known as the Alexandrian grape, is a hybrid vine species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and the production of small, sour-tasting berries. The plant has compound leaves with three to five lobes and produces small, greenish-white flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.

Due to its adaptability, it can thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
