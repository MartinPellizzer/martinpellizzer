Vincetoxicum pachyphyllum

Vincetoxicum pachyphyllum, commonly known as the broad-leaved devil's ivy, is a perennial shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often dark green with a glossy appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an attractive but somewhat invasive species in certain regions.
