Vicia amphicarpa f. parvifolia

Vicia amphicarpa f. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved field vetch, is a botanical variant of the common vetch.

It is a perennial legume belonging to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. It has small, ovate leaves and produces cylindrical pods containing several seeds.

This plant is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural settings.
