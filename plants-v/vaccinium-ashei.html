Vaccinium ashei

Vaccinium ashei, commonly known as the Florida huckleberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark red to black in color. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a slightly waxy texture.

Vaccinium ashei thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or coastal areas.
