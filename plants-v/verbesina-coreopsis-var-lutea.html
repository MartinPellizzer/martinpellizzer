Verbesina coreopsis var. lutea

Verbesina coreopsis var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has green, lance-shaped leaves and a tall, branched stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Its showy flowers and hardy nature make it a popular choice for gardens and ecological restoration projects.
