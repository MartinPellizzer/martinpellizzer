Veronica schmidtiana subsp. daisenensis

Veronica schmidtiana subsp.

daisenensis, commonly known as the Daisen Veronica, is a flowering plant native to Japan. It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and streamside areas. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
