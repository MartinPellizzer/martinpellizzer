Veronica campestris

Veronica campestris, commonly known as the common speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, blue or violet flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it often grows in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides and meadows. It is part of the Plantaginaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking hardy, low-maintenance plants.
