Vossia procera

Vossia procera, commonly known as the giant waterwheel plant, is a unique aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, spirally arranged leaves that resemble the spokes of a waterwheel, hence its common name. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams. Its leaves are typically submerged, with the upper surfaces exposed to air, allowing for gas exchange.

Vossia procera plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
