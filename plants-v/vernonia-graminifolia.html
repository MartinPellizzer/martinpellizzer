Vernonia graminifolia

Vernonia graminifolia, commonly known as slender ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems covered in fine, silver-gray hairs. The plant produces small, clustered blue to purple flower heads, which are typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, giving it its scientific name "graminifolia," meaning grass-like leaves.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
