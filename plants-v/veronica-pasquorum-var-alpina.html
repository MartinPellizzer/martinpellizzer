Veronica pasquorum var. alpina

Veronica pasquorum var. alpina, commonly known as the alpine speedwell, is a flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous environments.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to withstand cold temperatures.
