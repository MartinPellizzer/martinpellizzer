Verbascum vulcanicum

Verbascum vulcanicum, commonly known as the volcanic mullein, is a flowering plant native to volcanic regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a dense, spike-like inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often growing in rocky or disturbed habitats.

Verbascum vulcanicum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically for its soothing effects on respiratory ailments.
