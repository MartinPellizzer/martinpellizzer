Vitex barbata

Vitex barbata, commonly known as the Mexican chaste tree, is a shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in the summer months. Its fruits are elongated, dry capsules that contain several seeds.

Vitex barbata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.
