Vernonia mollissima

Vernonia mollissima, commonly known as the silky Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Vernonia mollissima is often found in open woods and along stream banks, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
