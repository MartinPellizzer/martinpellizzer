Valeriana trichostoma

Valeriana trichostoma, commonly known as the mountain valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its upright stem and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. Valeriana trichostoma is known for its aromatic properties and has been traditionally used in herbal medicine.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.
