Vitis heterophylla var. aconitifolia

Vitis heterophylla var. aconitifolia, commonly known as the laceleaf grape, is a unique varietal of the grapevine characterized by its strikingly intricate leaf structure.

This plant is native to the southeastern United States, particularly in Georgia and Florida, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a feathery appearance that gives it a delicate, almost fern-like aesthetic. The vine produces small, clustered flowers that develop into edible berries, though they are not typically cultivated for wine or table use.

Botanically, it is notable for its variability in leaf morphology and its role in the natural ecosystem of its native habitat.
