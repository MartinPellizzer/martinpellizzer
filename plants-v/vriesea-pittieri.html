Vriesea pittieri

Vriesea pittieri, commonly known as the "Carnival Bromeliad," is a striking ornamental plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that resemble flowers. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater to support its growth. Vriesea pittieri thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental displays.
