Vernonia villarii

Vernonia villarii, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads that appear in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia villarii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
