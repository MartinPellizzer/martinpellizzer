Valeriana cordata

Valeriana cordata, commonly known as heartleaf valerian, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive, slightly aromatic scent. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Valeriana cordata is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in herbal remedies.
