Viola poiretiana

Viola poiretiana, commonly known as the violet of Poiret, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to gardens and natural landscapes.

Viola poiretiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in horticulture for its beauty and hardiness.
