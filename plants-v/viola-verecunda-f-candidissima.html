Viola verecunda f. candidissima

Viola verecunda f. candidissima, commonly known as the white viola, is a rare and striking variant of the viola species.

It is characterized by its pale white to almost translucent petals, which contrast with its deep green foliage. This plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands or along streambanks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a delicate beauty to the underbrush.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
