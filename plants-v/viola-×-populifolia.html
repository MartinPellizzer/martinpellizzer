Viola × populifolia

Viola × populifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf viola, is a hybrid plant that exhibits a mix of characteristics from its parent species.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features heart-shaped, broad leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of down, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are typically violet-blue in color, with five petals and a distinct yellow or white center.

Viola × populifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
