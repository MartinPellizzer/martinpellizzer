Viola cassinensis subsp. lucana

Viola cassinensis subsp.

lucana, commonly known as the Lucan violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region. It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces clusters of violet-blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice among gardeners.
