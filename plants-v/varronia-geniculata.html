Varronia geniculata

Varronia geniculata, commonly known as the waxy begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-drained soil.

Varronia geniculata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
