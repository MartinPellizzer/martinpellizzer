Viola pedunculata var. tenuifolia

Viola pedunculata var. tenuifolia, commonly known as the slenderleaf violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers with five petals, often marked with yellow veins. It blooms in early spring and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

This variety is also noted for its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
