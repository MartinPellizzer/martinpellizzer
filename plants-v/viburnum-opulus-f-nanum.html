Viburnum opulus f. nanum

Viburnum opulus f. nanum, commonly known as dwarf European cranberrybush, is a compact shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white, clustered flowers that appear in spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence. It typically grows to a height of around 1 to 2 meters, making it suitable for smaller gardens or as a hedge.

Viburnum opulus f. nanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
