Viola × doii var. glabra

Viola × doii var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves, which distinguish it from other viola varieties. The plant typically produces violet or purple flowers with five petals, often with a distinctive yellow or white spot at the base. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in cool, moist environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ability to bloom in shaded garden settings.
