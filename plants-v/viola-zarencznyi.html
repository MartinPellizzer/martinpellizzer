Viola zarencznyi

Viola zarencznyi, commonly known as the Zarencznyi violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant violet-blue flowers. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in horticulture.
