Veronica alpina var. lapponica

Veronica alpina var. lapponica, commonly known as the Lapland speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has slender, branching stems covered with fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow, linear, and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in tundra and mountainous habitats.

This variety is adapted to cold climates and is an important component of alpine plant communities.
