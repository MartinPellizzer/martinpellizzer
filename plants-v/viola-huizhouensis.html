Viola huizhouensis

Viola huizhouensis, commonly known as the Huizhou violet, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the landscape.

Viola huizhouensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
