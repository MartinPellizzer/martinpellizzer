Vincetoxicum atrocoronatum

Vincetoxicum atrocoronatum, commonly known as black crown beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark purple, clustered berries that appear in late summer and persist into winter. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a smooth, reddish bark. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, often found in woodland edges and moist habitats.

The berries are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.
