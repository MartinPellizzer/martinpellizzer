Verbena canadensis var. lambertii

Verbena canadensis var. lambertii, commonly known as Lambert's verbena, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Botanically, it belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its showy inflorescences and aromatic foliage.
