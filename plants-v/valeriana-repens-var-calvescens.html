Valeriana repens var. calvescens

Valeriana repens var. calvescens, commonly known as sweet valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters. Its root system is fibrous and contains compounds responsible for its characteristic medicinal properties.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal remedies.
