Verbascum virgatum subsp. dertosense

Verbascum virgatum subsp.

dertosense, commonly known as the narrowleaf mullein, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, velvety, gray-green leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in soft hairs.

The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers in terminal clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in dry, rocky habitats.

Its hairy leaves and robust growth make it a distinctive and easily identifiable species within the genus Verbascum.
