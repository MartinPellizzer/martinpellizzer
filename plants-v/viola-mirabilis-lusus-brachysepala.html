Viola mirabilis lusus brachysepala

Viola mirabilis lusus brachysepala, commonly known as the short-sepaled wonder violet, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, spurred petals. The plant typically produces small, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive, elongated spur at the base of each petal. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
