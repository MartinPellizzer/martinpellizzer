Vitis byzantina

Vitis byzantina, commonly known as the Byzantine grape, is a species of vine native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and the production of small, often aromatic berries. The plant typically has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in tendrils that aid in climbing. Vitis byzantina is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in temperate climates.

Due to its historical significance and unique botanical features, it is of interest to both botanists and viticulturists.
