Viola × digenea

Viola × digenea, commonly known as the heartleaf violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant purple or violet flowers. Native to the eastern United States, this plant thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors. The flowers typically bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the landscape.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
