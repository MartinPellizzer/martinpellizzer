Verbena alata f. alba

Verbena alata f. alba, commonly known as white velvetleaf verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant a velvety texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for containers and hanging baskets.

This annual plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
