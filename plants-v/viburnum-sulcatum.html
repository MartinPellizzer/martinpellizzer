Viburnum sulcatum

Viburnum sulcatum, commonly known as the roughleaf viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers in spring, followed by dark blue, waxy berries in the fall. It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and thrives in partial to full shade.

Viburnum sulcatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and supporting local wildlife.
