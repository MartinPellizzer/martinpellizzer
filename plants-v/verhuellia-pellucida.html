Verhuellia pellucida

Verhuellia pellucida, commonly known as the glassy leaf or glassy plant, is a small, herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that give it a glass-like appearance, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
