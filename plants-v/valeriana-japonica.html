Valeriana japonica

Valeriana japonica, commonly known as Japanese valerian, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and have a serrated edge.

Valeriana japonica is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its calming properties.
