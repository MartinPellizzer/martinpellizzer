Vernonia icosantha

Vernonia icosantha, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads that resemble mist, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Vernonia icosantha is a perennial herb that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.

It is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ability to support pollinators.
