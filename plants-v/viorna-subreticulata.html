Viorna subreticulata

Viorna subreticulata, commonly known as the reticulate viorna, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has a woody stem with a distinctive reticulated pattern on its bark, which gives it its scientific name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Viorna subreticulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
