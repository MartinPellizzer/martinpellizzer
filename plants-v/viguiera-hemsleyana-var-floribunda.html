Viguiera hemsleyana var. floribunda

Viguiera hemsleyana var. floribunda, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to purple cone-shaped flower heads that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, with a strong aromatic scent.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for native gardens.
