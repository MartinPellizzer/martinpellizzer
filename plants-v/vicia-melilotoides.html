Vicia melilotoides

Vicia melilotoides, commonly known as the yellow sweet clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with Rhizobium bacteria in its root nodules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites.

Due to its medicinal and agricultural uses, it has been cultivated for centuries.
