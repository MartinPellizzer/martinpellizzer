Verbena juncea

Verbena juncea, commonly known as common verbena, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled blue or purple flowers, which bloom from summer to autumn. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Verbena juncea is known for its drought tolerance and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
