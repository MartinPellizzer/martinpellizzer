Vaccinium ovatum

Vaccinium ovatum, commonly known as the evergreen huckleberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the coastal regions of California and Oregon.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically dark blue to black in color. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and woodlands.

Vaccinium ovatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing food for various wildlife species.
