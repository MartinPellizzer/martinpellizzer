Vitellaria curvifolia

Vitellaria curvifolia, commonly known as shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Sahel region of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elongated, elliptic leaves that are alternately arranged. The tree produces yellowish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing a single large seed. The seeds are processed to extract shea butter, a valuable ingredient in cosmetics and food products.

It plays an important ecological and economic role in the communities where it is found.
