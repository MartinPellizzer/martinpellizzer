Valeriana rigidula

Valeriana rigidula, commonly known as rigid valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has dark green, opposite leaves and produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters. Its root system is known for containing volatile oils and other compounds that contribute to its medicinal properties.

Valeriana rigidula is often used in traditional medicine for its calming and sedative effects.
