Vincentella sapini

Vincentella sapini, commonly known as the European whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape and often have a slightly serrated margin. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that ripens to a dark red or black color.

The bark is smooth and greyish in younger trees, becoming fissured with age.
