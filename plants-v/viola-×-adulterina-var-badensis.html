Viola × adulterina var. badensis

Viola × adulterina var. badensis, commonly known as the Baden viola, is a hybrid species belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and garden beds. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in horticulture.

Its botanical features include a short, hairy stem and showy flowers with five petals.
