Viguiera rigida

Viguiera rigida, commonly known as stiffleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It features upright stems that are covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, textured surface.

Viguiera rigida is often found in open woods, prairies, and along roadsides, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
