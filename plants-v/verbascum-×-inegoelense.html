Verbascum × inegoelense

Verbascum × inegoelense, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
