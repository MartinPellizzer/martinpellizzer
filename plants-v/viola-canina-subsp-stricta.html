Viola canina subsp. stricta

Viola canina subsp.

stricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and produces violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow beard on the lower lip. It is part of the Violaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The subspecies stricta is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to other varieties of Viola canina.
