Vernonia albicans

Vernonia albicans, commonly known as white ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its conical flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and wetlands, and it thrives in full sun. Its white to pale yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
