Vernonia dorsiventralis

Vernonia dorsiventralis, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by bees and other pollinators. Its hard, heavy wood is highly valued for construction and tool-making due to its durability.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional remedies.
