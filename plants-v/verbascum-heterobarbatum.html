Verbascum heterobarbatum

Verbascum heterobarbatum, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in dense, soft, woolly hairs. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and also covered in a thick layer of silky hairs.

Verbascum heterobarbatum is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is known for its medicinal and ornamental uses.
