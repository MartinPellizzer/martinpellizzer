Veronica pectinata var. schizocalyx

Veronica pectinata var. schizocalyx, commonly known as the silverleaf speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in a dense layer of silvery-white hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a similar silvery coating, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
