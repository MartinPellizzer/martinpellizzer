Vernonia rosea

Vernonia rosea, commonly known as the rose vervain, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers that bloom throughout the dry season. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a common sight in savannas and open woodlands.

The genus Vernonia is part of the sunflower family, Asteraceae, and is known for its diverse array of species with medicinal and ornamental uses.
