Veronica anagallis-aquatica subsp. anagalloides

Veronica anagallis-aquatica subsp.

anagalloides, commonly known as the water speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist or aquatic environments, such as wet meadows, stream banks, and marshes.

The plant features slender, upright stems covered in small, blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to wet conditions and its role in supporting pollinators in riparian habitats.
