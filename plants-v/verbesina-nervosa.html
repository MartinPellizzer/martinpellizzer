Verbesina nervosa

Verbesina nervosa, commonly known as rough hound's tongue, is a flowering plant native to the United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough, textured surface. Its showy yellow flowers appear in late summer, forming clusters at the ends of the stems.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.
