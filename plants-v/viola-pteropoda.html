Viola pteropoda

Viola pteropoda, commonly known as the purple violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in moist, woodland habitats. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola pteropoda is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
