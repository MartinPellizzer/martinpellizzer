Vaccinium wisselianum

Vaccinium wisselianum, commonly known as the Wissel blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark blue or black when mature. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for cultivation, it is sometimes grown in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
