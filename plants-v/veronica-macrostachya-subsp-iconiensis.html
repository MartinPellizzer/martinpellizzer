Veronica macrostachya subsp. iconiensis

Veronica macrostachya subsp.

iconiensis, commonly known as the Icon Island veronica, is a flowering plant native to the island of Iconia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces spikes of blue to purple flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal environments.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is appreciated in horticulture for its vibrant blooms.
