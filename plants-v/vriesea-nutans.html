Vriesea nutans

Vriesea nutans, commonly known as the drooping bromeliad, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, recurved inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that collect water, forming a central pool that supports aquatic life. Its flowers, which are typically red or orange, bloom in a spiral arrangement and are accompanied by showy bracts.

Vriesea nutans is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance and ability to thrive in indoor environments.
