Vandellia punctata

Vandellia punctata, commonly known as the speckled water lily, is a aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that are often dotted with small, dark spots, giving it its common name. The plant produces white flowers that bloom above the water surface, typically during the warmer months. Its roots are anchored in the sediment at the bottom of lakes and ponds.

Vandellia punctata plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
