Viola epipsila var. repens

Viola epipsila var. repens, commonly known as the creeping violet, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are usually violet or purple in color, with five petals. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

The species is notable for its ability to spread vegetatively through runners, making it a common ground cover in its native habitat.
