Veronica longibracteata

Veronica longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces bracts that are longer than the flower buds, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
