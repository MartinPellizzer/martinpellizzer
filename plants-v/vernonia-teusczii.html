Vernonia teusczii

Vernonia teusczii, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in dense, conical clusters.

Vernonia teusczii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
