Veronica serpyllifolia subsp. graeca

Veronica serpyllifolia subsp.

graeca, commonly known as Greek speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, blue or violet flowers arranged in clusters.

The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically found along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its flowers bloom from spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
