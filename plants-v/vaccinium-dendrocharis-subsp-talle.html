Vaccinium dendrocharis subsp. talle

Vaccinium dendrocharis subsp.

talle, commonly known as the tall evergreen blueberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in early spring. Its berries are dark blue to black, with a tart flavor, and are typically harvested in late summer.

This subspecies is well adapted to acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coniferous forests.
