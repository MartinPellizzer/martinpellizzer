Veratrum fimbriatum

Veratrum fimbriatum, commonly known as the fringed cowbane, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence composed of white to pale green flowers with prominent, fringed petals. The plant has smooth, ridged, and often hairy stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy coating.

Veratrum fimbriatum contains toxic alkaloids and is typically found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.
