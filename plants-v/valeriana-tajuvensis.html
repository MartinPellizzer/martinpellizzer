Valeriana tajuvensis

Valeriana tajuvensis, commonly known as the Tajik valerian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly Tajikistan.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often violet or blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its rhizomatous root system is notable for containing essential oils and compounds used in traditional medicine.

Valeriana tajuvensis is valued for its potential calming properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
