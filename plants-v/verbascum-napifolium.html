Verbascum napifolium

Verbascum napifolium, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a terminal spike. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

Verbascum napifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically for its medicinal properties.
