Veronica anagallis-aquatica var. humilis

Veronica anagallis-aquatica var. humilis, commonly known as water speedwell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the spring and summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged opposite each other. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies such as ponds and streams.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for wetland gardens and ecological restoration projects.
