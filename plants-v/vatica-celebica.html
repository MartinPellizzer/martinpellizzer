Vatica celebica

Vatica celebica, commonly known as the Celebes ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its hard, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and has a dense canopy with elliptical, leathery leaves. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

Vatica celebica plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
