Vitis gilvotomentosa

Vitis gilvotomentosa, commonly known as the yellow-tomentose grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly in regions of Georgia, Florida, and Tennessee. The plant is characterized by its hairy, yellow-green stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are typically ovate with a serrated margin and can vary in size depending on environmental conditions.

The fruit, which is a small, round grape, is edible but not commonly cultivated for commercial use.
