Verbena glabrata var. hayekii

Verbena glabrata var. hayekii, commonly known as the Hayek's verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and vibrant clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom throughout the summer. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for arid landscapes.
