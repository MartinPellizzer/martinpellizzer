Vitex panshiniana

Vitex panshiniana, commonly known as the Chinese vitex or mountain vitex, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at higher elevations.

Vitex panshiniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
