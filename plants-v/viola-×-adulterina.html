Viola × adulterina

Viola × adulterina, commonly known as the common garden violet, is a hybrid species resulting from crosses between viola species.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments and is often found in gardens and woodland areas. It has a short flowering period in spring and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The species is also noted for its ability to naturalize and spread in suitable conditions.
