Verbena jessicae

Verbena jessicae, commonly known as the Jessica's verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in shades of blue, purple, and white, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Verbena jessicae is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to flourish in arid environments.
