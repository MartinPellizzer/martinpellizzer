Viola grandidentata

Viola grandidentata, commonly known as the eastern violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of about 10-20 centimeters, featuring heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple underside. The plant produces showy violet flowers with five petals, often with a yellow center, during the spring months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Viola grandidentata is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
