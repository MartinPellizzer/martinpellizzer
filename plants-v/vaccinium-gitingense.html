Vaccinium gitingense

Vaccinium gitingense, commonly known as the Gitingen blueberry, is a species of shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark blue or purple in color. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

Its botanical characteristics include evergreen leaves and a preference for acidic, well-drained soils.
