Valeriana hoffmeisteri

Valeriana hoffmeisteri, commonly known as Hoffmeister's valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine habitats. The plant features a robust root system and slender, hairy stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense umbels, and are usually pink to purple in color.

Valeriana hoffmeisteri is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its calming effects.
