Verbena peruviana f. alba

Verbena peruviana f. alba, commonly known as white verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial shrub that produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of white to pale pink. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Its foliage is narrow and gray-green, adding texture to the garden.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
