Verbascum × tundscheliense

Verbascum × tundscheliense, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
