Viola crassa var. yatsugatakeana

Viola crassa var. yatsugatakeana, commonly known as the Yatsugatake viola, is a perennial flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It produces small, spurred flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
