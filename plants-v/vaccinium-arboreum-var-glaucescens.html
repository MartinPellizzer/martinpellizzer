Vaccinium arboreum var. glaucescens

Vaccinium arboreum var. glaucescens, commonly known as the blueberry shrub, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green with a slightly waxy texture. Its flowers are bell-shaped and typically pink or white, and it bears edible berries that are rich in antioxidants.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in acidic, well-drained soils.
