Veronica willcoxii

Veronica willcoxii, commonly known as Willcox speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a dense mat.

Veronica willcoxii is a perennial species that contributes to the biodiversity of its native habitats.
