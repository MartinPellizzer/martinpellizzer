Vereia laciniata

Vereia laciniata, commonly known as the lace fern, is a species of fern native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that give it a lacy appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant's botanical structure includes sterile and fertile fronds, with the latter producing spores for reproduction.
