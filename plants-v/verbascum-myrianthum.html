Verbascum myrianthum

Verbascum myrianthum, commonly known as the tall mullein, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are large, broad, and covered in dense, soft hairs.

It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.
