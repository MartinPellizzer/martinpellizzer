Viola cucullata var. cordiformis

Viola cucullata var. cordiformis, commonly known as the heart-shaped violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and velvety in texture. The plant produces showy violet flowers with a distinctive hooded structure, often in shades of purple or blue. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
