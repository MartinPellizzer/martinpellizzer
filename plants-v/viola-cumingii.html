Viola cumingii

Viola cumingii, commonly known as the Cuming's violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-20 centimeters and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to woodland gardens.

Viola cumingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens.
