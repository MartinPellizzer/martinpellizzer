Vigna parkeri

Vigna parkeri, commonly known as the African bean or parker's vigna, is a leguminous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being oval to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used as a food source. Vigna parkeri is adapted to dry and semi-arid conditions, making it a hardy crop in regions with limited rainfall.

Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil also makes it beneficial for soil enrichment.
