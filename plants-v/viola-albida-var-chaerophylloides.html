Viola albida var. chaerophylloides

Viola albida var. chaerophylloides, commonly known as the white violet, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It has a short, upright stem and produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also noted for its ability to thrive in cool, temperate climates.
