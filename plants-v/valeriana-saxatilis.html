Valeriana saxatilis

Valeriana saxatilis, commonly known as the rock valerian, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in mountainous regions. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, pale pink or white flowers in clusters. Its roots contain essential oils and have been used in traditional medicine for their calming properties.

Valeriana saxatilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
