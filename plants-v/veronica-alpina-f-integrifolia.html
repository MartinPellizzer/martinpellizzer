Veronica alpina f. integrifolia

Veronica alpina f. integrifolia, commonly known as the Alpine speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender, spreading stems covered in small, dark green leaves that are typically entire, giving it its specific epithet *integrifolia*. The plant produces clusters of blue or violet flowers with five petals, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine meadows and grasslands.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
