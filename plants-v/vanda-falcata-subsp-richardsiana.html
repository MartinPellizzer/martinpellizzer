Vanda falcata subsp. richardsiana

Vanda falcata subsp.

richardsiana, commonly known as the Richards' vanda, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia. It is an epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests.

The plant has long, strap-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink. It thrives in high humidity and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
