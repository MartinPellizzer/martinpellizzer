Viola davidii

Viola davidii, commonly known as the Chinese violet, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Viola.

It is native to China and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland and shady areas, preferring moist, well-drained soil. Viola davidii is a perennial herb that blooms in early spring, producing small, showy flowers that attract pollinators.

Its botanical features make it a valued ornamental plant in gardens and botanical collections.
