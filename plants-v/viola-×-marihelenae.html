Viola × marihelenae

Viola × marihelenae, commonly known as the Marhelenae violet, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Viola lutea and Viola tricolor.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with heart-shaped leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range in color from purple to white. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to shaded areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ease of care.
