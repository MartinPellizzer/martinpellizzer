Vittaria filipes

Vittaria filipes, commonly known as the feather moss, is a member of the family Vittariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery appearance.

It is a small, epiphytic moss that typically grows on tree trunks and branches in moist, shaded environments. The plant has slender, branching stems covered with small, overlapping leaves that are usually green or reddish in color. Vittaria filipes reproduces through spores, which are released from capsules that develop on the stems.

This moss is often found in temperate and tropical regions, contributing to soil stability and moisture retention in forest ecosystems.
