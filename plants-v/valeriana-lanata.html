Valeriana lanata

Valeriana lanata, commonly known as woolly valerian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in temperate climates. It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is known for its aromatic properties.

Valeriana lanata is often used in traditional medicine for its calming effects.
