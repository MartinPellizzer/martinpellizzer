Vangueria infausta var. campanulata

Vangueria infausta var. campanulata, commonly known as the Cape bellvista, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and rocky habitats, characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom in summer. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
