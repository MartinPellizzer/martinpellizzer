Veronica rockii

Veronica rockii, commonly known as the Sierra rockspeedwell, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Veronica rockii is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
