Vicia coreana

Vicia coreana, commonly known as the Korean vetch, is a flowering plant native to East Asia, particularly Korea and parts of China.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, temperate climates and thrives in shaded to partially sunny environments. The plant features slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually pale blue to purple in color, and it produces small, spherical pods that contain several seeds.

Vicia coreana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
