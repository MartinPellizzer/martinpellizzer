Veronica orchidea f. nudicalyx

Veronica orchidea f. nudicalyx, commonly known as the naked-stem veronica, is a unique hybrid plant that combines characteristics of both Veronica and orchid species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant with slender, often bare stems that give it its distinguishing name. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of blue or violet, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This hybrid is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and botanical collections.
