Volvulus sepium var. biangulosagittata

Volvulus sepium var. biangulosagittata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed volvulus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated margin, and its stems are often prostrate or creeping. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
