Verbena peruviana var. glabriuscula

Verbena peruviana var. glabriuscula, commonly known as the glabrous verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, though the variety 'glabriuscula' is noted for its reduced hair coverage. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and containers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
