Vaccinium odontocerum

Vaccinium odontocerum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other ericaceous plants like heathers and cranberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, rich in antioxidants and nutrients. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sun for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
