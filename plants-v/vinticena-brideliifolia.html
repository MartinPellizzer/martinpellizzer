Vinticena brideliifolia

Vinticena brideliifolia, commonly known as the Brideliifolia flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a visually striking addition to gardens. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Vinticena brideliifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is popular in tropical landscaping.
