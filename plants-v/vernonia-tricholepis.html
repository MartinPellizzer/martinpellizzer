Vernonia tricholepis

Vernonia tricholepis, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Vernonia tricholepis is often found in open woods, prairies, and disturbed sites, thriving in full sun to partial shade.

Its fibrous roots and hardy nature make it a resilient plant in various soil types.
