Verbascum × hranicense

Verbascum × hranicense, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes species known for their large, fuzzy leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in clusters at the end of the stems.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
