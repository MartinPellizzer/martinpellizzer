Viola etrusca

Viola etrusca, commonly known as the Etruscan violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Italy.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the landscape.

Viola etrusca is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
