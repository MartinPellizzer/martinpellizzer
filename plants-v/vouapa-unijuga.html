Vouapa unijuga

Vouapa unijuga, commonly known as the single-leaf vine, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. Vouapa unijuga thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology makes it a subject of interest in botanical research and horticulture.
