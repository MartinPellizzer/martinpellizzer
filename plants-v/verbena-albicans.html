Verbena albicans

Verbena albicans, commonly known as white vervain, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate white or pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically opposite in arrangement. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Verbena albicans is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
