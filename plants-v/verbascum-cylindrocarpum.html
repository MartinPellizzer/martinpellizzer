Verbascum cylindrocarpum

Verbascum cylindrocarpum, commonly known as the cylindrical-fruited mullein, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, showy yellow flowers. The plant produces cylindrical, ribbed seed capsules that are distinctive in appearance. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and gravelly slopes.

Verbascum cylindrocarpum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
