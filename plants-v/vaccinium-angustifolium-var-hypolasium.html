Vaccinium angustifolium var. hypolasium

Vaccinium angustifolium var. hypolasium, commonly known as the lowbush blueberry, is a variant of the lowbush blueberry species native to North America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils in forested areas. The plant produces small, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and are widely used in culinary applications. Its leaves are narrow and dark green, often covered in a fine white bloom.

This variety is important for both wild ecosystems and commercial blueberry cultivation.
