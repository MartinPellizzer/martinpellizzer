Vangueria pachyantha

Vangueria pachyantha, commonly known as the red bush willow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, orange-red fruits. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates.

Vangueria pachyantha is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
