Viola × dissita

Viola × dissita, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant is native to Europe and has been naturalized in many parts of the world. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and gardens.

Viola × dissita is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
