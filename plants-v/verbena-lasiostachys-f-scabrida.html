Verbena lasiostachys f. scabrida

Verbena lasiostachys f. scabrida, commonly known as the scabrous verbena, is a compact perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine, scabrous hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces spikes of vibrant blue or violet flowers from summer to autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or containers.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a valued addition to drought-tolerant landscapes.
