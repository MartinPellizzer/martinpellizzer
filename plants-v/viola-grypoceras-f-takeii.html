Viola grypoceras f. takeii

Viola grypoceras f. takeii, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of violet or purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
