Vernonia jenssenii

Vernonia jenssenii, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces bright yellow flower clusters that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

Vernonia jenssenii is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodland edges and wetlands.
