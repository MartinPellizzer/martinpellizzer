Valeriana origanifolia

Valeriana origanifolia, commonly known as mountain valerian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or woodland soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive, strong scent, while its flowers are usually white or pink.

Valeriana origanifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its calming properties.
