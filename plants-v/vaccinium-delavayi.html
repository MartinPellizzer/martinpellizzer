Vaccinium delavayi

Vaccinium delavayi, commonly known as Delavay's blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark blue or black when mature. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at high elevations.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for cultivation, it is of interest to both horticulturists and botanists.
