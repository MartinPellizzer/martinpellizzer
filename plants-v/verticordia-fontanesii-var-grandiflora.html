Verticordia fontanesii var. grandiflora

Verticordia fontanesii var. grandiflora, commonly known as the Western Australian Christmas tree, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage and vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. Its unique floral structure consists of a central column surrounded by recurved petals, making it a distinctive feature in its native habitat.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual impact.
