Veronica derwentiana subsp. anisodonta

Veronica derwentiana subsp.

anisodonta, commonly known as the New Zealand veronica, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand. It features upright stems with lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically bluish-green in color.

The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers with five petals, which bloom from late winter to early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
