Viola corralensis

Viola corralensis, commonly known as the Corralensis violet, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It prefers well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight.

Viola corralensis is often found in rocky or open habitats, such as grasslands and canyon walls.
