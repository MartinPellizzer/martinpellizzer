Vaccinium altiterrae

Vaccinium altiterrae, commonly known as the highland blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It produces small, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires a cool, humid climate. Its leaves are typically dark green and oval-shaped, with a leathery texture.

Vaccinium altiterrae plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing food for various wildlife species.
