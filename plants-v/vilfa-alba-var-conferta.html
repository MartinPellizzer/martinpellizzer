Vilfa alba var. conferta

Vilfa alba var. conferta, commonly known as the white vilfa or dense vilfa, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its pale white to cream-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is noted for its compact growth habit and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
