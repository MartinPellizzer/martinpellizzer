Vicia cracca subsp. tenuifolia

Vicia cracca subsp.

tenuifolia, commonly known as slender vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with narrow, elongated leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. Its seed pods are slender and contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in dry grasslands.
