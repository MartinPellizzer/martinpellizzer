Valeriana sylvatica var. glabra

Valeriana sylvatica var. glabra, commonly known as smooth wood betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and opposite leaves that are lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from June to August.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks, and its roots are often used in traditional herbal medicine.
