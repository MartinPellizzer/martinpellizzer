Vaccinium obversum

Vaccinium obversum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved huckleberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on both sides. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and it produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.
