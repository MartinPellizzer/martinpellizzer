Vicia monantha subsp. cinerea

Vicia monantha subsp.

cinerea, commonly known as the gray vetch, is a perennial legume native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with a pale greenish-gray underside.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale yellow to cream in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

This subspecies is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and historical use in traditional medicine.
