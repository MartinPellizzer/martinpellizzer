Valeriana laxa

Valeriana laxa, commonly known as the loose valerian, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its aromatic leaves and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their calming properties.

Valeriana laxa is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
