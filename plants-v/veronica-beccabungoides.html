Veronica beccabungoides

Veronica beccabungoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender stems and small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
