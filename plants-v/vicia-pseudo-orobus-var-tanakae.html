Vicia pseudo-orobus var. tanakae

Vicia pseudo-orobus var. tanakae, commonly known as the Japanese vetch, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and elongated, pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and is often found in forest understories or along streambanks. Its flowers are usually pale blue or violet, and it produces small, cylindrical seeds within its pods.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its role in nitrogen fixation in soil.
