Valantia glaberrima

Valantia glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth valantia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth, waxy texture, which contributes to its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Valantia glaberrima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
