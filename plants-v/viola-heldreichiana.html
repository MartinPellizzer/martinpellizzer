Viola heldreichiana

Viola heldreichiana, commonly known as the Balkan violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces delicate, violet-blue flowers with yellow centers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to its natural habitat.

Viola heldreichiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
