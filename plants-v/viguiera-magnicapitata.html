Viguiera magnicapitata

Viguiera magnicapitata, commonly known as the Mexican coneflower, is a perennial plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, providing a textured appearance. Its flowering heads are surrounded by a series of yellow ray florets and a central disc of tubular flowers.

Viguiera magnicapitata is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky soils.
