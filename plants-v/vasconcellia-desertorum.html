Vasconcellia desertorum

Vasconcellia desertorum, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It blooms during the spring and summer months, relying on pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Vasconcellia desertorum is well adapted to desert conditions, making it a resilient and visually striking component of its ecosystem.
