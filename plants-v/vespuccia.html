Vespuccia

Vespuccia, commonly known as the Antarctic hair grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It features slender, grass-like stems covered in fine, silky hairs that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, adapted for pollination by wind rather than insects. Vespuccia thrives in cold, arid conditions and is often found in rocky or glacial landscapes.

Its unique morphology allows it to survive in extreme climates with minimal water availability.
