Vangueria madagascariensis var. abyssinica

Vangueria madagascariensis var. abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian vangueria, is a shrub native to parts of Africa, including Ethiopia and Sudan.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a woody stem with thorny branches, and its leaves are typically elliptical with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
