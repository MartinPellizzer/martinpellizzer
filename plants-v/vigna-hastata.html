Vigna hastata

Vigna hastata, commonly known as the sickle bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and slender, climbing stems. The plant produces elongated, yellowish pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds. Vigna hastata is often grown for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate for optimal growth.
