Viola purpurea subsp. dimorpha

Viola purpurea subsp.

dimorpha, commonly known as the purple violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly velvety texture.

The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with five petals, often with a distinctive yellow marking at the base. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and meadows.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental and hardy plants.
