Viola malvina

Viola malvina, commonly known as the Mexican viola, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola malvina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
