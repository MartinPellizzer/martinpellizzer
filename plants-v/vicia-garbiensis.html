Vicia garbiensis

Vicia garbiensis, commonly known as the Garbi vetch, is a flowering plant species belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Central Asia and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its pods contain several seeds. Vicia garbiensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in such habitats.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native range.
