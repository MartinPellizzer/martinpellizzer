Vigna pongolensis

Vigna pongolensis, commonly known as the African cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit, trifoliate leaves, and pods that contain several edible seeds. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, making it an important crop in dryland agriculture. Its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules enhances soil fertility.

Vigna pongolensis is valued for its nutritional content, including protein, fiber, and essential minerals.
