Viscum eucleae

Viscum eucleae, commonly known as the European mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of trees such as oak and apple.

It has a distinctive appearance with small, oval-shaped leaves and white, sticky berries that contain toxic seeds. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with its host, drawing water and nutrients while also providing shelter for various insects and birds. It is often found in temperate regions of Europe and has cultural significance in many traditions.

Its unique ecological role makes it an important species in forest ecosystems.
