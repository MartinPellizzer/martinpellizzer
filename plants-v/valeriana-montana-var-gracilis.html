Valeriana montana var. gracilis

Valeriana montana var. gracilis, commonly known as slender mountain valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette, and the stems are slender and somewhat hairy.

Valeriana montana var. gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
