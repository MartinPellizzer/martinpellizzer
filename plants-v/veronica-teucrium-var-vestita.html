Veronica teucrium var. vestita

Veronica teucrium var. vestita, commonly known as the white speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.
