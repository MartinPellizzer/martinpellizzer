Valeriana altoandina

Valeriana altoandina, commonly known as Andean valerian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to cool, temperate climates. Its root system is rich in volatile compounds, which contribute to its use in traditional medicine.
