Vangueria nana

Vangueria nana, commonly known as dwarf vangueria, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of white or gray hairs, which help reduce water loss. Its flowers are small and yellow, often clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, spherical fruits.

Vangueria nana is well adapted to drought conditions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
