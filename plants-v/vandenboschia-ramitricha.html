Vandenboschia ramitricha

Vandenboschia ramitricha, commonly known as the hairy branch cholla, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, branching stems covered in dense, white, hair-like structures that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the rainy season, which are followed by pale blue fruits. It is adapted to survive in extreme drought conditions through its succulent stems and shallow root system.

Vandenboschia ramitricha plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing shelter and food for various desert animals.
