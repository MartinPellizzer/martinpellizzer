Vitex cofassus var. puberula

Vitex cofassus var. puberula, commonly known as the silky flame tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces panicles of showy, violet or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species in tropical and subtropical regions.

The fruit is a dry, two-lobed capsule that releases small, wind-dispersed seeds.
