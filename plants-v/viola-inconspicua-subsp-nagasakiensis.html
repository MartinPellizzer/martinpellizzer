Viola inconspicua subsp. nagasakiensis

Viola inconspicua subsp.

nagasakiensis, commonly known as the Nagasaki violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan. It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces delicate violet flowers with a distinctive yellow center.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a subtle splash of color to its native habitat.

The subspecies is notable for its small size and ornamental value in horticulture.
