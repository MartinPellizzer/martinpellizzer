Viola kitaibeliana var. longiborgneana

Viola kitaibeliana var. longiborgneana, commonly known as the long-blooming viola, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with five petals, often with a distinctive white or yellow throat. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or alpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in garden settings.
