Veronica latifolia var. minor

Veronica latifolia var. minor, commonly known as narrowleaf speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, blue to purple flowers clustered in pairs. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
