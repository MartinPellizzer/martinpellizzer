Verbesina plowmanii

Verbesina plowmanii, commonly known as Plowman's beggar-tick, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes and swamps. The plant features showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is also known for its fibrous stem and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
