Vicia variegata subsp. bornmuelleri

Vicia variegata subsp.

bornmuelleri, commonly known as the variable vetch, is a perennial legume native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with a distinctive variegated pattern, often featuring white or yellow markings.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to reddish-purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
