Vernonia aosteana

Vernonia aosteana, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a tall, perennial herb that typically grows in open grasslands and along riverbanks. The plant produces showy, conical flower heads that range in color from yellow to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia aosteana is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
