Vellozia rosea

Vellozia rosea, commonly known as the roseleaf vellozia, is a striking plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a member of the Velloziaceae family and is characterized by its unique, fleshy, and often colorful leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its leaves are usually arranged in a rosette pattern, with some species displaying a pink or red hue, giving it its common name.

Vellozia rosea is also noted for its ability to store water in its thick, succulent leaves, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
