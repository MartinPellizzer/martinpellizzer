Vicia sativa subsp. cordata

Vicia sativa subsp.

cordata, commonly known as heartleaf vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along roadsides.

As a member of the Fabaceae family, it has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
