Viburnum americanum

Viburnum americanum, commonly known as common arrowwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall, and it produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers in the spring. The plant is characterized by its red, berry-like fruits that persist into winter, providing food for birds and other wildlife. Viburnum americanum typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
