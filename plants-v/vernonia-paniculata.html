Vernonia paniculata

Vernonia paniculata, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Vernonia paniculata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
