Vascoa acuminata

Vascoa acuminata, commonly known as the Brazilian star apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by round, star-shaped fruits with a sweet, milky pulp. The fruit is typically consumed fresh or used in jams and beverages.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.
