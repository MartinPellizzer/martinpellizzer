Vicia gracilis

Vicia gracilis, commonly known as slender vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or violet in color, followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. Vicia gracilis is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils.

It has a shallow root system and is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
