Viola reniformis

Viola reniformis, commonly known as the heartleaf violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch of color to the forest floor.

Viola reniformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
