Verbascum pycnostachyum

Verbascum pycnostachyum, commonly known as the dense-stamen mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, showy yellow flowers. The plant produces numerous stamens that give it a distinctive, fluffy appearance, especially in its inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The leaves are broad and fuzzy, contributing to its common name and aiding in water retention.
