Veronica fruticulosa var. viscosa

Veronica fruticulosa var. viscosa, commonly known as sticky veronica, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit with dense clusters of blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically hairy and glandular, giving the plant a sticky texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
