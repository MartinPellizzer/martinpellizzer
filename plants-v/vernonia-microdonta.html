Vernonia microdonta

Vernonia microdonta, commonly known as the smallleaf ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. Its showy, purple to violet flower heads appear in late summer and are arranged in dense clusters.

Vernonia microdonta is often found in moist woodlands and along streambanks, where it thrives in partial to full shade.
