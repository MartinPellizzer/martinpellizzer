Viola betonicifolia subsp. napaulensis

Viola betonicifolia subsp.

napaulensis, commonly known as the Nepal violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India. It typically grows in moist, shaded woodland environments, often along stream banks and in rocky crevices.

The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive violet or purple hue, and it produces small, delicate flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

The species is also noted for its potential medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
