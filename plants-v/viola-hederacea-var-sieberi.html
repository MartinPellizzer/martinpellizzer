Viola hederacea var. sieberi

Viola hederacea var. sieberi, commonly known as the Sieber's violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, and its flowers are typically violet or blue-purple in color. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both horticultural and ecological studies.
