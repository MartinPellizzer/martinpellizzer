Veronica pseudocapillipes

Veronica pseudocapillipes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved speedwell, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers in clusters, typically blooming from autumn to spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or rocky areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
