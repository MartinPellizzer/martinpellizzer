Vaccinium bracteatum var. glabratum

Vaccinium bracteatum var. glabratum, commonly known as the Florida huckleberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes blueberries and cranberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or black in color, which are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and pine flatwoods.
