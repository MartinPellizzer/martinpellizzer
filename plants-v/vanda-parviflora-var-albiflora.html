Vanda parviflora var. albiflora

Vanda parviflora var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered vanda, is a epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light. It requires well-draining potting media and regular watering to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for both indoor cultivation and botanical gardens.
