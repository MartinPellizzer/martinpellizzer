Veronica longifolia subsp. borealis

Veronica longifolia subsp.

borealis, commonly known as the northern speedwell, is a perennial herb native to arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments.

The plant features small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are often lance-shaped with a hairy texture. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in tundra and mountainous habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators in its native range.
