Viola suavis subsp. glabrescens

Viola suavis subsp.

glabrescens, commonly known as the sweet violet subspecies with glabrous stems, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers with five petals. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery.
