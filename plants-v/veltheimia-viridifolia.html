Veltheimia viridifolia

Veltheimia viridifolia, commonly known as the green-leafed veltheimia, is a striking member of the Arecaceae family, native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, feathery leaves that are a vibrant green, giving it its common name. The plant produces striking white to pale yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in panicles. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Veltheimia viridifolia is often used in landscape design for its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of climates.
