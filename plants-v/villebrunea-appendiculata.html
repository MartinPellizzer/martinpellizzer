Villebrunea appendiculata

Villebrunea appendiculata, commonly known as the Malabar apple, is a tropical tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by fleshy, red or orange berries that are edible. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in evergreen forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
