Vernonia barbinervis

Vernonia barbinervis, commonly known as the hairy ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, especially along the veins. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish to brownish flower heads that are typically surrounded by bracts. Vernonia barbinervis is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.

It thrives in moist to wet habitats and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along riverbanks.
