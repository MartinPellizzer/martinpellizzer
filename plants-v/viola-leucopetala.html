Viola leucopetala

Viola leucopetala, commonly known as the white violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, white or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Viola leucopetala is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
