Vernonia pedunculata

Vernonia pedunculata, commonly known as the slender ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. Its flowers are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

Vernonia pedunculata is often found in moist to wet soils in woodland or swampy areas.
