Viola mysurensis

Viola mysurensis, commonly known as the Persian violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to regions of Central Asia, including parts of Turkey, Iran, and Afghanistan. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with five petals, typically in shades of purple or blue. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Viola mysurensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.
