Vernonia leonensis

Vernonia leonensis, commonly known as the golden ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of golden-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Vernonia leonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
