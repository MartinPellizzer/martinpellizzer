Viscum monoicum

Viscum monoicum, commonly known as the mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees.

It belongs to the family Viscaceae and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, white or yellowish berries. The plant derives its nutrients from the host through specialized structures called haustoria. It plays an important ecological role by providing food for birds and other animals.

Viscum monoicum is often associated with Christmas traditions due to its ornamental appearance and symbolic significance.
