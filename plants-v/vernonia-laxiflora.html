Vernonia laxiflora

Vernonia laxiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense clusters.

Vernonia laxiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
