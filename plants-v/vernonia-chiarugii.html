Vernonia chiarugii

Vernonia chiarugii, commonly known as the golden ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers form dense, panicle-like clusters.

Vernonia chiarugii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
