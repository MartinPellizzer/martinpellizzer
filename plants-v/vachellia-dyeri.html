Vachellia dyeri

Vachellia dyeri, commonly known as the African bristlebush, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in clusters. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are often visited by bees and other pollinators. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to poor soil conditions.

Vachellia dyeri is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of firewood.
