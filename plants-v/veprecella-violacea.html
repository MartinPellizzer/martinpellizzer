Veprecella violacea

Veprecella violacea, commonly known as the violet tamarisk, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and saline environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils.
