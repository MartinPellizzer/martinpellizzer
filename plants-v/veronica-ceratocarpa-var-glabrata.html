Veronica ceratocarpa var. glabrata

Veronica ceratocarpa var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth horned speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks, and it has smooth, lance-shaped leaves.
