Viola chamissoniana

Viola chamissoniana, commonly known as the Chamisson's violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of violet-blue flowers with yellow markings. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate appearance and early spring blooming period.

Viola chamissoniana is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
