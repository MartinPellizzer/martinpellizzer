Vincentia falcata

Vincentia falcata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed climbing vine, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often clustered in racemes. Its fruits are elongated pods that split open to release seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

Vincentia falcata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
