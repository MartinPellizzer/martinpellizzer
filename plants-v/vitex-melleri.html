Vitex melleri

Vitex melleri, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, pentagonal flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, with five petals and a prominent yellow center. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in coastal areas and dry forests.

Vitex melleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in Hawaiian culture.
