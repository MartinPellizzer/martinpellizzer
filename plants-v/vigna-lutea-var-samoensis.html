Vigna lutea var. samoensis

Vigna lutea var. samoensis, commonly known as the Samoan cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, yellow to orange pods containing edible seeds. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its nutritious seeds.

The plant also plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
