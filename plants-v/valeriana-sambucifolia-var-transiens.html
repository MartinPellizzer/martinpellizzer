Valeriana sambucifolia var. transiens

Valeriana sambucifolia var. transiens, commonly known as the Transient Wood Betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features upright stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, adorned with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences, which bloom in the summer months. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological role in supporting biodiversity.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as woodland edges and stream banks.
