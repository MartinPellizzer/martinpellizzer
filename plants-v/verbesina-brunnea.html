Verbesina brunnea

Verbesina brunnea, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late summer, arranged in panicles that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in meadows and open woodlands.

Its showy blooms and ecological value make it a desirable addition to native plant gardens.
