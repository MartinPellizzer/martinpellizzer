Viola cunninghamii

Viola cunninghamii, commonly known as the Cunningham's violet, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Viola cunninghamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
