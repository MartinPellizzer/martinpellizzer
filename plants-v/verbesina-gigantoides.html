Verbesina gigantoides

Verbesina gigantoides, commonly known as the giant goldenrod, is a striking perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 6 feet, adorned with bright yellow inflorescences. The plant features lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Verbesina gigantoides is also noted for its ability to thrive in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.
