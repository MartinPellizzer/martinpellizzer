Viola confusa subsp. nagasakiensis

Viola confusa subsp.

nagasakiensis, commonly known as the Nagasaki violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-blue flowers.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
