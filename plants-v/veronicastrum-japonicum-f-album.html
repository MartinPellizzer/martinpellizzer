Veronicastrum japonicum f. album

Veronicastrum japonicum f. album, commonly known as the white giant hyssop, is a striking perennial plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous pale blue to white flowers. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped, medium-green leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
