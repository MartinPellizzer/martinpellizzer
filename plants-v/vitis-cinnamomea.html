Vitis cinnamomea

Vitis cinnamomea, commonly known as the cinnamon grape, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, palmately lobed leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn red when mature, containing seeds surrounded by a fleshy pulp. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

The species is noted for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
