Vicia grandiflora subsp. biebersteinii

Vicia grandiflora subsp.

biebersteinii, commonly known as the Siberian vetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia. It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, adorned with trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple.

The plant produces slender, hairy pods containing small, kidney-shaped seeds. It is well adapted to sandy and rocky soils and thrives in dry, temperate climates.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
