Verbascum campestre

Verbascum campestre, commonly known as common mullein, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that form a rosette at the base. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in a spike-like inflorescence. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as roadsides, fields, and waste areas.

Verbascum campestre is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its soothing effects on respiratory ailments.
