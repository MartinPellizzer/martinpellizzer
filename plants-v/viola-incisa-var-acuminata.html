Viola incisa var. acuminata

Viola incisa var. acuminata, commonly known as the acute-leaved viola, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped, serrated leaves with a distinctive acuminate tip, and produces delicate, violet-colored flowers with yellow markings. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks. It has a short growing season and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is also valued for its historical use in herbal medicine.
