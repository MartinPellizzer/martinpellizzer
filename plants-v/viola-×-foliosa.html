Viola × foliosa

Viola × foliosa, commonly known as the violetleaf violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often larger than those of its parent species. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple, violet, and white, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

Viola × foliosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
