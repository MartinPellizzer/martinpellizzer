Vicia hajastana

Vicia hajastana, commonly known as the Caucasian vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and slender stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated pods containing several seeds. It is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

Vicia hajastana is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
