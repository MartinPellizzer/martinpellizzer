Viola × maluqueri

Viola × maluqueri, commonly known as the velvetleaf viola, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Viola tricolor and Viola × wittrockiana.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of vibrant, semi-double flowers that come in a variety of colors, including purple, violet, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–15 cm, with heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and containers.

Viola × maluqueri is valued for its compact growth habit and ability to bloom profusely during the spring and early summer.
