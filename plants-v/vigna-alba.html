Vigna alba

Vigna alba, commonly known as white gram, is a leguminous plant native to South Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods containing small, round seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients. Vigna alba is often grown as a crop for both human consumption and animal feed.

It thrives in warm climates and is cultivated for its nutritional value and adaptability to various soil types.
