Viola tricolor var. raiblensis

Viola tricolor var. raiblensis, commonly known as the Raibl's violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, colorful flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant produces violet-blue, yellow, and white petals in a distinctive pattern, making it visually appealing. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, often found in woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
