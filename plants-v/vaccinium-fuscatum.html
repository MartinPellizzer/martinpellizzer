Vaccinium fuscatum

Vaccinium fuscatum, commonly known as the black huckleberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and azaleas. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are oval-shaped, leathery, and typically have a bluish-green hue.

Vaccinium fuscatum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.
