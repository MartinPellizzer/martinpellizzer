Vernonia cordiifolia

Vernonia cordiifolia, commonly known as heartleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
