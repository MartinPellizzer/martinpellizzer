Veronica multifida var. virescens

Veronica multifida var. virescens, commonly known as the green-leaved speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often tinged with silver or white on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in gardens and landscaping.
