Valeriana turkestanica

Valeriana turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkestan valerian, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often reddish-brown roots. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showing a high degree of adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowering stems bear small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer.

Valeriana turkestanica is valued in traditional medicine for its potential sedative and calming properties.
