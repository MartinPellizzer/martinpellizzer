Viola cucullata f. prionosepala

Viola cucullata f. prionosepala, commonly known as the narrow-sepaled violet, is a botanical variant of the common violet.

It is characterized by its distinctive narrow sepals that give it a unique appearance compared to other viola species. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and prefers moist, well-drained soil. Its flowers are usually purple or violet in color, with five petals arranged in a typical violet flower structure.

This ferny-leaved plant is often found in the eastern United States and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
