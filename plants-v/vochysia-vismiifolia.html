Vochysia vismiifolia

Vochysia vismiifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or velvet tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The tree produces edible, cherry-like fruits that are sweet and juicy, often consumed fresh or used in local cuisines. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a velvety texture, contributing to its common name.

Vochysia vismiifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in urban landscapes for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
