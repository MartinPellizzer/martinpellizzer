Valeriana pilosiuscula

Valeriana pilosiuscula, commonly known as the hairy valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine, hairy texture, and its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and also hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Valeriana pilosiuscula is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
