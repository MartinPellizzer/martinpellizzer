Vernonia fragrans

Vernonia fragrans, commonly known as the sweet vervain, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits known as cypselae, which contain the seeds. Vernonia fragrans is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
