Vernonia bakeriana

Vernonia bakeriana, commonly known as the Baker's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy inflorescences. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia bakeriana is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
