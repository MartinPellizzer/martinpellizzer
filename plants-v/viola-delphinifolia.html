Viola delphinifolia

Viola delphinifolia, commonly known as the blue violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland areas, meadows, and along stream banks, preferring moist, well-drained soil. Viola delphinifolia is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the landscape.
