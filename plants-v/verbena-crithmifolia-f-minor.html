Verbena crithmifolia f. minor

Verbena crithmifolia f. minor, commonly known as the lesser sea verbena, is a compact, low-growing plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, aromatic leaves with a distinctive, slightly peppery scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Its horticultural value lies in its ornamental appeal and adaptability to coastal and sandy environments.
