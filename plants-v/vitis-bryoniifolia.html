Vitis bryoniifolia

Vitis bryoniifolia, commonly known as the grapevines of the genus Vitis, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports. The leaves are typically lobed and have a rough texture, while the flowers are small and greenish-white in color. The plant produces small, round berries that are usually dark purple or black when mature.

Vitis bryoniifolia is native to parts of Asia and is often found in woodland or forested areas.
