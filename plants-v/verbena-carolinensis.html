Verbena carolinensis

Verbena carolinensis, commonly known as Carolina verbena, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender stems and opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy. The plant produces spikes of small, tubular flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, which bloom from late spring to early fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and naturalized areas.

Its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies adds ecological value to the landscape.
