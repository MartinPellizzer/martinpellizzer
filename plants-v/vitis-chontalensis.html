Vitis chontalensis

Vitis chontalensis, commonly known as the Chontal grape, is a species of flowering vine native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible berries, which are typically dark purple when mature. Vitis chontalensis is valued for its potential in both traditional use and as a horticultural species.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in plant ecology and agricultural research.
