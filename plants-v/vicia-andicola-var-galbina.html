Vicia andicola var. galbina

Vicia andicola var. galbina, commonly known as the Andean vetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils. It plays a vital role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil with nutrients.

Due to its adaptability and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
