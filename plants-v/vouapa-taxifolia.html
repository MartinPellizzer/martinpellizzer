Vouapa taxifolia

Vouapa taxifolia, commonly known as the lace fern, is a species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that give it a lacy appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually bioluminescent, emitting a faint blue glow at night.

Vouapa taxifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
