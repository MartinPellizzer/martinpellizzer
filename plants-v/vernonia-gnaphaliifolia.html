Vernonia gnaphaliifolia

Vernonia gnaphaliifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils, and it thrives in full sun. Its narrow, lance-shaped leaves are gray-green in color, and it produces small, blue to purple flowers in late summer.

Vernonia gnaphaliifolia is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
