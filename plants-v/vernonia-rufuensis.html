Vernonia rufuensis

Vernonia rufuensis, commonly known as the red vernonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, while its flowers range in color from yellow to orange.

Vernonia rufuensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
