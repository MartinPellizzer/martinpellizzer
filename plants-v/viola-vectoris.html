Viola vectoris

Viola vectoris, commonly known as the common violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy violet flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a common garden species. Viola vectoris is known for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution in temperate regions.
