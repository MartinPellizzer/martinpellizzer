Vernonia senecionea f. calvata

Vernonia senecionea f. calvata, commonly known as the smoothleaf ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically smooth and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy underside. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

This plant is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, contributing to ecological diversity in its habitat.
