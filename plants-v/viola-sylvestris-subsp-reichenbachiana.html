Viola sylvestris subsp. reichenbachiana

Viola sylvestris subsp.

reichenbachiana, commonly known as the Alpine violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy violet flowers with a distinctive white throat. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to withstand cold temperatures.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in high-altitude ecosystems.
