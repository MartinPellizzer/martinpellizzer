Vachellia tortilis subsp. campoptila

Vachellia tortilis subsp.

campoptila, commonly known as the umbrella thorn acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-shaped crown formed by numerous spreading branches.

The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular inflorescences, which are followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of small, feather-like leaflets, and it has a deep root system adapted to drought conditions.

This species is well-known for its ecological and cultural significance in desert ecosystems.
