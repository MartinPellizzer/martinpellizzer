Verbascum anisophyllum

Verbascum anisophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mullein, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
