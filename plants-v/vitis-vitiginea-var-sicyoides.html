Vitis vitiginea var. sicyoides

Vitis vitiginea var. sicyoides, commonly known as the wild grapevine, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems, compound leaves, and small, clustered greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, sour-tasting berries that are often used in local traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found along stream banks and in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and horticultural research.
