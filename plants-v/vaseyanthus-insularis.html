Vaseyanthus insularis

Vaseyanthus insularis, commonly known as the island bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are broad and oval, with a slightly hairy texture.

Vaseyanthus insularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
