Villaresia megaphylla var. obtusifolia

Villaresia megaphylla var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed puya, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, elongated, and often brightly colored leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces striking inflorescences with showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
