Viola nagamiana

Viola nagamiana, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a touch of color to gardens and natural landscapes.

Viola nagamiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
