Viola limbarae

Viola limbarae, commonly known as the Alpine violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and delicate, five-petaled flowers that range in color from violet to white. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils. Viola limbarae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to high-altitude conditions with limited nutrients.
