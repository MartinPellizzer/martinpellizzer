Verbascum isauricum

Verbascum isauricum, commonly known as the Isaurian mullein, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, hairy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, velvety leaves that are gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its common name reflects its historical use as a medicinal plant, particularly for respiratory ailments.
