Vilfa marginata

Vilfa marginata, commonly known as the Margined Vilfa, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or pale margin, giving it its common name. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring and early summer.

Vilfa marginata is often found in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and in woodland edges.
