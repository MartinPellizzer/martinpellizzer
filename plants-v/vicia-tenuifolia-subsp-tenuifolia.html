Vicia tenuifolia subsp. tenuifolia

Vicia tenuifolia subsp.

tenuifolia, commonly known as slender vetch, is a flowering plant species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats.

The plant features slender, climbing stems with trifoliate leaves composed of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are small, pea-like, and usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters along the stems.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
