Viola urophylla var. urophylla

Viola urophylla var. urophylla, commonly known as the purple viola, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow eye in the center. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and shaded areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded borders in horticulture.
