Vicia gigantea

Vicia gigantea, commonly known as giant vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to reddish-brown. Its compound leaves are typically pinnate, with small oval-shaped leaflets.

Vicia gigantea is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.
