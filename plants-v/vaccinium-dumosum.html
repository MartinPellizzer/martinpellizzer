Vaccinium dumosum

Vaccinium dumosum, commonly known as the dwarf blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other acid-loving plants like heathers and cranberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, containing high levels of antioxidants. Its leaves are elliptical, dark green, and slightly hairy, while its stems are woody and often form dense mats.

Vaccinium dumosum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
