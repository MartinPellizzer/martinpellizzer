Verbascum gracilescens

Verbascum gracilescens, commonly known as the slender mullein, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

Verbascum gracilescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal remedies.
