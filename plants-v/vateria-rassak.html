Vateria rassak

Vateria rassak, commonly known as the Rassak tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by fleshy, red berries. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its timber and ornamental qualities.

The plant has a moderate growth rate and can reach heights of up to 20 meters in suitable conditions.
