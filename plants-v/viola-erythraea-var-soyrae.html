Viola erythraea var. soyrae

Viola erythraea var. soyrae, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy violet-blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
