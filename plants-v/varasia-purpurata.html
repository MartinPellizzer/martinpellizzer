Varasia purpurata

Varasia purpurata, commonly known as the purple varasia, is a striking perennial plant native to the temperate regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive reddish-brown stem. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its unique coloration and adaptability, Varasia purpurata is often cultivated for both its aesthetic value and ecological benefits.
