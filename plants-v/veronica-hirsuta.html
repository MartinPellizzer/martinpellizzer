Veronica hirsuta

Veronica hirsuta, commonly known as hairy veronica, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Veronica hirsuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
