Vigna lanceolata var. lanceolata

Vigna lanceolata var. lanceolata, commonly known as the narrowleaf cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in warm, sunny climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.
