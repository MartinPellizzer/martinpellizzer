Vicia assyriaca

Vicia assyriaca, commonly known as the Assyrian vetch, is a flowering plant native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, hard pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Vicia assyriaca is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along rocky outcrops.
