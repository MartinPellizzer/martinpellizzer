Verbascum orientale subsp. orientale

Verbascum orientale subsp.

orientale, commonly known as the oriental mullein, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy perennial in temperate climates.

Its fibrous stems and leaves have been historically used for their soothing properties in herbal remedies.
