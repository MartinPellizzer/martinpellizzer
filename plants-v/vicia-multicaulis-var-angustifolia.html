Vicia multicaulis var. angustifolia

Vicia multicaulis var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The leaves are pinnately compound, with several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets. This variety is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it valuable for soil enrichment.
