Viola kusanoana f. alba

Viola kusanoana f. alba, commonly known as the white viola, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodland areas or along stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in garden settings for its aesthetic appeal.
