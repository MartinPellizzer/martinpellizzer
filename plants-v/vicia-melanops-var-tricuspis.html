Vicia melanops var. tricuspis

Vicia melanops var. tricuspis, commonly known as the three-lobed vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features trifoliate leaves with three deeply lobed leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in early spring. Its trifoliate structure and flowering habit are key botanical characteristics.

It is often found in rocky or disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
