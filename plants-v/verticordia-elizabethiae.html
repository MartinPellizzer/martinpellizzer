Verticordia elizabethiae

Verticordia elizabethiae, commonly known as Elizabeth's featherwood, is a striking member of the Myrtaceae family native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, feather-like floral structures that give it its common name, and it typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers during the summer months, attracting various pollinators. It is a shrub that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
