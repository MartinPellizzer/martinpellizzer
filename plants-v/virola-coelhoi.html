Virola coelhoi

Virola coelhoi, commonly known as the Amazonian ironwood, is a large evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its thick, dark bark and dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the early morning. Its leaves are ovate and glossy, and it bears small, round fruits that contain toxic seeds.

Virola coelhoi plays an important ecological role in the rainforest by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
