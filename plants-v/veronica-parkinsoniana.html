Veronica parkinsoniana

Veronica parkinsoniana, commonly known as the pink veronica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Veronica parkinsoniana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
