Voacanga africana

Voacanga africana, commonly known as the African arrowroot or African quinine tree, is a tropical plant native to West Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with smooth, grayish bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing numerous black, hard seeds. Its roots and bark contain alkaloids, making it significant in traditional medicine and pharmacology.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in its native habitat.
