Viola × murbeckii

Viola × murbeckii, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Viola lutea and Viola tricolor, characterized by its vibrant purple, yellow, and white flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 15–30 cm, with heart-shaped leaves that are pubescent beneath. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas across Europe.

Viola × murbeckii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its colorful blooms.
