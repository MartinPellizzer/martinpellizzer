Vernonia achyrocoma

Vernonia achyrocoma, commonly known as the silverleaf vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which helps reduce water loss in its dry habitat. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in dense, conical clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy environments.

Vernonia achyrocoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
