Viola grypoceras var. exilis

Viola grypoceras var. exilis, commonly known as the slender violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, heart-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically violet or purple, with five petals and a distinctive spur. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
