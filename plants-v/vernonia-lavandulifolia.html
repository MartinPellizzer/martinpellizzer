Vernonia lavandulifolia

Vernonia lavandulifolia, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert and chaparral habitats. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and have a strong, aromatic scent reminiscent of lavender.
