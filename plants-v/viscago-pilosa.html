Viscago pilosa

Viscago pilosa, commonly known as the hairy dog-violet, is a flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

Viscago pilosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
