Veronica pseudolongifolia subsp. sachalinensis

Veronica pseudolongifolia subsp.

sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin speedwell, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features small, blue to violet flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early to mid-summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
