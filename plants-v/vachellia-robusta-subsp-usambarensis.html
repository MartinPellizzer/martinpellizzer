Vachellia robusta subsp. usambarensis

Vachellia robusta subsp.

usambarensis, commonly known as the Usambarensis Acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa. It is characterized by its straight, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves that are dense and feathery.

The plant produces yellow flowers in compact inflorescences, which are followed by elongated, flat pods containing seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role and has been used traditionally for various purposes.
