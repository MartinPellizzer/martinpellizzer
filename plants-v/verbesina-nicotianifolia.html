Verbesina nicotianifolia

Verbesina nicotianifolia, commonly known as the tobacco leaf false beggarticks, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that resemble tobacco leaves, hence its common name. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, along stream banks, and in disturbed areas.

Verbesina nicotianifolia is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
