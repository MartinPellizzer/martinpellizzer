Viola saccata

Viola saccata, commonly known as the pouch violet, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and small, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple and blue. The plant belongs to the Violaceae family and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks. Viola saccata is an herbaceous perennial that produces capsule fruits containing several seeds.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
