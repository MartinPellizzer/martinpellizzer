Vaccinium lanceolatum f. marapiense

Vaccinium lanceolatum f. marapiense, commonly known as the Marapi blueberry, is a botanical variant of the blueberry plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, typically blue or purple berries. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers a humid, temperate climate. It is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits.

This form is notable for its unique coloration and adaptability to specific ecological conditions.
