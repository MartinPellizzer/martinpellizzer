Veronica cana var. miqueliana

Veronica cana var. miqueliana, commonly known as the Miquel's speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite pairs of lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces clusters of blue-violet flowers in early spring, which are borne on short pedicels. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
