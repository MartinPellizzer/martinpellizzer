Vincetoxicum sibiricum var. australe

Vincetoxicum sibiricum var. australe, commonly known as the Siberian bindweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its vigorous growth and creeping habit, often forming dense mats. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is commonly found in woodland areas.

Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.
