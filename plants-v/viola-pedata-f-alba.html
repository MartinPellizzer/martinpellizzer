Viola pedata f. alba

Viola pedata f. alba, commonly known as the white viola, is a flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, white or pale green leaves and produces showy, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive pattern of dark veins. Native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it forms dense colonies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its beauty in gardens and botanical collections.
