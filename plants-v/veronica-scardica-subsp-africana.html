Veronica scardica subsp. africana

Veronica scardica subsp.

africana, commonly known as the African speedwell, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves.

The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in racemes, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine in some regions.
