Vernonia poskeana var. centauroides

Vernonia poskeana var. centauroides, commonly known as the Centaur's Crown, is a striking herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features showy, spiky purple bracts that resemble a crown, giving it its common name. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
