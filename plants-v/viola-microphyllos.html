Viola microphyllos

Viola microphyllos, commonly known as the narrow-leaved violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in woodland and grassland habitats. The plant's flowers are typically less than 2 inches in diameter and have five petals.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
