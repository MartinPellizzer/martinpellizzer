Vilfa adusta

Vilfa adusta, commonly known as the crimson spire, is a striking flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem that can reach up to three meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, vibrant red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, which helps reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Vilfa adusta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is considered a symbol of resilience in botanical gardens.
