Viola longifolia

Viola longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces delicate, violet-blue flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola longifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in native plant gardens.
