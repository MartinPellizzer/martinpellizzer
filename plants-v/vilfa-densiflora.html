Vilfa densiflora

Vilfa densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered vilfa, is a herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It features a basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
