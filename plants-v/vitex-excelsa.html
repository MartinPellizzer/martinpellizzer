Vitex excelsa

Vitex excelsa, commonly known as the sacred fig or bodhi tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing multiple seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and cultural significance.

The tree is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
