Veronica graminea

Veronica graminea, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the plantain family, Plantaginaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and spikes of blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.

Veronica graminea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
