Vidoricum fulvum

Vidoricum fulvum, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange and yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, well-drained environments. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture.

Vidoricum fulvum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
