Viola × haynaldii

Viola × haynaldii, commonly known as the haynaldii violet, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Viola tricolor and Viola verecunda.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that typically range in color from purple to white. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Viola × haynaldii is also notable for its adaptability and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
