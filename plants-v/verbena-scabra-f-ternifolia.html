Verbena scabra f. ternifolia

Verbena scabra f. ternifolia, commonly known as the three-leaved verbena, is a compact, flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in threes, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom throughout the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
