Virea autumnalis var. laciniata

Virea autumnalis var. laciniata, commonly known as the cutleaf autumn vireya, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The leaves are typically lobed or deeply cut, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
