Viguiera nudicaulis

Viguiera nudicaulis, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has gray-green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.

Viguiera nudicaulis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
