Verbena erinacea

Verbena erinacea, commonly known as the whorled verbena, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit. The plant produces showy clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, narrow, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Verbena erinacea thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
