Vernonia albifila

Vernonia albifila, commonly known as the whiteflower ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy, and it produces small, achene fruits. Vernonia albifila typically blooms from late summer to early fall, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for native plant gardens.
