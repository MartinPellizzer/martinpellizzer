Valeriana excelsa subsp. salina

Valeriana excelsa subsp.

salina, commonly known as the salt-valerian, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and clusters of small, white, tubular flowers.

The plant thrives in saline soils and is adapted to coastal environments with high salt exposure. Its root system is often used in traditional medicine for its sedative properties.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and supporting local biodiversity.
