Verbascum stelurum

Verbascum stelurum, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that are covered in soft, white hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a loose, spire-like inflorescence. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Verbascum stelurum is a perennial species that is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators.
