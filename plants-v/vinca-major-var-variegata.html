Vinca major var. variegata

Vinca major var. variegata, commonly known as the Variegated Greater Periwinkle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with striking white or yellow variegation. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often used in ground cover due to its dense foliage and ornamental appeal.

Vinca major var. variegata is also valued for its ability to suppress weeds and its tolerance to various soil conditions.
