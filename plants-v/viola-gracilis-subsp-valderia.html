Viola gracilis subsp. valderia

Viola gracilis subsp.

valderia, commonly known as the slender violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of South America. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and woodland edges.

The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are violet-blue, with five petals and a distinctive yellow keel.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
