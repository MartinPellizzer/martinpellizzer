Vernonia oligophylla var. autumnalis

Vernonia oligophylla var. autumnalis, commonly known as autumn vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow to orange flower heads, which are typically visited by bees and other pollinators. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract beneficial insects in garden settings.
