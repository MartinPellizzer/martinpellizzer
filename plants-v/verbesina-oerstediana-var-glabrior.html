Verbesina oerstediana var. glabrior

Verbesina oerstediana var. glabrior, commonly known as the smooth coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small cones. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with prominent central disks.

This variety is distinguished by its smoother, less hairy appearance compared to other varieties of Verbesina oerstediana.
