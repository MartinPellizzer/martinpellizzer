Verbascum orgyale

Verbascum orgyale, commonly known as the Tibetan mullein, is a perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It is adapted to cold and arid environments, often growing in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been traditionally used in Tibetan medicine.
