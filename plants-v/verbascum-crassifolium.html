Verbascum crassifolium

Verbascum crassifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf mullein, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, spoon-shaped leaves that are broad and velvety. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a tall, terminal spike. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Verbascum crassifolium is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for respiratory ailments.
