Verbesina perlanata

Verbesina perlanata, commonly known as the golden coneflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has hairy, branching stems and heart-shaped, serrated leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
