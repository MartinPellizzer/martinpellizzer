Veronica arvensis

Veronica arvensis, commonly known as field speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, blue or violet flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Veronica arvensis thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
