Veronica schmidtiana subsp. yezo-alpina

Veronica schmidtiana subsp.

yezo-alpina, commonly known as the Yezo alpine speedwell, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun.

The plant features slender, upright stems that bear small, blue to violet flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall delicate appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
