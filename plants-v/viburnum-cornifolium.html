Viburnum cornifolium

Viburnum cornifolium, commonly known as the horned elder or cornleaf viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring dense, upright branches and ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. Viburnum cornifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.

It prefers partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to a variety of garden conditions.
