Vernonia albicaulis

Vernonia albicaulis, commonly known as white-stemmed ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Vernonia albicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
