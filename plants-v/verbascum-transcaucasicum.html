Verbascum transcaucasicum

Verbascum transcaucasicum, commonly known as the Caucasian mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in dense, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are large, lance-shaped, and also covered in a thick layer of soft down.

Verbascum transcaucasicum is often found in rocky or dry habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
