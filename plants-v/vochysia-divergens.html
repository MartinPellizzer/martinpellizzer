Vochysia divergens

Vochysia divergens, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pink trumpet tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, pink or red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The tree has smooth, grey bark and large, palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Vochysia divergens thrives in warm, humid climates and is adaptable to various soil types.
