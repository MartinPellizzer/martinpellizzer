Vaccinium albicans var. pubens

Vaccinium albicans var. pubens, commonly known as the pubescent blueberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces clusters of white to pale pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, acidic berries in the summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its culinary uses.
