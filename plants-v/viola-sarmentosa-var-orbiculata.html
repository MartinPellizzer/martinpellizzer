Viola sarmentosa var. orbiculata

Viola sarmentosa var. orbiculata, commonly known as the creeping violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. The flowers are typically violet or purple, with five petals and a distinctive yellow center. It grows low to the ground, often forming dense mats, and thrives in moist, shady environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
