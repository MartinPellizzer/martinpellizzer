Varronia riparia

Varronia riparia, commonly known as the swamp varroa or swamp senna, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, sandy soils near water bodies. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture. Varronia riparia is known for its ability to thrive in swampy environments, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
