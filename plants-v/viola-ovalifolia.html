Viola ovalifolia

Viola ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf violet, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, ovate leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola ovalifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
