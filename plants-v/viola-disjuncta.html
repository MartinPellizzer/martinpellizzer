Viola disjuncta

Viola disjuncta, commonly known as the Disjunct Violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are usually purple or violet in color, with five petals and a distinctive spur.

Viola disjuncta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
