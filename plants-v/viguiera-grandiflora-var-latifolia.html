Viguiera grandiflora var. latifolia

Viguiera grandiflora var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open woodlands and prairies. The plant features upright stems covered in grayish-green, hairy foliage. Its showy, pinkish-purple flower heads appear in late summer and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
