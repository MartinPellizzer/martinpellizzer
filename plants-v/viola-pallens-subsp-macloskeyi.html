Viola pallens subsp. macloskeyi

Viola pallens subsp.

macloskeyi, commonly known as the pale violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive pale green coloration and produces small, delicate flowers that range from white to pale purple.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a subtle beauty to the forest floor.

The subspecies is characterized by its reduced corolla and shorter pedicels compared to other viola species.
