Vaccinium adenotrichum

Vaccinium adenotrichum, commonly known as the creeping blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant features small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a glossy surface and a slightly waxy texture.

Vaccinium adenotrichum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.
