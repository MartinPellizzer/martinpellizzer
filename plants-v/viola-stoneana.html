Viola stoneana

Viola stoneana, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in cold, high-altitude environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of violet or white. Viola stoneana is known for its hardiness and ability to withstand harsh weather conditions.

It is often cultivated in alpine gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
