Valeriana lucida

Valeriana lucida, commonly known as shining valerian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, small white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. Its root system is fibrous and contains compounds responsible for its medicinal properties.

Valeriana lucida thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
