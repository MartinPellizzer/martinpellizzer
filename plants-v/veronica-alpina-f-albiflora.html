Veronica alpina f. albiflora

Veronica alpina f. albiflora, commonly known as the alpine speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white or grayish down, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to withstand harsh weather conditions.
