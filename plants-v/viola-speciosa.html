Viola speciosa

Viola speciosa, commonly known as the showy violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained, loamy soil. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola speciosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
