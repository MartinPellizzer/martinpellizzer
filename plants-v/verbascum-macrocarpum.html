Verbascum macrocarpum

Verbascum macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-seeded mullein, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a terminal spike. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Verbascum macrocarpum is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for respiratory ailments.
