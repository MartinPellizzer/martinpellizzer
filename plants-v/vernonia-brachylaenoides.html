Vernonia brachylaenoides

Vernonia brachylaenoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands. The plant features tall, slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, clustered purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Vernonia brachylaenoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
