Viola cucullata var. thurstonii

Viola cucullata var. thurstonii, commonly known as the Thurston's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded habitats and is often found in rich, well-drained soils. It produces capsule fruits that contain several small, dark seeds.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
