Viola phalacrocarpa f. subpubescens

Viola phalacrocarpa f. subpubescens, commonly known as the hairy violet, is a botanical variant of the common violet.

It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent leaves and stems, which distinguish it from other forms of the species. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
