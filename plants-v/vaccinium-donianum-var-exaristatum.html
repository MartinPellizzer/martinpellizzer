Vaccinium donianum var. exaristatum

Vaccinium donianum var. exaristatum, commonly known as the rough blueberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a rough, serrated texture. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are typically harvested in the fall. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and potential use in local ecosystems.
