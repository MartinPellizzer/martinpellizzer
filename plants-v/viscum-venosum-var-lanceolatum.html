Viscum venosum var. lanceolatum

Viscum venosum var. lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceolate mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in the eastern United States.

It has a distinctive appearance with lance-shaped, evergreen leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered together. Its berries are red and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in supporting bird populations and contributing to forest biodiversity.
