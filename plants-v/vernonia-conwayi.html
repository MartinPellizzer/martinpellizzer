Vernonia conwayi

Vernonia conwayi, commonly known as Conway's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are pale blue to purple in color.

Vernonia conwayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
