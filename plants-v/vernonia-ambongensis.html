Vernonia ambongensis

Vernonia ambongensis, commonly known as the East African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested or grassland habitats. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom during the dry season. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Vernonia ambongensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
