Vicia sativa subsp. heterophylla

Vicia sativa subsp.

heterophylla, commonly known as the variable vetch, is a leguminous plant characterized by its variable leaf morphology. It typically features trifoliate leaves with a range of leaflet shapes and sizes, which can vary depending on environmental conditions.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of climates.

This subspecies is valued for its adaptability and potential use in ecological restoration and as a forage crop.
