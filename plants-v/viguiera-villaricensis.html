Viguiera villaricensis

Viguiera villaricensis, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and dense clusters of small, purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation across its native habitat.

Viguiera villaricensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
