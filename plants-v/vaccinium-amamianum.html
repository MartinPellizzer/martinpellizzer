Vaccinium amamianum

Vaccinium amamianum, commonly known as the Amamian huckleberry, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark blue or black in color. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in local cuisines.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas with high humidity.
