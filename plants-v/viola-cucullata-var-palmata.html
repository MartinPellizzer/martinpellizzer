Viola cucullata var. palmata

Viola cucullata var. palmata, commonly known as the palmate violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces violet-blue or purple flowers with five petals, typically in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Violaceae family.
