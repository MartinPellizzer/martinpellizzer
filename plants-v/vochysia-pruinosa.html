Vochysia pruinosa

Vochysia pruinosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvet tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy bark and large, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Vochysia pruinosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
