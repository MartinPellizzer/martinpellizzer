Vernonia micrantha

Vernonia micrantha, commonly known as the smallflower ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its small, yellowish flowers are arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, dry fruits called cypsela.

Vernonia micrantha is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
