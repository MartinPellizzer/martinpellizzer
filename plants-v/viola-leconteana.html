Viola leconteana

Viola leconteana, commonly known as Leconte's violet, is a species of flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained soil. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the forest floor.

Viola leconteana is often found in association with other woodland flora, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
