Verbesina carangolensis

Verbesina carangolensis, commonly known as the Carangolensis ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and riverbanks. The plant features upright stems covered in stiff, bristly hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. Its showy yellow-orange flowers appear in late summer, forming dense, clustered inflorescences.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
