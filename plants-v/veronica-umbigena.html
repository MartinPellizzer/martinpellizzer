Veronica umbigena

Veronica umbigena, commonly known as the purple veronica, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring and summer, producing violet-blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Veronica umbigena is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
