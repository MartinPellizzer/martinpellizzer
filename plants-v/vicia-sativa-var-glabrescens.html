Vicia sativa var. glabrescens

Vicia sativa var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth vetch, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet. The leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval and slightly hairy, though the variety glabrescens is noted for its smoother texture. This plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a useful plant in soil enrichment.
