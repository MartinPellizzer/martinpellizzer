Viola arvensis subsp. caespitosa

Viola arvensis subsp.

caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, violet-blue flowers with five petals. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its compact growth habit and clustered flower arrangement.
