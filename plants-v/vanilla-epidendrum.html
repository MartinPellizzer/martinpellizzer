Vanilla epidendrum

Vanilla epidendrum, commonly known as the orchid vanilla, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that resemble those of the vanilla orchid. The plant produces pseudobulbs and has strap-like leaves that help it thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a distinctive, intricate structure that facilitates pollination by specific bees.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
