Verticordia mirabilis

Verticordia mirabilis, commonly known as the golden wattle of the Swan Coastal Plain, is a striking evergreen shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a popular species in native gardens and conservation efforts.
