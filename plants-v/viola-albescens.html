Viola albescens

Viola albescens, commonly known as the white violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, white or pale violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Viola albescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
