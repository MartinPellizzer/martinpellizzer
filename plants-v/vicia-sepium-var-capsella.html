Vicia sepium var. capsella

Vicia sepium var. capsella, commonly known as the common burr vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by distinctive, spiny pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in waste areas.

This species is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil enrichment.
