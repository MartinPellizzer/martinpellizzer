Viola kitamiana

Viola kitamiana, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a flowering plant native to Japan and parts of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing shades of purple, violet, and white. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or shaded gardens.

Viola kitamiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
