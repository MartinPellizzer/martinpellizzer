Vitex ilicifolia

Vitex ilicifolia, commonly known as the fragrant verbena or Australian lilac, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of purple, pink, or white, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Vitex ilicifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
