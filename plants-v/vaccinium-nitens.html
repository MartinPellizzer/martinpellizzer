Vaccinium nitens

Vaccinium nitens, commonly known as the dwarf blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically dark blue to black in color, with a tart flavor. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are oval-shaped, glossy, and remain green throughout the year.

Vaccinium nitens is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its compact size and attractive appearance.
