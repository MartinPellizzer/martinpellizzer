Viola sieboldii f. leucantha

Viola sieboldii f. leucantha, commonly known as the white viola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and forest floors. Its foliage is often covered in a dense layer of fine, soft hairs, which helps to retain moisture.

The flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
