Vitis vinifera var. nageliana

Vitis vinifera var. nageliana, commonly known as Nagel's grape, is a rare and ancient grapevine species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its small, dark berries and robust, thorny vines that thrive in temperate climates. The plant is valued for its unique flavor profile and is often used in traditional winemaking. Its leaves are lobed and have a distinctive, velvety texture.

Due to its limited distribution and genetic uniqueness, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
