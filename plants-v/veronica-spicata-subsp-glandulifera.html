Veronica spicata subsp. glandulifera

Veronica spicata subsp.

glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular spike speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of small, blue to purple flowers arranged in spikes.

The leaves are lance-shaped, with glandular dots that give the plant its subspecies name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
