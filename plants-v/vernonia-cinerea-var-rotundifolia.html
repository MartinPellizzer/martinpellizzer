Vernonia cinerea var. rotundifolia

Vernonia cinerea var. rotundifolia, commonly known as silky ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy purple-pink flower heads that appear in late summer. The plant has rough, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a waxy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it an important plant for ecological diversity.
