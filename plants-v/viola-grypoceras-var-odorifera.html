Viola grypoceras var. odorifera

Viola grypoceras var. odorifera, commonly known as the fragrant violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of violet-blue flowers with yellow centers, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
