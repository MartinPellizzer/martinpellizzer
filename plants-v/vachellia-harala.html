Vachellia harala

Vachellia harala, commonly known as the Harala thornbush, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its straight, often thorned branches and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important component of local ecosystems.

Vachellia harala is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of fodder for livestock.
