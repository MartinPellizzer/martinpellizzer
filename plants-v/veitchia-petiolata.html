Veitchia petiolata

Veitchia petiolata, commonly known as the giant cycad, is a striking evergreen plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its massive, trunk-like stem and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces cycad-like cones, with male and female cones typically appearing on separate plants. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
