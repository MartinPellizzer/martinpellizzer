Verbascum juruk

Verbascum juruk, commonly known as the Armenian mullein, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and steppe environments.

Verbascum juruk is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
