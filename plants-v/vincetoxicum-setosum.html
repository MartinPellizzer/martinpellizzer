Vincetoxicum setosum

Vincetoxicum setosum, commonly known as the hairy false bindweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring erect stems covered in fine, hairy textures. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough, serrated edge, while its roots are fibrous and can spread aggressively.

Vincetoxicum setosum is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, making it a common weed in temperate regions.
