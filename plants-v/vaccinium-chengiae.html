Vaccinium chengiae

Vaccinium chengiae, commonly known as the Cheng's blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark blue berries that are edible. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a glossy surface and are evergreen throughout the year.

Vaccinium chengiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential culinary uses.
