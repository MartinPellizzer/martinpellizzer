Vetiveria filipes var. arundinacea

Vetiveria filipes var. arundinacea, commonly known as giant bamboo or Chinese bamboo, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can grow up to 10 meters in height, with long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions, making it adaptable to different environments. Its rhizomes are fibrous and have been traditionally used for making mats, baskets, and other woven products.

The species is also valued for its ecological benefits, including soil stabilization and erosion control.
