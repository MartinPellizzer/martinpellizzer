Viola pinnata var. dissecta

Viola pinnata var. dissecta, commonly known as the dissected violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of purple, violet, and white. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
