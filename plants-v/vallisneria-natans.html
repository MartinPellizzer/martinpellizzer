Vallisneria natans

Vallisneria natans, commonly known as waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments across Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

It features long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically 30 to 100 centimeters in length, arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, with male flowers emerging above the water surface. Vallisneria natans plays a crucial role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen for various aquatic organisms.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense mats make it both a valuable aquatic vegetation and a potential nuisance in some water bodies.
