Viola × notabilis

Viola × notabilis, commonly known as the heartleaf viola, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to Europe and has been widely cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Viola × notabilis is often used in gardens for its vibrant color and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
