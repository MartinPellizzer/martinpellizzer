Veronica alternifolia

Veronica alternifolia, commonly known as the alternateleaf speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and small, sky-blue or white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are arranged in dense, terminal spikes, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Veronica alternifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
