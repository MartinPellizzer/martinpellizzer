Vernonia praealta var. hebecarpa

Vernonia praealta var. hebecarpa, commonly known as the tall ironweed, is a striking tall annual herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems that can reach heights of up to 6 feet. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flower heads that are clustered in loose, open arrays at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with serrated edges, and are covered in fine, hairy textures.

This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.
