Viola sepincola subsp. pontica

Viola sepincola subsp.

pontica, commonly known as the Pontic violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces delicate violet flowers with a distinct fragrance. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its short, upright stem.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly larger flowers and broader leaves compared to other viola species.
