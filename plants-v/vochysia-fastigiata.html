Vochysia fastigiata

Vochysia fastigiata, commonly known as the velvetwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and distinctive, clustered inflorescences that produce small, fragrant white flowers. The plant has a compact, columnar growth habit, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a leathery texture, while its bark is smooth and often has a reddish or brownish hue.

Vochysia fastigiata is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
