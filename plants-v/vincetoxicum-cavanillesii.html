Vincetoxicum cavanillesii

Vincetoxicum cavanillesii, commonly known as the purple false bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are hairy and often have a reddish tinge, while its roots are fibrous and deep-reaching.

Vincetoxicum cavanillesii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and blooms.
