Vigna pygmaea

Vigna pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf bean or small cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, climbing vines and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several edible seeds. Vigna pygmaea is well adapted to warm climates and can thrive in poor soils.

It is often cultivated for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein and essential nutrients.
