Viola nephrophylla f. albinea

Viola nephrophylla f. albinea, commonly known as the white viola, is a rare and striking variant of the viola genus.

It is characterized by its pale or white flowers, which contrast with the typical green and purple hues of its parent species. This plant is native to specific regions in the western United States, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
