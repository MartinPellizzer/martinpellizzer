Valerianoides commutata

Valerianoides commutata, commonly known as the Commutated Valerian, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the family Valerianaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its root system is often used in traditional medicine for its purported calming properties.

Valerianoides commutata is valued both for its ecological role and its potential medicinal applications.
