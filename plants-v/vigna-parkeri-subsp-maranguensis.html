Vigna parkeri subsp. maranguensis

Vigna parkeri subsp.

maranguensis, commonly known as the Maranguensis cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets.

The plant produces cylindrical, pale yellow pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein.

This subspecies is valued for its adaptability to various climatic conditions and its role in sustainable agriculture.
