Viola calcarata var. flava

Viola calcarata var. flava, commonly known as the yellow pansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves and vibrant yellow flowers with a distinctive dark center. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a member of the Violaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers bloom from spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
