Vitex trichantha

Vitex trichantha, commonly known as the African blueberry or wild coffee, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy blue or violet flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when mature, containing a single seed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Vitex trichantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of dye.
