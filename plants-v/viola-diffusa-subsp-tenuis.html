Viola diffusa subsp. tenuis

Viola diffusa subsp.

tenuis, commonly known as the slender violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple or violet. Its flowering period occurs in early spring, and it is often found in calcareous soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
