Verbesina macrantha

Verbesina macrantha, commonly known as the showy beggar-ticks, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features hairy, branching stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges and are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and streambanks.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
