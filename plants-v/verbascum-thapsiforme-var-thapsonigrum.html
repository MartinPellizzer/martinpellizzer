Verbascum thapsiforme var. thapsonigrum

Verbascum thapsiforme var. thapsonigrum, commonly known as the black-hairy mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in dense, dark-colored hairs. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and covered in a thick layer of soft, fuzzy hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and ornamental addition to gardens.
