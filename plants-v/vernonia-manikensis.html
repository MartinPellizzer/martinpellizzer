Vernonia manikensis

Vernonia manikensis, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. Its botanical structure includes a robust root system and fibrous stems, which help it thrive in various soil conditions.

The flowers are primarily visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
