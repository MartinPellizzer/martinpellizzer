Viola yedoensis f. sonoharae

Viola yedoensis f. sonoharae, commonly known as the Sonohara's violet, is a rare and elegant flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and small, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple and white. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other small insects, contributing to its ecological significance.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
