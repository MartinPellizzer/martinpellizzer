Viola sylvestris f. imberbis

Viola sylvestris f. imberbis, commonly known as the hairless woods violet, is a botanical variant of the common violet.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless leaves and stems, distinguishing it from the typical form of Viola sylvestris. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowers are usually violet-blue in color, with a distinctive heart-shaped mark on the lower petal.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
