Viola hirta var. foudrasii

Viola hirta var. foudrasii, commonly known as the Algerian violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly rough texture. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that range in color from violet to white, often with a distinctive yellow throat. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This variant is distinguished by its compact growth habit and unique floral characteristics compared to other viola species.
