Vernonia glandulicincta

Vernonia glandulicincta, commonly known as the glandular-veined ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, yellow to orange flower heads. The plant features glandular hairs on its stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance and may play a role in its ecological interactions. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes, and is often found in the understory of hardwood forests.

The genus Vernonia is known for its diverse species, many of which are valued for their ornamental and ecological benefits.
