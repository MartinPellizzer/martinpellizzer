Viola forskaalii

Viola forskaalii, commonly known as the Ethiopian violet, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia and parts of Eritrea.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Viola forskaalii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
