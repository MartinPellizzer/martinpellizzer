Verbascum × ignescens

Verbascum × ignescens, commonly known as the flame poppy, is a hybrid species within the genus Verbascum, native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are tubular and clustered in dense, showy spikes, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Verbascum × ignescens is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and heat tolerance.
