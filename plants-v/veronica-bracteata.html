Veronica bracteata

Veronica bracteata, commonly known as the blueweed or narrow-leaved speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Veronica bracteata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
