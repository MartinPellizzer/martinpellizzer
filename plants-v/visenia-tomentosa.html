Visenia tomentosa

Visenia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly elder, is a deciduous tree native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, grayish-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its bark is thick and deeply furrowed, providing structural support and protection.

Visenia tomentosa is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
