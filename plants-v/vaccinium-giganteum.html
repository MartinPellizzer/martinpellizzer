Vaccinium giganteum

Vaccinium giganteum, commonly known as the giant huckleberry, is a large evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and produces clusters of small, edible berries that range in color from red to black. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Vaccinium giganteum plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity.
