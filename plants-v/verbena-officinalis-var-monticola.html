Verbena officinalis var. monticola

Verbena officinalis var. monticola, commonly known as mountain verbena, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that are often branched and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped, oppositely arranged, and have a slightly rough texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
