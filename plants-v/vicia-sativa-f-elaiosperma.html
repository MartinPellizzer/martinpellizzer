Vicia sativa f. elaiosperma

Vicia sativa f. elaiosperma, commonly known as the fat-seeded vetch, is a perennial legume characterized by its robust growth and distinctive seed pods.

It features trifoliate leaves with a hairy texture and produces yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is notable for its large, round seeds that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source for livestock. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adaptable to various climates, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.

Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil enhances its ecological and agricultural significance.
