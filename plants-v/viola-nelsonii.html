Viola nelsonii

Viola nelsonii, commonly known as Nelson's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola nelsonii is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in native plant gardens.
