Viola alajensis

Viola alajensis, commonly known as the Alaj Valley violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a touch of color to the mountainous landscapes.

Viola alajensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
