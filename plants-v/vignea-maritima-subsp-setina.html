Vignea maritima subsp. setina

Vignea maritima subsp.

setina, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the early morning.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in sandy, coastal soils and is adapted to salt spray and wind.
