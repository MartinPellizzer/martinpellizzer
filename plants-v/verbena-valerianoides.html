Verbena valerianoides

Verbena valerianoides, commonly known as creeping verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that produces showy spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant features small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is drought-tolerant and prefers full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
