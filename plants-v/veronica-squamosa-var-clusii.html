Veronica squamosa var. clusii

Veronica squamosa var. clusii, commonly known as the Clusii speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems covered in small, scale-like structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, blue or violet flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
