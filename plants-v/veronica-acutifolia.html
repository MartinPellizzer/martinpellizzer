Veronica acutifolia

Veronica acutifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb with slender, upright stems and small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens and borders.

Its delicate appearance and ease of cultivation make it a popular choice among gardeners.
