Viola × nephrophylloides

Viola × nephrophylloides, commonly known as the purple heart viola, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant violet to purple flowers, which typically bloom in early spring. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a compact growth habit. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental garden plant.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a parent in the development of many modern viola hybrids.
