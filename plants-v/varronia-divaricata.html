Varronia divaricata

Varronia divaricata, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape and have a glossy, dark green surface. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, cylindrical fruits.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in traditional medicine.
