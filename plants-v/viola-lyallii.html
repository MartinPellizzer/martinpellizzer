Viola lyallii

Viola lyallii, commonly known as the Lyall's violet, is a species of flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky areas. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces violet or purple flowers with a distinctive, spurred petal. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate appearance and adaptability to cool climates.

Viola lyallii plays an important role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
