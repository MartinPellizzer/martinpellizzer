Verbesina stricta

Verbesina stricta, commonly known as stiff coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Verbesina stricta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also a source of nectar for pollinators.
