Vignea macrostylos

Vignea macrostylos, commonly known as the large-styled vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Vignea macrostylos thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and requires support for its climbing growth habit.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
