Vochysia tucanorum var. elongata

Vochysia tucanorum var. elongata, commonly known as the long-stemmed velvet tree, is a species native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender stems and dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth, waxy surface.

The flowers are small and clustered, often producing fragrant, yellowish blooms that attract various pollinators.
