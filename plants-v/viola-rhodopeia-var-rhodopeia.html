Viola rhodopeia var. rhodopeia

Viola rhodopeia var. rhodopeia, commonly known as the Bulgarian violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Rhodope Mountains in Bulgaria.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces delicate, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow eye in the center. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
