Volvulus sepium

Volvulus sepium, commonly known as the common morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its twining stems that climb or trail along the ground. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue, purple, or white in color. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a smooth texture, while its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous seeds.

Volvulus sepium is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
