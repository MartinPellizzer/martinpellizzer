Viola cornuta var. acaulis

Viola cornuta var. acaulis, commonly known as the hairless violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright, compact growth habit and lack of stem hairs, giving it a smooth appearance. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
