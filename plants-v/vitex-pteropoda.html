Vitex pteropoda

Vitex pteropoda, commonly known as the small-leaf chaste tree, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy beneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of purple or white, which bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are small, dry capsules that contain several seeds.

Vitex pteropoda is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
