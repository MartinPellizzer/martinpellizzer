Vicia sativa var. obovata

Vicia sativa var. obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with obovate leaflets, giving it a distinctive, broad-leafed appearance. The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. Its pods contain several seeds, which are often used as forage for livestock.

This variety is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
