Valeriana tripteris var. carpatica

Valeriana tripteris var. carpatica, commonly known as Carpathian valerian, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It features a rhizomatous root system and erect, branched stems that bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clustered inflorescences, typically blooming in late summer. Its aromatic foliage contains compounds such as valerenic acid, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.
