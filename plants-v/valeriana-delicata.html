Valeriana delicata

Valeriana delicata, commonly known as sweet valerian, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, fragrant, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

Valeriana delicata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
