Vernonia duncanii subsp. bartlettii

Vernonia duncanii subsp.

bartlettii, commonly known as the Bartlett's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, purple to brownish flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
