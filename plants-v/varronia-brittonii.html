Varronia brittonii

Varronia brittonii, commonly known as the brittlestem varnished vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody, twining stems and heart-shaped, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by distinctive, three-lobed fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swamps and along stream banks.

Varronia brittonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
