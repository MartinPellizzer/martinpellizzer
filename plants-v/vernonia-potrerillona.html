Vernonia potrerillona

Vernonia potrerillona, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Vernonia potrerillona is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
