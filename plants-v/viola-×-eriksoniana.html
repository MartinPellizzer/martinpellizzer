Viola × eriksoniana

Viola × eriksoniana, commonly known as the shield-leaf violet, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and showy, pansy-like flowers that come in a variety of colors including violet, purple, and white. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–20 cm and produces seed pods that split open to release small, dark seeds.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded borders in horticulture.
