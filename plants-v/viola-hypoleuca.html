Viola hypoleuca

Viola hypoleuca, commonly known as the white violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple underside and produces small, white to pale violet flowers. It is part of the Violaceae family and is characterized by its showy yet delicate blooms.

Viola hypoleuca is often found in moist soils and can thrive in partial to full shade.
