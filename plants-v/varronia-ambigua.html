Varronia ambigua

Varronia ambigua, commonly known as the woolly senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its dense, woolly covering on its stems and leaves, which provides a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Varronia ambigua is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
