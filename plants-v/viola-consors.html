Viola consors

Viola consors, commonly known as the common violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy purple or violet flowers with a distinctive yellow eye. Native to Europe, it thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas. The plant is known for its ornamental value and has been cultivated for centuries.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
