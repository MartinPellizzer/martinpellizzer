Veronica schmidtiana f. pubescens

Veronica schmidtiana f. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy leaves and stems, which give it its "pubescens" designation, meaning hairy in Latin. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its ornamental value comes from its compact growth habit and delicate floral display.
