Veronica spicata var. austrobulgarica

Veronica spicata var. austrobulgarica, commonly known as the Austrian bulgarica speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and spikes of blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
