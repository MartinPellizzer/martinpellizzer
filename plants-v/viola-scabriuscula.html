Viola scabriuscula

Viola scabriuscula, commonly known as the rough-leaved violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture, and small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is part of the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate appearance and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in native plant gardens and conservation efforts.
