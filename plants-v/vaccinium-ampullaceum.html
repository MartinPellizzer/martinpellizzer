Vaccinium ampullaceum

Vaccinium ampullaceum, commonly known as the Brazilian huckleberry, is a species of shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants such as blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are typically elliptical and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Vaccinium ampullaceum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, humid environments.
