Viscum chyuluense

Viscum chyuluense, commonly known as the Chyulu mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to the Eastern African highlands.

It belongs to the Viscaceae family and typically grows on the branches of trees such as Acacia and Euphorbia. The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces white or pale yellow flowers followed by small, fleshy berries.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for birds and other animals, while also relying on host trees for nutrients and structural support.
