Vernonia monosis

Vernonia monosis, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Vernonia monosis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
