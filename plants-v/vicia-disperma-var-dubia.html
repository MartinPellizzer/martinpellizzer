Vicia disperma var. dubia

Vicia disperma var. dubia, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a perennial legume native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features slender, climbing stems with trifoliate leaves composed of three small, heart-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are small, purple to violet in color, and it produces elongated pods containing several seeds.

As a legume, it has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
