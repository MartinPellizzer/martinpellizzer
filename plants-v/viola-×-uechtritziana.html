Viola × uechtritziana

Viola × uechtritziana, commonly known as the Uechtritziana violet, is a hybrid species native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–20 cm and prefers moist, well-drained soils in partial shade. It is a perennial herbaceous plant with a compact growth habit, often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
