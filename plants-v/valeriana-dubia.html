Valeriana dubia

Valeriana dubia, commonly known as the lesser valerian, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a creeping rhizome and upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its small, pinkish-white flowers are clustered in spikes and bloom in late summer.

Valeriana dubia is known for its aromatic foliage and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
