Viscum laxum var. pini

Viscum laxum var. pini, commonly known as the pine mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of pine trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light absorbed by its leaves. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green but can turn reddish in certain conditions. It produces small, white or yellowish flowers followed by berries that are often consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal.

This species is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is often found in forested areas where its host trees thrive.
