Vernonia paucisquamata

Vernonia paucisquamata, commonly known as the slender ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along streambanks, thriving in moist, sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with pale green centers.

Vernonia paucisquamata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
