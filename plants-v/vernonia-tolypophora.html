Vernonia tolypophora

Vernonia tolypophora, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small blue or purple clouds. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy and branching.

Vernonia tolypophora is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to support local pollinators.
