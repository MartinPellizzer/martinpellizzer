Veronica anagalloides subsp. taeckholmiorum

Veronica anagalloides subsp.

taeckholmiorum, commonly known as the alpine speedwell, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, blooming from late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to harsh, high-altitude conditions and is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes.
