Vitex altmannii

Vitex altmannii, commonly known as the African blueberry or blue vitex, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically arranged in clustered inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule containing numerous small, dark seeds.

Vitex altmannii is often used in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
