Vernonia cincta

Vernonia cincta, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive yellowish or white midrib, while its stems are covered in fine hairs.

Vernonia cincta is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
