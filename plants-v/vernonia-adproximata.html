Vernonia adproximata

Vernonia adproximata, commonly known as the slender ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn, are usually purple to pink in color and attract various pollinators.

The plant has a compact growth habit and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
