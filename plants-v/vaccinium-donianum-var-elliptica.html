Vaccinium donianum var. elliptica

Vaccinium donianum var. elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the eastern United States.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, with a tart flavor. The plant has elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green, and it thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands and forest understories, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential for cultivation in suitable climates.
