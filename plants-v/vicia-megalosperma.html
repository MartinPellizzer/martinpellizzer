Vicia megalosperma

Vicia megalosperma, commonly known as the giant vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its robust growth and trifoliate leaves, which are typically green with a hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring. Its pods contain large, elliptical seeds that are often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Vicia megalosperma is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in temperate climates.
