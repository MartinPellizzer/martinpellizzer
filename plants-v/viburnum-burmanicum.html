Viburnum burmanicum

Viburnum burmanicum, commonly known as the Burman viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with ovate to elliptic leaflets and produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring. The plant is characterized by its ornamental value, with showy, dark red to black berries appearing in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Viburnum burmanicum is also valued for its ecological role, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
