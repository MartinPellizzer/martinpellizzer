Vigna vexillata var. wightii

Vigna vexillata var. wightii, commonly known as the African bean or cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It produces small, edible pods containing round seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients. The plant has trifoliate leaves and is often grown for its nutritional value in both traditional and modern agriculture. It thrives in warm climates and is drought-tolerant, making it a valuable crop in arid regions.

The species is also used for forage and has ecological benefits in soil enrichment.
