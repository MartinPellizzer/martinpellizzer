Vaccaria Segetalis

Vaccaria segetalis is a small, flowering plant commonly known as the common sandparsley. It is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa and thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. This annual or biennial plant produces delicate white or yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its seeds are small and often dispersed by wind, allowing it to spread across various environments. Despite its modest appearance, Vaccaria segetalis plays an important role in supporting local ecosystems and has been used in traditional medicine.
