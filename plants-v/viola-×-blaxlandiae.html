Viola × blaxlandiae

Viola × blaxlandiae, commonly known as the Blaxland violet, is a hybrid species native to Australia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streamside areas. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Viola × blaxlandiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
