Valeriana mixta

Valeriana mixta, commonly known as mixed valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

Valeriana mixta is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its delicate foliage and blooms.
