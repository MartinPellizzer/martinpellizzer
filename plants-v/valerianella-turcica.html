Valerianella turcica

Valerianella turcica, commonly known as Turkish valerian, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and it produces tiny, white or pinkish flowers clustered in dense umbels. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. Its flowering period usually occurs in late spring to early summer.

Valerianella turcica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its mild sedative properties.
