Vaccinium marylandicum

Vaccinium marylandicum, commonly known as the Maryland huckleberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and azaleas. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the spring. Its berries, which are edible, are dark blue to black when mature and are often used in local foraging and culinary practices.

The shrub thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and wetlands.
