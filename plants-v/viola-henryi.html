Viola henryi

Viola henryi, commonly known as Henry's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, hairy leaves and vibrant violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or woodland habitats and prefers well-drained soil. Viola henryi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both natural and cultivated landscapes.
