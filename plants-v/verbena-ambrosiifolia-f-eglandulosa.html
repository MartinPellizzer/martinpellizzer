Verbena ambrosiifolia f. eglandulosa

Verbena ambrosiifolia f. eglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular verbena, is a compact, flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, narrow leaves with a hairy texture, and produces clusters of vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its glandular hairs, which give it a distinctive appearance and contribute to its aromatic properties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal and ecological benefits.
