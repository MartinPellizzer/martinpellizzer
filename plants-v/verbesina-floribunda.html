Verbesina floribunda

Verbesina floribunda, commonly known as the yellow beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early autumn. The plant has hairy stems and leaves with a rough texture, and its seeds are encased in a spiny, capsule-like structure. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Verbesina floribunda is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
