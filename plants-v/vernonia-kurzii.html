Vernonia kurzii

Vernonia kurzii, commonly known as the Kurz's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in heathlands and open forests. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Vernonia kurzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and long blooming period.
