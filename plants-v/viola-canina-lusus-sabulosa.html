Viola canina lusus sabulosa

Viola canina lusus sabulosa, commonly known as the sandy viola, is a low-growing perennial herb native to sandy soils in temperate regions.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of purple or violet, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in coastal or dune environments.

Its adaptability to harsh conditions makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
