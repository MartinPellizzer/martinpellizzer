Verbena × bealei

Verbena × bealei, commonly known as the hybrid verbena or Beale's verbena, is a flowering plant in the Verbenaceae family.

It is a cross between Verbena rigida and Verbena bonariensis, resulting in a compact, bushy shrub with showy, clustered flowers. The plant is native to the southern United States and is known for its long blooming period, typically from late spring to early autumn. Its flowers come in a range of colors, including blue, purple, pink, and white, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Verbena × bealei prefers full sun and well-drained soil, and it is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
