Viola acuminata f. alba

Viola acuminata f. alba, commonly known as the white viola, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, toothed leaves and showy white or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.

The white viola is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
