Veronica hirta

Veronica hirta, commonly known as hairy speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and also covered in fine hairs, contributing to its overall hairy appearance.

Veronica hirta is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
