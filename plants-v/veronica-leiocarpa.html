Veronica leiocarpa

Veronica leiocarpa, commonly known as the silverleaf speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray leaves and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
