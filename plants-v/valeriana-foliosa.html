Valeriana foliosa

Valeriana foliosa, commonly known as the leaf valerian, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or perennial herb, often reaching heights of up to two meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and emit a strong, spicy scent when crushed.

Valeriana foliosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported calming properties.
