Vigna lanceolata var. latifolia

Vigna lanceolata var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are often used as a food source. It thrives in warm climates and is cultivated for its nutritional value and adaptability to various soil types.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
