Vargasia viridiflora

Vargasia viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered vargasia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and a fibrous root system that helps it thrive in moist, shaded environments. It is often found in dense forests and along riverbanks, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens for ornamental purposes.
