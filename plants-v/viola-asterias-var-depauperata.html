Viola asterias var. depauperata

Viola asterias var. depauperata, commonly known as the slender violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland understories and along streambanks, where it forms dense mats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
