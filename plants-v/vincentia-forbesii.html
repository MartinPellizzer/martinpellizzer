Vincentia forbesii

Vincentia forbesii, commonly known as the Forbes' evergreen vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
