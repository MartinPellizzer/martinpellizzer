Verbena morenonis

Verbena morenonis, commonly known as the narrowleaf verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Verbena morenonis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
