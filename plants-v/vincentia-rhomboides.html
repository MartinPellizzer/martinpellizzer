Vincentia rhomboides

Vincentia rhomboides, commonly known as the diamondleaf vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a pod that splits open to release its seeds.

Vincentia rhomboides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
