Vernonia tengwallii

Vernonia tengwallii, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a perennial shrub native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often grayish-green in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

Vernonia tengwallii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
