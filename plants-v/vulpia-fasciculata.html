Vulpia fasciculata

Vulpia fasciculata, commonly known as slender vulpia, is a species of grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a low-growing perennial plant that typically forms dense, mat-like growths on the ground. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, fields, and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important component of grassland ecosystems.
