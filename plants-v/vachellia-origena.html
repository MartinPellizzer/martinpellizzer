Vachellia origena

Vachellia origena, commonly known as the African burr acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorned stems and bipinnate leaves that are small and feathery. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits that contain seeds with hooked spines. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry climates.

Vachellia origena is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber and fuelwood.
