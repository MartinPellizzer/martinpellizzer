Virgulus

Virgulus, commonly known as the silverleaf plant, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its distinctive silvery foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs that give it a metallic sheen, helping to reflect sunlight and reduce water loss.

Virgulus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
