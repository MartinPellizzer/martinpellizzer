Vaccinium parvulifolium

Vaccinium parvulifolium, commonly known as the dwarf blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Vaccinium parvulifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
