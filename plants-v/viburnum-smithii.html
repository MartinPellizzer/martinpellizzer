Viburnum smithii

Viburnum smithii, commonly known as Smith's viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and features opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue, edible berries. Viburnum smithii thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract wildlife.
