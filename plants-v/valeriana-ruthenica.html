Valeriana ruthenica

Valeriana ruthenica, commonly known as the Ruthenian valerian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Western Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant, flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or wooded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive, slightly hairy texture. Its root system is known for containing compounds that have been traditionally used in herbal medicine.

Valeriana ruthenica is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its historical medicinal significance.
