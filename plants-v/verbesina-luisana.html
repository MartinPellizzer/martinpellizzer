Verbesina luisana

Verbesina luisana, commonly known as Luisana ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow-orange flowers. The plant features rough, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish underneath. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.
