Viola cassinensis subsp. pseudogracilis

Viola cassinensis subsp.

pseudogracilis, commonly known as the slender violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States. It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically less than 5 cm in length.

The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers with five petals, often with a distinctive yellow or white mark at the base. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the Violaceae family.
