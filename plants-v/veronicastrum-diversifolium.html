Veronicastrum diversifolium

Veronicastrum diversifolium, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of small, blue-violet flowers. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its showy inflorescences make it a popular choice for native gardens and wildlife habitats.
