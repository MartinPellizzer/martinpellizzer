Viola culminis

Viola culminis, commonly known as the mountain violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Appalachian Mountains.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to cool, moist environments. Viola culminis is known for its resilience to harsh mountain conditions, including strong winds and low temperatures.

Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, adding a splash of color to alpine meadows.
