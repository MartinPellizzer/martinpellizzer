Viburnum oxycoccos

Viburnum oxycoccos, commonly known as the common cranberry bush, is a deciduous shrub native to northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, red berries that resemble cranberries, which are edible and often used in culinary applications. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall, and its flowers are white and clustered in umbels. Viburnum oxycoccos thrives in wet, acidic soils and is often found in swamps or along riverbanks.

It plays an important ecological role by providing food for birds and other wildlife.
