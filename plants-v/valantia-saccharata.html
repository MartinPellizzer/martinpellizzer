Valantia saccharata

Valantia saccharata, commonly known as the sugar vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and the production of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant's leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a smooth texture, while its stems are woody and can grow quite long. Valantia saccharata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
