Vernonia elsieae

Vernonia elsieae, commonly known as the Elsie's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture.

Vernonia elsieae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
