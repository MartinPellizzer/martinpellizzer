Viola × taradakensis

Viola × taradakensis, commonly known as the Armenian violet, is a hybrid species native to the Caucasus region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm, featuring heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Viola × taradakensis is valued for its hardiness and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
