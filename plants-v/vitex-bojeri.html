Vitex bojeri

Vitex bojeri, commonly known as the African blueberry or false chaste tree, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, blooming in summer. Its fruits are small, blue-black berries that are edible but not widely consumed.

Vitex bojeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to various soil types.
