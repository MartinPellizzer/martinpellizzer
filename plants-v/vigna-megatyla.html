Vigna megatyla

Vigna megatyla, commonly known as the large-seeded cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces pods that contain large, oval seeds, which are often used as a food source. Vigna megatyla is adapted to warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an important crop in regions where it is cultivated for both human consumption and animal feed.
