Vicia embergeri

Vicia embergeri, commonly known as the Emberger's vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Vicia embergeri is often found in open habitats such as slopes and wastelands.

Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can remain viable in the soil for several years.
