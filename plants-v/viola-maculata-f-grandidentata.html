Viola maculata f. grandidentata

Viola maculata f. grandidentata, commonly known as the showy yellow violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, toothed leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive purple-brown marking at the base. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
