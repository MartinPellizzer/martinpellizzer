Valeriana berteroi

Valeriana berteroi, commonly known as the Mexican valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring aromatic, dark green, and slightly hairy leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in the summer months. Its rhizomatous root system is notable for its medicinal properties, containing compounds like valerenic acid.

Valeriana berteroi is often cultivated for its calming effects and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
