Vernonia faradifani

Vernonia faradifani, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Vernonia faradifani is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
