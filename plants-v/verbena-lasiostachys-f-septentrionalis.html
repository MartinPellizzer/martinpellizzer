Verbena lasiostachys f. septentrionalis

Verbena lasiostachys f. septentrionalis, commonly known as the Northern verbena, is a flowering plant belonging to the Verbenaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, tubular blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This cultivar is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
