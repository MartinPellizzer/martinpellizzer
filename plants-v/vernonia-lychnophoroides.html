Vernonia lychnophoroides

Vernonia lychnophoroides, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to two meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in dense clusters, making it a striking addition to gardens.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
