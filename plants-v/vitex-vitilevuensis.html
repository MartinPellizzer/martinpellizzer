Vitex vitilevuensis

Vitex vitilevuensis, commonly known as the Lautoka vitex, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family, which includes vines and other climbing plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Vitex vitilevuensis is often found in coastal and lowland areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
