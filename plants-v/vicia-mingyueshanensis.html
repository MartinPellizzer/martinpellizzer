Vicia mingyueshanensis

Vicia mingyueshanensis, commonly known as the Mingyue Mountain vetch, is a flowering plant species native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and is adapted to temperate climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Vicia mingyueshanensis plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest in botanical research due to its limited distribution and unique ecological niche.
