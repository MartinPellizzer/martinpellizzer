Viola rostrata f. ramosa

Viola rostrata f. ramosa, commonly known as the ramose viola, is a botanical variant of the common violet.

It is characterized by its branched stems, giving it a distinctive appearance compared to typical viola species. The plant produces violet-blue flowers with a yellow throat, typical of the Violaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its foliage consists of heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental value.
