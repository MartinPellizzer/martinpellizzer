Veronica quezelii

Veronica quezelii, commonly known as the Quezel's speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

Veronica quezelii is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
