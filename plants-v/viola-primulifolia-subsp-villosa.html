Viola primulifolia subsp. villosa

Viola primulifolia subsp.

villosa, commonly known as the hairy violet, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive hairy leaves and violet-colored flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and the flowers bloom in early spring.

The subspecies is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
