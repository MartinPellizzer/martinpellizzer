Veronica coxiana

Veronica coxiana, commonly known as the mountain veronica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowering period usually occurs in late spring to early summer.

Veronica coxiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
