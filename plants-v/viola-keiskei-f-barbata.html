Viola keiskei f. barbata

Viola keiskei f. barbata, commonly known as the Mexican viola or the rock viola, is a compact, low-growing perennial herb native to Mexico.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white to pale green hairs. The plant produces delicate, violet-blue flowers with five petals, often borne in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun to partial shade conditions.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
