Viburnum jucundum var. jucundum

Viburnum jucundum var. jucundum, commonly known as sweet viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red, berry-like drupes. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Viburnum jucundum var. jucundum prefers partial shade to full sun and well-drained soil.
