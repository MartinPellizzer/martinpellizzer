Viola odorata proles incompta

Viola odorata proles incompta, commonly known as the sweet violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery and herbal medicine.
