Vernonia mucronifolia

Vernonia mucronifolia, commonly known as the narrow-pointed ironweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for gardens in temperate climates.

Vernonia mucronifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
