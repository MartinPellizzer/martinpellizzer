Vaccinium confertum var. eriocladum

Vaccinium confertum var. eriocladum, commonly known as the broadleaf huckleberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. Its berries, which are typically red or purple, are edible and often used in local cuisines or for wildlife habitat.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.
