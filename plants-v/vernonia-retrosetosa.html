Vernonia retrosetosa

Vernonia retrosetosa, commonly known as the silverleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and conical flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its erect growth habit and hairy stems. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia retrosetosa is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodlands.
