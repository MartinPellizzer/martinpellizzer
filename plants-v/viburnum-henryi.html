Viburnum henryi

Viburnum henryi, commonly known as the Chinese snowball, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a striking visual display. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Viburnum henryi is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
