Viburnum setigerum f. aurantiacum

Viburnum setigerum f. aurantiacum, commonly known as the orangeleaf viburnum, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by vibrant orange-red berries in the fall. The leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn a striking red in autumn, adding seasonal interest. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a versatile ornamental plant.

Its compact growth habit and colorful foliage make it a popular choice for small gardens and landscape designs.
