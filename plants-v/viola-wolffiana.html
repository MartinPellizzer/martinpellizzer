Viola wolffiana

Viola wolffiana, commonly known as the Wolff's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 10-20 centimeters, featuring heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy violet flowers with five petals, often in shades of purple or blue, during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Viola wolffiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
