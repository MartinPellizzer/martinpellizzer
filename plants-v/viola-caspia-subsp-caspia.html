Viola caspia subsp. caspia

Viola caspia subsp.

caspia, commonly known as the Caspian violet, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and forest edges.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.

The subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and adaptability to various soil types.
