Vernonia alsodea

Vernonia alsodea, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads that resemble mist. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands, swamps, and prairies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Vernonia alsodea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
