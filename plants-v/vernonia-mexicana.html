Vernonia mexicana

Vernonia mexicana, commonly known as Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from yellow to brownish-red.

Vernonia mexicana is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
