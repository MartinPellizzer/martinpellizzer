Vernonia ferreyrae

Vernonia ferreyrae, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are usually blue to purple in color.

Vernonia ferreyrae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
