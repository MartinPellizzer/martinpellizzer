Vitex mollis

Vitex mollis, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, followed by woody, pendulous fruits. Vitex mollis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it adaptable to various garden environments.
