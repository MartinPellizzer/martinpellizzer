Verbesina debilis

Verbesina debilis, commonly known as the weakleaf beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks and roadsides. The plant features yellow-orange flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are alternate and often have a soft, hairy texture.

Verbesina debilis is known for its ability to spread rapidly and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
