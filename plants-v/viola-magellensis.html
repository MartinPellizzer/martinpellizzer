Viola magellensis

Viola magellensis, commonly known as the Antarctic violet, is a flowering plant native to the extreme southern regions of South America.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in cold, alpine environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple and white. It is well adapted to harsh conditions, including strong winds and low temperatures.

Viola magellensis is a hardy species that plays an important role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
