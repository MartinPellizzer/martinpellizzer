Vernonia sangka

Vernonia sangka, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow or orange in color.

Vernonia sangka is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
