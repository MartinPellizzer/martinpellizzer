Vitis glaucophylla

Vitis glaucophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf grape, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and Georgia. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that have a distinctive silvery underside. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by edible berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Vitis glaucophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential in horticulture and ecological restoration.
