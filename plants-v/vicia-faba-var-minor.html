Vicia faba var. minor

Vicia faba var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf broad bean, is a variety of the broad bean plant native to southern Europe.

It is a herbaceous annual legume characterized by its compact growth habit and smaller, rounder pods compared to the standard broad bean. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets. It is valued for its edible seeds, which are used in various cuisines, and for its role in nitrogen fixation in soil.

This variety is often cultivated for both its nutritional value and its agricultural benefits.
