Voladeria carchiensis

Voladeria carchiensis, commonly known as the Carchi flutterleaf, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, waxy leaves that shimmer in the sunlight, and its clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests. Its unique structure and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists studying plant adaptation.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
