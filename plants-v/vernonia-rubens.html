Vernonia rubens

Vernonia rubens, commonly known as the red ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, bright red to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.

Vernonia rubens is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
