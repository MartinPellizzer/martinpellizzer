Vernonia verticillata

Vernonia verticillata, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its blue to purple flower heads that resemble mist. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. It has feathery leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in late summer.

Vernonia verticillata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
