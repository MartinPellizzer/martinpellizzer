Verbascum henriquesii

Verbascum henriquesii, commonly known as the Henriques' mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes species known for their fuzzy, showy flowers. The plant features tall, erect stems covered in soft, hairy foliage, and produces bright yellow flowers in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the arid climate of its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
