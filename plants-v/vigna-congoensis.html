Vigna congoensis

Vigna congoensis, commonly known as the Congo bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several edible seeds. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Vigna congoensis is valued for its nutritional content, including protein and essential minerals.

It also plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
