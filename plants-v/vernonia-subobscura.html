Vernonia subobscura

Vernonia subobscura, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along creek banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Vernonia subobscura is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
