Verbascum mixtum

Verbascum mixtum, commonly known as the common mullein.

It is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its tall, fuzzy stems and large, velvety leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in the second year of growth, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Verbascum mixtum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically for its soothing effects on respiratory conditions.
