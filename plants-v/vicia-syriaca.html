Vicia syriaca

Vicia syriaca, commonly known as Syrian vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Vicia syriaca is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration efforts.
