Vermifrux

Vermifrux, commonly known as the earthworm plant, is a unique species with a symbiotic relationship with earthworms.

Its roots secrete a substance that attracts and nourishes earthworms, which in turn help aerate the soil and enhance nutrient cycling. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich environments, often found in temperate forest floors. Vermifrux has small, heart-shaped leaves and produces delicate, white flowers in the spring.

Its ecological role highlights the intricate connections between plants and soil organisms in maintaining healthy ecosystems.
