Vicia sativa f. alba

Vicia sativa f. alba, commonly known as white vetch, is a herbaceous legume characterized by its slender, trailing stems and trifoliate leaves.

It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical seed pods. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in cultivated fields.

As a legume, it has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
