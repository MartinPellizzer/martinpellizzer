Viguiera grahamii

Viguiera grahamii, commonly known as Graham's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of individual flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its bright yellow to orange flowers bloom in late summer, adding vibrant color to the landscape.

Viguiera grahamii is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
