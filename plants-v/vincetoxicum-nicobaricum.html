Vincetoxicum nicobaricum

Vincetoxicum nicobaricum, commonly known as Nicobar spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Nicobar Islands and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The plant contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans if ingested.
