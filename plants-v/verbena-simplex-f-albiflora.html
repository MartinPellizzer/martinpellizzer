Verbena simplex f. albiflora

Verbena simplex f. albiflora, commonly known as the white vervain, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its prolonged blooming period, which extends from early summer to autumn.
