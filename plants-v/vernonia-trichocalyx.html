Vernonia trichocalyx

Vernonia trichocalyx, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy inflorescences. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, and it produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Vernonia trichocalyx thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
