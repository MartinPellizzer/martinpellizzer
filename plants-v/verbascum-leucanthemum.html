Verbascum leucanthemum

Verbascum leucanthemum, commonly known as white flax or white mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in soft, woolly hairs, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Verbascum leucanthemum is often found in dry, rocky soils and is known for its medicinal and ornamental uses.
