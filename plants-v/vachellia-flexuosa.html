Vachellia flexuosa

Vachellia flexuosa, commonly known as the umbrella thorn acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-shaped crown formed by numerous thin, drooping branches. The plant has small, compound leaves and produces yellow flowers that attract various pollinators. It is well adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system that helps it survive drought conditions.

Vachellia flexuosa is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its role in soil stabilization.
