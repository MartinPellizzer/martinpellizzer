Vernonia lehmannii

Vernonia lehmannii, commonly known as Lehmann's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust growth habit. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges. It produces small, yellowish to brownish flower heads that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Vernonia lehmannii is often found in grasslands and rocky areas, where it thrives in full sun and well-drained soils.
