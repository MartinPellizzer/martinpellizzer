Verbena serpyllifolia

Verbena serpyllifolia, commonly known as common vervain, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as fields and roadsides. The plant has slender, upright stems and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Verbena serpyllifolia is known for its resilience and is often used in traditional medicine and as a garden plant.
