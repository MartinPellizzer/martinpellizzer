Veronica hostii

Veronica hostii, commonly known as the silverleaf speedwell, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branched stems covered in silvery-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, blue-violet flowers in racemes during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and attractive foliage.
