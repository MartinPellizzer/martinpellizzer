Verbascum scabrum

Verbascum scabrum, commonly known as rough mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Verbascum scabrum is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat respiratory ailments.
