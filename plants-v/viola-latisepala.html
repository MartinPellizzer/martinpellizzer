Viola latisepala

Viola latisepala, commonly known as the broad-sepaled violet, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and mountainous regions. Its flowers have five petals, with the lower petal often being broader than the others, giving it its common name.

Viola latisepala is valued for its ornamental beauty and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
