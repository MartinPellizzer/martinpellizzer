Vernonia itapensis

Vernonia itapensis, commonly known as Brazilian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, wooded areas. The plant produces striking blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a distinctive texture.

Vernonia itapensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its showy flowers.
