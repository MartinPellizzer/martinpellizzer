Viola bakeri subsp. shastensis

Viola bakeri subsp.

shastensis, commonly known as Shasta violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and showy violet or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
