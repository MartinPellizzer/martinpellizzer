Valeriana melanocarpa

Valeriana melanocarpa, commonly known as black valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers and aromatic, often spicy scent. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and has opposite, heart-shaped leaves. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

Valeriana melanocarpa is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
