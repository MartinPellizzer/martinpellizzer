Viola cerasifolia subsp. selloiana

Viola cerasifolia subsp.

selloiana, commonly known as the Sello's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly velvety texture.

The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with five petals, often with a distinctive yellow or white spot at the base. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
