Valeriana radicalis

Valeriana radicalis, commonly known as the common valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a robust root system and upright stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically pink or white, during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a strong, often spicy scent.

Valeriana radicalis is known for its medicinal properties, particularly for its calming effects due to the presence of volatile oils.
