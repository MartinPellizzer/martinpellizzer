Vepris eugeniifolia

Vepris eugeniifolia, commonly known as the wild cinnamon tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark and leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant's aromatic oils contribute to its use in perfumery and as a flavoring agent.
