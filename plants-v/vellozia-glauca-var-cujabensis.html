Vellozia glauca var. cujabensis

Vellozia glauca var. cujabensis, commonly known as the Cuja Basin Vellozia, is a striking succulent native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, rosette-forming structure composed of thick, fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces striking, tall inflorescences with bright yellow flowers, which bloom during the dry season. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
