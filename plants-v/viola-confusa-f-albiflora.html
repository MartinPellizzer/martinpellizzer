Viola confusa f. albiflora

Viola confusa f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered viola, is a cultivar of the common violet.

It is characterized by its pale white to cream-colored flowers, which contrast with its heart-shaped, dark green leaves. Native to Europe, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade. It produces small, edible violet flowers that are often used in culinary applications.

The plant's botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and conservation efforts.
