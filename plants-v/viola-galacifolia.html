Viola galacifolia

Viola galacifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive silvery underside, which gives it its common name. The plant produces violet flowers with five petals, typically in shades of purple or blue. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Viola galacifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
