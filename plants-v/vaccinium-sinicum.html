Vaccinium sinicum

Vaccinium sinicum, commonly known as the Chinese blueberry or Chinese huckleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. Its flowers are bell-shaped, pink or white, and appear in clusters, followed by edible berries that range in color from blue to purple.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
