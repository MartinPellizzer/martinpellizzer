Verbesina scabrida

Verbesina scabrida, commonly known as roughhollow or roughhollow coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branched stems that are covered in a rough, sandpapery texture. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with a distinctive central cone, blooming from late summer into early fall. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

Verbesina scabrida thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.
