Vicia disperma var. subcapitata

Vicia disperma var. subcapitata, commonly known as the clustered vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep violet. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and soil stabilization.
