Vernonia mellifera

Vernonia mellifera, commonly known as the honeyflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom during the cooler months. The plant produces a distinctive, sweet-smelling nectar that attracts a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Vernonia mellifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
