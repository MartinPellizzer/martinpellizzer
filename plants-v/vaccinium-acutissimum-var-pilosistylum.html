Vaccinium acutissimum var. pilosistylum

Vaccinium acutissimum var. pilosistylum, commonly known as the hairy whortleberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants such as blueberries and heathers. The plant is characterized by its small, dark blue berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its dense growth habit makes it an effective ground cover in forested areas.
