Viscum bolleanum

Viscum bolleanum, commonly known as the African mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees in sub-Saharan Africa.

It belongs to the family Viscaceae and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are a source of food for birds and other animals. Its parasitic nature allows it to draw water and nutrients from its host, while also contributing to the ecosystem through seed dispersal.

Viscum bolleanum is often found in humid, forested areas and plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat.
