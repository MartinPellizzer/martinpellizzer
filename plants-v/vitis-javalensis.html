Vitis javalensis

Vitis javalensis, commonly known as the Javalan grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that help it ascend trees and other structures. Its leaves are typically compound, with three to five leaflets, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers.

The fruit is a small, round berry that ripens to a dark purple color, often used in local traditional practices and minor commercial applications.
