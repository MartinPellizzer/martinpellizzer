Vanda parviflora

Vanda parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered vanda, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features striking, showy flowers with bright colors such as yellow, orange, and red, often with contrasting patterns. The plant has long, strap-like leaves and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Vanda parviflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
