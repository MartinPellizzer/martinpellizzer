Verbascum × sarikamischense

Verbascum × sarikamischense, commonly known as the Karakoram mullein, is a hybrid plant belonging to the genus Verbascum in the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, lobed leaves that are often covered in soft, woolly hairs. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including the Karakoram and Pamir ranges.

Verbascum × sarikamischense is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.
