Verbascum graecum var. zuccarinii

Verbascum graecum var. zuccarinii, commonly known as the Greek mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are covered in soft, velvety hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a loose, branched inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This variety is closely related to other members of the genus Verbascum, known for their medicinal and ornamental uses.
