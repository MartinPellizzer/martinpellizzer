Verbena fissa

Verbena fissa, commonly known as the narrowleaf verbena, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Verbena fissa is known for its long blooming period, typically from spring to early autumn.
