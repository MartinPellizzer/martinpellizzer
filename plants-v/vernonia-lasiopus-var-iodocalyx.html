Vernonia lasiopus var. iodocalyx

Vernonia lasiopus var. iodocalyx, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, blue to purple flower heads that resemble mist, which appear in late summer to early fall. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

This variety is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
