Viola repens

Viola repens, commonly known as the common violet, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple, blue, or white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers bloom from spring to early summer, and the plant spreads via rhizomes and seeds.

Viola repens is often used in traditional medicine and as a decorative plant in gardens.
