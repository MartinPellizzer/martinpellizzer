Vignea foetida

Vignea foetida, commonly known as the stinking vine, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a strong, unpleasant odor, which is most noticeable when the flowers are in full bloom. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture.

Vignea foetida is often found in moist, wooded areas and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
