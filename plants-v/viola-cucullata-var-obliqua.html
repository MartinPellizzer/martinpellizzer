Viola cucullata var. obliqua

Viola cucullata var. obliqua, commonly known as the oblique hoodsnakevine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a trailing perennial that produces violet-colored flowers with distinctive hooded petals. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a unique and attractive addition to native plant gardens.
