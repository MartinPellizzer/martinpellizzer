Viburnum Nudum Var. Cassinoides

Viburnum nudum var. cassinoides is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States. It is known for its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces bright red berries in the fall, which are a food source for birds. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and partial shade. This versatile plant is popular in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
