Vriesea fluminensis

Vriesea fluminensis, commonly known as the "Bromeliad of the Amazon," is a striking epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It features a rosette of narrow, dark green leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater, which serves as a reservoir for its roots and the organisms that inhabit it. The plant produces vibrant, showy inflorescences with brightly colored bracts that can range from yellow to red, often resembling flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Vriesea fluminensis is popular as a decorative plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
