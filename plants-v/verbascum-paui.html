Verbascum paui

Verbascum paui, commonly known as the pale mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, gray-green leaves that are covered in a dense layer of soft, woolly hair. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in a terminal spike. Verbascum paui thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically for its medicinal properties.
