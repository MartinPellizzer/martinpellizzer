Vitiphoenix

Vitiphoenix, commonly known as the golden fan palm, is a striking tropical plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its elegant, fan-shaped leaves that radiate from a central stem, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into red, fleshy fruits, which are an important food source for local wildlife. Vitiphoenix thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

Due to its limited natural range and habitat threats, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
