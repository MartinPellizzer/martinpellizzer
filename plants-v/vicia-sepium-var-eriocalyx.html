Vicia sepium var. eriocalyx

Vicia sepium var. eriocalyx, commonly known as the hairy trefoil, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in a variety of soils but prefers well-drained, sunny areas.

Vicia sepium var. eriocalyx is often found in hedgerows, meadows, and along roadsides.
