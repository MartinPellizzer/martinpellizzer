Vignea × emmae

Vignea × emmae, commonly known as the hybrid grapevine, is a botanical species resulting from the crossbreeding of Vigna species.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and ability to produce clusters of small, sweet fruits. The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a glossy surface, and it produces flowers that transition into the edible fruits.

This hybrid is valued for its adaptability and potential in both ornamental and agricultural settings.
