Viola tenorei

Viola tenorei, commonly known as the Sicilian violet, is a species of flowering plant native to the island of Sicily.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in mild, temperate climates. Viola tenorei is often found in open woodlands and along limestone outcrops.

Its ornamental value has made it a sought-after plant in horticulture, though it is relatively rare in cultivation.
