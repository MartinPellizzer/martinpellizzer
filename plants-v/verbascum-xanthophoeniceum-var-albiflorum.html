Verbascum xanthophoeniceum var. albiflorum

Verbascum xanthophoeniceum var. albiflorum, commonly known as the whiteflower mullein, is a variant of the mullein plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in soft, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to white flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
