Verbena bracteosa var. albiflora

Verbena bracteosa var. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant produces showy, five-petaled blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping in arid regions.
