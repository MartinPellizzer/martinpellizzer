Vaccinium myrtillus subsp. compactum

Vaccinium myrtillus subsp.

compactum, commonly known as the dwarf blueberry, is a shrub native to northern regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, dark blue berries that ripen in late summer.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist climates. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a glossy surface and a serrated edge.

This subspecies is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits, which are often used in jams and desserts.
