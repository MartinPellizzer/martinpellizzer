Vaccinium oxycoccos

Vaccinium oxycoccos, commonly known as the cranberry bush, is a deciduous shrub native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of bright red berries. The plant thrives in acidic, wet soils and is often found in bogs and swamps. Its berries are edible and have been used historically for culinary and medicinal purposes.

The plant plays an important ecological role in supporting various wildlife species.
