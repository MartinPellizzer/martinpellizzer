Veronica pinnata subsp. laeta

Veronica pinnata subsp.

laeta, commonly known as the cheerful grass-vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has finely divided, grass-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. Veronica pinnata subsp.

laeta is often found in nutrient-poor soils and is considered a characteristic species of certain ecological communities.
