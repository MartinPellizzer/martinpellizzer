Veronica bombycina

Veronica bombycina, commonly known as the woolly speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal areas and is well adapted to dry conditions.

Veronica bombycina is a perennial herb that produces seeds in its second year, contributing to its survival in arid environments.
