Vicia communis proles angustifolia

Vicia communis proles angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale yellow to greenish in color. It is native to Europe and is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in meadows.

This species is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
