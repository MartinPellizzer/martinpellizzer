Vachellia lutea f. pachycarpa

Vachellia lutea f. pachycarpa, commonly known as the thick-leaved acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage, which is adapted to arid conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. Its woody pods contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in poor soils.
