Vernonia oppositifolia

Vernonia oppositifolia, commonly known as the oppositeleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite leaf arrangement and produces small, yellow to brown flowers in clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia oppositifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
