Vaccinium vitis-idaea var. major

Vaccinium vitis-idaea var. major, commonly known as the large cranberry bush, is a deciduous shrub native to northern regions of North America and Eurasia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and dense clusters of small, evergreen leaves. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in boreal and alpine habitats.

This variety is distinguished from other Vaccinium species by its larger size and more robust growth form.
