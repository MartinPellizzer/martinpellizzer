Vitex cooperi

Vitex cooperi, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring gray-green foliage and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers. The plant blooms in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Vitex cooperi is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to provide habitat for wildlife.
