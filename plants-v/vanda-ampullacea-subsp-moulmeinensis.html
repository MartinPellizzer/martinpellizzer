Vanda ampullacea subsp. moulmeinensis

Vanda ampullacea subsp.

moulmeinensis, commonly known as the Moulmein vanda, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its long, pendant inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple.

The plant typically grows as an epiphyte on trees or rocks, thriving in humid, warm climates with high light exposure. Its pseudobulbs are elongated and cylindrical, supporting the plant's growth and water storage.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to cultivation.
