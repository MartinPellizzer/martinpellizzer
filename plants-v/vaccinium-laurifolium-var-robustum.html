Vaccinium laurifolium var. robustum

Vaccinium laurifolium var. robustum, commonly known as the broadleaf huckleberry, is a variant of the common huckleberry native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions. The plant produces small, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and are used both in culinary applications and traditional medicine. Its leaves are broad and leathery, with a dark green color on the upper surface and a lighter green underside.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
