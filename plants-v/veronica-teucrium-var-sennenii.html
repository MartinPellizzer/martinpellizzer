Veronica teucrium var. sennenii

Veronica teucrium var. sennenii, commonly known as the silverleaf speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, silvery-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers in clusters, typically blooming from spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly wavy margin.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
