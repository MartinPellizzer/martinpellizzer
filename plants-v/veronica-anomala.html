Veronica anomala

Veronica anomala, commonly known as mouse ear or mouse ear speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Veronica anomala is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also known for its medicinal properties.
