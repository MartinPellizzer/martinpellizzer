Vaccinium virgatum var. angustifolium

Vaccinium virgatum var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, which bloom in late spring. Its berries are edible, turning from green to dark blue or purple as they mature.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of wild berries in suitable climates.
