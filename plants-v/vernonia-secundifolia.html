Vernonia secundifolia

Vernonia secundifolia, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its compact growth habit and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Vernonia secundifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
