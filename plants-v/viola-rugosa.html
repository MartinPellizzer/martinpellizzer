Viola rugosa

Viola rugosa, commonly known as the hairy violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, typically found in grassy areas and woodland edges. The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces violet-blue flowers with yellow markings. Viola rugosa is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
