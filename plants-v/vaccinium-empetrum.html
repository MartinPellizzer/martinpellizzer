Vaccinium empetrum

Vaccinium empetrum, commonly known as bearberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that remain green throughout the year. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible for humans but toxic to livestock. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and rocky areas.

Vaccinium empetrum plays a vital role in its ecosystem, providing food for wildlife and contributing to soil stabilization.
