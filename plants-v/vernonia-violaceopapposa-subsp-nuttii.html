Vernonia violaceopapposa subsp. nuttii

Vernonia violaceopapposa subsp.

nuttii, commonly known as Nuttall's ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of violet-blue flower heads.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically visited by bees and other pollinators. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
