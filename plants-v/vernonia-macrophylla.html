Vernonia macrophylla

Vernonia macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of blue to purple cylindrical flowers. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
