Vatica albiramis

Vatica albiramis, commonly known as the white-rammed ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, heavy wood and dense foliage, making it a valuable species in both ecological and economic contexts. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated, woody fruits containing flat, winged seeds.

Vatica albiramis thrives in humid, lowland tropical forests and is often found in secondary growth areas.
