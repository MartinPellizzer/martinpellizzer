Viola dumetorum var. araucana

Viola dumetorum var. araucana, commonly known as the Chilean violet, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers bloom in spring and are pollinated by insects like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
