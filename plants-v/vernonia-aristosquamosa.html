Vernonia aristosquamosa

Vernonia aristosquamosa, commonly known as the roughleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically herbaceous growth habit. The plant features narrow, rough-textured leaves and produces clusters of small, violet to blue-purple flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open woods and along roadsides.

Vernonia aristosquamosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
