Viscum subaureum

Viscum subaureum, commonly known as the golden mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees, primarily in southern Africa.

It possesses a unique root system composed of haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system to absorb water and nutrients. The plant produces small, white, waxy flowers that are pollinated by insects, followed by the development of small, yellowish berries. Its leaves are evergreen, glossy, and elliptical, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Viscum subaureum plays an ecological role by providing food for birds and other animals, which in turn help disperse its seeds.
