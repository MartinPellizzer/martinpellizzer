Viola plenigeri

Viola plenigeri, commonly known as the broadleaf violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of purple and white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola plenigeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
