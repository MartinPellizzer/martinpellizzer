Viola hirta f. keiskei

Viola hirta f. keiskei, commonly known as the Keiske's violet, is a compact perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically covered in fine, short pubescence. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers with five petals, often with a distinctive white or yellowish throat. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest floors.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
