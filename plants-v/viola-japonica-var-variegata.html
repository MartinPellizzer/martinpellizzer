Viola japonica var. variegata

Viola japonica var. variegata, commonly known as the Japanese violet or variegated violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive variegation pattern of white and green. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of purple and white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long flowering period.
