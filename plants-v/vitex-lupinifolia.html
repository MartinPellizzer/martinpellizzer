Vitex lupinifolia

Vitex lupinifolia, commonly known as the coastal vitex, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its grayish-green leaves that are opposite and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of blue or purple, which bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that release seeds when mature.

Vitex lupinifolia thrives in sandy or coastal soils and is often found in dune environments.
