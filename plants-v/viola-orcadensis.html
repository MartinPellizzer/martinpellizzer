Viola orcadensis

Viola orcadensis, commonly known as the Orkney violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Orkney Islands in Scotland.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in coastal and grassland habitats. Viola orcadensis is notable for its small, delicate flowers that bloom from April to June.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
