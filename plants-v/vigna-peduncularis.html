Vigna peduncularis

Vigna peduncularis, commonly known as the sickle bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated, cylindrical pods containing several kidney-shaped seeds. Vigna peduncularis is valued for its nutritional content, particularly its high protein and fiber levels.

It is also used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop in various parts of the world.
