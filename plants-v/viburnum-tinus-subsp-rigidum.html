Viburnum tinus subsp. rigidum

Viburnum tinus subsp.

rigidum, commonly known as the evergreen viburnum, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for hedges or ornamental gardens.

Its compact growth habit and year-round interest make it a popular choice for landscaping.
