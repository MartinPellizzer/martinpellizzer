Valeriana heterocarpa

Valeriana heterocarpa, commonly known as the heterocarp valerian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China and India.

It belongs to the Valerianaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and distinctive, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that are often borne in dense, round clusters called cymes. Its roots are valued in traditional medicine for their purported sedative and calming properties.

Valeriana heterocarpa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shady areas.
