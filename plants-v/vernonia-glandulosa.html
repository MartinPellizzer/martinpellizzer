Vernonia glandulosa

Vernonia glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy clusters of blue to purple flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

Vernonia glandulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
