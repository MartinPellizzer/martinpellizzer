Viola valderia

Viola valderia, commonly known as the violet valderia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family, which includes various species of violets and related plants. The plant features heart-shaped, softly hairy leaves and produces delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple and violet. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Viola valderia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
