Vahlia capensis subsp. ellipticifolia

Vahlia capensis subsp.

ellipticifolia, commonly known as the Cape vahlia, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa. It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along its stems.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the warmer months. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem and a preference for well-drained, sandy soils.

It is often found in coastal and rocky habitats, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
