Vittaria moritziana

Vittaria moritziana, commonly known as the Moritz's maidenhair fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Vittariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in the understory of tropical forests. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a typical feature of ferns.

Vittaria moritziana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant in warm climates.
