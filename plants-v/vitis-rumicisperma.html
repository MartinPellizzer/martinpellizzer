Vitis rumicisperma

Vitis rumicisperma, commonly known as the prickly grape or wild grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to parts of Central and South America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that help it ascend trees and other structures. Its leaves are typically lobed and have a rough texture, while its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, berry-like structure that ripens to a dark purple color, containing several seeds.
