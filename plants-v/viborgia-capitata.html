Viborgia capitata

Viborgia capitata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

Viborgia capitata is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
