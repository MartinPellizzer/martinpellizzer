Vanda denisoniana var. hebraica

Vanda denisoniana var. hebraica, commonly known as the Hebrew vanda, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendant inflorescences and showy, fragrant flowers that come in a range of colors including white, yellow, and pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or other structures in its natural habitat. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining potting media to thrive.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
