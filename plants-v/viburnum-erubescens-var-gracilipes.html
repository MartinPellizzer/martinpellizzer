Viburnum erubescens var. gracilipes

Viburnum erubescens var. gracilipes, commonly known as the redstem viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. The plant produces red berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn red in the fall.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.
