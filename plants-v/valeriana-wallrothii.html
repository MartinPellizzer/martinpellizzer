Valeriana wallrothii

Valeriana wallrothii, commonly known as the African valerian, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a stout, upright stem with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in dense, rounded clusters called cymes. It is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Valeriana wallrothii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
