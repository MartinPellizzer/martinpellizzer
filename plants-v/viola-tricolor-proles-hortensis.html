Viola tricolor proles hortensis

Viola tricolor proles hortensis, commonly known as the heartsease or common violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of violet, white, and yellow, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Viola tricolor proles hortensis is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
