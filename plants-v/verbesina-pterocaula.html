Verbesina pterocaula

Verbesina pterocaula, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Verbesina pterocaula is valued in gardens for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
