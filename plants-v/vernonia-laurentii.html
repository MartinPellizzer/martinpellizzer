Vernonia laurentii

Vernonia laurentii, commonly known as the ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia laurentii is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
