Veronica melanocaulon

Veronica melanocaulon, commonly known as the black-stemmed speedwell, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that are typically dark green to almost black in color, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Veronica melanocaulon is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
