Viola × stuartii

Viola × stuartii, commonly known as the Stuart viola, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often violet or purple flowers with a distinctive yellow eye-like marking. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–20 centimeters, featuring heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or shaded garden settings.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking colorful, hardy plants.
