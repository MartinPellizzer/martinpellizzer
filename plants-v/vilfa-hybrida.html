Vilfa hybrida

Vilfa hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid veldtgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are typically 30 to 60 cm in length. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is also cultivated in gardens and landscaping.
