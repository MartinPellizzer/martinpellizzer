Verbascum zuccarinii

Verbascum zuccarinii, commonly known as the Zuccarini's mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a loose spike. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Verbascum zuccarinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
