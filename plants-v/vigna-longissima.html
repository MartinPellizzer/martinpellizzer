Vigna longissima

Vigna longissima, commonly known as the long-seeded cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to Africa and widely cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit, trifoliate leaves, and elongated, narrow pods that contain small, yellowish seeds. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate with adequate sunlight for optimal growth. Vigna longissima is valued for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines.

It also plays an important role in crop rotation due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
