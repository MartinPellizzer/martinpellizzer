Vernonia praecox

Vernonia praecox, commonly known as the early vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, thriving in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Vernonia praecox is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
