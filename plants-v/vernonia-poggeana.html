Vernonia poggeana

Vernonia poggeana, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy, composite flower heads. Vernonia poggeana thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

It is a popular choice for native gardens due to its ornamental value and ability to support local pollinators.
