Veronica connata subsp. typica

Veronica connata subsp.

typica, commonly known as the typical blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, stream banks, and wetlands.

The plant features upright, slender stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are small, blue to violet, and clustered in dense spikes, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
