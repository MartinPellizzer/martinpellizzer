Voigtia laurifolia

Voigtia laurifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf voigtia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. Voigtia laurifolia thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various garden settings.
