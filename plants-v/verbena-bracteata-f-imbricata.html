Verbena bracteata f. imbricata

Verbena bracteata f. imbricata, commonly known as the imbricated verbena, is a flowering plant belonging to the Verbenaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine pubescence. Native to the southern United States, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun conditions.

This ornamental plant is valued for its vibrant blooms and is often used in garden landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
