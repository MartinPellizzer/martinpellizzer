Veronica anagallis-aquatica subsp. oxycarpa

Veronica anagallis-aquatica subsp.

oxycarpa, commonly known as the water speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters.

The leaves are typically opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found near streams, ponds, and marshes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
