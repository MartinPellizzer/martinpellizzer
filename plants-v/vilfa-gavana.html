Vilfa gavana

Vilfa gavana, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in the evening. It produces berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often avoided for consumption but is studied for its potential medicinal compounds.
