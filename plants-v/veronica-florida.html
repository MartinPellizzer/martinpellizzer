Veronica florida

Veronica florida, commonly known as the Florida speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 6 to 12 inches. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Veronica florida thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
