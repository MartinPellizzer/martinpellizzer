Vignea dacica

Vignea dacica, commonly known as the Transylvanian vetch, is a leguminous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Vignea dacica is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment and ecological restoration projects.
