Viola betonicifolia

Viola betonicifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved viola, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Viola betonicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
