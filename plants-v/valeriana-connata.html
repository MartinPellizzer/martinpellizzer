Valeriana connata

Valeriana connata, commonly known as the Canadian valerian, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive, slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and have a faintly aromatic scent.

Valeriana connata is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its calming effects.
