Verbena erinoides var. alba

Verbena erinoides var. alba, commonly known as white verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, white, five-petaled flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
