Vitis macrostachya

Vitis macrostachya, commonly known as the giant grapevine, is a woody vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that range in color from green to black, depending on the maturity stage. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Vitis macrostachya is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
