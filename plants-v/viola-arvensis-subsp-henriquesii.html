Viola arvensis subsp. henriquesii

Viola arvensis subsp.

henriquesii, commonly known as Henriques' violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its heart-shaped, hairy leaves and violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is distinguished by its subspecies-specific features, including its leaf morphology and flower structure.

Henriques' violet is often found in association with other woodland flora, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
