Veronica prostrata f. satureiifolia

Veronica prostrata f. satureiifolia, commonly known as the prostrate speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features small, blue or violet flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the Veronica genus. The foliage is characterized by narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense carpet on the ground. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to spread effectively in suitable conditions.
