Viola × helvetica

Viola × helvetica, commonly known as the Dutch viola, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant is native to Europe and has been cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile choice for gardeners.

Its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions contribute to its popularity in horticulture.
