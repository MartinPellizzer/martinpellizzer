Vulpia litardiereana var. pubivaginata

Vulpia litardiereana var. pubivaginata, commonly known as the pubescent vulpia, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, soft blades that are often covered in short, pubescent hairs. The inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like cluster of spikelets, which are typically pale green to reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow and have a bluish-green hue, with a distinct midrib that is slightly prominent.

This plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, disturbed habitats.
