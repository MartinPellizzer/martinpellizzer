Viola silvatica proles reichenbachiana

Viola silvatica proles reichenbachiana, commonly known as the Alpine violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and produces delicate, violet-blue flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in cooler climates.
