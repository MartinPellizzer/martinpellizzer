Viola diffusa var. glabella

Viola diffusa var. glabella, commonly known as the Woolly Violet, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, fuzzy leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. It prefers moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
