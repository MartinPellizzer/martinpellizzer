Vernonia ikopae

Vernonia ikopae, commonly known as the Ikopae ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Nigeria.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually purple or violet, and they bloom during the dry season.

Vernonia ikopae is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
