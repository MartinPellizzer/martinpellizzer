Valeriana secunda

Valeriana secunda, commonly known as the lesser valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its square, branched stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. Valeriana secunda is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Its aromatic foliage has been historically used in herbal medicine for its calming properties.
