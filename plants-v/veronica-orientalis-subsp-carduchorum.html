Veronica orientalis subsp. carduchorum

Veronica orientalis subsp.

carduchorum, commonly known as the Eastern Speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region. It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
