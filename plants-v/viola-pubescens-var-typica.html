Viola pubescens var. typica

Viola pubescens var. typica, commonly known as the common violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive purple-tinged underside. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple, violet, or blue, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
