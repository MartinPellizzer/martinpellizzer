Vicia cretica subsp. aegaea

Vicia cretica subsp.

aegaea, commonly known as the Aegean vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils, often found in Mediterranean regions.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its trifoliate leaves are narrow and hairy, adapting to arid conditions.

The subspecies is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock.
