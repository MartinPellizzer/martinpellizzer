Verbascum rotundifolium var. kabylianum

Verbascum rotundifolium var. kabylianum, commonly known as the Kabylian mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
