Veronica villicaulis

Veronica villicaulis, commonly known as the silky veronica, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance.

Veronica villicaulis is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
