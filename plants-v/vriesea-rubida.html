Vriesea rubida

Vriesea rubida, commonly known as the red-veined bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, spathaceous bracts that display a deep red color with contrasting green veins, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant's rosette-shaped leaves are typically dark green and covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat. Vriesea rubida produces inflorescences that consist of a central spadix surrounded by a colorful bract, often in shades of red or orange.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
