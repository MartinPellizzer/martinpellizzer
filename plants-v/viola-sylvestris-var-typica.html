Viola sylvestris var. typica

Viola sylvestris var. typica, commonly known as the common violet, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to Europe, it thrives in woodland and grassland habitats, preferring partial shade and moist, well-drained soil. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, dark seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
