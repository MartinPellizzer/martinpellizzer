Veronica holophylla var. maritima

Veronica holophylla var. maritima, commonly known as sea holly, is a perennial flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and silver-gray foliage, which help it thrive in salty, windy environments. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to maritime climates. Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
