Viguiera anchusifolia var. immarginata

Viguiera anchusifolia var. immarginata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features upright, branching stems that are covered in dense, gray-green foliage. The plant produces showy, composite flower heads in late summer to early fall, consisting of purple-red disk flowers surrounded by yellow ray flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a distinctive narrow margin, giving it its varietal name.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a valuable addition to xeriscaped gardens.
