Verbascum × nouelianum

Verbascum × nouelianum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant belonging to the Scrophulariaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves that resemble those of the common mullein, Verbascum blattaria. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and naturalized in various regions.
