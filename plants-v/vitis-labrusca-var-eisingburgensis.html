Vitis labrusca var. eisingburgensis

Vitis labrusca var. eisingburgensis, commonly known as the Eisingburg grape, is a cultivar native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, dark purple to black berries that are typically seedless. The plant is a vigorous vine with compound leaves and tendrils that aid in climbing. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its use in both fresh consumption and winemaking due to its rich flavor profile.
