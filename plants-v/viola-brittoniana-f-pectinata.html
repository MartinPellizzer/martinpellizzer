Viola brittoniana f. pectinata

Viola brittoniana f. pectinata, commonly known as the pectinate viola, is a rare and ornamental flowering plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive pectinate (comb-like) leaves. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, with five petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for both horticulturists and botanists.
