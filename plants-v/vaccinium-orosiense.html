Vaccinium orosiense

Vaccinium orosiense, commonly known as the Orosi blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark blue to black in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Vaccinium orosiense is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential for cultivation in suitable climates.
