Verbascum × steniense

Verbascum × steniense, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a flowering plant belonging to the genus Verbascum in the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Verbascum blattaria and Verbascum blattellum, both of which are native to southern Europe. The plant is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its showy yellow flowers appear in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Verbascum × steniense is typically found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
