Viola oblongosagittata var. violascens

Viola oblongosagittata var. violascens, commonly known as the purple viola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly pubescent surface. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with five petals, often with a distinctive white or pale violet throat. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a desirable species for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
