Veronica pilosa var. surculosa

Veronica pilosa var. surculosa, commonly known as the woolly speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to tolerate salt spray.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
