Viola mandshurica f. pubescens

Viola mandshurica f. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy or pubescent stems and leaves, which give it its specific epithet "pubescens.". The plant produces showy violet flowers with five petals, typically in shades of purple or blue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable environments.
