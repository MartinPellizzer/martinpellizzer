Vargasia tomentosa

Vargasia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly vargasia, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Vargasia tomentosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
