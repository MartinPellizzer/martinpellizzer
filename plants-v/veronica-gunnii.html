Veronica gunnii

Veronica gunnii, commonly known as the rock violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are aromatic when crushed.

Veronica gunnii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
