Verbesina oxylepis

Verbesina oxylepis, commonly known as the yellow crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has slender, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
