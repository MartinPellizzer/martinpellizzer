Veronica arvensis f. glandulosa

Veronica arvensis f. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular speedwell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

This variant is a common weed in agricultural fields and gardens, known for its rapid growth and adaptability.
