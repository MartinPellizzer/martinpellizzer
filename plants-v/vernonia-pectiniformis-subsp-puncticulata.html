Vernonia pectiniformis subsp. puncticulata

Vernonia pectiniformis subsp.

puncticulata, commonly known as the punctuated ironweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish to reddish-brown flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
