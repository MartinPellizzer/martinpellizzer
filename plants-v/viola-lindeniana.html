Viola lindeniana

Viola lindeniana, commonly known as the Lindena viola, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to its natural environment.

Viola lindeniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
