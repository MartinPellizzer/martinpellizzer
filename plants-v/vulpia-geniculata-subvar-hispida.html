Vulpia geniculata subvar. hispida

Vulpia geniculata subvar. hispida, commonly known as rough bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats in disturbed soils.

It is often found in grasslands, roadsides, and areas with compacted soil.
