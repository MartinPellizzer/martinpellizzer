Verbascum × germanum

Verbascum × germanum, commonly known as the common mullein.

It is a hybrid species belonging to the family Scrophulariaceae, characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in the summer, which are clustered in a raceme at the top of the stem. It is native to Europe and is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in dry meadows.

Verbascum × germanum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its soothing effects on respiratory ailments.
