Vernonia coerulea

Vernonia coerulea, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, blue-violet flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland areas and along streambanks. Its foliage is lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture.

The flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
