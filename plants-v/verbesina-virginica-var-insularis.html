Verbesina virginica var. insularis

Verbesina virginica var. insularis, commonly known as the Bahama verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Bahamas and parts of the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in coastal areas and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, erect stems and produces bright yellow flowers with five petals. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
