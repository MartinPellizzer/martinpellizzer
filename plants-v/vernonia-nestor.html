Vernonia nestor

Vernonia nestor, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in rough, hairy foliage. Its showy, yellow flower heads appear in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Vernonia nestor is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
