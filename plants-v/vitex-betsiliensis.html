Vitex betsiliensis

Vitex betsiliensis, commonly known as the Madagascar chaste tree, is a shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to purple. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
