Valeriana alliariifolia var. tiliifolia

Valeriana alliariifolia var. tiliifolia, commonly known as the sweet woodruff, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, fragrant foliage that emits a pleasant, vanilla-like aroma. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its rhizomatous root system makes it adaptable to various soil types and conditions.
