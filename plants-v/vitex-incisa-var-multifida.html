Vitex incisa var. multifida

Vitex incisa var. multifida, commonly known as the fragrant verbena or multifid verbena, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive, fern-like appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.

Vitex incisa var. multifida is typically found in moist, sunny habitats such as stream banks and forest edges.
