Viola vinealis

Viola vinealis, commonly known as the lesser violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, violet-blue leaves and small, delicate flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a trailing habit and can form dense mats, making it a common ground cover in its native habitat.

Viola vinealis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, early-blooming flowers.
