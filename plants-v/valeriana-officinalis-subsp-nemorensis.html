Valeriana officinalis subsp. nemorensis

Valeriana officinalis subsp.

nemorensis, commonly known as wood betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces small, pale blue to white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The roots of this subspecies are used in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.
