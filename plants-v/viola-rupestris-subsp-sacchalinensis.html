Viola rupestris subsp. sacchalinensis

Viola rupestris subsp.

sacchalinensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Russian Far East. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces violet to purple flowers with a distinctive spurred petal. It is part of the Violaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
