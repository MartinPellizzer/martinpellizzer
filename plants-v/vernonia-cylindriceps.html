Vernonia cylindriceps

Vernonia cylindriceps, commonly known as the cylindrical Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters, and bloom during the dry season.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
