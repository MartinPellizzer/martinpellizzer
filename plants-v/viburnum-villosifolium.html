Viburnum villosifolium

Viburnum villosifolium, commonly known as the woollyleaf viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with a distinctive silvery-white underside, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. Viburnum villosifolium is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
