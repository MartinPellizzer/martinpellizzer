Vitis semicordata var. roylei

Vitis semicordata var. roylei, commonly known as the Kashmir grape or Royle's grape, is a climbing vine native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its robust, woody stems that can reach several meters in length. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The berries contain compounds that have been traditionally used in herbal remedies for their purported health benefits.
