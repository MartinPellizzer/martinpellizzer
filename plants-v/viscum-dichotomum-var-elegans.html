Viscum dichotomum var. elegans

Viscum dichotomum var. elegans, commonly known as the elegant mistletoe, is a parasitic plant belonging to the Viscaceae family.

It grows on the branches of various host trees, primarily in temperate regions. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in pairs along the stems. Its flowers are typically white or greenish-white, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are a source of food for birds.

This species is notable for its ecological role in supporting bird populations through its fruit production.
