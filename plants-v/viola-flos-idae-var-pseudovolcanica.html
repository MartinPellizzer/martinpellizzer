Viola flos-idae var. pseudovolcanica

Viola flos-idae var. pseudovolcanica, commonly known as the volcanic viola, is a rare and unique variant of the viola genus.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-hued flowers with a subtle iridescent sheen. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit, with heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers cool, shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution and distinctive appearance, it is considered a botanical curiosity and a subject of interest for horticultural enthusiasts.
