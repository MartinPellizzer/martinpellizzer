Vicia lens subsp. ervoides

Vicia lens subsp.

ervoides, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae. It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green pea-like flowers that are typically clustered in loose spikes. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along fences.

This subspecies is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
