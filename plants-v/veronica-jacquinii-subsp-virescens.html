Veronica jacquinii subsp. virescens

Veronica jacquinii subsp.

virescens, commonly known as the greenleaf speedwell, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green.

The plant produces clusters of small, blue-violet flowers in early spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
