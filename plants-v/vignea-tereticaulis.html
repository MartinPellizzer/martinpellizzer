Vignea tereticaulis

Vignea tereticaulis, commonly known as the slender pigweed, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, alternate leaves that are usually smooth and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by triangular, three-angled seed capsules. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Vignea tereticaulis is an important species in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in weed dynamics.
