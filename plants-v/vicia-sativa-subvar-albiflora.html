Vicia sativa subvar. albiflora

Vicia sativa subvar. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

This subvariety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
