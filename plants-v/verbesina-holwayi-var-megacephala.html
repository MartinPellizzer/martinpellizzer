Verbesina holwayi var. megacephala

Verbesina holwayi var. megacephala, commonly known as the giant-headed beggar-ticks, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and produces clusters of yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture. This plant is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun.

It is known for its distinctive, large, spherical seed heads that persist through the winter, adding visual interest to the landscape.
