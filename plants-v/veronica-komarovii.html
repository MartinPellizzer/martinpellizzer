Veronica komarovii

Veronica komarovii, commonly known as the Komarov's speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Veronica komarovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
