Viola canina var. maculata

Viola canina var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted dog violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in woodland and grassland habitats, thriving in partial shade and well-drained soil. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces violet flowers with distinctive black spots on the petals. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch of color to the landscape.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
