Verbena chacensis

Verbena chacensis, commonly known as blue verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are opposite, narrow, and slightly hairy, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Verbena chacensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral displays.
