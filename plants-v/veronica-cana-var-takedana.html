Veronica cana var. takedana

Veronica cana var. takedana, commonly known as the Japanese speedwell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Native to Japan, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
