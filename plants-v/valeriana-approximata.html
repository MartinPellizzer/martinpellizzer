Valeriana approximata

Valeriana approximata, commonly known as American valerian, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered white or pinkish flowers. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their calming properties.

Valeriana approximata is valued for its aromatic foliage and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
