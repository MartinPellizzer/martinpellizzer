Viola mirabilis lusus parviflora

Viola mirabilis lusus parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered wonder violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from purple to white. Native to certain regions of Europe, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, facilitating its spread.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and ecological studies.
