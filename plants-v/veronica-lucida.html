Veronica lucida

Veronica lucida, commonly known as the blueweed or slender speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in whorls along the stems.

Veronica lucida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden settings for its delicate appearance.
