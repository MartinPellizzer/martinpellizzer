Viola formosana

Viola formosana, commonly known as the Taiwan violet, is a species of flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy violet flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to its natural habitat.

Viola formosana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
