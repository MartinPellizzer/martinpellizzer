Viola × abortiva

Viola × abortiva, commonly known as the heartleaf violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and moist soils, preferring partial to full shade. Its flowers have five petals, with the lower petal often marked with a distinctive yellow or white spot.

Viola × abortiva is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
