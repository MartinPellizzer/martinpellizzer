Viola hirta var. umbrosa

Viola hirta var. umbrosa, commonly known as the woodland violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of violet or purple, often with a yellow or white throat. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to cool, humid climates and rich, well-drained soils.
