Vernonia dendigulensis

Vernonia dendigulensis, commonly known as the giant ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of yellow to orange flowers, which are arranged in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. Vernonia dendigulensis is known for its ability to thrive in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to the unique environmental conditions of its native region.

It is a significant species in the flora of Madagascar and is of interest to botanists due to its distinctive morphology and ecological role.
