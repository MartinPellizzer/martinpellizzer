Verbascum pseudovarians

Verbascum pseudovarians, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in height. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Verbascum pseudovarians is often used in herbal medicine for its purported soothing properties.
