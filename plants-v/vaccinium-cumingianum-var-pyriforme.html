Vaccinium cumingianum var. pyriforme

Vaccinium cumingianum var. pyriforme, commonly known as the Cuming's cranberry bush, is a shrub native to the subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or orange in color, similar to other Vaccinium species. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and potential culinary uses.
