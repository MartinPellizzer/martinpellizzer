Vernonia napus

Vernonia napus, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads that resemble misty clouds. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as swamps and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Vernonia napus is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
