Valerianoides dichotoma

Valerianoides dichotoma, commonly known as the dichotomous valerian, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Valerianaceae and is characterized by its branched stems and small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a distinct, aromatic scent.

Valerianoides dichotoma is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported calming effects.
