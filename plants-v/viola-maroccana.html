Viola maroccana

Viola maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan violet, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soil conditions, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowers bloom in spring, adding a splash of color to gardens and natural landscapes.

Viola maroccana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
