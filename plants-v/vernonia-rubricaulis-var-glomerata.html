Vernonia rubricaulis var. glomerata

Vernonia rubricaulis var. glomerata, commonly known as the clustered ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. These flowers are typically arranged in terminal panicles, giving the plant a distinctive appearance.

It is well adapted to moist to wet soils and is often found in swampy areas or along creek banks.
