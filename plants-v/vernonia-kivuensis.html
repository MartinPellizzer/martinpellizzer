Vernonia kivuensis

Vernonia kivuensis, commonly known as the Kivu Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats, such as forest understories, and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Vernonia kivuensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has potential uses in traditional medicine.
