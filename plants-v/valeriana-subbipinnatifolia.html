Valeriana subbipinnatifolia

Valeriana subbipinnatifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved valerian, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves. The leaves are typically narrow and pinnatifid, giving the plant its specific epithet. It produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by small, dry fruits.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
