Veronica × major

Veronica × major, commonly known as the common speedwell, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is native to Europe and has been widely naturalized in North America.

Veronica × major is often used in garden settings for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
