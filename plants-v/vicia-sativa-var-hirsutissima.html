Vicia sativa var. hirsutissima

Vicia sativa var. hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy vetch, is a perennial legume characterized by its robust growth and distinctive hairy foliage.

It features trifoliate leaves with a dense covering of fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep red, depending on the variety. Its root system is well-developed, allowing it to thrive in various soil types and conditions.

This species is often cultivated as a cover crop due to its ability to fix nitrogen and prevent soil erosion.
