Vigna membranacea

Vigna membranacea, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several edible seeds. Vigna membranacea is often cultivated for its nutritious seeds, which are rich in protein and can be used in various culinary preparations.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.
