Vigna unguiculata subsp. mensensis

Vigna unguiculata subsp.

mensensis, commonly known as the West African cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its climbing habit, trifoliate leaves, and distinctive pods that contain edible seeds.

The plant is well adapted to dry conditions, making it an important crop in arid and semi-arid regions. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, and it produces a high yield of protein-rich seeds.

The subspecies is valued for its nutritional content and agricultural resilience.
