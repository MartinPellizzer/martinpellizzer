Viscum grandicaule

Viscum grandicaule, commonly known as the giant mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in the Mediterranean region.

It has a distinctive appearance with long, slender stems and small, leathery leaves that are usually arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species. Viscum grandicaule relies on its host for water and nutrients, while it provides shelter and food for wildlife.

Its life cycle is closely tied to the seasonal changes of its host tree, making it an important component of forest ecosystems.
