Viola pumilio

Viola pumilio, commonly known as the dwarf violet, is a small flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, featuring heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces delicate flowers in shades of violet, blue, or white, often with a distinctive yellow or green throat. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Viola pumilio is known for its hardy nature and is a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland plantings.
