Virectaria belingana

Virectaria belingana, commonly known as the Bellingana orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, slender stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, facilitating efficient water retention.

Virectaria belingana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
