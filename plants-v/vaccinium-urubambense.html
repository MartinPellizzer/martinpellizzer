Vaccinium urubambense

Vaccinium urubambense, commonly known as the Urubambense huckleberry, is a species of shrub native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark blue or black in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
