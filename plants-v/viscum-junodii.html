Viscum junodii

Viscum junodii, commonly known as the African mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on a variety of host trees, primarily in sub-Saharan Africa.

It belongs to the family Viscaceae and is characterized by its evergreen, leathery leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other animals. Its parasitic nature allows it to draw water and nutrients from its host, while still performing photosynthesis to some extent.

Viscum junodii plays a role in local ecosystems and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
