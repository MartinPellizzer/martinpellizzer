Viburnum tengyuehense

Viburnum tengyuehense, commonly known as the Tengyuehense honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters and features opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds and some animals. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in traditional Chinese gardens.

The species is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in partial shade.
