Vachellia allenii

Vachellia allenii, commonly known as the Allen's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, bipinnate leaves that reduce water loss in hot, dry climates. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular heads, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seeds are encased in a hard pod, and the plant is adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Vachellia allenii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
