Valeriana juncea

Valeriana juncea, commonly known as slender valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has aromatic leaves and is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks. Valeriana juncea belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar structural features.

It is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
