Vicia alpestris var. hypoleuca

Vicia alpestris var. hypoleuca, commonly known as the alpine vetch, is a flowering plant native to alpine and arctic regions.

It is a perennial legume characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish or greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

This variety is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its natural habitat.
