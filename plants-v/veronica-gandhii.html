Veronica gandhii

Veronica gandhii, commonly known as the Indian speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Veronica gandhii is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
