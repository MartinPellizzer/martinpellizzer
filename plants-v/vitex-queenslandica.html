Vitex queenslandica

Vitex queenslandica, commonly known as the Queensland glory flower, is a shrub or small tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant, blue-violet flowers that appear in clusters during the spring and summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.

Vitex queenslandica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
