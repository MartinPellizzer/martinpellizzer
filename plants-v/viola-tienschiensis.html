Viola tienschiensis

Viola tienschiensis, commonly known as the Chinese violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, purple-pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Viola tienschiensis is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
