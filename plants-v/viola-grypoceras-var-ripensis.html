Viola grypoceras var. ripensis

Viola grypoceras var. ripensis, commonly known as the river viola, is a perennial flowering plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland edges. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to its natural habitat.

The species is noted for its adaptability to wet soils and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
