Veillonia alba

Veillonia alba, commonly known as the white veillonia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a subtle elegance to its appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface.

The botanical significance of Veillonia alba lies in its unique floral structure and its role in local ecosystems.
