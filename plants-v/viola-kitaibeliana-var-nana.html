Viola kitaibeliana var. nana

Viola kitaibeliana var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf violet, is a compact, low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of violet, blue, or white, often with a distinctive yellow center. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and compact size, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded borders.
