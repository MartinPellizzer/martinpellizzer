Viguiera benziorum

Viguiera benziorum, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features silvery-blue foliage and produces showy, fluffy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Viguiera benziorum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
