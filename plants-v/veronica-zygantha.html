Veronica zygantha

Veronica zygantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of hair.

Veronica zygantha is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
