Vitex montevidensis var. multinervis

Vitex montevidensis var. multinervis, commonly known as the Montevidense chaste tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Uruguay and southern Brazil.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with a prominent venation pattern. The plant produces fragrant, clustered purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a dry, woody capsule containing several seeds.

It is often found in dry forests and scrublands, and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses.
