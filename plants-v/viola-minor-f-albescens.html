Viola minor f. albescens

Viola minor f. albescens, commonly known as the white viola, is a cultivated form of the common violet.

It is characterized by its pale white or creamy petals, which contrast with its deep green foliage. This plant is a herbaceous perennial, typically growing to a height of about 10-15 centimeters. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch of color to gardens and natural landscapes.
