Vitex hildebrandtii

Vitex hildebrandtii, commonly known as Hildebrandt's vitex, is a shrub native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in warmer climates.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
