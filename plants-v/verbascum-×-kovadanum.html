Verbascum × kovadanum

Verbascum × kovadanum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant that belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family.

It is a hybrid between Verbascum blattaria and Verbascum phlomoides, resulting in a plant with distinctive fuzzy, velvety leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to the Mediterranean region. It produces tall flowering stalks with yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and rocky landscapes.
