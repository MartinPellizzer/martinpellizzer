Vallisneria gracilis

Vallisneria gracilis, commonly known as narrowleaf waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Asia.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically 10 to 30 centimeters in length, arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are either male or female, with the female flowers developing into cylindrical, berry-like fruits. Vallisneria gracilis plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen for aquatic life.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense underwater vegetation make it both ecologically significant and sometimes invasive in non-native waters.
