Vitis vinifera var. corniculala

Vitis vinifera var. corniculala, commonly known as the grapevine or European grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports. The plant produces small, clustered berries that range in color from green to black, depending on the variety and ripening stage. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough, serrated edge.

This vine is widely cultivated for its fruit, which is used in winemaking and as a fruit crop.
