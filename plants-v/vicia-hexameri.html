Vicia hexameri

Vicia hexameri, commonly known as the six-flowered vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Vicia hexameri is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Its flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in early spring.
