Veronica arvensis var. silvicola

Veronica arvensis var. silvicola, commonly known as the woodland speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has a low-growing habit with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its presence indicates a healthy, undisturbed ecosystem due to its sensitivity to environmental changes.
