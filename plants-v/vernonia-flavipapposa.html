Vernonia flavipapposa

Vernonia flavipapposa, commonly known as the yellow pappus vervain, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces yellow flowers that are surrounded by a pappus, a feathery structure that aids in wind dispersal of its seeds. Vernonia flavipapposa is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of desert flora.
