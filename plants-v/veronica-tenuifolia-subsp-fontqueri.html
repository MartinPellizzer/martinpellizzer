Veronica tenuifolia subsp. fontqueri

Veronica tenuifolia subsp.

fontqueri, commonly known as Fontquer's speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer.

The leaves are typically narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
