Valeriana rubens

Valeriana rubens, commonly known as red valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has aromatic, slightly hairy leaves and produces small, dark berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. Valeriana rubens is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.

Its root and leaves contain compounds that have been used to treat anxiety and sleep disorders.
