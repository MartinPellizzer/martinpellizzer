Veronica peduncularis var. umbrosa

Veronica peduncularis var. umbrosa, commonly known as the shadowy speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in clusters along slender stems. The plant thrives in shady, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks, where it forms dense mats. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging, semi-shaded conditions.
