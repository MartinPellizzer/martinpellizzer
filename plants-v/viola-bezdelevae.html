Viola bezdelevae

Viola bezdelevae, commonly known as the Bezdelev's violet, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to its natural environment.

Viola bezdelevae is relatively rare and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations.
