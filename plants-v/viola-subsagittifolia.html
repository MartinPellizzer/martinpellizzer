Viola subsagittifolia

Viola subsagittifolia, commonly known as the arrowleaf violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, arrow-like leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive dark green color. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, with five petals arranged symmetrically. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Viola subsagittifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
