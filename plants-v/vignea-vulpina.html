Vignea vulpina

Vignea vulpina, commonly known as the fox grape, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible when ripe. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits. Its flowers are typically greenish-white and appear in clusters.

Vignea vulpina thrives in humid climates and requires support for its vine growth.
