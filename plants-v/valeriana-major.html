Valeriana major

Valeriana major, commonly known as Greater Valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a strong, somewhat unpleasant odor.

Valeriana major is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, and it is known for its medicinal properties.
