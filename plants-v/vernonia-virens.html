Vernonia virens

Vernonia virens, commonly known as slender ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and somewhat woody.

Vernonia virens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
