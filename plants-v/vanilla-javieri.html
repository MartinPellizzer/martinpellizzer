Vanilla javieri

Vanilla javieri, commonly known as the Java vanilla, is a climbing orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It produces fragrant, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by dark brown pods containing the aromatic vanilla beans. The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments with partial shade and well-drained soil. Its vanilla beans are used in cooking and perfumery, similar to the more well-known vanilla planifolia.

The species is valued for its unique aroma and is often cultivated for commercial purposes.
