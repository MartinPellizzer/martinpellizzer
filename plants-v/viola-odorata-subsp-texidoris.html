Viola odorata subsp. texidoris

Viola odorata subsp.

texidoris, commonly known as the Texas violet, is a species native to the central and southern United States. It is a herbaceous perennial with heart-shaped, dark green leaves and showy violet flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings. Its flowers emit a sweet, spicy fragrance, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to various garden conditions.
