Vitis thunbergii var. partita

Vitis thunbergii var. partita, commonly known as the Japanese grape or the split-leaf grape, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often divided into three to five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into clusters of small, dark blue berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its foliage and berries make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
