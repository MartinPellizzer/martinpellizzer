Vernonia blumeoides

Vernonia blumeoides, commonly known as Blume's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is known for its ornamental value due to its vibrant flower color and compact growth habit. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Vernonia blumeoides is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
