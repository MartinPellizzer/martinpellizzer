Verbena simplex var. eggertii

Verbena simplex var. eggertii, commonly known as the Mexican verbena, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
