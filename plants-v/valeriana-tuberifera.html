Valeriana tuberifera

Valeriana tuberifera, commonly known as the tuberous valerian, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant's tubers, which are its underground storage organs, are often used in traditional medicine. Valeriana tuberifera thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in forested areas.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in herbal remedies for its calming effects.
