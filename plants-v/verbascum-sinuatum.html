Verbascum sinuatum

Verbascum sinuatum, commonly known as the Sinuous Mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Verbascum sinuatum is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical uses in traditional medicine.
