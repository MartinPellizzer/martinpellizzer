Viscum saururoides

Viscum saururoides, commonly known as the lizard's tail mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on specific host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It belongs to the family Viscaceae and is characterized by its small, leafy stems and clusters of small, white or yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are often consumed by birds, which aid in seed dispersal. Viscum saururoides is native to Australia and is often found in coastal and semi-arid regions.

Its growth is slow, and it forms dense, bushy shrubs that can persist for many years on its host tree.
