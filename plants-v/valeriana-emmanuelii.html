Valeriana emmanuelii

Valeriana emmanuelii, commonly known as Emmanuel's valerian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Valerianaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant, flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are usually dark green and arranged in opposite pairs along the stem.

Valeriana emmanuelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
