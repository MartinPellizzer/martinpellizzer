Vicia americana var. lathyroides

Vicia americana var. lathyroides, commonly known as the American vetch, is a perennial legume native to North America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces cylindrical, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun.

This species is often used in ecological restoration due to its nitrogen-fixing ability and adaptability.
