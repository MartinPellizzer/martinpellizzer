Vitis flexuosa var. crassifolia

Vitis flexuosa var. crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved grapevine, is a variant of the flexible grapevine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves and vigorous climbing habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into clusters of edible berries. Its botanical features include tendrils for climbing and a woody vine structure.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
