Vitis hissarica subsp. hissarica

Vitis hissarica subsp.

hissarica, commonly known as the Hissar grape, is a species native to Central Asia. It is a deciduous vine characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, dark berries.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in regions with well-drained soils. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for its potential in viticulture.

The subspecies is distinguished by its robust growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
