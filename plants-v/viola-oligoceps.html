Viola oligoceps

Viola oligoceps, commonly known as the purple violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Viola oligoceps is often found in woodland habitats and is known for its ability to tolerate partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
