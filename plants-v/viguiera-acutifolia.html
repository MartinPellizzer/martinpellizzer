Viguiera acutifolia

Viguiera acutifolia, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its showy, purple-pink flowers bloom in late summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viguiera acutifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
