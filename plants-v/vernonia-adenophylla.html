Vernonia adenophylla

Vernonia adenophylla, commonly known as the woolly ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage. The plant produces small, pale yellow to brownish composite flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Vernonia adenophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
