Viola atriplicifolia

Viola atriplicifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically violet or purple with five petals. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds.

Viola atriplicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
