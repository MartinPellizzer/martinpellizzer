Veronica × sooana

Veronica × sooana, commonly known as the hybrid speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Veronica × sooana is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
