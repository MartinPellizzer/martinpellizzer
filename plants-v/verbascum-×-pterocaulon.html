Verbascum × pterocaulon

Verbascum × pterocaulon, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant belonging to the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is a hybrid between Verbascum blattaria and Verbascum pterocaulon, resulting in a plant with distinctive foliage and floral characteristics. The leaves are thick, fuzzy, and gray-green, while the flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
