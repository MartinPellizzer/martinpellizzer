Vaccinium lundellianum

Vaccinium lundellianum, commonly known as the Lundell's blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, acidic berries that are typically harvested in the fall. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in boreal forest ecosystems.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for both ecological studies and potential commercial cultivation.
