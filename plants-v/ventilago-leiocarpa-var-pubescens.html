Ventilago leiocarpa var. pubescens

Ventilago leiocarpa var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy yellowwood, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, pubescent foliage and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in winter. The plant has small, elliptical leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive yellowish hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
