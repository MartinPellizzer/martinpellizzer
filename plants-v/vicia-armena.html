Vicia armena

Vicia armena, commonly known as Armenian vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with small, heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces elongated pods containing small, lens-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Vicia armena is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil enrichment.
