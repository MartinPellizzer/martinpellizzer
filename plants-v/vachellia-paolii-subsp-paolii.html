Vachellia paolii subsp. paolii

Vachellia paolii subsp.

paolii, commonly known as the Red Bushwillow, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs, and its yellow to orange-pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces elongated pods containing several seeds, and it is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It is often found in savannas and along riverbanks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
