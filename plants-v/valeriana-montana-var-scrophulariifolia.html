Valeriana montana var. scrophulariifolia

Valeriana montana var. scrophulariifolia, commonly known as mountain valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features a robust rhizomatous root system and lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers in clustered inflorescences, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its aromatic properties, it has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its calming effects.
