Vulpia megastachya

Vulpia megastachya, commonly known as the giant squirreltail grass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lobed spikelets, which are a distinctive feature of the species. Vulpia megastachya thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
