Vernonia interior var. drummondii

Vernonia interior var. drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky slopes.

Drummond's ironweed is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
