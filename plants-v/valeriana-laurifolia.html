Valeriana laurifolia

Valeriana laurifolia, commonly known as the laurel valerian, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, small white or pinkish flowers. The plant features opposite, simple, and often aromatic leaves that are glossy and lanceolate in shape. Valeriana laurifolia is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

It is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its calming properties.
