Veronica micans

Veronica micans, commonly known as silverweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves that reflect light, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Veronica micans thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

It is a perennial species that spreads through rhizomes, forming dense mats in suitable habitats.
