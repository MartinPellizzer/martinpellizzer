Viola brachysepala

Viola brachysepala, commonly known as the short-stalked violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of violet or purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its flowers have five petals, with the lower petal often bearing a distinctive yellow or orange mark.

Viola brachysepala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
