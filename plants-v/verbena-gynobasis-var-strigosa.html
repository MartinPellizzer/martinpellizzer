Verbena gynobasis var. strigosa

Verbena gynobasis var. strigosa, commonly known as the hairy verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, stiff hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in dry, open habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
