Vernonia borinquensis var. stahlii

Vernonia borinquensis var. stahlii, commonly known as the Puerto Rican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean island of Puerto Rico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and forest margins. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and supporting local biodiversity.
