Viola favratii

Viola favratii, commonly known as the Favrat's violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, delicate flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. Viola favratii is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks, preferring well-drained, loamy soils.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
