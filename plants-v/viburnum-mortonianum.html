Viburnum mortonianum

Viburnum mortonianum, commonly known as the Mortonian hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 feet and features opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the autumn. Viburnum mortonianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its seasonal interest.

It prefers partial shade to full sun and well-drained soil.
