Viola oxyceras

Viola oxyceras, commonly known as the sharp-lobed violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and western Asia, typically growing in woodland and grassland habitats. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces violet-colored flowers with a distinctive, often yellow or white center. It is a perennial herb that thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
