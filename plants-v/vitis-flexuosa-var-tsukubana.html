Vitis flexuosa var. tsukubana

Vitis flexuosa var. tsukubana, commonly known as the Japanese grapevine, is a climbing vine native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, twisting stems and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into clusters of round, sweet-tasting berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

Its botanical features make it a unique species within the Vitaceae family.
