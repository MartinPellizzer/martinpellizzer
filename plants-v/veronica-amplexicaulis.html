Veronica amplexicaulis

Veronica amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, dark green leaves that clasp the stem, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers in racemes during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Veronica amplexicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
