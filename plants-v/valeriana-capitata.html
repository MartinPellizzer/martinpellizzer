Valeriana capitata

Valeriana capitata, commonly known as sweet valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters at the top of the plant. The leaves are typically narrow and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant is often found in meadows, woods, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically for its calming effects.
