Verticordia fontanesii var. brachyphylla

Verticordia fontanesii var. brachyphylla, commonly known as the Geraldton waxplant, is a distinctive shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, waxy, pink or red flowers that appear in late summer to autumn. The leaves are narrow and linear, often with a bluish-green hue, and the plant is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. It is a member of the family Scrophulariaceae and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

This variety is particularly noted for its shorter, more compact inflorescences compared to other verticordia species.
