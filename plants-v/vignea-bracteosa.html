Vignea bracteosa

Vignea bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteate vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and the presence of bracts that often resemble small leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its leaves are usually opposite and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Vignea bracteosa is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.
