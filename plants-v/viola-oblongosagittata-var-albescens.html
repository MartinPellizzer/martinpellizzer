Viola oblongosagittata var. albescens

Viola oblongosagittata var. albescens, commonly known as the white shield violet, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a distinctive white or pale underside. The flowers are typically violet or purple, with five petals and a spurred petal at the back. This variety is distinguished by its white or very pale flowers, which set it apart from other viola species.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
