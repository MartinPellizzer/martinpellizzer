Valerianella olitoria var. serratifolia

Valerianella olitoria var. serratifolia, commonly known as garden sorrel, is a low-growing herbaceous plant with succulent, lobed leaves that have a tangy, sour flavor.

It thrives in cool, moist conditions and is often grown as a leaf vegetable in temperate climates. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that appear in the summer months. Its leaves are typically harvested before flowering for culinary use.

It is a member of the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is valued for its nutritional content and mild, acidic taste.
