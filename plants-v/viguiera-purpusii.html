Viguiera purpusii

Viguiera purpusii, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial herb that belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its showy purple to pink flower heads bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viguiera purpusii is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
