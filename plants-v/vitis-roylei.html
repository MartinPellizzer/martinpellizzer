Vitis roylei

Vitis roylei, commonly known as the Afghan grape, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including Afghanistan, Pakistan, and parts of India. The plant is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by small, round berries.

Vitis roylei is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
