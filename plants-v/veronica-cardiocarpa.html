Veronica cardiocarpa

Veronica cardiocarpa, commonly known as the heartleaf veronica, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Veronica cardiocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
