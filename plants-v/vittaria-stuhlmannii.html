Vittaria stuhlmannii

Vittaria stuhlmannii, commonly known as the Stuhlmann's maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Vittariaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, giving it a finely divided appearance. The plant is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its reproductive structures, including sori and sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many ferns.

Vittaria stuhlmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
