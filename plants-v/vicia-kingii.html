Vicia kingii

Vicia kingii, commonly known as King's vetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Vicia kingii is valued for its role in nitrogen fixation and its use in ecological restoration projects.
