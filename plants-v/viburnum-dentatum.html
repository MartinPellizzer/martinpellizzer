Viburnum dentatum

Viburnum dentatum, commonly known as arrowwood viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges, typically arranged oppositely along its stems. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. Viburnum dentatum is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It also provides food and habitat for wildlife, making it a beneficial addition to native gardens.
