Veronica pulchella

Veronica pulchella, commonly known as the meadow veronica, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its delicate blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Veronica pulchella is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of light conditions.
