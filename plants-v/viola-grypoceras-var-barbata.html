Viola grypoceras var. barbata

Viola grypoceras var. barbata, commonly known as the barbed violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive, hairy texture and produces small, violet-colored flowers with a spurred petal. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The variety is characterized by its unique, barbed bracts that give it its common name.
