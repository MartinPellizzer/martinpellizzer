Viola chamissoniana subsp. tracheliifolia

Viola chamissoniana subsp.

tracheliifolia, commonly known as the slender violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. Its flowers are typically violet or purple, with five petals and a spurred lower petal.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
