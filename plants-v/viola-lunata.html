Viola lunata

Viola lunata, commonly known as the moonflower viola, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Viola lunata is known for its fragrant blooms that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both ornamental and ecological value.
