Vaccinium pubicalyx var. leucocalyx

Vaccinium pubicalyx var. leucocalyx, commonly known as the white-flowered huckleberry, is a variant of the huckleberry plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are white or pale pink, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a glossy surface, while the berries are small, dark blue, and edible.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for both its flowers and fruit.
