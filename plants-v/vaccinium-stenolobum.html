Vaccinium stenolobum

Vaccinium stenolobum, commonly known as the narrowleaf blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are usually pink or white, and it produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
