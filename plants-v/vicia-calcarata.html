Vicia calcarata

Vicia calcarata, commonly known as the Carpathian vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to Central and Eastern Europe, particularly found in forest margins and rocky habitats. The plant features slender, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets. Its flowers are typically pale purple or pink, arranged in clusters, and it produces elongated, twisted pods that contain several seeds.

Vicia calcarata is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for certain insects.
