Vicia communis proles amphicarpa

Vicia communis proles amphicarpa, commonly known as the two-seeded vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in early summer. The plant produces two distinct types of pods, a unique feature that gives it its specific epithet "amphicarpa.". It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

This species is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
