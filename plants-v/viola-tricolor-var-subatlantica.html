Viola tricolor var. subatlantica

Viola tricolor var. subatlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped lobes and produces small, colorful flowers in shades of violet, yellow, and white. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a ground cover.

Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
