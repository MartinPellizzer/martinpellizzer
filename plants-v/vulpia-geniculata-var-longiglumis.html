Vulpia geniculata var. longiglumis

Vulpia geniculata var. longiglumis, commonly known as the long glume vulpia, is a grass species characterized by its slender, erect growth habit.

It typically reaches heights of 20 to 60 centimeters and features narrow, linear leaves. The inflorescence is a spike-like cluster of spikelets, each bearing two to three bracts. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with three stamens and a single pistil.

This variety is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to temperate climates.
