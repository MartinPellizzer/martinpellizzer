Valeria

Valeria, commonly known as valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Caprifoliaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, reddish-brown flowers and aromatic leaves. Valerian grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its calming effects, which have been used for centuries in traditional medicine.

Its rhizomes and roots are the primary parts used in herbal remedies.
