Viola pedunculata subsp. typica

Viola pedunculata subsp.

typica, commonly known as the common violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple underside and produces delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple, violet, and blue.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and meadows. It is part of the Violaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branching stems.

The flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
