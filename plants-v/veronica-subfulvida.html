Veronica subfulvida

Veronica subfulvida, commonly known as the golden veronica, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.
