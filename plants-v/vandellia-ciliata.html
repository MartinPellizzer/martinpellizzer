Vandellia ciliata

Vandellia ciliata, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads surrounded by white bracts. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for coastal gardens and rockeries.

Vandellia ciliata is also valued for its ability to stabilize sand dunes and prevent erosion.
