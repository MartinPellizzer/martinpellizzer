Vicia aktoensis

Vicia aktoensis, commonly known as the Aktoen vetch, is a flowering plant species native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Vicia aktoensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Its biological features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
