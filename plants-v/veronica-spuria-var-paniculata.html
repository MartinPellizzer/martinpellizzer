Veronica spuria var. paniculata

Veronica spuria var. paniculata, commonly known as the panicled speedwell, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, branched inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to naturalize in suitable environments.
