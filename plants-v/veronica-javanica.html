Veronica javanica

Veronica javanica, commonly known as the Java speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often sprawling.

Veronica javanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
