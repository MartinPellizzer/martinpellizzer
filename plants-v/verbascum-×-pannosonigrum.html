Verbascum × pannosonigrum

Verbascum × pannosonigrum, commonly known as the blackleaf mullein, is a hybrid plant in the genus Verbascum, native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are covered in soft hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens across temperate climates.
