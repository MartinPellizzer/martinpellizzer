Vernonia exasperata

Vernonia exasperata, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has rough, hairy leaves that are often grayish-green in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Vernonia exasperata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional medicinal uses in some African cultures.
