Vaccinium haematochroum

Vaccinium haematochroum, commonly known as the blood-red huckleberry, is a rare and striking plant species native to certain regions of northern Europe.

It is characterized by its vivid crimson leaves and berries, which are rich in anthocyanins, giving them their intense coloration. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland or forested areas. Its flowers are small and white, with five petals, and it blooms in early summer.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
