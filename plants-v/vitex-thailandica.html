Vitex thailandica

Vitex thailandica, commonly known as Thai vitex, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, often appearing in summer. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.

Vitex thailandica thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
