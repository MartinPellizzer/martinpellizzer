Vicia baicalensis

Vicia baicalensis, commonly known as the Baikal vetch, is a flowering plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and the Russian Far East.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Vicia baicalensis is adapted to harsh environments, showing resilience to cold temperatures and poor soil conditions.

Its ecological role includes nitrogen fixation, which enriches the soil and supports other plant life.
