Valeriana longiflora

Valeriana longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped berries that are typically reddish when mature. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly rough texture.

Valeriana longiflora is often found in woodland areas and along the edges of forests, thriving in partial shade and well-drained soil.
