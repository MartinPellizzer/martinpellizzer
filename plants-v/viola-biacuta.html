Viola biacuta

Viola biacuta, commonly known as the violet of the Alps, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Violaceae family and typically grows in rocky or moist woodland habitats. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive pattern of purple and white markings. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Viola biacuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
