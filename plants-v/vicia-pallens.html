Vicia pallens

Vicia pallens, commonly known as the white clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is a perennial herb that thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in lawns, meadows, and disturbed areas. Vicia pallens has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and fixes nitrogen, enriching the soil.

It is also a valuable forage plant for livestock and supports various pollinators.
