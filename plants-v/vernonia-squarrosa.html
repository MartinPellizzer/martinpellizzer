Vernonia squarrosa

Vernonia squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrosa ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Vernonia squarrosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
