Viola maviensis var. kohalana

Viola maviensis var. kohalana, commonly known as the Kohalana violet, is a rare and endemic species native to the Hawaiian Islands.

This plant belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and small, colorful flowers that typically range from purple to violet hues. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in coastal forests and rocky areas.

The species is noted for its unique genetic makeup and is of particular interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and conservation status.
