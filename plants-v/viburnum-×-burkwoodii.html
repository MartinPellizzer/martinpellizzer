Viburnum × burkwoodii

Viburnum × burkwoodii, commonly known as the Burkwood viburnum, is a hybrid shrub that exhibits a range of botanical characteristics.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring dense, dark green foliage that turns vibrant red or orange in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by showy, pink to red berries in the summer. Viburnum × burkwoodii is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes.
