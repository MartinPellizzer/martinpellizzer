Veronica catenata subsp. catenata

Veronica catenata subsp.

catenata, commonly known as the chainflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It features slender, upright stems that bear whorled clusters of small, blue to violet flowers.

The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators.
