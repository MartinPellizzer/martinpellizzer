Vilfa rupestris

Vilfa rupestris, commonly known as the rock violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and arid regions.

It features a low-growing habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in spring, typically in clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Vilfa rupestris is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
