Vatica perakensis

Vatica perakensis, commonly known as the Perak ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its hard, dense wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a straight trunk and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and yellowish, arranged in catkins, and it produces woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

Vatica perakensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
