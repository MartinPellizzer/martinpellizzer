Vernonia lindheimeri var. leucophylla

Vernonia lindheimeri var. leucophylla, commonly known as the white-leaf ironweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy clusters of pale yellow to white composite flowers, which are composed of many tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
