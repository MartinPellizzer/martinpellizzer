Vitex schaueriana

Vitex schaueriana, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 feet. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in the summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to various soil types.

Vitex schaueriana is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
