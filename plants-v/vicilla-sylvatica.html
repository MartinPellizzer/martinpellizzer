Vicilla sylvatica

Vicilla sylvatica, commonly known as the wood violet, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in woodlands, hedgerows, and moist grassy areas. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Vicilla sylvatica is often found in shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
