Veronica amplexicaulis f. amplexicaulis

Veronica amplexicaulis f. amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping speedwell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of oval, glossy leaves that tightly clasp the stem, giving it its common name. The plant produces spikes of small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in rock gardens and borders.
