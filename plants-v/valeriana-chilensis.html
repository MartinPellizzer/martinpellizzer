Valeriana chilensis

Valeriana chilensis, commonly known as Chilean valerian, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its rhizomes and roots are used in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

Valeriana chilensis is valued both for its ecological role and its potential medicinal applications.
