Vicia ciceroidea

Vicia ciceroidea, commonly known as the Sicilian vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial legume that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its trifoliate leaves are compound, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Vicia ciceroidea is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
