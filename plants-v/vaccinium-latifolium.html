Vaccinium latifolium

Vaccinium latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf blueberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its oval, broad leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in late spring. The berries, which are typically blue or purple, are edible and have a tart flavor.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.
