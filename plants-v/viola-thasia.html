Viola thasia

Viola thasia, commonly known as the golden viola, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland environments and thrives in partial to full shade. Viola thasia is known for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its natural propagation.
