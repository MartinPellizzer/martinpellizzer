Vernonia streptoclada

Vernonia streptoclada, commonly known as the red vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its conical inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

The showy yellow flowers appear in late summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
