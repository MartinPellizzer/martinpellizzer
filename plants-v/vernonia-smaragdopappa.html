Vernonia smaragdopappa

Vernonia smaragdopappa, commonly known as the Emerald Pappa Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and wetlands, and it thrives in full sun. Its foliage is often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs, giving it a distinctive, almost velvety texture.

The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
