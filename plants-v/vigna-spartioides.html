Vigna spartioides

Vigna spartioides, commonly known as the desert cowpea, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a legume characterized by its small, compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, elongated pods containing edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines. Vigna spartioides is adapted to thrive in poor soil conditions and can withstand drought due to its deep root system.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility in its native habitats.
