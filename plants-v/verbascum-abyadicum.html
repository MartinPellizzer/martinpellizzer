Verbascum abyadicum

Verbascum abyadicum, commonly known as the Abyssinian mullein.

It is a perennial herb native to the highlands of Ethiopia, characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, gray-green leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

Verbascum abyadicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
