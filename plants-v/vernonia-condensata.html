Vernonia condensata

Vernonia condensata, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, blue-gray foliage and clusters of small, lavender-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is often found in wetlands and moist woodlands. Its compact growth habit and attractive appearance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Vernonia condensata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
