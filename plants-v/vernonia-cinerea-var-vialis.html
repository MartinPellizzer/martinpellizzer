Vernonia cinerea var. vialis

Vernonia cinerea var. vialis, commonly known as silverleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in USDA zones 6 to 9. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, which contribute to its silvery appearance.

The species is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
