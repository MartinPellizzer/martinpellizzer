Vernoniastrum ugandense

Vernoniastrum ugandense, commonly known as the Uganda ironweed, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and forest margins. It produces showy yellow to orange flower heads that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
