Viola perrobusta

Viola perrobusta, commonly known as the robust violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring and are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola perrobusta is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
