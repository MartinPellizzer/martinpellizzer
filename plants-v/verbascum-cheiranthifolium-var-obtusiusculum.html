Verbascum cheiranthifolium var. obtusiusculum

Verbascum cheiranthifolium var. obtusiusculum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in soft hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
