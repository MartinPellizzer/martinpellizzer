Vernonia baldwinii subsp. interior

Vernonia baldwinii subsp.

interior, commonly known as the interior ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered yellow flowers in late summer. Its flowering period attracts various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The species is characterized by its robust growth and adaptability to wet conditions.
