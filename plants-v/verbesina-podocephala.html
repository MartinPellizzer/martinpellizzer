Verbesina podocephala

Verbesina podocephala, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, violet-pink flower heads with a distinctive, spiky central cone, surrounded by narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has rough, hairy stems and is often found in moist, open woodlands or along stream banks.
