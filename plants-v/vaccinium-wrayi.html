Vaccinium wrayi

Vaccinium wrayi, commonly known as the Wray's blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale pink flowers. Its fruits are small, dark blue berries that ripen in late summer and are edible, though often tart.

Vaccinium wrayi thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild winters and warm, dry summers.
