Vernonia amoena

Vernonia amoena, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, blue-violet flower heads that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its feathery leaves and fluffy flower clusters make it an attractive addition to gardens and natural habitats.

Vernonia amoena is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
