Veronica scutellata var. pubescens

Veronica scutellata var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy mouse-eared chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The leaves are ovate and covered in fine, hairy texture, giving it its common name. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
