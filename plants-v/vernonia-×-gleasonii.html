Vernonia × gleasonii

Vernonia × gleasonii, commonly known as the hybrid ironweed, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various pollinators.
