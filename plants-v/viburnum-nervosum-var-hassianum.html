Viburnum nervosum var. hassianum

Viburnum nervosum var. hassianum, commonly known as the Hassia viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with a serrated margin and a prominent venation pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators and providing food for birds.
