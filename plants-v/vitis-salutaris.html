Vitis salutaris

Vitis salutaris, commonly known as the American grapevine, is a deciduous vine native to North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces edible berries that range in color from green to black, depending on the variety. Vitis salutaris is often found in wetland areas and along riverbanks, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity.
