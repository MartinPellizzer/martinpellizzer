Vernonia wittei

Vernonia wittei, commonly known as the Witte's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Vernonia wittei is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and beauty.
