Vetiveria venusta

Vetiveria venusta, commonly known as the golden beard grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, fibrous root system that helps prevent soil erosion. The plant produces tall, slender stems with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often yellowish or golden in color.

Vetiveria venusta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in soil stabilization.
