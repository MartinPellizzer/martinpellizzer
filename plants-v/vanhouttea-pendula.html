Vanhouttea pendula

Vanhouttea pendula, commonly known as the hanging vanhouttea, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous inflorescences, which consist of small, white or pale yellow flowers clustered in drooping clusters. The plant has simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Vanhouttea pendula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
