Verbascum spectabile var. cordatum

Verbascum spectabile var. cordatum, commonly known as the heart-leaved mullein, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, fuzzy, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tall spikes of yellow flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
