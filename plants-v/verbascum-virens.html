Verbascum virens

Verbascum virens, commonly known as green flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Verbascum virens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
