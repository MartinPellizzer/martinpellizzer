Vigna trilobata var. pusilla

Vigna trilobata var. pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain round, tender seeds used in various cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soil and is often grown as a cover crop or for its nutritional value.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to a range of environmental conditions.
