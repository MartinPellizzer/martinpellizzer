Veronica longifolia subsp. exortiva

Veronica longifolia subsp.

exortiva, commonly known as the tall speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous or coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and the stems are often branched.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
