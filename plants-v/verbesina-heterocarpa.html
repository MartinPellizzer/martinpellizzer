Verbesina heterocarpa

Verbesina heterocarpa, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in wetlands and along stream banks, preferring moist, well-drained soil.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
