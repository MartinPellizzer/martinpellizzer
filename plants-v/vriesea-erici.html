Vriesea erici

Vriesea erici, commonly known as the Christmas Vriesea, is a species of bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, spathaceous inflorescence that resembles a crown, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that collect water, which is used by the plant for hydration. Vriesea erici thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

It is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its vibrant blooms and compact size.
