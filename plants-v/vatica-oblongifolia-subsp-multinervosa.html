Vatica oblongifolia subsp. multinervosa

Vatica oblongifolia subsp.

multinervosa, commonly known as the multi-nerved ironwood, is a tropical tree species native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and distinctive leaf venation patterns.

The leaves are typically elliptical with a prominent midrib and multiple lateral veins, giving it the "multinervosa" designation. It thrives in humid, lowland forest environments and is often found in primary and secondary rainforests.

The plant is valued for its timber and has ecological significance in its native habitats.
