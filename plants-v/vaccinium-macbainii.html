Vaccinium macbainii

Vaccinium macbainii, commonly known as the MacBain huckleberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants such as blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by edible berries that are dark blue to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

Vaccinium macbainii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
