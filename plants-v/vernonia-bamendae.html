Vernonia bamendae

Vernonia bamendae, commonly known as the Cameroon ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Cameroon.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Vernonia bamendae is often found in open woodlands and along forest edges in its natural habitat.
