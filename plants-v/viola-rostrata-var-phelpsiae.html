Viola rostrata var. phelpsiae

Viola rostrata var. phelpsiae, commonly known as the Phelps' violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet or purple hue, often adorned with a white or light green midrib. The plant produces small, five-petaled flowers that are typically violet or blue, with a yellow throat. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
