Verbesina tomentosa

Verbesina tomentosa, commonly known as rough hound's tongue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and bright yellow, dandelion-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
