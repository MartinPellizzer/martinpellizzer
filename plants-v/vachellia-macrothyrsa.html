Vachellia macrothyrsa

Vachellia macrothyrsa, commonly known as the giant burr acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and compound leaves that are typically trifoliate. The plant produces yellow flowers in rounded or cylindrical clusters, which are followed by long, curved pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in savannas and rocky areas.

Vachellia macrothyrsa is well adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in preventing soil erosion.
