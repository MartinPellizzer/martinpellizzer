Verbesina parviflora

Verbesina parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered beggar-tick, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges and are often covered in sticky secretions. It thrives in moist, sunny environments such as meadows and streambanks.

This plant is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common sight in various habitats.
