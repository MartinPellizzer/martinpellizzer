Vernonia centauroides

Vernonia centauroides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads that resemble mist. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and has lance-shaped, hairy leaves. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Vernonia centauroides is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
