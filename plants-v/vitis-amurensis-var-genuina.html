Vitis amurensis var. genuina

Vitis amurensis var. genuina, commonly known as the Amur grape, is a hardy vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and ability to thrive in cold climates, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate regions. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are often used in winemaking and traditional medicine. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it has woody stems that can climb or trail along the ground.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
