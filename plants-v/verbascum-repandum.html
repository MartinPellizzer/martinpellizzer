Verbascum repandum

Verbascum repandum, commonly known as the common mullein, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of soft, woolly hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are grouped in clusters at the top of the stem. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, fields, and waste areas.

Verbascum repandum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its soothing effects on respiratory ailments.
