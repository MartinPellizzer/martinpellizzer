Verbesina minarum

Verbesina minarum, commonly known as the yellow beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in moist, disturbed soils. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with prominent bracts, which are often mistaken for petals. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and often branched.

It is commonly found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.
