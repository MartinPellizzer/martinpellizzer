Vriesea Ynghii

Ynghii's vriesea, scientifically known as Vriesea ynghii, is a striking member of the bromeliad family native to the cloud forests of Ecuador. This unique plant is named after the renowned botanist and horticulturist, Dr. Ynghii, who first documented its discovery in the 1980s. Its most notable feature is its vibrant, spiky inflorescence that resembles a crown, making it a favorite among plant enthusiasts. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to flourish. Due to its rarity and beauty, Vriesea ynghii has become a sought-after specimen in both botanical gardens and private collections.
