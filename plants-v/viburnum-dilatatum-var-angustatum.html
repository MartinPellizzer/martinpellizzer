Viburnum dilatatum var. angustatum

Viburnum dilatatum var. angustatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf arrowwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, lanceolate leaves that are typically dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by bright red, persistent berries in the fall. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
