Vulpiella incrassata var. tenuis

Vulpiella incrassata var. tenuis, commonly known as the slender foxglove, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The leaves are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This variant is known for its adaptability to high-altitude environments and its preference for well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
