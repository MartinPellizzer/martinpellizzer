Vignea polyphylla

Vignea polyphylla, commonly known as the many-leafed vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and compound leaves that can have multiple leaflets. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. Vignea polyphylla is often found in humid environments, such as rainforests or along riverbanks.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a common ornamental and sometimes invasive species in certain regions.
