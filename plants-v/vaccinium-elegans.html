Vaccinium elegans

Vaccinium elegans, commonly known as the slender blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of Oregon and California.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, which are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth. Its leaves are oval-shaped, with a glossy green surface and a slightly hairy underside.

Vaccinium elegans is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
