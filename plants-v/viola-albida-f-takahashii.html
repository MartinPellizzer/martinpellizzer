Viola albida f. takahashii

Viola albida f. takahashii, commonly known as the white violet or white viola, is a flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, white or pale lavender flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant is native to Japan and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a subtle beauty to the landscape.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
