Verbena pedicellata

Verbena pedicellata, commonly known as the common vervain, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces spikes of blue to violet flowers. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Verbena pedicellata is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
