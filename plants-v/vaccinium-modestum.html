Vaccinium modestum

Vaccinium modestum, commonly known as the lowbush blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other acid-loving plants like heathers and rhododendrons. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark blue to black in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests.

Vaccinium modestum is an important plant for both wildlife and commercial blueberry cultivation.
