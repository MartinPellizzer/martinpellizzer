Vitis linnaei

Vitis linnaei, commonly known as the Linnaean grape, is a woody vine native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with five to seven leaflets and small, clustered greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to dark purple as they mature. Vitis linnaei is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, thriving in partial shade.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in botanical studies.
