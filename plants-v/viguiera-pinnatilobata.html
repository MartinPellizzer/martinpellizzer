Viguiera pinnatilobata

Viguiera pinnatilobata, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Viguiera pinnatilobata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
