Vigna lozanii

Vigna lozanii, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often used in culinary applications. Vigna lozanii is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important crop in sustainable agriculture.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for warm climates and poor soils.
