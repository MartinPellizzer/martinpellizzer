Vernoniastrum

Vernoniastrum, commonly known as blueweed, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

These plants are native to various regions of the Americas and are characterized by their narrow, grass-like leaves and showy blue or purple flower heads. Vernoniastrum species typically grow in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. The flowers are usually arranged in dense, spike-like clusters and are visited by a variety of pollinators.
