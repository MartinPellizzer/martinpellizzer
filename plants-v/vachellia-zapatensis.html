Vachellia zapatensis

Vachellia zapatensis, commonly known as the Zapatilla acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Vachellia zapatensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates.
