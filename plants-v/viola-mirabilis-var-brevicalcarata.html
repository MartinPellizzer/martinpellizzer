Viola mirabilis var. brevicalcarata

Viola mirabilis var. brevicalcarata, commonly known as the dwarf shield violet, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow eye. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to woodland gardens.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in temperate climates.
