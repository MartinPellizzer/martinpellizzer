Veronica punctata

Veronica punctata, commonly known as the spotted speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue or violet flowers arranged in clusters. The leaves are typically ovate and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
