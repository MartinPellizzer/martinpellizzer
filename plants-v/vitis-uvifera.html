Vitis uvifera

Vitis uvifera, commonly known as the fox grape, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe, North Africa, and western Asia. The plant is characterized by its climbing vines, palmately lobed leaves, and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its berries, which are typically red or black when mature, are edible and have been used historically for winemaking and culinary purposes.

Vitis uvifera is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens.
