Viola robinsonii

Viola robinsonii, commonly known as the Robinson's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodland habitats, where it thrives in the understory of forests. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces delicate purple or violet flowers in early spring. Its botanical characteristics include a short, hairy stem and a root system composed of rhizomes.

Viola robinsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
