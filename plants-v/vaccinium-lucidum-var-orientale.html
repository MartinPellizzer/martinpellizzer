Vaccinium lucidum var. orientale

Vaccinium lucidum var. orientale, commonly known as the oriental huckleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The berries are not only ornamental but also have culinary and medicinal uses in traditional practices.
