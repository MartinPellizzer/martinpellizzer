Vinticena flavicans

Vinticena flavicans, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange flowers, which bloom in clusters during the wet season. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Vinticena flavicans is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display.
