Vernonia pseudojugalis

Vernonia pseudojugalis, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, erect growth habit. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its showy, yellow-orange flower heads appear in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid environments.
