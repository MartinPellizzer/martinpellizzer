Vitex negundo f. alba

Vitex negundo f. alba, commonly known as white vitex or white fragrant vitex, is a variant of the common vitex that is distinguished by its white flowers.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Asia, particularly China, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, fragrant flowers in panicles during the summer months. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, aromatic foliage, and a tendency to form dense, bushy growth.

It is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
