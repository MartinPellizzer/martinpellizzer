Varasia riparia

Varasia riparia, commonly known as the riverbank beauty, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland and riparian habitats.

It typically grows in moist soils along streams, rivers, and floodplains, showcasing its adaptability to aquatic environments. The plant features slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its small, white or greenish flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Varasia riparia plays a vital role in stabilizing riverbanks and supporting local biodiversity.
