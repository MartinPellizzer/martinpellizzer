Vaccinium canadense

Vaccinium canadense, commonly known as Canadian blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other berry-producing plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is commonly found in forested areas.

Vaccinium canadense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
