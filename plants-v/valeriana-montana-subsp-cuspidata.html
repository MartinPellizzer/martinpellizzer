Valeriana montana subsp. cuspidata

Valeriana montana subsp.

cuspidata, commonly known as the alpine valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high elevations where temperatures are cool and conditions are harsh.

The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a strong, often aromatic scent. Valeriana montana subsp.

cuspidata is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
