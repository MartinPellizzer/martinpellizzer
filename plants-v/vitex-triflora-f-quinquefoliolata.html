Vitex triflora f. quinquefoliolata

Vitex triflora f. quinquefoliolata, commonly known as the five-leaf chaste tree, is a variant of the chaste tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, featuring five leaflets per leaf, which gives it its scientific name. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy green surface.

The flowers are usually blue or purple, appearing in clusters and blooming in the summer months.
