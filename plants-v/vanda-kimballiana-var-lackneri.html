Vanda kimballiana var. lackneri

Vanda kimballiana var. lackneri, commonly known as the Kimball's vanda, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with a distinct red or purple lip. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining potting media.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate under proper care conditions.
