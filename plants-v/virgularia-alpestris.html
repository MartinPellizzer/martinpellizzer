Virgularia alpestris

Virgularia alpestris, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Virgularia alpestris is known for its resilience to cold climates and is often found in alpine meadows and tundra regions.
