Veronica perpusilla

Veronica perpusilla, commonly known as the small-speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks.

Veronica perpusilla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden landscapes for its delicate appearance.
