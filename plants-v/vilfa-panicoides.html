Vilfa panicoides

Vilfa panicoides, commonly known as the panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping or as a forage plant.
