Viola pseudogracilis subsp. cassinensis

Viola pseudogracilis subsp.

cassinensis, commonly known as the Cassin's viola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States. It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture.

The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of violet or purple, usually with five petals. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clustered inflorescence.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both ecological studies and horticultural interest.
