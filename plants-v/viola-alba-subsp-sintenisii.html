Viola alba subsp. sintenisii

Viola alba subsp.

sintenisii, commonly known as the white violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, pale green leaves and showy white or pale blue flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. Viola alba subsp.

sintenisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
