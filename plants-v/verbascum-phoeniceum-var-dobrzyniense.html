Verbascum phoeniceum var. dobrzyniense

Verbascum phoeniceum var. dobrzyniense, commonly known as the Dobrzynian thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features tall, erect stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, covered with dense, woolly hair. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in clustered inflorescences, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a distinctive silvery-white coating on the upper surface.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
