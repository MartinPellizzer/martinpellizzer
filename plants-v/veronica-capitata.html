Veronica capitata

Veronica capitata, commonly known as the clustered speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically features opposite leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Veronica capitata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
