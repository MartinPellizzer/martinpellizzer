Viola nuttallii subsp. vallicola

Viola nuttallii subsp.

vallicola, commonly known as the valley violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and forest understories.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, violet-colored flowers in early spring. It has a fibrous root system that helps it anchor in rocky or sandy soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to cool, temperate climates.
