Viola dyris var. orientalis

Viola dyris var. orientalis, commonly known as the Persian violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of violet-blue to purple flowers with yellow centers, typically blooming in spring. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
