Vernonia erigeroides

Vernonia erigeroides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flower heads that resemble mist. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Vernonia erigeroides is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
