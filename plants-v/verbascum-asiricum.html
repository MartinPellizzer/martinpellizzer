Verbascum asiricum

Verbascum asiricum, commonly known as the Asir mullein, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, fuzzy, ovate leaves that are covered in soft, woolly hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

Verbascum asiricum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
