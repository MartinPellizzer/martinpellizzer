Vachellia robusta

Vachellia robusta, commonly known as the Natal umbrella thorn, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its thorny branches and dense foliage. The plant produces yellow flowers in racemes, which are followed by flat, bean-like pods containing seeds. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small leaflets, and it thrives in dry, sandy soils.

Vachellia robusta is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing shelter and food for various wildlife.
