Vernonia lujae

Vernonia lujae, commonly known as the Luja's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks and in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small, yellowish, and arranged in dense clusters.

Vernonia lujae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
