Verbascum × pterocalyciniforme

Verbascum × pterocalyciniforme, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant belonging to the Scrophulariaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, velvety gray-green leaves that are covered in soft, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This hybrid species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
