Viola peramoena

Viola peramoena, commonly known as the purple violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are typically 2-3 cm in diameter, with five petals and a distinctive spur at the base.

Viola peramoena is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
