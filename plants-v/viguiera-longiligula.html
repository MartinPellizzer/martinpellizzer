Viguiera longiligula

Viguiera longiligula, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display. Viguiera longiligula thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.

It is also valued for its ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
