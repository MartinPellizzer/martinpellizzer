Vialia macrophylla

Vialia macrophylla, commonly known as the broad-leaf vialia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its botanical name reflects its distinctive large foliage, with "macrophylla" referring to the large leaves.

Vialia macrophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates.
