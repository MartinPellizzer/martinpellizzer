Verbascum lyratifolium var. linearilobum

Verbascum lyratifolium var. linearilobum, commonly known as the linear-lobed mullein, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.
