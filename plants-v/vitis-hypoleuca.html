Vitis hypoleuca

Vitis hypoleuca, commonly known as the whitebark grape, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white to pale green flowers. The plant produces small, sweet berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. Vitis hypoleuca is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
