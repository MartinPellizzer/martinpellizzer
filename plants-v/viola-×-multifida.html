Viola × multifida

Viola × multifida, commonly known as the broadleaf viola, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, often lobed leaves that are broader than those of its parent species. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of violet, purple, and white, typically in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or garden settings.

Viola × multifida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
