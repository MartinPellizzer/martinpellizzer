Viburnum cordifolium

Viburnum cordifolium, commonly known as the heartleaf viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to cordate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.

Viburnum cordifolium is also valued for its use in traditional herbal medicine.
