Vitis thunbergii

Vitis thunbergii, commonly known as the Chinese grapevine, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, round berries that range in color from green to dark purple, depending on the maturity. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.

Vitis thunbergii is also valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil conditions.
