Vitis vinifera var. rubra

Vitis vinifera var. rubra, commonly known as the red grapevine, is a cultivar of the common grapevine species.

It is characterized by its deep red to purple foliage and clusters of small, dark berries. The plant is widely cultivated for its fruit, which is used in winemaking and culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
