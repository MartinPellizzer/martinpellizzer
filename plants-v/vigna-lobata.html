Vigna lobata

Vigna lobata, commonly known as the large cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with pinnate leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several large, kidney-shaped seeds. Vigna lobata is valued for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines.

It also plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with Rhizobia bacteria.
