Vaccinium kunthianum

Vaccinium kunthianum, commonly known as the Florida huckleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible and often used in local cuisine. Its leaves are alternate, oval-shaped, and have a leathery texture.

Vaccinium kunthianum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and pine flatwoods.
