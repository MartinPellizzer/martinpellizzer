Viola mirabilis var. langsdorffii

Viola mirabilis var. langsdorffii, commonly known as the Langsdorff's violet, is a flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in moist, shady environments. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant colors and compact growth habit.

The species is also noted for its ability to hybridize with other viola species, contributing to its popularity in horticulture.
