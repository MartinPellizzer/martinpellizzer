Valeriana bertiscea

Valeriana bertiscea, commonly known as the mountain valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils, thriving in high-altitude environments. Its root system is fibrous and contains compounds that have been traditionally used in herbal medicine.

Valeriana bertiscea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitats.
