Viola consanguinea

Viola consanguinea, commonly known as the common violet, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Violaceae family and typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy violet flowers with a distinctive pattern of purple and white markings. It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to partial shade.

Viola consanguinea is also valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in herbal medicine.
