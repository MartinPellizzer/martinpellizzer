Viola mirabilis

Viola mirabilis, commonly known as the wonder violet, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the landscape.

Viola mirabilis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
