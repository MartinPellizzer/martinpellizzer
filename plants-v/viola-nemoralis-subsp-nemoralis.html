Viola nemoralis subsp. nemoralis

Viola nemoralis subsp.

nemoralis, commonly known as the common violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa. It typically grows in woodland edges, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in partial shade to full sun.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and produces violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center. It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, forming dense colonies in suitable habitats.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
