Vittadinia eremaea

Vittadinia eremaea, commonly known as the Emu Bush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring hairy, often glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots and a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Vittadinia eremaea is often used in native landscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
