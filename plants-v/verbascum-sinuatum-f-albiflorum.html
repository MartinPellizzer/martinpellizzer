Verbascum sinuatum f. albiflorum

Verbascum sinuatum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the whiteflower mullein, is a cultivated form of the common mullein.

It is characterized by its large, fuzzy, velvety leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, white flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.

This variety is often grown for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in gardens.
