Vitis teysmannii

Vitis teysmannii, commonly known as the Teyssmann's grape, is a species of flowering vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that range in color from green to red. Vitis teysmannii is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural and ecological studies.
