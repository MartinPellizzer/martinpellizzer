Veronica hectorii subsp. hectorii

Veronica hectorii subsp.

hectorii, commonly known as Hector's speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States. It typically features small, blue to violet flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer.

The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white, powdery wax. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
