Verbena erinoides var. contracta

Verbena erinoides var. contracta, commonly known as the narrowleaf verbena, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States.

It features small, vibrant blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, upright leaves and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant due to its drought tolerance. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it ideal for xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, ornamental plants.
