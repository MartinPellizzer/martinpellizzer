Viola cucullata var. cordata

Viola cucullata var. cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces showy violet flowers with purple petals and a distinctive yellow center. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
