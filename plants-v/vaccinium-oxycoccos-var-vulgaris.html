Vaccinium oxycoccos var. vulgaris

Vaccinium oxycoccos var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common bilberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It produces small, dark blue berries that are rich in antioxidants and anthocyanins. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in boreal and temperate forest understories. Its leaves are elliptical with a leathery texture and a distinctive waxy coating.

The species is valued both for its ecological role and for its edible fruits.
