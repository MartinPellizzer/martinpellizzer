Viola tricolor f. luteocoerulescens

Viola tricolor f. luteocoerulescens, commonly known as the blue and yellow violet, is a cultivar of the common violet that exhibits a striking coloration pattern.

Its petals display a unique combination of blue and yellow hues, with a distinctive cobalt-blue center and a yellow edge. The plant is a perennial herbaceous species native to Europe, characterized by heart-shaped leaves and clusters of showy flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The foliage is typically dark green, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.
