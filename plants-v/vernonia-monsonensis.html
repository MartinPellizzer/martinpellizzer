Vernonia monsonensis

Vernonia monsonensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian ironwood, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy yellow to orange composite flowers. The plant thrives in coastal and dry forest habitats, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It is valued for its hardiness and role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.

Vernonia monsonensis is also culturally significant to Native Hawaiians.
