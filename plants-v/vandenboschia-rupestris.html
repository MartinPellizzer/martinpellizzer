Vandenboschia rupestris

Vandenboschia rupestris, commonly known as the rock fig, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, pale green or yellowish flowers that are typically borne in clusters. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Vandenboschia rupestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh conditions.
