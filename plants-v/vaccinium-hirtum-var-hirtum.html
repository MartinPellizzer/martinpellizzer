Vaccinium hirtum var. hirtum

Vaccinium hirtum var. hirtum, commonly known as the hairy blueberry, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny environments. The plant features small, oval leaves that are covered in fine, hairy pubescence, giving it its common name. Its flowers are white and bell-shaped, and it produces small, edible berries that are often used in culinary applications.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators.
