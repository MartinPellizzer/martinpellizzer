Vanilla africana subsp. ramosa

Vanilla africana subsp.

ramosa, commonly known as the African vanilla or African orchid, is a climbing orchid native to tropical Africa. It produces fragrant, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically pollinated by specific bees.

The plant is characterized by its long, slender stems and dense, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The subspecies is notable for its potential in horticulture and as a source of natural vanilla flavoring.
