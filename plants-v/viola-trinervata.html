Viola trinervata

Viola trinervata, commonly known as the three-veined violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with three distinct veins, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. It is native to regions in Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

Viola trinervata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and woodland edges.
