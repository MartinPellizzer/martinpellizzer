Velezia tunicoides

Velezia tunicoides, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

Velezia tunicoides is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought conditions.
