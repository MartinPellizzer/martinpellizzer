Viola crassicornis

Viola crassicornis, commonly known as the broad-horned violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola crassicornis is known for its robust growth and adaptability to various soil types.
