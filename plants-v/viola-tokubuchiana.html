Viola tokubuchiana

Viola tokubuchiana, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of violet, pink, and white. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Viola tokubuchiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
