Vernonia pseudocorymbosa

Vernonia pseudocorymbosa, commonly known as the false ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed habitats such as wetlands and swampy areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers range from yellow to orange in color.

Vernonia pseudocorymbosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
