Veronica nevadensis var. langei

Veronica nevadensis var. langei, commonly known as the Lange's speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of white hair.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.
