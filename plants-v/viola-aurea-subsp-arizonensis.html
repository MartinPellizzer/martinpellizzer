Viola aurea subsp. arizonensis

Viola aurea subsp.

arizonensis, commonly known as the Arizona violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its heart-shaped, hairy leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually solitary and have five petals, with the lower petal being spurred.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens despite its preference for specific ecological conditions.
