Viola pumila var. cordifolia

Viola pumila var. cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf violet, is a compact perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy, with a distinctive violet-blue flower that blooms in early spring. The plant grows low to the ground, often in moist, shaded woodlands, and has a mat-forming habit. Its flowers have five petals, with the lower petal often bearing a distinctive yellow or white mark.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
