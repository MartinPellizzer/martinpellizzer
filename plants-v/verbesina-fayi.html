Verbesina fayi

Verbesina fayi, commonly known as the Florida beggar's tick, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetlands and disturbed soils. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

It is characterized by its ability to thrive in moist environments and is often found in swampy areas.
