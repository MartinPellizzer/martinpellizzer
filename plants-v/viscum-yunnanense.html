Viscum yunnanense

Viscum yunnanense, commonly known as the Yunnan mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees, primarily in the forests of China.

It belongs to the family Viscaceae and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of white or yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its parasitic nature allows it to draw water and nutrients from its host, while still performing photosynthesis to some extent.

Viscum yunnanense is often found in humid, temperate climates and plays a role in local ecosystems as both a parasite and a food source.
