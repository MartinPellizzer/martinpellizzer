Viola maculata f. chilensis

Viola maculata f. chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is characterized by its clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the landscape.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ease of care.
