Veronica cardiocarpa subsp. nanella

Veronica cardiocarpa subsp.

nanella, commonly known as the dwarf heartleaf veronica, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of only a few centimeters.

The plant features small, dark green leaves that are heart-shaped, giving it its common name. Its flowers are usually blue or purple and bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months.

It is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover due to its compact size and ornamental appeal.
