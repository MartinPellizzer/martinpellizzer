Veronica distans var. pubescens

Veronica distans var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy veronica, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, giving the plant its "hairy" appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variant is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
