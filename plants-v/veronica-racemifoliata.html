Veronica racemifoliata

Veronica racemifoliata, commonly known as the clustered speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal areas. The plant features small, blue or violet flowers arranged in racemes, which are elongated clusters. Its leaves are alternate and usually have a hairy texture.

Veronica racemifoliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
