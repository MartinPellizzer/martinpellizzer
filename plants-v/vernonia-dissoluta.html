Vernonia dissoluta

Vernonia dissoluta, commonly known as the brittleironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats. Its structure includes a branched stem with narrow, linear leaves that help reduce water loss.

Vernonia dissoluta is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its resilience in arid environments.
