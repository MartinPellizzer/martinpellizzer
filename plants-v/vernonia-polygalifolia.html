Vernonia polygalifolia

Vernonia polygalifolia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. Vernonia polygalifolia is a hardy perennial that is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.

It attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to wildlife-friendly landscapes.
