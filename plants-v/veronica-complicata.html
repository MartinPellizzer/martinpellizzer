Veronica complicata

Veronica complicata, commonly known as the clustered speedwell, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. The plant features opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland margins or along stream banks.

Veronica complicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
