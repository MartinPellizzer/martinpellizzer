Vitis pyrrhodasys

Vitis pyrrhodasys, commonly known as the red-leaf grapevine, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn red in the fall, and its small, dark blue berries that are typically not eaten by humans. The plant thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil. Its vigorous growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or arbors, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Vitis pyrrhodasys is also valued for its adaptability and resilience to various environmental conditions.
