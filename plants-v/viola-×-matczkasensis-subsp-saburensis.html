Viola × matczkasensis subsp. saburensis

Viola × matczkasensis subsp.

saburensis, commonly known as the Saburensis violet, is a hybrid species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm, featuring heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture.

The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with five petals, often with a distinctive white or yellowish throat. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
