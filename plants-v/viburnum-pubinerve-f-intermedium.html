Viburnum pubinerve f. intermedium

Viburnum pubinerve f. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate pubescent viburnum, is a compact, multi-stemmed shrub characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit.

It features ovate to elliptical leaves that are hairy on both sides, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in late spring, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits in the fall. It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in partial shade to full sun, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
