Vaccinium shikokianum

Vaccinium shikokianum, commonly known as the Shikoku huckleberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants such as blueberries and cranberries. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. Its small, dark blue berries are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in local culinary traditions.

The plant's foliage consists of leathery, elliptical leaves that remain green throughout the year.
