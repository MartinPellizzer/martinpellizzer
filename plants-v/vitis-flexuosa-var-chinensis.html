Vitis flexuosa var. chinensis

Vitis flexuosa var. chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese grapevine, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by yellow or orange berries that are edible. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
