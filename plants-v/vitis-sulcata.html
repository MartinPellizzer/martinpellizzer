Vitis sulcata

Vitis sulcata, commonly known as the Sulcate Grape, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to parts of Africa and is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by black, spherical berries. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a distinctive deep groove along the center.

Vitis sulcata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
