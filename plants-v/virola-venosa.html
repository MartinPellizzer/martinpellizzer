Virola venosa

Virola venosa, commonly known as the pink wood or pink mahogany, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its dark reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

Virola venosa is often found in lowland rainforests and is an important species in traditional medicine and timber industries.
