Viola pesneaui

Viola pesneaui, commonly known as the Sierra viola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of violet or purple. Viola pesneaui is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

It plays an important ecological role by supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
