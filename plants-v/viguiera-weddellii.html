Viguiera weddellii

Viguiera weddellii, commonly known as the Weddell's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces rough, hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Viguiera weddellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
