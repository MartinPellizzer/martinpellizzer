Viola mirabilis subsp. subglabra

Viola mirabilis subsp.

subglabra, commonly known as the velvetleaf viola, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are softly hairy, giving the plant its common name.

The flowers are typically violet or purple, with five petals and a distinctive spurred petal at the back. It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
