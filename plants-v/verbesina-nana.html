Verbesina nana

Verbesina nana, commonly known as dwarf beggar-ticks, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features small, hairy leaves and produces clusters of yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit and is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Verbesina nana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
