Verbascum sheilae

Verbascum sheilae, commonly known as Sheilae's mullein, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their fuzzy, woolly leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers, which are usually yellow or orange, bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Verbascum sheilae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
