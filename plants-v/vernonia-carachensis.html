Vernonia carachensis

Vernonia carachensis, commonly known as the Caribbean ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its conical flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as coastal areas and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are usually yellow or brown.

Vernonia carachensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some Caribbean cultures.
