Vitex amboniensis var. amaniensis

Vitex amboniensis var. amaniensis, commonly known as the Amani vitex, is a shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom during the wet season. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is often found in moist, forested areas and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal uses.
