Valeriana mathewsii

Valeriana mathewsii, commonly known as Mathews' valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant, leaves and flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or rocky slopes. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and are usually pink or white in color.

Valeriana mathewsii is valued for its medicinal properties, with historical use in traditional medicine for its calming effects.
