Vatica endertii

Vatica endertii, commonly known as the Endert's ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree typically grows in lowland rainforests and can reach heights of up to 40 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside.

Vatica endertii plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
