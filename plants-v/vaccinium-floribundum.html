Vaccinium floribundum

Vaccinium floribundum, commonly known as the common blueberry, is a shrub native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and vitamins. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires cool, humid conditions.

Vaccinium floribundum is widely cultivated for both its ornamental value and its fruit.
