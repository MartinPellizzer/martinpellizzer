Viola methodiana

Viola methodiana, commonly known as the Italian violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in woodland and grassland habitats. The plant produces showy violet flowers with a distinctive yellow center, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Viola methodiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
