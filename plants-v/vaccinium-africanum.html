Vaccinium africanum

Vaccinium africanum, commonly known as the African blueberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically blue or black in color, similar to other Vaccinium species. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or rocky habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
