Veronica tumida

Veronica tumida, commonly known as the clustered speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems covered in small, opposite leaves that are typically lance-shaped and hairy. The plant produces clusters of blue or violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Veronica tumida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
