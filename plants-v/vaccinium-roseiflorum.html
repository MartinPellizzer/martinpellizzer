Vaccinium roseiflorum

Vaccinium roseiflorum, commonly known as the pinkflower blueberry, is a species of shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are evergreen, oval-shaped, and have a slightly waxy texture.
