Vatica chartacea

Vatica chartacea, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and can grow up to 30 meters in height. The leaves are alternate, simple, and typically elliptical in shape, with a smooth surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, followed by woody capsules containing seeds.

This species is valued for its durable timber and is often found in lowland rainforests.
