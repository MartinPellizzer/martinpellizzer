Viola sylvestris subsp. riviniana

Viola sylvestris subsp.

riviniana, commonly known as the river violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as riverbanks, woodland edges, and meadows.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of violet or blue. Its flowers are usually borne on slender stalks and bloom from late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
