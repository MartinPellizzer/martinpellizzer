Vitis quinquangularis var. bellula

Vitis quinquangularis var. bellula, commonly known as the miniature grapevine, is a unique cultivar of the grapevine species characterized by its compact growth habit.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is prized for its ornamental value due to its small size and attractive foliage. The plant produces small, sweet, and edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically lobed and have a distinct, almost triangular shape, contributing to its visual appeal.

This variety is well-suited for container gardening and small spaces, making it a popular choice among horticultural enthusiasts.
