Vernonia riparia

Vernonia riparia, commonly known as the river vernonia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows along riverbanks and moist soils, showcasing its adaptability to aquatic environments. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flower heads. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, attracting various pollinators.

Vernonia riparia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
