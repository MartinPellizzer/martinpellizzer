Viola × neilreichiana

Viola × neilreichiana, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Viola tricolor and Viola wittrockii, showcasing a blend of characteristics from both parents. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of violet, purple, and white, often with a distinctive yellow center. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

Its foliage consists of heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, adding to its aesthetic appeal.
