Viola vaginata var. angustifolia

Viola vaginata var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. The plant produces violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
