Viola × orophila

Viola × orophila, commonly known as the Sierra violet, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of violet-blue flowers with yellow centers. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit.

Viola × orophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
