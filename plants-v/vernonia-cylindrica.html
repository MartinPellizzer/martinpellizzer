Vernonia cylindrica

Vernonia cylindrica, commonly known as the cylindrical ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along streambanks, thriving in moist, sandy soils. Its cylindrical, hairy stems and yellowish-green flower heads make it easily identifiable.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its habitat.
