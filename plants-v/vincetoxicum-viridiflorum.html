Vincetoxicum viridiflorum

Vincetoxicum viridiflorum, commonly known as greenflower adder's tongue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in late spring, which are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
