Viola retusa

Viola retusa, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to East Asia.

It produces delicate, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive white throat and yellow markings. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or shaded gardens.

Viola retusa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
