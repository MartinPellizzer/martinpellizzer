Viola retroscabra

Viola retroscabra, commonly known as the backward viola, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Viola retroscabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
