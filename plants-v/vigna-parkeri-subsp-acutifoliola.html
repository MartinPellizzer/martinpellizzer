Vigna parkeri subsp. acutifoliola

Vigna parkeri subsp.

acutifoliola, commonly known as the acute-leaved cowpea, is a species of legume native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its slender, climbing habit and trifoliate leaves with acute, lance-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

This subspecies is valued for its nutritional seeds and is cultivated in various parts of Africa and Asia.
