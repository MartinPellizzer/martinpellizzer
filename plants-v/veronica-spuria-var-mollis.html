Veronica spuria var. mollis

Veronica spuria var. mollis, commonly known as soft speedwell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, spreading stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and covered in fine, soft hairs.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky habitats.
