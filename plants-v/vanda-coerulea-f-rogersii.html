Vanda coerulea f. rogersii

Vanda coerulea f. rogersii, commonly known as the blue vanda, is a hybrid orchid prized for its striking blue and purple floral hues.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia, where it typically grows on tree branches or rocks. The plant features long, narrow leaves and produces large, showy flowers with a distinctive coloration pattern. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
