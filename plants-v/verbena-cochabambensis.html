Verbena cochabambensis

Verbena cochabambensis, commonly known as the Cochabamba verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Bolivia.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is well adapted to high-altitude environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and branching.

Verbena cochabambensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
