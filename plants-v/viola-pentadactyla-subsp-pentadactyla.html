Viola pentadactyla subsp. pentadactyla

Viola pentadactyla subsp.

pentadactyla, commonly known as the five-finger violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with five distinct lobes, giving it its common name. Its flowers are violet-blue, with five petals and a distinctive yellow beard on the lower lip.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
