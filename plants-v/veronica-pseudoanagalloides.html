Veronica pseudoanagalloides

Veronica pseudoanagalloides, commonly known as the rock violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, and produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers with five petals. The leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and often covered in a dense layer of fine pubescence. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in sunny, open habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
