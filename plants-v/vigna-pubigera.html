Vigna pubigera

Vigna pubigera, commonly known as the pubescent cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds used in various cuisines. Vigna pubigera thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an important crop in agricultural systems where nitrogen fixation through symbiotic bacteria enhances soil fertility.
