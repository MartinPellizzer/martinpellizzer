Vicia amphicarpa f. villosa

Vicia amphicarpa f. villosa, commonly known as the hairy vetch, is a leguminous plant characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves.

It produces cylindrical, hairy pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and improves soil fertility through nitrogen fixation.

Its adaptability makes it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and sustainable agriculture.
