Viola tricolor var. gracilis

Viola tricolor var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and delicate, colorful flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing flowers in shades of violet, white, and yellow. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a suitable candidate for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value and ease of cultivation have made it a popular choice among gardeners.
