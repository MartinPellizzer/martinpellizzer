Vangueria burttii subsp. burttii

Vangueria burttii subsp.

burttii, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by edible, chestnut-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

The botanical characteristics of this species make it a valuable plant for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
