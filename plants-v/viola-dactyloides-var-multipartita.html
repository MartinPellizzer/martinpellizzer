Viola dactyloides var. multipartita

Viola dactyloides var. multipartita, commonly known as the Mexican hybrid viola, is a variant of the common violet that exhibits distinct botanical characteristics.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant with heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and produces capsules as its fruiting structure.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
