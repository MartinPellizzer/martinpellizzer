Veronica miyabei var. glabrescens

Veronica miyabei var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth veronica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It features slender, upright stems covered with small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers arranged in whorls along the stem, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value and delicate appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
