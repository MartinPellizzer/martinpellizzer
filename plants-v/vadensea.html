Vadensea

Vadensea, commonly known as the silver fern, is a unique plant belonging to the family Dryopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are covered in a fine, silvery coating, which gives it its common name. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate regions. Vadensea has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

Its reproductive structures include sporangia, which release spores for propagation.
