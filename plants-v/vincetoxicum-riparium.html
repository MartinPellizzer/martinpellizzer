Vincetoxicum riparium

Vincetoxicum riparium, commonly known as water thornweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland and riparian habitats.

It features slender, branched stems that often grow along the edges of water bodies. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Vincetoxicum riparium is adapted to moist environments and can thrive in partial to full shade.
