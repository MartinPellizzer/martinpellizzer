Viola sempervirens subsp. orbiculoides

Viola sempervirens subsp.

orbiculoides, commonly known as the round-leaf violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, showy flowers in shades of purple or violet. Its flowers are typically borne on slender stems and bloom in early spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
