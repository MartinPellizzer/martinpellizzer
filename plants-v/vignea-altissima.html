Vignea altissima

Vignea altissima, commonly known as the tall morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple and pink. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to climb various supports. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Vignea altissima is also known for its fragrant blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
