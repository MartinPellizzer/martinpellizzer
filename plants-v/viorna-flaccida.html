Viorna flaccida

Viorna flaccida, commonly known as the limp viorna, is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries.

It is a shrub that typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils in temperate regions. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are often grayish-green in color. Its flowers are usually pink or white, appearing in clusters, and it produces small, dark berries as fruit.

Viorna flaccida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
