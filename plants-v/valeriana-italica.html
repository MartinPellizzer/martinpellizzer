Valeriana italica

Valeriana italica, commonly known as Italian valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are usually aromatic. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in summer. Its root system is fibrous and has been used traditionally for its sedative properties.

Valeriana italica thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
