Viola cryana

Viola cryana, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from violet to white, often with a distinctive yellow throat. Viola cryana thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions.

Its botanical features make it a unique and resilient member of the Violaceae family.
