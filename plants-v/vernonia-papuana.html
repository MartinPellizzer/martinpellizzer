Vernonia papuana

Vernonia papuana, commonly known as the Papuan ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands, particularly Papua New Guinea.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 4 meters in height, characterized by its rough, dark brown bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming during the dry season. Vernonia papuana is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for local wildlife.
