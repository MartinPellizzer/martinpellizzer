Verbascum × kotschyoides

Verbascum × kotschyoides, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Verbascum × kotschyoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
