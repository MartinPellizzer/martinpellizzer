Verbascum flavipannosum

Verbascum flavipannosum, commonly known as the yellow flax or yellow mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its fuzzy, gray-green leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
