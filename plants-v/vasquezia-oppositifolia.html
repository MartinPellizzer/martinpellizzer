Vasquezia oppositifolia

Vasquezia oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leafed vasquezia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its opposite leaf arrangement, where leaves are paired on opposite sides of the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Vasquezia oppositifolia plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
