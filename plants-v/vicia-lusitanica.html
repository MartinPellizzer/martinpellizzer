Vicia lusitanica

Vicia lusitanica, commonly known as the Iberian vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and northern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean regions. It produces slender, woody pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Vicia lusitanica is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
