Vernonia catumbensis

Vernonia catumbensis, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stature and produces clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers. The plant has rough, hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Vernonia catumbensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
