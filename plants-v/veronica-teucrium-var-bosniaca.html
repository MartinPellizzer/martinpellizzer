Veronica teucrium var. bosniaca

Veronica teucrium var. bosniaca, commonly known as Bosnian speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan region.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the Veronica genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in gardens and conservation efforts.
