Verbena demissa

Verbena demissa, commonly known as narrowleaf verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant produces small, oval leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for xeriscaping.

Verbena demissa is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
