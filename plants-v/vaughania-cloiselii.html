Vaughania cloiselii

Vaughania cloiselii, commonly known as the red-veined begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Begoniaceae and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with deep red veins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, often with prominent stamens. Vaughania cloiselii thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

It is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
