Verbascum glanduligerum

Verbascum glanduligerum, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, gray-green leaves that are covered in soft, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

Verbascum glanduligerum is also known for its medicinal properties, historically used to treat respiratory ailments.
