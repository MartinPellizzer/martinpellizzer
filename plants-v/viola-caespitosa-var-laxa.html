Viola caespitosa var. laxa

Viola caespitosa var. laxa, commonly known as the loose violet, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet or purple coloration. Its flowers are usually small, with five petals and a characteristic spur.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
