Vernonia accommodata

Vernonia accommodata, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads that resemble mist. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as swamps and wetlands, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a soft appearance.

Vernonia accommodata is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
