Veronica colocensis

Veronica colocensis, commonly known as the mountain veronica, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species adapted to rocky or mountainous environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Veronica colocensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
