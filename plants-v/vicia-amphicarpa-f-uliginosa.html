Vicia amphicarpa f. uliginosa

Vicia amphicarpa f. uliginosa, commonly known as the bog vicia, is a variant of the common vetch.

It is a perennial legume characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant thrives in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and marshes. Its leaves are trifoliate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
