Valeriana dubia var. angustifolia

Valeriana dubia var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf valerian, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lanceolate leaves that are typically hairy. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its root system is fibrous and contains compounds responsible for its medicinal properties.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.
