Viola bubanii var. tenuicula

Viola bubanii var. tenuicula, commonly known as the slender violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple or violet. Its flowers are often borne on slender stems and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Viola bubanii var. tenuicula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
