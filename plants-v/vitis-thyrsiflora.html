Vitis thyrsiflora

Vitis thyrsiflora, commonly known as the thrysoflora grapevine, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white or greenish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically not suitable for human consumption but are important for wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Vitis thyrsiflora is a member of the Vitaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
