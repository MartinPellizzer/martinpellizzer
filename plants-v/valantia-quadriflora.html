Valantia quadriflora

Valantia quadriflora, commonly known as the four-flowered valantia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils. Valantia quadriflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses due to its delicate appearance.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable choice for shaded garden beds.
