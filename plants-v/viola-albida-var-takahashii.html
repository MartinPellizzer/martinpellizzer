Viola albida var. takahashii

Viola albida var. takahashii, commonly known as the Takahashi's violet, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pale yellow flowers with purple veins. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
