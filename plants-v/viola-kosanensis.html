Viola kosanensis

Viola kosanensis, commonly known as the Korean violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and prefers moist, well-drained soils. It blooms in spring, producing clusters of small, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators.

Viola kosanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
