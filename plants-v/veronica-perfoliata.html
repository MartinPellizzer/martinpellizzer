Veronica perfoliata

Veronica perfoliata, commonly known as the leafy speedwell, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of opposite, linear to lance-shaped leaves that often have a bluish-gray hue. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in clusters, typically blooming from spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Veronica perfoliata is a perennial herb that is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
