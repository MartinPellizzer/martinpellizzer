Viola falconeri

Viola falconeri, commonly known as the Falconeri violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, showy flowers in shades of violet and blue. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, adapted to the cool, moist conditions of its mountainous habitat. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Viola falconeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
