Viburnum sandankwa

Viburnum sandankwa, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces bright red berries in the fall, which are attractive to birds. Viburnum sandankwa typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters and prefers well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in garden settings.
