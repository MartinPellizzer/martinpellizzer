Vernonia borinquensis var. hirsuta

Vernonia borinquensis var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Puerto Rico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky slopes. Its leaves are hairy, giving it a distinctive texture, and it produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
