Vesicaria macrocarpa

Vesicaria macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It is characterized by its inflated, bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic organisms. The plant's leaves are modified into bladder-like organs, which are used to capture prey. It thrives in nutrient-poor waters, supplementing its diet with captured prey.

Its unique morphology and carnivorous adaptation make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
