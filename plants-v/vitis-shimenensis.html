Vitis shimenensis

Vitis shimenensis, commonly known as the Shimen grapevine, is a species native to China.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, often dark blue or black berries. The plant is typically found in forested areas and thrives in temperate climates. Its botanical features include tendrils for climbing and a robust root system.

Vitis shimenensis is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its potential for cultivation and genetic diversity.
