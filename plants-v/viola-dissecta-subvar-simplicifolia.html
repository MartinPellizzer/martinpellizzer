Viola dissecta subvar. simplicifolia

Viola dissecta subvar. simplicifolia, commonly known as the slender violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of purple, violet, and white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens with proper conditions.
