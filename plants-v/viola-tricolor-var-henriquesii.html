Viola tricolor var. henriquesii

Viola tricolor var. henriquesii, commonly known as the Portuguese violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of small, colorful flowers in shades of violet, white, and yellow. The plant thrives in woodland and shaded areas, preferring moist, well-drained soil. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

The variety is noted for its compact growth and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
