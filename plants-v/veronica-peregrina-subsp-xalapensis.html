Veronica peregrina subsp. xalapensis

Veronica peregrina subsp.

xalapensis, commonly known as the Mexican speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico. It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, blue-to-purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
