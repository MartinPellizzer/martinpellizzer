Viola affinis var. langloisii

Viola affinis var. langloisii, commonly known as the Langlois's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of North America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
