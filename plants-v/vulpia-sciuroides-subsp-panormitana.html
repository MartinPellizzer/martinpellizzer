Vulpia sciuroides subsp. panormitana

Vulpia sciuroides subsp.

panormitana, commonly known as the Panormitan vulpia, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and coastal areas.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is well adapted to arid conditions and can tolerate saline environments.

Its ecological importance lies in its role as a pioneer species in disturbed or degraded habitats.
