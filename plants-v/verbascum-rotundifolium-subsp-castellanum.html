Verbascum rotundifolium subsp. castellanum

Verbascum rotundifolium subsp.

castellanum, commonly known as the Castellano's mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that are rounded in shape.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
