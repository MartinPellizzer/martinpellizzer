Vigna andongensis

Vigna andongensis, commonly known as the Andong cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with small, hairy stipules. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that develop into elongated pods containing several round, edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein and minerals.

Vigna andongensis thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight.
