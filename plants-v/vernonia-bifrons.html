Vernonia bifrons

Vernonia bifrons, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads that resemble mist, which bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Vernonia bifrons is a popular choice for gardens due to its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
