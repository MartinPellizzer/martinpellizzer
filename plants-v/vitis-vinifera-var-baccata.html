Vitis vinifera var. baccata

Vitis vinifera var. baccata, commonly known as the black currant, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and vitamins. The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its stems are often covered in short, stiff hairs.

This variety is widely cultivated for both ornamental purposes and for its edible fruits.
