Vincetoxicum pinguifolium

Vincetoxicum pinguifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces berries that are toxic to humans and animals, containing cardiac glycosides.

Despite its toxicity, it is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
