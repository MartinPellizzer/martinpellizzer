Vicia speciosa

Vicia speciosa, commonly known as the showy vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive purple or pink flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas.

Vicia speciosa is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
