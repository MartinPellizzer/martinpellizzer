Viburnum erubescens var. brevitubum

Viburnum erubescens var. brevitubum, commonly known as the dwarf sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by red berries that attract wildlife. The plant has dark green, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, making it a compact and ornamental choice for gardens.

Its adaptability to various soil types and partial shade makes it a versatile addition to native plant landscapes.
