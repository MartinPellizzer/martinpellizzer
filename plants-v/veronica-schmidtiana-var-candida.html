Veronica schmidtiana var. candida

Veronica schmidtiana var. candida, commonly known as the white speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

This species is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a ground cover.
