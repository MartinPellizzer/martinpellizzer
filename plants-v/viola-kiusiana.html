Viola kiusiana

Viola kiusiana, commonly known as the Kiusiana violet, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant violet flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its blossoms, which appear in early spring, are often fragrant and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola kiusiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
