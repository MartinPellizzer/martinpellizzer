Vernonia spenceriana

Vernonia spenceriana, commonly known as the Spencer's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and dense clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia spenceriana is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
