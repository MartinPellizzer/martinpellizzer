Veronica bellidioides subsp. lilacina

Veronica bellidioides subsp.

lilacina, commonly known as the lilac veronica, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers during the summer months.

The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant floral display and drought tolerance.
