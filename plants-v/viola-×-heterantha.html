Viola × heterantha

Viola × heterantha, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and prefers moist, well-drained soil in partial shade. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.

Viola × heterantha is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
