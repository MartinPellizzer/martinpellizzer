Vepris grandifolia

Vepris grandifolia, commonly known as the African birch or wild guava, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits that are edible. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a serrated margin, while the bark is characteristic for its reddish or brownish color.

Vepris grandifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
