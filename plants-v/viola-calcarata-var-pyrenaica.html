Viola calcarata var. pyrenaica

Viola calcarata var. pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean viola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces violet flowers with a deep purple hue, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland and rocky habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in its native range.
