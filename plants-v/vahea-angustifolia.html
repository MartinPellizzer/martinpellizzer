Vahea angustifolia

Vahea angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to salty air and sandy soils. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dry seeds.

Vahea angustifolia is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
