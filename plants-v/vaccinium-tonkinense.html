Vaccinium tonkinense

Vaccinium tonkinense, commonly known as the Vietnamese huckleberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and potential commercial use.
