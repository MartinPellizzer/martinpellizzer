Viola corsica subsp. ilvensis

Viola corsica subsp.

ilvensis, commonly known as the Corsican violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in mountainous areas.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet-blue coloration and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of purple and blue. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is well adapted to dry, sunny environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
