Vernonia expansa

Vernonia expansa, commonly known as the broadleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple cylindrical flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Vernonia expansa is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
