Verbena goyazensis

Verbena goyazensis, commonly known as the Brazilian verbena, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant flower clusters. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.

Verbena goyazensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
