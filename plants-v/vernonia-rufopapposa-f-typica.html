Vernonia rufopapposa f. typica

Vernonia rufopapposa f. typica, commonly known as the redflower ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, purple to red inflorescences. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers arranged in dense, conical clusters. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Its botanical structure supports pollination by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance.
