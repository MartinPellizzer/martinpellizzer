Viola fimbriatula f. albescens

Viola fimbriatula f. albescens, commonly known as the white-fringed violet, is a herbaceous perennial belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a distinctive white or pale margin. The flowers are typically violet-blue with a white or pale yellow throat, and they bloom in early spring. This variant is a natural form of V. fimbriatula, distinguished by its reduced pigmentation.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland habitats.
