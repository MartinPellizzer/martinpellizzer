Viola × variabilis

Viola × variabilis, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant is native to Europe and has been widely cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Viola × variabilis is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of climates, contributing to its widespread distribution.
