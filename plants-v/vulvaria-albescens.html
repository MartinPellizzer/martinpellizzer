Vulvaria albescens

Vulvaria albescens, commonly known as white vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds, typical of the legume family. It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.

Vulvaria albescens is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
