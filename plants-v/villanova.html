Villanova

Villanova, commonly known as the "flower of the desert," is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. Its root system is shallow but extensive, allowing it to absorb moisture from a wide area.

Villanova is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
