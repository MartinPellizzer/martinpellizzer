Viola kusnezowiana

Viola kusnezowiana, commonly known as the Siberian violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces delicate, violet-blue flowers with a yellow center. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas. Its blooms typically appear in early spring, adding a touch of color to the landscape.

Viola kusnezowiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
