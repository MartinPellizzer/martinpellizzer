Veronica arborea

Veronica arborea, commonly known as the tree speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a shrub-like species that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, providing a textured appearance.

Veronica arborea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
