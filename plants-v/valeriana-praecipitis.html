Valeriana praecipitis

Valeriana praecipitis, commonly known as the cliff valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often on cliffs or slopes, where it thrives in well-drained conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, aromatic scent, which is characteristic of the Valeriana genus.

Valeriana praecipitis is also noted for its medicinal properties, historically used for its calming effects.
