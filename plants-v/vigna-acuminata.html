Vigna acuminata

Vigna acuminata, commonly known as the African bean or cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into elongated pods containing edible seeds. Vigna acuminata is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dry conditions.

It is valued for its nutritional content and is an important crop in many regions for both human consumption and animal feed.
