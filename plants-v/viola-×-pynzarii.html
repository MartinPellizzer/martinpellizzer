Viola × pynzarii

Viola × pynzarii, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a member of the Violaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, colorful flowers.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Viola tricolor and Viola walteri, resulting in a diverse range of flower colors including purple, blue, and white. The plant typically produces heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its flowers are often borne in clusters at the ends of stems. Viola × pynzarii thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

Due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal, it is a popular choice for both gardeners and botanists alike.
