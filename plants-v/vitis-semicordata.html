Vitis semicordata

Vitis semicordata, commonly known as the heartleaf grape or red sandalwood vine, is a perennial vine native to the United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, dark purple to black berries. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas. It is a member of the Vitaceae family and is known for its ornamental value.

The berries are edible but are typically used in jams and wines due to their tart flavor.
