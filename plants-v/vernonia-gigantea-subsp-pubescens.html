Vernonia gigantea subsp. pubescens

Vernonia gigantea subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the hairy ironweed, is a tall, erect perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall.

The plant has rough, hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. Its foliage is typically lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence.

This species is often found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and woodland edges.
