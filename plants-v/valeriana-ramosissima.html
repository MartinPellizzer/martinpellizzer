Valeriana ramosissima

Valeriana ramosissima, commonly known as wood betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Valeriana ramosissima is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
