Veronica rosea var. leiocarpa

Veronica rosea var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the soft-hairy veronica, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant a velvety texture. It prefers well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
