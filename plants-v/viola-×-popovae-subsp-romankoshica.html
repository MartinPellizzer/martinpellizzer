Viola × popovae subsp. romankoshica

Viola × popovae subsp.

romankoshica, commonly known as the Romankoshic violet, is a hybrid species native to the Caucasus region. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 10–20 centimeters.

The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces clusters of violet-blue flowers with five petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
