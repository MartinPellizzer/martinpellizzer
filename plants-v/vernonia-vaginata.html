Vernonia vaginata

Vernonia vaginata, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads that appear in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

Vernonia vaginata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
