Viola silvatica var. ascoplectron

Viola silvatica var. ascoplectron, commonly known as the silverleaf violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the forests of Europe.

It features heart-shaped, silver-veined leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy pubescence. The plant produces violet-blue flowers with five petals, typically arranged in a showy cluster. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Violaceae family.
