Verbena guaibensis

Verbena guaibensis, commonly known as the Brazilian verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and branching.

Verbena guaibensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its vibrant blooms.
