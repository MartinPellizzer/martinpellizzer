Vincentia maingayi

Vincentia maingayi, commonly known as the yellow flame vine, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets and produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in racemes. The plant is characterized by its slender, twining stems and is often found in moist, sunny habitats. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods that split open to release the seeds.

Vincentia maingayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
