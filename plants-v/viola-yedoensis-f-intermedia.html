Viola yedoensis f. intermedia

Viola yedoensis f. intermedia, commonly known as the Japanese pansy, is a hybrid viola species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, bi-colored flowers that typically display a mix of purple, yellow, and white. The plant thrives in cool climates and prefers well-drained, moist soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a bedding plant.

The f. intermedia form is notable for its more compact growth habit compared to the species form.
