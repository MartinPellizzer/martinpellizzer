Veronica dubia

Veronica dubia, commonly known as the slender speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has thin, wiry stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Veronica dubia is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
