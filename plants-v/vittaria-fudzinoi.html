Vittaria fudzinoi

Vittaria fudzinoi, commonly known as the Japanese hair fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Vittariaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, hair-like fronds that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spores are borne on the underside of its fronds, and it reproduces through both sexual and asexual means.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Vittaria fudzinoi is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
