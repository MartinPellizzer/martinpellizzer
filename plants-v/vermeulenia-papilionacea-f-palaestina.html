Vermeulenia papilionacea f. palaestina

Vermeulenia papilionacea f. palaestina, commonly known as the Palestine milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid climates. Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment in degraded landscapes.

It is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
