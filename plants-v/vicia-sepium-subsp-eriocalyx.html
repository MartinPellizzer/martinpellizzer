Vicia sepium subsp. eriocalyx

Vicia sepium subsp.

eriocalyx, commonly known as the hairy vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers.

The plant produces cylindrical, hairy pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

This species is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and use in ecological restoration.
