Vaccinium ardisiiflorum

Vaccinium ardisiiflorum, commonly known as the redstem huckleberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its berries, which are typically red or black, are edible and often used in local cuisines or for wildlife food.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
