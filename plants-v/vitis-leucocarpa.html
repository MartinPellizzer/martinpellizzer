Vitis leucocarpa

Vitis leucocarpa, commonly known as the white grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to the southern United States, particularly Texas and Oklahoma, and is characterized by its white to pale green berries. The plant is a vigorous climber with tendrils and palmately lobed leaves. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, typically appearing in clusters.

Vitis leucocarpa is notable for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential in horticulture and ecological applications.
