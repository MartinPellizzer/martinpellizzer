Verbascum aureum var. joannis

Verbascum aureum var. joannis, commonly known as the golden mullein, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in dense, woolly gray-green leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in a loose, candle-like inflorescence. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
