Viola dirimliensis

Viola dirimliensis, commonly known as the Turkish violet, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It has small, velvety leaves and produces showy blossoms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola dirimliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
