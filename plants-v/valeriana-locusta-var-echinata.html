Valeriana locusta var. echinata

Valeriana locusta var. echinata, commonly known as the spiny valerian, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces tiny, clustered fruits called drupes, which contain the seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves and roots have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their calming properties.
