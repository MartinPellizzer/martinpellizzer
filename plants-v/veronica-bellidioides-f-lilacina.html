Veronica bellidioides f. lilacina

Veronica bellidioides f. lilacina, commonly known as the lilac-flowered speedwell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces clusters of small, lilac-colored flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant.
