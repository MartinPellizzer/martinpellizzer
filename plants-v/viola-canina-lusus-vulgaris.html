Viola canina lusus vulgaris

Viola canina lusus vulgaris, commonly known as the dog violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and meadows. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Viola canina lusus vulgaris is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
