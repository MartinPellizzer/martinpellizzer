Viola tricolor var. olyssiponensis

Viola tricolor var. olyssiponensis, commonly known as the Portuguese violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and northern Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped lobes and produces delicate, colorful flowers in shades of purple, white, and yellow. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

The species is also noted for its ability to naturalize and spread through self-seeding.
