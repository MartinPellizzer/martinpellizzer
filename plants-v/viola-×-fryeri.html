Viola × fryeri

Viola × fryeri, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a species resulting from the cross between Viola tricolor and Viola wittrockii.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of violet or blue flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and thrives in moist, well-drained soil in partial shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to gardens and natural habitats.

Viola × fryeri is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in horticulture for its beauty and resilience.
