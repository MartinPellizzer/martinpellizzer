Veronica spicata subsp. carpatica

Veronica spicata subsp.

carpatica, commonly known as Carpathian speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian Mountains. It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright, spiky inflorescence and small blue to purple flowers.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
