Vriesea turbinata

Vriesea turbinata, commonly known as the Turban Vriesea, is a striking bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, colorful, and long-lasting inflorescence that resembles a turban, giving it its common name. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in height. Its flowers, which are typically red or orange, bloom in a spiral pattern and can last for several months.

Vriesea turbinata is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its ability to thrive in bright, indirect light and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
