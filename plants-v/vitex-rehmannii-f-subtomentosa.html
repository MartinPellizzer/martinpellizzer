Vitex rehmannii f. subtomentosa

Vitex rehmannii f. subtomentosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf chaste tree, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in the summer months. Its fruit is a dry, two-valved capsule containing several small, flattened seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
