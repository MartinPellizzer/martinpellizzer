Veronica arvensis subsp. pennellii

Veronica arvensis subsp.

pennellii, commonly known as the creeping speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features small, blue or violet flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer.

The plant has thin, spreading stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, ditches, and along roadsides.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common weed in agricultural and garden settings.
