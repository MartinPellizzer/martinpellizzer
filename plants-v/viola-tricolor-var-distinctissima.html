Viola tricolor var. distinctissima

Viola tricolor var. distinctissima, commonly known as the Purple Heart Viola, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet and white coloration, and its flowers are typically violet, white, and yellow. The plant thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens. It produces small, delicate flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The variety is noted for its compact growth habit and ornamental value in garden settings.
