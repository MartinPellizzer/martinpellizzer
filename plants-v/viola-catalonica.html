Viola catalonica

Viola catalonica, commonly known as the Catalan violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and forest floors. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a touch of color to the landscape.

Viola catalonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
