Vernonia acilepis

Vernonia acilepis, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and riverbanks. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, purple to brownish flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a robust root system and a height that can reach up to 2 meters.

Vernonia acilepis is often found in wetland ecosystems and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
