Verbena mysorensis

Verbena mysorensis, commonly known as the Mysore verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of India.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of bright blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its foliage is often covered in fine, silky hairs, adding to its distinctive appearance.
