Vangueria verruculosa

Vangueria verruculosa, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with woody stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature. Its fruit is edible and has a tangy, slightly sour flavor, making it a traditional food in some regions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
