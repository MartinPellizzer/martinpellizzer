Veronica stenophylla var. oliveri

Veronica stenophylla var. oliveri, commonly known as the Oliver's speedwell, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
