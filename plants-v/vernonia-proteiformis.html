Vernonia proteiformis

Vernonia proteiformis, commonly known as the variable Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, and the flowers are small, yellowish to brownish, arranged in dense clusters.

Vernonia proteiformis is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
