Viola × sermenika

Viola × sermenika, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a popular ornamental plant resulting from the cross between Viola tricolor and Viola cornuta.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of vibrant, showy flowers in shades of purple, violet, and white. The plant is native to Europe and thrives in cool, moist environments, making it suitable for garden beds and containers. It is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.

The hybrid is known for its hardiness and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
