Vitis heterophylla variegata

Vitis heterophylla variegata, commonly known as the variegated muscadine vine, is a unique cultivar of the muscadine grape.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves that display shades of white, green, and yellow. This plant is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in warm, humid climates. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while the vines can grow quite long.

It produces small, dark purple berries that are edible and often used in local culinary traditions.
