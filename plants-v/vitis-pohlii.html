Vitis pohlii

Vitis pohlii, commonly known as the Chinese wild grape, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish-white flowers in the spring. The plant bears small, round berries that are typically red or black when mature. Vitis pohlii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and autumn color.

It is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
