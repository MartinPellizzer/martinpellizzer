Veratrum album var. viridiflorum

Veratrum album var. viridiflorum, commonly known as green-flowered white hellebore, is a variant of the white hellebore native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
