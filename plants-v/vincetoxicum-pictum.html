Vincetoxicum pictum

Vincetoxicum pictum, commonly known as the spotted bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that features small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with distinctive white spots, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Vincetoxicum pictum is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
