Veronica longifolia var. puberula

Veronica longifolia var. puberula, commonly known as the hairy veronica, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
