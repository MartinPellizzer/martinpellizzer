Viola oppositifolia

Viola oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaved violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features heart-shaped, opposite leaves that are typically green with a slight pubescence. The plant produces delicate, violet-blue flowers with five petals, often with a distinctive yellow or white keel. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Viola oppositifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
