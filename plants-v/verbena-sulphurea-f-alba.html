Verbena sulphurea f. alba

Verbena sulphurea f. alba, commonly known as sulphur verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces showy, five-petaled blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and opposite, with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
