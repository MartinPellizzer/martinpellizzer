Vitis havanica

Vitis havanica, commonly known as the Cuban grapevine, is a species of flowering vine native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with elongated leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for medicinal use, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical regions.
