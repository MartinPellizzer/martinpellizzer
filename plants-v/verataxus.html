Verataxus

Verataxus, commonly known as the golden star tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble stars, which bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant in some regions.
