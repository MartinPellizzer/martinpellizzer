Vitis leucophlea

Vitis leucophlea, commonly known as the whiteleaf grapevine, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive white or pale green undersides on its leaves, which contrast with its darker upper surfaces. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into clusters of berries, typically ranging in color from green to red. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a smooth texture.

Vitis leucophlea is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
