Veronica ascendens

Veronica ascendens, commonly known as the upright speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems that bear opposite pairs of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Veronica ascendens thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine arrangements.
