Viola ivonis

Viola ivonis, commonly known as the English violet, is a flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple, violet, and white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Viola ivonis is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
