Vignea heleonastes

Vignea heleonastes, commonly known as the golden vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. Vignea heleonastes thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners and horticultural enthusiasts.
