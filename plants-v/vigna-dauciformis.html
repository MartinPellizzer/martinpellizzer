Vigna dauciformis

Vigna dauciformis, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that give way to elongated, cylindrical pods containing edible seeds. Vigna dauciformis is adapted to poor soils and can thrive in arid conditions, making it a valuable crop in semi-arid regions.

It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
