Vasconcellea × pentagona

Vasconcellea × pentagona, commonly known as the pentagon leaf vine, is a hybrid plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive pentagonal-shaped leaves, which give it its common name. The plant is a climbing vine that produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in rainforest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some tropical gardens for its unique foliage.
