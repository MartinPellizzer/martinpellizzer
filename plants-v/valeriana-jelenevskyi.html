Valeriana jelenevskyi

Valeriana jelenevskyi, commonly known as the Jelenevsky valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often glandular leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its roots are valued in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.
