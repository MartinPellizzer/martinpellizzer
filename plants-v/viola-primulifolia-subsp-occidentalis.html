Viola primulifolia subsp. occidentalis

Viola primulifolia subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet or purple coloration, and its flowers are usually violet or blue-violet, with five petals. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
