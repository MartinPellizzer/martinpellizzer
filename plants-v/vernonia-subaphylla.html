Vernonia subaphylla

Vernonia subaphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and disturbed soils. It is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in wet conditions, making it a common sight in swampy areas.

The flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
