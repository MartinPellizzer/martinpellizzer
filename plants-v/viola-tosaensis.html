Viola tosaensis

Viola tosaensis, commonly known as the Tosa violet, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the landscape.

Viola tosaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
