Vernonia elbertiana

Vernonia elbertiana, commonly known as Elbert's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its conical flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from yellow to orange in color.

Vernonia elbertiana is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in saturated soils.
