Valeriana saliunca

Valeriana saliunca, commonly known as lesser valerian, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

Valeriana saliunca thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
