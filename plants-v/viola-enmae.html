Viola enmae

Viola enmae, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 10-20 centimeters, featuring heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of violet, lavender, and white, usually blooming in spring. Viola enmae prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or shaded gardens.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to ecological landscapes.
