Vietorchis aurea

Vietorchis aurea, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforests, where it thrives in humid and shaded conditions. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the forest floor, and it often forms symbiotic relationships with specific fungi.

The botanical features of Vietorchis aurea make it a unique and valuable component of its native ecosystem.
