Vincetoxicum ovatum

Vincetoxicum ovatum, commonly known as the red baneberry, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Vincetoxicum ovatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and unique fruit.
