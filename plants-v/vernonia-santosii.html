Vernonia santosii

Vernonia santosii, commonly known as Santos' ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia santosii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
