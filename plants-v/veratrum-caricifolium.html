Veratrum caricifolium

Veratrum caricifolium, commonly known as the Canadian cowbane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features tall, leafy stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces clusters of greenish-yellow flowers in late summer, which are arranged in umbels. Its berries are dark blue to black when mature and contain toxic compounds, making it a plant to avoid in gardens.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Melanthiaceae and is characterized by its milky sap and showy floral display.
