Viburnum hartwegii

Viburnum hartwegii, commonly known as the Hartweg viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and features opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and grayish-white underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. Viburnum hartwegii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.

It prefers partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to a variety of garden conditions.
