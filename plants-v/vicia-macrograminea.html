Vicia macrograminea

Vicia macrograminea, commonly known as the large-leaf vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in grassy areas and disturbed soils. Its pods contain several seeds, which are typically dispersed by wind.

Vicia macrograminea is often used in ecological studies due to its role in nitrogen fixation and its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
