Valeriana capensis var. nana

Valeriana capensis var. nana, commonly known as dwarf valerian, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features small, clustered white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has aromatic leaves with a strong, pleasant scent, often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, decorative plant.
