Vaccinium corymbosum var. atrocarpum

Vaccinium corymbosum var. atrocarpum, commonly known as black huckleberry, is a variant of the blueberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has erect, shrub-like growth habit with alternate, oval-shaped leaves that are often hairy beneath. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
