Vernonia valenzuelae

Vernonia valenzuelae, commonly known as the Valenzuela ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellowish-green.

Vernonia valenzuelae is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
