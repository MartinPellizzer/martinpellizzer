Veronica hookeriana

Veronica hookeriana, commonly known as Hooker's speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of New Zealand.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, often forming dense mats or low shrubs. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Veronica hookeriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
