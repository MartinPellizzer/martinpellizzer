Vernonia perrottetii

Vernonia perrottetii, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy inflorescences. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
