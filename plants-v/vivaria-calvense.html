Vivaria calvense

Vivaria calvense, commonly known as the Calvense Vivarium Plant, is a unique species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and distinctive, clustered inflorescences. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil. Its leaves are typically dark green with a waxy texture, and it produces small, fragrant flowers during the spring and summer months.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments such as greenhouses.
