Viguiera dentata var. lancifolia

Viguiera dentata var. lancifolia, commonly known as the lanceleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that are covered in fine, silver-gray hairs. The plant produces showy, composite flower heads that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. Its lance-shaped leaves are serrated and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for native gardens and pollinator habitats.
