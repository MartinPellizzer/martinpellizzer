Vincetoxicum obtusifolium

Vincetoxicum obtusifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with broad, ovate leaflets and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Vincetoxicum obtusifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
