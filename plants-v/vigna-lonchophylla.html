Vigna lonchophylla

Vigna lonchophylla, commonly known as the long-leaf cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces cylindrical pods containing small, edible seeds that are used in various cuisines. Vigna lonchophylla is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and thrives in warm climates with adequate sunlight.

Its botanical features make it a valuable crop for both nutritional and agricultural purposes.
