Vicia angustifolia var. willkommii

Vicia angustifolia var. willkommii, commonly known as the narrowleaf vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, often hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish to greenish pea-like flowers that are typically clustered in loose inflorescences. Its pods are slender and contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

This species is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed or semi-natural habitats.
