Vulpia sciuroides var. longearistata

Vulpia sciuroides var. longearistata, commonly known as the longawn vulpia, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed florets. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions, contributing to its widespread distribution.
