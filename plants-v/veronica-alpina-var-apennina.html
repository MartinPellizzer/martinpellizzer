Veronica alpina var. apennina

Veronica alpina var. apennina, commonly known as the Alpine speedwell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in dense clusters. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and grasslands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
