Veronica thymifolia

Veronica thymifolia, commonly known as thyme-leaved speedwell, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Plantaginaceae.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in rocky and coastal habitats. The plant features small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are aromatic, resembling those of thyme, and are often used in herbal teas.

Veronica thymifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural landscapes.
