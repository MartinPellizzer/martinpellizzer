Verbena × argentina

Verbena × argentina, commonly known as hybrid verbena, is a flowering plant belonging to the Verbenaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Verbena rigida and Verbena bonariensis, combining traits from both parent species. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom throughout the summer. Its foliage is typically gray-green, with small, narrow leaves that are densely packed along the stems.

Verbena × argentina is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
