Viburnum luzonicum var. floribundum

Viburnum luzonicum var. floribundum, commonly known as the Philippine beautyberry, is a shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, vibrant purple berries that persist through winter, adding ornamental value to the landscape. The plant typically grows to a height of 1.5 to 3 meters and features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

The berries are not only visually striking but also serve as a food source for various bird species.
