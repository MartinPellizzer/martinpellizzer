Vernonia madagascariensis

Vernonia madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar ironwood, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is a small shrub or tree that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are characteristic of the Vernonia genus. Its leaves are often narrow and leathery, adapted to withstand arid conditions.

The wood of this plant is highly valued for its durability and is used in traditional construction and crafts.
