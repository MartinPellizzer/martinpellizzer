Vicia hedysaroides

Vicia hedysaroides, commonly known as the broad-leafed vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate, and its clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It is native to temperate regions of Europe and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.

Vicia hedysaroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
