Vigna serotina

Vigna serotina, commonly known as the late-phase bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are typically pale yellow to brown in color. Vigna serotina is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and thrives in warm climates with adequate sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are used in various culinary traditions across its native range.
