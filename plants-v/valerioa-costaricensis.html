Valerioa costaricensis

Valerioa costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican valerian, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Valerianaceae and is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Valerioa costaricensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
