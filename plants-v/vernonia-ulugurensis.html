Vernonia ulugurensis

Vernonia ulugurensis, commonly known as the Uluguru Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the Uluguru Mountains in Tanzania.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Vernonia ulugurensis is also valued for its potential medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
