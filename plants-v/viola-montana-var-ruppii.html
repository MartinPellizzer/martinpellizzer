Viola montana var. ruppii

Viola montana var. ruppii, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces showy violet-blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and partial shade.
