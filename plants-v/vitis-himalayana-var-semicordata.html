Vitis himalayana var. semicordata

Vitis himalayana var. semicordata, commonly known as the Himalayan grapevine, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the mountainous regions of Central and South Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and distinctive heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into clusters of edible berries, which are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for cultivation, it is sometimes grown in gardens and horticultural settings.
