Viola × rouyana

Viola × rouyana, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a cross between Viola tricolor and Viola verecunda.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that come in various colors such as violet, purple, and white. The plant typically thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds.

Viola × rouyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
