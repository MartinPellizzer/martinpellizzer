Vernonia amambaia

Vernonia amambaia, commonly known as the Amazonian ironweed, is a shrub native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties. Vernonia amambaia thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in the undergrowth of dense forests.

Its fibrous roots and sturdy stems make it an important component of the local ecosystem.
