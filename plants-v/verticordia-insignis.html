Verticordia insignis

Verticordia insignis, commonly known as the golden wattle.

This plant is native to Western Australia and is part of the myrtaceae family. It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

It is a small shrub that can reach up to 1 meter in height, with narrow, linear leaves.
