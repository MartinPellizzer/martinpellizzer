Veronica × smirnovii

Veronica × smirnovii, commonly known as the hybrid speedwell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage, with small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of blue or purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a range of morphological variations in leaf shape and flower color.
