Vittaria taeniophylla

Vittaria taeniophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Vittariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble those of other maidenhair ferns. The plant has a creeping rhizome system, allowing it to spread along the forest floor in shaded, humid environments. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, with a distinctive pattern of veins.

Vittaria taeniophylla is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical rainforests, where it thrives in the understory vegetation.
