Vernonia luschnathiana

Vernonia luschnathiana, commonly known as Luschnath's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in prairies, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its showy purple to reddish-purple flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped, often with hairy surfaces, and the plant can reach heights of up to six feet.
