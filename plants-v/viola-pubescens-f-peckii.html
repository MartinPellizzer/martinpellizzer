Viola pubescens f. peckii

Viola pubescens f. peckii, commonly known as the Peck's violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow throat, blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded woodland habitats and is often found in rich, loamy soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
