Valeriana oligodonta

Valeriana oligodonta, commonly known as the slender valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its roots are often harvested for their aromatic properties and traditional medicinal uses.

Valeriana oligodonta is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.
