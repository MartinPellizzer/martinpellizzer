Valeriana pratensis

Valeriana pratensis, commonly known as meadow valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, grassy areas and has a distinctive appearance with dark green, lance-shaped leaves and small, pale pink or white flowers. The plant produces a strong, aromatic scent due to the presence of volatile oils in its roots and leaves. It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along riverbanks.

Valeriana pratensis is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its calming effects.
