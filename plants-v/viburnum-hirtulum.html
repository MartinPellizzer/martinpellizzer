Viburnum hirtulum

Viburnum hirtulum, commonly known as American hazelnut, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets, and its flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and often consumed by birds. Viburnum hirtulum typically grows in moist woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and showy spring blooms.
