Viola violacea f. albida

Viola violacea f. albida, commonly known as the white violet, is a unique variant of the common violet, characterized by its pale or white petals.

This cultivar belongs to the Violaceae family and is native to certain regions of Europe. It typically features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, delicate flowers. The plant prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its botanical interest lies in its contrasting coloration and its role in horticultural diversity within the viola genus.
