Viburnum martini

Viburnum martini, commonly known as the red chokeberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, dark red berries that ripen in the fall. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, white flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Viburnum martini is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing food for birds and wildlife.
