Veronica verna subsp. brevistyla

Veronica verna subsp.

brevistyla, commonly known as the early speedwell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to June.

The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native garden plantings.
