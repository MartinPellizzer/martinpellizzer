Vaccinium donianum var. hangchouense

Vaccinium donianum var. hangchouense, commonly known as the Hangchow blueberry, is a variant of the Vaccinium genus native to China.

It is a shrub that typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist climates. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it bears edible berries that are often used in local cuisine.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential for cultivation in subtropical regions.
