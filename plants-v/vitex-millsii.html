Vitex millsii

Vitex millsii, commonly known as the scarlet globe thornapple, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and often have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by spherical, spiny fruits. Vitex millsii is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in warmer climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and unique fruiting structures.
