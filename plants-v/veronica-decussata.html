Veronica decussata

Veronica decussata, commonly known as the common speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

Veronica decussata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens for its ability to spread and cover ground effectively.
