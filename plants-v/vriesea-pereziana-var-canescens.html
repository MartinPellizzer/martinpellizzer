Vriesea pereziana var. canescens

Vriesea pereziana var. canescens, commonly known as the silver vriesea, is a striking bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, strap-like leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, powdery bloom, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces tall, branching inflorescences with vibrant red and orange flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This ornamental plant is popular in tropical gardens and as a houseplant due to its striking foliage and long-lasting blooms.
