Vaccinium erythrococcum

Vaccinium erythrococcum, commonly known as the red everlet, is a species of dwarf shrub belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California, where it thrives in sandy soils and maritime climates. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. Its flowers are bell-shaped, pink to reddish in color, and appear in clusters during the spring.

The berries, which are small and red, are edible and often used in local culinary traditions.
