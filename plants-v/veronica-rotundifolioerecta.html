Veronica rotundifolioerecta

Veronica rotundifolioerecta, commonly known as the round-leaved speedwell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and features rounded, often velvety leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Native to parts of Europe and western Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and along stream banks.
