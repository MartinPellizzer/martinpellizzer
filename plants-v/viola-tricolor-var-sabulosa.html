Viola tricolor var. sabulosa

Viola tricolor var. sabulosa, commonly known as the sand viola, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of violet, yellow, and white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, making it well adapted to harsh environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
