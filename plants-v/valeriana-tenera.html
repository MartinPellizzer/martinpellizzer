Valeriana tenera

Valeriana tenera, commonly known as slender valerian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features upright, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and aromatic. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Valeriana tenera is valued for its medicinal properties, with the roots containing compounds used in traditional remedies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
