Vilfa pilosa

Vilfa pilosa, commonly known as the hairy vilfa, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
