Viola canina var. ruppii

Viola canina var. ruppii, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet-blue coloration and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of purple and blue. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
