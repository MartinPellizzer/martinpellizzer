Vicia setifolia var. bonariensis

Vicia setifolia var. bonariensis, commonly known as the Argentine vetch, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial legume characterized by its slender, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters. Its seeds are encased in elongated, cylindrical pods, and it is often grown as a forage crop or for ornamental purposes.

Vicia setifolia var. bonariensis has a deep root system that helps in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation.
