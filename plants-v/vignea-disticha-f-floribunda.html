Vignea disticha f. floribunda

Vignea disticha f. floribunda, commonly known as the clustered morning glory, is a variant of the morning glory family (Convolvulaceae).

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue or purple. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its foliage is heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and it produces edible beans that are often used in traditional cuisines.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to climb or trail in gardens.
