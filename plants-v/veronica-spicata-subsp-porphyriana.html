Veronica spicata subsp. porphyriana

Veronica spicata subsp.

porphyriana, commonly known as the purple loosestrife, is a flowering plant native to Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence composed of numerous blue to purple flowers.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture. This species thrives in moist environments such as wet meadows and stream banks.

It is known for its ornamental value in gardens and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
