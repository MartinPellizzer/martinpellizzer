Viviania rosea var. villosa

Viviania rosea var. villosa, commonly known as the woolly rose mallow, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to wetland restoration projects and native plant gardens.
