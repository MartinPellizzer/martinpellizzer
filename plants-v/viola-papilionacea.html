Viola papilionacea

Viola papilionacea, commonly known as the butterfly violet, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking, butterfly-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers range in color from white to purple, and they bloom in early spring.

Viola papilionacea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
