Veronica angustata

Veronica angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf speedwell, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a perennial herb with slender, upright stems and small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are narrow and opposite, often with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
