Vaccinium griffithianum var. glabratum

Vaccinium griffithianum var. glabratum, commonly known as the smooth blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval, dark green leaves that are glabrous, or smooth to the touch. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in early summer. Its fruits are edible, small berries that are typically blue or red, and are prized for their tart flavor.

This variety is adapted to cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine soils.
