Vitex agnus-castus var. trifolia

Vitex agnus-castus var. trifolia, commonly known as the three-leaved chaste tree, is a variant of the chaste tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets, and its showy, fragrant purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 6 meters. Its fruits are woody capsules containing small, dark seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
