Verbena elegans

Verbena elegans, commonly known as the elegant verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Verbena elegans is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
