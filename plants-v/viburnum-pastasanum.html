Viburnum pastasanum

Viburnum pastasanum, commonly known as the pastasanum viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring. The plant is characterized by its fragrant blossoms and red berries that ripen in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Viburnum pastasanum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits to wildlife.
