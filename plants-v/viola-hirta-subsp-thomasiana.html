Viola hirta subsp. thomasiana

Viola hirta subsp.

thomasiana, commonly known as the Thomas's violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America. It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically dark green in color.

The plant produces delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple or violet, often with a white or yellow throat. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Violaceae family.
