Viola nummularia

Viola nummularia, commonly known as the Dutch Violet, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple, violet, or white, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is often used in horticultural settings for its aesthetic value.
