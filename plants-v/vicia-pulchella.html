Vicia pulchella

Vicia pulchella, commonly known as the pretty vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. The plant features slender, climbing stems with small, trifoliate leaves and produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several elliptical seeds.

Vicia pulchella is often noted for its ornamental value and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
