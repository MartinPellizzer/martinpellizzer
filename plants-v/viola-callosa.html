Viola callosa

Viola callosa, commonly known as the rough violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture, and its flowers are typically violet or purple with yellow markings. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Viola callosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
