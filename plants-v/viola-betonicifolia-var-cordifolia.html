Viola betonicifolia var. cordifolia

Viola betonicifolia var. cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf viola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and covered in a fine pubescence. The plant produces violet flowers with five petals, often in shades of purple or blue, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Violaceae family.
