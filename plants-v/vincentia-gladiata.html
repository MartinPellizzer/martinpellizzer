Vincentia gladiata

Vincentia gladiata, commonly known as the sword vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, butterfly-like flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and green spaces.
