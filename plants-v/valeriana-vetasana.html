Valeriana vetasana

Valeriana vetasana, commonly known as the Brazilian valerian, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, while its flowers are usually pink or white.

Valeriana vetasana is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
