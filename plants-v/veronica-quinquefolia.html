Veronica quinquefolia

Veronica quinquefolia, commonly known as the five-leaved blueweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the plantain family, Plantaginaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, sky-blue flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in whorls of five, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to support local pollinator populations.
