Viburnum erosum var. osumianum

Viburnum erosum var. osumianum, commonly known as the Osumianum honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and have a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale pink, which bloom in the spring. Its fruits are small, red drupes that mature in the fall and are attractive to birds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
