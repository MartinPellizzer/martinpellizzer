Vernonia actaea

Vernonia actaea, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Vernonia actaea is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
