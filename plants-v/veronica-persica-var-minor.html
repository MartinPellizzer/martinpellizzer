Veronica persica var. minor

Veronica persica var. minor, commonly known as Persian speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and forms dense mats, making it well-suited for rock gardens or as a ground cover. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

This variety is closely related to other species in the Veronica genus, sharing similar botanical characteristics.
