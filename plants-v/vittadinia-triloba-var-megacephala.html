Vittadinia triloba var. megacephala

Vittadinia triloba var. megacephala, commonly known as the large-headed everlasting daisy, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that consist of white ray florets surrounding a central disk of yellow tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and heathlands, often in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.
