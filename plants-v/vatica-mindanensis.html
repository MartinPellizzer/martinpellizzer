Vatica mindanensis

Vatica mindanensis, commonly known as the Mindanao ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant typically grows in lowland to hill forest areas, reaching heights of up to 40 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

Vatica mindanensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
