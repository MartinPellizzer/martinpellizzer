Viola × lacmonica

Viola × lacmonica, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Viola tricolor and Viola lutea, combining traits from both parents. The plant typically features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple, blue, and white. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or gardens.

Viola × lacmonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
