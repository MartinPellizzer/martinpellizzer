Vernonia jubifera

Vernonia jubifera, commonly known as the Hawaiian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and its clusters of small, yellow to orange-red tubular flowers. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its hardy nature, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia jubifera is often found in coastal areas and open woodlands, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
