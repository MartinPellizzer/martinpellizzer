Valeriana rhizophora

Valeriana rhizophora, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies. Its roots are valued for their aromatic properties and are used in perfumery and herbal remedies.

Valeriana rhizophora plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
