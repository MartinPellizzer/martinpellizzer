Veronica longifolia f. glabra

Veronica longifolia f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth leaf speedwell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and glabrous. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in gardens and landscapes.
