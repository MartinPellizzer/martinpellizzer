Viola ingolensis

Viola ingolensis, commonly known as the Ingolstadt violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, producing a range of colors from pale violet to deep purple.

Viola ingolensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
