Vanilla fragrans

Vanilla fragrans, commonly known as the vanilla orchid, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Mexico and the Caribbean.

It produces fragrant, greenish-yellow flowers that are pollinated by specific bee species. The plant's most valuable part is its fruit, a dark brown pod containing tiny seeds, which is harvested and processed to create vanilla extract. The vanilla bean undergoes a complex curing process involving drying, sweating, and aging to develop its rich flavor and aroma.

This plant is highly valued in the food and fragrance industries for its unique scent and taste.
