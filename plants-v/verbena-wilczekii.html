Verbena wilczekii

Verbena wilczekii, commonly known as the Mexican verbena, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink, purple, or white, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Verbena wilczekii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
