Valeriana sciophila

Valeriana sciophila, commonly known as the shade valerian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Japan.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Valeriana sciophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with partial shade.
