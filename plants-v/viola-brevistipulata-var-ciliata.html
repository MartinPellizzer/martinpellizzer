Viola brevistipulata var. ciliata

Viola brevistipulata var. ciliata, commonly known as the ciliated violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features heart-shaped leaves with hairy margins. The plant produces showy violet flowers with a distinctive purple-pink coloration. Its flowers have five petals, with the lower petal often marked by a yellowish or white spot.

The species is noted for its delicate foliage and is often found in shaded environments.
