Valota hitchcockii

Valota hitchcockii, commonly known as Hitchcock's valota, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which bloom during the summer months. Valota hitchcockii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
