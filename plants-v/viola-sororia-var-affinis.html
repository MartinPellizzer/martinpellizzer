Viola sororia var. affinis

Viola sororia var. affinis, commonly known as the woodland violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands and thrives in rich, well-drained soils. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet underside and produces small, showy flowers in shades of purple and white. It is part of the Violaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is also valued for its ecological role, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
