Vernonia plumbaginifolia var. plumbaginifolia

Vernonia plumbaginifolia var. plumbaginifolia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
