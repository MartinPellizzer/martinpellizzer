Viola tricolor var. albidocoerulescens

Viola tricolor var. albidocoerulescens, commonly known as the white and blue viola, is a variant of the common violet characterized by its distinctive bicolor flowers.

This perennial herb typically features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and stems that are often hairy. The flowers display a striking combination of white and blue petals, with a yellow throat, making it visually appealing. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, often found in woodland gardens.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its charming, delicate blooms.
