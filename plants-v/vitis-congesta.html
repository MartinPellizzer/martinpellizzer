Vitis congesta

Vitis congesta, commonly known as the clustered grapevine, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that range in color from green to black, depending on the maturity. Vitis congesta is adapted to moist, woodland environments and is often found in shaded areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
