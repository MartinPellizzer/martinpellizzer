Vernonia abbatobiana

Vernonia abbatobiana, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit. The plant produces dense clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia abbatobiana is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
