Veronica hispidula subsp. ixodes

Veronica hispidula subsp.

ixodes, commonly known as the hairy speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, grassy areas and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, blue or violet flowers.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a common sight in temperate regions where it contributes to the local flora.
