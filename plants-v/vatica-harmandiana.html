Vatica harmandiana

Vatica harmandiana, commonly known as the Harmand's ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody capsules containing its seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland and hill forest areas.

Vatica harmandiana is valued for its durable timber and is also studied for its potential ecological and medicinal properties.
