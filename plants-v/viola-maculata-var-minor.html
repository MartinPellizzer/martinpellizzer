Viola maculata var. minor

Viola maculata var. minor, commonly known as the heartleaf violet, is a low-growing perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinct purple or violet coloration. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with a distinctive dark spot near the base. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
