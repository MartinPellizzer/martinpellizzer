Veronica gigantea

Veronica gigantea, commonly known as the giant speedwell, is a striking perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem.

Veronica gigantea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
