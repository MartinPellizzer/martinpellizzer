Veronica polita var. agrestis

Veronica polita var. agrestis, commonly known as the rough veronica, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the plantain family, Plantaginaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically narrow, rough to the touch, and have a slightly hairy texture. This perennial plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas, along roadsides, and in disturbed habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
