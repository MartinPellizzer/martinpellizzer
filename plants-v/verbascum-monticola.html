Verbascum monticola

Verbascum monticola, commonly known as the mountain mullein, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Verbascum monticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
