Viburnum weberbaueri

Viburnum weberbaueri, commonly known as the Chinese snowball, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features large, showy clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant produces bright red berries in the fall, which are a food source for birds. Viburnum weberbaueri typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 feet and has dark green, oval-shaped leaves.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
