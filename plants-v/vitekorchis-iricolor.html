Vitekorchis iricolor

Vitekorchis iricolor, commonly known as the rainbow orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful flowers that display a unique blend of red, orange, and yellow hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal, it is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
