Vouapa violacea

Vouapa violacea, commonly known as the violet-flowered vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-colored flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and compound leaves with three to five leaflets.

Vouapa violacea is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
