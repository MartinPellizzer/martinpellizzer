Verbascum charputense var. adenophorum

Verbascum charputense var. adenophorum, commonly known as the Armenian mullein, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in late summer, which are followed by fluffy, seed-bearing capsules. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
