Vanilla inodora

Vanilla inodora, commonly known as the fragrant vanilla orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique vanilla-scented flowers. The plant produces elongated, fleshy pods known as vanilla beans, which are harvested and dried for their aromatic properties. Vanilla inodora is typically grown in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil.

Its cultivation is often associated with traditional agricultural practices in its native habitats.
