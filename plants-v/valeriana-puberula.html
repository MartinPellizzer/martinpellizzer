Valeriana puberula

Valeriana puberula, commonly known as the hairy valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caprifoliaceae family and typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or white, arranged in clustered inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite and often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, hairy appearance.

Valeriana puberula is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
