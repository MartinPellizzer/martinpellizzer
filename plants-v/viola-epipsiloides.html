Viola epipsiloides

Viola epipsiloides, commonly known as the violet leaf viola, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds.

Viola epipsiloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
