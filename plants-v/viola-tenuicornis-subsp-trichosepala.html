Viola tenuicornis subsp. trichosepala

Viola tenuicornis subsp.

trichosepala, commonly known as the hairy-sepaled violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as forests and rocky slopes.

The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinct hairy texture on the underside. Its flowers are violet to purple in color, with five sepals that are notably hairy, giving it its subspecific name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
