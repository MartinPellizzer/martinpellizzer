Viola silvatica var. bertotii

Viola silvatica var. bertotii, commonly known as the Bertot's woodland violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Europe.

It typically grows in shaded, moist woodland environments, where it thrives in the dappled light under forest canopies. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces delicate, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive pattern of markings. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.

Botanically, it belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit.
