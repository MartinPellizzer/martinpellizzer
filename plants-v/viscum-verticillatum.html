Viscum verticillatum

Viscum verticillatum, commonly known as the clustered mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It belongs to the family Viscaceae and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and clusters of white, fleshy berries. The plant derives some of its nutrients from its host through specialized roots called haustoria, while also performing photosynthesis. It is native to regions of Africa, Madagascar, and the Arabian Peninsula.

Viscum verticillatum plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for birds and other wildlife.
