Verbena porrigens var. paulsenii

Verbena porrigens var. paulsenii, commonly known as the purple verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in summer, producing clusters of small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is aromatic and often covered in fine, soft hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
