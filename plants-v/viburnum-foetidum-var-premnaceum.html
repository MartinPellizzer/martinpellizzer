Viburnum foetidum var. premnaceum

Viburnum foetidum var. premnaceum, commonly known as the sweet viburnum, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a smooth texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clustered inflorescences, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a dark blue-black drupe that persists through the winter, providing food for birds.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.
