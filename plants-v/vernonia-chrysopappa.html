Vernonia chrysopappa

Vernonia chrysopappa, commonly known as the golden Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of yellow to orange flower heads that bloom during the warmer months. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The genus Vernonia is part of the sunflower family, Asteraceae, and is known for its showy, composite flower structures.
