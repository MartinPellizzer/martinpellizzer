Viburnum utile var. minor

Viburnum utile var. minor, commonly known as dwarf arrowwood, is a compact shrub native to eastern North America.

It features small, dark green leaves that turn red or orange in the fall, adding seasonal interest to gardens. The plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries that attract birds. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is hardy in USDA zones 4 through 8.

Viburnum utile var. minor is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
