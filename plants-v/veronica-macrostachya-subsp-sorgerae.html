Veronica macrostachya subsp. sorgerae

Veronica macrostachya subsp.

sorgerae, commonly known as the giant speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
