Veronica fruticulosa var. saxatilis

Veronica fruticulosa var. saxatilis, commonly known as the rock veronica, is a perennial plant native to rocky and alpine environments.

It features wiry, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs, adapted to withstand harsh conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
