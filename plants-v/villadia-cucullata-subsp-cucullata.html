Villadia cucullata subsp. cucullata

Villadia cucullata subsp.

cucullata, commonly known as the hooded bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, blooming in late summer.

The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

The subspecies is characterized by its distinctive hooded calyx that encloses the flower bud before it opens.
