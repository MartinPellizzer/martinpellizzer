Vanda sumatrana

Vanda sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran vanda, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, strap-like leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks. Vanda sumatrana has a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, aiding its survival in its natural habitat.

Its flowers bloom in clusters and are popular in horticulture for their beauty and longevity.
