Veronica formanekii

Veronica formanekii, commonly known as the mountain veronica, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its erect, sparsely hairy stems. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

Veronica formanekii thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cool, high-altitude environments.
