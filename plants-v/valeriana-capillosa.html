Valeriana capillosa

Valeriana capillosa, commonly known as the silky valerian, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its fragrant flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Valeriana capillosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported calming properties.
