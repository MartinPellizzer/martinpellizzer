Vernonia assimilis

Vernonia assimilis, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Vernonia assimilis is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

It also serves as a nectar source for various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
