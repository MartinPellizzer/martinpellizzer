Vincetoxicum excelsum

Vincetoxicum excelsum, commonly known as the giant false bindweed, is a vigorous climbing vine native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture and produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its strong, twining stems that can grow up to 10 meters in length. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its invasive potential in new environments.

Due to its rapid growth and allelopathic properties, it is considered a noxious weed in many regions.
