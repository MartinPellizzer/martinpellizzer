Vaccinium pennsylvanicum

Vaccinium pennsylvanicum, commonly known as the Pennsylvania blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other heath and blueberry species. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or black in color, rich in antioxidants and flavor. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Vaccinium pennsylvanicum is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
