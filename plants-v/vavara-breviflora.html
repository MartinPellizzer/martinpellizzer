Vavara breviflora

Vavara breviflora, commonly known as the short-flowered vavara, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of violet and blue. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests and alpine meadows. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine, silvery down, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.

Vavara breviflora is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and limited distribution.
