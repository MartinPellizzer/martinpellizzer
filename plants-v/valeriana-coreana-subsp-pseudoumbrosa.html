Valeriana coreana subsp. pseudoumbrosa

Valeriana coreana subsp.

pseudoumbrosa, commonly known as Korean valerian, is a perennial herb native to the Korean Peninsula. It typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, often found in mountainous regions.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that have a distinctive aromatic scent. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and are usually pink or white in color. Valeriana coreana subsp.

pseudoumbrosa is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
