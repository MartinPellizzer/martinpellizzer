Vitex elakelakensis

Vitex elakelakensis, commonly known as the Kollam vitex, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of Kerala, India.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in coastal environments and is often found in sandy soils near the shoreline.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
