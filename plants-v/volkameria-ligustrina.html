Volkameria ligustrina

Volkameria ligustrina, commonly known as the sweet shrub, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and a woody stem, which contribute to its adaptability in tropical and subtropical environments.

Volkameria ligustrina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
