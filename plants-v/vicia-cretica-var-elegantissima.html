Vicia cretica var. elegantissima

Vicia cretica var. elegantissima, commonly known as the elegant common vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial plant characterized by its slender, trailing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep purple, depending on the variety. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and wastelands.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant in soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
