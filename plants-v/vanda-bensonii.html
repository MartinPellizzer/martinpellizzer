Vanda bensonii

Vanda bensonii, commonly known as the Bensoni orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendant-like inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has fleshy, strap-like leaves that are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Vanda bensonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for with proper light and moisture conditions.

Its flowers bloom throughout the year, making it a popular choice for indoor and greenhouse cultivation.
