Verbascum nudatum var. nudatum

Verbascum nudatum var. nudatum, commonly known as the common mullein, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces yellow flowers in the summer, which are followed by fluffy, seed-filled capsules. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in meadows.

Its leaves and flowers have been historically used for their medicinal properties, including treating respiratory ailments.
