Veronica obtusata

Veronica obtusata, commonly known as the blunt-leaved speedwell, is a flowering plant native to regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically oval with a blunt or rounded tip, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
