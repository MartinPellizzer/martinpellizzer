Vaccinium henrici

Vaccinium henrici, commonly known as Henry's blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many other shrubs and small trees adapted to acidic soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible berries that are dark blue to black in color. Its leaves are oval-shaped, leathery, and densely packed, providing dense cover for wildlife.

Vaccinium henrici thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
