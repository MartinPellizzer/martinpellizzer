Viburnum erosum var. atratocarpum

Viburnum erosum var. atratocarpum, commonly known as black-fruited snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that persist through winter, providing food for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture, turning reddish in autumn.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.
