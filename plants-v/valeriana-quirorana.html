Valeriana quirorana

Valeriana quirorana, commonly known as the Peruvian valerian, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. Its rhizomes and roots are used in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

Valeriana quirorana is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its vibrant flower colors and adaptability to diverse ecological conditions.
