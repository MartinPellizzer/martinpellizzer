Vitex grandidiana var. angustifolia

Vitex grandidiana var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf chaste tree, is a species native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in spikes and range in color from white to purple, attracting various pollinators.

The fruit is a dry capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
