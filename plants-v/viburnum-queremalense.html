Viburnum queremalense

Viburnum queremalense, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright purple berries that appear in late summer to early fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and thrives in partial to full sun.

Viburnum queremalense is valued in gardens for its ornamental berries and attractive foliage.
