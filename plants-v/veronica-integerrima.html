Veronica integerrima

Veronica integerrima, commonly known as the upright speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear opposite pairs of lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and appear in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Veronica integerrima is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
