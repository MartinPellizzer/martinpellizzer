Viola patrinii var. minor

Viola patrinii var. minor, commonly known as the lesser violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to regions of Europe and western Asia, this plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The flowers are usually borne on slender stems and are pollinated by bees and other insects.

It is a perennial herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.
