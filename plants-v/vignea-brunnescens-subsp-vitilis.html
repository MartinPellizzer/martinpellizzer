Vignea brunnescens subsp. vitilis

Vignea brunnescens subsp.

vitilis, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands.

The plant features showy yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

The subspecies is distinguished by its pale or white-colored veins, giving it a distinctive appearance.
