Viola × paradoxa

Viola × paradoxa, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a species resulting from the cross between Viola tricolor and Viola verecunda.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, bi- or tri-colored flowers that range from purple to white. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and prefers moist, well-drained soil in partial shade. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to woodland gardens.
