Viola brevistipulata var. hidakana

Viola brevistipulata var. hidakana, commonly known as the Hidaka violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces violet-blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after species in horticulture.
