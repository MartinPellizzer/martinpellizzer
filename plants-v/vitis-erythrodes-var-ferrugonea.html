Vitis erythrodes var. ferrugonea

Vitis erythrodes var. ferrugonea, commonly known as the rusty grape, is a variant of the red grapevine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown bark and small, clustered, dark purple berries. The plant is a vigorous vine with compound leaves that have serrated edges and a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
