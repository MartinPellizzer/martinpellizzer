Vitex agelaeifolia var. rufula

Vitex agelaeifolia var. rufula, commonly known as the rough-leaved vitex, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and clusters of fragrant, purple or blue flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in coastal areas and can thrive in sandy or well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some cultures.
