Viola silvatica var. reichenbachiana

Viola silvatica var. reichenbachiana, commonly known as the Reichenbach's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It typically grows in shaded, moist woodland areas, where it thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and produces violet flowers with a distinctive yellow center. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the forest floor.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
