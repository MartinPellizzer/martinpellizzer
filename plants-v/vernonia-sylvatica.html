Vernonia sylvatica

Vernonia sylvatica, commonly known as woodland ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist woodlands and thickets. The plant produces clusters of purple to pinkish-red tubular flowers, which bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Vernonia sylvatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
