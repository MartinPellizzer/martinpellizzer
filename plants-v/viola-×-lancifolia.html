Viola × lancifolia

Viola × lancifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, often violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and garden beds. It has a compact growth habit and is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Botanically, it exhibits a range of morphological variations depending on its geographic origin and hybridization history.
