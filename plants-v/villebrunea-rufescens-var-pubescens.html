Villebrunea rufescens var. pubescens

Villebrunea rufescens var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy villebrunea, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within lowland forests. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is noted for its ornamental value due to its distinctive foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
