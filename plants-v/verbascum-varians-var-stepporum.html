Verbascum varians var. stepporum

Verbascum varians var. stepporum, commonly known as the steppe mullein, is a perennial herb native to the steppes of Central Asia.

It features tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, covered with dense, woolly hairs. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green to reddish flowers in elongated spikes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are large, broad, and also covered with soft, fuzzy hairs.

It is adapted to dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.
