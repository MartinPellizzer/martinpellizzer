Vaccinium corymbosum var. fuscatum

Vaccinium corymbosum var. fuscatum, commonly known as the black huckleberry, is a shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the fall. The plant has elliptical leaves that are leathery and dark green in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
