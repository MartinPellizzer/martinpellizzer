Vanda watsonii

Vanda watsonii, commonly known as the Watson's vanda, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, strap-like leaves and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air, making it well-suited for hanging or mounted displays.

Vanda watsonii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience in cultivation.
