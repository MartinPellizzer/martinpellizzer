Viola carlesii

Viola carlesii, commonly known as the Carles's violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and the Tian Shan. The plant produces small, heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Viola carlesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
