Vigna heterophylla var. lanceolata

Vigna heterophylla var. lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, lanceolate leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 to 2 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its protein-rich seeds.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
