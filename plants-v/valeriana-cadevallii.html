Valeriana cadevallii

Valeriana cadevallii, commonly known as the Spanish valerian, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, covered with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers arranged in dense, rounded clusters called cymes. Its roots are aromatic and have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their calming properties.

Valeriana cadevallii thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
