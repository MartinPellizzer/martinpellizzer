Vaccinium humboldtii

Vaccinium humboldtii, commonly known as the Humboldt huckleberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many plants adapted to acidic soils. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in temperate rainforests.

Its leaves are dark green and leathery, with a dense growth habit that provides habitat for various wildlife species.
