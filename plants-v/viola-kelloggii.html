Viola kelloggii

Viola kelloggii, commonly known as the showy lady's slipper, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers feature a unique pouch-like structure that aids in pollination by bees and other insects.

Viola kelloggii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
