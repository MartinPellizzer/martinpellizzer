Vigna sesquipedalis

Vigna sesquipedalis, commonly known as the giant cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and elongated, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 meters, with a sprawling or climbing habit. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or white, and it produces a significant amount of biomass.

Vigna sesquipedalis is valued for its nutritional seeds and is often cultivated for both food and forage.
