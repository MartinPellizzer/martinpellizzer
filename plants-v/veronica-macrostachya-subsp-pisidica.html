Veronica macrostachya subsp. pisidica

Veronica macrostachya subsp.

pisidica, commonly known as the Carpathian speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian Mountains. It is a perennial herb with erect, branched stems that bear small, blue to purple flowers.

The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky habitats.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, facilitating its spread in suitable habitats.
