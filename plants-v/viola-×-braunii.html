Viola × braunii

Viola × braunii, commonly known as the velvetleaf viola, is a hybrid perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are dark green in color and its clusters of violet or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 inches and prefers well-drained, moist soil in partial shade. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and its ability to thrive in cooler climates.

Viola × braunii is also valued for its role in supporting local pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
