Vaccinium randaiense

Vaccinium randaiense, commonly known as the Japanese huckleberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, followed by edible berries that range in color from red to black. Vaccinium randaiense thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with cool, moist climates.

It is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its use in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
