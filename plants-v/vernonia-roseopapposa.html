Vernonia roseopapposa

Vernonia roseopapposa, commonly known as the rose-flowered ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in soft, grayish-green foliage. Its most distinctive feature is the showy, pink to rose-colored flower heads that appear in late summer to early fall.

Vernonia roseopapposa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
