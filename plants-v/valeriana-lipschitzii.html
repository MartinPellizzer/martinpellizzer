Valeriana lipschitzii

Valeriana lipschitzii, commonly known as the Armenian valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often reddish or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or woodland soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a strong, pleasant fragrance.

Valeriana lipschitzii is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its calming effects.
