Vatica obovata

Vatica obovata, commonly known as the broad-ovate ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in hue. Its hard, dense wood is highly valued for construction and furniture-making.

Vatica obovata thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
