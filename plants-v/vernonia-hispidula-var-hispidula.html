Vernonia hispidula var. hispidula

Vernonia hispidula var. hispidula, commonly known as roughleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and tall, upright stems that bear clusters of small, blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
