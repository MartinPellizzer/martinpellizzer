Veronica ovalifolia

Veronica ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaved speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Veronica ovalifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
