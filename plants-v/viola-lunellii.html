Viola lunellii

Viola lunellii, commonly known as the moon violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a touch of color to the landscape.

Viola lunellii is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
