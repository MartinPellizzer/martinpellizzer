Vitex bojeri var. suborbicularis

Vitex bojeri var. suborbicularis, commonly known as the orbicular vitex, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, orbicular-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are woody capsules that split open to release small, black seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
