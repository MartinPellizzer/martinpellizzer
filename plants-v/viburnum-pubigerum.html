Viburnum pubigerum

Viburnum pubigerum, commonly known as the pubescent viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are typically ovate with a hairy texture, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Viburnum pubigerum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.
