Verbascum virgatum

Verbascum virgatum, commonly known as the slender mullein, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in dry, rocky habitats.

Verbascum virgatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
