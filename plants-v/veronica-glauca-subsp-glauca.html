Veronica glauca subsp. glauca

Veronica glauca subsp.

glauca, commonly known as blueweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, riverbanks, and woodland edges.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine blue or greyish pubescence. Its showy blue to purple flowers are arranged in spike-like clusters, blooming from late spring to early summer.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
