Verbascum urticifolium

Verbascum urticifolium, commonly known as the stinging mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that can cause skin irritation upon contact. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats like fields and roadsides.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used historically for respiratory ailments and as a source of fiber.
