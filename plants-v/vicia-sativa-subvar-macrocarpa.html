Vicia sativa subvar. macrocarpa

Vicia sativa subvar. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-seeded vetch, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and long, slender pods that contain large, kidney-shaped seeds. The plant is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often used as a forage crop and in ecological restoration projects due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.

Its flowers are usually pink or purple, adding ornamental value to the landscape.
