Verbascum × dimorphum

Verbascum × dimorphum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a cross between Verbascum blattaria and Verbascum blattellum, resulting in a plant with distinctive characteristics from both parents. The plant features large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It produces tall spikes of yellow flowers in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Verbascum × dimorphum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in various garden conditions.
