Viola × colliniformis

Viola × colliniformis, commonly known as the creeping violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of purple, violet, and white, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Viola × colliniformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
