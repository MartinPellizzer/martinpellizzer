Veronica anagallis-aquatica subvar. grandiflora

Veronica anagallis-aquatica subvar. grandiflora, commonly known as the water speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats.

It features slender, branching stems that often grow from water or moist soil. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers in clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and typically have a slightly hairy texture.

It is well adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic environments and is often found in marshes, stream banks, and pond edges.
