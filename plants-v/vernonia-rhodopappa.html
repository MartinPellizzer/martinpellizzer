Vernonia rhodopappa

Vernonia rhodopappa, commonly known as the red-leaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Vernonia rhodopappa is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
