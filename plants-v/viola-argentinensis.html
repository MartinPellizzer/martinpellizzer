Viola argentinensis

Viola argentinensis, commonly known as the Argentine violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in spring and are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola argentinensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
