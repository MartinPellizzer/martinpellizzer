Verbascum ferrugineum

Verbascum ferrugineum, commonly known as the rust-hair mullein, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces large, fuzzy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are gray-green with a velvety texture and are covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
