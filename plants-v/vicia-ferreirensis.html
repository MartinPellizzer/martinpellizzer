Vicia ferreirensis

Vicia ferreirensis, commonly known as the Ferreirensis vetch, is a flowering plant species native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grassland and rocky habitats. The plant features slender, erect stems with trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually purple or violet in color and appear in racemes.

Vicia ferreirensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
