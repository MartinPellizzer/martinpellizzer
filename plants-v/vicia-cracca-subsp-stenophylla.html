Vicia cracca subsp. stenophylla

Vicia cracca subsp.

stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed vetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with narrow, elliptical leaflets.

The flowers are typically violet to blue-purple, arranged in racemes, and are pollinated by insects. It produces small, flat, pod-like fruits that contain several elliptical seeds.

This subspecies is adapted to a variety of habitats, including disturbed soils and open woodlands.
