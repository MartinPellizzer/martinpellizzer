Veronica bombycina subsp. bombycina

Veronica bombycina subsp.

bombycina, commonly known as the woolly speedwell, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, upright stems covered in soft, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers arranged in whorls along the stems, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

This subspecies is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
