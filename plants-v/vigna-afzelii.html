Vigna afzelii

Vigna afzelii, commonly known as the African horsegram, is a leguminous plant native to Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. Vigna afzelii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a valuable crop in drought-prone regions.

It is also known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enhances soil fertility.
