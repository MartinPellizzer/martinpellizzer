Veronica villosa

Veronica villosa, commonly known as hairy speedwell, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

Veronica villosa is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
