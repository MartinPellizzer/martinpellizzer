Viola palmata var. dilatata

Viola palmata var. dilatata, commonly known as the broadleaf violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of delicate, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
