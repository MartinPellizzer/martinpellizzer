Viola odorata var. alba

Viola odorata var. alba, commonly known as the white sweet violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces fragrant, white or pale purple flowers with five petals. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

The flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
