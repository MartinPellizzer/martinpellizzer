Vigna tisserantiana

Vigna tisserantiana, commonly known as the Tisserant's cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters, with hairy stems and yellow flowers. Its seeds are often used in traditional cuisines and are valued for their nutritional content.

Vigna tisserantiana is also known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
