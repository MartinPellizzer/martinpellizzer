Valeriana virescens

Valeriana virescens, commonly known as blue valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems and clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. The plant produces tiny, round fruits known as caryopses, which contain the seeds. Blue valerian thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and has a mild, aromatic scent.
