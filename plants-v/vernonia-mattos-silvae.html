Vernonia mattos-silvae

Vernonia mattos-silvae, commonly known as the Brazilian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow to brown in color.

Vernonia mattos-silvae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
