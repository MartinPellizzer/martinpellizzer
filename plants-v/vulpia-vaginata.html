Vulpia vaginata

Vulpia vaginata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved foxtail, is a perennial grass species native to Eurasia.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and waste areas. The plant has slender, tufted inflorescences that resemble a foxtail, composed of spikelets arranged in a panicle. Its leaves are narrow and tapering, with a rough texture and prominent veins.

Vulpia vaginata is often found in temperate climates and is adapted to thrive in poor soil conditions.
