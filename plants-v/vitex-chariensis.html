Vitex chariensis

Vitex chariensis, commonly known as the white chaste tree, is a member of the Verbenaceae family.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, fragrant white or pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule containing several seeds.

Vitex chariensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
