Viola jalapaensis

Viola jalapaensis, commonly known as the Mexican violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers bloom in spring and are often pollinated by bees and butterflies.

Viola jalapaensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
