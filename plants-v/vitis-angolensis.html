Vitis angolensis

Vitis angolensis, commonly known as the Angolan grapevine, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a woody vine belonging to the Vitaceae family, characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, yellow to orange berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for fruit production, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
