Vernonia patula

Vernonia patula, commonly known as the slender ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

Vernonia patula is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
