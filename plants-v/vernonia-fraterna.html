Vernonia fraterna

Vernonia fraterna, commonly known as fraterna ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and meadows. Its showy purple to pink flower heads appear in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Vernonia fraterna is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
