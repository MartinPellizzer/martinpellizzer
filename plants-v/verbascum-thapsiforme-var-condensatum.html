Verbascum thapsiforme var. condensatum

Verbascum thapsiforme var. condensatum, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem covered in dense, soft, woolly hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are large, broad, and also covered in a thick layer of fine hairs.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses.
