Vinca herbacea subsp. libanotica

Vinca herbacea subsp.

libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanese vinca, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing perennial with fleshy, oval-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bright blue or purple flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
