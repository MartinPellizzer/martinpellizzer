Veronica recta

Veronica recta, commonly known as the upright speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodlands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Veronica recta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
