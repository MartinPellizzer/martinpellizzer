Virgilia grandis

Virgilia grandis, commonly known as the giant virgilia, is a striking flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers. The plant can grow up to several meters in height, with compound leaves and a woody stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological importance.
