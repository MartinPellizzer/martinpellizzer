Viscum nervosum var. angustifolium

Viscum nervosum var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are often inconspicuous, followed by berries that are red or orange in color. It is native to regions of Europe and is often found in temperate forests.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique and ecologically significant species in its habitat.
