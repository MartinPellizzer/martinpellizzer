Valerianella capitata

Valerianella capitata, commonly known as garden sorrel, is a flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and heart-shaped leaves that are typically eaten raw in salads. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soil. It produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Valerianella capitata is often cultivated for its edible leaves and ornamental value in gardens.
