Verbena connatibracteata

Verbena connatibracteata, commonly known as the narrow-bract verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue or purple. The plant has opposite, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.

Verbena connatibracteata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
