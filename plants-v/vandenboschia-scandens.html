Vandenboschia scandens

Vandenboschia scandens, commonly known as the climbing vandenboschia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and fleshy, cylindrical leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is popular among succulent enthusiasts.
