Valeriana araucana

Valeriana araucana, commonly known as the Andean valerian, is a perennial herb native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It features a robust rhizomatous root system and lance-shaped, aromatic leaves with a distinctive violet hue. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Valeriana araucana is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its calming effects.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for both ornamental and therapeutic purposes.
