Verbascum vulcanicum var. viridans

Verbascum vulcanicum var. viridans, commonly known as the greenleaf mullein, is a perennial plant native to the volcanic regions of the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, velvety, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green in color. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in the summer, which are clustered in dense, branched inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
