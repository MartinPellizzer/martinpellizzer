Vitex harveyana

Vitex harveyana, commonly known as Harvey's vitex, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. These flowers are followed by small, woody fruits containing several seeds.

It is well adapted to coastal conditions, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.
