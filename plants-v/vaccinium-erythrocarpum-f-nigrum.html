Vaccinium erythrocarpum f. nigrum

Vaccinium erythrocarpum f. nigrum, commonly known as the black huckleberry, is a variant of the red-fruited huckleberry.

It is a shrub native to the northern regions of the United States and Canada. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants. The leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a slightly serrated margin.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, forested areas.
