Veronica anagallis-aquatica var. nilotica

Veronica anagallis-aquatica var. nilotica, commonly known as the water speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It features small, blue or purple flowers clustered in racemes, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near water bodies such as ponds and streams.

Its adaptability to aquatic conditions makes it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
