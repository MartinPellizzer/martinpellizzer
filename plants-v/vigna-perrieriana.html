Vigna perrieriana

Vigna perrieriana, commonly known as the Perrier's bean, is a species of legume native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a climbing plant with trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated, cylindrical pods. The seeds are typically small and have a high protein content, making them a potential food source. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate with adequate rainfall.

Due to its ornamental value and nutritional properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
