Vepris glaberrima

Vepris glaberrima, commonly known as African birch or African ironwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by small, round, orange-red fruits. Its bark is thick and deeply fissured, and it is known for its hard, dense wood that is highly valued for construction and carving.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
