Viburnum opulus f. glabrifolium

Viburnum opulus f. glabrifolium, commonly known as the smoothleaf dogwood, is a cultivar of the European dogwood characterized by its distinctive, showy bracts.

It features clusters of small white flowers that appear in spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The leaves are smooth and oval, providing an attractive appearance throughout the growing season. This plant is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic value and adaptability to various soil conditions.

It prefers partial shade to full sun and is relatively low maintenance once established.
