Vallaris solanacea

Vallaris solanacea, commonly known as the nightshade vine, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or purple in color. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Vallaris solanacea is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
