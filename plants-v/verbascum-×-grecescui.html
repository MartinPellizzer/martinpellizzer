Verbascum × grecescui

Verbascum × grecescui, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes several species of flowering plants known for their fuzzy, woolly leaves. This particular hybrid is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or orange. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant ornamentals.

Its botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically valuable, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
