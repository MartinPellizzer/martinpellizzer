Vaccinium oreogenes

Vaccinium oreogenes, commonly known as the Pacific silverleaf blueberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and heathers. The plant is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and produces edible, dark blue berries. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
