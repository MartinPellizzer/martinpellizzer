Veronica corriganii

Veronica corriganii, commonly known as Corrigan's speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous regions. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces capsules that contain its seeds.

Veronica corriganii is a relatively rare species, making it of interest to botanists and conservationists.
