Verticordia cunninghamii

Verticordia cunninghamii, commonly known as the Cunningham's featherblades, is a striking member of the Myrtaceae family native to Western Australia.

This species is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, thread-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces vibrant, red or orange flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, adding a dramatic visual impact. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in heathland and coastal environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
