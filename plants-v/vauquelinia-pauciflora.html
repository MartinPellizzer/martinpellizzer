Vauquelinia pauciflora

Vauquelinia pauciflora, commonly known as the Florida vauquelinia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and emit a mild, pleasant fragrance. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by red, spherical berries. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Vauquelinia pauciflora is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
