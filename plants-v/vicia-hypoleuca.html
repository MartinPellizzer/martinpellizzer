Vicia hypoleuca

Vicia hypoleuca, commonly known as the white vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces cylindrical pods containing several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas.

Vicia hypoleuca is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration and soil enrichment.
