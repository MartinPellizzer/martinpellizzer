Viscum congolense

Viscum congolense, commonly known as the African mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Viscaceae and is characterized by its evergreen, leathery leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties. Viscum congolense is also noted for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its unique mode of seed dispersal via birds.

Its ecological role includes providing food for certain bird species and contributing to the biodiversity of its forest habitats.
