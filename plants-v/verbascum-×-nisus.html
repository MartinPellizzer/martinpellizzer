Verbascum × nisus

Verbascum × nisus, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes various species of mullein known for their fuzzy, velvety leaves. The hybrid exhibits characteristics of both parent species, often displaying large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically broad and covered in fine, soft hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

This plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and adaptability to different soil types.
