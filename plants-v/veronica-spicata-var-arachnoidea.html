Veronica spicata var. arachnoidea

Veronica spicata var. arachnoidea, commonly known as the spider-hair speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine, hair-like structures that give the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
