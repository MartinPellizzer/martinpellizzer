Verbascum tiberiadis

Verbascum tiberiadis, commonly known as the Tiberian mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Verbascum tiberiadis is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
