Vatica soepadmoi

Vatica soepadmoi, commonly known as the Java ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and evergreen foliage. The plant belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is typically found in lowland rainforests. Vatica soepadmoi has small, inconspicuous flowers that are often borne in clusters.

Its seeds are dispersed by animals, contributing to its ecological significance in forest ecosystems.
