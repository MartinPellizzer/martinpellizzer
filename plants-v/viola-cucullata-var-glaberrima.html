Viola cucullata var. glaberrima

Viola cucullata var. glaberrima, commonly known as the glabrous hoodsnakevine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows along stream banks and in moist, shaded woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its distinctive hood-like shape of the flower, which gives it its common name.
