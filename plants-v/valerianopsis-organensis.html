Valerianopsis organensis

Valerianopsis organensis, commonly known as the Organ Mountains valerian, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the family Valerianaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid environments. Valerianopsis organensis has narrow, linear leaves and produces tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring.

It is often found in mountainous regions and is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological adaptations.
