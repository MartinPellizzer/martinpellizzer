Veronica perbella

Veronica perbella, commonly known as the slender speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Veronica perbella is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
