Vatica mangachapoi

Vatica mangachapoi, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy timber that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree has large, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by woody capsules containing seeds.

Vatica mangachapoi thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland and hill forests.
