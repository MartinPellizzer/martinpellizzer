Viola × megapolitana

Viola × megapolitana, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to the temperate regions of Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens or shaded borders.

Viola × megapolitana is also valued for its ability to thrive in cool climates and its resilience to various environmental conditions.
