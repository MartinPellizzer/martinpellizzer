Vernonia imbricata

Vernonia imbricata, commonly known as the silverleaf Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in dense, conical clusters. The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It thrives in full sun and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The plant's ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in arid regions.
