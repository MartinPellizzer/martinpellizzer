Viola betonicifolia subsp. betonicifolia

Viola betonicifolia subsp.

betonicifolia, commonly known as the purple marsh violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wet meadows and marshy areas in Europe. It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with purple, and its flowers are typically violet or purple with five petals.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in temperate regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Violaceae family.
