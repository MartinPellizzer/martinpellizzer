Vanda multiflora

Vanda multiflora, commonly known as the giant vanda, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy pseudobulbs and long, arching stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often blooming multiple times a year. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

Vanda multiflora thrives in warm, humid environments with bright indirect light and is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and resilience.
