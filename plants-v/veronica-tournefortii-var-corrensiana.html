Veronica tournefortii var. corrensiana

Veronica tournefortii var. corrensiana, commonly known as the Corsican speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with dark green, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of blue or violet flowers in late spring to early summer, which are borne on slender stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and hardiness in mild climates.
