Vitex agnus

Vitex agnus, commonly known as the chaste tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces fragrant, blue or purple flowers in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Vitex agnus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
