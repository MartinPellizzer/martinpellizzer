Valeriana lobata

Valeriana lobata, commonly known as the mountain valerian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant leaves and flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and range in color from white to pink, attracting various pollinators.

Valeriana lobata is also valued for its medicinal properties, with historical use in treating anxiety and sleep disorders.
