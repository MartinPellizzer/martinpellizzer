Verbascum gadarense

Verbascum gadarense, commonly known as the Gadarense mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes many species of mullein plants characterized by their large, fuzzy leaves and showy yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, adapting to the unique environmental conditions of its native habitat. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Verbascum gadarense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
