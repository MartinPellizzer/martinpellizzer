Vachellia campeachiana f. houghii

Vachellia campeachiana f. houghii, commonly known as the Hough's screwpod tree, is a variant of the screwpod acacia native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, twisted pod-like fruits that give it its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, feathery leaflets, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique form and hardy nature.
