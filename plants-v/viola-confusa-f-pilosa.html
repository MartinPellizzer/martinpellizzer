Viola confusa f. pilosa

Viola confusa f. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple and white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
