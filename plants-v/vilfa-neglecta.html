Vilfa neglecta

Vilfa neglecta, commonly known as the neglected violet, is a small flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces delicate purple or violet flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a short stem, hairy foliage, and a preference for well-drained soil.

Vilfa neglecta is often found in disturbed or semi-natural habitats, where it plays a minor role in local ecosystems.
