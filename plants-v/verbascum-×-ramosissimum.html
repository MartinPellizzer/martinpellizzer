Verbascum × ramosissimum

Verbascum × ramosissimum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, covered in soft, fuzzy gray-green leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, terminal spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for dry gardens.

Verbascum × ramosissimum is also valued for its medicinal properties, with historical uses in respiratory treatments.
