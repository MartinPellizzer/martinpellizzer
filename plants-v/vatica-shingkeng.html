Vatica shingkeng

Vatica shingkeng, commonly known as the Shingkeng ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its hard, dense wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and thrives in humid, warm climates with high rainfall. It has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered together.

The tree is also known for its slow growth and long lifespan, making it an important component of its native ecosystems.
