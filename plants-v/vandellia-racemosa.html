Vandellia racemosa

Vandellia racemosa, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that are clustered in a raceme, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Vandellia racemosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
