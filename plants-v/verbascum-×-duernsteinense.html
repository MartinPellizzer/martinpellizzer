Verbascum × duernsteinense

Verbascum × duernsteinense, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant belonging to the Scrophulariaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in various regions with suitable climates.
