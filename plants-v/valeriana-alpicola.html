Valeriana alpicola

Valeriana alpicola, commonly known as alpine valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often growing in rocky soils at elevations above 1,500 meters. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white to pink. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapted to withstand cold and harsh conditions.

Valeriana alpicola is valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat anxiety and sleep disorders.
