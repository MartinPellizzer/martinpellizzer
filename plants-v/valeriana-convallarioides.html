Valeriana convallarioides

Valeriana convallarioides, commonly known as the sweet woodruff, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring heart-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, aromatic scent, which is often described as reminiscent of vanilla or green apple. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

Valeriana convallarioides thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
