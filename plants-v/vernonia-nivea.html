Vernonia nivea

Vernonia nivea, commonly known as white ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant produces slender, bract-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Vernonia nivea is typically found in moist, open habitats like wetlands and disturbed soils.
