Veronica anagallis-aquatica subsp. cordata

Veronica anagallis-aquatica subsp.

cordata, commonly known as water speedwell, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland and aquatic environments. It features small, blue or violet flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer.

The plant has slender, branching stems that often grow in water or along the edges of wetlands. Its leaves are typically opposite, with a rounded or cordate shape, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often found in shallow water, marshes, and along stream banks, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
