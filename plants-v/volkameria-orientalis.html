Volkameria orientalis

Volkameria orientalis, commonly known as the Chinese hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble those of hibiscus species, with five petals and a prominent central column. The plant produces bright yellow stamens and a red or orange pistil, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Volkameria orientalis is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
