Veronica × kirkii

Veronica × kirkii, commonly known as the blueweed or silverleaf veronica, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, silver-tomentose leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Veronica × kirkii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in coastal and maritime climates.
