Verbascum blattaria var. albiflorum

Verbascum blattaria var. albiflorum, commonly known as the whiteflower mullein, is a variant of the common mullein plant.

It is a herbaceous perennial that features tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late summer. It is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soils.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
