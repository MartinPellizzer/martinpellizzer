Vigna ricciardiana var. macrocarpa

Vigna ricciardiana var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa.

It produces large, pod-like fruits that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, containing edible seeds. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its nutritious seeds.

The species is notable for its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable crop in sustainable agriculture.
