Veronica mollis

Veronica mollis, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It features small, blue to purple flowers and hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is known for its ability to spread via runners.

Its delicate appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in shaded areas.
