Valeriana tuberosa var. ateridoi

Valeriana tuberosa var. ateridoi, commonly known as the dark violet valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine for their sedative properties. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

Valeriana tuberosa var. ateridoi thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.
