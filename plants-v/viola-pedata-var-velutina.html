Viola pedata var. velutina

Viola pedata var. velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green with a soft texture. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-purple flowers with five petals, often appearing in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically valuable member of the Violaceae family.
