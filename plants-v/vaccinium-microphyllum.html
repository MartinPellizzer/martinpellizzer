Vaccinium microphyllum

Vaccinium microphyllum, commonly known as the dwarf blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like rhododendrons and azaleas. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or dark purple in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and used in the production of jams and other culinary products.
