Viola × perplexa

Viola × perplexa, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Viola tricolor and Viola verecunda, resulting in a plant with diverse morphological traits. The species is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas across its native range.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
