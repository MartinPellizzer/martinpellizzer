Vitis vitiginea var. tamoides

Vitis vitiginea var. tamoides, commonly known as the Tamoid grapevine, is a vigorous climbing vine native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its robust stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that give way to berries, which can be either green or red depending on the variety. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along streams.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for its edible fruits in some regions.
