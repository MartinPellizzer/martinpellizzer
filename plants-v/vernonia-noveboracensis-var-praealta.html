Vernonia noveboracensis var. praealta

Vernonia noveboracensis var. praealta, commonly known as the tall ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem that can reach heights of up to 6 feet. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and often have a grayish-green appearance. Its showy purple flower heads appear in late summer and are arranged in loose, cylindrical clusters.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
