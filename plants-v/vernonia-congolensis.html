Vernonia congolensis

Vernonia congolensis, commonly known as African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Vernonia congolensis is often found in moist grasslands and forest margins, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses make it a notable species in botanical studies.
