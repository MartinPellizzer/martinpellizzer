Viola curvicalcarata

Viola curvicalcarata, commonly known as the curled calyx violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant is characterized by its distinctive violet flowers with a unique, curled calyx that gives it its common name. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and often trailing.

Viola curvicalcarata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
