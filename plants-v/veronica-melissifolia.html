Veronica melissifolia

Veronica melissifolia, commonly known as meadow veronica, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodlands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer.

Veronica melissifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
