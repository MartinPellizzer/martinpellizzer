Verbesina caracasana

Verbesina caracasana, commonly known as the Caracas beauty or yellow crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaves. The plant features showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy and branched.

Verbesina caracasana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
