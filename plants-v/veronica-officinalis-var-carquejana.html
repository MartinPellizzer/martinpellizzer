Veronica officinalis var. carquejana

Veronica officinalis var. carquejana, commonly known as Carquejana speedwell, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in dry, open habitats such as steppes and coastal areas. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, blue or purple, and arranged in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
