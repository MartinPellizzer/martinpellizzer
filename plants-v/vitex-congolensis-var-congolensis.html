Vitex congolensis var. congolensis

Vitex congolensis var. congolensis, commonly known as the African chaste tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant, blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, followed by winged fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
