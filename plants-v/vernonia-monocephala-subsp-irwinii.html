Vernonia monocephala subsp. irwinii

Vernonia monocephala subsp.

irwinii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces dense clusters of small, bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
