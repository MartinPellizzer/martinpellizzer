Viburnum grandifolium

Viburnum grandifolium, commonly known as the Allegheny viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with five to seven ovate leaflets, which are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Viburnum grandifolium is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
