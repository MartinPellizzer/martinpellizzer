Vaccinium imbricans

Vaccinium imbricans, commonly known as the creeping blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like cranberries and heathers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, containing high levels of antioxidants. Its leaves are alternate, oval-shaped, and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

Vaccinium imbricans thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or bogs.
