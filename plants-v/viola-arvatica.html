Viola arvatica

Viola arvatica, commonly known as the woodland violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shaded, moist woodland areas and has heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces violet flowers with five petals, often in shades of purple or blue. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance.

Viola arvatica is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
