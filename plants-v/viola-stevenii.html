Viola stevenii

Viola stevenii, commonly known as the Steven's violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and cool, moist conditions. Viola stevenii is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small invertebrates.
