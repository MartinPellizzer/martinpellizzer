Vitis lanceolata

Vitis lanceolata, commonly known as the river grape, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple coloration on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into clusters of dark blue to black berries. Its stems are woody and can grow up to 30 feet long, often climbing trees or fences.

Vitis lanceolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
