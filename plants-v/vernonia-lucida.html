Vernonia lucida

Vernonia lucida, commonly known as the silverleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and conical flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is often found in open woods and along roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are typically blue to purple in color and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
