Vicia sativa var. elaiosperma

Vicia sativa var. elaiosperma, commonly known as the Persian vetch, is a leguminous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and slender, erect stems that bear small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods containing small, hard seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and has been used historically in agriculture and forage.
