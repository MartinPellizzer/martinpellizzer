Viburnum cordifolium var. hypsophilum

Viburnum cordifolium var. hypsophilum, commonly known as the highland viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in cooler, high-altitude environments. Its fruits are small, dark berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Viburnum cordifolium var. hypsophilum is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
