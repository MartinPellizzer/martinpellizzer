Verbena galapagosensis

Verbena galapagosensis, commonly known as the Galápagos verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Galápagos Islands.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant purple or blue flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to the arid climate of the islands. It has small, narrow leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom throughout the dry season.

Verbena galapagosensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing nectar for pollinators.
