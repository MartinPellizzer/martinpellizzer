Veronica anagalloides

Veronica anagalloides, commonly known as the alpine speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, blue or violet flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine layer of white, woolly hair.

Veronica anagalloides thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and dry grasslands.
