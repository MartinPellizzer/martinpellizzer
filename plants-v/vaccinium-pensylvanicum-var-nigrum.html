Vaccinium pensylvanicum var. nigrum

Vaccinium pensylvanicum var. nigrum, commonly known as black huckleberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark blue to black berries that are rich in antioxidants. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a leathery texture and a deep green color.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
