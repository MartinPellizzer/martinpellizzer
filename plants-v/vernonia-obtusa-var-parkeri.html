Vernonia obtusa var. parkeri

Vernonia obtusa var. parkeri, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.

Its botanical features make it an important species for pollinators and ecological restoration projects.
