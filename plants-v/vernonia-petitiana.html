Vernonia petitiana

Vernonia petitiana, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its woody stem and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in heads, typical of the genus Vernonia. It is known for its hard, dense wood, which is highly valued for construction and fuel.

Vernonia petitiana is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.
