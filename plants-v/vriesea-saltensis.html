Vriesea saltensis

Vriesea saltensis, commonly known as the Salt's Vriesea, is a bromeliad native to South America, specifically Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, recurved leaves that form a rosette shape, which helps collect rainwater for the plant's survival. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with colorful, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its foliage is often variegated with white and green stripes, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Vriesea saltensis thrives in bright, indirect light and is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
