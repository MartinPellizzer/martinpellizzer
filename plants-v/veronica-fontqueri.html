Veronica fontqueri

Veronica fontqueri, commonly known as the desert speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is well adapted to arid environments. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular ornamental plant in desert gardens.
