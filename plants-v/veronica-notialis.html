Veronica notialis

Veronica notialis, commonly known as the common speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with opposite, linear to lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and wetlands.

Veronica notialis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens for its ability to spread and cover ground effectively.
