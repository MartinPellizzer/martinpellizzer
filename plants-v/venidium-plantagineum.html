Venidium plantagineum

Venidium plantagineum, commonly known as the common tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in umbels. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has a taproot system and is often found in temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

Venidium plantagineum is known for its aromatic properties and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
