Vernonia yamboyensis

Vernonia yamboyensis, commonly known as the Yamboyensis Ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of individual flowers. The plant typically grows in swampy or marshy areas, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from yellow to orange in color.

Vernonia yamboyensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
