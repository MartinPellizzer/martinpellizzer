Vaccinium australe

Vaccinium australe, commonly known as the Australian heath, is a low-growing shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are evergreen, glossy, and oval in shape, contributing to its ornamental value.

Vaccinium australe thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.
