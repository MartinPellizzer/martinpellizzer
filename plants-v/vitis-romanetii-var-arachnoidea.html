Vitis romanetii var. arachnoidea

Vitis romanetii var. arachnoidea, commonly known as the spider-vine, is a rare and unique vine species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and often have a reddish or purple tinge.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
