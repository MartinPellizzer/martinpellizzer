Vaccinium quinquefidum

Vaccinium quinquefidum, commonly known as the five-lobed blueberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and parts of Georgia. The plant is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring small, elliptical leaves with five distinct lobes. Its flowers are bell-shaped, white or pale pink, and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, similar to other Vaccinium species.
