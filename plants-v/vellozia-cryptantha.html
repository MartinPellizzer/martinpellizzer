Vellozia cryptantha

Vellozia cryptantha, commonly known as the cryptantha vellozia, is a striking member of the Velloziaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powder. The plant produces striking, cone-shaped inflorescences with showy, red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It is adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in drought conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a popular subject in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
