Vaccinium angustifolium var. myrtilloides

Vaccinium angustifolium var. myrtilloides, commonly known as the narrowleaf blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, dark green leaves and produces small, edible blue berries. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist environments. It is often found in boreal and temperate forests, particularly in the northern United States and Canada.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and for its use in local ecosystems and culinary applications.
