Viola nivalis

Viola nivalis, commonly known as the snow violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, delicate, and usually violet or white, with five petals and a distinctive spur.

Viola nivalis is known for its early spring blooming, often appearing before many other wildflowers.
