Viola montana var. pratensis

Viola montana var. pratensis, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from purple to white, often with a distinctive yellow or greenish throat. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Viola montana var. pratensis is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
