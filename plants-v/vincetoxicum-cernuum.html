Vincetoxicum cernuum

Vincetoxicum cernuum, commonly known as creeping jenny, is a perennial flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped, opposite leaves that are typically bright green and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Vincetoxicum cernuum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
