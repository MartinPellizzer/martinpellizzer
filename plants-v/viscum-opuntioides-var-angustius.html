Viscum opuntioides var. angustius

Viscum opuntioides var. angustius, commonly known as the narrowleaf mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the family Viscaceae and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of white, fleshy berries. The plant has a symbiotic relationship with its host, drawing water and nutrients through its haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system. Its flowers are typically small and greenish-white, appearing in the spring, followed by the development of its distinctive berries.

This species is often found in regions such as Texas, Arizona, and parts of Mexico, where it thrives in harsh, dry environments.
