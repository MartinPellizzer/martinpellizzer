Valeriana pratensis subsp. pratensis

Valeriana pratensis subsp.

pratensis, commonly known as meadow valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges.

The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white. Its root system is fibrous and contains compounds responsible for its medicinal properties.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its calming effects.
