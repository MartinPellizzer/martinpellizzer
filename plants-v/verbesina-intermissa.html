Verbesina intermissa

Verbesina intermissa, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as swamps and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are bright yellow with prominent central disks.

Verbesina intermissa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
