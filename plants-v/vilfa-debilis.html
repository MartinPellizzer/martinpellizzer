Vilfa debilis

Vilfa debilis, commonly known as the weak willow, is a small shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like branches and delicate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually appearing in clusters, and it produces small, woody fruits.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Vilfa debilis is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
