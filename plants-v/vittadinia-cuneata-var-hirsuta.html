Vittadinia cuneata var. hirsuta

Vittadinia cuneata var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its adaptability to arid conditions and is often found in regions with low rainfall.
