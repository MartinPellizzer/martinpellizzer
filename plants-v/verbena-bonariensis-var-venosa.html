Verbena bonariensis var. venosa

Verbena bonariensis var. venosa, commonly known as the variegated verbena, is a striking perennial plant native to South America.

It features slender, branching stems and produces clusters of small, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a distinctive variegation pattern with white or cream markings. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
