Verbascum rotundifolium subsp. rotundifolium

Verbascum rotundifolium subsp.

rotundifolium, commonly known as the round-leaf mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety, gray-green leaves that are rounded in shape.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers in clusters at the top of the stem, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
