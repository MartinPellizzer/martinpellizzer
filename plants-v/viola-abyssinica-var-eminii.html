Viola abyssinica var. eminii

Viola abyssinica var. eminii, commonly known as the Ethiopian violet, is a species of flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant purple or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in montane forests or along stream banks. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
