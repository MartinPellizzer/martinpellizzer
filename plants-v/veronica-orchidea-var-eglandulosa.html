Veronica orchidea var. eglandulosa

Veronica orchidea var. eglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular veronica, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, glandular hairs. Native to southern Africa, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun conditions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its attractive and long-lasting blooms.
