Viola karakalensis

Viola karakalensis, commonly known as the Karakalinsky violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, violet-blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
