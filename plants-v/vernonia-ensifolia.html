Vernonia ensifolia

Vernonia ensifolia, commonly known as the swordleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its narrow, sword-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in prairies, wetlands, and along streambanks.

Vernonia ensifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
