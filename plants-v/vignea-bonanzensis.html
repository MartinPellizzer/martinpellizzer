Vignea bonanzensis

Vignea bonanzensis, commonly known as the Bonanza vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and long, slender stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a glossy surface, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Vignea bonanzensis is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
