Vanda scandens

Vanda scandens, commonly known as the climbing orchid, is a epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, often blooming in clusters. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding surfaces.

Vanda scandens thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
