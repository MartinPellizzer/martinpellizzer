Valeriana battandieri

Valeriana battandieri, commonly known as the Alpine valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are usually pink or white.

Valeriana battandieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
