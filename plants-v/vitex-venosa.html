Vitex venosa

Vitex venosa, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers that are typically purple or blue in color, arranged in terminal or axillary clusters. Its fruit is a smooth, yellowish drupe that splits open to reveal a single seed.

Vitex venosa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
