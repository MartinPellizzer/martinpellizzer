Veronica minniana

Veronica minniana, commonly known as the Minnesota speedwell, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, blue to purple flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Veronica minniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
