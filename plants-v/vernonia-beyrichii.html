Vernonia beyrichii

Vernonia beyrichii, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, hard wood, which is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters. Vernonia beyrichii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various local wildlife species.
