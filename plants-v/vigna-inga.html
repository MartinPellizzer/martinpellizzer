Vigna inga

Vigna inga, commonly known as the African snail bean, is a climbing leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used as a food source. The plant has compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and is characterized by its yellow flowers. Vigna inga is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both its edible seeds and ornamental value.
