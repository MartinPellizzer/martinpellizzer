Vernonia schlechteri

Vernonia schlechteri, commonly known as the Schlechter's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its showy, purple to violet flower heads appear in late summer to early autumn.

Vernonia schlechteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
