Veronica pimeleoides

Veronica pimeleoides, commonly known as the slender speedwell, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, blue to violet flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
