Vachellia bellula

Vachellia bellula, commonly known as the silver flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance through its deep root system. Its flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and birds, contributing to local biodiversity.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
