Veronica agrestis subsp. polita

Veronica agrestis subsp.

polita, commonly known as the meadow speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically thrives in moist, grassy habitats such as meadows, ditches, and woodland edges.

The plant features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to support pollinators.
