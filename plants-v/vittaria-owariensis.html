Vittaria owariensis

Vittaria owariensis, commonly known as the Japanese spikemoss, is a member of the family Vittariaceae and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, terrestrial fern that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors. The plant has a unique structure with a short, erect stem and a rosette of lance-shaped leaves. Its spore-bearing structures, called sporangia, are clustered on the underside of the leaves.

Vittaria owariensis is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in humid, indoor environments.
