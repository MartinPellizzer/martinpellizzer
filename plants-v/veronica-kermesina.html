Veronica kermesina

Veronica kermesina, commonly known as the purple speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Veronica kermesina is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its attractive appearance and hardiness.
