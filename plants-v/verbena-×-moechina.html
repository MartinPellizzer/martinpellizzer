Verbena × moechina

Verbena × moechina, commonly known as Mexican verbena, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Verbenaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy spikes of blue to purple flowers that bloom throughout the summer. The plant typically reaches heights of 12 to 24 inches and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Verbena × moechina is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
