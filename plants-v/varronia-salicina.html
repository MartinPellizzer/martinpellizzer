Varronia salicina

Varronia salicina, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

Varronia salicina is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
