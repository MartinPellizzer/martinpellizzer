Verbascum oocarpum

Verbascum oocarpum, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, gray-green leaves that are covered in soft, downy hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

Verbascum oocarpum is a perennial herb that is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and historical medicinal uses.
