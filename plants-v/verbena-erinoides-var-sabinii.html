Verbena erinoides var. sabinii

Verbena erinoides var. sabinii, commonly known as the silver verbena, is a compact, flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, often appearing in clusters. The plant has narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
