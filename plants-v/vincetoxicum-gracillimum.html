Vincetoxicum gracillimum

Vincetoxicum gracillimum, commonly known as the slender milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features slender, erect stems that are often tinged with red or purple, and its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white with five petals, blooming in late summer. Its seed pods are slender, cylindrical, and contain numerous tiny seeds with silky hairs.

Vincetoxicum gracillimum is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, thriving in partial to full shade.
