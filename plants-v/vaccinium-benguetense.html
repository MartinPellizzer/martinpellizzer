Vaccinium benguetense

Vaccinium benguetense, commonly known as the Benguet huckleberry, is a shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
