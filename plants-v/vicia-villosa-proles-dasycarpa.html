Vicia villosa proles dasycarpa

Vicia villosa proles dasycarpa, commonly known as hairy vetch, is a leguminous plant characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves.

It produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in temperate climates and is often used as a cover crop or forage.

Its proles dasycarpa variety is distinguished by its dense, hairy fruit pods.
