Vicia noeana var. megalodonta

Vicia noeana var. megalodonta, commonly known as the giant vetch, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its robust, sprawling growth habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to greenish, arranged in dense clusters.

This variety is notable for its large, thick leaves and is often found in disturbed soils and rocky habitats.
