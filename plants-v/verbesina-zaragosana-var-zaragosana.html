Verbesina zaragosana var. zaragosana

Verbesina zaragosana var. zaragosana, commonly known as the Zaragoza verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. The plant features slender, branching stems and produces showy yellow-orange flowers in the late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
