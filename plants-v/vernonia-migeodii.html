Vernonia migeodii

Vernonia migeodii, commonly known as the Migeod's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and dense clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces fibrous, woody stems and has lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and wetlands.

Vernonia migeodii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
