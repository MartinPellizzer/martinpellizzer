Viguiera knobiana

Viguiera knobiana, commonly known as the Knobstick coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking, conical-shaped flower heads. The plant produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, sticky hairs.

Viguiera knobiana is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and roadsides.
