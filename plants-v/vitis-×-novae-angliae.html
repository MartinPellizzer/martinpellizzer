Vitis × novae-angliae

Vitis × novae-angliae, commonly known as the New England grape, is a hybrid vine native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and clusters of small, dark blue to black berries. The plant typically thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained, fertile soil. Its leaves are heart-shaped with coarsely serrated edges, and it produces fragrant, greenish-white flowers in the spring.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in horticultural research due to its adaptability and genetic diversity.
