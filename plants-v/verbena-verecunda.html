Verbena verecunda

Verbena verecunda, commonly known as the sacred verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Verbena verecunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
