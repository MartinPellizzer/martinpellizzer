Veronica kurdica subsp. filicaulis

Veronica kurdica subsp.

filicaulis, commonly known as the Kurdish speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey. It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves.

The plant produces clusters of blue or violet flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
