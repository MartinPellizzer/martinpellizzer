Vochya tabascana

Vochya tabascana, commonly known as the Mexican milkweed, is a herbaceous plant native to the southern United States and Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the summer months. Vochya tabascana is adapted to thrive in dry, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
