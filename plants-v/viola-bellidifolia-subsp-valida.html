Viola bellidifolia subsp. valida

Viola bellidifolia subsp.

valida, commonly known as the English violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy violet flowers that bloom in early spring.

Native to Europe, this perennial herb thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant has a compact growth habit with a short, creeping stem and is often found in clusters.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
