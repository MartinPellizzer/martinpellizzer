Valeriana denudata

Valeriana denudata, commonly known as the naked valerian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slight pubescence.
