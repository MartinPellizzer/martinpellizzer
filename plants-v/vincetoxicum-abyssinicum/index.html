Vincetoxicum Abyssinicum

Abyssinian milkweed, scientifically known as Vincetoxicum abyssinicum, is a striking plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia. It is recognized for its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of orange and yellow. Despite its beauty, it contains toxic sap that can be harmful to humans and animals. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a subject of both admiration and caution.
