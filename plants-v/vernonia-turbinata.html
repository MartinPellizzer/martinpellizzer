Vernonia turbinata

Vernonia turbinata, commonly known as the turbinated ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as swamps and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are blue to violet in color.

Vernonia turbinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in herbal medicine.
