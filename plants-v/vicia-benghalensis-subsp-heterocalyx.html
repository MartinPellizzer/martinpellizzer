Vicia benghalensis subsp. heterocalyx

Vicia benghalensis subsp.

heterocalyx, commonly known as the Bengal vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia. It is a perennial legume that typically grows in moist, fertile soils.

The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers in clusters. Its pods contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing abilities in agricultural settings.
