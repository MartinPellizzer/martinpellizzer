Vernonia sparsiflora

Vernonia sparsiflora, commonly known as the sparseflower ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Vernonia sparsiflora is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
