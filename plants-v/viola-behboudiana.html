Viola behboudiana

Viola behboudiana, commonly known as the Persian violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, hairy leaves and delicate, spurred flowers that range in color from purple to white. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its beauty in gardens.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
