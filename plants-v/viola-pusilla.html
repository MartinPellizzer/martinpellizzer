Viola pusilla

Viola pusilla, commonly known as the small violet, is a delicate flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Violaceae family and is characterized by its tiny, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Viola pusilla is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is known for its ability to thrive in cooler climates.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a subtle splash of color to the landscape.
