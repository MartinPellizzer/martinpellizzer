Vicia sativa var. persica

Vicia sativa var. persica, commonly known as Persian vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is a legume characterized by its trifoliate leaves and slender, climbing stems. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

This species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
