Veronica peduncularis

Veronica peduncularis, commonly known as the clustered speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features dark green, oval-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
