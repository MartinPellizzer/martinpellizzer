Vagnera amplexicaulis

Vagnera amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clinging vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique growth habit, with its leaves clasping the stems in a spiral pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
