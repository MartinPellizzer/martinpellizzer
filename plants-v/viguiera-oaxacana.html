Viguiera oaxacana

Viguiera oaxacana, commonly known as Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from white to pink.

Viguiera oaxacana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
