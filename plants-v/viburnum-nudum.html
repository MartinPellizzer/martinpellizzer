Viburnum nudum

Viburnum nudum, commonly known as the arrowwood viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and features compound leaves that are opposite and ovate in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. Viburnum nudum thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
