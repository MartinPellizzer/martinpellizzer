Vitis cinerea var. rufescens

Vitis cinerea var. rufescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed grapevine, is a lesser-known variant of the grapevine species.

It is characterized by its reddish-brown stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant is native to parts of North America, particularly the southeastern United States. It produces small, sour-tasting berries that are not typically used for winemaking.

This variety is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
