Veatchia discolor

Veatchia discolor, commonly known as the pink bamboo or pink veatchia, is a striking ornamental plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and gracefully arching fronds. The plant is notable for its vibrant pinkish-red coloration on the underside of its leaves, which gives it its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Veatchia discolor is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in both indoor and outdoor settings.
