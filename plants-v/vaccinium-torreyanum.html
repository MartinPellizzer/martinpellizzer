Vaccinium torreyanum

Vaccinium torreyanum, commonly known as the Torrey blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and heathers. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible berries that are deep blue when ripe. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its foliage is dense and leathery, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
