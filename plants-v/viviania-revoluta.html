Viviania revoluta

Viviania revoluta, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in moist, well-drained soils and full to partial sun. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are ovate and slightly hairy, with a distinctive rolled-under appearance.

Viviania revoluta is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
