Vignea lagopodioides

Vignea lagopodioides, commonly known as the woolly legume, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes and beans, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in shrubland and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, and it produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds.

Vignea lagopodioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
