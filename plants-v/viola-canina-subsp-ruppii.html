Viola canina subsp. ruppii

Viola canina subsp.

ruppii, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces delicate, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive yellow center. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in wildflower gardens and alpine landscapes.

This subspecies is also noted for its ability to withstand cold climates and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
