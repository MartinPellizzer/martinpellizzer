Vicia peduncularis

Vicia peduncularis, commonly known as the common vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with a small, stipitate leaflet, and its flowers are typically yellow or violet, arranged in racemes. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural and ecological contexts.

Vicia peduncularis thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas, meadows, and along roadsides.
