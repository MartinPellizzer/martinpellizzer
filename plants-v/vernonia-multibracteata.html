Vernonia multibracteata

Vernonia multibracteata, commonly known as the many-bract vernonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring erect stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow to orange tubular flowers, which are arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges and often have a rough texture.

Vernonia multibracteata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
