Vandellia sericea

Vandellia sericea, commonly known as the silver vandellia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with a fragrant scent that attracts pollinators.
