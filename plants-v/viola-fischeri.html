Viola fischeri

Viola fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and small, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Viola fischeri is known for its short growing season and is often found in high-altitude habitats.

Its botanical features make it a unique and rare species within the viola genus.
