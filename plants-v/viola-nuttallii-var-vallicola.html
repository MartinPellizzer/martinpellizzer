Viola nuttallii var. vallicola

Viola nuttallii var. vallicola, commonly known as the Sierra violet, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, such as meadows and slopes. It has a short stature, usually reaching heights of less than 10 inches, and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

The Sierra violet is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.
