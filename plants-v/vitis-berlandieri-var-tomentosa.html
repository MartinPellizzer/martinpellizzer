Vitis berlandieri var. tomentosa

Vitis berlandieri var. tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly grape or Mexican wild grape, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its hairy, grayish stems and broad, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into clusters of small, edible berries. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, often growing in rocky soils and along canyon walls.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
