Verbesina biflora

Verbesina biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered beggarticks, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features hairy, upright stems that can grow up to 2 meters tall, often branching near the base. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, with two florets per capitulum, giving it its common name. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It is often found in moist, disturbed soils along roadsides and in open woods.
