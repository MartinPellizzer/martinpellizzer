Viola hirtipes f. glabra

Viola hirtipes f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its smooth, hairless stems and leaves distinguish it from other viola species.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
