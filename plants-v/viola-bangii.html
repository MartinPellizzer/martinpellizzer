Viola bangii

Viola bangii, commonly known as the velvetleaf violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. It produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Viola bangii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
