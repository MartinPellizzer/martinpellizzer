Viola heterophylla var. graeca

Viola heterophylla var. graeca, commonly known as the Greek violet, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves, which vary in shape depending on the environment, a trait known as heterophylly. The plant produces small, delicate violet flowers that bloom in spring, typically in shades of purple or blue. It thrives in moist, shaded areas and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both garden use and botanical study.
