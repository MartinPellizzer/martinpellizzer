Viburnum plicatum f. parvifolium

Viburnum plicatum f. parvifolium, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a compact, deciduous shrub characterized by its unique, pleated, ovate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red in autumn.

It produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers in early spring, which are followed by dark blue, edible berries. The plant is native to Japan and thrives in partial shade to full sun, preferring moist, well-drained soil. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

Its foliage and flowering display make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both aesthetic and ecological benefits.
