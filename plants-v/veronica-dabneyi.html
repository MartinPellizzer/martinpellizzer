Veronica dabneyi

Veronica dabneyi, commonly known as Dabney's speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the plantain family, Plantaginaceae, and is characterized by its small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in woodland edges and open areas. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Veronica dabneyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
