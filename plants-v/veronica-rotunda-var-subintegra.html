Veronica rotunda var. subintegra

Veronica rotunda var. subintegra, commonly known as the woolly speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has rounded, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
