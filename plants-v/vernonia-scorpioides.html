Vernonia scorpioides

Vernonia scorpioides, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring upright stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale yellow to orange in color, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a slightly serrated edge.

Vernonia scorpioides is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soils, making it a common sight in dry, open habitats.
