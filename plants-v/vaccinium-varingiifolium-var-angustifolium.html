Vaccinium varingiifolium var. angustifolium

Vaccinium varingiifolium var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blueberry, is a species of dwarf shrub native to the boreal forests of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces bell-shaped, white to pale pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
