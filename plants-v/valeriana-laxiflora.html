Valeriana laxiflora

Valeriana laxiflora, commonly known as the looseflower valerian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It features upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, adorned with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and has a strong, aromatic scent, which is characteristic of the Valeriana genus.

Valeriana laxiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its calming properties.
