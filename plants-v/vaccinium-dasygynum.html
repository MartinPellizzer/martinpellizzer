Vaccinium dasygynum

Vaccinium dasygynum, commonly known as the broad-leaved huckleberry, is a species of shrub native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants such as blueberries and cranberries. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a waxy surface, and it produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white.

The berries, which are edible, are dark blue to black when mature and are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
