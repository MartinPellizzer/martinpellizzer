Viola rothomagensis

Viola rothomagensis, commonly known as the Rothomagensis Violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland and meadow habitats, preferring moist, well-drained soil. It blooms in early spring, producing small, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
