Vaccinium nhatrangense

Vaccinium nhatrangense, commonly known as Nhatrang blueberry, is a species of shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers temperate to subtropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for cultivation, it has attracted interest among botanists and horticulturists.
