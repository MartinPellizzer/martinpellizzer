Vigna unguiculata subsp. taiwanianus

Vigna unguiculata subsp.

taiwanianus, commonly known as the Taiwan cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to Taiwan. It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves with small, hairy stipules.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that develop into elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is adapted to warm, tropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its nutritional content and is sometimes cultivated for both food and ornamental purposes.
