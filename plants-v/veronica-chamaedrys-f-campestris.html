Veronica chamaedrys f. campestris

Veronica chamaedrys f. campestris, commonly known as the creeping speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its prostrate or spreading growth habit.

It features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has oval, toothed leaves that are typically covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and ground covers.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to spread easily in suitable conditions.
