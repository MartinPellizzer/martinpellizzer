Vitis costata

Vitis costata, commonly known as the Japanese grapevine, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, sour-tasting berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Vitis costata is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
