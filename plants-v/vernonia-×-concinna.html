Vernonia × concinna

Vernonia × concinna, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to brownish flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Vernonia × concinna is often found in open woodlands, prairies, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
