Viola sororia f. beckwithiae

Viola sororia f. beckwithiae, commonly known as the Beckwith's violet, is a botanical variant of the common violet, characterized by its distinctive floral morphology.

This subspecies features showy, violet-blue flowers with a darker throat and a prominent white or yellowish mark at the base of the petals. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks. The leaves are heart-shaped with a hairy texture, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
