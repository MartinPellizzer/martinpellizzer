Vernonia cymosa

Vernonia cymosa, commonly known as the clustered ironweed, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Vernonia cymosa is typically found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
