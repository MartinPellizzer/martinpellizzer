Verbesina auriculigera var. cajamarcensis

Verbesina auriculigera var. cajamarcensis, commonly known as the Peruvian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces showy yellow-orange flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its striking floral display.
