Vicia trichocalyx

Vicia trichocalyx, commonly known as the hairy vetch, is a leguminous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and long, cylindrical pods that contain small, yellowish seeds. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. Vicia trichocalyx has a deep root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

It is also used in agriculture as a cover crop and for forage.
