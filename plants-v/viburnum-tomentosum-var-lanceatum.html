Viburnum tomentosum var. lanceatum

Viburnum tomentosum var. lanceatum, commonly known as the lanceleaf arrowwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are typically hairy on the underside, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in partial to full shade and is valued for its ornamental appeal and wildlife habitat.

Viburnum tomentosum var. lanceatum is relatively low maintenance and suitable for garden use in moist, well-drained soil.
