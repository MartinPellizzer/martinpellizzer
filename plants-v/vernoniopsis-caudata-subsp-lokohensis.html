Vernoniopsis caudata subsp. lokohensis

Vernoniopsis caudata subsp.

lokohensis, commonly known as the Lokohense Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to central Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flower heads composed of ray and disc florets.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are erect and often hairy.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
