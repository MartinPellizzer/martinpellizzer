Vilfa villarsii

Vilfa villarsii, commonly known as the Alpine violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are usually violet-blue in color, with five petals arranged in a symmetrical pattern.

Vilfa villarsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine plant collections.
