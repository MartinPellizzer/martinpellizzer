Veronica erinoides

Veronica erinoides, commonly known as the slender speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy as well. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, ornamental ground cover.
