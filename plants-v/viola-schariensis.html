Viola schariensis

Viola schariensis, commonly known as the Scharien's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and delicate, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola schariensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
