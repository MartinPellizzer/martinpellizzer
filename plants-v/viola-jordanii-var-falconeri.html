Viola jordanii var. falconeri

Viola jordanii var. falconeri, commonly known as the Jordanian violets, is a rare and endemic species native to specific regions in Jordan.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the local landscape.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
