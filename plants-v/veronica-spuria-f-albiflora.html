Veronica spuria f. albiflora

Veronica spuria f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered speedwell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas or along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine layer of soft hairs.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
