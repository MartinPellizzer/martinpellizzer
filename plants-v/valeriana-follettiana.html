Valeriana follettiana

Valeriana follettiana, commonly known as the Mexican valerian, is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

Valeriana follettiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
