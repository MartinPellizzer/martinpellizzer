Vandenboschia cyrtotheca

Vandenboschia cyrtotheca, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ice plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
