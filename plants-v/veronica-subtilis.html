Veronica subtilis

Veronica subtilis, commonly known as the slender speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its small, blue or purple flowers arranged in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.
