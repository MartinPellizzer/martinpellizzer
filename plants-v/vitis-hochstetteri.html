Vitis hochstetteri

Vitis hochstetteri, commonly known as the Hochstetter's grape, is a species of flowering vine native to the highlands of South America.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and clusters of small, dark blue to black berries. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of its edible fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of fruit in certain regions.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.
