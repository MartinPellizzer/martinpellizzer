Vatica canaliculata

Vatica canaliculata, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and sturdy, aromatic wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody pods containing flat, leathery seeds. Vatica canaliculata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forest ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and commercial purposes.
