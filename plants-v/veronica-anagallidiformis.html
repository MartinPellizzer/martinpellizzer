Veronica anagallidiformis

Veronica anagallidiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
