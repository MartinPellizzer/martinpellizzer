Vangueria randii subsp. acuminata

Vangueria randii subsp.

acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved vangueria, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa. It features fleshy, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, aiding in water retention.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental leaves make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
