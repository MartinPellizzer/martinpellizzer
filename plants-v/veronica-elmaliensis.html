Veronica elmaliensis

Veronica elmaliensis, commonly known as the Armenian speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and Turkey.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit with dense clusters of small, blue-violet flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silky hair. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Veronica elmaliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
