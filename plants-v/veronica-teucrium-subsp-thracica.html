Veronica teucrium subsp. thracica

Veronica teucrium subsp.

thracica, commonly known as Thrace speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous regions.

The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers arranged in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
