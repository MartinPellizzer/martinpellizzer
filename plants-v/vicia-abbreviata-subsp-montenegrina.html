Vicia abbreviata subsp. montenegrina

Vicia abbreviata subsp.

montenegrina, commonly known as the Montenegrin vetch, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Balkan region. It is characterized by its slender stems and trifoliate leaves with small, heart-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Vicia abbreviata subsp.

montenegrina is valued for its role in nitrogen fixation and as a forage plant for livestock.
