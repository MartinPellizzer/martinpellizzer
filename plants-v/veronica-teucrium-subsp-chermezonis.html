Veronica teucrium subsp. chermezonis

Veronica teucrium subsp.

chermezonis, commonly known as the Alpine Speedwell, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and dense clusters of blue or violet flowers.

The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and arranged in spikes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
