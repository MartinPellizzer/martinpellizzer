Viola arborescens var. serratifolia

Viola arborescens var. serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated woodland violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has heart-shaped, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy violet flowers with a distinctive yellow throat, which bloom in spring. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and can reach heights of up to 30 inches.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
