Vicia monanthos

Vicia monanthos, commonly known as the one-flowered vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or pink. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. Vicia monanthos is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
