Vitis strigosa

Vitis strigosa, commonly known as the scaly grapevine, is a woody vine native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, ovate leaves with a rough, scaly texture on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by clusters of round, edible berries. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the eastern United States.

Vitis strigosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
