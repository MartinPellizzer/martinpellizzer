Viola mrkvickana

Viola mrkvickana, commonly known as the Mrkvicka violet, is a species of flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Viola mrkvickana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
