Verbascum virgatum var. lanceolatum

Verbascum virgatum var. lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf mullein, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features tall, upright stems covered in soft, velvety gray-green leaves that are lance-shaped and slightly hairy. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a terminal spike. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry meadows and rocky areas.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used for respiratory ailments due to its soothing effects.
