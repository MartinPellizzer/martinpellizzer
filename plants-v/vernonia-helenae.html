Vernonia helenae

Vernonia helenae, commonly known as Helen's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its conical flower heads. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from white to purple.

Vernonia helenae is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
