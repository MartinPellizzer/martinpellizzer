Viola sequoiensis

Viola sequoiensis, commonly known as the Sierra violet, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers have five petals.

Viola sequoiensis plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small organisms.
