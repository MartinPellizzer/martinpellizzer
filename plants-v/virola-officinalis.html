Virola officinalis

Virola officinalis, commonly known as the copal tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Myristicaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and large, simple leaves. The tree produces fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing sticky, resinous seeds. Virola officinalis is valued for its copal resin, which has been used traditionally in medicinal and ritual contexts.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
