Viola canina subsp. elatior

Viola canina subsp.

elatior, commonly known as the greater dog violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and grasslands.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet-blue coloration and produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of purple and blue. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its erect stems and clustered inflorescences.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
