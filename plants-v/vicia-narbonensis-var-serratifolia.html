Vicia narbonensis var. serratifolia

Vicia narbonensis var. serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated vetch, is a leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces elongated pods containing several elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
