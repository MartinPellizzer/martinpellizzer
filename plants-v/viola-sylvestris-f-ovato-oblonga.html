Viola sylvestris f. ovato-oblonga

Viola sylvestris f. ovato-oblonga, commonly known as the broad-ovate violet, is a horticultural form of the common violet.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate-oblong leaves and vibrant purple flowers. This plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in partial shade. The foliage is often more robust and larger than the typical species, making it a striking addition to gardens.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable environments.
