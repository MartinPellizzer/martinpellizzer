Verbesina gigantea

Verbesina gigantea, commonly known as the giant beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that can grow up to 6 feet tall, featuring hairy stems and alternate leaves with toothed edges. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late summer to early fall. Its seeds are encased in burs that adhere to clothing and animals, aiding in seed dispersal.

Verbesina gigantea is often found in moist, sunny habitats such as roadsides and wetlands.
