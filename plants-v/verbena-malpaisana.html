Verbena malpaisana

Verbena malpaisana, commonly known as the Malpais verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its small, lance-shaped leaves are opposite and often covered in fine hairs.

Verbena malpaisana is known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
