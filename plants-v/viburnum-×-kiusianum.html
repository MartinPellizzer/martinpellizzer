Viburnum × kiusianum

Viburnum × kiusianum, commonly known as the Canadian arrowwood, is a hybrid shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces bright red berries in the fall, which are a food source for birds and other wildlife. Viburnum × kiusianum typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
