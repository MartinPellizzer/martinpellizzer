Viguiera subdentata

Viguiera subdentata, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, purple-pink composite flowers. The plant features gray-green, hairy leaves that are opposite and lance-shaped, with finely toothed margins. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Viguiera subdentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
