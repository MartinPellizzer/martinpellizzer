Vanda tessellata subsp. tesselloides

Vanda tessellata subsp.

tesselloides, commonly known as the tessellated orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking, mottled green and white leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. It has a sympodial growth habit, with each pseudobulb supporting a pair of leaves and a flower spike.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
