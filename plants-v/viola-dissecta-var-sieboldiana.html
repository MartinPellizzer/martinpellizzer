Viola dissecta var. sieboldiana

Viola dissecta var. sieboldiana, commonly known as the Siebold's violet, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with finely dissected lobes and clusters of violet or blue-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
