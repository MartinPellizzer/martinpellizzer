Viguiera triloba

Viguiera triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and large, showy flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The plant produces yellow-orange disk flowers surrounded by pink to purple ray florets, making it a striking addition to gardens. Its leaves are lance-shaped with three distinct lobes, contributing to its ornamental value.

Viguiera triloba is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
