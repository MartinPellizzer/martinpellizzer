Veronica sciaphila

Veronica sciaphila, commonly known as the shade-loving speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. The plant features small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in early to mid-summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Veronica sciaphila is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
