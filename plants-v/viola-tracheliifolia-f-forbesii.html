Viola tracheliifolia f. forbesii

Viola tracheliifolia f. forbesii, commonly known as the Forbes' violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers with five petals, often adorned with distinctive markings. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is cultivated in specialty gardens and botanical collections.
