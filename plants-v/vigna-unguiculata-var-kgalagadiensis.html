Vigna unguiculata var. kgalagadiensis

Vigna unguiculata var. kgalagadiensis, commonly known as the Kgalagadi cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that are followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing edible seeds. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a valuable crop for drought-prone regions.

The seeds are rich in protein and are often used in traditional cuisines across the region.
