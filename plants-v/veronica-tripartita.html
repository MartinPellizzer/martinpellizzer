Veronica tripartita

Veronica tripartita, commonly known as the three-part speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, blue or purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Veronica tripartita thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
