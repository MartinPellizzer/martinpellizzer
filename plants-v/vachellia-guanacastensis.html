Vachellia guanacastensis

Vachellia guanacastensis, commonly known as the Guanacaste acacia, is a leguminous shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow floral clusters. The plant is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dry or semi-arid regions. It plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.

Its wood and foliage are also used in traditional medicine and as forage for livestock.
