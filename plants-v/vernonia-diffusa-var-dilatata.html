Vernonia diffusa var. dilatata

Vernonia diffusa var. dilatata, commonly known as the broadleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture. This plant is often found in moist to wet soils in woodland areas or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
