Vanda lilacina

Vanda lilacina, commonly known as the lilac orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of fragrant, lilac-colored flowers. The plant typically blooms during the summer months, producing showy, pendulous flowers that attract pollinators. Vanda lilacina has a fibrous root system and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and unique floral scent.
