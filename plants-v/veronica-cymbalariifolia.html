Veronica cymbalariifolia

Veronica cymbalariifolia, commonly known as the coastal veronica, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair. It thrives in coastal environments and is adapted to sandy or rocky soils.

Veronica cymbalariifolia is often used in coastal gardens for its ornamental value and ability to withstand salt spray.
