Veronica ossetica

Veronica ossetica, commonly known as the Ossetian speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Veronica ossetica is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to temperate climates.
