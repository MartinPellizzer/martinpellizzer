Vignea × mithala

Vignea × mithala, commonly known as the hybrid pigeon pea, is a cross between Vigna unguiculata and Vigna mungo.

It is a leguminous plant characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds used in various cuisines. It is cultivated for its nutritional value, providing protein-rich seeds high in lysine.

Vignea × mithala is also valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
