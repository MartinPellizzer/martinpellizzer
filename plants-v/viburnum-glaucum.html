Viburnum glaucum

Viburnum glaucum, commonly known as the sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in a fine, white or grayish pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. Viburnum glaucum typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
