Viburnum cassinoides

Viburnum cassinoides, commonly known as the fragrant snowball bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a dense, snowball-like appearance. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. Its fruit is a dark blue to black drupe, which ripens in the fall and is edible for birds.

Viburnum cassinoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
