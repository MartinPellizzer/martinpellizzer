Viola langsdorffii var. ursina

Viola langsdorffii var. ursina, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with five petals, often with a yellow or white throat, during the late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Violaceae family, valued for both its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
