Vignaldia occidentalis

Vignaldia occidentalis, commonly known as the western vignaldia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to its harsh environment. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread across the region.

Vignaldia occidentalis plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
