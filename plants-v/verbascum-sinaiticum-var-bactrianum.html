Verbascum sinaiticum var. bactrianum

Verbascum sinaiticum var. bactrianum, commonly known as the Bactrian mullein, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, covered with soft, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are large, lance-shaped, and covered with a dense layer of fine, silky hairs.

This variety is well adapted to harsh environments, thriving in sandy soils and requiring minimal water.
