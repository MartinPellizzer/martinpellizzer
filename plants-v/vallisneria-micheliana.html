Vallisneria micheliana

Vallisneria micheliana, commonly known as the narrow-leaf waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically hidden within a spathe, a characteristic feature of the Araceae family. Vallisneria micheliana thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments and plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen.

Its rapid growth makes it both a valuable aquatic plant and a potential invasive species in non-native waters.
