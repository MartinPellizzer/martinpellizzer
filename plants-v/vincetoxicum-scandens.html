Vincetoxicum scandens

Vincetoxicum scandens, commonly known as the creeping false bindweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, pale yellow or greenish-white flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Vincetoxicum scandens is known for its ornamental value but can be invasive in certain regions.
