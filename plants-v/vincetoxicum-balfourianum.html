Vincetoxicum balfourianum

Vincetoxicum balfourianum, commonly known as the Himalayan false bindweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features a fibrous root system and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves with a rough texture. It is often found growing in rocky or forested areas at high altitudes.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
