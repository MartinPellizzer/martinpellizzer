Valeriana congesta

Valeriana congesta, commonly known as the clustered valerian, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, small white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Valeriana congesta is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its calming properties.
