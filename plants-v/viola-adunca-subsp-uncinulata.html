Viola adunca subsp. uncinulata

Viola adunca subsp.

uncinulata, commonly known as the hooked viola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces violet-blue flowers with five petals. Its subspecies is distinguished by the presence of hooked hairs on the calyx and leaf surfaces.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
