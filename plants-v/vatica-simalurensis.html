Vatica simalurensis

Vatica simalurensis, commonly known as the Malacca ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for construction and furniture. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, and its fruit is a capsule containing several seeds.

Vatica simalurensis is typically found in lowland and hill dipterocarp forests, where it plays an important ecological role.
