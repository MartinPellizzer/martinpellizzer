Vitex megapotamica f. albiflora

Vitex megapotamica f. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower chaste tree, is a variant of the chaste tree native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy white or pale lavender flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its floral display is particularly notable for its prolonged blooming period during the summer months.
