Viola × williamsii

Viola × williamsii, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid plant in the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and prefers moist, well-drained soil. It is native to Europe and has been naturalized in various parts of North America.

Viola × williamsii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
