Vernonia mandrarensis

Vernonia mandrarensis, commonly known as the Madagascar ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, yellow to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Vernonia mandrarensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
