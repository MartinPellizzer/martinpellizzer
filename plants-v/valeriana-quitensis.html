Valeriana quitensis

Valeriana quitensis, commonly known as the Peruvian valerian, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Valeriana quitensis is known for its medicinal properties, particularly for its calming effects, and has been used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or mountainous environments.
