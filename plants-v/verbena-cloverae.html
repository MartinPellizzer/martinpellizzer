Verbena cloverae

Verbena cloverae, commonly known as the silverleaf verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, showy blue or purple flowers and silver-gray foliage. The plant belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Verbena cloverae is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
