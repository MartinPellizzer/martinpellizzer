Veronica cymbalaria subsp. trichadena

Veronica cymbalaria subsp.

trichadena, commonly known as the narrow-leaved speedwell, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, often calcareous soils in grasslands, woodland edges, and along riverbanks.

The plant features upright, slender stems with opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, which bloom from late spring to early autumn.

It is a perennial herb that spreads through rhizomes and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
