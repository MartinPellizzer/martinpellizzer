Veronica tsugitakensis

Veronica tsugitakensis, commonly known as the Japanese speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rock gardens. It has opposite, narrow leaves and produces clusters of tiny flowers in late spring.

Veronica tsugitakensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture.
