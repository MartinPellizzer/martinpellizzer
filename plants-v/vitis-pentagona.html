Vitis pentagona

Vitis pentagona, commonly known as the five-angled grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to parts of East Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. The plant is characterized by its distinctive five-angled stem and heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that give way to edible berries.

Vitis pentagona is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
