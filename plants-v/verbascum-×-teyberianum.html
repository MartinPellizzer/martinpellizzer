Verbascum × teyberianum

Verbascum × teyberianum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes several species of flowering plants known for their large, showy flowers. The hybrid is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a common sight in rocky or dry environments.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
