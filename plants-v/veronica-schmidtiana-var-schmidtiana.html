Veronica schmidtiana var. schmidtiana

Veronica schmidtiana var. schmidtiana, commonly known as the Schmidt's speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated by gardeners in colder climates.
