Veronica noeana

Veronica noeana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, grassy habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features small, blue or purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

Veronica noeana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
