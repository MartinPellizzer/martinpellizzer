Veronica anagallis-aquatica subsp. aquatica

Veronica anagallis-aquatica subsp.

aquatica, commonly known as water speedwell, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a basal rosette.

The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers with five petals, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies such as ponds and streams.

Its flowers bloom from spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
