Viola montcaunica

Viola montcaunica, commonly known as the Montcada violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soil. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola montcaunica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
