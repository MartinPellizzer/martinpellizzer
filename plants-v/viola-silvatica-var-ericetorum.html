Viola silvatica var. ericetorum

Viola silvatica var. ericetorum, commonly known as the heath violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in woodland edges, heaths, and rocky soils, preferring partial shade to full sun. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces violet-blue or purple flowers in early spring. Its flowers are often borne on slender stems and are visited by bees and other pollinators.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting insect life.
