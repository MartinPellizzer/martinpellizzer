Valeriana amazonum

Valeriana amazonum, commonly known as Amazonian valerian, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant leaves and flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and marshy areas. Its root system is known for containing compounds with potential medicinal properties.

Valeriana amazonum is valued both for its ecological role and its traditional use in herbal remedies.
