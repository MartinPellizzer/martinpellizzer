Vincetoxicum magnifolium

Vincetoxicum magnifolium, commonly known as the giant false bindweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It is a vigorous climber with strong, woody stems that can rapidly spread through vegetative propagation.

Due to its invasive nature, it is often considered a noxious weed in regions where it is introduced.
