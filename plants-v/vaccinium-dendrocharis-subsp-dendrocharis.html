Vaccinium dendrocharis subsp. dendrocharis

Vaccinium dendrocharis subsp.

dendrocharis, commonly known as the dwarf huckleberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of edible berries.

The plant typically thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers shaded to partially sunny environments. Its flowers are bell-shaped and pinkish-white, appearing in early spring, followed by the development of dark blue to black berries in late summer.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and its role in local wildlife habitats.
