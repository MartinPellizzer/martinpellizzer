Veronica drakiensis

Veronica drakiensis, commonly known as the Dragon's Blood Veronica, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in early spring.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
