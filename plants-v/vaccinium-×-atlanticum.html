Vaccinium × atlanticum

Vaccinium × atlanticum, commonly known as the Atlantic blueberry, is a hybrid evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, low-growing form with dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and sandy soils near the coast.

Vaccinium × atlanticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
