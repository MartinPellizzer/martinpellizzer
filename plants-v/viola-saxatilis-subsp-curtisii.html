Viola saxatilis subsp. curtisii

Viola saxatilis subsp.

curtisii, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces delicate, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive white throat, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
