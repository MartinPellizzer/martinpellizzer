Vitis vinifera

Vitis vinifera, commonly known as the common grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the family Vitaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is widely cultivated for its fruit, which is used in winemaking, table consumption, and juice production. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that help it ascend supports. Its leaves are alternate, compound, and typically have three to five leaflets.

Vitis vinifera is also valued for its ornamental qualities, with its attractive foliage and clusters of small, usually green or red berries.
