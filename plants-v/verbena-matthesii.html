Verbena matthesii

Verbena matthesii, commonly known as the golden verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces vibrant yellow blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Verbena matthesii is drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance once established.
