Veronica monstrosa

Veronica monstrosa, commonly known as the strange veronica, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, spire-like inflorescence composed of small blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Veronica monstrosa is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
