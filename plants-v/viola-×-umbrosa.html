Viola × umbrosa

Viola × umbrosa, commonly known as the shade viola, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Viola tricolor and Viola sororia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of colorful, violet-pink flowers that bloom in early spring. This species thrives in partial to full shade, making it ideal for woodland gardens and shaded borders. Its flowers are typically borne on slender stems and are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola × umbrosa is known for its hardiness and ability to naturalize in moist, well-drained soil.
