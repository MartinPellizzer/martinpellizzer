Valerianella patellaria

Valerianella patellaria, commonly known as the mossy valerian, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the Valerianaceae family.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, moss-like coating. The plant produces tiny, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Valerianella patellaria is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting insect life.
