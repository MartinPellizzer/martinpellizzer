Valeriana condamoana

Valeriana condamoana, commonly known as the Madagascar valerian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often violet or blue flowers. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its medicinal properties.

Its roots contain compounds used in traditional medicine for their calming effects.
