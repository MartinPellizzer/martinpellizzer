Veronica rugosa

Veronica rugosa, commonly known as hairy veronica, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Veronica rugosa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
