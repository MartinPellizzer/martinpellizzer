Vinsonia utilis

Vinsonia utilis, commonly known as the useful vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, characterized by its pod-bearing fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with three leaflets and produces small, often pale flowers. Its roots are known for their medicinal properties, used traditionally in herbal remedies.

Vinsonia utilis is valued both for its ecological role and its potential in pharmaceutical applications.
