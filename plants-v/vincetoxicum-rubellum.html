Vincetoxicum rubellum

Vincetoxicum rubellum, commonly known as red bittersweet, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy berries that persist into winter. Its berries are toxic to humans and animals, containing compounds like strychnine.

Vincetoxicum rubellum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and autumn color.
