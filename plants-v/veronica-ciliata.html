Veronica ciliata

Veronica ciliata, commonly known as the slender speedwell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade.

This plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
