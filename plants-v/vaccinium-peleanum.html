Vaccinium peleanum

Vaccinium peleanum, commonly known as the Pelean huckleberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly the Philippines. This shrub typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has evergreen leaves that are oval-shaped and glossy. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, followed by edible berries that are dark blue to black in color.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and potential medicinal studies.
