Vaccinium merrillianum

Vaccinium merrillianum, commonly known as the Merril's blueberry, is a species of dwarf shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, acidic berries that are typically dark blue or purple in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Vaccinium merrillianum is often found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental and culinary potential.
