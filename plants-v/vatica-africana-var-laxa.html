Vatica africana var. laxa

Vatica africana var. laxa, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a tropical tree native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody capsules containing elliptical seeds.

This species is often found in moist lowland forests and plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
