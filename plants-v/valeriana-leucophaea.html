Valeriana leucophaea

Valeriana leucophaea, commonly known as white valerian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.
