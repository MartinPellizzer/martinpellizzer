Vitis ficifolia f. glabrata

Vitis ficifolia f. glabrata, commonly known as the smoothleaf grape, is a botanical variant of the species Vitis ficifolia.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless leaves and is native to parts of East Asia, particularly China. The plant produces small, clustered berries that are typically green when mature. It is often found in forested areas and thrives in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
