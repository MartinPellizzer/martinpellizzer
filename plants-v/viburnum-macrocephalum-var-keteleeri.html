Viburnum macrocephalum var. keteleeri

Viburnum macrocephalum var. keteleeri, commonly known as the Keteleeri viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern China.

It features showy, fragrant, white to pale pink flower clusters that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its elegant appearance.
