Vernonia exsertiflora var. tenuicalyx

Vernonia exsertiflora var. tenuicalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and the plant often grows in open woodlands or disturbed soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in full sun.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
