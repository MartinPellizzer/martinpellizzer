Vismia acuminata var. caparosa

Vismia acuminata var. caparosa, commonly known as the Cape vismia, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation areas.
