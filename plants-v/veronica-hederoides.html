Veronica hederoides

Veronica hederoides, commonly known as creeping veronica, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has rounded, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in a variety of climates, including USDA hardiness zones 4 through 8.
