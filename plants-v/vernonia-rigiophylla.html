Vernonia rigiophylla

Vernonia rigiophylla, commonly known as the rigid-leaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, rigid leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers arranged in dense, conical clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Vernonia rigiophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
