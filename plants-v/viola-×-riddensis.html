Viola × riddensis

Viola × riddensis, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Viola tricolor and Viola lutea, resulting in a plant with distinctive foliage and vibrant flowers. The leaves are heart-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and the flowers typically feature a combination of purple, yellow, and white petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
