Viola × mirabiliformis

Viola × mirabiliformis, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a species resulting from the cross between Viola tricolor and Viola verecunda.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 10-20 cm and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
