Veronica cilicica var. surculosa

Veronica cilicica var. surculosa, commonly known as the Balkan speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The species is also noted for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
