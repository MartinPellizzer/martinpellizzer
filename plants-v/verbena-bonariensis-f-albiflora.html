Verbena bonariensis f. albiflora

Verbena bonariensis f. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower verbena, is a cultivar of the annual verbena species native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking white flowers, which contrast with its gray-green foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet, producing clusters of small, tubular blooms throughout the summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
