Vernonia simplex var. regnellii

Vernonia simplex var. regnellii, commonly known as the Regnell's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along the edges of ponds, showing a preference for moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
