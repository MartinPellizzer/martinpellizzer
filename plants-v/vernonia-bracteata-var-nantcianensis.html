Vernonia bracteata var. nantcianensis

Vernonia bracteata var. nantcianensis, commonly known as the Nanticoke ironweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of yellow to orange-red composite flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in moist to wet soils along stream banks and in swampy areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
