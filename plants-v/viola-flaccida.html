Viola flaccida

Viola flaccida, commonly known as the drooping violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that range in color from pale purple to white, often with a distinctive yellow throat. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Viola flaccida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
