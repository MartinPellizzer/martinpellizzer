Vernonia shevaroyensis

Vernonia shevaroyensis, commonly known as the Shevaroy hill Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its erect stem and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom during the dry season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
