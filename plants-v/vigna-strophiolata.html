Vigna strophiolata

Vigna strophiolata, commonly known as the broadleaf cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often trifoliate or pinnate, and its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces cylindrical pods containing edible seeds, which are used as a food source in many parts of the world. Vigna strophiolata is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a useful cover crop.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
