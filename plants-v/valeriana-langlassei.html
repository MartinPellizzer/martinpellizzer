Valeriana langlassei

Valeriana langlassei, commonly known as the Langlasse valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, somewhat woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the cooler months. Its root system is known for containing essential oils and alkaloids, which contribute to its historical use in traditional medicine.

Valeriana langlassei is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes.
