Vernonia jelskii

Vernonia jelskii, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. It produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Vernonia jelskii is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils.
