Viola krugiana

Viola krugiana, commonly known as the violet of Krug, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly in the Appalachian region. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces clusters of small, violet-colored flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Viola krugiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
