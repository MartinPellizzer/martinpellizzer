Vitis esquirolii

Vitis esquirolii, commonly known as the Esquiroli's grapevine, is a rare and woody vine native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and ability to climb using tendrils. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its berries are usually small and can vary in color from green to dark purple.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Vitis esquirolii is considered a species of conservation concern.
