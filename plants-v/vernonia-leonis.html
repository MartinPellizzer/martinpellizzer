Vernonia leonis

Vernonia leonis, commonly known as the golden ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features tall, erect stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly rough texture.

Vernonia leonis is often found in moist, open habitats such as prairies and along stream banks.
