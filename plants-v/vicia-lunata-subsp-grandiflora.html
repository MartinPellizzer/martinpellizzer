Vicia lunata subsp. grandiflora

Vicia lunata subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields or along roadsides. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
