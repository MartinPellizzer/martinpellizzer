Vaccinium orientale

Vaccinium orientale, commonly known as the oriental blueberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It produces small, elliptical berries that are typically blue or purple in color when mature. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

Vaccinium orientale is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits, making it a popular choice in gardens and agricultural settings.
