Vicia sativa var. variabilis

Vicia sativa var. variabilis, commonly known as variable vetch, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow to green pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in disturbed soils, fields, and along roadsides.

Vicia sativa var. variabilis is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant for soil enrichment.
