Vernonia eriophylla

Vernonia eriophylla, commonly known as the silky goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellow to golden-orange composite flowers. The plant has hairy, silver-gray foliage that helps it conserve moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species adapted to desert conditions.

Vernonia eriophylla is often found in rocky areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
