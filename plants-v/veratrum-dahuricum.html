Veratrum dahuricum

Veratrum dahuricum, commonly known as the Siberian buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the colder regions of Asia.

It features tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with large, waxy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, and they contain various alkaloids, making the plant toxic.

It is often found in moist meadows and along riverbanks in its natural habitat.
