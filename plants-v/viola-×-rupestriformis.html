Viola × rupestriformis

Viola × rupestriformis, commonly known as the rock viol, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces vibrant violet or purple flowers with yellow centers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and it is often used in rock gardens due to its ability to tolerate harsh conditions.

Viola × rupestriformis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in garden settings.
