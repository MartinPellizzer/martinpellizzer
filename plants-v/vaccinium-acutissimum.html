Vaccinium acutissimum

Vaccinium acutissimum, commonly known as sharpleaf blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like heathers and cranberries. The plant is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and have a pointed tip. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, which give way to edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color.

This plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in boreal and subarctic regions.
