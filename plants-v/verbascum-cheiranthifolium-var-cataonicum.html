Verbascum cheiranthifolium var. cataonicum

Verbascum cheiranthifolium var. cataonicum, commonly known as the Cataonic mullein, is a perennial plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in dense, soft, velvety hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and also covered in a thick layer of woolly hairs.

This variety is often found in rocky or dry habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
