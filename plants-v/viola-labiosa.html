Viola labiosa

Viola labiosa, commonly known as the Brazilian violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy flowers with five petals, typically in shades of violet or purple, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Viola labiosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
