Viola caricetorum

Viola caricetorum, commonly known as the Caricetum violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola caricetorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
