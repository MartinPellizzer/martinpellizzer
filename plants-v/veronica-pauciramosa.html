Veronica pauciramosa

Veronica pauciramosa, commonly known as slender speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has wiry stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Veronica pauciramosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
