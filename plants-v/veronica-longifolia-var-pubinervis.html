Veronica longifolia var. pubinervis

Veronica longifolia var. pubinervis, commonly known as the hairy nerve veronica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, hairy pubescence, especially along the nerves of the leaves. The leaves are lanceolate to elliptic in shape, with a prominent central vein and a softly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and clustered in dense spikes, typically blooming from June to August.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky habitats.
