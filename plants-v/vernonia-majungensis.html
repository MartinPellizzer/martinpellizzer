Vernonia majungensis

Vernonia majungensis, commonly known as the Majungensis ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellowish-green.

Vernonia majungensis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its distinctive foliage.
