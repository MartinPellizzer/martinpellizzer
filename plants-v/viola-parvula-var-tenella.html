Viola parvula var. tenella

Viola parvula var. tenella, commonly known as the small viola or dwarf violet, is a compact perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of violet or purple. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 6 inches, making it well-suited for rock gardens or as a ground cover. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, and it is known for its ability to bloom early in the spring.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
