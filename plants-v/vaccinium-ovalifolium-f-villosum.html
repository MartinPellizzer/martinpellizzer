Vaccinium ovalifolium f. villosum

Vaccinium ovalifolium f. villosum, commonly known as the hairy ovalleaf blueberry, is a variant of the ovalleaf blueberry native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and small, dark blue berries that are typically edible. This plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. It is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental foliage and fruit.

The species is also used in horticulture for its hardiness and adaptability to various growing conditions.
