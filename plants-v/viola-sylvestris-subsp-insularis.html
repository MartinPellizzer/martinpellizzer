Viola sylvestris subsp. insularis

Viola sylvestris subsp.

insularis, commonly known as the island violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain islands in the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple and violet. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with five petals, and are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
