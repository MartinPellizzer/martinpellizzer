Viola alburnica

Viola alburnica, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It is a perennial herb with heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Viola alburnica is characterized by its compact growth habit and its ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, contributing to the biodiversity of alpine ecosystems.
