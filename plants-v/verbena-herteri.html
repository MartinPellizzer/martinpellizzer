Verbena herteri

Verbena herteri, commonly known as the Mexican verbena, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 3 feet. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and branched.

Verbena herteri thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant ornamental plants.
