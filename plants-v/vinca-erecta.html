Vinca erecta

Vinca erecta, commonly known as the Crown of Thorns, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its woody, succulent stems and dense clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slight waxy coating. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The flowers bloom in spring and are often used in ornamental gardening due to their vibrant color and long-lasting appearance.
