Viola canina proles kutzingiana

Viola canina proles kutzingiana, commonly known as the Kutzinger's viola, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments. The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

Its flowers are typically borne on short pedicels and are visited by various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
