Veronica laingii

Veronica laingii, commonly known as Laing's speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the plantain family, Plantaginaceae, and is characterized by its small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Veronica laingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
