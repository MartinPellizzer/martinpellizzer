Vernonia poskeana subsp. samfyana

Vernonia poskeana subsp.

samfyana, commonly known as the Cape vernonia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect growth habit and showy yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in dense, conical inflorescences.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
