Vallisneria americana

Vallisneria americana, commonly known as American waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to the eastern United States.

It has long, narrow, ribbon-like leaves that float at the water's surface, with the lower portion submerged. The plant reproduces through rhizomes, which spread horizontally along the bottom of water bodies. It thrives in slow-moving to still freshwater environments, often forming dense underwater stands.

Vallisneria americana plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen for various aquatic organisms.
