Vanda striata

Vanda striata, commonly known as the streaked vanda, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically produces pendulous inflorescences with brightly colored blossoms in shades of white, yellow, and orange. Vanda striata thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
