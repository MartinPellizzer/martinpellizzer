Veronica anagallis

Veronica anagallis, commonly known as mouse-ear hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically hairy and oval-shaped, with a distinctive silver-gray appearance. This plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and rocky areas.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
