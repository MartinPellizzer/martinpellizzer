Valeriana coronata

Valeriana coronata, commonly known as the crowned valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer. Valeriana coronata is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its historical use in traditional medicine.
