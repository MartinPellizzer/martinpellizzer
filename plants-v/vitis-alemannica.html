Vitis alemannica

Vitis alemannica, commonly known as the Alsatian grapevine, is a species of flowering vine native to Europe.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically dark blue to black in color. Vitis alemannica is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

It is also used in traditional winemaking in certain regions of Europe.
