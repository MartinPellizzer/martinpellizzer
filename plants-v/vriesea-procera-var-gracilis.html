Vriesea procera var. gracilis

Vriesea procera var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender star sedge, is a striking bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in height, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant produces striking, long-lasting inflorescences with bright orange or red flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular among bromeliad enthusiasts for its ornamental value and ease of care.
