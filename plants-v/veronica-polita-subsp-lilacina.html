Veronica polita subsp. lilacina

Veronica polita subsp.

lilacina, commonly known as the lilacflower speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to lilac.

The leaves are typically narrow and lanceolate, with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the summer season.
