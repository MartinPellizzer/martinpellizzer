Veratrum dolichopetalum

Veratrum dolichopetalum, commonly known as the long-petaled white hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Melanthiaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, showy white or pale blue flowers. The plant has large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
