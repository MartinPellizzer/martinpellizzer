Vicia aegaea

Vicia aegaea, commonly known as the Aegean vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial legume that typically grows in rocky or dry soils. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its trifoliate leaves are compound, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Vicia aegaea is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
