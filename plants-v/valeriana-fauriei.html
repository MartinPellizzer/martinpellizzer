Valeriana fauriei

Valeriana fauriei, commonly known as the Faurie valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often reddish or purple flowers. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. Valeriana fauriei is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its calming effects, and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment.
