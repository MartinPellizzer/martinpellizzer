Veronica austriaca var. neiceffii

Veronica austriaca var. neiceffii, commonly known as the Alpine speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features upright, branching stems that bear small, blue to violet flowers arranged in clusters. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, typical of many Veronica species. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
