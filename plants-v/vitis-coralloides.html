Vitis coralloides

Vitis coralloides, commonly known as the coral vine, is a climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive coral-like inflorescences, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into round, blue-black berries. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets.

Vitis coralloides is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
