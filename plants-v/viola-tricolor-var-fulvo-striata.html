Viola tricolor var. fulvo-striata

Viola tricolor var. fulvo-striata, commonly known as the golden-streaked violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, typically with three heart-shaped leaflets, and vibrant flowers that display a mix of purple, white, and yellow hues. The flowers often have distinctive yellow streaks or markings, which give the variety its name. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
