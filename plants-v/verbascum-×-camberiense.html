Verbascum × camberiense

Verbascum × camberiense, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes several species of flowering plants known for their soft, fuzzy leaves. The plant produces tall, erect stems that bear yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in rocky or dry habitats.

Its fibrous leaves and showy flowers make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
