Vernonia capituliflora

Vernonia capituliflora, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, bract-like leaves and has a fibrous root system. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

The genus Vernonia is part of the sunflower family, Asteraceae, and is known for its composite flower heads.
