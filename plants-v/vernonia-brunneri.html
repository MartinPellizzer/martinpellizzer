Vernonia brunneri

Vernonia brunneri, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy, blue to purple blossoms. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and streambanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a silvery underside, contributing to its ornamental value.

Vernonia brunneri is valued in gardens for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
