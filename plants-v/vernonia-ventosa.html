Vernonia ventosa

Vernonia ventosa, commonly known as the slender ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of tiny flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough, hairy texture, while its flowers are pale yellow to brownish in color.

Vernonia ventosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
