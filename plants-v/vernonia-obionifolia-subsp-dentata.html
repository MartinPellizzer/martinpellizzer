Vernonia obionifolia subsp. dentata

Vernonia obionifolia subsp.

dentata, commonly known as the toothed ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces showy clusters of yellow to orange-red flowers during the summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
