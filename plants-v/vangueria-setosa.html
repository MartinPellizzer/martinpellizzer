Vangueria setosa

Vangueria setosa, commonly known as the hairy vangueria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant features small, narrow leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-yellow, and they appear in clusters along the stems.

Vangueria setosa is known for its resilience to drought and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
