Vignea nardina

Vignea nardina, commonly known as the narrowleaf vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant produces small, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Vignea nardina is typically found in dry, disturbed soils and thrives in temperate climates.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it a valuable plant for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
