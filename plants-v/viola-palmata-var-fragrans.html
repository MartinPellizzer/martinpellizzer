Viola palmata var. fragrans

Viola palmata var. fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of fragrant, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild aromatic properties.
