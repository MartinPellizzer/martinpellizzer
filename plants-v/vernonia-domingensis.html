Vernonia domingensis

Vernonia domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Vernonia domingensis thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in dry to moist habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
