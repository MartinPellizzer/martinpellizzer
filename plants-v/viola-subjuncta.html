Viola subjuncta

Viola subjuncta, commonly known as the yellow violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Viola subjuncta is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, spurred petals, a feature common in the genus Viola.

It plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
