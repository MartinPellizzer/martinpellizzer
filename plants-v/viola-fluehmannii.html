Viola fluehmannii

Viola fluehmannii, commonly known as the Fluehmann's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
