Vaccinium corymbosum var. albiflorum

Vaccinium corymbosum var. albiflorum, commonly known as the whiteflower blueberry, is a variant of the highbush blueberry.

It is characterized by its white or pale pink flowers, which contrast with its dark green foliage. This plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. It produces small, sweet berries that are similar to those of the common blueberry.

The variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits.
