Veronica longifolia var. albiflora

Veronica longifolia var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in mild climates.
