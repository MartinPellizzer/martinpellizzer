Viola palmata var. pedatifida

Viola palmata var. pedatifida, commonly known as the broad-leaved violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features palmately lobed leaves that are often deeply divided, giving it a distinctive, fern-like appearance. The plant produces clusters of violet-blue flowers with five petals, typically in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its foliage and flowers contribute to its ornamental value in gardens and natural habitats.
