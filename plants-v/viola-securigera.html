Viola securigera

Viola securigera, commonly known as the shield-leaf violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often marked with distinctive patterns, and its flowers are typically violet or blue, with five petals. The plant grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces capsule fruits that contain several small, dark seeds.

Viola securigera is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
