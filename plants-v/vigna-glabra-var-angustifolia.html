Vigna glabra var. angustifolia

Vigna glabra var. angustifolia, commonly known as slender cowpea, is an annual legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into cylindrical, dehiscent pods containing several angular seeds. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a valuable crop in arid environments.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients.
