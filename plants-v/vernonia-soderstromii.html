Vernonia soderstromii

Vernonia soderstromii, commonly known as Soderstrom's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, dark purple to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia soderstromii is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential use in ornamental gardening.
