Viburnum shweliense

Viburnum shweliense, commonly known as the Shweli viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters and features opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. Viburnum shweliense is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
