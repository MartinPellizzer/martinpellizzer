Vaccinium incarum

Vaccinium incarum, commonly known as the red bilberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces bell-shaped, violet flowers that develop into small, dark blue berries rich in antioxidants. These berries are often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to their nutritional and medicinal properties.

Vaccinium incarum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
