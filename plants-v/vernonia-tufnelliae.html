Vernonia tufnelliae

Vernonia tufnelliae, commonly known as Tufnell's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of purple to pinkish-red flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in prairie and woodland habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in dense, spherical inflorescences.

Vernonia tufnelliae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
