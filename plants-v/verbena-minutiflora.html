Verbena minutiflora

Verbena minutiflora, commonly known as the dwarf verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Verbena minutiflora is known for its hardiness and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
