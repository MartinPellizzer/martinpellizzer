Viburnum triphyllum f. macrophyllum

Viburnum triphyllum f. macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaved swamp honeysuckle, is a variant of the swamp honeysuckle species.

It is characterized by its notably larger leaves compared to the typical form, which can reach up to 5 cm in length. The plant is native to wetland habitats in eastern North America, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. It produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, followed by edible, red berries that attract various wildlife.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in riparian zones.
