Viola minor var. albescens

Viola minor var. albescens, commonly known as the white violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, hairy leaves and small, showy white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
