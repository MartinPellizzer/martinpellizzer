Vaccinium margarettae

Vaccinium margarettae, commonly known as Margaretta's blueberry, is a species of shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a waxy surface, and it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

This species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its potential as a food source for wildlife.
