Vaccinium pumilum

Vaccinium pumilum, commonly known as the lowbush blueberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to North America.

It typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that develop into edible berries, which are rich in antioxidants. Its leaves are dark green and leathery, and it is often found in heathlands and bogs.

Vaccinium pumilum plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects.
