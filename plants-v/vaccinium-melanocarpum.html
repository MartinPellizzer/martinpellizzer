Vaccinium melanocarpum

Vaccinium melanocarpum, commonly known as black crowberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically eaten by birds and small mammals. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in boreal and temperate forest ecosystems.

Vaccinium melanocarpum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
