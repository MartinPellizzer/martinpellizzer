Viola renifolia var. brainerdii

Viola renifolia var. brainerdii, commonly known as the Brainerd's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with five petals, often with a yellow center, during the spring season. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
