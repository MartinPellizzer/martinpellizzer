Viburnum garrettii

Viburnum garrettii, commonly known as Garrett's viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Viburnum garrettii is valued for its attractive foliage, flowering habit, and use in native plant gardens.
