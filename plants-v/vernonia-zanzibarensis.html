Vernonia zanzibarensis

Vernonia zanzibarensis, commonly known as Zanzibar ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of East Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shores. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom throughout the dry season. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Vernonia zanzibarensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
