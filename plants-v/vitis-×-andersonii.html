Vitis × andersonii

Vitis × andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's grape, is a hybrid vine species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. The plant produces clusters of small, dark blue to black berries that are typically edible. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a serrated margin, and it often has tendrils for climbing.

Vitis × andersonii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
