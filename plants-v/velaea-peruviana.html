Velaea peruviana

Velaea peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian velvetleaf, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Velaea peruviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
