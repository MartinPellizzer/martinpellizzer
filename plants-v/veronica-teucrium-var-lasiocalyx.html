Veronica teucrium var. lasiocalyx

Veronica teucrium var. lasiocalyx, commonly known as the woolly speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features upright stems covered in dense, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The leaves are lance-shaped and also covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its tactile texture. Its small, blue or purple flowers bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.
