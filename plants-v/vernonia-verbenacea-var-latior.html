Vernonia verbenacea var. latior

Vernonia verbenacea var. latior, commonly known as broadleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open woodlands and along roadsides.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
