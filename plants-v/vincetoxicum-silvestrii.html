Vincetoxicum silvestrii

Vincetoxicum silvestrii, commonly known as wild white bindweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant is known for its climbing habit, often using tendrils to attach to supports. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is commonly found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Vincetoxicum silvestrii is valued for its ornamental appeal but can be invasive in certain regions.
