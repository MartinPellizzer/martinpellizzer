Viola cinerea var. soyrae

Viola cinerea var. soyrae, commonly known as the gray violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, grayish-green leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, producing a cluster of small, showy petals that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
