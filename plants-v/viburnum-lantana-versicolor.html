Viburnum lantana versicolor

Viburnum lantana versicolor, commonly known as the variable viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, depending on the variety. The plant produces small, dark berries that are a food source for various birds and wildlife. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin.

Viburnum lantana versicolor thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats.
