Vernonia inulifolia

Vernonia inulifolia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive blue or purple flower color. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

Vernonia inulifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
