Vriesea sarcolepis

Vriesea sarcolepis, commonly known as the red-tipped vriesea, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It features vibrant, strap-like leaves that form a rosette, creating a water-holding cup at the center. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with brightly colored, long-lasting bracts that range from red to orange. Its flowers are typically small and white, emerging from the base of the bracts.

Vriesea sarcolepis thrives in bright, indirect light and is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its adaptability and ornamental appeal.
