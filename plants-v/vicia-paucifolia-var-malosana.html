Vicia paucifolia var. malosana

Vicia paucifolia var. malosana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems with trifoliate leaves composed of small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of purple or pink, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing capabilities in ecological restoration.
