Vincetoxicum tanakae

Vincetoxicum tanakae, commonly known as Japanese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, violet-blue berries that appear in the fall, which are a striking feature of the plant. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract wildlife.
