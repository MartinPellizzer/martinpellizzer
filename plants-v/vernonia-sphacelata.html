Vernonia sphacelata

Vernonia sphacelata, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to purple flower heads that resemble mist. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its leaves are narrow and often have a silvery underside, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Vernonia sphacelata is valued in gardens for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
