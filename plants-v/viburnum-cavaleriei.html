Viburnum cavaleriei

Viburnum cavaleriei, commonly known as Chinese snowball, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, creating a striking visual display. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding to its seasonal appeal. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Viburnum cavaleriei is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic value and adaptability.
