Verbena prismatica

Verbena prismatica, commonly known as the prism verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in shades of purple, pink, and white, with the flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, often with a slight serration along the edges.

Verbena prismatica is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
