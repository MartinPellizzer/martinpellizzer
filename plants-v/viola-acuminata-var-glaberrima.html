Viola acuminata var. glaberrima

Viola acuminata var. glaberrima, commonly known as the glabrous violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, well-drained soils. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its flowers are violet-purple with a distinctive yellow beard on the lower lip. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and ornamental value in native plant gardens.
