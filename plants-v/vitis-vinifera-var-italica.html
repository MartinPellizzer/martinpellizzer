Vitis vinifera var. italica

Vitis vinifera var. italica, commonly known as the Italian grapevine, is a cultivar of the common grapevine native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and the production of small, thick-skinned berries that are typically used for winemaking. The plant thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils and requires a period of winter dormancy. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces clusters of fruit that ripen in late summer.

This variety is valued for its adaptability and contribution to regional wine traditions.
