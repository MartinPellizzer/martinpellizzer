Viguiera gracillima

Viguiera gracillima, commonly known as the slender coneflower, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue-to-purple flower heads that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Viguiera gracillima is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
