It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and its ability to thrive in high-altitude environments. It produces microsporangia and megasporangia on separate structures, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

Verojuncus alpinoarticulatus is often found in rocky, well-drained soils in alpine and subalpine regions.
