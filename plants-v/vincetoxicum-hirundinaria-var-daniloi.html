Vincetoxicum hirundinaria var. daniloi

Vincetoxicum hirundinaria var. daniloi, commonly known as the Danilov's ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Europe.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along streambanks.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
