Vicia angustifolia subvar. roseiflora

Vicia angustifolia subvar. roseiflora, commonly known as the narrowleaf vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Vicia angustifolia subvar. roseiflora is a perennial legume that can fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
