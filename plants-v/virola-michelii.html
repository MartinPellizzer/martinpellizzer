Virola michelii

Virola michelii, commonly known as the Amazonian ironwood, is a large evergreen tree native to the humid lowland forests of South America.

It belongs to the family Myristicaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and strength. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall and has broad, lanceolate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine.
