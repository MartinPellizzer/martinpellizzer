Vitis watsoniana

Vitis watsoniana, commonly known as the Watson's grape, is a species of flowering vine native to China.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and compound leaves that are often trifoliate. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible, round berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Vitis watsoniana is also valued for its potential in viticulture and horticulture.
