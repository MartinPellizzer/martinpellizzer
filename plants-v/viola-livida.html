Viola livida

Viola livida, commonly known as the pale violet, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive pale violet to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers have five petals with a characteristic spur.

Viola livida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
