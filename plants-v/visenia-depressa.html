Visenia depressa

Visenia depressa, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 6 to 12 inches. The plant features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Visenia depressa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in natural habitats.
