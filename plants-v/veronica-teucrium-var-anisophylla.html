Veronica teucrium var. anisophylla

Veronica teucrium var. anisophylla, commonly known as the blueweed or narrow-leaved speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Western Asia.

It typically grows in grassy areas, woodland edges, and rocky soils, thriving in temperate climates. The plant features slender stems that often branch near the base, supporting clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
