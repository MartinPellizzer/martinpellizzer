Verbascum millelacuum

Verbascum millelacuum, commonly known as the yellow mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a spike-like inflorescence. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Verbascum millelacuum is a perennial species that is relatively easy to grow in gardens with well-drained soil.
