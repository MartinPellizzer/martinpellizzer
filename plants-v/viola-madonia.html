Viola madonia

Viola madonia, commonly known as the Madonna violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces striking violet-blue flowers with yellow centers. The plant typically grows to a height of 10-20 cm and thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola madonia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
