Vernonia daphnoides

Vernonia daphnoides, commonly known as the silverleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and tall, sturdy stems that support its clusters of small, blue-violet flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine. Vernonia daphnoides thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies adds to its ecological value in gardens and natural habitats.
