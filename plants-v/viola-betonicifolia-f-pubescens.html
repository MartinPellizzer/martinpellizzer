Viola betonicifolia f. pubescens

Viola betonicifolia f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy heartleaf violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, pubescent hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-colored flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a delicate, ornamental plant.
