Vereia aegyptiaca

Vereia aegyptiaca, commonly known as the Egyptian violet, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of violet or purple, often in clusters. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it conserve water in dry environments.

Vereia aegyptiaca is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a resilient choice for xeriscaping.
