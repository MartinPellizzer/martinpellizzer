Verbena × fecunda

Verbena × fecunda, commonly known as the common verbena, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Verbenaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from blue to purple, often with a white or yellow center. The plant is native to the southern United States and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils. It produces small, four-lobed leaves that are opposite along the stems, and it is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in full sun.

Verbena × fecunda is widely cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
