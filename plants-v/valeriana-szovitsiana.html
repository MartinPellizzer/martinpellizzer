Valeriana szovitsiana

Valeriana szovitsiana, commonly known as Szovits' valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often strongly scented leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its rhizomes and roots are the primary parts used in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

Valeriana szovitsiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal, with its delicate white to pale pink flowers adding visual interest to gardens.
