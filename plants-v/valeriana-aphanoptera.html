Valeriana aphanoptera

Valeriana aphanoptera, commonly known as the ghost valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, inconspicuous white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has aromatic, often hairy leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stem. Its root system is fibrous and contains compounds that contribute to its medicinal properties.

Valeriana aphanoptera is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.
