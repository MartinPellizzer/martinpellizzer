Viola sieboldii f. albiflora

Viola sieboldii f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces showy, white to pale lavender flowers with a distinctive violet hue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
