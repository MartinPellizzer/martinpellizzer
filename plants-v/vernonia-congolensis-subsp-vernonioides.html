Vernonia congolensis subsp. vernonioides

Vernonia congolensis subsp.

vernonioides, commonly known as the Congo Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to Central and West Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters, with lance-shaped leaves and yellow-orange flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
