Verbesina scandens

Verbesina scandens, commonly known as yellow woodflower, is a climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive reddish or purplish underside. The plant produces showy, golden-yellow flowers that are typically arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, long-blooming ornamental plants.

The seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to spread and colonize new areas.
