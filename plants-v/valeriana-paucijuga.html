Valeriana paucijuga

Valeriana paucijuga, commonly known as the narrow-leaved valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are aromatic and have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their sedative properties.

Valeriana paucijuga thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
