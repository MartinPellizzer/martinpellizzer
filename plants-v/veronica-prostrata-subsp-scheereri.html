Veronica prostrata subsp. scheereri

Veronica prostrata subsp.

scheereri, commonly known as the prostrate speedwell, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to southern Europe. It features small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has slender, spreading stems with opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
