Vicia tanakae

Vicia tanakae, commonly known as Tanaka's vetch, is a flowering plant species native to parts of East Asia, including Japan and China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Vicia tanakae is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
