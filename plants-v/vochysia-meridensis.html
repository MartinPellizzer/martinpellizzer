Vochysia meridensis

Vochysia meridensis, commonly known as the Mexican velvet tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and northern South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant has a compact, rounded shape with a smooth, grayish bark that becomes more fissured with age. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Vochysia meridensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
