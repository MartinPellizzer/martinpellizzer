Vaccinium attenuatum

Vaccinium attenuatum, commonly known as the trailing blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, often harvested in late summer. The plant has needle-like leaves that are dark green and densely packed, giving it a dense, mat-like appearance. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Vaccinium attenuatum is also valued for its ecological role, providing food for various wildlife species.
