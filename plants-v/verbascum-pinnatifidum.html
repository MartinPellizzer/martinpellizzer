Verbascum pinnatifidum

Verbascum pinnatifidum, commonly known as the narrowleaf mullein, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy, lobed leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers in the summer, which are clustered in a terminal spike. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Verbascum pinnatifidum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
