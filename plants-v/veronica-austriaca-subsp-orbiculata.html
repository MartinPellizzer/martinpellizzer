Veronica austriaca subsp. orbiculata

Veronica austriaca subsp.

orbiculata, commonly known as the round-leaved speedwell, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe. It features small, blue to violet flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer.

The plant has oval-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Plantaginaceae family.
