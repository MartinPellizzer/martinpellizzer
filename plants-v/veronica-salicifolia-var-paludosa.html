Veronica salicifolia var. paludosa

Veronica salicifolia var. paludosa, commonly known as bog speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, fens, and other wetland environments.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
