Viola × segniensis

Viola × segniensis, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Viola tricolor and Viola lutea, showcasing a mix of traits from both parent species. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive pattern of darker markings, often arranged in a symmetrical manner. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its foliage consists of heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental value.
