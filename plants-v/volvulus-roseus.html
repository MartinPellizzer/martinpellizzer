Volvulus roseus

Volvulus roseus, commonly known as the roseate morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, rose-pink to lavender-colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces spiral-shaped seed pods. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Volvulus roseus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a member of the Convolvulaceae family.
