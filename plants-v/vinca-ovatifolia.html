Vinca ovatifolia

Vinca ovatifolia, commonly known as the ovalleaf periwinkle, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers. The plant is evergreen and thrives in shady, moist environments. It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and as a ground cover.
