Veronica gentianoides

Veronica gentianoides, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem.

Veronica gentianoides is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional herbal medicine.
