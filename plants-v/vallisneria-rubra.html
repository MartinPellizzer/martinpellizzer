Vallisneria rubra

Vallisneria rubra, commonly known as the red waterweed, is a species of aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant typically thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Vallisneria rubra reproduces through the production of small, floating fruits that contain multiple seeds.

Its dense growth helps stabilize sediment and provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.
