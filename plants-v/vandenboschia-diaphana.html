Vandenboschia diaphana

Vandenboschia diaphana, commonly known as the transparent ice plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a waxy, translucent coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the winter months, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise subdued appearance. Its leaves are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Vandenboschia diaphana is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils, thriving in regions with high salt exposure and low rainfall.
