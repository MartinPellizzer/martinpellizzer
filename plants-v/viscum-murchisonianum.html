Viscum murchisonianum

Viscum murchisonianum, commonly known as the Murchison mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Viscaceae and is characterized by its leafless stems and small, fleshy, white or yellowish berries. The plant derives water and some nutrients from its host through specialized roots called haustoria, while it performs photosynthesis using its green stems. It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for birds and other animals that consume its berries.

Viscum murchisonianum is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments.
