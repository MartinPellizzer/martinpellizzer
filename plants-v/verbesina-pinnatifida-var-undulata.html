Verbesina pinnatifida var. undulata

Verbesina pinnatifida var. undulata, commonly known as the wavy-leaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with a pinnate structure and undulating edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
