Vandenboschia draytoniana

Vandenboschia draytoniana, commonly known as the desert stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features compact, rosette-shaped growth with thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting to its harsh environment. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating that reduces water loss.

Vandenboschia draytoniana is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and drought tolerance.
