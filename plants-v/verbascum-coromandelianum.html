Verbascum coromandelianum

Verbascum coromandelianum, commonly known as the Coromandel mullein, is a perennial herb native to southern India.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes species known for their fuzzy, woolly leaves. The plant features tall, erect stems that bear large, showy yellow flowers, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive, soft appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
