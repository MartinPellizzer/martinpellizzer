Vriesea gelatinosa

Vriesea gelatinosa, commonly known as the jelly plant, is a bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy, translucent leaves that form a rosette shape. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with vibrant, showy flowers that range from red to orange. Its unique appearance makes it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

Vriesea gelatinosa thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
