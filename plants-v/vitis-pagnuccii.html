Vitis pagnuccii

Vitis pagnuccii, commonly known as the Pagnucci grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into berries, which are typically used for winemaking. Vitis pagnuccii is valued for its resilience to drought and its ability to thrive in rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both horticulture and viticulture.
