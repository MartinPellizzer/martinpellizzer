Viola magellanica

Viola magellanica, commonly known as the Magellan violet, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in forest understories or along streambanks. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola magellanica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blossoms.
