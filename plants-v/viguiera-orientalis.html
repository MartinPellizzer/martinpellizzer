Viguiera orientalis

Viguiera orientalis, commonly known as the Mexican aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Viguiera orientalis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
