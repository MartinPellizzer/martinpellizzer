Verbascum maroccanum

Verbascum maroccanum, commonly known as the Moroccan mullein, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Verbascum maroccanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
