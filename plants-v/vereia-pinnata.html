Vereia pinnata

Vereia pinnata, commonly known as the featherleaf verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers, which range in color from white to violet, bloom throughout the summer months.

Vereia pinnata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
