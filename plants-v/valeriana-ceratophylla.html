Valeriana ceratophylla

Valeriana ceratophylla, commonly known as the horned valerian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces distinctive, horn-like structures on its flower stalks.

Valeriana ceratophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with specific growing conditions.
