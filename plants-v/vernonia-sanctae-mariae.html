Vernonia sanctae-mariae

Vernonia sanctae-mariae, commonly known as sacred vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and rough, hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to salty, well-drained soils. Its yellow-orange flower heads appear in clusters and bloom during the dry season.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
