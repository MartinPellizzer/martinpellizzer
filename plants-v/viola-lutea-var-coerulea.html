Viola lutea var. coerulea

Viola lutea var. coerulea, commonly known as the blue violet, is a variant of the common yellow violet, characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in woodlands and meadows. The plant features heart-shaped, toothed leaves that are often covered in a fine, downy coating. Its flowers bloom in early spring, producing a cluster of five petals with a distinctive dark purple throat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
