Vaccinium deliciosum

Vaccinium deliciosum, commonly known as the highbush blueberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart, sweet flavor. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Vaccinium deliciosum is widely cultivated for both commercial and home garden use due to its ornamental value and nutritional benefits.
