Vincetoxicum officinale var. acutatum

Vincetoxicum officinale var. acutatum, commonly known as the sharp-leaved milkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a pointed tip, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is known for its milky sap, which can be toxic to animals and humans if ingested.
