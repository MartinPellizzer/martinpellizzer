Varronia calocephala var. malvifolia

Varronia calocephala var. malvifolia, commonly known as the white-flowered varronia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton, and is characterized by its showy, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters, with rough, often hairy stems and broad, palmately lobed leaves.

Its flowers are clustered in panicles and bloom during the wet season, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
