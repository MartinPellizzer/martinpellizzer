Verbascum smyrnaeum

Verbascum smyrnaeum, commonly known as the Smyrna mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in soft hairs. Its showy yellow flowers appear in clusters at the top of the stems, blooming in late summer to early autumn.

Verbascum smyrnaeum is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
