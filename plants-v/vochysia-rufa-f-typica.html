Vochysia rufa f. typica

Vochysia rufa f. typica, commonly known as the red vochysia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded crown and smooth, dark-brown bark. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy upper surface.

The red vochysia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
