Veronica haustrata

Veronica haustrata, commonly known as the clustered speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, blue or purple flowers clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant features opposite, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Veronica haustrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
