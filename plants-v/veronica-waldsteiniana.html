Veronica waldsteiniana

Veronica waldsteiniana, commonly known as Waldstein's speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom from late spring to early summer.
