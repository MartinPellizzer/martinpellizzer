Viola × wychensis

Viola × wychensis, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in woodland areas and moist meadows. It prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Viola × wychensis is known for its ornamental value and has been widely used in horticulture for centuries.
