Viola verecunda var. excisa

Viola verecunda var. excisa, commonly known as the cutleaf violet, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a distinctive purplish tinge. The flowers are typically violet or purple, with five petals and a distinctive spur at the back. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and prefers partial shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
