Vicia sativa var. diploleuca

Vicia sativa var. diploleuca, commonly known as the white vetch, is a perennial legume characterized by its slender, trailing stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

It produces trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its pods are slender, hairy, and contain several seeds. This plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in meadows.

It is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a useful plant in ecological restoration and agriculture.
