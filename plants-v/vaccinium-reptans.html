Vaccinium reptans

Vaccinium reptans, commonly known as trailing blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are oval, dark green, and have a leathery texture, providing a dense ground cover.

The plant is well adapted to acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and bogs.
