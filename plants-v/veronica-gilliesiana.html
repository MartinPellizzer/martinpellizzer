Veronica gilliesiana

Veronica gilliesiana, commonly known as the silverleaf speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
