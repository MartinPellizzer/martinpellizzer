Vitis vinifera var. dulcis

Vitis vinifera var. dulcis, commonly known as the sweet grapevine, is a variety of the common grapevine native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, sweet, and aromatic berries that are widely used in winemaking and culinary applications. The plant typically grows as a deciduous vine with heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fruit. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and requires a cold winter dormancy period.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both commercial cultivation and ornamental purposes.
