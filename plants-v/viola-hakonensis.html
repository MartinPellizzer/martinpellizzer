Viola hakonensis

Viola hakonensis, commonly known as the Hakone violet, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to its natural habitat.

Viola hakonensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated for its resilience in cooler climates.
