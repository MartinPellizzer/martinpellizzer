Vicia peregrina var. gracilior

Vicia peregrina var. gracilior, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink or purple, which are typically arranged in clusters. Its trifoliate leaves consist of three small, heart-shaped leaflets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
