Vicia lathyroides subsp. arenivaga

Vicia lathyroides subsp.

arenivaga, commonly known as the sand vetch, is a perennial legume native to arid and semi-arid regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant has small, pinnate leaves with stipules and produces slender, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It is well adapted to sandy soils and can tolerate drought conditions.

This subspecies plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland dunes.
