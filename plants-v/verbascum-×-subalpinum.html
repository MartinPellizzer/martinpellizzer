Verbascum × subalpinum

Verbascum × subalpinum, commonly known as the Alpine mullein, is a hybrid plant species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stalks and large, fuzzy leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a dense, cylindrical shape. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes.

Verbascum × subalpinum is a hardy perennial that is adapted to cold climates and high altitudes.
