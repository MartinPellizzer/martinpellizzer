Veronica spicata f. setulosa

Veronica spicata f. setulosa, commonly known as the hairy spike speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its upright, spiky inflorescences that bear clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant has fine, hairy leaves that give it its common name, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

This cultivar is valued for its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
