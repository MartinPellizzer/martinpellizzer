Vaccinium eugenioides

Vaccinium eugenioides, commonly known as the bog bilberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in acidic, nutrient-poor soils and is commonly found in bogs, heathlands, and wetlands.

Vaccinium eugenioides plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food for various wildlife species.
