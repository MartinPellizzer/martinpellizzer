Vangueria tomentosa

Vangueria tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly vangueria, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
