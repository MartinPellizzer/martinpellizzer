Vachellia myrmecophila

Vachellia myrmecophila, commonly known as the African bristlebrush, is a shrub or small tree native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny branches and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, elliptical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in savannas and rocky areas.

Vachellia myrmecophila has a symbiotic relationship with ants, which reside in the hollow thorns of the plant.
