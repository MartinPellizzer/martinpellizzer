Vilfa hamiensis

Vilfa hamiensis, commonly known as the Hami vilfa, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and white, forming clustered umbels.

Vilfa hamiensis is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
