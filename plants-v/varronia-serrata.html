Varronia serrata

Varronia serrata, commonly known as the white senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils.

Varronia serrata is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
