Viola × fujisanensis

Viola × fujisanensis, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Viola lutea and Viola mandshurica.

It is native to Japan and thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in mountainous regions. The plant produces vibrant violet or purple flowers with yellow markings, typically blooming in early spring. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are dark green in color.

Viola × fujisanensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to garden conditions.
