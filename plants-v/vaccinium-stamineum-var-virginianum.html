Vaccinium stamineum var. virginianum

Vaccinium stamineum var. virginianum, commonly known as the common blueberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces edible, tart berries that are typically harvested in the summer months. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and dark green, providing dense foliage that supports various wildlife species.

This variety is well adapted to acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight.
