Voyriella parviflora

Voyriella parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered voyriella, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It blooms during the cooler months, typically between late autumn and early spring, and relies on insect pollination for reproduction.

Due to its unique adaptations, Voyriella parviflora is a valuable species for ecological studies in xerophytic ecosystems.
