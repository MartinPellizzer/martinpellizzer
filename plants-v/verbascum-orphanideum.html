Verbascum orphanideum

Verbascum orphanideum, commonly known as the Armenian mullein.

It is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia. The plant features thick, woolly leaves that are typically gray-green in color, and it produces showy yellow flowers in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its common name reflects its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
