Vaccinium dictyoneuron var. dictyoneuron

Vaccinium dictyoneuron var. dictyoneuron, commonly known as the dwarf blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible, bright blue berries in late summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
