Vitis angustissima

Vitis angustissima, commonly known as the narrowleaf grape or Chinese wild grape, is a species of flowering vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers followed by black, berry-like fruits. It is often found in forested areas and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Vitis angustissima is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine.
