Viorna douglasii mut. rosea

Viorna douglasii mut.

rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered viorna, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family. It is characterized by its showy, pink to rose-colored flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows as a shrub with slender, arching branches and dark green, ovate leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
