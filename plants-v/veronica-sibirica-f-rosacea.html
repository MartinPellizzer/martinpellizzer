Veronica sibirica f. rosacea

Veronica sibirica f. rosacea, commonly known as the Rosacean speedwell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant features hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped and serrated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
