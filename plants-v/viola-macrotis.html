Viola macrotis

Viola macrotis, commonly known as the large-flowered violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a yellow center. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 inches and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. It is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks, where it blooms in early spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
