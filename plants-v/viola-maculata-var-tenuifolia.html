Viola maculata var. tenuifolia

Viola maculata var. tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender-leaved violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or pale greenish underside, and its flowers are typically purple or violet with a yellow center. The plant grows low to the ground, often forming dense mats in woodland or shaded areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
