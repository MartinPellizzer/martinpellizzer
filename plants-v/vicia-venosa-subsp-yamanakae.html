Vicia venosa subsp. yamanakae

Vicia venosa subsp.

yamanakae, commonly known as the Yamanaka vetch, is a perennial legume native to Japan. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces.

The flowers are small, pea-shaped, and typically pink or purple in color, arranged in loose clusters. This subspecies is adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation and supports local pollinator populations.
