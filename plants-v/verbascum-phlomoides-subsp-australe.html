Verbascum phlomoides subsp. australe

Verbascum phlomoides subsp.

australe, commonly known as the Australian mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It is a herbaceous perennial that features tall, hairy stems and large, showy yellow flowers.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in soft, woolly hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
