Vignea acroandra

Vignea acroandra, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom profusely during the rainy season. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The seeds are encased in a pod and are used in traditional medicine in some regions.
