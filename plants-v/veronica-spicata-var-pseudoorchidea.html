Veronica spicata var. pseudoorchidea

Veronica spicata var. pseudoorchidea, commonly known as the spiked speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate blooms and compact form, which make it suitable for both garden landscaping and container planting.
