Vaccinium corymbosum var. virgatum

Vaccinium corymbosum var. virgatum, commonly known as the black huckleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, dark blue berries in the fall. These berries are edible and often used in jams and wines.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is commonly found in forested areas.
