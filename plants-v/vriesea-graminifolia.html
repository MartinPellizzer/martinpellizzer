Vriesea graminifolia

Vriesea graminifolia, commonly known as the grass-leafed vriesea, is a species of flowering plant in the Bromeliad family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking, colorful inflorescences that resemble pineapples, often in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used as an ornamental plant in gardens and indoor settings.

Vriesea graminifolia is relatively low-maintenance, making it a popular choice for both novice and experienced plant enthusiasts.
