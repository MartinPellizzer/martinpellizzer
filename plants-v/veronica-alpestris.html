Veronica alpestris

Veronica alpestris, commonly known as alpine speedwell, is a flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender stems and small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in meadows, slopes, and mountainous areas where the climate is cool and moist.

Veronica alpestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
