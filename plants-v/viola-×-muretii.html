Viola × muretii

Viola × muretii, commonly known as the violet hybrid, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Viola lutea and Viola tricolor, combining traits from both parent species. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of purple, violet, and white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or shady gardens.

Viola × muretii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable environments.
