Veronica × janchenii

Veronica × janchenii, commonly known as the hybrid speedwell, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically reaches a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and produces small, oval leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from late spring through early autumn.
