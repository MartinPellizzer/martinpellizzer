Viola tricolor f. pulcherrima

Viola tricolor f. pulcherrima, commonly known as the heartsease or violet, is a cultivar of the common violet.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and vibrant, three-color flowers that typically display purple, yellow, and white. The plant is native to Europe and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often grown as an ornamental plant in gardens and borders due to its attractive appearance.

Viola tricolor f. pulcherrima prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a versatile choice for various garden settings.
