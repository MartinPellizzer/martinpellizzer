Viola walteri var. appalachiensis

Viola walteri var. appalachiensis, commonly known as the Appalachian violet, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial shade. Its foliage is often covered in fine, soft hairs, and it produces small, showy blooms in early spring.

The Appalachian violet is a perennial herb that plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its habitat.
