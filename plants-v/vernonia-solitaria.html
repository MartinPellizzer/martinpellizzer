Vernonia solitaria

Vernonia solitaria, commonly known as the solitary ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and swampy areas. It produces showy, purple to brown flower heads that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
