Viola curtisii

Viola curtisii, commonly known as Curtis's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It typically grows to a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters, featuring heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with purple. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with five petals, usually blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both horticultural and ecological studies.
