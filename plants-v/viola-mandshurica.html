Viola mandshurica

Viola mandshurica, commonly known as the Manchurian violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.

Viola mandshurica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for horticultural purposes.
