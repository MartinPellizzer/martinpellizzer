Virga pilosa

Virga pilosa, commonly known as the hairy willow, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often drooping stems covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Virga pilosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.
