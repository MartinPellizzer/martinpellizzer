Vernonia lettermannii

Vernonia lettermannii, commonly known as the Lettermann's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies, and it thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are dark purple to black, giving it a striking appearance.

Vernonia lettermannii is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
