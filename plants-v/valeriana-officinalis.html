Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis, commonly known as valerian, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, often disagreeable odor and its distinctive, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, reddish or pinkish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Valerian is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its use in treating insomnia and anxiety.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
