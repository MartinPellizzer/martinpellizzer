Vernonia erigerontis

Vernonia erigerontis, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Vernonia erigerontis is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native plants that attract pollinators.
