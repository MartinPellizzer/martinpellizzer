Viburnum alnifolium f. praecox

Viburnum alnifolium f. praecox, commonly known as early leaf viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, ovate leaves that turn a striking red or purple in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in early spring, which are followed by dark blue, edible berries. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability in garden settings.
