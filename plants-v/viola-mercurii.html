Viola mercurii

Viola mercurii, commonly known as Mercury's violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or woodland soils and prefers cool, moist environments. Viola mercurii is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, adding a touch of color to its natural habitat.
