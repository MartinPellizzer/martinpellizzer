Vinticena flavescens

Vinticena flavescens, commonly known as the golden leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, yellowish-green leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil.

Vinticena flavescens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
