Veronica austriaca var. praeterita

Veronica austriaca var. praeterita, commonly known as the Austrian speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, blue to purple flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and slightly serrated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
