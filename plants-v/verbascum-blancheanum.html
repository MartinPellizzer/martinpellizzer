Verbascum blancheanum

Verbascum blancheanum, commonly known as the White Mullein, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in soft, silvery-gray fuzzy hairs. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pale yellow to white, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and also covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Verbascum blancheanum is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
