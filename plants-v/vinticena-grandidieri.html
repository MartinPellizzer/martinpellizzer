Vinticena grandidieri

Vinticena grandidieri, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, star-shaped flowers with a prominent central column. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories where it can receive adequate moisture and light. Its flowers are usually a vibrant mix of purple and white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
