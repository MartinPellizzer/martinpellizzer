Viola diffusa var. tomentosa

Viola diffusa var. tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of violet or purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its foliage and flowers are both valued in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.
