Vernonia permollis

Vernonia permollis, commonly known as the silky Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish to brownish flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Vernonia permollis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
