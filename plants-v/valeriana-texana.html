Valeriana texana

Valeriana texana, commonly known as Texas valerian, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodlands. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

Valeriana texana is also valued for its aromatic foliage and its role in supporting local pollinators.
