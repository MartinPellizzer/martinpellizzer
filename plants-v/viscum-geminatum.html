Viscum geminatum

Viscum geminatum, commonly known as the twinleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It belongs to the family Viscaceae and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and white, sticky berries. The plant derives some of its nutrients from its host through specialized roots called haustoria. It is native to regions of North America, particularly the eastern United States.

Viscum geminatum plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for birds and other animals that consume its berries.
