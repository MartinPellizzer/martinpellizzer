Vernonia brachylepis

Vernonia brachylepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical sun-like appearance. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a grayish-white underside, while its flowers are small and yellowish-green.

Vernonia brachylepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
