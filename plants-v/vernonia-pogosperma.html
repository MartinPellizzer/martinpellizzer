Vernonia pogosperma

Vernonia pogosperma, commonly known as the desert ironweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in rocky soils and dry habitats. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish to brownish flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a robust root system adapted to drought conditions.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in harsh environments, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.
