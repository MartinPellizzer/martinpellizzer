Veronica densiflora var. soongorica

Veronica densiflora var. soongorica, commonly known as the Soongor Veronica, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid environment.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
