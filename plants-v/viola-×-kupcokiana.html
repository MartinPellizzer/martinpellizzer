Viola × kupcokiana

Viola × kupcokiana, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a species resulting from the crossbreeding of Viola tricolor and Viola × wittrockiana.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers. The flowers typically come in shades of purple, violet, and white, often with contrasting veins. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

The plant belongs to the Violaceae family and is known for its adaptability to various soil types.
