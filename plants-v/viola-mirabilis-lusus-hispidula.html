Viola mirabilis lusus hispidula

Viola mirabilis lusus hispidula, commonly known as the hispid violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and distinctive violet or purple flowers. Native to certain regions of South America, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
