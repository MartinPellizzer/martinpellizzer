Viola × teriana

Viola × teriana, commonly known as the common heartleaf viola, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, often purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and gardens. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Viola × teriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in horticulture for its beauty and hardiness.
