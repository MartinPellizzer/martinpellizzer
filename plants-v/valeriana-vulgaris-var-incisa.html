Valeriana vulgaris var. incisa

Valeriana vulgaris var. incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved valerian, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, adorned with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly serrated. The plant produces small, pale pink or white flowers in spikes during the summer months. Its roots are rich in essential oils and have been traditionally used in herbal medicine.

The species is characterized by its distinctive aromatic foliage and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.
