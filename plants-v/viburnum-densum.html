Viburnum densum

Viburnum densum, commonly known as the thickleaf viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features dense, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. Viburnum densum thrives in partial to full shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

It is also valued for its ability to attract birds and support local wildlife.
