Valeriana longitubulosa

Valeriana longitubulosa, commonly known as the long-tubular valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its root system is known for containing compounds that have been traditionally used in herbal medicine.

Valeriana longitubulosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its delicate foliage and flowering habit.
