Vitis umbellata

Vitis umbellata, commonly known as the grapevines of the genus Vitis, is a species of flowering plant in the family Vitaceae.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into clusters of berries, typically ranging in color from green to black. Vitis umbellata is native to various regions in the Americas and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

It plays an important role in both ecological systems and has historical significance in the cultivation of grapes for wine and other products.
