Viola beckwithii var. cachensis

Viola beckwithii var. cachensis, commonly known as the Cachan viola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant has a compact growth habit, often reaching heights of 10 to 20 centimeters. Its flowers bloom in spring and are known for their delicate appearance and fragrance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in shaded garden environments.
