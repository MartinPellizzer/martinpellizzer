Vitex rotundifolia var. heterophylla

Vitex rotundifolia var. heterophylla, commonly known as the variegated chaste tree, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that often display variegation with white or cream margins. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of purple or blue, which bloom in the summer months. Its woody stems are covered in a distinctive, grayish bark that becomes more pronounced with age.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
