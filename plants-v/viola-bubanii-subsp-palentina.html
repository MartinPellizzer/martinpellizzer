Viola bubanii subsp. palentina

Viola bubanii subsp.

palentina, commonly known as the Palentine violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan region. It typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy violet flowers.

The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and is often found in mountainous habitats. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
