Vincentia

Vincentia, commonly known as the everlasting or iron vine, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

These plants are typically shrubs or small trees, characterized by their compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. They are native to various regions in Africa, Asia, and Australia, often growing in tropical and subtropical climates. Vincentia species are known for their ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making them valuable in ecological and agricultural contexts.

Their seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.
