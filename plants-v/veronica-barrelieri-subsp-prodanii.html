Veronica barrelieri subsp. prodanii

Veronica barrelieri subsp.

prodanii, commonly known as the Barrelier's speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

It is a low-growing perennial that is well adapted to arid and coastal environments.
