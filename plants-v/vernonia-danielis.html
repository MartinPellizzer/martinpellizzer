Vernonia danielis

Vernonia danielis, commonly known as Daniel's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and dense clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia danielis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
