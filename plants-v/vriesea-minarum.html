Vriesea minarum

Vriesea minarum, commonly known as the silver stripe bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that form a rosette, with a striking silver-green coloration and dark green veins. The plant produces showy, long-lasting inflorescences that are typically pink or red, adding visual interest to its foliage. Vriesea minarum thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
