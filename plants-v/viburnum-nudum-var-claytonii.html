Viburnum nudum var. claytonii

Viburnum nudum var. claytonii, commonly known as Clayton's arrowwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter green underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it suitable for small gardens or as a hedge.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.
