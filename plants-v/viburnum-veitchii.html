Viburnum veitchii

Viburnum veitchii, commonly known as the Veitch's viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features opposite, simple, ovate to elliptic leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by showy, red, berry-like fruits in the fall. Viburnum veitchii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its seasonal interest.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
