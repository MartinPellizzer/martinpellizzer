Viola pseudoselkirkii

Viola pseudoselkirkii, commonly known as the Selkirk violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest floors and rocky stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces small, showy flowers in shades of purple and white. It belongs to the Violaceae family, which includes many other violets and related species.

Viola pseudoselkirkii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
