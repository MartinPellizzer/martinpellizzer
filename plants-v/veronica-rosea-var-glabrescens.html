Veronica rosea var. glabrescens

Veronica rosea var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth veronica, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture that gives the variety its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and borders.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
