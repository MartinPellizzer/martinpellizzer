Vitaliana primuliflora subsp. sesleri

Vitaliana primuliflora subsp.

sesleri, commonly known as the Sesler's vitalia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features upright, branching stems that are often covered in soft, hairy foliage.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or containers.
