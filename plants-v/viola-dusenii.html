Viola dusenii

Viola dusenii, commonly known as the Dusen's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central China.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at elevations ranging from 2,000 to 4,000 meters. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and produces small, violet-blue flowers with five petals. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate structure and adaptability to high-altitude environments.

Viola dusenii is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
