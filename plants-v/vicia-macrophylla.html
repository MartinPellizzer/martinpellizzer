Vicia macrophylla

Vicia macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including meadows and woodland edges. Its seed pods are long and cylindrical, containing several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Vicia macrophylla is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
