Viola palustris f. uliginosa

Viola palustris f. uliginosa, commonly known as the swamp violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green and often have a waxy coating. The plant produces showy violet flowers with five petals, usually in shades of purple or blue, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is distinguished by its compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
