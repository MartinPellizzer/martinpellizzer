Vachellia malacocephala

Vachellia malacocephala, commonly known as the umbrella thorn acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-shaped crown formed by its dense, thornless branches. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical heads. It is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in savannas and rocky areas.

The species is also valued for its hard, durable wood and its role in local ecosystems.
