Vitex tracyana

Vitex tracyana, commonly known as Tracy's vitex, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Vitex tracyana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping for its attractive foliage and showy blooms.
