Viola alata

Viola alata, commonly known as the winged violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or pale green margin. The plant produces showy flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, with a unique wing-like pattern on the petals. Viola alata thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for use in gardens and as a decorative plant.
