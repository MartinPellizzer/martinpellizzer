Viola ovata

Viola ovata, commonly known as the oval violet, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, ovate leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and moist soils, preferring partial shade. Viola ovata produces small, nodding flowers that bloom in early spring, often with a distinct yellow throat.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded garden environments.
