Vincetoxicum triflorum

Vincetoxicum triflorum, commonly known as the three-flowered iron vine, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that appear in late spring. The plant produces small, oval leaves that are typically opposite in arrangement. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Vincetoxicum triflorum is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
