Viola melanosperma

Viola melanosperma, commonly known as the black-seeded violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant produces small, dark-colored seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. Viola melanosperma thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological study.
