Vangueria kerstingii

Vangueria kerstingii, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a red throat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
