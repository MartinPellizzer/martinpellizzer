Verbesina acapulcensis

Verbesina acapulcensis, commonly known as the Acapulco goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Verbesina acapulcensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
