Vernonia glauca f. longiaristata

Vernonia glauca f. longiaristata, commonly known as the long-stemmed ironweed, is a variant of the ironweed genus characterized by its elongated, slender stems.

This plant is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The flowers are typically blue to purple and are arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters.

It is a herbaceous perennial that blooms in late summer and early fall, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
