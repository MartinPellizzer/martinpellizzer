Vernonia drummondii

Vernonia drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, yellowish-green to brownish flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Vernonia drummondii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
