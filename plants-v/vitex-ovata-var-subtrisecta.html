Vitex ovata var. subtrisecta

Vitex ovata var. subtrisecta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf chaste tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed, typically divided into three segments, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces small, fragrant, pale blue or violet flowers in panicles during the summer months. These flowers are followed by small, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
