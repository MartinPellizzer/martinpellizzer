Verbena landbeckii

Verbena landbeckii, commonly known as the Mexican verbena, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically produces vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Verbena landbeckii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
