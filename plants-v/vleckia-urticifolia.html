Vleckia urticifolia

Vleckia urticifolia, commonly known as the stinging nettle plant, is a herbaceous perennial belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins and fine, irritating hairs that cause a skin reaction upon contact. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Vleckia urticifolia is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
