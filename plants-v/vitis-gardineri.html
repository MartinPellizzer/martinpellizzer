Vitis gardineri

Vitis gardineri, commonly known as Gardner's grapevine, is a perennial vine native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into clusters of berries, typically ranging in color from red to black. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Vitis gardineri is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in the region.
