Vincetoxicum hirundinaria subsp. glaucum

Vincetoxicum hirundinaria subsp.

glaucum, commonly known as blue milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers.

The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a bluish-green hue, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, facilitating its spread in suitable habitats.
