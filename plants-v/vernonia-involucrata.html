Vernonia involucrata

Vernonia involucrata, commonly known as the velvetleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in arid and semi-arid regions.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
