Valantia pusilla

Valantia pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf valantia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to specific regions in South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features delicate, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery coating. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually white or pale green, and appear in clusters.

Valantia pusilla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity and partial shade.
