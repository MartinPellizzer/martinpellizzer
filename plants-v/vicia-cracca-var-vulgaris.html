Vicia cracca var. vulgaris

Vicia cracca var. vulgaris, commonly known as common vetch, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in disturbed soils and is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish-green pea-like flowers. Its pods contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

Vicia cracca var. vulgaris is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in ecological restoration projects.
