Vatica coriacea

Vatica coriacea, commonly known as the hard-shelled ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and thick, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Vatica coriacea is valued for its durable timber and is often found in lowland and hill forest ecosystems.
