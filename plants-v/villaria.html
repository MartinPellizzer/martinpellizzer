Villaria

Villaria, commonly known as the common vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. Villaria is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
