Viola tokubuchiana f. variegata

Viola tokubuchiana f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated Japanese violet, is a striking hybrid species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves with striking white or yellow variegation patterns. The plant produces clusters of delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal.

The variegation in its foliage makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking unique visual interest.
