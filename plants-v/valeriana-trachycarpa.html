Valeriana trachycarpa

Valeriana trachycarpa, commonly known as rough-seeded valerian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often reddish-brown roots and dark green, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer. Valeriana trachycarpa is known for its medicinal properties, particularly for its calming effects, and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.
