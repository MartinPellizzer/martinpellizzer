Vigna hainiana

Vigna hainiana, commonly known as the Hainan bean, is a leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain one to three seeds, which are typically round and smooth. Vigna hainiana is valued for its edible seeds, which are used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.
