Viburnum alnifolium f. roseum

Viburnum alnifolium f. roseum, commonly known as the rose-flowered snowberry, is a cultivar of the snowberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries in the fall, which are edible but often used as ornamental features. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Viburnum alnifolium f. roseum is relatively low maintenance and adaptable to various soil types.
