Justicia carayana

Justicia carayana, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of tubular, red or orange flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its showy flowers attract hummingbirds and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological value.
