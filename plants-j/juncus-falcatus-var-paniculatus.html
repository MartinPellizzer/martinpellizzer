Juncus falcatus var. paniculatus

Juncus falcatus var. paniculatus, commonly known as the panicled rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often reach heights of up to 2 meters, with whorled, linear leaves that are typically hairless. The plant produces panicles of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer, making it a notable species in moist habitats. It thrives in shallow water or saturated soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its dense growth habit helps stabilize soil and provides habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
