Jacea segetalis

Jacea segetalis, commonly known as the grain amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in agricultural fields and disturbed habitats.

Jacea segetalis is valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional cuisines.
