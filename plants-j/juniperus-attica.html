Juniperus attica

Juniperus attica, commonly known as the Greek juniper, is a species of juniper native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in rocky or dry soils. The plant produces small, scale-like leaves and has blue-gray foliage that is often aromatic. Its berries are small, fleshy, and contain a single seed, which is dispersed by birds.

Juniperus attica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
