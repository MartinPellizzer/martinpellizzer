Jamesonia areniticola

Jamesonia areniticola, commonly known as the sandstone aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils, often found in rocky habitats such as cliffs and slopes. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in the fall, adding vibrant color to its arid environment. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
