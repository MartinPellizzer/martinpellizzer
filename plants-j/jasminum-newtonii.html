Jasminum newtonii

Jasminum newtonii, commonly known as the Newton's jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and ovate with a glossy surface.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for both garden cultivation and ornamental use.
