Justicia chlorantha

Justicia chlorantha, commonly known as the green-flowered justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often blue or violet, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, featuring opposite leaves that are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its floral structure includes two lips, with the upper lip being smaller than the lower lip, which is typical of the genus Justicia.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
