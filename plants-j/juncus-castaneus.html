Juncus castaneus

Juncus castaneus, commonly known as the chestnut rush, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has slender, grass-like stems that are often reddish-brown in color, and it produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers in the summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and they tend to wither as the plant goes dormant in the winter.

Juncus castaneus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
