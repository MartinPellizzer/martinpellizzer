Justicia thymifolia

Justicia thymifolia, commonly known as the thyme-leaved dragonhead, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit. The plant features aromatic, narrow leaves that resemble those of thyme, hence its common name. Its showy, tubular flowers are typically blue or violet and bloom in summer.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
