Justicia acaulis

Justicia acaulis, commonly known as the rock justicia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
