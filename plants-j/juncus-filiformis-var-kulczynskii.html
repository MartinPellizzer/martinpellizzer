Juncus filiformis var. kulczynskii

Juncus filiformis var. kulczynskii, commonly known as the slender rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in cylindrical spikelets. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil in aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.

This variant is distinguished by its slender stems and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and resilience.
