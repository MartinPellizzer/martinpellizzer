Juniperus uncinata

Juniperus uncinata, commonly known as the creeping juniper, is a shrub-like conifer native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and small, scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces tiny, berry-like cones that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for various birds and small mammals. Juniperus uncinata is well adapted to harsh, rocky environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Its slow growth rate and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions make it a resilient and hardy species.
