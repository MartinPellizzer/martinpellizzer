Juncus melananthos

Juncus melananthos, commonly known as black rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in the Northern Hemisphere.

It features tall, slender, cylindrical stems that are typically dark brown to black in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense tufts at the base of the plant.

Juncus melananthos plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
