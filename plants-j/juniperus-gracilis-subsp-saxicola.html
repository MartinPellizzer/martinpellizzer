Juniperus gracilis subsp. saxicola

Juniperus gracilis subsp.

saxicola, commonly known as the slender juniper or saxicola juniper, is a shrub-like evergreen conifer native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and produces blue or purple berries that are edible for birds and small mammals.

This subspecies typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid climates. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
