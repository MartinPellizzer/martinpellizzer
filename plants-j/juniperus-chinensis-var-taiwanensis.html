Juniperus chinensis var. taiwanensis

Juniperus chinensis var. taiwanensis, commonly known as the Taiwan juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Cupressaceae family and is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with a temperate climate.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
