Juniperus abyssinica

Juniperus abyssinica, commonly known as African juniper, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Ethiopia and Eritrea.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves and small, berry-like cones that mature to a dark blue color. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and can thrive in arid or semi-arid environments. Juniperus abyssinica is often used in traditional medicine and has ecological importance as a habitat for various wildlife species.

Its resilience to harsh conditions makes it a significant component of the local flora in its native range.
