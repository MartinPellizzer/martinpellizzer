Jocaste purpurea

Jocaste purpurea, commonly known as the purple tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds, often used in traditional cuisine. Its roots are also valued for their medicinal properties, containing compounds with potential health benefits.

Jocaste purpurea thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
