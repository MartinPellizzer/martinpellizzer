Juncus ellmanii

Juncus ellmanii, commonly known as the Ellman's rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams, preferring moist to saturated soil conditions. The plant has cylindrical, hollow stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense clumps.

Juncus ellmanii plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
