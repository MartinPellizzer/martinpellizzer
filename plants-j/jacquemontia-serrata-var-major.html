Jacquemontia serrata var. major

Jacquemontia serrata var. major, commonly known as the large-leafed false mallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features palmate leaves with deeply lobed margins and a distinctive, showy inflorescence of pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
