Jacksonia lehmannii

Jacksonia lehmannii, commonly known as the Lehmann's mistflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Jacksonia lehmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant yellow to orange flower heads.
