Jaegeria glabra var. australis

Jaegeria glabra var. australis, commonly known as the Australian hairless jaegeria, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant features small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is known for its ability to tolerate salty and windy conditions.

This species is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
