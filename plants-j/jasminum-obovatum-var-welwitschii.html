Jasminum obovatum var. welwitschii

Jasminum obovatum var. welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitsch's jasmine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the cooler months. The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aromatic blooms.
