Josephia pulchella

Josephia pulchella, commonly known as the pink fairy fan, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its unique, fan-like inflorescence. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
