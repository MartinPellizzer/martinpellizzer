Justicia modesta

Justicia modesta, commonly known as the common flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial or shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often hairy, while its stems are woody and branched.

Justicia modesta thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
