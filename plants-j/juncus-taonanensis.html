Juncus taonanensis

Juncus taonanensis, commonly known as the Taonan rush, is a perennial flowering plant native to East Asia, particularly found in China, Japan, and Korea.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, cylindrical leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Juncus taonanensis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
