Jasminum zanzibariense

Jasminum zanzibariense, commonly known as Zanzibar jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found growing near the coast or in shaded areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses worldwide.
