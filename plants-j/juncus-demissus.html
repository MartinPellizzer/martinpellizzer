Juncus demissus

Juncus demissus, commonly known as the drooping rush, is a perennial plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It features tall, slender stems that often droop gracefully, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of greenish-yellow flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and they are adapted to thrive in saturated soil conditions.

Juncus demissus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
