Jussiaea octovalvis

Jussiaea octovalvis, commonly known as the eight-leaf waterplant, is a small aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in South America.

It is characterized by its compact, floating growth habit and the presence of eight leaf-like structures, which are actually specialized stems called phyllodes. The plant typically has small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by wind or insects. Its leaves are usually green and slightly hairy, and it thrives in still or slow-moving water.

Jussiaea octovalvis plays a minor role in aquatic ecosystems but is valued in some regions for ornamental purposes.
