Juncus macer var. williamsii

Juncus macer var. williamsii, commonly known as Williams' soft rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that are typically green to reddish in color, often growing in dense clusters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, pond edges, and other wet habitats.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
