Jacquemontia apiculata

Jacquemontia apiculata, commonly known as the yellow jewelweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that burst open to release seeds.

Jacquemontia apiculata is often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as wetlands and streambanks.
