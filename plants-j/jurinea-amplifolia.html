Jurinea amplifolia

Jurinea amplifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in dune environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of blue or violet flowers. It is adapted to salt spray and strong winds, making it resilient to harsh coastal conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for coastal stabilization and ecological diversity.
