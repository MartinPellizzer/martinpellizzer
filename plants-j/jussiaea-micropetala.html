Jussiaea micropetala

Jussiaea micropetala, commonly known as the small-petioled water-plantain, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to South America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and wetlands. The plant has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are often submerged or floating, with small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in clusters. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats in its environment.

Jussiaea micropetala plays a role in stabilizing aquatic sediments and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
