Jasminum fluminense subsp. socotranum

Jasminum fluminense subsp.

socotranum, commonly known as Socotran jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Socotra Archipelago. It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a strong, sweet scent.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is typically found in coastal and rocky habitats. It is well adapted to arid conditions and can thrive in sandy soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.
