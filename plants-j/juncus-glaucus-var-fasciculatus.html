Juncus glaucus var. fasciculatus

Juncus glaucus var. fasciculatus, commonly known as the blue rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in spike-like clusters, typical of the Juncaceae family. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, pond edges, and along streams.

Due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife, it is an important component of wetland ecosystems.
