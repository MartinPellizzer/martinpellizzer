Juniperus neaboriensis

Juniperus neaboriensis, commonly known as the Neaborn juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cupressaceae family and is characterized by its scale-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations. Its cones are small and often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
