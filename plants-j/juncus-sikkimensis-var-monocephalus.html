Juncus sikkimensis var. monocephalus

Juncus sikkimensis var. monocephalus, commonly known as the Sikkim rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that are typically unbranched and covered in a dense layer of short, stiff hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and mountain meadows.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
