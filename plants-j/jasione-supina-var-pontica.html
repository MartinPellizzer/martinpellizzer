Jasione supina var. pontica

Jasione supina var. pontica, commonly known as the Pontic bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southeastern Europe.

It features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces showy blue to violet flowers in late summer. The plant thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive floral structure.

Jasione supina var. pontica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
