Jasminum italicum

Jasminum italicum, commonly known as Italian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring slender, woody stems and dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its flowers are often used in perfumery and traditional medicine due to their aromatic properties.

Jasminum italicum is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils with adequate sunlight.
