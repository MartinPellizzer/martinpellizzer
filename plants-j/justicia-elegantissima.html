Justicia elegantissima

Justicia elegantissima, commonly known as the elegant flameflower, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often reaching heights of up to 3 feet. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a velvety texture, while the flowers are borne in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
