Jamesonia brasiliensis

Jamesonia brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian firetree, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its vibrant blossoms. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to habitat loss, Jamesonia brasiliensis is considered vulnerable, making conservation efforts essential for its survival.
