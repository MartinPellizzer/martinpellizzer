Jasione perennis var. pygmaea

Jasione perennis var. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact growth habit, with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover due to its ornamental value and hardiness.
