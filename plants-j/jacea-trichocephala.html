Jacea trichocephala

Jacea trichocephala, commonly known as the woolly-headed grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to parts of Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and compact growth habit, which helps it conserve water in harsh conditions. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered at the ends of the stems.

Jacea trichocephala plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small desert organisms.
