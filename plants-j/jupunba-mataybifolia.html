Jupunba mataybifolia

Jupunba mataybifolia, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a contrasting throat. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to cool, humid environments. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of hair.

Jupunba mataybifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
