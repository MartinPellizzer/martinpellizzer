Jasminum pseudopinnatum

Jasminum pseudopinnatum, commonly known as the Arabian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its evergreen leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The essential oil extracted from its flowers is widely used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
