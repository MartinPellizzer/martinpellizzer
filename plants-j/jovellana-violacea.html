Jovellana violacea

Jovellana violacea, commonly known as the violetleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking violet-colored leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it can receive indirect sunlight. Its leaves are broad and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its native habitat.

Jovellana violacea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
