Justicia callopsoidea

Justicia callopsoidea, commonly known as the flame flower or red flame plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a visually appealing and ecologically beneficial plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
