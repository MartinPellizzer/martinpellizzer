Jacobaea × ostenfeldii

Jacobaea × ostenfeldii, commonly known as the Everlasting Dandelion, is a hybrid perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that resemble those of the dandelion, with a distinctive fluffy seed head that persists through winter. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is cultivated in gardens and used in floral arrangements.
