Jasminum spectabile

Jasminum spectabile, commonly known as the Western jasmine, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that turn reddish in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Jasminum spectabile is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and pleasant fragrance.
