Juglans compressa

Juglans compressa, commonly known as the Chinese walnut, is a deciduous tree native to China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Juglandaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with 15-23 leaflets. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by edible, oval-shaped nuts encased in a thick, woody husk. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its nuts, which are used in traditional Chinese cuisine and medicine.
