Jussiaea inflata

Jussiaea inflata, commonly known as the inflated bladderwort, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its specialized bladder-like structures.

These bladders are used to trap small aquatic organisms, allowing the plant to function as a carnivorous species. It typically grows in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater environments. The plant has a delicate, branching structure with small, scale-like leaves.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on plant adaptation and nutrient acquisition.
