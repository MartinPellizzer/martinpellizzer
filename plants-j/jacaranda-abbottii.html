Jacaranda abbottii

Jacaranda abbottii, commonly known as the blue jacaranda, is a deciduous tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The tree has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Its rapid growth and showy flowers make it a notable feature in landscapes and gardens.
