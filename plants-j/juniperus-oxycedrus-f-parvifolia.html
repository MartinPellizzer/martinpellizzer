Juniperus oxycedrus f. parvifolia

Juniperus oxycedrus f. parvifolia, commonly known as the dwarf juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces blue or black berries that are often used in culinary applications, such as flavoring gin. It is well adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a hardy plant in arid environments.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for garden use.
