Jatropha carthagenensis

Jatropha carthagenensis, commonly known as the Caper spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems, which are often greenish or reddish in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing toxic seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Due to the toxicity of its seeds, it is important to handle the plant with care.
