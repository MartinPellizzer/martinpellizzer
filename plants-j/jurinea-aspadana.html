Jurinea aspadana

Jurinea aspadana, commonly known as the coastal bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils along coastal areas. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds.

Jurinea aspadana plays an important role in stabilizing coastal dunes and supporting local biodiversity.
