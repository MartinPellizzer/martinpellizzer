Justicia villosa

Justicia villosa, commonly known as velvetleaf or Mexican verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and clusters of tubular, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to gardens.
