Juglans hindsii var. quercina

Juglans hindsii var. quercina, commonly known as the Mexican walnut, is a variant of the Hinds' walnut native to Mexico.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its compound leaves and male and female flowers on separate trees. The plant produces edible nuts enclosed in a hard shell, which are typically harvested in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.

The botanical features of this variant make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
