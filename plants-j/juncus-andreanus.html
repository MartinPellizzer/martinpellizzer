Juncus andreanus

Juncus andreanus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pale green or brown, appearing in late spring to early summer. Juncus andreanus thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, pond edges, and along stream banks.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
