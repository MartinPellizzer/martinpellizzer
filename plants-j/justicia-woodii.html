Justicia woodii

Justicia woodii, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular, red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Justicia woodii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.
