Juncus ratkowskyanus

Juncus ratkowskyanus, commonly known as the Ratkowsky's rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to moist conditions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spike-like clusters. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and colonize new areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Juncaceae, which includes other rush species known for their ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
