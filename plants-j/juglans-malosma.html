Juglans malosma

Juglans malosma, commonly known as the California black walnut, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the walnut family, Juglandaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and edible nuts. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by the development of its distinctive, thick-shelled nuts. Its bark is deeply furrowed and dark in color, providing a rugged appearance.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various wildlife species.
