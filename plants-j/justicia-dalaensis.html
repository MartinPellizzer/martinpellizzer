Justicia dalaensis

Justicia dalaensis, commonly known as the African violet or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to white. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with a dense layer of fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture.

The flowers are often clustered in spikes and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
