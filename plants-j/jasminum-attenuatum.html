Jasminum attenuatum

Jasminum attenuatum, commonly known as the slender jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a sweet, delicate aroma. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, featuring slender, twisting stems and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually borne in the axils of the leaves, and it thrives in well-drained soil with ample sunlight.

Jasminum attenuatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in perfumery due to its aromatic properties.
