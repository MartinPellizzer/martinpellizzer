Jatropha urens var. inermis

Jatropha urens var. inermis, commonly known as the softleaf spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces toxic, milky sap that can cause skin irritation, making it a cautionary species for handlers. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and alternate along the stem, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
