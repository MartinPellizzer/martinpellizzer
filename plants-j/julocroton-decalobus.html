Julocroton decalobus

Julocroton decalobus, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking plant characterized by its vivid orange to red bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers.

Native to the tropical regions of Africa, it thrives in warm, sunny environments with well-draining soil. The plant features a woody stem with a spiky appearance, and its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped. It produces clusters of small yellow flowers that are followed by bright red or orange seed pods.

Julocroton decalobus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be grown in gardens with adequate sunlight and drainage.
