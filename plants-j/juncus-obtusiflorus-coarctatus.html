Juncus obtusiflorus coarctatus

Juncus obtusiflorus coarctatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like, cylindrical stems that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered near the base of the stem. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
