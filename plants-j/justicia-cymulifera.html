Justicia cymulifera

Justicia cymulifera, commonly known as the clustered flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slight pubescence.

The flowers are clustered in cymes, which give the plant its scientific name, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
