Juncus macrandrus

Juncus macrandrus, commonly known as the tall rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically growing in dense clusters at the base.

Juncus macrandrus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
