Juncus aristulatus

Juncus aristulatus, commonly known as the arrowhead rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, forming dense stands that help stabilize shorelines. The plant has cylindrical, grass-like stems that are often green or reddish in color, and it produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to filter water.

Juncus aristulatus plays an important role in providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
