Justicia atropurpurea

Justicia atropurpurea, commonly known as flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or purple flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is known for its vibrant, showy blooms that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
