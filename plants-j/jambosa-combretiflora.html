Jambosa combretiflora

Jambosa combretiflora, commonly known as the African star apple, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by edible, star-shaped fruits. The fruit has a sweet, acidic flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in traditional remedies.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.
