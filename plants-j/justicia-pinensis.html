Justicia pinensis

Justicia pinensis, commonly known as the red torchflower, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that features showy, tubular red flowers clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds and bees, making it an important species in pollinator habitats.
