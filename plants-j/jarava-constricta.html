Jarava constricta

Jarava constricta, commonly known as narrowleaf jarava, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to brownish flowers.

Jarava constricta plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert wildlife.
