Jupunba villosa

Jupunba villosa, commonly known as the silky jupunba, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features soft, hairy foliage that gives it its common name, and it produces small yellow to orange flowers. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Jupunba villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by local communities.
