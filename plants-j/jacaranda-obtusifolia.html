Jacaranda obtusifolia

Jacaranda obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved jacaranda, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pendulous clusters of violet-blue flowers. The plant belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance and are pollinated primarily by birds and insects.
