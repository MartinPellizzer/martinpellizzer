Juniperus hemisphaerica

Juniperus hemisphaerica, commonly known as the spherical juniper, is a shrub-like plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical growth form, with tightly packed, scale-like leaves that give it a compact appearance. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It is well adapted to harsh, dry environments and can thrive in poor soils.

Juniperus hemisphaerica is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
