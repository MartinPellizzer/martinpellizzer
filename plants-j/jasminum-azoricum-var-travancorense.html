Jasminum azoricum var. travancorense

Jasminum azoricum var. travancorense, commonly known as the Travancore jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly serrated margin.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
