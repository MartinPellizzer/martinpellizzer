Juniperus conferta var. pseudorigida

Juniperus conferta var. pseudorigida, commonly known as the stiff juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are typically dark blue when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for landscaping in arid regions.
