Juncus trifidus var. vaginatus

Juncus trifidus var. vaginatus, commonly known as the three-lobed rush, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems that grow in dense clusters, often reaching heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are typically three-lobed, giving it the species name "trifidus," and they are often sheathed at the base, a feature reflected in the variety name "vaginatus.".

This plant plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
