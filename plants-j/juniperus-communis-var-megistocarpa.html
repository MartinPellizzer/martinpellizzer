Juniperus communis var. megistocarpa

Juniperus communis var. megistocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited juniper, is a variant of the common juniper native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and its distinctive, berry-like cones that are typically larger and more fleshy than those of the typical variety. The plant produces aromatic, resinous compounds that contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses. Its cones mature over a two-year cycle and are often used in herbal remedies and as a spice.

This variant thrives in well-drained soils and is adaptable to a range of climates, making it a resilient and widely distributed species.
