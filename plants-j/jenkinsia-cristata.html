Jenkinsia cristata

Jenkinsia cristata, commonly known as the crested jenkinsia, is a rare and unusual plant species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, branched inflorescence that resembles a crown, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, which bloom during the late spring and early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine, silvery fuzz.

Jenkinsia cristata is highly sought after by botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphology and limited natural habitat.
