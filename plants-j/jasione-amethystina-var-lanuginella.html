Jasione amethystina var. lanuginella

Jasione amethystina var. lanuginella, commonly known as the velvet bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet, often with a purplish tinge. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that give it a velvety texture, contributing to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
