Juglans pyriformis

Juglans pyriformis, commonly known as the Mexican walnut, is a species of flowering plant in the family Juglandaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its large, elongated, and often heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces distinctive, spiny husks that enclose its edible nuts. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils.

The Mexican walnut is valued for its hard wood and is also cultivated for its nuts, which are rich in nutrients.
