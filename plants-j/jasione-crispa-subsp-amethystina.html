Jasione crispa subsp. amethystina

Jasione crispa subsp.

amethystina, commonly known as the amethysty bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale purple to deep violet.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
