Justicia tribracteata

Justicia tribracteata, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
