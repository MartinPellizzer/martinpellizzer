Justicia kasamae

Justicia kasamae, commonly known as the Kasama flameflower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and bees. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, providing a textured appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The genus Justicia is part of the Acanthaceae family, known for its showy floral displays and medicinal properties in some species.
