Josephia foliolata

Josephia foliolata, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky soils and dry environments. The plant features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive red throat. Its leaves are small, oval, and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The plant is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
