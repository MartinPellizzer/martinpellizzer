Juncellus laevigatus

Juncellus laevigatus, commonly known as the smooth rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are smooth and often grow in dense clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences, typically blooming in early summer. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil in wetland habitats.

Juncellus laevigatus plays a role in providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
