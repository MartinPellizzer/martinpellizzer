Justicia inconspicua

Justicia inconspicua, commonly known as the common justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of blue or purple flowers. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to gardens.
