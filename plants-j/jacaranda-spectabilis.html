Jacaranda spectabilis

Jacaranda spectabilis, commonly known as the blue jacaranda, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in the spring. The tree has a spreading canopy with pinnate leaves that turn yellow in the autumn. Its wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for construction and furniture.

The plant is also cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy flowers and adaptability to various climates.
