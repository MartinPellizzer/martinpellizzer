Jussiaea angustifolia

Jussiaea angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf waterweed, is a flowering plant species native to wetland and aquatic environments in the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 1 cm wide, arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often floating at the water's surface. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats and is often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes.

Jussiaea angustifolia plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and contributing to water filtration.
