Jambosa mappacea

Jambosa mappacea, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its dark, almost black, edible berries. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet, tart flavor. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often covered in fine hairs.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.
