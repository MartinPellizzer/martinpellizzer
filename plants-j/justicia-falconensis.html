Justicia falconensis

Justicia falconensis, commonly known as the Falcon's Beak, is a striking flowering plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its showy blooms attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees, contributing to its ecological significance.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes.
