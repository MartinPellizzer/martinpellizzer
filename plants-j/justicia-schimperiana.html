Justicia schimperiana

Justicia schimperiana, commonly known as the flame flower, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant color make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
