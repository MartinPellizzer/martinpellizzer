Juncus phalereus

Juncus phalereus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It features tall, slender cylindrical stems that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters near the top of the stems, which are often surrounded by bracts. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, growing in dense clusters at the base.

Juncus phalereus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
