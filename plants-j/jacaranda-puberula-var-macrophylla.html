Jacaranda puberula var. macrophylla

Jacaranda puberula var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved jacaranda, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in the spring. The leaves are large, ovate, and covered in a soft, velvety pubescence. The tree has a spreading crown and can grow up to 20 meters tall.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates due to its showy flowers and distinctive foliage.
