Justicia clemensiorum

Justicia clemensiorum, commonly known as the red-tipped pigeon pea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking red bracts that surround clusters of small white flowers, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant produces small, oval pods containing lens-shaped seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing as a shrub or small tree.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically and culturally significant in its native habitats.
