Justicia chacoensis

Justicia chacoensis, commonly known as Chaco false arum, is a herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly found in the Chaco region of Paraguay and Argentina.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of bright red or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
