Julocroton subpannosus var. chacoensis

Julocroton subpannosus var. chacoensis, commonly known as the Chaco croton, is a shrub native to the dry regions of South America, particularly in Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, and its flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, typical of the genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Due to its hardy nature and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated as an ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical gardens.
