Jasminum lindleyanum

Jasminum lindleyanum, commonly known as the white jasmine or Lindley's jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant is a shrub or small tree, often reaching heights of up to 5 meters, with opposite, oval-shaped leaves. Its flowering stems are usually covered in fine hairs, and the flowers emit a strong, sweet scent that attracts pollinators.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
