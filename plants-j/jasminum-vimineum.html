Jasminum vimineum

Jasminum vimineum, commonly known as the climbing jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its twining stems and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually hairy leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing along fences or trellises.

Jasminum vimineum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in gardens and as a hedging plant.
