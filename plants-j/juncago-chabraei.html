Juncago chabraei

Juncago chabraei, commonly known as the Chabra's rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family, which includes various species of rushes and sedges. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along stream banks. Its slender, cylindrical stems are usually green or reddish in color and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Juncago chabraei produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated.
