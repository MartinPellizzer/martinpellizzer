Juncus kraussii var. parviflorus

Juncus kraussii var. parviflorus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rush, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, spike-like clusters of white or pale green flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as along riverbanks and in swampy areas. Its leaves are narrow and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist environments.
