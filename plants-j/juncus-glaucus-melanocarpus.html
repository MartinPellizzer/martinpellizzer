Juncus glaucus melanocarpus

Juncus glaucus melanocarpus, commonly known as the black fruit rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, dark purple to black berries that are distinctive in appearance. The plant thrives in moist to aquatic conditions, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its reproductive structures are adapted for water dispersal, aiding in its spread across wetland habitats.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
