Jasminum adenophyllum

Jasminum adenophyllum, commonly known as the white jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
