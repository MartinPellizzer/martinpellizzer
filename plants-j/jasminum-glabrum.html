Jasminum glabrum

Jasminum glabrum, commonly known as the white jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Jasminum glabrum is also used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
