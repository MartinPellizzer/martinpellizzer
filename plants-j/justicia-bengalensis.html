Justicia bengalensis

Justicia bengalensis, commonly known as the flame-of-the-forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is known for its showy, trumpet-shaped inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture, adding to its ornamental value.
