Jalapa longiflora

Jalapa longiflora, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, edible fruits that contain seeds.

Jalapa longiflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
