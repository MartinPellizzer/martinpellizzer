Jasminum rigidum

Jasminum rigidum, commonly known as the rigid jasmine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features woody stems that can twine around supports, and its leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, blooming in clusters. These flowers are often borne on short pedicels and emit a sweet, delicate scent.

Jasminum rigidum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and as a climbing plant in gardens.
