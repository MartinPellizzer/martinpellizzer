Juncus communis

Juncus communis, commonly known as common rush, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant has tall, cylindrical, grass-like leaves that are often bright green and densely packed. Its flowering stems produce small, spike-like clusters of white or greenish flowers.

Juncus communis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
