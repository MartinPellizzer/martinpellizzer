Justicia carnea

Justicia carnea, commonly known as red-veined hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with deep red veins against a dark green background. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Justicia carnea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
