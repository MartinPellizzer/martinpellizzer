Juniperus semiglobosa var. talassica

Juniperus semiglobosa var. talassica, commonly known as the Talassic juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that forms compact, spherical clusters. The leaves are typically needle-like, glossy, and dark green, with a strong aromatic scent. It produces small, blue-black berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

This plant is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.
