Jungia pubescens

Jungia pubescens, commonly known as the hairy jungle bean, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its pubescent (hairy) stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Jungia pubescens thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed habitats.
