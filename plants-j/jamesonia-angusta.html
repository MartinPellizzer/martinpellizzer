Jamesonia angusta

Jamesonia angusta, commonly known as the narrowleaf jamesonia, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, opposite, lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Jamesonia angusta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
