Jonquilla × incomparabilis

Jonquilla × incomparabilis, commonly known as the giant daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Jonquilla aurantiaca and Narcissus poeticus.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with bright yellow petals and a prominent, elongated trumpet-shaped corona. The plant is native to the Iberian Peninsula and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It is a perennial bulbous plant that produces multiple stems and flowers during the spring season.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant and striking appearance.
