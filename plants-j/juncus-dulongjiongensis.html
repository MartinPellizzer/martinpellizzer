Juncus dulongjiongensis

Juncus dulongjiongensis, commonly known as the Dulongjiang rush, is a flowering plant species native to China.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along riverbanks. It has fibrous roots that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
