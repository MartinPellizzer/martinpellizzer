Jacksonia rigida

Jacksonia rigida, commonly known as the rigid false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Jacksonia rigida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
