Justicia reptans

Justicia reptans, commonly known as creeping justicia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Justicia reptans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in water gardens or as a ground cover.
