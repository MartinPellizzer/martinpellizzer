Jussiaea subacaulis

Jussiaea subacaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water-mint, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and wetlands. The plant has slender, often floating stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its small, white or pale blue flowers are arranged in spikes and bloom during the summer months.

Jussiaea subacaulis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and amphibians.
