Jacksonia sternbergiana var. puberula

Jacksonia sternbergiana var. puberula, commonly known as the pubescent Jacksonia, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

The flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
