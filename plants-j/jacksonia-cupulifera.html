Jacksonia cupulifera

Jacksonia cupulifera, commonly known as the yellow daisy or cupulifera, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a compact, shrub-like habit. Its flowers bloom in spring and summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Jacksonia cupulifera is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
