Jasminum insigne var. sublanceolatum

Jasminum insigne var. sublanceolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its pleasant aroma and delicate appearance.
