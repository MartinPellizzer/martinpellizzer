Juncus arcticus subsp. alaskanus

Juncus arcticus subsp.

alaskanus, commonly known as the Alaska rush, is a perennial plant native to arctic and subarctic regions of North America. It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

This species thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils, often found in tundra and wetland environments. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps it survive in harsh, cold climates.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of Arctic ecosystems.
