Juniperus polycarpos var. turcomanica

Juniperus polycarpos var. turcomanica, commonly known as the Turkestan juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its scale-like leaves and produces small, berry-like cones that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and thriving in well-drained soils. It is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in its native habitat.

The species has limited distribution and is considered a rare and relict plant in its range.
