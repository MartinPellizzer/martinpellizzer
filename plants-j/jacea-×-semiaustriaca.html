Jacea × semiaustriaca

Jacea × semiaustriaca, commonly known as the hybrid bellflower, is a perennial plant that belongs to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with white or yellow throats. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.

Jacea × semiaustriaca is relatively easy to cultivate and is often used in mixed borders or as a cut flower.
