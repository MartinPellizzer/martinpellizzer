Julocroton lepidus

Julocroton lepidus, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Crotonaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, velvety foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are often lance-shaped and have a rough, textured surface.

Julocroton lepidus is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance.
