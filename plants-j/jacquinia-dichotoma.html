Jacquinia dichotoma

Jacquinia dichotoma, commonly known as the forked bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as cliffs and slopes. The plant features erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are blue to purple in color, with five petals arranged in a bell shape, and they bloom in late spring to early summer.

Jacquinia dichotoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
