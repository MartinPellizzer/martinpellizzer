Juniperus deppeana var. deppeana

Juniperus deppeana var. deppeana, commonly known as the Mexican redcedar, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, scaly foliage and small, berry-like cones that are typically red or orange in color. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. It has aromatic leaves that release a resinous scent when crushed.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
