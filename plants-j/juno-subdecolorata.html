Juno subdecolorata

Juno subdecolorata, commonly known as the yellow-flowered juniper, is a species of shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and clusters of yellowish-green berries. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Juno subdecolorata is part of the Cupressaceae family, which includes other juniper species.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
