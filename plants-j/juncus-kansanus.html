Juncus kansanus

Juncus kansanus, commonly known as the Kansas rush, is a perennial plant native to the central United States.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like clusters of cylindrical leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late spring to early summer, produce small, spike-like flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Juncus kansanus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
