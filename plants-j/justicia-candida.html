Justicia candida

Justicia candida, commonly known as the white-flowered false arum, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking white bracts that surround small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
