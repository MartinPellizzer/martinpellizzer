Juncus triglumis var. typicus

Juncus triglumis var. typicus, commonly known as the common rush, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are usually unbranched and covered in fine, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it produces dark brown, three-lobed seeds.

This species is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife.
