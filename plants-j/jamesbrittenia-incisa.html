Jamesbrittenia incisa

Jamesbrittenia incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed jamesbrittenia, is a member of the Proteaceae family native to South Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
