Juniperus depressa

Juniperus depressa, commonly known as the low juniper, is a shrub-like evergreen conifer native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions due to its small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are typically blue or black in color and contain toxic compounds. It is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ability to thrive in challenging environments.

Juniperus depressa plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert wildlife.
