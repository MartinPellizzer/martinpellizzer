Jacobaea vernalis

Jacobaea vernalis, commonly known as spring hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Jacobaea vernalis is often found in open habitats and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens.
