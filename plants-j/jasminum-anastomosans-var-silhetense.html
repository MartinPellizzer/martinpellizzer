Jasminum anastomosans var. silhetense

Jasminum anastomosans var. silhetense, commonly known as the Silhet jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Himalayas, particularly in Bangladesh and India.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a sweet, intoxicating aroma. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with opposite, lanceolate leaves. Its flowers are usually found at the ends of the branches, and it thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic properties.
