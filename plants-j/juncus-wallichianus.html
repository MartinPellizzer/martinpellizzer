Juncus wallichianus

Juncus wallichianus, commonly known as the Himalayan rush, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often used in floral arrangements. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a fluffy appearance that attracts pollinators.

Juncus wallichianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
