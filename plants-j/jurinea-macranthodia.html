Jurinea macranthodia

Jurinea macranthodia, commonly known as the silverweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Jurinea macranthodia is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and is a common species in desert ecosystems.
