Juncus subuliflorus f. laxus

Juncus subuliflorus f. laxus, commonly known as the loose-leaved rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Juncaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, needle-like leaves that are often arranged in a loose, open structure. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, stream banks, and damp woodlands. Its inflorescence consists of clusters of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
