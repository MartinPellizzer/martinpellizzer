Juniperus wittmanniana

Juniperus wittmanniana, commonly known as the Mexican redcedar, is a species of juniper native to Mexico.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has scale-like leaves that are closely appressed to the branches, and its cones are small and berry-like, containing toxic seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

The plant is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and adaptability.
