Jasminum auriculatum

Jasminum auriculatum, commonly known as the golden jasmine or auriculate jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, elliptic leaves with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually yellow or white, blooming in clusters during the summer months. Its flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.
