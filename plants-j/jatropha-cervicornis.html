Jatropha cervicornis

Jatropha cervicornis, commonly known as the horned morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain toxic seeds, which are a notable botanical feature. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Jatropha cervicornis is often found in disturbed habitats and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability.
