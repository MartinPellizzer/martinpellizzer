Juttadinteria decumbens

Juttadinteria decumbens, commonly known as the desert lily, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, sprawling stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that help it store water and survive in dry conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
