Justicia ianthina

Justicia ianthina, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, typically blooming in the spring and summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Justicia ianthina is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
