Jagera macrophylla

Jagera macrophylla, commonly known as the broad-leaved jagera, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

Jagera macrophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native habitats.
