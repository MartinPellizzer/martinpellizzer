Jumellea floribunda

Jumellea floribunda, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Jumellea floribunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
