Juncus plebeius

Juncus plebeius, commonly known as the common rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshes, stream banks, and pond edges. The plant has cylindrical, grass-like stems that are usually green or reddish in color. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are borne in dense, spike-like clusters at the top of the stems.

Juncus plebeius plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
