Jambosa pseudolaeta

Jambosa pseudolaeta, commonly known as the yellow jambosa, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to those of the common jambosa. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and research settings.
