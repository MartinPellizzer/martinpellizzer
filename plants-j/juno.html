Juno

Juno, commonly known as the Juniper plant, is a genus of evergreen shrubs belonging to the family Cupressaceae.

It is characterized by its scale-like leaves and small, blue or purple berries that are often used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine. Juno plants are native to the Northern Hemisphere and thrive in well-drained soils and full sun. They are widely cultivated for their ornamental value and for the essential oils extracted from their berries.

Juno has a long history of use in herbal remedies and is also an important component of many ecosystems.
