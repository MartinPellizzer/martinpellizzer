Justicia brachionodes

Justicia brachionodes, commonly known as the flame violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by birds, particularly hummingbirds, due to their bright colors and nectar-rich structure.
