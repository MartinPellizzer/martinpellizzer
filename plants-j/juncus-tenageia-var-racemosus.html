Juncus tenageia var. racemosus

Juncus tenageia var. racemosus, commonly known as the racemose rush, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and covered in fine, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale green flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the warmer months. It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as along riverbanks or in swampy areas.

Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil, making it ecologically significant in its native habitat.
