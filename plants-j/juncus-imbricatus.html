Juncus imbricatus

Juncus imbricatus, commonly known as the common rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments across the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves that form a rosette at the base. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes during the summer months. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, pond edges, and along streams.

Juncus imbricatus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
