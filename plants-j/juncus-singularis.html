Juncus singularis

Juncus singularis, commonly known as the slender rush, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of grass-like, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers that are typically arranged in spike-like clusters. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Juncus singularis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
