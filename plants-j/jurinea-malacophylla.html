Jurinea malacophylla

Jurinea malacophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and silver-gray foliage, which helps it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are succulent and covered in fine, silky hairs, which provide additional protection against desiccation.

Jurinea malacophylla is well adapted to sandy and rocky soils, making it a resilient species in semi-arid ecosystems.
