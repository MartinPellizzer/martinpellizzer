Juglans catappa

Juglans catappa, commonly known as the Indian walnut, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of South and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are alternate and pinnate, with 11 to 23 leaflets. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are either male or female, with the female flowers being solitary and clustered at the base of the leaf. Its fruit is a large, rough-shelled drupe that contains a single edible seed.

The tree is valued for its hard wood and is also cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
