Juncus supinus proles husnotii

Juncus supinus proles husnotii, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically less than 1 millimeter wide. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows.

This species is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils.
