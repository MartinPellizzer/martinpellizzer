Jacksonia sternbergiana f. alata

Jacksonia sternbergiana f. alata, commonly known as the slender flax or narrow-leaved flax, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in a cylindrical inflorescence, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
