Juncus fontanesii var. kotschyi

Juncus fontanesii var. kotschyi, commonly known as the Kotschyi rush, is a perennial plant native to parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often densely packed. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear greenish-yellow flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies.

Its fibrous roots help in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
