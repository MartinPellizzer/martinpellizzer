Juncus tenuis var. anthelatus

Juncus tenuis var. anthelatus, commonly known as the purple rush, is a variant of the common rush found in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems that often display a striking purple hue, especially along the leaf sheaths. This plant typically grows in moist soils, such as those found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, three-lobed flowers.

The botanical features of Juncus tenuis var. anthelatus make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting a variety of wildlife.
