Juncus marginatus

Juncus marginatus, commonly known as the marginspike rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish or brownish in color, with dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers at the tips. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a tight spiral around the stem. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in temperate regions.

Juncus marginatus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
