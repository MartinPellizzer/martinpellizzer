Jatus grandis

Jatus grandis, commonly known as the grand magnolia, is a large evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 meters, with a dense, rounded canopy. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with a distinctive, cup-shaped structure.

Jatus grandis prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical gardens.
