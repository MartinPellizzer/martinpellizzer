Justicia vasculosa

Justicia vasculosa, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped red flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a contrasting yellow center. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are usually hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Justicia vasculosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
