Jamesonia galeana

Jamesonia galeana, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a perennial shrub native to the highlands of Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, red-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
