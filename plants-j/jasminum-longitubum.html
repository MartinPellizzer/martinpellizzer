Jasminum longitubum

Jasminum longitubum, commonly known as the long-tubed jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. Its stems are often woody and can grow as shrubs or climbers.

The flowers are notable for their long, slender tubes, which are adapted for specific pollination by insects.
