Jatropha clavuligera subsp. clavuligera

Jatropha clavuligera subsp.

clavuligera, commonly known as the purple leaf jatropha, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking purple leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces capsule fruits containing toxic, black seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for medicinal use, it is cultivated in various regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
