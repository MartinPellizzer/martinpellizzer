Juncus mauritanicus

Juncus mauritanicus, commonly known as the Mauritanian rush, is a perennial plant native to North Africa and parts of the Middle East.

It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, stream banks, and coastal areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It is often used in landscaping for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
