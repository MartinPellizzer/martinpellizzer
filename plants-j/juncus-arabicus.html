Juncus arabicus

Juncus arabicus, commonly known as the Arabian rush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces numerous small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Juncus arabicus plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native environment.
