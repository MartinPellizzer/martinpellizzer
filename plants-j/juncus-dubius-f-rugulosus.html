Juncus dubius f. rugulosus

Juncus dubius f. rugulosus, commonly known as the rugose rush, is a variant of the common rush species.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves. The leaves are typically narrow and have a rough, textured surface, giving it its distinguishing name. It thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

This plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
