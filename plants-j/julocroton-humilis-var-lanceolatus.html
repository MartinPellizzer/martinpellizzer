Julocroton humilis var. lanceolatus

Julocroton humilis var. lanceolatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf croton, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features lanceolate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with prominent veins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
