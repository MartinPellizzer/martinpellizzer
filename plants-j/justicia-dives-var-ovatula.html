Justicia dives var. ovatula

Justicia dives var. ovatula, commonly known as the broadleaf flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a container plant.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds and bees, making it an important plant for attracting pollinators.
