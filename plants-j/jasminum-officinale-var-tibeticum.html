Jasminum officinale var. tibeticum

Jasminum officinale var. tibeticum, commonly known as the Tibetan jasmine, is a perennial shrub native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant typically grows in rocky and mountainous terrain, adapting to cold and arid conditions. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
