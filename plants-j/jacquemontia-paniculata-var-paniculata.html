Jacquemontia paniculata var. paniculata

Jacquemontia paniculata var. paniculata, commonly known as the yellow wood vine, is a climbing perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is characterized by its twining habit and can grow up to 15 feet in length. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
