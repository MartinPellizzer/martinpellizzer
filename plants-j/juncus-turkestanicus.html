Juncus turkestanicus

Juncus turkestanicus, commonly known as the Turkestan rush, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, riverbanks, and wet meadows. It produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into cylindrical, three-sided seed capsules.

Juncus turkestanicus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
