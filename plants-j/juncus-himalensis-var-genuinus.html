Juncus himalensis var. genuinus

Juncus himalensis var. genuinus, commonly known as the Himalayan rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the mountainous regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky or alpine habitats and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny greenish-white flowers.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
