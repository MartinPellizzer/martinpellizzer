Juniperus virginiana f. plumosa

Juniperus virginiana f. plumosa, commonly known as the silky juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-blue foliage, which is covered in fine, soft needles that give it a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in regions with suitable climates.
