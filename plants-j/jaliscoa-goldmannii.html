Jaliscoa goldmannii

Jaliscoa goldmannii, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to Mexico.

This plant is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters and are highly attractive to pollinators. It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its unique appearance.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
