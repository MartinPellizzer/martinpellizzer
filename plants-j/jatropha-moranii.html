Jatropha moranii

Jatropha moranii, commonly known as the Moran's physic nut, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that contain toxic seeds, which are not suitable for human consumption. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and savannas.

Due to its toxicity, it is important to handle the plant with care.
