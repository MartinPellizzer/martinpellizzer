Justicia buxifolia

Justicia buxifolia, commonly known as the waxleaf justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often mistaken for those of the boxwood plant. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to high light exposure.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
