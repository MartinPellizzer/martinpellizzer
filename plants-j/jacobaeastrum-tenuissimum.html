Jacobaeastrum tenuissimum

Jacobaeastrum tenuissimum, commonly known as slender burrweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical heads, typical of the genus Jacobaea. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and the plant often grows in dry, disturbed soils.

Jacobaeastrum tenuissimum is known for its persistent, spiny fruits that can adhere to animals and clothing, aiding in seed dispersal.
