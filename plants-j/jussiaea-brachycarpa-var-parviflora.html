Jussiaea brachycarpa var. parviflora

Jussiaea brachycarpa var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered jussiaea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a glossy surface, while its fruit is a small, capsule-like structure.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in aquatic gardens.
