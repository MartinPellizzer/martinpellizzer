Juncus papillosus

Juncus papillosus, commonly known as the silky rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features grass-like, cylindrical stems covered in soft, silky hairs, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense stands in marshes and pond edges. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in spike-like clusters, and it produces lightweight seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Juncus papillosus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
