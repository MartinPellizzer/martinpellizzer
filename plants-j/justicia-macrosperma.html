Justicia macrosperma

Justicia macrosperma, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small white to yellowish flowers in clusters, often appearing in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Justicia macrosperma is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
