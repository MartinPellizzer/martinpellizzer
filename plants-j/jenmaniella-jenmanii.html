Jenmaniella jenmanii

Jenmaniella jenmanii, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a rare and unique species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, vividly colored flowers that range from deep red to orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its small, lance-shaped leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces intricate, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
