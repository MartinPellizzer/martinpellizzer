Jambosa brevicyma

Jambosa brevicyma, commonly known as the short-stemmed jambosa, is a tropical shrub native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in humid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible fruits resembling small, round berries. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to waterlogged soils, allowing it to thrive in marshy areas.

Jambosa brevicyma is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
