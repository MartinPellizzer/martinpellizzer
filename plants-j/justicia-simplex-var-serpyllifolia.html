Justicia simplex var. serpyllifolia

Justicia simplex var. serpyllifolia, commonly known as silverleaf false heather, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, silver-gray leaves that are densely arranged along the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its foliage and floral structure are adapted to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
