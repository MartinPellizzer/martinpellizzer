Jacea alata

Jacea alata, commonly known as the winged bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive winged stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Jacea alata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
