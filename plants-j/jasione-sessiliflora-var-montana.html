Jasione sessiliflora var. montana

Jasione sessiliflora var. montana, commonly known as mountain jasione, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features basal rosettes of lanceolate, glaucous leaves and produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers in the summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
