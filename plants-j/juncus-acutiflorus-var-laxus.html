Juncus acutiflorus var. laxus

Juncus acutiflorus var. laxus, commonly known as the slender rush, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features slender, grass-like stems that grow in dense clusters, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its roots help stabilize soil in marshy areas, making it ecologically important for wetland ecosystems.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and resilience in waterlogged conditions.
