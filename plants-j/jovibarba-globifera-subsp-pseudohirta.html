Jovibarba globifera subsp. pseudohirta

Jovibarba globifera subsp.

pseudohirta, commonly known as the woolly cushion plant, is a succulent species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and thick, fleshy leaves covered in a fine, woolly coating.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months, which are borne on short pedicels. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in cold climates, though it is sensitive to prolonged damp conditions.
