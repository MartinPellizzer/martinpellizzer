Juniperus nipponica

Juniperus nipponica, commonly known as Japanese juniper, is a species of evergreen conifer native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage and small, berry-like cones that mature to a blue-black color. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. It has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters.

Juniperus nipponica is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also valued for its aromatic wood.
