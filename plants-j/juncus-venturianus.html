Juncus venturianus

Juncus venturianus, commonly known as the Venturi's rush, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in clusters at the top of the stems. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its adaptability, it plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
