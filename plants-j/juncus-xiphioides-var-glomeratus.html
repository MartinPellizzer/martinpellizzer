Juncus xiphioides var. glomeratus

Juncus xiphioides var. glomeratus, commonly known as the clustered rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikes, which are often clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, stream banks, and pond edges.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil and contributes to wetland ecosystems.
