Jatropha fragrans

Jatropha fragrans, commonly known as the sweet-smelling physic nut, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a reddish or purple hue. The plant produces capsule fruits containing toxic seeds, which are not suitable for human consumption. Its leaves are alternate and simple, typically with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Jatropha fragrans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been studied for potential use in biofuel production.
