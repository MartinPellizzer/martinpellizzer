Jacksonia rubra

Jacksonia rubra, commonly known as the red kowhai, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its bright red flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Jacksonia rubra thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
