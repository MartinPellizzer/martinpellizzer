Justicia hookeriana

Justicia hookeriana, commonly known as the Hooker's flame flower, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts in its native habitat.
