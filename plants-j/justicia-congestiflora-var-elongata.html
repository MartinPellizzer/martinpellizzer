Justicia congestiflora var. elongata

Justicia congestiflora var. elongata, commonly known as the elongated flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in dense clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped, opposite leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in tropical and subtropical regions.
