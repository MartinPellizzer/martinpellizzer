Jacksonia horrida

Jacksonia horrida, commonly known as the spiked everlasting or the desert everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, yellow to orange flower heads that bloom in spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Jacksonia horrida is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting flowers and striking appearance.
