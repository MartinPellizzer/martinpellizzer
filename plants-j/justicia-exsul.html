Justicia exsul

Justicia exsul, commonly known as the wandering jewel, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking spikes of tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Justicia exsul is popular in gardens for its vibrant blooms and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.
