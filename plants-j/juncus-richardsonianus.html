Juncus richardsonianus

Juncus richardsonianus, commonly known as the Richard's rush, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in marshy areas and along the edges of ponds and lakes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of white or greenish flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in regions with high groundwater tables.

Juncus richardsonianus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
