Justicia carthagenensis

Justicia carthagenensis, commonly known as the flame bush, is a shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

The genus Justicia belongs to the Acanthaceae family, known for its showy flowers and ornamental value.
