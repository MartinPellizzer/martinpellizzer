Jussiaea sericea var. villosissima

Jussiaea sericea var. villosissima, commonly known as the silky waterplantain, is a flowering plant species native to wetland environments in South America.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in shallow, freshwater habitats such as marshes and pond margins.

Its hairy foliage and adaptability to aquatic conditions make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
