Justicia nuttii

Justicia nuttii, commonly known as red flame or Mexican flamebush, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in clusters, which are typically red or orange in color, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its botanical characteristics include small, opposite leaves and a flowering period that occurs throughout the growing season.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
