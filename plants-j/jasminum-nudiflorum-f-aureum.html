Jasminum nudiflorum f. aureum

Jasminum nudiflorum f. aureum, commonly known as the golden jasminum, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a cultivar of the common jasminum, characterized by its striking yellow flowers instead of the typical white or pale yellow. The plant is a deciduous shrub that produces fragrant blooms in early spring. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a smooth texture.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant color and pleasant scent.
