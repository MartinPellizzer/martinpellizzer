Juncus amplifolius

Juncus amplifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a layer of fine, white hairs. Its leaves are broad and flat, with a grass-like appearance, and they are arranged in dense, tufted clusters at the base.

Juncus amplifolius plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
