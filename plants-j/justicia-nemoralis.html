Justicia nemoralis

Justicia nemoralis, commonly known as the wood's spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers with two lips, typical of the Acanthaceae family. Its flowering period typically occurs in the spring and early summer.

Justicia nemoralis is often found in moist, wooded areas and can spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent groundcover.
