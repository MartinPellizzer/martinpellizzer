Justicia heterotricha

Justicia heterotricha, commonly known as the velvetleaf justicia, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the spring and summer. Its flowering stems are often branched and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

Justicia heterotricha is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
