Juncus thompsonianus

Juncus thompsonianus, commonly known as the Thompson's rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and pond edges. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish-brown in color and lack leaves, with small, spike-like inflorescences at the top. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow and are arranged in a dense, narrow cluster.

Juncus thompsonianus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
