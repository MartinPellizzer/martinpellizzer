Juncus austrobrasiliensis

Juncus austrobrasiliensis, commonly known as the southern Brazilian rush, is a perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems that often grow in dense clusters. The plant typically thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it forms dense vegetation. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late summer.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
