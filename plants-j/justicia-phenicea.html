Justicia phenicea

Justicia phenicea, commonly known as the flame flower, is a striking tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Justicia phenicea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
