Justicia straminea

Justicia straminea, commonly known as the silverleaf false arum, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It features vibrant, showy flowers that are typically bright red or orange with a prominent white spathe. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant can grow as a shrub or small tree, depending on the conditions.
