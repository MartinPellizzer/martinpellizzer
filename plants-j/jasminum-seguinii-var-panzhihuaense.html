Jasminum seguinii var. panzhihuaense

Jasminum seguinii var. panzhihuaense, commonly known as the Panzhihua jasmine, is a rare and endemic species native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. The plant has slender, woody stems and oval-shaped leaves that are dark green in color. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a valuable species in botanical research and conservation efforts.
