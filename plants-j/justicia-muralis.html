Justicia muralis

Justicia muralis, commonly known as the wallflower or blue mist shrub, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring dense, upright foliage. The plant produces showy clusters of blue or violet flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Justicia muralis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun.
