Jasminum pilosicalyx

Jasminum pilosicalyx, commonly known as the hairy jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has slender, woody stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Jasminum pilosicalyx is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
