Justicia glabra var. pubescens

Justicia glabra var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and clusters of bright orange to red tubular flowers. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and slightly pubescent, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and butterflies.
