Jambolifera imperforata

Jambolifera imperforata, commonly known as the Indian date palm, is a flowering plant native to southern India and Sri Lanka.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible fruits resembling dates. The fruit has a sweet, chewy texture and is often consumed fresh or processed into various food products.

It plays an important role in both local ecosystems and human agriculture.
