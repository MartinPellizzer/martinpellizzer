Justicia clinopodia

Justicia clinopodia, commonly known as narrowleaf false dragonhead, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of tubular, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
