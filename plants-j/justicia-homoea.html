Justicia homoea

Justicia homoea, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun. The plant features slender, upright stems and produces clusters of small, sky-blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Justicia homoea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
