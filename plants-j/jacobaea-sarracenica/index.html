Jacobaea Sarracenica

Jacobaea sarracenica is a striking flowering plant known for its vibrant yellow blooms and distinctive, spiky appearance. Native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. This plant is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas, showcasing its adaptability to various environments. Its common name, "Giant Ragwort," reflects both its size and its membership in the ragwort family. Despite its beauty, it is important to note that it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
