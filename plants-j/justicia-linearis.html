Justicia linearis

Justicia linearis, commonly known as the narrowleaf flamebeard, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking spikes of tubular, brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Justicia linearis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
