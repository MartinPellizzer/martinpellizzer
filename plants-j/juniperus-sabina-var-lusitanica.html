Juniperus sabina var. lusitanica

Juniperus sabina var. lusitanica, commonly known as the Portuguese juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are toxic to humans and livestock. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
