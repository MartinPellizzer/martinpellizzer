Justicia chaetocephala

Justicia chaetocephala, commonly known as the hairy-headed dragonhead, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple tubular flowers. The plant features dense, hairy foliage that gives it its common name, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
