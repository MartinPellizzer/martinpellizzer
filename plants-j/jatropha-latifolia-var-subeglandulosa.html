Jatropha latifolia var. subeglandulosa

Jatropha latifolia var. subeglandulosa, commonly known as the broadleaf jatropha, is a variant of the jatropha genus native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers that develop into orange-red berries. The plant is typically shrub-like, with stout stems and can grow up to several meters in height. Its foliage and floral structure are adapted to thrive in humid, sunny environments.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has potential uses in traditional medicine.
