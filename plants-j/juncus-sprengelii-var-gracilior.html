Juncus sprengelii var. gracilior

Juncus sprengelii var. gracilior, commonly known as the slender rush, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color, emerging from the upper portions of the stems. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows.

This variant is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its elegant appearance and adaptability to various water conditions.
