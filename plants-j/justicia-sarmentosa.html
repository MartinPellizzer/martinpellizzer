Justicia sarmentosa

Justicia sarmentosa, commonly known as the creeping justicia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Acanthaceae family, characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces clusters of bright blue or purple flowers, which are often visited by bees and other pollinators. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soil.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to spread vigorously.
