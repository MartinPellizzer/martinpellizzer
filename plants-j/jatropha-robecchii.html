Jatropha robecchii

Jatropha robecchii, commonly known as the white jatropha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces toxic, orange-red berries that contain seeds used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats.
