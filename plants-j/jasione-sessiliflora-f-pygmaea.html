Jasione sessiliflora f. pygmaea

Jasione sessiliflora f. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf jasione, is a compact perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, delicate flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple, typically in early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are rounded and hairy, with stems that are short and often branching. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a species of interest in botanical gardens and its adaptation to Mediterranean climates.
