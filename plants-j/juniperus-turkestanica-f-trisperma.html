Juniperus turkestanica f. trisperma

Juniperus turkestanica f. trisperma, commonly known as the Turkestan juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces blue-black berries that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
