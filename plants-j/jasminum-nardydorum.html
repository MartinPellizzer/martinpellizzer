Jasminum nardydorum

Jasminum nardydorum, commonly known as the narrowleaf jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often releasing a sweet scent during the night. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
