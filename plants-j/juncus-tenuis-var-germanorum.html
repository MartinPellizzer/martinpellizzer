Juncus tenuis var. germanorum

Juncus tenuis var. germanorum, commonly known as the slender rush, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and damp woodlands. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively and colonize new areas.

The flowers are usually greenish or brownish in color and are wind-pollinated, contributing to its ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
