Justicia clauda

Justicia clauda, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring lance-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Justicia clauda is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to provide habitat for wildlife.
