Jatropha aconitifolia var. multipartita

Jatropha aconitifolia var. multipartita, commonly known as the multibract jatropha, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with multiple leaflets and striking, showy flowers that range from yellow to red. The plant produces capsule fruits containing toxic seeds, which are a key feature in its botanical classification. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various tropical environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for biofuel production, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
