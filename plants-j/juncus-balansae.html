Juncus balansae

Juncus balansae, commonly known as the Balansae rush, is a perennial plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and slender, cylindrical stems that often grow in clusters. The plant typically thrives in moist or aquatic environments, such as marshes and shallow water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and cylindrical, with a smooth surface, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters near the base of the stems.

Juncus balansae plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
