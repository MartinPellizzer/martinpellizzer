Juncus laeviusculus

Juncus laeviusculus, commonly known as slender rush, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant has grass-like, cylindrical stems that are usually hairless or only slightly hairy. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters and are typically greenish or brownish in color.

Juncus laeviusculus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
