Juncus capitatus versicolor

Juncus capitatus versicolor, commonly known as the variable rush, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with slender, cylindrical stems. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as along the edges of ponds and streams. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, often with a distinctive color variation.

The species is noted for its adaptability to different soil conditions and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
