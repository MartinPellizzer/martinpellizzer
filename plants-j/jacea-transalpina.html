Jacea transalpina

Jacea transalpina, commonly known as the Transalpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Jacea transalpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
