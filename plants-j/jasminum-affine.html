Jasminum affine

Jasminum affine, commonly known as the Japanese jasmine, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, usually during the spring and summer seasons. Its flowers are followed by small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

Jasminum affine is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its pleasant scent and aesthetic appeal.
