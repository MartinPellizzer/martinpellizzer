Juncus epimetrocarpus

Juncus epimetrocarpus, commonly known as the tall rush, is a perennial plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes, which are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, stream banks, and marshy areas.

Juncus epimetrocarpus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
