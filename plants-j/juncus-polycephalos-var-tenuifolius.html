Juncus polycephalos var. tenuifolius

Juncus polycephalos var. tenuifolius, commonly known as narrowleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish or greenish in color, growing in dense clusters. The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and typically have a slightly hairy texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters at the ends of the stems, which are often surrounded by bracts.

This plant is adapted to moist or aquatic conditions and is often found in marshes, pond edges, and along streams.
