Jurinea pumila var. major

Jurinea pumila var. major, commonly known as the dwarf sea bladder, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant found in coastal environments.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand harsh maritime conditions. The plant features small, fleshy leaves that help retain moisture in arid conditions. Its flowers are typically pale blue or white, and it blooms during the summer months.

This variety is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is often found in intertidal zones along the Pacific coast.
