Jasione humilis subsp. sessiliflora

Jasione humilis subsp.

sessiliflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved jasmine weed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features basal leaves that are lanceolate and often hairy, with small, yellowish flowers clustered in loose inflorescences.

The plant typically blooms from spring to early summer, producing numerous tiny flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable component of Mediterranean flora and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
