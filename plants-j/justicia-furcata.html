Justicia furcata

Justicia furcata, commonly known as the flame flower or red flame bush, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, often with contrasting yellow markings. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.

Justicia furcata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds.
