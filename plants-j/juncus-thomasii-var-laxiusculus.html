Juncus thomasii var. laxiusculus

Juncus thomasii var. laxiusculus, commonly known as the lax flower rush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its tall, grass-like stems and small, clustered spikelets. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its foliage is usually narrow and cylindrical, with a bluish-green color.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
