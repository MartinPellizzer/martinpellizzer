Jacquemontia pycnocephala

Jacquemontia pycnocephala, commonly known as the thick-headed morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with distinctive, showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
