Jacquemontia secundiflora

Jacquemontia secundiflora, commonly known as the showy golden vine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its trailing habit and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a vibrant, flowering vine.
