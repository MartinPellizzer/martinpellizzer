Justicia pusilla

Justicia pusilla, commonly known as dwarf flame flower, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bright red or orange flowers that attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for container gardening and rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, Justicia pusilla is widely cultivated in warm climates.
