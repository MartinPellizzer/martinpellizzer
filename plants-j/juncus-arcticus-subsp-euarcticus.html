Juncus arcticus subsp. euarcticus

Juncus arcticus subsp.

euarcticus, commonly known as the Arctic rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arctic and alpine regions. It typically grows in moist, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold climates.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish flowers in spike-like clusters. It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its harsh environment and is often found in tundra and mountainous areas.

Its botanical features reflect its evolutionary adaptation to extreme environmental conditions.
