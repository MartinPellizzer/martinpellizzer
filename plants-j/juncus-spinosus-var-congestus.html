Juncus spinosus var. congestus

Juncus spinosus var. congestus, commonly known as the spiny rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in spines, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers that appear at the tips of the stems, typically in late summer. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
