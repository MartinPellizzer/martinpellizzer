Jasminum seguinii

Jasminum seguinii, commonly known as the Seguin's jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the summer months. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and ovate, with a glossy green surface.

Jasminum seguinii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
