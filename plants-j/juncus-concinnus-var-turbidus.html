Juncus concinnus var. turbidus

Juncus concinnus var. turbidus, commonly known as the turbid rush, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves that grow from a rhizomatous root system. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to aquatic habitats, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
