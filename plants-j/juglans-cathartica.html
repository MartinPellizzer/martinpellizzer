Juglans cathartica

Juglans cathartica, commonly known as the white walnut, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the walnut family, Juglandaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and spherical, greenish-gray fruits. The tree produces edible seeds enclosed in a hard, woody shell, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

The white walnut is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in woodworking.
