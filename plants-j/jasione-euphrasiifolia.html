Jasione euphrasiifolia

Jasione euphrasiifolia, commonly known as the European bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, with a height typically reaching up to 30 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple bell-shaped flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Jasione euphrasiifolia is often found in grasslands, woodland edges, and rocky habitats.
