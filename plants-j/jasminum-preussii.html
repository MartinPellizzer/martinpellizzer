Jasminum preussii

Jasminum preussii, commonly known as the Persian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Jasminum preussii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its pleasant, sweet fragrance.
