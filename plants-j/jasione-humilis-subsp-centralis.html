Jasione humilis subsp. centralis

Jasione humilis subsp.

centralis, commonly known as the Central European bellflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to central Europe. It typically reaches a height of about 10-20 cm, featuring slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often found in grassy areas, rocky soils, and along woodland edges.
