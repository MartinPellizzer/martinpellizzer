Juglans indochinensis

Juglans indochinensis, commonly known as the Indochina walnut, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Juglandaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, edible nuts. The tree typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates, often in moist, well-drained soils. Its bark is smooth and grayish, while its fruits are encased in a thick, husky outer layer.

The plant is valued for its timber and nuts, which are used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
