Jacobaea renardii

Jacobaea renardii, commonly known as the Renard's ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by dark, globe-shaped seed heads.

Jacobaea renardii is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
