Jasminum griffithii

Jasminum griffithii, commonly known as the Himalayan jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of northern India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a smooth, dark green surface.

Jasminum griffithii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its pleasant aroma.
