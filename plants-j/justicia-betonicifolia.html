Justicia betonicifolia

Justicia betonicifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of tubular, blue or purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and adaptable plant for both landscaping and cultivation.
