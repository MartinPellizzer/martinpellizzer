Jasione perennis var. prostrata

Jasione perennis var. prostrata, commonly known as creeping bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has prostrate stems that spread along the ground, forming a dense mat. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas.
