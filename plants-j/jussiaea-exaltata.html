Jussiaea exaltata

Jussiaea exaltata, commonly known as the slender water-milfoil, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming during the spring and summer months. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common species in marshes and pond margins.

Jussiaea exaltata plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
