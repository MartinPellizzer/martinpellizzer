Juniperus oxycedrus subsp. deltoides

Juniperus oxycedrus subsp.

deltoides, commonly known as the Canary Island juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of scale-like leaves.

The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are edible but have a sharp, resinous flavor. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the subtropical climate of its native habitat.

This subspecies is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
