Jatropha zeyheri

Jatropha zeyheri, commonly known as the Zeyher's physic nut, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers that develop into capsule fruits. The plant contains toxic compounds, particularly in its seeds and stems, which can be harmful if ingested. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Due to its toxicity, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine but requires careful handling.
