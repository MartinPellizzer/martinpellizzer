Jussiaea epilobioides

Jussiaea epilobioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved willow-herb, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and floodplains. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or red, appearing in late summer to early autumn.

Jussiaea epilobioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils.
