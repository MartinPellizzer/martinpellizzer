Jambosa firma

Jambosa firma, commonly known as the African blackwood, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and glossy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are similar in appearance to cherries. Jambosa firma is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its fruit, which is used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, sunny climate.
