Jungia formosa

Jungia formosa, commonly known as the Chinese hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from red to pink and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Jungia formosa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
