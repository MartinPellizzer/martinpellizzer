Jasminum grandiflorum subsp. floribundum

Jasminum grandiflorum subsp.

floribundum, commonly known as the common jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its fragrant, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer.

The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 to 2 meters, with opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. Its flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is well-suited for gardens and can be used in hedges or as a climbing plant with proper support.
