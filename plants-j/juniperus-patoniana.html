Juniperus patoniana

Juniperus patoniana, commonly known as the Paton's juniper, is a species of evergreen conifer native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Juniperus patoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
