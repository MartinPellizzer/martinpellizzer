Jumellea brachycentra

Jumellea brachycentra, commonly known as the narrow-leafed jumellea, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and compact growth habit, which help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It is well adapted to sandy or rocky soils and can tolerate drought conditions.

Jumellea brachycentra is often found in fynbos vegetation, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
