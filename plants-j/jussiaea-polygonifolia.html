Jussiaea polygonifolia

Jussiaea polygonifolia, commonly known as the marsh willow or water willow, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in South America.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes and along riverbanks. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth surface and a distinct midrib.

Jussiaea polygonifolia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
