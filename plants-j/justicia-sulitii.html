Justicia sulitii

Justicia sulitii, commonly known as the Cape rose, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are borne in spikes and bloom during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
