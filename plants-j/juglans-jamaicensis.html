Juglans jamaicensis

Juglans jamaicensis, commonly known as the Jamaican hickory, is a deciduous tree native to the Caribbean islands, particularly Jamaica and Cuba.

It belongs to the walnut family, Juglandaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and edible nuts. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, rounded crown. Its bark is smooth and grayish, while its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, typically with 11 to 23 leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers and is known for its hard, heavy wood used in construction and tool-making.
