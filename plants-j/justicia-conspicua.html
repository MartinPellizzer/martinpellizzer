Justicia conspicua

Justicia conspicua, commonly known as the showy justicia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tubular, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

The flowers bloom throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
