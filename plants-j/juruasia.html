Juruasia

Juruasia, commonly known as the "ghost flower," is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the dense rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that emit a faint bioluminescent glow at night, a unique adaptation that may aid in attracting nocturnal pollinators. The plant's flowers are small, delicate, and typically appear in clusters, often resembling ghostly apparitions in the dim forest undergrowth. Juruasia thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific soil conditions to flourish.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered a conservation priority in its native habitat.
