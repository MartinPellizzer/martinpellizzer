Justicia eylesii

Justicia eylesii, commonly known as the Eyles' flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble flames. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tubular, brightly colored flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near water sources.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
