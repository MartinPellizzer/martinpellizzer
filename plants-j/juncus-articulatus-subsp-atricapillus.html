Juncus articulatus subsp. atricapillus

Juncus articulatus subsp.

atricapillus, commonly known as the black-tipped rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems that are typically green to reddish in color, with distinctive black-tipped nodes.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes, which are usually greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, pond edges, and along stream banks.

This species plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
