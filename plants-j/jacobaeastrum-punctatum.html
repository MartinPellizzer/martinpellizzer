Jacobaeastrum punctatum

Jacobaeastrum punctatum, commonly known as the spotted coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Jacobaeastrum punctatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
