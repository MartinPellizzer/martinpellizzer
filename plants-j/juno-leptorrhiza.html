Juno leptorrhiza

Juno leptorrhiza, commonly known as the slender root junco, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces clusters of delicate, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Juno leptorrhiza thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests or rocky crevices.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
