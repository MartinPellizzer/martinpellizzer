Juncus lechleri

Juncus lechleri, commonly known as sangre de drago, is a perennial shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its dark red to black bark, which contains high concentrations of balms and resins. The plant is often found growing in moist, shaded areas along riverbanks and in dense forest understories. Its botanical features include cylindrical, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used by indigenous communities for centuries.
