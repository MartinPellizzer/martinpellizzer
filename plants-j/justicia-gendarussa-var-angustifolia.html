Justicia gendarussa var. angustifolia

Justicia gendarussa var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
