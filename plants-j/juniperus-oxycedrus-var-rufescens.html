Juniperus oxycedrus var. rufescens

Juniperus oxycedrus var. rufescens, commonly known as the rough juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage, which is typically blue-green in color and covered with a powdery bloom. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or coastal environments.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for horticultural use.
