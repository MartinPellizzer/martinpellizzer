Jurinea arachnoidea

Jurinea arachnoidea, commonly known as the spider bladderplant, is a flowering plant native to the cold, arid regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its unique, inflated, bladder-like structures that encase its small flowers. These structures, which resemble spider webs, help protect the plant's reproductive organs from harsh environmental conditions. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in low-nutrient, high-altitude environments.

Its distinctive morphology and ecological adaptations make it a fascinating subject in botanical studies.
