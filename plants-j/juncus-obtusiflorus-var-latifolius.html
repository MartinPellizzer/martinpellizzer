Juncus obtusiflorus var. latifolius

Juncus obtusiflorus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with broad, flat leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale green flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
