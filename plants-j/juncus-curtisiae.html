Juncus curtisiae

Juncus curtisiae, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in moist or wet environments such as marshes and stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 1 mm wide, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Juncus curtisiae is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
