Jussiaea myrtifolia f. brevifolia

Jussiaea myrtifolia f. brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved myrtleleaf jussiaea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes many aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and tea trees. The species is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, clustered in small groups, and are borne on short, hairy pedicels.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and subtle floral display.
