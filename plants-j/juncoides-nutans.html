Juncoides nutans

Juncoides nutans, commonly known as the drooping rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems that often bend downward, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it produces small, dark brown seeds.

Juncoides nutans plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
