Justicia richardii

Justicia richardii, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds, which are attracted to its bright colors and nectar-rich blooms.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant display.
