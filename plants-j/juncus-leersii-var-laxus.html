Juncus leersii var. laxus

Juncus leersii var. laxus, commonly known as the slender rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Juncaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, typically found at the tips of the stems. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil in wetland habitats, making it an ecologically important species.
