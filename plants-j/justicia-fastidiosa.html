Justicia fastidiosa

Justicia fastidiosa, commonly known as the flame flower, is a striking tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its showy blooms and adaptability, it is a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in tropical and subtropical regions.
