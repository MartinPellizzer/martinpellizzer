Juncus glaucus var. yokoscensis

Juncus glaucus var. yokoscensis, commonly known as the Yokosuka rush, is a perennial plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like foliage that grow from rhizomes. The plant typically reaches heights of 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers moist, well-drained soils in shaded or semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, while its flowering spikes are usually pale green or white.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
