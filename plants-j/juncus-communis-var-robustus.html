Juncus communis var. robustus

Juncus communis var. robustus, commonly known as the common rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that are typically green to reddish in color, often growing up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, which are usually greenish or brownish in hue. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming dense stands that help stabilize soil in aquatic habitats.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in filtering water and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
