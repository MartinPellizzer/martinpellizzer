Jatropha urens var. genuina

Jatropha urens var. genuina, commonly known as the Mexican cup plant, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 1 meter in height, featuring lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain toxic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
