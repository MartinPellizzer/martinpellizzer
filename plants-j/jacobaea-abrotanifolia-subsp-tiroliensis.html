Jacobaea abrotanifolia subsp. tiroliensis

Jacobaea abrotanifolia subsp.

tiroliensis, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is a perennial herb with a rosette of basal leaves and stems that bear yellow composite flowers.

The plant has finely divided, silvery-green leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
