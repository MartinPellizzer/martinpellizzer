Juncus leersii var. subuliflorus

Juncus leersii var. subuliflorus, commonly known as the slender flowering rush, is a grass-like plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikes of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist soils, often in marshes or along the edges of ponds and streams. It is a perennial species that forms dense clumps, contributing to erosion control in wetland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
