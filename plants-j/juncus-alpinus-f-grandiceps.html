Juncus alpinus f. grandiceps

Juncus alpinus f. grandiceps, commonly known as the alpine rush, is a variant of the alpine rush species native to mountainous regions.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are narrow, stiff, and often have a bluish-green hue.

It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered along the central axis of the plant.
