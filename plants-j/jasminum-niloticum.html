Jasminum niloticum

Jasminum niloticum, commonly known as the Egyptian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Africa and the Middle East.

It produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often releasing a strong, sweet scent at night. The plant is a shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

Jasminum niloticum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
