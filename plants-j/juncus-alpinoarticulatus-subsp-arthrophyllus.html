Juncus alpinoarticulatus subsp. arthrophyllus

Juncus alpinoarticulatus subsp.

arthrophyllus, commonly known as the alpine rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, spike-like flower heads.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in alpine tundra and mountainous environments. It is adapted to cold climates and can withstand harsh winter conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine ecosystems.
