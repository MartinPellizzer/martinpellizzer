Jatropha scaposa

Jatropha scaposa, commonly known as the scarlet jatropha, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often lance-shaped or ovate. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are covered in a sticky substance.

Jatropha scaposa is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant floral display.
