Jatropha porrecta

Jatropha porrecta, commonly known as the creeping jatropha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub that produces yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red berries. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and milky sap, which can be toxic. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Jatropha porrecta is also sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
