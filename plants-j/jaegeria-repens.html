Jaegeria repens

Jaegeria repens, commonly known as creeping jaegeria, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, branching stems that spread along the ground, often rooting at the nodes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Jaegeria repens is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
