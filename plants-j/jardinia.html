Jardinia

Jardinia, commonly known as the flame flower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Melastomataceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, South America, and Southeast Asia. The plants are characterized by their showy, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Jardinia species typically have opposite, ovate leaves and produce small, capsule-like fruits.

They are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
