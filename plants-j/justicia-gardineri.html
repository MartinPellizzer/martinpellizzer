Justicia gardineri

Justicia gardineri, commonly known as the desert jeweled flower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with minimal water. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Its unique appearance and ability to thrive in harsh conditions make it a notable species in xerophytic ecosystems.
