Juncus alpinus f. giganteus

Juncus alpinus f. giganteus, commonly known as the giant alpine rush, is a striking plant native to high-altitude regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, making it one of the tallest members of the Juncaceae family. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for alpine gardens and ecological studies.
