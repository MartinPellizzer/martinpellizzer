Jurinea Mollis Subsp. Moschata

Jurinea mollis subsp. moschata is a rare and resilient plant native to the coastal regions of the Mediterranean. It thrives in harsh, saline environments where few other species can survive. This subspecies is distinguished by its compact growth and aromatic, fuzzy leaves that emit a subtle scent. Its flowers, though small, are often pale yellow and bloom in late summer. Due to its unique adaptations, it is considered an important indicator of coastal ecosystem health.
