Jasminum peninsulare

Jasminum peninsulare, commonly known as the Philippine jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It is a shrub that typically grows up to 2 meters tall, featuring simple, opposite, and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a strong, sweet aroma. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Jasminum peninsulare is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its use in traditional medicine.
