Jacobaea ferganensis

Jacobaea ferganensis, commonly known as the Fergana hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Jacobaea ferganensis is noted for its medicinal properties, with traditional uses in treating various ailments.
