Justicia sciera

Justicia sciera, commonly known as the flame flower or red torch, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, showy inflorescences of red, orange, or yellow flowers. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and is often grown for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for warm climates.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
