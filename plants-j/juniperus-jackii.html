Juniperus jackii

Juniperus jackii, commonly known as the Chinese juniper, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage that is typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Juniperus jackii is also valued for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions, making it adaptable to various landscapes.
