Juncus tenellus

Juncus tenellus, commonly known as the slender rush, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and typically grows in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Juncus tenellus is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in saturated soil conditions.
