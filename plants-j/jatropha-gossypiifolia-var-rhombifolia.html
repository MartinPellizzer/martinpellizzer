Jatropha gossypiifolia var. rhombifolia

Jatropha gossypiifolia var. rhombifolia, commonly known as the diamondleaf jatropha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its lanceolate to rhombic-shaped leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that are followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits containing toxic seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it adaptable to various tropical and subtropical environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
