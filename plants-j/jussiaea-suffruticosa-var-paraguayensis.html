Jussiaea suffruticosa var. paraguayensis

Jussiaea suffruticosa var. paraguayensis, commonly known as Paraguay willow, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Brazil.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, and it is typically found in moist, shaded environments. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.
