Juncoides rufescens

Juncoides rufescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae, which includes various species of rushes and sedges. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its slender, cylindrical stems are often reddish in color, especially when mature, and it produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers in clusters.

Juncoides rufescens plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
