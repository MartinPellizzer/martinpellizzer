Juniperus virginiana f. pyramidalis

Juniperus virginiana f. pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramid juniper, is a cultivar of the eastern red cedar.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dense, scale-like leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. It is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

This form is particularly valued for its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal in gardens and urban settings.
