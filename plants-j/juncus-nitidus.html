Juncus nitidus

Juncus nitidus, commonly known as the glossy rush, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features tall, slender stems that are often covered with a waxy coating, giving them a glossy appearance. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in early summer.

Juncus nitidus is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
