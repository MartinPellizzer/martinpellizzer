Juncus nigroviolaceus

Juncus nigroviolaceus, commonly known as black violet rush, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like stems that often grow in dense clusters. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in summer, adding a striking visual element to its habitat. Its leaves are narrow and cylindrical, adapted to thrive in moist or waterlogged soils.

Juncus nigroviolaceus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
