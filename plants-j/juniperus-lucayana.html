Juniperus lucayana

Juniperus lucayana, commonly known as the Lucayan juniper, is a species of juniper native to the Bahamas and the Turks and Caicos Islands.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in coastal areas and dry habitats. The plant has needle-like leaves that are blue-green in color and often have a waxy coating. Its cones, which are typically female, are small and berry-like, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Juniperus lucayana plays an important role in the local ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
