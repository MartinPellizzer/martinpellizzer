Justicia madagascariensis

Justicia madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar glorybower, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

The flowers are borne in terminal clusters and are pollinated primarily by birds, particularly sunbirds.
