Jasione supina subsp. tmolea

Jasione supina subsp.

tmolea, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe. It features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy blue or purple flowers in early summer.

The species is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, often forming dense mats on rocky or dry soils. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
