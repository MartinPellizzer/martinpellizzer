Juncus benghalensis var. kyongnoslae

Juncus benghalensis var. kyongnoslae, commonly known as the Kyongnosla rush, is a variant of the Bengal rush native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and along the edges of streams and rivers. Its flowering spikes bear small, inconspicuous greenish flowers that are often overlooked.

This variant is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
