Jambosa pteropoda

Jambosa pteropoda, commonly known as the Brazilian wax tree, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its native arid environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its propagation.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
