Juncus indescriptus

Juncus indescriptus, commonly known as the common rush, is a perennial plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescence. This plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense stands, which can help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Juncus indescriptus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to thrive in diverse aquatic conditions.
