Jussiaea longifolia

Jussiaea longifolia, commonly known as the slender water plant, is a flowering plant native to wetland and aquatic environments in South America.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that float on the water surface, supported by slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming during the warmer months. Jussiaea longifolia thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, often forming dense mats that provide habitat for aquatic life.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation efforts.
