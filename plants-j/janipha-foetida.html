Janipha foetida

Janipha foetida, commonly known as the stinking fig, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. The plant produces a fruit that is typically a small, round, and fleshy structure, which is edible but has a strong, unpleasant odor. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique scent and appearance, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
