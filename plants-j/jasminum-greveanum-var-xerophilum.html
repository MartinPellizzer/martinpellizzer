Jasminum greveanum var. xerophilum

Jasminum greveanum var. xerophilum, commonly known as the desert jasmine, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has adapted to survive in dry conditions with thick, fleshy leaves that store water. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal watering, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate fragrance and resilience in harsh environments.
