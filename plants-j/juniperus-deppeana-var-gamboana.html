Juniperus deppeana var. gamboana

Juniperus deppeana var. gamboana, commonly known as the Mexican redcedar, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in arid environments.
