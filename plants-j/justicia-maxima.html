Justicia maxima

Justicia maxima, commonly known as the Mexican verbena, is a tropical perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. Justicia maxima is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.

It prefers well-drained soil and can be propagated through seeds or stem cuttings.
