Juncus echinuloides

Juncus echinuloides, commonly known as the spiny bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense clusters of spike-like flower heads. Its flowering stems are often covered with small, spiny bracts, giving it its common name.

Juncus echinuloides plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
