Juncus caesariensis

Juncus caesariensis, commonly known as the blue rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes and stream banks. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are typically blue-green in color, giving it its common name. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually green or brown and appear in clusters at the tips of the stems.

Juncus caesariensis is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its striking appearance and ability to tolerate waterlogged soils.
