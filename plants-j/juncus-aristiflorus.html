Juncus aristiflorus

Juncus aristiflorus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and pond edges. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in dense clusters. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late spring to early summer, consist of small, greenish-white flowers.

Juncus aristiflorus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
