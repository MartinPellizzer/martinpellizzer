Juncus haenkei

Juncus haenkei, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that grow in dense clusters, typically reaching heights of 1 to 3 feet. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in early summer, often in clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and pond edges.

Juncus haenkei plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
