Juncus arizonicus

Juncus arizonicus, commonly known as Arizona rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and pond edges. The plant has cylindrical, grass-like stems that are usually green to reddish in color. Its small, spike-like inflorescence consists of numerous tiny, three-lobed flowers.

Juncus arizonicus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
