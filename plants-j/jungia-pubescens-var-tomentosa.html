Jungia pubescens var. tomentosa

Jungia pubescens var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly jungle plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
