Jacaranda oxyphylla

Jacaranda oxyphylla, commonly known as the silky jacaranda, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in the spring. The leaves are palmate, with five to seven leaflets that are smooth and glossy. The tree produces long, slender pods that contain flat, winged seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
