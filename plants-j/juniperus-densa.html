Juniperus densa

Juniperus densa, commonly known as the dense juniper, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Chile.

It is characterized by its compact, dense growth habit and its ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude environments. The plant produces small, scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, providing a striking visual contrast against the rocky terrain. Its cones are small and berry-like, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds and other animals.

Juniperus densa plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, offering shelter and food to various wildlife species.
