Justicia haematodes

Justicia haematodes, commonly known as bloodroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling a cluster of blood droplets, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a reddish tint, complementing the vibrant flowers.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties, with various traditional uses in herbal remedies.
