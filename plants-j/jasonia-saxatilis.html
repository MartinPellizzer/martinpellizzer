Jasonia saxatilis

Jasonia saxatilis, commonly known as the rock jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in rocky and dry environments. The plant features small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are typically narrow and gray-green, adapted to reduce water loss.

Jasonia saxatilis is often used in rock gardens due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
