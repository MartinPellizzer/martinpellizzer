Jacksonia horrida var. racemosa

Jacksonia horrida var. racemosa, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to Australia.

It features tall, spiky inflorescences that are typically bright red or orange, giving it its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

The flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
