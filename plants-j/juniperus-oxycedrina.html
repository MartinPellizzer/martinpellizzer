Juniperus oxycedrina

Juniperus oxycedrina, commonly known as the narrowleaf juniper, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Juniperus oxycedrina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and structural beauty.
