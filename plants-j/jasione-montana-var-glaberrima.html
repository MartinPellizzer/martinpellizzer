Jasione montana var. glaberrima

Jasione montana var. glaberrima, commonly known as the glabrous mountain jasmine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, glabrous leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
