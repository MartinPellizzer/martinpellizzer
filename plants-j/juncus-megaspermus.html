Juncus megaspermus

Juncus megaspermus, commonly known as the giant rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland and marsh environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often forming dense clumps. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in spike-like clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to thrive in waterlogged soils.

Juncus megaspermus plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
