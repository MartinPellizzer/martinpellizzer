Juncus thomasii

Juncus thomasii, commonly known as Thomas' rush, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae and is characterized by its dense clusters of grass-like leaves and spikes of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and pond edges. It has fibrous roots and is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil.

Thomas' rush is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in aquatic ecosystems.
