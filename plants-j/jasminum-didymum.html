Jasminum didymum

Jasminum didymum, commonly known as the double jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, double-petaled flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy green surface.

Jasminum didymum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its pleasant aroma and showy blossoms.
