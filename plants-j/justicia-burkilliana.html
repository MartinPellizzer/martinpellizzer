Justicia burkilliana

Justicia burkilliana, commonly known as the Burkill's glorybower, is a striking tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from deep red to orange, often appearing in clusters at the ends of its branches. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its vibrant floral display.
