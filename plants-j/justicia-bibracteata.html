Justicia bibracteata

Justicia bibracteata, commonly known as the flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that resemble petals, which are typically red or orange in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are pollinated by birds and insects. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
