Jacobaea abrotanifolia subsp. abrotanifolia

Jacobaea abrotanifolia subsp.

abrotanifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It features finely divided, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in herbal remedies.
