Juncus alpinoarticulatus subsp. alpinus

Juncus alpinoarticulatus subsp.

alpinus, commonly known as the alpine rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It features slender, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in fine, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in alpine meadows and tundra environments.

Its ability to withstand cold temperatures makes it an important component of high-altitude ecosystems.
