Jacaranda bahiensis

Jacaranda bahiensis, commonly known as the Brazilian jacaranda, is a deciduous tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in spring. The tree has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets and produces long, cylindrical fruit pods. Its bark is smooth and grayish, while its roots are deep and extensive, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
