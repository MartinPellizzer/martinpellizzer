Juncus novikovii

Juncus novikovii, commonly known as the Novikov's rush, is a perennial plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, often waterlogged soils, such as those found in tundra and wet meadows. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late summer, consist of small, clustered flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Juncus novikovii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various arctic fauna.
