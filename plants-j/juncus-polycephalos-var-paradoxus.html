Juncus polycephalos var. paradoxus

Juncus polycephalos var. paradoxus, commonly known as the paradoxical rush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and wetlands. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a bluish-gray hue.

The species is notable for its unique floral structure and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
