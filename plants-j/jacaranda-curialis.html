Jacaranda curialis

Jacaranda curialis, commonly known as the Brazilian jacaranda, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in the spring. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy and dark green, bipinnate leaves. Its flowers are borne in racemes and are often used in ornamental horticulture due to their showy appearance.

The plant is also valued for its wood, which is used in furniture-making and construction.
