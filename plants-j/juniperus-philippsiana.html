Juniperus philippsiana

Juniperus philippsiana, commonly known as the Philp's juniper, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage and small, berry-like cones that are typically blue or dark purple in color. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

The wood of Juniperus philippsiana is also valued for its aromatic properties and durability.
