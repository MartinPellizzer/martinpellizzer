Jasminum ochroleucum

Jasminum ochroleucum, commonly known as the yellow jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a smooth texture.

The flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
