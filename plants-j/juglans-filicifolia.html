Juglans filicifolia

Juglans filicifolia, commonly known as the fernleaf walnut, is a species of deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the genus Juglans, which includes various types of walnuts, and is characterized by its compound leaves that resemble ferns. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, followed by edible nuts enclosed in a thick, woody husk. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and mountainous areas.

The botanical features of Juglans filicifolia make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
