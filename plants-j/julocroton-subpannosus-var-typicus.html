Julocroton subpannosus var. typicus

Julocroton subpannosus var. typicus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf croton, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsules that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the genus Julocroton within the family Euphorbiaceae.
