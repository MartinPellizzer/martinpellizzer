Justicia dyschoristeoides

Justicia dyschoristeoides, commonly known as the red-flowered justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring upright stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in clusters, usually in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. Its flowers bloom in summer and are followed by small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
