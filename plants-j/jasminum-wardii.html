Jasminum wardii

Jasminum wardii, commonly known as the African jasmine, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with dense, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, dark green leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant fragrance. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and indoor cultivation.

Jasminum wardii is also valued for its use in traditional medicine in some regions.
