Jasminum amplexicaule

Jasminum amplexicaule, commonly known as the climbing jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has a twining growth habit and produces small, heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its flowers are used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
