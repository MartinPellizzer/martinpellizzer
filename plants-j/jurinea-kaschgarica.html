Jurinea kaschgarica

Jurinea kaschgarica, commonly known as the Karakorum sandflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in sandy and rocky soils, often found in high-altitude environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
