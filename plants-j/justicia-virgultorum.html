Justicia virgultorum

Justicia virgultorum, commonly known as the flame flower or red flame plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its rapid growth and ability to produce abundant blooms make it a versatile plant for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
