Justicia quadrifida

Justicia quadrifida, commonly known as the four-leafed jewelweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are borne in terminal clusters and are known for their ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
