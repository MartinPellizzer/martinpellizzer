Jurinea mirabilis

Jurinea mirabilis, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy and gravelly soils, often found in steppes and desert margins. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to store water in harsh environments.

Jurinea mirabilis plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
