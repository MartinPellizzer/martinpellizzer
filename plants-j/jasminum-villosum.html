Jasminum villosum

Jasminum villosum, commonly known as the hairy jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Jasminum villosum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
