Juglans equatoriensis

Juglans equatoriensis, commonly known as the African walnut, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the walnut family, Juglandaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and edible nuts. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, rounded crown. Its bark is smooth and grayish, becoming fissured with age.

The nuts are enclosed in a thick, spiky husk and are an important food source for various wildlife species.
