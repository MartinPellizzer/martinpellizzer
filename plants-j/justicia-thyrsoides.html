Justicia thyrsoides

Justicia thyrsoides, commonly known as the flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring opposite, simple, and ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in clusters, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which attract pollinators like bees and birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

The genus Justicia is part of the Acanthaceae family, known for its ornamental value and ecological importance in various habitats.
