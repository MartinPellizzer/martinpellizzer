Justicia euosmia

Justicia euosmia, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the Andes, is a striking plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowering period typically occurs in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The botanical characteristics of Justicia euosmia make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
