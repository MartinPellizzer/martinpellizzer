Juncus tibeticus

Juncus tibeticus, commonly known as the Tibetan rush, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and slender, cylindrical stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in alpine and subalpine environments. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in its harsh habitat.

Juncus tibeticus is an important component of the local flora, adapted to the extreme climatic conditions of its native region.
