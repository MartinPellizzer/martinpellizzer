Juncus texanus

Juncus texanus, commonly known as the Texas rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the southern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, preferring moist to saturated soil conditions. The plant has cylindrical, grass-like stems that are often green or reddish in color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense mats, which can help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Juncus texanus plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
