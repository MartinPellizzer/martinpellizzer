Jacksonia capitata var. rigida

Jacksonia capitata var. rigida, commonly known as the rigid dandelion, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical inflorescence composed of numerous yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disc. The leaves are basal, alternate, and have a rough, hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
