Justicia loureiroana

Justicia loureiroana, commonly known as the flame flower, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its showy blooms and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
