Juncus striatus subsp. lagenarius

Juncus striatus subsp.

lagenarius, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and pond edges.

The plant features slender, cylindrical stems that are often slightly hairy, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spike of small, three-lobed flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
