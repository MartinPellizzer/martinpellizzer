Jasminum macrophyllum

Jasminum macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaved jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant white flowers and large, glossy green leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers bloom in clusters and are used in traditional medicine and perfumery.
