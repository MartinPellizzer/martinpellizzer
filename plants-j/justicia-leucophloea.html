Justicia leucophloea

Justicia leucophloea, commonly known as white-veined justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with prominent white veins, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple, often with a yellow throat. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Justicia leucophloea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
