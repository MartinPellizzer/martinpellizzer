Jasminum angulare

Jasminum angulare, commonly known as the angular jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a trailing shrub that produces fragrant, white flowers with five petals. The plant is characterized by its angular, often zigzagging stems and small, opposite leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Jasminum angulare is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
