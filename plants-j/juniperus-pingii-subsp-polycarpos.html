Juniperus pingii subsp. polycarpos

Juniperus pingii subsp.

polycarpos, commonly known as the Sierra juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage and small, berry-like cones that mature to a blue-black color.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It has a slow growth rate and can live for several decades, often forming dense thickets.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
