Justicia interrupta

Justicia interrupta, commonly known as the flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Justicia interrupta is also known for its ability to produce seed pods that split open to release its seeds.
