Juncus interior

Juncus interior, commonly known as the common rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of greenish or brownish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, pond edges, and stream banks.

Juncus interior plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
