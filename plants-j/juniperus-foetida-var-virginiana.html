Juniperus foetida var. virginiana

Juniperus foetida var. virginiana, commonly known as the eastern redcedar, is a deciduous conifer native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its scale-like leaves that turn reddish in the fall, and its small, berry-like cones that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for birds. The plant has a deep taproot system, allowing it to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to provide habitat for wildlife.

The tree is also known for its role in the production of cedarwood oil, which has various industrial applications.
