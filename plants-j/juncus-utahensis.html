Juncus utahensis

Juncus utahensis, commonly known as Utah rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and lake margins. The plant features cylindrical, grass-like stems that are often reddish or greenish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered flowers in early summer.

Utah rush plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
