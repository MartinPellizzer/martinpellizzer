Juncus howellii

Juncus howellii, commonly known as Howell's rush, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late spring to early summer, consist of small, greenish-white flowers.

Juncus howellii plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
