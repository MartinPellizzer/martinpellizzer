Jurinella absinthifolia

Jurinella absinthifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and other sunflowers. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

Jurinella absinthifolia is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
