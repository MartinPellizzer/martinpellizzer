Joniris uniflora var. caricina

Joniris uniflora var. caricina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pale blue. The leaves are narrow and lanceolate, often with a slightly hairy surface. This variety is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardeners.
