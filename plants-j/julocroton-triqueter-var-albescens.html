Julocroton triqueter var. albescens

Julocroton triqueter var. albescens, commonly known as the white-stemmed croton, is a variant of the croton species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinct, often variegated foliage with a pale or white underside. The plant typically features thick, fleshy leaves that are arranged in an alternate pattern along the stem. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in panicles, and are typically greenish-yellow in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in warm climates.
