Jacaranda decurrens

Jacaranda decurrens, commonly known as the Brazilian jacaranda, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The tree has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces elongated, woody pods that contain flat, seed-like structures. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture-making.
