Justicia aequilabris subsp. riograndina

Justicia aequilabris subsp.

riograndina, commonly known as the Rio Grande justicia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central and South America. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in terminal spikes, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, providing a textured appearance.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to well-drained soils, making it a versatile choice for gardens and landscapes.
