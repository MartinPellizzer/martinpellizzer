Justicia cataractae

Justicia cataractae, commonly known as the waterfall plant or cascade plant, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of tubular red or orange flowers that resemble tiny lanterns. The plant typically grows along streambanks and in moist, shaded environments, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

Justicia cataractae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
