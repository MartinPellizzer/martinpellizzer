Jasminocereus howellii

Jasminocereus howellii, commonly known as the night-blooming cereus, is a cacti species native to Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and is known for its large, fragrant white flowers that open at night. The plant produces showy, star-shaped blooms that emit a sweet scent to attract nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

The flowers typically close by dawn, giving the plant its common name.
