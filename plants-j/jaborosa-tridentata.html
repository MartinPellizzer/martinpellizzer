Jaborosa tridentata

Jaborosa tridentata, commonly known as the three-toothed jaborosa, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant features a hairy, branching stem and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with three distinct teeth, giving it its common name.

Jaborosa tridentata is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing in rocky soils and open landscapes.
