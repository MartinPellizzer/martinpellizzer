Justicia lorteae

Justicia lorteae, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters, featuring showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces clusters of vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that attract hummingbirds and other pollinators. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a velvety texture, while its bark is smooth and grayish.

Justicia lorteae thrives in full sun to partial shade and is well-suited for tropical and subtropical climates.
