Justicia procumbens var. linearifolia

Justicia procumbens var. linearifolia, commonly known as the linear-leaf flame flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features prostrate growth habit with slender, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom in clusters along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

This species is popular in ornamental horticulture for its vibrant blooms and ability to spread quickly in suitable conditions.
