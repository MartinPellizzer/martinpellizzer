Justicia perrieri

Justicia perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's flame flower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are often visited by hummingbirds and bees, making it an important pollinator attractant.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in tropical gardens for its vibrant display.
