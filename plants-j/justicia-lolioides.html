Justicia lolioides

Justicia lolioides, commonly known as the silverleaf justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in clusters, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun to partial shade.

The plant is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.
