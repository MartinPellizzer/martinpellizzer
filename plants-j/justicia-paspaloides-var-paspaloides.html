Justicia paspaloides var. paspaloides

Justicia paspaloides var. paspaloides, commonly known as the silverleaf false panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial grass-like plant characterized by its silver-gray foliage and tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and low-maintenance ornamental plant in arid landscapes.
