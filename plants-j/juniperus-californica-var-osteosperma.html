Juniperus californica var. osteosperma

Juniperus californica var. osteosperma, commonly known as the desert juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States.

It has a dense, sprawling growth habit with scale-like leaves that are blue-gray in color. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are typically blue or purple when mature. It is adapted to arid environments and has deep roots that allow it to survive in dry conditions.

The leaves and cones are used in traditional medicine and have a distinctive aromatic scent.
