Jennyella conspersa

Jennyella conspersa, commonly known as the hairy Jennyella, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the arid regions of Australia.

It features small, narrow leaves that are densely covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to harsh, dry environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
