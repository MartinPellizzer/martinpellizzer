Jatropha augusti

Jatropha augusti, commonly known as the African Crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky purple flowers that bloom in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Jatropha augusti is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
