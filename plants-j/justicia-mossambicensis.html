Justicia mossambicensis

Justicia mossambicensis, commonly known as the Mozambique spider flower, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Justicia mossambicensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil types.
