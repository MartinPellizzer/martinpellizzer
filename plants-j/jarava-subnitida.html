Jarava subnitida

Jarava subnitida, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-blue leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and sandy soils. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Jarava subnitida is adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
