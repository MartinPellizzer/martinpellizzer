Juglans × intermedia quadrangulata

Juglans × intermedia quadrangulata, commonly known as the Chinese black walnut, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Juglans mandshurica and Juglans regia.

It is characterized by its large, oblong nuts enclosed in a thick, ridged husk. The tree produces compound leaves with 15–23 leaflets that are lanceolate in shape and have a serrated margin. Its bark is dark, rugged, and fissured, providing a distinctive appearance.

This species is native to eastern Asia and is valued for its hard, durable wood and edible nuts.
