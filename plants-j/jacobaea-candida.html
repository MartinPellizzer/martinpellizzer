Jacobaea candida

Jacobaea candida, commonly known as the meadow cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides. It produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Jacobaea candida is often found in open habitats and is relatively hardy, though it prefers well-drained soils.
