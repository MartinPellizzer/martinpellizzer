Juncus glaucus curvatus

Juncus glaucus curvatus, commonly known as the blue rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of pale green to white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds.

Juncus glaucus curvatus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
