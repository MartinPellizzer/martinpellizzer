Jakkia excelsa

Jakkia excelsa, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic life. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes, where it can anchor itself with its rhizomes. Its showy white flowers bloom in the early morning and close by midday, a characteristic known as nyctinasty.

The botanical features of Jakkia excelsa make it a notable species in both ecological and ornamental contexts.
