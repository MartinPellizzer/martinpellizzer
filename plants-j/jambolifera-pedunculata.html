Jambolifera pedunculata

Jambolifera pedunculata, commonly known as the Indian date palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, sweet fruits resembling dates. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Jambolifera pedunculata is often cultivated for its fruit and is also valued for its ornamental appearance.
