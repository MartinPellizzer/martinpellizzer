Jasminum lessertianum

Jasminum lessertianum, commonly known as the lesser jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often producing a sweet, delicate aroma. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, with opposite, simple, and usually elliptic-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Jasminum lessertianum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and as a climbing plant.
