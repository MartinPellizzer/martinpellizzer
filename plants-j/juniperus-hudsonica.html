Juniperus hudsonica

Juniperus hudsonica, commonly known as Hudson's juniper, is a hardy evergreen shrub or small tree native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage and small, blue-gray berries that are typically harvested for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant thrives in cold, dry climates and is often found in tundra and boreal forest ecosystems. Its aromatic wood is resistant to decay, making it valuable for construction and craft.

Juniperus hudsonica plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
