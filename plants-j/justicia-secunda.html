Justicia secunda

Justicia secunda, commonly known as the velvetleaf or purple-leaf justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking purple or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a velvety texture that gives it its common name.

Justicia secunda is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
