Jasminum sambac var. normale

Jasminum sambac var. normale, commonly known as the common jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant is an evergreen shrub with glossy, dark green leaves and a woody stem structure. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its aromatic properties make it popular in perfumery and traditional medicine.
