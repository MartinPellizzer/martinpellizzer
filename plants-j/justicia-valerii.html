Justicia valerii

Justicia valerii, commonly known as the white flame flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular white flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Justicia valerii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
