Juniperus phoenicea var. lycia

Juniperus phoenicea var. lycia, commonly known as the Lycian juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and produces small, berry-like cones that are typically blue or black in color. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in landscaping and traditional medicine.

The species is also noted for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
