Juncus articulatus var. nigrescens

Juncus articulatus var. nigrescens, commonly known as blackstem rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features tall, slender stems that are dark purple to black in color, distinguishing it from other rush species. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, stream banks, and other aquatic habitats.

Its dense growth habit makes it an effective stabilizer of soil in wetland ecosystems.
