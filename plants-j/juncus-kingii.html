Juncus kingii

Juncus kingii, commonly known as King's rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall spikes of small, greenish-white flowers. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in saturated soil conditions.

Juncus kingii plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
