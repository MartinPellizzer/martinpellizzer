Juncus secundus

Juncus secundus, commonly known as the common rush, is a perennial plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage composed of narrow, cylindrical leaves that grow in clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikes, often emerging from the base of the leaves. Juncus secundus thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows.

It plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
