Jasione pyrenaea var. hirsuta

Jasione pyrenaea var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy rock-jasmine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. The plant features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
