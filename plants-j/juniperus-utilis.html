Juniperus utilis

Juniperus utilis, commonly known as the useful juniper, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage and small, blue-gray berries that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of rocky and arid environments. Its aromatic leaves and cones contribute to its ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.

Juniperus utilis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh climates.
