Juncus inconspicuus

Juncus inconspicuus, commonly known as the slender rush, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and lack leaves, with small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in clusters at the top. Its root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in wetland habitats.

Juncus inconspicuus plays an important role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining ecosystem health.
