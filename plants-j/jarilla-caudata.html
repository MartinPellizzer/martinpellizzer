Jarilla caudata

Jarilla caudata, commonly known as Mexican tea or wild tea, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils in arid or semi-arid environments.

Jarilla caudata is often used in traditional herbal remedies and is valued for its aromatic properties.
