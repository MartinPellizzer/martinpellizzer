Jamesonia hirta

Jamesonia hirta, commonly known as the hairy Jamesonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are dense and hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Jamesonia hirta is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to add texture and color to woodland gardens.
