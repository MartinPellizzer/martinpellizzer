Juncus pallescens

Juncus pallescens, commonly known as the gray rush, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its slender, cylindrical stems are often covered in a grayish-white bloom, which gives it its common name.

Juncus pallescens plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
