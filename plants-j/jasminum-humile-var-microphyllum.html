Jasminum humile var. microphyllum

Jasminum humile var. microphyllum, commonly known as the small-leaved jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, typically measuring up to 2 inches in length. This variety thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for container gardening.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in hedges or as a ground cover.
