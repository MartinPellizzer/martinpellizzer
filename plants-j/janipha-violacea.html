Janipha violacea

Janipha violacea, commonly known as the violet bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling small bells. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Janipha violacea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique floral structure.
