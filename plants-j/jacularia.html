Jacularia

Jacularia, commonly known as the "butterfly pea," is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its leguminous structure and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of violet or blue flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its seeds are encased in a pod-like fruit, and the plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and traditional medicinal uses.

The vibrant colors of its flowers also make it a popular ingredient in teas and natural dyes.
