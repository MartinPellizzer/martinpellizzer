Justicia simplex

Justicia simplex, commonly known as the common justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bright blue or violet tubular flowers. The plant is part of the Acanthaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Justicia simplex is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
