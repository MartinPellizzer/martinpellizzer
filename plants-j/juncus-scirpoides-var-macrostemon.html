Juncus scirpoides var. macrostemon

Juncus scirpoides var. macrostemon, commonly known as the broadstem rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish-brown in color and can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous tiny, three-lobed flowers. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a slightly waxy coating.

This species is adapted to moist soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
