Juncinella pygmaea

Juncinella pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf rush, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. This plant thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, pond edges, and stream banks. Its flowers are small, clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it produces tiny, three-lobed seeds.

Juncinella pygmaea plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
