Janusia muricata

Janusia muricata, commonly known as the roughleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds when mature.

Janusia muricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
