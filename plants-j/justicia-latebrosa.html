Justicia latebrosa

Justicia latebrosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and hummingbirds, contributing to its ecological significance.
