Jubelina riparia

Jubelina riparia, commonly known as the riverbank bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows along riverbanks and moist soils, showcasing its adaptability to wet environments. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Jubelina riparia is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in stabilizing soil in riparian zones.
