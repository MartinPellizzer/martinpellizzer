Jacaranda jasminoides

Jacaranda jasminoides, commonly known as the blue jacaranda, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of blue or violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Jacaranda jasminoides thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
