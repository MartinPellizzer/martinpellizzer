Juncus conglomeratus var. effusus

Juncus conglomeratus var. effusus, commonly known as the soft rush, is a flowering plant species belonging to the family Juncaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage composed of narrow, cylindrical stems that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clustered spikes, which are usually found at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
