Jasminum adiantifolium

Jasminum adiantifolium, commonly known as the silver leaf jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
