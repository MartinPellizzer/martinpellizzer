Justicia glaucoviolacea

Justicia glaucoviolacea, commonly known as the purple velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in dense clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows as an annual or short-lived perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a velvety texture and a bluish-green hue.

The genus Justicia is part of the Acanthaceae family, known for its showy bracts and diverse floral structures.
