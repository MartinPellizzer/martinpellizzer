Jacquemontia prostrata

Jacquemontia prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate Jacquemontia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a low-growing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy soils.

Jacquemontia prostrata is often found in open woods, prairies, and along roadsides, where it contributes to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
