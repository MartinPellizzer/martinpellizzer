Jossinia indica

Jossinia indica, commonly known as the Indian joss tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are often used in religious ceremonies, particularly in Hindu and Buddhist traditions. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that contains a single seed.

Jossinia indica is valued not only for its ornamental qualities but also for its cultural and spiritual significance.
