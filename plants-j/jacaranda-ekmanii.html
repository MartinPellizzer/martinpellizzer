Jacaranda ekmanii

Jacaranda ekmanii, commonly known as the Cape Jacaranda, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that appear in clusters during the warmer months. The tree has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces elongated, woody pods that contain flat, fibrous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

The plant is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
