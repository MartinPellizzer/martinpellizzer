Jacaranda pulcherrima

Jacaranda pulcherrima, commonly known as the jacaranda tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet or blue-violet flowers that bloom in summer. The tree has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets and produces woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its ability to provide shade and its role in supporting local wildlife.
