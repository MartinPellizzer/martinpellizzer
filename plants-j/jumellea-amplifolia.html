Jumellea amplifolia

Jumellea amplifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf jumellea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its robust, woody stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is popular among gardeners in arid regions.
