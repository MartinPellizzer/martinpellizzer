Juncus repens

Juncus repens, commonly known as the creeping rush, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage that grows in clusters. The plant typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and produces small, greenish-white flowers in early summer. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, which helps stabilize soil in marshy areas.

Juncus repens thrives in saturated soils and is often found in swamps, bogs, and along the edges of ponds.
