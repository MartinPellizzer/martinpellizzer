Jasminum angustilobium

Jasminum angustilobium, commonly known as the narrow-lobed jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing or shrub-like growth habit and produces fragrant white flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a glossy surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic blooms.
