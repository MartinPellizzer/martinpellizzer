Jacaranda arrabidae

Jacaranda arrabidae, commonly known as the Portuguese jacaranda, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in spring. The tree has a straight trunk with a broad, spreading crown and produces long, pendulous inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with a pointed tip.
