Jasminum hockii var. brevitubum

Jasminum hockii var. brevitubum, commonly known as the short-stemmed jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically has slender, woody stems and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species within the jasmine family.
