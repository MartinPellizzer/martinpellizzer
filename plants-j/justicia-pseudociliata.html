Justicia pseudociliata

Justicia pseudociliata, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking clusters of blue or purple tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is well-suited for warm climates.

The flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
