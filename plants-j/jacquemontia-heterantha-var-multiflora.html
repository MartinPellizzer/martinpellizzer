Jacquemontia heterantha var. multiflora

Jacquemontia heterantha var. multiflora, commonly known as the multiflora moonflower, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It produces large, white, tubular flowers that bloom at night, emitting a strong fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and hairy stems, which are characteristic of the Jacquemontia genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique flowering behavior.
