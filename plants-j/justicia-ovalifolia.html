Justicia ovalifolia

Justicia ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaved flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, glossy leaves and clusters of vibrant red or orange tubular flowers. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is known for its showy, long-lasting blooms that attract hummingbirds and other pollinators. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
