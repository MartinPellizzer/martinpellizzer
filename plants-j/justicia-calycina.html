Justicia calycina

Justicia calycina, commonly known as the flame flower, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.

Botanically, it belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and adaptability.
