Jasminum dinklagei

Jasminum dinklagei, commonly known as the Dinklage's jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with opposite, simple, and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

Due to its unique appearance and fragrance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
