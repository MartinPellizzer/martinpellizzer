Juncus tenuis var. occidentalis

Juncus tenuis var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish or greenish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters near the base of the plant.

This species is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
