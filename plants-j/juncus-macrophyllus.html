Juncus macrophyllus

Juncus macrophyllus, commonly known as the broadleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, and its leaves are broad, flat, and grass-like. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in spike-like clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in shallow water or along the edges of ponds and lakes.

Juncus macrophyllus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
