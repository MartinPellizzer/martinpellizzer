Juncus aureus

Juncus aureus, commonly known as golden rush, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are bright yellow to golden green in color. It produces small, spike-like clusters of white or pale yellow flowers in the summer.

Juncus aureus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
