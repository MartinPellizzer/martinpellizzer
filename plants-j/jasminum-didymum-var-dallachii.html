Jasminum didymum var. dallachii

Jasminum didymum var. dallachii, commonly known as the Dallachii jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its pleasant aroma.
