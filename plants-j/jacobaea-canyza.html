Jacobaea canyza

Jacobaea canyza, commonly known as the common tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in woodland edges.

Jacobaea canyza is also known for its strong aromatic properties and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
