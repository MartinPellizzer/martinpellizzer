Justicia rhomboidea

Justicia rhomboidea, commonly known as the arrowleaf false heather, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often tinged with red or purple on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink or red, typically blooming during the warmer months. It is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.

Justicia rhomboidea prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for gardens in tropical and subtropical climates.
