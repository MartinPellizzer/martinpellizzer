Jacea phrygia

Jacea phrygia, commonly known as the Persian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.
