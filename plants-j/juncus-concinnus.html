Juncus concinnus

Juncus concinnus, commonly known as the slender rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of white or pale green flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds.

Juncus concinnus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
