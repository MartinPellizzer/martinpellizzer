Jambosa tawahensis

Jambosa tawahensis, commonly known as the Tawah date palm, is a unique species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and clustered fronds. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into edible dates, which are prized for their sweetness and nutritional value. Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a resilient plant in humid climates.

However, it is relatively rare and requires specific conditions to thrive.
