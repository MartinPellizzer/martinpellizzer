Justicia salicaria

Justicia salicaria, commonly known as redroot joyweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a reddish stem. The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences with vibrant red or pink flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Botanically, it belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
