Jarava acuta

Jarava acuta, commonly known as sharp-jarava, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a dense, cylindrical cluster.

Jarava acuta is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in its native range.
