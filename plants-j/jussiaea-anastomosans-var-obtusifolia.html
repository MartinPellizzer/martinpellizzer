Jussiaea anastomosans var. obtusifolia

Jussiaea anastomosans var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved water plantain, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in South America.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often coated with a waxy layer to prevent water loss. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming during the summer months. It thrives in shallow water or damp soil, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats that help stabilize aquatic ecosystems.
