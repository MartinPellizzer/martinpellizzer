Juncus sylvaticus var. rochelianus

Juncus sylvaticus var. rochelianus, commonly known as the Rochel's rush, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often densely packed. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear greenish or brownish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically growing in dense clusters at the base.

It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and damp woodlands.
