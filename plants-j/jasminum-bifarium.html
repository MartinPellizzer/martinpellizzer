Jasminum bifarium

Jasminum bifarium, commonly known as the two-flowered jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with two distinct petals. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a glossy green appearance.

Jasminum bifarium is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its pleasant aroma.
