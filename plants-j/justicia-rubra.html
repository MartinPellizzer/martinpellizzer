Justicia rubra

Justicia rubra, commonly known as red flame or redroot, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking red to orange tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 3 feet. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are often reddish in color.

Justicia rubra thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
