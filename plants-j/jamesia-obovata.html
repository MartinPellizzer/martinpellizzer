Jamesia obovata

Jamesia obovata, commonly known as the mountain snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has opposite, ovate to obovate leaves that are often hairy and turn red in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Jamesia obovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
