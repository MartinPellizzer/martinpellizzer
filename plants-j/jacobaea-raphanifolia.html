Jacobaea raphanifolia

Jacobaea raphanifolia, commonly known as the parsley-leaved ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are finely divided, resembling those of parsley, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Jacobaea raphanifolia is often found in dry, open habitats and is considered a hardy, low-maintenance plant.
