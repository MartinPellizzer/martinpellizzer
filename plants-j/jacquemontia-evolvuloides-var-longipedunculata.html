Jacquemontia evolvuloides var. longipedunculata

Jacquemontia evolvuloides var. longipedunculata, commonly known as the long pedunculate golden vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its climbing habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces yellow-orange blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its long, slender peduncles support the flowers, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens.
