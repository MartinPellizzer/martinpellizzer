Juniperus scopulorum f. argentea

Juniperus scopulorum f. argentea, commonly known as the silver juniper, is a variant of the Rocky Mountain juniper.

It is characterized by its silvery-white or grayish foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically scale-like and closely pressed to the branches, contributing to its dense, compact growth habit. This form is often found in rocky or well-drained soils in higher elevations.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
