Juniperus sabina var. monosperma

Juniperus sabina var. monosperma, commonly known as the single-seeded juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its stiff, often twisted branches and scale-like leaves that are grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are typically solitary, giving it its common name. It is well adapted to arid and rocky environments, often growing at high elevations.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
