Jatropha collina

Jatropha collina, commonly known as the creeping jatropha, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in umbels, often accompanied by red bracts.

Jatropha collina is known for its toxic sap, which contains irritants and can cause skin inflammation.
