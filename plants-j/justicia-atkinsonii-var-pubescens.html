Justicia atkinsonii var. pubescens

Justicia atkinsonii var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent flame flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It features striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has hairy, pubescent leaves that give it its varietal name, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant floral display and unique foliage texture.
