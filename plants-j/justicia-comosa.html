Justicia comosa

Justicia comosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf false arum, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces striking, spatulate-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses worldwide.
