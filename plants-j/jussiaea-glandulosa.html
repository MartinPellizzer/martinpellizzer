Jussiaea glandulosa

Jussiaea glandulosa, commonly known as the sticky willow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a woody stem with glandular hairs that give it a sticky texture. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near streams or in wetlands.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in its native habitat.
