Justicia extensa

Justicia extensa, commonly known as the broadleaf flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in clusters, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated for both its beauty and ecological benefits.
