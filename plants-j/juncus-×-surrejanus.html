Juncus × surrejanus

Juncus × surrejanus, commonly known as the hybrid rush, is a perennial plant that belongs to the family Juncaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in dense, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, making it a common sight in wetlands and along the edges of ponds.

Juncus × surrejanus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
