Juncus micranthus

Juncus micranthus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 1 millimeter wide, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowering spikes consist of small, greenish-brown flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical clusters.

Juncus micranthus plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
