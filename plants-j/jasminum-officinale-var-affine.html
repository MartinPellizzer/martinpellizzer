Jasminum officinale var. affine

Jasminum officinale var. affine, commonly known as the common jasmine or garden jasmine, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, featuring simple, opposite, and ovate leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and ornamental purposes.

Its essential oils are also used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
