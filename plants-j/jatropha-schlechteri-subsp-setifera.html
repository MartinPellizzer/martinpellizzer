Jatropha schlechteri subsp. setifera

Jatropha schlechteri subsp.

setifera, commonly known as the hairy jatropha, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, hairy leaves that are often glandular.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange-red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves and stems contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in semi-arid environments.
