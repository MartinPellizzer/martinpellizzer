Jambosa madagascariensis

Jambosa madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar date palm, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, date-like fruits. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and fruit, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental and culinary purposes.
