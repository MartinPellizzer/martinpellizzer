Juniperus excelsa var. turcomanica

Juniperus excelsa var. turcomanica, commonly known as the Turkestan juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of scale-like leaves arranged in flat sprays. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are typically blue or black in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
