Jacquiniella gigantea

Jacquiniella gigantea, commonly known as the giant Jacquiniella, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 inches in diameter, often displaying vibrant colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant features a robust, woody stem with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green and glossy. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
