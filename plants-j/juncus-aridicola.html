Juncus aridicola

Juncus aridicola, commonly known as the desert rush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Juncus aridicola is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep root systems that help it access water from deeper soil layers.

Its ability to thrive in harsh environments makes it an important component of desert ecosystems.
