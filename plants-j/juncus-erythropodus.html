Juncus erythropodus

Juncus erythropodus, commonly known as the red-root rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and tall, spike-like flower heads that produce small, reddish-brown seeds. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its root system is dense and fibrous, which helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Juncus erythropodus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
