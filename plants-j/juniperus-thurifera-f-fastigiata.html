Juniperus thurifera f. fastigiata

Juniperus thurifera f. fastigiata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved juniper, is a compact, shrub-like form of the juniper species.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, needle-like foliage that is typically a deep green color. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are typically blue or purple in color, which are a key feature in its botanical description. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils and full sun conditions.

This variety is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
