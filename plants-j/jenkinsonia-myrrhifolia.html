Jenkinsonia myrrhifolia

Jenkinsonia myrrhifolia, commonly known as the myrrh-leaved jenkinsonia, is a rare and unique plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, aromatic leaves that emit a resinous scent reminiscent of myrrh, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in areas with high altitude and cool temperatures. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of white and pale green, and it produces unique seed capsules that disperse its seeds via wind.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Jenkinsonia myrrhifolia is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
