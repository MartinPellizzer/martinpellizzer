Juchia

Juchia, commonly known as Jerusalem artichoke, is a tuberous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its hairy, yellow flowers and edible, inedible tubers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates. Its tubers are rich in inulin, a prebiotic fiber, and have a nutty, sweet flavor.

Juchia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use by indigenous peoples.
