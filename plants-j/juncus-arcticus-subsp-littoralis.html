Juncus arcticus subsp. littoralis

Juncus arcticus subsp.

littoralis, commonly known as the Arctic rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Arctic and subarctic regions. It typically grows in moist, coastal environments such as tundra and salt marshes.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish flowers in spikes during the short growing season. It is adapted to cold climates and can tolerate salt spray and strong winds.

Its root system helps stabilize coastal soils and provides habitat for various small organisms.
