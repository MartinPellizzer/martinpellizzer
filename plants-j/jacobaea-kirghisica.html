Jacobaea kirghisica

Jacobaea kirghisica, commonly known as the Kirghiz hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Jacobaea kirghisica is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
