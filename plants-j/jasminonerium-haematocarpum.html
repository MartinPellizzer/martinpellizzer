Jasminonerium haematocarpum

Jasminonerium haematocarpum, commonly known as the bloodcarp jasmine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, red berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
