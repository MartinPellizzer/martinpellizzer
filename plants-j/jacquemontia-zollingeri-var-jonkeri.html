Jacquemontia zollingeri var. jonkeri

Jacquemontia zollingeri var. jonkeri, commonly known as the Jonker's golden vine, is a climbing herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and emits a mild, sweet fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and role in local ecosystems.
