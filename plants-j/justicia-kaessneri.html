Justicia kaessneri

Justicia kaessneri, commonly known as the flame flower, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-draining soil.

Justicia kaessneri is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
