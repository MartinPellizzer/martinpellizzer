Justicia campenonii

Justicia campenonii, commonly known as the flame flower or red flame plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in dense clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement, and it thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Justicia campenonii is also valued for its ability to tolerate partial shade and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
