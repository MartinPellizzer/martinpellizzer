Justicia tunicata

Justicia tunicata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or tunicate justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often with a distinctive red or orange throat. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that give it its common name, and it thrives in moist, sunny environments. It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Justicia tunicata is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
