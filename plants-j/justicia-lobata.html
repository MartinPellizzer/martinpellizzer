Justicia lobata

Justicia lobata, commonly known as the whorled false heather, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The leaves are typically opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
