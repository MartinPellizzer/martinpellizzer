Jarava vaginata f. rigida

Jarava vaginata f. rigida, commonly known as rigid jarava, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with stiff, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its dense growth makes it useful for erosion control and as a forage plant for livestock.
