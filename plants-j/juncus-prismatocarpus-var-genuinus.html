Juncus prismatocarpus var. genuinus

Juncus prismatocarpus var. genuinus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and bear small, scale-like leaves. Its flowers are inconspicuous and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is notable for its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
