Jarava brevipes

Jarava brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed jarava, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that bloom during the warmer months. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to survive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

Jarava brevipes plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for small desert animals.
