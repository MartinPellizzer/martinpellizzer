Jasminum caudatum

Jasminum caudatum, commonly known as the trailing jasmine or wild jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a sweet, intoxicating scent. The plant typically grows as a vine or shrub, with slender, trailing stems that can climb or cascade from supports. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture.

Jasminum caudatum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
