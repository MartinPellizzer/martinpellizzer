Juniperus communis subsp. sibirica

Juniperus communis subsp.

sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the cold regions of Asia and Europe. It is characterized by its needle-like leaves and small, blue-black berries that are used in various traditional and commercial applications.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges, rocky areas, and alpine environments. It has a slow growth rate and can live for many decades, making it a long-lived species.

The Siberian juniper is also valued for its aromatic wood and its role in supporting local wildlife.
