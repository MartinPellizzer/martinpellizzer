Jacquemontia fruticulosa f. genuina

Jacquemontia fruticulosa f. genuina, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a variant of the species native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, smooth seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
