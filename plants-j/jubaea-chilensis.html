Jubaea chilensis

Jubaea chilensis, commonly known as the Chiltepe or Chilean palm, is a large, slow-growing palm native to central Chile.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that develop into small, edible fruits resembling cherries. Jubaea chilensis is well adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, with deep root systems that allow it to thrive in dry conditions.

It is also valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in indigenous cultures.
