Jacobinia candicans

Jacobinia candicans, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical species native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant is a deciduous tree or shrub with pinnately compound leaves and smooth, gray bark. Its botanical features include showy inflorescences and a tendency to grow in coastal or sandy soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
