Justicia resupinata

Justicia resupinata, commonly known as the creeping justicia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its showy, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in clusters at the leaf axils.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
