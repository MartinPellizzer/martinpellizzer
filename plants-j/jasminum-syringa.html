Jasminum syringa

Jasminum syringa, commonly known as the Japanese jasmine or spring jasmine, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Jasminum syringa is often used in ornamental gardening for its pleasant scent and seasonal beauty.
