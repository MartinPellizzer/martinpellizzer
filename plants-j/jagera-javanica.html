Jagera javanica

Jagera javanica, commonly known as the Javanese tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, spiny capsules containing numerous seeds. It is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which have a sour flavor and are used in traditional cuisine.

Additionally, the tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
