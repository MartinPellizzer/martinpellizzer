Justicia valida

Justicia valida, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red-flowered justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of tubular, vibrantly colored flowers that range from red to orange. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas. Its showy flowers attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds, contributing to its ecological significance.

Justicia valida is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes.
