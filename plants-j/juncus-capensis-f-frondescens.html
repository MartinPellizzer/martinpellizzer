Juncus capensis f. frondescens

Juncus capensis f. frondescens, commonly known as the Cape rush, is a variant of the Cape rush species native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as riverbanks and wetlands, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered, often in green or reddish hues.

This plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
