Justicia membranifolia

Justicia membranifolia, commonly known as the paperleaf flameflower, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It features lance-shaped, thin, and often translucent leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in clusters, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and greenhouses.
