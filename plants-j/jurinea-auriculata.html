Jurinea auriculata

Jurinea auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate jurinea, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in desert or semi-desert environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh conditions.

Jurinea auriculata is well adapted to extreme temperatures and is a key component of its ecosystem.
