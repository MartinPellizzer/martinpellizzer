Jussiaea biacuminata

Jussiaea biacuminata, commonly known as the Brazilian water hyacinth, is a floating aquatic plant native to South America.

It possesses broad, waxy leaves that allow it to remain buoyant on the water's surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Jussiaea biacuminata is known for its rapid growth rate, which can lead to dense populations in still or slow-moving water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems, though it can also become invasive in non-native environments.
