Juncus falcatus subsp. falcatus

Juncus falcatus subsp.

falcatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America. It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often in marshes, swamps, or along the edges of ponds and streams.

The plant has slender, grass-like stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, spike-like clusters of greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midvein, and it is often found in dense stands.

The species is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
