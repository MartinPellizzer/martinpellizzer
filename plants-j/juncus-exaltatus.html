Juncus exaltatus

Juncus exaltatus, commonly known as the tall rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often forming dense clumps. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green and slightly hairy. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters at the top of the stems, which are wind-pollinated.

Juncus exaltatus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
