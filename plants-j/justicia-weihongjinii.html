Justicia weihongjinii

Justicia weihongjinii, commonly known as the Weihongjin's flame flower, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, producing clusters of showy bracts that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
