Juncus acutus microcarpus

Juncus acutus microcarpus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a species of flowering plant in the family Juncaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 1 millimeter in width. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense spikes, and it produces small, three-lobed seeds.

This plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
