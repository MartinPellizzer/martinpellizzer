Jacksonia dendrospinosa

Jacksonia dendrospinosa, commonly known as the tree dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and yellow, dandelion-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Jacksonia dendrospinosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
