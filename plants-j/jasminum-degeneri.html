Jasminum degeneri

Jasminum degeneri, commonly known as the Arabian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, usually during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Jasminum degeneri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in landscaping and traditional medicine.
