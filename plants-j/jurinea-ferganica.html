Jurinea ferganica

Jurinea ferganica, commonly known as the Fergana sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its spiny, leaf-like bracts that surround its small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in saline and alkaline soils, often in desert or semi-desert environments. It is adapted to withstand extreme drought and temperature fluctuations.

Jurinea ferganica is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its distinctive appearance.
