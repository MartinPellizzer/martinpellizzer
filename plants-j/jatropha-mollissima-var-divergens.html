Jatropha mollissima var. divergens

Jatropha mollissima var. divergens, commonly known as the velvetleaf jatropha, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers followed by orange-red berries containing toxic seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for medicinal use, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional medicine practices.
