Juncus spretus

Juncus spretus, commonly known as the slender rush, is a flowering plant in the family Juncaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and cylindrical flowering spikes. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its slender stems are often used in basket weaving and as a decorative plant in water gardens.

Juncus spretus plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
