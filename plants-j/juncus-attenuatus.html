Juncus attenuatus

Juncus attenuatus, commonly known as the slender rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in moist to aquatic habitats such as marshes, swamps, and stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters near the base of the plant. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, which helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Juncus attenuatus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
