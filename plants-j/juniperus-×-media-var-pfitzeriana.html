Juniperus × media var. pfitzeriana

Juniperus × media var. pfitzeriana, commonly known as the Pfitzer juniper, is a hybrid conifer native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and blue-green foliage that turns bronze in winter. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are typically dark blue when mature. It is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.

Botanically, it is a cross between Juniperus communis and Juniperus silicicola, exhibiting traits of both parents.
