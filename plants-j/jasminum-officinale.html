Jasminum officinale

Jasminum officinale, commonly known as common jasmine, is a flowering plant native to regions of southern Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, simple, and usually hairy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the evening, releasing a strong, sweet scent. These flowers are rich in essential oils and are widely used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
