Jupunba cochleata var. cochleata

Jupunba cochleata var. cochleata, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a species native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in alpine ecosystems.
