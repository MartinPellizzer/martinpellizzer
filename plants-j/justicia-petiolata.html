Justicia petiolata

Justicia petiolata, commonly known as the red flame plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant floral display.

Justicia petiolata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
