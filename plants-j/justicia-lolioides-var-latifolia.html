Justicia lolioides var. latifolia

Justicia lolioides var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf flame bush, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking clusters of bright red, tubular flowers. The leaves are broad and ovate, often with a waxy texture, and the plant produces small, blue-black berries that are edible for some animals. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
