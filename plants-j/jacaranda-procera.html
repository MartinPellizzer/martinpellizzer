Jacaranda procera

Jacaranda procera, commonly known as the African jacaranda, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The tree has a broad, spreading canopy with pinnate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for construction and furniture.

The plant is also popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, sunny climates.
