Justicia pulgarensis

Justicia pulgarensis, commonly known as Mexican flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical gardens.
