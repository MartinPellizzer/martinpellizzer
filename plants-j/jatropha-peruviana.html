Jatropha peruviana

Jatropha peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian physic nut, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing toxic seeds. The leaves and seeds contain various toxic compounds, including bufotenin and other alkaloids.

It is often grown for its ornamental value and has been studied for potential use in medicinal and agricultural applications.
