Juncus acuminatus var. debilis

Juncus acuminatus var. debilis, commonly known as the slender rush, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, which are usually greenish or brownish in hue. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, pond edges, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
