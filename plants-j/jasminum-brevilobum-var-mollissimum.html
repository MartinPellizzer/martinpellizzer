Jasminum brevilobum var. mollissimum

Jasminum brevilobum var. mollissimum, commonly known as the silky jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a sweet, intoxicating scent. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silken hair, giving it its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic benefits.
