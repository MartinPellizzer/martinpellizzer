Jasminum bidwillii

Jasminum bidwillii, commonly known as the golden jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant typically grows as a shrub or a small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture.

Jasminum bidwillii thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
