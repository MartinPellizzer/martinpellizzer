Julocroton triqueter var. maculatus

Julocroton triqueter var. maculatus, commonly known as the spotted croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that display a mix of green, yellow, and red hues, with distinctive red or orange spots. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
