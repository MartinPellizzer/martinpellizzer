Juniperus communis var. suecica

Juniperus communis var. suecica, commonly known as the Swedish juniper, is a subspecies of the common juniper native to northern Europe.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, scale-like leaves and is characterized by its blue-green foliage. Its berries, which are toxic to humans, are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

This subspecies is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine or coastal regions.
