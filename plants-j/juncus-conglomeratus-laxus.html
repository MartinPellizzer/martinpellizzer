Juncus conglomeratus laxus

Juncus conglomeratus laxus, commonly known as the loose clustered rush, is a perennial plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves that form a compact, mat-like growth. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color, arranged in spikelets along the stems. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in areas with standing water or saturated ground.

Juncus conglomeratus laxus plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
