Justicia viridiflora

Justicia viridiflora, commonly known as the greenflower justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows as an herb or shrub, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine pubescence, while its stems are often hairy.

The flowers are borne in whorled clusters and are pollinated primarily by birds, particularly hummingbirds.
