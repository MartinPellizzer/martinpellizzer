Justicia heterocarpoides

Justicia heterocarpoides, commonly known as the velvetleaf justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are typically hairy and have a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinct purple or red throat. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and clearings.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
