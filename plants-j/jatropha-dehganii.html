Jatropha dehganii

Jatropha dehganii, commonly known as the Persian physic plant, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Jatropha.

It is native to parts of Asia, including Iran and Afghanistan, and is typically found in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. Jatropha dehganii produces small, greenish fruits that contain toxic seeds, which are not suitable for human consumption.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been studied for potential medicinal properties.
