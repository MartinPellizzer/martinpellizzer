Jasminum officinale f. grandiflorum

Jasminum officinale f. grandiflorum, commonly known as the grandiflorum jasmine, is a flowering plant native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, featuring opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. Its flowers are highly valued in perfumery and traditional medicine due to their strong aromatic properties.

The species is also cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as a hedge plant.
