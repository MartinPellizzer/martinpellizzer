Juncus filiformis var. transsilvanicus

Juncus filiformis var. transsilvanicus, commonly known as the Transylvanian rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and damp woodlands. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering spikes consist of small, pale green to brownish flowers that appear in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
