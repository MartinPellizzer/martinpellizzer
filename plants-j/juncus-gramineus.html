Juncus gramineus

Juncus gramineus, commonly known as slender rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland areas in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a fine, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in moist or wet soils, making it a common sight in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in late spring or early summer.

Juncus gramineus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
