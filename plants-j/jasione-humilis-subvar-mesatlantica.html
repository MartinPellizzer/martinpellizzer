Jasione humilis subvar. mesatlantica

Jasione humilis subvar. mesatlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic bellflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the coastal regions of the eastern United States.

It features delicate blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline. It is well adapted to salt spray and can tolerate periodic flooding.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
