Jacksonia arenicola

Jacksonia arenicola, commonly known as the sandhill daisy, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it often forms dense, low-growing clumps.

Jacksonia arenicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
