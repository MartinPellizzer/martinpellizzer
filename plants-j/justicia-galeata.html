Justicia galeata

Justicia galeata, commonly known as the red fountain plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and containers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Justicia galeata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
