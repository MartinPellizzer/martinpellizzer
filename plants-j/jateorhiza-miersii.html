Jateorhiza miersii

Jateorhiza miersii, commonly known as the Miers' ginseng, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Araliaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, clustered root system that resembles the structure of true ginseng. Due to habitat destruction and overharvesting, Jateorhiza miersii is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
