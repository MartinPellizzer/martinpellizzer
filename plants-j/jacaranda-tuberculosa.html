Jacaranda tuberculosa

Jacaranda tuberculosa, commonly known as the velvet jacaranda, is a deciduous tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters during the winter season. The tree has compound leaves with small, velvety leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its bark is smooth and greyish, often developing fissures with age.

Jacaranda tuberculosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
