Justicia inficiens

Justicia inficiens, commonly known as the red-veined strobilanthes, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are often marked with distinctive red veins, adding to its ornamental value.

It is cultivated for its vibrant blooms and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
