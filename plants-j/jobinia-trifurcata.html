Jobinia trifurcata

Jobinia trifurcata, commonly known as the three-branch tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive branching pattern, with three main branches emerging from the base. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a rough texture.

Jobinia trifurcata is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
