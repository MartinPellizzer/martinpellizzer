Jasminum didymum f. subacutum

Jasminum didymum f. subacutum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic flowers that bloom in clusters, typically white or pale yellow. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often described as narrow and pointed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in gardens and as a hedge plant.
