Jasminum mitchellii

Jasminum mitchellii, commonly known as Mitchell's jasmine, is a flowering plant belonging to the Oleaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of India, Thailand, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

The leaves are opposite, simple, and have a glossy dark green appearance, while the flowers emit a sweet, intoxicating fragrance.
