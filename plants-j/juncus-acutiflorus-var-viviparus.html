Juncus acutiflorus var. viviparus

Juncus acutiflorus var. viviparus, commonly known as the viviparous rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and its ability to produce bulbils, which are small plantlets that can grow into new plants. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, inconspicuous flowers.

This variety is notable for its reproductive strategy, as the bulbils allow for rapid vegetative propagation and colonization of new habitats.
