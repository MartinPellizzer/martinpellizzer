Juncus fauriensis var. iwatensis

Juncus fauriensis var. iwatensis, commonly known as the Iwat Islands rush, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and cylindrical flower clusters. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and stream banks, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soil. Its slender stems and small, spike-like inflorescences make it a distinctive member of the rush family.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
