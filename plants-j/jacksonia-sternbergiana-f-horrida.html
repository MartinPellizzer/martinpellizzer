Jacksonia sternbergiana f. horrida

Jacksonia sternbergiana f. horrida, commonly known as the Horrid Jacksonia, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its striking, dark purple to black bracts that surround the small yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, often in the Southern Alps. It has a compact growth habit with a thick, hairy stem and is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

This fern-like plant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
