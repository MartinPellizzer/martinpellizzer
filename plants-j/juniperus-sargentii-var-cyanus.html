Juniperus sargentii var. cyanus

Juniperus sargentii var. cyanus, commonly known as the blue juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark blue-green foliage that gives it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that mature into blue or purple berries, which are often used in landscaping for their ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
