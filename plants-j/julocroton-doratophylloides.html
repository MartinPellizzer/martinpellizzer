Julocroton doratophylloides

Julocroton doratophylloides, commonly known as the golden croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, which often displays shades of yellow, orange, and red. The leaves are typically large, palmately lobed, and have a waxy texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates.
