Justicia ecbolium

Justicia ecbolium, commonly known as the red-rooted false arum, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, violet-purple bracts that resemble those of a true arum. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture and emits a mild, sweet fragrance. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of small, tubular flowers.

The plant is often grown for its ornamental value in shaded garden settings.
