Justicia infracta

Justicia infracta, commonly known as the red mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Justicia infracta is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
