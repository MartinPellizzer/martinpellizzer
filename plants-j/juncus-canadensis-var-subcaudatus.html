Juncus canadensis var. subcaudatus

Juncus canadensis var. subcaudatus, commonly known as the Canadian rush or narrowleaf rush, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems that often grow in wet or moist environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically arranged in a tight, cylindrical cluster.

This variety is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams.
