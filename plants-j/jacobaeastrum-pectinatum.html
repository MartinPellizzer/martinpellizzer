Jacobaeastrum pectinatum

Jacobaeastrum pectinatum, commonly known as the pectinately-leaved ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its spiny stems and yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant has pinnately divided, pectinate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, demonstrating a high degree of adaptability to harsh conditions.

Jacobaeastrum pectinatum is often found in alpine meadows and is a component of the local flora in its native habitat.
