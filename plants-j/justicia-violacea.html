Justicia violacea

Justicia violacea, commonly known as purpleleaf false arum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple foliage, which contrasts beautifully with its white to pink flowers. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Justicia violacea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant leaves and unique floral display.
