Juglans brasiliensis

Juglans brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian walnut, is a large deciduous tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It belongs to the Juglandaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, edible nuts. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a broad, spreading crown. Its bark is thick and fissured, often featuring a rough, dark brown texture.

The plant produces yellowish-green flowers and has a deep root system that helps stabilize the soil in its natural habitat.
