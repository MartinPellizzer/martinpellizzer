Justicia calyculata

Justicia calyculata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Justicia calyculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
