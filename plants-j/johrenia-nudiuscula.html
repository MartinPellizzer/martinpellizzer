Johrenia nudiuscula

Johrenia nudiuscula, commonly known as the slender johrenia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and heat. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and limestone outcrops.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
