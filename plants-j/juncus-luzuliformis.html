Juncus luzuliformis

Juncus luzuliformis, commonly known as the silver rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, stream banks, and woodland edges. The plant features narrow, cylindrical leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom, giving it its common name. Its flowering spikes are usually erect and bear small, greenish-brown flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences.

Juncus luzuliformis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native wetland habitats.
