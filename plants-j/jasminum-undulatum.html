Jasminum undulatum

Jasminum undulatum, commonly known as the wavy jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a wavy margin, giving it its common name.

Jasminum undulatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
