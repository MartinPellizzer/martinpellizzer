Justicia charthaginensis

Justicia charthaginensis, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The genus Justicia is part of the Acanthaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often used in ornamental horticulture.
