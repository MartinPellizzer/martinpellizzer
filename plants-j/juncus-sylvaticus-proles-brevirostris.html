Juncus sylvaticus proles brevirostris

Juncus sylvaticus proles brevirostris, commonly known as the narrow-beak rush, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage composed of narrow, cylindrical leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Its adaptability makes it a common species in both natural and cultivated landscapes.
