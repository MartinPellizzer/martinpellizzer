Juncus interior var. neomexicanus

Juncus interior var. neomexicanus, commonly known as the New Mexico rush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and tall, cylindrical flower spikes. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks, wetlands, and other aquatic habitats. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that are often green or brown in color.

This variety is distinguished by its slightly larger size and more robust growth compared to other juncus species.
