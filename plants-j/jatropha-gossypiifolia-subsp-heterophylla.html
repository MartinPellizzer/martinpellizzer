Jatropha gossypiifolia subsp. heterophylla

Jatropha gossypiifolia subsp.

heterophylla, commonly known as the Indian physic or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant produces toxic, orange-red berries that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy pulp. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for use in biofuel production, it has been introduced to various parts of the world.
