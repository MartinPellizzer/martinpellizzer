Juniperus occidentalis var. utahensis

Juniperus occidentalis var. utahensis, commonly known as the Utah juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves and small, berry-like cones that are typically bluish-black in color. This variety is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant is often used in landscaping due to its hardiness and ability to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
