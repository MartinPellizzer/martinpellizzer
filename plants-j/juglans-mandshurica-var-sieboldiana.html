Juglans mandshurica var. sieboldiana

Juglans mandshurica var. sieboldiana, commonly known as the Siberian walnut, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Juglandaceae family and is characterized by its large, doubly serrated leaves. The tree produces distinctive, spiny husks that encase its edible, oily nuts. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

The plant is valued for its hard, durable wood and its potential economic and ecological importance.
