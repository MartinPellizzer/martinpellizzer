Jacobaea alpina

Jacobaea alpina, commonly known as Alpine groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, dry conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Jacobaea alpina is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
