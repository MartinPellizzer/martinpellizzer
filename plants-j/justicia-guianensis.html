Justicia guianensis

Justicia guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan flamebeard, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy inflorescences that resemble flame-like clusters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically red or orange with a distinctive yellow or orange beard-like structure at the base.

Justicia guianensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
