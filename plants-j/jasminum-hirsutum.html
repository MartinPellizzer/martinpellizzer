Jasminum hirsutum

Jasminum hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has slender, woody stems and hairy leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Jasminum hirsutum is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
