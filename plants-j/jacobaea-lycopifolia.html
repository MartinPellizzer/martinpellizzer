Jacobaea lycopifolia

Jacobaea lycopifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ragwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features lance-shaped, often hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by elongated, cylindrical seed heads.

Jacobaea lycopifolia is known for its toxic properties, containing pyrrolizidine alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock and humans.
