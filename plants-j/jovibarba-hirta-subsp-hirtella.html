Jovibarba hirta subsp. hirtella

Jovibarba hirta subsp.

hirtella, commonly known as the hairy stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are covered in fine, hairy structures, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
