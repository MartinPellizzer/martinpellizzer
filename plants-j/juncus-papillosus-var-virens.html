Juncus papillosus var. virens

Juncus papillosus var. virens, commonly known as the green tufted rush, is a variant of the tufted rush characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves.

It is a perennial plant native to wetland and marshy environments, often found in temperate regions. The plant typically grows in dense clumps, with its cylindrical stems reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are usually narrow, slightly hairy, and have a bluish-green hue.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
