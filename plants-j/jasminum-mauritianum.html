Jasminum mauritianum

Jasminum mauritianum, commonly known as the African jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring opposite, simple, and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It has a woody stem with a rough, fissured bark, and its root system is fibrous and extensive.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
