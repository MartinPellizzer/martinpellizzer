Jasminum fragrans

Jasminum fragrans, commonly known as the common jasmine, is a flowering plant native to regions of southern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, emitting a sweet, intoxicating aroma. The plant is a shrub or vine, often cultivated for its ornamental value and use in perfumery. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

The flowers are typically four-petaled and are pollinated by insects, contributing to its ecological significance.
