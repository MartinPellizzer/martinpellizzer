Jacaranda copaia subsp. copaia

Jacaranda copaia subsp.

copaia, commonly known as the copaia jacaranda, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its striking violet-pink flowers that bloom in clusters.

The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets. The bark is smooth and grayish in color, often with a fragrant scent.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its shade and floral display.
