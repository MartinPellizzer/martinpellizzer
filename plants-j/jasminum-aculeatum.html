Jasminum aculeatum

Jasminum aculeatum, commonly known as the thorned jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has woody stems covered with sharp, thorn-like projections, which serve as a defense mechanism. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate with a glossy surface.

Jasminum aculeatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant scent.
