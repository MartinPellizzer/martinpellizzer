Jacquemontia azurea

Jacquemontia azurea, commonly known as blue jacquemontia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a distinct, slightly hairy stem. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or shaded areas.

Jacquemontia azurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique foliage.
