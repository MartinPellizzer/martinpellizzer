Jamesonia caracasana

Jamesonia caracasana, commonly known as the Caracas waxplant, is a perennial shrub native to the Caribbean region.

It features showy, waxy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has leathery, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
