Juncus foliosus

Juncus foliosus, commonly known as the foliaceous rush, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are often densely packed. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its flowering stems produce small, clustered inflorescences that are often green or reddish in color.

Juncus foliosus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
