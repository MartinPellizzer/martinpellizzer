Juglans regia var. filicifolia

Juglans regia var. filicifolia, commonly known as the Chinese walnut, is a variant of the Persian walnut.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that resemble ferns, giving it its specific epithet "filicifolia.". The plant produces edible nuts enclosed in a thick, green husk that splits open when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its high-quality nuts.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
