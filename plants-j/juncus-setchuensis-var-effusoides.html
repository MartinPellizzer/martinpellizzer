Juncus setchuensis var. effusoides

Juncus setchuensis var. effusoides, commonly known as the soft-haired rush, is a perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant features slender, grass-like stems that are often covered in soft, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered spikes of brownish or yellowish flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
