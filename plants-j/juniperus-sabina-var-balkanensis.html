Juniperus sabina var. balkanensis

Juniperus sabina var. balkanensis, commonly known as the Balkan juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage and small, blue-black berries. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates. It has aromatic leaves that release a sharp, resinous scent when crushed.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.
