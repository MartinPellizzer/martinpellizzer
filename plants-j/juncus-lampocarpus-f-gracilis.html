Juncus lampocarpus f. gracilis

Juncus lampocarpus f. gracilis, commonly known as the slender rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Juncaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and flowering spikes that bear small, greenish flowers. The plant is native to coastal regions of southern California and thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. It plays a role in stabilizing dune ecosystems and provides habitat for various invertebrates.

Its growth habit and ecological importance make it a notable species in coastal botany.
