Jussiaea amasonica

Jussiaea amasonica, commonly known as the Amazonian waterplant, is a species of aquatic plant native to the freshwater ecosystems of South America.

It typically grows in slow-moving or still waters, such as rivers and lakes, and is often found in the Amazon Basin. The plant has slender, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface, while its roots anchor it to the substrate. It plays an important role in aquatic habitats by providing shelter for small fish and invertebrates.

Jussiaea amasonica is also valued for its ornamental qualities in water gardens.
