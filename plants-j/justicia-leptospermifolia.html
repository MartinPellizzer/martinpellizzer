Justicia leptospermifolia

Justicia leptospermifolia, commonly known as the red mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, spike-like inflorescences of pink to red flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for wetland restoration and ornamental gardening.
