Jacksonia grevilleoides

Jacksonia grevilleoides, commonly known as the Grevillea-like daisy or the coastal daisy, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in coastal regions and is adapted to sandy soils and saline conditions. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in a head.

Jacksonia grevilleoides is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
