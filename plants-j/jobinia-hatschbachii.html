Jobinia hatschbachii

Jobinia hatschbachii, commonly known as the Namib Desert lichen, is a unique plant that thrives in the harsh conditions of the Namib Desert.

It is a symbiotic lichen, composed of a fungal component and a cyanobacterial partner, which allows it to survive in arid environments with minimal water. The plant forms dense, cushion-like growths that help reduce water loss and protect against extreme temperatures. Its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen makes it an important contributor to soil fertility in nutrient-poor regions.

Due to its specialized adaptations, Jobinia hatschbachii plays a crucial ecological role in sustaining desert ecosystems.
