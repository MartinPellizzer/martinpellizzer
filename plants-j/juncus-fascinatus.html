Juncus fascinatus

Juncus fascinatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in marshes, pond edges, and along streams, where it thrives in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often green to reddish in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters during the summer months. Juncus fascinatus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to the ecological structure of wetland ecosystems.
