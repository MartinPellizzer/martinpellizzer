Juglans obcordata

Juglans obcordata, commonly known as the Arizona walnut, is a deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the walnut family, Juglandaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and edible nuts. The tree produces obcordate-shaped nuts, which are encased in a thick, hairy husk. Its bark is rough and fissured, and it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant plays an important role in local ecosystems and has been used by indigenous peoples for food and traditional medicine.
