Juncus hesperius

Juncus hesperius, commonly known as the western rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant has cylindrical, grass-like leaves that are often densely packed, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Juncus hesperius is adapted to tolerate periodic flooding and is an important component of wetland ecosystems.
