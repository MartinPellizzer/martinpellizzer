Juncus anceps var. sparsiflorus

Juncus anceps var. sparsiflorus, commonly known as the sparseflower rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged in a dense, tufted form at the base.

This variety is characterized by its sparse flowering pattern, which distinguishes it from other juncus species.
