Jambosa buxifolia

Jambosa buxifolia, commonly known as the waxleaf jambosa, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its glossy, dark green, almost waxy leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish-green flowers that are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Jambosa buxifolia is relatively low-maintenance and can be grown in containers or in gardens with well-draining soil.
