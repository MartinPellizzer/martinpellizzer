Justicia frondosa

Justicia frondosa, commonly known as the fronded flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Justicia frondosa is also known for its ability to grow in partial shade to full sun, making it adaptable to various garden settings.
