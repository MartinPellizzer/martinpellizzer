Juncus salsuginosus subsp. tuvinicus

Juncus salsuginosus subsp.

tuvinicus, commonly known as the Tuvinian rush, is a perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and flowering spikes that typically bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Its narrow, cylindrical leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy or saline soils.
