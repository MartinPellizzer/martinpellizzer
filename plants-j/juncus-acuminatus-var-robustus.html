Juncus acuminatus var. robustus

Juncus acuminatus var. robustus, commonly known as the robust rush, is a flowering plant native to wetland and marsh environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, covered in narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers at the top of the stem, typically in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in saturated soils and is often found in tidal areas, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is notable for its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic ecosystems.
