Justicia tenella f. crenulata

Justicia tenella f. crenulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf flame flower, is a compact shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in clusters, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which attract hummingbirds and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

This ornamental plant is valued for its vibrant blooms and ability to add color to gardens in warm climates.
