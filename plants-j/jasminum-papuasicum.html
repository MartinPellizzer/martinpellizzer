Jasminum papuasicum

Jasminum papuasicum, commonly known as the Papuan jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Indonesia.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a smooth texture.

Jasminum papuasicum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its pleasant aroma.
