Julbernardia magnistipulata

Julbernardia magnistipulata, commonly known as the broad-leaved Julbernardia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces woody pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
