Juncus tenageia subsp. acicularis

Juncus tenageia subsp.

acicularis, commonly known as the slender rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of greenish-white flowers.

The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and roadside ditches. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense stands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
