Juniperus communis f. crispa

Juniperus communis f. crispa, commonly known as the curled juniper, is a compact, slow-growing shrub characterized by its dense, twisted foliage.

Its leaves are typically scale-like and tightly pressed against the stems, giving the plant a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and as a decorative hedge.

This variety is prized for its unique form and hardiness in colder climates.
