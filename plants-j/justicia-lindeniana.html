Justicia lindeniana

Justicia lindeniana, commonly known as the Lindena's flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular, red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by birds, especially hummingbirds, due to their bright colors and nectar-rich structure.
