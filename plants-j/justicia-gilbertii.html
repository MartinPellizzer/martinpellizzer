Justicia gilbertii

Justicia gilbertii, commonly known as Gilbert's flame flower, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vivid, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet, with lance-shaped leaves and showy bracts that enhance its visual appeal. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping in arid regions.

Its flowering season spans from spring to fall, adding continuous color to gardens and landscapes.
