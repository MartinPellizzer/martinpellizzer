Juncus bufonius f. giganteus

Juncus bufonius f. giganteus, commonly known as the giant bog rush, is a tall, herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features long, cylindrical, grass-like leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in height, making it a striking presence in marshy areas. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers at the top of its stems, which are typical of the Juncaceae family. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its height and dense growth habit, it plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
