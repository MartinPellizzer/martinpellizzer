Juncus dombeyanus

Juncus dombeyanus, commonly known as the Dombey's rush, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist or wet environments such as marshes, stream banks, and damp woodlands. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and bear small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming dense clumps that help stabilize soil in wetland habitats.

Juncus dombeyanus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
