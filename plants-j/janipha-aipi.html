Janipha aipi

Janipha aipi, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange and yellow flowers, which resemble flames and bloom during the warmer months. The plant has strap-like, dark green leaves that are thick and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in humid environments. It thrives in partial shade and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil to flourish.

Janipha aipi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of resilience and beauty in its natural habitat.
