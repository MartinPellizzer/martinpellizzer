Jasione crispa subsp. segurensis

Jasione crispa subsp.

segurensis, commonly known as Segurens' hoary vetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in dry, rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny environments.

The plant features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are hairy and grayish-green, contributing to its common name.

It is often found in Mediterranean regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role.
