Juniperus virginiana var. silicicola

Juniperus virginiana var. silicicola, commonly known as the sandhill juniper, is a variant of the eastern redcedar that thrives in sandy or well-drained soils.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and small, scale-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It is native to the southeastern United States and is adapted to harsh, dry environments.

This variety is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
