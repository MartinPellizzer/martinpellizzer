Justicia dasycarpa

Justicia dasycarpa, commonly known as the rough-seeded justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs, while the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release its seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
