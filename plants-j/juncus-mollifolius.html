Juncus mollifolius

Juncus mollifolius, commonly known as soft-leaved rush, is a perennial plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It features grass-like, cylindrical stems that are often reddish or greenish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters at the ends of its stems, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are soft and hair-like, contributing to its common name.

Juncus mollifolius plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
