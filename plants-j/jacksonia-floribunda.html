Jacksonia floribunda

Jacksonia floribunda, commonly known as the New Zealand daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and white. The plant typically grows in coastal and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are often hairy.

Jacksonia floribunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
