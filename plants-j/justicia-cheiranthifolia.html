Justicia cheiranthifolia

Justicia cheiranthifolia, commonly known as the flame flower or red flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Justicia cheiranthifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in warm climates.
