Juncus gubanovii

Juncus gubanovii, commonly known as the Gubanovii rush, is a species of flowering plant in the family Juncaceae.

It is native to the steppes and semi-desert regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Russia. This plant typically grows in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish or brownish flowers in dense clusters.

Juncus gubanovii is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance.
