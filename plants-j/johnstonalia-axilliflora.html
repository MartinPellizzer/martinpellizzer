Johnstonalia axilliflora

Johnstonalia axilliflora, commonly known as the axillary-flowered Johnstonalia, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces delicate, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in the spring, typically in clusters along the leaf axils. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
