Jasminum roaeoalbum

Jasminum roaeoalbum, commonly known as the white-flowered jasmine, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white blooms that emit a sweet scent, particularly at night. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, with opposite, simple leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. Its flowers are clustered in axillary inflorescences, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Jasminum roaeoalbum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and in traditional medicine.
