Juncus bachitii

Juncus bachitii, commonly known as the Sierra bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. It produces tiny blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Juncus bachitii is often found in meadows and along stream banks, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
