Juncus pediformis

Juncus pediformis, commonly known as the pedicellate rush, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often grow in dense clusters, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically borne on elongated pedicels. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and they are often covered with a fine layer of hairs.

Juncus pediformis plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
