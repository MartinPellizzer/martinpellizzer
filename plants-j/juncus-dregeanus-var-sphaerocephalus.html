Juncus dregeanus var. sphaerocephalus

Juncus dregeanus var. sphaerocephalus, commonly known as the spherical rush, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves that form a compact rosette. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spherical or club-shaped inflorescence, which gives it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.
