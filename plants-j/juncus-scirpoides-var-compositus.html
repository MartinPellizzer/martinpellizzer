Juncus scirpoides var. compositus

Juncus scirpoides var. compositus, commonly known as the composite rush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It typically grows in marshy areas, pond edges, and along streams, where it thrives in moist to wet soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often overlooked due to their subtle appearance. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which can help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

This species plays a vital role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
