Juncus vaginatus

Juncus vaginatus, commonly known as the common rush, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow, hollow, and often have a whitish or yellowish sheath at the base.

Juncus vaginatus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
