Jamesbrittenia myriantha

Jamesbrittenia myriantha, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the study of tropical flora.
