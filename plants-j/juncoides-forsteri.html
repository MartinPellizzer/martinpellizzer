Juncoides forsteri

Juncoides forsteri, commonly known as the Forster's rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its reproductive structures consist of spikelets that bear both male and female flowers, facilitating wind pollination.

Juncoides forsteri plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
