Justicia tremulifolia

Justicia tremulifolia, commonly known as the trembling justicia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which are arranged in terminal spikes. Its scientific name reflects its trembling leaves, which are easily disturbed by wind or touch.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils in full sun.
