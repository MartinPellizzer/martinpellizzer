Jacobaeastrum sulcatum

Jacobaeastrum sulcatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a distinctive wavy margin, contributing to its ornamental value.

Jacobaeastrum sulcatum plays a role in supporting local pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
