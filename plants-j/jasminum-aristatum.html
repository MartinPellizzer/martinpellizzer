Jasminum aristatum

Jasminum aristatum, commonly known as the mountain jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its aromatic, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture, while the stems are woody and often covered in small spines. This species is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrant blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.
