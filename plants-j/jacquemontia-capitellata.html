Jacquemontia capitellata

Jacquemontia capitellata, commonly known as the purpleleaf morning glory, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant features heart-shaped, palmate leaves with a distinctive purple underside, which contribute to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Jacquemontia capitellata is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is also valued in ecological gardens for its ability to attract pollinators.
