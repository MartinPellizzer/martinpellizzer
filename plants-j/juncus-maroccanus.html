Juncus maroccanus

Juncus maroccanus, commonly known as the Moroccan rush, is a perennial plant native to North Africa, particularly Morocco.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and typically grows in wet or marshy environments. The plant has slender, grass-like stems that are often used in basket weaving and traditional crafts. Its flowering spikes consist of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Juncus maroccanus is valued for its adaptability to moist soils and its ecological role in stabilizing wetland habitats.
