Justicia calcicola

Justicia calcicola, commonly known as the limestone justicia, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions. The plant produces striking spikes of bright blue or purple flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows on rocky outcrops or in limestone soils, showcasing its preference for calcareous environments.

Due to its unique adaptations and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in xeriscapes and rock gardens.
