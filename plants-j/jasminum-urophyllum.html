Jasminum urophyllum

Jasminum urophyllum, commonly known as the mountain jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a sweet scent. The plant typically grows in forested areas and is adapted to humid, tropical climates. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, with a glossy texture.

Jasminum urophyllum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
