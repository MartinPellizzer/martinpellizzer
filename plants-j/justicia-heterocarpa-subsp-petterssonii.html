Justicia heterocarpa subsp. petterssonii

Justicia heterocarpa subsp.

petterssonii, commonly known as the narrowleaf justicia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central America and the Caribbean. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of tubular, violet-blue flowers.

The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
