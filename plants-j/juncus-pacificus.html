Juncus pacificus

Juncus pacificus, commonly known as the Pacific rush, is a perennial plant native to coastal regions of the Pacific Northwest.

It typically grows in moist, sandy or gravelly soils along shorelines and wetlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a valuable species in coastal ecosystems.
