Justicia Procumbens Subsp. Latispica

Justicia procumbens subsp. latispica is a unique flowering plant native to parts of South America. It is known for its vibrant, showy bracts that attract a variety of pollinators. This subspecies is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories. Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a fascinating subject for botanists and gardeners alike.
