Juncoides gigantea

Juncoides gigantea, commonly known as the giant rush, is a tall, herbaceous plant native to certain regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Juncaginaceae and is characterized by its long, cylindrical stems that are often used in floral arrangements. The plant thrives in moist environments such as wetlands and marshes, where it forms dense stands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Juncoides gigantea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
