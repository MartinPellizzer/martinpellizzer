Juniperus oxycedrus subsp. badia

Juniperus oxycedrus subsp.

badia, commonly known as the Corsican juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are edible and have a spicy, aromatic flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic wood.
