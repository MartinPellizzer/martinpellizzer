Juanulloa umbellata

Juanulloa umbellata, commonly known as the white umbellate tree, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white, umbellate flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests. Its botanical features include a woody stem and small, elliptical leaves with a smooth surface.

Juanulloa umbellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
