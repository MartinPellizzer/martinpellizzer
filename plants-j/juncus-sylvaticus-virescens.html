Juncus sylvaticus virescens

Juncus sylvaticus virescens, commonly known as the green rush, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in early summer, adding a subtle floral element to its appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
