Jasminum alongense

Jasminum alongense, commonly known as the African jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually hairy leaves, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Jasminum alongense is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
