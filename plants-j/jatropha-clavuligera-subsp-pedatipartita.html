Jatropha clavuligera subsp. pedatipartita

Jatropha clavuligera subsp.

pedatipartita, commonly known as the broadleaf jatropha, is a shrub native to parts of Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that are often green with a waxy coating.

The plant produces yellow flowers followed by orange or red berries containing toxic seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
