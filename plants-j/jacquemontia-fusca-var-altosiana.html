Jacquemontia fusca var. altosiana

Jacquemontia fusca var. altosiana, commonly known as the white-flowered morning glory, is a herbaceous vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has a climbing habit, with tendrils that help it ascend supports. Its flowers are typically trumpet-shaped and emit a mild fragrance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.
