Juniperus excelsa subsp. turcomanica

Juniperus excelsa subsp.

turcomanica, commonly known as the Turkestan juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its scale-like leaves and produces small, bluish-black berries.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh conditions. It has a slow growth rate and can live for several decades.

Its botanical features make it an important species in its native ecosystems.
