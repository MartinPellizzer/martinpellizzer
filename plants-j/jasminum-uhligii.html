Jasminum uhligii

Jasminum uhligii, commonly known as the Arabian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often at the ends of the branches. These flowers are known for their strong, sweet scent, which is released in the evening.

Jasminum uhligii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
