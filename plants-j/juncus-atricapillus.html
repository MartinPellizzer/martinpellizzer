Juncus atricapillus

Juncus atricapillus, commonly known as black-tipped rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and moist environments in North America.

It has tall, cylindrical stems that are typically dark green to reddish in color, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of brownish flowers at the top of the stems. It is often found in marshes, pond edges, and along streams, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.

Juncus atricapillus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wet conditions.
