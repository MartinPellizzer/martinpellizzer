Justicia jamaicensis

Justicia jamaicensis, commonly known as Jamaican flame flower, is a tropical perennial shrub native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular, red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a woody stem that can reach up to 3 meters in height. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers attract hummingbirds and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
