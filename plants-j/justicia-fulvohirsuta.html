Justicia fulvohirsuta

Justicia fulvohirsuta, commonly known as the hairy flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for warm climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
