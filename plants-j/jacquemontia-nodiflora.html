Jacquemontia nodiflora

Jacquemontia nodiflora, commonly known as the clustered yellow vine, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features trailing stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are usually hairy. The plant produces clusters of showy yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
