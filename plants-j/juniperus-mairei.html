Juniperus mairei

Juniperus mairei, commonly known as the Maire juniper, is a species of evergreen conifer native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves and small, berry-like cones that mature to a dark blue color. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its foliage provides habitat for various native birds and insects, contributing to local biodiversity.

Juniperus mairei is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
