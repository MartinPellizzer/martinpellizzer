Jasminanthes pilosa

Jasminanthes pilosa, commonly known as the hairy jasminanthes, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine, soft hairs.

Jasminanthes pilosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated for its pleasant aroma.
