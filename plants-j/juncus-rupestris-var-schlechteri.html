Juncus rupestris var. schlechteri

Juncus rupestris var. schlechteri, commonly known as the Schlechter's rush, is a perennial plant native to rocky and alpine environments in Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and compact growth habit, typically forming tight clusters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in crevices and on slopes.

Its botanical features make it an adaptable and resilient species suitable for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
