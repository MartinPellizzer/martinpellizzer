Justicia hassleri

Justicia hassleri, commonly known as the Mexican flame bush, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking red, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Justicia hassleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various climates.
