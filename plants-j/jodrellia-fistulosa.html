Jodrellia fistulosa

Jodrellia fistulosa, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain seeds.

Jodrellia fistulosa is often found in sagebrush steppe and other semi-arid habitats.
