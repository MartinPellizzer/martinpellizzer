Justicia teucriflora

Justicia teucriflora, commonly known as the orange flame plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red bracts that resemble flames, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Justicia teucriflora is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is known for its ability to grow in a variety of soil types.
