Jambosa longipes

Jambosa longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed jambosa, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stem and large, elongated leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. Its fruit is a fleshy, berry-like structure that contains numerous seeds.

Jambosa longipes is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
