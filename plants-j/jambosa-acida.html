Jambosa acida

Jambosa acida, commonly known as the sour jambosa, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically sour and acidic, hence its common name. Its botanical features include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a preference for humid, warm climates.

It is often cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value.
