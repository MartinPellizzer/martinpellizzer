Jacquemontia glabrescens

Jacquemontia glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth jacquemontia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in open woods. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals and a distinctive, spurred structure at the base of the flower. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs.

The smooth jacquemontia is often found in wetland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
