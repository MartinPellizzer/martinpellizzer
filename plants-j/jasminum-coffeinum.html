Jasminum coffeinum

Jasminum coffeinum, commonly known as coffee jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often releasing a sweet scent in the evening. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, dark blue berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

The species is related to other jasmine plants but is distinguished by its unique fragrance and fruiting characteristics.
