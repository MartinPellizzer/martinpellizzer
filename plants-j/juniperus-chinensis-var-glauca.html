Juniperus chinensis var. glauca

Juniperus chinensis var. glauca, commonly known as the Chinese juniper or blue spruce, is a shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves that are typically a striking blue-gray color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are edible and often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and is relatively drought-tolerant, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping and horticultural purposes.
