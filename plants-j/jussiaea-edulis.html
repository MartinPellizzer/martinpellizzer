Jussiaea edulis

Jussiaea edulis, commonly known as the edible water plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its floating leaves and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are often harvested for consumption, as they are rich in nutrients and have a mild, slightly sweet taste.

Jussiaea edulis plays an important ecological role by providing habitat for aquatic organisms and contributing to water quality through oxygen production.
