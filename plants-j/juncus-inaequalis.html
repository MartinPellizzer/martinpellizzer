Juncus inaequalis

Juncus inaequalis, commonly known as the unequal rush, is a flowering plant species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and pond edges. Its slender, grass-like leaves are usually green to reddish in color, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Juncus inaequalis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
