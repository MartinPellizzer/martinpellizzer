Juncus bulbosus var. acutiflorus

Juncus bulbosus var. acutiflorus, commonly known as the sharpflower rush, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and damp woodlands. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. It produces small, spike-like flower clusters that bloom in late summer, adding ornamental value to wetland gardens.

This variety is valued for its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and its ecological role in supporting aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
