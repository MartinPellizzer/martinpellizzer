Jamesonia lindigii

Jamesonia lindigii, commonly known as the Lindig's evergreen lily, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, strap-like leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and a short, sturdy stem.

It is a member of the Liliaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like tulips and onions.
