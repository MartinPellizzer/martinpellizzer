Juniperus pingii var. pingii

Juniperus pingii var. pingii, commonly known as the Chinese juniper or mountain juniper, is a slow-growing evergreen shrub or small tree native to regions of China, Japan, and Korea.

It is characterized by its dense, scaly foliage that ranges from gray-green to blue-green in color. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are typically dark blue or purple when mature. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in colder climates for its winter interest and architectural form.
