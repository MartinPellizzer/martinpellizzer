Juncus pygmaeus f. submersus

Juncus pygmaeus f. submersus, commonly known as the submerged rush, is a variant of the common rush that thrives in aquatic or semi-aquatic environments.

This plant exhibits submerged growth habits, with its stems and leaves adapted to underwater conditions. Its leaves are typically narrow and cylindrical, designed to reduce water resistance and facilitate gas exchange. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged or partially submerged, typical of its wetland habitat.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
