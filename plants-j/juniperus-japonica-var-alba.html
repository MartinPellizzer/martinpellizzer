Juniperus japonica var. alba

Juniperus japonica var. alba, commonly known as the white Japanese juniper, is a cultivar of the Japanese juniper that is prized for its ornamental value.

It is a coniferous evergreen shrub or small tree characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features scale-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a distinctive silvery-blue hue on the underside. Its cones are small and berry-like, producing a fragrant, edible fruit that is used in culinary and medicinal applications.

This variety is well-suited for rock gardens and as a foundation plant in landscapes due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
