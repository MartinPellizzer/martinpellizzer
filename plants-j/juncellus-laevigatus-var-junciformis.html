Juncellus laevigatus var. junciformis

Juncellus laevigatus var. junciformis, commonly known as the smooth rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, forming dense clumps of cylindrical, grass-like stems. The plant has narrow, hollow leaves that are smooth and often slightly waxy in texture. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, usually appearing in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic wildlife.
