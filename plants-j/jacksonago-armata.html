Jacksonago armata

Jacksonago armata, commonly known as the Jackson's agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that bears small, white flowers, which bloom in the spring. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its leaves and stems.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and rockeries.
