Jacobaeastrum candollei

Jacobaeastrum candollei, commonly known as the golden coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange ray flowers and a prominent, cone-shaped central disk. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Jacobaeastrum candollei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
