Juncus scirpoides subvar. major

Juncus scirpoides subvar. major, commonly known as the tall rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences, typically in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly rough texture.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
