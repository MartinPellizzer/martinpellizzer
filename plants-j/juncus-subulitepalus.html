Juncus subulitepalus

Juncus subulitepalus, commonly known as the slender spike rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, forming dense clumps with tall, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, which are often surrounded by bracts. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in wetland habitats.

Juncus subulitepalus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
