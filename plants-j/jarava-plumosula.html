Jarava plumosula

Jarava plumosula, commonly known as the silky jarava, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce pale blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures. Its slender, creeping growth habit allows it to form dense mats, which help prevent soil erosion.

Jarava plumosula plays a role in stabilizing desert ecosystems and provides habitat for various small animals and insects.
