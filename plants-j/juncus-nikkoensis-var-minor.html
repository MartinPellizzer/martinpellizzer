Juncus nikkoensis var. minor

Juncus nikkoensis var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf Nikko rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a glossy sheen, and it produces tiny white or greenish flowers in early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings for its compact growth habit.
