Juncus potaninii subsp. potaninii

Juncus potaninii subsp.

potaninii, commonly known as the Potanin's rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Asia. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that are usually unbranched and covered in fine, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
