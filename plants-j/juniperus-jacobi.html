Juniperus jacobi

Juniperus jacobi, commonly known as the Jacob's juniper, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features scale-like leaves that are dense and dark green, providing a dense canopy. Its small, blue-gray berries are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Juniperus jacobi is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
