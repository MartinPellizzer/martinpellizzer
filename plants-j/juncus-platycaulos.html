Juncus platycaulos

Juncus platycaulos, commonly known as broadstem rush, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that are often clustered closely together. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in moist to wet soils.

Juncus platycaulos plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
