Jarava inconspicua

Jarava inconspicua, commonly known as the slender jarava, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, coastal dunes, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

Jarava inconspicua plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
