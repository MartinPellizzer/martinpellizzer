Jovibarba globifera var. preissiana

Jovibarba globifera var. preissiana, commonly known as the Preiss's cushion plant, is a succulent species native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit, forming a low, rounded cushion of fleshy, green leaves. The leaves are typically spoon-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, adding a delicate touch of color to its otherwise muted appearance.

This plant is well adapted to rocky or sandy soils and requires minimal water, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
