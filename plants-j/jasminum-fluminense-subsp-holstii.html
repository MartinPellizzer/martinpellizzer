Jasminum fluminense subsp. holstii

Jasminum fluminense subsp.

holstii, commonly known as the Brazilian yellow jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Brazil. It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, characterized by its fragrant, yellow flowers.

The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves, and it produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.

The subspecies holstii is distinguished by its specific floral and foliar characteristics compared to other subspecies of Jasminum fluminense.
