Juncus laevis var. diffusus

Juncus laevis var. diffusus, commonly known as the soft rush, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often slightly hairy and can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, greenish flowers. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
