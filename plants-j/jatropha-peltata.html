Jatropha peltata

Jatropha peltata, commonly known as the velvetleaf jatropha, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are deeply lobed and covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, berry-like fruits. Jatropha peltata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

It is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
