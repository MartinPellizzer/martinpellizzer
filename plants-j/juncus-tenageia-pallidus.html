Juncus tenageia pallidus

Juncus tenageia pallidus, commonly known as the pale rush, is a perennial plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems that typically grow in dense clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil in wetland habitats, making it ecologically significant.
