Justicia nudicaulis

Justicia nudicaulis, commonly known as the slender false heather, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central and South America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of blue or violet. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or stream banks. It belongs to the Acanthaceae family, which is known for its showy floral displays.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a nectar source for various pollinators.
