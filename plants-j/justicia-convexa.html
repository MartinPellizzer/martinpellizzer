Justicia convexa

Justicia convexa, commonly known as the flame flower or red hot poker, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in dense clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in opposite pairs along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Justicia convexa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.
