Juniperus horizontalis f. lobata

Juniperus horizontalis f. lobata, commonly known as creeping juniper or variegated juniper, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with dark green leaves that have a slightly bluish hue. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact form and ornamental appeal make it a versatile plant for landscaping purposes.
