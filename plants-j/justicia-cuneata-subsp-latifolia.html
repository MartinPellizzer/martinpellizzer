Justicia cuneata subsp. latifolia

Justicia cuneata subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf flame bush, is a shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually broad and glossy.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in clusters, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which attract pollinators like birds and insects. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
