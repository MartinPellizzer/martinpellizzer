Juncus scirpoides

Juncus scirpoides, commonly known as the soft rush, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that often grow in dense clusters, typically in wet or marshy environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color, arranged in spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in temperate regions across Europe, Asia, and North America.

Juncus scirpoides plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
