Jatropha campanulata

Jatropha campanulata, commonly known as the bell jatropha, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain toxic seeds, which are a key feature of the genus Jatropha. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
