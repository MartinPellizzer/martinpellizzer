Jasminum octocuspe

Jasminum octocuspe, commonly known as the fragrant jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, white, highly fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, with opposite, simple, and usually aromatic leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The essential oils extracted from its flowers are widely used in perfumes and traditional medicine.
