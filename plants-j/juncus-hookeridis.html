Juncus hookeridis

Juncus hookeridis, commonly known as Hooker's rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish flowers in spikes during the spring. The plant thrives in moist to waterlogged soils, making it a common sight in marshy areas and along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which can stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife.

Juncus hookeridis is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in wet garden environments.
