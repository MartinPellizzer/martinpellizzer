Jasminum fulvum

Jasminum fulvum, commonly known as the golden jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with a smooth texture.

Jasminum fulvum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its pleasant aroma.
