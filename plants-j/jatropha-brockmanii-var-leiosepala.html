Jatropha brockmanii var. leiosepala

Jatropha brockmanii var. leiosepala, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often hairy. The plant produces toxic, milky sap that can cause skin irritation. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna habitats.

This species is also valued for its potential in biofuel production due to its high oil content in the seeds.
