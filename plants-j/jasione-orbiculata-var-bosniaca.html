Jasione orbiculata var. bosniaca

Jasione orbiculata var. bosniaca, commonly known as Bosnian rock-jasmine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan region.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone-rich soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early summer. Its flowers are clustered in umbels and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
