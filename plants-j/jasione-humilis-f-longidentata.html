Jasione humilis f. longidentata

Jasione humilis f. longidentata, commonly known as the long-toothed bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, blooming from late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, linear leaves that are often gland-dotted, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
