Jungia gunnerifolia

Jungia gunnerifolia, commonly known as the Mexican flameflower, is a member of the Malvaceae family.

It is a shrub native to Mexico and Central America, characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from spring to autumn.
