Jussiaea linifolia

Jussiaea linifolia, commonly known as the slender waterwheel, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in South America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, linear leaves that give it a grass-like appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in dense, wheel-like clusters. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along riverbanks.

Jussiaea linifolia plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
