Jatropha stuhlmannii subsp. somalensis

Jatropha stuhlmannii subsp.

somalensis, commonly known as the Somali physic nut, is a shrub native to the Horn of Africa. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and yellowish-green flowers.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain toxic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.
