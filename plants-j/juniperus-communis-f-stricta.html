Juniperus communis f. stricta

Juniperus communis f. stricta, commonly known as the upright juniper or dwarf juniper, is a compact, shrub-like form of the common juniper.

It is characterized by its dense, columnar growth habit and small, scale-like leaves that are typically bluish-gray in color. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for birds and other wildlife. It is often used in horticulture as a decorative plant due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

This variety is particularly valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability in landscaping.
