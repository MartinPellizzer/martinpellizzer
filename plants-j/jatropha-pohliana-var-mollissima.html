Jatropha pohliana var. mollissima

Jatropha pohliana var. mollissima, commonly known as the silky jatropha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow berries that contain toxic seeds, making it important to handle with care. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often used in ornamental gardening.

Its growth habit is shrub-like, with multiple stems and a compact form.
