Jacobaea andrzejowskyi

Jacobaea andrzejowskyi, commonly known as the Andrzejowski's ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky areas. It produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a rough texture.

Jacobaea andrzejowskyi is known for its toxic properties, containing pyrrolizidine alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock and humans.
