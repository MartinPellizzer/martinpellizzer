Justicia amazonica

Justicia amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian glorybower, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows up to 2 meters in height, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, and it thrives in well-drained soil with ample sunlight.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
