Jasminum barrelieri var. latifolium

Jasminum barrelieri var. latifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaf jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has glossy, broad leaves that are opposite and often emit a sweet scent. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to tropical gardens and greenhouses.
