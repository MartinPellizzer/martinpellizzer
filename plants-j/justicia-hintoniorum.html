Justicia hintoniorum

Justicia hintoniorum, commonly known as the Hinton's flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular, brightly colored flowers that range from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds and bees, making it an important species in local ecosystems.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
