Jasminum duclouxii

Jasminum duclouxii, commonly known as the Ducloux jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are dark green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fragrance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts interested in rare plants.
