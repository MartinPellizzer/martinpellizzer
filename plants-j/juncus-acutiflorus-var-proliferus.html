Juncus acutiflorus var. proliferus

Juncus acutiflorus var. proliferus, commonly known as the clustered rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish-brown in color and are covered in small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to stabilize soil, it plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
