Juno linifoliiformis

Juno linifoliiformis, commonly known as the slender-leaf junco, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves that are often arranged in a dense, compact growth pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Juno linifoliiformis is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
