Jambosa maritima

Jambosa maritima, commonly known as the sea jambosa, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, often growing in dune environments. Its ability to tolerate salt spray and strong winds makes it well adapted to coastal ecosystems.

Jambosa maritima plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
