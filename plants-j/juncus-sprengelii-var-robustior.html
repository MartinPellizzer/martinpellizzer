Juncus sprengelii var. robustior

Juncus sprengelii var. robustior, commonly known as the robust rush, is a perennial plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Korea.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which can help stabilize soil in aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
