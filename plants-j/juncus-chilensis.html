Juncus chilensis

Juncus chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes, pond edges, and along streams, where it thrives in moist to water-saturated soils. The plant has slender, grass-like stems that are often used in floral arrangements due to their elegant appearance. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late summer, consist of small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Juncus chilensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
