Jasminum trifoliatum

Jasminum trifoliatum, commonly known as the three-leaved jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant white flowers and trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets. The plant typically grows as a climbing shrub or vine, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its flowers bloom in clusters and emit a sweet, aromatic scent, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Jasminum trifoliatum is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its use in traditional medicine.
