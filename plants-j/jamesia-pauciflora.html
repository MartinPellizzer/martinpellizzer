Jamesia pauciflora

Jamesia pauciflora, commonly known as the Sierra snowflake, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a low, mat-forming growth habit with waxy, blue-green leaves. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Its botanical features make it a unique and valuable species in alpine ecosystems.
