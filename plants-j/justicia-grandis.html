Justicia grandis

Justicia grandis, commonly known as the giant flame flower, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with contrasting yellow or white markings. The plant is a shrub or small tree, capable of reaching heights of up to 10 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. Its flowers are typically arranged in terminal clusters and are adapted for pollination by birds, particularly hummingbirds.

Justicia grandis thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
