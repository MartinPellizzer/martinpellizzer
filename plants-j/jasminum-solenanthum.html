Jasminum solenanthum

Jasminum solenanthum, commonly known as the sweet box or Chinese jessamine, is a flowering plant native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in spring or early summer. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth texture with a glossy appearance.

Jasminum solenanthum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and pleasant scent.
