Jaliscoa pappifera

Jaliscoa pappifera, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a perennial plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright orange to red flower heads, which resemble the sun. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves and can grow up to 3 meters tall, making it a striking addition to gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Jaliscoa pappifera is also known for attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
