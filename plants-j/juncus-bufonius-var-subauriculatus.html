Juncus bufonius var. subauriculatus

Juncus bufonius var. subauriculatus, commonly known as the golden rush, is a variant of the common rush that exhibits distinct botanical features.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically appearing in dense clumps. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, pale yellow to golden brown flowers.

This plant is often found in wetland environments, thriving in moist to aquatic conditions.
