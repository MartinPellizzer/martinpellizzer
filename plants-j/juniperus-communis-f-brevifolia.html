Juniperus communis f. brevifolia

Juniperus communis f. brevifolia, commonly known as the dwarf juniper, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to northern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, providing a dense, ornamental appearance. The plant produces blue-black berries that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its hardy nature, it is well-suited for colder climates and requires minimal maintenance.
