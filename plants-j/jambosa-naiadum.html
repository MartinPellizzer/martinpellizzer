Jambosa naiadum

Jambosa naiadum, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the Amazon, is a striking aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white, often floating on the water's surface. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that provide shade and habitat for various aquatic life. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the surrounding sediment, contributing to water clarity.

Jambosa naiadum is also culturally significant to indigenous communities, who use it in rituals and traditional medicine.
