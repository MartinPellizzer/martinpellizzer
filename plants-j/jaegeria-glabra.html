Jaegeria glabra

Jaegeria glabra, commonly known as the smooth jaegeria, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance and ease of care.

Jaegeria glabra is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
