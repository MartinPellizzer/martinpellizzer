Jasminum paucinervium

Jasminum paucinervium, commonly known as the narrow-veined jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant has slender, woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and fragrance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
