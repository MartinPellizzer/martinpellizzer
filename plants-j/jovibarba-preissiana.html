Jovibarba preissiana

Jovibarba preissiana, commonly known as the rock saxifrage, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Saxifragaceae and is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern with thick, fleshy leaves that store water. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils makes it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Jovibarba preissiana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and resilience.
