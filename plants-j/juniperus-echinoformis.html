Juniperus echinoformis

Juniperus echinoformis, commonly known as the spiny juniper, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and blue-gray foliage that becomes more pronounced with age. The plant produces small, round cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, containing aromatic seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Juniperus echinoformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
