Juncus ernesti-barrosi

Juncus ernesti-barrosi, commonly known as the Barros's rush, is a flowering plant species native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikes, which are often wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, making it an important component of riparian and wetland ecosystems.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species within its habitat.
