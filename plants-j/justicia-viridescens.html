Justicia viridescens

Justicia viridescens, commonly known as the greenleaf flamebeard, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring regular watering during its growing season.
