Jobinia formosa

Jobinia formosa, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has a deciduous habit, shedding its leaves during the dry season. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture.

Jobinia formosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
