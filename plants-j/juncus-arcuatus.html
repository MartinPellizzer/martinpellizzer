Juncus arcuatus

Juncus arcuatus, commonly known as the arched rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that form dense clumps, often growing in shallow water or moist soil. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes during the summer months. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and stabilize shoreline areas.

Juncus arcuatus plays an important ecological role in filtering water and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
