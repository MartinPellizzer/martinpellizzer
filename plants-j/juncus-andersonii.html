Juncus andersonii

Juncus andersonii, commonly known as Anderson's rush, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and wetlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in late summer. It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for gardeners in suitable climates.
