Jatropha guaranitica

Jatropha guaranitica, commonly known as the Guarani physic nut, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and distinctive yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, green, fleshy fruits that turn red when mature, containing seeds surrounded by a toxic latex.

Jatropha guaranitica is known for its ornamental value and has been studied for potential uses in traditional medicine and as a source of biofuel.
