Juglans rugosa

Juglans rugosa, commonly known as the Chinese black walnut, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the walnut family, Juglandaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by oval, green fruits that mature into hard-shelled nuts. Its bark is thick and ridged, and it can grow up to 20 meters tall in suitable conditions.

The wood is dense and valued for its strength and durability.
