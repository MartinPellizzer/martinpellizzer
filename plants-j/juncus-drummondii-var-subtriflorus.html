Juncus drummondii var. subtriflorus

Juncus drummondii var. subtriflorus, commonly known as the Drummond's rush, is a variant of the Drummond rush native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its inflorescence consists of small, clustered spikelets that produce tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variant is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
