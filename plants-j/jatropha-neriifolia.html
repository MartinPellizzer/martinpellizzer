Jatropha neriifolia

Jatropha neriifolia, commonly known as the blackleaf jatropha, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces toxic, orange-red berries that contain seeds used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded areas.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitats.
