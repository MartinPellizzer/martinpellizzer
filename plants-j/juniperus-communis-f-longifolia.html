Juniperus communis f. longifolia

Juniperus communis f. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved juniper, is a subspecies of the common juniper.

It is a evergreen shrub or small tree native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa. The plant features elongated, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. Its small, dark blue berries are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for birds.

This subspecies is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh, coastal environments.
