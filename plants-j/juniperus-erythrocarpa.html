Juniperus erythrocarpa

Juniperus erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red berry juniper, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage and small, berry-like cones that turn red in the fall. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It has a slow growth rate and can live for several decades, reaching heights of up to 15 feet.

The botanical features of Juniperus erythrocarpa make it a hardy and resilient plant suitable for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
