Juniperus occidentalis subsp. australis

Juniperus occidentalis subsp.

australis, commonly known as the coastal juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the western coast of North America. It is characterized by its needle-like leaves and small, blue-black berries that are used in various traditional and modern applications.

The plant thrives in coastal environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils along the Pacific coast. It is well adapted to maritime climates, tolerating salt spray and strong winds.

Its botanical features make it an important species in coastal ecosystems and horticulture.
