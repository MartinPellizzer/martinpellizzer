Juncus supinus subsp. kochii

Juncus supinus subsp.

kochii, commonly known as the bent rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, pond margins, and stream banks.

The plant has grass-like, cylindrical leaves that are often spirally arranged around the stem. Its flowering spikes consist of small, clustered greenish-white flowers that are wind-pollinated.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
