Juncus acutiflorus subsp. genuinus

Juncus acutiflorus subsp.

genuinus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, spike-like clusters of greenish-white flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
