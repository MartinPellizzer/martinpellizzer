Juncus articulatus f. pauciflorus

Juncus articulatus f. pauciflorus, commonly known as the sparse-flowered rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and its reduced number of small, spike-like flowers compared to the typical species. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of tiny, greenish-white flowers.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various organisms.
