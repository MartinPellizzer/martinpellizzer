Juno baldshuanica

Juno baldshuanica, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Juno baldshuanica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh alpine conditions.
