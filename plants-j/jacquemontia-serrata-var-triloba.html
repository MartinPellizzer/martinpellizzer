Jacquemontia serrata var. triloba

Jacquemontia serrata var. triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed goldenweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has palmately lobed leaves, which are often trifoliate, giving it its varietal name *triloba*. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the morning glory family, Convolvulaceae.
