Juniperus monosperma var. knightii

Juniperus monosperma var. knightii, commonly known as the Knight's juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves and small, berry-like cones that mature to a blue-black color. The plant is adapted to harsh, dry environments and has a deep root system for water conservation. It produces aromatic foliage that contains essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic uses.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
