Jacobaea × albescens

Jacobaea × albescens, commonly known as the white ragwort, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and basal rosettes of gray-green, woolly leaves. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers arranged in composite heads, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It is native to the British Isles and thrives in disturbed soils, often found in fields and along roadsides.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a weed and can pose a risk to livestock.
