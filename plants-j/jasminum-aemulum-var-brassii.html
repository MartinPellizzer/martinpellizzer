Jasminum aemulum var. brassii

Jasminum aemulum var. brassii, commonly known as the Arabian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the evening. It thrives in warm, dry climates and is well-suited for cultivation in regions with mild winters.

The leaves are lance-shaped and have a glossy texture, contributing to its ornamental value.
