Juncus balticus subsp. mexicanus

Juncus balticus subsp.

mexicanus, commonly known as the Mexican rush, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Mexico. It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems covered in narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in aquatic habitats.
