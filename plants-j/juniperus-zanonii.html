Juniperus zanonii

Juniperus zanonii, commonly known as the Argentine juniper, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the arid regions of Argentina.

It is characterized by its scale-like leaves that are tightly pressed against the stems, giving it a dense, compact appearance. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Juniperus zanonii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
