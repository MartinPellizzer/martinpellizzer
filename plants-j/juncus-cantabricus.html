Juncus cantabricus

Juncus cantabricus, commonly known as the Cantabrian rush, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as riverbanks and coastal areas. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, pale green to brownish spikelets.

Juncus cantabricus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
