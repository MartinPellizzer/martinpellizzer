Junellia selaginoides

Junellia selaginoides, commonly known as the silverleaf trailing juncus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Juncaginaceae and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in humid, tropical climates. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, silvery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Junellia selaginoides is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and ability to tolerate waterlogged soils.
