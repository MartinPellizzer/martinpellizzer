Jasminum azoricum

Jasminum azoricum, commonly known as the Azores jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Azores archipelago in the North Atlantic.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its flowers have a sweet, jasmine-like scent and are often used in perfumery.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment.
