Juglans nigra f. laciniata

Juglans nigra f. laciniata, commonly known as the splitleaf black walnut, is a variant of the black walnut tree characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves.

The leaves are typically divided into several narrow, elongated segments, giving the plant a unique appearance. This form is a natural variant that occurs in certain regions of the eastern United States. The tree produces edible nuts enclosed in a hard, green shell, which are valued for their flavor and oil content.

It is adapted to a variety of soil types but thrives in well-drained, fertile ground.
