Juglans arguta

Juglans arguta, commonly known as the Rocky Mountain walnut, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 10 to 20 inches long and have 15 to 30 pairs of leaflets. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by edible nuts enclosed in a spiky husk. Its seeds are an important food source for various wildlife species.

The plant is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.
