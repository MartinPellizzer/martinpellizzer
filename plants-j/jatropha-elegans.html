Jatropha elegans

Jatropha elegans, commonly known as the elegant spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often hairy. It produces small, toxic berries that contain seeds, which are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Jatropha elegans is typically found in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
