Justicia sejuncta

Justicia sejuncta, commonly known as the desert willow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, willow-like stems and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from pink to purple.

Justicia sejuncta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive with minimal water.
