Juncus torreyi var. prolifer

Juncus torreyi var. prolifer, commonly known as the desert rush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and tall, cylindrical spikes of small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It reproduces through both vegetative propagation and seed dispersal, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
