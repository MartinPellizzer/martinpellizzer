Jurinea sarhaddensis

Jurinea sarhaddensis, commonly known as the Kashmir sandflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and succulent leaves. The plant is adapted to arid and cold environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It has a low-growing habit and is typically found in alpine meadows.

Due to its unique ecological niche, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native range.
