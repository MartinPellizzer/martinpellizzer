Juncus bulbosus f. maroccanus

Juncus bulbosus f. maroccanus, commonly known as the Moroccan bulb rush, is a rare and distinctive variant of the bulbous rush species.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and short, stout stems that often bear small, clustered inflorescences. This plant thrives in wet or marshy environments, typically found in the mountainous regions of Morocco. Its leaves are usually narrow and cylindrical, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces pale green to brown flowers during the spring and early summer.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant plant in its native range.
