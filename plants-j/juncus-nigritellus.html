Juncus nigritellus

Juncus nigritellus, commonly known as blackstem rush, is a perennial plant native to the wetlands of North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late spring to early summer, consist of small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers.

Juncus nigritellus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
