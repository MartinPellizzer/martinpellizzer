Juncus tenageia

Juncus tenageia, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant has slender, grass-like stems that are often clustered together, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like in appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color.

Juncus tenageia plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native ecosystem.
