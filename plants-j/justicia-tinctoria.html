Justicia tinctoria

Justicia tinctoria, commonly known as the Mexican verbena, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue or purple flowers and lance-shaped leaves. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

The flowers are also used in traditional medicine and dyeing practices.
