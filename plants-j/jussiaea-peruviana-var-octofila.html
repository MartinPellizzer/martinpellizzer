Jussiaea peruviana var. octofila

Jussiaea peruviana var. octofila, commonly known as the Peruvian water willow, is a semi-aquatic plant native to South America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found near rivers and wetlands. The plant features slender, ribbon-like leaves that are adapted for water environments. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish-white in color.

It plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
