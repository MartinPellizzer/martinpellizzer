Justicia austrosinensis

Justicia austrosinensis, commonly known as the Australian flame flower, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is well-suited for coastal gardens due to its salt tolerance.

The flowers attract birds and insects, making it a valuable addition to native landscapes.
