Juncus jeholensis

Juncus jeholensis, commonly known as the Jehol rush, is a flowering plant native to northeastern China.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae and is characterized by its tall, grass-like stems and clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and cylindrical, often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Juncus jeholensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
