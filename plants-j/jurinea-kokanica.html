Jurinea kokanica

Jurinea kokanica, commonly known as the Kokanica sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features silver-gray foliage with a fuzzy texture, adapted to tolerate saline and windy conditions. Its flowers are typically blue or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Jurinea kokanica is often found in sandy or rocky coastal habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
