Justicia toroensis

Justicia toroensis, commonly known as the yellow flame plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Justicia toroensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
