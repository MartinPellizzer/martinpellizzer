Justicia excalcea

Justicia excalcea, commonly known as the flame flower or red flame bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking clusters of tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens in warm climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various growing conditions.
