Justicia cydoniifolia

Justicia cydoniifolia, commonly known as the orange ball tree, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its bright orange, spherical flower heads that resemble small balls, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white, five-petaled flowers that develop into the distinctive orange seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture, adding to its visual appeal.
