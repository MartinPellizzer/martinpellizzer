Juncus afghanicus

Juncus afghanicus, commonly known as the Afghan rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. It is adapted to cold climates and can withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Juncus afghanicus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small wildlife in its native range.
