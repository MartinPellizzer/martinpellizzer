Juncus schischkinii

Juncus schischkinii, commonly known as the Schischkin's rush, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of greenish-white flowers, which are typically borne at the top of the stems. Juncus schischkinii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Its fibrous roots and dense growth habit make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
