Jessenia repanda

Jessenia repanda, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Jessenia repanda is valued for its durable, aromatic wood, which has been used historically in furniture and carving.
