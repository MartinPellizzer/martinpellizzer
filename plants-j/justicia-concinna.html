Justicia concinna

Justicia concinna, commonly known as the red mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in moist, well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight. The plant produces showy, red or pink bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to gardens.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
