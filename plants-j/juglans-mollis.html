Juglans mollis

Juglans mollis, commonly known as the Mexican walnut, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Juglandaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves with 15-23 pairs of leaflets. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, followed by edible, walnut-like fruits that mature in late summer. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it can grow up to 20 meters tall.

The plant is valued for its timber and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
