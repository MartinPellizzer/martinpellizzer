Justicia aurea

Justicia aurea, commonly known as the golden flame or yellow flame plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular, orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its vibrant color and ease of care.

Justicia aurea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and butterflies.
