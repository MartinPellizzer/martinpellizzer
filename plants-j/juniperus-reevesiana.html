Juniperus reevesiana

Juniperus reevesiana, commonly known as Reeves' juniper, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to China.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves and small, blue-gray berries that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has a slow growth rate and can live for several decades, making it a long-lived species.

Reeves' juniper is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
