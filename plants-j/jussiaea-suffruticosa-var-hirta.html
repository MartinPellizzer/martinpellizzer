Jussiaea suffruticosa var. hirta

Jussiaea suffruticosa var. hirta, commonly known as the hairy willow or swamp willow, is a shrub native to wetland environments in South America.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, typical of the Salicaceae family. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in moist habitats.
