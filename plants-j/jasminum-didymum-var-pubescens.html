Jasminum didymum var. pubescens

Jasminum didymum var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy jasmine, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including India and Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender, often hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, usually during the summer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered with fine pubescence, contributing to its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its sweet fragrance and visual beauty.
