Jacobaea nebrodensis

Jacobaea nebrodensis, commonly known as the Nebrodensis Groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its small, yellowish flowers bloom in late summer, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Jacobaea nebrodensis is often found in mountainous areas of Italy, Austria, and Switzerland, where it thrives in sunny, well-drained habitats.
