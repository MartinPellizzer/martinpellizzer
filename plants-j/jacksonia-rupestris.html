Jacksonia rupestris

Jacksonia rupestris, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. The plant is typically found in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, elongated fruit capsules that release seeds when mature.

Jacksonia rupestris is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive blooms and drought tolerance.
