Justicia calcarata var. baddabunae

Justicia calcarata var. baddabunae, commonly known as the Baddabunae flame flower, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement, providing a lush backdrop to its showy blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
