Juncus castaneus var. czetzii

Juncus castaneus var. czetzii, commonly known as the redstem rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and reddish stems that become more prominent as the plant matures. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that bloom in late summer, adding a delicate floral display.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic habitats.
