Jacea segetum

Jacea segetum, commonly known as the field gentian, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Jacea segetum has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain its seeds.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
