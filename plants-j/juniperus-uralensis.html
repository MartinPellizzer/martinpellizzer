Juniperus uralensis

Juniperus uralensis, commonly known as Siberian juniper, is a coniferous evergreen shrub or small tree native to the cold climates of Russia and Siberia.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage and small, berry-like cones that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, arctic or alpine environments. Its aromatic leaves and cones are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.

Juniperus uralensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
