Juncus acutus proles tommasinii

Juncus acutus proles tommasinii, commonly known as the Tommasini's sharp rush, is a flowering plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, cylindrical leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that contain wind-pollinated flowers, typical of many rush species. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshes, wet meadows, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various insect and bird life.
