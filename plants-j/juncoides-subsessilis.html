Juncoides subsessilis

Juncoides subsessilis, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and stream banks.

Juncoides subsessilis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
