Justicia caracasana

Justicia caracasana, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

The flowers are pollinated by birds, particularly hummingbirds, due to their bright colors and nectar-rich structure.
