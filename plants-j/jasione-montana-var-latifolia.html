Jasione montana var. latifolia

Jasione montana var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf rock jasmine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features basal rosettes of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers in the summer months, which are borne on slender stalks. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
