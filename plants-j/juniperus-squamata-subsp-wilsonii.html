Juniperus squamata subsp. wilsonii

Juniperus squamata subsp.

wilsonii, commonly known as the Wilson's juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, providing a striking visual contrast.

The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, cold climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
