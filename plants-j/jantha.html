Jantha

Jantha, commonly known as the Indian cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate regions and is often found in disturbed soils. Jantha is known for its edible leaves and seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various conditions make it a common weed in agricultural fields.
