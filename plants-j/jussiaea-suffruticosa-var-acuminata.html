Jussiaea suffruticosa var. acuminata

Jussiaea suffruticosa var. acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved jussiaea, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
