Jasminum floridum subsp. giraldii

Jasminum floridum subsp.

giraldii, commonly known as the Florida jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a glossy green surface.

It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
