Jasione humilis var. atlantica

Jasione humilis var. atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic Jasione, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the coastal regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It features small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces slender, branching stems. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal and maritime climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
