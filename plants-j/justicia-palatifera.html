Justicia palatifera

Justicia palatifera, commonly known as the Mexican verbena, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs, while the stems are woody and branched.

Justicia palatifera thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
