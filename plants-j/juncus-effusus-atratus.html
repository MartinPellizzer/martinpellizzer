Juncus effusus atratus

Juncus effusus atratus, commonly known as the black rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green to blackish stems that grow in clumps, often reaching heights of 1 to 3 feet. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers in spikes during the summer months. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in wetland habitats.

Juncus effusus atratus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in saturated soils.
