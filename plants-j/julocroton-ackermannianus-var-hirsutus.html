Julocroton ackermannianus var. hirsutus

Julocroton ackermannianus var. hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy julocroton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the variety. Its root system is robust, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
