Josephia nivea

Josephia nivea, commonly known as the snow josephia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hair, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in xeriscapes due to its low water requirements.
