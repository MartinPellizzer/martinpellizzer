Johnsonia lupulina var. robustior

Johnsonia lupulina var. robustior, commonly known as the robust bitterroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its stout, hairy stems and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and open woodlands.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used by indigenous peoples for various therapeutic purposes.
