Jacobaeastrum trilobum

Jacobaeastrum trilobum, commonly known as the three-lobed thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

The plant has a taproot system and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing food for wildlife.
