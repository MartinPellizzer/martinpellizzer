Jussiaea seminuda

Jussiaea seminuda, commonly known as the slender waterplantain, is a semi-aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as marshes and the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are often submerged or floating, with a smooth, waxy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, usually greenish-white in color.

Jussiaea seminuda plays an important role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
