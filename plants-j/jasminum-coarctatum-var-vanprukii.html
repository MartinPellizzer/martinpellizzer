Jasminum coarctatum var. vanprukii

Jasminum coarctatum var. vanprukii, commonly known as the Vanprukii jasmine, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It features fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves and a woody stem structure typical of many jasmine species. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.

This variety is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
