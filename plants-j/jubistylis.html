Jubistylis

Jubistylis, commonly known as beakgrass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

These plants are typically found in wet or moist habitats, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Jubistylis species are characterized by their slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, pointed leaves. The flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, often with bracts that resemble beaks, giving the plant its common name.

They play an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
