Jatropha jacquinii

Jatropha jacquinii, commonly known as the yellow jatropha, is a shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and milky sap, which can be toxic. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain toxic seeds, making it important to handle with care. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Euphorbiaceae family.
