Juncus prismatocarpus subsp. teretifolius

Juncus prismatocarpus subsp.

teretifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks.

The plant has slender, grass-like stems that are often clustered together, and its leaves are narrow and cylindrical. Its flowering stems produce small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically borne at the tips of the stems.

This subspecies is characterized by its distinct leaf morphology and adaptability to aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.
