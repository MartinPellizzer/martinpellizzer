Juglans sieboldiana f. acutissima

Juglans sieboldiana f. acutissima, commonly known as the Japanese walnut or acutely toothed walnut, is a cultivar of the Japanese chestnut tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive, sharply toothed leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant is native to Japan and is valued for its ornamental qualities as well as its edible nuts. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
