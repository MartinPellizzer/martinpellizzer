Justicia vasculosoides

Justicia vasculosoides, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false chaffflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring and summer. Its flowering stems are often branched and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

The plant is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.
