Jasminum multipetalum

Jasminum multipetalum, commonly known as the Persian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including Iran and Afghanistan.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils due to their aromatic properties.
