Jussiaea decurrens

Jussiaea decurrens, commonly known as the creeping waterplant, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to South America.

It typically grows in shallow waters and wetlands, forming dense mats on the surface of the water. The plant has long, slender leaves that are often submerged, with small white flowers that bloom in clusters. It is adapted to tolerate fluctuating water levels and can thrive in both still and flowing water environments.

Jussiaea decurrens plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
