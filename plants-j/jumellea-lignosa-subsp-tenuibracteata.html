Jumellea lignosa subsp. tenuibracteata

Jumellea lignosa subsp.

tenuibracteata, commonly known as the narrow-bract jumellea, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its small, narrow bracts and woody stems, which are typical of the Ericaceae family.

The plant produces white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are usually small, leathery, and arranged in whorls around the stems.

It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.
