Jasminum melastomifolium

Jasminum melastomifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf jasmine, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including India and China.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, opposite, and often ovate or elliptical leaves that are usually smooth and glossy. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

The flowers are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
