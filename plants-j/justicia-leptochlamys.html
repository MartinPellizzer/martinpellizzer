Justicia leptochlamys

Justicia leptochlamys, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flameflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting blue or purple throat. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial or shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
