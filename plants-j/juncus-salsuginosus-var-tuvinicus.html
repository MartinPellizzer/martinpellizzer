Juncus salsuginosus var. tuvinicus

Juncus salsuginosus var. tuvinicus, commonly known as the Tuvinian rush, is a perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and is adapted to saline and alkaline soils. The plant typically grows in compact clusters, with slender stems that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are narrow and cylindrical, often covered in a waxy layer to reduce water loss.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
