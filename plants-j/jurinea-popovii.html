Jurinea popovii

Jurinea popovii, commonly known as the desert sandflower, is a low-growing annual plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It thrives in sandy and gravelly soils, often found in desert and steppe environments. The plant features small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine layer of hairs to reduce water loss.

Jurinea popovii plays a role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments and is adapted to extreme temperature fluctuations.
