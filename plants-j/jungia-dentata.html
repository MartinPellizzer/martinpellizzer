Jungia dentata

Jungia dentata, commonly known as the toothed jungle plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its serrated leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in dense, humid forests.
