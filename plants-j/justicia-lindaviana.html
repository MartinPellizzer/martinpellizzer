Justicia lindaviana

Justicia lindaviana, commonly known as the Lindaviana flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble flames, composed of clusters of bright red or orange tubular flowers. The plant has opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-draining soil.

Justicia lindaviana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
