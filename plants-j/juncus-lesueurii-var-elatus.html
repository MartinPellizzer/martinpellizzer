Juncus lesueurii var. elatus

Juncus lesueurii var. elatus, commonly known as the tall rush, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, pond edges, and stream banks.

Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
