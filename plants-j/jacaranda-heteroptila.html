Jacaranda heteroptila

Jacaranda heteroptila, commonly known as the purple jacaranda, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking purple flowers that bloom in pendant clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant is a deciduous tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters, with compound leaves that are bipinnate. Its flowers are composed of five petals and are often used in ornamental gardening due to their vibrant color.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
