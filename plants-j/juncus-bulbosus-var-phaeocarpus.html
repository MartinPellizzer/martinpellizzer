Juncus bulbosus var. phaeocarpus

Juncus bulbosus var. phaeocarpus, commonly known as the purple-fruited rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces distinctive purple to reddish-brown seed heads. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically arranged in spike-like clusters.

This variant is notable for its unique fruiting structures, which contribute to its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
