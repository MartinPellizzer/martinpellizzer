Junellia glauca

Junellia glauca, commonly known as the blue junellia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. Junellia glauca is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 2 feet.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
