Justicia racemulosa

Justicia racemulosa, commonly known as the clustered flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that often display vibrant red, orange, or yellow hues. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are clustered in racemes, giving it the species name "racemulosa," and it is well adapted to full sun and well-drained soils.

The plant is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
