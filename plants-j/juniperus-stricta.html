Juniperus stricta

Juniperus stricta, commonly known as the dwarf juniper, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, needle-like leaves that are dark green in color. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It is well adapted to harsh environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Juniperus stricta is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate various climate conditions.
