Juncus sikkimensis

Juncus sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim rush, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and forest edges. It produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Juncus sikkimensis is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
