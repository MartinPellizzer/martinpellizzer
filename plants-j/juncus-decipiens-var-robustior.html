Juncus decipiens var. robustior

Juncus decipiens var. robustior, commonly known as the robust swamp rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features tall, stiff, grass-like stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often forming dense clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes during the summer months. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and colonize wet soils effectively.

It is adapted to saturated conditions and plays a role in stabilizing shoreline vegetation.
