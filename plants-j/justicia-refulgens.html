Justicia refulgens

Justicia refulgens, commonly known as the flame flower, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy, providing a contrasting backdrop to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its rapid growth and showy appearance make it a favorite among horticultural enthusiasts.
