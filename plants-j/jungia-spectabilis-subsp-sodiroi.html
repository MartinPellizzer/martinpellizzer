Jungia spectabilis subsp. sodiroi

Jungia spectabilis subsp.

sodiroi, commonly known as the Ecuadorian beautyberry, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America. It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, clusters of purple to violet berries that persist through winter.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats and is often found in cloud forests. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth texture, while its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters.

The berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for various bird species.
