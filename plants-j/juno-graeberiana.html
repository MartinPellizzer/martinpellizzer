Juno graeberiana

Juno graeberiana, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Juno graeberiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
