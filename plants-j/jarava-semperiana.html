Jarava semperiana

Jarava semperiana, commonly known as the desert feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted clumps and fine, wiry leaves that are adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
