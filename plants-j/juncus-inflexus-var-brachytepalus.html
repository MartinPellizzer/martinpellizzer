Juncus inflexus var. brachytepalus

Juncus inflexus var. brachytepalus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rush, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that contain tiny flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, wetlands, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is distinguished by its shorter, narrower leaves compared to other forms of Juncus inflexus.
