Juncus decipiens subsp. sundaicus

Juncus decipiens subsp.

sundaicus, commonly known as the Sunda rush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to moist soils and its role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems.
