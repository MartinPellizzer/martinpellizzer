Juncus luzuloxiphium

Juncus luzuloxiphium, commonly known as the silver rush, is a perennial plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems that are often covered in a silvery bloom. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges. Its leaves are narrow and cylindrical, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer.

Juncus luzuloxiphium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
