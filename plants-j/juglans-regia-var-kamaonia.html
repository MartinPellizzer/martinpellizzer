Juglans regia var. kamaonia

Juglans regia var. kamaonia, commonly known as the Kamaonia walnut, is a variant of the Persian walnut native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, robust nuts with thick, dark husks and a rich, oily kernel. The tree is deciduous, reaching heights of up to 20 meters, with broad, lobed leaves and a distinctive, aromatic bark. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its high-quality nuts, which are used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
