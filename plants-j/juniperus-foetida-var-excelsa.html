Juniperus foetida var. excelsa

Juniperus foetida var. excelsa, commonly known as the tall juniper, is a variant of the foetida juniper species.

It is a evergreen coniferous shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant is characterized by its scale-like leaves and small, berry-like cones that are often used in traditional medicine. It has a slow growth rate and is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
