Juniperus sabina var. macrocarpa

Juniperus sabina var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, dark blue berries. The plant has scale-like leaves that are tightly packed, giving it a compact appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as an ornamental plant.

The berries are often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring in culinary applications.
