Jasminum puberulum

Jasminum puberulum, commonly known as the Mexican jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the southern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces fragrant white flowers, which bloom in the spring and summer. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its pleasant aroma and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
