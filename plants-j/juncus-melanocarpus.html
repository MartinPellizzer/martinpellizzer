Juncus melanocarpus

Juncus melanocarpus, commonly known as blackberry rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland and marshy areas in North America.

It has slender, cylindrical stems that are often dark green to reddish in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer. The plant is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and its ability to thrive in saturated soil conditions. Its seeds are dispersed by water, contributing to its spread in wetland ecosystems.

Juncus melanocarpus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
