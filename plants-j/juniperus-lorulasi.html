Juniperus lorulasi

Juniperus lorulasi, commonly known as the narrowleaf juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of scale-like leaves that are tightly packed along the branches. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, dry climates.

Juniperus lorulasi plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various species.
