Jacquemontia multivalvis

Jacquemontia multivalvis, commonly known as the redstem morning glory, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, alternate leaves that are typically green with a slightly pubescent surface. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a red or purple throat. Its fruit is a distinctive, two-valved capsule that opens to release seeds.

Jacquemontia multivalvis is adapted to moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
