Justicia spiculifera

Justicia spiculifera, commonly known as the spiky justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spiky bracts that surround its small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers, which are usually red or orange, bloom in clusters and attract various pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

Justicia spiculifera is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
