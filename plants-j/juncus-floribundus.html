Juncus floribundus

Juncus floribundus, commonly known as the flowering rush, is a perennial wetland plant native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, cylindrical spikes of small, white or greenish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that form dense clumps, often growing in shallow water or moist soil. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Juncus floribundus is an important part of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
