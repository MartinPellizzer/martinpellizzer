Jasione crispa subsp. cedretorum

Jasione crispa subsp.

cedretorum, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe. It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale blue.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cool, high-altitude environments. Jasione crispa subsp.

cedretorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
