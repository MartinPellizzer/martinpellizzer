Jacobaea alpina subsp. samnitum

Jacobaea alpina subsp.

samnitum, commonly known as the Alpine tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. It is known for its strong aromatic scent and contains compounds with potential medicinal properties.

Its distribution is limited to specific alpine areas, making it a rare and ecologically significant species.
