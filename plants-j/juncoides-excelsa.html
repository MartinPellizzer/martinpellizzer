Juncoides excelsa

Juncoides excelsa, commonly known as the tall rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae, which includes various species of rushes and sedges. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems that grow upright and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its narrow, linear leaves are typically arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters at the ends of its stems.

Juncoides excelsa is often found in moist, wetland habitats and is adapted to thrive in environments with high humidity and poor soil conditions.
