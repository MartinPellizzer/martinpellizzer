Justicia putata

Justicia putata, commonly known as the Mexican verbena, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy spikes of tubular flowers, often in shades of red, purple, or pink, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

The genus Justicia is characterized by its brightly colored flowers and is part of the Acanthaceae family.
