Jungia micrantha

Jungia micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered jungia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and succulent leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in humid environments. Its botanical features include a short stem, lance-shaped leaves, and showy, pinkish-white blooms.

Jungia micrantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
