Jasminum pedunculatum

Jasminum pedunculatum, commonly known as the clustered jasmine, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are typically borne on peduncles, which are short, flowering stems.
