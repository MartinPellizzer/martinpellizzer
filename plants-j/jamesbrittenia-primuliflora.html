Jamesbrittenia primuliflora

Jamesbrittenia primuliflora, commonly known as the primrose-flowered jamesbrittenia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.
