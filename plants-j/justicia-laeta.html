Justicia laeta

Justicia laeta, commonly known as the silver bush or white mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale blue flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and showy blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Justicia laeta is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
