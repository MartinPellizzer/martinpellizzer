Jamesbrittenia atropurpurea

Jamesbrittenia atropurpurea, commonly known as the purpleleaf jamesbrittenia, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple foliage, which becomes more intense during the cooler months. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
