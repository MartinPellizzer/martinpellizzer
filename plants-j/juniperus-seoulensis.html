Juniperus seoulensis

Juniperus seoulensis, commonly known as Seoul juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to Korea.

It belongs to the Cupressaceae family and is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves and small, berry-like cones. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to temperate climates. Its foliage is evergreen, providing year-round visual interest, and it is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.

Juniperus seoulensis is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
