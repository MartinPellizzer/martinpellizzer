Jacobaea paniculata

Jacobaea paniculata, commonly known as the African pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in panicles. The plant has feathery, finely divided leaves that are often aromatic. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Jacobaea paniculata is known for its potential use in natural pest control due to the presence of pyrethrin compounds in its flowers.
