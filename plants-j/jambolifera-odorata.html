Jambolifera odorata

Jambolifera odorata, commonly known as the fragrant jambolai, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that emit a sweet fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit, a small, yellowish drupe, contains a single seed surrounded by a fleshy pulp.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
