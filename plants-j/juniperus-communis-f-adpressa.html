Juniperus communis f. adpressa

Juniperus communis f. adpressa, commonly known as the dwarf juniper, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to northern regions of Europe, Asia, and North America.

It features dense, needle-like foliage that is typically a deep green, sometimes with a bluish or grayish tinge. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are usually blue or black when mature, containing toxic compounds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and as a ground cover.

Its hardy nature and ornamental appeal make it a valued plant in horticulture.
