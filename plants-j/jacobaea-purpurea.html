Jacobaea purpurea

Jacobaea purpurea, commonly known as the purple ragwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture. Its flowers are typically yellow with a purple or reddish border, blooming from late summer to autumn.

Jacobaea purpurea is often found in dry meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils, and it can spread aggressively through its extensive root system.
