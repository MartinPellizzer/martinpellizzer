Jasminum grahamii

Jasminum grahamii, commonly known as Graham's jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has narrow, pointed leaves and is adapted to the arid environment of its native habitat. It is often found growing in rocky areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities.

Jasminum grahamii is also notable for its unique floral structure and is a subject of botanical interest due to its evolutionary adaptations.
