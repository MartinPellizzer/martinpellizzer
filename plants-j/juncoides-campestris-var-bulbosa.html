Juncoides campestris var. bulbosa

Juncoides campestris var. bulbosa, commonly known as the bulbous rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands, ditches, and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which can help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
