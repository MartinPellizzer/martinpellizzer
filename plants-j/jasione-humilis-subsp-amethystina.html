Jasione humilis subsp. amethystina

Jasione humilis subsp.

amethystina, commonly known as the amethyst windflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, hairy stems that bear small, clustered flowers with purple to violet petals.

The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing delicate, bell-shaped flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its charming, early-season blooms.
