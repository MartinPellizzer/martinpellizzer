Justicia sulphuriflora

Justicia sulphuriflora, commonly known as sulfur-flowered false heather, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetlands and swampy areas. The plant features small, yellowish-orange flowers that are clustered in spikes, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetland ecosystems.
