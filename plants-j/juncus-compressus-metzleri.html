Juncus compressus metzleri

Juncus compressus metzleri, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a flowering plant species belonging to the family Juncaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and is typically found in moist or wet environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of white or greenish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense stands that stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife.

Juncus compressus metzleri is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological benefits and adaptability to waterlogged conditions.
