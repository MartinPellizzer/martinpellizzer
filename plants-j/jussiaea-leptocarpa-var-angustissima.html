Jussiaea leptocarpa var. angustissima

Jussiaea leptocarpa var. angustissima, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water willow, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 1 centimeter wide. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important species for aquatic ecosystems and a subject of study in wetland botany.
