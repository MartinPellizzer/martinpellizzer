Juncus menziesii var. californicus

Juncus menziesii var. californicus, commonly known as the California rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and pond edges. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like clusters of small, white or greenish flowers. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

This variety is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological benefits and adaptability to various water conditions.
