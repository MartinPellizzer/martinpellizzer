Jasminum macrocarpum

Jasminum macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited jasmine, is a flowering plant belonging to the Oleaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of India, China, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a sweet aroma. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a glossy green surface.

The fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its propagation in natural habitats.
