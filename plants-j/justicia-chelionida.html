Justicia chelionida

Justicia chelionida, commonly known as the flame violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, surrounded by colorful, leaf-like structures. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Justicia chelionida is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
