Juncoides carolinae

Juncoides carolinae, commonly known as the Carolina rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family, which includes rushes and sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, cylindrical leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are pollinated by wind rather than insects.

Juncoides carolinae plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
