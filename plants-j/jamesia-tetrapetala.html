Jamesia tetrapetala

Jamesia tetrapetala, commonly known as the Sierra plum or mountain snowbells, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at higher elevations. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Jamesia tetrapetala is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
