Justicia striata subsp. melampyrum

Justicia striata subsp.

melampyrum, commonly known as the red-veined joyweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange.

The leaves are typically ovate with prominent veins, and the plant often grows in moist, shady environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.
