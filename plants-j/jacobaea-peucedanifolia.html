Jacobaea peucedanifolia

Jacobaea peucedanifolia, commonly known as the European sneezeweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and yellow flowers. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and wastelands.

Jacobaea peucedanifolia is known for its ability to release a strong scent when disturbed, which can cause sneezing in some individuals.
