Juno zenaidae

Juno zenaidae, commonly known as the golden trumpet.

This flowering plant is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its nectar attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
