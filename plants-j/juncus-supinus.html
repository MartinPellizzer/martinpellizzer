Juncus supinus

Juncus supinus, commonly known as the upright rush, is a perennial plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It features tall, stiff, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often reddish-brown in appearance. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found along stream banks and in swamps.

Juncus supinus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
