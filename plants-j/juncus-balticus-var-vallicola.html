Juncus balticus var. vallicola

Juncus balticus var. vallicola, commonly known as the valley rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, spike-like flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to environments with high water tables and periodic flooding.
