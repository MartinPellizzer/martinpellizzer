Juniperus suecica

Juniperus suecica, commonly known as the Swedish juniper, is a species of evergreen conifer native to northern Europe.

It is characterized by its compact, spherical shape and dark blue to purple berries that mature in the second year. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color and are arranged in whorls around the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Juniperus suecica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
