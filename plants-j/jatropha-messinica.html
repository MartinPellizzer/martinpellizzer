Jatropha messinica

Jatropha messinica, commonly known as the Sicilian croton, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces capsules that contain toxic seeds, which are a defense mechanism against herbivores. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for medicinal use, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
