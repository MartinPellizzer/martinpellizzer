Justicia tenuispica

Justicia tenuispica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flame bush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which can extend from spring to autumn.
