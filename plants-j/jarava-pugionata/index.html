Jarava Pugionata

Jarava pugionata is a small, grass-like plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa. It thrives in sandy and rocky soils, often found along the shores of the Atlantic Ocean. This species is known for its ability to withstand harsh coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds. Its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit make it a hardy plant well adapted to its environment. Despite its modest appearance, Jarava pugionata plays an important role in stabilizing coastal dunes and supporting local biodiversity.
