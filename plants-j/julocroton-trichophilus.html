Julocroton trichophilus

Julocroton trichophilus, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the variety. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with multiple stems and a dense foliage structure.

Julocroton trichophilus is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
