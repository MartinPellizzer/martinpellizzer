Juniperus microsperma

Juniperus microsperma, commonly known as the dwarf juniper, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with dense, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for birds and other wildlife. Juniperus microsperma is often used in landscaping due to its hardiness and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat in arid and semi-arid environments.
