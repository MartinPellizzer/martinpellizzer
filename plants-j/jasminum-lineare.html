Jasminum lineare

Jasminum lineare, commonly known as the linear jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, vine-like growth habit and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically narrow and elongated, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
