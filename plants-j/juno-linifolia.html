Juno linifolia

Juno linifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf juniper, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, scale-like leaves that give it a silvery appearance. The plant produces blue or grayish-blue berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open woodlands.

Juno linifolia is valued for its drought tolerance and is sometimes used in xeriscaping.
