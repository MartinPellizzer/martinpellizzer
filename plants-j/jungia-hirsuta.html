Jungia hirsuta

Jungia hirsuta, commonly known as hairy jungia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Jungia hirsuta is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance and adaptability.
