Juniperus squamata

Juniperus squamata, commonly known as the Japanese juniper, is a coniferous evergreen shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, scaly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Juniperus squamata is also valued for its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
