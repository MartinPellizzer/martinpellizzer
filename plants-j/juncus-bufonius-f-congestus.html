Juncus bufonius f. congestus

Juncus bufonius f. congestus, commonly known as the clustered bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth of narrow, grass-like leaves that form compact clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, pond edges, and along streams.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various wildlife species.
