Jussiaea velutina

Jussiaea velutina, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety hair, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often appearing in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Jussiaea velutina is an important species in ecological studies due to its role in supporting local biodiversity.
