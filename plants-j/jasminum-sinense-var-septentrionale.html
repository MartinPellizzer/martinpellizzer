Jasminum sinense var. septentrionale

Jasminum sinense var. septentrionale, commonly known as the northern Chinese jasmine, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes in temperate climates.
