Justicia wynaadensis

Justicia wynaadensis, commonly known as the Wynaad flame flower, is a perennial shrub native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking red to orange flowers that bloom in terminal clusters. The plant has lance-shaped, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
