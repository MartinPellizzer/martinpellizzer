Jasminum ternum

Jasminum ternum, commonly known as the lesser jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a smooth texture with a slightly glossy appearance.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its use in traditional medicine.
