Juncus holoschoenus var. multiflorus

Juncus holoschoenus var. multiflorus, commonly known as the many-flowered rush, is a perennial wetland plant native to parts of eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, such as marshes, pond edges, and along streams. The plant has cylindrical, grass-like stems that are often reddish or brownish in color. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, pale green to brown spikelets.

This variant is distinguished by its numerous flowering spikes, which give it its common name.
