Jasminum mixtinervium

Jasminum mixtinervium, commonly known as the Mexican jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a distinctive veining pattern that gives it its scientific name.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
