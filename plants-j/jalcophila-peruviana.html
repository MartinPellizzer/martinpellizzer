Jalcophila peruviana

Jalcophila peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

Jalcophila peruviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
