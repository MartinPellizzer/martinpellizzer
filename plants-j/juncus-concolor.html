Juncus concolor

Juncus concolor, commonly known as the common rush, is a perennial plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually arranged in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Juncus concolor plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
