Jasminum pospischilii

Jasminum pospischilii, commonly known as the Arabian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the winter months. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and fragrance, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
