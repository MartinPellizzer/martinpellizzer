Juniperus hartwissiana

Juniperus hartwissiana, commonly known as the Sierra juniper, is a species of juniper native to the western United States.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has scale-like leaves that are closely packed, giving it a dense, compact appearance. Its cones are small and berry-like, containing toxic seeds that are not suitable for human consumption.

It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
