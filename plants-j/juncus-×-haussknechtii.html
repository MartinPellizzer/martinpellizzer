Juncus × haussknechtii

Juncus × haussknechtii, commonly known as the hybrid rush, is a perennial plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Juncus effusus and Juncus roemerianus, characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and tidal flats, where it thrives in brackish or fresh water.

Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
