Jacaranda semiserrata

Jacaranda semiserrata, commonly known as the African jacaranda, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are compound, with oval-shaped leaflets that have serrated edges, giving the plant its scientific name. The tree produces hard, woody pods that contain flat, fibrous seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
