Jasione montana var. humilis

Jasione montana var. humilis, commonly known as the lesser rock-jasmine, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features hairy, basal leaves that are typically spoon-shaped and arranged in a rosette. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers with five petals, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Jasione montana var. humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
