Jacquemontia sandwicensis var. laevis

Jacquemontia sandwicensis var. laevis, commonly known as the smooth false nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments, often found in native forests on the islands of Hawaii.
