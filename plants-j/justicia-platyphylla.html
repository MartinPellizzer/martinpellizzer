Justicia platyphylla

Justicia platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leaved flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often attracting hummingbirds and other pollinators. The plant has opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warm climates.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
