Jasonia lopadusana

Jasonia lopadusana, commonly known as the rock fig, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fleshy, edible fruits. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a leathery texture.

Jasonia lopadusana is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
