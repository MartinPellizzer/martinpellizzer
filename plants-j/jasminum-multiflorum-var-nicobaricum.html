Jasminum multiflorum var. nicobaricum

Jasminum multiflorum var. nicobaricum, commonly known as the Nicobar jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Nicobar Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant has slender, woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas or near water bodies.

Due to its ornamental value and fragrance, it is cultivated in gardens and for aromatic purposes.
