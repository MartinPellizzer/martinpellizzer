Juniperus macrocarpa var. ellipsoidea

Juniperus macrocarpa var. ellipsoidea, commonly known as the elliptic juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, scale-like leaves that are arranged in flat sprays. The plant produces blue-gray berries that are typically retained on the plant for several years. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various landscape conditions.
