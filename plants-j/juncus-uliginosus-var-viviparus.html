Juncus uliginosus var. viviparus

Juncus uliginosus var. viviparus, commonly known as the viviparous rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, greenish-white flowers. One of its most distinctive features is the viviparous reproduction, where the plant produces plantlets at the base of its stems that can root and grow independently. This adaptation allows it to thrive in saturated soils and propagate efficiently in moist environments.

It plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
