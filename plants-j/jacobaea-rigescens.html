Jacobaea rigescens

Jacobaea rigescens, commonly known as the rigid ragwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a strong, bitter scent, while its flowers are yellow with dark centers.

Jacobaea rigescens is also known for its toxic properties, containing pyrrolizidine alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
