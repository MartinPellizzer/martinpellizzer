Jamesonia auriculata

Jamesonia auriculata, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Jamesonia auriculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
