Jussiaea michelii

Jussiaea michelii, commonly known as the marsh willow or water willow, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in South America.

It is characterized by its slender, herbaceous stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Jussiaea michelii thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of riparian and wetland ecosystems.

Its adaptability to aquatic conditions contributes to its role in stabilizing shoreline vegetation.
