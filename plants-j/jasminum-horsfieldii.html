Jasminum horsfieldii

Jasminum horsfieldii, commonly known as the Chinese jasmine, is a flowering plant native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces fragrant, white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually ovate or elliptic leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for both garden cultivation and container planting.

The blossoms are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of perfume due to their strong, sweet fragrance.
