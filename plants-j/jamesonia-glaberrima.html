Jamesonia glaberrima

Jamesonia glaberrima, commonly known as the smoothleaf jamesonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and glossy, with a deep green color. Its flowers are small, bell-shaped, and usually pink or white, appearing in clusters during the spring.

Jamesonia glaberrima is often found in acidic, well-drained soils in woodlands and rocky areas.
