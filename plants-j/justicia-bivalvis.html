Justicia bivalvis

Justicia bivalvis, commonly known as the two-lobed false arum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, which are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Justicia bivalvis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
