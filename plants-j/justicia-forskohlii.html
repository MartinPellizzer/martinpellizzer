Justicia forskohlii

Justicia forskohlii, commonly known as Indian borage or forskohlii, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple tubular flowers. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, particularly due to the presence of the compound forskolin. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

Justicia forskohlii is often cultivated for both ornamental and therapeutic purposes.
