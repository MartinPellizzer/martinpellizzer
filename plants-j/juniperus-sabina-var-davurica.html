Juniperus sabina var. davurica

Juniperus sabina var. davurica, commonly known as the Siberian juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the cold climates of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage and small, blue-gray berries that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments. Its aromatic leaves and cones contribute to its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture due to its hardy nature and winter interest.
