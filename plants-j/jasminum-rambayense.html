Jasminum rambayense

Jasminum rambayense, commonly known as the Philippine jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly the Philippines.

It is a woody shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement and have a glossy surface. Its flowers bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season, and emit a sweet, intoxicating fragrance.

Jasminum rambayense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
