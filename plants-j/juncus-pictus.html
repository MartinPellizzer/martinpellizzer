Juncus pictus

Juncus pictus, commonly known as the painted rush, is a perennial plant native to the wetlands of North America.

It features slender, grass-like stems that are often tinged with reddish or purple hues, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, forming dense clusters that help stabilize shoreline habitats. Its flowers, which appear in early summer, are small and clustered at the tips of the stems, producing a soft, feathery appearance.

Juncus pictus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
