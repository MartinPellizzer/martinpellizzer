Justicia longipes

Justicia longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed flame flower, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its rapid growth and vibrant blooms make it a favorite among horticulturists and plant enthusiasts.
