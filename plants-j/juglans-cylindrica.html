Juglans cylindrica

Juglans cylindrica, commonly known as the western black walnut, is a deciduous tree native to western North America.

It belongs to the Juglandaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical, ridged bark and large, compound leaves. The tree produces small, green, catkin-like male flowers and female flowers in separate clusters. Its fruit is a woody drupe, containing a single hard-shelled nut surrounded by a husk.

The wood of Juglans cylindrica is highly valued for its durability and rich, aromatic scent.
