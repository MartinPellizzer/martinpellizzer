Jussiaea elevata

Jussiaea elevata, commonly known as the marsh willow or water willow, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas in South America.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like stems that often grow in shallow water or along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often floating or submerged depending on the water level.

Jussiaea elevata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic insects and other wildlife.
