Jacobinia sellowiana var. warmingiana

Jacobinia sellowiana var. warmingiana, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to the landscape. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a lush backdrop to its showy blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.
