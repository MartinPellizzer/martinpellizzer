Jatropha tubulosa var. quinqueloba

Jatropha tubulosa var. quinqueloba, commonly known as the five-lobed spurge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces toxic sap that can cause skin irritation, making it a potential hazard. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in disturbed soils.

Despite its toxicity, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance.
