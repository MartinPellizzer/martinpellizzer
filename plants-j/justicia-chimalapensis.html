Justicia chimalapensis

Justicia chimalapensis, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a striking ornamental plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family, characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy.

Its flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade with well-drained soil.
