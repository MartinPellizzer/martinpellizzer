Jamesonia rufescens

Jamesonia rufescens, commonly known as the redleaf barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features compound leaves that are typically reddish in color, especially during the fall, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, which are followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and woodland landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its seasonal color changes and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
