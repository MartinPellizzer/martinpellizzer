Jasminum volubile

Jasminum volubile, commonly known as creeping jasmine, is a flowering plant native to southern and eastern Asia.

It is a vigorous, climbing vine that produces fragrant, white flowers with a sweet scent. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually hairy leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

Jasminum volubile thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
