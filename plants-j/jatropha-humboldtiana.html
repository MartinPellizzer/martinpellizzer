Jatropha humboldtiana

Jatropha humboldtiana, commonly known as the Humboldt's spurge, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers and spiny, lobed leaves. The plant produces toxic latex, which contains compounds such as jatrophone and physostigmine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed soils and along riverbanks.

Jatropha humboldtiana is also noted for its potential use in traditional medicine.
