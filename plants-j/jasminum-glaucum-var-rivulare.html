Jasminum glaucum var. rivulare

Jasminum glaucum var. rivulare, commonly known as the river jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and fragrance, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
