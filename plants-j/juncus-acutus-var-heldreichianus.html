Juncus acutus var. heldreichianus

Juncus acutus var. heldreichianus, commonly known as the Heldreich's rush, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in early summer, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and marshy areas.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
