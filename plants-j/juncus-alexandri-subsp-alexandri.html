Juncus alexandri subsp. alexandri

Juncus alexandri subsp.

alexandri, commonly known as the Alexandrian rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as pond margins and damp soils.

The plant has narrow, cylindrical leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The subspecies is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
