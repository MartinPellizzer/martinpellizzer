Juncus luzuliformis var. potaninii

Juncus luzuliformis var. potaninii, commonly known as the Potanin's rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tundra and alpine habitats. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is adapted to cold climates with a short growing season.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
