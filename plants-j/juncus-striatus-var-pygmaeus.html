Juncus striatus var. pygmaeus

Juncus striatus var. pygmaeus, commonly known as the dwarf rush, is a low-growing perennial plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in height, giving it a compact appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes during the summer months. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, pond edges, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various wildlife species.
