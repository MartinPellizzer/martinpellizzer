Jatropha rzedowskii

Jatropha rzedowskii, commonly known as the Mexican physic nut, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain toxic seeds, which are a key feature of the genus Jatropha. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed soils and along forest edges.

Due to its toxicity, it is important to handle the plant with care.
