Jacquemontia palmeri var. varians

Jacquemontia palmeri var. varians, commonly known as the Palmer's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, showy flowers that typically bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
