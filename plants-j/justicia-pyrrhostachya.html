Justicia pyrrhostachya

Justicia pyrrhostachya, commonly known as flame grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central and South America.

It features showy, tubular red flowers that bloom in dense spikes, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is drought-tolerant and prefers well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
