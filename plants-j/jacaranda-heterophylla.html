Jacaranda heterophylla

Jacaranda heterophylla, commonly known as the variable jacaranda, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often fragrant, violet or purple flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has compound leaves that vary in shape, contributing to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The seeds are encased in a woody pod, which splits open when mature to release the seeds.
