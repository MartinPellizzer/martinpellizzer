Juncus acutiflorus var. macrocephalus

Juncus acutiflorus var. macrocephalus, commonly known as the large-headed rush, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical spikes of small, pale green to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. It thrives in wet to moist soils, typically found in marshy areas, pond edges, and along streams.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of wetland ecosystems and a popular choice for water gardeners.
