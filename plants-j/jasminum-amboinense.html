Jasminum amboinense

Jasminum amboinense, commonly known as the Amboina jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and simple, opposite leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, blooming throughout the year in warm climates. Its flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical regions with well-drained soil and ample sunlight.
