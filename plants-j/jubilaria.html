Jubilaria

Jubilaria, commonly known as the jubilee plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It consists of only one species, Jubilaria monogyna, which is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale pink. It produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, and it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

Jubilaria is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
