Justicia subcymosa

Justicia subcymosa, commonly known as the silverleaf justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink or purple, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
