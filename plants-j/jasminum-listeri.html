Jasminum listeri

Jasminum listeri, commonly known as the Lister's jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often releasing a sweet aroma at night. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance.

Jasminum listeri thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
