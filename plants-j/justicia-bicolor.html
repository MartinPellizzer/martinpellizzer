Justicia bicolor

Justicia bicolor, commonly known as the red and white flame flower, is a tropical plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its striking bi-colored flowers, with red upper petals and white lower petals, which attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows as an annual or short-lived perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 feet. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The plant thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in warm climates.
