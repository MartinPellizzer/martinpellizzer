Jacobaea flosculosa

Jacobaea flosculosa, commonly known as the alpine groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, hairy, and arranged in a rosette, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Jacobaea flosculosa is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
