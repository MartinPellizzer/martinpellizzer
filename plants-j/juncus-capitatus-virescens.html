Juncus capitatus virescens

Juncus capitatus virescens, commonly known as the green-headed rush, is a flowering plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical spikes of small, greenish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in coastal areas or freshwater habitats.

Juncus capitatus virescens plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland species.
