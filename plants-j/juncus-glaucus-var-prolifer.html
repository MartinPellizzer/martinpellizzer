Juncus glaucus var. prolifer

Juncus glaucus var. prolifer, commonly known as the silver rush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage that grows in clumps, with narrow, bluish-gray leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in spike-like clusters during the summer months. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and stream banks.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for wetland gardens and ecological restoration projects.
