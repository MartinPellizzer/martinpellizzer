Jaumea mimuloides

Jaumea mimuloides, commonly known as the yellow water lily, is a perennial aquatic plant native to southern Africa.

It features floating leaves that are oval and waxy, with a yellowish-green color and a distinctive white underside. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow with red or orange markings, blooming from late winter to early summer. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water gardens and wetland restoration projects.
