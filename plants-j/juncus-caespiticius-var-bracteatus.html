Juncus caespiticius var. bracteatus

Juncus caespiticius var. bracteatus, commonly known as the bracted rush, is a perennial plant native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish or brownish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

This variety is characterized by the presence of bracts, which are modified leaves that protect the flower clusters.
