Justicia tetramerioides

Justicia tetramerioides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight. The plant features showy, blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
