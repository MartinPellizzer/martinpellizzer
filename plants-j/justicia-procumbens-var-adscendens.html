Justicia procumbens var. adscendens

Justicia procumbens var. adscendens, commonly known as the climbing justicia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with smooth margins and produces clusters of tubular, bright blue or purple flowers. Its stems are usually prostrate or ascending, allowing it to climb or spread along the ground.

The flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
