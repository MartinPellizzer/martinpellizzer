Juncus triformis var. stylosus

Juncus triformis var. stylosus, commonly known as the slender rush, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical, grass-like leaves and dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
