Juncus fuscoater subsp. arthrophyllus

Juncus fuscoater subsp.

arthrophyllus, commonly known as the dark-stemmed rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in wet or damp environments such as marshes, stream banks, and roadside ditches.

The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are usually dark green to brown in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and they are often covered with a fine layer of hair.

This subspecies is characterized by its robust growth habit and adaptability to moist soils.
