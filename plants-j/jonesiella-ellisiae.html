Jonesiella ellisiae

Jonesiella ellisiae, commonly known as the Elysian ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent petals and a unique floral structure that resembles a ghostly apparition. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific soil conditions rich in organic matter. Its blooming period is brief, often occurring only once every few years, making it a subject of fascination for botanists.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Jonesiella ellisiae is considered endangered and is the focus of conservation efforts.
