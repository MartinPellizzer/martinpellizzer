Juncus paniculatus

Juncus paniculatus, commonly known as the panicled rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features grass-like, cylindrical leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist to water-saturated soils and is often found in marshes, pond edges, and along stream banks.

Juncus paniculatus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
