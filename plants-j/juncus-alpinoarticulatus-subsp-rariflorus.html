Juncus alpinoarticulatus subsp. rariflorus

Juncus alpinoarticulatus subsp.

rariflorus, commonly known as the alpine rush, is a perennial plant native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, rocky or alpine habitats, often forming dense mats in its native environment. Its leaves are narrow and cylindrical, often with a waxy surface to reduce water loss in harsh conditions.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to cold climates and its role in stabilizing soil in mountainous regions.
