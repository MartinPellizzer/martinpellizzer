Joinvillea ascendens subsp. glabra

Joinvillea ascendens subsp.

glabra, commonly known as the smooth jointvillia, is a succulent plant native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a smooth, glabrous surface.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is appreciated by both horticulturists and collectors.
