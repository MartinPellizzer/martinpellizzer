Juniperus sheppardii var. pyramidalis

Juniperus sheppardii var. pyramidalis, commonly known as the Pyramidal Juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its conical shape and scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
