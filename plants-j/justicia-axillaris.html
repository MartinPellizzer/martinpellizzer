Justicia axillaris

Justicia axillaris, commonly known as the velvetleaf justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils, making it suitable for cultivation in gardens and landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
