Jasminum silhetense

Jasminum silhetense, commonly known as Silhet jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Bangladesh and India.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and lanceolate, with a smooth texture.

The flowers emit a strong, sweet fragrance, especially at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths.
