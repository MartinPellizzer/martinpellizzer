Julocroton subpannosus var. uruguayensis

Julocroton subpannosus var. uruguayensis, commonly known as the Uruguay croton, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Uruguay and parts of Brazil.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers, which are followed by green, fleshy capsules that mature to a reddish color.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.
