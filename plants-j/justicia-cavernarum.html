Justicia cavernarum

Justicia cavernarum, commonly known as the cave jewel or desert jewel, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling a cluster of jewels. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it store water, adapting to its harsh, dry environment. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in dry climates.
