Juglans mandshurica var. mandshurica

Juglans mandshurica var. mandshurica, commonly known as the Manchurian walnut, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 20–40 cm long and consist of 15–25 pairs of lance-shaped leaflets. The tree produces large, green, palmately lobed nuts enclosed in a thick, spiny husk. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soils.

The wood of this species is valued for its strength and durability, making it useful in furniture and construction.
