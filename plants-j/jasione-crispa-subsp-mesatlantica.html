Jasione crispa subsp. mesatlantica

Jasione crispa subsp.

mesatlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the coastal regions of the Atlantic coast. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are blue to violet in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize coastal dunes.
