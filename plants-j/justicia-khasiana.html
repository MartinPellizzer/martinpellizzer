Justicia khasiana

Justicia khasiana, commonly known as the Khasi flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, while its stems are often branched and covered in fine pubescence.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by birds, particularly sunbirds, making it an important plant in avian-insect pollination systems.
