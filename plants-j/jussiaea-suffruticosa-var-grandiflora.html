Jussiaea suffruticosa var. grandiflora

Jussiaea suffruticosa var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered jussiaea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub-like species characterized by its woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
