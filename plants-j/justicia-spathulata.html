Justicia spathulata

Justicia spathulata, commonly known as the flame violet, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a perennial shrub that produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

The flowers are pollinated by birds, particularly hummingbirds, making it an important species in attracting wildlife.
