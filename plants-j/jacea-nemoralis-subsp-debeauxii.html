Jacea nemoralis subsp. debeauxii

Jacea nemoralis subsp.

debeauxii, commonly known as the Debeaux's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas and thrives in rich, well-drained soils.

The plant features showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged in a basal rosette, and it has a fibrous root system.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens and natural habitats.
