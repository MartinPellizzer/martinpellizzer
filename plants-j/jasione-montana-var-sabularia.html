Jasione montana var. sabularia

Jasione montana var. sabularia, commonly known as the sandjasmine, is a low-growing perennial herb native to rocky and sandy soils in the Mediterranean region.

It features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that help reduce water loss in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in limestone areas.

Jasione montana var. sabularia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
