Justicia clavata

Justicia clavata, commonly known as the clustered flame flower, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement, providing a lush backdrop to its showy blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a favored plant for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
