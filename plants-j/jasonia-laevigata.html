Jasonia laevigata

Jasonia laevigata, commonly known as the silky hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Jasonia laevigata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
