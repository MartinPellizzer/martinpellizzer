Jasminum angustifolium var. sessiliflorum

Jasminum angustifolium var. sessiliflorum, commonly known as the sessile-flower jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, white, highly fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in shrub form, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and prefers well-drained soil and full sun. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and a woody stem structure, making it a distinctive member of the Oleaceae family.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
