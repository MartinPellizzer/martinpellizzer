Jurinea pseudocyanoides

Jurinea pseudocyanoides, commonly known as the coastal bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid and coastal regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in dry, saline environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, which attract various pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water in harsh conditions.

Jurinea pseudocyanoides plays a role in stabilizing dune ecosystems and is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptations to extreme climates.
