Jasminum simplicifolium subsp. funale

Jasminum simplicifolium subsp.

funale, commonly known as the Funale jasmine, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its pleasant aroma.
