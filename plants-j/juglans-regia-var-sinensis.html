Juglans regia var. sinensis

Juglans regia var. sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese walnut, is a variant of the Persian walnut native to China.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 10–20 meters, featuring compound leaves with 15–23 pairs of leaflets. The tree produces large, green, spherical fruits that mature into hard-shelled nuts. The wood is valued for its strength and fine grain, making it suitable for furniture and carving.

This variety is also cultivated for its edible nuts, which are rich in oils and nutrients.
