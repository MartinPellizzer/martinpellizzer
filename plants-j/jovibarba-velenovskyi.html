Jovibarba velenovskyi

Jovibarba velenovskyi, commonly known as the Velenovsky's houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant typically forms dense rosettes and produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It is a slow-growing species that prefers well-draining soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in rock gardens and collections.
