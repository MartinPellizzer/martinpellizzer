Jasione humilis var. fallax

Jasione humilis var. fallax, commonly known as the pale centaury, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and its stems are often prostrate or ascending. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

This variety is distinguished by its pale flower color and compact growth habit compared to other Jasione species.
