Juncus bufonius var. major

Juncus bufonius var. major, commonly known as the giant marsh rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowering spikes consist of small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense panicles.

This species plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
