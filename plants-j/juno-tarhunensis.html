Juno tarhunensis

Juno tarhunensis, commonly known as the Armenian juniper, is a species of juniper native to the highlands of Armenia.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in rocky, mountainous regions. The plant has needle-like leaves and produces small, blue-black berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Juno tarhunensis is characterized by its aromatic foliage and its ability to thrive in harsh, arid environments.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing shelter and food for local wildlife.
