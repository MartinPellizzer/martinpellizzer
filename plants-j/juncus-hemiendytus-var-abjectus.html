Juncus hemiendytus var. abjectus

Juncus hemiendytus var. abjectus, commonly known as the slender rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense clusters of small, greenish-brown flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, thriving in marshes and pond edges. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and ecological restoration projects.
