Jamesonia imbricata var. scalaris

Jamesonia imbricata var. scalaris, commonly known as the scalloped bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a scalloped edge, which gives it its varietal name. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
