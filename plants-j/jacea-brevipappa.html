Jacea brevipappa

Jacea brevipappa, commonly known as the short-papilled jacea, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered inflorescences and fleshy, succulent leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, adapting well to aquatic environments. Its flowers are usually pale green to white, and it produces small, round fruits that disperse via water.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a species of interest for botanical studies and conservation efforts.
