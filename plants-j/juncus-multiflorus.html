Juncus multiflorus

Juncus multiflorus, commonly known as the many-flowered rush, is a perennial plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae and is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in spikes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Juncus multiflorus thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in shallow water or along stream edges.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
