Jurinea variabilis

Jurinea variabilis, commonly known as the variable sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the western Pacific, where it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant features basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in compact clusters. It is well adapted to salty and windy environments, making it a hardy species found in maritime habitats.

Jurinea variabilis plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and supporting local biodiversity.
