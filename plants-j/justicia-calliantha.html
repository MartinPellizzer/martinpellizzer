Justicia calliantha

Justicia calliantha, commonly known as the flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Justicia calliantha thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
