Juncoides campestris var. comosa

Juncoides campestris var. comosa, commonly known as the woolly rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in soft, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, ditches, and along waterways.

Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil, making it ecologically important in wetland habitats.
