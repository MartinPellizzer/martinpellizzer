Justicia humuliflora

Justicia humuliflora, commonly known as golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange inflorescences composed of numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in a crown-like pattern. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
