Justicia phlebophylla

Justicia phlebophylla, commonly known as the rainbow plant or red-veined begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that often display vibrant red, pink, and white veins against a green background. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
