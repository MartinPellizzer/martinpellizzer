Justicia breviracemosa

Justicia breviracemosa, commonly known as the narrow raceme justicia, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which bloom in the summer months. Its inflorescence is a short raceme, giving it its specific epithet "breviracemosa.".

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to full sun to partial shade conditions.
