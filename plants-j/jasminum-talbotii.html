Jasminum talbotii

Jasminum talbotii, commonly known as the Talbot's jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface.

Jasminum talbotii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its pleasant fragrance.
