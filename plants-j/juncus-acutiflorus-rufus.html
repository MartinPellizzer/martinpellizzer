Juncus acutiflorus rufus

Juncus acutiflorus rufus, commonly known as the redflower rush, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, reddish-brown flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which can help stabilize soil in wetland areas.
