Jarava chrysophylla

Jarava chrysophylla, commonly known as the golden leaf jarava, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often tinged with a golden hue, especially under sunlight. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale green to yellow flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Jarava chrysophylla is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
