Juncus canadensis var. euroauster

Juncus canadensis var. euroauster, commonly known as the European rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, pond edges, and stream banks. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and covered with small, scale-like leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, with stamens that are usually longer than the petals.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
