Jacobaea erucifolia var. tenuifolia

Jacobaea erucifolia var. tenuifolia, commonly known as slenderleaf ragwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The leaves are narrow and linear, with a bluish-green hue and a distinctive, slightly serrated margin. It produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, typical of the Asteraceae family.

The plant contains pyrrolizidine alkaloids, which can be toxic to livestock and wildlife.
