Justicia stricta

Justicia stricta, commonly known as the red flame plant, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Justicia stricta thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
