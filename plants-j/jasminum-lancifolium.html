Jasminum lancifolium

Jasminum lancifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its flowers emit a sweet scent, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Jasminum lancifolium is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its use in traditional medicine.
