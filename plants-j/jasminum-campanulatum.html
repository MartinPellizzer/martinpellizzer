Jasminum campanulatum

Jasminum campanulatum, commonly known as the bell jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with opposite, simple, and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its sweet scent and delicate appearance.
