Juncus collinus

Juncus collinus, commonly known as the mountain rush, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish flowers in spikes. It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Juncus collinus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms.
