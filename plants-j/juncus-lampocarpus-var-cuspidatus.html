Juncus lampocarpus var. cuspidatus

Juncus lampocarpus var. cuspidatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant has slender, grass-like stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, spike-like clusters of white or pale green flowers. Its leaves are narrow and tapering, with a sharp point at the tip, giving it its specific epithet "cuspidatus.".

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
