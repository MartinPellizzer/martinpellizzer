Juniperus communis f. pungens

Juniperus communis f. pungens, commonly known as the prickly juniper, is a variant of the common juniper, characterized by its dense, sharp, and spiny foliage.

It is a shrub or small tree native to the northern hemisphere, typically found in rocky, mountainous, or coastal areas. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, contributing to its distinctive scent and ecological role.

The form of this plant is well adapted to harsh environments, making it a resilient and hardy species.
