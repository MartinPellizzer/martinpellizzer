Juncus laevis var. conglomeratus

Juncus laevis var. conglomeratus, commonly known as the clustered rush, is a variant of the smooth rush native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves that form a mat-like growth habit. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, pond edges, and along streams. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variant is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
