Jurinea tadshikistanica

Jurinea tadshikistanica, commonly known as the Tadzhikistan sandflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure adapted to harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs to reduce water loss. Jurinea tadshikistanica thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert habitats.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small desert organisms.
