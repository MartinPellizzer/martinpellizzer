Juniperus oxycedrus var. macrocarpa

Juniperus oxycedrus var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the common juniper, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves and small, blue-black berries that are borne in cone-like structures. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its foliage provides habitat for various insects and birds, while its berries are an important food source for wildlife.

The plant is also valued for its aromatic wood and has been used historically in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
