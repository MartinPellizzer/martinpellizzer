Juniperus silicicola

Juniperus silicicola, commonly known as the silicicola juniper, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and produces blue or purple berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for birds and small mammals. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its slow growth rate and adaptability to harsh conditions make it a resilient component of its ecosystem.

Juniperus silicicola plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
