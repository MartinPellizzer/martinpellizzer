Jacea ropalon

Jacea ropalon, commonly known as the silver leaf orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage and fragrant, tubular flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and requires partial shade to flourish. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Due to habitat loss, Jacea ropalon is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
