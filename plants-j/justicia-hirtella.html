Justicia hirtella

Justicia hirtella, commonly known as the hairy false dragonhead, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet. The plant features opposite, hairy, ovate leaves and produces spikes of showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink or purple. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, sandy soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
