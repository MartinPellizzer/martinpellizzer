Jasione montana subsp. penicillata

Jasione montana subsp.

penicillata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments.

The plant features slender stems with lance-shaped leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
