Juncus balticus f. contractus

Juncus balticus f. contractus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a compact, herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are typically less than 30 cm in height, covered with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes during the summer months, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, streambanks, and other aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.

Its dense growth habit makes it an effective pioneer species in disturbed or wetland environments.
