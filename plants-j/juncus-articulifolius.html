Juncus articulifolius

Juncus articulifolius, commonly known as the jointleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that often grow in dense clusters, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are segmented at the base, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist environments such as wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

Juncus articulifolius plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
