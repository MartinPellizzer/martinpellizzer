Jatropha pohliana f. luxurians

Jatropha pohliana f. luxurians, commonly known as the showy jatropha, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant produces capsule fruits containing toxic seeds, which are a key feature of the genus Jatropha. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its vibrant foliage and floral display.
