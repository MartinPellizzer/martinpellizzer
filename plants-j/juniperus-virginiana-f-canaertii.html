Juniperus virginiana f. canaertii

Juniperus virginiana f. canaertii, commonly known as the Smoothleaf Juniper, is a botanical variant of the Eastern Red Cedar.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous foliage and smaller, more compact leaves compared to the typical form. This evergreen shrub or small tree is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in well-drained soils. Its cones are typically blue to black in color and contain aromatic, edible berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
