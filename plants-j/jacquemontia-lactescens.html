Jacquemontia lactescens

Jacquemontia lactescens, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with distinctive yellow-orange flowers that open in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Botanically, it is characterized by its twining stems and capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.
