Juncus gerardi subsp. persicus

Juncus gerardi subsp.

persicus, commonly known as Persian rush, is a perennial plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and covered in fine, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers that appear in dense spikes during the summer months. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, wetlands, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
