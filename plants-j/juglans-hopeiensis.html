Juglans hopeiensis

Juglans hopeiensis, commonly known as the Hope's hickory, is a species of hickory native to China.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, pinnately compound leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by edible, woody nuts enclosed in a thick, spiny husk.

Its seeds are valued for their nutritional content and are used in traditional Chinese cuisine and medicine.
