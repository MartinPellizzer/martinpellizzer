Jacea jordaniana

Jacea jordaniana, commonly known as Jordanian jacea, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and fleshy, which helps in reducing water loss.

Jacea jordaniana is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
