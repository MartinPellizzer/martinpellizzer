Justicia americana

Justicia americana, commonly known as flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Justicia americana is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds.
