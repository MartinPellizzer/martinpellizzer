Justicia varians

Justicia varians, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces vibrant red or orange tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Justicia varians is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
