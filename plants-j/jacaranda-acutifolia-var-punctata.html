Jacaranda acutifolia var. punctata

Jacaranda acutifolia var. punctata, commonly known as the spotted jacaranda, is a deciduous tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of violet-blue flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The leaves are bipinnate, with small, pointed leaflets that give the plant its species name "acutifolia.". The varietal name "punctata" refers to the distinctive spots on the leaves, which are often a lighter green or white.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant floral displays.
