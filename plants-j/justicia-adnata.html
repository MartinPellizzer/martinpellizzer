Justicia adnata

Justicia adnata, commonly known as the flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

The botanical features of Justicia adnata make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical and subtropical regions.
