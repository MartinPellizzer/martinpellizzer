Juniperus foetida var. thurifera

Juniperus foetida var. thurifera, commonly known as the sweet juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of needle-like leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

The aromatic properties of its leaves and berries contribute to its historical use in perfumery and traditional medicine.
