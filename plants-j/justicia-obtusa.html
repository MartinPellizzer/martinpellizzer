Justicia obtusa

Justicia obtusa, commonly known as the broadleaf false arum, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often velvety to the touch. The plant produces clusters of small, colorful flowers that range from red to purple, resembling those of the arum family. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its foliage and floral display make it a popular choice for shade gardens and indoor spaces.
