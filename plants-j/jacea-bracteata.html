Jacea bracteata

Jacea bracteata, commonly known as the bracted jacea, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Jacea bracteata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage.
