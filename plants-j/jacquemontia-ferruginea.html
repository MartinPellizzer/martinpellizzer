Jacquemontia ferruginea

Jacquemontia ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty jacquemontia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, wooded areas. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with distinctive red veins, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

Jacquemontia ferruginea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
