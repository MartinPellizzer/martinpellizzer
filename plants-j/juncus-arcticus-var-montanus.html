Juncus arcticus var. montanus

Juncus arcticus var. montanus, commonly known as the alpine rush, is a perennial plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in short, stiff leaves. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tundra and alpine habitats. Its flowering spikes produce small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green or brown in color.

Juncus arcticus var. montanus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various arctic fauna.
