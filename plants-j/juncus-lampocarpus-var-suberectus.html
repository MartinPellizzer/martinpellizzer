Juncus lampocarpus var. suberectus

Juncus lampocarpus var. suberectus, commonly known as the stiff rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has cylindrical, grass-like stems that are often erect and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, spike-like clusters of brownish flowers.

This species is adapted to moist or waterlogged soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
