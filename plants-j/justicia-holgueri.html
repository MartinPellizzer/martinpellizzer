Justicia holgueri

Justicia holgueri, commonly known as Mexican flameflower, is a tropical shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, typically from spring through summer.
