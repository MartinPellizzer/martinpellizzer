Juncus capensis var. gracilior

Juncus capensis var. gracilior, commonly known as the slender rush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, cylindrical leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and damp woodlands.

Its rapid growth and ability to stabilize soil make it an important plant in wetland ecosystems.
