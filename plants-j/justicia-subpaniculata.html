Justicia subpaniculata

Justicia subpaniculata, commonly known as the panicle flameflower, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange inflorescences that resemble panicles, which are dense clusters of small flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Justicia subpaniculata is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
