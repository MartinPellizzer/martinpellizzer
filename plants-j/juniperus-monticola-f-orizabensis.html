Juniperus monticola f. orizabensis

Juniperus monticola f. orizabensis, commonly known as the Orizaba juniper, is a subspecies of the Mexican juniper native to the high elevations of central Mexico.

It is a coniferous evergreen shrub or small tree, characterized by its needle-like leaves and small, blueish-black berries. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine, and it plays an important role in the ecosystems of its native habitat.

The subspecies is notable for its hardiness and ability to withstand harsh mountainous conditions.
