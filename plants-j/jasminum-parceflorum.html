Jasminum parceflorum

Jasminum parceflorum, commonly known as the fragrant jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white, star-shaped flowers that emit a sweet, aromatic scent. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

Jasminum parceflorum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and in perfumery due to its pleasant fragrance.
