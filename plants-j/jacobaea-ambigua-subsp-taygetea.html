Jacobaea ambigua subsp. taygetea

Jacobaea ambigua subsp.

taygetea, commonly known as the Greek tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands.

The plant features aromatic, finely divided leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are clustered in loose inflorescences. Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and flies.

The plant contains essential oils and has been used historically for its medicinal properties.
