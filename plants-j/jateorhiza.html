Jateorhiza

Jateorhiza, commonly known as the yellow root, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features a stout, yellow-colored root system that is the primary source of its medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, often appearing in white or pale yellow hues.

Jateorhiza is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
