Jasminum callianthum

Jasminum callianthum, commonly known as the golden jasmin, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a hint of orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
