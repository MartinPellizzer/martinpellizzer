Julocroton pycnophyllus f. latifolius

Julocroton pycnophyllus f. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf julocroton, is a variant of the julocroton species characterized by its broader leaves compared to the typical form.

It is a shrub native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in tropical climates. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically in inflorescences that add to its ornamental value.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its attractive foliage and adaptability to warm, humid environments.
