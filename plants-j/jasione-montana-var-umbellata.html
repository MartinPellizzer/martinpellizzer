Jasione montana var. umbellata

Jasione montana var. umbellata, commonly known as the mountain jasmine or umbellate bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale blue. The plant typically blooms from June to August, and its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Jasione montana var. umbellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
