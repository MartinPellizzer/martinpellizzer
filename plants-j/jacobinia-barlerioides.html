Jacobinia barlerioides

Jacobinia barlerioides, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, spike-like inflorescences that are typically bright orange or red, giving it its common name. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Jacobinia barlerioides is known for its long blooming period, often lasting from late spring through early autumn.
