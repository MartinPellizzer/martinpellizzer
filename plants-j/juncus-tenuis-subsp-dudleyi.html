Juncus tenuis subsp. dudleyi

Juncus tenuis subsp.

dudleyi, commonly known as Dudley's rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and pond edges.

The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and are covered in small, scale-like leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, which are typically greenish or brownish in color.

This subspecies is characterized by its ability to thrive in saturated soils and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
