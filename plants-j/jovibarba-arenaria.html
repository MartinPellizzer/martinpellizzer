Jovibarba arenaria

Jovibarba arenaria, commonly known as the sandstone houseleek, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Jovibarba arenaria is known for its resilience and ability to survive in harsh, dry conditions.
