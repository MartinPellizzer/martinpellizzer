Jambosa elegans

Jambosa elegans, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to West Africa.

It produces edible fruits with a distinctive star-shaped appearance and a sweet, juicy pulp. The plant has compound leaves with long, pointed leaflets and is typically found in moist, shady environments. Its flowers are small and fragrant, often clustered in panicles, and it is cultivated for both its fruit and ornamental value.

The tree is relatively easy to grow and is well-suited to tropical and subtropical climates.
