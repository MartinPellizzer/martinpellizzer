Jasminum myrtophyllum

Jasminum myrtophyllum, commonly known as the Australian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture, while its stems are often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are clustered in panicles and emit a sweet, floral scent, making it a popular ornamental plant.
