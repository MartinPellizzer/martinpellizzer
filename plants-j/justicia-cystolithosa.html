Justicia cystolithosa

Justicia cystolithosa, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its showy inflorescences attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
