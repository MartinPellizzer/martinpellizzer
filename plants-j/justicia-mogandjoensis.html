Justicia mogandjoensis

Justicia mogandjoensis, commonly known as the Mozambique flame flower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes in warm climates.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
