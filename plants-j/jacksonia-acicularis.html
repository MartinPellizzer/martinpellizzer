Jacksonia acicularis

Jacksonia acicularis, commonly known as the needle bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It is typically found in arid or semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils.

Jacksonia acicularis is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
