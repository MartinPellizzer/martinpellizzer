Jaegeria macrocephala

Jaegeria macrocephala, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features broad, waxy leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in diameter, floating on the water's surface. The plant produces large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it in shallow water while absorbing nutrients from the surrounding environment.

Jaegeria macrocephala plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
