Juncus alpinoarticulatus subsp. fischerianus

Juncus alpinoarticulatus subsp.

fischerianus, commonly known as the Fischer's rush, is a perennial plant native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist, rocky or sandy soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its stems are usually stiff and upright, with a fibrous texture that aids in its survival in harsh climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in mountainous areas.
