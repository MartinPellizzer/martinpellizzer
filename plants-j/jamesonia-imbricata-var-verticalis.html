Jamesonia imbricata var. verticalis

Jamesonia imbricata var. verticalis, commonly known as the upright golden bell, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact, upright growth habit with narrow, leathery leaves that are dark green in color. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a standout plant in botanical gardens and private collections.
