Jacaranda caroba

Jacaranda caroba, commonly known as the blue jacaranda, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in furniture-making.

The tree is also popular in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and seasonal blooms.
