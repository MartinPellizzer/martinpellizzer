Jennata radiata

Jennata radiata, commonly known as the Radiata Plant, is a unique species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, radiating floral structure that resembles a sunburst, giving it its common name. The plant features broad, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and they bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, Jennata radiata is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and herbal collections.
