Justicia huacanensis

Justicia huacanensis, commonly known as the Huacanensis flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

The flowers are usually red or orange, and they bloom in terminal clusters, attracting various pollinators.
