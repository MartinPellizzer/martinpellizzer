Jasminum bifarium var. glabrum

Jasminum bifarium var. glabrum, commonly known as the smooth jasmine, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has slender, woody stems and elliptical leaves that are usually smooth and glossy. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and as a climbing plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its pleasant aroma, which attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
