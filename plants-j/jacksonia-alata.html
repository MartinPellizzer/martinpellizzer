Jacksonia alata

Jacksonia alata, commonly known as the New Zealand flax or punga, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. Jacksonia alata thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal and temperate gardens.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and birds.
