Jatropha diffusa

Jatropha diffusa, commonly known as the spreading jatropha, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It features erect, branched stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers followed by distinctive red, fleshy berries containing toxic seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides due to its adaptability.

Its botanical characteristics make it both an ornamental plant and a species of ecological interest.
