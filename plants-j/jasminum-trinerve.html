Jasminum trinerve

Jasminum trinerve, commonly known as the three-veined jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often releasing a sweet aroma in the evening. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually entire leaves, with a prominent three-veined structure on the underside. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Jasminum trinerve is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
