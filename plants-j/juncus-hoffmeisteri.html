Juncus hoffmeisteri

Juncus hoffmeisteri, commonly known as Hoffmeister's rush, is a perennial plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. This plant thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in wetlands, along stream banks, and in swampy areas. Its flowering spikes consist of small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers that appear in late spring to early summer.

Juncus hoffmeisteri plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
