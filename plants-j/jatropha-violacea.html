Jatropha violacea

Jatropha violacea, commonly known as the purple leaf jatropha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking purple foliage and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain toxic seeds, which are a defense mechanism against herbivores. Jatropha violacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant leaf color and unique floral display.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
