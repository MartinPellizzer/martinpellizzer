Juncus secundiflorus

Juncus secundiflorus, commonly known as the lowland rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has cylindrical, grass-like stems that are usually green to reddish in color. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in dense spikes and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Juncus secundiflorus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
