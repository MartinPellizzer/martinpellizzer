Jasminum simplicifolium subsp. suavissimum

Jasminum simplicifolium subsp.

suavissimum, commonly known as sweet jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months.

The plant has slender, woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
