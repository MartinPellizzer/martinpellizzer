Justicia crassiuscula

Justicia crassiuscula, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking spikes of bright red bracts that resemble poker chips. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are opposite and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered within the bracts, producing a showy display during the summer months.

The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
