Jacquemontia hirsuta var. parvifolia

Jacquemontia hirsuta var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved false morning glory, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers with a distinct, sweet scent. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and riverbanks. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
