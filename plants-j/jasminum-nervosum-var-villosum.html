Jasminum nervosum var. villosum

Jasminum nervosum var. villosum, commonly known as the hairy jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild fragrance, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
