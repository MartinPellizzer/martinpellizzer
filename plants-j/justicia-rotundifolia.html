Justicia rotundifolia

Justicia rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leafed false arum, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and along stream banks. Its flowering period occurs during the wet season, and it is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage.

The species belongs to the Acanthaceae family, which is known for its showy flowers and diverse leaf structures.
