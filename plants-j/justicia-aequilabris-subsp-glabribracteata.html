Justicia aequilabris subsp. glabribracteata

Justicia aequilabris subsp.

glabribracteata, commonly known as the velvetleaf flameflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America. It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting throat.

The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth, waxy surface that gives the plant its common name.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
