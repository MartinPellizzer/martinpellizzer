Jambosa lancifolia

Jambosa lancifolia, commonly known as the African blackwood tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and has simple, alternate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red when mature.

Jambosa lancifolia is valued for its hard, durable timber and is often used in traditional medicine.
