Juncus quartinianus

Juncus quartinianus, commonly known as the Quartinian rush, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowering spikes consist of small, greenish-white flowers that appear in late spring to early summer.

Juncus quartinianus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
