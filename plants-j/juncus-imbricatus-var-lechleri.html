Juncus imbricatus var. lechleri

Juncus imbricatus var. lechleri, commonly known as the lechleri rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that often reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom during the late spring and early summer. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in moist to waterlogged soils, making it a common sight in marshy environments.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture that contributes to its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
