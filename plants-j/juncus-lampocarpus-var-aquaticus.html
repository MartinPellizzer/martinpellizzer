Juncus lampocarpus var. aquaticus

Juncus lampocarpus var. aquaticus, commonly known as the water rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that are typically green or reddish in color, often growing in dense clusters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Its rapid growth and ability to stabilize soil make it valuable for ecological restoration projects.
