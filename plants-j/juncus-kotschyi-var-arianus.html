Juncus kotschyi var. arianus

Juncus kotschyi var. arianus, commonly known as the Armenian rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, greenish flowers in spike-like clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and they form dense clumps that help stabilize the soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native environment.
