Jamesonia ceracea

Jamesonia ceracea, commonly known as the mountain snowbell, is a perennial herb native to the United States, particularly in the Appalachian Mountains.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Jamesonia ceracea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
