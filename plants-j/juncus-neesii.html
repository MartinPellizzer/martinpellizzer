Juncus neesii

Juncus neesii, commonly known as the Nees' rush, is a perennial plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil, Paraguay, and Argentina.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage composed of narrow, cylindrical leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist to water-saturated soils. Juncus neesii produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually arranged in spike-like clusters, and it serves as an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization and habitat provision for various wildlife species.
