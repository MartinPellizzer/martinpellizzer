Juncus bulbosus f. uliginosus

Juncus bulbosus f. uliginosus, commonly known as the bog rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland environments, such as bogs and marshes. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often densely packed, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor, water-saturated soils.

This species plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
