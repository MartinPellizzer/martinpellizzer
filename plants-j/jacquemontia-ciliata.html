Jacquemontia ciliata

Jacquemontia ciliata, commonly known as the silkyleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with distinctive, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to gardens, though it can be invasive in some areas.
