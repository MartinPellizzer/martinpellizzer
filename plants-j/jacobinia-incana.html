Jacobinia incana

Jacobinia incana, commonly known as the woolly jacobinia, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Jacobinia incana is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
