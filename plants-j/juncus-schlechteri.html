Juncus schlechteri

Juncus schlechteri, commonly known as the Schlechter's rush, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often sparsely branched. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear greenish-white flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along streams.

Juncus schlechteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland habitats.
