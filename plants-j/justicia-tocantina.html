Justicia tocantina

Justicia tocantina, commonly known as the Tocantins false arum, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows in wetlands and riparian areas, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually clustered in dense inflorescences, often with a spatulate or lanceolate shape.

The leaves are usually opposite, ovate to elliptic, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.
