Juno chlorotica

Juno chlorotica, commonly known as the green junco, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. Juno chlorotica thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

It is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
