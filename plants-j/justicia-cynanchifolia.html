Justicia cynanchifolia

Justicia cynanchifolia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall, creating a mist-like appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
