Justicia erythrantha

Justicia erythrantha, commonly known as the red-flowered false arnoldia, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking red, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with lance-shaped, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its vibrant flowers attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees, contributing to its ecological value.
