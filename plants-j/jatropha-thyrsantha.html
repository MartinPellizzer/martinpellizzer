Jatropha thyrsantha

Jatropha thyrsantha, commonly known as the thyrsothorn, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing toxic seeds. Jatropha thyrsantha is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Its toxic properties make it important to handle with care.
