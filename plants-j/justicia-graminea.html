Justicia graminea

Justicia graminea, commonly known as the grass pink, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and spikes of tubular, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, are pollinated by birds and insects.

Justicia graminea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a ground cover.
