Juncus polyanthemus var. cheesemanii

Juncus polyanthemus var. cheesemanii, commonly known as the Cheeseman's rush, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical spikes of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or damp environments such as stream banks, swamps, and marshes. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often form dense clumps.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
