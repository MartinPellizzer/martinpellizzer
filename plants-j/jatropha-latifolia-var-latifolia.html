Jatropha latifolia var. latifolia

Jatropha latifolia var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf jatropha, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow-orange flowers. It produces toxic, milky sap that contains latex, which is a key feature of the genus Jatropha.

This plant is often grown as an ornamental due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
