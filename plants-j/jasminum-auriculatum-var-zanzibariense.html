Jasminum auriculatum var. zanzibariense

Jasminum auriculatum var. zanzibariense, commonly known as Zanzibar jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its unique fragrance makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
