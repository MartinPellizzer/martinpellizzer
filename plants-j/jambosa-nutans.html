Jambosa nutans

Jambosa nutans, commonly known as the hanging jambosa, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its pendulous, fleshy fruits that hang from the branches. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange to red, spiny capsules containing the edible seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture with prominent veins.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
