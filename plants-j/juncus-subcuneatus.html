Juncus subcuneatus

Juncus subcuneatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 1 millimeter wide. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense, spike-like clusters, which are often subtended by bracts. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is commonly found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Juncus subcuneatus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
