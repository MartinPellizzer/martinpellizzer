Juglans latifolia

Juglans latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf walnut, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Juglandaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves. The tree produces compound fruits called walnuts, which are encased in a spiky husk. Its bark is thick and deeply furrowed, providing a rugged appearance.

Juglans latifolia is valued for its timber and is also an important species in ecological restoration projects.
