Jatropha loristipula

Jatropha loristipula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved physostegia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. Jatropha loristipula is typically found in moist, shaded areas of tropical and subtropical regions.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
