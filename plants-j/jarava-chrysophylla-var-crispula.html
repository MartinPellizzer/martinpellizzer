Jarava chrysophylla var. crispula

Jarava chrysophylla var. crispula, commonly known as the crinkled golden grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, golden-yellow inflorescences that are both attractive and functional for pollination. It is adapted to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils with low moisture availability.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes and native plant gardens.
