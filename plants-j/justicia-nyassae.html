Justicia nyassae

Justicia nyassae, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white, surrounded by vibrant, colorful leaves. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Justicia nyassae is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
