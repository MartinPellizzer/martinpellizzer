Juncus sonderianus

Juncus sonderianus, commonly known as the Sonder's rush, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of grass-like, cylindrical stems. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish flowers.

Juncus sonderianus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
