Jatropha tepiquensis

Jatropha tepiquensis, commonly known as the Tepic jatropha, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces capsules that contain toxic seeds, which are not suitable for human consumption. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Jatropha tepiquensis is also noted for its ornamental value and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
