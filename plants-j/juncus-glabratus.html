Juncus glabratus

Juncus glabratus, commonly known as the smooth rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are usually smooth and slightly woody at the base. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered flowers in early summer.

Juncus glabratus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
