Juncus pilosus subsp. maritimus

Juncus pilosus subsp.

maritimus, commonly known as the coastal rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions. It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences.

This subspecies thrives in saline and brackish environments, often found in tidal marshes and coastal dunes. Its fibrous roots help stabilize shorelines, making it ecologically significant in coastal ecosystems.

The plant is adapted to withstand salt spray and periodic flooding, showcasing its resilience in harsh maritime conditions.
