Jasminum aureum

Jasminum aureum, commonly known as the golden jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and a trailing growth habit, making it suitable for hanging baskets or as a ground cover. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Jasminum aureum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
