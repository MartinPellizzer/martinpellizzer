Juncus polycephalus

Juncus polycephalus, commonly known as the many-headed rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that often grow in dense clusters. The plant produces numerous small, spike-like inflorescences at the top of the stems, which bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically growing in a rosette at the base of the plant.

Juncus polycephalus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
