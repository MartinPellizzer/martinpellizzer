Juniperus communis var. variegata-aurea

Juniperus communis var. variegata-aurea, commonly known as the golden variegated juniper, is a compact, evergreen shrub characterized by its slow growth and dense, needle-like foliage.

Its leaves are typically bright yellow-green, with a striking variegation pattern that adds visual interest to gardens. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are ornamental and attract birds, though they are not typically edible for humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

This variety is prized for its unique coloration and hardy nature, thriving in temperate climates.
