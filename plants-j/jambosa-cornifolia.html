Jambosa cornifolia

Jambosa cornifolia, commonly known as the horned jambosa, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, branched stems and large, waxy leaves that are often ribbed or ridged. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink in color. Its fruit is a fleshy, berry-like structure that contains numerous seeds.

Jambosa cornifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
