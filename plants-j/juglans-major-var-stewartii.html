Juglans major var. stewartii

Juglans major var. stewartii, commonly known as Stewart's black walnut, is a variant of the black walnut tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces edible, walnut-like fruits that are encased in a thick, spiny husk. The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant member of the Juglandaceae family.
