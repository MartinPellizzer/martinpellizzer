Juniperus monosperma var. pinchotii

Juniperus monosperma var. pinchotii, commonly known as the Pinchot juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces blue-gray berries that are typically retained on the plant for several years. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
