Juniperus virginiana var. montana

Juniperus virginiana var. montana, commonly known as the mountain juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves and small, blue-gray berries that are often used in herbal remedies. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its aromatic foliage and resilience make it an important component of high-elevation ecosystems.
