Jacquemontia ovalifolia var. tomentosa

Jacquemontia ovalifolia var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly golden vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy, tomentose stems and leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and greenhouses.
