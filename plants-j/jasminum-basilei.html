Jasminum basilei

Jasminum basilei, commonly known as the Armenian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly rough texture.

Jasminum basilei is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its pleasant scent and visual appeal.
