Juncellus distachyos

Juncellus distachyos, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Juncaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows.

Juncellus distachyos plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
