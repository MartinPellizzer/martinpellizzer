Juncus bufonius subsp. turkestanicus

Juncus bufonius subsp.

turkestanicus, commonly known as the Turkestan rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wet meadows, and along riverbanks.

The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often slightly hairy, and it produces small, clustered spikelets of greenish or brownish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to wet environments and its role in stabilizing soil in riparian zones.
