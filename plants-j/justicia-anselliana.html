Justicia anselliana

Justicia anselliana, commonly known as the velvetleaf false arum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescence that resembles a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often dark green and glossy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and water features.
