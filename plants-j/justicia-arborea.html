Justicia arborea

Justicia arborea, commonly known as the tree justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of showy, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically bright red or orange, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

The plant is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various climates.
