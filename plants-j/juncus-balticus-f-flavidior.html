Juncus balticus f. flavidior

Juncus balticus f. flavidior, commonly known as the yellow bulrush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered spikes of yellowish-green flowers. Native to the coastal regions of the Baltic Sea, this plant thrives in brackish or saline wetlands. Its slender stems and narrow leaves are adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils and salt spray.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
