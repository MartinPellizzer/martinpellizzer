Juncus lampocarpus f. natans

Juncus lampocarpus f. natans, commonly known as the floating rush, is a variant of the common rush that exhibits unique aquatic adaptations.

This plant is characterized by its elongated, flexible stems that allow it to float on the surface of still or slow-moving freshwater bodies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense mats that provide habitat for small aquatic organisms. The plant reproduces through rhizomes, spreading vegetatively to colonize wetland areas.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing water edges and filtering water.
