Juncus arcticus

Juncus arcticus, commonly known as the arctic rush, is a perennial plant native to Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in tundra and alpine environments. Its cylindrical stems are usually unbranched and can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

Juncus arcticus plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small Arctic wildlife.
