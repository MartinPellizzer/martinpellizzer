Jasminum pipolyi

Jasminum pipolyi, commonly known as the Persian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran and Turkey.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has slender, woody stems and opposite, simple leaves that are typically oval in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The essential oil extracted from its flowers is valued for its aromatic properties and use in perfumery and traditional medicine.
