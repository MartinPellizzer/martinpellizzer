Justicia virgata

Justicia virgata, commonly known as the silverleaf justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Justicia virgata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
