Jacobaea aquatica

Jacobaea aquatica, commonly known as water ragwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found near streams, ponds, and other water bodies. The plant has dark green, alternate leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clustered inflorescences, blooming from late summer to early autumn.

Jacobaea aquatica is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and it plays a role in supporting local insect populations.
