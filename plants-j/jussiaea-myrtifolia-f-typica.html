Jussiaea myrtifolia f. typica

Jussiaea myrtifolia f. typica, commonly known as the typical form of the myrtle-leaved jussiaea, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
