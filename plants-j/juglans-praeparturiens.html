Juglans praeparturiens

Juglans praeparturiens, commonly known as the preparturient walnut, is a rare and enigmatic species of walnut native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and distinctive, edible nuts that mature in late summer. The plant exhibits unique reproductive traits, including a prolonged flowering period that coincides with specific environmental cues. Its botanical features suggest adaptations to high-altitude environments, with deep root systems and thick bark for moisture retention.

However, due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it remains a subject of ongoing botanical study.
