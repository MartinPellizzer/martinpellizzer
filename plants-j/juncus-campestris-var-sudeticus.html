Juncus campestris var. sudeticus

Juncus campestris var. sudeticus, commonly known as the Sudetic rush, is a perennial plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, grass-like leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or reddish-brown, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, ditches, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is native to Europe and is often found in temperate regions with adequate water availability.
