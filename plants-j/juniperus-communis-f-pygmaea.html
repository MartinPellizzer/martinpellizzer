Juniperus communis f. pygmaea

Juniperus communis f. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf juniper, is a low-growing shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, scale-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces blue-black berries that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and as a ground cover.

This variety is prized for its hardiness and ornamental value in horticulture.
