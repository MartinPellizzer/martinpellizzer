Juncus echinatus

Juncus echinatus, commonly known as the spiny rush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and pond edges. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers are usually green or brownish in color.

Juncus echinatus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
