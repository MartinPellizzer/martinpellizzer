Juncus dregeanus var. genuinus

Juncus dregeanus var. genuinus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 1 mm in width. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or reddish in color. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and stream banks.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization in its habitat.
