Jacobaea arenaria

Jacobaea arenaria, commonly known as the sand sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial with small, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water in dry environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dunes or rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for xerophytic conditions, showcasing its resilience to harsh climates.
