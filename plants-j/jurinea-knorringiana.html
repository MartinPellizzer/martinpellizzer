Jurinea knorringiana

Jurinea knorringiana, commonly known as the Arctic sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces delicate, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming in late summer. It has a low-growing habit with succulent stems and leaves adapted to withstand harsh, cold climates.

Jurinea knorringiana plays a role in stabilizing soil in its arctic environment and is often found in tundra and mountainous habitats.
