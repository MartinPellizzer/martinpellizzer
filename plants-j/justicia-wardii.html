Justicia wardii

Justicia wardii, commonly known as the velvetleaf false arum, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, spathaceous inflorescence that resembles a large, velvety leaf. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

Justicia wardii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
