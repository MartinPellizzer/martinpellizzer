Jasminum simplicifolium subsp. australiense

Jasminum simplicifolium subsp.

australiense, commonly known as the Australian jasmine, is a species native to Australia. It is a shrub characterized by its aromatic, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged oppositely on the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscaping.
