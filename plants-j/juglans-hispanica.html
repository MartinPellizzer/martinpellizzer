Juglans hispanica

Juglans hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish walnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the walnut family, Juglandaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive green husks that encase its edible nuts. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, rounded crown and rough, fissured bark. Its nuts are typically harvested in the autumn and are valued for their rich flavor and nutritional content.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food for various wildlife species.
