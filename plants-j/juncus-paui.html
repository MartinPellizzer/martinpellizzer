Juncus paui

Juncus paui, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It typically grows in dense clusters, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with slender, cylindrical stems that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in elongated spikes during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Juncus paui plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
