Jasminum rottlerianum var. thwaitesii

Jasminum rottlerianum var. thwaitesii, commonly known as the Arabian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a shrub that produces fragrant white flowers, which bloom in clusters during the winter months. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties. It thrives in warm, dry climates and requires well-drained soil.

The essential oil extracted from its flowers is used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
