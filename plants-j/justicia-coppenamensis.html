Justicia coppenamensis

Justicia coppenamensis, commonly known as the Congo flame or red flame plant, is a striking flowering plant native to Central and West Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically red or orange-red, with a distinctive blue or purple throat, and are arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
