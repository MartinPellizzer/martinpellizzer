Juncus paradoxus

Juncus paradoxus, commonly known as the paradox grass or water rush, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and typically grows in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of white or greenish flowers. It thrives in moist to water-saturated soils and is often used in wetland restoration projects.

Juncus paradoxus plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
