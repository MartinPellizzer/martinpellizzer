Jasminum didymum subsp. racemosum

Jasminum didymum subsp.

racemosum, commonly known as the racemosum jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forested areas and along riverbanks.
