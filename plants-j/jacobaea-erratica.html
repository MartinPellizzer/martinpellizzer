Jacobaea erratica

Jacobaea erratica, commonly known as the creeping tansy, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a low-growing, sprawling habit with aromatic, yellowish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in flat-topped inflorescences, producing yellow-orange blooms.

Jacobaea erratica is often found in moist, calcareous soils, growing in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.
