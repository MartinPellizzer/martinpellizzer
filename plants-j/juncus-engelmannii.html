Juncus engelmannii

Juncus engelmannii, commonly known as Engelmann's rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and covered in small, scale-like leaves. Its flowering spikes consist of numerous tiny, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Engelmann's rush plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
