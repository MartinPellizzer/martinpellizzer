Justicia mandonii

Justicia mandonii, commonly known as the red-flowered justicia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its most distinguishing feature is its showy, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters at the ends of the stems.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds and bees, making it an important plant in local ecosystems.
