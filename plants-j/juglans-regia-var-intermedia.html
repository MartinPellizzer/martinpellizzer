Juglans regia var. intermedia

Juglans regia var. intermedia, commonly known as the Persian walnut, is a variant of the English walnut that exhibits distinct botanical characteristics.

It is native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East, where it thrives in temperate climates. The tree produces large, robust nuts enclosed in a thick, green husk that splits open as the fruit matures. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets arranged alternately along the stem.

This variety is valued for its high-quality nuts and is often cultivated for both commercial and ornamental purposes.
