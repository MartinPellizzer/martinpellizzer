Juncus llanquihuensis

Juncus llanquihuensis, commonly known as the Llanquihue rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the temperate regions of South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, cylindrical stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks, wetlands, and other aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are often wind-pollinated.

Juncus llanquihuensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
