Justicia obtusicapsula

Justicia obtusicapsula, commonly known as the blunt-capsuled justicia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking spikes of tubular, colorful flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a rough texture.

The flowers, which bloom in summer, are primarily blue or purple, and they attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
