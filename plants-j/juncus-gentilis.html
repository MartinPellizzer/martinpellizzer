Juncus gentilis

Juncus gentilis, commonly known as the noble rush, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form dense clumps, and it produces small, greenish-brown flowers in spike-like clusters. It is often used in landscaping for its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.

Botanically, it is characterized by its rhizomatous growth and its preference for moist to wet soil conditions.
