Jacobaeastrum athanasiae

Jacobaeastrum athanasiae, commonly known as the golden coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow petals and dark brown central cone. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Jacobaeastrum athanasiae is valued for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to arid environments.
