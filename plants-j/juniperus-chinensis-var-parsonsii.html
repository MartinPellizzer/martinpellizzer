Juniperus chinensis var. parsonsii

Juniperus chinensis var. parsonsii, commonly known as Chinese juniper or Parsons' juniper, is a slow-growing evergreen shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, scale-like leaves that are arranged in dense clusters. The plant produces blue-gray berries that are typically consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to tolerate urban conditions, making it a versatile addition to gardens and landscapes.
