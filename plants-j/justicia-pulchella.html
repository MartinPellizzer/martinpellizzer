Justicia pulchella

Justicia pulchella, commonly known as the Mexican wheelcush or red wheelcush, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves and stems, which help it store water, making it drought-tolerant. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds, contributing to its ecological significance.
