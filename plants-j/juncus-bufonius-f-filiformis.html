Juncus bufonius f. filiformis

Juncus bufonius f. filiformis, commonly known as the slender bog rush, is a variant of the bog rush species native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are often densely packed, forming a dense mat on the ground. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, which are typically found at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist to waterlogged soils and is often found in marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wetland organisms.
