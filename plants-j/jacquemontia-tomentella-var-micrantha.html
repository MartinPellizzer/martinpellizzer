Jacquemontia tomentella var. micrantha

Jacquemontia tomentella var. micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered jenny.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers with five petals, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its growth habit is typically low and sprawling, often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The variety micrantha distinguishes it from other forms of Jacquemontia by its smaller, more compact flowers.
