Juniperus oxycedrus var. wittmanniana

Juniperus oxycedrus var. wittmanniana, commonly known as the Corsican juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically blue or black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
