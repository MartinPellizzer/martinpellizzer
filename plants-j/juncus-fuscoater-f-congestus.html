Juncus fuscoater f. congestus

Juncus fuscoater f. congestus, commonly known as the dense tufted rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish-brown in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows.

This subspecies is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
