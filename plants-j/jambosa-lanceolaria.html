Jambosa lanceolaria

Jambosa lanceolaria, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical fruit-bearing tree native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that develop into yellow or orange fruits. The fruit has a unique star-shaped appearance when cut open, with a sweet, juicy pulp and a central core containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
