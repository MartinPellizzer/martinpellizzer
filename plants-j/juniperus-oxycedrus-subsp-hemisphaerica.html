Juniperus oxycedrus subsp. hemisphaerica

Juniperus oxycedrus subsp.

hemisphaerica, commonly known as the Mediterranean juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, scale-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color.

The plant produces blue-black berries that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rocky or coastal environments.

Its aromatic properties and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
