Justicia imbricata

Justicia imbricata, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is commonly cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ability to grow in a variety of soil types.

Justicia imbricata is also known for its adaptability to warm climates and its use in traditional medicine in some regions.
