Jatropha diacantha

Jatropha diacantha, commonly known as the coral tree or red jatropha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling coral in appearance. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. Jatropha diacantha is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to several meters in height.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been studied for potential use in biofuel production.
