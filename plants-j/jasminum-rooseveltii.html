Jasminum rooseveltii

Jasminum rooseveltii, commonly known as Roosevelt's jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often releasing a sweet scent during the night. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and lance-shaped, with a glossy green surface.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
