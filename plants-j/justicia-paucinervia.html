Justicia paucinervia

Justicia paucinervia, commonly known as the narrow-veined flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with prominent, narrow veins, giving it its scientific name. Its showy, tubular flowers are usually red or orange, with a distinctive two-lipped structure, and are arranged in spikes.

Justicia paucinervia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
