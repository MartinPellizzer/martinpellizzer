Joachima phalaroides

Joachima phalaroides, commonly known as the slender marsh grass, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often branching and covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in spikes and bloom in late summer.

Joachima phalaroides plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic insects and birds.
