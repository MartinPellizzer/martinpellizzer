Jacquemontia nipensis

Jacquemontia nipensis, commonly known as the Florida morning glory, is a herbaceous annual vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in the spring and early summer, and it is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
