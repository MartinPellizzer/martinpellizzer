Jurinea leptoloba

Jurinea leptoloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sea bladder, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming species that thrives in saline and sandy environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or pale yellow in color. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to retain moisture in arid conditions.
