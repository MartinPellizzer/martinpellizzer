Joycea

Joycea, commonly known as the silver fern, is a genus of flowering plants native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery fronds that resemble a fern, with a silvery sheen on the underside. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Joycea species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are often cultivated in gardens.

They play an important ecological role in their native habitats by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
