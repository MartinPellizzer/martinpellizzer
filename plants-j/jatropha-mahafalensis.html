Jatropha mahafalensis

Jatropha mahafalensis, commonly known as the Madagascar physic nut, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by capsule fruits containing toxic seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Due to its toxicity, it is not typically cultivated for agricultural purposes.
