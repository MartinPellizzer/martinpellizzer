Jubelina bracteosa

Jubelina bracteosa, commonly known as the goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its yellow to orange flowers appear in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Jubelina bracteosa is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
