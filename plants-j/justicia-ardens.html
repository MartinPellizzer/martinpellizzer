Justicia ardens

Justicia ardens, commonly known as the flame flower, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red or orange flowers that resemble flames. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy bracts that enhance its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Justicia ardens is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant due to its eye-catching appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
