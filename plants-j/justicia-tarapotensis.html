Justicia tarapotensis

Justicia tarapotensis, commonly known as the Andean flame flower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly the Andean regions of Peru and Ecuador.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine pubescence, while its flowers are clustered in terminal spikes.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
