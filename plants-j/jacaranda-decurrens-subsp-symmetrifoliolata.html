Jacaranda decurrens subsp. symmetrifoliolata

Jacaranda decurrens subsp.

symmetrifoliolata, commonly known as the symmetrical-leaved jacaranda, is a striking tropical tree native to South America. It is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in pendant clusters, adding a dramatic visual impact to the landscape.

The leaves are palmately divided with a symmetrical arrangement of leaflets, giving the plant its subspecies name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree's wood is also valued for its durability and is used in furniture and construction.
