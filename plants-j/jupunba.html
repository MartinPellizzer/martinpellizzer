Jupunba

Jupunba, commonly known as the mountain pepper plant, is a shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, peppery leaves and small, white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive flavor and potential medicinal properties.

Jupunba thrives in high-altitude regions with cool, temperate climates.
