Juncus xiphioides var. montanus

Juncus xiphioides var. montanus, commonly known as the mountain rush, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae and is characterized by its grass-like, cylindrical stems that are often hairy. The plant produces small, greenish or brownish flowers in dense spikes, typically during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, streambanks, and rocky slopes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
