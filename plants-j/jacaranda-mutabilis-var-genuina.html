Jacaranda mutabilis var. genuina

Jacaranda mutabilis var. genuina, commonly known as the Australian jacaranda, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of pale purple to lilac flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple small, oval-shaped leaflets. The tree produces long, cylindrical pods that contain flat, winged seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
