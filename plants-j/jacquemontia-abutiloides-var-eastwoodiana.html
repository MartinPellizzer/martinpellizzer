Jacquemontia abutiloides var. eastwoodiana

Jacquemontia abutiloides var. eastwoodiana, commonly known as the Eastwood's mimosa, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and distinctive pod-like fruits. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its showy yellow flowers are arranged in clusters. It typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in partial to full sun.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinators.
