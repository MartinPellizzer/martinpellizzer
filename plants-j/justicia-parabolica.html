Justicia parabolica

Justicia parabolica, commonly known as the flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble flames, composed of tubular red or orange bracts. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in full sun and well-drained soil. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Justicia parabolica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
