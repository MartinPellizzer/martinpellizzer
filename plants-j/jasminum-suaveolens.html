Jasminum suaveolens

Jasminum suaveolens, commonly known as the Arabian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula and parts of southern Asia.

It is a shrub that can grow up to 2 meters tall, featuring opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the night. Its flowers emit a strong, sweet aroma, which is highly valued in perfumery and traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in warm, sunny climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
