Juno tadshikorum

Juno tadshikorum, commonly known as the Tibetan juniper, is a shrub native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and cone-bearing structure typical of the Cupressaceae family. The plant is adapted to harsh, cold environments and can thrive at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its woody stems form dense thickets, providing habitat for various alpine species.

Juno tadshikorum plays a crucial role in stabilizing soil in its mountainous habitat.
