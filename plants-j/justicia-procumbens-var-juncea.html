Justicia procumbens var. juncea

Justicia procumbens var. juncea, commonly known as the silverleaf flamebeard, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that produces striking, tubular red or orange flowers. The plant features opposite, narrow, silver- or gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy soils.

The flowers attract hummingbirds and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to pollinator gardens.
