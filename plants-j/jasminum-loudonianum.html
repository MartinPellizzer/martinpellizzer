Jasminum loudonianum

Jasminum loudonianum, commonly known as the Loudon jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has slender, woody stems and heart-shaped, dark green leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
