Justicia anisacanthus

Justicia anisacanthus, commonly known as flame flower, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and the southern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often covered in spiny hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
