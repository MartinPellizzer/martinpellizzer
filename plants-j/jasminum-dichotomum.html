Jasminum dichotomum

Jasminum dichotomum, commonly known as the two-branched jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has opposite, simple, and entire leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

Jasminum dichotomum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
