Juniperus occidentalis f. robinsonii

Juniperus occidentalis f. robinsonii, commonly known as the Robinson's western juniper, is a subspecies native to the western United States.

It is a shrub or small tree with needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that contain aromatic, toxic seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to harsh climates and it is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
