Jasminum calophyllum

Jasminum calophyllum, commonly known as the Cuban jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters tall, featuring opposite, simple, and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that are usually borne in clusters, emitting a strong, sweet scent. Its flowers bloom in the summer and are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and therapeutic properties.

Jasminum calophyllum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
