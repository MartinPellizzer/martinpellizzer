Jatropha stephani

Jatropha stephani, commonly known as the Sierra Leone jatropha, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that contain toxic seeds, which are not suitable for human consumption. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for biofuel applications, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
