Jatropha orangeana

Jatropha orangeana, commonly known as the Orangeleaf Jatropha, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange-colored leaves, which are often clustered at the ends of its branches. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that give way to capsule fruits containing toxic seeds. Jatropha orangeana is known for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm, humid climates.
