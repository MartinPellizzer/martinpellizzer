Jasminum multiflorum

Jasminum multiflorum, commonly known as the winter jasmine, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its stems are typically reddish-brown and covered with fine hairs.

Jasminum multiflorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to provide early seasonal color in gardens.
