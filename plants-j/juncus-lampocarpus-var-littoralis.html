Juncus lampocarpus var. littoralis

Juncus lampocarpus var. littoralis, commonly known as the coastal rush, is a perennial plant native to coastal regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that grow in dense clusters, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant thrives in saline and brackish wetlands, demonstrating a high tolerance for salt and fluctuating water levels. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, greenish flowers in spike-like clusters.

This variety plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
