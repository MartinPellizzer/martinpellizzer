Jambosa syzygioides

Jambosa syzygioides, commonly known as the Brazilian apple or jambolão, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of 3–5 meters. The plant produces small, round fruits with a yellow or orange skin and a sweet, juicy pulp. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy surface.

The plant is valued for its edible fruit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well.
