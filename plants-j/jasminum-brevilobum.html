Jasminum brevilobum

Jasminum brevilobum, commonly known as the narrow-lobed jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has slender, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Jasminum brevilobum is also used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
