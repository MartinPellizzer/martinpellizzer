Jacksonia spinosa

Jacksonia spinosa, commonly known as the New Zealand spinous daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny leaves and showy yellow-orange flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal and subalpine areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Jacksonia spinosa is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
