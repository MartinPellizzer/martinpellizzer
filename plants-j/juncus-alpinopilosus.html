Juncus alpinopilosus

Juncus alpinopilosus, commonly known as the alpine hairgrass, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, typical of the Juncaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Juncus alpinopilosus plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environments and provides habitat for various microorganisms and small invertebrates.
