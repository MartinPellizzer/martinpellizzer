Jasminum sambac var. heyneanum

Jasminum sambac var. heyneanum, commonly known as the Arabian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to regions of the Middle East and South Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often releasing a sweet scent at night. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and use in perfumery and traditional medicine.
