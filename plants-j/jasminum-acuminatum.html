Jasminum acuminatum

Jasminum acuminatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

Jasminum acuminatum is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic benefits.
