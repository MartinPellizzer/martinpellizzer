Jacquemontia obtusata

Jacquemontia obtusata, commonly known as the showy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with climbing stems that can reach lengths of up to 10 feet. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue to lavender in color, opening in the morning and closing by midday. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its seeds are enclosed in a distinctive, inflated fruit.

Jacquemontia obtusata thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, easy-to-grow ornamental plants.
