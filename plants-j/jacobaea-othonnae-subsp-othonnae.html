Jacobaea othonnae subsp. othonnae

Jacobaea othonnae subsp.

othonnae, commonly known as the Cape dandelion, is a perennial herb native to South Africa. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves.

The plant produces bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It has a fibrous root system and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to its arid environment.

The species is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
