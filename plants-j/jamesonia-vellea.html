Jamesonia vellea

Jamesonia vellea, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or shaded areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth, waxy texture.

Jamesonia vellea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
