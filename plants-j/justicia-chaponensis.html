Justicia chaponensis

Justicia chaponensis, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular, violet-red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Justicia chaponensis is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract hummingbirds.
