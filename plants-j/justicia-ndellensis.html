Justicia ndellensis

Justicia ndellensis, commonly known as the Natal flame bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, providing a distinctive texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

The genus Justicia is part of the Acanthaceae family, known for its showy flowers and ornamental value.
