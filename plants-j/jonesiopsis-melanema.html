Jonesiopsis melanema

Jonesiopsis melanema, commonly known as the black velvet fern, is a rare and striking member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark, velvety fronds that range from deep green to almost black in color. Native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, this fern thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental cultivation.

The plant's dense, leathery leaves contribute to its distinctive visual appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
