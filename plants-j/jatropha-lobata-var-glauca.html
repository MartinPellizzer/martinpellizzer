Jatropha lobata var. glauca

Jatropha lobata var. glauca, commonly known as the blue-leafed jatropha, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into orange or red berries. Its leaves are typically lobed and have a rough texture.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
