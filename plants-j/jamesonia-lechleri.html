Jamesonia lechleri

Jamesonia lechleri, commonly known as the Amazonian rubber tree, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is notable for its latex-producing properties, which have been traditionally used by indigenous communities. Its ecological role includes supporting various pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.

However, due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it faces conservation challenges.
