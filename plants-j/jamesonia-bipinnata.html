Jamesonia bipinnata

Jamesonia bipinnata, commonly known as the mountain snowberry, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations. The plant features compound leaves with two pairs of leaflets, giving it a distinctive, feathery appearance. Its showy white to pink flowers appear in clusters during the spring and early summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in harsh alpine environments.
