Jasminum monticola

Jasminum monticola, commonly known as the mountain jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often releasing a sweet scent during the night. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly glossy surface.

Jasminum monticola is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic properties.
