Jacquemontia evolvuloides var. brevipedunculata

Jacquemontia evolvuloides var. brevipedunculata, commonly known as the short-stalked golden vine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its climbing habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant features hairy, alternate leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
