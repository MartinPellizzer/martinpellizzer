Jumellea phalaenophora

Jumellea phalaenophora, commonly known as the orchid bell, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, bell-shaped flowers that resemble orchids. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, with intricate petal structures.

Jumellea phalaenophora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
