Justicia panduriformis

Justicia panduriformis, commonly known as the flame bush, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in tropical and subtropical regions.
