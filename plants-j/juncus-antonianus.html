Juncus antonianus

Juncus antonianus, commonly known as the tall rush, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in wet or moist soil conditions, often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
