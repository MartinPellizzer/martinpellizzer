Jambosa polypetala

Jambosa polypetala, commonly known as the white jambosa, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its leaves are broad and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The fruit, when mature, is a small, fleshy berry that contains seeds, making it a potential food source for wildlife.
