Juno pseudocaucasica

Juno pseudocaucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Cupressaceae family and is characterized by its scale-like leaves and blue-gray foliage. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain aromatic seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Juno pseudocaucasica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
