Juniperus virginiana var. elegans

Juniperus virginiana var. elegans, commonly known as the Eastern redcedar, is a deciduous conifer native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and produces blue-black berries that are an important food source for wildlife. The plant has a dense, pyramidal shape and is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

The species is also notable for its ability to tolerate urban environments and air pollution.
