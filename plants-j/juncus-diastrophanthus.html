Juncus diastrophanthus

Juncus diastrophanthus, commonly known as the silver rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale green flowers. It is often used in ornamental horticulture for its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in wetland environments.

Juncus diastrophanthus is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
