Juncus verticillatus

Juncus verticillatus, commonly known as the tufted rush, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often green or reddish in color. Its flowering spikes consist of small, greenish-brown flowers that appear in late spring to early summer.

Juncus verticillatus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
