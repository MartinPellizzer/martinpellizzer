Johnstonella diplotricha

Johnstonella diplotricha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water plantain, is a perennial aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or floating at the water's surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and are pollinated by insects.

Johnstonella diplotricha plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems by preventing erosion and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
