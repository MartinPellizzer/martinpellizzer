Jasminum carinatum

Jasminum carinatum, commonly known as the Mexican jasmine or the golden jasmine, is a flowering shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, tubular yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Jasminum carinatum is a member of the Oleaceae family, which includes other jasmine species.
