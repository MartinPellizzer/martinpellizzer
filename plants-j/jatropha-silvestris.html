Jatropha silvestris

Jatropha silvestris, commonly known as the wild jatropha, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain toxic seeds, which are not edible for humans or livestock. Jatropha silvestris is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in semi-arid environments.

Its latex sap contains irritants and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
