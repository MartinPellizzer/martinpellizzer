Jonesiopsis × lavandulacea

Jonesiopsis × lavandulacea, commonly known as the hybrid lavender hibiscus, is a cross between Jonesiopsis and Lavandula, combining traits from both parent species.

It is a shrub with woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping and coastal gardens.

Its unique appearance and fragrance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
