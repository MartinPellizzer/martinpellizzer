Jungia rugosa

Jungia rugosa, commonly known as the rough jungle grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardening in warm climates.

Jungia rugosa is also valued for its resilience and ability to withstand dry conditions.
