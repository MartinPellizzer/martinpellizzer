Justicia chaconii

Justicia chaconii, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a tropical plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular, red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in warm climates.

The flowers attract hummingbirds and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
