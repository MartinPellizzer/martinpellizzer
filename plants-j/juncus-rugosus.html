Juncus rugosus

Juncus rugosus, commonly known as the rough sedgeland, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like stems that are often covered in small, rough hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it produces small, three-lobed seeds.

Juncus rugosus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
