Juncus rochelianus

Juncus rochelianus, commonly known as the Rochel's rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikes, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist to waterlogged soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife, Juncus rochelianus is an important component of wetland ecosystems.
