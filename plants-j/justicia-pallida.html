Justicia pallida

Justicia pallida, commonly known as the white flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking clusters of white to pale pink tubular flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds and bees, making it an important plant for attracting pollinators.
