Juncus brunneus

Juncus brunneus, commonly known as brown rush, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish-brown in color, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, spike-like clusters of brownish flowers.

Juncus brunneus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
