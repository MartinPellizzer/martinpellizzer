Justicia inermis

Justicia inermis, commonly known as the velvetleaf or woollyleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in summer. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Justicia inermis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
