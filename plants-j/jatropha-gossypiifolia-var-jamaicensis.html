Jatropha gossypiifolia var. jamaicensis

Jatropha gossypiifolia var. jamaicensis, commonly known as the Jamaican cup plant, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its distinctive cup-shaped leaves that hold water, a feature that helps it survive in arid conditions. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species in tropical and subtropical climates.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
