Juncus gerardi subsp. atrofuscus

Juncus gerardi subsp.

atrofuscus, commonly known as blackstem rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are dark green to blackish in color, giving it its common name. It produces small, pale green flowers in dense spikes during the summer months.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
