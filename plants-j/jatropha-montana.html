Jatropha montana

Jatropha montana, commonly known as the mountain jatropha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly rough texture.

Jatropha montana is also noted for its ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a versatile addition to garden landscapes.
