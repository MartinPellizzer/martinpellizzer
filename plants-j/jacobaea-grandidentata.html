Jacobaea grandidentata

Jacobaea grandidentata, commonly known as the Great Coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features robust, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Jacobaea grandidentata is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
