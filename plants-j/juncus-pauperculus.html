Juncus pauperculus

Juncus pauperculus, commonly known as the poor man's rush, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like stems that are usually less than 1 meter in height. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spike-like inflorescences and are often greenish or brownish in color.

Juncus pauperculus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
