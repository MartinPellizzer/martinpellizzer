Justicia ovata

Justicia ovata, commonly known as the ovalleaf false arum, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, ovate-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple, with a spatulate shape and a prominent, hood-like bract. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The genus Justicia is known for its diverse floral structures, and Justicia ovata is no exception.
