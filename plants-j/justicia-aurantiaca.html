Justicia aurantiaca

Justicia aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange flame plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Justicia aurantiaca is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
