Jonesiopsis voigtii

Jonesiopsis voigtii, commonly known as the Voigt's ghost fern, is a rare and enigmatic fern species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that exhibit a unique, almost translucent appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, contributing to its limited propagation in the wild.

Due to habitat loss and its elusive nature, Jonesiopsis voigtii remains a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
