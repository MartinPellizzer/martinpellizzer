Juncus alpinus var. erectus

Juncus alpinus var. erectus, commonly known as the erect sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Juncaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like appearance with cylindrical, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This variety is notable for its hardy nature and ability to withstand cold temperatures.
