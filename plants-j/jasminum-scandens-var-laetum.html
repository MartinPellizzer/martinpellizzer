Jasminum scandens var. laetum

Jasminum scandens var. laetum, commonly known as the sweet woodruff vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing shrub that produces fragrant, white flowers clustered in umbels. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are opposite and emit a mild, sweet aroma. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for trellis coverage and landscape design.
