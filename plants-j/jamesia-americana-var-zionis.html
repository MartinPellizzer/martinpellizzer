Jamesia americana var. zionis

Jamesia americana var. zionis, commonly known as the Zion's flamebrush, is a shrub native to the western United States, particularly found in Utah and neighboring regions.

It is characterized by its striking red, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing year-round structure.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
