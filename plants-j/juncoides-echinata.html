Juncoides echinata

Juncoides echinata, commonly known as the spiny rush, is a perennial plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae, which includes various species of rushes and sedges. The plant is characterized by its cylindrical, grass-like stems that are often covered in small, spiny structures. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

Juncoides echinata is often found in moist or wetland environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
