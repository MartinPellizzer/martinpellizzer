Jumellea pandurata

Jumellea pandurata, commonly known as the sandmat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-like growth habit and is often found in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Jumellea pandurata is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
