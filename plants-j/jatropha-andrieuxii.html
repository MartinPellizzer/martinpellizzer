Jatropha andrieuxii

Jatropha andrieuxii, commonly known as the Madagascar spurge, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with smooth, often reddish bark and alternate, simple leaves. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, clustered in cyathia, which are cup-like structures typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Jatropha andrieuxii is also noted for its potential use in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage and unique appearance.
