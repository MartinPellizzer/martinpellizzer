Justicia fuentesii

Justicia fuentesii, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees, contributing to its ecological significance.
