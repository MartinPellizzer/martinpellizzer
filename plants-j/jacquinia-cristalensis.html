Jacquinia cristalensis

Jacquinia cristalensis, commonly known as the Crystal Mountain bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh mountain conditions.

Jacquinia cristalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
