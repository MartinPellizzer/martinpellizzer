Justicia gingiana

Justicia gingiana, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a striking tropical plant native to Central America.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Justicia gingiana thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
