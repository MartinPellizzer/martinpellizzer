Jacaranda obtusifolia subsp. obtusifolia

Jacaranda obtusifolia subsp.

obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved jacaranda, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of showy violet or purple flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant has a smooth, greyish bark and can grow up to 10 meters in height. Its flowers are followed by elongated, woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to warm, dry climates.
