Jambosa borneensis

Jambosa borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean jambul, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the island of Borneo.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces edible fruits that are similar in appearance to lychees but have a distinct, tart flavor. Its bark and leaves contain compounds used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
