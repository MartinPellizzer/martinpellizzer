Jacquemontia bifurcata

Jacquemontia bifurcata, commonly known as the split-leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Jacquemontia bifurcata is a herbaceous perennial that blooms in the spring and early summer.
