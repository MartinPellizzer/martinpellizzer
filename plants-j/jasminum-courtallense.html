Jasminum courtallense

Jasminum courtallense, commonly known as the Ceylon jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant has slender, woody stems and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

Jasminum courtallense is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic benefits.
