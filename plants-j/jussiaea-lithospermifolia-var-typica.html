Jussiaea lithospermifolia var. typica

Jussiaea lithospermifolia var. typica, commonly known as the stone root plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair, which helps reduce water loss. Its flowers are usually pale blue or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

This species is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
