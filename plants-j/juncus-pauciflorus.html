Juncus pauciflorus

Juncus pauciflorus, commonly known as the few-flowered rush, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae and is characterized by its cylindrical, grass-like stems that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense spikes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Juncus pauciflorus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other wet habitats.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
