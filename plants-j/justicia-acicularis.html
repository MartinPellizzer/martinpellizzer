Justicia acicularis

Justicia acicularis, commonly known as the needleleaf justicia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions. Its flowers are usually bright red or orange, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
