Jasminum welwitschii

Jasminum welwitschii, commonly known as the giant jasmine, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and can grow up to 10 meters in length, making it one of the tallest species in the jasmine genus. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are typically nocturnal, releasing their scent at night to attract pollinators.
