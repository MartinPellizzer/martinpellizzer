Juncus gracilis var. humilis

Juncus gracilis var. humilis, commonly known as the slender rush, is a flowering plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and dense clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems. It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to fluctuating water levels and nutrient-rich environments.
