Justicia reflexiflora var. glandulosa

Justicia reflexiflora var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, ascending stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with glandular hairs. The plant produces clusters of showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming during the wet season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in warm climates.
