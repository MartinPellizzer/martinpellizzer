Juncus acutiflorus var. multiflorus

Juncus acutiflorus var. multiflorus, commonly known as the many-flowered rush, is a perennial plant native to the wetlands of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers, usually in shades of white or pale pink, during the summer months. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
