Jasminum fruticans var. speciosum

Jasminum fruticans var. speciosum, commonly known as the fragrant honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its stems are often woody and can become semi-woody with age, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic flowers.
