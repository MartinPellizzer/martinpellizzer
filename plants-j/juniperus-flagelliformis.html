Juniperus flagelliformis

Juniperus flagelliformis, commonly known as the creeping juniper, is a shrub-like evergreen conifer native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its low, sprawling growth habit with flexible, needle-like leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are typically blue or purple in color and serve as a food source for various wildlife species. It is well adapted to harsh environments, thriving in sandy or rocky soils with minimal water.

Juniperus flagelliformis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat in its native range.
