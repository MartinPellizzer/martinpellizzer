Juncellus pallidiflorus

Juncellus pallidiflorus, commonly known as the paleflower rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its inflorescence is a spike-like cluster, and the flowers are usually greenish-white in color.

Juncellus pallidiflorus plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
