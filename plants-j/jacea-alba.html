Jacea alba

Jacea alba, commonly known as white jacea, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy white ray florets surrounded by yellow disk florets, making it visually striking. Jacea alba typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight.

It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
