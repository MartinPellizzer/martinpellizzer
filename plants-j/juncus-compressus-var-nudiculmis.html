Juncus compressus var. nudiculmis

Juncus compressus var. nudiculmis, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 1 mm wide. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of greenish or brownish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist to waterlogged soils and is often found in coastal areas and freshwater wetlands.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
