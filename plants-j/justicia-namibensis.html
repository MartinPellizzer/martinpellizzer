Justicia namibensis

Justicia namibensis, commonly known as the Namibian flame flower, is a striking shrub native to the arid regions of Namibia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall.

Justicia namibensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like birds and insects.
