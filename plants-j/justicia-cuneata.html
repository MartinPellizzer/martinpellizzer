Justicia cuneata

Justicia cuneata, commonly known as the narrowleaf flamebeard, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking spikes of bright red or orange tubular flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Justicia cuneata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
