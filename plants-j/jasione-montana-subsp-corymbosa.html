Jasione montana subsp. corymbosa

Jasione montana subsp.

corymbosa, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and hairy, with stems that are usually unbranched. Its flowers are blue to purple, arranged in dense cymes, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
