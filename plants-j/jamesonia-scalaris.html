Jamesonia scalaris

Jamesonia scalaris, commonly known as the mountain snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the Appalachian Mountains in the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, rocky soils at high elevations. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers in early spring, which are arranged in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Jamesonia scalaris is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
