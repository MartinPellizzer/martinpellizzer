Juglans oblonga

Juglans oblonga, commonly known as the Chinese black walnut, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the walnut family, Juglandaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, edible nuts. The tree produces oval-shaped nuts enclosed in a thick, green husk that turns yellowish as it matures. Its bark is typically dark and fissured, and it can grow up to 20 meters tall in suitable conditions.

The plant is valued for its hard, durable wood and its role in traditional medicine and culinary uses.
