Juniperus venusta

Juniperus venusta, commonly known as the silver fir or desert juniper, is a species of shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which is typically blue-gray in color and covered in a fine layer of wax. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for various wildlife species. Its adaptability to arid and semi-arid environments makes it a resilient plant in harsh climates.

Juniperus venusta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and attractive appearance.
