Jussiaea elegans var. typica

Jussiaea elegans var. typica, commonly known as the slender water-milfoil, is a semi-aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are arranged spirally along its stems, and it produces small, white flowers in clusters. It is known for its ability to tolerate both full sun and partial shade, making it a versatile aquatic plant.

Jussiaea elegans var. typica plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small fish and invertebrates.
