Jungia revoluta

Jungia revoluta, commonly known as the red-flowered jungle hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red bracts that surround its yellow flowers, which bloom throughout the warm season. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

Jungia revoluta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
