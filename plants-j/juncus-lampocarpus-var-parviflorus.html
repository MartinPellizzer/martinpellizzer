Juncus lampocarpus var. parviflorus

Juncus lampocarpus var. parviflorus, commonly known as the narrow-flowered rush, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like, cylindrical stems that grow in dense clusters. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color, blooming in late summer to early fall. It thrives in saline or brackish wetlands and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salinity and wind exposure.

This variety is often found in tidal marshes and is an important component of the local ecosystem.
