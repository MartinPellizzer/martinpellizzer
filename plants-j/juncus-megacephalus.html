Juncus megacephalus

Juncus megacephalus, commonly known as the giant rush, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets at the top of the stems, which contain the tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats in moist soils.

Juncus megacephalus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
