Jacobinia paniculata

Jacobinia paniculata, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that bloom in pendulous clusters, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets and can grow as a tree or shrub depending on the conditions. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Jacobinia paniculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is popular in gardens and landscapes.
