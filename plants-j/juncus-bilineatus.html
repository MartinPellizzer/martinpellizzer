Juncus bilineatus

Juncus bilineatus, commonly known as the two-lined rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has cylindrical, grass-like stems that are often reddish in color and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are narrow and tapering, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters during the summer months.

The plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
