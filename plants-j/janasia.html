Janasia

Janasia, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters. Janasia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
