Jasminum longisetum

Jasminum longisetum, commonly known as the long-hair jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are opposite, simple, and have a rough texture with long, stiff hairs. This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
