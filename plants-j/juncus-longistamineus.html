Juncus longistamineus

Juncus longistamineus, commonly known as the long-stamen rush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, cylindrical leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and pond edges.

Juncus longistamineus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
