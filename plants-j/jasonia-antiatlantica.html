Jasonia antiatlantica

Jasonia antiatlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic rock rose, is a perennial shrub native to the coastal regions of western Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are adapted to withstand saline and arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Jasonia antiatlantica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
