Juncus articulatus subsp. fontanesii

Juncus articulatus subsp.

fontanesii, commonly known as the Fontana rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems that are often jointed, a feature typical of the Juncaceae family.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in dense spikes, which are typically found at the tips of the stems. It thrives in wet or damp environments such as marshes, stream sides, and pond margins.

Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
