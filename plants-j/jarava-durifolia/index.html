Jarava Durifolia

Jarava durifolia is a hardy perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It thrives in harsh environments with minimal water and nutrient availability. This plant is known for its dense, fibrous growth habit and ability to stabilize soil in degraded landscapes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of dust or salt. Jarava durifolia plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife and preventing erosion in desert ecosystems.
