Jamesia americana

Jamesia americana, commonly known as the American flamebrush, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking red to orange-pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blossoms. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Jamesia americana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
