Jarava megapotamia

Jarava megapotamia, commonly known as the South American feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a common species in dry grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects and birds.
