Jobinia schizocorona

Jobinia schizocorona, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant has a woody stem with a distinctive crown-like structure, giving it its scientific name. Its flowers emit a strong fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.
