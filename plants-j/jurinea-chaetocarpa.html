Jurinea chaetocarpa

Jurinea chaetocarpa, commonly known as the hairy bladdergrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, and its leaves are usually hairy and fleshy. It is adapted to survive in harsh conditions with its succulent tissues that store water.

The species is native to regions of Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.
