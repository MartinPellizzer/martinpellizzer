Juniperus chinensis var. leeana

Juniperus chinensis var. leeana, commonly known as the Chinese juniper or creeping juniper, is a low-growing shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, scale-like leaves that are tightly packed along the stems. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are typically retained on the plant for extended periods. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

This variety is also valued for its hardiness and adaptability to different climates.
