Jacobaeastrum algoense

Jacobaeastrum algoense, commonly known as the Algoense thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features spiny leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grassland and scrub habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
