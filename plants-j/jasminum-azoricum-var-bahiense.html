Jasminum azoricum var. bahiense

Jasminum azoricum var. bahiense, commonly known as the Azores jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Azores Islands.

It is a shrub characterized by its fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its sweet scent and delicate appearance.
