Juglandicarya

Juglandicarya, commonly known as the Chinese walnut tree, is a species native to China and belongs to the Juglandaceae family.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a broad, spreading crown and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in catkins, followed by edible nuts enclosed in a thick, woody husk. Its seeds are valued for their nutritional content and are used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The tree is also appreciated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
