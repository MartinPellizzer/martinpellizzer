Jatropha phyllacantha var. repanda

Jatropha phyllacantha var. repanda, commonly known as the broad-leaved jatropha, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features elliptical to ovate leaves that are often broad and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers followed by orange-red berries containing toxic seeds. Its growth habit is typically upright, with branching stems that can reach several meters in height.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
