Jatropha spathulata var. genuina

Jatropha spathulata var. genuina, commonly known as the narrow-leaf physic nut, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, ovate to lanceolate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits containing toxic seeds, which are a key feature of the genus Jatropha. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
