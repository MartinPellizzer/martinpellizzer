Juniperus gracilior var. saxicola

Juniperus gracilior var. saxicola, commonly known as the saxicola juniper, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, often prostrate form and small, scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are usually blue or purple and contain aromatic, edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid and alpine habitats.
