Jussiaea alata

Jussiaea alata, commonly known as the broadleaf water plantain, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are typically floating or semi-submerged in water. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in ponds, marshes, and along the edges of streams.

Jussiaea alata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
