Juncus clarkei var. marginatus

Juncus clarkei var. marginatus, commonly known as the marginate rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of South America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and floodplains. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often slightly hairy, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it produces lightweight seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
