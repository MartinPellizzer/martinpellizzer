Juniperus gymnocarpa

Juniperus gymnocarpa, commonly known as the smooth-seed juniper, is a species of juniper native to parts of Asia, including China and the Russian Far East.

It is a coniferous evergreen shrub or small tree that typically grows in mountainous regions. The plant produces small, scale-like leaves and female cones that mature into blue-black berries. Its botanical features include compact growth habits and the ability to thrive in harsh, cold climates.

Juniperus gymnocarpa is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
