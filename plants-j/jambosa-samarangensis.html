Jambosa samarangensis

Jambosa samarangensis, commonly known as the Samarang mango, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Indonesia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its large, elongated fruits with a thick, fibrous skin. The plant produces yellow to orange flesh that is sweet and aromatic, often used in both fresh consumption and culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil and requires a long growing season.

Jambosa samarangensis is valued not only for its fruit but also for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and growth habit.
