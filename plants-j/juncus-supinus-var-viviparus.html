Juncus supinus var. viviparus

Juncus supinus var. viviparus, commonly known as the viviparous rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is notable for its viviparous reproduction, where buds form at the base of the stems and can root and grow independently. It thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in coastal and freshwater habitats.

This variant is closely related to other juncus species but is distinguished by its unique reproductive strategy.
