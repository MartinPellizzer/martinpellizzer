Jussiaea inclinata

Jussiaea inclinata, commonly known as the creeping jussiaea, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands and marshes of South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dense mats, with slender stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Jussiaea inclinata is adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic environments, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
