Jussiaea suffruticosa var. subglabra

Jussiaea suffruticosa var. subglabra, commonly known as the smoothleaf jussiaea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and glabrous. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of riparian habitats.
