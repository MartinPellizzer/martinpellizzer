Juniperus erectopatens

Juniperus erectopatens, commonly known as the upright-leaved juniper, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves that are arranged in compact clusters, giving it a distinct appearance. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Juniperus erectopatens is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
