Juniperus przewalskii f. pendula

Juniperus przewalskii f. pendula, commonly known as the hanging juniper, is a unique variant of the Przewalski juniper.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that cascade downward, giving it an elegant, weeping appearance. This form is a natural mutation of the typical upright growth habit of Juniperus przewalskii. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions of central Asia.

The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species.
