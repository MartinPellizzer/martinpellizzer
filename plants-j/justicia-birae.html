Justicia birae

Justicia birae, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a striking flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in full sun to partial shade, preferring well-drained, moist soil. The plant is characterized by its clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, creating a mist-like appearance. Its lance-shaped leaves are typically a deep green, and the plant is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant color.

Justicia birae is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
