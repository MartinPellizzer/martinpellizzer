Juncus sikkimensis var. longiflorus

Juncus sikkimensis var. longiflorus, commonly known as the long-flowered sikkim rush, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance with cylindrical, hollow stems. The plant produces elongated, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
