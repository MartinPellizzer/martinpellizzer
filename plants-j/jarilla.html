Jarilla

Jarilla, commonly known as *Lycium carolinianum*, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and wetlands. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a bright red berry that ripens in the fall and is often consumed by birds and small mammals.

Botanically, Jarilla is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants.
