Justicia congrua

Justicia congrua, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and a strong, spicy scent. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.

Justicia congrua is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in containers or as a landscape plant.
