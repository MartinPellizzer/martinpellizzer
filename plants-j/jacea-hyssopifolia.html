Jacea hyssopifolia

Jacea hyssopifolia, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Jacea hyssopifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
