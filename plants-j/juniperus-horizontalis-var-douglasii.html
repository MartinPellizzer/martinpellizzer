Juniperus horizontalis var. douglasii

Juniperus horizontalis var. douglasii, commonly known as creeping juniper, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to North America.

It features dense, scale-like foliage that forms a mat-like structure, often used in rock gardens and as ground cover. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various climates.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ornamental appearance in landscaping.
