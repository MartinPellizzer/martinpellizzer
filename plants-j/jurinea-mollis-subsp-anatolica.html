Jurinea mollis subsp. anatolica

Jurinea mollis subsp.

anatolica, commonly known as Anatolian sea holly, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas, sandy soils, and rocky habitats, demonstrating a high degree of salt tolerance. Its small, hardy stature makes it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
