Juncus lampocarpus f. acutipetalus

Juncus lampocarpus f. acutipetalus, commonly known as the sharp-petioled rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets at the tips of the stems. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
