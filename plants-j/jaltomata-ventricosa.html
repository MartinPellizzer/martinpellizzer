Jaltomata ventricosa

Jaltomata ventricosa, commonly known as the balloon flower, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It is characterized by its tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has a short, compact stem. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often covered in a fine, hairy texture.

Jaltomata ventricosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
