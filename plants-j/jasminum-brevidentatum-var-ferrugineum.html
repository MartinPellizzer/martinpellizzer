Jasminum brevidentatum var. ferrugineum

Jasminum brevidentatum var. ferrugineum, commonly known as the rusty-leaf jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically ovate with a distinctive rusty or reddish-brown underside, giving it its common name. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant aroma.
