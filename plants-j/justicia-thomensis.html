Justicia thomensis

Justicia thomensis, commonly known as the Thomas's jewelweed, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
