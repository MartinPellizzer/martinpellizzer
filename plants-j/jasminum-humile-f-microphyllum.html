Jasminum humile f. microphyllum

Jasminum humile f. microphyllum, commonly known as the dwarf jasmine, is a compact, trailing shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate fragrance and compact growth habit.
