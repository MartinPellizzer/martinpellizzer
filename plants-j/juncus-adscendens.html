Juncus adscendens

Juncus adscendens, commonly known as the tall rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often forming dense clumps. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish flowers in spikes during the summer months. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, pond edges, and along streams.

Juncus adscendens plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
