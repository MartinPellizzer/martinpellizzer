Juniperus rigida var. conferta

Juniperus rigida var. conferta, commonly known as the dense juniper or compact juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are typically dark blue to black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and as a landscape ornamental.

Juniperus rigida var. conferta is also valued for its resistance to pests and diseases, contributing to its popularity in horticulture.
