Jacobinia caducifolia

Jacobinia caducifolia, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a waxy coating.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.
