Justicia gonatistachya

Justicia gonatistachya, commonly known as the flame flower or red flame plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical name reflects its distinctive floral structure and its native habitat.
