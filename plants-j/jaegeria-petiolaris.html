Jaegeria petiolaris

Jaegeria petiolaris, commonly known as the spider plant or staghorn fern, is a unique epiphytic plant that thrives in humid environments.

It has a distinctive appearance with its long, arching leaves that resemble spider legs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by spore-bearing capsules, which are used for propagation. It is often found growing on trees or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ability to absorb moisture from the air makes it well-suited for indoor cultivation with proper humidity levels.
