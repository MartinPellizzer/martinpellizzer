Juniperus virginiana var. smithii

Juniperus virginiana var. smithii, commonly known as the Smith's redcedar, is a variety of the eastern redcedar.

It is a coniferous evergreen shrub or tree native to the southeastern United States. The plant features scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in compact clusters. Its cones are small, woody, and contain aromatic, berry-like seeds.

This variety is adapted to a range of soil types and is often found in dry, open habitats.
