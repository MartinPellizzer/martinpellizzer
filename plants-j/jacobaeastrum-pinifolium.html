Jacobaeastrum pinifolium

Jacobaeastrum pinifolium, commonly known as the pine-leaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has needle-like leaves that resemble those of pine trees, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in dry, open habitats.

Jacobaeastrum pinifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
