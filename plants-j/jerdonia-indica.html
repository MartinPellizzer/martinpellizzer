Jerdonia indica

Jerdonia indica, commonly known as the Indian jerdonia, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
