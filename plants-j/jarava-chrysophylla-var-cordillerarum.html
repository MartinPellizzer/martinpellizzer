Jarava chrysophylla var. cordillerarum

Jarava chrysophylla var. cordillerarum, commonly known as the Sierra grass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a silvery sheen. This variety thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in late spring to early summer.

The plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
