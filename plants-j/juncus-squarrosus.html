Juncus squarrosus

Juncus squarrosus, commonly known as the woolly rush, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in soft, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer.

Juncus squarrosus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
