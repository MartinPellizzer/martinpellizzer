Juniperus chinensis subsp. taiwanensis

Juniperus chinensis subsp.

taiwanensis, commonly known as the Taiwan juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to Taiwan. It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
