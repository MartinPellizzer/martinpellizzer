Juncus juzepczukii

Juncus juzepczukii, commonly known as the Juzepczuk's rush, is a perennial plant native to parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters near the tips of the stems. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes and along water bodies.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense vegetation in suitable habitats.
