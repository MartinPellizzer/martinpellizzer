Juno allisonii

Juno allisonii, commonly known as the Allison's juniper, is a species of shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, cone-like fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are needle-like and retain their color year-round, contributing to its ornamental value.

Juno allisonii plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
