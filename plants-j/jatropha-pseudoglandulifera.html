Jatropha pseudoglandulifera

Jatropha pseudoglandulifera, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, often hairy stems and alternate, simple leaves that are usually elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing toxic, black seeds. It is part of the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its milky sap, which is irritant to skin.

Jatropha pseudoglandulifera is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in arid to semi-arid environments.
