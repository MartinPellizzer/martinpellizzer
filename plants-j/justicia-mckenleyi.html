Justicia mckenleyi

Justicia mckenleyi, commonly known as McKenley's glorybower, is a striking flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture, while its stems are often reddish in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
