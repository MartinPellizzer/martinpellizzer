Juniperus communis var. pendens

Juniperus communis var. pendens, commonly known as the hanging juniper, is a variant of the common juniper that exhibits distinctive pendulous growth.

It is a shrub or small tree native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, often found in rocky or coastal areas. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are used in gin production and have medicinal properties. Its foliage consists of scale-like leaves, and it is evergreen, retaining its leaves year-round.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
