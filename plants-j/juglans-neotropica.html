Juglans neotropica

Juglans neotropica, commonly known as the tropical walnut, is a species of flowering plant in the family Juglandaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, warm climates. The tree produces large, lobed leaves and distinctive, spiky fruits that contain edible seeds. Its wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for construction and furniture.

The plant also plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
