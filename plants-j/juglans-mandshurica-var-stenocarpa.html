Juglans mandshurica var. stenocarpa

Juglans mandshurica var. stenocarpa, commonly known as the Siberian walnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Far East of Asia.

It belongs to the walnut family, Juglandaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and edible nuts. The tree produces small, hard-shelled nuts that are typically harvested in the autumn. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and potential for nut production.
