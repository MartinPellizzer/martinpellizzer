Jambosa myrtifolia

Jambosa myrtifolia, commonly known as the African snowberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in winter. The plant produces small, round, white berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Jambosa myrtifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
