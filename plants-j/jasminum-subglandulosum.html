Jasminum subglandulosum

Jasminum subglandulosum, commonly known as the pale jasmine or white jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually covered in fine, glandular hairs. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

Jasminum subglandulosum is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
