Juncus suboxycarpus

Juncus suboxycarpus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a species of flowering plant in the family Juncaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often found in temperate regions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in moist soil conditions.

Juncus suboxycarpus plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various small organisms.
