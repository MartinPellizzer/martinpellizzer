Juno kuschakewiczii

Juno kuschakewiczii, commonly known as the Kuschakewicz's juniper, is a species of juniper native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree, typically growing to a height of 1 to 5 meters. The plant has needle-like leaves that are dark green and often have a bluish or grayish tint. Its small, blue-black berries are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

The plant is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.
