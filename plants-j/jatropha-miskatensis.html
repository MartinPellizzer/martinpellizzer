Jatropha miskatensis

Jatropha miskatensis, commonly known as the Sudanese physic nut, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain toxic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid regions, often growing in disturbed soils.

Due to its toxicity, it is important to handle the plant with care.
