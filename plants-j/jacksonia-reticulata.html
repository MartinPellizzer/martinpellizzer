Jacksonia reticulata

Jacksonia reticulata, commonly known as the New Zealand flame flower, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow disk flowers surrounded by bright petals. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Jacksonia reticulata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
