Juncellus pervillei

Juncellus pervillei, commonly known as the Perville's rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to humid conditions. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems that are often densely packed, forming dense clumps. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a prominent midrib.

The flowering spikes are usually borne at the top of the stems, producing small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.
