Jasione humilis subvar. tazzekana

Jasione humilis subvar. tazzekana, commonly known as the Tazzekan bellflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal rosettes of linear to oblanceolate leaves and produces small, blue to violet flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is relatively drought-tolerant.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
