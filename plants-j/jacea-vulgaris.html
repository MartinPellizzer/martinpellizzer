Jacea vulgaris

Jacea vulgaris, commonly known as common jacea, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in umbels. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is often found in woodlands, along stream banks, and in damp meadows.

Jacea vulgaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
