Jungia pauciflora subsp. amplistipula

Jungia pauciflora subsp.

amplistipula, commonly known as the broad-leafed jungia, is a member of the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers arranged in compact clusters. It is native to high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in the Andes.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
