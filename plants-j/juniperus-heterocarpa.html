Juniperus heterocarpa

Juniperus heterocarpa, commonly known as the smooth juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces blue or black berries that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Juniperus heterocarpa plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
