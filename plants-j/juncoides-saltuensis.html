Juncoides saltuensis

Juncoides saltuensis, commonly known as the salt marsh rush, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant thrives in saline or brackish wetlands, often growing in dense stands along tidal flats and marshes. Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil and prevent erosion in coastal environments.

Juncoides saltuensis plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
