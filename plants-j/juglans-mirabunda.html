Juglans mirabunda

Juglans mirabunda, commonly known as the Mexican walnut, is a species of deciduous tree native to central Mexico.

It belongs to the walnut family, Juglandaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and male and female flowers on separate trees. The tree produces a large, edible nut enclosed in a spiky husk, which matures in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant has historical significance in traditional medicine and is valued for its hard, durable wood.
