Jasminum sambac var. gimea

Jasminum sambac var. gimea, commonly known as the Gimea jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a sweet, intoxicating scent. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with opposite, simple, and ovate leaves. Its flowers are primarily pollinated by nocturnal pollinators such as moths and bats.

This variety is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
