Justicia dianthera var. tonduzii

Justicia dianthera var. tonduzii, commonly known as the Crimson Beauty, is a striking tropical plant native to Central America.

It features showy, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are glossy and arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
