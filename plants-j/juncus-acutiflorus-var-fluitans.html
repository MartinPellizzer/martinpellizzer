Juncus acutiflorus var. fluitans

Juncus acutiflorus var. fluitans, commonly known as floating rush, is a variant of the common rush that exhibits aquatic adaptations.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. This species typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in spikes, and it produces lightweight seeds that can float on water.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing aquatic habitats and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
