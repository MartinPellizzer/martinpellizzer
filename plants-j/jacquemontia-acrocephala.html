Jacquemontia acrocephala

Jacquemontia acrocephala, commonly known as the scarlet lily or red morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its climbing habit, often using tendrils to ascend supports. It blooms in late spring to early summer, and its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
