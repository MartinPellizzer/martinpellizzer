Jussiaea suffruticosa var. pilosolinearis

Jussiaea suffruticosa var. pilosolinearis, commonly known as the silky willow or silky jussiaea, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and is often found in wetland or swampy environments. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically greenish-white and appear in clusters, contributing to its ecological role in supporting pollinators.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens or as a ground cover in moist soils.
