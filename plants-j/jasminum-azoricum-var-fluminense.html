Jasminum azoricum var. fluminense

Jasminum azoricum var. fluminense, commonly known as the Brazilian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring aromatic, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are simple, opposite, and lanceolate, with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that contain seeds, and it thrives in humid, tropical climates.

It is valued for its fragrant blooms and is often cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
