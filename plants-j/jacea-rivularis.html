Jacea rivularis

Jacea rivularis, commonly known as the river jasmine, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as along riverbanks and in forest understories. The plant features slender, climbing stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters.

Jacea rivularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and light conditions.
