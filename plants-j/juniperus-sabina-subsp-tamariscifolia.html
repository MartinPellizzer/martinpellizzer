Juniperus sabina subsp. tamariscifolia

Juniperus sabina subsp.

tamariscifolia, commonly known as the Tamarisk juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its grayish-green foliage and small, scale-like leaves that are arranged in flat, sprout-like clusters.

The plant produces tiny, blue-black berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It is adapted to poor, well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy plant in harsh environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
