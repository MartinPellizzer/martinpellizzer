Juncus czetzii

Juncus czetzii, commonly known as the Czetzsche's rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the wetlands of eastern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually green to reddish in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. Juncus czetzii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
