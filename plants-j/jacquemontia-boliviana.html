Jacquemontia boliviana

Jacquemontia boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian golden vine, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of five-petaled flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
