Justicia macarenensis

Justicia macarenensis, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in clusters at the ends of the branches, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant display.
