Jacea decumbens

Jacea decumbens, commonly known as the prostrate jacea, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It features a sprawling growth habit with stems that lie close to the ground, often rooting at the nodes. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Jacea decumbens is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky outcrops.
