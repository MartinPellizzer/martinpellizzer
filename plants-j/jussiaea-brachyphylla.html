Jussiaea brachyphylla

Jussiaea brachyphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved waterweed, is a perennial aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in South America.

It typically grows in slow-moving or still water, such as ponds and lakes, and is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are arranged spirally along its stems. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged or floating, and it plays a role in providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

Jussiaea brachyphylla is also used in phytoremediation due to its ability to absorb nutrients and heavy metals from the water.
