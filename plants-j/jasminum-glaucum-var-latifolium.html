Jasminum glaucum var. latifolium

Jasminum glaucum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf jasmine, is a flowering plant native to southern China.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

The flowers bloom in spring and are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants.
