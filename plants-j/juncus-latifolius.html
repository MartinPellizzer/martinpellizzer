Juncus latifolius

Juncus latifolius, commonly known as broadleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that are often used in floral arrangements due to their aesthetic appeal. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into three-winged seeds, facilitating wind pollination. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall grassy appearance.

Juncus latifolius plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
