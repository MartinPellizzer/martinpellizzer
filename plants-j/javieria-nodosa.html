Javieria nodosa

Javieria nodosa, commonly known as the nodular sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and its distinctive blue to purple flowers. The plant features basal leaves that are linear and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched. Javieria nodosa thrives in coastal environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
