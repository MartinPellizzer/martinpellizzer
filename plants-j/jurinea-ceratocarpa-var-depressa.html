Jurinea ceratocarpa var. depressa

Jurinea ceratocarpa var. depressa, commonly known as the dwarf bladderwrack, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to coastal areas of the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It thrives in saline, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in intertidal zones. The plant features small, bladder-like structures called pneumatocysts, which aid in gas exchange and buoyancy. Its leaves are typically narrow and succulent, adapted to withstand harsh maritime conditions.

This variety is closely related to other members of the Chenopodiaceae family, sharing similar morphological and ecological traits.
