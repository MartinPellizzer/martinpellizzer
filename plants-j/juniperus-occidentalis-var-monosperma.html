Juniperus occidentalis var. monosperma

Juniperus occidentalis var. monosperma, commonly known as the single-seeded juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves and produces small, berry-like cones that contain a single seed. This variety is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and mountainous regions. Its botanical features include aromatic foliage and a distinctive, compact growth habit.

The plant is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.
