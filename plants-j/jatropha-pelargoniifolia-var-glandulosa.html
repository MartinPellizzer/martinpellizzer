Jatropha pelargoniifolia var. glandulosa

Jatropha pelargoniifolia var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular jatropha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a yellow center. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are typically alternate and ovate in shape. It produces capsule fruits that contain toxic seeds, a common trait among the genus Jatropha.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
