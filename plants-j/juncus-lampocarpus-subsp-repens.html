Juncus lampocarpus subsp. repens

Juncus lampocarpus subsp.

repens, commonly known as the creeping rush, is a perennial plant native to coastal areas of southern California. It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and slender, cylindrical stems that often root at the nodes.

The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along dunes and tidal flats, showcasing its adaptability to coastal environments. Its flowering spikes appear in late summer, producing small, greenish-brown flowers that are clustered in spikelets.

This subspecies plays an important role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
