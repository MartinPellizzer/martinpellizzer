Juncus squarrosus subsp. ellmanii

Juncus squarrosus subsp.

ellmanii, commonly known as the Ellman's bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams.

The plant has cylindrical, grass-like stems that are often reddish or purplish in color. Its small, spike-like inflorescence consists of numerous tiny, three-lobed flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
