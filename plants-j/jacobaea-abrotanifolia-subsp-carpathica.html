Jacobaea abrotanifolia subsp. carpathica

Jacobaea abrotanifolia subsp.

carpathica, commonly known as Carpathian ragwort, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian Mountains. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant features basal leaves that are lobed and covered in fine hairs, while the stem is hairy and often branched. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It contains pyrrolizidine alkaloids, which can be toxic to livestock.
