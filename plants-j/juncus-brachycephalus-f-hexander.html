Juncus brachycephalus f. hexander

Juncus brachycephalus f. hexander, commonly known as the dwarf rush, is a compact, herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of short, cylindrical stems that often form a low-growing mat. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, three-lobed flowers, which are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp environments.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for small wildlife.
