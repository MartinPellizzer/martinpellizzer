Justicia pilosula

Justicia pilosula, commonly known as the silky justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in clusters, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, Justicia pilosula is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and easy care requirements.
