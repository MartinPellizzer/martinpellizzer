Juncus balticus f. tenuis

Juncus balticus f. tenuis, commonly known as the slender bog rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Juncaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as bogs and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, often clustered at the tip of the stem.

This plant plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
