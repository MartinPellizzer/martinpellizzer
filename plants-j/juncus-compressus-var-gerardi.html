Juncus compressus var. gerardi

Juncus compressus var. gerardi, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often slightly compressed, and it produces small, clustered spikelets of greenish or brownish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and they are often used in basket weaving and other traditional crafts.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
