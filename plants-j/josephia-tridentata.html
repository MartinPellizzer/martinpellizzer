Josephia tridentata

Josephia tridentata, commonly known as the three-toothed jojoba, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, waxy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has three distinct lobes on its leaves, giving it its common name. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

The plant is valued for its hardy nature and ability to withstand drought conditions.
