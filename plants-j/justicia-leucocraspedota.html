Justicia leucocraspedota

Justicia leucocraspedota, commonly known as the white-veined justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with prominent white veins, giving it its common name. It produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers in shades of white and purple.

Justicia leucocraspedota is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
