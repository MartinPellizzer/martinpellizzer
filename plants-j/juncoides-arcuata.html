Juncoides arcuata

Juncoides arcuata, commonly known as the bent rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Juncaginaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems that often curve or bend, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and stream banks.

Juncoides arcuata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
