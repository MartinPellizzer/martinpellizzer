Jasminum ixoroides

Jasminum ixoroides, commonly known as the sweet scent jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, with opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its pleasant aroma and is often cultivated in gardens and as a hedging plant.
