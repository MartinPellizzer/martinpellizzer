Justicia sulphurea

Justicia sulphurea, commonly known as the sulfur plant or yellow justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows as a shrub or perennial herb, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and a square stem, typical of the mint family (Lamiaceae).

Justicia sulphurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its bright, showy blooms.
