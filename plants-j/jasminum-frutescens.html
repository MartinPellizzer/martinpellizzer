Jasminum frutescens

Jasminum frutescens, commonly known as the wild jasmine or evergreen jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a sweet, heady scent. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, providing a lush green backdrop to the delicate blooms.

Jasminum frutescens is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also used in traditional medicine.
