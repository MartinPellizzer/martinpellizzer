Juncus sphaerocephalus

Juncus sphaerocephalus, commonly known as the globe sedge, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, spherical inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and pond margins, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

The flowers are small and clustered, producing seeds that are dispersed by water, contributing to its ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
