Juncus commixtus

Juncus commixtus, commonly known as the mixed rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often used in floral arrangements due to their aesthetic appeal. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Juncus commixtus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
