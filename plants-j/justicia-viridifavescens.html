Justicia viridifavescens

Justicia viridifavescens, commonly known as the green-yellow justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually green with a slight yellowish tinge. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant blooms and ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.

The species is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
