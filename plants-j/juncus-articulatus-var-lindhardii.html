Juncus articulatus var. lindhardii

Juncus articulatus var. lindhardii, commonly known as the Lindhard's rush, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, jointed stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered at the base of the stems. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, ditches, and stream banks.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
