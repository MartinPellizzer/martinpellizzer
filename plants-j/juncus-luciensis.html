Juncus luciensis

Juncus luciensis, commonly known as the shining rush, is a species of flowering plant in the family Juncaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often bright green or reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It is often found in wetland habitats, including marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Juncus luciensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wetland biodiversity.
