Juncus communis var. conglomeratus

Juncus communis var. conglomeratus, commonly known as the clustered rush, is a perennial plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in Europe and parts of Asia.

It forms dense, grass-like clusters with cylindrical, often reddish stems that are covered in small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes during the summer months, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in saturated soils and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
