Jacquemontia evolvuloides f. microphylla

Jacquemontia evolvuloides f. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf heliotrope, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in diameter, giving it its distinguishing "dwarf" characteristic. The plant produces clusters of showy, violet-blue flowers that resemble those of the common heliotrope, though smaller in size. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Its botanical features make it a unique and ornamental addition to gardens, particularly in warm climates.
