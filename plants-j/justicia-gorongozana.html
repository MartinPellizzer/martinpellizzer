Justicia gorongozana

Justicia gorongozana, commonly known as the African shield fern, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, shield-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which attract pollinators like birds and insects. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses in warm climates.
