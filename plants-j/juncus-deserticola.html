Juncus deserticola

Juncus deserticola, commonly known as the desert rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions. The plant has cylindrical, grass-like stems that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. Its narrow leaves are usually short and clustered at the base, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Juncus deserticola plays a role in stabilizing soil in desert environments and provides minimal forage for wildlife.
