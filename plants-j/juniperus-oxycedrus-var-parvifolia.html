Juniperus oxycedrus var. parvifolia

Juniperus oxycedrus var. parvifolia, commonly known as the dwarf juniper or Mediterranean juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that is typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are typically dark blue when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice in horticulture.
