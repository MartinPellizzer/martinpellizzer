Jacobaea erucifolia subsp. argunensis

Jacobaea erucifolia subsp.

argunensis, commonly known as the Armenian tansy, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish-green foliage and bright yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in compact, disc-like heads.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
