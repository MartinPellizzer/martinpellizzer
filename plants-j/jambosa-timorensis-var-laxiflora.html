Jambosa timorensis var. laxiflora

Jambosa timorensis var. laxiflora, commonly known as the Timor jambosa, is a small evergreen shrub native to the island of Timor.

It features simple, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers clustered in loose inflorescences, which bloom during the warmer months. Its fruit is a small, edible drupe with a sweet, juicy pulp.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential culinary uses in local traditions.
