Jacquemontia pentanthos

Jacquemontia pentanthos, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and twining stems that climb or trail along the ground. Its flowers are typically five-petaled and emit a mild fragrance.

Jacquemontia pentanthos is often found in moist, woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
