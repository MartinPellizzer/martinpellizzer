Jasminum brachyscyphum

Jasminum brachyscyphum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has opposite, simple, and lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy green. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for both garden cultivation and container planting.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its pleasant aroma and compact growth habit.
