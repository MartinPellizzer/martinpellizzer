Juncus manasiensis

Juncus manasiensis, commonly known as the Manas River rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of the northeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of streams and rivers. The plant has tall, slender cylindrical stems that are usually unbranched and covered in a dense layer of fine, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small, clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and are typically greenish or brownish in color.

Juncus manasiensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
