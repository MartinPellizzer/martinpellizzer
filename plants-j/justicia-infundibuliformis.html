Justicia infundibuliformis

Justicia infundibuliformis, commonly known as the candle bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that resemble candles, hence its common name. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Justicia infundibuliformis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
