Juncus crassifolius

Juncus crassifolius, commonly known as broadleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with broad, flat leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are often surrounded by bracts. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Juncus crassifolius plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
