Justicia repanda

Justicia repanda, commonly known as the red-veined begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with prominent red veins against a deep green background. The plant produces showy clusters of tubular, pink to red flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
