Jatropha globosa

Jatropha globosa, commonly known as the globular jatropha, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its spherical, green, fleshy fruits that resemble small balls, which contain toxic seeds. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, often with a hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow and clustered in racemes.

Jatropha globosa is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
