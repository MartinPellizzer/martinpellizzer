Juncus acutus var. leopoldii

Juncus acutus var. leopoldii, commonly known as the Leopold's rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and pond edges.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization in wetland habitats.
