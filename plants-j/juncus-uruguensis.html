Juncus uruguensis

Juncus uruguensis, commonly known as the Uruguay rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its flowering spikes produce small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are often overlooked.

Juncus uruguensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
