Jacksonia fasciculata

Jacksonia fasciculata, commonly known as the New Zealand dandelion, is a low-growing perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer, followed by fluffy white seed heads. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with hairy, narrow leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in damp, shady areas.

Jacksonia fasciculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native insect populations.
