Jasminum brevipes

Jasminum brevipes, commonly known as the dwarf jasmine, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 meter and features dark green, glossy leaves. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Jasminum brevipes is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
