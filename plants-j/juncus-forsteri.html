Juncus forsteri

Juncus forsteri, commonly known as the Forster's rush, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and cylindrical, spike-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its narrow, cylindrical leaves are usually green to reddish in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Juncus forsteri plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
