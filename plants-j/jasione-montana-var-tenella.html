Jasione montana var. tenella

Jasione montana var. tenella, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in open woodland or scrub habitats. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are blue to purple in color, forming bell-shaped blooms that appear in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
