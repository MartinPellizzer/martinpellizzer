Juniperus virginiana f. globosa

Juniperus virginiana f. globosa, commonly known as the globe juniper, is a compact, shrub-like form of the eastern red cedar.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical growth habit and small, scale-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

This form is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
