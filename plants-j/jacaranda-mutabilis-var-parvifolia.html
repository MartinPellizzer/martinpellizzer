Jacaranda mutabilis var. parvifolia

Jacaranda mutabilis var. parvifolia, commonly known as the parvifolia jacaranda, is a small, ornamental tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The leaves are compound, with slender, elongated leaflets that give the plant a light, airy appearance. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its showy flowers and relatively compact size, it is often cultivated in gardens and urban landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
