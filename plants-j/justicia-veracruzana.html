Justicia veracruzana

Justicia veracruzana, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds, contributing to its ecological significance.
