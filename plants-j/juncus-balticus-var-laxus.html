Juncus balticus var. laxus

Juncus balticus var. laxus, commonly known as the loose rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that grow in dense clusters, often reaching heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous tiny, grass-like flowers, which are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, pond edges, and along stream banks.

Its adaptability to wet environments makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
