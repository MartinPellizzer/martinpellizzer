Justicia glischrantha

Justicia glischrantha, commonly known as the purple false arum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple bracts that surround small, white flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
