Jacksonia chappilliae

Jacksonia chappilliae, commonly known as the Chappill's knapweed, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with a central disk. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and often covered in a waxy coating.

Jacksonia chappilliae is relatively small, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters, and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
