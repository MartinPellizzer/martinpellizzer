Juglans mandshurica f. shinanoana

Juglans mandshurica f. shinanoana, commonly known as the Shinano walnut, is a variant of the Manchurian walnut native to Japan.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its large, compound leaves and distinctive, elongated nuts. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil. Its botanical features include a rugged, fissured bark and male and female flowers on separate trees.

The nuts are valued for their hard shell and rich, oily kernels, making them both ornamental and economically significant.
