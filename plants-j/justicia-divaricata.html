Justicia divaricata

Justicia divaricata, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly rough texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Justicia divaricata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
