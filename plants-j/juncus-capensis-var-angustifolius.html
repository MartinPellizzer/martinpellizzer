Juncus capensis var. angustifolius

Juncus capensis var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrowleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as riverbanks and lake shores. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, which are usually greenish or brownish in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
