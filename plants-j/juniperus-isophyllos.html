Juniperus isophyllos

Juniperus isophyllos, commonly known as the smoothleaf juniper, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage that is typically arranged in flat sprays, giving it a distinct appearance. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are an important food source for various birds and small mammals. Juniperus isophyllos thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested habitats at high elevations.

Its slow growth rate and hardy nature make it a valuable ornamental plant in colder climates.
