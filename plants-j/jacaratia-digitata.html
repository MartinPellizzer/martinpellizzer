Jacaratia digitata

Jacaratia digitata, commonly known as the Mexican flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
