Jasminum azoricum var. aureovariegatum

Jasminum azoricum var. aureovariegatum, commonly known as the golden variegated jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a distinctive yellow-green variegation pattern. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its pleasant aroma and attractive foliage.
