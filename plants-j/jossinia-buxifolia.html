Jossinia buxifolia

Jossinia buxifolia, commonly known as the boxleaf jossinia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy foliage, with leaves that are oval-shaped and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by round, red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its compact growth and aesthetic appeal.

Jossinia buxifolia is also valued for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and its use in traditional medicine in some cultures.
