Jaffrea erubescens

Jaffrea erubescens, commonly known as the red-flowered jaffrea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant red blooms. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of hair. Its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Jaffrea erubescens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
