Jatropha alamanii

Jatropha alamanii, commonly known as the Mexican physic nut, is a shrub native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by capsule fruits containing toxic seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
