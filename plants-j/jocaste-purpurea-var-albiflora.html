Jocaste purpurea var. albiflora

Jocaste purpurea var. albiflora, commonly known as the White-flowered Purple Jocaste, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.

Its blooms appear in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant touch to landscapes.
