Juncus alpinoarticulatus var. marshallii

Juncus alpinoarticulatus var. marshallii, commonly known as the marsh jointed rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish or purple in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming dense clumps that help stabilize soil in wet areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
