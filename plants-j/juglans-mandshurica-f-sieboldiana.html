Juglans mandshurica f. sieboldiana

Juglans mandshurica f. sieboldiana, commonly known as the Manchurian walnut, is a variant of the black walnut native to eastern Asia.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its large, lobed leaves and distinctive, edible nuts enclosed in a thick, green husk. The tree produces yellowish-green male catkins and female flowers, typical of the Juglandaceae family. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Its wood is highly valued for its strength and durability, making it useful in furniture and construction.
