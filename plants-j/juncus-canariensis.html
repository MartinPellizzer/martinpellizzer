Juncus canariensis

Juncus canariensis, commonly known as the Canary rush, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands and parts of Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, ditches, and stream banks. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often used for basket weaving due to their fibrous texture. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spike-like clusters and are pollinated by wind.

Juncus canariensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
