Justicia aurantiimutata

Justicia aurantiimutata, commonly known as the orange flame plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant orange-red bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, which are typically less showy than the colorful bracts. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

The ornamental value of Justicia aurantiimutata makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
