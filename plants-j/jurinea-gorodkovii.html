Jurinea gorodkovii

Jurinea gorodkovii, commonly known as the desert sandflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has a succulent stem and leaves adapted to conserve water, making it well-suited for harsh, dry environments. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in steppes and deserts.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitats.
