Jacobaea disjuncta

Jacobaea disjuncta, commonly known as the Disjunct hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats at high elevations, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Jacobaea disjuncta is a perennial herb that plays a role in stabilizing alpine ecosystems.
