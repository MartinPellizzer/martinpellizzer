Jatropha harmsiana

Jatropha harmsiana, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and milky sap, which can be toxic. The plant typically grows as an herb or shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and often have a hairy texture.

Jatropha harmsiana is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.
