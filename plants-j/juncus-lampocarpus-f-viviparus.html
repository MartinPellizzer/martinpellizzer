Juncus lampocarpus f. viviparus

Juncus lampocarpus f. viviparus, commonly known as the viviparous rush, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of California.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. One notable feature is its viviparous nature, where the plant produces viable seeds while still attached to the mother plant.

This adaptation allows for rapid propagation in disturbed or saline environments.
