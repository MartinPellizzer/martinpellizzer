Juglans steyermarkii

Juglans steyermarkii, commonly known as the Steyermark hickory, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the walnut family, Juglandaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with odd pinnate structure. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by edible nuts enclosed in a thick, spiny husk.

Its bark is deeply furrowed and dark gray in color, while its wood is strong and durable, making it valuable for construction and furniture.
