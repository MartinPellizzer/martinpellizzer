Jacquinotia myrsinites

Jacquinotia myrsinites, commonly known as the golden saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early summer. The plant has small, rounded leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help protect it from harsh environmental conditions. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a valued species in alpine botany.
