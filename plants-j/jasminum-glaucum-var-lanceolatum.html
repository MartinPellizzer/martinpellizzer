Jasminum glaucum var. lanceolatum

Jasminum glaucum var. lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf jasmine, is a flowering plant native to southern China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers emit a sweet, aromatic scent, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, thriving in temperate climates.
