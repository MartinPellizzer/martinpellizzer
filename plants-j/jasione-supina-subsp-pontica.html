Jasione supina subsp. pontica

Jasione supina subsp.

pontica, commonly known as the Pontic jasione, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its low-growing habit and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in a loose cluster, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
