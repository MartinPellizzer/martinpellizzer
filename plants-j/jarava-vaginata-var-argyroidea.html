Jarava vaginata var. argyroidea

Jarava vaginata var. argyroidea, commonly known as silverleaf jarava, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from dense covering of fine, hair-like structures called trichomes. The plant typically grows in compact, tufted clumps, with narrow, linear leaves that are often rolled when young. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelets that are usually pale green to brown in color.

This species is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including high temperatures and low water availability.
