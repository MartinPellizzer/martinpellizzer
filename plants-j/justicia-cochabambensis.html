Justicia cochabambensis

Justicia cochabambensis, commonly known as the Cochabamba flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually bright red or orange, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
