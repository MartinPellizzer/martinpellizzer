Jacquemontia asarifolia

Jacquemontia asarifolia, commonly known as the mountain morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking blue to purple flowers with five petals. The plant has heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive addition to native plant gardens and ecological landscapes.
