Josephia conferta

Josephia conferta, commonly known as the Cape rose or white rose of the Cape, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, compound leaves and produces legume-like pods that contain its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
