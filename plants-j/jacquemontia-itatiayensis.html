Jacquemontia itatiayensis

Jacquemontia itatiayensis, commonly known as the Itatiaya morning glory, is a herbaceous vine native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple and blue. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
