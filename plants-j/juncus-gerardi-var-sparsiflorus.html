Juncus gerardi var. sparsiflorus

Juncus gerardi var. sparsiflorus, commonly known as the sparse-flower rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, pond edges, and along stream banks. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems that are usually green or reddish in color, and it produces small, spike-like clusters of white or pale green flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil.

Juncus gerardi var. sparsiflorus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
