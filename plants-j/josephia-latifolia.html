Josephia latifolia

Josephia latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf josephia, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats. Its flowering period typically occurs in the spring and early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local pollinators.
