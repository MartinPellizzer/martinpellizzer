Jacaratia hassleriana

Jacaratia hassleriana, commonly known as the silverleaf jacaranda, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are silver-gray in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink or white flowers during the spring. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, sunny climates.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
