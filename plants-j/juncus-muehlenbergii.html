Juncus muehlenbergii

Juncus muehlenbergii, commonly known as Muhly grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often display a reddish or purplish hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pink or red in color, adding ornamental value to landscapes. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swales, and pond edges.

Juncus muehlenbergii is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
