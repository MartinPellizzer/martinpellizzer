Jaumea speciosa

Jaumea speciosa, commonly known as the silverleaf flax, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
