Juncus trifidus var. triflorus

Juncus trifidus var. triflorus, commonly known as the three-flowered rush, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Juncaginaceae and is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, three-flowered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms, it plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
