Jacaranda filicifolia

Jacaranda filicifolia, commonly known as the lacebark jacaranda, is a deciduous tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that resemble ferns, giving it its common name. The tree produces striking clusters of violet-blue flowers in the summer months. Its bark is fibrous and has a distinctive lacy appearance, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and shade-providing canopy.
