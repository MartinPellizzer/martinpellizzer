Jacobinia pubescens

Jacobinia pubescens, commonly known as the velvetleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its spread in the wild.
