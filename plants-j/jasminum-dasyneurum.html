Jasminum dasyneurum

Jasminum dasyneurum, commonly known as the Chinese jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, with opposite, simple, and usually hairy leaves. Its flowers are primarily pollinated by insects, and it produces small, dry fruits containing seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a hedging plant.
