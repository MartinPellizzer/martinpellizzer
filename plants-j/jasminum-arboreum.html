Jasminum arboreum

Jasminum arboreum, commonly known as the tree jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and evergreen leaves, which are typically oval-shaped and glossy. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the spring and summer months. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
