Juncus phyllophorus

Juncus phyllophorus, commonly known as the broadleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage that grows in clusters, with long, narrow leaves that are often broader than those of other rush species. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes, which are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found along the edges of ponds, lakes, and streams.

Juncus phyllophorus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
