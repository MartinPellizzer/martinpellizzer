Juglans pitteursii

Juglans pitteursii, commonly known as the Mexican walnut, is a deciduous tree native to the highlands of Mexico.

It belongs to the walnut family, Juglandaceae, and is characterized by its large, compound leaves and distinctive, edible nuts. The tree produces yellowish-green flowers in catkins, which are wind-pollinated. Its nuts are encased in a thick, woody shell and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its hardy nature and potential economic importance in sustainable agriculture.
