Jambosa melastomifolia

Jambosa melastomifolia, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, where new shoots arise from the base of the plant. The plant produces showy, often violet-colored flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. Its leaves are usually opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy texture.

Jambosa melastomifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is widely cultivated as a houseplant.
