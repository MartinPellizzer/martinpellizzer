Justicia arcuata

Justicia arcuata, commonly known as the red mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that resemble small flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 6 feet, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or red and are borne in dense, terminal clusters.

Justicia arcuata is known for its ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
