Jasminum pteropodum

Jasminum pteropodum, commonly known as the silver jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
