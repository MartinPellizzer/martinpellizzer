Jasminum farreri

Jasminum farreri, commonly known as the Chinese jasmine, is a flowering plant native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, with opposite, simple, and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Its flowers are highly aromatic and are often used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a source of essential oil.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated for its beauty and scent.
