Jussiaea speciosa

Jussiaea speciosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf water plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that often have a distinctive velvet-like texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in ponds, marshes, and along the edges of slow-moving water bodies.

Jussiaea speciosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to help stabilize aquatic ecosystems.
