Justicia quadrifaria

Justicia quadrifaria, commonly known as the four-leaf justicia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. It is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
