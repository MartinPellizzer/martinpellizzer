Jasminum wrayi

Jasminum wrayi, commonly known as the African jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, usually during the rainy season. Its flowers are followed by small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

Jasminum wrayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
