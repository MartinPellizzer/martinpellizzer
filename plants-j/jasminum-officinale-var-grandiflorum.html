Jasminum officinale var. grandiflorum

Jasminum officinale var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the common jasmine, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually entire leaves that are glossy and dark green. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

This variety is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and its use in perfumery and aromatherapy.
