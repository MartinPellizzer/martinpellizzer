Jacaranda cuspidifolia

Jacaranda cuspidifolia, commonly known as the African jacaranda, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has compound leaves composed of numerous small, pointed leaflets, giving it its scientific name "cuspidifolia," meaning "pointed leaves.". It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its fragrant blossoms.
