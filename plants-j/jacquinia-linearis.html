Jacquinia linearis

Jacquinia linearis, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, sunny environments.

Jacquinia linearis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
