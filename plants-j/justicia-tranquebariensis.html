Justicia tranquebariensis

Justicia tranquebariensis, commonly known as the Transkei glorybower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture, while its inflorescences are terminal and consist of clusters of vibrant flowers.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
