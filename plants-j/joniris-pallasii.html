Joniris pallasii

Joniris pallasii, commonly known as the Pallas's lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its striking white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive red or purple dorsal sepal. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stem is usually short and sturdy.

Joniris pallasii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
