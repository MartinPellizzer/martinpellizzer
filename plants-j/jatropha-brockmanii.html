Jatropha brockmanii

Jatropha brockmanii, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.

The plant has been studied for its potential in phytoremediation due to its ability to accumulate heavy metals.
