Jacquemontia coerulea

Jacquemontia coerulea, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant is characterized by its twining habit, with stems that climb or trail along the ground. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

The flowers are pollinated by bees and other insects, making it an important plant for pollinators in its native habitat.
