Jatropha gossypiifolia var. dissecta

Jatropha gossypiifolia var. dissecta, commonly known as the Indian flame flower, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its vibrant red bracts and yellow flowers.

It is native to regions of India and Southeast Asia, where it thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant features palmate leaves with deeply lobed segments, giving it a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its showy floral display.

Jatropha gossypiifolia var. dissecta is also known for its ability to produce toxic compounds, making it important to handle with care.
