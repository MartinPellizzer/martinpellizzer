Juncus hybridus

Juncus hybridus, commonly known as the hybrid rush, is a tall, grass-like plant native to wetlands and moist soils in North America.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with cylindrical, jointed stems that are often reddish or greenish in color. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers at the top of the stems, which are usually greenish or brownish in hue. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Juncus hybridus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
