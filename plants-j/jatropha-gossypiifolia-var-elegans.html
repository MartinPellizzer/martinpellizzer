Jatropha gossypiifolia var. elegans

Jatropha gossypiifolia var. elegans, commonly known as the velvetleaf jatropha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange or red berries containing toxic seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for medicinal use, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and research settings.
