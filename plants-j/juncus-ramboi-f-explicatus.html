Juncus ramboi f. explicatus

Juncus ramboi f. explicatus, commonly known as the broadleaf rush, is a variant of the genus Juncus, characterized by its robust, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, exhibiting a preference for full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of multiple flowering spikes that bloom in late summer.

This plant plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
