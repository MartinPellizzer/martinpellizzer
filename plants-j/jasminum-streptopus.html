Jasminum streptopus

Jasminum streptopus, commonly known as the spiral jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its helical, twisting growth pattern, which gives it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and as a houseplant.
