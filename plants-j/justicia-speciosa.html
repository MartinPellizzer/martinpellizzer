Justicia speciosa

Justicia speciosa, commonly known as the showy justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as an herb or shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with opposite, ovate leaves. Its inflorescences are terminal and consist of clusters of tubular flowers, often with two lips, which attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.

Justicia speciosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
