Juncus bulbosus f. fluitans

Juncus bulbosus f. fluitans, commonly known as the floating rush, is a variant of the bulbous rush characterized by its unique ability to float on water surfaces.

This plant exhibits a rhizomatous growth habit, with its stems and leaves adapted to thrive in wetland environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of spikelets that bear small, three-lobed flowers. The plant is often found in shallow water or moist soils, contributing to the ecological dynamics of aquatic habitats.

Due to its adaptability, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
