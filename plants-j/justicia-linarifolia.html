Justicia linarifolia

Justicia linarifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved jewelweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, and it thrives in moist, sunny environments.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and along stream banks, making it a common weed in many regions.
