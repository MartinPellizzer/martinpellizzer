Justicia racemosa

Justicia racemosa, commonly known as the whorled flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers in clusters at the ends of branches. The flowers are usually bright red or orange, attracting birds and insects for pollination.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant blooms.
