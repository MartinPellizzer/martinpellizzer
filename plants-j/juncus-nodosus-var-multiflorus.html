Juncus nodosus var. multiflorus

Juncus nodosus var. multiflorus, commonly known as the clustered rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that grow in dense clusters, and its stems are often reddish or purplish in color. The plant produces small, clustered flower spikes that bloom in late summer, adding a subtle ornamental value to its habitat. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife, it is valued in ecological restoration projects.
