Jarava chubutensis

Jarava chubutensis, commonly known as the Chubut grass, is a perennial grass native to the Patagonian region of Argentina.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and resilience to harsh climatic conditions.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing forage for local wildlife.
