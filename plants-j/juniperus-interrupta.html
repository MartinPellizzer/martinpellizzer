Juniperus interrupta

Juniperus interrupta, commonly known as the interrupted juniper, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves and small, blue-gray berries that are typically borne on female plants. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and open woodlands. Its compact growth habit and ability to withstand drought make it well adapted to harsh desert conditions.

Juniperus interrupta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping in suitable climates.
