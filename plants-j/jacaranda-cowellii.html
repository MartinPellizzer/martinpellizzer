Jacaranda cowellii

Jacaranda cowellii, commonly known as the coastal jacaranda, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its bark is typically smooth and grayish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions with a Mediterranean climate.

The coastal jacaranda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its vibrant floral display.
