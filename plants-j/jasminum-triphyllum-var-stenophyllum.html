Jasminum triphyllum var. stenophyllum

Jasminum triphyllum var. stenophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved nightshade jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a fan-like pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
