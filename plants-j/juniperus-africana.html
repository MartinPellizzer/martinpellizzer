Juniperus africana

Juniperus africana, commonly known as the African juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, scale-like leaves that are arranged in flat sprays. The plant produces blue-black berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, displaying remarkable drought tolerance.

Its woody structure and aromatic properties make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
