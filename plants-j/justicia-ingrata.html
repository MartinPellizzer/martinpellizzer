Justicia ingrata

Justicia ingrata, commonly known as the weeping justicia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of tubular, violet-blue flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant flowers and cascading form.
