Juniperus recurva var. densa

Juniperus recurva var. densa, commonly known as the dense juniper or thickleaf juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain wildlife. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Juniperus recurva var. densa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
