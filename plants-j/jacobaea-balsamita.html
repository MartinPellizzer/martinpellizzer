Jacobaea balsamita

Jacobaea balsamita, commonly known as tansy, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and strong aromatic scent. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and grows up to 1 meter in height. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Jacobaea balsamita contains compounds such as thujone, which has been used historically for its medicinal properties.
