Jasminum subtriplinerve

Jasminum subtriplinerve, commonly known as the three-veined jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The leaves are simple, opposite, and have three prominent veins, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing as a shrub or small tree.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly used in horticulture and traditional medicine.
