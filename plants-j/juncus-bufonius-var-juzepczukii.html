Juncus bufonius var. juzepczukii

Juncus bufonius var. juzepczukii, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments in eastern North America.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that are typically green to reddish in color, growing in dense clusters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to water-saturated soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical structure makes it well adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats, contributing to its ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
