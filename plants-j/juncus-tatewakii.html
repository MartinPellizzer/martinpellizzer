Juncus tatewakii

Juncus tatewakii, commonly known as the Japanese rush, is a perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems that grow in dense clusters. The plant typically thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer.

Juncus tatewakii is often used in ornamental gardening due to its ability to add texture and structure to wetland landscapes.
