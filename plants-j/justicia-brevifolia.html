Justicia brevifolia

Justicia brevifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Justicia brevifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
