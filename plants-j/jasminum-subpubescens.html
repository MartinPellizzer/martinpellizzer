Jasminum subpubescens

Jasminum subpubescens, commonly known as the white jasmine or the Chinese jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant has slender, woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.

Jasminum subpubescens prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
