Jacksonia dilatata

Jacksonia dilatata, commonly known as the New Zealand daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and salt spray. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Jacksonia dilatata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
