Juniperus patoniana f. obscura

Juniperus patoniana f. obscura, commonly known as the dark juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark blue to blackish berries and dense, scale-like foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in forested areas. Its botanical features include small, aromatic leaves and a compact growth habit.

This form is a naturally occurring variant of Juniperus patoniana, distinguished by its darker coloration and unique ecological adaptations.
