Juncus mertensianus var. filifolius

Juncus mertensianus var. filifolius, commonly known as the threadleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage that forms dense clumps. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in spike-like clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
