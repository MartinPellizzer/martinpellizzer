Juncus subuliflorus

Juncus subuliflorus, commonly known as the slender spike rush, is a perennial plant native to wetlands and moist soils in North America.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and tall, cylindrical flower spikes. The plant typically grows in shallow water or damp areas, often forming dense colonies. Its inflorescence consists of numerous small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes.

Juncus subuliflorus plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
