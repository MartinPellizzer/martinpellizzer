Juniperus caesia

Juniperus caesia, commonly known as the silver juniper, is a slow-growing evergreen shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage, which gives it a striking appearance in both natural and cultivated landscapes. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are typically blue or black in color and are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Juniperus caesia is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping due to its hardiness and low maintenance requirements.
