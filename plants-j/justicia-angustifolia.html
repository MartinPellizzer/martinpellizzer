Justicia angustifolia

Justicia angustifolia, commonly known as flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
