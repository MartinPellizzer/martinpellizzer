Jurinea proteoides

Jurinea proteoides, commonly known as the Antarctic hairgrass, is a flowering plant native to the cold, coastal regions of Antarctica.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that forms dense mats to withstand the harsh environmental conditions of its habitat. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are adapted to pollination by wind. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for microorganisms in the extreme environment.

Due to its unique adaptations, Jurinea proteoides is a subject of study in botanical research on plant survival in extreme climates.
