Juncus bufonius var. vulgaris

Juncus bufonius var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common rush, is a perennial plant belonging to the family Juncaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like, cylindrical stems that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, ditches, and along waterways.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization and habitat for various wildlife.
