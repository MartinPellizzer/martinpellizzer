Juncus punctorius subsp. mauritanicus

Juncus punctorius subsp.

mauritanicus, commonly known as the Mauritania rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North Africa. It typically grows in wet or semi-aquatic environments such as marshes, ditches, and along the edges of water bodies.

The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and bear small, spike-like clusters of flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib.

The species is adapted to tolerate periodic flooding and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
