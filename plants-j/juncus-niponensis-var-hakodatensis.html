Juncus niponensis var. hakodatensis

Juncus niponensis var. hakodatensis, commonly known as the Hakodate rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as riverbanks and coastal areas. It is known for its adaptability to humid conditions and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.

The Hakodate rush is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and ecological benefits.
