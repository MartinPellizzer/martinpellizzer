Jacaranda cuspidifolia var. calycina

Jacaranda cuspidifolia var. calycina, commonly known as the calycine jacaranda, is a deciduous tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking violet to blue-pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are compound, with elongated, pointed leaflets that give the plant its species name *cuspidifolia*. The tree produces woody pods that contain flat, seed-like structures.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
