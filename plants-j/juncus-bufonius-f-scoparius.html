Juncus bufonius f. scoparius

Juncus bufonius f. scoparius, commonly known as the frog's-bit sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Juncaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in shallow water or damp soil. It has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in temperate regions across Europe and parts of Asia.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates and serving as a food source for certain bird species.
