Juniperus polycarpos var. seravschanica

Juniperus polycarpos var. seravschanica, commonly known as the Caucasian juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, mountainous climates.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
