Julostylis angustifolia

Julostylis angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and tapering. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers, which are usually greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Julostylis angustifolia plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
