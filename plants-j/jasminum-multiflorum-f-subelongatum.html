Jasminum multiflorum f. subelongatum

Jasminum multiflorum f. subelongatum, commonly known as the long-flowered jasmine, is a cultivar of the jasmine plant characterized by its elongated, fragrant flowers.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is native to regions of Asia, particularly China. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of small, white flowers with a sweet, intoxicating aroma. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as a houseplant due to its beauty and fragrance.

This variety is well-suited for warm climates and requires well-drained soil and partial sunlight to thrive.
