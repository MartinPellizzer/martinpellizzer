Juncoides campestris subsp. congesta

Juncoides campestris subsp.

congesta, commonly known as the clustered rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

The plant features dense clusters of slender, cylindrical stems that are usually green to reddish in color. Its flowering occurs in summer, producing small, inconspicuous greenish flowers arranged in spikes.

The subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in wetland environments.
