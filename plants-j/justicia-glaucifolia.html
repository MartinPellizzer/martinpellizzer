Justicia glaucifolia

Justicia glaucifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf false arum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that resemble leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. It produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink or purple, which are typically borne on terminal spikes.

Justicia glaucifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
