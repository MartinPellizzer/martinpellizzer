Juncoides campestris var. picta

Juncoides campestris var. picta, commonly known as the Painted Rush, is a variant of the common rush characterized by its striking, variegated foliage.

It is a perennial plant native to wetlands and marshy areas, typically found in temperate regions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that display a mix of green, white, and sometimes pink or red hues. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes, typical of the Juncaceae family.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in water gardens and moist landscapes.
