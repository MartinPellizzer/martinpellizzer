Jamesonia imbricata var. nivea

Jamesonia imbricata var. nivea, commonly known as the white-flowered golden bell, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from white to pale yellow, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture and dark green upper surfaces. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its striking flowers and compact growth habit.
