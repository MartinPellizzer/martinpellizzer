Justicia fimbriata

Justicia fimbriata, commonly known as the flame flower or red flame plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The flowers attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees, making it a valuable addition to gardens.
