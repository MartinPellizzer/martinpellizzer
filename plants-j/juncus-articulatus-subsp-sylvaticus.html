Juncus articulatus subsp. sylvaticus

Juncus articulatus subsp.

sylvaticus, commonly known as the common rush, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and damp woodlands.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in dense clusters at the base, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their astringent properties.

The species is characterized by its jointed stems, which give it its scientific name "articulatus.".
