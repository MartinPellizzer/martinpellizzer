Jurinea xeranthemoides

Jurinea xeranthemoides, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features silvery-gray foliage covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well adapted to desert conditions.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping in dry climates.
