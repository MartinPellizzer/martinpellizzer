Jambosa acuminatissima

Jambosa acuminatissima, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and large, dark green leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that develop into edible, dark purple to black berries. These berries are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet, tangy flavor.

It is often cultivated for its fruit and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
