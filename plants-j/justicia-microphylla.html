Justicia microphylla

Justicia microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are small, opposite, and often covered with fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant's showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds, contributing to its ecological value.
