Justicia pseudopolystachya

Justicia pseudopolystachya, commonly known as the red-flowered justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds, contributing to its ecological significance.
