Jambosa caryophyllata

Jambosa caryophyllata, commonly known as the desert rose, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, often with a velvety texture. Its stems are woody and can branch out, forming a shrub-like structure.

Jambosa caryophyllata is well adapted to hot, sunny climates and requires minimal water once established.
