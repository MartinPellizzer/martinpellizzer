Jussiaea alternifolia

Jussiaea alternifolia, commonly known as the slender water-plantain, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in South America.

It is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are alternately arranged along its stem. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming during the wet season. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important species in aquatic ecosystems.

Jussiaea alternifolia plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
