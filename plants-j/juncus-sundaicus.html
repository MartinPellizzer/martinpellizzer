Juncus sundaicus

Juncus sundaicus, commonly known as the Sunda rush, is a perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Juncaginaceae and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. Its flowering spikes produce small, greenish flowers that are clustered at the top of the stems.

Juncus sundaicus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
