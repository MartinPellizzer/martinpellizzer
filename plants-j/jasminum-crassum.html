Jasminum crassum

Jasminum crassum, commonly known as the thick-leaf jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Jasminum crassum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
