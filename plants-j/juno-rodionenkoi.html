Juno rodionenkoi

Juno rodionenkoi, commonly known as the Rodionenko's juniper, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, blue-gray needles that provide year-round visual interest. The plant produces clusters of tiny, red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid climates.

Juno rodionenkoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environments.
