Justicia myuros

Justicia myuros, commonly known as the flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Justicia myuros is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
