Jacobaea mouterdei

Jacobaea mouterdei, commonly known as the Mouterde's ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Jacobaea mouterdei is known for its toxic properties, containing pyrrolizidine alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
