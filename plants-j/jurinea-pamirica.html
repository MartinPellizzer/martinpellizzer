Jurinea pamirica

Jurinea pamirica, commonly known as the Pamir sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in arid, rocky environments and is adapted to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations.

Its fibrous roots help it anchor in loose, sandy soils.
