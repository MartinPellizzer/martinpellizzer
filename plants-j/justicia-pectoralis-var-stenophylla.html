Justicia pectoralis var. stenophylla

Justicia pectoralis var. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf false arum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are usually bright red or orange, with a distinctive spathe that resembles a modified leaf.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
