Juncus compressus var. subcompressus

Juncus compressus var. subcompressus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in wet or damp environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often compressed and arranged in dense tufts. Its flowering stems produce small, spike-like clusters of greenish-white flowers.

This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and adaptability to moist soil conditions.
