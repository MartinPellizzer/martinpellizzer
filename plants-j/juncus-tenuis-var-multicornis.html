Juncus tenuis var. multicornis

Juncus tenuis var. multicornis, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush with multi-corned stems, is a flowering plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its stems are often covered with small, bristle-like structures, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
