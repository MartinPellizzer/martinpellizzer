Jovibarba × smithii

Jovibarba × smithii, commonly known as the hybrid houseleek, is a succulent plant belonging to the Crassulaceae family.

It is a hybrid between Jovibarba hepaticiformis and Jovibarba oppositifolia, characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple during the summer months.

Jovibarba × smithii is prized for its ornamental value and ease of care, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
