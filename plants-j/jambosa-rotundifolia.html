Jambosa rotundifolia

Jambosa rotundifolia, commonly known as the Natal plum, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical, dark green leaves that are often slightly lobed. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits resembling plums. The fruit is rich in vitamin C and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.
