Justicia uxpanapensis

Justicia uxpanapensis, commonly known as Mexican verbena, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are square and sturdy.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
