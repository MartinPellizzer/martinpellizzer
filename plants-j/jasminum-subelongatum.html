Jasminum subelongatum

Jasminum subelongatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and aromatic properties.
