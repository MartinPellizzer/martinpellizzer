Jasminum nudiflorum f. pulvinatum

Jasminum nudiflorum f. pulvinatum, commonly known as the pulvinated jasmine, is a unique cultivar of the common jasmine.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rounded, and often swollen flower buds that resemble small, fleshy balls. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves appear. It is a compact shrub with glossy, dark green leaves and is often grown as a decorative plant in gardens.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and subtle floral scent.
