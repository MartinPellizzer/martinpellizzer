Jacobaea × laggeri

Jacobaea × laggeri, commonly known as the ragwort.

It is a hybrid plant in the Asteraceae family, characterized by its yellowish to greenish flowers and fuzzy, lobed leaves. Native to Europe, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant contains pyrrolizidine alkaloids, which are toxic to livestock.

It is often considered a weed but has historical uses in herbal medicine.
