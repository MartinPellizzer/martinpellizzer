Julocroton montevidensis var. stipularis

Julocroton montevidensis var. stipularis, commonly known as the Stipular Julocroton, is a species native to South America, particularly Uruguay and parts of Brazil.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, woody stems and small, ovate leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm, tropical climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and long-lasting blooms.
