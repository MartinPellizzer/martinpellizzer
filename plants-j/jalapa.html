Jalapa

Jalapa, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 feet, with thick, waxy leaves. Its flowers bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Jalapa is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
