Jurinea erythrolepis

Jurinea erythrolepis, commonly known as the red-leaved jurinea, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, particularly Kazakhstan and Mongolia. The plant is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and its striking red or purple foliage, which is a result of high concentrations of anthocyanins. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically green or pale yellow, and it produces small, dry fruits called utricles.

Jurinea erythrolepis is adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting succulent leaves and deep root systems to conserve water.
