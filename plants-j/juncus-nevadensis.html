Juncus nevadensis

Juncus nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish-brown in color and lack leaves, with small, inconspicuous flowers emerging at the tips. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in wetland habitats.

Juncus nevadensis plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
