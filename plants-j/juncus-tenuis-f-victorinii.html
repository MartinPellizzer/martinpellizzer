Juncus tenuis f. victorinii

Juncus tenuis f. victorinii, commonly known as the slender rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Juncaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of greenish-white flowers. This variety is typically found in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, ditches, and along water edges. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal.

The plant is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable environments.
