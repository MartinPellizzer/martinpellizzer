Juncus biglumis var. ajonskensis

Juncus biglumis var. ajonskensis, commonly known as the Ajonskian rush, is a variant of the common rush that exhibits distinct botanical characteristics.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant typically found in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually green and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, three-lobed flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to moist soils and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
