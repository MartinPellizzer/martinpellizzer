Jussiaea maderaspatana

Jussiaea maderaspatana, commonly known as the Indian water plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands and marshy areas of South and Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, featuring long, narrow leaves that are often submerged or floating. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in spikes, which bloom during the summer months. Jussiaea maderaspatana is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense mats in aquatic environments.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
