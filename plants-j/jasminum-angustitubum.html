Jasminum angustitubum

Jasminum angustitubum, commonly known as narrow-stemmed jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded or semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

Jasminum angustitubum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic properties.
