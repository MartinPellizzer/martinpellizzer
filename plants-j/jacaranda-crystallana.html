Jacaranda crystallana

Jacaranda crystallana, commonly known as the crystal jacaranda, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking violet or lilac-colored flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has compound leaves composed of several narrow, lance-shaped leaflets. It produces small, round, woody fruits that contain seeds covered in a silky, white coating.

Jacaranda crystallana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and full sun.
