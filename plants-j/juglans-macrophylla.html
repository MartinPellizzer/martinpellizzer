Juglans macrophylla

Juglans macrophylla, commonly known as the California black walnut, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and can grow up to 25 meters tall. The tree produces edible nuts enclosed in a thick, green husk that splits open when mature. Its bark is dark and deeply furrowed, and it has a strong, aromatic scent.

The plant is valued for its wood, which is durable and used in furniture and cabinetry.
