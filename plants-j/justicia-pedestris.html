Justicia pedestris

Justicia pedestris, commonly known as the dwarf flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It features showy, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are glossy and arranged opposite along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.

Its compact size and vibrant flowers make it an ornamental favorite in both landscapes and containers.
