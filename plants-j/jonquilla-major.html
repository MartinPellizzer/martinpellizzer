Jonquilla major

Jonquilla major, commonly known as the large yellow daffodil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically bright yellow with a white or green throat. The plant features strap-like, sword-shaped leaves that are basal and often covered in fine hairs. Jonquilla major thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its blooming period usually occurs in early spring, adding a vibrant splash of color to the flora.
