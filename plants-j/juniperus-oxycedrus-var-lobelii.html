Juniperus oxycedrus var. lobelii

Juniperus oxycedrus var. lobelii, commonly known as the Corsican juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of scale-like leaves that are often blue-gray in color. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are typically blue or black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to rocky or arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
