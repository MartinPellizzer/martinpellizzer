Jatropha ribifolia var. breviloba

Jatropha ribifolia var. breviloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved jatropha, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits containing toxic seeds, which are a key feature of the genus Jatropha. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for biofuel production, it has been cultivated in some regions.
