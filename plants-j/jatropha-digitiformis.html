Jatropha digitiformis

Jatropha digitiformis, commonly known as the fingerleaf jatropha, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its striking, digitately lobed leaves that resemble fingers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage.

Jatropha digitiformis is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soils and tolerate drought conditions.
