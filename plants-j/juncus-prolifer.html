Juncus prolifer

Juncus prolifer, commonly known as the common rush, is a perennial plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a cylindrical stem. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Juncus prolifer produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in spike-like inflorescences.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
