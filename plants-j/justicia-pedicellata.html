Justicia pedicellata

Justicia pedicellata, commonly known as flame violet, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

The flowers are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to gardens.
