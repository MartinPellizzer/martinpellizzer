Jacobaea trapezuntina

Jacobaea trapezuntina, commonly known as the Trapezuntine ragwort, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is found in areas such as Turkey and northern Syria. The plant features basal rosettes of blue-green leaves and produces yellow flowers arranged in a single, terminal head. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

Jacobaea trapezuntina is known for its medicinal properties and has been traditionally used in herbal remedies.
