Juniperus gracilior var. gracilior

Juniperus gracilior var. gracilior, commonly known as the slender juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and needle-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Juniperus gracilior var. gracilior is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its hardy nature and year-round visual interest.
