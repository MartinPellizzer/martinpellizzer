Juncus acutiflorus var. pauciflorus

Juncus acutiflorus var. pauciflorus, commonly known as the sparse-flowered rush, is a perennial plant native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features tall, slender stems that are usually unbranched and covered in small, scale-like leaves. Its flowers are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, with the variety pauciflorus having fewer blooms compared to the typical form.

This plant plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
