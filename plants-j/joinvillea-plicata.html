Joinvillea plicata

It belongs to the family Onagraceae and is characterized by its unique, waxy, and often spiny leaves. The plant typically grows on the branches of host trees, drawing moisture and nutrients from them. Its small, white or yellowish flowers are followed by bright red berries that attract birds.
