Jasminum tortuosum var. latifolium

Jasminum tortuosum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
