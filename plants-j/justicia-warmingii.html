Justicia warmingii

Justicia warmingii, commonly known as the pink flame plant, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy bracts that give it a fiery appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental gardens.

Its botanical name honors the British botanist John Warming.
