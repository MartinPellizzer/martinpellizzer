Junellia bisulcata var. campestris

Junellia bisulcata var. campestris, commonly known as the broadleaf ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy purple to pink composite flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are often reddish in color. It typically blooms in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Junellia bisulcata var. campestris thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a suitable choice for native gardens and natural habitats.
