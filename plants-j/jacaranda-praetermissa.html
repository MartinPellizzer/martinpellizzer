Jacaranda praetermissa

Jacaranda praetermissa, commonly known as the slender jacaranda, is a deciduous tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and delicate, feathery foliage. The plant produces showy, pendulous clusters of violet-blue flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple small leaflets, and it thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
