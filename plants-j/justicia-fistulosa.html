Justicia fistulosa

Justicia fistulosa, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a striking plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces tall, spike-like inflorescences bearing bright red bracts. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its flowers are small and tubular, often arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

The botanical name reflects its historical use in traditional medicine and its ornamental value in landscaping.
