Justicia costata

Justicia costata, commonly known as the narrowleaf false heather, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and rise on slender, upright stalks.

Justicia costata is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
