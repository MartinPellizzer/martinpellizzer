Justicia nitida

Justicia nitida, commonly known as the silverleaf false heather, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 cm, featuring silver-gray, velvety foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of white, pink, or red, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact size and long-lasting blooms.
