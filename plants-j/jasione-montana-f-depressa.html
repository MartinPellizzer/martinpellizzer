Jasione montana f. depressa

Jasione montana f. depressa, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features a compact rosette of basal leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale blue. The plant typically thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and alpine displays.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging garden conditions.
