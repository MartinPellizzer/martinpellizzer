Jasminum diversifolium var. subhumile

Jasminum diversifolium var. subhumile, commonly known as the dwarf jasmine, is a low-growing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
