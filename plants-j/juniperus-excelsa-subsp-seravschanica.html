Juniperus excelsa subsp. seravschanica

Juniperus excelsa subsp.

seravschanica, commonly known as the Pamir juniper, is a subspecies native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia. It is a coniferous evergreen shrub or small tree, characterized by its needle-like leaves and small, blue-gray berries.

The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils. Its cones are typically retained on the plant for several years, a feature common among juniper species.

This subspecies plays a role in stabilizing mountainous terrain and provides habitat for various wildlife.
