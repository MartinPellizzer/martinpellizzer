Jasminum sylvestre

Jasminum sylvestre, commonly known as wild jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its flowers are borne on pedicels and are known for their strong, sweet aroma.

Jasminum sylvestre is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has been used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
