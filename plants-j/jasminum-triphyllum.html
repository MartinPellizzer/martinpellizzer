Jasminum triphyllum

Jasminum triphyllum, commonly known as the fragrant three-leaved jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white, highly fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Jasminum triphyllum thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
