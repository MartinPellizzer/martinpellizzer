Justicia consanguinea var. pubescens

Justicia consanguinea var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy red sage, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring erect stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a hairy texture, and the plant produces showy, tubular red or pink flowers arranged in spikes. It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.

The plant is also known for its attractive foliage and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
