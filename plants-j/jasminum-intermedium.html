Jasminum intermedium

Jasminum intermedium, commonly known as the Chinese jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a sweet, intoxicating scent. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a glossy green surface.

Jasminum intermedium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a hedge plant.
