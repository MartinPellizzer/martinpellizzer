Jambosa macrophylla

Jambosa macrophylla, commonly known as the Natal plum, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves and produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The tree is known for its edible, yellow-orange fruits that are rich in vitamin C and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and fruit production.

The plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
