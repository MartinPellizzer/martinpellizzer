Juno nicolai

Juno nicolai, commonly known as the Nicolo's juniper, is a species of juniper native to the high altitudes of the eastern Himalayas.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant features scale-like leaves and produces small, blue-black berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.

Juno nicolai is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
