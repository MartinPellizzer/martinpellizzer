Juglans × intermedia pyriformis

Juglans × intermedia pyriformis, commonly known as the hybrid walnut, is a cross between the black walnut (Juglans nigra) and the English walnut (Juglans regia).

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 10–20 meters, featuring a broad, spreading crown. The leaves are compound, with 5–9 pairs of lance-shaped leaflets, and they turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by edible nuts encased in a thick, husky shell.

It is valued for its hardy nature and the high quality of its nuts, making it a popular choice for cultivation in temperate regions.
