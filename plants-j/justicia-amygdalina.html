Justicia amygdalina

Justicia amygdalina, commonly known as the African breadfruit, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces edible, starchy tubers that are a staple food in many African communities.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for both its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.
