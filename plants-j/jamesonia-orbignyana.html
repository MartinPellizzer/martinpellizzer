Jamesonia orbignyana

Jamesonia orbignyana, commonly known as the golden bell, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
