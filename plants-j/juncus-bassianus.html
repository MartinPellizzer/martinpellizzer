Juncus bassianus

Juncus bassianus, commonly known as the common rush, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves that grow from a rhizomatous base. The plant typically thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wetlands. Its flowers are small, greenish-brown spikes that appear at the top of the stems.

Juncus bassianus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
