Juncus × buchenaui

Juncus × buchenaui, commonly known as the hybrid rush, is a perennial wetland plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Juncus effusus and Juncus roemerianus, characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in brackish or freshwater marshes, tolerating varying salinity levels. Its flowering stems produce small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

Juncus × buchenaui plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
