Justicia planchonii

Justicia planchonii, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are oppositely arranged along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Justicia planchonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
