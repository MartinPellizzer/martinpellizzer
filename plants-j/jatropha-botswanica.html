Jatropha botswanica

Jatropha botswanica, commonly known as the Botswana physic nut, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain toxic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid regions, often growing in rocky soils and along riverbanks.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
