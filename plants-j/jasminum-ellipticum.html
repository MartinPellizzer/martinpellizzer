Jasminum ellipticum

Jasminum ellipticum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers emit a sweet, intoxicating aroma, especially during the night, attracting nocturnal pollinators.

Jasminum ellipticum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its beauty and fragrance.
