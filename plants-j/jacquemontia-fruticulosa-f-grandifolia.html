Jacquemontia fruticulosa f. grandifolia

Jacquemontia fruticulosa f. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered Jacquemontia, is a variant of the Jacquemontia fruticulosa species.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and Georgia. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange with a distinctive red or purple throat. Its foliage consists of alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
