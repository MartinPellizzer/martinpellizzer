Justicia ramulosa

Justicia ramulosa, commonly known as the silverleaf justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom in summer. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.
