Juncus scabriusculus var. subglandulosus

Juncus scabriusculus var. subglandulosus, commonly known as the rough spike rush, is a variant of the spike rush genus Juncus.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its cylindrical, grass-like stems that are typically found in wetland environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, contributing to its common name.

This variant is adapted to moist or aquatic habitats and plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils.
