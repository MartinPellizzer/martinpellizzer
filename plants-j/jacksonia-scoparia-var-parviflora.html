Jacksonia scoparia var. parviflora

Jacksonia scoparia var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered flax, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has silver-gray foliage that helps it withstand salt and wind. It is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.

The flowers bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
