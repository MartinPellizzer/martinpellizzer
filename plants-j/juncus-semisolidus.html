Juncus semisolidus

Juncus semisolidus, commonly known as the partial solid rush, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, cylindrical leaves and spikes of small, greenish-yellow flowers. Native to moist habitats such as wet meadows and marshes, it thrives in areas with standing water or saturated soil. The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense vegetation in suitable environments.
