Juncus kangpuensis

Juncus kangpuensis, commonly known as the Kangpu rush, is a species of flowering plant in the family Juncaceae.

It is native to China, specifically the Yunnan Province, and is adapted to wetland environments. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in early summer.

Juncus kangpuensis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and supports local biodiversity.
