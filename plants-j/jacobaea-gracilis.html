Jacobaea gracilis

Jacobaea gracilis, commonly known as slender ragwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in grasslands, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

Jacobaea gracilis is often found in open habitats and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
