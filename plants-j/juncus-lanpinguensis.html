Juncus lanpinguensis

Juncus lanpinguensis, commonly known as the Chinese rush, is a perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Juncaginaceae and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and along the edges of ponds. It has fibrous roots that help stabilize soil and improve water retention.

Juncus lanpinguensis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in moist environments.
