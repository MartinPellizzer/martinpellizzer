Jussiaea brachycarpa var. paraguayensis

Jussiaea brachycarpa var. paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay waterleaf, is a tropical aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that are typically light green in color. The plant has a short, stout stem and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellowish. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
