Juniperus communis f. densa

Juniperus communis f. densa, commonly known as the dense juniper, is a compact, evergreen shrub or small tree native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage that forms a spherical or conical shape, providing a dense canopy. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for birds and other wildlife. Its aromatic leaves release a fragrant resin, which contributes to its use in landscaping and traditional medicine.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in cold climates.
