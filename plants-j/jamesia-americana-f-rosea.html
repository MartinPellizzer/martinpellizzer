Jamesia americana f. rosea

Jamesia americana f. rosea, commonly known as the roseleaf mountain maple, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are clustered in umbels. Its ornamental value lies in its striking foliage and adaptability to well-drained soils.

It thrives in partial shade and is often used in native plant gardens for its ecological and aesthetic benefits.
