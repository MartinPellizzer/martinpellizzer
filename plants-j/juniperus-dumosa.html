Juniperus dumosa

Juniperus dumosa, commonly known as the desert juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for various wildlife species. Juniperus dumosa is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in poor soils and extreme temperatures.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat in desert ecosystems.
