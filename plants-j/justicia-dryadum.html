Justicia dryadum

Justicia dryadum, commonly known as the sacred flame or flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Justicia dryadum thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-draining soil to flourish.
