Juniperus chinensis var. pendula

Juniperus chinensis var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping Chinese juniper, is a slow-growing evergreen shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its cascading, drooping branches that create a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, blue-gray needle-like leaves and is often cultivated for its unique form and hardiness. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a specimen plant.

This variety is valued for its ability to tolerate urban conditions and its year-round visual interest.
