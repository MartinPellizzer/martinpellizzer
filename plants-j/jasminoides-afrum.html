Jasminoides afrum

Jasminoides afrum, commonly known as the African jasmine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a sweet, jasmine-like scent. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

Jasminoides afrum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aromatic flowers and evergreen foliage.
