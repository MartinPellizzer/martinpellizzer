Jasminum violascens

Jasminum violascens, commonly known as the violet-flowered jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, with opposite, elliptic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are borne on short pedicels and are known for their sweet, intoxicating fragrance.

Jasminum violascens is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and in perfumery due to its aromatic properties.
