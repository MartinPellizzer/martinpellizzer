Jarava vaginata f. laevis

Jarava vaginata f. laevis, commonly known as the smooth jarava, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in a smooth, hairless surface. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with low rainfall and poor soil conditions.

Due to its resilience, it is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
