Juniperus pingii subsp. chengii

Juniperus pingii subsp.

chengii, commonly known as the Yunnan juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to China. It is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain wildlife. It thrives in mountainous regions with well-drained soils and a temperate climate.

This subspecies is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
