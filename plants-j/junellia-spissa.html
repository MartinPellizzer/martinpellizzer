Junellia spissa

Junellia spissa, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of Central America and northern South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments within cloud forests. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and are often adorned with distinctive markings. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a velvety texture, while its stems are woody and branched.

Junellia spissa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
