Jasminum tortuosum var. campanulatum

Jasminum tortuosum var. campanulatum, commonly known as the bell jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
