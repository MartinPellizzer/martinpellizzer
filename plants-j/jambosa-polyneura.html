Jambosa polyneura

Jambosa polyneura, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellowish-green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces clusters of small, red, fleshy berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Jambosa polyneura is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
