Justicia decumbens

Justicia decumbens, commonly known as the prostrate flame flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central and South America.

It features sprawling, low-growing stems that often lie flat on the ground, producing clusters of bright red, tubular flowers. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant blooms.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds, contributing to its ecological value.
