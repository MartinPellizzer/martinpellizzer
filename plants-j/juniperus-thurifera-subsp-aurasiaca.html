Juniperus thurifera subsp. aurasiaca

Juniperus thurifera subsp.

aurasiaca, commonly known as the Himalayan juniper, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of Central and South Asia. It is a coniferous evergreen shrub or small tree, typically growing in high-altitude environments.

The plant produces small, scale-like leaves and blue-gray foliage, which are characteristic of many juniper species. Its cones are typically berry-like, containing aromatic seeds that are used in traditional medicine.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh, cold climates and plays a role in stabilizing soil in alpine ecosystems.
