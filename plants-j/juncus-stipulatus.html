Juncus stipulatus

Juncus stipulatus, commonly known as the stipulate rush, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has tall, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, usually appearing in late spring to early summer.

Juncus stipulatus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
