Juncus mexicanus

Juncus mexicanus, commonly known as Mexican rush, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, stream banks, and wetlands.

Juncus mexicanus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
