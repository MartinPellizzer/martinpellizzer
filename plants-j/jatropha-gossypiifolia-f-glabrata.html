Jatropha gossypiifolia f. glabrata

Jatropha gossypiifolia f. glabrata, commonly known as the smoothleaf jatropha, is a variant of the common jatropha species.

It is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific. The plant features small, greenish-white flowers and produces capsule fruits containing toxic, yellowish seeds. Its leaves are typically smooth and glossy, distinguishing it from other forms of the species.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and unique flowering habit.
