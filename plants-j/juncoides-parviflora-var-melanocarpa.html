Juncoides parviflora var. melanocarpa

Juncoides parviflora var. melanocarpa, commonly known as the black-fruited marshweed, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like, cylindrical stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces dark purple to black berries, which are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
