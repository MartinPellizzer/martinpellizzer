Jambosa caryophylloides

Jambosa caryophylloides, commonly known as the pink star apple, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its glossy dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically pink or red when ripe, with a sweet, juicy pulp. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and is valued for its unique flavor and ornamental appeal.
