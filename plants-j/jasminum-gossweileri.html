Jasminum gossweileri

Jasminum gossweileri, commonly known as the African jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and a woody stem, making it a striking ornamental species. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Jasminum gossweileri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
