Jumellea dendrobioides

Jumellea dendrobioides, commonly known as the tree orchid, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, clustered inflorescences that bear small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its flowers are often white or pale yellow, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Jumellea dendrobioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or tropical gardens.
