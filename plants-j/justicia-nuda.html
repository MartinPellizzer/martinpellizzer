Justicia nuda

Justicia nuda, commonly known as the naked justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its simple, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically bright red or orange, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
