Juncus ustulatus

Juncus ustulatus, commonly known as the scorched rush, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas in North America.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that often have a reddish or scorched appearance. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters at the top of the stems, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Juncus ustulatus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
