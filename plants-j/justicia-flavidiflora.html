Justicia flavidiflora

Justicia flavidiflora, commonly known as the yellow-flowered justicia, is a tropical perennial shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in warm climates.
