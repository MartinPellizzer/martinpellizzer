Jacobaea tatarica

Jacobaea tatarica, commonly known as Siberian tansy, is a flowering plant native to the colder regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, aromatic leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Jacobaea tatarica is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
