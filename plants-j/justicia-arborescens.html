Justicia arborescens

Justicia arborescens, commonly known as flame flower, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Its rapid growth and vibrant blooms make it a striking addition to gardens and landscapes.
