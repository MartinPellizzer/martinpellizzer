Justicia andrographioides

Justicia andrographioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf false arum, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. Its flowers are clustered in spikes and are usually greenish-white with a subtle fragrance.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
