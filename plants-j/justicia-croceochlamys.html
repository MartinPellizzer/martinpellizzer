Justicia croceochlamys

Justicia croceochlamys, commonly known as the golden chalice or yellow justicia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in warm climates.
