Juncus articulatus var. littoralis

Juncus articulatus var. littoralis, commonly known as the saltmarsh rush, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, jointed stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in saline or brackish wetlands, where it thrives in muddy or sandy soils. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikes and are wind-pollinated.

Juncus articulatus var. littoralis plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife.
