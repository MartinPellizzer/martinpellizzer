Jatropha pusilla

Jatropha pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf physic nut, is a small shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces toxic, orange-red berries that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy pulp. Jatropha pusilla is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.

It is part of the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes many species with milky sap and toxic properties.
