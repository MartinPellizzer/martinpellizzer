Juncus pilosus subsp. albus

Juncus pilosus subsp.

albus, commonly known as the white-haired rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows.

The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in short, white or grayish hairs, giving it its characteristic appearance. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters at the top of the stems.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
