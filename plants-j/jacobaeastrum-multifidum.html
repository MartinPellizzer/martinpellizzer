Jacobaeastrum multifidum

Jacobaeastrum multifidum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are often finely divided, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and resilience.
