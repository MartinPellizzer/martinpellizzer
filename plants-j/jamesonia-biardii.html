Jamesonia biardii

Jamesonia biardii, commonly known as the biard's bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem.

Jamesonia biardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
