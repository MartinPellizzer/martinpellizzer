Juniperus communis subsp. pannonica

Juniperus communis subsp.

pannonica, commonly known as the Pannonian juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to Central and Southeastern Europe. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its foliage consists of scale-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional herbal remedies.

The subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
