Jasminum elatum

Jasminum elatum, commonly known as the everblooming jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to yellowish flowers that bloom continuously throughout the year. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are dark green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid environment to flourish.

Jasminum elatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
