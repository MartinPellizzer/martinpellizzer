Jatropha vitifolia var. grisebachii

Jatropha vitifolia var. grisebachii, commonly known as the gray-leaved cupplant, is a variant of the cupplant species native to certain regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive grayish-green, hairy leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces capsule fruits containing toxic seeds, which are a key feature in its botanical classification. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
