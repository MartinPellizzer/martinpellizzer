Jambosa stelechantha

Jambosa stelechantha, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and dense, aromatic wood that is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits containing hard, black seeds.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the production of essential oils.
