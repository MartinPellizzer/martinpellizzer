Juniperus flaccida subsp. martinezii

Juniperus flaccida subsp.

martinezii, commonly known as the Mexican red cedar, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that is typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
