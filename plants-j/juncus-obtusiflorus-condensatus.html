Juncus obtusiflorus condensatus

Juncus obtusiflorus condensatus, commonly known as the dense flowered rush, is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, cylindrical spikes of small, pale green to brown flowers.

It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly rough to the touch. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil.

The species is native to North America and is adapted to a variety of aquatic and semi-aquatic environments.
