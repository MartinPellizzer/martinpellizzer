Jasminum quinquenervium

Jasminum quinquenervium, commonly known as the common jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Jasminum quinquenervium is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
