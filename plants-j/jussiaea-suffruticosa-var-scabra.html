Jussiaea suffruticosa var. scabra

Jussiaea suffruticosa var. scabra, commonly known as the rough jussiaea, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features woody stems covered in a rough, scaly texture, giving it its varietal name "scabra.". The plant has small, opposite leaves that are often hairy and have a serrated margin. It produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers, which are typically borne in terminal panicles.

This species is adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal or riverine habitats.
