Janipha

Janipha, commonly known as the Indian star anise, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and oval-shaped fruits. The plant is often cultivated for its aromatic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are also used in herbal remedies due to their medicinal properties.

Janipha thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
