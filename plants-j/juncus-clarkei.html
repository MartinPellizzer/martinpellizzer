Juncus clarkei

Juncus clarkei, commonly known as the Clarke's rush, is a perennial plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that often reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of greenish-white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, stream banks, and pond edges.

Juncus clarkei plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
