Justicia selangorensis

Justicia selangorensis, commonly known as the Malayan false arum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
