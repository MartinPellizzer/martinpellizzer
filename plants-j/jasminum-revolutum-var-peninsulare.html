Jasminum revolutum var. peninsulare

Jasminum revolutum var. peninsulare, commonly known as the Peninsular jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the western coast of India.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has slender, woody stems and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in sandy soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
