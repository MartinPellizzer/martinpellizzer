Jasonia dichotoma

Jasonia dichotoma, commonly known as the mountain pincushion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps reduce water loss.

Jasonia dichotoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
