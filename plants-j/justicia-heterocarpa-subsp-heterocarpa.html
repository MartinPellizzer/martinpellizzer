Justicia heterocarpa subsp. heterocarpa

Justicia heterocarpa subsp.

heterocarpa, commonly known as the coral vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas. It is characterized by its striking clusters of bright red or orange tubular flowers, which are borne along the stems.

The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
