Jamesonia rotundifolia

Jamesonia rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf jamesonia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features a basal rosette of thick, waxy, round leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale pink during the spring. Its foliage is adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
