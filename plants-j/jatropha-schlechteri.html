Jatropha schlechteri

Jatropha schlechteri, commonly known as the Schlechter's physic nut, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a toxic, sticky substance.

It is often grown for its ornamental value and has been studied for potential use in biofuel production due to its high oil content.
