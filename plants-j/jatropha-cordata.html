Jatropha cordata

Jatropha cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf jatropha, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often pubescent, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by orange or red berries containing toxic seeds. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.

However, caution is advised as all parts of the plant contain toxic compounds.
