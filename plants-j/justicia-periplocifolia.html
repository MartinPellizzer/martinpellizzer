Justicia periplocifolia

Justicia periplocifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf false arum, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple bracts that surround its small, white flowers. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a rough texture and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated primarily by insects.
