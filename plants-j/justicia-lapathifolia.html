Justicia lapathifolia

Justicia lapathifolia, commonly known as the common justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows as an herb or shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its prolonged blooming period, which can last several months during the warmer seasons.
