Juncus salsuginosus

Juncus salsuginosus, commonly known as saltgrass, is a flowering plant native to coastal and saline environments.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in poorly drained soils and is often found in salt marshes and tidal flats. Its adaptability to high salinity makes it an important component of coastal ecosystems.

Juncus salsuginosus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
