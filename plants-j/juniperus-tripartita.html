Juniperus tripartita

Juniperus tripartita, commonly known as the three-part juniper, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage that is typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for birds and other wildlife. Juniperus tripartita thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Its aromatic foliage and adaptability make it a notable species in its ecological niche.
