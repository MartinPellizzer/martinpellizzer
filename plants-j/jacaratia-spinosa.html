Jacaratia spinosa

Jacaratia spinosa, commonly known as the spiny jacaranda, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems covered in sharp, spiny projections, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

Jacaratia spinosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
