Juncus grubovii

Juncus grubovii, commonly known as the Grubov's rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of white or pale green flowers. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in saturated soils and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

Juncus grubovii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and aesthetic value.
