Juniperus occidentalis var. gymnocarpa

Juniperus occidentalis var. gymnocarpa, commonly known as the smooth-fruit juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and produces small, berry-like cones that are typically blue or black when mature. The plant thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its cones are used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
