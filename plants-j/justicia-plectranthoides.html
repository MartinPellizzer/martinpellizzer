Justicia plectranthoides

Justicia plectranthoides, commonly known as the velvetleaf false arum, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features dark green, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, and it produces striking clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a rhizomatous root system and showy bracts that enhance its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens.
