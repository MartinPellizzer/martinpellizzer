Justicia paraguayensis

Justicia paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay flameflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

The flowers are borne in clusters at the tips of the stems, making it a visually striking addition to gardens.
