Jasminum siamense

Jasminum siamense, commonly known as the Siam jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Its flowers are tubular with five petals and emit a sweet, intoxicating aroma.

Jasminum siamense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in horticulture and traditional medicine.
