Justicia bridsoniana

Justicia bridsoniana, commonly known as the Bridson's flame flower, is a striking ornamental plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red to orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
