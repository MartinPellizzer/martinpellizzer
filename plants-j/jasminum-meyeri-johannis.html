Jasminum meyeri-johannis

Jasminum meyeri-johannis, commonly known as the Meyer's jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often releasing a sweet, intoxicating scent. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and aromatic value.
