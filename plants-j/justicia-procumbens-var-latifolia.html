Justicia procumbens var. latifolia

Justicia procumbens var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf flame flower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, attracting hummingbirds and other pollinators. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that are typically broad and lance-shaped, adapted to retain moisture in dry conditions. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-draining soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
