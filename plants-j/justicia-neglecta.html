Justicia neglecta

Justicia neglecta, commonly known as the wild verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of tubular, violet-blue flowers. The plant has opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Justicia neglecta is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
