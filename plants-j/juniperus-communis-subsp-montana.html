Juniperus communis subsp. montana

Juniperus communis subsp.

montana, commonly known as the mountain juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the northern hemisphere. It is characterized by its needle-like leaves and small, blue-black berries that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant is adapted to cold climates and is typically found in alpine and subalpine regions. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in poor, rocky soils.

Its evergreen nature provides year-round habitat for various wildlife species.
