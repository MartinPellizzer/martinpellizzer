Jasminum grandiflorum subsp. grandiflorum

Jasminum grandiflorum subsp.

grandiflorum, commonly known as the common jasmine, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East. It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, simple, and aromatic leaves.

The plant produces fragrant, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its flowers are often used in perfumery and traditional medicine due to their strong scent and medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and ornamental planting.
