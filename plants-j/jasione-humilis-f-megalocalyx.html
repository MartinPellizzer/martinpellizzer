Jasione humilis f. megalocalyx

Jasione humilis f. megalocalyx, commonly known as the giant meadow cranesbill, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, pinkish-purple flowers in early summer. The plant is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and is often found in grasslands and rocky soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

The subspecies megalocalyx is distinguished by its larger flowers compared to the typical form of Jasione humilis.
