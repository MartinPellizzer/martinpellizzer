Jungia aceroides

Jungia aceroides, commonly known as the mapleleaf spurge, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, maple-like leaves that are usually green with a reddish tint. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by orange berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Jungia aceroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
