Juniperus foetida

Juniperus foetida, commonly known as the stinking juniper, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves and small, blue-black berries that mature in the fall. The plant is adapted to harsh, arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils. Its aromatic foliage contains volatile oils that contribute to its strong, resinous scent.

Juniperus foetida is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of ornamental plants due to its hardy nature and distinctive appearance.
