Jarava frigida

Jarava frigida, commonly known as the alpine jarava, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes, particularly in countries like Bolivia and Peru. This plant is characterized by its compact growth habit, with slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale blue flowers.

Jarava frigida is adapted to cold, arid environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
