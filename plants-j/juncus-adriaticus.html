Juncus adriaticus

Juncus adriaticus, commonly known as the Adriatic rush, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage composed of narrow, cylindrical leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and saline soils. It produces small, clustered spikes of white or pale green flowers during the summer months.

Juncus adriaticus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
