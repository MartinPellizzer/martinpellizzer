Joniris biglumis

Joniris biglumis, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage and produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that range from yellow to orange in color. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly serrated, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Joniris biglumis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy maintenance.
