Juncus nebrodensis

Juncus nebrodensis, commonly known as the Sicilian rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as along the edges of lakes and rivers. The plant has slender, grass-like stems that are often densely clustered, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and cylindrical, with a fibrous texture, and it is often used in ornamental gardening.

Juncus nebrodensis plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
