Juncus nodosus var. texanus

Juncus nodosus var. texanus, commonly known as the Texas rush, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, greenish-white flowers. The plant thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds, where it helps in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which can provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.

The species is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its adaptability and ecological benefits.
