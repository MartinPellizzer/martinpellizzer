Junia triflora

Junia triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered junia, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and often edible roots. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Junia triflora is often found in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its unique botanical appearance.
