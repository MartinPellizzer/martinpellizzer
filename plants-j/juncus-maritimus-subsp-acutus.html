Juncus maritimus subsp. acutus

Juncus maritimus subsp.

acutus, commonly known as the sharp-tipped rush, is a perennial plant native to coastal regions of the northern hemisphere. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of white to greenish flowers.

The plant thrives in saline and brackish environments, often found in marshes, salt meadows, and along tidal shores. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense stands, which stabilize soils and provide habitat for various wildlife.

The species is valued for its ecological role in coastal ecosystems and its ability to tolerate extreme environmental conditions.
