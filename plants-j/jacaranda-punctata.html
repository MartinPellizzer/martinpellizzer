Jacaranda punctata

Jacaranda punctata, commonly known as the spotted jacaranda, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of purple and blue. The leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces woody pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and urban landscapes.
