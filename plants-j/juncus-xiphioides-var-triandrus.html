Juncus xiphioides var. triandrus

Juncus xiphioides var. triandrus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 1 mm wide, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowering spikes are usually erect and produce small, greenish-white flowers.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support wetland biodiversity.
