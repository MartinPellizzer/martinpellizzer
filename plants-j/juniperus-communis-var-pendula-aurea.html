Juniperus communis var. pendula-aurea

Juniperus communis var. pendula-aurea, commonly known as the golden weeping juniper, is a variant of the common juniper characterized by its pendulous, cascading branches.

It features bright golden-yellow foliage that contrasts with its needle-like leaves, making it a striking ornamental plant. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are typically harvested for use in gin production. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, showing tolerance to colder climates.

This cultivar is valued for its unique appearance and adaptability in landscaping.
