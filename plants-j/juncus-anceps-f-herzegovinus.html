Juncus anceps f. herzegovinus

Juncus anceps f. herzegovinus, commonly known as the Herzegovina rush, is a variant of the common rush species native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its flowering stems bear small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to waterlogged soils and its ecological role in stabilizing wetland habitats.
