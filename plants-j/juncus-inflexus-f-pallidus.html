Juncus inflexus f. pallidus

Juncus inflexus f. pallidus, commonly known as the pale rush, is a variant of the common rush species native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that are typically pale green to almost white in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters at the base of the stems, which are usually greenish or brownish. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, stream banks, and other wet habitats.

The pale rush is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
