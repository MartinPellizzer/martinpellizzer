Justicia uninervis

Justicia uninervis, commonly known as the narrow-veined justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a single prominent vein. The plant produces striking clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas for its beauty and ecological benefits.
