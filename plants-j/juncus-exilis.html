Juncus exilis

Juncus exilis, commonly known as slender rush, is a flowering plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and tall, cylindrical flower spikes. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense stands that provide habitat for various wildlife. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, relying on wind pollination rather than insects.

Juncus exilis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and filtering water in its wetland ecosystems.
