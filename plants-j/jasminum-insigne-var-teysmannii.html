Jasminum insigne var. teysmannii

Jasminum insigne var. teysmannii, commonly known as the Teysmann's jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, with opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
