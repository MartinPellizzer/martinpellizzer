Justicia prunellifolia

Justicia prunellifolia, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds, which are attracted to its bright colors and nectar.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
