Jacquemontia browniana var. grandiflora

Jacquemontia browniana var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered jacquemontia, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and dry habitats, often found in desert or semi-desert regions. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with hairy surfaces, and it has a climbing or trailing growth habit.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
