Jupunba trapezifolia var. trapezifolia

Jupunba trapezifolia var. trapezifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed jupunba, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its lanceolate, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, typically in rocky or alpine soils. Its botanical features include a woody stem, aromatic foliage, and a preference for cool, moist conditions.

It is often found in cloud forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
