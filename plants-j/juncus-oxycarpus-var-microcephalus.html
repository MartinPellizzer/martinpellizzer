Juncus oxycarpus var. microcephalus

Juncus oxycarpus var. microcephalus, commonly known as the dwarf rush, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its small, clustered flowers are usually greenish or brownish, and it produces small, three-lobed fruits.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
