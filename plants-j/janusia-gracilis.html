Janusia gracilis

Janusia gracilis, commonly known as the slender glasswort, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a halophyte, meaning it thrives in saline environments such as salt marshes and estuaries. The plant has thin, succulent stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. Its small, clustered flowers are typically pink or red, and they bloom during the warmer months.

Janusia gracilis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
