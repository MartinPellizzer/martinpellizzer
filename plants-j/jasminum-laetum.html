Jasminum laetum

Jasminum laetum, commonly known as the sweet jasmine or white jasmine, is a flowering plant belonging to the Oleaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its fragrant, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance.

Jasminum laetum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its pleasant aroma.
