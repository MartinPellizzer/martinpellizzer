Jasminum officinale var. aureum

Jasminum officinale var. aureum, commonly known as golden jasmine, is a variant of the common jasmine plant.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers, which contrast with the typical white blooms of the standard variety. This plant is native to regions of Asia, including China and Japan, and thrives in temperate climates. It has small, dark green leaves and produces a fragrant scent, making it popular in ornamental gardening.

The golden hue of its flowers makes it a visually striking addition to gardens and floral arrangements.
