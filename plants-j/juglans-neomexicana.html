Juglans neomexicana

Juglans neomexicana, commonly known as the New Mexico walnut, is a deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the genus Juglans, which includes other walnut species, and is characterized by its compound leaves and edible nuts. The tree produces small, green, winged fruits that mature into hard-shelled nuts. Its botanical features include a deep root system and thick, fissured bark that provides resistance to drought.

This species is valued for its ecological role and potential for sustainable forestry.
