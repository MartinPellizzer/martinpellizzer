Juncus amuricus var. wui

Juncus amuricus var. wui, commonly known as the Amur rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has slender, grass-like stems that are often clustered and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, greenish flowers in dense spikes during the summer months.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to moist soils and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
