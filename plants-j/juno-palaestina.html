Juno palaestina

Juno palaestina, commonly known as the Palestine juniper, is a species of juniper native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments. The plant has scale-like leaves that are closely arranged, and its berries are blue-black and toxic to humans. Juno palaestina is characterized by its compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in harsh, dry conditions.

It plays a significant role in the local flora of its native habitat.
