Jonquilla × triloba

Jonquilla × triloba, commonly known as the Spanish daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Jonquilla (Narcissus tazetta) and Triloba (Narcissus trilobus).

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 15–30 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This species is noted for its hardiness and ability to naturalize, often returning year after year in suitable climates.
