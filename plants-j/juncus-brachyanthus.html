Juncus brachyanthus

Juncus brachyanthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rush, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 1 inch wide. Its flowering spikes consist of small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences.

Juncus brachyanthus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
