Jasminum gilgianum

Jasminum gilgianum, commonly known as the Gilgit jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of Pakistan and India.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude areas, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. The plant produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, usually during the summer months. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Jasminum gilgianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
