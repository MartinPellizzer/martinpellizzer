Juniperus lobelii

Juniperus lobelii, commonly known as the Lobel's juniper, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves and small, berry-like cones that mature to a dark blue color. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It has a slow growth rate and can live for several decades.

Juniperus lobelii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
