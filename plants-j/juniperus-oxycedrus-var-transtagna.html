Juniperus oxycedrus var. transtagna

Juniperus oxycedrus var. transtagna, commonly known as the Transylvania juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that is typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spherical blue-black berries that are rich in essential oils. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
