Justicia forskaolii

Justicia forskaolii, commonly known as the flame violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, the Americas, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to purple, often resembling orchids. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

The flowers are pollinated by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological importance in native habitats.
