Juncus pilosus subsp. cymosus

Juncus pilosus subsp.

cymosus, commonly known as the tufted rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows.

The plant features grass-like leaves that are usually dense and cylindrical, with a fibrous texture. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, three-lobed flowers arranged in a compact cluster.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
