Juncus homalophyllus

Juncus homalophyllus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 1 millimeter wide. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows.

Juncus homalophyllus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
