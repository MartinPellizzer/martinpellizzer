Justicia harleyi

Justicia harleyi, commonly known as the Mexican flameflower, is a tropical shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to produce long-lasting blooms throughout the growing season.
