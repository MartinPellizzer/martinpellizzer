Juncellus limosus

Juncellus limosus, commonly known as the bog rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant has cylindrical, grass-like stems that are usually green or reddish in color. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikes and are typically wind-pollinated.

Juncellus limosus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
