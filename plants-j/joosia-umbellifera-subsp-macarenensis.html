Joosia umbellifera subsp. macarenensis

Joosia umbellifera subsp.

macarenensis, commonly known as the Macarenensis Joosia, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in an umbel.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its botanical structure includes fibrous roots and a short, stout stem.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
