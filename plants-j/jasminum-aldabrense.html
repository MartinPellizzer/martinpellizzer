Jasminum aldabrense

Jasminum aldabrense, commonly known as the Aldabra jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Comoros Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to arid and saline conditions. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Jasminum aldabrense is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
