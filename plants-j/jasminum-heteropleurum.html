Jasminum heteropleurum

Jasminum heteropleurum, commonly known as the Chinese jasmine or the yellow jasmine, is a flowering plant native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. It is typically grown as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

Its ornamental value is due to its pleasant fragrance and attractive foliage, making it a popular choice in gardens and landscapes.
