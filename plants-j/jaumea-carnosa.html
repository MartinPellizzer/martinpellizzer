Jaumea carnosa

Jaumea carnosa, commonly known as the swamp lily, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy white flowers that bloom in clusters, often emerging from water or moist soil. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically green and slightly waxy in texture. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes or along the edges of water bodies.

Jaumea carnosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable moisture conditions.
