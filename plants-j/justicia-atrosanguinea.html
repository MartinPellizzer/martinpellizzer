Justicia atrosanguinea

Justicia atrosanguinea, commonly known as black bloodplant, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking dark red to black flowers. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

The flowers are tubular and attract hummingbirds and other pollinators with their vibrant color and nectar-rich structure.
