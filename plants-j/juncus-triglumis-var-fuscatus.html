Juncus triglumis var. fuscatus

Juncus triglumis var. fuscatus, commonly known as the dark rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that are typically reddish-brown to dark brown in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense clumps.

This variety is adapted to moist or aquatic environments and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
