Juncus alpinus var. fuscescens

Juncus alpinus var. fuscescens, commonly known as the dark rush, is a perennial plant native to alpine and arctic regions.

It is characterized by its dark green to reddish-brown stems, which are often covered in a layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, typically arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to cold climates and high-altitude environments.
