Juncus viviparus

Juncus viviparus, commonly known as the viviparous rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to reproduce vegetatively through the production of bulbils, which develop on the stems and can take root independently. It thrives in moist to waterlogged soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
