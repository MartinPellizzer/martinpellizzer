Justicia crinita

Justicia crinita, commonly known as flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 3 feet. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are clustered in terminal spikes.

Justicia crinita thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
