Jungia grossulariifolia

Jungia grossulariifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf gooseberry, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
