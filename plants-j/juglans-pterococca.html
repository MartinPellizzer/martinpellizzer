Juglans pterococca

Juglans pterococca, commonly known as the Mexican walnut, is a species of deciduous tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Juglandaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with long, slender leaflets. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkin-like clusters, followed by hard, woody fruits that contain one or two edible seeds. Its bark is thick and deeply fissured, providing a distinctive appearance.

The plant is valued for its timber and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
