Jussiaea variabilis var. pilosa

Jussiaea variabilis var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy jussiaea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters. It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of wetland flora.
