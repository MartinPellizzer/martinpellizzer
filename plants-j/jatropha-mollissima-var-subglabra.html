Jatropha mollissima var. subglabra

Jatropha mollissima var. subglabra, commonly known as the silky jatropha, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its smooth, often hairy leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow berries that contain toxic seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
