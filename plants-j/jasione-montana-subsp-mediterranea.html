Jasione montana subsp. mediterranea

Jasione montana subsp.

mediterranea, commonly known as the Mediterranean bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe. It features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers in summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in Mediterranean climates.
