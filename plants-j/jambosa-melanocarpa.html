Jambosa melanocarpa

Jambosa melanocarpa, commonly known as the black sapote, is a tropical evergreen tree native to southern Mexico and Central America.

It produces a large, egg-shaped fruit with a thick, leathery skin that turns dark purple to black when mature. The fruit's interior contains a smooth, creamy, sweet pulp that is rich in vitamins and minerals. Botanically, it belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers.

The tree is valued for its edible fruit and is often cultivated for both commercial and ornamental purposes.
