Jacaratia spinosa var. digitata

Jacaratia spinosa var. digitata, commonly known as the fingered jacaratia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finger-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
