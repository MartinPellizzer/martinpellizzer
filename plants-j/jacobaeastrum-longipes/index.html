Jacobaeastrum Longipes

Jacobaeastrum longipes is a rare and lesser-known member of the Asteraceae family, native to the mountainous regions of South America. This plant is characterized by its striking, elongated flower heads that resemble small, delicate umbrellas. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests at high elevations. Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it has become a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists. Despite its beauty, Jacobaeastrum longipes remains under-studied and faces threats from habitat loss and climate change.
