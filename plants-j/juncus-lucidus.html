Juncus lucidus

Juncus lucidus, commonly known as the shining rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. Juncus lucidus thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its dense growth helps stabilize soil and provides habitat for various wetland organisms.
