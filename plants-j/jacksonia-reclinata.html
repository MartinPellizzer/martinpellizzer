Jacksonia reclinata

Jacksonia reclinata, commonly known as the reclinata daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in coastal and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and somewhat woody.

Jacksonia reclinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
