Johrenia tortuosa

Johrenia tortuosa, commonly known as the spiral vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its twisting, serpentine stems that form intricate spirals as it grows. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, and it bears small, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Johrenia tortuosa is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
