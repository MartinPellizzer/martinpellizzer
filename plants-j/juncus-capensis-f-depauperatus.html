Juncus capensis f. depauperatus

Juncus capensis f. depauperatus, commonly known as the Cape rush, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically grayish-green in color, adapted to arid conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical structure reflects its ability to survive in harsh, dry environments.
