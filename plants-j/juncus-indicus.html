Juncus indicus

Juncus indicus, commonly known as the Indian rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to various regions of Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that are usually hollow and covered in fine, grass-like leaves. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are arranged in spikes and bloom during the summer months.

Juncus indicus plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
