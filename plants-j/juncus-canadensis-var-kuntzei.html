Juncus canadensis var. kuntzei

Juncus canadensis var. kuntzei, commonly known as Canadian rush, is a flowering plant species native to North America.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and cylindrical flower spikes. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has narrow, hollow stems and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
