Janipha urens

Janipha urens, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to violet flowers, which bloom in spring and are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white or silver hairs. Janipha urens is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil.

Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and its role in the unique ecosystems of its native habitat.
