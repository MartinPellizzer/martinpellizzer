Juncus decipiens var. gracilis

Juncus decipiens var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender false rush, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
