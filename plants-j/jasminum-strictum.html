Jasminum strictum

Jasminum strictum, commonly known as the evergreen jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit and aromatic, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Jasminum strictum is often cultivated for its fragrant blossoms and is used in traditional medicine and perfumery.
