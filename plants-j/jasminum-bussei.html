Jasminum bussei

Jasminum bussei, commonly known as the Arabian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, usually during the cooler months. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

Jasminum bussei is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
