Juniperus phoenicea subsp. canariensis

Juniperus phoenicea subsp.

canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Island juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and small, blue-black berries.

The plant is adapted to the arid and coastal environments of the subtropical region. It has a slow growth rate and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The species is of interest in horticulture due to its ornamental value and resilience to harsh conditions.
