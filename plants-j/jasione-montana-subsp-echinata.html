Jasione montana subsp. echinata

Jasione montana subsp.

echinata, commonly known as the spiked rock-jasmine, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright, branched stems that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and gravelly soils, making it a hardy plant in well-drained environments.
