Jacea alutacea

Jacea alutacea, commonly known as the narrowleaf jacea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Jacea alutacea has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
