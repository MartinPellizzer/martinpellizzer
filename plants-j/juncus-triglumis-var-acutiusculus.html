Juncus triglumis var. acutiusculus

Juncus triglumis var. acutiusculus, commonly known as the sharpleaf rush, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and bear small, clustered spikes of greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and have a sharp, pointed tip, giving it its common name.

It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
