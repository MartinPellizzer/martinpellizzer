Justicia trisulca

Justicia trisulca, commonly known as the three-lobed flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular, red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with three distinct lobes, giving it its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Justicia trisulca is typically grown as a perennial in USDA hardiness zones 9 through 11.
