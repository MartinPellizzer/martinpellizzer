Jasminum bahiense

Jasminum bahiense, commonly known as the Bahamian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is a trailing shrub that produces fragrant, white flowers with five petals. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly glossy appearance.

Jasminum bahiense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
