Justicia candicans

Justicia candicans, commonly known as the flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble flames, with bright red or orange bracts. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are pollinated by birds and insects. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Justicia candicans is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
