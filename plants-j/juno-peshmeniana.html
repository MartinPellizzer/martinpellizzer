Juno peshmeniana

Juno peshmeniana, commonly known as the Peshmenian juniper, is a species of juniper native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in rocky and arid environments. The plant features needle-like leaves that are blue-green in color and produce small, fleshy berries that are edible for birds. Juno peshmeniana is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in harsh climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of the local flora in its native habitat.
