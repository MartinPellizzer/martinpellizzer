Juncellus minutus

Juncellus minutus, commonly known as the dwarf rush, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical stems that are typically less than 10 centimeters tall. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, which are usually greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Juncellus minutus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
