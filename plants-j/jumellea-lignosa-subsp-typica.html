Jumellea lignosa subsp. typica

Jumellea lignosa subsp.

typica, commonly known as the African milkwood tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its hard wood and has traditional uses in various indigenous cultures.
