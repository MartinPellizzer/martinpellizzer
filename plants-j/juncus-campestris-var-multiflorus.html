Juncus campestris var. multiflorus

Juncus campestris var. multiflorus, commonly known as the field rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, greenish-white flowers. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, ditches, and along stream banks. It has rhizomatous growth and can form dense vegetation that stabilizes soil.

The flowers are often wind-pollinated, and the plant is adapted to tolerate waterlogged conditions.
