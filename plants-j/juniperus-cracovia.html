Juniperus cracovia

Juniperus cracovia, commonly known as the Polish juniper, is a species of juniper native to central and eastern Europe.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in rocky or dry soils. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent. Its foliage consists of scale-like leaves that are dark green and dense.

Juniperus cracovia is adapted to harsh climates and is often found in mountainous regions.
