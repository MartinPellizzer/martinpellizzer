Jarava leptostachya

Jarava leptostachya, commonly known as the slender bottlebrush grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that resemble a bottlebrush. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Jarava leptostachya is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
