Justicia latior subsp. longifolia

Justicia latior subsp.

longifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas. It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds, contributing to its ecological value.
