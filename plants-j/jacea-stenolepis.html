Jacea stenolepis

Jacea stenolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved jacea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and discoid florets. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture, and its flowers are small, yellowish, and arranged in dense clusters.

Jacea stenolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
