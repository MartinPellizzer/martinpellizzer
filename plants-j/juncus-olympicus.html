Juncus olympicus

Juncus olympicus, commonly known as the Olympic rush, is a perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender, grass-like stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of white or pale green flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, stream banks, and pond edges.

Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
