Junellia clavata

Junellia clavata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive purple throat. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in clusters. Junellia clavata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
