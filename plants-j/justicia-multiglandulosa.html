Justicia multiglandulosa

Justicia multiglandulosa, commonly known as the red-rooted false dragonhead, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring square, hairy stems and opposite leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.
