Justicia crebrinodis

Justicia crebrinodis, commonly known as the hairy leaf justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
