Juno khassanovii

Juno khassanovii, commonly known as the Khassanov's juniper, is a species of juniper native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in rocky and arid environments. The plant has needle-like leaves and produces small, blue-black berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. Juno khassanovii is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in harsh climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
