Juncus capitatus

Juncus capitatus, commonly known as the tufted rush, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are usually unbranched and covered with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowering heads are compact, consisting of numerous small, spike-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Juncus capitatus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
