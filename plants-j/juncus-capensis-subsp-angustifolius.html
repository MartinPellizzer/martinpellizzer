Juncus capensis subsp. angustifolius

Juncus capensis subsp.

angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as riverbanks and swampy areas.

The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and bear narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, usually appearing in late summer.

The species is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
