Jasminum pubescens var. bracteatum

Jasminum pubescens var. bracteatum, commonly known as the Chinese jasmine or bracteatum jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly hairy texture, while the stems are covered in fine pubescence.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
