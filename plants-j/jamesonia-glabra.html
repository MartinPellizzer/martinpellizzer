Jamesonia glabra

Jamesonia glabra, commonly known as the smoothleaf jamesonia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its woody stems and evergreen foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded forest environments. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in upright clusters, adding ornamental value to its natural habitat.

The leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth texture, contributing to its common name.
