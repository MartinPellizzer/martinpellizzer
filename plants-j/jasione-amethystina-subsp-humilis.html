Jasione amethystina subsp. humilis

Jasione amethystina subsp.

humilis, commonly known as the purple scabious, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features slender, branching stems and produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its unique floral structure and adaptability to various soil conditions.
