Juglans californica f. quercina

Juglans californica f. quercina, commonly known as the California black walnut, is a variant of the black walnut tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dark, rugged bark and alternate, compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by edible, round nuts enclosed in a thick, woody husk. Its fruits are highly valued for their rich, oily kernels used in culinary and industrial applications.

This subspecies is also notable for its ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
