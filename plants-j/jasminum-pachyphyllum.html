Jasminum pachyphyllum

Jasminum pachyphyllum, commonly known as the thick-leaved jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its natural habitat. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for tropical and subtropical gardens.
