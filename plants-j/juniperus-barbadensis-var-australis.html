Juniperus barbadensis var. australis

Juniperus barbadensis var. australis, commonly known as the Australian juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which consists of scale-like leaves arranged in dense clusters. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are typically blue or black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
