Juncus prismatocarpus subvar. pluritubulosus

Juncus prismatocarpus subvar. pluritubulosus, commonly known as the multibranched rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Juncaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves that form a mat-like structure. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing greenish to brownish flowers. It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as along stream banks or in swampy areas.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense vegetation, contributing to soil stabilization in its habitat.
