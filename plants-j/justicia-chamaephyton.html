Justicia chamaephyton

Justicia chamaephyton, commonly known as the creeping justicia, is a perennial flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically has opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces, and its flowers are often arranged in clusters at the ends of branches. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its ability to spread and cover ground effectively.
