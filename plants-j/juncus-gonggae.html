Juncus gonggae

Juncus gonggae, commonly known as the Chinese rush, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and dense, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in spikes at the top of the plant.

Juncus gonggae is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in aquatic habitats.
