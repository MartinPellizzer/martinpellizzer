Japarandiba augusta

Japarandiba augusta, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry tree, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, red, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits.
