Jacea nervosa

Jacea nervosa, commonly known as the slender leaf bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming in late summer. Jacea nervosa thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in alpine and rocky habitats.
