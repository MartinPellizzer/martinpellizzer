Jasminum brieyi

Jasminum brieyi, commonly known as the Burrell's jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and oval-shaped with a smooth surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its pleasant aroma.
