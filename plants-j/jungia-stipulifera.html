Jungia stipulifera

Jungia stipulifera, commonly known as the stipule-bearing jungle plant, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3-5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape and have prominent stipules. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, usually yellow or greenish in color.

Jungia stipulifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
