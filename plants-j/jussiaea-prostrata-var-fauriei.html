Jussiaea prostrata var. fauriei

Jussiaea prostrata var. fauriei, commonly known as the swamp spikerush, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically forms dense mats near the surface of still or slow-moving water, with its rhizomes anchoring it in the muddy substrate. The plant produces small, spadix-like inflorescences that are often hidden beneath the water's surface, a characteristic feature of the Cyperaceae family. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often floating or partially submerged, aiding in photosynthesis in aquatic conditions.

This variety is adapted to tolerate fluctuating water levels and poor nutrient conditions, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
