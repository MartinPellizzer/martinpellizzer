Jamesonia pearcei

Jamesonia pearcei, commonly known as the pearce's spurge, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has smooth, waxy leaves that are typically alternate and lance-shaped. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Jamesonia pearcei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
