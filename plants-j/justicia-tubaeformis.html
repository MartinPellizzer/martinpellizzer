Justicia tubaeformis

Justicia tubaeformis, commonly known as the flame flower or red flame plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in warm, humid environments. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can grow up to 3 meters in height.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate partial shade and its adaptability to various soil types.
