Jacobaea ambracea

Jacobaea ambracea, commonly known as the common ragwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can be found in grasslands, waste areas, and along roadsides. It has rough, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Jacobaea ambracea is also known for its toxic properties, making it a plant to avoid in livestock areas.
