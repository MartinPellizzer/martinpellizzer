Jasminum didymum subsp. didymum

Jasminum didymum subsp.

didymum, commonly known as the white jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and glossy, with a pointed tip.

The flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils due to their aromatic properties.
