Jacaranda clausseniana

Jacaranda clausseniana, commonly known as the African jacaranda, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The tree has a straight trunk with a broad, umbrella-like canopy and produces long, pendulous inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with a pointed tip.

The wood is lightweight and has been used historically for construction and furniture-making.
