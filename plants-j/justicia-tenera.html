Justicia tenera

Justicia tenera, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Mexican verbena, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Justicia tenera is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.
