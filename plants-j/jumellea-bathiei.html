Jumellea bathiei

Jumellea bathiei, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils. The plant produces large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

Jumellea bathiei is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance in its native habitat.
