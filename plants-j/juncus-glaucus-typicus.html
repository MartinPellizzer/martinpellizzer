Juncus glaucus typicus

Juncus glaucus typicus, commonly known as the blue rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist soil and is often found in areas with standing water or high humidity.

Juncus glaucus typicus is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
