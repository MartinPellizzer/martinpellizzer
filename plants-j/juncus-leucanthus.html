Juncus leucanthus

Juncus leucanthus, commonly known as white flowering rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish or purplish in color, growing up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters at the top of the stems. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a bluish-gray hue.

Juncus leucanthus thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, pond edges, and along stream banks.
