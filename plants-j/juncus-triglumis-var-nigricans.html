Juncus triglumis var. nigricans

Juncus triglumis var. nigricans, commonly known as black rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It features tall, slender, dark green to blackish stems that are often used in basketry and crafts. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale green flowers in spikes during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, growing from the base of the plant.

It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in swampy or pond edges.
