Jacobaea carniolica

Jacobaea carniolica, commonly known as the Carpathian ragwort, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Jacobaea carniolica is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
