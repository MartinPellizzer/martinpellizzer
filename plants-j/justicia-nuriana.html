Justicia nuriana

Justicia nuriana, commonly known as the red-veined justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers with contrasting green veins, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often veined with a reddish color, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it valuable for both aesthetic and ecological purposes.
