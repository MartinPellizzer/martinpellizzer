Jatropha urens var. herbacea

Jatropha urens var. herbacea, commonly known as the stinging nettle plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a dense covering of fine stinging hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

The species is known for its defensive mechanisms, including the irritant hairs that deter herbivores.
