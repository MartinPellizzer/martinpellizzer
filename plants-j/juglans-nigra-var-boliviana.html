Juglans nigra var. boliviana

Juglans nigra var. boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian black walnut, is a variant of the black walnut tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, dark green leaves and distinctive, edible nuts enclosed in a thick, husky shell. The plant thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth. Its wood is highly valued for its strength and rich color, making it a sought-after material in woodworking.

The tree also plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
