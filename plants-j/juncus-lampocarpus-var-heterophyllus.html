Juncus lampocarpus var. heterophyllus

Juncus lampocarpus var. heterophyllus, commonly known as the coastal rush, is a perennial plant native to the western coast of North America.

It typically grows in wet or brackish environments such as tidal marshes and estuaries. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered spikes of white or pale yellow flowers.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to saline conditions and its role in stabilizing coastal soils.
