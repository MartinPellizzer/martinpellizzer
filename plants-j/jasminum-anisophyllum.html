Jasminum anisophyllum

Jasminum anisophyllum, commonly known as the small-leaved jasmine, is a species of flowering plant in the Oleaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the summer months. Its leaves are small, elliptical, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
