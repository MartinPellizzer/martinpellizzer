Juncus virgatus

Juncus virgatus, commonly known as the slender rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in areas with standing water or saturated ground.

Juncus virgatus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
