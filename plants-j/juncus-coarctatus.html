Juncus coarctatus

Juncus coarctatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, pond edges, and stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 1 inch in diameter. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from leafy clusters in late spring to early summer.

Juncus coarctatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
