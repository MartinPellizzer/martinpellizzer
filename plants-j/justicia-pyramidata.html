Justicia pyramidata

Justicia pyramidata, commonly known as the flame flower, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Justicia pyramidata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
