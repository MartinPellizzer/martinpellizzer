Jasminoides flaccidum

Jasminoides flaccidum, commonly known as the soft jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has slender, climbing stems and lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and horticultural collections.
