Jambosa urceolata

Jambosa urceolata, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical shrub native to southern Africa.

It produces small, yellowish-green fruits with a distinctive star-shaped pattern on its surface. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The fruit is edible, with a sweet, slightly acidic flavor, and is used both for food and traditional medicine.
