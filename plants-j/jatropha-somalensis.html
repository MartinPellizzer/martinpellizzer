Jatropha somalensis

Jatropha somalensis, commonly known as the Somali physic nut, is a shrub native to the Horn of Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red berries containing toxic seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically cultivated for agricultural purposes.
