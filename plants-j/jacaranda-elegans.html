Jacaranda elegans

Jacaranda elegans, commonly known as the elegant jacaranda, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring a slender trunk and a rounded crown. The plant is characterized by its showy, drooping clusters of purple-pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and along streets for its beauty and fragrance.
