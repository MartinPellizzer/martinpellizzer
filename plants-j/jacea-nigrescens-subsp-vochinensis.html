Jacea nigrescens subsp. vochinensis

Jacea nigrescens subsp.

vochinensis, commonly known as the Vochni hillsplant, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to the high-altitude regions of the Eastern Arc Mountains in Tanzania. This perennial herb is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom during the dry season. It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the cool, moist conditions of its mountainous habitat.

The plant is relatively rare and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and potential medicinal properties.
