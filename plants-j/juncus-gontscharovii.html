Juncus gontscharovii

Juncus gontscharovii, commonly known as the Gontscharov's rush, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear greenish-yellow flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Juncus gontscharovii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
