Jasminum ligustroides

Jasminum ligustroides, commonly known as the Brazilian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Jasminum ligustroides is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
