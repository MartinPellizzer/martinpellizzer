Juncus palensis

Juncus palensis, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland and marsh environments in North America.

It has tall, cylindrical stems that are typically green to reddish in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late summer. The plant thrives in moist to water-saturated soils and is often found in swamps, bogs, and along the edges of lakes and ponds. Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil and prevent erosion, making it ecologically significant in wetland ecosystems.

Juncus palensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its upright growth habit.
