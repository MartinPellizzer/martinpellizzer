Juncus deosaicus

Juncus deosaicus, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems that often have a silvery or grayish hue due to the presence of fine, scaly hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands, marshes, or along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in spike-like clusters, and it produces wind-dispersed seeds.

Juncus deosaicus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
