Jasminum viridescens

Jasminum viridescens, commonly known as the green jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lush, dark green foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy appearance.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic blooms.
