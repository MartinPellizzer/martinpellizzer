Justicia salicifolia

Justicia salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf false heather, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that resemble willow leaves, hence its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
