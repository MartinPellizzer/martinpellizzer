Juglans olanchana var. standleyi

Juglans olanchana var. standleyi, commonly known as the Mexican black walnut, is a variant of the black walnut species native to central Mexico.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive bark. The tree produces edible nuts enclosed in a thick, green husk, which ripen in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates with distinct seasonal changes.

The plant is valued for its hard wood and historical use in traditional medicine.
