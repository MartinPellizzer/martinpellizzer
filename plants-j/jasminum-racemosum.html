Jasminum racemosum

Jasminum racemosum, commonly known as the clustered jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, with opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. Its flowers are usually 5-petaled and emit a sweet, intoxicating aroma.

Jasminum racemosum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
