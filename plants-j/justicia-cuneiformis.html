Justicia cuneiformis

Justicia cuneiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf false arum, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spathaceous inflorescence that resembles a spatula. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to semi-shaded environments.

Justicia cuneiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
