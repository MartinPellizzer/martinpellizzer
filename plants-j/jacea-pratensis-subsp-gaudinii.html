Jacea pratensis subsp. gaudinii

Jacea pratensis subsp.

gaudinii, commonly known as the Gaudin's meadow grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in moist meadows and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny flowers. It is often found in open, sunny habitats and is adapted to temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland and grassland ecosystems.
