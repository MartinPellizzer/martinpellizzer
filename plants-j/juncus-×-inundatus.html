Juncus × inundatus

Juncus × inundatus, commonly known as the hybrid rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes during the summer months. It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.

Its adaptability to various wetland conditions makes it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
