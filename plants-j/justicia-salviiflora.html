Justicia salviiflora

Justicia salviiflora, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the Americas, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes.
