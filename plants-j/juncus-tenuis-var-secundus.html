Juncus tenuis var. secundus

Juncus tenuis var. secundus, commonly known as the slender rush, is a flowering plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of aquatic ecosystems. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

This variety is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its adaptability and ecological benefits.
