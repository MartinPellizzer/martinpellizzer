Jacquemontia cuspidata

Jacquemontia cuspidata, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces edible pods that contain seeds, which are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and agricultural settings.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and useful in sustainable farming practices.
