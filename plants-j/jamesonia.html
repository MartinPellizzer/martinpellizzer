Jamesonia

Jamesonia, commonly known as the "Jameson's lily," is a genus of flowering plants native to the eastern United States.

It consists of only two species, Jamesonia multiflora and Jamesonia oreophila, both of which are perennial herbs. The plants produce showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pink, with a distinctive, recurved shape. They thrive in moist, shaded environments and are often found in woodland areas.

The genus is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
