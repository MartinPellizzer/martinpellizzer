Jasminum anodontum

Jasminum anodontum, commonly known as the white jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Jasminum anodontum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
