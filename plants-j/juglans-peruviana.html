Juglans peruviana

Juglans peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian walnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the walnut family, Juglandaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, edible nuts. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy. Its fruit is a drupe that matures in the fall, containing a single, hard-shelled nut.

The wood of Juglans peruviana is valued for its strength and is used in construction and furniture-making.
