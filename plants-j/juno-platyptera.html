Juno platyptera

Juno platyptera, commonly known as the broad-winged junco, is a small passerine bird native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive plumage, with males displaying a striking black head, white cheek, and gray body. The species is typically found in open habitats such as fields and gardens, where it forages for seeds and insects. Juno platyptera is known for its melodic song, which is often heard during the breeding season.

Its ecological role includes seed dispersal and insect population control in its native range.
