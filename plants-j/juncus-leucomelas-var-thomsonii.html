Juncus leucomelas var. thomsonii

Juncus leucomelas var. thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's rush, is a variant of the silver rush species native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial grass-like plant that typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a white or silver bloom, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters near the base of the stems.

Thomson's rush plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
