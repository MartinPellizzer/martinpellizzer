Justicia bentii

Justicia bentii, commonly known as the Mexican wheelcactus, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spiny stems that resemble a wheel, hence its common name. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, Justicia bentii is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
