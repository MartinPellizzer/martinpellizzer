Juniperus turcomanica var. seravschanica

Juniperus turcomanica var. seravschanica, commonly known as the Seravschan juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves and small, berry-like cones that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and mountainous regions. It has a slow growth rate and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
