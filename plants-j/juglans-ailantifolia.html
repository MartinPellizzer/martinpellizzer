Juglans ailantifolia

Juglans ailantifolia, commonly known as the Chinese walnut, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged with 15 to 35 leaflets. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by edible nuts enclosed in a spiky husk. Its bark is thick and fissured, often appearing rough and flaky.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
