Justicia fulgida

Justicia fulgida, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular, red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces showy bracts that often resemble petals, enhancing its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Justicia fulgida is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses due to its vibrant blooms.
