Jatropha × hastifolia

Jatropha × hastifolia, commonly known as the hybrid jatropha, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Jatropha curcas and Jatropha multifida, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with palmate leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Jatropha × hastifolia is native to tropical regions of Africa and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
