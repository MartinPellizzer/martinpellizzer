Juncus castaneus subsp. triceps

Juncus castaneus subsp.

triceps, commonly known as the three-veined rush, is a perennial plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands. It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often near freshwater sources.

The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are usually green or reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
