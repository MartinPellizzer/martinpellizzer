Justicia grandiflora

Justicia grandiflora, commonly known as the flame bush, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

Justicia grandiflora thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
