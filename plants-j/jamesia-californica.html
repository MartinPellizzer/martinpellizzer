Jamesia californica

Jamesia californica, commonly known as the California flamebrush, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking red, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has woody stems that are often covered in a layer of fine, white hairs. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a rough texture and serrated edges.

Jamesia californica thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
