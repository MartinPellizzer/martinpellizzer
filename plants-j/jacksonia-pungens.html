Jacksonia pungens

Jacksonia pungens, commonly known as the firecracker flower, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers that resemble small fireworks, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Jacksonia pungens thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.
