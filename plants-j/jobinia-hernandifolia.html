Jobinia hernandifolia

Jobinia hernandifolia, commonly known as the Mexican heather, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant features small, showy flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring. Its leaves are evergreen, glossy, and arranged in whorls around the stems.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.
