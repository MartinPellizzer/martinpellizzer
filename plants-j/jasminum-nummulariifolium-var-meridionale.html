Jasminum nummulariifolium var. meridionale

Jasminum nummulariifolium var. meridionale, commonly known as the Southern jasmine, is a flowering plant native to southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant typically grows as a shrub with glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged in a compact, bushy form. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its pleasant fragrance and compact growth habit.
