Juncus lampocarpus var. microcephalus

Juncus lampocarpus var. microcephalus, commonly known as the dwarf rush, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of southern California.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like clusters of narrow, cylindrical leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in salt marshes and tidal flats.

This variety is distinguished by its compact growth habit and small, rounded flower heads.
