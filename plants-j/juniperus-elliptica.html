Juniperus elliptica

Juniperus elliptica, commonly known as the elliptical juniper, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical shape and dense foliage composed of scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are typically blue or purple in color, containing toxic seeds. Juniperus elliptica is well adapted to harsh, cold climates and can thrive in rocky or well-drained soils.

Its slow growth rate and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for horticultural use in suitable climates.
