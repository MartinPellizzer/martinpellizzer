Jatropha variegata

Jatropha variegata, commonly known as the variegated physic nut, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves that display shades of white, yellow, and green. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by spiny capsules containing toxic seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage.

However, it should be handled with care as all parts of the plant are poisonous.
