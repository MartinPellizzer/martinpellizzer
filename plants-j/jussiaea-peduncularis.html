Jussiaea peduncularis

Jussiaea peduncularis, commonly known as the slender water-mint, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its small, white or pale blue flowers are arranged in whorls along the stem, and it produces small, oval seeds.

Jussiaea peduncularis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
