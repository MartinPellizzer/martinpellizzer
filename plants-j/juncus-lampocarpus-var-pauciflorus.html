Juncus lampocarpus var. pauciflorus

Juncus lampocarpus var. pauciflorus, commonly known as the few-flowered rush, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and spikes of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil.

The few-flowered rush is an important component of riparian ecosystems, providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
