Jasminum pedale

Jasminum pedale, commonly known as the Persian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

The flowers are highly aromatic, making it a popular choice for ornamental and perfumery purposes.
