Jasminum fruticans subsp. mariae

Jasminum fruticans subsp.

mariae, commonly known as the Marie jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a strong, sweet scent.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
