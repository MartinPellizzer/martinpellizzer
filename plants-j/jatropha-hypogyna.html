Jatropha hypogyna

Jatropha hypogyna, commonly known as the purple leaf jatropha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking purple foliage and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, toxic seeds that are encased in a fleshy, orange-red capsule. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in disturbed habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
