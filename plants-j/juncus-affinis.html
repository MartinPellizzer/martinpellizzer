Juncus affinis

Juncus affinis, commonly known as the soft rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It typically grows in dense stands, reaching heights of 30 to 60 centimeters, with cylindrical, grass-like stems that are often reddish or greenish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes during the summer months, which are followed by dark, berry-like seeds. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in wetland habitats.

Juncus affinis plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
