Juncus glaucus var. laxiflorus

Juncus glaucus var. laxiflorus, commonly known as the slender bog rush, is a variant of the bog rush species native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like stems that often grow in dense clusters, and its narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in a waxy, bluish coating. The plant produces small, pale green to white flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, which are usually borne on elongated peduncles. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in wet garden settings.
