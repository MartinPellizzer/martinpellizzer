Juglans regia var. fragilis

Juglans regia var. fragilis, commonly known as the Persian walnut, is a variant of the English walnut.

It is characterized by its smaller, more delicate nuts and thinner husks compared to the standard variety. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in temperate climates. It produces heart-shaped leaves with a serrated margin and bears edible, oily seeds.

This variety is often cultivated for its high-quality nuts and ornamental value.
