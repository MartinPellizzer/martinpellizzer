Juncus lampocarpus var. prolifer

Juncus lampocarpus var. prolifer, commonly known as the coastal rush, is a flowering plant native to the western coast of North America.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, saline environments such as tidal flats and salt marshes.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
