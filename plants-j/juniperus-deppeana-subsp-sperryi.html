Juniperus deppeana subsp. sperryi

Juniperus deppeana subsp.

sperryi, commonly known as the Sperry's juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are often used as a spice in traditional cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in desert ecosystems.
