Juncus acutus tyraicus

Juncus acutus tyraicus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often hairless and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and arranged in dense clusters at the base.

The flowers are small, clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and are usually greenish or brownish in color.
