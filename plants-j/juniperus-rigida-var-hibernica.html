Juniperus rigida var. hibernica

Juniperus rigida var. hibernica, commonly known as the Irish juniper, is a subspecies of the juniper shrub native to western Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, blue-gray needles. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils, often in mountainous or coastal areas. It produces small, berry-like cones that are edible and have a sharp, aromatic flavor.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
