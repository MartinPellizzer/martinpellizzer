Juniperus ekmanii

Juniperus ekmanii, commonly known as the Sierra juniper, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage and small, cone-like berries that are typically blue or black in color. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and high elevations. Its leaves are typically blue-gray in color, providing a distinctive appearance in its native habitat.

Juniperus ekmanii plays an important role in local ecosystems, offering habitat and food for various wildlife species.
