Juglans sieboldiana var. rugosa

Juglans sieboldiana var. rugosa, commonly known as the Japanese walnut or rugose walnut, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins and rough, textured bark. The tree produces edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk, which mature in the fall. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nut production.

The species is also valued for its hard, durable wood.
