Juncus densiflorus

Juncus densiflorus, commonly known as denseflower rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and stream banks. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and bear dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and usually have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Juncus densiflorus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
