Juncus macer f. williamsii

Juncus macer f. williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' rush, is a variant of the common rush species.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, stream banks, and damp woodlands. Its inflorescence consists of small, clustered spikes of greenish-white flowers.

Botanically, it is notable for its adaptability to waterlogged soils and its role in stabilizing shoreline ecosystems.
