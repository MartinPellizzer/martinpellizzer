Jasminum subhumile

Jasminum subhumile, commonly known as the dwarf jasmine or Indian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub with fragrant, white or yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant fragrance. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for garden borders or containers.

Jasminum subhumile is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
