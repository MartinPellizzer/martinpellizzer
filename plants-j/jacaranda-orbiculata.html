Jacaranda orbiculata

Jacaranda orbiculata, commonly known as the African jacaranda, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking purple-pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The leaves are oval-shaped and arranged oppositely along the branches. The tree produces long, narrow pods that contain flat, fibrous seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to dry, well-drained soils.
