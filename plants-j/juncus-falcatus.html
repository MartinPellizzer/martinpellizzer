Juncus falcatus

Juncus falcatus, commonly known as the sword rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland and marshy environments in the Americas.

It features tall, grass-like stems that are often used in floral arrangements due to their durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of brown or yellowish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in moist soils.

Juncus falcatus plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
