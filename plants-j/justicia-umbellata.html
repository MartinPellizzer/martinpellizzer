Justicia umbellata

Justicia umbellata, commonly known as the whorled false heather, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both ecological and horticultural landscapes.
