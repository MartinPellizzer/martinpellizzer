Jatropha aconitifolia var. genuina

Jatropha aconitifolia var. genuina, commonly known as the Indian physic or blue milkweed, is a shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and milky sap, which contains toxic compounds. The plant has elliptical leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
