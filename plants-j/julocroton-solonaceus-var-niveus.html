Julocroton solonaceus var. niveus

Julocroton solonaceus var. niveus, commonly known as the white-flowered julocroton, is a variant of the julocroton species characterized by its distinctive white flowers.

This plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa. It features lance-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and produces small, clustered inflorescences. The varietal form 'niveus' is noted for its pale, almost white petals, which set it apart from other julocroton varieties.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
