Juncus liebmannii

Juncus liebmannii, commonly known as Liebmann's rush, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of grass-like, cylindrical stems. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish or brownish and appear at the base of the stems.

Juncus liebmannii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
