Jasione montana var. prolifera

Jasione montana var. prolifera, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to violet bell-shaped flowers. The plant is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, often spreading along the ground to form dense mats. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
