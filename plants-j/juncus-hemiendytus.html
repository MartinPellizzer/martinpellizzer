Juncus hemiendytus

Juncus hemiendytus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a species of flowering plant in the family Juncaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in clusters. This plant is often found in moist or wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.

Juncus hemiendytus is native to parts of North America and is adapted to thrive in environments with high water content.
