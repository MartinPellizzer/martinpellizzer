Juniperus turcomanica

Juniperus turcomanica, commonly known as the Turkestan juniper, is a species of juniper native to Central Asia.

It is a small, evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has needle-like leaves that are blue-green in color and are arranged in spirals along the branches. Its cones, which are typically reddish-brown, contain the aromatic berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.

Juniperus turcomanica is well adapted to harsh climates and can thrive in poor soils.
