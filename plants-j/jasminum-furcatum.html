Jasminum furcatum

Jasminum furcatum, commonly known as the fragrant jasmine or the cut-leaf jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often deeply lobed. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Jasminum furcatum thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic blooms.
