Juncus acutomaritimus

Juncus acutomaritimus, commonly known as the seaside rush, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that often grow in dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to tolerate saline and windy conditions.

Juncus acutomaritimus plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
