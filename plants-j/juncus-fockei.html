Juncus fockei

Juncus fockei, commonly known as the Fockei rush, is a flowering plant species native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae, which includes various species of rushes and sedges. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its appearance includes tall, slender stems with narrow, grass-like leaves.

Juncus fockei produces small, clustered flower heads that are often inconspicuous, typical of many rush species.
