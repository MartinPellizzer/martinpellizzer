Jatropha mollissima

Jatropha mollissima, commonly known as the velvet leaf jatropha, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that give it its common name, and it produces yellow flowers followed by orange or red berries. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. It is also known for its ornamental value due to its distinctive foliage.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
