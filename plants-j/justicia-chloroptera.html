Justicia chloroptera

Justicia chloroptera, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its stems are woody at the base and can branch out profusely, forming dense shrubs.

The flowers are borne on long, slender inflorescences, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
