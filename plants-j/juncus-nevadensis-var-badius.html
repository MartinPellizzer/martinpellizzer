Juncus nevadensis var. badius

Juncus nevadensis var. badius, commonly known as the dark rush, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swales, and riparian zones. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that are often dark green to nearly black in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer.

This variety is adapted to moist soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
