Juniperus gracilis

Juniperus gracilis, commonly known as the slender juniper, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It typically grows in rocky soils and high elevations, showcasing a compact, dense growth habit. The plant produces small, scale-like leaves that are usually bluish-green in color, providing a striking visual contrast against its rugged habitat. Its berries, which are actually fleshy cones, are a valuable food source for birds and other wildlife.

Juniperus gracilis is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient and hardy species.
