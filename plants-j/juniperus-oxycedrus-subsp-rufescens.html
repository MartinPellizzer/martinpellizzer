Juniperus oxycedrus subsp. rufescens

Juniperus oxycedrus subsp.

rufescens, commonly known as the Turkish juniper, is a subspecies native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It is a medium-sized evergreen shrub or small tree characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves.

The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are typically harvested for use in gin production. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates with mild winters.

The subspecies is distinguished by its reddish-brown bark and its ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
