Jacksonia divisa

Jacksonia divisa, commonly known as the divided waratah, is a striking evergreen shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features broad, leathery leaves that are often divided, giving it its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Jacksonia divisa is a popular choice for gardens due to its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
