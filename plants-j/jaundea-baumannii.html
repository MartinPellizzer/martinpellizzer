Jaundea baumannii

Jaundea baumannii, commonly known as the golden beard plant, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellowish-green foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Jaundea baumannii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
