Jacksonia stricta

Jacksonia stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf jacob's ladder, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the gentian family (Gentianaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and slender, branching stems. The plant features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are narrow and arranged in a whorled pattern along the stems.

Jacksonia stricta is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides.
