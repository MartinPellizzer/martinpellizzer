Juniperus communis f. suecica

Juniperus communis f. suecica, commonly known as the Swedish juniper, is a subspecies of the common juniper characterized by its compact growth habit and dark blue berries.

It is a deciduous shrub native to northern Europe, particularly found in rocky and acidic soils. The plant produces small, scale-like leaves that are typically glaucous in appearance. Its berries are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to other juniper species.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting wildlife.
