Juniperus virginiana subsp. scopulorum

Juniperus virginiana subsp.

scopulorum, commonly known as the Rocky Mountain juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States. It is characterized by its scale-like leaves and small, blue-black berries that are typically retained on the plant for several years.

This subspecies is adapted to arid and rocky environments, often growing in open woodlands and mountainous regions. Its foliage is aromatic, and it produces a resinous substance that contributes to its ecological and historical significance.

The plant plays an important role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
