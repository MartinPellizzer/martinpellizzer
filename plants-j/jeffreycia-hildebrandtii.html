Jeffreycia hildebrandtii

Jeffreycia hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid climates.
