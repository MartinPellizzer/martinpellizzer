Justicia furcata var. terminalis

Justicia furcata var. terminalis, commonly known as the flame flower or red flame plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces showy, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters at the ends of branches, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant is also known for its ability to tolerate drought conditions once established.
