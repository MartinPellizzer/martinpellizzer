Juniperus virginiana var. tripartita

Juniperus virginiana var. tripartita, commonly known as the eastern redcedar, is a shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its scale-like leaves that are typically reddish-brown, and it produces small, berry-like cones that are toxic to livestock. The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.

Its aromatic foliage and resilience make it a common sight in woodlands and along roadsides.
