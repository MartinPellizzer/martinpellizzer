Juno bucharica

Juno bucharica, commonly known as the Balkan juniper, is a shrub belonging to the Cupressaceae family.

It is native to the Balkan Peninsula and parts of southeastern Europe. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, scale-like leaves. It produces blue-black berries that are edible but typically used in traditional medicine.

Juno bucharica is often found in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.
