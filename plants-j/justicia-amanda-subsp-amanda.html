Justicia amanda subsp. amanda

Justicia amanda subsp.

amanda, commonly known as the Amanda's flame or flame violet, is a flowering plant native to South America. It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange.

The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, while its stems are square and branched.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
