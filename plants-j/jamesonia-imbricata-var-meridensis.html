Jamesonia imbricata var. meridensis

Jamesonia imbricata var. meridensis, commonly known as the Mexican golden bell, is a striking perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and unique foliage structure.
