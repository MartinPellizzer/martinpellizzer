Jasione laevis subsp. gredensis

Jasione laevis subsp.

gredensis, commonly known as the Gredensian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in open woodland or grassland habitats.

The plant features basal and stem leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, with a distinct, bell-shaped flower cluster. Its flowers are usually pale blue to violet, with five petals and a prominent central gland.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
