Justicia borinquensis

Justicia borinquensis, commonly known as the Boriqua flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean island of Puerto Rico.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds, which are attracted to its nectar-rich blooms.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
