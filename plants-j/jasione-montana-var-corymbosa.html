Jasione montana var. corymbosa

Jasione montana var. corymbosa, commonly known as the mountain jasmine or clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it contributes to the biodiversity of these ecosystems.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
