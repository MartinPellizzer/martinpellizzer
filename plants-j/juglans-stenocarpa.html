Juglans stenocarpa

Juglans stenocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf black walnut, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the walnut family, Juglandaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with 15 to 23 leaflets. The tree produces small, green, edible nuts enclosed in a rough, spiky husk. Its bark is thick and furrowed, and it can grow up to 25 meters tall.

The plant is valued for its wood and has ecological importance in its native habitat.
