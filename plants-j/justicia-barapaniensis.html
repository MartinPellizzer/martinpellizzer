Justicia barapaniensis

Justicia barapaniensis, commonly known as the Barapani justicia, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture, while its flowers are clustered in inflorescences at the ends of the branches.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or near water bodies.
