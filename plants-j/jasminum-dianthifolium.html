Jasminum dianthifolium

Jasminum dianthifolium, commonly known as the Chinese jasmine or the white jasmine, is a flowering plant native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, simple leaves, and a preference for well-drained soil and partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
