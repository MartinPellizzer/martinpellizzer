Jasminum fruticans

Jasminum fruticans, commonly known as the climbing jasmines, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems that can climb or trail, often using its tendrils to attach to supports. The plant produces fragrant, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and elliptic in shape, with a glossy surface.

Jasminum fruticans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
