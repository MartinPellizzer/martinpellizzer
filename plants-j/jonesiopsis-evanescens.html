Jonesiopsis evanescens

Jonesiopsis evanescens, commonly known as the fleeting Jonesiopsis, is a rare and delicate fern native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its ephemeral fronds, which often wither quickly, giving the plant its evanescens name. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of moisture and organic matter in its soil. Its small, intricate leaves are typically arranged in a spiral pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a challenging plant to cultivate outside its native range.
