Jasminum ovalifolium

Jasminum ovalifolium, commonly known as the Persian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Jasminum ovalifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its use in perfumery due to its sweet, delicate fragrance.
