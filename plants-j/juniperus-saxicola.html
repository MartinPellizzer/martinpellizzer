Juniperus saxicola

Juniperus saxicola, commonly known as the saxicola juniper, is a small evergreen shrub native to arid and rocky regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves and small, blue-gray berries that are often used in herbal medicine. The plant is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots and a compact growth habit that helps it conserve water. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found growing on slopes or in crevices.

Juniperus saxicola plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
