Justicia cayennensis var. parvifolia

Justicia cayennensis var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered flame flower, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
