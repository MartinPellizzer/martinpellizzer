Juncus communis subvar. viridiflorus

Juncus communis subvar. viridiflorus, commonly known as greenflower rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowering stems are typically erect and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic insects and birds.
