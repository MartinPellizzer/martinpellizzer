Justicia candelariae

Justicia candelariae, commonly known as the candlestick flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, candle-like inflorescences that are often brightly colored. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its flowers, which bloom in clusters, are usually shades of red, pink, or white, and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
