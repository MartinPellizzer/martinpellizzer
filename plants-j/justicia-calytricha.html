Justicia calytricha

Justicia calytricha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flame flower, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking clusters of tubular, brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom from summer to fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators, it is widely cultivated in warm climates.
