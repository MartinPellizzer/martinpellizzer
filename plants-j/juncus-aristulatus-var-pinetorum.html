Juncus aristulatus var. pinetorum

Juncus aristulatus var. pinetorum, commonly known as the pine barrens rush, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in wet, acidic soils found in pine forests and wetlands. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 1 meter in height. Its flowers are small, clustered spikes that appear in late spring to early summer.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
