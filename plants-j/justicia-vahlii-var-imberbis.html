Justicia vahlii var. imberbis

Justicia vahlii var. imberbis, commonly known as the smoothleaf flameflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The leaves are typically smooth and lance-shaped, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in warm climates.

The plant is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display and is relatively low maintenance once established.
