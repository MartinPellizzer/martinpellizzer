Jejewoodia jiewhoei

Jejewoodia jiewhoei, commonly known as the Bluebell of the Eastern Highlands, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. It is often found in shaded woodland areas, where it forms dense clusters.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is considered a conservation priority in its native habitat.
