Juniperus brevifolia subsp. brevifolia

Juniperus brevifolia subsp.

brevifolia, commonly known as the shortleaf juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves that are typically less than 1 millimeter in length.

The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is part of the Cupressaceae family and is known for its aromatic foliage and resilience to drought.
