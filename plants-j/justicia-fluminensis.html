Justicia fluminensis

Justicia fluminensis, commonly known as the river justicia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows along riverbanks and moist soils, thriving in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Justicia fluminensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
