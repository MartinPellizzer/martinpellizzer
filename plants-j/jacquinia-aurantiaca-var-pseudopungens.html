Jacquinia aurantiaca var. pseudopungens

Jacquinia aurantiaca var. pseudopungens, commonly known as the scarlet bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine white bloom.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained, sunny conditions.
