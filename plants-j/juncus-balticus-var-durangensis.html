Juncus balticus var. durangensis

Juncus balticus var. durangensis, commonly known as the Durango rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and wetlands. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
