Juglans regia var. monophylla

Juglans regia var. monophylla, commonly known as the Persian walnut, is a variant of the English walnut.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Iran and Afghanistan. This variety is characterized by its large, elongated nuts with a thick, leathery hull. The leaves are typically larger and more elongated compared to the standard walnut, and the tree can grow to significant heights.

It is valued for its high-quality nuts, which are widely used in culinary and commercial applications.
