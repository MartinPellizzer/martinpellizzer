Justicia patentiflora

Justicia patentiflora, commonly known as the showy false heather, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Justicia patentiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partially shaded environments.
