Japonasarum caulescens

Japonasarum caulescens, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo fern, is a species native to eastern Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, upright stems and feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are compound, with a delicate, lace-like appearance that contributes to its ornamental value.

Japonasarum caulescens is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
