Juncus bufonius subsp. insulanus

Juncus bufonius subsp.

insulanus, commonly known as the island rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Juncaceae family. It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as along the edges of ponds and in shallow water.

This subspecies is native to certain island regions and is adapted to its specific environmental conditions.
