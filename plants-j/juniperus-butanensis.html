Juniperus butanensis

Juniperus butanensis, commonly known as the Persian juniper, is a species of juniper native to parts of Central Asia, including regions of Iran, Afghanistan, and Pakistan.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, typically growing up to 5 meters in height. The plant has scale-like leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs, and its cones are berry-like, containing toxic seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
