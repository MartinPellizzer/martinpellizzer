Jacobaea sarracenica

Jacobaea sarracenica, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Jacobaea sarracenica is part of the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads.
