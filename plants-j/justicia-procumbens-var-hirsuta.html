Justicia procumbens var. hirsuta

Justicia procumbens var. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy commelina, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy texture, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for wetland restoration and ornamental gardening.
