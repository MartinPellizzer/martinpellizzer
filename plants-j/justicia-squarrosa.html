Justicia squarrosa

Justicia squarrosa, commonly known as the square-stemmed verbena, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its square, ribbed stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Justicia squarrosa is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
