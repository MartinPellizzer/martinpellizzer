Jacaranda pubescens

Jacaranda pubescens, commonly known as the silky jacaranda, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are pinnately compound, with small, velvety, and hairy leaflets that give the plant its common name. The tree produces long, cylindrical seed pods that contain numerous seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, sunny climates.
