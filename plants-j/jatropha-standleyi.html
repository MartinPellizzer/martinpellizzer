Jatropha standleyi

Jatropha standleyi, commonly known as the Mexican physic nut, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces toxic, orange-red berries that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy pulp. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Jatropha standleyi is notable for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential in biofuel production.
