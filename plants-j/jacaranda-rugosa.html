Jacaranda rugosa

Jacaranda rugosa, commonly known as the rough jacaranda, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The leaves are compound, with serrated edges and a rough texture, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

The tree is also valued for its ability to provide shade and its role in supporting local wildlife.
