Juniperus barbadensis

Juniperus barbadensis, commonly known as the Cape jasmine or Barbados juniper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cupressaceae.

It is native to the island of Barbados and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish flowers that are followed by blue-black berries, which are often used in perfumery and traditional medicine. Juniperus barbadensis thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and sunny environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in warm climates.
