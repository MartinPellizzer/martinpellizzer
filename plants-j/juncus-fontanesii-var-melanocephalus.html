Juncus fontanesii var. melanocephalus

Juncus fontanesii var. melanocephalus, commonly known as the black-headed rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as pond edges and damp meadows. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces dense clusters of small, dark purple to black flowers. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their astringent properties.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and its ornamental value in wetland gardens.
