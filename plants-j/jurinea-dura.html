Jurinea dura

Jurinea dura, commonly known as the hard sea bladder, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are adapted to arid and coastal environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in sandy or saline soils and is often found in coastal regions.

Jurinea dura is known for its ability to store water, making it well-suited for harsh, dry climates.
