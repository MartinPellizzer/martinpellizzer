Juniperus deppeana var. patoniana

Juniperus deppeana var. patoniana, commonly known as the Paton's juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves and small, blue-gray berries that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It has a slow growth rate and is often found in mountainous regions.

The leaves and berries contain essential oils that contribute to its aromatic properties.
