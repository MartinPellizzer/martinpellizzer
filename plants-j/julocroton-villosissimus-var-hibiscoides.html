Julocroton villosissimus var. hibiscoides

Julocroton villosissimus var. hibiscoides, commonly known as the woolly croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, hairy stems and large, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with five petals, which are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a decorative plant in some regions.
