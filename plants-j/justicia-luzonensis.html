Justicia luzonensis

Justicia luzonensis, commonly known as the Luzon flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
