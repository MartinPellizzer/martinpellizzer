Juncus acutus var. tommasinii

Juncus acutus var. tommasinii, commonly known as Tommasini's rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage composed of narrow, cylindrical leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in spike-like clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of riparian ecosystems.
