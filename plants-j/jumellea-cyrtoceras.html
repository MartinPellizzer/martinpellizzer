Jumellea cyrtoceras

Jumellea cyrtoceras, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its flowers are followed by follicles that contain the plant's toxic, milky sap.

The botanical characteristics of Jumellea cyrtoceras make it an important species in its native ecosystem.
