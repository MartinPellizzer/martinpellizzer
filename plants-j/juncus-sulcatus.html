Juncus sulcatus

Juncus sulcatus, commonly known as the grooved rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and covered with small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense spikes during the summer months. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Juncus sulcatus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
