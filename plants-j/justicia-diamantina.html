Justicia diamantina

Justicia diamantina, commonly known as the diamond plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, diamond-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tubular, violet or pink flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and indoor spaces.
