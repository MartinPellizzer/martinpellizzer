Justicia daidalea

Justicia daidalea, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a striking tropical plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 feet tall, featuring lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant is renowned for its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting white or yellow throat. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its floral display is particularly prominent during the summer months, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.
