Juncus lampocarpus var. lindhardii

Juncus lampocarpus var. lindhardii, commonly known as the Lindhard's rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous tiny, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and pond edges.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and a subject of study in plant ecology.
