Justicia kulalensis

Justicia kulalensis, commonly known as the kulalensis false arum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often showy inflorescences. The plant typically features broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
