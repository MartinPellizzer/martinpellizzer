Jurinea charcoviensis

Jurinea charcoviensis, commonly known as the Crimean sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of the Crimea and southern Ukraine.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features narrow, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in its dry habitat. Its small, white or pink flowers bloom in clusters, typically in late spring to early summer.
