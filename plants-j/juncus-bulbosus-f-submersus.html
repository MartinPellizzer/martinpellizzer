Juncus bulbosus f. submersus

Juncus bulbosus f. submersus, commonly known as the submerged rush, is a variant of the bulbous rush that thrives in aquatic or semi-aquatic environments.

This plant is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage that grows submerged or partially submerged in water. Its leaves are typically narrow and cylindrical, adapted for underwater conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged or just at the water's surface.

It plays an important ecological role in wetland habitats by stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for aquatic life.
