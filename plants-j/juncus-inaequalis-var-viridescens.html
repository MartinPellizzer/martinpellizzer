Juncus inaequalis var. viridescens

Juncus inaequalis var. viridescens, commonly known as the greenish rush, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist or wet environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant features slender, grass-like stems that are usually green to bluish-green in color, with small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense clumps that help stabilize soil in wet areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
