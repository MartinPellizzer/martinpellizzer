Juncus supinus f. submersus

Juncus supinus f. submersus, commonly known as the submerged rush, is a variant of the common rush that thrives in aquatic environments.

This plant is characterized by its submerged growth habit, with stems that are typically fully underwater. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to the water environment. It plays a role in stabilizing aquatic sediments and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.

The plant is often found in slow-moving or still freshwater habitats such as ponds and marshes.
