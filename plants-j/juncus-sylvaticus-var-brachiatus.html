Juncus sylvaticus var. brachiatus

Juncus sylvaticus var. brachiatus, commonly known as the branched rush, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in hue. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and damp woodlands.

Its fibrous roots and dense growth make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
