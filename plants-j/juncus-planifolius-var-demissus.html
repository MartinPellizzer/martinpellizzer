Juncus planifolius var. demissus

Juncus planifolius var. demissus, commonly known as the flatleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments.

It features slender, grass-like stems that grow in dense clusters, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters at the tips of the stems, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are flat and linear, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is adapted to moist or aquatic conditions and is often found in marshes, stream banks, and other wet habitats.
