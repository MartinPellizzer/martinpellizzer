Jatropha pohliana var. divergens

Jatropha pohliana var. divergens, commonly known as the velvetleaf jatropha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slight bluish tinge. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds covered in a sticky, viscid substance.

Jatropha pohliana var. divergens is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
