Juniperus phoenicea var. macrocarpa

Juniperus phoenicea var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces blue-black berries that are typically clustered in groups of three, which are an important food source for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate salt spray, making it suitable for coastal landscaping.
