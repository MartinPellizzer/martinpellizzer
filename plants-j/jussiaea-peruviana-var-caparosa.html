Jussiaea peruviana var. caparosa

Jussiaea peruviana var. caparosa, commonly known as the Peruvian waterwheel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique whorled arrangement of leaves and striking blue or violet flowers. The plant thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas along rivers and lakes. It has a creeping rhizome and produces small, showy blooms that attract pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in aquatic gardens.
