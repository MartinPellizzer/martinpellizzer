Justicia tubulosa subsp. tubulosa

Justicia tubulosa subsp.

tubulosa, commonly known as the trumpet flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.

The plant typically grows as an herbaceous annual or perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
