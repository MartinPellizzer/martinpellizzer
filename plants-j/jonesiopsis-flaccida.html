Jonesiopsis flaccida

It is a hemiparasitic plant, meaning it derives some of its nutrients from the roots of other plants while also performing photosynthesis. Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in shaded, moist forest understories. The plant features small, pale green leaves and produces delicate, white or pale yellow flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
