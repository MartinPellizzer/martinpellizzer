Juncus bufonius var. pumilus

Juncus bufonius var. pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf bog rush, is a small, herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in bogs, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often brown or reddish in color. It is a perennial species that spreads via rhizomes, forming dense mats in its habitat.

Due to its preference for wet conditions, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
