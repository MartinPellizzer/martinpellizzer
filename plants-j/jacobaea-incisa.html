Jacobaea incisa

Jacobaea incisa, commonly known as the narrowleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragweed.

Jacobaea incisa is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
