Jasminum cuspidatum

Jasminum cuspidatum, commonly known as the Florida jasmine, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features fragrant, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, oval leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its pleasant fragrance and ability to attract pollinators.
