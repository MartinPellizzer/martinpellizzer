Juncus bracteosus

Juncus bracteosus, commonly known as the bracted rush, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of North America.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and tall, slender flowering stems. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a distinctive, spike-like inflorescence.

Juncus bracteosus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
