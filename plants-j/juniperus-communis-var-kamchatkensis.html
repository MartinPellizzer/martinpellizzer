Juniperus communis var. kamchatkensis

Juniperus communis var. kamchatkensis, commonly known as the Kamchatka juniper, is a hardy evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Russian Far East and parts of Alaska.

It features needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss in its cold, arid habitats. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This variety is notable for its resilience to harsh climates and its role in stabilizing soil in mountainous regions.
