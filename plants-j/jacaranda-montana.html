Jacaranda montana

Jacaranda montana, commonly known as the blue jacaranda, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and a rounded crown. The tree produces clusters of striking blue-violet flowers during the summer months, which are followed by long, bean-like pods. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Jacaranda montana thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental tree in warm climates.
