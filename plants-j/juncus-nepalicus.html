Juncus nepalicus

Juncus nepalicus, commonly known as the Nepalese rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and stream banks. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish-brown in color, and it produces small, spike-like clusters of greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and they are adapted to withstand cold and windy conditions.

Juncus nepalicus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native environment.
