Justicia albovelata

Justicia albovelata, commonly known as white velvety justicia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of small, white flowers with a distinctive violet or purple calyx. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

The flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
