Justicia leonardii

Justicia leonardii, commonly known as Leonard's flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warmer climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant blooms and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds and butterflies.
