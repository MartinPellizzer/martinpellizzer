Justicia papilionacea

Justicia papilionacea, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling a mist or haze. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Justicia papilionacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
