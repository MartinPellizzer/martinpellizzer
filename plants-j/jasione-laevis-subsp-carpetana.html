Jasione laevis subsp. carpetana

Jasione laevis subsp.

carpetana, commonly known as the carpet bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features slender stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are small, bell-shaped, and usually pale blue or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in wildflower gardens and naturalized areas.
