Juncus auritus

Juncus auritus, commonly known as the ear-tipped rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland and marshy areas in North America.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that are typically reddish-brown in color and covered with small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers at the tips of its stems, which bloom in late summer. Juncus auritus is adapted to moist environments and can thrive in a variety of soil types as long as there is adequate water.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
