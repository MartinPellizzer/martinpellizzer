Juglans mandshurica var. sachalinensis

Juglans mandshurica var. sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin walnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Russian Far East and northeastern China.

It belongs to the walnut family, Juglandaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and edible, oily seeds. The tree produces small, green, spherical fruits that mature to a dark brown color. Its wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for carpentry and furniture-making.

This species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
