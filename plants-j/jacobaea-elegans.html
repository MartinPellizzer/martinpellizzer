Jacobaea elegans

Jacobaea elegans, commonly known as the elegant ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has a basal rosette of leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs.

Jacobaea elegans is also known for its toxic properties, containing pyrrolizidine alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
