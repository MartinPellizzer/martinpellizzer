Justicia matudae

Justicia matudae, commonly known as the Matudae false heather, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of bright red or orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The leaves are usually opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
