Juniperus conferta

Juniperus conferta, commonly known as the dense juniper, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Juniperus conferta thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous or rocky habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
