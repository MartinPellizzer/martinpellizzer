Jurinea darvasica

Jurinea darvasica, commonly known as the Darvasian sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its silver-gray foliage and striking blue-violet flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense clusters at the ends of its stems.

Jurinea darvasica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
