Jovibarba hirta f. neilreichii

Jovibarba hirta f. neilreichii, commonly known as the Neilreich's hirta saxifrage, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Saxifragaceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit, with thick, fleshy leaves that store water. The plant produces small, white flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its resilience and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid climates.
