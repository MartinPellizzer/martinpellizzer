Jacquemontia parviflora var. tomentosa

Jacquemontia parviflora var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly false nettle, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are often covered in a dense, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant thrives in moist, woodland environments.

It is often found in shaded areas and is characterized by its distinctive, woolly appearance.
