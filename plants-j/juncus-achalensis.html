Juncus achalensis

Juncus achalensis, commonly known as the Achalensis rush, is a perennial plant native to the arid regions of Pakistan and Afghanistan.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems that grow close to the ground. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its unique ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists in arid ecosystems.
