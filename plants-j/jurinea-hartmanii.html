Jurinea hartmanii

Jurinea hartmanii, commonly known as Hartman's sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has silver-gray foliage with a fuzzy texture, adapted to the harsh coastal environment. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant.

Jurinea hartmanii plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
