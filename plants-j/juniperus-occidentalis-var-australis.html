Juniperus occidentalis var. australis

Juniperus occidentalis var. australis, commonly known as the coastal juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the western coast of North America.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves and small, blue-black berries that are often used in herbal remedies. This variety thrives in coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils. Its aromatic foliage provides habitat for various wildlife species.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh maritime conditions.
