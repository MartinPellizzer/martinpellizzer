Jambolana

Jambolana, commonly known as the Java apple or Indian date, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It produces edible fruits that are rich in nutrients and have a sweet, honey-like flavor. The plant belongs to the Lychee family (Sapindaceae) and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and typically white or pink, while the fruit has a thick, leathery rind and a juicy, seed-filled interior.

Jambolana is often cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value, thriving in tropical and subtropical climates.
