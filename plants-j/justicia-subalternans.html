Justicia subalternans

Justicia subalternans, commonly known as the redstem false dragonhead, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of tubular, red to purple flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
