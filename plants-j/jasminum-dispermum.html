Jasminum dispermum

Jasminum dispermum, commonly known as the two-seeded jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release two seeds, hence its scientific name.
