Justicia punduana

Justicia punduana, commonly known as the red-flowered justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant typically grows as an herb or shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The genus Justicia is part of the Acanthaceae family, known for its showy flowers and medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
