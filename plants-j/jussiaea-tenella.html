Jussiaea tenella

Jussiaea tenella, commonly known as the narrowleaf waterplantain, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that grow from a rhizomatous stem, allowing it to spread vegetatively. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Jussiaea tenella plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
