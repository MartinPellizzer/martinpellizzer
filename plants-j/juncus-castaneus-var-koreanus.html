Juncus castaneus var. koreanus

Juncus castaneus var. koreanus, commonly known as the Korean rush, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and swampy areas. The plant has slender, grass-like stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and cylindrical, and it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

This variety is closely related to other members of the Juncus genus, sharing similar botanical features.
