Juncus pseudokrameri

Juncus pseudokrameri, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 1 millimeter wide. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Juncus pseudokrameri plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
