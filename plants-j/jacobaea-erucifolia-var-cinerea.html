Jacobaea erucifolia var. cinerea

Jacobaea erucifolia var. cinerea, commonly known as the grayleaf ragwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features gray-green, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a bluish tinge. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is noted for its toxic properties, containing pyrrolizidine alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
