Jagera madida

Jagera madida, commonly known as the blackwood tree, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark, aromatic timber. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a glossy, waxy surface.

Jagera madida is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for both its ecological and commercial significance.
