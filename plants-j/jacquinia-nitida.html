Jacquinia nitida

Jacquinia nitida, commonly known as the silverleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in late spring, followed by black, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
