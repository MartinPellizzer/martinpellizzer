Juergensia anguifera

Juergensia anguifera, commonly known as the snakevine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its long, serpentine stems that can reach several meters in length. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating, while the flowers are small and clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous tiny seeds.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
