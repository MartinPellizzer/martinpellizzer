Juncus sylvaticus var. confertus

Juncus sylvaticus var. confertus, commonly known as the dense rush, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, which are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along streambanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
