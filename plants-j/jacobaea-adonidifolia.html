Jacobaea adonidifolia

Jacobaea adonidifolia, commonly known as the alpine groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Jacobaea adonidifolia is often found in high-altitude environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
