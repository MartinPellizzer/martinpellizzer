Justicia distincta

Justicia distincta, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, blue-violet bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its showy inflorescences attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.

The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.
