Justicia mariae

Justicia mariae, commonly known as the Marie's glorybower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant typically features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
