Justicia amieuensis

Justicia amieuensis, commonly known as the Mexican flamebush, is a shrub native to Central America and northern South America.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often arranged in clusters at the ends of branches. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The genus Justicia is known for its diverse species, many of which are valued for their ornamental qualities and ability to attract pollinators.
