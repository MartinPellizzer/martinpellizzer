Justicia mcdowellii

Justicia mcdowellii, commonly known as the McDowell's flamebeard, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Justicia mcdowellii is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodland edges and wetlands.
