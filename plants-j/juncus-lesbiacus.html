Juncus lesbiacus

Juncus lesbiacus, commonly known as the Lesbia's rush, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in moist, saline soils along coastal areas and estuaries. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish flowers in dense spikes. It is adapted to withstand salt spray and tidal influences.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
