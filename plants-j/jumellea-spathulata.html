Jumellea spathulata

Jumellea spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaved jumellea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are often heart-shaped or spatulate in form. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Jumellea spathulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
