Juncus albescens

Juncus albescens, commonly known as the white rush, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems that often grow in dense clusters. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in clusters at the tips of the stems. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly in moist soils, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Juncus albescens plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
