Justicia campii

Justicia campii, commonly known as the Cape rose or red-hot poker, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in dense clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and drought tolerance.
