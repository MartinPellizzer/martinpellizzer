Juncus supinus subsp. fluitans

Juncus supinus subsp.

fluitans, commonly known as the floating rush, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems that float on the surface of water bodies.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Its roots help stabilize sediment and provide habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
