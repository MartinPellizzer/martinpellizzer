Jatropha guaranitica var. stipularis

Jatropha guaranitica var. stipularis, commonly known as the Brazilian physic nut, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with stipules. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by capsule fruits containing toxic seeds. It is often cultivated for its potential in biofuel production due to its high oil content.

However, it is important to note that all parts of the plant are poisonous.
