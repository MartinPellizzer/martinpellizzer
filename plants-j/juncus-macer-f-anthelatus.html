Juncus macer f. anthelatus

Juncus macer f. anthelatus, commonly known as the purple rush, is a variant of the common rush plant.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clumps of grass-like leaves that are typically dark green to purple in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, which are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, ditches, and along the edges of ponds.

This ornamental plant is often used in water gardens and wetland restoration projects due to its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
