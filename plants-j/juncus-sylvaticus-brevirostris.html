Juncus sylvaticus brevirostris

Juncus sylvaticus brevirostris, commonly known as the short-beaked rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland and marshy environments in Europe.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spike-like inflorescences. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense vegetation in moist soils.

Juncus sylvaticus brevirostris plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
