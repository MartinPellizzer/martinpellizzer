Juncus kern-reichgeltii

Juncus kern-reichgeltii, commonly known as Reichgelt's rush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikes of small, white or pale green flowers. This species typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. It has a dense, fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Reichgelt's rush is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in wetland environments.
