Jacquemontia densifolia var. involuta

Jacquemontia densifolia var. involuta, commonly known as the curled jacquemontia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces distinctive, curling leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
