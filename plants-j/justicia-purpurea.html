Justicia purpurea

Justicia purpurea, commonly known as the purpleleaf fanflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet to purple foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 3 feet. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a metallic sheen, while the flowers bloom in clusters at the tips of the stems.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant appearance.
