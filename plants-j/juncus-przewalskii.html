Juncus przewalskii

Juncus przewalskii, commonly known as the Przewalski's rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Juncus przewalskii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
