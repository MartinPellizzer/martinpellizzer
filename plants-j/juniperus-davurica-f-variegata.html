Juniperus davurica f. variegata

Juniperus davurica f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated savin juniper, is a unique variant of the savin juniper, characterized by its striking variegated foliage.

The leaves are typically bright yellow or white, contrasting with the dark green of the main foliage, creating a visually appealing appearance. It is a low-growing shrub that thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and full sun conditions. This plant is native to regions of eastern Asia, including parts of China and Siberia.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and as a landscape plant in suitable climates.
