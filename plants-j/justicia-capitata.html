Justicia capitata

Justicia capitata, commonly known as the purple crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers that form dense, showy clusters at the top of the plant. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially sunny environments.

Justicia capitata is cultivated for its ornamental value and is popular in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
