Juncus longiflorus

Juncus longiflorus, commonly known as the long-flower rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often used in floral arrangements due to their elegant appearance. Its flowering spikes produce small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer.

Juncus longiflorus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
