Jacobaea pulicariiformis

Jacobaea pulicariiformis, commonly known as the meadow cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Jacobaea pulicariiformis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, daisy-like blooms.
