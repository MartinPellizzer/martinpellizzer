Jacaranda obtusifolia var. rhombifolia

Jacaranda obtusifolia var. rhombifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf jacaranda, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces long, woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive addition to gardens and landscapes.
