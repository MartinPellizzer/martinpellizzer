Justicia paspaloides var. mandrakensis

Justicia paspaloides var. mandrakensis, commonly known as the Cape grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features erect, slender stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, often branching near the base. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, typically in shades of pink or purple, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.
