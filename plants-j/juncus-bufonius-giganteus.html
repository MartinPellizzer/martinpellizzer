Juncus bufonius giganteus

Juncus bufonius giganteus, commonly known as the giant marsh rush, is a tall, grass-like plant native to wetland environments.

It features narrow, cylindrical leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of brownish flowers. The plant is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various insects and birds.
