Justicia paruana

Justicia paruana, commonly known as the flame flower or red flame plant, is a striking tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often reddish in color. Justicia paruana thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
