Juno pseudocapnoides

Juno pseudocapnoides, commonly known as the slender juniper, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and produces blue-gray berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to cold, dry environments. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.

Juno pseudocapnoides is also valued in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
