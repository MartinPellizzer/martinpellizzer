Jatropha lacertii

Jatropha lacertii, commonly known as the desert croton, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces yellowish or greenish flowers, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Jatropha lacertii is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in harsh environmental conditions.

Its ability to survive in drought-prone areas makes it an important component of desert ecosystems.
