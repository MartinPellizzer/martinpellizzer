Jatropha spinosissima

Jatropha spinosissima, commonly known as the spiny jatropha, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and spiny leaves, which provide a natural defense against herbivores. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange-red berries containing toxic seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ornamental gardening despite its toxic properties.
