Justicia lythroides

Justicia lythroides, commonly known as the silverleaf justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
