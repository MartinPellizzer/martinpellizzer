Justicia strobilacea

Justicia strobilacea, commonly known as the candle bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces distinctive, spike-like inflorescences that resemble candles, giving it its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Justicia strobilacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
