Justicia campechiana subsp. vestita

Justicia campechiana subsp.

vestita, commonly known as the velvetleaf flame flower, is a striking tropical plant native to Central America. It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting hummingbirds and other pollinators.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence, giving it its subspecies name "vestita," meaning "cloaked" in Latin. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This ornamental plant is popular in gardens for its showy blooms and ecological benefits.
