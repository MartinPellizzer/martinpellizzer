Juncus tenageia var. brunneus

Juncus tenageia var. brunneus, commonly known as the brown rush, is a variant of the rush plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its stiff, grass-like leaves that are typically brown to reddish in color. This plant thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found along riverbanks and in swampy areas. Its cylindrical stems are usually clustered and can grow up to several feet in height.

The brown rush is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various organisms.
