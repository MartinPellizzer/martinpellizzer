Juncus monostichus

Juncus monostichus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually arranged in a single row, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikes and bloom in the early spring.

Juncus monostichus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
