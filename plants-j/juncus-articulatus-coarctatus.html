Juncus articulatus coarctatus

Juncus articulatus coarctatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and is typically found in wet or marshy environments. The plant has a distinctive jointed stem and produces small, spike-like clusters of white or greenish flowers. It thrives in temperate regions and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
