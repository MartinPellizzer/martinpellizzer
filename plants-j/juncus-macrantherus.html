Juncus macrantherus

Juncus macrantherus, commonly known as the tall rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, covered in narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes, which are typically arranged in a loose panicle. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in aquatic habitats.

Juncus macrantherus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
