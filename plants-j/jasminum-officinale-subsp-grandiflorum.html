Jasminum officinale subsp. grandiflorum

Jasminum officinale subsp.

grandiflorum, commonly known as the common jasmine, is a flowering plant native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its fragrant, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

The plant is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and for the production of essential oils used in perfumes and traditional medicine.
