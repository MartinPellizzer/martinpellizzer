Jussiaea racemosa

Jussiaea racemosa, commonly known as the marsh willow or water willow, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland and aquatic environments in South America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense stands along riverbanks and lakeshores. The plant features slender, branching stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom during the wet season, contributing to its ecological role in supporting aquatic life.

Jussiaea racemosa is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality in its habitat.
