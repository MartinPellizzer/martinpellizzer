Jasminum rarum

Jasminum rarum, commonly known as the rare jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has slender, woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are dark green in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and delicate nature, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized nurseries.
