Justicia blumeana

Justicia blumeana, commonly known as the Blue Trumpet, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and can grow as a shrub or small tree. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are typically pollinated by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance.
