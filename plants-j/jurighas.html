Jurighas

Jurighas, commonly known as the "ghost plant," is a unique aquatic plant that thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves that resemble ghostly apparitions, giving it its mysterious name. The plant lacks true roots and instead anchors itself using rhizoids, which absorb nutrients from the surrounding water. Jurighas is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds, where it contributes to the ecological balance by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.

Its botanical significance lies in its ability to indicate water quality, as it only grows in clean, oxygen-rich environments.
