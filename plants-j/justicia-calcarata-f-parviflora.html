Justicia calcarata f. parviflora

Justicia calcarata f. parviflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flameflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of tubular, brightly colored flowers. The flowers typically range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
