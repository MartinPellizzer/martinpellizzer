Jacaranda mutabilis f. integra

Jacaranda mutabilis f. integra, commonly known as the Mexican jacaranda, is a striking ornamental tree native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, pendulous clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in spring and summer. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and its bark is smooth and greyish in appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for tropical and subtropical gardens.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and ability to provide dense shade, though it can be somewhat invasive in certain regions.
