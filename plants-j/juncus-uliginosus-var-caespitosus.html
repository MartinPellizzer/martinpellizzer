Juncus uliginosus var. caespitosus

Juncus uliginosus var. caespitosus, commonly known as swamp rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments.

It features dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes, which are often hidden among the foliage. It thrives in moist to wet soils, making it a common sight in marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
