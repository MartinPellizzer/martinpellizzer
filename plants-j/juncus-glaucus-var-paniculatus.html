Juncus glaucus var. paniculatus

Juncus glaucus var. paniculatus, commonly known as the panicled rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, cylindrical spikes of small, pale green to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence is composed of numerous spikelets arranged in a panicle, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
