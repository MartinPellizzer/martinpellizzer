Justicia longepetiolata

Justicia longepetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled flameflower, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with long petioles, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, attracting hummingbirds and other pollinators. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
