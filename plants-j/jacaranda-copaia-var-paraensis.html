Jacaranda copaia var. paraensis

Jacaranda copaia var. paraensis, commonly known as the Paraense Jacaranda, is a deciduous tree native to the Amazon rainforest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy and smooth, grey bark. The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in local medicine.
