Jatropha spinosa var. crenulata

Jatropha spinosa var. crenulata, commonly known as the warty spurge, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features thick, waxy leaves with a distinctive warty texture and serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by orange-red berries containing toxic seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.

Its latex contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans.
