Jacobaea auriculata

Jacobaea auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green, hairy leaves that have auriculate (ear-like) lobes at the base. The plant produces yellow flowers in elongated, branched inflorescences, which bloom from late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Jacobaea auriculata is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
