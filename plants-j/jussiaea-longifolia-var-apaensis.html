Jussiaea longifolia var. apaensis

Jussiaea longifolia var. apaensis, commonly known as the Apaensis water plant, is a semi-aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the early morning, adding a delicate beauty to its aquatic habitat. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for ornamental water gardens and wetland restoration projects.
