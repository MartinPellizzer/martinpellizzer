Jatropha gossypiifolia f. transiens

Jatropha gossypiifolia f. transiens, commonly known as the wandering sow-thistle, is a variant of the common jatropha plant.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils. The plant features alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive white, milky sap. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces orange to red berries containing toxic seeds.

This species is often found in temperate regions and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
