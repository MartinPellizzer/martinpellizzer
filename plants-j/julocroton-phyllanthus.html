Julocroton phyllanthus

Julocroton phyllanthus, commonly known as the African croton, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional purposes.
