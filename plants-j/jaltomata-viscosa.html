Jaltomata viscosa

Jaltomata viscosa, commonly known as stickyleaf or stickyleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a sticky texture. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Jaltomata viscosa is a perennial species with a relatively short flowering period in the spring.
