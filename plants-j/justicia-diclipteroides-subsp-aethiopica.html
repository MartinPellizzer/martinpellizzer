Justicia diclipteroides subsp. aethiopica

Justicia diclipteroides subsp.

aethiopica, commonly known as the Ethiopian flame bush, is a shrub native to tropical Africa. It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a reddish or purple hue.

The plant produces clusters of tubular, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which attract pollinators like birds and insects. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
