Jobinia umbellata

Jobinia umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, umbrella-like arrangement of leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its leaves are palmately lobed and can grow quite large, making it a striking ornamental plant.

Jobinia umbellata is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
