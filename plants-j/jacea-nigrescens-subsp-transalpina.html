Jacea nigrescens subsp. transalpina

Jacea nigrescens subsp.

transalpina, commonly known as the Transalpine bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
