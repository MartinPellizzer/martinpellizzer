Justicia rhodantha

Justicia rhodantha, commonly known as the red flame plant, is a flowering shrub native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking red to orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

The flowers attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees, making it a valuable addition to gardens.
