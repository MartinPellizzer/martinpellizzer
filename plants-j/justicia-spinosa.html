Justicia spinosa

Justicia spinosa, commonly known as the spiked jewelweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking orange or red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shady areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

Justicia spinosa is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
