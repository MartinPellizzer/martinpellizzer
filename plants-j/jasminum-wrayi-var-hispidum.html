Jasminum wrayi var. hispidum

Jasminum wrayi var. hispidum, commonly known as the hispid jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically ovate and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant a distinctive texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant's medicinal properties have also been studied for their potential therapeutic uses.
