Juncus fontanesii subsp. pyramidatus

Juncus fontanesii subsp.

pyramidatus, commonly known as the pyramidal rush, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that form dense, upright clumps.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, growing in a tight spiral around the stem.

It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along stream banks.
