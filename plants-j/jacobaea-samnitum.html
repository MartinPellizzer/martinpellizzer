Jacobaea samnitum

Jacobaea samnitum, commonly known as the Italian tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are aromatic and have a bitter scent, while its stems are covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
