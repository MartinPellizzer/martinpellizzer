Juncus glaucoturgidus

Juncus glaucoturgidus, commonly known as the blue-turkey rush, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and spikes of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. It has fibrous roots and produces numerous small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into capsule fruits.

Juncus glaucoturgidus is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
