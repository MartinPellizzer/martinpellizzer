Jamesbrittenia aurantiaca

Jamesbrittenia aurantiaca, commonly known as the goldenbloom orchid, is a striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange and yellow flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its botanical features include lance-shaped leaves and a compact, pseudobulbous structure that supports its growth.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
