Jacquemontia paniculata var. lanceolata

Jacquemontia paniculata var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf yellowjacket, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that are clustered in panicles, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its growth habit is low-growing, with stems that often lie prostrate on the ground.

The species is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
