Juncus macrostigma

Juncus macrostigma, commonly known as the broad-leaved rush, is a perennial plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, often with a reddish tinge. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers arranged in spike-like clusters at the top of the stems. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a waxy coating to help repel water.

Juncus macrostigma plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
