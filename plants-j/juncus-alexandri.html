Juncus alexandri

Juncus alexandri, commonly known as Alexandrian rush, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as pond edges and stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish flowers in dense spikes. It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
