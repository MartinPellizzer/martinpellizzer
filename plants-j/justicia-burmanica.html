Justicia burmanica

Justicia burmanica, commonly known as Burman's glorybower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Justicia burmanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
