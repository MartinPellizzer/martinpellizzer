Justicia rusbyi

Justicia rusbyi, commonly known as the Rusby's flameflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually bright red or orange, clustered in terminal spikes, and bloom during the summer months.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
