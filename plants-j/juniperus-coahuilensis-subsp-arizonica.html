Juniperus coahuilensis subsp. arizonica

Juniperus coahuilensis subsp.

arizonica, commonly known as the Arizona juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which consists of scale-like leaves arranged in opposite pairs.

The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are a food source for various birds and small mammals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and limestone areas.

The subspecies is notable for its adaptability to harsh desert conditions and its role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat.
