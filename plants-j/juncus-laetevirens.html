Juncus laetevirens

Juncus laetevirens, commonly known as the cheerful green rush, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers in early summer, which are arranged in spike-like inflorescences.

Juncus laetevirens is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
