Jarava pulchra

Jarava pulchra, commonly known as the showy jarava, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces tiny, white to pale blue flowers that are typically arranged in panicle-like structures. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Jarava pulchra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
