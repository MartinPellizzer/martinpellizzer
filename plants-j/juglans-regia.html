Juglans regia

Juglans regia, commonly known as the Persian walnut, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 20–40 cm long and have 15–23 leaflets. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by oval, green husks that enclose the edible nuts. The nuts are encased in a hard shell and are highly valued for their rich flavor and nutritional content.

It thrives in temperate climates and is widely cultivated for both ornamental and commercial purposes.
