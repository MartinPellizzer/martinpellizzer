Justicia suaveolens

Justicia suaveolens, commonly known as sweet justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The genus Justicia is part of the Acanthaceae family, known for its ornamental and sometimes medicinal uses.
