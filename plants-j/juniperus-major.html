Juniperus major

Juniperus major, commonly known as the common juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces red berries that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in woodland edges and rocky habitats.

Juniperus major is also valued for its aromatic properties and historical use in traditional medicine.
