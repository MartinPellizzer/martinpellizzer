Juncus transsilvanicus

Juncus transsilvanicus, commonly known as the Transylvanian rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae and is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and damp woodlands. Its flowering spikes consist of small, greenish-white flowers that appear in late summer.

Juncus transsilvanicus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
