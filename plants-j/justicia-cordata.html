Justicia cordata

Justicia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf jewelweed, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, orange-red tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in humid climates.

The flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
