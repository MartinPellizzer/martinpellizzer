Jacaranda obovata

Jacaranda obovata, commonly known as the broad-leaf jacaranda, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in the spring. The leaves are large, oblong, and have a glossy green surface. The tree produces long, woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to warm, sunny climates.
