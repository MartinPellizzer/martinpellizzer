Juncus bulbosus var. soranthus

Juncus bulbosus var. soranthus, commonly known as the bulbous rush, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as pond edges and damp meadows. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, spike-like clusters of greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is characterized by its bulbous base, which stores nutrients.

It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
