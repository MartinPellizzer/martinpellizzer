Justicia riviniifolia

Justicia riviniifolia, commonly known as the pink shower vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of showy, tubular pink or purple flowers. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are often covered in fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets or trellises.

The flowers attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees, contributing to its ecological significance.
