Jacobaeastrum oligoglossum

Jacobaeastrum oligoglossum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features basal leaves that are narrow and often hairy, with a distinctive yellow flower head composed of small, daisy-like florets. It produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower structures and medicinal uses in some traditional practices.
