Justicia wilhelminensis

Justicia wilhelminensis, commonly known as the Wilhelmina's glorybower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in terminal inflorescences.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
