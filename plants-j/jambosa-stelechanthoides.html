Jambosa stelechanthoides

Jambosa stelechanthoides, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, including Nigeria and Sudan.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with a dense, rounded canopy. The plant has smooth, grayish-brown bark and produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. Its fruit is a round, yellowish-green berry that turns red when ripe, containing a sweet, juicy pulp with a mild, acidic flavor.

The plant is valued for its edible fruit and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
