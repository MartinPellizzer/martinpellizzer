Juncoides divaricata

Juncoides divaricata, commonly known as the variable rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae, which includes various species of rushes and willows. The plant typically grows in dense clusters, with tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer.

Juncoides divaricata plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
