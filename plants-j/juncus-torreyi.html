Juncus torreyi

Juncus torreyi, commonly known as Torrey's rush, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like clusters of narrow, cylindrical leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its flowers are small, greenish-brown, and clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

Torrey's rush plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
