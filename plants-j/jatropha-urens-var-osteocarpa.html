Jatropha urens var. osteocarpa

Jatropha urens var. osteocarpa, commonly known as the bone plant or sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often near the shoreline. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, woody, capsule-like fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered with fine hairs.

The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in saline or arid conditions.
