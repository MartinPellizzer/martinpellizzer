Juncus arcticus var. andicola

Juncus arcticus var. andicola, commonly known as the Andean rush, is a perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is often found in mountainous areas with limited nutrients. Its flowering stems produce small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in the summer months.

This variant is notable for its resilience to harsh climatic conditions and its ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
