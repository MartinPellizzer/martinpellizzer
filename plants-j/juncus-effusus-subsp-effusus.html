Juncus effusus subsp. effusus

Juncus effusus subsp.

effusus, commonly known as soft rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, pond margins, and stream banks.

The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often slightly hairy and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and tapering, with a fibrous texture.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.
