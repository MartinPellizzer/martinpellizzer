Jeanneretia littoralis

Jeanneretia littoralis, commonly known as the coastal sedge, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet, saline environments such as salt marshes and tidal flats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spikes. It plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.

Its adaptability to harsh coastal conditions makes it a significant component of the local flora.
