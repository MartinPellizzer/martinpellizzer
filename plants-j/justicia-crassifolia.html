Justicia crassifolia

Justicia crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf flameflower, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting yellow or white throat. The plant has opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are usually glossy and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Justicia crassifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
