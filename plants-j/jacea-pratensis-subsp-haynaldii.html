Jacea pratensis subsp. haynaldii

Jacea pratensis subsp.

haynaldii, commonly known as the haynald's meadow gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, thriving in nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain invertebrates.
