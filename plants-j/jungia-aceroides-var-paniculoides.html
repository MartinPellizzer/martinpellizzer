Jungia aceroides var. paniculoides

Jungia aceroides var. paniculoides, commonly known as the paniculate jungle acer, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicle-like inflorescences, which bloom during the rainy season. Its bark and leaves contain bioactive compounds that have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
