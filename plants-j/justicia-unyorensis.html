Justicia unyorensis

Justicia unyorensis, commonly known as the New Guinea flame or the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are clustered in racemes and are often visited by birds and insects, making it an important plant in pollination networks.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
