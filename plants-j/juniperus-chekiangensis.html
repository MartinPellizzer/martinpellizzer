Juniperus chekiangensis

Juniperus chekiangensis, commonly known as the Chinese juniper, is a coniferous evergreen shrub or tree native to China.

It is characterized by its scale-like leaves and small, berry-like cones that are typically blue or red in color. The plant is highly valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. Juniperus chekiangensis is also known for its slow growth rate and long lifespan, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

Its foliage provides year-round visual interest, and it is often used in rock gardens or as a specimen plant in gardens with well-drained soil.
