Juncus gerardi maximus

Juncus gerardi maximus, commonly known as the tall rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically green to reddish in color, growing in dense clumps. The plant produces tall, cylindrical spikes of small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife, it plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
