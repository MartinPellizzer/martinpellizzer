Juniperus communis f. aureovariegata

Juniperus communis f. aureovariegata, commonly known as the golden variegated juniper, is a compact, evergreen shrub characterized by its distinctive foliage.

Its leaves are narrow, needle-like, and exhibit a striking variegation of yellow and green, making it visually appealing. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a decorative hedge.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and hardiness in temperate climates.
