Juncus tingitanus

Juncus tingitanus, commonly known as the Moroccan rush, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often near freshwater sources. Its narrow leaves are usually green to reddish in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Juncus tingitanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
