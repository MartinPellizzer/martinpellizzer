Juniperus deppeana var. sperryi

Juniperus deppeana var. sperryi, commonly known as the Sperry's juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces cone-like structures that contain aromatic, berry-like fruits, which are a source of food for wildlife. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils and in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
