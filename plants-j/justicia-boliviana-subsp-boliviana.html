Justicia boliviana subsp.

boliviana, commonly known as Bolivian flameflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Bolivia. It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters.

It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical and subtropical gardens.
