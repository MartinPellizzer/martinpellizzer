Jacobaea racemosa

Jacobaea racemosa, commonly known as the common tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense, elongated clusters called racemes.

Jacobaea racemosa is known for its aromatic properties and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
