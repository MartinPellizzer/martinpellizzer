Juncus capensis subsp. delicatulus

Juncus capensis subsp.

delicatulus, commonly known as the Cape rush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist to waterlogged soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces showy, greenish-white flowers during the summer months.

This subspecies is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various wetland conditions.
