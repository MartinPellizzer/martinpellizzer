Juniperus formosana var. sinica

Juniperus formosana var. sinica, commonly known as the Taiwan juniper, is a species of juniper native to China and Taiwan.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in mountainous regions with acidic soils. The plant produces small, scale-like leaves and has distinctive blue-gray foliage. It bears cone-like structures that contain aromatic berries, which are used in traditional medicine.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
