Jamesonia imbricata

Jamesonia imbricata, commonly known as the mountain snowbell, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a rhizomatous root system and typically grows in moist, rocky habitats. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The botanical features of Jamesonia imbricata make it a distinctive and ecologically important plant in its native range.
