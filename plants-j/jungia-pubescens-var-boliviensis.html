Jungia pubescens var. boliviensis

Jungia pubescens var. boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and is often found in shaded areas within the undergrowth of forests. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.
