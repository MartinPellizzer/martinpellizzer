Jamesonia sellowiana

Jamesonia sellowiana, commonly known as the Sellow's bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements contribute to its appeal among gardeners.
