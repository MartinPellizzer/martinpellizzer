Juncus × nodosiformis

Juncus × nodosiformis, commonly known as the nodding rush, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and bear dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, pond edges, and stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, making it a common wetland plant.

The flowers are usually greenish or brownish and are pollinated by wind, contributing to its role in stabilizing soil in aquatic habitats.
