Junellia rosulata f. alba

Junellia rosulata f. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered junellia, is a botanical variant of the species Junellia rosulata.

It is characterized by its striking white petals, which contrast with its green foliage. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodland areas or stream sides. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
