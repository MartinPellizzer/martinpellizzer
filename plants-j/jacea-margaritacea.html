Jacea margaritacea

Jacea margaritacea, commonly known as the pearlweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its unique foliage and resilience.
