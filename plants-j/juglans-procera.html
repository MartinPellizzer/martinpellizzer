Juglans procera

Juglans procera, commonly known as the California black walnut, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, doubly serrated leaves and distinctive, ridged bark. The tree produces large, green, spherical fruits that contain hard, edible nuts enclosed in a thick husk. Its seeds are a valuable food source for various wildlife species.

The plant has significant ecological and economic importance due to its timber and nut production.
