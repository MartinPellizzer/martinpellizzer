Jatropha podagrica

Jatropha podagrica, commonly known as the blistering spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, green, spherical fruits that turn red when mature. It contains toxic compounds, including latex and alkaloids, which can cause skin irritation and other health issues.

Jatropha podagrica is often found in disturbed habitats and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
