Jacobaea vulgaris subsp. pannonica

Jacobaea vulgaris subsp.

pannonica, commonly known as the Pannonian tansy, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish-green leaves with a strong, camphor-like scent.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Its chemical composition includes compounds such as thujone and camphor, which contribute to its medicinal and pesticidal properties.
