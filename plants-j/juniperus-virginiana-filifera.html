Juniperus virginiana filifera

Juniperus virginiana filifera, commonly known as the eastern redcedar, is a deciduous conifer native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its scale-like leaves and produces small, berry-like cones that are toxic to livestock. The plant has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and can thrive in a variety of soil types. It is often used in landscaping for its dense growth habit and ornamental value.

The tree also provides habitat and food for various wildlife species.
