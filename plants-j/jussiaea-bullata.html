Jussiaea bullata

Jussiaea bullata, commonly known as the broadleaf waterplantain, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. The plant has broad, waxy leaves that are often floating or semi-submerged, with a distinctive ribbed structure. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in spikes, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Jussiaea bullata plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
