Juncus loureiroana

Juncus loureiroana, commonly known as the Loureiro's rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and tall, cylindrical spikes of small, inconspicuous flowers. Native to southern Africa, it thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes and stream banks. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into three-winged berries.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
