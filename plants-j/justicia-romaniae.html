Justicia romaniae

Justicia romaniae, commonly known as the Romanian flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular, brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

The flowers, which bloom in summer, are borne in spikes and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
