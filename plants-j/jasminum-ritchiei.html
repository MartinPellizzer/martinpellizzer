Jasminum ritchiei

Jasminum ritchiei, commonly known as the Mexican jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture. The plant produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Jasminum ritchiei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
