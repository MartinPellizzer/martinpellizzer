Juncus validus var. fascinatus

Juncus validus var. fascinatus, commonly known as the fasciated rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish or purple in color, and it produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically growing in dense tufts. This variety is characterized by its distinctive fasciated growth pattern, where the stems exhibit a striped or marbled appearance.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
