Justicia mucronata

Justicia mucronata, commonly known as the scarlet false arum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

The flowers are clustered in dense, spathaceous bracts, with bright red or orange petals that attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.
