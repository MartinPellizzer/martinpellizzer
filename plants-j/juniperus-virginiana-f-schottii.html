Juniperus virginiana f. schottii

Juniperus virginiana f. schottii, commonly known as the Schott's redcedar, is a naturally occurring variant of the eastern redcedar.

It is characterized by its dense, pyramidal growth habit and scale-like leaves that are typically reddish-brown in color. This form is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

It is widely distributed across the central and southern United States, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
