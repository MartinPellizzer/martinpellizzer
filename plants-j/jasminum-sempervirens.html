Jasminum sempervirens

Jasminum sempervirens, commonly known as the evergreen jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with dark green, leathery leaves that remain throughout the year. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

Jasminum sempervirens prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.
