Juniperus virginiana var. plumosa-argentea

Juniperus virginiana var. plumosa-argentea, commonly known as the silver fir juniper, is a variant of the eastern red cedar.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-blue foliage, which gives it a striking appearance in both cultivated and natural landscapes. The plant is a slow-growing evergreen with a conical shape, typical of many juniper species. Its leaves are scale-like and arranged in flattened sprays, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
