Juglans coreensis

Juglans coreensis, commonly known as the Chinese black walnut, is a deciduous tree native to eastern China.

It belongs to the walnut family, Juglandaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive bark. The tree produces edible nuts enclosed in a thick, green husk that splits open when mature. Its seeds are valued for their high oil content and are used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife and soil stabilization.
