Juncus siculus

Juncus siculus, commonly known as the Sicilian rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often found near freshwater bodies such as lakes, ponds, and wetlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually green to reddish in color, and it produces small, spike-like clusters of white or pale green flowers. Juncus siculus is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and filter water, making it an important species in wetland ecosystems.

It is also sometimes used in ornamental gardening due to its elegant appearance and adaptability.
