Juncus lampocarpus var. subobtusus

Juncus lampocarpus var. subobtusus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rush, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 1 millimeter wide. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and disturbed areas.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
