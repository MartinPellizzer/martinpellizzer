Jamesonia longipetiolata

Jamesonia longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled Jamesonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves with long petioles, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
