Jacea linifolia

Jacea linifolia, commonly known as the silky leaf jacea, is a perennial plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage that helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. Its leaves are elongated and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Jacea linifolia is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to survive with minimal water supply.
