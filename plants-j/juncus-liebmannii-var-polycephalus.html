Juncus liebmannii var. polycephalus

Juncus liebmannii var. polycephalus, commonly known as the clustered rush, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves that form a compact rosette. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in a spike at the top of the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation.
