Juncus nigricans

Juncus nigricans, commonly known as black rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland and marshy areas in North America.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that are often dark green to black in color, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of brownish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize soil in wetland environments.

Juncus nigricans is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to filter water and support diverse wildlife.
