Juncus sphenostemon

Juncus sphenostemon, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems that grow in dense clusters, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, which are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib, and it thrives in moist to wet soils.

Juncus sphenostemon plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
