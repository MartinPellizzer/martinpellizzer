Jasminum shimadae

Jasminum shimadae, commonly known as the Shimada jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
