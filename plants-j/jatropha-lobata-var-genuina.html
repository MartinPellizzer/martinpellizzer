Jatropha lobata var. genuina

Jatropha lobata var. genuina, commonly known as the Mexican physic nut, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright orange to red berries containing toxic seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

Due to its toxicity, it is important to handle the plant with care.
