Juncus dubius

Juncus dubius, commonly known as the slender rush, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and pond edges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually pale green to silver in color. Its inflorescence consists of dense, cylindrical clusters of small, spike-like flowers.

Juncus dubius is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
