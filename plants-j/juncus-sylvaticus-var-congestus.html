Juncus sylvaticus var. congestus

Juncus sylvaticus var. congestus, commonly known as the dense rush, is a variant of the common rush that exhibits dense, compact growth.

It is a perennial plant native to moist or wet habitats, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green or reddish in color, depending on the season. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or brown, which bloom in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
