Juncus atratus var. pauciflorus

Juncus atratus var. pauciflorus, commonly known as the black rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features narrow, dark green, cylindrical leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of hair. The plant produces small, dark purple to black flowers in dense, spike-like clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is a valuable species for wetland restoration projects.
