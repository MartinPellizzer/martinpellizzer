Jacaranda morii

Jacaranda morii, commonly known as the African jacaranda, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in pendulous clusters during the rainy season. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple small, feather-like leaflets. The tree produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
