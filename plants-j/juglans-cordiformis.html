Juglans cordiformis

Juglans cordiformis, commonly known as the heart-shaped walnut, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and have a distinctive silvery underside. The tree produces large, green, husk-covered nuts that mature in the fall. Its bark is thick, deeply furrowed, and often has a rough, scaly texture.

The plant is valued for its hardy nature and is often found in dry, rocky soils.
