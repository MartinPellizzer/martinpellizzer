Juncus brachycephalus

Juncus brachycephalus, commonly known as the dwarf rush, is a species of flowering plant in the family Juncaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form dense clumps, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color. Juncus brachycephalus is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and semi-aquatic ecosystems.
