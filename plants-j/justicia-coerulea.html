Justicia coerulea

Justicia coerulea, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
