Juncus inflexus proles longicornis

Juncus inflexus proles longicornis, commonly known as the long-horned rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that grow in dense clusters, often in wet or marshy environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically growing close to the ground.

This species is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils.
