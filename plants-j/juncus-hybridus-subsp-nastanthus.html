Juncus hybridus subsp. nastanthus

Juncus hybridus subsp.

nastanthus, commonly known as the nasturtium rush, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, streambanks, and damp meadows.

The plant features slender, grass-like stems that are often reddish or purple in color. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense spikes and bloom in late summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
