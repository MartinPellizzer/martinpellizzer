Juncus nikkoensis var. pinifolius

Juncus nikkoensis var. pinifolius, commonly known as the Japanese rush, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its foliage is often used in traditional Japanese floral arrangements due to its aesthetic appeal.

The species is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in humid climates.
