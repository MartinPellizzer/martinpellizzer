Jaumea alternifolia

Jaumea alternifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf waterplantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in southern Africa.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often submerged or floating, adapted to aquatic conditions. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in spring. Its root system is fibrous and helps stabilize mud and sediment in water bodies.

Jaumea alternifolia plays a role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for aquatic organisms and contributing to water filtration.
