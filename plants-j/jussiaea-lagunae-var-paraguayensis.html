Jussiaea lagunae var. paraguayensis

Jussiaea lagunae var. paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay water willow, is a semi-aquatic plant native to South America.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of slow-moving water bodies. The plant has slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating, aiding in photosynthesis in aquatic environments. Its flowering stems produce small, white or pale yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and insects.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
