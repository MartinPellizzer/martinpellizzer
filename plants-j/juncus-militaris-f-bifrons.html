Juncus militaris f. bifrons

Juncus militaris f. bifrons, commonly known as the two-branched rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves that form a compact, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, pond edges, and damp meadows.

This variety is distinguished by its bifurcated or branched appearance, which gives it its specific epithet.
