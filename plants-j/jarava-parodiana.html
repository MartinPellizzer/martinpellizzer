Jarava parodiana

Jarava parodiana, commonly known as the Parodi's jarava, is a perennial grass native to the arid regions of South America.

It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in coastal or inland desert environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that help it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
