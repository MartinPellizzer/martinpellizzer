Juncus hizenensis

Juncus hizenensis, commonly known as the Hizen rush, is a perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, showcasing its adaptability to moist environments. Its slender, cylindrical stems are often used in ornamental gardening due to their aesthetic appeal.

Juncus hizenensis is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
