Juncus decipiens var. glomeratus

Juncus decipiens var. glomeratus, commonly known as the clustered rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves that form a compact, mat-like structure. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are typically found in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in moist to water-saturated soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
