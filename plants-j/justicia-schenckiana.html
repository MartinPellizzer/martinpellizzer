Justicia schenckiana

Justicia schenckiana, commonly known as the Schenck's flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange-red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it typically grows in well-drained soils. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The genus Justicia is known for its showy inflorescences, and Schenckiana is no exception, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.
