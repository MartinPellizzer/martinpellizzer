Juniperus jaliscana

Juniperus jaliscana, commonly known as the Mexican juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which consists of scale-like leaves arranged in dense clusters. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. Juniperus jaliscana is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and exhibits drought tolerance.

It plays an important ecological role in its native environment, providing food and shelter for various species.
