Justicia dubiosa

Justicia dubiosa, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It features striking, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive purple or violet throat. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks or in forest understories. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
