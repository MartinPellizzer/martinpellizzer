Jungia pauciflora

Jungia pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered jungle plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America, particularly in Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and succulent-like leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

Jungia pauciflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
