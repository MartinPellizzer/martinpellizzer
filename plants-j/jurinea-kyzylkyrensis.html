Jurinea kyzylkyrensis

Jurinea kyzylkyrensis, commonly known as the Kyzylkyr Desertflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in extreme desert conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. It typically blooms in the summer months, producing clusters of greenish-white flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and fragile habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
