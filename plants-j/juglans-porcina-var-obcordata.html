Juglans porcina var. obcordata

Juglans porcina var. obcordata, commonly known as the obcordate hickory, is a variant of the Persian hickory.

It is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia, particularly China and the Himalayas. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and produces edible nuts enclosed in a thick, spiky husk. Its bark is deeply furrowed and has a distinctive grayish color.

This variant is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also cultivated for its nutritional nuts.
