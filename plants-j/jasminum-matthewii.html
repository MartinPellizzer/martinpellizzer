Jasminum matthewii

Jasminum matthewii, commonly known as the Matthew's jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are clustered in terminal panicles and are known for their strong, sweet fragrance.

Jasminum matthewii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
