Juncus gerardi var. attenuatus

Juncus gerardi var. attenuatus, commonly known as the slender rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage, which consists of narrow, cylindrical leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated, and it thrives in moist, poorly drained soils. It is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and streams.

This species plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
