Jasminum officinale var. acutum

Jasminum officinale var. acutum, commonly known as the acute jasmine, is a flowering plant native to southern China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, emitting a strong, sweet scent. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is closely related to the common jasmine, but it has distinct leaf and flower structures.
