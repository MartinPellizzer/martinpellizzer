Juncus pringlei

Juncus pringlei, commonly known as Pringle's rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-brown flowers that are typically clustered at the ends of its stems. Juncus pringlei thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and mountain meadows.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
