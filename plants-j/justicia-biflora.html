Justicia biflora

Justicia biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as an herb or shrub, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a glossy texture, while its inflorescence consists of paired flowers that bloom sequentially.

Justicia biflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
