Juncus lampocarpus subsp. nigritellus

Juncus lampocarpus subsp.

nigritellus, commonly known as the black-stemmed rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dark, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

This subspecies thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in late summer to early autumn.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
