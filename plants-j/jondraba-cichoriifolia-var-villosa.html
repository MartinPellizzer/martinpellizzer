Jondraba cichoriifolia var. villosa

Jondraba cichoriifolia var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy leaf jondraba, is a lesser-known plant with distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its hairy, serrated leaves that give it a unique texture and appearance. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and prefers well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and yellow, resembling those of the dandelion, and it blooms during the spring and early summer.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often found in specialized environments such as rocky outcrops or shaded forest floors.
