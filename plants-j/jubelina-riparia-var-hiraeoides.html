Jubelina riparia var. hiraeoides

Jubelina riparia var. hiraeoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in riparian zones. The plant has slender, often arching stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. Its flowers are catkin-like, appearing in early spring before the leaves fully emerge.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
