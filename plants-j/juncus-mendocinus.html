Juncus mendocinus

Juncus mendocinus, commonly known as the Mendocino rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to California.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually green and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. It produces small, clustered flower spikes that bloom in the spring.

Juncus mendocinus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland species.
