Juncus scheuchzerioides var. inconspicuus

Juncus scheuchzerioides var. inconspicuus, commonly known as the inconspicuous rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that are typically less than 1 meter in height, covered in fine, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, which are usually greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in riparian zones or along the edges of ponds.

This variety is closely related to other juncus species and is adapted to withstand periodic flooding.
