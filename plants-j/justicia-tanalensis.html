Justicia tanalensis

Justicia tanalensis, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that often resemble petals. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub or herb, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

The flowers are borne in terminal clusters and are usually pink to red in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
