Juniperus monosperma f. gymnocarpa

Juniperus monosperma f. gymnocarpa, commonly known as the smooth-seeded juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, spherical berries that are typically blue-black when mature. These berries are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in culinary applications.

Its adaptability to harsh environments makes it a resilient and ecologically significant species.
