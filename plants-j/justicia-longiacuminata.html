Justicia longiacuminata

Justicia longiacuminata, commonly known as the red-flowered justicia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine pubescence, while its stems are square and hairy.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
