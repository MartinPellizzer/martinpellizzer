Juniperus pinchotii var. erythrocarpa

Juniperus pinchotii var. erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its scale-like leaves and produces small, berry-like cones that are typically red when mature. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has a slow growth rate and is relatively drought-tolerant.

The red berries are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.
