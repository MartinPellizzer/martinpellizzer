Juncus dinteri

Juncus dinteri, commonly known as the Dinter's rush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and cylindrical flower spikes. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, three-lobed seeds.

Juncus dinteri is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
