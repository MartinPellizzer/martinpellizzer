Juncellus pumilus

Juncellus pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf rush, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has cylindrical, grass-like stems that are often clustered closely together. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters near the base of the plant.

Juncellus pumilus plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
