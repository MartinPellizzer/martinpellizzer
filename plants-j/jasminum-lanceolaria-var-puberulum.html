Jasminum lanceolaria var. puberulum

Jasminum lanceolaria var. puberulum, commonly known as the hairy jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are opposite, elliptical, and often covered with fine, soft hairs, giving it the "hairy" designation. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its pleasant scent.
