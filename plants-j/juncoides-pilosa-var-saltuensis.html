Juncoides pilosa var. saltuensis

Juncoides pilosa var. saltuensis, commonly known as the saltbrush junct, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in salt-affected areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its root system helps stabilize soil, making it valuable for erosion control.

The species is also of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to saline conditions.
