Juno vicaria

Juno vicaria, commonly known as the mountain juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its scale-like leaves and produces small, blue-gray berries that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Juno vicaria is valued for its hardiness and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental appeal.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
