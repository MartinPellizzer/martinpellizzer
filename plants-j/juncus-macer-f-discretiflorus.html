Juncus macer f. discretiflorus

Juncus macer f. discretiflorus, commonly known as the discreet-flowered rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Juncaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences, typically found at the tips of the stems. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, ditches, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
