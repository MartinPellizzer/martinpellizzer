Jasminum kwangense

Jasminum kwangense, commonly known as the Chinese jasmine, is a flowering plant native to southern China.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite, simple leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the spring and summer seasons. Its flowers are five-petaled and emit a strong, sweet scent, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and as a hedging plant.
