Justicia baenitzii

Justicia baenitzii, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that bear bright red bracts, which are often mistaken for petals. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or yellow, surrounded by the showy red bracts. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Justicia baenitzii is known for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
