Juglans nana

Juglans nana, commonly known as the dwarf walnut, is a small deciduous tree or shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its rounded, dense foliage and compound leaves with 15 to 23 leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by edible, wrinkled nuts enclosed in a thick husk. Juglans nana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.

Its growth habit and botanical features make it a unique addition to the Juglandaceae family.
