Jacobaea aquatica subsp. ornata

Jacobaea aquatica subsp.

ornata, commonly known as the water ragwort, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as ponds, ditches, and streambanks.

The plant features yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are usually alternate, with a rough, hairy texture and a strong, often bitter scent.

This subspecies is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some traditional uses.
