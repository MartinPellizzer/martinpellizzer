Justicia secundiflora

Justicia secundiflora, commonly known as desert mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Justicia secundiflora is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
