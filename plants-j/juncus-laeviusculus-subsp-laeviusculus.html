Juncus laeviusculus subsp. laeviusculus

Juncus laeviusculus subsp.

laeviusculus, commonly known as the slender rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, ditches, and along riverbanks.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually green to reddish in color. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, three-lobed flowers that are often pale green or brownish.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
