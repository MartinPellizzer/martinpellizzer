Jatropha tupifolia

Jatropha tupifolia, commonly known as the hairy jatropha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cluster of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain toxic seeds, a common trait among the genus Jatropha. Its leaves are typically hairy and lobed, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
