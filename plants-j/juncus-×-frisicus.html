Juncus × frisicus

Juncus × frisicus, commonly known as the marsh rush, is a hybrid plant species belonging to the family Juncaginaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes, often appearing in late summer. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Juncus × frisicus plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
