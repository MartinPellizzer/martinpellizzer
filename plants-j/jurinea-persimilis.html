Jurinea persimilis

Jurinea persimilis, commonly known as the Persian sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or white in color. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to store water in its native environment.

Jurinea persimilis is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is well-suited to harsh, dry climates.
