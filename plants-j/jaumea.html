Jaumea

Jaumea, commonly known as the sea holly, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky flower heads and succulent, often blue or purple, foliage. These plants are typically found in coastal areas and saline environments, showcasing their adaptability to harsh conditions. Jaumea species are often used in ornamental gardening due to their unique appearance and drought tolerance.

They play a role in stabilizing sandy soils and supporting local wildlife.
