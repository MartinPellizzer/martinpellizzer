Jacksonia racemosa

Jacksonia racemosa, commonly known as the New Zealand flax or kōwhai, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flower heads that resemble dandelions. The plant has strap-shaped, fibrous leaves that are often used traditionally by Māori for weaving and other crafts. Jacksonia racemosa typically grows in coastal and forested areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

It plays an important ecological role in supporting native pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its habitat.
