Juncus hemiendytus var. hemiendytus

Juncus hemiendytus var. hemiendytus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that are often green or reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows.

Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil, making it ecologically important in riparian zones.
