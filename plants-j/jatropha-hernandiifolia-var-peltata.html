Jatropha hernandiifolia var. peltata

Jatropha hernandiifolia var. peltata, commonly known as the broadleaf jatropha, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often deeply lobed. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers followed by orange-red berries containing toxic seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for medicinal use, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
