Jambosa cladoptera

Jambosa cladoptera, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It produces small, yellow, star-shaped fruits that are edible and have a tart flavor. The plant is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and opposite, oval-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The fruit is used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
