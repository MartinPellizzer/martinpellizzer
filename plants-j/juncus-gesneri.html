Juncus gesneri

Juncus gesneri, commonly known as the Gesner's rush, is a perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its dense, upright clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks, wetlands, and forest edges. It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.

Juncus gesneri plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
