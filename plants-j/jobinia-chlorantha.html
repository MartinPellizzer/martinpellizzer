Jobinia chlorantha

Jobinia chlorantha, commonly known as the bluebell of the genus Jobinia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and has a preference for well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a silvery underside, adding to its ornamental value.

Jobinia chlorantha is valued in horticulture for its beauty and ability to thrive in partial shade.
