Jatropha setifera

Jatropha setifera, commonly known as the slender leaf jatropha, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive shape. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing toxic seeds. It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its milky sap and diverse ecological roles.

Jatropha setifera is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
