Justicia praetermissa

Justicia praetermissa, commonly known as the Florida rainflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetlands and along the edges of water bodies. The plant features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a contrasting purple or red throat. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Justicia praetermissa is valued for its ability to thrive in moist conditions and its role in supporting local pollinators.
