Jacaranda rhombifolia

Jacaranda rhombifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf jacaranda, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically narrow and oval-shaped, giving the plant its scientific name. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its showy blossoms.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for warm climates.
