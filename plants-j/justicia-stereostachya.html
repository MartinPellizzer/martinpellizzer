Justicia stereostachya

Justicia stereostachya, commonly known as the spike gentian, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking spikes of blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
