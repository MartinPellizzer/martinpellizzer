Juncus luzuloides

Juncus luzuloides, commonly known as the silver rush, is a perennial plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom, giving it its common name. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in woodland edges, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with leaves that are usually flat and arranged in a spiral pattern.

Juncus luzuloides produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.
