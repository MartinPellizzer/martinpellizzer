Juncus gerardi subsp. libanoticus

Juncus gerardi subsp.

libanoticus, commonly known as the Libanotic rush, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often slightly hairy and can grow up to 1.5 meters in height.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically growing in dense clusters at the base of the plant.

This species is adapted to well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
