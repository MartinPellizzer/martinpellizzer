Juglans formosana

Juglans formosana, commonly known as the Formosan walnut, is a deciduous tree native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the walnut family, Juglandaceae, and is characterized by its large, compound leaves and distinctive, edible nuts. The tree produces male and female flowers on the same plant, with the female flowers developing into the hard-shelled nuts. Its wood is strong and durable, making it valuable for construction and furniture.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food for various wildlife species.
