Juncus tenageia var. filiformis

Juncus tenageia var. filiformis, commonly known as the slender rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are usually found at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wetland organisms.
