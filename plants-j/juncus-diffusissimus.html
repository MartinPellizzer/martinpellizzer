Juncus diffusissimus

Juncus diffusissimus, commonly known as the broadleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often grow in dense clusters, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture that makes it suitable for erosion control in wetland areas.

Juncus diffusissimus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
