Justicia corumbensis

Justicia corumbensis, commonly known as the Brazilian flame flower, is a striking flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant orange-red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy texture, while its stems are sturdy and branched.

Justicia corumbensis thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
