Jasminum multiflorum f. acuminatissimum

Jasminum multiflorum f. acuminatissimum, commonly known as the sharp-pointed jasmine, is a fragrant flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, white, star-shaped flowers that emit a sweet aroma. The leaves are opposite, simple, and have a glossy, dark green appearance. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens and as a decorative plant.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
