Juncus heldreichianus subsp. heldreichianus

Juncus heldreichianus subsp.

heldreichianus, commonly known as the Balkan rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish or brownish spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in wetland and riparian habitats.

The subspecies is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
