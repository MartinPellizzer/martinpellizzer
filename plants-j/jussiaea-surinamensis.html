Jussiaea surinamensis

Jussiaea surinamensis, commonly known as the Surinam water-millet, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous green flowers. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions.

Jussiaea surinamensis is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological benefits.
