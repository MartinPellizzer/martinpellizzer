Jasminum angulare var. glabratum

Jasminum angulare var. glabratum, commonly known as the smooth-leafed jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually smooth and glossy. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for gardens and as a houseplant.
