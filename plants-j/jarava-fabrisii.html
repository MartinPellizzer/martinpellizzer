Jarava fabrisii

Jarava fabrisii, commonly known as the desert bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of small, pinkish flowers. The plant is well adapted to dry conditions, with a deep root system that helps it access water from deeper soil layers. Its foliage is typically narrow and grass-like, and it produces lightweight seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Jarava fabrisii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
