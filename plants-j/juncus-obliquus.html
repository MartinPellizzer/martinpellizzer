Juncus obliquus

Juncus obliquus, commonly known as the slantjoint rush, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and pond edges. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. Its flowering spikes consist of small, greenish-brown flowers that are usually wind-pollinated.

Juncus obliquus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
