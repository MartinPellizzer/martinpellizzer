Juncus lampocarpus congestus

Juncus lampocarpus congestus, commonly known as the clustered rush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae and is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and streambanks.

Its rapid growth and ability to stabilize soil make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
