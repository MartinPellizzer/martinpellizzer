Juncus meiguensis

Juncus meiguensis, commonly known as the Meigu rush, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences that produce tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Juncus meiguensis is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to high-altitude environments.
