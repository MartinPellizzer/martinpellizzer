Jasminum revolutum

Jasminum revolutum, commonly known as the revolute jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or a climbing vine, with opposite, simple leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are usually fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Jasminum revolutum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
