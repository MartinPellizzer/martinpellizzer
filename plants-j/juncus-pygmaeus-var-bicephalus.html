Juncus pygmaeus var. bicephalus

Juncus pygmaeus var. bicephalus, commonly known as the two-headed rush, is a small, herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that are typically less than 10 centimeters in height. The plant produces dense clusters of tiny, spike-like flowers at the top of its stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense tufts.

It is adapted to moist or waterlogged soils and is often found in marshes, pond edges, and along streams.
