Juglans coarctata

Juglans coarctata, commonly known as the Chinese hickory, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the walnut family, Juglandaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and edible nuts. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

The nuts are enclosed in a spiky husk and are often harvested for their rich, oily kernels.
