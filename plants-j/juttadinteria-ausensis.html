Juttadinteria ausensis

Juttadinteria ausensis, commonly known as the Ausense ghost flower, is a rare and striking succulent native to the arid regions of Namibia.

It is characterized by its unique, ghost-like appearance with translucent, waxy leaves that resemble skeletal structures. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in late winter or early spring. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
