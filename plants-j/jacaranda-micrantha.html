Jacaranda micrantha

Jacaranda micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered jacaranda, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-pink flowers that bloom in summer. The tree has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated, woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its ability to provide shade and its role in supporting local wildlife.
