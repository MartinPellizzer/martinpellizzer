Juglans orientis

Juglans orientis, commonly known as the oriental walnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Juglandaceae and is characterized by its large, compound leaves and distinctive, edible nuts. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. Its flowers are dioecious, with separate male and female plants, and it produces nuts that are encased in a thick, woody husk.

The oriental walnut is valued for its timber and nuts, which have been used for centuries in local economies and cuisines.
