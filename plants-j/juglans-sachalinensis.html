Juglans sachalinensis

Juglans sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin walnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Russian Far East and northeastern China.

It belongs to the Juglandaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and edible nuts. The tree produces small, green, oval-shaped fruits that mature to a dark brown color. Its bark is thick and deeply furrowed, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

The species is valued for its timber and has limited cultivation due to its specific environmental requirements.
