Juliania amplifolia

Juliania amplifolia, commonly known as the giant marsh orchid, is a striking plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous violet or purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in swampy or marshy habitats. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Juliania amplifolia is valued in horticulture for its dramatic floral display and ability to add a unique element to water gardens.
