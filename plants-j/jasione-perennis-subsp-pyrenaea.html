Jasione perennis subsp. pyrenaea

Jasione perennis subsp.

pyrenaea, commonly known as the Pyrenean everlasting, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula and southern France. It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy slopes, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate, often with a bluish-gray hue.

The flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, are pale pink to white, contributing to its ornamental value in alpine gardens.
