Juncus gymnocarpus

Juncus gymnocarpus, commonly known as narrowleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical flower spikes. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowering occurs in late spring to early summer, producing small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense spikes.

Juncus gymnocarpus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
