Juncus gussonei

Juncus gussonei, commonly known as the Gussone's rush, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves that grow from a rhizomatous base. The plant typically reaches heights of 30 to 60 centimeters and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and marshy areas.

Juncus gussonei plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
