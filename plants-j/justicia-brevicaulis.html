Justicia brevicaulis

Justicia brevicaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flame flower, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant produces showy clusters of red or orange tubular flowers that attract hummingbirds and other pollinators. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Justicia brevicaulis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warm climates.
