Justicia chapareensis

Justicia chapareensis, commonly known as the Chapare flame flower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Bolivia and Peru.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its vibrant floral display.
