Justicia alanae

Justicia alanae, commonly known as the Alan's glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Jamaica.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in clusters, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in coastal and limestone areas.

The genus Justicia is part of the Acanthaceae family, known for its ornamental and sometimes medicinal uses.
