Justicia cardiochlamys

Justicia cardiochlamys, commonly known as the heartleaf justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tubular, brightly colored flowers. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
