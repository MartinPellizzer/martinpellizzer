Juniperus thurifera var. gallica

Juniperus thurifera var. gallica, commonly known as the Common Juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic evergreen foliage, which is typically needle-like and blue-green in color. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rocky or coastal environments.

The plant's dense growth habit provides habitat for various wildlife species.
