Juniperus virginiana var. freneloides

Juniperus virginiana var. freneloides, commonly known as the fremont juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves and small, blue-gray berries that are often used as a spice in culinary applications. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh, dry conditions. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a valuable plant for xeriscaping and erosion control.

The botanical features of this variety reflect its evolutionary adaptations to the specific ecological niches it occupies.
