Jasminum simplicifolium subsp. leratii

Jasminum simplicifolium subsp.

leratii, commonly known as the Lerat's jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
