Jacobaea tarokoensis

Jacobaea tarokoensis, commonly known as the Taroko goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils and sunny conditions. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Jacobaea tarokoensis is valued for its potential traditional medicinal uses and ecological role in its native habitat.
