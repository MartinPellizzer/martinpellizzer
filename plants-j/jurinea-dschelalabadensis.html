Jurinea dschelalabadensis

Jurinea dschelalabadensis, commonly known as the Dschelalabad sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and drought conditions. Its yellowish or pale pink flowers are typically borne in dense inflorescences, and it plays a role in stabilizing desert ecosystems.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
