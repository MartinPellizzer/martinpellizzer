Jatropha gossypiifolia var. intermedia

Jatropha gossypiifolia var. intermedia, commonly known as the Indian balsam or flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range from red to orange, often with a yellow or white center. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and it produces capsule fruits containing toxic seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
