Jatropha pseudocurcas

Jatropha pseudocurcas, commonly known as the Indian spurge or false physic, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, greenish fruits that contain toxic seeds, making it potentially hazardous to humans and animals. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in poor, dry conditions.

Despite its toxicity, it has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
