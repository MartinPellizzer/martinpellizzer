Juncoides parviflora subsp. melanocarpa

Juncoides parviflora subsp.

melanocarpa, commonly known as the black-fruited rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows.

The plant features slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish or purplish in color. Its small, clustered flowers are usually greenish or brownish, and the fruit is a dark, berry-like structure that turns black when mature.

This subspecies is characterized by its distinctive dark-colored fruits, which contribute to its common name.
