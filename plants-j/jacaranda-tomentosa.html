Jacaranda tomentosa

Jacaranda tomentosa, commonly known as the velvet jacaranda, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking purple-pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are ovate and covered in a dense, velvety pubescence, giving the plant its common name. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and has a broad, spreading canopy.

Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often peeling in thin strips.
