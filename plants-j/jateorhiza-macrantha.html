Jateorhiza macrantha

Jateorhiza macrantha, commonly known as the African giant hyacinth, is a striking perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with large, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, hollow stem that is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Jateorhiza macrantha is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its cultural significance in African communities.
