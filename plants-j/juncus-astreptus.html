Juncus astreptus

Juncus astreptus, commonly known as the silver rush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its tall, grass-like stems that are often covered in a silvery bloom. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wetlands. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spike-like clusters and are pollinated by wind.

Juncus astreptus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic habitats.
