Juncus lagenarius

Juncus lagenarius, commonly known as the bottlebrush sedge, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence that resembles a bottle brush, composed of dense, spike-like clusters of small, reddish-brown flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, pond edges, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

Juncus lagenarius plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
