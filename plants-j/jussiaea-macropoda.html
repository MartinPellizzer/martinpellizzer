Jussiaea macropoda

Jussiaea macropoda, commonly known as the broadleaf water shield, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in shallow waters, often forming dense mats on the surface of ponds and marshes. The plant has broad, floating leaves that are often covered in a waxy layer, aiding in buoyancy and reducing water loss. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white and appear in clusters, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

Jussiaea macropoda plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
