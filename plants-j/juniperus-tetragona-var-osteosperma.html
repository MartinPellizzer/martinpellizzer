Juniperus tetragona var. osteosperma

Juniperus tetragona var. osteosperma, commonly known as the cliff juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and needle-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain the aromatic berries, which are typically dark blue when mature. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The variety is notable for its hardiness and ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
