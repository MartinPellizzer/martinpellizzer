Juncus megakoleos

Juncus megakoleos, commonly known as the giant rush, is a tall, grass-like plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically bright green and can grow up to several feet in height. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are often brown or reddish in color. It thrives in moist or flooded soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Juncus megakoleos plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
