Jasminum funale subsp. sootepense

Jasminum funale subsp.

sootepense, commonly known as the Sootep jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white to yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with opposite, simple leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. Its flowers are borne in clusters and emit a sweet scent, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and aromatic properties.
