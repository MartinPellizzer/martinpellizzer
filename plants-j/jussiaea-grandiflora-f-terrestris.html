Jussiaea grandiflora f. terrestris

Jussiaea grandiflora f. terrestris, commonly known as the creeping jussiaea, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features prostrate stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across the ground. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
