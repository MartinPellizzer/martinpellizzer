Jacobaeastrum linifolium

Jacobaeastrum linifolium, commonly known as the slender leaf goatweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its finely divided, linear leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. It has a fibrous root system and produces achenes as its seed dispersal mechanism.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
