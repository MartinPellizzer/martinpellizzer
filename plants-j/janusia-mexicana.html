Janusia mexicana

Janusia mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture.

Janusia mexicana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
