Julostylis

Julostylis, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, elongated leaves and striking, pale greenish-white flowers. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surfaces of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Julostylis thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific conditions to flourish.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern in many regions.
