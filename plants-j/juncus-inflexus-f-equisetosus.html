Juncus inflexus f. equisetosus

Juncus inflexus f. equisetosus, commonly known as the hairy rush, is a variant of the common rush species characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage.

It is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are densely covered with fine, soft hairs, giving it a soft, textured appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in spikes during the summer months.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist environments.
