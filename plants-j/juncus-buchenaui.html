Juncus buchenaui

Juncus buchenaui, commonly known as the Buchenau's rush, is a perennial plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves that grow from a rhizomatous base. The plant typically thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late spring to early summer, consist of small, greenish-yellow flowers.

Juncus buchenaui is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
