Juncus torreyi var. paniculatus

Juncus torreyi var. paniculatus, commonly known as the panicled rush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences composed of small, three-lobed flowers. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of streams.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
