Jasminum floribundum

Jasminum floribundum, commonly known as the common jasmine, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Jasminum floribundum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
