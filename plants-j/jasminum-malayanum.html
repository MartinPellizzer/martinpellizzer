Jasminum malayanum

Jasminum malayanum, commonly known as the Malayan jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing shrub that produces fragrant white flowers, which bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually hairy leaves, and its stems are often woody. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forest undergrowth.

The flowers are used in traditional medicine and as a source of perfume due to their strong, sweet scent.
