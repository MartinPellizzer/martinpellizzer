Jalapa dichotoma

Jalapa dichotoma, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces edible seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found growing along riverbanks and in disturbed soils.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and restoration projects.
