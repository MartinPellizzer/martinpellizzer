Jacksonia spicata

Jacksonia spicata, commonly known as the New Zealand flax, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Jacksonia spicata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and resilience to coastal conditions.
