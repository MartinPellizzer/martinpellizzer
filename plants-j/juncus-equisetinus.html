Juncus equisetinus

Juncus equisetinus, commonly known as the rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are typically unbranched and covered in a layer of fine, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Juncus equisetinus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
