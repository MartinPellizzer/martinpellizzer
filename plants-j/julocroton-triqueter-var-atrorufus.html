Julocroton triqueter var. atrorufus

Julocroton triqueter var. atrorufus, commonly known as the black-red croton, is a variant of the croton genus native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, which features a mix of green, red, and black tones. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture.

This variant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soil with adequate sunlight.
