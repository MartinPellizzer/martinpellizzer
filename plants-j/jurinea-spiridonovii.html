Jurinea spiridonovii

Jurinea spiridonovii, commonly known as the Siberian sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of Central Asia and Siberia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Jurinea spiridonovii is adapted to harsh climatic conditions and is often found in alpine or steppe habitats.
