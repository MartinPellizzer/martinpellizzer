Juncus subincurvus

Juncus subincurvus, commonly known as the slender jointed rush, is a flowering plant in the family Juncaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has grass-like leaves that are cylindrical and often have a slightly curved appearance. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Juncus subincurvus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
