Jasminum subulatum

Jasminum subulatum, commonly known as the needle jasmine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, often found in rocky or coastal areas. It produces yellowish-orange berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

Jasminum subulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aromatic flowers.
