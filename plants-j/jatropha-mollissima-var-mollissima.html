Jatropha mollissima var. mollissima

Jatropha mollissima var. mollissima, commonly known as the silky jatropha, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain toxic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine in some regions. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal research.
