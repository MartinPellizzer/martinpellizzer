Juniperus sanderi

Juniperus sanderi, commonly known as the Sander's juniper, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves and small, berry-like cones that mature to a dark blue color. This plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, often found in mountainous regions. Juniperus sanderi is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and resilient plant suitable for landscaping in dry climates.
