Jatropha viminea

Jatropha viminea, commonly known as the creeping jatropha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is characterized by its trailing stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds covered in a sticky substance.

Jatropha viminea is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
