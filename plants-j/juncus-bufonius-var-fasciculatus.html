Juncus bufonius var. fasciculatus

Juncus bufonius var. fasciculatus, commonly known as the clustered bog rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in dense clusters, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes, which are usually greenish or brownish in hue. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
