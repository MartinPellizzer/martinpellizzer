Juncus filiformis var. aphyllus

Juncus filiformis var. aphyllus, commonly known as the threadleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are often reduced to thread-like structures, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in spike-like clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, pond edges, and along stream banks.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
