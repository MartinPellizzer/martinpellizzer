Juncus suksdorfii

Juncus suksdorfii, commonly known as the Suksdorf's rush, is a perennial plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and tall, cylindrical spikes of small, often green or brown flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in tundra or alpine habitats. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, which help stabilize soil in harsh environments.

Juncus suksdorfii plays a vital role in providing habitat and food for various arctic and subarctic wildlife species.
