Justicia apiculata

Justicia apiculata, commonly known as the flame flower or red flame plant, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is typically shrub-like, with opposite, ovate leaves that have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees, contributing to its ecological significance.
