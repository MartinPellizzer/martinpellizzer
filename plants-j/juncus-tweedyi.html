Juncus tweedyi

Juncus tweedyi, commonly known as Tweedy's rush, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and lack leaves, with flowering spikes emerging from the base. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Tweedy's rush plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
