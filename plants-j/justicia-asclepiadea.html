Justicia asclepiadea

Justicia asclepiadea, commonly known as the red flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking red bracts that resemble flowers, though they are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, which are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Justicia asclepiadea is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
