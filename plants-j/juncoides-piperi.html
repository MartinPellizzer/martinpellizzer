Juncoides piperi

Juncoides piperi, commonly known as the pipe-root sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands and marshy areas of eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters, with small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
