Jamesbrittenia megaphylla

Jamesbrittenia megaphylla, commonly known as the giant leaf cycad, is a unique plant species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, feather-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant belongs to the Cycadaceae family and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan. Its botanical structure includes a stout, unbranched stem and a crown of pinnately compound leaves.

Due to its striking appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
