Jatropha pelargoniifolia var. pelargoniifolia

Jatropha pelargoniifolia var. pelargoniifolia, commonly known as the "Coral Beauty" or "Coral Plant," is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, coral-like inflorescences that bloom in clusters, often in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and as a houseplant.
