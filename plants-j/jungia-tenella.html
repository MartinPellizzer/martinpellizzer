Jungia tenella

Jungia tenella, commonly known as the Mexican daisy, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Jungia tenella is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
