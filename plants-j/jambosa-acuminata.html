Jambosa acuminata

Jambosa acuminata, commonly known as the Natal plum, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
