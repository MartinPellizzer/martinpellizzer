Jamesbrittenia calciphila

Jamesbrittenia calciphila, commonly known as the limestone lichen, is a unique species that thrives in calcium-rich environments.

It is a composite organism consisting of a fungal component and algal or cyanobacterial partners, showcasing a symbiotic relationship. The plant's structure is typically crustose, forming a thin, adherent layer on rock surfaces. It is particularly adapted to harsh, arid conditions and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
