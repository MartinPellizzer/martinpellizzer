Jatropha crotalariiformis

Jatropha crotalariiformis, commonly known as the black milkweed, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces toxic, milky sap that contains various alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to animals and humans. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in its native environment.
