Juncus canadensis

Juncus canadensis, commonly known as Canadian rush, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often used for basket weaving. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, greenish flowers in spikes during the summer months.

Canadian rush is valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
