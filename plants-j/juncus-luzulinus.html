Juncus luzulinus

Juncus luzulinus, commonly known as the Luzon rush, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization.

Juncus luzulinus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
