Justicia championii

Justicia championii, commonly known as the flame violet, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet to red flowers that bloom in terminal clusters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
