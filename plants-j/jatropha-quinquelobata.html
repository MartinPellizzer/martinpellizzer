Jatropha quinquelobata

Jatropha quinquelobata, commonly known as the five-lobed spurge, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive five-lobed leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces toxic, milky sap that contains latex, which can cause skin irritation. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Jatropha quinquelobata is also noted for its ability to grow in poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in diverse environments.
