Jodina rhombifolia subsp. rhombifolia

Jodina rhombifolia subsp.

rhombifolia, commonly known as the rhombic jodina, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South Africa. It is characterized by its rhomboid-shaped leaves, which are typically arranged alternately along the stem.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
