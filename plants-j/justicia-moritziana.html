Justicia moritziana

Justicia moritziana, commonly known as the Moritz's glorybower, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a glossy surface.

Justicia moritziana thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
