Juncus acutiflorus var. genuinus

Juncus acutiflorus var. genuinus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly curved and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color, which bloom in late summer.

This plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
