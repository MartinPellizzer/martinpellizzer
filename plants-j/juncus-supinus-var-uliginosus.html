Juncus supinus var. uliginosus

Juncus supinus var. uliginosus, commonly known as the bog rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to reddish in color, and it produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in bogs, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. It plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.

Its fibrous roots help in filtering water and preventing erosion in aquatic ecosystems.
