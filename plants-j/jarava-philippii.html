Jarava philippii

Jarava philippii, commonly known as Philippi's jarava, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny flowers. It is often found in coastal and inland dunes, contributing to soil stabilization.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration projects in its native habitat.
