Jasminum laurifolium var. sublineare

Jasminum laurifolium var. sublineare, commonly known as the narrow-leaved jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a smooth texture, often with a slight waxy appearance. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
