Justicia macrophylla

Justicia macrophylla, commonly known as the flame bush, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often appearing in clusters at the ends of its branches. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy and have a reddish or purple underside. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Justicia macrophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and butterflies.
