Justicia genistifolia

Justicia genistifolia, commonly known as the golden trumpet or yellow flame, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring slender, branching stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking clusters of yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, making it a valuable plant in ecological gardens.
