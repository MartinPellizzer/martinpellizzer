Jatropha mollissima var. villosa

Jatropha mollissima var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy jatropha, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by spiny capsules containing toxic seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for use in ecological restoration, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
