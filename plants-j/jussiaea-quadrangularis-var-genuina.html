Jussiaea quadrangularis var. genuina

Jussiaea quadrangularis var. genuina, commonly known as the narrow-leafed willowherb, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, quadrangular stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale pink to white flowers during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often found in riparian zones.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
