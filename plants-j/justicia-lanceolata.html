Justicia lanceolata

Justicia lanceolata, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Botanically, it belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and adaptability to various climates.
