Justicia verticillaris

Justicia verticillaris, commonly known as the whorled flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Justicia verticillaris is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
