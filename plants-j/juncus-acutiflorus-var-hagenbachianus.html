Juncus acutiflorus var. hagenbachianus

Juncus acutiflorus var. hagenbachianus, commonly known as Hagenbach's rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It has slender, cylindrical stems that are typically green or reddish in color, and it produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.

This variety is distinguished by its unique floral structure and is an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
