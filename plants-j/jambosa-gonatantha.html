Jambosa gonatantha

Jambosa gonatantha, commonly known as the red-fruit jambosa, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its botanical features include smooth, dark green leaves and a compact growth habit.

Jambosa gonatantha is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological and culinary significance.
