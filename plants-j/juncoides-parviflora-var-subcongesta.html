Juncoides parviflora var. subcongesta

Juncoides parviflora var. subcongesta, commonly known as the clustered flowering rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in wet or moist environments such as marshes and pond edges. It is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
