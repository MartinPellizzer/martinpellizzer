Juglans mandshurica

Juglans mandshurica, commonly known as the Manchurian walnut, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the walnut family, Juglandaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and edible nuts. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, rounded crown and rough, fissured bark. Its nuts are enclosed in a thick, spiky husk and are valued for their high oil content and culinary use.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mixed forests across China, Korea, and Russia.
