Justicia moctli

Justicia moctli, commonly known as Mexican flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular, salmon-pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Justicia moctli is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and hummingbirds.
