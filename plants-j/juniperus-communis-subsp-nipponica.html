Juniperus communis subsp. nipponica

Juniperus communis subsp.

nipponica, commonly known as Japanese juniper, is a subspecies of the common juniper native to East Asia. It is a coniferous evergreen shrub or small tree with needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in horticulture.
