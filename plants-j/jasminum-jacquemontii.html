Jasminum jacquemontii

Jasminum jacquemontii, commonly known as the golden jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with a smooth surface.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
