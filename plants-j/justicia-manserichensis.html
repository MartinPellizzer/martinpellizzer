Justicia manserichensis

Justicia manserichensis, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy texture, while its stems are sturdy and covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
