Jasminum wrayi var. axillaris

Jasminum wrayi var. axillaris, commonly known as the axillary jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, with opposite, simple leaves that are elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
