Jatropha vitifolia var. obtusifolia

Jatropha vitifolia var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved jatropha, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features compound leaves with oval, broad, and slightly toothed leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries containing toxic seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in horticultural and ecological studies.
