Justicia hodgei

Justicia hodgei, commonly known as the Hodge's flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils, often found in dry, open habitats. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Justicia hodgei is valued in horticulture for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to arid environments.
