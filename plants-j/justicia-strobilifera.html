Justicia strobilifera

Justicia strobilifera, commonly known as the Christmas star or silverleaf justicia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its lance-shaped, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
