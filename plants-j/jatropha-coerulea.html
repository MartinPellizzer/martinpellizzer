Jatropha coerulea

Jatropha coerulea, commonly known as blue jatropha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring simple, alternate leaves and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, toxic seeds that are encased in a fleshy, orange-colored capsule. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and dry forests.

The plant is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as an ornamental species.
