Juncus bogotensis var. compactus

Juncus bogotensis var. compactus, commonly known as the compact rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
