Justicia involucrata

Justicia involucrata, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Justicia involucrata is often used in ornamental landscaping for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
