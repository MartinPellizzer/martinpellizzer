Juncus compressus var. subtriflorus

Juncus compressus var. subtriflorus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and cylindrical, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

The species is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
