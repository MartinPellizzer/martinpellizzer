Jungia paniculata

Jungia paniculata, commonly known as the panicled jungia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy inflorescences. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Jungia paniculata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil conditions.
