Juniperus chinensis var. japonica

Juniperus chinensis var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese juniper, is a hardy evergreen shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It features needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color and are arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive form and resilience.

Juniperus chinensis var. japonica thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and landscape designs.
