Jacaima

Jacaima, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible but often have a spicy or peppery taste. Jacaima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is adaptable to various soil types.
