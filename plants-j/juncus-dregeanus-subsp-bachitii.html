Juncus dregeanus subsp. bachitii

Juncus dregeanus subsp.

bachitii, commonly known as the Bachitii rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as along riverbanks and in swampy areas.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of greenish or brownish flowers. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

This subspecies is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and adaptability.
