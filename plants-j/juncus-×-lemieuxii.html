Juncus × lemieuxii

Juncus × lemieuxii, commonly known as the hybrid rush, is a perennial plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is a hybrid between Juncus effusus and Juncus roemerianus, characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and coastal areas, where it thrives in brackish or freshwater conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

Juncus × lemieuxii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
