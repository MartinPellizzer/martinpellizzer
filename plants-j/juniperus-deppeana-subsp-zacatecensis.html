Juniperus deppeana subsp. zacatecensis

Juniperus deppeana subsp.

zacatecensis, commonly known as the Zacatecas juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico. It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and small, blue-black berries.

This subspecies thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in limestone soils. It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in some regions.
