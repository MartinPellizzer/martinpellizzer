Juncus maritimus var. rigidus

Juncus maritimus var. rigidus, commonly known as stiff sea rush, is a coastal plant native to salt marshes and brackish environments.

It is characterized by its stiff, grass-like foliage that grows in dense clumps, often reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant has narrow, cylindrical leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes during the summer months.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
