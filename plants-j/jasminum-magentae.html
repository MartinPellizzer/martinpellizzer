Jasminum magentae

Jasminum magentae, commonly known as the Magenta jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Jasminum magentae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
