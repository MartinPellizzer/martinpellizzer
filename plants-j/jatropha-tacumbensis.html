Jatropha tacumbensis

Jatropha tacumbensis, commonly known as the Mexican physic nut, is a shrub native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and red, fleshy fruits that contain toxic seeds. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces a milky sap that can cause skin irritation. It is often found in dry, disturbed habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Jatropha tacumbensis is also known for its potential use in biofuel production due to its high oil content.
