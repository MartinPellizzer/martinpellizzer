Jungia glandulifera

Jungia glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular jungle plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy, glandular leaves that aid in water retention and deter herbivores. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Jungia glandulifera is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
