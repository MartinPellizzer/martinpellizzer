Jasminum lanceolaria subsp. lanceolaria

Jasminum lanceolaria subsp.

lanceolaria, commonly known as the lanceolate jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

The subspecies is distinguished by its specific leaf morphology and floral structure compared to other jasmine species.
