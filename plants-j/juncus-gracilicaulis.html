Juncus gracilicaulis

Juncus gracilicaulis, commonly known as slender rush, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers in dense clusters near the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Juncus gracilicaulis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
