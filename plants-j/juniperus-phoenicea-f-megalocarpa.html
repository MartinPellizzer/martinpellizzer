Juniperus phoenicea f. megalocarpa

Juniperus phoenicea f. megalocarpa, commonly known as the large-seeded juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and conical or spherical crown. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are edible but often used as a spice. Its botanical features include scale-like leaves and female cones that mature into the distinctive berries.

This variety is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental and historical significance.
