Juniperus virginiana f. chamberlaynii

Juniperus virginiana f. chamberlaynii, commonly known as the Chamberlain's redcedar, is a botanical variant of the eastern redcedar.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and reddish-brown bark. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are typically blue or black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This form is valued for its ornamental qualities and resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
