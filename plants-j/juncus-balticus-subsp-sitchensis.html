Juncus balticus subsp. sitchensis

Juncus balticus subsp.

sitchensis, commonly known as Sitka rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions of Alaska and western Canada. It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves that are typically green to reddish in color.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes, which are usually green or brown, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in tidal zones, estuaries, and along the shores of lakes and rivers.

Sitka rush plays an important ecological role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
