Justicia chiapensis

Justicia chiapensis, commonly known as the Chiapas flame flower, is a tropical perennial shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular, red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant in tropical regions.
