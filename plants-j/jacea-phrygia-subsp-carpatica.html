Jacea phrygia subsp. carpatica

Jacea phrygia subsp.

carpatica, commonly known as the Carpathian bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Carpathian Mountains. It features showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family, known for its bell-shaped flowers.

Its subspecies status highlights its unique adaptations to the specific environmental conditions of the Carpathian region.
