Juncus leucochlamys

Juncus leucochlamys, commonly known as the white-chaliced rush, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist or wet environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in dense, spike-like clusters. Its inflorescence is often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts, which are white and resemble petals.

Juncus leucochlamys plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
