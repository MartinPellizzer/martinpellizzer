Jatropha glauca

Jatropha glauca, commonly known as the blue jatropha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and it produces small, toxic berries that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Due to its ornamental appeal and potential for seed production, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
