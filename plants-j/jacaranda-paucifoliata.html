Jacaranda paucifoliata

Jacaranda paucifoliata, commonly known as the slender jacaranda, is a small to medium-sized tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and produces clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers during the cooler months. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and along streets for its visual beauty.
