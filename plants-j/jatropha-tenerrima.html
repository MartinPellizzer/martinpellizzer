Jatropha tenerrima

Jatropha tenerrima, commonly known as the crimson cup plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has dark green, heart-shaped leaves and produces toxic seeds that contain compounds such as ricin. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive addition to gardens and natural habitats.
