Juncus fontanesii subsp. brachyanthus

Juncus fontanesii subsp.

brachyanthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil.

The subspecies brachyanthus is distinguished by its shorter, more condensed flowering structure compared to other members of the species.
