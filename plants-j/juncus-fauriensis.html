Juncus fauriensis

Juncus fauriensis, commonly known as the Fauri's rush, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that often grow in dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spike-like clusters. Juncus fauriensis thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along stream banks.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form extensive colonies in suitable habitats.
