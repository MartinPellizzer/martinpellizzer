Juncus triglumis subsp. triglumis

Juncus triglumis subsp.

triglumis, commonly known as the common rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows.

The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often hollow and covered in fine, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers that are usually greenish or reddish in color.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
