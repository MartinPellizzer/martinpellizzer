Juncus lampocarpus pallescens

Juncus lampocarpus pallescens, commonly known as the pale fringed rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to western North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and pond edges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. Its flowering spikes are usually pale green to brown, and the plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This species is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wetland organisms.
