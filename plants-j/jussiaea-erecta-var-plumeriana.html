Jussiaea erecta var. plumeriana

Jussiaea erecta var. plumeriana, commonly known as the silverleaf jussiaea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and wetland restoration projects.
