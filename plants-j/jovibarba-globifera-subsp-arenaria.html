Jovibarba globifera subsp. arenaria

Jovibarba globifera subsp.

arenaria, commonly known as the sandstone haworthia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with white or yellow markings.

The plant thrives in arid environments and is adapted to store water in its leaves. It produces small, white or yellow flowers during the warmer months.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
