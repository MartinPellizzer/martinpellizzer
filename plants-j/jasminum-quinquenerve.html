Jasminum quinquenerve

Jasminum quinquenerve, commonly known as the five-veined jasmine, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves with five prominent veins. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or yellow, blooming in clusters. Its flowering period usually occurs during the summer months.

Jasminum quinquenerve is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and as a climbing plant.
