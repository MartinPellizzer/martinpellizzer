Juncus effusus pauciflorus

Juncus effusus pauciflorus, commonly known as the slender rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of grass-like, cylindrical leaves and is native to wetland habitats. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist to wet environments such as marshes, bogs, and stream banks.

Its limited flowering, as suggested by its specific epithet, makes it less showy compared to other rush species.
