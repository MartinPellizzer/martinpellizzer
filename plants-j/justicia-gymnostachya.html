Justicia gymnostachya

Justicia gymnostachya, commonly known as the flame flower or red flame plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for both garden beds and container planting.

Botanically, it belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is valued for its vibrant color and ease of cultivation.
