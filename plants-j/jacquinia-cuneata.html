Jacquinia cuneata

Jacquinia cuneata, commonly known as the narrowleaf jacquinia, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands or along streambanks. It is often found in the Pacific Northwest and has a preference for cooler, shaded environments.

Jacquinia cuneata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
