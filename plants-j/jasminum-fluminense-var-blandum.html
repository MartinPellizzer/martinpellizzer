Jasminum fluminense var. blandum

Jasminum fluminense var. blandum, commonly known as the Brazilian jasmine or sweet senna, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, characterized by its fragrant white flowers. The plant features opposite, simple, and lance-shaped leaves, often with a glossy surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, emitting a sweet scent.

The botanical structure of this species is well adapted to its humid, warm climate, supporting its growth in moist, well-drained soils.
