Jatropha lobata subsp. glauca

Jatropha lobata subsp.

glauca, commonly known as the blue jatropha, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America. It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and hairy, glandular leaves.

The plant is typically shrub-like, with stout stems and often grows in dry, rocky habitats. It produces capsule fruits that contain toxic seeds, a common trait among the genus Jatropha.

The subspecies glauca is distinguished by its glaucous (bluish-gray) appearance, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments.
