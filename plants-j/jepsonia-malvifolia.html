Jepsonia malvifolia

Jepsonia malvifolia, commonly known as the Malvifolia jepsonia, is a flowering plant native to California.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It typically grows in rocky soils and is often found in coastal scrub habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
