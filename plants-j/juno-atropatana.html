Juno atropatana

Juno atropatana, commonly known as the Atropatana juniper, is a species of juniper native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows at high altitudes, often in rocky or well-drained soils. The plant produces small, scale-like leaves and is characterized by its blue-gray foliage. Its berries are toxic to humans and animals, making it an important plant in traditional medicine and ecological studies.

The botanical features of Juno atropatana make it well adapted to harsh, arid environments.
