Jasminum pentaneurum

Jasminum pentaneurum, commonly known as the Java jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces fragrant white flowers, which bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

The flowers are used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oil.
