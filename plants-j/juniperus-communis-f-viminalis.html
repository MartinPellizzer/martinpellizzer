Juniperus communis f. viminalis

Juniperus communis f. viminalis, commonly known as the common juniper, is a subspecies of the juniper shrub native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of scale-like leaves and small, blue-black berries that are used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has a slow growth rate and can live for several decades, making it a hardy and long-lived species.

Its aromatic properties and adaptability contribute to its widespread use in landscaping and traditional herbal remedies.
