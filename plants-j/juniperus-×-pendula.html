Juniperus × pendula

Juniperus × pendula, commonly known as the weeping juniper, is a hybrid evergreen conifer that exhibits distinctive cascading foliage.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its drooping branches that create a unique, ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, blue-gray needles and male and female cones, typical of the Juniperus genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

Its slow growth rate and hardy nature make it suitable for a variety of climates, though it prefers cooler temperatures.
