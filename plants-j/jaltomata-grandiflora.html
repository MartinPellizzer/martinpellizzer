Jaltomata grandiflora

Jaltomata grandiflora, commonly known as the largeflower morning glory, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its trumpet-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are slender and hairy as well. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks.

Jaltomata grandiflora is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
