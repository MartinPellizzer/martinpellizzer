Jacaranda orinocensis

Jacaranda orinocensis, commonly known as the Orinoco jacaranda, is a species native to South America, particularly Venezuela and Colombia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a broad, spreading canopy. The plant is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The tree produces woody pods that contain flat, elliptical seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
