Jatropha oblanceolata

Jatropha oblanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf physic nut, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features opposite, oblanceolate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers followed by orange-red berries containing toxic seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in dry conditions.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not recommended for ornamental or edible use.
