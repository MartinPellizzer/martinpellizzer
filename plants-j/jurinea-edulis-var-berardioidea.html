Jurinea edulis var. berardioidea

Jurinea edulis var. berardioidea, commonly known as the edible sea bladder, is a variant of the sea bladder plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its succulent, bladder-like structures that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in saline and arid environments. The plant's leaves are typically reduced to small scales, while its stems are fleshy and often covered in a waxy coating. It reproduces through vegetative propagation, forming dense mats that stabilize sandy or rocky shores.

This plant is valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional cuisines for centuries.
