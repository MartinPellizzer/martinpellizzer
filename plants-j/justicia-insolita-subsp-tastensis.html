Justicia insolita subsp. tastensis

Justicia insolita subsp.

tastensis, commonly known as the Tastens Island justicia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands. It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical gardens.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for horticultural collections in warmer climates.
