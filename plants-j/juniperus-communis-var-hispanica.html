Juniperus communis var. hispanica

Juniperus communis var. hispanica, commonly known as Spanish juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and northern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves and produces small, blue-black berries that are used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant is evergreen, with a slow growth rate and a rugged, shrub-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Spanish juniper is valued for its aromatic wood and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
