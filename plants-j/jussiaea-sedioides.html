Jussiaea sedioides

Jussiaea sedioides, commonly known as the swamp myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to wetland habitats in South America.

It typically grows in marshy areas and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant has small, glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink and are clustered in small, compact inflorescences.

Jussiaea sedioides is adapted to waterlogged soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
