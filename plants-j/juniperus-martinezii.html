Juniperus martinezii

Juniperus martinezii, commonly known as the Martinez juniper, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves and small, berry-like cones that mature to a bluish-black color. This plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The tree can reach heights of up to 15 meters and is often found in chaparral and desert scrub habitats.

Its slow growth rate and longevity make it a valuable component of its ecosystem.
