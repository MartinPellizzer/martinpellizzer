Jasminum trineuron

Jasminum trineuron, commonly known as the three-veined jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a sweet aroma. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have three prominent veins, giving it its scientific name.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.
