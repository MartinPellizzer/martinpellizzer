Juniperus squamata f. wilsonii

Juniperus squamata f. wilsonii, commonly known as the Wilson's juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, providing a striking visual contrast against its woody stems. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic wood. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, dry climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
