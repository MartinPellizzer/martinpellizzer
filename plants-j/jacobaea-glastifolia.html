Jacobaea glastifolia

Jacobaea glastifolia, commonly known as the creeping tansy, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features a creeping growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Jacobaea glastifolia is often found in grassy areas, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.
