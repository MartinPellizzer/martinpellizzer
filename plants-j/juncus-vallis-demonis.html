Juncus vallis-demonis

Juncus vallis-demonis, commonly known as the demon's valley rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves, which are typically green and covered in a fine layer of hair. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that contain tiny flowers, which are often wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and marshy areas.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and ecologists studying wetland ecosystems.
