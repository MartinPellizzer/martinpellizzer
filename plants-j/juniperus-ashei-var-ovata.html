Juniperus ashei var. ovata

Juniperus ashei var. ovata, commonly known as the Ashe juniper or Texas cedar, is a shrub or small tree native to the Southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color and its small, berry-like cones that mature to a dark blue or black. The plant is evergreen and has a slow growth rate, often forming dense thickets in its native habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The foliage and cones are used in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic wood.
