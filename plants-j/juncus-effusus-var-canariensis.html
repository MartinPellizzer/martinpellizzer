Juncus effusus var. canariensis

Juncus effusus var. canariensis, commonly known as the Canary rush, is a perennial plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers that appear in dense clusters at the top of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
