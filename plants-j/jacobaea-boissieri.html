Jacobaea boissieri

Jacobaea boissieri, commonly known as the Algerian tansy, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture. Its yellow flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Jacobaea boissieri is often found in rocky or sandy soils in arid environments.
