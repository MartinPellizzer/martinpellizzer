Justicia totonaca

Justicia totonaca, commonly known as the Totonac false heather, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to violet. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine pubescence, while its stems are square and hairy.

Justicia totonaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
