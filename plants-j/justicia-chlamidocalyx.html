Justicia chlamidocalyx

Justicia chlamidocalyx, commonly known as the velvetleaf justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple bracts that enclose its small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color and bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
