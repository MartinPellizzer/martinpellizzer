Jamesonia crespiana

Jamesonia crespiana, commonly known as the mountain lily, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
