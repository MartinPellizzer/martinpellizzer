Jacaranda rachidoptera

Jacaranda rachidoptera, commonly known as the flame tree, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, narrow leaflets and produces long, bean-like pods that contain seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark is smooth and grey, with a distinct aromatic scent when crushed.
