Jacquemontia guayaquilensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian moonflower, is a member of the Convolvulaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Andean regions of Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and large, showy white flowers that open at night. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Jacquemontia guayaquilensis is often found growing in moist, shaded environments along riverbanks and in cloud forests.
