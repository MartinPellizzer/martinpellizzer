Jurinea ledebourii

Jurinea ledebourii, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a low-growing annual plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its mat-forming growth habit, with small, fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its adaptations to harsh environments include deep root systems and the ability to store water in its succulent tissues.

Jurinea ledebourii is often found in sandy soils and is a common species in coastal and inland dune ecosystems.
