Juncus rivularis

Juncus rivularis, commonly known as the river rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments.

It typically grows in marshes, stream banks, and other moist habitats. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish or greenish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Juncus rivularis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
