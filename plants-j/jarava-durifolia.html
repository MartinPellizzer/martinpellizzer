Jarava durifolia

Jarava durifolia, commonly known as hardleaf jarava, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky soils, where it forms dense, low-growing clumps. The plant has narrow, rigid leaves that are covered in a fine layer of hairs, which helps reduce water loss in harsh conditions. Its inflorescence consists of compact, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Jarava durifolia plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
