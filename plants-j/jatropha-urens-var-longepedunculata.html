Jatropha urens var. longepedunculata

Jatropha urens var. longepedunculata, commonly known as the long pedunculate spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the genus Jatropha. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for medicinal use, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
