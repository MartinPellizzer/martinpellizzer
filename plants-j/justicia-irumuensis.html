Justicia irumuensis

Justicia irumuensis, commonly known as the Irumu flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy inflorescences that resemble flames. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers, which bloom in clusters, are usually red or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.
