Jensoa ensata

Jensoa ensata, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to East Asia.

It produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a white throat. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Jensoa ensata is valued in gardens for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various climates.
