Jacquemontia menispermoides var. canescens

Jacquemontia menispermoides var. canescens, commonly known as the silky jacquemontia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with a distinct, sweet scent. The plant features hairy stems and leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, flat seeds.

This species thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
