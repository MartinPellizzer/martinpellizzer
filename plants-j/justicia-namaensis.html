Justicia namaensis

Justicia namaensis, commonly known as the Namibian flame flower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, showcasing its adaptation to dry environments. Its leaves are often narrow and lance-shaped, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Justicia namaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
