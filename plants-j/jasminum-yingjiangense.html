Jasminum yingjiangense

Jasminum yingjiangense, commonly known as the Yingjiang jasmine, is a flowering plant native to China, specifically the Yunnan Province.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with opposite, simple, and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are borne on axillary or terminal clusters and are known for their sweet, intoxicating fragrance.

This species is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its use in traditional Chinese medicine.
