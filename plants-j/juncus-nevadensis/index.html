Juncus Nevadensis

Juncus nevadensis is a hardy wetland plant native to the western United States. It thrives in moist soil and is commonly found in marshes and along riverbanks. This grass-like plant has narrow, grassy leaves and produces small, spike-like flower clusters. It plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for wildlife. Due to its adaptability, it is often used in ecological restoration projects.
