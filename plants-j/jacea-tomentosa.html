Jacea tomentosa

Jacea tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly jacea, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green foliage and small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape and covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

Jacea tomentosa is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
