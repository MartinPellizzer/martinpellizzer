Justicia procumbens var. riukiuensis

Justicia procumbens var. riukiuensis, commonly known as the Ryukyu flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It is a perennial herb with prostrate growth habit, featuring hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking clusters of tubular, red-orange flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes in tropical and subtropical regions.
