Jasminocereus thouarsii subsp. delicatus

Jasminocereus thouarsii subsp.

delicatus, commonly known as the delicate night-blooming cereus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to Madagascar. It is a small, branching cactus characterized by its spiny stems and nocturnal flowering habits.

The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that open at night, attracting moths and other nocturnal pollinators. Its growth is slow, and it thrives in well-drained soil with minimal water.

Due to its ornamental value and unique blooming pattern, it is often cultivated in warm, arid environments.
