Jungia tomentosa

Jungia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly jungle plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly white hairs covering its stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its unique morphology makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
