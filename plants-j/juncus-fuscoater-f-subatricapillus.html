Juncus fuscoater f. subatricapillus

Juncus fuscoater f. subatricapillus, commonly known as the dark-haired rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Juncaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, cylindrical leaves that are typically dark green to brownish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, which are often hidden among the foliage. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, stream banks, and damp woodlands.

This subspecies is distinguished by its darker, more densely hairy leaves compared to the typical form of Juncus fuscoater.
