Juniperus przewalskii subsp. pendula

Juniperus przewalskii subsp.

pendula, commonly known as the weeping juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its drooping branches that give it a distinctive, cascading appearance.

The plant produces small, scale-like leaves and blue-gray berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in regions with suitable climates.
