Justicia opaca

Justicia opaca, commonly known as the narrowleaf false arum, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swampy areas. Its flowering period occurs in the spring and early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant's ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native, low-maintenance plants.
