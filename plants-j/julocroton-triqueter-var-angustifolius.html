Julocroton triqueter var. angustifolius

Julocroton triqueter var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved croton, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its rough, often hairy stems and alternate leaves. The leaves are typically narrow and elliptical, with a pointed tip, and may vary in color from green to reddish. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in green or reddish bracts, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
