Jussiaea sagittata

Jussiaea sagittata, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of white or pale yellow.

Jussiaea sagittata is often used in water gardens and wetland restoration projects due to its ability to improve water quality.
