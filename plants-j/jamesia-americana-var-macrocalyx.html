Jamesia americana var. macrocalyx

Jamesia americana var. macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered mountain snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from white to pale pink, blooming in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
