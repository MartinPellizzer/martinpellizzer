Jasminum agastyamalayanum

Jasminum agastyamalayanum, commonly known as the Western Ghats jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and aromatic, white flowers. The leaves are simple, opposite, and have a glossy green surface with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the evening, and is noted for its ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for cultivation in subtropical and tropical climates.
