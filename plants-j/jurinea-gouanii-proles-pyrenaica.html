Jurinea gouanii proles pyrenaica

Jurinea gouanii proles pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean sandwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its compact rosette of linear, often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. It is adapted to rocky and sandy soils, typically found in mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

This species is noted for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions, making it a hardy plant in its native habitat.
