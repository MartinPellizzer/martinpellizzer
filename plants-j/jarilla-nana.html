Jarilla nana

Jarilla nana, commonly known as dwarf evening primrose, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Onagraceae family and is characterized by its small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, opposite leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.
