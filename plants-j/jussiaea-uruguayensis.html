Jussiaea uruguayensis

Jussiaea uruguayensis, commonly known as the Uruguay waterleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly Uruguay and southern Brazil.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in umbels, blooming during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near wetlands or along riverbanks.

Jussiaea uruguayensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
