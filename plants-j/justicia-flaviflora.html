Justicia flaviflora

Justicia flaviflora, commonly known as the yellow flame plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are tubular with two lips, and they attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
