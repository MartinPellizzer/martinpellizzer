Jasminum coarctatum

Jasminum coarctatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf jasmine, is a flowering plant native to regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and small, aromatic white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

It is often cultivated for its fragrant blooms and ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
