Jambosa tenuiramis

Jambosa tenuiramis, commonly known as the white jambosa, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are similar in appearance to lychees but have a distinct, sweet flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is often cultivated in gardens and tropical regions.
