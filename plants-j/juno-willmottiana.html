Juno willmottiana

Juno willmottiana, commonly known as the willmott's juniper, is a species of juniper native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves and clusters of blue-gray berries that are produced in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Juno willmottiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and as a landscape plant in suitable climates.
