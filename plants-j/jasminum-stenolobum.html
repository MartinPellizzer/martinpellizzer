Jasminum stenolobum

Jasminum stenolobum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant has slender, woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy in appearance. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Jasminum stenolobum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
