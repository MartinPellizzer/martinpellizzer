Jasione humilis var. longidentata

Jasione humilis var. longidentata, commonly known as the long-toothed bellflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in early summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly toothed margin, giving it its varietal name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
