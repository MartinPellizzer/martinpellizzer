Jaborosa

Jaborosa, commonly known as the "bitterleaf" or "jaborosa herb," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas and is known for its medicinal properties. Jaborosa contains various bioactive compounds, including flavonoids and alkaloids, which contribute to its traditional use in herbal medicine.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its delicate flower clusters.
