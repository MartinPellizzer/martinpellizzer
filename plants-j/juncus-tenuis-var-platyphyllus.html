Juncus tenuis var. platyphyllus

Juncus tenuis var. platyphyllus, commonly known as the broadleaf rush, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and broad, flat leaves that distinguish it from other rush species. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically borne on tall, slender inflorescences. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This variant is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
