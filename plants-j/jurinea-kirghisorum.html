Jurinea kirghisorum

Jurinea kirghisorum, commonly known as the Kirghiz sandflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and succulent leaves adapted to withstand harsh, dry conditions. The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments. Its ability to store water in its fleshy stems and leaves makes it well-suited for survival in nutrient-poor, drought-prone habitats.

Due to its ecological importance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species in its native range.
