Justicia camerunensis

Justicia camerunensis, commonly known as the Cameroon flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Central and West Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, tubular flowers are typically red or orange, with five petals, and are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to semi-shaded environments.
