Jasminum sambac var. kerianum

Jasminum sambac var. kerianum, commonly known as the Kerianum jasmine, is a fragrant flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white, star-shaped flowers that emit a strong, sweet aroma, particularly at night. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with dark green, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and in perfumery due to its aromatic properties.
