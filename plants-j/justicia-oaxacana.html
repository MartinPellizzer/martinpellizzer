Justicia oaxacana

Justicia oaxacana, commonly known as the Oaxacan flameflower, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Justicia oaxacana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
