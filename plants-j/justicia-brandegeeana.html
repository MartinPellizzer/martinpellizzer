Justicia brandegeeana

Justicia brandegeeana, commonly known as the flame flower, is a striking tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are opposite in arrangement, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its showy blooms and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking a dramatic focal point in tropical or subtropical landscapes.
