Juglans pterocarpa

Juglans pterocarpa, commonly known as the narrowleaf hickory, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the walnut family, Juglandaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with long, slender leaflets. The tree produces edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk, which are typically harvested in the fall. Its bark is deeply furrowed and dark in color, while its wood is strong and durable.

This species plays an important role in local ecosystems and is valued for both its ecological and economic significance.
