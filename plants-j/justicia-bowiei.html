Justicia bowiei

Justicia bowiei, commonly known as flame grass, is a striking ornamental plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is known for its ability to thrive in full sun and well-drained soil. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Justicia bowiei is also valued for its role in attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
