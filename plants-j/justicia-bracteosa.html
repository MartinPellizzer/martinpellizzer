Justicia bracteosa

Justicia bracteosa, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Justicia bracteosa is a member of the Acanthaceae family, known for its showy inflorescences and ornamental value in gardens.

It is often cultivated for its vibrant color and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
