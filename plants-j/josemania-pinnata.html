Josemania pinnata

Josemania pinnata, commonly known as the featherleaf juncus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Josemania pinnata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens.
