Jasminum coarctatum var. coarctatum

Jasminum coarctatum var. coarctatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, evergreen shrub form and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are narrow and elliptical, with a glossy dark green surface. It prefers cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
