Jasminonerium tomentosum

Jasminonerium tomentosum, commonly known as the velvet jasmine, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine, velvety pubescence.

The flowers emit a sweet, intoxicating fragrance, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
