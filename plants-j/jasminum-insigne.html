Jasminum insigne

Jasminum insigne, commonly known as the white jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is a woody shrub or vine, often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical in shape.

Jasminum insigne thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.
