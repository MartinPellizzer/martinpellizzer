Jacobinia ovata var. subglabra

Jacobinia ovata var. subglabra, commonly known as the smooth-leaved Jacobinia, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and glabrous. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and modest floral display.
