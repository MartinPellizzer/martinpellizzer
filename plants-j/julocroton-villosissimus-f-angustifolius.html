Julocroton villosissimus f. angustifolius

Julocroton villosissimus f. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved croton, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, spiky fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
