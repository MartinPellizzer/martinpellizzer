Justicia brycei

Justicia brycei, commonly known as the Bryce's glorybower, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are glossy and arranged in whorls along its stems. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom in late spring to early summer. These flowers are typically borne in clusters at the ends of the branches, creating a dramatic visual display.

Justicia brycei thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.
