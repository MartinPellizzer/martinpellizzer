Jasminum somaliense

Jasminum somaliense, commonly known as Somali jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Horn of Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant white flowers and woody stems, which are typical of the jasmine genus. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a glossy green surface.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
