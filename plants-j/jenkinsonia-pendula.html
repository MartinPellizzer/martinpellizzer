Jenkinsonia pendula

Jenkinsonia pendula, commonly known as the hanging jenkinsonia, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, showy inflorescences that bloom in shades of white and pink, attracting various pollinators. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, which helps to reflect sunlight and reduce water loss. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests.
