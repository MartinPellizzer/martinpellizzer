Justicia torresii

Justicia torresii, commonly known as the desert mallow, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub with slender, branching stems and small, woolly leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Its botanical features make it an important species in xeriscaping and desert ecosystems.
