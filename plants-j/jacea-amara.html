Jacea amara

Jacea amara, commonly known as bitter jacea, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Jacea amara is known for its bitter taste, which is due to the presence of various secondary metabolites.

It is often found in dry, open habitats and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
