Justicia kirkiana

Justicia kirkiana, commonly known as the flame flower, is a striking tropical plant native to regions of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its stems are sturdy and can be somewhat woody, especially in mature plants.

Justicia kirkiana thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.
