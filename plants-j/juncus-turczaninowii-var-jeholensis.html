Juncus turczaninowii var. jeholensis

Juncus turczaninowii var. jeholensis, commonly known as the Jehol rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Juncaginaceae and is characterized by its cylindrical, grass-like stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly fibrous texture.

This variety is notable for its historical significance in paleobotanical studies due to its presence in fossil records.
