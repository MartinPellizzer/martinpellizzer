Jacquemontia mucronifera

Jacquemontia mucronifera, commonly known as the spurred cucumber, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces yellow flowers with distinctive spurred petals. The plant has hairy, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its fruits are fleshy, green berries that turn orange when mature.

Jacquemontia mucronifera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
