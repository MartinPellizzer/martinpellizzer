Jasione montana var. lusitanica

Jasione montana var. lusitanica, commonly known as the Lusitanian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features slender, branched stems and produces clusters of blue to violet flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
