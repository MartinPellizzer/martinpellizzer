Jacobaeastrum serra

Jacobaeastrum serra, commonly known as the serrated hawkweed, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, giving it its common name. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Jacobaeastrum serra is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
