Juncus subulatus f. salinus

Juncus subulatus f. salinus, commonly known as the salt marsh rush, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It thrives in coastal areas and wetlands with high salt content, showcasing its ability to tolerate and even flourish in such conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. Its flowering spikes are typically short and bear small, greenish flowers.

This variety is an important component of salt marsh ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various organisms.
