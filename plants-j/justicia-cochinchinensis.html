Justicia cochinchinensis

Justicia cochinchinensis, commonly known as the flame flower or red flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and grows as an erect shrub or small tree. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
