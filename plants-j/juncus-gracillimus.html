Juncus gracillimus

Juncus gracillimus, commonly known as slender rush, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender, grass-like stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters near the base of the stems. It typically grows in moist or wet environments such as marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Its narrow leaves and dense growth habit make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
