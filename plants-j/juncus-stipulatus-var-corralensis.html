Juncus stipulatus var. corralensis

Juncus stipulatus var. corralensis, commonly known as the Corralensis rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the southwestern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, stream banks, and pond edges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of white to pale green flowers. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in wetland restoration projects.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its specific leaf morphology and habitat preferences.
