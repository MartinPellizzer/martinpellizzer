Jasminum nobile var. quinquenerve

Jasminum nobile var. quinquenerve, commonly known as the five-veined jasmine, is a flowering shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has opposite, simple leaves with five distinct veins, giving it its botanical name. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its pleasant fragrance and compact growth habit.
