Jasminum breviflorum

Jasminum breviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered jasmine, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its sweet fragrance and compact growth habit.
