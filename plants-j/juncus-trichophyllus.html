Juncus trichophyllus

Juncus trichophyllus, commonly known as the hairy rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes, which are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and they are often adapted to thrive in moist or submerged soil conditions.

Juncus trichophyllus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
