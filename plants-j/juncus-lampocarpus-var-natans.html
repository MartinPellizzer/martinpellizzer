Juncus lampocarpus var. natans

Juncus lampocarpus var. natans, commonly known as floating rush, is a variant of the rush plant that exhibits unique adaptations for aquatic environments.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that float on the surface of freshwater bodies, allowing it to thrive in shallow, still waters. The plant's rhizomes form dense colonies, which help stabilize sediments and prevent erosion. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and lakes.

This species plays a vital role in providing habitat for various aquatic organisms and contributing to water filtration.
