Juncus antarcticus

Juncus antarcticus, commonly known as the Antarctic rush, is a flowering plant native to the southern hemisphere, particularly found in Antarctica and surrounding regions.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is adapted to extreme cold and nutrient-poor environments. The plant typically grows in dense, mat-like formations, with slender, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife in its harsh environment.

Its resilience makes it an important indicator species for studying ecological responses to climate change.
