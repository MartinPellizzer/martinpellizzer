Juncus hostii

Juncus hostii, commonly known as the giant rush, is a perennial plant native to the wetlands of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height, making it one of the tallest members of the Juncaceae family. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers in clusters near the base of the stems, typically in late summer. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a slight sheen, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Juncus hostii thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.
