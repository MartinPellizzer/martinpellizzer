Juncus crassifolius var. fascinatus

Juncus crassifolius var. fascinatus, commonly known as the fasciated rush, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and its ability to thrive in wet or moist environments. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that often form clumps, and its leaves are typically broad and flat. It is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

The fasciated variety is distinguished by its striking, variegated leaf patterns, making it a desirable ornamental plant.
