Jussiaea suffruticosa proles salicifolia

Jussiaea suffruticosa proles salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaved jussiaea, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves that are alternate and ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of riparian habitats.
