Jussiaea lagunae

Jussiaea lagunae, commonly known as the marsh willow or water willow, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems that often grow close to the water's surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, adapted to thrive in waterlogged soils.

Jussiaea lagunae plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
