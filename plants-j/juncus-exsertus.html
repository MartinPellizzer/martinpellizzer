Juncus exsertus

Juncus exsertus, commonly known as the tall rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and tall, cylindrical flower spikes that rise above the foliage. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense colonies in marshes and pond edges. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing light green to brown seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Juncus exsertus plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
