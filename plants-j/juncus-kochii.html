Juncus kochii

Juncus kochii, commonly known as the Koch's rush, is a perennial plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical flower spikes. The plant typically grows in moist or wet environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, producing small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense spikes.

Juncus kochii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
