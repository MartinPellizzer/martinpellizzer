Juncus pilosus subsp. fascicularis

Juncus pilosus subsp.

fascicularis, commonly known as the fascicled rush, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Australia. It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and slender, cylindrical stems that often grow in clusters.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems and a subject of study in plant taxonomy.
