Jacobanthus crassifolius

Jacobanthus crassifolius, commonly known as the broad-leafed sand verbena, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its robust, upright stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to violet. The plant has lance-shaped, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair, aiding in water retention. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Jacobanthus crassifolius is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
