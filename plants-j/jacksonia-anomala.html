Jacksonia anomala

Jacksonia anomala, commonly known as the New Zealand daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow or orange flowers surrounded by white ray florets. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and saline environments. It has small, narrow leaves and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Jacksonia anomala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
