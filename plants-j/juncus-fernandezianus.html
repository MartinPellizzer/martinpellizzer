Juncus fernandezianus

Juncus fernandezianus, commonly known as the Fernandez's rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and slender, cylindrical stems. Native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and wetlands. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
