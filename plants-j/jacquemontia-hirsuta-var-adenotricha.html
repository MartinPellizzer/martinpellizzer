Jacquemontia hirsuta var. adenotricha

Jacquemontia hirsuta var. adenotricha, commonly known as the hairy false nettle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, pale yellow flowers clustered in umbels. Its fruit is a capsule that releases tiny seeds when mature.

The species is notable for its distinctive foliage and its role in supporting local pollinators.
