Jatrops umbellata

Jatrops umbellata, commonly known as the yellow owl's clover, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for arid environments.

Jatrops umbellata is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
