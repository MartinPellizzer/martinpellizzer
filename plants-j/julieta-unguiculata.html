Julieta unguiculata

Julieta unguiculata, commonly known as the red spider lily, is a striking plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features strap-like, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its bulbs are toxic and contain alkaloids, making it important to handle with care.

Julieta unguiculata thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental foliage and blooms.
