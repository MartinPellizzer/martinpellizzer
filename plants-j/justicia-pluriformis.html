Justicia pluriformis

Justicia pluriformis, commonly known as the red-veined ivy, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and opposite, ovate leaves with prominent red veins. The plant produces clusters of tubular, spurred flowers that are typically pink or red, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, Justicia pluriformis is widely cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
