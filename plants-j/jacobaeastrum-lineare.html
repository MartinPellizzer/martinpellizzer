Jacobaeastrum lineare

Jacobaeastrum lineare, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a rough texture, and its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
