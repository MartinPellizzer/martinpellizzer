Juncus uliginosus var. subverticillatus

Juncus uliginosus var. subverticillatus, commonly known as the clustered rush, is a variant of the common rush that exhibits distinctive botanical features.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in poorly drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-lobed flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color.

This subspecies is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland habitats.
