Justicia humilis var. lanceolata

Justicia humilis var. lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking red or orange tubular flowers, which attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
