Juniperus occidentalis var. pleiosperma

Juniperus occidentalis var. pleiosperma, commonly known as the coastal juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and small, blue-gray berries that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. This variety is adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils. It typically grows in open woodlands and along shorelines, contributing to local biodiversity.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
