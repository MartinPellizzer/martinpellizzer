Justicia kempeana

Justicia kempeana, commonly known as the flame flower, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Justicia kempeana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand drought conditions.
