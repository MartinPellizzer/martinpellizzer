Justicia ramosissima

Justicia ramosissima, commonly known as the Texas starshade, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its branching structure and striking blue-violet flowers. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

The flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
