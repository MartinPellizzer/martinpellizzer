Juncus nodosus var. vulgaris

Juncus nodosus var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common rush, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and damp woodlands. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems that are often green or reddish in color, and it produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Juncus nodosus var. vulgaris plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
