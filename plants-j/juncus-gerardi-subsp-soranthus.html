Juncus gerardi subsp. soranthus

Juncus gerardi subsp.

soranthus, commonly known as the redstem sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds.

The plant features tall, slender stems that are reddish-brown in color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly rough texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
