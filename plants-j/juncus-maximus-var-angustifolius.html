Juncus maximus var. angustifolius

Juncus maximus var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrowleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 1 millimeter wide, growing in dense clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist to aquatic conditions, often found in marshes, ponds, and along stream banks.

Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
