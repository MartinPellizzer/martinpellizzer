Jambosa luzonensis

Jambosa luzonensis, commonly known as the Luzon jambosa, is a tropical shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and elliptical, leathery leaves. The plant produces edible fruits that are similar in appearance to lychees, with a rough, red outer skin and a sweet, white pulp. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
