Juncus lesueurii

Juncus lesueurii, commonly known as the Lesueur's rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and pond edges. The plant has narrow, cylindrical leaves that are usually green or reddish in color, and it produces small, clustered flowers during the spring and summer. Juncus lesueurii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
