Julocroton solonaceus f. parvifolia

Julocroton solonaceus f. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved julocroton, is a compact shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a glossy green.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for garden borders or container cultivation. Its foliage remains dense throughout the growing season, offering year-round visual interest.

The variety is noted for its compact growth habit and resilience to urban conditions, making it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
