Juncus scheuchzerioides

Juncus scheuchzerioides, commonly known as the European rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, pond edges, and stream banks. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Juncus scheuchzerioides plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
