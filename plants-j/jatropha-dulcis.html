Jatropha dulcis

Jatropha dulcis, commonly known as the sweet jatropha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain toxic seeds, which are not suitable for human consumption. Its botanical features include milky latex sap and a preference for well-drained soils.

Jatropha dulcis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
