Jatropha hieronymi

Jatropha hieronymi, commonly known as the African crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange-red, spiny capsules containing toxic seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a rough texture.

Jatropha hieronymi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some African countries.
