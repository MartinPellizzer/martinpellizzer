Juncus trachyphyllus

Juncus trachyphyllus, commonly known as the roughleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in the northern hemisphere.

It typically grows in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. The plant has slender, grass-like stems that are often reddish in color and bear small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are narrow and tough, with a rough texture that gives it its common name.

Juncus trachyphyllus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
