Juniperus recurva var. uncinata

Juniperus recurva var. uncinata, commonly known as the hooked juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The variety is distinguished by its hooked or curved leaf tips, which give it its scientific name.
