Jatropha tropaeolifolia

Jatropha tropaeolifolia, commonly known as the Mexican beauty leaf, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals, often in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing toxic seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
