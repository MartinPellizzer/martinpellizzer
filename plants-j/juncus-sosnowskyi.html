Juncus sosnowskyi

Juncus sosnowskyi, commonly known as Sosnowsky's rush, is a perennial plant native to the cold regions of northern Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, upright clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that appear in dense, spike-like clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of lakes and rivers.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and wetland restoration projects.
