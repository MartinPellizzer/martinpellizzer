Jurinea ciscaucasica

Jurinea ciscaucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is a perennial herb with silver-gray foliage and striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is characterized by its robust, branching stems and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It thrives in well-drained environments and is adapted to harsh, arid conditions.

Jurinea ciscaucasica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
