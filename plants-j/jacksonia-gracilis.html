Jacksonia gracilis

Jacksonia gracilis, commonly known as the slender Jacksonia, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its delicate, small yellow flowers surrounded by pale green bracts. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and forest margins, preferring well-drained soils. It is often found in the North Island and parts of the South Island of New Zealand.

Jacksonia gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
