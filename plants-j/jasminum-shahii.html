Jasminum shahii

Jasminum shahii, commonly known as the Persian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Jasminum shahii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its pleasant aroma.
