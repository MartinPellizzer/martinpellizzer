Jatropha mirandana

Jatropha mirandana, commonly known as the Mirandana or the Philippine physic nut, is a shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by distinctive orange-red berries containing toxic seeds. It is often found in moist, lowland areas and is adapted to tropical climates.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically cultivated for ornamental or agricultural purposes.
