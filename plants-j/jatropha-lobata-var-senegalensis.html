Jatropha lobata var. senegalensis

Jatropha lobata var. senegalensis, commonly known as the Senegalese physic nut, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces toxic, orange-red berries that contain seeds used for oil extraction. It thrives in dry, arid conditions and is often found in savannas and rocky areas.

Due to its toxicity, it is important to handle the plant with care.
