Justicia dodonaeifolia

Justicia dodonaeifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Mexican beauty, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that give it its common name, and it thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and foliage.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
