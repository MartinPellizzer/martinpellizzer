Juniperus communis var. nipponica

Juniperus communis var. nipponica, commonly known as Japanese juniper, is a variant of the common juniper native to Japan.

It is a coniferous evergreen shrub or small tree characterized by its scale-like leaves and blue-gray foliage. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are typically dark blue when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Cupressaceae family.
