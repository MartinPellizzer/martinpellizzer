Juniperus monticola subsp. orizabensis

Juniperus monticola subsp.

orizabensis, commonly known as the Orizaba juniper, is a subspecies native to high-altitude regions of Mexico. It is a coniferous evergreen shrub or small tree, characterized by its needle-like leaves and small, blue-gray berries.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, mountainous climates. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat for wildlife.

The subspecies is notable for its ecological resilience and is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptation to extreme environments.
