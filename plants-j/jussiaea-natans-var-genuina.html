Jussiaea natans var. genuina

Jussiaea natans var. genuina, commonly known as the floating meadow plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its floating leaves that are typically oval or rounded in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the water, making it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
