Jacaranda copaia subsp. spectabilis

Jacaranda copaia subsp.

spectabilis, commonly known as the purple jacaranda, is a deciduous tree native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in pendulous clusters during the spring and summer months.

The tree has lance-shaped leaves and produces woody, elongated pods that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark is smooth and light gray, with a distinctive fragrance when cut.
