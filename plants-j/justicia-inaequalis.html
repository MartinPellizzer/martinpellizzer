Justicia inaequalis

Justicia inaequalis, commonly known as the unequal false arum, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Acanthaceae family, characterized by its striking, showy bracts that resemble leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, often contrasting with its vibrant foliage. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and can vary in color from dark green to reddish hues.
