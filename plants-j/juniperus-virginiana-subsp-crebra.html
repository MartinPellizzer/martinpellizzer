Juniperus virginiana subsp. crebra

Juniperus virginiana subsp.

crebra, commonly known as the thickleaf juniper, is a subspecies native to the southeastern United States. It is a evergreen shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, sandy soils.

The plant features dense, scale-like leaves that are dark green and often arranged in flat, sprays. Its cones, which are small and berry-like, contain the aromatic seeds used in various traditional and commercial applications.

The thickleaf juniper is well adapted to harsh environments and plays an important role in local ecosystems.
