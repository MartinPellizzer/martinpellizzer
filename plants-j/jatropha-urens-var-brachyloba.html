Jatropha urens var. brachyloba

Jatropha urens var. brachyloba, commonly known as the short-stalked spurge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features small, yellow flowers clustered in umbels, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
