Justicia polymorpha

Justicia polymorpha, commonly known as red hot poker, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has erect, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Botanically, it belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in full sun and well-drained soil.
