Jatropha gossypiifolia f. angustiloba

Jatropha gossypiifolia f. angustiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved physic nut, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is known for its toxic latex, which contains compounds such as ricin, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
