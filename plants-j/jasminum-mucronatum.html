Jasminum mucronatum

Jasminum mucronatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often climbing stems and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
