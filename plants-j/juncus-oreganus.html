Juncus oreganus

Juncus oreganus, commonly known as Oregon rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as stream banks, wet meadows, and marshes. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often densely packed, and it produces small, spike-like clusters of greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and they are often used in traditional basket-making by indigenous peoples.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
