Justicia fulvicoma

Justicia fulvicoma, commonly known as the golden flame or yellow flame plant, is a flowering shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
