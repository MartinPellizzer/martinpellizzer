Joosia obtusa

Joosia obtusa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lanceolate leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. Joosia obtusa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in rock gardens and alpine plant collections.
