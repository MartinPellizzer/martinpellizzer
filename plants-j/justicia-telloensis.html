Justicia telloensis

Justicia telloensis, commonly known as Tello's glorybower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are arranged in dense spikes and are attractive to pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its vibrant blooms.
