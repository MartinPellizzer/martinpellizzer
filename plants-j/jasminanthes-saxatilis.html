Jasminanthes saxatilis

Jasminanthes saxatilis, commonly known as the rock jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub that thrives in rocky or limestone soils, often found in mountainous regions. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Jasminanthes saxatilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aromatic blooms.
