Justicia riedeliana

Justicia riedeliana, commonly known as the Riedel's flame flower, is a striking tropical plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red bracts that resemble flowers, though they are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, which are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Justicia riedeliana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
