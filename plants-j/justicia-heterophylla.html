Justicia heterophylla

Justicia heterophylla, commonly known as the variable leaf flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting blue or purple throat. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and features opposite, variable-shaped leaves that can be ovate, elliptic, or even lanceolate. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds and bees, making it an important species in ecological gardens.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant blooms.
