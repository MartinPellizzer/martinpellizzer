Juncus hallii

Juncus hallii, commonly known as the Hall's rush, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often used in floral arrangements due to their durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in spikes during the summer months. Juncus hallii thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
