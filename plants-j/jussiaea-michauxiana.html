Jussiaea michauxiana

Jussiaea michauxiana, commonly known as the marsh willow or river willow, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows near water, such as along riverbanks or in marshes. The plant has slender, willow-like branches and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slight waxy coating to help retain moisture.
