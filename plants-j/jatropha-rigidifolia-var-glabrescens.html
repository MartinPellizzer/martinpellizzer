Jatropha rigidifolia var. glabrescens

Jatropha rigidifolia var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed physic nut, is a shrub native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing toxic seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
