Justicia cuneifolia

Justicia cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf flamebeard, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking spikes of tubular, red or orange flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually opposite along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often grown as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms.

Justicia cuneifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
