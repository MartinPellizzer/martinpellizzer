Jasminum dzumacense

Jasminum dzumacense, commonly known as the Ecuadorian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a sweet, intoxicating aroma. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a smooth texture.

Jasminum dzumacense is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic properties.
