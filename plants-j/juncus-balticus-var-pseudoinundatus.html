Juncus balticus var. pseudoinundatus

Juncus balticus var. pseudoinundatus, commonly known as the Baltic rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in Europe.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish-brown in color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in moist to aquatic conditions, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
