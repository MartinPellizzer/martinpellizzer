Juniperus thurifera var. africana

Juniperus thurifera var. africana, commonly known as the African juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain the aromatic berries, which are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
