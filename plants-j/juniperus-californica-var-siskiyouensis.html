Juniperus californica var. siskiyouensis

Juniperus californica var. siskiyouensis, commonly known as the Siskiyou juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the Klamath Mountains in northern California and southern Oregon.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the cool, moist climates of its native range.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
