Juncus koidzumii

Juncus koidzumii, commonly known as the Koidzumi rush, is a perennial plant native to Japan.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems that often form dense clumps. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, streambanks, and wet meadows. Its narrow leaves are usually green and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late summer.

Juncus koidzumii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in waterlogged soils.
