Juncus striatus var. lagenarius

Juncus striatus var. lagenarius, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and covered with fine, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of greenish-white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
