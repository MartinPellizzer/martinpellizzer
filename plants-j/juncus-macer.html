Juncus macer

Juncus macer, commonly known as the soft rush, is a perennial grass-like plant that belongs to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, hollow stems that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually clustered in spikes at the top of the stems. Juncus macer thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
