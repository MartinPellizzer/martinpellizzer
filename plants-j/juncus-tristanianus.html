Juncus tristanianus

Juncus tristanianus, commonly known as the Tristan juncus, is a perennial plant native to the subantarctic region of the Southern Ocean.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a low-growing rosette. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in coastal environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
