Jasminocereus thouarsii subsp. howellii

Jasminocereus thouarsii subsp.

howellii, commonly known as the Howell's night-blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America. It is characterized by its spineless stems and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that open at night.

The plant belongs to the Cactaceae family and is adapted to thrive in hot, dry environments. Its flowers emit a sweet scent to attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

This subspecies is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
