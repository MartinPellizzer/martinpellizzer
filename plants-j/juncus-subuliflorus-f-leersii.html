Juncus subuliflorus f. leersii

Juncus subuliflorus f. leersii, commonly known as the Leers' rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Juncaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, needle-like leaves. This plant produces small, clustered spikelets that bear tiny flowers, typically in shades of green or brown. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, stream banks, and swampy areas.

The subspecies f. leersii is distinguished by its unique floral structure and growth habits compared to the typical form of Juncus subuliflorus.
