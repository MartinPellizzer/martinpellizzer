Justicia chlorostachya

Justicia chlorostachya, commonly known as the silverleaf false arum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and emit a mild fragrance, especially in the evening.

The flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
