Juncus fontanesii

Juncus fontanesii, commonly known as the Fontanesii rush, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bright green and can reach heights of up to 2 feet. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters at the tips of its stems, usually in late spring to early summer.

Juncus fontanesii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
