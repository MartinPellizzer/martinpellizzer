Jatropha longipedunculata

Jatropha longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-peduncled physic nut, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, toxic berries that contain seeds used in the production of biofuel. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and savannas.

Due to its potential for renewable energy, it has attracted attention for sustainable development projects.
