Jussiaea repens var. inflata

Jussiaea repens var. inflata, commonly known as the inflated bladderwort, is a fascinating aquatic plant characterized by its unique, inflated, bladder-like structures.

These specialized organs are used to trap small aquatic organisms, enabling the plant to obtain nutrients through carnivory. It typically grows in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes. The plant's stems are often prostrate, allowing it to spread across the water's surface.

Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, relying on water dispersal for propagation.
