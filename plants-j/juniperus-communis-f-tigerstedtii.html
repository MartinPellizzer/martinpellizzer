Juniperus communis f. tigerstedtii

Juniperus communis f. tigerstedtii, commonly known as the Tigerstedt's juniper, is a compact, slow-growing shrub native to northern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage with a distinctive bluish-white underside, creating a striking visual contrast. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is well-suited for colder climates and can withstand harsh winter conditions.
