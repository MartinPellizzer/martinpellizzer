Juncus himalensis var. schlagintweitii

Juncus himalensis var. schlagintweitii, commonly known as the Himalayan rush, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in dense, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in spike-like clusters at the top of the stems.

This variant is noted for its adaptability to cold climates and its ability to thrive in high-altitude environments.
