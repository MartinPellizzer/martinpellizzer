Justicia denticulata

Justicia denticulata, commonly known as the toothed justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with finely serrated edges, giving it its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Justicia denticulata is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
