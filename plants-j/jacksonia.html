Jacksonia

Jacksonia, commonly known as the New Zealand daisy, is a genus of flowering plants in the daisy family, Asteraceae.

It consists of several species, most notably Jacksonia hederifolia, which is native to New Zealand. These plants are characterized by their showy, daisy-like flowers with bright yellow centers and white or pale blue petals. They typically grow in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soils.

Jacksonia species are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
