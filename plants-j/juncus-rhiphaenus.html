Juncus rhiphaenus

Juncus rhiphaenus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in North America.

It has slender, cylindrical stems that are often green or reddish in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes during the summer months. The plant is adapted to moist environments and has fibrous roots that help stabilize soil in wet areas.

Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically growing in dense clumps, and it is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to filter water and prevent erosion.
