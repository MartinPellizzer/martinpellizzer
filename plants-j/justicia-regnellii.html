Justicia regnellii

Justicia regnellii, commonly known as red hot poker, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that bear bright red bracts, which are often mistaken for petals. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Justicia regnellii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
