Juniperus sargentii var. coeruleum

Juniperus sargentii var. coeruleum, commonly known as the blue juniper, is a compact, evergreen shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, bluish-gray foliage that provides a striking contrast to its dark bark. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are typically blue or purple in color, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its slow growth rate and ornamental value, it is often used in landscape design for its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
