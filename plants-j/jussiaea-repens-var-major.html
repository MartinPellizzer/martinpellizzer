Jussiaea repens var. major

Jussiaea repens var. major, commonly known as the creeping jussiaea, is a flowering plant native to wetland and marshy environments in South America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with thin, fleshy stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the wet season. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and covered with fine, soft hairs.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
