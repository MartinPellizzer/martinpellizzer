Jussiaea myrtifolia f. hassleriana

Jussiaea myrtifolia f. hassleriana, commonly known as the Hassler's jussiaea, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, often accompanied by aromatic leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
