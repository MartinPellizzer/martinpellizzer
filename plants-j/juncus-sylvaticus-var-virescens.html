Juncus sylvaticus var. virescens

Juncus sylvaticus var. virescens, commonly known as the green rush, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, which are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along streams.

Its dense growth habit makes it useful for erosion control and as a ornamental plant in water gardens.
