Juncus filiformis

Juncus filiformis, commonly known as the slender rush, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikes of small, greenish flowers. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its slender stems and fibrous roots make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Juncus filiformis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
