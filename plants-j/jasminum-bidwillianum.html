Jasminum bidwillianum

Jasminum bidwillianum, commonly known as the golden jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, simple, and usually lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or golden in color, blooming in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its flowers are tubular with five petals and are often visited by insects such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its pleasant fragrance.
