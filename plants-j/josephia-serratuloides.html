Josephia serratuloides

Josephia serratuloides, commonly known as the mountain jewelflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapting to the harsh conditions of its natural habitat.
