Juncus acuminatus var. legitimus

Juncus acuminatus var. legitimus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems that grow in dense clusters, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a pointed tip, and are usually covered in a fine layer of white wax.

This variety is often found in marshes, pond edges, and other moist soils, contributing to the ecological stability of these habitats.
