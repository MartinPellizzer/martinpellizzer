Jasminum bicorollatum

Jasminum bicorollatum, commonly known as the Arabian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often releasing a sweet scent at night. The plant has opposite, simple, and lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green. It thrives in warm, arid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Jasminum bicorollatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
