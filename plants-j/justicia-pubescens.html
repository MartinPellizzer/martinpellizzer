Justicia pubescens

Justicia pubescens, commonly known as the silverleaf false arum, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, dark green, velvety leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden areas.

Due to its attractive foliage and unique floral display, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
