Juniperus shepherdii

Juniperus shepherdii, commonly known as the mountain juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are typically dark blue when mature. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and mountainous regions.

Juniperus shepherdii is well adapted to harsh conditions, making it a hardy and resilient species.
