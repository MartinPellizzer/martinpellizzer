Juncus megalophyllus

Juncus megalophyllus, commonly known as the giant rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, covered with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, arranged in spike-like inflorescences. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands in moist soils.

Juncus megalophyllus plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
