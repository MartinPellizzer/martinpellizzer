Jussiaea frutescens

Jussiaea frutescens, commonly known as the water hyssop, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in spikes. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Jussiaea frutescens plays an important role in supporting aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small aquatic organisms.
