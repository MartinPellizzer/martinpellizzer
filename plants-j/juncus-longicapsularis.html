Juncus longicapsularis

Juncus longicapsularis, commonly known as the long-capsule rush, is a flowering plant species native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and cylindrical flower clusters. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and pond edges, and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in spike-like inflorescences, and it produces small, three-lobed seeds.

Juncus longicapsularis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
