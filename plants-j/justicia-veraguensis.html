Justicia veraguensis

Justicia veraguensis, commonly known as the Veragua glorybower, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

The flowers are borne in terminal clusters and are pollinated primarily by birds, especially hummingbirds.
