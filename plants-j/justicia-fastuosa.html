Justicia fastuosa

Justicia fastuosa, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Justicia fastuosa is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
