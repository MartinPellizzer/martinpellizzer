Justicia craibii

Justicia craibii, commonly known as the Cape jasmine, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in warmer climates.

The flowers are often visited by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance.
