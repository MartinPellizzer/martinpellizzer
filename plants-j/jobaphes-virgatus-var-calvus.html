Jobaphes virgatus var. calvus

Jobaphes virgatus var. calvus, commonly known as the smoothleaf jobaphes, is a variant of the Jobaphes virgatus species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves, which distinguish it from other varieties within the same species. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in high humidity. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a leathery texture, contributing to its adaptability in humid conditions.

The flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in late spring to early summer, adding to its ornamental value in botanical gardens.
