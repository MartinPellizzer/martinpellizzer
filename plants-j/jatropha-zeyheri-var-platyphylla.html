Jatropha zeyheri var. platyphylla

Jatropha zeyheri var. platyphylla, commonly known as the broadleaf jatropha, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that are followed by orange-red berries containing toxic seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for use in traditional medicine, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
