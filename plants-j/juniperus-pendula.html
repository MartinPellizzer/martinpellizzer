Juniperus pendula

Juniperus pendula, commonly known as the weeping juniper, is a coniferous evergreen shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that droop gracefully, giving it a distinctive, cascading appearance. The plant produces small, scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and whitish underneath, providing a striking visual contrast. Its cones are typically red or purple and contain aromatic, berry-like seeds that are a food source for various wildlife.

Juniperus pendula thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
