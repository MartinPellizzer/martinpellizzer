Juncus flaccidus

Juncus flaccidus, commonly known as the slender rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and covered with fine, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in hue, appearing in late summer to early fall. Juncus flaccidus thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in areas with standing water or saturated ground.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
