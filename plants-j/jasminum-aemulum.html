Jasminum aemulum

Jasminum aemulum, commonly known as the lesser jasmine, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly India and the Himalayas.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring small, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The flowers are usually borne in axillary or terminal clusters and bloom during the summer months. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
