Juniperus deppeana subsp. pachyphlaea

Juniperus deppeana subsp.

pachyphlaea, commonly known as the Sierra Madre juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which consists of scale-like leaves arranged in dense clusters.

The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its morphology and ecological role make it a notable species in the Juniperus genus.
