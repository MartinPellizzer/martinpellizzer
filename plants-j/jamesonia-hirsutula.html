Jamesonia hirsutula

Jamesonia hirsutula, commonly known as the hirsute jamesonia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant blooms with showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy habitats.

Its unique botanical structure makes it a distinctive member of the Ericaceae family.
