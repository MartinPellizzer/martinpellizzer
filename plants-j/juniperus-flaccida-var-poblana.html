Juniperus flaccida var. poblana

Juniperus flaccida var. poblana, commonly known as the Mexican juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
