Juncus gerardi var. acutiflorus

Juncus gerardi var. acutiflorus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish-brown in color, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
