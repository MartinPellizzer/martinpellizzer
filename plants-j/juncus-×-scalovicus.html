Juncus × scalovicus

Juncus × scalovicus, commonly known as the hybrid rush, is a perennial plant that thrives in wetland environments.

It is a natural hybrid between Juncus effuses and Juncus tenuis, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant features dense clusters of grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, greenish-white flowers. It is often found in marshes, pond edges, and other moist habitats.

Its adaptability makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
