Justicia lindaui

Justicia lindaui, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds and bees, making it an important species for pollinators.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
