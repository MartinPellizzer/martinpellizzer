Jurinea ambigua

Jurinea ambigua, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime environments. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs.

Jurinea ambigua is well adapted to salt spray and strong winds, making it a resilient species in harsh coastal conditions.
