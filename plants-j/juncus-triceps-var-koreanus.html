Juncus triceps var. koreanus

Juncus triceps var. koreanus, commonly known as the Korean three-branched rush, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems that often branch into three distinct shoots, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist or wet environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered spikes of greenish or brownish flowers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
