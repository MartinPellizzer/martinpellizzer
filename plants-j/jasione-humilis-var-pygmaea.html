Jasione humilis var. pygmaea

Jasione humilis var. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, linear leaves that are clustered at the base, and it prefers well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils. It is often found in open habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands.

Jasione humilis var. pygmaea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
