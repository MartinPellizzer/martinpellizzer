Juncus balticus f. laxior

Juncus balticus f. laxior, commonly known as the loose-junco, is a variant of the common rush that exhibits distinctive botanical features.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, grass-like stems that grow in dense clusters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, three-lobed flowers. It typically thrives in wet or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to moist soils.

Its morphology and growth habit make it a notable species within the Juncaceae family.
