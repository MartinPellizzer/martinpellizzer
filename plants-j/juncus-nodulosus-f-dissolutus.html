Juncus nodulosus f. dissolutus

Juncus nodulosus f. dissolutus, commonly known as the loosehead rush, is a variant of the common rush species characterized by its loosely clustered, often drooping inflorescences.

It is a perennial plant native to wetlands and moist soils in temperate regions, typically found in marshes, pond margins, and along streams. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, three-lobed flowers that are usually greenish or reddish in color. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
