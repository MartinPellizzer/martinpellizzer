Jacksonia calycina

Jacksonia calycina, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is well adapted to sandy soils. It produces small, green, egg-shaped fruits that contain seeds.
