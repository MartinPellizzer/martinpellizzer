Jurinea georgei

Jurinea georgei, commonly known as the George's sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its rosette of narrow, silver-gray leaves and striking blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to saline and windy coastal environments. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Jurinea georgei is valued for its ornamental beauty and resilience in harsh conditions.
