Juglans gonroku

Juglans gonroku, commonly known as the Japanese black walnut, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the walnut family, Juglandaceae, and is characterized by its large, compound leaves and distinctive, edible nuts. The tree produces yellowish-green flowers in catkins, and its fruit is a hard-shelled nut with a sweet, oily kernel inside. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

The wood of Juglans gonroku is highly valued for its strength and fine grain, making it a prized material in carpentry and furniture making.
