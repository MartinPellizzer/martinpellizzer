Jasione supina subsp. akmanii

Jasione supina subsp.

akmanii, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies akmanii is distinguished by its compact growth habit and specific geographic distribution within the region.
