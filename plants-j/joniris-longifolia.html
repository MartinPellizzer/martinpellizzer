Joniris longifolia

Joniris longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved starflower, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

The species is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
