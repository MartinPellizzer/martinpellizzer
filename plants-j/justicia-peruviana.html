Justicia peruviana

Justicia peruviana, commonly known as the flame flower or Peruvian whipstick, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bright, showy blooms that attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

Justicia peruviana is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of growing conditions, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
