Jurinea nargalensis

Jurinea nargalensis, commonly known as the Nargalensis sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the cold, arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features silver-gray foliage with a dense covering of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its flowers are typically blue or violet, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Jurinea nargalensis is well adapted to rocky and saline soils, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
