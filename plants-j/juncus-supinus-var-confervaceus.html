Juncus supinus var. confervaceus

Juncus supinus var. confervaceus, commonly known as the silky bulrush, is a flowering plant species native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of soft, grass-like leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences, which are often submerged or partially submerged in water. It thrives in shallow, freshwater habitats such as marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes.

This species plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
