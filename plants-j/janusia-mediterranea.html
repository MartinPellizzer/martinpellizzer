Janusia mediterranea

Janusia mediterranea, commonly known as the Mediterranean spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect stems and opposite, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often toxic species.
