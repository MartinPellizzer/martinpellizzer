Justicia schliebenii

Justicia schliebenii, commonly known as the Schlieben's flameflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by birds, particularly sunbirds, due to their bright colors and nectar-rich structure.
