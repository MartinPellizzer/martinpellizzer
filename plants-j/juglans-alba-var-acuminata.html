Juglans alba var. acuminata

Juglans alba var. acuminata, commonly known as the white walnut variant with acuminate leaves, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the walnut family, Juglandaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with long, pointed leaflets. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by edible nuts enclosed in a thick, husky shell. Its wood is valued for its strength and durability, making it useful in furniture and construction.

This variety is often found in temperate forests and thrives in well-drained soils.
