Juncus planifolius

Juncus planifolius, commonly known as the flatleaf rush, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and typically grows in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like stems that are often used in basket weaving and thatching. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish or brownish and appear in clusters at the tips of the stems.

Juncus planifolius plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
