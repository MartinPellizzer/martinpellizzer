Jussiaea repens var. hispida

Jussiaea repens var. hispida, commonly known as the hairy trailing jussiaea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial that thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, adding delicate beauty to its foliage. Its leaves are typically hairy and elliptical, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
