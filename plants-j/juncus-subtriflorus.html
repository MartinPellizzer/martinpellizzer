Juncus subtriflorus

Juncus subtriflorus, commonly known as the three-flowered rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae and is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Juncus subtriflorus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
