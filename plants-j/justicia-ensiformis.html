Justicia ensiformis

Justicia ensiformis, commonly known as the swordleaf false arum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its sword-shaped, lanceolate leaves that are typically bright green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, white to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its attractive foliage and flowers.
