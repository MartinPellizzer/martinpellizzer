Justicia hygrobia

Justicia hygrobia, commonly known as the waterleaf or waterleaf plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped, succulent leaves that are adapted to retain moisture, making it drought-tolerant. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, often found in moist or wet environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
