Juniperus foetidissima var. squarrosa

Juniperus foetidissima var. squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrosa juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, scaly foliage and small, berry-like cones that mature to a dark blue color. The plant is evergreen, with needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

This variety is noted for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh conditions.
