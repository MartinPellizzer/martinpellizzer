Juncoides hyperborea

Juncoides hyperborea, commonly known as the Arctic rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-poor soils, often in tundra or alpine environments. Its leaves are narrow and cylindrical, adapted to withstand harsh cold and strong winds.

Juncoides hyperborea plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for Arctic wildlife.
