Jacaranda obtusifolia subsp. rhombifolia

Jacaranda obtusifolia subsp.

rhombifolia, commonly known as the African jacaranda, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its striking purple-pink flowers that bloom in summer, forming long, drooping panicles.

The leaves are ovate to rhombic in shape, with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. The tree produces woody pods that contain flat, elliptical seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.
