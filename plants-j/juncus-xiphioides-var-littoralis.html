Juncus xiphioides var. littoralis

Juncus xiphioides var. littoralis, commonly known as the salt marsh rush, is a perennial plant native to coastal areas of the United States.

It thrives in brackish or saline wetlands, demonstrating a high tolerance for saltwater conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. It produces small, spike-like clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety plays an important ecological role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various wetland species.
