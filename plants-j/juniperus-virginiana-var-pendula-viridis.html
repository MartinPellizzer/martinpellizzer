Juniperus virginiana var. pendula-viridis

Juniperus virginiana var. pendula-viridis, commonly known as the hanging green juniper, is a variant of the eastern red cedar.

It is characterized by its drooping branches and bluish-green foliage, which give it a distinctive appearance. This plant is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in well-drained soils. It is a slow-growing evergreen shrub or small tree that provides habitat for various wildlife species.

The cones of this juniper produce edible berries that are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
