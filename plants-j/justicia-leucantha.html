Justicia leucantha

Justicia leucantha, commonly known as the white flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy white to pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are known to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
