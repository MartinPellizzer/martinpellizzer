Jacquinia obovata var. obovata

Jacquinia obovata var. obovata, commonly known as the broad-ovate jacquinia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features opposite, ovate to broadly ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly serrated margin. Its flowers are small, clustered in terminal panicles, and are usually pink to purple in color.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
