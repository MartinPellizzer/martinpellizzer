Juncus capillaris

Juncus capillaris, commonly known as the slender rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green and have a slight reddish tint at the base. The plant produces small, pale green flowers in dense, spike-like clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Juncus capillaris plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
