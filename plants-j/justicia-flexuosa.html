Justicia flexuosa

Justicia flexuosa, commonly known as the flexible flameflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring upright, branched stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Justicia flexuosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to warm, humid climates.
