Justicia dispar

Justicia dispar, commonly known as the spreading flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting blue or purple throat. The plant produces showy inflorescences that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Justicia dispar thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
