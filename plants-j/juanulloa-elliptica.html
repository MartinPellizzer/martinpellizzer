Juanulloa elliptica

Juanulloa elliptica, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Erythroxylum ellipticum*, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and clusters of small, red flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in humid, shaded environments.

Juanulloa elliptica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
