Jasminum burmannianum

Jasminum burmannianum, commonly known as the Burmannian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a sweet, honey-like scent. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Jasminum burmannianum is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its use in traditional medicine.
