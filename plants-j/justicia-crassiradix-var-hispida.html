Justicia crassiradix var. hispida

Justicia crassiradix var. hispida, commonly known as the roughleaf flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It features showy, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has hairy, rough-textured leaves that give it its varietal name, "hispida.". It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in tropical and subtropical regions.
