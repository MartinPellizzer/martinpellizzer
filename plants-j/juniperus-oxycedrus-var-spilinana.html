Juniperus oxycedrus var. spilinana

Juniperus oxycedrus var. spilinana, commonly known as the Spanish juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of scale-like leaves that are blue-green in color. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are typically toxic to humans but serve as a food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
