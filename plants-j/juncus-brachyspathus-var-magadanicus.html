Juncus brachyspathus var. magadanicus

Juncus brachyspathus var. magadanicus, commonly known as the Magadan rush, is a perennial plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and compact growth habit, typically forming dense mats or clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wet, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in tundra and moist meadow habitats.

Its botanical structure reflects its adaptation to cold, harsh environments, making it an important component of high-latitude ecosystems.
