Juncus balticus f. opulentus

Juncus balticus f. opulentus, commonly known as the opulent rush, is a variant of the common rush that exhibits distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its ability to thrive in wetland environments. The plant produces tall, cylindrical spikes of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats.
