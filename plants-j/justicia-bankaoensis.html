Justicia bankaoensis

Justicia bankaoensis, commonly known as the Bankao flameflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth surface with a slightly glossy appearance.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by birds, particularly sunbirds, due to their bright color and nectar-rich structure.
