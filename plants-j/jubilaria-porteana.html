Jubilaria porteana

Jubilaria porteana, commonly known as the Brazilian broom, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, branching structure and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring. Jubilaria porteana is adapted to coastal and sandy soils, often found in dune ecosystems.

Its rapid growth and hardy nature make it a useful plant for stabilizing soils and preventing erosion.
