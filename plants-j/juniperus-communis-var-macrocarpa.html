Juniperus communis var. macrocarpa

Juniperus communis var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the common juniper, is a subspecies of the juniper shrub native to the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are rich in essential oils and used in various traditional and commercial applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This subspecies is notable for its aromatic properties and its role in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
