Jasminum pubescens

Jasminum pubescens, commonly known as the hairy jasmine, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Jasminum pubescens is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
