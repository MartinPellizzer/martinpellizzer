Jacquemontia fruticulosa f. subsericea

Jacquemontia fruticulosa f. subsericea, commonly known as the silky jacquemontia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

The subspecies subsericea is distinguished by its finely hairy stems and leaves, giving it a silky appearance.
