Justicia panarensis

Justicia panarensis, commonly known as the Panamanian flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Justicia panarensis thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.
