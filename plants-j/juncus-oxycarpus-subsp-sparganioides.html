Juncus oxycarpus subsp. sparganioides

Juncus oxycarpus subsp.

sparganioides, commonly known as the slender rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America. It typically grows in dense clumps, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often reddish at the base.

The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of greenish-white flowers during the summer months. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form extensive colonies in moist soils.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
