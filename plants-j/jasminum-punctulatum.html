Jasminum punctulatum

Jasminum punctulatum, commonly known as the dotted jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically opposite, simple, and have a slightly rough texture. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is commonly found in tropical and subtropical regions.
