Justicia liebmanii

Justicia liebmanii, commonly known as the Liebman's flame flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to gardens.
