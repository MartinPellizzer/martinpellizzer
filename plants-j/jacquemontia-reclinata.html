Jacquemontia reclinata

Jacquemontia reclinata, commonly known as the hanging morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped, pale yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain seeds with a distinctive spiral pattern. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural collections.
