Jasminum umbellulatum

Jasminum umbellulatum, commonly known as the umbellate jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it the name "umbellate.". The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, with opposite, simple leaves that are usually oval or elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

The flowers are used in perfumery and traditional medicine, highlighting its significance in both horticulture and cultural practices.
