Juncus dregeanus subsp. dregeanus

Juncus dregeanus subsp.

dregeanus, commonly known as the dwarf rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in wet or damp environments such as marshes, stream banks, and seasonal pools.

The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that are often hairy and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish-white flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
