Jasminum lonchophyllum

Jasminum lonchophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a woody shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy appearance.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its use in traditional medicine.
