Justicia forbesii

Justicia forbesii, commonly known as the silverleaf jewelweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking orange to red tubular flowers, which bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
