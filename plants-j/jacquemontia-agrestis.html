Jacquemontia agrestis

Jacquemontia agrestis, commonly known as the prairie morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows up to 2 meters tall, featuring sprawling vines with heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually blue to purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils.

Jacquemontia agrestis is often found in prairies, roadsides, and other open habitats where it can spread aggressively.
