Jarandersonia yahyantha

Jarandersonia yahyantha, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a rare and striking plant native to the cloud forests of the Andes.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in dense, mist-like clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, enhancing its ornamental appeal. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its limited natural range and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
