Jamesia americana var. americana

Jamesia americana var. americana, commonly known as the American roseleaf, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white bloom. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and limited natural range, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
