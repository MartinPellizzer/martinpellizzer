Jatropha excisa var. viridiflora

Jatropha excisa var. viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered jatropha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking green flowers, which contrast with its dark green leaves. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain toxic seeds, a common trait among the genus Jatropha. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
