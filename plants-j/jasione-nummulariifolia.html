Jasione nummulariifolia

Jasione nummulariifolia, commonly known as the silverweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats. The plant features a low-growing rosette of silver-gray, spoon-shaped leaves that give it its common name. It produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in the summer months.

Jasione nummulariifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
