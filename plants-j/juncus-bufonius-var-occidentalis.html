Juncus bufonius var. occidentalis

Juncus bufonius var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western bullrush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial grass-like plant that grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and pond edges. The plant has cylindrical, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, greenish flowers. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its adaptability to moist environments makes it a common indicator species in wetland ecosystems.
