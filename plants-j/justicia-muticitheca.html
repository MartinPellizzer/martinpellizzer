Justicia muticitheca

Justicia muticitheca, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with contrasting yellow or white markings. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are borne in clusters at the ends of branches, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.

Botanically, it belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to various soil types.
