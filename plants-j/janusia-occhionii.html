Janusia occhionii

Janusia occhionii, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical name reflects its distinctive floral structure and historical significance in herbal medicine.
