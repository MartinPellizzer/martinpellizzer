Juncus aletaiensis

Juncus aletaiensis, commonly known as the Aletai rush, is a perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage composed of narrow, cylindrical leaves. The plant typically grows in saline or alkaline soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

Juncus aletaiensis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
