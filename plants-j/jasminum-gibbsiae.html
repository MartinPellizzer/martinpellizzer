Jasminum gibbsiae

Jasminum gibbsiae, commonly known as the Gibbs' jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the winter months. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is adapted to thrive in arid and coastal environments. It is often found growing in rocky soils and is considered a rare and endemic species.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
