Juncus sphaerocarpus var. amuricus

Juncus sphaerocarpus var. amuricus, commonly known as the Amur rush, is a perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves and produces small, spherical seed heads. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
