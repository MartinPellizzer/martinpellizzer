Jasminum biflorum

Jasminum biflorum, commonly known as the two-flowered jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a sweet scent. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy appearance.

Jasminum biflorum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
