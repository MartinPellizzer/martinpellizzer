Jatropha serrulata

Jatropha serrulata, commonly known as the serrated physic nut, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with serrated edges, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing toxic seeds. Jatropha serrulata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and potential applications in biofuel research.
