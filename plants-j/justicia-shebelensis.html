Justicia shebelensis

Justicia shebelensis, commonly known as the Shebel's flameflower, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of white pubescence.

Justicia shebelensis thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
