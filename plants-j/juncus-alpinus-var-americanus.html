Juncus alpinus var. americanus

Juncus alpinus var. americanus, commonly known as the American rush, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and is often found growing in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. It has fibrous roots and rhizomes, which help it thrive in waterlogged soils.

This variety is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
