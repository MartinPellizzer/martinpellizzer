Justicia alchorneeticola

Justicia alchorneeticola, commonly known as the red-veined justicia, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves with prominent red veins. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its vibrant foliage and floral display.
