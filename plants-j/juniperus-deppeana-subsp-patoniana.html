Juniperus deppeana subsp. patoniana

Juniperus deppeana subsp.

patoniana, commonly known as the Paton's juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which consists of scale-like leaves arranged in flattened sprays.

The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and its role in local ecosystems.
