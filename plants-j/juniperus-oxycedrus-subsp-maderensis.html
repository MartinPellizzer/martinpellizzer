Juniperus oxycedrus subsp. maderensis

Juniperus oxycedrus subsp.

maderensis, commonly known as the Maderan juniper, is a subspecies native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is a small evergreen shrub or tree with needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain wildlife. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
