Juglans pubescens

Juglans pubescens, commonly known as the Chinese hickory, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 20–40 cm long and have 15–23 pairs of ovate leaflets. The tree produces edible nuts enclosed in a spiky husk, which ripen in late summer to early autumn. Its bark is thick and ridged, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Juglans pubescens is valued for its timber and is also cultivated for its nutritious nuts.
