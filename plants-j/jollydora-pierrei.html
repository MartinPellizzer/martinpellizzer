Jollydora pierrei

Jollydora pierrei, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that resemble a jolly face, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in the humid, shaded environments of the island's rainforests. Its botanical features include long, slender stems and large, showy blooms that come in shades of yellow, orange, and red.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered a vulnerable species.
