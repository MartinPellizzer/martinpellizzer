Jurinea bipinnata

Jurinea bipinnata, commonly known as the silverleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with silver-gray foliage due to dense pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important species in desert ecosystems.
