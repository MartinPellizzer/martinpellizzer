Juniperus blancoi var. mucronata

Juniperus blancoi var. mucronata, commonly known as the Mexican juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various desert wildlife species.
