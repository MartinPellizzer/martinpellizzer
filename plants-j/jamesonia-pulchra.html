Jamesonia pulchra

Jamesonia pulchra, commonly known as the pink-flowered saxifrage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant features basal rosettes of lance-shaped, blue-green leaves and produces showy, pink to purple flowers in early spring. Its flowering stems are slender and often bear multiple inflorescences.

Jamesonia pulchra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens with suitable conditions.
