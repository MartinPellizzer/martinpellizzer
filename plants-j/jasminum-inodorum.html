Jasminum inodorum

Jasminum inodorum, commonly known as the sweet shrub or winter jasmine, is a flowering plant native to southern China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to yellow flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a delicate, sweet aroma that attracts pollinators.

Jasminum inodorum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens and as a hedge plant.
