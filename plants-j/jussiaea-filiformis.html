Jussiaea filiformis

Jussiaea filiformis, commonly known as the slender water-milfoil, is a semi-aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in ponds, streams, and wetlands. The plant has finely divided, ribbon-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a delicate appearance. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences and bloom in early summer.

Jussiaea filiformis plays a role in stabilizing aquatic habitats and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
