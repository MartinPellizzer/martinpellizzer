Justicia linarioides

Justicia linarioides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender, branched stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, with a silvery-green coloration.

Justicia linarioides thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
