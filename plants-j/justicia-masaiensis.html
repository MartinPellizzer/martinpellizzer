Justicia masaiensis

Justicia masaiensis, commonly known as the Masai glorybower, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The flowers are pollinated by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
