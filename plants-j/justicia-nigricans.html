Justicia nigricans

Justicia nigricans, commonly known as black jewelweed, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, dark purple to black flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and forest understories. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
