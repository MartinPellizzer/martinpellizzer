Josephia speciosa

Josephia speciosa, commonly known as the showy josephia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its vibrant yellow to orange blooms. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
