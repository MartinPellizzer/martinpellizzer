Jamesonia imbricata var. cinnamomea

Jamesonia imbricata var. cinnamomea, commonly known as the cinnamonleaf hollyhock, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive cinnamon-colored underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, showy, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value lies in its striking foliage and long-lasting blooms.
