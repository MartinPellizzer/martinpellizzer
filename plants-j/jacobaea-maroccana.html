Jacobaea maroccana

Jacobaea maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan ragwort, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces a milky sap that contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to livestock. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Jacobaea maroccana is often found in dry, rocky soils and is part of the Asteraceae family.
