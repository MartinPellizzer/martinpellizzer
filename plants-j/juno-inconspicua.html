Juno inconspicua

Juno inconspicua, commonly known as the slender juniper, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and produces blue or purple berries that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and as a landscape plant.

Juno inconspicua is also valued for its fragrant foliage and ability to attract wildlife.
