Justicia leptantha subsp. late-ovata

Justicia leptantha subsp.

late-ovata, commonly known as the late-ovate justicia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central America and northern South America. It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of red or orange.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. It is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other riparian habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
