Jasminum seguinii var. cordatulum

Jasminum seguinii var. cordatulum, commonly known as the heart-shaped jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its aromatic blooms and attractive foliage in gardens and landscapes.
