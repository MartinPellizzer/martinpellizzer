Jacaranda irwinii

Jacaranda irwinii, commonly known as the Sydney Jacaranda, is a striking ornamental tree native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in spring. The tree has a broad, spreading crown with lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its bark is smooth and greyish, with a pleasant aromatic scent.
