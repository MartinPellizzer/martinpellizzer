Juniperus sabina var. tamariscifolia

Juniperus sabina var. tamariscifolia, commonly known as the dwarf juniper or Tamarisk juniper, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in dense clusters. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are highly aromatic and used in various traditional and commercial applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its slow growth rate and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for landscaping in arid and semi-arid climates.
