Juncus capensis var. capensis

Juncus capensis var. capensis, commonly known as the Cape rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. The plant has slender, grass-like stems that are usually green or reddish in color, and it produces small, spike-like clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in wetland areas.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and water fluctuations.
