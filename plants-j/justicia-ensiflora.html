Justicia ensiflora

Justicia ensiflora, commonly known as the swordleaf false arum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its sword-shaped leaves and striking, often violet or purple, bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both aesthetic and ecological benefits.
