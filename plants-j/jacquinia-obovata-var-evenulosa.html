Jacquinia obovata var. evenulosa

Jacquinia obovata var. evenulosa, commonly known as the smoothleaf jacquinia, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its obovate, smooth-edged leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an attractive species for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
