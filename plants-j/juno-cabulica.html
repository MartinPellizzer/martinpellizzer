Juno cabulica

Juno cabulica, commonly known as the Kabul juniper, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cupressaceae family and is characterized by its compact, dense foliage and needle-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. It produces small, reddish-brown cones that contain seeds, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

Juno cabulica is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environments.
