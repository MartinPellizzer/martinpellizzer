Justicia vitellina

Justicia vitellina, commonly known as the yellow false arum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, yellowish-green bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Justicia vitellina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage and unique appearance.
