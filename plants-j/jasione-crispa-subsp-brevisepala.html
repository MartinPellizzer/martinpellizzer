Jasione crispa subsp. brevisepala

Jasione crispa subsp.

brevisepala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

The plant features small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually blue or violet in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lanceolate, with a rough texture, and the plant has a compact growth habit.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
