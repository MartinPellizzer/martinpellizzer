Jubistylis mollis

Jubistylis mollis, commonly known as softleaf bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, often growing in dense clusters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

Jubistylis mollis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
