Jasione perennis subsp. humilis

Jasione perennis subsp.

humilis, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe. It features basal rosettes of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces delicate blue or violet flowers in early summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in grasslands and rocky areas. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
