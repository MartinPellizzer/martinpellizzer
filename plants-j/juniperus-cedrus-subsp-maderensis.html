Juniperus cedrus subsp. maderensis

Juniperus cedrus subsp.

maderensis, commonly known as the Maderan cedar juniper, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula. It is a evergreen shrub or small tree, characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage and aromatic, scale-like leaves.

The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates with mild winters.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
