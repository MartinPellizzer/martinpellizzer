Jumellea longivaginans

Jumellea longivaginans, commonly known as the long sheath lily, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its elongated, tubular leaves that form a sheath around the stem, giving it its specific epithet. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Jumellea longivaginans is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
