Juniperus sabina var. humilis

Juniperus sabina var. humilis, commonly known as the creeping juniper, is a low-growing shrub native to arid and rocky regions of the Mediterranean.

It features dense, scale-like foliage that is typically blue-gray in color, providing a distinctive visual appearance. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in culinary applications. It is well adapted to harsh environments, thriving in poor soils and with minimal water requirements.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for landscaping in dry climates.
