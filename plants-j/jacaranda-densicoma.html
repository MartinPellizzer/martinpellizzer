Jacaranda densicoma

Jacaranda densicoma, commonly known as the densepod jacaranda, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in pendulous clusters during the summer months. The plant produces woody pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Jacaranda densicoma is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its showy flowers and attractive foliage.

It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
