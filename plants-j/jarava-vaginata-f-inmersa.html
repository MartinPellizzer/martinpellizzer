Jarava vaginata f. inmersa

Jarava vaginata f. inmersa, commonly known as the submerged jarava, is a variant of the jarava grass that exhibits unique adaptations to aquatic environments.

This plant typically grows in shallow water or wetlands, with its stems and leaves partially or fully submerged. Its leaves are narrow and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its moist habitat. The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are adapted for water dispersal, enhancing its reproductive success.

Overall, Jarava vaginata f. inmersa showcases structural and physiological traits that enable it to thrive in aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions.
