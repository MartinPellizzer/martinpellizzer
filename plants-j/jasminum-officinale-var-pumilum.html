Jasminum officinale var. pumilum

Jasminum officinale var. pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf jasmine, is a compact shrub native to southern Europe and parts of the Mediterranean.

It produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant has small, dark green leaves and a trailing growth habit, making it suitable for hanging baskets or as a ground cover. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Its aromatic flowers are often used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
