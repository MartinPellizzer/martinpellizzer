Justicia procumbens var. hispida

Justicia procumbens var. hispida, commonly known as hairy commelina, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing perennial with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water gardens and bog landscapes.
