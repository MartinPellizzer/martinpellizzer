Jaborosa araucana

Jaborosa araucana, commonly known as the Araucaria tree, is a species of coniferous evergreen native to South America.

It belongs to the family Araucariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, feather-like leaves and a tall, slender trunk. The plant is known for its slow growth and longevity, with some specimens living over 1,000 years. Its cones are woody and contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The Araucaria tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
