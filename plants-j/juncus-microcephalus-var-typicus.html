Juncus microcephalus var. typicus

Juncus microcephalus var. typicus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and are covered with small, scale-like leaves. Its flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences and are usually greenish or brownish in color.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
