Jurinea merxmuelleri

Jurinea merxmuelleri, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It thrives in sandy and gravelly soils, often found in desert and semi-desert environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are small, fleshy, and covered in a fine layer of hairs to reduce water loss.

Jurinea merxmuelleri is adapted to extreme drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
