Jussiaea suffruticosa var. brevifolia

Jussiaea suffruticosa var. brevifolia, commonly known as the dwarf jussiaea, is a shrub-like plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil and Paraguay.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant features small, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces tiny, yellowish flowers clustered in dense inflorescences. Its botanical structure is characterized by its compact growth habit and adaptive features suited to humid, tropical climates.

It is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique foliage and ecological significance in its native habitat.
