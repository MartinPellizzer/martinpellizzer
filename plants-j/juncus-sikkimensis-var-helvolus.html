Juncus sikkimensis var. helvolus

Juncus sikkimensis var. helvolus, commonly known as the Sikkim rush, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often used in basket weaving. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of greenish-white flowers, which are typically arranged in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas with cool climates.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
