Juncus tracyi f. utahensis

Juncus tracyi f. utahensis, commonly known as Utah rush, is a flowering plant species native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces spike-like inflorescences that bloom in early to mid-summer.

Utah rush plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
