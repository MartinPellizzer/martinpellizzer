Juncus prismatocarpus

Juncus prismatocarpus, commonly known as the prism-leaved rush, is a perennial plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often ribbed or ridged, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, which appear at the top of the stems. It thrives in moist or wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Juncus prismatocarpus is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique foliage and ability to add texture to wetland landscapes.
