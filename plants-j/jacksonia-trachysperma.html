Jacksonia trachysperma

Jacksonia trachysperma, commonly known as the New Zealand daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow to brown seeds in its flower heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

Jacksonia trachysperma is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native ecosystems.
