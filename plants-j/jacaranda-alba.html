Jacaranda alba

Jacaranda alba, commonly known as the white jacaranda, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of pale blue or white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its ability to provide shade and its role in supporting local wildlife.
