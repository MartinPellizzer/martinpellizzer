Juncus anceps var. genuinus

Juncus anceps var. genuinus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often green or reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and arranged in a rosette at the base.

It produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color.
