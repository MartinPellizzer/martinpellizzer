Jasminum diversifolium

Jasminum diversifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves that vary in size and shape, giving it its species name "diversifolium.". The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and pleasant scent.
