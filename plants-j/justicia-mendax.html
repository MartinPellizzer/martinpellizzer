Justicia mendax

Justicia mendax, commonly known as the false arum, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of bright red or orange flowers that resemble small berries. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
