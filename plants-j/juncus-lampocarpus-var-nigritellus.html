Juncus lampocarpus var. nigritellus

Juncus lampocarpus var. nigritellus, commonly known as the black-stemmed rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and pond edges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are densely packed, and its stems are dark brown to black, giving it its distinctive appearance. It produces small, clustered flowers that are often greenish or brownish in color.

This species is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
