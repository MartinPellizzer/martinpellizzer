Juncus gerardi var. soranthus

Juncus gerardi var. soranthus, commonly known as the red stemmed bulrush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, reddish-brown flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil and prevent erosion, contributing to wetland health.

The variety is distinguished by its reddish stem color, which sets it apart from other juncus species.
