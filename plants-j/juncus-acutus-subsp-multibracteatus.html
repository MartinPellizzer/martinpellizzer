Juncus acutus subsp. multibracteatus

Juncus acutus subsp.

multibracteatus, commonly known as the many-bract rush, is a flowering plant species belonging to the family Juncaceae. It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and covered with dense, scale-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
