Jupica albiflora

Jupica albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower jupica, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate white petals and small, clustered flowers that bloom during the warmer months. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are often woody at the base.

Jupica albiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
