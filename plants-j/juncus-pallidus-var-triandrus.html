Juncus pallidus var. triandrus

Juncus pallidus var. triandrus, commonly known as the pale rush, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and damp woodlands. Its leaves are narrow, cylindrical, and often have a bluish-green hue, while the flowers are usually pale green or brown.

This variety is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
