Juniperus californica var. monosperma

Juniperus californica var. monosperma, commonly known as the coastal juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to coastal areas of California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its aromatic, scale-like leaves and small, blue-gray berries that mature in the fall. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with salt spray and strong winds. It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for landscaping in coastal regions.
