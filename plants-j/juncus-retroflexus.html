Juncus retroflexus

Juncus retroflexus, commonly known as the bentstem rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland areas of North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its flowering spikes consist of small, pale green to brownish flowers that bloom in late summer.

Juncus retroflexus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
