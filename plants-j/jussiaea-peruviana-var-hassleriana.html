Jussiaea peruviana var. hassleriana

Jussiaea peruviana var. hassleriana, commonly known as the Peruvian water plant, is a species native to South America, particularly Peru and Ecuador.

It is a floating aquatic plant that thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. The plant has elongated, ribbon-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, and it is often used in ornamental water gardens.

This variety is valued for its ability to provide habitat for aquatic life and its role in water purification.
