Jacaranda acutifolia

Jacaranda acutifolia, commonly known as the slender jacaranda, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking violet to blue-purplish flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated edge. The tree produces long, flat pods that contain seeds covered in a silky, hairy coating.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well adapted to warm, dry climates.
