Jambosa grandis

Jambosa grandis, commonly known as the Natal plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellow or orange fruits with a sweet, tangy flavor. Jambosa grandis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in regions with limited water availability.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
