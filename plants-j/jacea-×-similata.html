Jacea × similata

Jacea × similata, commonly known as the hybrid jasmine, is a perennial shrub native to the warmer regions of Asia.

It features fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value and pleasant aroma, it is often used in landscaping and as a cut flower.
