Justicia martinsoniana

Justicia martinsoniana, commonly known as the Mexican wheelcactus, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, segmented stems that resemble a wheel, hence its common name. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for arid climates.

Botanically, it belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appearance and low maintenance requirements.
