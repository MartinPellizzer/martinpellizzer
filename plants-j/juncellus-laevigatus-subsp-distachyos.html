Juncellus laevigatus subsp. distachyos

Juncellus laevigatus subsp.

distachyos, commonly known as the smooth rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, ditches, and along stream banks.

The plant features cylindrical, grass-like stems that are smooth and often reddish in color. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers arranged in two rows.

The leaves are narrow, strap-shaped, and often have a slight sheen, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
