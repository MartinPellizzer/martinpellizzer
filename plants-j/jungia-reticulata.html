Jungia reticulata

Jungia reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, net-like vein patterns on its leaves. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in tropical and subtropical regions. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pink and purple.

Jungia reticulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
