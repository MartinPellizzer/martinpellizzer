Juncus pilosus var. vernalis

Juncus pilosus var. vernalis, commonly known as the spring tussock rush, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and tall, cylindrical spikes of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are slender, wiry, and covered with fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

The species plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
