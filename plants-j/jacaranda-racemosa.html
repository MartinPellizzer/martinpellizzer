Jacaranda racemosa

Jacaranda racemosa, commonly known as the blue jacaranda, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in the spring. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, feather-like leaflets. The tree produces small, round pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
