Julocroton pycnophyllus

Julocroton pycnophyllus, commonly known as the dense-leaved croton, is a tropical shrub characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish or purple hue on the underside.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in cyathia, typical of the genus.

Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.
