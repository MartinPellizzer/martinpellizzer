Jatropha longipes

Jatropha longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed physic nut, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing toxic seeds. Jatropha longipes is part of the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its milky sap and diverse ecological roles.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.
