Jatropha caricifolia

Jatropha caricifolia, commonly known as the redroot jatropha, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange roots and its clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain toxic seeds, which are a key feature of the genus Jatropha. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a rough, hairy texture.

Jatropha caricifolia is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
