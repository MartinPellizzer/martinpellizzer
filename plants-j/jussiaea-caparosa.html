Jussiaea caparosa

Jussiaea caparosa, commonly known as the Brazilian water lily, is a perennial aquatic plant native to South America.

It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. The plant features broad, floating leaves that provide shade and habitat for aquatic life. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive cup-like shape.

Jussiaea caparosa plays an important role in water ecosystems by stabilizing sediments and supporting biodiversity.
