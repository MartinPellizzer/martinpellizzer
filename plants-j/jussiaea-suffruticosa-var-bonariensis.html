Jussiaea suffruticosa var. bonariensis

Jussiaea suffruticosa var. bonariensis, commonly known as the Brazilian water hyacinth, is a semi-aquatic plant native to South America.

It has a mat-forming growth habit, with thick, waxy leaves that float on the water surface. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers with three petals and three sepals. It is often found in slow-moving freshwater bodies such as ponds and marshes.

Its roots are adapted for anchoring in aquatic environments and can absorb nutrients from the water.
