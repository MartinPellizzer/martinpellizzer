Juncus orizabae

Juncus orizabae, commonly known as the Orizaba rush, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often near water sources. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Juncus orizabae is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential for conservation efforts.
