Juniperus deppeana subsp. robusta

Juniperus deppeana subsp.

robusta, commonly known as the Mexican redcedar, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, scale-like leaves.

The plant produces blue-black berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
