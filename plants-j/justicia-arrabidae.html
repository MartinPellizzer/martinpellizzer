Justicia arrabidae

Justicia arrabidae, commonly known as the flame flower, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for warm climates.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological significance.
