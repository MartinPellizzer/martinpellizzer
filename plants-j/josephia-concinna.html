Josephia concinna

Josephia concinna, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy or rocky soils, often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads that disperse easily by wind. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in hot, dry conditions.

Josephia concinna is adapted to survive in extreme environments with minimal water and nutrient availability.
