Juglans pecan

Juglans pecan, commonly known as the pecan tree, is a species native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a deciduous tree that produces edible nuts enclosed in a hard shell. The pecan tree belongs to the walnut family (Juglandaceae) and is characterized by its compound leaves and large, ornamental fruits. Its nuts are highly valued for their rich flavor and are used in various culinary applications.

The tree also plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for wildlife.
