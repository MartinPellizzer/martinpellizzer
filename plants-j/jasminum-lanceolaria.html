Jasminum lanceolaria

Jasminum lanceolaria, commonly known as the Chinese jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring slender, somewhat woody stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, usually in the spring and summer months. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and lance-shaped, giving it its scientific name.

The flowers are known for their strong, sweet scent, which attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
