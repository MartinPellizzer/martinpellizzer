Jasminum sambac var. syringifolium

Jasminum sambac var. syringifolium, commonly known as the Chinese jasmine, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often releasing a sweet scent in the evening. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are typically ovate or elliptic in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

This variant is closely related to the common jasmine, but it has distinct foliage and floral characteristics.
