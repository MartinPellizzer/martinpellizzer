Juncus angustifolius

Juncus angustifolius, commonly known as narrowleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and pond edges. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 1 mm wide, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late spring to early summer, consist of small, inconspicuous flowers.

Juncus angustifolius plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
