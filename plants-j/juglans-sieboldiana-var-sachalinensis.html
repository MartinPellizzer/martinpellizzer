Juglans sieboldiana var. sachalinensis

Juglans sieboldiana var. sachalinensis, commonly known as the Far Eastern black walnut, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the walnut family, Juglandaceae, and is characterized by its large, doubly serrated leaves and distinctive, edible nuts. The tree produces compound fruits that mature in late summer, containing a single hard-shelled nut enclosed in a fibrous husk. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in mixed forests at elevations ranging from 500 to 2,000 meters.

This species is valued for its timber and has cultural significance in its native regions.
