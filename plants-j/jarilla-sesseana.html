Jarilla sesseana

Jarilla sesseana, commonly known as desert sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its fibrous root system helps it survive in harsh, drought-prone conditions.
