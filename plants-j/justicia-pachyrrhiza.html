Justicia pachyrrhiza

Justicia pachyrrhiza, commonly known as the woolyleaf false arum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that give it a distinctive, almost fuzzy appearance. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that are typically bright red or orange, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
