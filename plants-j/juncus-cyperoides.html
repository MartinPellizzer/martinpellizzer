Juncus cyperoides

Juncus cyperoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are typically unbranched and covered in small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Juncus cyperoides plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
