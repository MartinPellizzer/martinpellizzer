Jacquemontia violacea var. guatemalensis

Jacquemontia violacea var. guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant typically grows as a vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shady environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
