Juncus longifolius

Juncus longifolius, commonly known as the longleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like, cylindrical stems that are often hollow and can grow up to several feet in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a waxy coating, aiding in water retention.

Juncus longifolius plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
