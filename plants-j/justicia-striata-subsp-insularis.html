Justicia striata subsp. insularis

Justicia striata subsp.

insularis, commonly known as the Island flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands. It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in terminal inflorescences.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.
