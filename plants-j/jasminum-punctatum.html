Jasminum punctatum

Jasminum punctatum, commonly known as the spotted jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The leaves are typically elliptical and have a glossy surface, while the plant often exhibits a trailing or climbing growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also noted for its ability to produce small, yellowish spots on its leaves, which give it its common name.
