Juncus patens

Juncus patens, commonly known as the spreading rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that are often branched and covered in dense, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikes, characteristic of the Juncaceae family. It thrives in moist or waterlogged soils and is often found in marshes, pond edges, and along stream banks.

Its adaptability makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
