Johrenia persica

Johrenia persica, commonly known as Persian rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Despite its beauty, it is relatively rare in cultivation and requires specific care to thrive.
