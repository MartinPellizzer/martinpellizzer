Juncus tasmanicus

Juncus tasmanicus, commonly known as the Tasmanian rush, is a perennial plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and tall, slender flowering stems. The plant typically grows in wet or damp environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, often blooming in spring or early summer.

Juncus tasmanicus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
