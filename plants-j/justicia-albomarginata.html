Justicia albomarginata

Justicia albomarginata, commonly known as the white-margin verbena, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of white and pink, with distinctive white margins on the petals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in warm climates.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
