Junellia spathulata var. glauca

Junellia spathulata var. glauca, commonly known as the blue-leafed bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and bell-shaped flowers that range from purple to blue. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its ornamental value lies in its unique coloration and delicate floral display.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
