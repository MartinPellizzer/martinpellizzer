Juncus articulatus f. submersus

Juncus articulatus f. submersus, commonly known as the submerged rush, is a variant of the common rush that exhibits aquatic adaptations.

This plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and wetlands. Its stems are often fully submerged, and it lacks the typical upright growth form of its terrestrial relatives. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a dense, fibrous structure that aids in buoyancy.

It plays a role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
