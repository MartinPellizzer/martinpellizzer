Juniperus lusitanica

Juniperus lusitanica, commonly known as the Portuguese juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, blue-gray leaves that are typically arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain aromatic, berry-like seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Juniperus lusitanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
