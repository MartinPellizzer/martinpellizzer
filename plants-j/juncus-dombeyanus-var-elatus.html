Juncus dombeyanus var. elatus

Juncus dombeyanus var. elatus, commonly known as the tall rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, covered with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets of flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, pond margins, and along streams.

Its dense growth habit makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
