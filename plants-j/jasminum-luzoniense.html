Jasminum luzoniense

Jasminum luzoniense, commonly known as the Luzon jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the summer months. Its flowers are tubular with five petals and are highly valued for their sweet aroma.

Jasminum luzoniense is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and in traditional medicine.
