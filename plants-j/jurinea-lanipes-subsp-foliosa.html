Jurinea lanipes subsp. foliosa

Jurinea lanipes subsp.

foliosa, commonly known as the leafy sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Eurasia. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, succulent leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers.

The subspecies foliosa is distinguished by its more elongated and leafy appearance compared to other subspecies of Jurinea lanipes. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes or dry riverbeds.

This plant is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, including high salinity and extreme temperature fluctuations.
