Juncus glaucus microcarpus

Juncus glaucus microcarpus, commonly known as the blue rush, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage that forms tufts or mats, typically growing in wet or moist environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and cylindrical, often with a bluish-gray hue, contributing to its common name.

This species is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
