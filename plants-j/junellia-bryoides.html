Junellia bryoides

Junellia bryoides, commonly known as the moss-like fern, is a unique plant species belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit, which resembles that of moss, hence its common name. The plant features small, delicate fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. Junellia bryoides thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds, facilitating spore dispersal.
