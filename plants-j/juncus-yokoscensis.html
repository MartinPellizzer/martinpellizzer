Juncus yokoscensis

Juncus yokoscensis, commonly known as the Japanese rush, is a perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae and is characterized by its stiff, grass-like leaves and tall, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist or wet environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

Juncus yokoscensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
