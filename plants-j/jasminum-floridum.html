Jasminum floridum

Jasminum floridum, commonly known as the Florida jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and oval-shaped with a smooth margin.

Jasminum floridum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
