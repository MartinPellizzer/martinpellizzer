Juttadinteria deserticola

Juttadinteria deserticola, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a succulent shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a splash of color to the desert landscape. Its roots are deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in nutrient-poor soils.

Juttadinteria deserticola is adapted to extreme temperatures and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
