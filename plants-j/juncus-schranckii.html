Juncus schranckii

Juncus schranckii, commonly known as the Schranck's rush, is a perennial plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and is typically found in moist or wetland environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in temperate climates and is often seen in marshes, stream banks, and other waterlogged areas.

Juncus schranckii plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
