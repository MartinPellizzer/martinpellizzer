Juchia hastata

Juchia hastata, commonly known as the narrowleaf juchia, is a herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. Juchia hastata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suited for xerophytic conditions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
