Jacobinia albicaulis

Jacobinia albicaulis, commonly known as the white-stemmed jacobinia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, white-veined stems and small, pale green leaves. The plant produces clusters of delicate, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its graceful appearance and drought tolerance.
