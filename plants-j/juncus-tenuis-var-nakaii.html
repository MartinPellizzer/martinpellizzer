Juncus tenuis var. nakaii

Juncus tenuis var. nakaii, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a grass-like plant native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its dense growth habit makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
