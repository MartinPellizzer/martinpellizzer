Juniperus communis f. pendulina

Juniperus communis f. pendulina, commonly known as the hanging juniper, is a shrub or small tree characterized by its pendulous branches that droop gracefully.

It is a subspecies of the common juniper, native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa. The plant produces small, scale-like leaves and blue-black berries that are used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its unique form makes it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
