Juniperus carinata

Juniperus carinata, commonly known as the Sierra juniper, is a species of juniper native to the western United States.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has scale-like leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs, and its cones are small and berry-like, containing toxic seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

The Sierra juniper plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
