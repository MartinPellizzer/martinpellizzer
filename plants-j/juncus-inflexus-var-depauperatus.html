Juncus inflexus var. depauperatus

Juncus inflexus var. depauperatus, commonly known as the sparse juncus, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of North America.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers are usually greenish or brownish in color.

This variety is noted for its reduced floral structures compared to other members of the species, giving it a more sparse appearance.
