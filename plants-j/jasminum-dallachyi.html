Jasminum dallachyi

Jasminum dallachyi, commonly known as the Yemen jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a leathery texture, which helps reduce water loss.

Jasminum dallachyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in regions with suitable climates.
