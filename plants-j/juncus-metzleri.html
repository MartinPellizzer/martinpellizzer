Juncus metzleri

Juncus metzleri, commonly known as the Metzler's rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, ditches, and stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of pale green to brown flowers. It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.

Juncus metzleri is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
