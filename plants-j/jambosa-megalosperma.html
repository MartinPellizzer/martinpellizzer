Jambosa megalosperma

Jambosa megalosperma, commonly known as the giant false jambosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has woody stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and botanical collections.
