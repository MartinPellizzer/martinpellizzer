Jasminum triplinervium

Jasminum triplinervium, commonly known as the three-veined jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The leaves are simple, opposite, and have three distinct veins, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in forest understories.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic properties.
