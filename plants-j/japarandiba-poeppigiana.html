Japarandiba poeppigiana

Japarandiba poeppigiana, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and a broad, rounded canopy. The plant is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a glossy green surface.

Japarandiba poeppigiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its vibrant floral display.
