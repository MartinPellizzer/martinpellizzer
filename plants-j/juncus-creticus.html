Juncus creticus

Juncus creticus, commonly known as the Mediterranean rush, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and typically grows in wet or marshy environments. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often used in basket weaving and other crafts. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring.

Juncus creticus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
