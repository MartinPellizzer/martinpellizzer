Jambosa vulgaris

Jambosa vulgaris, commonly known as the common jambosa, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems, large broad leaves, and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit yield, it is cultivated in many subtropical regions.
