Justicia longipetiolata

Justicia longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that are typically bright red or orange, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with long petioles, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The genus Justicia belongs to the Acanthaceae family, which includes many species with showy flowers and medicinal uses.
