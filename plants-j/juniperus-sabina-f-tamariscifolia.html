Juniperus sabina f. tamariscifolia

Juniperus sabina f. tamariscifolia, commonly known as the Tamarisk-leaved juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that resemble those of tamarisk, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in arid environments.
