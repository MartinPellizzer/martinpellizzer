Juglans fraxinifolia

Juglans fraxinifolia, commonly known as the Persian walnuts, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Iran and Turkey.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a distinctive ash-like appearance. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elongated, woody fruits containing one or two edible nuts. Its nuts are highly valued for their rich flavor and are commonly used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant has also been studied for its potential ecological benefits and role in traditional herbal medicine.
