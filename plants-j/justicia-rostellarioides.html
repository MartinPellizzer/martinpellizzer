Justicia rostellarioides

Justicia rostellarioides, commonly known as the red-flowered false arum, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that features striking, bright red bracts that resemble those of a true arum. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, which are often surrounded by colorful, modified leaves. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and unique floral display.
