Justicia alopecuroidea

Justicia alopecuroidea, commonly known as silverleaf false heather, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or lavender in color, arranged in spikes along the stem. The plant has narrow, silver-gray leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Botanically, it belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
