Josephia squarrosa

Josephia squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrose josephia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, showy yellow flowers. The plant features hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. It thrives in rocky soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
