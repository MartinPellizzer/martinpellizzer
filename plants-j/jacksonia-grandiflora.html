Jacksonia grandiflora

Jacksonia grandiflora, commonly known as the New Zealand daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with white centers. The plant typically grows in coastal and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

Jacksonia grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
