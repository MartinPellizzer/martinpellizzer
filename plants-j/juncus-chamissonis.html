Juncus chamissonis

Juncus chamissonis, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and pond edges. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 1 millimeter wide. Its flowering stems produce small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in late summer.

Juncus chamissonis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
