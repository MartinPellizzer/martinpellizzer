Justicia serotina

Justicia serotina, commonly known as late-flowering jewelweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems with alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces showy, pendulous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of orange, red, or yellow, which bloom from late summer into fall. Its flowers have five petals and are accompanied by prominent, spurred sepals.

The plant is adapted to moist, sandy soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight.
