Juniperus deltoides var. spilinana

Juniperus deltoides var. spilinana, commonly known as the Spiny juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces blue-black berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
