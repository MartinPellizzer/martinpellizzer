Juncus alpinus subsp. australis

Juncus alpinus subsp.

australis, commonly known as the alpine rush, is a perennial plant native to the southern hemisphere. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in alpine or subalpine regions.

The plant features slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and are covered in small, grass-like leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes, which are usually green or brown.

This subspecies is adapted to cold climates and is often found in mountainous areas with high elevations.
