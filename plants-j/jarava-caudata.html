Jarava caudata

Jarava caudata, commonly known as the long-bracted jarava, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Jarava caudata is adapted to arid conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
