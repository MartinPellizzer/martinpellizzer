Jefea phyllocephala

Jefea phyllocephala, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its feathery, compound leaves that turn yellow in the fall, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in panicles, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

Jefea phyllocephala is also valued for its ability to withstand drought and its use in traditional medicine.
