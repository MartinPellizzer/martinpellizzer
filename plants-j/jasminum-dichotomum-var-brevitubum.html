Jasminum dichotomum var. brevitubum

Jasminum dichotomum var. brevitubum, commonly known as the short-stemmed jasmine, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant has slender, woody stems with a tendency to branch at the nodes, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a glossy green surface.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.
