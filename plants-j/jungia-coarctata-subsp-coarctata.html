Jungia coarctata subsp. coarctata

Jungia coarctata subsp.

coarctata, commonly known as the narrowleaf jungia, is a perennial herb native to the humid regions of South America. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks.

The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, clustered flowers are usually white or pale green, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity and partial shade.
