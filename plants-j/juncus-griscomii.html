Juncus griscomii

Juncus griscomii, commonly known as Griscom's rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and pond edges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually bright green and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. It produces small, clustered flowers that are often green or brown in color, which bloom in late summer.

Juncus griscomii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
