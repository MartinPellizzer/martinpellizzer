Jarava eriostachya

Jarava eriostachya, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that reflects sunlight, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Jarava eriostachya plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
