Jupunba truncata

Jupunba truncata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed jupunba, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, Jupunba truncata is of interest to botanists studying adaptation to extreme climates.
