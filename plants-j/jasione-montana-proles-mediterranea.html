Jasione montana proles mediterranea

Jasione montana proles mediterranea, commonly known as the Mediterranean rock jasmine, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and drought-tolerant species suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
