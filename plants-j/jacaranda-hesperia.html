Jacaranda hesperia

Jacaranda hesperia, commonly known as the yellow jacaranda, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

The yellow jacaranda is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice in urban landscaping.
