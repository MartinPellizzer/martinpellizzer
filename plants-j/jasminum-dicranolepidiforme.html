Jasminum dicranolepidiforme

Jasminum dicranolepidiforme, commonly known as the golden jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

The flowers are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
