Julocroton triqueter var. subulatus

Julocroton triqueter var. subulatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved croton, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
