Jacquemontia maximiliani

Jacquemontia maximiliani, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, showy flowers that bloom in shades of purple and white. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical climates.
