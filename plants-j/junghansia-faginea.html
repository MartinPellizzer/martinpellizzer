Junghansia faginea

Junghansia faginea, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of East Asia.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Junghansia faginea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
