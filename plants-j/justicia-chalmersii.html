Justicia chalmersii

Justicia chalmersii, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Justicia chalmersii thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
