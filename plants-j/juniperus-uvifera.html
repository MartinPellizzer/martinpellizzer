Juniperus uvifera

Juniperus uvifera, commonly known as the Indian juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas and southern China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces blue to black berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Juniperus uvifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
