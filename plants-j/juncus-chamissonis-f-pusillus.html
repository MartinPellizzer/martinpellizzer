Juncus chamissonis f. pusillus

Juncus chamissonis f. pusillus, commonly known as the dwarf rush, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically forms dense mats of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear greenish-white flowers. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its compact size and ability to tolerate waterlogged soils make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
