Juncus homalocaulis

Juncus homalocaulis, commonly known as the tufted rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems that are often clustered at the base, forming dense tufts. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, spike-like clusters of greenish-yellow flowers in the summer.

Juncus homalocaulis plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
