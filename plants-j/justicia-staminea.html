Justicia staminea

Justicia staminea, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in dense spikes, often giving the plant its common name. The plant is part of the Acanthaceae family and is known for its vibrant, showy inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Justicia staminea is commonly cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in full sun.
