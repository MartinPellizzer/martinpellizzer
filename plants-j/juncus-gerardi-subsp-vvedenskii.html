Juncus gerardi subsp. vvedenskii

Juncus gerardi subsp.

vvedenskii, commonly known as the Vvedenskii rush, is a flowering plant species native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of Eurasia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, often boggy environments, such as tundra and wet meadows.

The plant features slender, cylindrical stems that are usually unbranched and covered in fine, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers, which are typically greenish or brownish in color.

This subspecies is adapted to cold climates and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
