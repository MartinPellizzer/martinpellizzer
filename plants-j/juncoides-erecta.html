Juncoides erecta

Juncoides erecta, commonly known as the upright rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are usually hollow and covered in fine, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Juncoides erecta thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization in wetland ecosystems.
