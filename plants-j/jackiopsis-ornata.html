Jackiopsis ornata

Jackiopsis ornata, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and the presence of yellow, star-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

Jackiopsis ornata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
