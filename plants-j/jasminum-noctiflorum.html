Jasminum noctiflorum

Jasminum noctiflorum, commonly known as night-flowering jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, with opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. Its flowers are tubular and have five petals, which open in the evening and close by dawn.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine and perfumery.
