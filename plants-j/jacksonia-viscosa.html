Jacksonia viscosa

Jacksonia viscosa, commonly known as the stickyweed, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and hairy, sticky leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It has a low-growing habit and produces achenes as its fruit, which are dispersed by wind.

Jacksonia viscosa is adapted to arid environments and is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value.
