Juncus nevadensis var. inventus

Juncus nevadensis var. inventus, commonly known as the desert rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that are often mistaken for grasses but are actually composed of solid, fibrous tissue. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters near the top of the stems, typical of the Juncaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and forage for various desert wildlife.
