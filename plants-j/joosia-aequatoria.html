Joosia aequatoria

Joosia aequatoria, commonly known as the African waterwheel, is a perennial aquatic plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of leaves that resemble a waterwheel, hence its common name. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface.

Joosia aequatoria plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
