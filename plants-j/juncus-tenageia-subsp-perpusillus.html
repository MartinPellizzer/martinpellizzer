Juncus tenageia subsp. perpusillus

Juncus tenageia subsp.

perpusillus, commonly known as the dwarf rush, is a compact, herbaceous plant native to southern South America. It typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas or along riverbanks.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 10 centimeters in length. It produces small, greenish flowers in dense spikes during the late spring and early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wetland environments.
