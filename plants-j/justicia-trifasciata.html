Justicia trifasciata

Justicia trifasciata, commonly known as the red-stemmed false arum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces striking, arrow-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in clusters along the stems. Its botanical name reflects its ornamental value and unique floral structure.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its vibrant, showy blossoms.
