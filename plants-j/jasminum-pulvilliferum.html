Jasminum pulvilliferum

Jasminum pulvilliferum, commonly known as the white jasmine or the mountain jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant has opposite, simple, and lance-shaped leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in forested areas or along stream banks.

Jasminum pulvilliferum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
