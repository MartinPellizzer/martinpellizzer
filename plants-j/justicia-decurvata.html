Justicia decurvata

Justicia decurvata, commonly known as the curly willow or red curl, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, pendulous branches and clusters of bright red or orange tubular flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Justicia decurvata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
