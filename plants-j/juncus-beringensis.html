Juncus beringensis

Juncus beringensis, commonly known as the Bering rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America and Eurasia.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as tundra, wet meadows, and along the edges of lakes and rivers. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are usually unbranched and covered in dense, grass-like leaves. Its flowering spikes consist of small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences.

Juncus beringensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various arctic and subarctic wildlife.
