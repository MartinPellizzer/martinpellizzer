Johrenia fungosa

Johrenia fungosa, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Johrenia fungosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
