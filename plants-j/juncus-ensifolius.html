Juncus ensifolius

Juncus ensifolius, commonly known as the swordleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant features tall, grass-like leaves that are narrow and sword-shaped, giving it its common name. Its flowering spikes consist of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Juncus ensifolius plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
