Jatropha

Jatropha, commonly known as the physic nut, is a genus of flowering plants in the spurge family, Euphorbiaceae.

It includes several species, many of which are native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. The plants are characterized by their milky sap, which can be toxic, and their showy, often brightly colored flowers. Jatropha species are typically shrubs or small trees, with alternate, simple leaves.

Some species are cultivated for their seeds, which can be used to produce biodiesel.
