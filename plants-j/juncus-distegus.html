Juncus distegus

Juncus distegus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in short, stiff hairs. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib.

Juncus distegus produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, which are usually found at the tips of the stems.
