Jacaranda poitaei

Jacaranda poitaei, commonly known as the golden jacaranda, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces small, woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is notable for its adaptability and ability to withstand drought conditions.
