Juncus cordobensis

Juncus cordobensis, commonly known as the Cordoba rush, is a perennial plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems that grow in dense clusters. The plant typically thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of lakes and rivers. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

Juncus cordobensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
