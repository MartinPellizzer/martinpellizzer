Juncus emmanuelis var. inflatifolius

Juncus emmanuelis var. inflatifolius, commonly known as the inflated leaf rush, is a variant of the juncus genus characterized by its unique foliage structure.

This plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are notably broader and more inflated compared to typical juncus species, which aids in water retention and provides structural support. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes, which are often borne on slender stalks.

It is a hardy, perennial species that plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and supporting local wildlife.
