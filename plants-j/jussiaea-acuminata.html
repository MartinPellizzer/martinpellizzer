Jussiaea acuminata

Jussiaea acuminata, commonly known as the slender water primrose, is a semi-aquatic flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually floating or partially submerged, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in aquatic environments.

Jussiaea acuminata is also used in phytoremediation due to its ability to absorb heavy metals from contaminated water.
