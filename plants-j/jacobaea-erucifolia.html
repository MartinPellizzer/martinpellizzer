Jacobaea erucifolia

Jacobaea erucifolia, commonly known as the woolly cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads.

Jacobaea erucifolia is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
