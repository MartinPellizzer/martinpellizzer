Justicia laxa

Justicia laxa, commonly known as the slender flameflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically opposite or whorled along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Justicia laxa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
