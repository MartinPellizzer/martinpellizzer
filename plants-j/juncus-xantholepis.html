Juncus xantholepis

Juncus xantholepis, commonly known as the yellow rush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and tall, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in cylindrical spikes, giving it its common name. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and stream banks, and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Juncus xantholepis is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and its aesthetic appeal in water gardens.
