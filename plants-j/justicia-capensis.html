Justicia capensis

Justicia capensis, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features showy, torch-like inflorescences that are typically bright red or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its botanical name reflects its historical use in traditional medicine for treating various ailments.
