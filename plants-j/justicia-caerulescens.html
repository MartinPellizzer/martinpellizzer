Justicia caerulescens

Justicia caerulescens, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its inflorescence is a panicle, which consists of multiple bracts that create a mist-like appearance.

Justicia caerulescens prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a versatile choice for garden landscapes.
