Justicia chamaeranthemoides

Justicia chamaeranthemoides, commonly known as the Cape ivy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically blue or violet in color and are clustered in terminal inflorescences, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in coastal regions, where it adapts well to sandy or rocky soils.

The plant is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and salt spray, making it a hardy ornamental species.
