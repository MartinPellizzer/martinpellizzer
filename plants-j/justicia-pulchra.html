Justicia pulchra

Justicia pulchra, commonly known as the flame violet, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its blooms appear in late spring to early summer, and the plant thrives in full sun to partial shade with well-drained soil.

Justicia pulchra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting flowers.
