Jackia

Jackia, commonly known as the New Zealand flax, is a genus of flowering plants native to New Zealand.

It consists of several species, including Jackia magellanica and Jackia microsperma, which are characterized by their distinctive, spiky flowers. The plants typically grow as shrubs or subshrubs, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Jackia species are often found in coastal and forested areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their unique floral display.
