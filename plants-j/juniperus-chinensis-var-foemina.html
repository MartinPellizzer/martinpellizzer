Juniperus chinensis var. foemina

Juniperus chinensis var. foemina, commonly known as the Chinese juniper or the dwarf Chinese juniper, is a compact, slow-growing evergreen shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, scale-like foliage that is typically a deep green, with a bluish or silver-white hue on the undersides. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

This variety is often used in rock gardens and as a foundation plant due to its hardiness and low maintenance requirements.
