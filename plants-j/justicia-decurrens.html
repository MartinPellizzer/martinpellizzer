Justicia decurrens

Justicia decurrens, commonly known as the spreading flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its attractive, tubular, red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-draining soil.

Justicia decurrens is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display.
