Justicia helonoma

Justicia helonoma, commonly known as the flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
