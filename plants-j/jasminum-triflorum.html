Jasminum triflorum

Jasminum triflorum, commonly known as the three-flowered jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters of three, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

Jasminum triflorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
