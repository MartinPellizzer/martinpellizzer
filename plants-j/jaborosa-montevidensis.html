Jaborosa montevidensis

Jaborosa montevidensis, commonly known as the Uruguay dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Uruguay and parts of Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its yellow flowers bloom in the spring and summer, and it produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Jaborosa montevidensis is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
