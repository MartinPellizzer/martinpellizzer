Jacobinia sericeotomentosa

Jacobinia sericeotomentosa, commonly known as the silky-tomentose jacaranda, is a deciduous tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The leaves are compound, with small, ovate leaflets covered in a fine, silky white pubescence. The bark is smooth and grayish, while the wood is dense and hard.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
