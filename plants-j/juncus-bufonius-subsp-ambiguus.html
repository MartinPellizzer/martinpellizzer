Juncus bufonius subsp. ambiguus

Juncus bufonius subsp.

ambiguus, commonly known as the ambiguous rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, ditches, and stream banks.

The plant has cylindrical, grass-like stems that are usually green to reddish in color. Its small, spike-like inflorescence contains numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

It is often found in disturbed or nutrient-rich soils, making it a common indicator species in wetland habitats.
