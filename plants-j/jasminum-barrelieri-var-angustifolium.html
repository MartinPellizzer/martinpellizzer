Jasminum barrelieri var. angustifolium

Jasminum barrelieri var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers bloom in clusters during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its pleasant fragrance.
