Juncus balticus var. pictus

Juncus balticus var. pictus, commonly known as the painted rush, is a striking wetland plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems that often display a distinctive pink or red coloration, especially along the nodes. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers that are often green or reddish in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging wetland environments.
