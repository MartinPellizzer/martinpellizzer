Jailoloa

Jailoloa, commonly known as the "ghost flower," is a rare and mysterious plant native to the remote highlands of Papua, Indonesia.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent petals that appear to shimmer with an ethereal glow, especially under moonlight. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found growing in shaded, moist forest areas. Its unique appearance has made it a subject of fascination among botanists and local folklore.

Due to its elusive nature and limited distribution, Jailoloa remains one of the least studied plants in the region.
