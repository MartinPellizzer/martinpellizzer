Juglans allardiana var. acuta

Juglans allardiana var. acuta, commonly known as the Acute Hackberry, is a variant of the hackberry tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a rough, waxy texture. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are borne in catkins, and the fruit is a small, round drupe that matures to a dark blue or black color.

The Acute Hackberry is valued for its hardiness and is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental features.
