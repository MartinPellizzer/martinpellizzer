Jussiaea clavata

Jussiaea clavata, commonly known as the slender water plant, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, ribbon-like leaves that float on the surface of water, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal spikes, typically blooming in the spring. Jussiaea clavata thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and marshes.

Its roots help stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.
