Juncus mertensianus var. paniculatus

Juncus mertensianus var. paniculatus, commonly known as the Mertens' rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has cylindrical, grass-like leaves and produces panicles of small, white or pale green flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands.

The plant is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
