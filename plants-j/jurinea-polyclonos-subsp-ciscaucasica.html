Jurinea polyclonos subsp. ciscaucasica

Jurinea polyclonos subsp.

ciscaucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its striking blue or purple ray florets and a central disk of yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, showing adaptations to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
