Jussiaea quadrangularis var. australis

Jussiaea quadrangularis var. australis, commonly known as the Australian waterplantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has slender, quadrangular stems that are often submerged or emergent, and its leaves are narrow and elongated. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is notable for its ability to tolerate varying water conditions and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
