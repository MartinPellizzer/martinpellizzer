Jurinea karabugasica

Jurinea karabugasica, commonly known as the Karabugas thornweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, succulent stems and small, spiny leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with poor soil conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
