Justicia dives var. dives

Justicia dives var. dives, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, long-lasting blue-violet flowers that bloom in dense, spike-like clusters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, fuzzy layer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its vibrant color and ease of care.
