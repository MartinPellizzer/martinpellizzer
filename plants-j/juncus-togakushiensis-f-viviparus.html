Juncus togakushiensis f. viviparus

Juncus togakushiensis f. viviparus, commonly known as the viviparous rush, is a rare and unique variant of the Japanese rush species.

It is characterized by its ability to produce live offspring through vivipary, a rare reproductive phenomenon where embryos develop on the plant's stems before reaching maturity. This feature makes it distinct from typical grass-like plants, as it exhibits a form of vegetative propagation. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions of Japan.

Its botanical significance lies in its unusual reproductive strategy and its role in ecological studies of plant adaptation.
