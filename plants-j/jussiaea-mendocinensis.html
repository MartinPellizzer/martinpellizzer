Jussiaea mendocinensis

Jussiaea mendocinensis, commonly known as the Mendoza waterplant, is a species of aquatic plant native to South America, particularly found in the Andean region of Argentina.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as lakes and rivers. The plant has elongated, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface, with roots anchoring it to the substrate. It is characterized by its simple, unbranched stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge above the water.

Jussiaea mendocinensis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to aquatic life.
