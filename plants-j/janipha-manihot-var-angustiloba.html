Janipha manihot var. angustiloba

Janipha manihot var. angustiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cassava, is a variant of the cassava plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and stout, woody stems. The plant produces edible tuberous roots that are rich in carbohydrates but contain cyanogenic glycosides, requiring proper processing before consumption. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its adaptability and resilience to pests and diseases.
