Jasminum fraternum

Jasminum fraternum, commonly known as fraternum jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually hairy leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

The flowers are often used in perfumes and traditional medicine due to their aromatic properties.
