Juncus sylvaticus f. divaricatus

Juncus sylvaticus f. divaricatus, commonly known as the forked rush, is a variant of the common rush species.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its stems are often branched, giving it the distinctive "forked" appearance that defines the cultivar. Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
