Jovibarba allionii

Jovibarba allionii, commonly known as the Allion's cushion saxifrage, is a low-growing, mat-forming succulent native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features fleshy, rounded leaves that are typically green or bluish-gray in color, adapted to retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers during the summer months, adding a delicate touch to its compact form. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas with high altitudes.

Jovibarba allionii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine habitats.
