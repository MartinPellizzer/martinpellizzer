Jacea carpatica

Jacea carpatica, commonly known as the Carpathian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It features delicate blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and grows in rocky or gravelly soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and forest edges.

Jacea carpatica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
