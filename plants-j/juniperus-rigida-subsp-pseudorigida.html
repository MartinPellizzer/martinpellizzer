Juniperus rigida subsp. pseudorigida

Juniperus rigida subsp.

pseudorigida, commonly known as the rigid juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Its slow growth rate and hardiness make it a resilient plant in harsh climatic conditions.
