Jonidiopsis fruticulosa

Jonidiopsis fruticulosa, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shady environments, often found in the understory of tropical forests. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a delicate, mist-like appearance. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Jonidiopsis fruticulosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
