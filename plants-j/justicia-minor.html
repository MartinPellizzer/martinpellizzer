Justicia minor

Justicia minor, commonly known as the lesser flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warm climates.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees, contributing to its ecological value.
