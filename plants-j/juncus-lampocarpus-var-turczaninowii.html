Juncus lampocarpus var. turczaninowii

Juncus lampocarpus var. turczaninowii, commonly known as the Turczaninow's rush, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves that form a compact growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks or in wet meadows.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and ecological restoration projects.
