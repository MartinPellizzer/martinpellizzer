Juniperus taxifolia var. taxifolia

Juniperus taxifolia var. taxifolia, commonly known as the Japanese juniper, is a coniferous evergreen shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its scale-like leaves that are arranged in flat, linear sprays, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and as a ornamental plant.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and adaptability in various garden settings.
