Juncus articulatus f. subobtusatus

Juncus articulatus f. subobtusatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rush, is a variant of the common rush characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and pond margins. The plant features a tall, cylindrical stem with nodes that are often used for propagation. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly curved, with a pointed tip.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
