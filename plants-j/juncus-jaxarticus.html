Juncus jaxarticus

Juncus jaxarticus, commonly known as the Central Asian rush, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often found near rivers and lakes. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are usually green or reddish in color. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in spike-like clusters.

Juncus jaxarticus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
