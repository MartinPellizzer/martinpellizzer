Julocroton ackermannianus var. ovatus

Julocroton ackermannianus var. ovatus, commonly known as the broad-leaf croton, is a striking tropical shrub characterized by its large, ovate leaves that are often brightly colored with shades of red, orange, and yellow.

Native to regions of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Its foliage is a key ornamental feature, making it popular in horticulture for its vibrant and varied leaf patterns. The plant is typically propagated through stem cuttings, which root readily under suitable conditions.

Due to its aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a potted plant.
