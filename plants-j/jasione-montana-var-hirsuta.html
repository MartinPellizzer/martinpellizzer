Jasione montana var. hirsuta

Jasione montana var. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy mountain everlasting, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features wiry, ascending stems covered with dense, white to greyish hairs, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Jasione montana var. hirsuta is often found in dry, calcareous soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
