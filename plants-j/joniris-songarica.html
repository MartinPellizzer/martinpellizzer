Joniris songarica

Joniris songarica, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, round fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy surface.
