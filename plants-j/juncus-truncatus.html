Juncus truncatus

Juncus truncatus, commonly known as the cutleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are often truncate at the base, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish or brownish flowers in dense spikes during the summer months. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in shallow water or along the edges of ponds.

Juncus truncatus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
