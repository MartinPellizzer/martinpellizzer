Jacea × austriacoides

Jacea × austriacoides, commonly known as the Austrian bellflower, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Jacea grandiflora and Jacea austriaca.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30–60 cm. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually blue or violet in color, with five petals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The species is native to Central Europe and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
