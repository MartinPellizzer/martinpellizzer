Jacquinia curtissii var. lomensis

Jacquinia curtissii var. lomensis, commonly known as the Lomandra lomandra, is a perennial grass-like plant native to eastern Australia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
