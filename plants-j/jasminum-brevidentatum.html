Jasminum brevidentatum

Jasminum brevidentatum, commonly known as the narrow-toothed jasmine, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has simple, opposite leaves with serrated edges, which are typically dark green in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Jasminum brevidentatum is a shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, making it suitable for both garden and landscape use.
