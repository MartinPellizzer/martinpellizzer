Juncus acutus subsp. littoralis

Juncus acutus subsp.

littoralis, commonly known as the coastal rush, is a perennial plant native to coastal regions of North America. It thrives in brackish or saline wetlands, often found in tidal areas and estuaries.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies plays an important ecological role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
