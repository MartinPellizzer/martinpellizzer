Juniperus folio-tamarisci

Juniperus folio-tamarisci, commonly known as the Chinese tamarisk juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to northern China and the Russian Far East.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs along the branches. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Juniperus folio-tamarisci is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and winter interest.
