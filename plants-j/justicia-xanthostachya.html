Justicia xanthostachya

Justicia xanthostachya, commonly known as the yellow flame plant, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking orange to yellow flowers that bloom in terminal clusters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant due to its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
