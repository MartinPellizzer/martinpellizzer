Juncus novae-zelandiae

Juncus novae-zelandiae, commonly known as New Zealand rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as swamps and stream banks, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often green or reddish in color, and it produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is well adapted to the temperate climate of its native habitat.

Juncus novae-zelandiae plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
