Juncus arcticus var. mexicanus

Juncus arcticus var. mexicanus, commonly known as the Mexican rush, is a perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Mexico.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in spike-like clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as a ornamental plant in dry landscapes.
