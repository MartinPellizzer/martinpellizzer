Jurinea derderioides

Jurinea derderioides, commonly known as the Derderi's sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and produces striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has silver-gray foliage covered in fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Jurinea derderioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
