Justicia caerulea

Justicia caerulea, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of blue to purple tubular flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Justicia caerulea is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
