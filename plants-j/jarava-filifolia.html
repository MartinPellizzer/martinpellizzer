Jarava filifolia

Jarava filifolia, commonly known as slender jarava, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It has a shallow root system, which helps it survive in dry conditions.

Jarava filifolia plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small wildlife in its native range.
