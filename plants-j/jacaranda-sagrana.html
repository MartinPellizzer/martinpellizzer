Jacaranda sagrana

Jacaranda sagrana, commonly known as the sacred jacaranda, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The tree has a broad, spreading canopy and can grow up to 20 meters tall. Its leaves are pinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces long, woody pods containing seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens and urban landscapes.
