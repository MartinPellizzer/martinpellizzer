Juncus glaucus subsp. angelisii

Juncus glaucus subsp.

angelisii, commonly known as the angel's hair rush, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems that are often covered in a waxy, bluish coating.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in spikes during the summer months. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and damp woodlands.

Its foliage is often used in floral arrangements due to its delicate, feathery appearance.
