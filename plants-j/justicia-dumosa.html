Justicia dumosa

Justicia dumosa, commonly known as the hairy justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a perennial shrub or subshrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy, giving it its common name. Its showy, tubular flowers are usually pink to red, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often used in ornamental gardening.
