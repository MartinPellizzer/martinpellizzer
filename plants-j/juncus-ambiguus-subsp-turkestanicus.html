Juncus ambiguus subsp. turkestanicus

Juncus ambiguus subsp.

turkestanicus, commonly known as the Turkestan rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as marshes, riverbanks, and wet meadows.

The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are usually hairless and often clustered in dense stands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing greenish to brownish flowers.

This subspecies is adapted to temperate climates and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
