Justicia gibsoniae

Justicia gibsoniae, commonly known as the Gibson's glorybower, is a strikingly beautiful flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are borne in dense, terminal clusters, making it a visually impressive specimen in gardens.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.
