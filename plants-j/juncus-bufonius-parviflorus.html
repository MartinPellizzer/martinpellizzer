Juncus bufonius parviflorus

Juncus bufonius parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered rush, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, cylindrical leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikes, often green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its adaptability, it plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
