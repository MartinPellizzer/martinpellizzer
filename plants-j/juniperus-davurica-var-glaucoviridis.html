Juniperus davurica var. glaucoviridis

Juniperus davurica var. glaucoviridis, commonly known as the blue spruce, is a coniferous evergreen shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and its distinctive bluish-gray foliage, which is covered in a waxy coating. The leaves are scale-like and tightly pressed against the branches, giving the plant a compact appearance. It produces small, cone-like structures that contain the plant's seeds, typically maturing over two years.

This variety is highly valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
