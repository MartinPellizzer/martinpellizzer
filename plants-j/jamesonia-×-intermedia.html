Jamesonia × intermedia

Jamesonia × intermedia, commonly known as the hybrid Jamesonia, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. Its foliage is evergreen, with dark green, leathery leaves that remain on the plant throughout the winter.

Jamesonia × intermedia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in acidic, well-drained soils.
