Jatropha janipha

Jatropha janipha, commonly known as the Indian spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange or red berries. Jatropha janipha is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and colorful fruits.

It is also known for its latex sap, which can be toxic if ingested.
