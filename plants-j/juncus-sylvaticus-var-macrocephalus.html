Juncus sylvaticus var. macrocephalus

Juncus sylvaticus var. macrocephalus, commonly known as the large-headed rush, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and tall, cylindrical flower spikes. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its showy, spike-like inflorescence consists of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
