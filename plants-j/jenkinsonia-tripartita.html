Jenkinsonia tripartita

Jenkinsonia tripartita, commonly known as the three-parted jenkinsonia, is a rare flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive tripartite inflorescence, where three clusters of flowers arise from a single stem. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within cloud forests. Its leaves are usually elliptical with a slightly serrated margin.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in conservation efforts.
