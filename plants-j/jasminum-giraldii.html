Jasminum giraldii

Jasminum giraldii, commonly known as the golden jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

Jasminum giraldii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its pleasant fragrance and visual appeal.
