Juncus alpinus subsp. fuscoater

Juncus alpinus subsp.

fuscoater, commonly known as the alpine rush, is a perennial plant native to mountainous regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets of brownish flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, rocky or alpine habitats, often at high elevations. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and the plant is adapted to cold climates.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil in its harsh environments.
