Jasione supina subsp. supina

Jasione supina subsp.

supina, commonly known as the prostrate bellflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features a mat-forming growth habit with slender, creeping stems that root at the nodes.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

This species thrives in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils, showcasing its adaptability to Mediterranean climates.
