Juncus pallescens var. achalensis

Juncus pallescens var. achalensis, commonly known as the Achalensis Rush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to conserve water in dry environments.

This variety is often found in rocky outcrops and sandy soils, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
