Juncus glaucus brunneus

Juncus glaucus brunneus, commonly known as the brownish rush, is a perennial plant native to moist or wet environments in North America.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae and is characterized by its cylindrical, grass-like stems that are typically brownish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters at the base of the stems, which are often surrounded by bracts. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp habitats.

Juncus glaucus brunneus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
