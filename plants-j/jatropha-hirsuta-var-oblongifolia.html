Jatropha hirsuta var. oblongifolia

Jatropha hirsuta var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the hairy jatropha, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red berries containing toxic seeds. Its leaves and seeds contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans.

It is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its distinctive foliage and seasonal flowering.
