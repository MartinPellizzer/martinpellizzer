Jacobaea carniolica subsp. insubrica

Jacobaea carniolica subsp.

insubrica, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, thriving in well-drained, nutrient-poor soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
