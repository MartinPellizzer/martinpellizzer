Jussiaea nervosa var. typica

Jussiaea nervosa var. typica, commonly known as the slender water plant, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands and marshes of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that float on the water surface, supported by long, slender stems. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a subtle ornamental value to its habitat. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often forming dense mats that provide habitat for aquatic life.

Due to its preference for specific conditions, it is not commonly cultivated outside its native range.
