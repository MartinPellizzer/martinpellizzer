Junopsis collettii var. acaulis

Junopsis collettii var. acaulis, commonly known as the slender juniper, is a low-growing shrub native to the southern Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and small, scale-like leaves that are densely packed along its stems. The plant produces blue-black berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in rocky or alpine regions.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
