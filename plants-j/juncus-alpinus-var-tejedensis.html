Juncus alpinus var. tejedensis

Juncus alpinus var. tejedensis, commonly known as the Tejed alpine rush, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, mountainous environments.

This variety is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh climates.
