Jasminum fruticans var. syriacum

Jasminum fruticans var. syriacum, commonly known as Syrian jasmine, is a climbing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant has slender, woody stems that can twine around supports, making it suitable for trellises or hedges. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical, with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its pleasant aroma and decorative appeal.
