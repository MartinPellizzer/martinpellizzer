Juncus fauriensis subsp. fauriensis

Juncus fauriensis subsp.

fauriensis, commonly known as the Fauri's rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America. It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as along riverbanks and in floodplain areas.

The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often green or reddish in color. Its flowering spikes consist of small, spike-like clusters of white or pale yellow flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
