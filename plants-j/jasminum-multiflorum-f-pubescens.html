Jasminum multiflorum f. pubescens

Jasminum multiflorum f. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent jasmine, is a fragrant flowering plant native to southern China.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that emit a sweet, delicate aroma. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly rough texture, and the flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens and as a fragrant hedge.
