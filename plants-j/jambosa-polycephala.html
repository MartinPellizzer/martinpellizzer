Jambosa polycephala

Jambosa polycephala, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical plant native to Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that develop into round, green fruits with a rough, spiny exterior. The fruit contains a sweet, white, juicy pulp surrounded by a fibrous layer.

It is valued for its edible fruit and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
