Juglans porcina var. ficiformis

Juglans porcina var. ficiformis, commonly known as the giant walnut or the giant hickory, is a variant of the Persian walnut.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces large, heavy fruits with thick, husky shells enclosing the edible nuts. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

This variant is notable for its robust growth and potential for both ornamental and commercial use.
