Jasminum domatiigerum var. orogenes

Jasminum domatiigerum var. orogenes, commonly known as the Alpine jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are woody and often branched.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
