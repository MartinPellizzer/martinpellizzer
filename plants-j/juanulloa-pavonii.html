Juanulloa pavonii

Juanulloa pavonii, commonly known as the pink velvet tree, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in high-altitude environments. It is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to the cool, humid climates of its region.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
