Justicia cynosuroides

Justicia cynosuroides, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Justicia cynosuroides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be grown in gardens or as a houseplant.
