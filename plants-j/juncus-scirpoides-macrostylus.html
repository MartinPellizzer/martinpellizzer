Juncus scirpoides macrostylus

Juncus scirpoides macrostylus, commonly known as the broad-styled rush, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems that grow in dense clusters, typically in wet or marshy habitats. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of greenish to brownish flowers, which are usually wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense vegetation in wetlands.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
