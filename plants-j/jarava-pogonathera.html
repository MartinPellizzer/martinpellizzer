Jarava pogonathera

Jarava pogonathera, commonly known as the hairy jarava, is a perennial grass species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Jarava pogonathera plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for various small animals and insects.
