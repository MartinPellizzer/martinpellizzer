Justicia aethes

Justicia aethes, commonly known as the flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It prefers well-drained soil and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display.

The genus Justicia is known for its showy bracts, which are often mistaken for petals in this species.
