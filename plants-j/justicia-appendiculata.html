Justicia appendiculata

Justicia appendiculata, commonly known as the red hot poker or flame grass, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in dense spikes, often resembling a torch. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Justicia appendiculata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
