Josephia blechnifolia

Josephia blechnifolia, commonly known as the silver fern, is a rare and endangered plant species native to the mountainous regions of New Zealand.

It belongs to the Blechnaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, silvery-green fronds that resemble a fern. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in shaded forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and have a unique, intricate pattern that gives it its common name.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Josephia blechnifolia is of significant conservation interest.
