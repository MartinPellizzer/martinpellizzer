Jacquemontia turneroides

Jacquemontia turneroides, commonly known as the Turner's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking blue to violet trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

The flowers typically bloom in the spring and summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
