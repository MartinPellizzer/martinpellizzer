Justicia tenuissima

Justicia tenuissima, commonly known as the narrowleaf flameflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of showy, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically bright red or orange, with two lips, and are arranged in spikes at the ends of the branches. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
