Julocroton ackermannianus

Julocroton ackermannianus, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often covering the entire plant during the flowering season. The leaves are typically palmate, with three to five lobes, and the plant has a woody stem with a rough, bark-like surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional remedies.
