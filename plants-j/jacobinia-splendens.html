Jacobinia splendens

Jacobinia splendens, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has compound leaves that are opposite and pinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, making it a prominent feature in landscapes.
