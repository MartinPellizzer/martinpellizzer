Justicia adscendens

Justicia adscendens, commonly known as red hot poker, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and striking clusters of red, tubular flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces showy bracts that give it its common name, while the actual flowers are small and clustered. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Justicia adscendens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.
