Jatropha dichtar

Jatropha dichtar, commonly known as the velvetleaf jatropha, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers followed by orange-red berries containing toxic seeds. It is adapted to thrive in disturbed soils and can often be found in agricultural areas.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
