Juncus sphacelatus

Juncus sphacelatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 1 millimeter wide. Its flowering spikes consist of small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences.

Juncus sphacelatus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
