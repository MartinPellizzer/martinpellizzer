Juglans ambigua

Juglans ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous walnut, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the walnut family, Juglandaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with 15 to 23 leaflets. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by oval, edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk. Its bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age, and it thrives in well-drained soils in temperate climates.

The plant is notable for its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
