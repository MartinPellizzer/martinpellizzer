Jasminum cordifolium subsp. andamanicum

Jasminum cordifolium subsp.

andamanicum, commonly known as the Andaman jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Andaman Islands. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom during the rainy season.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in warm regions.
