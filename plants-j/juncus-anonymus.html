Juncus anonymus

Juncus anonymus, commonly known as the common rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Juncaceae.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a cylindrical stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish or brownish flowers in spikes during the summer months. It thrives in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Juncus anonymus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
