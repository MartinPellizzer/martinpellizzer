Juncus conglomeratus subsp. subuliflorus

Juncus conglomeratus subsp.

subuliflorus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

The plant has slender, grass-like stems that form dense clusters, and its leaves are narrow and pointed. Its small, clustered flowers are usually greenish or brownish and appear in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
