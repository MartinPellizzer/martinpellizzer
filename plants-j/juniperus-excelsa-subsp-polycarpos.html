Juniperus excelsa subsp. polycarpos

Juniperus excelsa subsp.

polycarpos, commonly known as the Sierra juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of scale-like leaves arranged in compact clusters.

The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more robust growth and broader leaves compared to other juniper species.
