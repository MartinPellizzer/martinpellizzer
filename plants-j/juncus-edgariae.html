Juncus edgariae

Juncus edgariae, commonly known as the Edgariae rush, is a perennial plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often used in floral arrangements. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
