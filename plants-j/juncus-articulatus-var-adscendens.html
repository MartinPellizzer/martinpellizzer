Juncus articulatus var. adscendens

Juncus articulatus var. adscendens, commonly known as the ascending rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that often grow in dense clumps, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in spike-like inflorescences, which are typically found at the tips of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a jointed structure typical of the Juncaceae family.

This plant thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, pond edges, and along stream banks.
