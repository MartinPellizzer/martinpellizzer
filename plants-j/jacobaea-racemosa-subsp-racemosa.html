Jacobaea racemosa subsp. racemosa

Jacobaea racemosa subsp.

racemosa, commonly known as the spiked ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, branched inflorescences that bear yellow-orange flowers.

The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its toxic properties.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though caution is advised due to its potential harm to livestock.
