Juncus glaucus oligocarpus

Juncus glaucus oligocarpus, commonly known as the blue rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage that forms compact tufts, often growing in moist soils along stream banks and marshes. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear greenish-white flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense vegetation, contributing to soil stabilization in wetland habitats.

Juncus glaucus oligocarpus is valued for its ecological role in filtering water and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
