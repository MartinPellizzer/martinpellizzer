Jacobaea ambigua subsp. nebrodensis

Jacobaea ambigua subsp.

nebrodensis, commonly known as the Nebroden's ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It features basal and erect stems with lance-shaped, hairy leaves.

Its yellowish-orange flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
