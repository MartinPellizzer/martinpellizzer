Jasminum sambac var. nemocalyx

Jasminum sambac var. nemocalyx, commonly known as the Arabian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often at night. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is typically shrub-like in growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and use in perfumery and traditional medicine.
