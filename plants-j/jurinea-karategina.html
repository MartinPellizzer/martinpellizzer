Jurinea karategina

Jurinea karategina, commonly known as the Karategina sandflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with hairy stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer, adapted to the harsh, dry conditions of its habitat. It is well-suited to sandy and gravelly soils, often found in desert and semi-desert environments.

Jurinea karategina plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small desert organisms.
