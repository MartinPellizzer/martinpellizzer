Jacaranda mendoncaei

Jacaranda mendoncaei, commonly known as the Brazilian jacaranda, is a deciduous tree native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The tree has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces distinctive fruit pods that split open to release seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The wood is hard and durable, making it useful for furniture and construction.
