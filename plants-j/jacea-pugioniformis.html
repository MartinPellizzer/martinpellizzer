Jacea pugioniformis

Jacea pugioniformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf jacea, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters and features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Jacea pugioniformis is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
