Justicia obtusifolia

Justicia obtusifolia, commonly known as the blunt-leaf false arum, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, hooded flowers that range in color from white to purple, often with a distinctive spathe. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Justicia obtusifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
