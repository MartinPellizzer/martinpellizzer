Jasminum tenuifolium

Jasminum tenuifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has slender, woody stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

Jasminum tenuifolium is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing benefits.
