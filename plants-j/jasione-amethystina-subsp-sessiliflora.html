Jasione amethystina subsp. sessiliflora

Jasione amethystina subsp.

sessiliflora, commonly known as the purplehead bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of small, violet to purple flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
