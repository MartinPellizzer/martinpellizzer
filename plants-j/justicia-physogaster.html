Justicia physogaster

Justicia physogaster, commonly known as the fat-leaf justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a favored plant in gardens and landscapes.
