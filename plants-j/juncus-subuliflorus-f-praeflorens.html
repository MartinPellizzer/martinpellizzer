Juncus subuliflorus f. praeflorens

Juncus subuliflorus f. praeflorens, commonly known as the flowering rush, is a flowering plant species native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical spikes of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or partially submerged in water. It is typically found in marshes, pond margins, and other aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local wildlife.
