Juncus atricapillus subsp. anceps

Juncus atricapillus subsp.

anceps, commonly known as the black-tipped rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish or purplish in color, with small, scale-like leaves. Its flowering spikes produce clusters of tiny, spikelet-like flowers that are typically brown or black in color.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
