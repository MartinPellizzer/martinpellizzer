Justicia patulinervis

Justicia patulinervis, commonly known as the narrow-veined justicia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves with prominent, narrow veins. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, attracting pollinators like bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its vibrant blooms and foliage.
