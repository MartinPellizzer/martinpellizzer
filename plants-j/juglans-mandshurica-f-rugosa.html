Juglans mandshurica f. rugosa

Juglans mandshurica f. rugosa, commonly known as the rough-juglans, is a variant of the Manchurian walnut.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces edible nuts enclosed in a thick, woody husk. It thrives in temperate climates and is native to regions of eastern Asia.

This species is valued for its hard wood and has ecological importance in its native habitats.
