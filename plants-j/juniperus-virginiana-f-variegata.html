Juniperus virginiana f. variegata

Juniperus virginiana f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated eastern red cedar, is a unique variant of the eastern red cedar.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, which features white and green coloration on the leaves. This form is a natural mutation of the typical species, often found in specific ecological niches. The plant is evergreen, with small, scale-like leaves and produces blue-gray berries.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to a variety of environmental conditions.
