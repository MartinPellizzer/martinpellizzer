Juncoides sylvatica

Juncoides sylvatica, commonly known as the woodland rush, is a perennial plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color. Its leaves are narrow and cylindrical, contributing to its ability to thrive in wet conditions.

Juncoides sylvatica plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
