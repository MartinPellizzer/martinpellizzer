Jungia pauciflora subsp. pauciflora

Jungia pauciflora subsp.

pauciflora, commonly known as the slender jungle grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale yellow to white flowers, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, humid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in warmer climates.
