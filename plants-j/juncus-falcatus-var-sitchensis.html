Juncus falcatus var. sitchensis

Juncus falcatus var. sitchensis, commonly known as the Sitka rush, is a variant of the common rush species native to coastal regions of the Pacific Northwest.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as tidal zones, marshes, and along shorelines. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight curvature, and it produces achenes as its seeds.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to saltwater conditions and its role in stabilizing coastal soils.
