Justicia flexibilis

Justicia flexibilis, commonly known as the flexible justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, often vine-like stems that can trail or climb, making it adaptable to various growth forms. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which attract pollinators such as birds and insects. Its leaves are typically opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy texture.

Justicia flexibilis thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its vibrant blooms.
