Jasminum dumicola

Jasminum dumicola, commonly known as the rock jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Jasminum dumicola is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional medicine.
