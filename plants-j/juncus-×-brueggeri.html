Juncus × brueggeri

Juncus × brueggeri, commonly known as the hybrid rush, is a perennial plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is a hybrid between Juncus effusus and Juncus roemerianus, resulting in a plant with unique botanical characteristics. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and shores, where it thrives in moist to saline soils. Its cylindrical, grass-like stems are often green or reddish in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Juncus × brueggeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing shorelines.
