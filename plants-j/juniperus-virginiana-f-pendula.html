Juniperus virginiana f. pendula

Juniperus virginiana f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping red cedar, is a unique form of the eastern red cedar.

It is characterized by its cascading, drooping branches that create a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are often used in ornamental landscaping. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adaptable to various light conditions.

This cultivar is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in garden settings.
