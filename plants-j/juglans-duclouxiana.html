Juglans duclouxiana

Juglans duclouxiana, commonly known as the Ducloux hickory, is a species of hickory native to Mexico.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by edible, walnut-like nuts enclosed in a spiky husk.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
