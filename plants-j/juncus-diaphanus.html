Juncus diaphanus

Juncus diaphanus, commonly known as the ghost rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that often appear translucent, giving it a ghostly appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous brown flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Juncus diaphanus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
