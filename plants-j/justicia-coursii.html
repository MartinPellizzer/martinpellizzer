Justicia coursii

Justicia coursii, commonly known as the Mexican flamebush, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tubular, red or orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant floral display and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
