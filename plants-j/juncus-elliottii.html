Juncus elliottii

Juncus elliottii, commonly known as the Elliott's rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. The plant features dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, cylindrical spikes of small, greenish-white flowers. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Juncus elliottii is well adapted to saturated soils and can tolerate periodic flooding.
