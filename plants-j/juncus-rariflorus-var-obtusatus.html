Juncus rariflorus var. obtusatus

Juncus rariflorus var. obtusatus, commonly known as the blunt-flowered rush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and streambanks. Its flowers are usually greenish or brownish in color and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The blunt-flowered rush plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
