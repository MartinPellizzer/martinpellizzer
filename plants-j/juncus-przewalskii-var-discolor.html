Juncus przewalskii var. discolor

Juncus przewalskii var. discolor, commonly known as the Przewalski's rush, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems that grow in dense clusters, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. The leaves are narrow and cylindrical, typically green with a slight bluish tint, and the plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and rocky slopes.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
