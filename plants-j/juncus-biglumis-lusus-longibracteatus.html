Juncus biglumis lusus longibracteatus

Juncus biglumis lusus longibracteatus, commonly known as the long-bracted rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
