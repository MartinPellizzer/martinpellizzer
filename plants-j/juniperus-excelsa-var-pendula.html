Juniperus excelsa var. pendula

Juniperus excelsa var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping juniper, is a slow-growing evergreen shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its cascading, drooping branches that create a distinctive, weeping appearance. The plant produces small, blue-gray berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its unique form and hardy nature make it a striking addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
