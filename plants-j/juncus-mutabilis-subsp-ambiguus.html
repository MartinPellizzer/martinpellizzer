Juncus mutabilis subsp. ambiguus

Juncus mutabilis subsp.

ambiguus, commonly known as the variable rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of North America. It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as pond edges and damp meadows.

The plant features narrow, cylindrical leaves that are often green to reddish in color, depending on environmental conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, which are usually pale green to brown.

This subspecies is notable for its ability to thrive in fluctuating water levels, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
