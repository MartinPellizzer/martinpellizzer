Johnstonella angustifolia

Johnstonella angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed Johnstonella, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or shaded areas. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white or pale pink flowers appear in clusters during the spring and early summer.

Johnstonella angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and flowering habit.
