Juncoides subcongesta

Juncoides subcongesta, commonly known as the dense rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Juncaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves that form a compact rosette. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color, arranged in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Juncoides subcongesta plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
