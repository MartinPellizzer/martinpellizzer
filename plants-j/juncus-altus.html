Juncus altus

Juncus altus, commonly known as tall rush, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers arranged in spike-like clusters. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil in marshy environments.

Juncus altus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
