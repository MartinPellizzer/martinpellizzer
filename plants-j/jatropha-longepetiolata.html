Jatropha longepetiolata

Jatropha longepetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled physic nut, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often hairy leaves with long petioles. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing toxic seeds. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and potential in biofuel production.

However, its seeds contain toxic compounds, making it potentially hazardous to livestock and humans.
