Justicia corymbulosa

Justicia corymbulosa, commonly known as the clustered flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and often branched.

Justicia corymbulosa thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
