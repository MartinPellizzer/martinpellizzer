Julocroton lanatus

Julocroton lanatus, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety layer of fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in terminal panicles, followed by orange-red berries containing several seeds. Julocroton lanatus is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields due to its adaptability.

Its hairy foliage and rapid growth make it both a common weed and a plant of interest in ecological studies.
