Jungia crenata

Jungia crenata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often forming dense colonies in wetlands or along water edges. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive crenate or wavy margin.

Jungia crenata produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the sedge family.
