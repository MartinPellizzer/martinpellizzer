Juncus sellowianus

Juncus sellowianus, commonly known as the Sellow's rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and covered with small, scale-like leaves. Its flowering spikes consist of numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that bloom in late summer.

Juncus sellowianus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
