Justicia serrana

Justicia serrana, commonly known as the serrated justicia, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces striking clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from white to violet, often with a contrasting throat. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, Justicia serrana is widely cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
