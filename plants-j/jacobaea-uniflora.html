Jacobaea uniflora

Jacobaea uniflora, commonly known as the singleflower ragwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. It features yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a rough texture.

Jacobaea uniflora is also known for its toxic properties, containing pyrrolizidine alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
