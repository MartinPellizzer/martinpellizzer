Justicia aurea f. erythrantha

Justicia aurea f. erythrantha, commonly known as the red-flowered flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy red bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
