Justicia miguelii

Justicia miguelii, commonly known as the Mexican wheelcactus, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern. The plant features thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. Its flowers are typically tubular and come in shades of red, orange, or yellow, attracting pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
