Juniperus virginiana var. glauca

Juniperus virginiana var. glauca, commonly known as the smooth juniper, is a subspecies of the eastern red cedar.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in dry, sandy soils across the central and eastern United States. The plant has scale-like leaves that are blue-gray in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its cones, which are technically berries, are typically blue and contain toxic seeds.

The plant is known for its aromatic foliage and is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
