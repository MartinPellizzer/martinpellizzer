Jatropha lanciniosa

Jatropha lanciniosa, commonly known as the arrowleaf jatropha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces toxic, orange-red berries that contain seeds used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for biofuel production, it is cultivated in various parts of the world.
