Juncus obtusiflorus var. condensatus

Juncus obtusiflorus var. condensatus, commonly known as the denseflower rush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence that bears small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
