Jussiaea repens f. hookeri

Jussiaea repens f. hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's trailing jussiaea, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically forms dense mats of slender, prostrate stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread along the ground. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers clustered in terminal inflorescences, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is well adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
