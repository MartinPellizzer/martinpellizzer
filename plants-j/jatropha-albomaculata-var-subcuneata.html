Jatropha albomaculata var. subcuneata

Jatropha albomaculata var. subcuneata, commonly known as the white-spotted physic nut, is a flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to parts of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive white spots on the leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing toxic seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage.

However, it should be handled with care as all parts of the plant are poisonous.
