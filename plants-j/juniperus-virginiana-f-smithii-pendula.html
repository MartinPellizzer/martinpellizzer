Juniperus virginiana f. smithii-pendula

Juniperus virginiana f. smithii-pendula, commonly known as the weeping eastern red cedar, is a unique form of the eastern red cedar characterized by its drooping branches.

This ornamental plant features scale-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, providing a distinct visual appeal. It is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for landscaping in regions with suitable climates.
