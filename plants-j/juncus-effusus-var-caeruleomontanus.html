Juncus effusus var. caeruleomontanus

Juncus effusus var. caeruleomontanus, commonly known as the blue mountain rush, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. It is a perennial species that is adapted to cool climates and high altitudes.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking to add texture and color to wetland or bog gardens.
