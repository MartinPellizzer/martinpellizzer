Juncus gerardi pallidus

Juncus gerardi pallidus, commonly known as the pale rush, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually pale green to yellowish in color. Its flowering spikes are usually erect and produce small, inconspicuous flowers.

It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
