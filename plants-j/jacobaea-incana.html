Jacobaea incana

Jacobaea incana, commonly known as the stiff groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features hairy, gray-green leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Jacobaea incana is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
