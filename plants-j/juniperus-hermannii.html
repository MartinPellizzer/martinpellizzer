Juniperus hermannii

Juniperus hermannii, commonly known as the Japanese juniper, is a coniferous evergreen shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Juniperus hermannii is also valued for its hardiness and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
