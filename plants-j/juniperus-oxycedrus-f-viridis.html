Juniperus oxycedrus f. viridis

Juniperus oxycedrus f. viridis, commonly known as the green juniper, is a rare and distinctive subspecies of the juniper genus.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage that retains a vibrant green color throughout the year. This evergreen shrub or small tree is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly in southern France and Spain. Its compact form and aromatic leaves make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
