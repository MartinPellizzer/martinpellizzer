Juncus bufonius var. gracilis

Juncus bufonius var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender bog rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters near the top of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and they are usually arranged in a dense, tufted manner at the base.

This species is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
