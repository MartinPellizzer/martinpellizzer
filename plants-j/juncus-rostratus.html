Juncus rostratus

Juncus rostratus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It typically grows in dense clusters, reaching heights of 1 to 3 feet, with slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish or greenish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes during the summer months, which are clustered near the tips of the stems. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a waxy coating, helping to repel water.

Juncus rostratus plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
