Juncus thomasii melanocephalus

Juncus thomasii melanocephalus, commonly known as the black-headed rush, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and pond edges. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish or purplish in color, and it produces small, clustered flowers. Its leaves are grass-like and narrow, contributing to its overall rush-like appearance.

The black-headed rush is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
