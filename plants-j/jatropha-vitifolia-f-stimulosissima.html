Jatropha vitifolia f. stimulosissima

Jatropha vitifolia f. stimulosissima, commonly known as the prickly leaf jatropha, is a variant of the jatropha genus native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that are often covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red berries containing toxic seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for medicinal use, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and research settings.
