Jaltomata cajamarca

Jaltomata cajamarca, commonly known as the Cajamarca morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky soils, thriving in cooler climates. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from purple to blue.

Jaltomata cajamarca is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
