Justicia paupercula

Justicia paupercula, commonly known as the poor man's verbena, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that come in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Justicia paupercula is known for its long blooming period, typically from spring to early autumn.
