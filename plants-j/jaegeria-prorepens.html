Jaegeria prorepens

Jaegeria prorepens, commonly known as the creeping jenny, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, creeping stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across moist soils. Its foliage is heart-shaped and often covered in fine, soft hairs.

It thrives in wet or damp environments, making it a popular choice for water gardens and bog landscapes.
