Jacksonago sericea

Jacksonago sericea, commonly known as the silky jacksonago, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems covered in fine, silky hairs, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with low rainfall.

Jacksonago sericea is often found in rocky soils and is used in traditional medicine by local communities.
