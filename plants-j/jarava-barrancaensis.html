Jarava barrancaensis

Jarava barrancaensis, commonly known as the canyon jarava, is a perennial grass native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in rocky soils and along canyon walls, where it thrives in full sun and low moisture conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil and prevent erosion in its native habitat.

Jarava barrancaensis is well adapted to harsh environments and plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
