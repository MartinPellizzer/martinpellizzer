Jacksonia dodecandra

Jacksonia dodecandra, commonly known as the Christmas flower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the daisy family, Asteraceae, and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers with a central disc. The plant typically grows in coastal regions and is adapted to sandy soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance and long-lasting blooms.

Jacksonia dodecandra is also used in traditional medicine by Indigenous Australians.
