Juglans mucronata

Juglans mucronata, commonly known as the Mexican walnut, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Juglandaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated leaflets. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, followed by oval, edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it thrives in well-drained soils in temperate to subtropical climates.

The Mexican walnut is valued for its timber and has cultural significance in traditional medicine.
