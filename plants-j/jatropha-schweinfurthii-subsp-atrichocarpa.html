Jatropha schweinfurthii subsp. atrichocarpa

Jatropha schweinfurthii subsp.

atrichocarpa, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a shrub native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy or pubescent.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by capsule fruits containing toxic, oily seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
