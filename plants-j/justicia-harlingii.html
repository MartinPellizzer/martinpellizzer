Justicia harlingii

Justicia harlingii, commonly known as the hummingbird trumpet, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and Mexico.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

The nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees, contributing to its ecological significance.
