Justicia corynimorpha

Justicia corynimorpha, commonly known as the silverleaf justicia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, velvety leaves and clusters of bright blue or violet flowers. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

The flowering period typically occurs in the summer months, adding a vibrant touch to landscapes.
