Juncus tenuis

Juncus tenuis, commonly known as the slender rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments across North America.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that are often green or reddish in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters near the tips of the stems. The plant thrives in moist to water-saturated soils, making it a common sight in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil, contributing to the ecological health of wetland habitats.

Juncus tenuis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its upright growth habit.
