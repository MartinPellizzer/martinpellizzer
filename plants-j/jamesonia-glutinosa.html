Jamesonia glutinosa

Jamesonia glutinosa, commonly known as the sticky jamesonia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its glabrous, often sticky stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a suitable addition to woodland gardens.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a valuable plant for ecological and ornamental purposes.
