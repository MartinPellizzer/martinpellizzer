Justicia depauperata

Justicia depauperata, commonly known as the poor man's justicia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
