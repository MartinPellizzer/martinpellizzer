Jasminum dasyphyllum

Jasminum dasyphyllum, commonly known as the silver jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the spring and summer. Its leaves are elliptical and covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic blooms.
