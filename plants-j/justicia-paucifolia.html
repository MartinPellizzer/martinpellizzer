Justicia paucifolia

Justicia paucifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or coralplant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to purple, often with a distinctive yellow or orange throat. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its botanical features include showy bracts that resemble petals, which help attract pollinators.

Justicia paucifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
