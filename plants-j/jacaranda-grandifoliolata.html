Jacaranda grandifoliolata

Jacaranda grandifoliolata, commonly known as the large-leaved jacaranda, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The leaves are large, compound, and often have a glossy appearance, contributing to its ornamental value. The tree produces long, slender pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its beauty and ability to provide shade.
