Justicia ovalis

Justicia ovalis, commonly known as the ovalleaf flamebeard, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with opposite, oval-shaped leaves. Its blooming period occurs during the warmer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
