Jasminum zenkeri var. glabratum

Jasminum zenkeri var. glabratum, commonly known as the smooth jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has slender, woody stems and simple, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy when young. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant scent.
