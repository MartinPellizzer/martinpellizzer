Juncus pseudacutus

Juncus pseudacutus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are often dark green and slightly hairy. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are usually green or brownish in color.

Juncus pseudacutus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
