Justicia hylobates

Justicia hylobates, commonly known as the silverleaf justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, silver-gray leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its ability to attract hummingbirds and butterflies adds to its ecological value in gardens.
