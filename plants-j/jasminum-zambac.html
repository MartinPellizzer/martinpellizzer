Jasminum zambac

Jasminum zambac, commonly known as Zambian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and lance-shaped, with a glossy green surface.

The flowers are often used in perfumery and traditional medicine due to their aromatic properties.
