Jasminum fluminense subsp. fluminense

Jasminum fluminense subsp.

fluminense, commonly known as the Brazilian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a sweet scent.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with a glossy green surface.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
