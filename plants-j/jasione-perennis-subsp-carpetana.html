Jasione perennis subsp. carpetana

Jasione perennis subsp.

carpetana, commonly known as the carpet flower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers.

The plant typically thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
