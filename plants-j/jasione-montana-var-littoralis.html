Jasione montana var. littoralis

Jasione montana var. littoralis, commonly known as the coastal bellflower, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of Europe.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along sea shores and cliffs. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy blue to violet flowers in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds typical of coastal environments.

Its flowering period attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
