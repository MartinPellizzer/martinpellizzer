Juncus acutus var. paniculatus

Juncus acutus var. paniculatus, commonly known as the panicled rush, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems that grow in dense clusters. The plant produces erect, branched inflorescences bearing small, spike-like flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its adaptability, it plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
