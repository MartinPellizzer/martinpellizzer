Jasminum neocaledonicum var. angustifolium

Jasminum neocaledonicum var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leafed garden jasmine, is a flowering plant native to New Caledonia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its foliage is evergreen, providing dense coverage, while its flowers emit a sweet, delicate aroma.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
