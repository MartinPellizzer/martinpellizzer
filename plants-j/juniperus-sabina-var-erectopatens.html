Juniperus sabina var. erectopatens

Juniperus sabina var. erectopatens, commonly known as the upright juniper, is a shrub-like evergreen conifer native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, compact foliage composed of small, scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spherical blue berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and as a decorative hedge.

Its hardy nature and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice in horticulture.
