Justicia thunbergioides

Justicia thunbergioides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in dense, spike-like clusters. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The genus Justicia is part of the Acanthaceae family, known for its ornamental value and unique floral structures.
