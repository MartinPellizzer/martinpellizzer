Justicia kotschyi

Justicia kotschyi, commonly known as the flame flower or desert flame, is a perennial shrub native to arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, brightly colored flowers that range from red to orange, often appearing in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, adapting to its dry environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds, contributing to its ecological importance in its native habitat.
