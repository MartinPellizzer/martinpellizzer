Juncus fasciculiflorus

Juncus fasciculiflorus, commonly known as the clustered flower rush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils along the edges of ponds, marshes, and streams. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that bear dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often form a rosette at the base.

Juncus fasciculiflorus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
