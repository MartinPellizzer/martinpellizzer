Juncus scirpoides var. meridionalis

Juncus scirpoides var. meridionalis, commonly known as the southern rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has cylindrical, grass-like stems that are usually green or reddish in color, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late spring to early summer. It is often found in moist to saturated soils and is an important component of wetland ecosystems.

The species is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
