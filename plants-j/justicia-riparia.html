Justicia riparia

Justicia riparia, commonly known as the river hibiscus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, typically blooming from late spring to early fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils near water sources such as streams and wetlands.

The flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
