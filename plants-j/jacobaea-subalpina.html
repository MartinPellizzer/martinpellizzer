Jacobaea subalpina

Jacobaea subalpina, commonly known as Alpine ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is often found at high altitudes, where it withstands cold temperatures and strong winds.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
