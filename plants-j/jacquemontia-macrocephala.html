Jacquemontia macrocephala

Jacquemontia macrocephala, commonly known as the large-headed vine.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of yellow to orange blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Jacquemontia macrocephala thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and shaded areas.
