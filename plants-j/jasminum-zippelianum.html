Jasminum zippelianum

Jasminum zippelianum, commonly known as the Zippel's jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a climbing shrub or vine, with opposite, simple, and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and horticultural collections.
