Justicia marmorata

Justicia marmorata, commonly known as the marble plant or marble queen, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a glossy appearance.

Justicia marmorata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage and long-lasting blooms.
