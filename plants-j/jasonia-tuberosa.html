Jasonia tuberosa

Jasonia tuberosa, commonly known as the tuberous jasonia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots, which store nutrients and water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.

Jasonia tuberosa prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure for optimal growth.
