Juncus neomexicanus

Juncus neomexicanus, commonly known as New Mexico rush, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and tall, cylindrical flower spikes. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and pond edges. Its slender, hollow stems are often used in basket weaving and traditional crafts.

Juncus neomexicanus plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
