Justicia latispica

Justicia latispica, commonly known as the broad-spined jewelweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with contrasting yellow markings. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge, while the flowers are borne in clusters at the tips of the branches.

Justicia latispica thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.
