Juglans rubra

Juglans rubra, commonly known as the eastern black walnut, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with 15 to 30 leaflets and its distinctive, edible nuts encased in a thick, green husk. The tree produces yellowish-green flowers in catkins, with male and female flowers on separate trees. Its bark is dark, rugged, and deeply furrowed, while its wood is highly valued for its strength and rich, aromatic scent.

The plant plays an important ecological role by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
