Justicia reginaldii

Justicia reginaldii, commonly known as the Reginald's false heather, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
