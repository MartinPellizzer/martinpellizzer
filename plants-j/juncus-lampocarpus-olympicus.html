Juncus lampocarpus olympicus

Juncus lampocarpus olympicus, commonly known as the Olympic rush, is a perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like clusters of narrow, cylindrical leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks, wet meadows, and other wetland habitats. It produces small, greenish flowers in late summer, which are clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

This species is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
