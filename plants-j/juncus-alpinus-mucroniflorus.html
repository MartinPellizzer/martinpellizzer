Juncus alpinus mucroniflorus

Juncus alpinus mucroniflorus, commonly known as the alpine rush, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage that grows in tufts or clumps, with slender, cylindrical stems that often bear small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color, arranged in clusters along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows, rocky slopes, and wetlands.

Juncus alpinus mucroniflorus is adapted to cold climates and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in difficult environments.
