Justicia schimperi subsp. fistulosa

Justicia schimperi subsp.

fistulosa, commonly known as the red-flowered justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its striking red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its stems are often woody and can become somewhat succulent, allowing it to store water in arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
