Juncus conglomeratus umbrosus

Juncus conglomeratus umbrosus, commonly known as the dusky rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage composed of narrow, cylindrical leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in shaded areas, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.

Its structure and growth pattern make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
