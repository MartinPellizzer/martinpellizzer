Jurinea crispoundulata

Jurinea crispoundulata, commonly known as the brittleleaf sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish flowers during the spring and early summer. Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to access moisture from deeper soil layers.

Jurinea crispoundulata is adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in areas with minimal rainfall.
