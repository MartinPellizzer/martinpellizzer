Jasminum chiae

Jasminum chiae, commonly known as the Chinese jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and elliptical in shape, with a smooth texture.

The flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic oils.
