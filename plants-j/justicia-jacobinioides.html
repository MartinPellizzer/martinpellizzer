Justicia jacobinioides

Justicia jacobinioides, commonly known as the Jacobine false heather, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces striking clusters of tubular, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
