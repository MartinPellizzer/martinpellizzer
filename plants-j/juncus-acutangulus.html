Juncus acutangulus

Juncus acutangulus, commonly known as the sharp-tipped rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer. It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.

Juncus acutangulus is also valued for its ornamental appeal in garden settings.
