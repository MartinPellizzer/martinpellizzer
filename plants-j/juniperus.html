Juniperus

Juniperus, commonly known as juniper, is a genus of evergreen coniferous shrubs and trees belonging to the family Cupressaceae.

It is characterized by its scale-like or awl-shaped leaves, which are typically arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are often blue or purple in color, containing aromatic seeds. Juniperus species are widely distributed across the Northern Hemisphere, thriving in a variety of climates and soils.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal, medicinal properties, and use in landscaping and traditional remedies.
