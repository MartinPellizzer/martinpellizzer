Jurinea caespitosa

Jurinea caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted sea bladder, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in coastal environments, particularly in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or yellowish in color. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid conditions.

Jurinea caespitosa is often found in maritime climates and is well-suited to withstand salt spray and strong winds.
