Juniperus navicularis

Juniperus navicularis, commonly known as the narrowleaf juniper, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for various birds and animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Juniperus navicularis is valued for its hardiness and is commonly used in landscaping and as a windbreak in arid environments.
