Justicia kessleri

Justicia kessleri, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking spikes of red, orange, or yellow tubular flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its vibrant blooms attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
