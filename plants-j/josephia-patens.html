Josephia patens

Josephia patens, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant has a fibrous root system adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils. Its leaves are typically narrow and succulent, helping to store water.

Josephia patens plays an important role in desert ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small animals.
