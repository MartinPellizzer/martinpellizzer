Jacea nevadensis

Jacea nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada jacea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high elevations of the Sierra Nevada mountain range in California.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open meadows and alpine habitats. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale blue flowers in late spring. It is adapted to cold climates and has a short growing season.

Jacea nevadensis is considered a rare and endemic species, highlighting the unique botanical diversity of the Sierra Nevada ecosystem.
