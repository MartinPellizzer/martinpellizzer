Justicia yungensis

Justicia yungensis, commonly known as Yung's glorybower, is a flowering plant native to South America, specifically Ecuador.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as cloud forests, where it thrives in the understory. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are somewhat woody and branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
