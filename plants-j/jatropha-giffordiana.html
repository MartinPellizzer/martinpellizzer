Jatropha giffordiana

Jatropha giffordiana, commonly known as the Gifford's spurge, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and wetlands. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, orange-red capsule that splits open when mature.

Jatropha giffordiana is often found in wetland ecosystems and is adapted to fluctuating water levels.
