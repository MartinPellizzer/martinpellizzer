Juncus macranthus

Juncus macranthus, commonly known as the tall rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often forming dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences topped with pale green to brownish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically growing in a rosette at the base of the plant.

Juncus macranthus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
