Justicia hylaea

Justicia hylaea, commonly known as the flame violet, is a tropical perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting blue or purple throat. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Justicia hylaea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
