Jovibarba hirta

Jovibarba hirta, commonly known as the hairy stonecrop, is a perennial succulent native to the Mediterranean region.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Jovibarba hirta is known for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
