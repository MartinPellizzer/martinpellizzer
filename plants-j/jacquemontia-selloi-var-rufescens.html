Jacquemontia selloi var. rufescens

Jacquemontia selloi var. rufescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed golden vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy, reddish stems. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dry seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, flowering climbers.
