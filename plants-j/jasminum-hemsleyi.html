Jasminum hemsleyi

Jasminum hemsleyi, commonly known as the Hemsley's jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Malaysia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with opposite, simple, and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, usually during the summer months. Its botanical features include a woody stem, aromatic foliage, and a preference for well-drained soil and partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
