Jurinea ceratocarpa var. ceratocarpa

Jurinea ceratocarpa var. ceratocarpa, commonly known as the desert sandflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Eurasia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features small, yellowish to greenish flowers. The plant has hairy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in dry environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes and inland deserts.
