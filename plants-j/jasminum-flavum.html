Jasminum flavum

Jasminum flavum, commonly known as the yellow jessamine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are usually dark green in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Jasminum flavum is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
