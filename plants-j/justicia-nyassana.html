Justicia nyassana

Justicia nyassana, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, with upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually clustered in terminal spikes and come in shades of red, pink, and white.

Justicia nyassana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
