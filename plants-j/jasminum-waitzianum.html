Jasminum waitzianum

Jasminum waitzianum, commonly known as the Vietnamese jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a glossy green surface.

The flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
