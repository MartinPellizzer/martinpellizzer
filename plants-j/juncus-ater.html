Juncus ater

Juncus ater, commonly known as black rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland areas of North America.

It produces tall, cylindrical spikes of small, greenish-brown flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green to blackish in color, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Juncus ater plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
