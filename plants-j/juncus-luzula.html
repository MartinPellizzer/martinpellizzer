Juncus luzula

Juncus luzula, commonly known as the slender rush, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and cylindrical, often hollow stems. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and roadside ditches. Its flowering spikes are usually borne at the top of the stems and consist of small, greenish or brownish flowers.

Juncus luzula is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
