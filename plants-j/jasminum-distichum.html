Jasminum distichum

Jasminum distichum, commonly known as the two-ranked jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, linear leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its flowers bloom in clusters, emitting a sweet scent that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The botanical features of Jasminum distichum make it a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
