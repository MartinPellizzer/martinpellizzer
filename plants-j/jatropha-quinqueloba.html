Jatropha quinqueloba

Jatropha quinqueloba, commonly known as the five-lobed physic nut, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are divided into five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries containing toxic seeds. It is part of the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its latex-producing species.

Due to its toxicity, it is not typically cultivated for ornamental or agricultural purposes.
