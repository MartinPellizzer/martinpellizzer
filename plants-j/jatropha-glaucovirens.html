Jatropha glaucovirens

Jatropha glaucovirens, commonly known as the silverleaf jatropha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, waxy hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to bright orange or red berries. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.

However, it is toxic to humans and animals, containing compounds that can cause severe illness if ingested.
