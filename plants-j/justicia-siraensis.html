Justicia siraensis

Justicia siraensis, commonly known as the Sira flameflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine, silky hair, while its flowers are clustered in whorls at the ends of the stems.

Justicia siraensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
