Justicia squamosa

Justicia squamosa, commonly known as the flame violet, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking clusters of tubular, violet to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical name reflects its ornamental value, with "squamosa" referring to the scale-like appearance of its leaves.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
