Jacaranda elliptica

Jacaranda elliptica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf jacaranda, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, elliptical leaves and striking clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in the spring. The tree produces long, woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its foliage and flowers make it a popular choice for urban landscaping and garden decoration.
