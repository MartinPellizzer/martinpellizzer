Juncus pseudocastaneus

Juncus pseudocastaneus, commonly known as the red stemmed bulrush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the wetlands of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often turn reddish in color as they mature. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of white to pale green flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds.

Juncus pseudocastaneus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
