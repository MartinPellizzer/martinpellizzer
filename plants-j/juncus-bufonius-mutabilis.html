Juncus bufonius mutabilis

Juncus bufonius mutabilis, commonly known as the variable rush, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and tall spikes of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist soils, often in marshes, swamps, or along the edges of ponds. It is known for its ability to thrive in saturated conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife.

The species is also notable for its variability in flower color and form, which contributes to its common name.
