Juncus communis var. pauciflorus

Juncus communis var. pauciflorus, commonly known as the sparse-flowered rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that are typically green or reddish in color, often growing in dense clumps. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish or brownish, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a rosette at the base.

This variety is adapted to moist soils and is often found in marshes, pond edges, and along stream banks.
