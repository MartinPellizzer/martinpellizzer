Juncus germanorum

Juncus germanorum, commonly known as the German rush, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as along stream banks and in wet meadows. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, spike-like clusters of greenish-yellow flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and they are usually covered with a fine, powdery coating.

Juncus germanorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist environments.
