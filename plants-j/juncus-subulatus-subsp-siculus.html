Juncus subulatus subsp. siculus

Juncus subulatus subsp.

siculus, commonly known as the Sicilian rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as marshes, ditches, and stream banks.

The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly pointed. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

The species is characterized by its adaptability to wet environments and its role in stabilizing soil in aquatic ecosystems.
