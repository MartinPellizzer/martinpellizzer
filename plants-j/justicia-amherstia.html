Justicia amherstia

Justicia amherstia, commonly known as the Amherst's bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are arranged in dense spikes and attract various pollinators, including hummingbirds and bees.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in warm, humid climates.
