Juncus articulatus f. natans

Juncus articulatus f. natans, commonly known as the floating rush, is a variant of the common rush that exhibits unique aquatic adaptations.

This plant is characterized by its ability to float on the water surface, with its stems and leaves extending into the water. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged or just at the water's surface.

It is commonly found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds, ditches, and wet meadows.
