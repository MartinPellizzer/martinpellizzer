Jambosa pulchella

Jambosa pulchella, commonly known as the pink fairy tern, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, round fruits that are edible. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
