Justicia emarginata

Justicia emarginata, commonly known as the margined justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a distinctive white or yellow margin.

Justicia emarginata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
