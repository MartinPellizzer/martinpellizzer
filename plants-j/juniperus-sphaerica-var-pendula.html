Juniperus sphaerica var. pendula

Juniperus sphaerica var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that give it a distinctive, cascading appearance. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. Its berries are blue or black and are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant once established.
