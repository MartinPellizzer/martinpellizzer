Justicia magentea

Justicia magentea, commonly known as the purple flame or magenta justicia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular, magenta-pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly rough texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Justicia magentea is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and butterflies.
