Juncus erecticulmis

Juncus erecticulmis, commonly known as the erect-spiked rush, is a perennial plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are typically unbranched and covered in fine, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows.

Juncus erecticulmis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
