Juncus urvillei

Juncus urvillei, commonly known as the Urville's rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China and the Korean Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Juncus urvillei thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows.

Its fibrous roots and dense growth habit make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
