Juglans torreyi

Juglans torreyi, commonly known as the California black walnut, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the walnut family, Juglandaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and edible nuts. The tree produces green, hairy fruits that mature to a dark brown color, containing a single hard-shelled nut. Its bark is thick and deeply furrowed, providing a distinctive appearance.

The plant is valued for its wood and has ecological significance in its native habitat.
