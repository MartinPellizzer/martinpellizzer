Juno schischkinii

Juno schischkinii, commonly known as the Schischkin's juniper, is a species of juniper native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of up to 3 meters. The plant has scale-like leaves that are tightly pressed against the branches, giving it a dense, compact appearance. Its small, blue-gray berries are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Juno schischkinii is adapted to harsh, arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
