Justicia incana

Justicia incana, commonly known as the velvetleaf or white-flowered justicia, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Justicia incana is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and prolonged flowering period.
