Japarandiba angustifolia

Japarandiba angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the spring. Its aromatic foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Japarandiba angustifolia is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Brazilian medicine.
