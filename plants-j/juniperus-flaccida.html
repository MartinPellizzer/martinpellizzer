Juniperus flaccida

Juniperus flaccida, commonly known as the desert juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, scale-like leaves that are adapted to conserve water in dry environments. The plant produces blue to purple berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Juniperus flaccida is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.

It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in poor, well-drained soils.
