Juglans fallax

Juglans fallax, commonly known as the Arizona walnut, is a deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 10 to 20 inches long and have 15 to 30 leaflets. The tree produces small, round green fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color, encased in a thick, spiny husk. Its bark is rugged and fissured, often appearing gray to brown in color.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species in regions with low rainfall.
