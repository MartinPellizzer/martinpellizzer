Johnsonia lupulina

Johnsonia lupulina, commonly known as the golden bell orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

This plant is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically golden yellow in color. The leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Johnsonia lupulina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
