Juncus polyanthemus

Juncus polyanthemus, commonly known as the pink rush or red rush, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and spikes of small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowering season occurs in late spring to early summer, and it produces both male and female flowers on separate plants.

Juncus polyanthemus is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
