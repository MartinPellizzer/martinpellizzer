Jambosa koenigii

Jambosa koenigii, commonly known as the African blackberry, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval or elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small, white or pale green, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye.
