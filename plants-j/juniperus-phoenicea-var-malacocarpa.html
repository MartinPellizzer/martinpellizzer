Juniperus phoenicea var. malacocarpa

Juniperus phoenicea var. malacocarpa, commonly known as the Mediterranean juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It has needle-like leaves that are blue-green in color and often have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are typically blue or black when mature. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in arid environments.
