Juncus crassistylus

Juncus crassistylus, commonly known as the broad-stemmed rush, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters near the tips of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil.

Juncus crassistylus is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
