Jurinea brachypappa

Jurinea brachypappa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with succulent stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Jurinea brachypappa is often found in steppe and desert environments, contributing to the ecological stability of these habitats.
