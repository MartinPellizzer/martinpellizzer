Jacobaea sandrasica

Jacobaea sandrasica, commonly known as the sandrasica ragwort, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sandy soils. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster.

Jacobaea sandrasica is known for its toxic properties, containing pyrrolizidine alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
