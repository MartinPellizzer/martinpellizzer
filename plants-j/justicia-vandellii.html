Justicia vandellii

Justicia vandellii, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in spikes. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
