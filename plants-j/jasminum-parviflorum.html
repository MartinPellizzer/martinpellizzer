Jasminum parviflorum

Jasminum parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has opposite, simple, and lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

Jasminum parviflorum is also used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic benefits.
