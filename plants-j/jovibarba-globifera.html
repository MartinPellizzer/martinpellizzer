Jovibarba globifera

Jovibarba globifera, commonly known as the globe saxifrage, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and fleshy, gray-green leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the summer months, which are arranged in a compact inflorescence. It is well adapted to rocky and gravelly soils, often growing in high-altitude areas with strong sunlight.

Jovibarba globifera is a popular choice for rock gardens due to its hardiness and ornamental appearance.
