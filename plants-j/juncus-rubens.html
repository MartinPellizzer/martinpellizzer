Juncus rubens

Juncus rubens, commonly known as the red rush, is a flowering plant native to wetland and marshy areas in North America.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of grass-like, cylindrical stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color, which bloom in late summer. Juncus rubens thrives in moist soils and can often be found in shallow water or along the edges of ponds and streams.

It plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
