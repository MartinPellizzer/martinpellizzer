Juncus lancangensis

Juncus lancangensis, commonly known as the Lancang rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that are typically hollow and covered in fine, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are usually greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies.

Juncus lancangensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
