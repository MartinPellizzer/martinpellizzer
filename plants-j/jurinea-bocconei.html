Jurinea bocconei

Jurinea bocconei, commonly known as the Boccone's sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in shades of white and yellow.

Jurinea bocconei is well adapted to salt spray and strong winds, making it a hardy species of the coastal ecosystem.
