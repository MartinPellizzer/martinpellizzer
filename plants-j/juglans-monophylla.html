Juglans monophylla

Juglans monophylla, commonly known as the single-leaf walnut, is a species of flowering plant in the family Juglandaceae.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its single, large, palmately lobed leaf that gives it its common name. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by edible, walnut-like fruits.

The wood of Juglans monophylla is strong and durable, making it valuable for crafting and construction.
