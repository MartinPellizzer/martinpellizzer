Juncus exploratorum

Juncus exploratorum, commonly known as the exploring rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its flowering spikes produce small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Juncus exploratorum plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
