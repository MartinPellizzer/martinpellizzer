Juniperus communis var. erecta

Juniperus communis var. erecta, commonly known as the upright juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, blue-gray needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and adaptable species.

Juniperus communis var. erecta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in landscaping.
