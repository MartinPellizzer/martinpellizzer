Jasminum aldabranum

Jasminum aldabranum, commonly known as the Aldabra jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the island of Aldabra in the Seychelles.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to the arid and saline conditions of its habitat. The plant produces small, white, highly fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, and it has a woody stem with a smooth, greyish bark.

Due to its unique characteristics, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
