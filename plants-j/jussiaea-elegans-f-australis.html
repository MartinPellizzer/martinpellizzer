Jussiaea elegans f. australis

Jussiaea elegans f. australis, commonly known as the Australian waterplantain, is a flowering plant species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating in aquatic environments. The plant produces small, white to greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and wetlands.

Jussiaea elegans f. australis is valued for its ecological role in supporting aquatic biodiversity and its aesthetic appeal in water gardens.
