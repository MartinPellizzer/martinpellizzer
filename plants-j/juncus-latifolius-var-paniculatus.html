Juncus latifolius var. paniculatus

Juncus latifolius var. paniculatus, commonly known as the broadleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and are covered with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This species is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
