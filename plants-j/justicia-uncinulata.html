Justicia uncinulata

Justicia uncinulata, commonly known as the hooked justicia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking spikes of tubular, two-lipped flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or perennial herb, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The flowers, which range in color from red to purple, attract various pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
