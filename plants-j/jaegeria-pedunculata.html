Jaegeria pedunculata

Jaegeria pedunculata, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and prairies. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in small, fuzzy leaves. Its most striking feature is its clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

Jaegeria pedunculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide nectar for pollinators.
