Justicia tenuifolia

Justicia tenuifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf flameflower, is a perennial herb native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and striking clusters of tubular, brightly colored flowers that range from red to orange. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for tropical gardens. It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees, contributing to its ecological significance.
