Juncus acutiflorus var. divaricatus

Juncus acutiflorus var. divaricatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
