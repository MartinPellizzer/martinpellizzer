Jambosa pterocaulis

Jambosa pterocaulis, commonly known as the African violet, is a tropical plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are usually broad and have a waxy texture, which helps reduce water loss in its native arid environment.

Jambosa pterocaulis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to indoor growing conditions.
