Juncus platyphyllus

Juncus platyphyllus, commonly known as broadleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features grass-like leaves that are flat and broad, distinguishing it from other rush species. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Juncus platyphyllus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
