Juncoides bulbosa

Juncoides bulbosa, commonly known as the bulbous rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. The plant is often found in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its bulbous base allows it to store water and nutrients, aiding survival in fluctuating moisture conditions.

Juncoides bulbosa plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
