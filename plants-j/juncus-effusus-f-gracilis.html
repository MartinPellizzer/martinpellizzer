Juncus effusus f. gracilis

Juncus effusus f. gracilis, commonly known as the slender soft rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Juncaceae.

It is a herbaceous perennial that forms dense clumps of grass-like foliage, with slender, cylindrical stems that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, stream banks, and damp woodlands.

Its delicate appearance and adaptability make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and wetland habitats.
