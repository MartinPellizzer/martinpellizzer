Juncus stenopetalus

Juncus stenopetalus, commonly known as the narrow-petaled rush, is a perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with slender, cylindrical stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes and stream banks.

Juncus stenopetalus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
