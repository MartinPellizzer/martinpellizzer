Jasminum scortechinii

Jasminum scortechinii, commonly known as the Chinese jasmine or the mountain jasmine, is a flowering plant native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a sunny to partially shaded location.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
