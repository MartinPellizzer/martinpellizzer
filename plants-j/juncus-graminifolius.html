Juncus graminifolius

Juncus graminifolius, commonly known as the grass-like rush, is a perennial plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, grass-like leaves that are typically green and densely packed. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist or wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Juncus graminifolius plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
