Jacobaea abrotanifolia

Jacobaea abrotanifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, often hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are aromatic when crushed. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Jacobaea abrotanifolia is typically found in dry, sunny habitats like rocky soils and open woodlands.
