Jatropha latifolia var. angustata

Jatropha latifolia var. angustata, commonly known as narrow-leaf jatropha, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that are followed by orange-red berries containing toxic seeds. It is characterized by its milky sap, which is a common feature among plants in the Euphorbiaceae family.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been studied for potential medicinal uses.
