Jacaranda lasiogyne

Jacaranda lasiogyne, commonly known as the silky jacaranda, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that are clustered in long, drooping racemes. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a silvery underside, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Jacaranda lasiogyne is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and public spaces.
