Justicia martinicensis

Justicia martinicensis, commonly known as the flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting throat. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged opposite each other on the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds and bees, making it an important species in local ecosystems.
