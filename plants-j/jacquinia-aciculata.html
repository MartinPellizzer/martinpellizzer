Jacquinia aciculata

Jacquinia aciculata, commonly known as the slenderleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Jacquinia aciculata is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
