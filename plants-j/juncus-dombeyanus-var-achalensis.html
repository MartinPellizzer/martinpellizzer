Juncus dombeyanus var. achalensis

Juncus dombeyanus var. achalensis, commonly known as the Achalensis rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the mountainous regions of western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine, wiry leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower spikes that bloom in the summer months, adding a delicate floral element to its appearance. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ability to thrive in wet or boggy conditions.

This variety is closely related to other members of the Juncaceae family, sharing similar structural and ecological traits.
