Jasminum lanceolatum

Jasminum lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

The flowers are used in traditional medicine and as a source of perfume.
