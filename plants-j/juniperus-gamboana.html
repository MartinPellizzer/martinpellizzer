Juniperus gamboana

Juniperus gamboana, commonly known as the Mexican redcedar, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, compact form and scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and whitish underneath. The plant produces small, blue-gray berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Juniperus gamboana is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
