Juncus turczaninowii

Juncus turczaninowii, commonly known as the Turczaninow's rush, is a perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is adapted to survive in environments with limited water availability. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, which can help prevent soil erosion.

Juncus turczaninowii is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in xeriscaping.
