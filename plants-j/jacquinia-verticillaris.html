Jacquinia verticillaris

Jacquinia verticillaris, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear whorled clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in tight, circular patterns around the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
