Justicia psammophila

Justicia psammophila, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are usually small, narrow, and covered in a fine, powdery coating that helps reduce water loss.

Justicia psammophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
