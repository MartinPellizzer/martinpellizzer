Justicia irwinii

Justicia irwinii, commonly known as the red-flowered justicia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Justicia irwinii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
