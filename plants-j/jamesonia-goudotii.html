Jamesonia goudotii

Jamesonia goudotii, commonly known as the Goudot's waxplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are glossy and leathery, and it produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in the spring.

Jamesonia goudotii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
