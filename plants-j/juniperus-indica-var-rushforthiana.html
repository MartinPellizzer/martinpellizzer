Juniperus indica var. rushforthiana

Juniperus indica var. rushforthiana, commonly known as the Rushforth's Indian juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces blue or black berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in mountain ecosystems.
