Justicia agria

Justicia agria, commonly known as sourgrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in spikes at the tips of the branches. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streams.

Justicia agria is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
