Jasminum rottlerianum var. glabrior

Jasminum rottlerianum var. glabrior, commonly known as the Indian jasmine or white jasmine, is a flowering plant native to southern India.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1-3 meters, featuring woody stems and simple, opposite leaves. The plant produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the summer months. Its flowers are notable for their sweet, intoxicating scent, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
