Juncus alpinus subsp. requienii

Juncus alpinus subsp.

requienii, commonly known as the alpine rush, is a perennial plant native to high-altitude regions of the Alps and other mountainous areas. It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and slender, cylindrical stems that often grow in clusters.

The plant typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, harsh climates.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and resilient species suitable for alpine gardens and ecological restoration projects.
