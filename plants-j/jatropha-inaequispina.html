Jatropha inaequispina

Jatropha inaequispina, commonly known as the unequal-spined jatropha, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features a woody stem with rough, fissured bark and produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant bears capsule fruits that split open to release seeds with short, hairy appendages. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and often have spines along the edges.

Jatropha inaequispina is known for its ornamental value and has been studied for potential use in biofuel production.
