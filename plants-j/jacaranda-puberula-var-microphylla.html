Jacaranda puberula var. microphylla

Jacaranda puberula var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved jacaranda, is a deciduous tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The tree has a rounded crown and smooth, grey bark that peels in thin layers. Its small, ovate leaves are arranged oppositely along the stems.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
