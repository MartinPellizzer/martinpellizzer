Jasminum rufohirtum

Jasminum rufohirtum, commonly known as the red-haired jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often releasing a sweet, intoxicating scent. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Jasminum rufohirtum is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
