Juniperus saxatilis

Juniperus saxatilis, commonly known as the rock juniper, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in harsh, rocky environments. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for birds and other wildlife. Its needle-like leaves are typically dark green and arranged in spirals around the stems.

Juniperus saxatilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in arid and rocky terrains.
