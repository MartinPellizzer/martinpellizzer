Jasminum parvifolium

Jasminum parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant white flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
