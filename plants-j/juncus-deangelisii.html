Juncus deangelisii

Juncus deangelisii, commonly known as Angel's rush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and tall, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a distinctive appearance that makes it a desirable ornamental plant.

Juncus deangelisii is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
