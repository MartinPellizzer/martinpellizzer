Jambosa commersonii

Jambosa commersonii, commonly known as the Brazilian white sapote, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces a smooth, yellow or orange fruit with a thick, sweet pulp and a single large seed. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.

The plant has a shallow root system, making it susceptible to drought and requiring regular watering for optimal growth.
