Juncus balticus var. eremicus

Juncus balticus var. eremicus, commonly known as the desert rush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish or purplish in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is well adapted to dry conditions, with a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soil.

Its fibrous leaves and stems make it a valuable resource for wildlife and a hardy plant for landscaping in drought-prone areas.
