Juniperus tetragona

Juniperus tetragona, commonly known as the four-cornered juniper, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive four-angled branches and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces blue-black berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Juniperus tetragona is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its aromatic wood and resilience in harsh climates.
