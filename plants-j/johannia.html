Johannia

Johannia, commonly known as the rock cress, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately divided leaves. Johannia species are typically found in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in alpine or arctic regions. The plants are generally herbaceous, with some species exhibiting a cushion-like growth form.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their delicate appearance.
