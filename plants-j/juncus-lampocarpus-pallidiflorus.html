Juncus lampocarpus pallidiflorus

Juncus lampocarpus pallidiflorus, commonly known as the pale-flowered rush, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of North America.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, cylindrical leaves. The plant produces small, pale flowers that are typically clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation.
