Justicia pseudoruellia

Justicia pseudoruellia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower clusters that resemble mist. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are arranged in spikes at the ends of the stems.

Justicia pseudoruellia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
