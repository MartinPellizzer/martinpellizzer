Jussiaea torulosa

Jussiaea torulosa, commonly known as the narrowleaf water-mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, white or pale purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. Jussiaea torulosa is characterized by its aromatic leaves and its ability to thrive in saturated soil conditions.

It plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and wildlife.
