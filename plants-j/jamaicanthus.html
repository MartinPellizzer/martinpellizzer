Jamaicanthus

Jamaicanthus, commonly known as the Jamaican star-of-Bahamas, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in coastal areas and forest understories. Jamaicanthus has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
