Juncus exsertus subsp. lesuticus

Juncus exsertus subsp.

lesuticus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows.

The plant features slender, cylindrical stems that are often slightly hairy, and its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and slightly folded. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, greenish-brown flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
