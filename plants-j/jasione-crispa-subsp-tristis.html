Jasione crispa subsp. tristis

Jasione crispa subsp.

tristis, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features slender, branching stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, bell-shaped, and usually pink or violet, blooming from late spring to early summer. Jasione crispa subsp.

tristis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in wildflower gardens.
