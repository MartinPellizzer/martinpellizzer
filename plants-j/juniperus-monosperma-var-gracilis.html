Juniperus monosperma var. gracilis

Juniperus monosperma var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit and scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Juniperus monosperma var. gracilis is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperature fluctuations.
