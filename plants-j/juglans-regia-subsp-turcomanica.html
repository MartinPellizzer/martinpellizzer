Juglans regia subsp. turcomanica

Juglans regia subsp.

turcomanica, commonly known as the Turkestan walnut, is a subspecies of the Persian walnut native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and distinctive, edible nuts encased in a thick, green husk.

The tree produces a hard, dark wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils and requires a cold winter for proper fruit development.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its nuts and timber, contributing to both local economies and ecological landscapes.
