Juncus oxycarpus subsp. australis

Juncus oxycarpus subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian rush, is a perennial plant native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves that form a compact, upright growth habit.

The plant produces small, greenish-brown flowers in early spring, which are arranged in spikelets along the stem. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as swampy areas and along waterways.

Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil, making it ecologically significant in wetland ecosystems.
