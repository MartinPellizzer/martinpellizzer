Justicia morona-santiagoensis

Justicia morona-santiagoensis, commonly known as the Morona-Santiagoensis flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a glossy surface.

The flowers are usually red or orange, arranged in terminal spikes, and bloom during the wet season.
