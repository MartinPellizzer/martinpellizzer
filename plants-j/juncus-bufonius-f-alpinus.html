Juncus bufonius f. alpinus

Juncus bufonius f. alpinus, commonly known as the alpine rush, is a variant of the bulrush plant native to high-altitude regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas. Its growth habit is typically clump-forming, with dense foliage that helps it withstand harsh environmental conditions.

The botanical features of Juncus bufonius f. alpinus make it well adapted to cold climates and high elevations.
