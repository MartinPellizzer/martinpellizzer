Juniperus sabina var. yulinensis

Juniperus sabina var. yulinensis, commonly known as the Yulin juniper, is a subspecies of the common juniper native to China.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in mountainous regions with well-drained soils. The plant produces small, scale-like leaves and has distinctive blue-gray foliage. It is known for its aromatic wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The berries of this plant are toxic to humans and animals but are used as a food source by certain bird species.
