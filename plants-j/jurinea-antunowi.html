Jurinea antunowi

Jurinea antunowi, commonly known as the Antarctic pearlwort, is a flowering plant native to the extreme environments of Antarctica and the southern regions of South America.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming species that thrives in cold, arid conditions with minimal soil. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the brief summer months. Its leaves are succulent, adapted to store water and withstand harsh weather.

Jurinea antunowi plays a crucial role in stabilizing the fragile soil of its extreme habitat.
