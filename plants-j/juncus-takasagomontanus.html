Juncus takasagomontanus

Juncus takasagomontanus, commonly known as the Japanese rush, is a perennial plant native to Japan and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and pond margins. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often densely packed, forming dense clusters. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and usually have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

The flowers are small and clustered in spikes, often appearing in late spring to early summer.
