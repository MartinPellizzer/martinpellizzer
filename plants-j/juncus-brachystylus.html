Juncus brachystylus

Juncus brachystylus, commonly known as the short-stemmed rush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and pond edges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are often greenish or brownish in color. It is characterized by its short, compact stature and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.

Juncus brachystylus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and supporting biodiversity.
