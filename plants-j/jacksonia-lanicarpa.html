Jacksonia lanicarpa

Jacksonia lanicarpa, commonly known as the silky centipede plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of small yellow flowers arranged in a dense, spike-like cluster.

Jacksonia lanicarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
