Julocroton cooperianus

Julocroton cooperianus, commonly known as the Cooper's julocroton, is a species native to specific regions in Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming during the dry season. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and ecologists studying plant adaptation in arid environments.
