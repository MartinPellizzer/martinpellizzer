Juncus agrostophyllus

Juncus agrostophyllus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are typically grass-like in appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Juncus agrostophyllus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
