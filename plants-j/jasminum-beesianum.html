Jasminum beesianum

Jasminum beesianum, commonly known as the Persian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its botanical characteristics include a complex inflorescence structure and a preference for well-drained soil in full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
