Justicia effusa

Justicia effusa, commonly known as the Mexican verbena, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with opposite, ovate leaves that have a rough texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Justicia effusa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
