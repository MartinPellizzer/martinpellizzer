Jenmaniella isoetifolia

Jenmaniella isoetifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf waterweed, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the family Isoetaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in a spiral around the stem. This plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. Its simple morphology and specialized adaptations allow it to thrive in submerged or partially submerged conditions.

Jenmaniella isoetifolia plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen through photosynthesis.
