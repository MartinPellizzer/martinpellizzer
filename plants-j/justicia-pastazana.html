Justicia pastazana

Justicia pastazana, commonly known as the Pastaza flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically bright red or orange, with a distinctive two-lipped structure that attracts pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
