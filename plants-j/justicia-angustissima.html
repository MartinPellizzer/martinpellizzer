Justicia angustissima

Justicia angustissima, commonly known as narrowleaf flamebeard, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and striking spikes of tubular, brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of red or orange. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds, making it a valuable addition to ecological landscapes.
