Juniperus barbadensis subsp. saxicola

Juniperus barbadensis subsp.

saxicola, commonly known as the rock juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the harsh, arid conditions of its native habitat.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in coastal and mountainous environments.
