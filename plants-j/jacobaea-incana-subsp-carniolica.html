Jacobaea incana subsp. carniolica

Jacobaea incana subsp.

carniolica, commonly known as the Carpathian ragwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is characterized by its upright stems and yellow flowers.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture, and it produces numerous small, bright yellow flowers in clusters. It is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a poisonous plant and should be handled with care.
