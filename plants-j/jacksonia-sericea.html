Jacksonia sericea

Jacksonia sericea, commonly known as the silky everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flower heads. The plant features narrow, silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

Jacksonia sericea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
