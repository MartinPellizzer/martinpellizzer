Jasminum domatiigerum

Jasminum domatiigerum, commonly known as the Indian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the summer months. Its botanical characteristics include opposite leaf arrangement and the presence of aromatic oils in its foliage.

Jasminum domatiigerum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
