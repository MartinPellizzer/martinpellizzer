Jussiaea hirta

Jussiaea hirta, commonly known as the hairy water-mint, is a flowering plant native to wetland and marsh environments in temperate regions.

It is characterized by its opposite, hairy leaves that are often reddish or purple in color. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near water bodies such as ponds and streams.

Jussiaea hirta is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
