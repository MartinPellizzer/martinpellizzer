Juniperus Communis F. Pygmaea

Juniperus communis f. pygmaea is a compact, dwarf form of the common juniper known for its dense, spherical shape and evergreen foliage. This variety thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting. Its small, scale-like leaves provide a lush, textured appearance throughout the year. The plant produces blue-gray berries that attract birds and add winter interest to the landscape. Due to its hardiness and low maintenance, it is a favored addition to gardens in colder climates.
