Jacobaea delphiniifolia

Jacobaea delphiniifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tansy, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellow-orange flowers in clusters. Jacobaea delphiniifolia is typically found in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and along stream banks.

It is known for its aromatic properties and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
