Jacobaea arnautorum

Jacobaea arnautorum, commonly known as the Armenian ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing adaptability to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Jacobaea arnautorum is also noted for its potential medicinal properties, though its use is limited due to its toxic nature.
