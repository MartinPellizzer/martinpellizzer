Juncus hostii var. monanthos

Juncus hostii var. monanthos, commonly known as the silver rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the western United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in a silvery-gray bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of white or pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is a popular choice for wetland gardens and water features.
