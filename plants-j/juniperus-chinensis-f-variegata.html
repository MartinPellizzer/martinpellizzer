Juniperus chinensis f. variegata

Juniperus chinensis f. variegata, commonly known as the Variegated Chinese Juniper, is a compact, slow-growing evergreen shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, featuring white and green patterns on its needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are typically ornamental rather than edible. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and as a foundation plant.

Its hardy nature and unique appearance make it a valuable addition to both landscape design and horticultural collections.
