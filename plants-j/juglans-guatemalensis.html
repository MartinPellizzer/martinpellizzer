Juglans guatemalensis

Juglans guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan walnut, is a species of flowering plant in the Juglandaceae family.

It is native to Central America, particularly Guatemala, and grows in humid lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its large, compound leaves and produces edible nuts enclosed in a spiky husk. Its seeds are valued for their rich flavor and are used in traditional cuisine.

The tree also plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
