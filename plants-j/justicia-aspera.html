Justicia aspera

Justicia aspera, commonly known as roughleaf flamebeard, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and striking clusters of tubular, red or orange flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, heat-tolerant plants.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
