Juniperus phoenicea subsp. eumediterranea

Juniperus phoenicea subsp.

eumediterranea, commonly known as the Mediterranean juniper, is a subspecies native to the Mediterranean region. It is a small evergreen shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

The plant has needle-like leaves that are blue-gray in color and are arranged in whorls around the stems. Its cones are small and berry-like, containing toxic seeds that are not suitable for human consumption.

This subspecies is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.
