Juniperus megalocarpa

Juniperus megalocarpa, commonly known as the giant juniper, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves and produces small, berry-like cones that are typically blue or black in color. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in harsh, arid environments, showcasing remarkable adaptability to extreme conditions. Juniperus megalocarpa is also valued for its aromatic wood, which has been used historically in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

Its ecological significance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species in its native range.
