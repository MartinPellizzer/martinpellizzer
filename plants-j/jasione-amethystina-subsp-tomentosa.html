Jasione amethystina subsp. tomentosa

Jasione amethystina subsp.

tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly jasione, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features basal rosettes of gray-green, hairy leaves that are densely covered with fine, white pubescence.

The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in clustered inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for rock gardens and alpine settings.
