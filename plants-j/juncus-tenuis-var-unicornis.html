Juncus tenuis var. unicornis

Juncus tenuis var. unicornis, commonly known as the slender rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, which are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, pond edges, and along streams.

This variety is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wetland organisms.
