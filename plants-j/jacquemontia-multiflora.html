Jacquemontia multiflora

Jacquemontia multiflora, commonly known as the many-flowered morning glory, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, opening in the morning and closing by midday. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a vigorous, flowering plant.
