Justicia phyllocalyx

Justicia phyllocalyx, commonly known as the flame flower, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

The flowers attract hummingbirds and other pollinators, making it a valuable addition to gardens.
