Jungia nutans

Jungia nutans, commonly known as the drooping jungle grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, often drooping stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of grass species. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture.

Jungia nutans is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
