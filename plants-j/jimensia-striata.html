Jimensia striata

Jimensia striata, commonly known as the striped jimsonweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with red veins. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins. Jimensia striata is an annual herb that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

It is known for its toxic properties, containing compounds like scopolamine and hyoscyamine.
