Juniperus cannartii

Juniperus cannartii, commonly known as the Corsican juniper, is a species of juniper native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small, evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas. The plant has needle-like leaves that are blue-green in color and produce small, dark blue berries. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping.

Juniperus cannartii is also known for its aromatic foliage and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
