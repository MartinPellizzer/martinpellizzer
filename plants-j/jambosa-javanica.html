Jambosa javanica

Jambosa javanica, commonly known as the Java jambosa, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are similar in appearance to persimmons, with a sweet, juicy pulp. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Jambosa javanica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of nutritious fruit.
