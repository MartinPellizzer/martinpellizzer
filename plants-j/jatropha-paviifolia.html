Jatropha paviifolia

Jatropha paviifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf jatropha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces toxic, orange-red berries that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy pulp. Jatropha paviifolia is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in warm climates.
