Jasminum geniculatum

Jasminum geniculatum, commonly known as the climbing jasmine or sweet woodruff, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a sweet, honey-like scent. The plant has a vine-like growth habit, with slender stems that can climb or trail along the ground. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and glossy, providing an attractive appearance.

Jasminum geniculatum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and in perfumery due to its aromatic properties.
