Junellia scoparia

Junellia scoparia, commonly known as the rock lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, branched stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, with flowers ranging in color from white to pale yellow. Junellia scoparia thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or arid environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
