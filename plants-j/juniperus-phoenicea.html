Juniperus phoenicea

Juniperus phoenicea, commonly known as the Palestine juniper, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky soils and coastal areas, thriving in well-drained, sunny conditions. The plant has scale-like leaves that are arranged in flat sprays, and its cones are small and berry-like, containing toxic seeds. It is often used in traditional medicine and has cultural significance in various regions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
