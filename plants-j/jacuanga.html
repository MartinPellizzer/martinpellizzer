Jacuanga

Jacuanga, commonly known as the Brazilian bean tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to pod fruits containing edible seeds. Jacuanga is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.

Its growth requires warm, humid climates and well-drained soil.
