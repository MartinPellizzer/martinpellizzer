Josephia stupposa

Josephia stupposa, commonly known as the desert rose or desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from pink to white, often with a reddish tinge. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in its harsh environment. Its stems are typically upright and can grow up to several feet tall.

Josephia stupposa is adapted to survive in extreme drought conditions, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
