Jurinea mesopotamica

Jurinea mesopotamica, commonly known as the Mesopotamian sea rocket, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of the Middle East and parts of Central Asia. The plant typically grows in saline or coastal environments, exhibiting halophytic traits that allow it to thrive in salty soils. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in spring, and it produces small, elongated pods containing numerous seeds.

Jurinea mesopotamica is often found in dry, sandy habitats and is adapted to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations.
