Juniperus marschalliana

Juniperus marschalliana, commonly known as the Siberian juniper, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and small, blue-gray berries that mature in the second year. The plant is highly adaptable, thriving in poor, well-drained soils and tolerating harsh environmental conditions. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging climates.

Juniperus marschalliana plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
