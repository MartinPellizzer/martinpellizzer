Juncus gregiflorus

Juncus gregiflorus, commonly known as the clustered rush, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Juncaginaceae and is characterized by its dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikes, which bloom during the rainy season. Juncus gregiflorus thrives in moist to aquatic environments, often forming dense vegetation in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and establish itself in suitable habitats.
