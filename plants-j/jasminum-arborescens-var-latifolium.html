Jasminum arborescens var. latifolium

Jasminum arborescens var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and broad, ovate leaves that are usually glossy in appearance. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a sweet, jasmine-like aroma. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
