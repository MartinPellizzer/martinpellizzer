Juncus schimperi

Juncus schimperi, commonly known as the Ethiopian rush, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia and Eritrea.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as along riverbanks and in swampy areas. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the summer.

Juncus schimperi is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has cultural significance in the regions where it is found.
