Jatropha pelargoniifolia var. glabra

Jatropha pelargoniifolia var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth jatropha, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, which are smooth and glabrous, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers followed by orange-red berries containing toxic seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage and striking blooms.

However, caution is advised as all parts of the plant contain toxic compounds.
