Juncus decipiens f. utilis

Juncus decipiens f. utilis, commonly known as the useful rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its cylindrical, grass-like stems that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, which are usually borne on the upper parts of the stems. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
