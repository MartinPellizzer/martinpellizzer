Jarava chrysophylla f. modica

Jarava chrysophylla f. modica, commonly known as the Modica form of the golden leaf jarava, is a rare botanical variant native to specific regions of southern Italy.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, upright growth and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with a golden hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.
