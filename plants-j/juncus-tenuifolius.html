Juncus tenuifolius

Juncus tenuifolius, commonly known as narrowleaf rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It typically grows in dense clumps, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in spike-like clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Juncus tenuifolius plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
