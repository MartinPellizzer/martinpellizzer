Juniperus tairukouensis

Juniperus tairukouensis, commonly known as the Tairukou juniper, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the mountainous regions of central China.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves and small, blue-gray berries that are typically borne in clusters. The plant is adapted to cold and arid environments, showcasing resilience to harsh climatic conditions. Its cones mature over a two-year cycle, and the species plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife.

Juniperus tairukouensis is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
