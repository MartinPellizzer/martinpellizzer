Justicia maculata

Justicia maculata, commonly known as the spotted joyweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range from pink to red, often with contrasting white or yellow markings. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are borne in clusters at the ends of the stems, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Justicia maculata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
