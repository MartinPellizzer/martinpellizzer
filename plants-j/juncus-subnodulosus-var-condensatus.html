Juncus subnodulosus var. condensatus

Juncus subnodulosus var. condensatus, commonly known as the dense club rush, is a perennial wetland plant characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves.

It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, which can help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

This variety is distinguished by its more compact growth compared to other juncus species, making it a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
