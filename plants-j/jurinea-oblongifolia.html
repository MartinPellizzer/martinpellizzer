Jurinea oblongifolia

Jurinea oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and narrow, elongated leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in the spring. It is well adapted to sandy soils and can tolerate extreme drought conditions.

Jurinea oblongifolia plays a role in stabilizing desert soils and is of interest in ecological restoration projects.
