Jussiaea paniculata

Jussiaea paniculata, commonly known as the water willow, is a flowering plant native to wetland and aquatic environments in South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense mats that help stabilize shorelines. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in its wet habitat.

Jussiaea paniculata plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and semi-aquatic species.
