Justicia leptantha

Justicia leptantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its vibrant flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds, contributing to its ecological significance.
