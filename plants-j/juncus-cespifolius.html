Juncus cespifolius

Juncus cespifolius, commonly known as the tufted rush, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually arranged in dense tufts at the base. Its flowering stems bear small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers.

Juncus cespifolius plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
