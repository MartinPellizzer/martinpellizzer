Jacksonia angulata

Jacksonia angulata, commonly known as the angular flax, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its striking, bright yellow daisy-like flowers. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, angular seed capsules that are distinctive in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Jacksonia angulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
