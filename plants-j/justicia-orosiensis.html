Justicia orosiensis

Justicia orosiensis, commonly known as the orange flame plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant orange-red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The showy flowers attract hummingbirds and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
