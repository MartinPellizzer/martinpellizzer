Jasione laevis var. pygmaea

Jasione laevis var. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf bluebell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features slender, hairy stems that bear small, blue to violet flowers arranged in loose clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils, often in Mediterranean climates.

Due to its delicate form and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
