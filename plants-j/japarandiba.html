Japarandiba

Japarandiba, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a vibrant display during the warmer months. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in full sunlight and well-drained soil, making it suitable for warm climates.

Japarandiba is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and public spaces.
