Jatropha berteroi

Jatropha berteroi, commonly known as the Mexican physic nut, is a shrub or small tree native to Central America and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces capsules that contain toxic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a natural pesticide.

It is also noted for its ability to thrive in arid and disturbed environments, making it a resilient species in tropical regions.
