Juniperus jarkendensis

Juniperus jarkendensis, commonly known as the Central Asian juniper, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and produces blue-black berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for birds and small mammals. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing at high altitudes where temperatures are cold and precipitation is limited. Its wood is durable and has been historically used for construction and fuel, while its essential oils are sometimes utilized in traditional medicine.

This resilient species plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
