Jansonia

Jansonia, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. Jansonia species are typically found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and meadows. The plant is often used in ornamental gardening due to its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in wet conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
