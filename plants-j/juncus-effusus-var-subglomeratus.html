Juncus effusus var. subglomeratus

Juncus effusus var. subglomeratus, commonly known as the clustered rush, is a variant of the common rush that exhibits distinct botanical characteristics.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments, typically found in temperate regions. The plant features dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves that are often reddish or brownish in color. Its flowering stems produce small, clustered spikes of greenish-yellow flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
