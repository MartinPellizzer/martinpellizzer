Jaumea peduncularis

Jaumea peduncularis, commonly known as the slender jaumea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Amaryllis family (Amaryllidaceae) and typically grows in coastal and sandy environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers. It blooms during the summer months and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky areas.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
