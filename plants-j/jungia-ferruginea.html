Jungia ferruginea

Jungia ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty jungle grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a reddish-brown hue along the midrib, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale yellow flowers arranged in loose spikes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Jungia ferruginea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
