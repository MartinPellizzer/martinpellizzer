Jurinea karatavica

Jurinea karatavica, commonly known as the Karatavica sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in early summer. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to conserve water in harsh conditions.

Jurinea karatavica plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is of interest in ecological restoration projects.
