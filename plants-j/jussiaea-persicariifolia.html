Jussiaea persicariifolia

Jussiaea persicariifolia, commonly known as the Persian waterwheel, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive whorled arrangement of leaves, which resemble the blades of a waterwheel, and its small, delicate white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and stream banks, and is often found in shallow water. It has a creeping rhizome and is known for its ability to thrive in aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions.

Jussiaea persicariifolia is a perennial herb that is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes in water gardens.
