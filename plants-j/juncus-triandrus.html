Juncus triandrus

Juncus triandrus, commonly known as the three-part rush, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist or wet environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant features cylindrical, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of white or pale green flowers. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and its tolerance for partial shade.

Juncus triandrus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
