Jasminocereus thouarsii var. chathamensis

Jasminocereus thouarsii var. chathamensis, commonly known as the Chatham Island night-blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to the Chatham Islands.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and nocturnal blooming habit. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its growth is adapted to the harsh coastal environment of the Chatham Islands, with thick, waxy stems that store water.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique evolutionary adaptations and limited geographic distribution.
