Juniperus formosana var. mairei

Juniperus formosana var. mairei, commonly known as the Formosan juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia, particularly Taiwan.

It belongs to the Cupressaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or mountainous environments.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
