Junellia aspera var. longidentata

Junellia aspera var. longidentata, commonly known as the long-toothed junellia, is a variant of the junellia plant characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and toothed leaves.

This plant is native to specific regions in South America, particularly in Brazil, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical environments. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a rough texture due to the presence of small, stiff hairs. The flowers are small and clustered, often in dense inflorescences, and may produce a mild fragrance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
