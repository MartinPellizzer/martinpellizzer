Jacquinia ferruginea

Jacquinia ferruginea, commonly known as the rustyleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with a reddish or rusty hue, giving it its common name. Its showy, bell-shaped flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Jacquinia ferruginea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
