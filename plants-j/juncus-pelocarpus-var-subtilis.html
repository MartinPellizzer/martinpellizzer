Juncus pelocarpus var. subtilis

Juncus pelocarpus var. subtilis, commonly known as the slender rush, is a flowering plant native to wetland and marsh environments in North America.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and flowering spikes that bear small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense colonies that help stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.

Its subterranean rhizomes allow it to spread vegetatively, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
