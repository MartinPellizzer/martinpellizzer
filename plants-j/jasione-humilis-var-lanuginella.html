Jasione humilis var. lanuginella

Jasione humilis var. lanuginella, commonly known as the woolly jasione, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage, which gives it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
