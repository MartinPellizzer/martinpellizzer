Justicia distichophylla

Justicia distichophylla, commonly known as the two-leaf justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in clusters, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its compact growth habit and vibrant blooms make it a valuable addition to tropical and subtropical landscapes.
