Jasione foliosa subsp. foliosa

Jasione foliosa subsp.

foliosa, commonly known as the leafy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate to ovate in shape, often with hairy surfaces. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
