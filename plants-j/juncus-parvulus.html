Juncus parvulus

Juncus parvulus, commonly known as the dwarf rush, is a flowering plant native to wetland and marsh environments in North America.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and typically grows in dense, compact clumps. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes during the summer months. Its root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in wetland habitats.

Juncus parvulus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ecological benefits.
