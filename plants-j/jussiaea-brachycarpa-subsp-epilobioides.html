Jussiaea brachycarpa subsp. epilobioides

Jussiaea brachycarpa subsp.

epilobioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf jussiaea, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to South America. It typically thrives in moist, sandy or rocky soils along coastal areas and riverbanks.

The plant features small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers. It is adapted to salt spray and wind exposure, making it a hardy species in coastal environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of coastal vegetation in its native habitat.
