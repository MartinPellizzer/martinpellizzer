Jasminum quinqueflorum

Jasminum quinqueflorum, commonly known as the five-flowered jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy green appearance.

The flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
