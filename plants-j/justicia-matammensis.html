Justicia matammensis

Justicia matammensis, commonly known as the Matambo or Matambo flower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a glossy appearance.

The flowers are usually bright red or orange, and they bloom in terminal clusters, attracting birds and insects.
