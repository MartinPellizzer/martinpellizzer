Jussiaea hyssopifolia

Jussiaea hyssopifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved willow herb, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes and stream banks. The plant features slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in spikes and are usually pink or purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Jussiaea hyssopifolia plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small aquatic organisms.
