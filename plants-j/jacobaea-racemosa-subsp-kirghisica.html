Jacobaea racemosa subsp. kirghisica

Jacobaea racemosa subsp.

kirghisica, commonly known as the Kirghiz ragwort, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia. It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes.

The plant features a robust root system and upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a raceme, and are produced in late summer.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
