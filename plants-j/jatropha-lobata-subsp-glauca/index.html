Jatropha Lobata Subsp. Glauca

Blueleaf jatropha, scientifically known as Jatropha lobata subsp. glauca, is a striking plant native to parts of Africa and Asia. It is characterized by its deep blue-green foliage that gives it its common name. This species belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes many toxic and ornamental plants. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique leaf color and structure. Despite its beauty, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
