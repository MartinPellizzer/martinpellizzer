Juncus falcatus subsp. sitchensis

Juncus falcatus subsp.

sitchensis, commonly known as Sitka rush, is a perennial plant native to the coastal regions of Alaska and the western coast of North America. It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often forming dense clumps.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically growing in a rosette at the base of the plant.

This species is adapted to moist, sandy soils and is often found in tidal zones and along riverbanks.
