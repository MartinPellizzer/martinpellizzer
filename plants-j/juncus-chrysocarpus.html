Juncus chrysocarpus

Juncus chrysocarpus, commonly known as the golden club, is a flowering plant native to parts of eastern North America.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, preferring moist to saturated soils. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it plays a role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens and conservation landscapes.
