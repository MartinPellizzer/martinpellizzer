Juniperus caroliniana

Juniperus caroliniana, commonly known as the Florida juniper, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are typically dark blue to black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils.

Juniperus caroliniana is also noted for its ability to tolerate salt spray, making it a common plant in coastal environments.
