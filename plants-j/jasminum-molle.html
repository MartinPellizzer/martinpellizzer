Jasminum molle

Jasminum molle, commonly known as the soft jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a sweet scent. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate with a smooth texture.

Jasminum molle thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
