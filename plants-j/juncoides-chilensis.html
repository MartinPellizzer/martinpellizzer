Juncoides chilensis

Juncoides chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean rush, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae, which includes rushes and sedges. The plant is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, cylindrical leaves and small, clustered flowers. It typically grows in moist, often saline environments such as coastal areas and wetlands.

Juncoides chilensis is adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions, making it an important component of its native ecosystems.
