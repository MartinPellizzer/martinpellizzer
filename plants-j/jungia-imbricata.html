Jungia imbricata

Jungia imbricata, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often forming dense clumps. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including purple, pink, and white, and are pollinated by insects.

Jungia imbricata is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
