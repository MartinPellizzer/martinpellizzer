Jatropha cecropiifolia

Jatropha cecropiifolia, commonly known as the flame tree or fire tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance.

Jatropha cecropiifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
