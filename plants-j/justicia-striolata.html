Justicia striolata

Justicia striolata, commonly known as the spotted jewelweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of small, tubular, orange-red flowers. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly serrated edge. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its flowering period typically occurs from late summer to early fall, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
