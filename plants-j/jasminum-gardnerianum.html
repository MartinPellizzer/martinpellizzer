Jasminum gardnerianum

Jasminum gardnerianum, commonly known as the Arabian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a woody shrub that produces fragrant, white flowers with a strong scent, particularly at night. The plant has small, elliptical leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties. It thrives in warm, arid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Jasminum gardnerianum is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic uses.
