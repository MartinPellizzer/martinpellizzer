Jacobaea auricula

Jacobaea auricula, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of bright yellow, velvety leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine. Jacobaea auricula thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

However, it is considered toxic to livestock and should be handled with care.
