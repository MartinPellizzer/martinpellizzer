Justicia pulegium

Justicia pulegium, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb with showy, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for wetland restoration and garden landscapes.
