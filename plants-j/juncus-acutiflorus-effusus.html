Juncus acutiflorus effusus

Juncus acutiflorus effusus, commonly known as the soft rush, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like clusters of narrow, cylindrical leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers that appear at the ends of its stems. It thrives in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Juncus acutiflorus effusus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
