Jasminum sieboldianum

Jasminum sieboldianum, commonly known as the Siebold's jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite, simple leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its flowers are often used in perfumery and traditional medicine due to their aromatic properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive floral structure and adaptability to humid, temperate climates.
