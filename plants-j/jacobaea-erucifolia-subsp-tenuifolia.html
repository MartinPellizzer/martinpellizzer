Jacobaea erucifolia subsp. tenuifolia

Jacobaea erucifolia subsp.

tenuifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf ragwort, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a noxious weed in some areas.
