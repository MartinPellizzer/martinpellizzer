Jasminum tomentosum var. tomentosum

Jasminum tomentosum var. tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly night-blooming jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy foliage and fragrant, white flowers that bloom at night. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a velvety texture, and the flowers emit a sweet, intoxicating scent.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
