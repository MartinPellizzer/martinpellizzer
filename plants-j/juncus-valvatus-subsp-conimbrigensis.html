Juncus valvatus subsp. conimbrigensis

Juncus valvatus subsp.

conimbrigensis, commonly known as the Conimbrig rush, is a flowering plant species native to specific regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Juncaceae and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, ditches, and the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and often have a distinct waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The flowers are small and greenish, arranged in dense clusters, and the plant plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils.
