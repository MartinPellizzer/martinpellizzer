Justicia procumbens f. japonica

Justicia procumbens f. japonica, commonly known as Japanese flame flower, is a compact, trailing shrub native to East Asia.

It features showy, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and hummingbirds. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for container gardening or hanging baskets.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flowers and compact growth habit.
