Justicia diclipteroides

Justicia diclipteroides, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences that resemble mist or clouds. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain areas.
