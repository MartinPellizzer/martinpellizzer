Justicia procumbens var. latispica

Justicia procumbens var. latispica, commonly known as the broad-spined jewelweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features sprawling stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces clusters of small, showy orange-red flowers that bloom from summer to fall. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in moist, shady environments, making it a common sight in woodland areas.
