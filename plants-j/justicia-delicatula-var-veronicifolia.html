Justicia delicatula var. veronicifolia

Justicia delicatula var. veronicifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf false heather, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
