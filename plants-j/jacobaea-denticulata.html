Jacobaea denticulata

Jacobaea denticulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, serrated leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands.

Jacobaea denticulata is known for its aromatic properties and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
