Jasminum pedicellatum

Jasminum pedicellatum, commonly known as the Chinese jessamine, is a flowering plant native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces fragrant, white, star-shaped flowers during the spring and summer months. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are typically dark green in color. Its flowers are borne on pedicels, giving it its scientific name, and are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oil.

Jasminum pedicellatum is also cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its pleasant aroma and attractive foliage.
