Justicia infelix

Justicia infelix, commonly known as the "Devil's Backbone," is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems that often appear twisted or contorted, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Justicia infelix is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types and light conditions.
