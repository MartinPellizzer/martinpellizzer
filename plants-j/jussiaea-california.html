Jussiaea california

Jussiaea california, commonly known as California willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features slender, willow-like stems that often grow along the ground or as low shrubs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. Its leaves are narrow, elongated, and have a slightly rough texture.

Jussiaea california thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and along stream banks.
