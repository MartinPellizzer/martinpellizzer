Juniperus oxycedrus subsp. macrocarpa

Juniperus oxycedrus subsp.

macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited juniper, is a subspecies native to the Mediterranean region. It is a evergreen shrub or small tree that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas.

The plant is characterized by its needle-like leaves and clusters of blue-gray berries that mature in the second year. It has a slow growth rate and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to its arid environment.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
