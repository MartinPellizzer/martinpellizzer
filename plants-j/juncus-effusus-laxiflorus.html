Juncus effusus laxiflorus

Juncus effusus laxiflorus, commonly known as the soft rush, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers that are typically clustered at the ends of its stems. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Juncus effusus laxiflorus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
