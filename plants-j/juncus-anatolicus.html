Juncus anatolicus

Juncus anatolicus, commonly known as the Anatolian rush, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Turkey and parts of Greece.

It is a perennial herb with tall, cylindrical stems that are often used in basket weaving and as a decorative plant. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of greenish-white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Juncus anatolicus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
