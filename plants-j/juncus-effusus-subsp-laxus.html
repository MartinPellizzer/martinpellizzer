Juncus effusus subsp. laxus

Juncus effusus subsp.

laxus, commonly known as soft rush, is a flowering plant in the family Juncaceae. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

The plant has slender, grass-like stems that are often slightly drooping, and it produces small, clustered spikelets of flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

The species is notable for its adaptability to wetland habitats and its role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.
