Jurinea heterophylla

Jurinea heterophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with succulent, silver-gray leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand high salinity and extreme temperatures.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, it is often used in xeriscaping and coastal restoration projects.
