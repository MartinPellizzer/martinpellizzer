Juniperus coreana

Juniperus coreana, commonly known as the Korean juniper, is a coniferous evergreen shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage that is typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Juniperus coreana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
