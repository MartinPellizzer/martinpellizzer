Juncus mauritianus

Juncus mauritianus, commonly known as the Mauritius rush, is a flowering plant native to the island of Mauritius.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and slender, cylindrical stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along streambanks. Its small, clustered flowers are usually greenish or brownish, and it produces minute, wind-dispersed seeds.

Juncus mauritianus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native environment.
