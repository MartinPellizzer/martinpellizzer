Juniperus gracilior

Juniperus gracilior, commonly known as the slender juniper, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, cylindrical form and dense, scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring in alcoholic beverages. Juniperus gracilior is adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots and a slow growth rate that allows it to survive in poor soil conditions.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
