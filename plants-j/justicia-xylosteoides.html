Justicia xylosteoides

Justicia xylosteoides, commonly known as the redroot false arum, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking, arrow-shaped leaves with a reddish tint and produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in moist, shady environments. It is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens with appropriate conditions.
