Juncus apiculatus

Juncus apiculatus, commonly known as the spike rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, forming dense clumps with tall, cylindrical stems. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers at the top of the stems, which are usually green to brown in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil.

Juncus apiculatus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
