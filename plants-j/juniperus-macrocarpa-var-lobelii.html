Juniperus macrocarpa var. lobelii

Juniperus macrocarpa var. lobelii, commonly known as the Lobel’s juniper, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a pyramidal shape, and its leaves are scale-like, measuring about 1–2 mm in length. The plant produces small, spherical blue-black berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant for garden use.

This variety is distinguished by its compact growth habit and unique foliage compared to other juniper species.
