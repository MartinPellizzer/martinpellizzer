Justicia disjuncta

Justicia disjuncta, commonly known as the Disjunct Flameflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking clusters of tubular, red or orange flowers. Its blooming period occurs primarily in the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
