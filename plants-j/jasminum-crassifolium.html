Jasminum crassifolium

Jasminum crassifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, broad oval leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Jasminum crassifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
