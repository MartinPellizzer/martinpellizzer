Jussiaea longifolia subsp. filiformis

Jussiaea longifolia subsp.

filiformis, commonly known as the slender water milfoil, is a aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams.

The plant is characterized by its finely divided, thread-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. It produces small, floating flowers that are usually white or pale pink, and it plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms. Jussiaea longifolia subsp.

filiformis is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize sediments and improve water quality.
