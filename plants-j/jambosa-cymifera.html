Jambosa cymifera

Jambosa cymifera, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or carambola, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Amazon basin.

It is characterized by its star-shaped, yellow-orange fruits that are rich in vitamin C and antioxidants. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Jambosa cymifera is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.
