Jasminum unifoliolatum

Jasminum unifoliolatum, commonly known as the single leaf jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its flowers emit a sweet aroma, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The botanical features of J. unifoliolatum make it a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
