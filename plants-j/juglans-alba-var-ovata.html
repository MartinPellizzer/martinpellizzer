Juglans alba var. ovata

Juglans alba var. ovata, commonly known as the white walnut, is a variant of the white walnut tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and distinctive, edible nuts enclosed in a thick, green husk. The tree produces yellowish-green flowers in catkins, with male and female flowers on separate trees. Its wood is strong and durable, making it valuable for furniture and construction.

The plant is also noted for its ecological importance, providing food for various wildlife species.
