Justicia aristata

Justicia aristata, commonly known as the velvetleaf false arum, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces striking clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

While it is not typically cultivated for ornamental purposes, it plays an important role in local ecosystems.
