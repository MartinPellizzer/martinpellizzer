Jatropha elliptica var. guaranitica

Jatropha elliptica var. guaranitica, commonly known as the Guarani jatropha, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces striking orange-red flowers that are clustered in cymes, and its leaves are elliptical with a slightly hairy texture. It is also characterized by its milky sap, which contains toxic compounds, and its seeds are encased in a hard, spiny capsule.

This plant is often found in disturbed habitats and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
