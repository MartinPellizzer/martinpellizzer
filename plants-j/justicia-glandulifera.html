Justicia glandulifera

Justicia glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular justicia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with small glands, giving it its scientific name.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
