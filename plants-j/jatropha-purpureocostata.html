Jatropha purpureocostata

Jatropha purpureocostata, commonly known as the purple-costate jatropha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking purple-tinged leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces toxic, milky sap that contains compounds such as latex and alkaloids. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Jatropha purpureocostata is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.
