Justicia beguinotii

Justicia beguinotii, commonly known as the red-veined begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with deep red veins. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
