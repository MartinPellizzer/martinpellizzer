Juncus falcatus var. alaskensis

Juncus falcatus var. alaskensis, commonly known as the Alaska rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of lakes and streams.

This variety is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine and arctic regions.
