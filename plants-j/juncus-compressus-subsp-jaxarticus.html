Juncus compressus subsp. jaxarticus

Juncus compressus subsp.

jaxarticus, commonly known as the Jaxartian rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It typically grows in moist, often saline soils along riverbanks and wet meadows.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear greenish-yellow flowers. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.

This subspecies is of interest in ecological studies due to its adaptation to arid and semi-arid regions.
