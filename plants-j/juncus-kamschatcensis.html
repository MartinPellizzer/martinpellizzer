Juncus kamschatcensis

Juncus kamschatcensis, commonly known as the Kamchatka rush, is a perennial plant native to the cold climates of the Russian Far East and Alaska.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like flowers that are typically green or brown, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and coastal areas.

Juncus kamschatcensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
