Juncus fuscoater coarctatus

Juncus fuscoater coarctatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a species of flowering plant in the family Juncaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, spike-like flowers. This plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often in temperate regions. Its morphology includes a fibrous root system and stiff, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

The flowers are usually greenish or brownish, and the plant plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
