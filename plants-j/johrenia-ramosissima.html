Johrenia ramosissima

Johrenia ramosissima, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a bushy, branching structure with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Johrenia ramosissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
