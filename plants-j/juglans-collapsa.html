Juglans collapsa

Juglans collapsa, commonly known as the Chinese black walnut, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the walnut family, Juglandaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and edible nuts. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy. Its bark is dark and fissured, and it produces yellow-green flowers in early spring.

The nuts are encased in a thick, woody husk and are used both for food and in traditional medicine.
