Jovibarba sobolifera subsp. hirtella

Jovibarba sobolifera subsp.

hirtella, commonly known as the hairy stonecress, is a perennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with fleshy, succulent leaves covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. It is native to arid and rocky regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and the ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
