Jacobaea aurea

Jacobaea aurea, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and basal rosette of leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Jacobaea aurea is often found in disturbed areas and can thrive in poor soil conditions.
