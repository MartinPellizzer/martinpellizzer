Justicia acuminata

Justicia acuminata, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Justicia acuminata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
