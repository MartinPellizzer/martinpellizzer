Jasminum officinale var. piliferum

Jasminum officinale var. piliferum, commonly known as the hairy jasmine, is a variant of the common jasmine plant.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which distinguish it from other jasmine varieties. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
