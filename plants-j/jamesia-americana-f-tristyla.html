Jamesia americana f. tristyla

Jamesia americana f. tristyla, commonly known as the Sierra pink, is a low-growing shrub native to the western United States.

It features showy, pink to reddish-pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive flowers.
