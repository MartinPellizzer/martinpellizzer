Justicia oblonga

Justicia oblonga, commonly known as the narrowleaf flamebeard, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Justicia oblonga is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability.

It is also used in traditional medicine in some regions for its purported health benefits.
