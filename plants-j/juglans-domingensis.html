Juglans domingensis

Juglans domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican walnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Caribbean, particularly Dominica and other islands in the Lesser Antilles.

It belongs to the walnut family, Juglandaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with 15 to 23 leaflets. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, followed by edible nuts enclosed in a thick, woody husk.

Its wood is highly valued for its strength and durability, making it useful in construction and furniture-making.
