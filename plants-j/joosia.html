Joosia

Joosia, commonly known as the common sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, erect leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into three-angled berries. Joosia typically grows in wet or marshy environments, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and resilience.
