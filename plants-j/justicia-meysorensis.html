Justicia meysorensis

Justicia meysorensis, commonly known as the Mysore jewelweed, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that resemble small flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest understories and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Justicia meysorensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and unique floral display.
