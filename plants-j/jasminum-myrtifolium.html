Jasminum myrtifolium

Jasminum myrtifolium, commonly known as the Indian jasmine or myrtle-leaved jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a strong, sweet scent. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its delicate blossoms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

Jasminum myrtifolium is also used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic benefits.
