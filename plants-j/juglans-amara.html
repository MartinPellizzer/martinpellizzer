Juglans amara

Juglans amara, commonly known as bitter walnut, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the walnut family, Juglandaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, spiny husks. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, which are wind-pollinated. Its seeds, enclosed in a hard, bitter shell, are used in traditional medicine and as a natural insecticide.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
