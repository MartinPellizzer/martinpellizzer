Jasminum malabaricum

Jasminum malabaricum, commonly known as the Malabar jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the early morning. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

The leaves are simple, glossy, and have a slightly serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value.
