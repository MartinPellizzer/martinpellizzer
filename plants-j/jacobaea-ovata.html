Jacobaea ovata

Jacobaea ovata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ragwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that can grow up to 1 meter tall, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a strong, bitter scent.

Jacobaea ovata is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it is considered a potential weed in some regions.
