Juncus vaseyi

Juncus vaseyi, commonly known as the vasey's rush, is a perennial plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as marshes and stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in dense spikes during the summer months. It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.

Juncus vaseyi is an important part of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
