Juniperus deppeana var. pachyphlaea

Juniperus deppeana var. pachyphlaea, commonly known as the Mexican redcedar, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of scale-like leaves that are typically reddish-brown in color. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that contain aromatic, toxic seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its role in local ecosystems.
