Jacobaea othonnae

Jacobaea othonnae, commonly known as the Cape dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions and has a deep root system for water absorption.

Jacobaea othonnae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
