Justicia eustachiana

Justicia eustachiana, commonly known as the scarlet sage, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, heat-tolerant plants.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds and butterflies.
