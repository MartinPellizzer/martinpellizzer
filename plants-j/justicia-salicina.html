Justicia salicina

Justicia salicina, commonly known as the slender flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
