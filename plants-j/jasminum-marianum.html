Jasminum marianum

Jasminum marianum, commonly known as the Marian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a sweet, intoxicating aroma. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and oval-shaped, with a glossy green surface.

Jasminum marianum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often used in perfumery due to its strong fragrance.
