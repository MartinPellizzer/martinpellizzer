Justicia haughtii

Justicia haughtii, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a striking perennial plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that resemble flowers. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering period typically occurs in the summer months, producing clusters of small, tubular flowers surrounded by colorful, papery bracts.

Justicia haughtii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.
