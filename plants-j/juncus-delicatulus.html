Juncus delicatulus

Juncus delicatulus, commonly known as the slender rush, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae and is characterized by its grass-like, cylindrical stems that grow in dense clusters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to greenish flowers. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often covered with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Juncus delicatulus thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.
