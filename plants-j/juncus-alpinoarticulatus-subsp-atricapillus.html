Juncus alpinoarticulatus subsp. atricapillus

Juncus alpinoarticulatus subsp.

atricapillus, commonly known as the black-tipped rush, is a perennial plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions. It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, greenish flowers.

The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in tundra and alpine habitats. Its dark purple or black-tipped flowers distinguish it from other rush species.

The subspecies is adapted to cold climates and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its harsh environment.
