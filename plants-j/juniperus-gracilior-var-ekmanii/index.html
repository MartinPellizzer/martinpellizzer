Juniperus Gracilior Var. Ekmanii

Juniperus gracilior var. ekmanii is a rare and unique subspecies of juniper native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas. It is distinguished by its slender, needle-like leaves and compact growth habit, which help it survive in harsh mountain environments. This plant thrives in cold, dry conditions and is often found in rocky soils at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its small, blue-gray berries are not only visually striking but also serve as a food source for local wildlife. Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
