Juncus divaricatus

Juncus divaricatus, commonly known as the variable rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in moist soils and can be found in marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, greenish-brown flowers in late summer. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which can help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Juncus divaricatus is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
