Juniperus tamariscifolia

Juniperus tamariscifolia, commonly known as the desert juniper, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its scale-like leaves and small, blue-gray berries that are typically retained on the plant for several years. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry, rocky soils. It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Its slow growth rate and longevity make it a valuable component of desert ecosystems.
