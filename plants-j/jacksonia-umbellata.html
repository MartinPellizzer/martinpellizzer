Jacksonia umbellata

Jacksonia umbellata, commonly known as the New Zealand dandelion, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that resemble dandelions. The plant has a rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces a cluster of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Jacksonia umbellata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
