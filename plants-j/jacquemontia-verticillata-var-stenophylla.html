Jacquemontia verticillata var. stenophylla

Jacquemontia verticillata var. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf false morning glory, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, alternate leaves that are typically narrow and lanceolate in shape. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a contrasting throat. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered invasive in certain regions.
