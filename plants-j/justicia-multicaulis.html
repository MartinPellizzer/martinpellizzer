Justicia multicaulis

Justicia multicaulis, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that bear bright red or orange flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a whorled pattern along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The genus Justicia belongs to the Acanthaceae family, known for its showy and often colorful flowers.
