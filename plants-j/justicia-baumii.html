Justicia baumii

Justicia baumii, commonly known as the red-veined strobilanthes, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with deep red veins. The plant produces clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
