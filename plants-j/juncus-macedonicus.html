Juncus macedonicus

Juncus macedonicus, commonly known as the Macedonian rush, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It typically grows in moist or wet environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and bear small, clustered spikes of white or pale green flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming dense clumps that help stabilize soil in wetland habitats.

Juncus macedonicus plays an important ecological role in supporting biodiversity and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
