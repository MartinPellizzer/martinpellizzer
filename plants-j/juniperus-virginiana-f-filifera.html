Juniperus virginiana f. filifera

Juniperus virginiana f. filifera, commonly known as the threadleaf juniper, is a variant of the eastern red cedar.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that are typically less than 1 millimeter in width. This form is often found in rocky or dry soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
