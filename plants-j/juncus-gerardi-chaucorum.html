Juncus gerardi chaucorum

Juncus gerardi chaucorum, commonly known as the Chaucor marsh rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and bear small, spike-like clusters of greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and pointed, growing in dense tufts at the base.

This species plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various insects and birds.
