Jurinea mugodsharica

Jurinea mugodsharica, commonly known as the Karakum sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that thrives in sandy and saline soils, often found in the deserts of Kazakhstan and Turkmenistan. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months, adapting to the extreme environmental conditions of its habitat. Its leaves are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss and protect against intense sunlight.

Jurinea mugodsharica plays a role in stabilizing dunes and is of interest for its resilience in harsh climates.
