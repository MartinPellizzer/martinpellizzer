Juncoides macusaniensis

Juncoides macusaniensis, commonly known as the Andean rush, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It belongs to the family Juncaginaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, cylindrical leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets. It thrives in cold, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of high-altitude ecosystems.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
