Jessenia amazonum

Jessenia amazonum, commonly known as the Amazonian Jessenia, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Jessenia amazonum is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in secondary growth forests.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
