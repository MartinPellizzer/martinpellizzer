Justicia brunelloides

Justicia brunelloides, commonly known as the red-flowered justicia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including hummingbirds and bees, making it a valuable addition to gardens.

The plant prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, thriving in warm, humid climates.
