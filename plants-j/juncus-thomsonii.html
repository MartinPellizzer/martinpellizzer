Juncus thomsonii

Juncus thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to western North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and lack leaves, with small, spike-like inflorescences at the top. Its flowers are usually greenish or brownish and are clustered in dense spikes.

Thomson's rush plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
