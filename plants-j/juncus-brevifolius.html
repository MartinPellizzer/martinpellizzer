Juncus brevifolius

Juncus brevifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It typically grows in dense clusters, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with slender, grass-like stems that are often reddish or brownish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes during the summer months, which are usually green or brown. Its leaves are narrow and short, contributing to its overall compact appearance.

Juncus brevifolius plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
