Justicia quinqueangularis

Justicia quinqueangularis, commonly known as the five-angled justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that typically range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and prominent veins. Its stems are woody and often branched, with a distinct five-angled cross-section that gives it its scientific name.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
