Juncus brevifolius var. quitensis

Juncus brevifolius var. quitensis, commonly known as the Quitense Rush, is a perennial plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and high-altitude meadows. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually short and dense, forming compact clumps. Its flowering spikes consist of small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that bloom in early spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
