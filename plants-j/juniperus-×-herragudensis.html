Juniperus × herragudensis

Juniperus × herragudensis, commonly known as the hybrid juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is a natural hybrid between Juniperus communis and Juniperus oxycedrus, resulting in a plant with distinctive foliage and fruiting characteristics. The leaves are typically needle-like, arranged in whorls, and the plant produces small, blue-black berries that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
