Jacobaea multibracteolata

Jacobaea multibracteolata, commonly known as the multibracteolate ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features yellowish to greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, with lance-shaped leaves that have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, and it is often found in disturbed soils.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
