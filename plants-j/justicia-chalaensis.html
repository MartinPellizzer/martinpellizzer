Justicia chalaensis

Justicia chalaensis, commonly known as the red-flowered justicia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a container plant.
