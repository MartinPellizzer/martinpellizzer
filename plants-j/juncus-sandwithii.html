Juncus sandwithii

Juncus sandwithii, commonly known as the Sandwith's rush, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of white or greenish flowers. It is a member of the Juncaceae family, which includes other rush species.

Juncus sandwithii plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
