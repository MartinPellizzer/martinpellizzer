Juncus brasiliensis

Juncus brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian rush, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Juncus brasiliensis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
