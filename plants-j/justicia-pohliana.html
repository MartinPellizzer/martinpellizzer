Justicia pohliana

Justicia pohliana, commonly known as the red torch plant, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking red bracts that resemble flames. The plant produces small white to pink flowers clustered in terminal spikes, which are often overshadowed by the vibrant bracts. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting colorful display throughout the growing season.
