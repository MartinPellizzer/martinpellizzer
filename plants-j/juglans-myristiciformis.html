Juglans myristiciformis

Juglans myristiciformis, commonly known as the Mexican walnut, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the walnut family, Juglandaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with 15 to 23 leaflets. The tree produces small, green, spherical fruits that mature into hard-shelled nuts. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

The plant is valued for its timber and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
