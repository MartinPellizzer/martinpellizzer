Justicia pseudotenella

Justicia pseudotenella, commonly known as the narrow-leafed flame flower, is a species of flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of bright red or orange tubular flowers. These flowers are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds and bees, making it an important nectar source in its native habitat.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
