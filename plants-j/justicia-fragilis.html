Justicia fragilis

Justicia fragilis, commonly known as the brittleleaf justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its delicate, narrow leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous annual or perennial, often found in moist, open habitats. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and hummingbirds.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
