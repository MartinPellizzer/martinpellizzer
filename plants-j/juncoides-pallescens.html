Juncoides pallescens

Juncoides pallescens, commonly known as the pale rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It belongs to the family Juncaginaceae and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems that often grow in dense clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are narrow and cylindrical, adapted to thrive in waterlogged soils.

Juncoides pallescens plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
