Justicia lutea

Justicia lutea, commonly known as the yellow flame plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in terminal clusters. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are square and erect.

Justicia lutea thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
