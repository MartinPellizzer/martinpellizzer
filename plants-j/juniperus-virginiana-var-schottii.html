Juniperus virginiana var. schottii

Juniperus virginiana var. schottii, commonly known as the Schott's redcedar, is a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves that are typically reddish-brown in color. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in xeric environments such as rocky slopes and sandy soils.

This variety is notable for its adaptability and resilience in harsh, dry conditions.
