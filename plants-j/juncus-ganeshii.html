Juncus ganeshii

Juncus ganeshii, commonly known as Ganesh's rush, is a perennial plant native to the wetlands of southern India.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae and is characterized by its grass-like, cylindrical stems that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant typically grows in marshy or waterlogged soils, thriving in humid environments. Its small, spike-like inflorescences bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Juncus ganeshii plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
