Juncus gerardi subsp. montanus

Juncus gerardi subsp.

montanus, commonly known as mountain rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions.

The plant has slender, grass-like stems that are often clustered and can reach heights of up to 60 cm. Its small, spike-like inflorescence consists of numerous greenish or brownish flowers.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in alpine and subalpine habitats.
