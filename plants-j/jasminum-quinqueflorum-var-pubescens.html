Jasminum quinqueflorum var. pubescens

Jasminum quinqueflorum var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy jasminum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has small, elliptical leaves with a hairy texture, which gives it its common name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

The hairy jasminum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in horticulture and traditional medicine.
