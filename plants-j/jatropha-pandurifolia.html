Jatropha pandurifolia

Jatropha pandurifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf jatropha, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are divided into five to seven lobes resembling a heart shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into toxic, orange-red berries. Its growth habit is typically upright, with hairy stems and alternate foliage.

Jatropha pandurifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
