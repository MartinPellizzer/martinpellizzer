Juncus compressus var. pseudogerardii

Juncus compressus var. pseudogerardii, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that grow close to the ground, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a slight sheen, adapted for water retention in moist habitats.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
