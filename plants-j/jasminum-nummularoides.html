Jasminum nummularoides

Jasminum nummularoides, commonly known as the silver jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white wax, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Jasminum nummularoides prefers partial shade to full sun and is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
