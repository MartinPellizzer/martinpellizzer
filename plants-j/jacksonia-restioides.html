Jacksonia restioides

Jacksonia restioides, commonly known as the slender mistflower, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained soils. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Jacksonia restioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
