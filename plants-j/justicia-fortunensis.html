Justicia fortunensis

Justicia fortunensis, commonly known as the Fortune's spiderflower, is a tropical perennial shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting blue or purple throat. The plant produces showy bracts that resemble petals, enhancing its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation in warm climates.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
