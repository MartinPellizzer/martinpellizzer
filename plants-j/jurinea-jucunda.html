Jurinea jucunda

Jurinea jucunda, commonly known as the silky sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that helps reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding a subtle floral display. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access moisture from lower soil layers.

Jurinea jucunda is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall.
