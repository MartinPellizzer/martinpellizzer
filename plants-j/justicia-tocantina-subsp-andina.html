Justicia tocantina subsp. andina

Justicia tocantina subsp.

andina, commonly known as Andean flameflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America. It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange.

The plant typically grows as an erect shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, attracting various pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
