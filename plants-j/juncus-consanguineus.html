Juncus consanguineus

Juncus consanguineus, commonly known as the redstem bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of white or greenish flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which can help stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.

Juncus consanguineus is valued for its ecological role in wetland ecosystems and its ability to tolerate a range of water conditions.
