Jussiaea affinis var. genuina

Jussiaea affinis var. genuina, commonly known as the narrowleaf water primrose, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and along riverbanks.

Jussiaea affinis var. genuina is often used in aquatic gardening and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize shoreline vegetation.
