Justicia dubia

Justicia dubia, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Justicia dubia is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
