Jacquemontia rufa

Jacquemontia rufa, commonly known as the redflower morning glory, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers open in the morning and close in the evening, hence its common name.

Jacquemontia rufa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
