Johnsonia pubescens f. longibracteata

Johnsonia pubescens f. longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract Johnsonia, is a unique variant of the Johnsonia pubescens species.

It is characterized by its elongated bracts, which are modified leaves that often resemble floral structures. This plant is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, tropical conditions. Its botanical features include hairy stems and clustered inflorescences, making it a distinctive subject in botanical studies.
