Justicia debilis var. angustifolia

Justicia debilis var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf flamebeard, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and striking clusters of tubular, red or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade. It is a perennial shrub that produces showy inflorescences during the summer months.

Botanically, it belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
