Jacobaea suaveolens

Jacobaea suaveolens, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in herbal remedies due to their medicinal properties.

Jacobaea suaveolens is also known for its long-lasting flowers, which remain intact for extended periods, hence its common name.
