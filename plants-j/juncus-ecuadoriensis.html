Juncus ecuadoriensis

Juncus ecuadoriensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian rush, is a perennial plant native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and cylindrical flower spikes. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in wetlands. Its slender, wiry stems are often used in basket weaving and traditional crafts.

Juncus ecuadoriensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
