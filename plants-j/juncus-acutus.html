Juncus acutus

Juncus acutus, commonly known as sharp rush, is a perennial plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in coastal areas and freshwater marshes.

Juncus acutus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
