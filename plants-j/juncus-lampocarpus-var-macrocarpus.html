Juncus lampocarpus var. macrocarpus

Juncus lampocarpus var. macrocarpus, commonly known as the giant rush, is a perennial plant native to coastal areas of southern California.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters at the top of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense clumps.

This species is adapted to saline and brackish environments, making it an important part of coastal ecosystems.
