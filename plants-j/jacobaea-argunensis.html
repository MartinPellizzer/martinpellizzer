Jacobaea argunensis

Jacobaea argunensis, commonly known as the Armenian ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually grey-green and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Jacobaea argunensis is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
