Jasminum pendulum

Jasminum pendulum, commonly known as the trailing jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its long, drooping stems that often cascade from supports, making it popular in hanging baskets or trained on trellises. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

Jasminum pendulum prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, thriving in warm, humid climates.
