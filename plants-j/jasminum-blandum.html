Jasminum blandum

Jasminum blandum, commonly known as the Mexican jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite, simple leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, blooming in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its botanical characteristics include a sympodial growth habit and the ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, though it prefers well-drained conditions.

Jasminum blandum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant fragrance.
