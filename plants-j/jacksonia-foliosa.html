Jacksonia foliosa

Jacksonia foliosa, commonly known as the silky oxeye, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its soft, silvery foliage and bright yellow daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads.

Jacksonia foliosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
