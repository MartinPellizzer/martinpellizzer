Jussiaea sprengeri

Jussiaea sprengeri, commonly known as the water bladderwort, is a semi-aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and marshes. The plant features small, floating leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in buoyancy and gas exchange. Its flowers are usually small and inconspicuous, with four petals, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Jussiaea sprengeri is known for its ability to absorb nutrients from the water, making it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
