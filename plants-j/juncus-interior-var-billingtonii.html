Juncus interior var. billingtonii

Juncus interior var. billingtonii, commonly known as the Billington's rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of North America.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies. Its flowering stems bear small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
