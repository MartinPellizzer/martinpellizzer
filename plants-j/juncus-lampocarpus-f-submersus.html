Juncus lampocarpus f. submersus

Juncus lampocarpus f. submersus, commonly known as the submerged rush, is a variant of the common rush that exhibits unique adaptations to aquatic environments.

This plant is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical stems that are typically fully submerged in water, allowing it to thrive in marshy or wetland habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense mats that help stabilize the substrate. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in water filtration and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
