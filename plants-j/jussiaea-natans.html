Jussiaea natans

Jussiaea natans, commonly known as the floating bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its floating, bladder-like structures that help it stay afloat on the water's surface. The plant has tiny, thread-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge above the water. It is a carnivorous plant, capable of trapping and digesting small aquatic organisms.

Jussiaea natans plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and contributing to nutrient cycling.
