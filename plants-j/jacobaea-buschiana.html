Jacobaea buschiana

Jacobaea buschiana, commonly known as the Busch's ragwort, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features dark green, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

Jacobaea buschiana is noted for its toxic properties, containing pyrrolizidine alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
