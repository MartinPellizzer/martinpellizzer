Juncus atratus

Juncus atratus, commonly known as the black rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland and marsh environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender, grass-like stems that often grow in dense clusters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture, and it thrives in moist, poorly drained soils.

Juncus atratus plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
