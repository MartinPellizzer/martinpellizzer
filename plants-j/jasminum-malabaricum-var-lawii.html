Jasminum malabaricum var. lawii

Jasminum malabaricum var. lawii, commonly known as the Law's jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the summer months. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in its native region.
