Jumellea pailleri

Jumellea pailleri, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often blooming during the rainy season. Its leaves are small, succulent, and adapted to minimize water loss, while its bark is rough and fissured.

Jumellea pailleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping in drought-prone areas.
