Justicia urophylla

Justicia urophylla, commonly known as the red-flowered false arum, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red bracts that surround small, white flowers, creating an eye-catching display. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and glossy, adding to its visual appeal.

Justicia urophylla thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
