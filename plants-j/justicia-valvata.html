Justicia valvata

Justicia valvata, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, featuring attractive, blue-violet flower clusters. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

The showy bracts that surround its small flowers make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
