Jacquemontia hirsuta f. racemosa

Jacquemontia hirsuta f. racemosa, commonly known as the hairy jacquemontia, is a variant of the species native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a notable subject in the study of plant morphology and taxonomy.
