Jacaranda robertii

Jacaranda robertii, commonly known as the Cape jasmine or yellow jacaranda, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the late winter to early spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. Its bark is smooth and greyish, often exfoliating to reveal a lighter inner layer.

Jacaranda robertii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
