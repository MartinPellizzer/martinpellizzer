Justicia sirensis

Justicia sirensis, commonly known as the hummingbird flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are typically red or orange with a prominent yellow or orange throat, enhancing its visual appeal.
