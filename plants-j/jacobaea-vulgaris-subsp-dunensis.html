Jacobaea vulgaris subsp. dunensis

Jacobaea vulgaris subsp.

dunensis, commonly known as the Scottish Groundsel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the British Isles. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands, heaths, and woodland edges.

The plant features basal leaves that are spoon-shaped with a hairy surface, and its flowers are bright yellow, arranged in a composite head. It produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
