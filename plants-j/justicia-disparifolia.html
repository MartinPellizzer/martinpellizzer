Justicia disparifolia

Justicia disparifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf flameflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Justicia disparifolia thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
