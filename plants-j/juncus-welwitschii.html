Juncus welwitschii

Juncus welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitsch's rush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or marshy environments. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often used for thatching in traditional construction. Its flowers are small and clustered, forming dense spikes at the top of the stems.

Juncus welwitschii is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils and is often found in riverine habitats.
