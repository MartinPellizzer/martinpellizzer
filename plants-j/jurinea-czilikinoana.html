Jurinea czilikinoana

Jurinea czilikinoana, commonly known as the Czilikinoan sandflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with small, narrow leaves that reduce water loss. Its yellowish flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Jurinea czilikinoana is often found in sandy or gravelly soils, thriving in areas with low precipitation.
