Juncus fugongensis

Juncus fugongensis, commonly known as the Fugong rush, is a perennial plant native to China, specifically found in the Yunnan Province.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems that grow in dense clusters. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late summer, consist of small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers.

Juncus fugongensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
