Juncus canadensis f. conglobatus

Juncus canadensis f. conglobatus, commonly known as the clustered rush, is a compact, grass-like plant native to North America.

It features dense clusters of narrow, cylindrical leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, which are arranged in spike-like structures. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
