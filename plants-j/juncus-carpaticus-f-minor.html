Juncus carpaticus f. minor

Juncus carpaticus f. minor, commonly known as the dwarf rush, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It typically reaches heights of only a few centimeters, making it an ideal choice for small garden spaces or as a ground cover. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often green or reddish in color, depending on the season. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Its compact size and ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions make it a versatile and hardy ornamental plant.
