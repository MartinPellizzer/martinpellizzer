Jacaranda mutabilis

Jacaranda mutabilis, commonly known as the jacaranda tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of violet or blue flowers that appear in spring. The tree has a broad, spreading canopy and can grow quite large, reaching heights of up to 25 meters. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple small leaflets, and its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also attract bees and other pollinators, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
