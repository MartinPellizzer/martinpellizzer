Jagera javanica subsp. australiana

Jagera javanica subsp.

australiana, commonly known as the Australian boxwood, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of small, oval-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale green, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry forest habitats.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and used in horticultural landscapes.
