Jumellea sagittata

Jumellea sagittata, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, arrow-shaped leaves that are often glossy and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in clusters, typically during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Jumellea sagittata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance.
