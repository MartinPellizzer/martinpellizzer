Juglans × notha

Juglans × notha, commonly known as the hybrid walnut, is a naturally occurring cross between the black walnut (Juglans nigra) and the English walnut (Juglans regia).

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 10–20 meters, featuring a broad, rounded canopy and compound leaves with 15–23 leaflets. The tree produces small, yellow-green flowers in clusters, followed by edible nuts enclosed in a thick, green husk. Its wood is valued for its strength and durability, making it useful in furniture and construction.

The plant is native to the United States and is often found in the central and eastern regions.
