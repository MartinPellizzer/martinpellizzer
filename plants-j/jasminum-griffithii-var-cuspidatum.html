Jasminum griffithii var. cuspidatum

Jasminum griffithii var. cuspidatum, commonly known as the Kashmiri jasmine, is a climbing shrub native to the Himalayan regions of India.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a strong, sweet scent. The plant has elliptical leaves with a slightly pointed tip, which gives it the specific epithet *cuspidatum*. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aromatic blooms.
