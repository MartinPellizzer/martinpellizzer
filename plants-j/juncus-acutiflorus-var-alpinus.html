Juncus acutiflorus var. alpinus

Juncus acutiflorus var. alpinus, commonly known as the alpine rush, is a perennial plant native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, rocky or gravelly soils in alpine and subalpine environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, often white or greenish flowers. It is adapted to cold climates and can withstand strong winds and low temperatures.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine ecosystems.
