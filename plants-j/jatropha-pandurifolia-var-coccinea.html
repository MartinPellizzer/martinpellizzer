Jatropha pandurifolia var. coccinea

Jatropha pandurifolia var. coccinea, commonly known as the red-veined jatropha, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves with bright red veins contrasting against dark green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange-red berries containing toxic seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests or disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant in suitable regions.
