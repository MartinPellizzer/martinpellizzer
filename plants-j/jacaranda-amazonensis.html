Jacaranda amazonensis

Jacaranda amazonensis, commonly known as the Amazon jacaranda, is a deciduous tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy and flaky, grey bark. Its leaves are opposite, compound, and have a distinctive, fern-like appearance.

The plant is valued for its ornamental flowers and its role in the ecological diversity of the Amazon region.
