Japarandiba pterocarpa

Japarandiba pterocarpa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Erythroxylum pterocarpum*, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and red or orange berries. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves seasonally, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It is often found in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.

The fruit is edible, though the leaves contain compounds that have been studied for potential pharmacological uses.
