Juncus krameri

Juncus krameri, commonly known as the slender rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes during the summer months.

Juncus krameri is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
