Jasminum domatiigerum var. domatiigerum

Jasminum domatiigerum var. domatiigerum, commonly known as the Persian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its pleasant fragrance.
