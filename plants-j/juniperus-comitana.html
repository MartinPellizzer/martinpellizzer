Juniperus comitana

Juniperus comitana, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada juniper, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its scale-like leaves and small, berry-like cones that are typically blue or black in color. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its foliage provides habitat for various wildlife species, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.

Juniperus comitana is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping.
