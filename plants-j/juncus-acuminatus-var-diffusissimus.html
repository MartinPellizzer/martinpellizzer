Juncus acuminatus var. diffusissimus

Juncus acuminatus var. diffusissimus, commonly known as the broad-leaved rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It features grass-like, cylindrical stems that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, which are usually green or reddish-brown. Its leaves are broad and flat, often reaching lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

This variety is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
