Justicia dejecta var. albipila

Justicia dejecta var. albipila, commonly known as the white-flowered false arum, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy white bracts that resemble flowers, though they are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
