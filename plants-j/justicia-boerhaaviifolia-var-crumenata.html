Justicia boerhaaviifolia var. crumenata

Justicia boerhaaviifolia var. crumenata, commonly known as the Crumenata justicia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.

Its botanical features make it both a valuable addition to horticulture and an important species for ecological studies.
