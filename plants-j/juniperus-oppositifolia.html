Juniperus oppositifolia

Juniperus oppositifolia, commonly known as the oppositeleaf juniper, is a coniferous evergreen shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its scale-like leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs along the branches, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in high elevations, where it contributes to the ecological diversity of its habitat.

Juniperus oppositifolia is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and attractive form.
