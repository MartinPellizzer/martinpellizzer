Julocroton montevidensis f. longipetiolata

Julocroton montevidensis f. longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled julocroton, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with long petioles, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is not commonly found in horticultural trade outside its native region.
