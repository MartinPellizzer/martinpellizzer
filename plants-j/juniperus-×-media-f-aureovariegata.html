Juniperus × media f. aureovariegata

Juniperus × media f. aureovariegata, commonly known as the golden variegated juniper, is a compact, evergreen shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which features yellow-green variegation on the leaves, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and as a landscape specimen.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its unique coloration and hardy nature.
