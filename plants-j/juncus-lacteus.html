Juncus lacteus

Juncus lacteus, commonly known as the milk rush, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas in North America.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its tall, grass-like stems that often grow in dense clusters. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant thrives in moist or aquatic environments.

Juncus lacteus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
