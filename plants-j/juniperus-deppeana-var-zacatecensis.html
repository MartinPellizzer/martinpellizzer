Juniperus deppeana var. zacatecensis

Juniperus deppeana var. zacatecensis, commonly known as the Zacatecas juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color and often have a powdery coating. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are usually blue or black when mature. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open woodlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native habitats.
