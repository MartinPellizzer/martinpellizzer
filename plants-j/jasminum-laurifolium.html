Jasminum laurifolium

Jasminum laurifolium, commonly known as the evergreen jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, lance-shaped leaves and its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

The flowers are used in perfumery and traditional medicine, highlighting its botanical and economic significance.
