Jatropha neopauciflora

Jatropha neopauciflora, commonly known as the Mexican croton, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by orange or red berries containing toxic seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy coating.

Jatropha neopauciflora is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant foliage and unique fruit.
