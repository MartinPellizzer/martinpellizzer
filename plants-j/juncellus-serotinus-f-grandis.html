Juncellus serotinus f. grandis

Juncellus serotinus f. grandis, commonly known as the giant rush, is a variant of the serotinous rush found in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

This subspecies is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
