Juncus × alpiniformis

Juncus × alpiniformis, commonly known as the alpine rush, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Juncus alpina and Juncus bufonius.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish or purple in color, and its narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Juncus × alpiniformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland areas.
