Juniperus virginiana pendula-nana

Juniperus virginiana pendula-nana, commonly known as the dwarf creeping juniper, is a low-growing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features dense, compact foliage that forms a mat-like growth habit, with small, scale-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces small, spherical blue berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

This juniper is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in rocky or sandy environments.
