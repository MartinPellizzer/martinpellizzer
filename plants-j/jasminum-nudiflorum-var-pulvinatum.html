Jasminum nudiflorum var. pulvinatum

Jasminum nudiflorum var. pulvinatum, commonly known as the Chinese jessamine, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves appear. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine.
