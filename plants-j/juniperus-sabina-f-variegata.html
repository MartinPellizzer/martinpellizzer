Juniperus sabina f. variegata

Juniperus sabina f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated juniper, is a compact evergreen shrub characterized by its unique foliage.

Its leaves are needle-like and exhibit a striking variegation of green and white or yellow, adding visual interest to gardens. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It is well-suited for rock gardens or as a border plant due to its hardy nature and low maintenance requirements.

This cultivar thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
