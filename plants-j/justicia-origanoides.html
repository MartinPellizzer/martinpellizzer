Justicia origanoides

Justicia origanoides, commonly known as red mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, reaching heights of up to 3 feet. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display.
