Juncus mertensianus var. suksdorfii

Juncus mertensianus var. suksdorfii, commonly known as the Suxdorf's rush, is a perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions of Eurasia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tundra and mountainous habitats. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a rough, textured surface.

This variant is notable for its adaptability to extreme environmental conditions.
