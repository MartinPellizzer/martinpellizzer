Jatropha kamerunica var. ueleensis

Jatropha kamerunica var. ueleensis, commonly known as the Uele jatropha, is a shrub native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, red-orange flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits containing toxic seeds, which are a key feature of the genus Jatropha. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for biofuel production, it has been cultivated in some regions.
