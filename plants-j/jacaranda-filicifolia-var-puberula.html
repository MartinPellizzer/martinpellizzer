Jacaranda filicifolia var. puberula

Jacaranda filicifolia var. puberula, commonly known as the silky jacaranda, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery leaves that give it a fern-like appearance. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal.
