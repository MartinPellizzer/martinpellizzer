Justicia alata

Justicia alata, commonly known as the spreading flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant blooms.

Justicia alata is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
