Jacksonia arida

Jacksonia arida, commonly known as the desert daisy, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to arid environments, featuring succulent leaves that store water. It produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Jacksonia arida is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
