Juncus acutiflorus var. minor

Juncus acutiflorus var. minor, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland and marsh environments in North America.

It features slender, grass-like stems that are typically less than 1 meter in height, with small, spike-like clusters of greenish-white flowers. The plant thrives in moist to water-saturated soils and is often found in shallow ponds, ditches, and along the edges of streams. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often have a slight sheen, contributing to its aesthetic appeal in natural landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
