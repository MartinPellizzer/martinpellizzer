Jatropha cathartica

Jatropha cathartica, commonly known as the physic nut or castor bean plant, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and milky sap, which contains toxic compounds. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain seeds used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a hairy texture.

Jatropha cathartica is often grown for its ornamental value and has historical use in herbal remedies.
