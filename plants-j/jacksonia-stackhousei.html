Jacksonia stackhousei

Jacksonia stackhousei, commonly known as the yellow daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and dark purple central disk. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Jacksonia stackhousei is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
