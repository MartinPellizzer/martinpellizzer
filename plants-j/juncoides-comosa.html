Juncoides comosa

Juncoides comosa, commonly known as the hairy rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and stream banks, and is often found in temperate regions.

Juncoides comosa plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
