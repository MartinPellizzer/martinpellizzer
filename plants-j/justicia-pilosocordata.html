Justicia pilosocordata

Justicia pilosocordata, commonly known as the velvetleaf false arum, is a tropical perennial shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple bracts that surround small, white flowers, creating an eye-catching display. The plant has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for tropical gardens.
