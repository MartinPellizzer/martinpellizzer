Justicia heterocarpa subsp. dinteri

Justicia heterocarpa subsp.

dinteri, commonly known as the Dinter's flame bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and garden ornamental use.
