Jussiaea uruguayensis f. major

Jussiaea uruguayensis f. major, commonly known as the Uruguay waterplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant features slender, branching stems that are often submerged or floating. Its small, opposite leaves are usually oval-shaped with a smooth surface.

It produces tiny, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, contributing to its ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.
