Justicia catharinensis

Justicia catharinensis, commonly known as the Brazilian verbena, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are square and somewhat woody.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.
