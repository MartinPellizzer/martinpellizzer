Juncus lampocarpus viridiflorus

Juncus lampocarpus viridiflorus, commonly known as the greenflower rush, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grassy leaves and spikes of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowering stems are often taller than the foliage, and the flowers are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
