Juncus brachystigma

Juncus brachystigma, commonly known as the short-stemmed rush, is a perennial plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and compact growth habit, typically forming clumps of narrow, cylindrical leaves. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated.

Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil, making it a valuable plant for erosion control in wetland habitats.
