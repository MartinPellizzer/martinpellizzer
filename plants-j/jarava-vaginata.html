Jarava vaginata

Jarava vaginata, commonly known as the narrowleaf jarava, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, rocky outcrops, and sandy soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are usually arranged in dense clusters.

Jarava vaginata is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
