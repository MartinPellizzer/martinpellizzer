Jacobaea gallerandiana

Jacobaea gallerandiana, commonly known as the Italian tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

Jacobaea gallerandiana is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
