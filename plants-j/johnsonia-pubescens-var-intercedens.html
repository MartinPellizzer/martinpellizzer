Johnsonia pubescens var. intercedens

Johnsonia pubescens var. intercedens, commonly known as the hairy Johnsonia, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense pubescence, or hairy texture, covering its stems and leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories where it can thrive without direct sunlight. Its foliage is broad and leathery, with a deep green coloration that helps it retain moisture.

While relatively understudied, it is of interest to botanists due to its potential ecological role and unique morphological features.
