Justicia pozuzoensis

Justicia pozuzoensis, commonly known as the Pozuzo flameflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in a fine, silky texture.

The flowers, which are usually red or orange, bloom in terminal spikes and are highly attractive to pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
