Jacksonia purpurascens

Jacksonia purpurascens, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, upright stems that bear clusters of vivid red, orange, or yellow tubular flowers, which are highly attractive to pollinators. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are slightly hairy and arranged in a rosette at the base. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Jacksonia purpurascens is known for its long blooming season, typically from late spring to early autumn.
