Juncus canadensis var. sparsiflorus

Juncus canadensis var. sparsiflorus, commonly known as the sparseflower rush, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and spikes of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are arranged sparsely along the stem, giving it its common name.

This variety is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
