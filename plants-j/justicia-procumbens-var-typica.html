Justicia procumbens var. typica

Justicia procumbens var. typica, commonly known as the creeping justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing herb with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of bright blue or violet tubular flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
