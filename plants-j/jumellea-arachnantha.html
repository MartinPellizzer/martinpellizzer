Jumellea arachnantha

Jumellea arachnantha, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a rare and striking plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which resembles a spider's web, hence its common name. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated in specialized horticultural settings.
