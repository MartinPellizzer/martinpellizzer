Juncus cyprius

Juncus cyprius, commonly known as the Cyprus rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as along the edges of ponds, ditches, and streams. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish or brownish in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers in spikes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and they are often used in floral arrangements due to their aesthetic appeal.

Juncus cyprius plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
