Jatropha platanifolia

Jatropha platanifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf jatropha, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain toxic seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for biofuel production, it has been cultivated in various parts of the world.
