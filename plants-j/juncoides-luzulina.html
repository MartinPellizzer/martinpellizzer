Juncoides luzulina

Juncoides luzulina, commonly known as the purple moor grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Juncaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, cylindrical stems that often bear small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant produces tiny, three-lobed flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom in late summer. Juncoides luzulina is typically found in moist, acidic soils in heathlands and moorland habitats.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
