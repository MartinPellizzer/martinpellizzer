Juglans soratensis

Juglans soratensis, commonly known as the Japanese black walnut, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Juglandaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with 15-23 pairs of leaflets. The tree produces edible nuts encased in a thick, green husk that turns yellow or brown as it matures. Its bark is rugged and fissured, and it can grow up to 20 meters tall.

The plant is valued for its timber and has been used in traditional Japanese medicine.
