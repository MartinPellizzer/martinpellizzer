Jatropha contrerasii

Jatropha contrerasii, commonly known as the Mexican physic nut, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by green, spiny capsules containing toxic seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential in biofuel production.
