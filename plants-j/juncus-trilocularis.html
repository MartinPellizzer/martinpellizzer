Juncus trilocularis

Juncus trilocularis, commonly known as the three-chambered rush, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Juncus trilocularis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
