Justicia culubritae

Justicia culubritae, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, velvety leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are usually violet or blue in color, with prominent stamens that are often more visible than the petals.

Justicia culubritae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
