Juncus acuminatus var. multiceps

Juncus acuminatus var. multiceps, commonly known as the multiceps rush, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or damp environments such as marshes, stream banks, and roadside ditches. The plant produces slender, grass-like stems that are often reddish or brownish in color, and it bears small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly hairy texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist conditions, making it a popular choice for wetland gardens.
