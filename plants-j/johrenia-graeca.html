Johrenia graeca

Johrenia graeca, commonly known as the Greek bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30-60 cm, with lance-shaped leaves that are opposite along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Johrenia graeca is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
