Jasione caespitans

Jasione caespitans, commonly known as the tufted bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of blue or violet flowers in late spring. Jasione caespitans is known for its compact growth habit and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
