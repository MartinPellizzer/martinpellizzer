Juglans × intermedia vilmoreana

Juglans × intermedia vilmoreana, commonly known as the Chinese black walnut, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Juglans mandshurica and Juglans regia.

It is native to regions of China and is characterized by its large, dark green leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces edible nuts enclosed in a thick, husky shell, which are valued for their flavor and oil content. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and economic value.

The tree is known for its rapid growth and ability to adapt to various soil conditions.
