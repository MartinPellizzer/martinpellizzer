Justicia marginata

Justicia marginata, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a striking plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that features tall, spike-like inflorescences covered in vibrant red bracts. The flowers are actually small, tubular blossoms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also known for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
