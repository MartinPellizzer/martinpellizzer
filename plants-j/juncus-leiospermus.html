Juncus leiospermus

Juncus leiospermus, commonly known as softseed rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has slender, grass-like stems that are often clustered together, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer. Its seeds are enclosed in smooth, fleshy capsules, which give it its common name.

Juncus leiospermus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
