Justicia serrulata

Justicia serrulata, commonly known as the serrated flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has narrow, serrated leaves that give it its common name, and it thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

Justicia serrulata is relatively easy to care for, requiring well-drained soil and regular watering.
