Jussiaea calycina

Jussiaea calycina, commonly known as the water starwort, is a small aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes. The plant has slender, branching stems that float on the water surface, with small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and ribbon-like, often floating on the water or submerged beneath it.

Jussiaea calycina plays a role in maintaining water quality by absorbing nutrients and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
