Jarava castellanosii

Jarava castellanosii, commonly known as the Castellanos' jarava, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit with fine, wiry leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and desert environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small desert fauna.
