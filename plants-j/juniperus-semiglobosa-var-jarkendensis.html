Juniperus semiglobosa var. jarkendensis

Juniperus semiglobosa var. jarkendensis, commonly known as the Jarkend Desert Juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and scale-like leaves that are often blue-gray in color. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are typically blue or purple when mature. It is well adapted to harsh, dry environments, showing resistance to drought and extreme temperatures.

This variety is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
