Juncus parous

Juncus parous, commonly known as the common rush, is a perennial plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in spike-like inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Juncus parous thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows.

It plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
