Jumellea longivaginans var. grandis

Jumellea longivaginans var. grandis, commonly known as the giant sandmat, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth habit and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting to its dry environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its roots are extensive and deep, allowing it to access moisture from lower soil layers.

This species is well-suited for xeriscaping and is often used in dryland restoration projects due to its resilience and low maintenance requirements.
