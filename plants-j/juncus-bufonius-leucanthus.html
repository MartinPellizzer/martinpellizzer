Juncus bufonius leucanthus

Juncus bufonius leucanthus, commonly known as the white-flowered rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish or purple in color, growing up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in spike-like clusters at the top of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically growing in dense, tufted clusters at the base.

This plant plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
