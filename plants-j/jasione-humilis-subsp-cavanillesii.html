Jasione humilis subsp. cavanillesii

Jasione humilis subsp.

cavanillesii, commonly known as the Cavanilles' bellflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features slender, hairy stems and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color.

The plant prefers well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
