Juncus leptospermus

Juncus leptospermus, commonly known as the slender spike rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, forming dense stands that help stabilize shoreline areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are often greenish or brownish in color. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and colonize new areas effectively.

Juncus leptospermus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
