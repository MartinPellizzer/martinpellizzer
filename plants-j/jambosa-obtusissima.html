Jambosa obtusissima

Jambosa obtusissima, commonly known as the Brazilian apple, is a tropical plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are thick and leathery with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a yellow or orange berry with a sweet, juicy pulp and a single seed.

Jambosa obtusissima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.
