Justicia venulosa

Justicia venulosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf false arum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by colorful bracts, typically in shades of red or purple. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
