Justicia cardiophylla

Justicia cardiophylla, commonly known as the heartleaf justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that consist of clusters of tubular, brightly colored flowers. The leaves are heart-shaped, which gives the plant its common name, and they are often glossy and arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and hummingbirds.
