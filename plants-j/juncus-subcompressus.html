Juncus subcompressus

Juncus subcompressus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and lack leaves, instead having spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters at the top of the stems.

Juncus subcompressus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
