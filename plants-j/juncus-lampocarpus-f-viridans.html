Juncus lampocarpus f. viridans

Juncus lampocarpus f. viridans, commonly known as the green rush, is a variant of the rush plant native to coastal areas of southern California.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green and stiff. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments.

This subspecies is adapted to salt spray and is an important part of the coastal ecosystem.
