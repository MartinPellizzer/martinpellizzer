Justicia angustiflora

Justicia angustiflora, commonly known as narrowleaf flamebeard, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking clusters of tubular, red or orange flowers that bloom in summer. Its inflorescence is a raceme, with each flower having five petals and two stamens.

Justicia angustiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
