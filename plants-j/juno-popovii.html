Juno popovii

Juno popovii, commonly known as the Popov's juniper, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, blue-gray needles that provide year-round cover. The plant produces tiny, dark blue berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Juno popovii thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, arid environments.

Its resilience makes it a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
