Jacksonia anthoclada

Jacksonia anthoclada, commonly known as the velvet flower or mountain daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine areas, thriving in cool, moist environments. It is often found in the Southern Alps and is a notable component of New Zealand's native flora.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species.
