Justicia metallicorum

Justicia metallicorum, commonly known as the silverleaf justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of tubular, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its foliage and floral display make it a visually appealing addition to gardens and green spaces.
