Justicia oblongoelliptica

Justicia oblongoelliptica, commonly known as the narrow-elliptic flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its elongated, elliptical leaves and vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
