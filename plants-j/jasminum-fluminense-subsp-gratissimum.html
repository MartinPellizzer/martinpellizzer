Jasminum fluminense subsp. gratissimum

Jasminum fluminense subsp.

gratissimum, commonly known as the Brazilian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil. It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring aromatic, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and tropical environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fragrant blossoms.
