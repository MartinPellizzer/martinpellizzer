Jambosa longifolia

Jambosa longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf jambosa, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, followed by edible, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
