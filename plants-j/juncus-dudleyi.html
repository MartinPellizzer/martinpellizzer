Juncus dudleyi

Juncus dudleyi, commonly known as Dudley's rush, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish-brown in color. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense spikes during the summer months. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes and along stream banks.

Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
