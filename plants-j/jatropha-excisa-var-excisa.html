Jatropha excisa var. excisa

Jatropha excisa var. excisa, commonly known as the scarlet nectarine tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.
