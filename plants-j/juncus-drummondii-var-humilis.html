Juncus drummondii var. humilis

Juncus drummondii var. humilis, commonly known as the Drummond's rush, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its flowers are small, clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife.
