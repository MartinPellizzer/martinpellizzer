Jaegeria calendulacea

Jaegeria calendulacea, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped lobes and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its botanical name reflects its historical use in herbal medicine and its association with the calendula family.

Jaegeria calendulacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
