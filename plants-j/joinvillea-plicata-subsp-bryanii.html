Joinvillea plicata subsp. bryanii

Joinvillea plicata subsp.

bryanii, commonly known as the Bryan's silverleaf, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, silver-green foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers.

The plant is evergreen and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Its leaves are succulent, and it produces seeds in capsule-like structures.
