Juglans ailantifolia var. cordiformis

Juglans ailantifolia var. cordiformis, commonly known as the heart-shaped Chinese walnut, is a variant of the Chinese walnut tree characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves.

This plant is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage. The leaves are typically large, palmately compound, and have a glossy green surface. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible nuts that mature in the fall.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
