Juncus cephalotes

Juncus cephalotes, commonly known as the tall rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged in a dense cluster at the base.

Juncus cephalotes plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
