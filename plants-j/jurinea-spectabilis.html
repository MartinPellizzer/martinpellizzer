Jurinea spectabilis

Jurinea spectabilis, commonly known as the showy jurinea, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow florets arranged in a radiate pattern, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand harsh, dry conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
