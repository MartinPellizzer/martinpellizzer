Juncus georgianus

Juncus georgianus, commonly known as the Georgian rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams, thriving in moist to water-saturated soils. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that are often used in floral arrangements due to their aesthetic appeal. Its small, spike-like inflorescence contains numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Juncus georgianus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
