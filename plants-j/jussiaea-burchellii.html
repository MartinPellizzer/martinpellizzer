Jussiaea burchellii

Jussiaea burchellii, commonly known as the water bladdernut, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive bladder-like fruits that encase the seeds, which are adapted for water dispersal. The plant typically grows in wetland areas, such as marshes and along the edges of lakes and rivers. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Jussiaea burchellii plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing food and shelter for various aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
