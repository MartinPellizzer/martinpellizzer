Jaborosa bipinnatifida

Jaborosa bipinnatifida, commonly known as the Brazilian feverfew, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally in folk remedies for its potential anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.
