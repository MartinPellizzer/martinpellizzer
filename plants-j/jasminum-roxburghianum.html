Jasminum roxburghianum

Jasminum roxburghianum, commonly known as the common jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces fragrant white flowers, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are usually dark green in color. Its flowers are highly aromatic and are often used in perfumes and traditional medicine.

Jasminum roxburghianum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
