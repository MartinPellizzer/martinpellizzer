Juncus latior

Juncus latior, commonly known as the broad rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that are often used in floral arrangements due to their elegant appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers in dense clusters near the top of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically growing in dense tufts at the base.

Juncus latior plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
