Juniperus cinerea

Juniperus cinerea, commonly known as the silver fir or white juniper, is a coniferous evergreen shrub or small tree native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its grayish-blue foliage, which is often covered in a fine layer of wax, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain the aromatic berries, which are used in various culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous or coastal regions.

Juniperus cinerea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
