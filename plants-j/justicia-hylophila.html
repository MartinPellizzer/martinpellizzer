Justicia hylophila

Justicia hylophila, commonly known as the red-veined begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with deep red veins and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests. It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
