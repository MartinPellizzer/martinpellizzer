Justicia assurgens

Justicia assurgens, commonly known as the red flame plant, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking clusters of tubular, bright red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The foliage is often variegated with shades of green and white, adding visual interest to its appearance.
