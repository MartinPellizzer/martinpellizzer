Jasminum amoenum var. rugosum

Jasminum amoenum var. rugosum, commonly known as the rugose jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and a woody stem, making it suitable for both ornamental and hedging purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, requiring moderate watering.

Due to its pleasant aroma and attractive appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in perfumery.
