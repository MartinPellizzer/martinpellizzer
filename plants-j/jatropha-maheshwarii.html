Jatropha maheshwarii

Jatropha maheshwarii, commonly known as the Maheshwari croton, is a shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that vary in shades of green, red, and yellow. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits that contain toxic seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to the toxicity of its seeds, caution is advised when handling or consuming any part of the plant.
