Juncus tenageia f. pauciflorus

Juncus tenageia f. pauciflorus, commonly known as the sparse-flowered rush, is a variant of the common rush species native to South America.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, distinguishing it from other rush species. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and pond edges.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various insect and bird life.
