Jasminum laurifolium var. villosum

Jasminum laurifolium var. villosum, commonly known as the hairy-leaf jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage with hairy textures on the leaves. The plant produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing as a shrub or small tree.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and horticultural collections.
