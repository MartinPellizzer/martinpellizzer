Justicia kapiriensis

Justicia kapiriensis, commonly known as the Kapiriensis flameflower, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by birds and insects, making it an important species in local ecosystems.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
