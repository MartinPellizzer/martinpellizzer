Jumellea arborescens

Jumellea arborescens, commonly known as the red-flowered rock fig, is a shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often consumed by birds and small mammals. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Jumellea arborescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
