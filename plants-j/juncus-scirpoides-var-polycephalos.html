Juncus scirpoides var. polycephalos

Juncus scirpoides var. polycephalos, commonly known as the clustered rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in North America.

It has cylindrical, grass-like stems that are typically green or reddish in color, and it grows in dense clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, which are often wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense vegetation, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
