Juncus valvatus

Juncus valvatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and tall spikes of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist soils, often in marshes, swamps, or along the edges of ponds and streams. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization in aquatic habitats.

Juncus valvatus plays a vital role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
