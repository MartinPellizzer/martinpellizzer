Jurinea kasakorum

Jurinea kasakorum, commonly known as the Caspian sandflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and succulent, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is well-suited to harsh, dry environments. It plays a role in stabilizing desert ecosystems and is often found in areas with limited rainfall.

Due to its specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
