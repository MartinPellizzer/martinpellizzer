Jacobaea othonnae subsp. anomala

Jacobaea othonnae subsp.

anomala, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Jacobaea othonnae subsp.

anomala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
