Juglans ovata

Juglans ovata, commonly known as the Chinese walnut, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Juglandaceae family and is characterized by its large, ovate leaves and distinctive compound fruits. The tree produces edible nuts enclosed in a thick, woody husk, which are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its bark is deeply furrowed, and it can grow up to 20 meters tall in optimal conditions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitats.
