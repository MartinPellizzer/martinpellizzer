Justicia asystasioides

Justicia asystasioides, commonly known as the red-rooted false dragonhead, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially sunny environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
