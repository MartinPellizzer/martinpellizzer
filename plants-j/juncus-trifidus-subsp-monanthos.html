Juncus trifidus subsp. monanthos

Juncus trifidus subsp.

monanthos, commonly known as the three-awned rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, pond edges, and stream banks.

The plant features slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and bear small, clustered spikes of white or greenish flowers. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and usually have three longitudinal ridges, giving it its species name "trifidus.".

This subspecies is distinguished by its monoecious flowering pattern, with both male and female flowers occurring on the same plant.
