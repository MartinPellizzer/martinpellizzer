Juniperus communis subsp. megistocarpa

Juniperus communis subsp.

megistocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited juniper, is a subspecies of the common juniper native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is a evergreen shrub or small tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and aromatic, blue-green foliage.

The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are typically blue or purple when mature, containing toxic compounds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands, rocky areas, and coastal regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
