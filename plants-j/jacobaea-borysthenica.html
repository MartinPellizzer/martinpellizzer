Jacobaea borysthenica

Jacobaea borysthenica, commonly known as the Crimean hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, exhibiting a low-growing habit with spreading stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central dark center.

Jacobaea borysthenica is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environments, making it a resilient species in its native range.
