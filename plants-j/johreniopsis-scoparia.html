Johreniopsis scoparia

Johreniopsis scoparia, commonly known as the desert beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy, cylindrical inflorescences that resemble a beard, giving it its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to conserve water, and its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Johreniopsis scoparia plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
