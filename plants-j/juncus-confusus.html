Juncus confusus

Juncus confusus, commonly known as the confused rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in dense clusters, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with cylindrical, grass-like stems that are often reddish or greenish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like inflorescences, which are usually greenish or brownish in hue. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize soil in marshy environments, making it an important species for wetland ecosystems.

Juncus confusus is often found in association with other wetland plants, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
