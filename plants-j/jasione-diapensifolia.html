Jasione diapensifolia

Jasione diapensifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, linear leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. It blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Jasione diapensifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
