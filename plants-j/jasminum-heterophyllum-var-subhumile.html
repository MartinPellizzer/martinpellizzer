Jasminum heterophyllum var. subhumile

Jasminum heterophyllum var. subhumile, commonly known as the Himalayan jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has slender, woody stems and elliptical leaves with a glossy green surface. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and horticultural collections.
