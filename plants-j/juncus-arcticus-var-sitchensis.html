Juncus arcticus var. sitchensis

Juncus arcticus var. sitchensis, commonly known as the Sitka rush, is a perennial plant native to the northern regions of North America and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage that forms compact clumps, typically growing in moist to wet environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense vegetation in wetlands and streambanks.

This variety is particularly adapted to cold climates and is often found in coastal areas with high humidity.
