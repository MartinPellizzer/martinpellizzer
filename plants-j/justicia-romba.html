Justicia romba

Justicia romba, commonly known as the red-hot catwhiskers, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red bracts that surround clusters of small, white flowers, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is known for its upright growth habit and showy inflorescences. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and containers.
