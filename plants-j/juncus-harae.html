Juncus harae

Juncus harae, commonly known as the Harae rush, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae and is characterized by its dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist to aquatic environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its adaptability, Juncus harae plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
