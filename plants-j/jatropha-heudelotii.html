Jatropha heudelotii

Jatropha heudelotii, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its latex sap is milky and toxic, containing compounds that can cause skin irritation.

Jatropha heudelotii is often found in dry, open woodlands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
