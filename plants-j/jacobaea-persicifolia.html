Jacobaea persicifolia

Jacobaea persicifolia, commonly known as the Persian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive silvery underside, and its bright yellow flowers bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
