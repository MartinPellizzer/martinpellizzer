Japarandiba speciosa

Japarandiba speciosa, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. Its bark is smooth and grayish, often exuding a milky sap when damaged.

Japarandiba speciosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
