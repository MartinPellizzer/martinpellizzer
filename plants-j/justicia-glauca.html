Justicia glauca

Justicia glauca, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers, which bloom in the summer months. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Justicia glauca is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
