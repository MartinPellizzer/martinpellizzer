Juglans regia subsp. sinensis

Juglans regia subsp.

sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese walnut, is a deciduous tree native to China. It belongs to the Juglandaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, edible nuts.

The tree produces oblong, green fruits that mature to a dark brown color, encased in a thick husk. Its bark is rugged and furrowed, while its wood is valued for its strength and fine grain.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its nuts and ornamental value.
