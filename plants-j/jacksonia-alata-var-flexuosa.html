Jacksonia alata var. flexuosa

Jacksonia alata var. flexuosa, commonly known as the flexible flame flower, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, orange-red bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces small, yellow disk flowers surrounded by these vibrant bracts, which are often the primary ornamental feature. It is typically found in coastal and lowland areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
