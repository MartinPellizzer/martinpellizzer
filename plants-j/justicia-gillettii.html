Justicia gillettii

Justicia gillettii, commonly known as Gillett's flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are pollinated by birds, particularly hummingbirds, due to their bright color and nectar-rich structure.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
