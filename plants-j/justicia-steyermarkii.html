Justicia steyermarkii

Justicia steyermarkii, commonly known as the Mexican flameflower, is a striking tropical plant native to Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that features showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that attract hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This species is popular in gardens for its vibrant blooms and ability to add color to landscapes.
