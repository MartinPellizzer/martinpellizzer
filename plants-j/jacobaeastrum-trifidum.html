Jacobaeastrum trifidum

Jacobaeastrum trifidum, commonly known as the three-lobed thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and shrubland habitats.

Jacobaeastrum trifidum is known for its resilience to drought and its role in supporting local pollinators.
