Juncus uliginosus var. ericetorum

Juncus uliginosus var. ericetorum, commonly known as the heather rush, is a flowering plant native to northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and spike-like inflorescences. This variant thrives in moist, acidic soils, often found in heathlands and bogs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
