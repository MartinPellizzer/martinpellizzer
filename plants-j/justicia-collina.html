Justicia collina

Justicia collina, commonly known as the narrowleaf false arum, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, wooded areas. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking clusters of violet or purple flowers. Its inflorescence is a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

Justicia collina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native habitats.
