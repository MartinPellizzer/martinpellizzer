Jacobaea incana subsp. insubrica

Jacobaea incana subsp.

insubrica, commonly known as the Hoary Fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and small, yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.

Its resilience and adaptability make it a common sight in alpine and subalpine environments.
