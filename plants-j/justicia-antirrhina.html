Justicia antirrhina

Justicia antirrhina, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spike-like inflorescence that bears bright orange or red tubular flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
