Juniperus utahensis var. megalocarpa

Juniperus utahensis var. megalocarpa, commonly known as the Utah juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its scale-like leaves and small, blue-gray berries that are typically borne in clusters. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It has a slow growth rate and can live for several decades, making it a long-lived component of its ecosystem.

The foliage and berries are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic compounds.
