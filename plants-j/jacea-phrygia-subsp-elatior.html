Jacea phrygia subsp. elatior

Jacea phrygia subsp.

elatior, commonly known as the greater red brome, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit.

The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets, each containing several tiny, reddish-brown florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and dry meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
