Jasminum officinale var. tenuifolium

Jasminum officinale var. tenuifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in summer, producing a sweet, heady scent that attracts pollinators. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for gardens and containers.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and use in perfumery and herbal medicine.
