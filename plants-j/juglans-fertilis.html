Juglans fertilis

Juglans fertilis, commonly known as the Persian walnuts, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Iran and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Juglandaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and male and female flowers on separate trees. The tree produces edible nuts enclosed in a hard shell, which are highly valued for their flavor and nutritional content. Juglans fertilis thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
