Justicia alternifolia

Justicia alternifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Mexican flameflower, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, violet to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Justicia alternifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
