Justicia assamica

Justicia assamica, commonly known as the Assam flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers. The plant typically grows as an herb or shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Justicia assamica thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
