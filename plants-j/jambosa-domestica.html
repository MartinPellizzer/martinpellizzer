Jambosa domestica

Jambosa domestica, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a small evergreen tree that typically grows to a height of 5–10 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into bright red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy surface.

The fruit is highly valued for its sweet, tangy flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in jams and juices.
