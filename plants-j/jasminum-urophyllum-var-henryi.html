Jasminum urophyllum var. henryi

Jasminum urophyllum var. henryi, commonly known as Henry's evergreen jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with a compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for both garden and container planting.

Its evergreen nature provides year-round visual interest, especially during the flowering season.
