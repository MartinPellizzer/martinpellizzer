Juncus nodosus var. megacephalus

Juncus nodosus var. megacephalus, commonly known as the giant clubmoss juncea, is a variant of the clubmoss plant characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often clustered inflorescences.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. The plant features small, scale-like leaves and produces tiny, wind-pollinated spores rather than seeds. Its botanical structure reflects its classification within the order Microspermales, which includes other primitive vascular plants.

The variation in its morphology, particularly the enlarged terminal spore-bearing structures, sets it apart from other members of the Juncus genus.
