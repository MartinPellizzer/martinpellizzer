Justicia arbuscula

Justicia arbuscula, commonly known as the dwarf flame flower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant red bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces small, tubular white flowers that are clustered in whorls along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Justicia arbuscula is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
