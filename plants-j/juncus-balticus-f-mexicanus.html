Juncus balticus f. mexicanus

Juncus balticus f. mexicanus, commonly known as the Mexican rush, is a variant of the common rush species native to parts of North America.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowering spikes produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are often wind-pollinated.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
