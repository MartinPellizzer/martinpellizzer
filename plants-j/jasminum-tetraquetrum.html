Jasminum tetraquetrum

Jasminum tetraquetrum, commonly known as the four-angled jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
