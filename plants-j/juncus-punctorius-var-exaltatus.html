Juncus punctorius var. exaltatus

Juncus punctorius var. exaltatus, commonly known as the tall spike rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters, often growing in dense clusters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically emerging from the base of the plant.

This variety is often found in marshes, pond edges, and other moist habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic life.
