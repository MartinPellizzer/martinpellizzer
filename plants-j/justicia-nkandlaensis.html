Justicia nkandlaensis

Justicia nkandlaensis, commonly known as the Nkandla flame flower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in terminal clusters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blossoms and compact growth habit.

The species is also noted for its adaptability to dry conditions, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid regions.
