Jasione sessiliflora var. eriantha

Jasione sessiliflora var. eriantha, commonly known as the hairy jasione, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and gravelly fields. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The variety eriantha is distinguished by its hairy calyx and bracts, which give it a distinctive appearance.
