Juncus tenageia subsp. sphaerocarpus

Juncus tenageia subsp.

sphaerocarpus, commonly known as the globular rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America. It typically grows in moist or wetland environments, such as marshes and stream banks.

The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and bear dense clusters of small, greenish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall grassy appearance.

The subspecies is characterized by its unique, spherical inflorescence, which sets it apart from other members of the Juncus genus.
