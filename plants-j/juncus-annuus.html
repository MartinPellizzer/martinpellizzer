Juncus annuus

Juncus annuus, commonly known as the common rush, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves that grow from a rhizomatous stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, wetlands, and along the edges of ponds.

Juncus annuus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
