Juncus nevskii

Juncus nevskii, commonly known as the Nevskii rush, is a perennial plant native to the marshy and wetland habitats of Eastern Europe and parts of Russia.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often used in floral arrangements. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered at the top of the stem, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Juncus nevskii thrives in moist to waterlogged soils and is often found in peat bogs and along the edges of ponds.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
