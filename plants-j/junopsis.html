Junopsis

Junopsis, commonly known as the sea holly, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Asteraceae.

These plants are characterized by their striking blue or purple flowers and spiny, leaf-like bracts that surround the flower heads. Junopsis species are typically found in coastal areas and saline environments, showcasing their adaptability to harsh conditions. They are often used in ornamental gardening due to their unique appearance and hardy nature.

The genus includes several species, such as Junopsis maritima, which is native to the Mediterranean region.
