Juncus glaucus magnagutii

Juncus glaucus magnagutii, commonly known as the blue rush, is a perennial plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, pale blue flowers in late summer. This species is native to arid and semi-arid regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils. The plant is often found in rocky areas or along stream banks, adapting to harsh environmental conditions.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an important component of its native habitats.
