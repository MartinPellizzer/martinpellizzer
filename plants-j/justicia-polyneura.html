Justicia polyneura

Justicia polyneura, commonly known as the flame vine, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Justicia polyneura is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping and as a houseplant.
