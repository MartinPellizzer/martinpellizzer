Juncus triglumis var. copelandii

Juncus triglumis var. copelandii, commonly known as Copeland's rush, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It has a dense root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
