Justicia corymbosa

Justicia corymbosa, commonly known as the flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in dense clusters, giving it a fiery appearance. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Justicia corymbosa is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant display and ability to attract pollinators.
