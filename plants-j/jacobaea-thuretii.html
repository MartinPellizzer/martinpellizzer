Jacobaea thuretii

Jacobaea thuretii, commonly known as the Thuret's ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has finely divided, aromatic leaves and produces a milky sap.

Jacobaea thuretii is often found in Mediterranean climates and is noted for its ecological importance to pollinators.
