Juncus stoechadanthos

Juncus stoechadanthos, commonly known as the marsh rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or waterlogged habitats such as marshes, wet meadows, and the edges of ponds. The plant produces tall, cylindrical spikes of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often form dense clumps.

Juncus stoechadanthos plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
