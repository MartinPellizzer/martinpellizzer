Justicia sylvatica

Justicia sylvatica, commonly known as the woodland flame or wild flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
