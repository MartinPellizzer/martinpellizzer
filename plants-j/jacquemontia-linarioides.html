Jacquemontia linarioides

Jacquemontia linarioides, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking blue to violet flowers with five petals. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to wetland gardens and a valuable species for ecological studies.
