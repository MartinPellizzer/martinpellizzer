Juncus scirpoides var. carolinianus

Juncus scirpoides var. carolinianus, commonly known as the Carolina rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae and is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil, making it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
