Jasminum simplicifolium subsp. simplicifolium

Jasminum simplicifolium subsp.

simplicifolium, commonly known as the simple-leaf jasmine, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its simple, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

The subspecies is notable for its compact growth habit and suitability for container gardening.
