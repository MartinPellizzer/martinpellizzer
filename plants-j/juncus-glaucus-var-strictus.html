Juncus glaucus var. strictus

Juncus glaucus var. strictus, commonly known as the blue rush, is a perennial plant native to northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage that forms compact clumps, with narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of white to pale blue flowers in the summer months. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil, making it valuable in wetland restoration projects.
