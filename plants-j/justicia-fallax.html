Justicia fallax

Justicia fallax, commonly known as the Texas false arum, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetland or swampy environments. The plant features striking, arrow-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in moist conditions.

The plant's botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation.
