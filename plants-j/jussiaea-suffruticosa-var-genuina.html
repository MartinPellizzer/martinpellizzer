Jussiaea suffruticosa var. genuina

Jussiaea suffruticosa var. genuina, commonly known as the slender jussiaea, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil and Paraguay.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, elliptic leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often near water bodies such as rivers and wetlands. Its botanical features include hairy stems and a compact growth habit, making it well adapted to its wetland habitat.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
