Jacobaea nemorosa

Jacobaea nemorosa, commonly known as common tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and meadows, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are pinnately divided and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and bloom from June to September.

Jacobaea nemorosa is also known for its strong aromatic scent and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
