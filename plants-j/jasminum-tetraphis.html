Jasminum tetraphis

Jasminum tetraphis, commonly known as the four-leaf jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has oval-shaped, dark green leaves with a smooth texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Jasminum tetraphis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
