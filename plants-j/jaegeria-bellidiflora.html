Jaegeria bellidiflora

Jaegeria bellidiflora, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Jaegeria bellidiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
