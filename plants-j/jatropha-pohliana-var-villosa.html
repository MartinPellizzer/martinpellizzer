Jatropha pohliana var. villosa

Jatropha pohliana var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy jatropha, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange and are followed by capsule fruits containing toxic seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been studied for potential medicinal properties.
