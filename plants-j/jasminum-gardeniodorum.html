Jasminum gardeniodorum

Jasminum gardeniodorum, commonly known as the Gardenia jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy green surface.

The flowers are often used in perfumery and traditional medicine due to their aromatic properties.
