Juncus × murbeckii

Juncus × murbeckii, commonly known as the hybrid rush, is a perennial plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes, which are usually green or brown, and it thrives in moist to wet environments. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams.

Juncus × murbeckii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic habitats.
