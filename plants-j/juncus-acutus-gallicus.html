Juncus acutus gallicus

Juncus acutus gallicus, commonly known as the French rush, is a perennial plant native to wetlands and moist soils in Europe.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that are typically green or reddish in color, often with a fibrous texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters at the tips of the stems, which are usually green or brown in hue. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.

Juncus acutus gallicus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
