Juncus pilosus subsp. maximus

Juncus pilosus subsp.

maximus, commonly known as the silky rush, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, ditches, and along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that are arranged in a spike.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall herbaceous structure.
