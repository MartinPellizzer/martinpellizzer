Juncus longistylis

Juncus longistylis, commonly known as the long-styled rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that often reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters at the top of the stem, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense mats, stabilizing soil in marshy areas.

Juncus longistylis plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
