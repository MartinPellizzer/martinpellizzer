Juncus punctorius var. schimperi

Juncus punctorius var. schimperi, commonly known as the African rush, is a perennial plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often used in basket weaving due to their flexibility and strength. The plant thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found near water bodies such as rivers and lakes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes at the top of the stems.

This variety is valued both for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and for its traditional uses in crafts and construction.
