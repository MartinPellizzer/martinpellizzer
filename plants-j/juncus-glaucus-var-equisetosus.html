Juncus glaucus var. equisetosus

Juncus glaucus var. equisetosus, commonly known as the silver rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a powdery, whitish bloom, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters at the top of the stems, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize soil in marshy areas, making it an important species in wetland ecosystems.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires consistently moist to aquatic conditions.
