Justicia spiciformis

Justicia spiciformis, commonly known as the spiked justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking spikes of tubular, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial or shrub, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture, while its stems are usually square in cross-section.

Justicia spiciformis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
