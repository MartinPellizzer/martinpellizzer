Josephia tenuifolia

Josephia tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved josephia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and open woodlands. Its botanical features include a low-growing habit and a preference for full sun exposure.

It is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
