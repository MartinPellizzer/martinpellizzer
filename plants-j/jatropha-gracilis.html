Jatropha gracilis

Jatropha gracilis, commonly known as the slender spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features upright stems that are often branched and covered with fine, sticky hairs. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin. Small yellow flowers are clustered in umbels, followed by orange or red berries containing toxic seeds.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an invasive species in some areas.
