Juncus × micranthus

Juncus × micranthus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a hybrid plant species within the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that are often grass-like in appearance, and it typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes and stream banks. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color, which are followed by cylindrical seed heads. Juncus × micranthus is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
