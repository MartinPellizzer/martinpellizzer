Justicia latibracteata

Justicia latibracteata, commonly known as the broad bract justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that typically bloom in clusters, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.
