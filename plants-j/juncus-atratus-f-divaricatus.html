Juncus atratus f. divaricatus

Juncus atratus f. divaricatus, commonly known as the black rush variant, is a flowering plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers arranged in spike-like clusters.

This plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
