Juncus acutus var. effusus

Juncus acutus var. effusus, commonly known as the soft rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like clusters of cylindrical, leaf-like stems that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or brown, arranged in spikes at the tips of the stems. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
