Justicia guineensis

Justicia guineensis, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, velvety leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are usually purple or blue, with a distinctive two-lipped structure, and it produces small, round fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Justicia guineensis is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is popular in gardens and as a houseplant.
