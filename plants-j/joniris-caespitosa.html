Joniris caespitosa

Joniris caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features a clump-forming habit with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.

Joniris caespitosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
