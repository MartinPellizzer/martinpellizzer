Jacquemontia capitata

Jacquemontia capitata, commonly known as the yellow balsam, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in clusters at the top of its stems. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The flowers bloom in the spring and are pollinated by bees and other insects.
