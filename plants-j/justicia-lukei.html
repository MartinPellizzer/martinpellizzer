Justicia lukei

Justicia lukei, commonly known as the velvetleaf false arum, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain areas.
