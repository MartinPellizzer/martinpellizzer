Juniperus pingii var. carinata

Juniperus pingii var. carinata, commonly known as the Chinese juniper or mountain juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to China and Korea.

It is characterized by its scale-like leaves that are often reddish-brown and its small, blue-gray berries. The plant is evergreen, with a compact growth habit and can reach heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is used in horticulture for its hardiness and resilience.
