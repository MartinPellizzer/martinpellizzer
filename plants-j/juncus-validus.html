Juncus validus

Juncus validus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often slightly hairy and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, spike-like clusters of greenish-white flowers in the summer.

Juncus validus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
