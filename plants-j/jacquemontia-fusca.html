Jacquemontia fusca

Jacquemontia fusca, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has hairy, alternate leaves that are heart-shaped and often have a reddish tinge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Jacquemontia fusca is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
