Jasminum officinale var. bosphoricum

Jasminum officinale var. bosphoricum, commonly known as the Bosphorus jasmine, is a fragrant evergreen shrub native to the regions around the Bosphorus Strait.

It features small, white, star-shaped flowers that emit a sweet, intoxicating aroma, particularly at night. The plant produces opposite, simple, and usually hairy leaves that are dark green in color. It is well adapted to coastal environments and thrives in well-drained soil with ample sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
