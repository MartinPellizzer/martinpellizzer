Jamesonia retroflexa

Jamesonia retroflexa, commonly known as the redroot jessamine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and bright yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in the spring. Its root system is characterized by reddish-brown roots, which contribute to its common name.

The plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas.
