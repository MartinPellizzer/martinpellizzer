Juncus balticus var. lesueurii

Juncus balticus var. lesueurii, commonly known as the Lesueur's rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like clusters of greenish-white flowers. Native to wetland environments, this plant thrives in moist to aquatic conditions, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization.

The plant is valued for its ecological role in supporting wetland biodiversity and its ornamental appeal in water garden settings.
