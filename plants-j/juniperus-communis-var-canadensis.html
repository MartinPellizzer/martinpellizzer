Juniperus communis var. canadensis

Juniperus communis var. canadensis, commonly known as Canadian juniper, is a hardy evergreen shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its scale-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to a variety of environmental conditions, including cold climates.

Canadian juniper is also valued for its aromatic wood and its role in ecological systems.
