Justicia linifolia

Justicia linifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf justicia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, silver-gray foliage and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for arid and semi-arid landscapes. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in xeriscaping.
