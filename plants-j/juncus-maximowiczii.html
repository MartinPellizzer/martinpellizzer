Juncus maximowiczii

Juncus maximowiczii, commonly known as the tall rush, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like, cylindrical stems that are often used in floral arrangements. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters near the base of the plant.

Juncus maximowiczii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
