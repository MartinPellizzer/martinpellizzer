Justicia linearifolia

Justicia linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flamebeard, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and is often found near water bodies. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds and bees, making it an important species in local ecosystems.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
