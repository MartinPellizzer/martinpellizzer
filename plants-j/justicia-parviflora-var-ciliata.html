Justicia parviflora var. ciliata

Justicia parviflora var. ciliata, commonly known as the Ciliate False Flax, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with ciliate (fringed) margins. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming from late spring to early fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
