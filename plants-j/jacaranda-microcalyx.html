Jacaranda microcalyx

Jacaranda microcalyx, commonly known as the small-fruited jacaranda, is a species of flowering tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers that bloom in pendulous clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple small leaflets, and it thrives in warm, tropical climates.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
