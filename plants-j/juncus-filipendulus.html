Juncus filipendulus

Juncus filipendulus, commonly known as the soft rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant features cylindrical, grass-like stems that are often reddish or purplish in color. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spike-like inflorescences at the top of the stems.

Juncus filipendulus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
