Juncoides multiflora

Juncoides multiflora, commonly known as the many-flowered rush, is a perennial plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and is typically found in wet or moist environments such as marshes and stream banks. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which can help stabilize soil in wetland areas.

Juncoides multiflora is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in saturated soils.
