Juncus compressus

Juncus compressus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often compressed and dark green in color. Its flowering spikes consist of small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Juncus compressus plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
