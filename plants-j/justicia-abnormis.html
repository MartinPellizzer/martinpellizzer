Justicia abnormis

Justicia abnormis, commonly known as the purple false heather, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring dense clusters of small, tubular flowers. The flowers are usually purple or violet in color, with some varieties displaying white or pink hues. It has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
