Jasminum laurifolium var. laurifolium

Jasminum laurifolium var. laurifolium, commonly known as the evergreen jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, leathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its versatility makes it suitable for use in gardens, as a hedge, or in containers.
