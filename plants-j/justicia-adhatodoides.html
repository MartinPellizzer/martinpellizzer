Justicia adhatodoides

Justicia adhatodoides, commonly known as the flame bush, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for warm climates.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant in gardens.
