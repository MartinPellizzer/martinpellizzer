Juncus arcticus subsp. intermedius

Juncus arcticus subsp.

intermedius, commonly known as the Arctic rush, is a flowering plant native to arctic and alpine regions. It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters near the top of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small arctic organisms.
