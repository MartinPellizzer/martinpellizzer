Juncus arcticus subsp. grubovii

Juncus arcticus subsp.

grubovii, commonly known as Arctic rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Arctic and alpine regions. It typically grows in moist, rocky or sandy soils, often in tundra or mountainous environments.

The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are usually leafless, with spikes of small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to withstand cold temperatures and strong winds.

This subspecies is an important part of the Arctic flora, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for small organisms.
