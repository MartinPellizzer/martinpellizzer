Jambosa teysmannii

Jambosa teysmannii, commonly known as the Teyssmann's jambosa, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries, which are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
