Justicia cordata var. pubescens

Justicia cordata var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy heartleaf justicia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped, pubescent leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warm climates.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its use in both ornamental gardening and ecological landscapes.
