Jasminum tubiflorum

Jasminum tubiflorum, commonly known as the sweet pitcher plant, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive tubular, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a climbing habit and produces waxy, green leaves with a velvety texture. Its flowers emit a strong, sweet scent, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
