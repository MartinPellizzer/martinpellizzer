Justicia platicarpa

Justicia platicarpa, commonly known as the broadleaf flameflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The genus Justicia belongs to the Acanthaceae family, known for its showy floral displays and diverse species.
