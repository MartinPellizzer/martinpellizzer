Justicia buchii

Justicia buchii, commonly known as the velvetleaf or blue mistflower, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It is known for its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes and as a bedding plant.
