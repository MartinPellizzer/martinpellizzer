Jasminum simplicifolium

Jasminum simplicifolium, commonly known as the simple-leaved jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a sweet scent. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Jasminum simplicifolium is also used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
