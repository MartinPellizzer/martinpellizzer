Justicia aequiloculata var. ranohelensis

Justicia aequiloculata var. ranohelensis, commonly known as the Madagascar flame flower, is a perennial shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for warm climates.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and conservation efforts.
