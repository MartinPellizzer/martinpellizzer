Juniperus communis subsp. eucommunis

Juniperus communis subsp.

eucommunis, commonly known as common juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its needle-like leaves and clusters of blue-black berries that are used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant is evergreen, with a rugged, often sprawling growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adaptable to a variety of climates, making it a resilient species.

Its aromatic foliage and berries contribute to its ecological and horticultural significance.
