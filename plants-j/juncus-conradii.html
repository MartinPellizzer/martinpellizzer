Juncus conradii

Juncus conradii, commonly known as the Conradi's rush, is a perennial plant native to the wetlands of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant thrives in moist to wet soil conditions, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil and prevent erosion in aquatic environments.

Juncus conradii plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
