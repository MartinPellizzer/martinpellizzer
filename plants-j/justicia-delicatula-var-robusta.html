Justicia delicatula var. robusta

Justicia delicatula var. robusta, commonly known as the velvetleaf false arum, is a tropical plant characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch.

It produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers with prominent, showy spathes that resemble those of true arums. The plant is native to Central and South America and thrives in warm, humid climates with partial to full shade. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and texture.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate a range of soil types, making it a versatile choice for landscapers.
