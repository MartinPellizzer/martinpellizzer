Jollydora pedunculosa

Jollydora pedunculosa, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of yellow-orange flowers that resemble bells, which bloom throughout the warm seasons. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.

Jollydora pedunculosa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
