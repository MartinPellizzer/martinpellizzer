Jatropha arizonica

Jatropha arizonica, commonly known as Arizona spurge, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features succulent, fleshy leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. It is adapted to hot, dry climates and has a shallow root system for efficient water absorption.

The plant contains toxic compounds, making it unpalatable to herbivores.
