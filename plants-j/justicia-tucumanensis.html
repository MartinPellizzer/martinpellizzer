Justicia tucumanensis

Justicia tucumanensis, commonly known as the Tucumán flame flower, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Bolivia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of tubular, red or orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. Its botanical name honors the province of Tucumán, reflecting its regional significance.

The plant is well-suited for warm climates and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
