Juncus ramboi subsp. colombianus

Juncus ramboi subsp.

colombianus, commonly known as the Colombian rush, is a member of the Juncaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and cylindrical stems. It is native to South America, particularly Colombia, where it thrives in wetland and marshy environments.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense vegetation in moist soils.

This subspecies is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
