Juno coerulea

Juno coerulea, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a striking flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet petals, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are dark green and broad, providing a striking contrast to its colorful blooms.

Juno coerulea is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological importance in its native habitat.
