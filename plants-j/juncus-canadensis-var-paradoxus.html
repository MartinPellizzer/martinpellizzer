Juncus canadensis var. paradoxus

Juncus canadensis var. paradoxus, commonly known as the Canadian rush, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often green to reddish in color. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
