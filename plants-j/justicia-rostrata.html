Justicia rostrata

Justicia rostrata, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red bracts that surround its small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial or shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Justicia rostrata thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
