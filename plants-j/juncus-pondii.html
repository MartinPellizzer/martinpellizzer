Juncus pondii

Juncus pondii, commonly known as the pond rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are usually green or brown in color. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

Juncus pondii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to tolerate and improve water quality.
