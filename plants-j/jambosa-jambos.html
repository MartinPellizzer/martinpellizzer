Jambosa jambos

Jambosa jambos, commonly known as the Natal plum, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, yellow or orange fruits. The fruit has a sweet, tangy flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in jams and jellies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, subtropical climates.
