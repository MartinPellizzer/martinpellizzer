Justicia betonicoides

Justicia betonicoides, commonly known as Mexican devil's backbone, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces spikes of tubular, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, or purple, which attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Justicia betonicoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
