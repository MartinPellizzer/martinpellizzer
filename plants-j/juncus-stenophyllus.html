Juncus stenophyllus

Juncus stenophyllus, commonly known as narrowleaf rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually less than an inch wide, giving it its common name. Its flowering spikes consist of small, greenish-white flowers that appear in late spring to early summer.

Juncus stenophyllus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
