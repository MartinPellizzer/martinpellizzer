Juncus subglaucus

Juncus subglaucus, commonly known as the smooth rush, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its stems are often hollow and jointed, a common feature among the Juncaceae family.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, with three sepals and three petals, typically arranged in a dense cluster.
