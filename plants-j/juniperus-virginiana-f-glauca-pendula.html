Juniperus virginiana f. glauca-pendula

Juniperus virginiana f. glauca-pendula, commonly known as the blue spruce weeping form, is a unique variant of the eastern red cedar.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and pendulous branches that create a cascading effect. This form is a naturally occurring variant, often found in specific microclimates where it thrives. The plant is evergreen, with small, scale-like leaves that contribute to its ornamental value.

It is well-suited for landscaping due to its distinctive appearance and hardy nature.
