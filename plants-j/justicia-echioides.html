Justicia echioides

Justicia echioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf false arnoldia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are opposite, narrow, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Justicia echioides is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
