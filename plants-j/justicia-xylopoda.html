Justicia xylopoda

Justicia xylopoda, commonly known as flame bush, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds, making it a valuable addition to wildlife gardens.
