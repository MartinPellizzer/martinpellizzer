Johnsonia pubescens f. hirta

Johnsonia pubescens f. hirta, commonly known as the hirsute Johnsonia, is a rare and ornamental plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, fuzzy foliage that gives it a unique, textured appearance. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft pubescence, contributing to its distinctive look. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color.

This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in botanical gardens.
