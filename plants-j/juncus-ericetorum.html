Juncus ericetorum

Juncus ericetorum, commonly known as the heath rush, is a flowering plant native to northern Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils, often found in heathlands, moorlands, and along the edges of bogs. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers in clusters. It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage.

Juncus ericetorum plays a role in stabilizing soil in wet or marginal habitats and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
