Jungia pubescens var. grandistipulata

Jungia pubescens var. grandistipulata, commonly known as the large-leaf jungle plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
