Jussiaea peruviana var. longifolia

Jussiaea peruviana var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved jussiaea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often creeping stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that can reach several centimeters in length. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or marshy habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
