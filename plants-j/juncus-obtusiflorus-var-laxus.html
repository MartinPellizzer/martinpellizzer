Juncus obtusiflorus var. laxus

Juncus obtusiflorus var. laxus, commonly known as the looseflower bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features tall, cylindrical inflorescences that produce small, clustered flowers, typically in shades of greenish-white to pale yellow. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and along pond edges.

This species plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
