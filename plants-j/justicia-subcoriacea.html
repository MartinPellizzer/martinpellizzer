Justicia subcoriacea

Justicia subcoriacea, commonly known as the woolly false dragonhead, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and spikes of showy, tubular flowers that range from blue to purple. The plant typically grows as a perennial shrub, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
