Juniperus communis f. pendula

Juniperus communis f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping juniper, is a unique form of the common juniper.

It is characterized by its gracefully drooping branches that create a cascading effect, making it an ornamental plant. The foliage consists of scale-like leaves that are typically a deep green color. It produces small, blue-black berries that are edible but often used for flavoring in gin.

This variety thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and as a landscape accent.
