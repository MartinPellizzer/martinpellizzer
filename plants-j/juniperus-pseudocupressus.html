Juniperus pseudocupressus

Juniperus pseudocupressus, commonly known as the Chinese juniper, is a coniferous evergreen shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Cupressaceae and is characterized by its scale-like leaves arranged in dense, flat sprays. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Juniperus pseudocupressus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticulture for its hardiness and adaptability.
