Justicia vernalis

Justicia vernalis, commonly known as the spring justicia, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Justicia vernalis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to bloom early in the growing season.
