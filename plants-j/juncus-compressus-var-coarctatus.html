Juncus compressus var. coarctatus

Juncus compressus var. coarctatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as along the edges of ponds, ditches, and wet meadows. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are densely packed, giving it a dense, bushy appearance. Its flowering stems produce small, greenish-yellow spikelets that are clustered at the tips.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
