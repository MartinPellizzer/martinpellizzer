Juniperus communis var. reflexa

Juniperus communis var. reflexa, commonly known as creeping juniper, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features dense, needle-like leaves that are often curved or twisted, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.

This variety is particularly adapted to harsh climates and can form extensive mats in rocky or sandy environments.
