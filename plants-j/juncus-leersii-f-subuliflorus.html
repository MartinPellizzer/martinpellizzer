Juncus leersii f. subuliflorus

Juncus leersii f. subuliflorus, commonly known as the slender-flowered rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spike-like clusters. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is often found in nutrient-poor soils.

The subspecies subuliflorus is distinguished by its elongated, needle-like flower bracts, which contribute to its unique botanical appearance.
