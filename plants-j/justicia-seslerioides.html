Justicia seslerioides

Justicia seslerioides, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warm climates.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
