Justicia eudoxa

Justicia eudoxa, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in clusters at the ends of branches. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Justicia eudoxa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
