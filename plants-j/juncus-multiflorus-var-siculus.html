Juncus multiflorus var. siculus

Juncus multiflorus var. siculus, commonly known as the Sicilian rush, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often used in basket weaving. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that bloom in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found near water sources.

Its fibrous stems make it a valuable resource for traditional crafts and ecological restoration projects.
