Jacea corcubionensis

Jacea corcubionensis, commonly known as the Corcubion wildflower, is a rare and endemic plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, clustered leaves and delicate, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, and it plays a vital role in the local ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, Jacea corcubionensis is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
