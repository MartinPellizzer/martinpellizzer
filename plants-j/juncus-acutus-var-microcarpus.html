Juncus acutus var. microcarpus

Juncus acutus var. microcarpus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in coastal areas and freshwater habitats.

Its compact growth habit and adaptability make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
