Juncus satakei

Juncus satakei, commonly known as the Satake rush, is a species of flowering plant in the family Juncaceae.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spike-like clusters. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands and stream banks.

Juncus satakei is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic habitats.
