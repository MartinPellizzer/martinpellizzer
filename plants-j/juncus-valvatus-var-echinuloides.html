Juncus valvatus var. echinuloides

Juncus valvatus var. echinuloides, commonly known as the spiny bulrush, is a variant of the bulrush species native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often forming dense stands in shallow water or moist soil. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically reddish-brown in color, which contribute to its ornamental value. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

This plant plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
