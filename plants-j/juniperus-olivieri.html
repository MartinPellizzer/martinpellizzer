Juniperus olivieri

Juniperus olivieri, commonly known as the olive juniper, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, scaly foliage and small, berry-like cones that mature to a bluish-black color. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are needle-like and retain their color year-round, contributing to its ornamental value.

Juniperus olivieri plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
