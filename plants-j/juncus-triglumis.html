Juncus triglumis

Juncus triglumis, commonly known as the slender rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish-brown in color and are covered with small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers in clusters near the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in areas with standing water or saturated soil.

Juncus triglumis plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
