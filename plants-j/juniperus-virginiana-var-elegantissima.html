Juniperus virginiana var. elegantissima

Juniperus virginiana var. elegantissima, commonly known as the Eastern Redcedar, is a deciduous conifer native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its scale-like leaves that turn a vibrant red in the fall, and its small, berry-like cones that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for birds. The plant typically grows as a tall, narrow tree or shrub, often reaching heights of 20 to 40 feet. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Its aromatic foliage and unique appearance make it a notable species in both ecological and ornamental contexts.
