Juncus heptopotamicus var. yiningensis

Juncus heptopotamicus var. yiningensis, commonly known as the Yining rush, is a perennial plant native to specific regions of China.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and cylindrical, often covered with a fine layer of hair, which helps reduce water loss.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
