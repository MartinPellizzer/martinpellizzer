Jamesonia flexuosa

Jamesonia flexuosa, commonly known as the zigzag gentian, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features a zigzagging stem that gives it its common name, and its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Botanically, it is part of the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, tubular flowers.
