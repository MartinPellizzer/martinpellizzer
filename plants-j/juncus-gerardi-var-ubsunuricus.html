Juncus gerardi var. ubsunuricus

Juncus gerardi var. ubsunuricus, commonly known as the Ussuri rush, is a perennial plant native to the eastern regions of Russia and parts of China.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered with fine, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its flowering spikes produce small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
