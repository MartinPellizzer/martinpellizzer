Jasminum odorum

Jasminum odorum, commonly known as the Arabian jasmine, is a flowering plant belonging to the Oleaceae family.

It is native to regions of the Middle East and South Asia, where it thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant produces fragrant white flowers that bloom at night, releasing a sweet, intoxicating aroma. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a glossy green surface.

Jasminum odorum is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
