Juncus torreyi f. longipes

Juncus torreyi f. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed beak rush, is a flowering plant in the Juncaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Juncus torreyi f. longipes is often found in the western United States, particularly in California and Nevada.
