Juncus alpinus var. uniceps

Juncus alpinus var. uniceps, commonly known as the alpine rush, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a perennial herb with slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets of brown flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil in harsh environments, making it an important component of alpine ecosystems.

The variety uniceps is distinguished by its unique floral structure, which sets it apart from other juncus species.
