Jussiaea africana

Jussiaea africana, commonly known as the African waterleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands, where it thrives in high humidity and well-drained soils. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white and appear in clusters, while its fruits are capsules that release tiny seeds.

Jussiaea africana is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
