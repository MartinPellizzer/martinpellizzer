Juniperus indica var. caespitosa

Juniperus indica var. caespitosa, commonly known as the shrub juniper, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Himalayas.

It typically grows in rocky or mountainous terrain, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant features small, scale-like leaves that are often blue-green in color, and it produces blue or purple berries that are edible for birds. It is valued for its hardiness and ornamental appeal in gardens.

This variety is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
