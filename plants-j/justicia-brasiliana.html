Justicia brasiliana

Justicia brasiliana, commonly known as the Brazilian flame flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular, bright red or orange flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and branching.

Justicia brasiliana thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
