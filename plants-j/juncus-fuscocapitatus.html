Juncus fuscocapitatus

Juncus fuscocapitatus, commonly known as the dark-capped rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like stems that are often clustered together, and its leaves are narrow and cylindrical. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually arranged in dense, spike-like clusters at the ends of the stems.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
