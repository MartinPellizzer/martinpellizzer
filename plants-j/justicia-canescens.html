Justicia canescens

Justicia canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf flameplant, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and striking clusters of tubular, red or orange flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, silky hairs that give it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers bloom in summer and are pollinated primarily by birds and insects.
