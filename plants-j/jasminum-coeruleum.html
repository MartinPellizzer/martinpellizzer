Jasminum coeruleum

Jasminum coeruleum, commonly known as the blue jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring small, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The flowers are usually borne in clusters and bloom in spring, emitting a sweet, intoxicating scent. The plant is evergreen, with a compact growth habit and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for coastal gardens.
