Justicia sagrana

Justicia sagrana, commonly known as the sacred justicia, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in spikes.

Justicia sagrana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by various indigenous cultures.
