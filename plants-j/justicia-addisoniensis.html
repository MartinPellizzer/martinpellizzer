Justicia addisoniensis

Justicia addisoniensis, commonly known as the Addison's false heather, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of violet or blue-purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Justicia addisoniensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
