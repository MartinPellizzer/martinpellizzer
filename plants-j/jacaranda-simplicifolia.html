Jacaranda simplicifolia

Jacaranda simplicifolia, commonly known as the blue jacaranda, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive clusters of pendant, violet-blue flowers that appear in the spring. The tree has compound leaves composed of several narrow, lance-shaped leaflets. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well adapted to warm, sunny climates.
