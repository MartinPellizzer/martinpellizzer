Justicia polita

Justicia polita, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf false arum, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces striking clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Justicia polita is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
