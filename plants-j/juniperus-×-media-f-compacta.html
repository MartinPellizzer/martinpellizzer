Juniperus × media f. compacta

Juniperus × media f. compacta, commonly known as the dwarf juniper, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features dense, needle-like foliage that is typically a deep green, with small, blue-gray berries that appear in winter. The plant is highly adaptable, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its slow growth rate makes it ideal for rock gardens, borders, or as a foundation plant in landscaping.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resistance to pests and diseases.
