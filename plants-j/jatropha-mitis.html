Jatropha mitis

Jatropha mitis, commonly known as the whiteleaf jatropha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing toxic seeds. Jatropha mitis is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and potential applications in sustainable agriculture.
