Joseanthus

Joseanthus, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a genus of flowering plants native to the Andes Mountains.

These plants are characterized by their showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including blue, purple, and white. Joseanthus species typically grow in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. They have hairy leaves and stems, which help protect them from harsh weather.

The genus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens in temperate regions.
