Juncus castaneus var. pallidus

Juncus castaneus var. pallidus, commonly known as the pale rush, is a perennial plant native to the wetlands of North America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically pale green to yellowish in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale brown to reddish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in marshes, pond edges, and along streams.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
