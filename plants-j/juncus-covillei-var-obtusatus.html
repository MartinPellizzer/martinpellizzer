Juncus covillei var. obtusatus

Juncus covillei var. obtusatus, commonly known as the smooth rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like foliage that are typically smooth and hairless. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
