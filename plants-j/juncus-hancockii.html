Juncus hancockii

Juncus hancockii, commonly known as Hancock's rush, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Hancock's rush produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or brown in color.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
