Juncus tyraicus

Juncus tyraicus, commonly known as the tall rush, is a perennial flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often forming dense stands in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of greenish-white flowers in late summer, which are pollinated by wind. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in wetland habitats.

Juncus tyraicus is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
