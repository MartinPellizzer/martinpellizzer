Justicia clarkii

Justicia clarkii, commonly known as Clark's false heather, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically features small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bright blue or purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
