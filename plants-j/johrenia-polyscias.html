Johrenia polyscias

Johrenia polyscias, commonly known as the African nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are borne on short pedicels. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savanna habitats.
