Jussiaea pubescens

Jussiaea pubescens, commonly known as the hairy jussiaea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland and marshy environments. The plant features opposite, hairy leaves that are often elliptical in shape. Its small, white flowers are clustered in spikes and bloom during the spring and summer months.

Jussiaea pubescens is known for its ability to thrive in moist, nutrient-poor soils.
