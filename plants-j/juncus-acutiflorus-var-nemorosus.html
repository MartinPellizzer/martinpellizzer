Juncus acutiflorus var. nemorosus

Juncus acutiflorus var. nemorosus, commonly known as the forest rush, is a perennial plant native to moist, shaded environments in North America.

It typically grows in dense clusters, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with slender, grass-like leaves that are dark green and often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers in early summer, which are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland floors, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form thick mats, making it an important component of riparian and wetland ecosystems.
