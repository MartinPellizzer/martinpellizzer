Juncus karelinii

Juncus karelinii, commonly known as the Karelin's rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color, appearing in late spring to early summer. Juncus karelinii thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows.

Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil, making it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
