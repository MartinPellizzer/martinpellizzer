Juniperus rigida

Juniperus rigida, commonly known as rigid juniper, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves and small, berry-like cones that are typically blue or black in color. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. Juniperus rigida is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in landscaping for its ornamental appeal.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystems it inhabits.
