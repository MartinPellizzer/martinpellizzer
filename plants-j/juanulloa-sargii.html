Juanulloa sargii

Juanulloa sargii, commonly known as the red-leafed evergreen, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and striking red to purple leaves that provide visual interest throughout the year. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm, humid climates.
