Jacaranda mutabilis f. subcoaetanea

Jacaranda mutabilis f. subcoaetanea, commonly known as the silver jacaranda, is a striking ornamental tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, cascading clusters of violet-blue flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, ovate leaflets. The tree has a rapid growth rate and can reach heights of up to 20 meters, making it a popular choice for urban landscapes.

Its unique foliage and vibrant blooms contribute to its aesthetic appeal in gardens and public spaces.
