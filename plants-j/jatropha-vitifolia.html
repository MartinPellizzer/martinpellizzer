Jatropha vitifolia

Jatropha vitifolia, commonly known as the grapeleaf jatropha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that resemble grape leaves, with elongated, serrated leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing toxic seeds. Jatropha vitifolia is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
