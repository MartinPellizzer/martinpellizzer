Jasminum officinale var. aureovariegatum

Jasminum officinale var. aureovariegatum, commonly known as the golden variegated jasmine, is a hybrid evergreen shrub native to southern China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves with striking golden-yellow variegation, making it an ornamental favorite. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, requiring regular watering and moderate pruning for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and pleasant aroma.
