Juglans regia gibbosa

Juglans regia gibbosa, commonly known as the Persian walnut, is a variant of the Persian walnut tree native to the mountains of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and produces edible, oil-rich nuts encased in a hard shell. The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its fruit is widely cultivated for its nutritional value and use in culinary and industrial applications.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and timber.
