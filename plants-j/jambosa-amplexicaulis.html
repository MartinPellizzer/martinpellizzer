Jambosa amplexicaulis

Jambosa amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clinging false arum, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique foliage, with heart-shaped leaves that wrap around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
