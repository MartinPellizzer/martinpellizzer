Juncus gerardi var. pedicellatus

Juncus gerardi var. pedicellatus, commonly known as the slender rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that are typically green or reddish in color, growing in dense clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, which are often borne on long, slender pedicels. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This species is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
