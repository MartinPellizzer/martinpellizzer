Justicia diclipteroides subsp. praetervisa

Justicia diclipteroides subsp.

praetervisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf false arum, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple or red at the base.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that are arranged in dense, showy inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for tropical gardens and greenhouses.
