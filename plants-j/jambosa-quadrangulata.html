Jambosa quadrangulata

Jambosa quadrangulata, commonly known as the four-angled jambosa, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its quadrangular stems, which give it its scientific name, and its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by edible, fleshy fruits resembling small apples. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate.

Jambosa quadrangulata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
