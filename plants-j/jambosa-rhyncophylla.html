Jambosa rhyncophylla

Jambosa rhyncophylla, commonly known as the Brazilian cheese nut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elongated, elliptical leaves. It is valued for its edible seeds, which have a nutty flavor and are used in local cuisines.
