Justicia balansae

Justicia balansae, commonly known as the red bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that resemble bottle brushes. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Justicia balansae thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.
