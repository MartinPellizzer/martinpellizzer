Jasminum singuliflorum

Jasminum singuliflorum, commonly known as the single-flower jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, single flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually elliptic leaves. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its small, tubular flowers are typically visited by insects, aiding in pollination.
