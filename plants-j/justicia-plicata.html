Justicia plicata

Justicia plicata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false arum, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

Justicia plicata is often found in wetlands and disturbed soils, and it is known for its ability to thrive in moist environments.
