Juncus acutissimus

Juncus acutissimus, commonly known as the sharp-tailed rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves that are typically bright green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils, often in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds.

Juncus acutissimus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
