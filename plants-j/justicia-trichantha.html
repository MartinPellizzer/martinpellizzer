Justicia trichantha

Justicia trichantha, commonly known as the spiked flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an herb or shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its inflorescence consists of clusters of showy bracts that enclose the small, true flowers.

Justicia trichantha thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
