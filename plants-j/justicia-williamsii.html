Justicia williamsii

Justicia williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Justicia williamsii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a container plant.
