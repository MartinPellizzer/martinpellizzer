Jacksonia capitata

Jacksonia capitata, commonly known as the New Zealand dandelion, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It is often found in coastal areas and on disturbed soils, showing a preference for well-drained conditions.

Jacksonia capitata is also known for its ability to thrive in harsh environments, making it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
