Jurinea amplifolia var. silvatica

Jurinea amplifolia var. silvatica, commonly known as the silverleaf sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-adapted to harsh, dry environments.
