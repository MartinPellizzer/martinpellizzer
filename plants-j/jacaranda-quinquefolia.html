Jacaranda quinquefolia

Jacaranda quinquefolia, commonly known as the Mexican jacaranda, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The tree has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, giving it its scientific name. It produces long, pendulous clusters of flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for furniture and construction.
