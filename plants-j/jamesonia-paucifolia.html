Jamesonia paucifolia

Jamesonia paucifolia, commonly known as the rock laurel, is a perennial evergreen shrub native to the Appalachian Mountains of the United States.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a leathery texture and emits a fragrant, spicy scent when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits that attract wildlife. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded woodland environments.

Jamesonia paucifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
