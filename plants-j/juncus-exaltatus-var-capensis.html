Juncus exaltatus var. capensis

Juncus exaltatus var. capensis, commonly known as the Cape rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, slender, grass-like stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often forming dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and cylindrical, typically with a slight sheen, and are adapted to thrive in wet or moist environments.

It is often found in marshes, stream banks, and other waterlogged habitats, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
