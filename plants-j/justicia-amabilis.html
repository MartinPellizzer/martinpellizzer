Justicia amabilis

Justicia amabilis, commonly known as flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet, with lance-shaped, dark green leaves. Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant flowers and ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.

It prefers well-drained soil and is often used in landscaping for its visual appeal and ecological benefits.
