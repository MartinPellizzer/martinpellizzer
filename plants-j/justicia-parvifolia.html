Justicia parvifolia

Justicia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of tubular red or orange flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including hummingbirds and bees, contributing to its ecological value.
