Jatropha mollissima var. glabra

Jatropha mollissima var. glabra, commonly known as the velvetleaf jatropha, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, often waxy leaves that are typically dark green and heart-shaped. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers followed by orange to red berries containing toxic seeds. It is adapted to thrive in disturbed soils and can be found in savannas and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for medicinal use, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
