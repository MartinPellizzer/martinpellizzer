Jacquemontia crassifolia

Jacquemontia crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped blue to purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun. Its stems are often hairy and can climb or trail along the ground.

Jacquemontia crassifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
