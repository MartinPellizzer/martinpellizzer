Juncus salinus

Juncus salinus, commonly known as saltmarsh rush, is a perennial plant native to coastal regions.

It thrives in saline and brackish wetlands, often found in marshes and estuaries. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

Juncus salinus plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
