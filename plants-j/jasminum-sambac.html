Jasminum sambac

Jasminum sambac, commonly known as Arabian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical and southern regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is a deciduous shrub with opposite, simple, and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are highly aromatic and are often used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

Jasminum sambac thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
