Juncus striatus var. macrocephalus

Juncus striatus var. macrocephalus, commonly known as the broad-headed rush, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that often exceed two meters in height. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers at the top of the stem, which are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds, and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

Its fibrous stems are also sometimes harvested for basket-making or as a natural erosion control measure.
