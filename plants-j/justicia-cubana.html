Justicia cubana

Justicia cubana, commonly known as Cuban flame flower, is a striking plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It is a herbaceous perennial that features showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Justicia cubana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
