Jollydora glandulosa

Jollydora glandulosa, commonly known as the sticky leaf orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves that often secrete a sticky substance, which may serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming during the wet season. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, allowing it to thrive in shaded, humid environments.

Jollydora glandulosa is also noted for its ability to form symbiotic relationships with certain fungi, enhancing its nutrient absorption capabilities.
