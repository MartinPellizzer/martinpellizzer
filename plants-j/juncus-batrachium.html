Juncus batrachium

Juncus batrachium, commonly known as the frog's eye sedge, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its scientific name reflects its distinctive, frog-eye-like appearance of the flower heads.

Juncus batrachium plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
