Jacquemontia pannosa

Jacquemontia pannosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its scientific name reflects its historical association with the botanist Henri Jacquemont.

The species is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
