Juncus viridifolius

Juncus viridifolius, commonly known as the greenleaf rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually green to bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of dense spikes of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Juncus viridifolius plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
