Jacaranda sparrei

Jacaranda sparrei, commonly known as the slender jacaranda, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched structure and its showy, violet-blue flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. The leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain flat, winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.
