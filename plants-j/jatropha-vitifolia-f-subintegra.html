Jatropha vitifolia f. subintegra

Jatropha vitifolia f. subintegra, commonly known as the smooth-leafed jatropha, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features compound leaves with elongated, smooth-edged leaflets that give it its distinguishing appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange-red, fleshy berries. Its growth habit is typically upright, with woody stems that can reach several feet in height.

This species is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its potential use in ornamental horticulture.
