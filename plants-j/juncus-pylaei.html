Juncus pylaei

Juncus pylaei, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, pond margins, and stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 1 mm wide, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, greenish-yellow flower clusters in late summer, which are arranged in spike-like structures.

Juncus pylaei plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
