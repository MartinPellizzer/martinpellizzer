Juncus sphaerocarpus

Juncus sphaerocarpus, commonly known as the globe sedge, is a perennial plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces distinctive spherical seed heads that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and pond edges. Its flowering occurs in late summer, with small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters.

Juncus sphaerocarpus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
