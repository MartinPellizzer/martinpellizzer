Jasminum subtriplinerve var. parvifolium

Jasminum subtriplinerve var. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved garden jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a sweet aroma. The plant has slender, woody stems and elliptical leaves with a smooth texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and pleasant fragrance.
