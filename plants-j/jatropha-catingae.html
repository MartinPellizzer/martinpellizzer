Jatropha catingae

Jatropha catingae, commonly known as the Brazilian physic nut, is a shrub native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that develop into capsules containing toxic, orange seeds. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as savannas and disturbed soils.

Due to its toxicity, it is considered a weed in agricultural areas.
