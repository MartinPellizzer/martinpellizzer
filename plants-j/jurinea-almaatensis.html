Jurinea almaatensis

Jurinea almaatensis, commonly known as the Almaty sandwort, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia, specifically found in the arid regions of Kazakhstan.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that thrives in sandy and rocky soils, often in dry, open habitats. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically linear and covered in a fine layer of hair, helping to reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Jurinea almaatensis is adapted to extreme conditions, showcasing resilience typical of desert flora.
