Justicia lamprophylla

Justicia lamprophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage and clusters of tubular, often red or purple, flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial or shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
