Justicia serrata

Justicia serrata, commonly known as the serrated justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its narrow, serrated leaves and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial or shrub, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The flowers attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees, making it an important species in ecological gardens.
