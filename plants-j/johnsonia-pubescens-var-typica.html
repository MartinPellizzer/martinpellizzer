Johnsonia pubescens var. typica

Johnsonia pubescens var. typica, commonly known as the hairy Johnsonia, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, almost fuzzy appearance. The plant produces strikingly large, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly sought after by botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
