Jasminum cardiomorphum

Jasminum cardiomorphum, commonly known as the heart-shaped jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a smooth texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aromatic blooms.
