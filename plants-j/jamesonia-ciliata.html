Jamesonia ciliata

Jamesonia ciliata, commonly known as the silkyleaf bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a soft, fuzzy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique and ornamental addition to native plant gardens.
