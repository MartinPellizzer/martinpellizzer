Jambolifera oblongifolia

Jambolifera oblongifolia, commonly known as the long-leaf jambolai, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to red berries containing several seeds. Its woody structure and dense foliage make it suitable for use in ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
