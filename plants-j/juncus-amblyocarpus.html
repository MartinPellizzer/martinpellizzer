Juncus amblyocarpus

Juncus amblyocarpus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has slender, grass-like stems that are often clustered together, and its leaves are narrow and slightly curved. Its flowering spikes appear in late spring to early summer, bearing small, greenish flowers.

Juncus amblyocarpus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
