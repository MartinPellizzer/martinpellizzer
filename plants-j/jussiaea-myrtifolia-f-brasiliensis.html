Jussiaea myrtifolia f. brasiliensis

Jussiaea myrtifolia f. brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and glossy, elliptical leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in riparian forests.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
