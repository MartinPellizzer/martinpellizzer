Justicia ligulata

Justicia ligulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Justicia ligulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
