Juncus scirpoides brachystylus

Juncus scirpoides brachystylus, commonly known as the short-stemmed rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in dense clumps, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This species plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
