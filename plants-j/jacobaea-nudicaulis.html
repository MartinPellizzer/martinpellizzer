Jacobaea nudicaulis

Jacobaea nudicaulis, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its slender stems and small, yellowish flowers make it a distinctive yet often overlooked member of the flora in high-altitude environments.

Jacobaea nudicaulis is also noted for its ability to thrive in harsh conditions, showcasing adaptability to cold and arid climates.
