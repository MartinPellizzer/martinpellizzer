Juncoides caricina

Juncoides caricina, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a member of the Juncaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact, cylindrical flower clusters. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and stream banks. Its small, greenish flowers are usually arranged in dense spikes, and it produces small, three-lobed seeds.

Juncoides caricina plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
