Jungia pubescens var. floribunda

Jungia pubescens var. floribunda, commonly known as the showy jungle cactus, is a species native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a pubescent appearance. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to red, often blooming in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
