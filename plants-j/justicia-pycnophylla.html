Justicia pycnophylla

Justicia pycnophylla, commonly known as the dense-leaved false arum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often colorful bracts that resemble flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are densely packed, giving it its common name. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in spikes, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
