Josepha

Josepha, commonly known as the Josephine's lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the genus Alstroemeria, which is part of the family Alstroemeriaceae. The plant is characterized by its showy, colorful flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

Josepha thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
