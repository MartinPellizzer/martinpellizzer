Jasione humilis var. campestris

Jasione humilis var. campestris, commonly known as the field jasmine, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features slender, spreading stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas.
