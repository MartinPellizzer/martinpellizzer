Justicia lepida

Justicia lepida, commonly known as the common justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of bright blue or violet tubular flowers, which are borne on erect spikes and attract various pollinators. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitats.
