Juncus dongchuanensis

Juncus dongchuanensis, commonly known as the Dongchuan rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. It is often found in alpine meadows and forest margins, thriving in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
