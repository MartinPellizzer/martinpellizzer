Juncoides plumosa

Juncoides plumosa, commonly known as the feather grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Juncaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and feathery, plume-like inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing both male and female parts, making it monoecious.

Juncoides plumosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
