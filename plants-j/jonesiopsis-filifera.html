Jonesiopsis filifera

Jonesiopsis filifera, commonly known as the threadleaf fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, feathery appearance. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments, where it can absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. Its rhizomes are wiry and help anchor it to its host, while its fronds are usually arranged in a compact, rosette-like structure.

Jonesiopsis filifera is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in controlled indoor environments.
