Justicia malacophylla

Justicia malacophylla, commonly known as the velvetleaf false arum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features striking, broad, velvety leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in moist woodlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for tropical gardens and conservation efforts.
