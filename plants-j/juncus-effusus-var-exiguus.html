Juncus effusus var. exiguus

Juncus effusus var. exiguus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments across North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 1 millimeter wide. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense spikes during the summer months. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, bogs, and along stream banks.

Due to its adaptability, it plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
