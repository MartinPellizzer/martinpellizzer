Julocroton triqueter var. ackermannii

Julocroton triqueter var. ackermannii, commonly known as the Ackermann's croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves with three to five lobes, which are often brightly colored in shades of green, red, and yellow. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
