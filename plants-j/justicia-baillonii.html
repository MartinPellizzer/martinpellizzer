Justicia baillonii

Justicia baillonii, commonly known as the red-flowered false arum, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red bracts that surround small, inconspicuous flowers, creating an eye-catching display. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, warm climates. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Justicia baillonii is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses due to its bold visual impact.
