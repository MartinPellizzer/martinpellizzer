Jambosa latifolia

Jambosa latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf jambosa, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are similar in appearance to cherries, with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

Jambosa latifolia is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
