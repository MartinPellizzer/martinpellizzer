Juncus marginatus var. paucicapitatus

Juncus marginatus var. paucicapitatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 1 mm wide, and it produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters. It is characterized by its reduced number of inflorescences, which is reflected in its scientific name "paucicapitatus.".

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
