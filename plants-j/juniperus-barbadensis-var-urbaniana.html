Juniperus barbadensis var. urbaniana

Juniperus barbadensis var. urbaniana, commonly known as the Mediterranean juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, providing a striking visual contrast. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
