Jarava braun-blanquetii

Jarava braun-blanquetii, commonly known as Braun-Blanquet's jarava, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial grass that typically thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-gray hue, which helps reduce water loss in dry conditions.

This species is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
