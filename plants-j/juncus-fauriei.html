Juncus fauriei

Juncus fauriei, commonly known as the Faurie's rush, is a perennial plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage that forms compact clumps. The plant typically grows in moist or wet environments, such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its cylindrical, spike-like inflorescence bears small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

Juncus fauriei is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
