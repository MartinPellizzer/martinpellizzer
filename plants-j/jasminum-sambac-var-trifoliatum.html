Jasminum sambac var. trifoliatum

Jasminum sambac var. trifoliatum, commonly known as the trifoliate jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often releasing a sweet, intoxicating scent. The plant features trifoliate leaves, meaning each leaf is divided into three leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and in perfumery.

Its botanical structure supports both its aesthetic value and its use in traditional medicine.
