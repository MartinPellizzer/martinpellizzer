Justicia quadrigona

Justicia quadrigona, commonly known as the four-square jewelweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and clusters of small, orange-red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a slightly waxy texture.
