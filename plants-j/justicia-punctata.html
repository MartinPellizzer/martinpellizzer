Justicia punctata

Justicia punctata, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are typically bright red or orange, with two prominent lips that attract pollinators.

Justicia punctata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
