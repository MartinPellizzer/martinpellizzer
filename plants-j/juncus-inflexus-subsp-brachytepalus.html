Juncus inflexus subsp. brachytepalus

Juncus inflexus subsp.

brachytepalus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rush, is a perennial plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as marshes, wet meadows, and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, three-lobed flowers.

This subspecies is adapted to wet environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife.
