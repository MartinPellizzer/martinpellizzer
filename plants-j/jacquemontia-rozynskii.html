Jacquemontia rozynskii

Jacquemontia rozynskii, commonly known as the roseleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is a vine that typically grows along the ground or climbing supports, with hairy stems and foliage. It blooms in the spring and early summer, producing edible seeds that are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
