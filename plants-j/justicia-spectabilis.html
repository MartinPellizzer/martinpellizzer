Justicia spectabilis

Justicia spectabilis, commonly known as flame flower, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often arranged in clusters at the ends of its branches. The plant is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Justicia spectabilis is frequently cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and as a cut flower due to its vibrant blooms.
