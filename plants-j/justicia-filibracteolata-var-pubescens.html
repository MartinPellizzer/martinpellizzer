Justicia filibracteolata var. pubescens

Justicia filibracteolata var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy flame bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The leaves are typically opposite, simple, and covered with fine, soft hairs, giving the plant its "hairy" appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

The plant is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display and drought tolerance.
