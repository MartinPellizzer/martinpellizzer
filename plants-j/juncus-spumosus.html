Juncus spumosus

Juncus spumosus, commonly known as the foamy rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland and marsh environments in North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and distinctive, fluffy seed heads that give it its common name. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in swamps, bogs, and along the edges of ponds. Its slender, cylindrical stems are typically green or reddish in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring.

Juncus spumosus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
