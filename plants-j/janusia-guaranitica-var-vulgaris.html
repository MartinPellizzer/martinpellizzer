Janusia guaranitica var. vulgaris

Janusia guaranitica var. vulgaris, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It features large, showy orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames, which attract pollinators like bees and birds. The plant has compound leaves with opposite arrangement and possesses a woody, fibrous stem that can grow up to several meters in height. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in traditional crafts and construction.
