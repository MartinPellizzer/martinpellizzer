Juncoides spadicea var. effusa

Juncoides spadicea var. effusa, commonly known as the broadleaf rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, greenish-white flowers in cylindrical spikes. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, preferring moist, nutrient-poor soils. It is often found in temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

Its morphology and ecological preferences make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
