Jasminum sootepense var. tremulifolium

Jasminum sootepense var. tremulifolium, commonly known as the trembling-leaf jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are elliptical and have a distinctive trembling motion when touched, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated for both garden use and aromatic purposes.
