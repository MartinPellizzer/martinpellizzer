Jatropha integerrima var. coccinea

Jatropha integerrima var. coccinea, commonly known as the red-veined jatropha, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that display vibrant red veins against a deep green background. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that transition into bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value rather than for edibility.
