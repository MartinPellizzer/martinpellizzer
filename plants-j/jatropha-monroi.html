Jatropha monroi

Jatropha monroi, commonly known as the dwarf physic nut, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by capsule fruits containing toxic seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture.

Jatropha monroi is noted for its ornamental value and has been studied for potential uses in biofuel production.
