Jacksonia tenuifolia

Jacksonia tenuifolia, commonly known as the New Zealand flax or silver fern, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive silver-green leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is typically found in coastal and montane regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are often used in traditional Māori weaving, and the flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Jacksonia tenuifolia is an important species in New Zealand's flora and holds cultural significance for the indigenous Māori people.
