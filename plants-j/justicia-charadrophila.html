Justicia charadrophila

Justicia charadrophila, commonly known as the flame-of-the-forest, is a flowering plant native to parts of India and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of bright red, orange, or yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.
