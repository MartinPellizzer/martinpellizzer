Juncus fontanesii var. pyramidatus

Juncus fontanesii var. pyramidatus, commonly known as the pyramidal rush, is a perennial plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that form a cylindrical shape. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers in early summer. It thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
