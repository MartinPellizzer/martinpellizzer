Jacquemontia nummularia

Jacquemontia nummularia, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant features heart-shaped, silver-velvet leaves that give it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
