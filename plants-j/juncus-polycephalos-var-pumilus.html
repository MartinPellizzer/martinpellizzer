Juncus polycephalos var. pumilus

Juncus polycephalos var. pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf rush, is a low-growing perennial plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It features dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a compact, mat-like growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in shallow water or along the edges of ponds and streams.

This variety is characterized by its compact size and adaptability to various aquatic habitats.
