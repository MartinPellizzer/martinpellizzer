Juncus nodatus

Juncus nodatus, commonly known as the nodding rush, is a perennial plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of grass-like leaves and its distinctive, nodding flower heads. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, pond edges, and stream banks. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late spring to early summer, consist of tiny, clustered flowers that are often greenish or brownish in color.

Juncus nodatus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
