Jambosa lanceolata

Jambosa lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf jambosa, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the spring. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

Jambosa lanceolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual interest and adaptability to various soil types.
