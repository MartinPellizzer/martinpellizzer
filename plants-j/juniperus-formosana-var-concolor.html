Juniperus formosana var. concolor

Juniperus formosana var. concolor, commonly known as the Formosan juniper, is a subspecies of the juniper tree native to Taiwan.

It is a slow-growing evergreen shrub or small tree with dense, needle-like foliage that is typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with a temperate climate.

The botanical features of this plant make it a valuable component of its native ecosystem.
