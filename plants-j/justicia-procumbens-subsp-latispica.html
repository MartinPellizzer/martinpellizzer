Justicia procumbens subsp. latispica

Justicia procumbens subsp.

latispica, commonly known as the broad-spined jewelweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. It is a herbaceous perennial with prostrate growth habit, often forming dense mats on the ground.

The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with wavy margins and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow. Its flowers are typically borne on short pedicels, and the plant is known for its showy, ornamental appearance.

The subspecies is distinguished by its broader, more robust spines compared to other Justicia species.
