Jatropha glandulosa var. sublobata

Jatropha glandulosa var. sublobata, commonly known as the glandular jatropha, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that are often lobed or serrated. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that develop into toxic, orange-red berries containing seeds. Its growth habit includes woody stems and a tendency to form dense thickets.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in semi-arid conditions.
