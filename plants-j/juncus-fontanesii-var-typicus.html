Juncus fontanesii var. typicus

Juncus fontanesii var. typicus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often slightly zigzagged, and its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and tapering to a point. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers in dense, spike-like clusters during the summer months.

This species is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
