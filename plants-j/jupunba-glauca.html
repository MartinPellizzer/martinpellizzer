Jupunba glauca

Jupunba glauca, commonly known as the blue poplar, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and showy yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine by local communities.

Jupunba glauca is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
