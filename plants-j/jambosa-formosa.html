Jambosa formosa

Jambosa formosa, commonly known as the beauty apple, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, lantern-shaped fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by the distinctive, fleshy, orange-colored fruits. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a waxy texture.
