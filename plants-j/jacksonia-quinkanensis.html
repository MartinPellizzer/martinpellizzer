Jacksonia quinkanensis

Jacksonia quinkanensis, commonly known as the Quinkan's knobby burr, is a flowering plant native to northern Queensland, Australia.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, spherical seed heads that resemble small, knobby balls. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a striking visual element to its habitat. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
