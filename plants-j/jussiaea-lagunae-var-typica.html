Jussiaea lagunae var. typica

Jussiaea lagunae var. typica, commonly known as the marsh willow or water willow, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in South America.

It is characterized by its slender, herbaceous stems that often grow in dense clusters along the edges of marshes and ponds. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and flies. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils and low light conditions.

Jussiaea lagunae var. typica plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland habitats and providing food for aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
