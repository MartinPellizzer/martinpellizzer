Julocroton montevidensis var. genuinus

Julocroton montevidensis var. genuinus, commonly known as the Montevidense Croton, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Uruguay and southern Brazil.

It features showy, brightly colored leaves with intricate patterns of green, red, and yellow, which are characteristic of the Croton genus. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens with suitable conditions.
