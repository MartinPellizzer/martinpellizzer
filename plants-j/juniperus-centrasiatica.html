Juniperus centrasiatica

Juniperus centrasiatica, commonly known as the Central Asian juniper, is a coniferous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its scale-like leaves and small, berry-like cones that are typically blue-black in color. The plant is adapted to harsh, arid environments and can thrive in rocky soils. It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.

Juniperus centrasiatica is also valued for its aromatic wood and has been used in traditional medicine.
