Justicia matthewsii

Justicia matthewsii, commonly known as the Matthews' glorybower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by birds, particularly sunbirds, which are attracted to its nectar-rich blooms.
