Jacquemontia curtissii

Jacquemontia curtissii, commonly known as the Texas morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in dry, rocky soils. It blooms in the spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Jacquemontia curtissii is adapted to arid environments and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
