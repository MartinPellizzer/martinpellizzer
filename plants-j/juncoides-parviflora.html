Juncoides parviflora

Juncoides parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, cylindrical leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant produces seeds in small, capsule-like structures, which are dispersed by wind.

Juncoides parviflora plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and small invertebrates.
