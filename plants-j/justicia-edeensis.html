Justicia edeensis

Justicia edeensis, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows as a shrub or perennial herb, often with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually clustered in spikes and come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and purple.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant.
