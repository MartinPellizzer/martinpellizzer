Juncus tenageia var. rhiphaenus

Juncus tenageia var. rhiphaenus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are usually green or reddish in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense clusters at the base of the plant.

This variety is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
