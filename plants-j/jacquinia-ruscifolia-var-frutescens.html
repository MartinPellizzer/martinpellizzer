Jacquinia ruscifolia var. frutescens

Jacquinia ruscifolia var. frutescens, commonly known as the dwarf false hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, organic soils. The plant features upright, branched stems covered with narrow, waxy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters during late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in naturalistic garden settings.
