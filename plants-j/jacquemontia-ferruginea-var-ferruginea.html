Jacquemontia ferruginea var. ferruginea

Jacquemontia ferruginea var. ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty jacquemontia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features hairy, ascending stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with orange markings, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in native plant gardens.
