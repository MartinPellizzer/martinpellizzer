Josemania truncata

Josemania truncata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed josemonia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warm climates.

Josemania truncata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
