Juncus filiformis f. pusillus

Juncus filiformis f. pusillus, commonly known as the dwarf rush, is a compact, low-growing plant native to moist, temperate regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wet or waterlogged soils, often found in marshes, stream banks, and pond edges.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water gardens and bog landscapes.
