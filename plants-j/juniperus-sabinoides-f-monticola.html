Juniperus sabinoides f. monticola

Juniperus sabinoides f. monticola, commonly known as the mountain juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the high elevations of the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage and small, berry-like cones that are typically blue or black in color. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its resilience and is an important component of mountain ecosystems.
