Jamesonia warscewiczii

Jamesonia warscewiczii, commonly known as the eastern bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and role in local ecosystems.
