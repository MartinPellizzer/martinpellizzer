Jatropha intermedia

Jatropha intermedia, commonly known as the Mexican physic nut, is a shrub or small tree native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing toxic seeds. It is often found in dry, disturbed soils and can thrive in arid environments.
