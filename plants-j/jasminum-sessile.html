Jasminum sessile

Jasminum sessile, commonly known as the sessile jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, with opposite, simple leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. Its flowers emit a sweet scent, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Jasminum sessile is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
