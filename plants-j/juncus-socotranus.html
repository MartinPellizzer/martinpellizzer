Juncus socotranus

Juncus socotranus, commonly known as Socotra rush, is a unique plant native to the island of Socotra.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often used as a construction material by local communities. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to withstand drought conditions. Its flowering spikes, which appear at the top of the stems, produce small, greenish flowers that are pollinated by wind.

This species is also notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
