Jambosa occidentalis

Jambosa occidentalis, commonly known as the western jambosa, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically eaten raw or used in culinary applications. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
