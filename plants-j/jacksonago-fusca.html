Jacksonago fusca

Jacksonago fusca, commonly known as the dark Jacksonia, is a member of the Campanulaceae family.

It is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Brazil. The plant features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture.
