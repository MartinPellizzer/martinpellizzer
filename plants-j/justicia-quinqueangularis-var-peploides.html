Justicia quinqueangularis var. peploides

Justicia quinqueangularis var. peploides, commonly known as velvetleaf justicia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, five-angled stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, attracting pollinators like bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its adaptability and vibrant blooms make it a valuable addition to both garden landscapes and ecological habitats.
