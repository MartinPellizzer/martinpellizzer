Juglans honorei

Juglans honorei, commonly known as the Honore hickory, is a species of hickory native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and a broad, rounded crown. The leaves are alternate, simple, and pinnately compound, with 5 to 9 leaflets that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are dioecious, with separate male and female trees, and it produces edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk.

The wood of Juglans honorei is highly valued for its strength and workability in woodworking.
