Juniperus scopulorum

Juniperus scopulorum, commonly known as the rock juniper, is a species of evergreen conifer native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves and small, berry-like cones that are typically blue or purple in color. This plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has a slow growth rate and can live for several decades, making it a long-lived shrub or small tree.

The rock juniper is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping due to its hardiness and adaptability.
