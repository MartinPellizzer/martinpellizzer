Jasminum nudiflorum var. aureum

Jasminum nudiflorum var. aureum, commonly known as the golden jessamine, is a variant of the common jessamine characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers.

It is a deciduous shrub native to China and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens. The plant produces small, fragrant blossoms that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. Its foliage is typically dark green and glossy, providing a lush backdrop for the vibrant flowers.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
