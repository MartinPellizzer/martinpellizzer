Justicia grandifolia

Justicia grandifolia, commonly known as the showy justicia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Justicia grandifolia thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its striking floral display.
