Justicia yuyoensis

Justicia yuyoensis, commonly known as Yuyo flameflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Peru and Bolivia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular, brightly colored flowers that range from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture, while the flowers are arranged in dense, terminal clusters.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
