Justicia lucindae

Justicia lucindae, commonly known as the Lucinda's glorybower, is a striking flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are usually red or orange, with yellow stamens, and it blooms throughout the year in suitable conditions.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
