Jacquemontia hirsuta

Jacquemontia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy false morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Jacquemontia hirsuta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
