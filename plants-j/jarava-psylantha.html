Jarava psylantha

Jarava psylantha, commonly known as the slender jarava, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Jarava psylantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
