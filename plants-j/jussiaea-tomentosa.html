Jussiaea tomentosa

Jussiaea tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly willow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and slender, branching stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats.

Jussiaea tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
