Jasione montana subsp. blepharodon

Jasione montana subsp.

blepharodon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrower leaves and more elongated corolla compared to other Jasione species. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in open habitats such as hillsides and meadows.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
