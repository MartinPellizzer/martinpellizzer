Japarandiba fastuosa

Japarandiba fastuosa, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has a slender, multi-branched trunk and thrives in full sunlight with well-drained soil. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Japarandiba fastuosa is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various climates.
