Jasminum foveatum

Jasminum foveatum, commonly known as the mountain jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a smooth texture with a slightly glossy appearance.

Jasminum foveatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate fragrance.
