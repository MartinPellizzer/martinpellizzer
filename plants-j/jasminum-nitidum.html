Jasminum nitidum

Jasminum nitidum, commonly known as the white jasmine or sweet jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Its flowers are tubular with five petals and are known for their sweet, intoxicating aroma.

Jasminum nitidum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and in perfumery due to its aromatic properties.
