Juncus articulatus var. sylvaticus

Juncus articulatus var. sylvaticus, commonly known as the woodland rush, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that are segmented and often have a reddish or brownish hue. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
