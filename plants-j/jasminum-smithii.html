Jasminum smithii

Jasminum smithii, commonly known as the Smith's jasmine, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a woody shrub characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, simple, and elliptic leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Jasminum smithii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its pleasant aroma.
