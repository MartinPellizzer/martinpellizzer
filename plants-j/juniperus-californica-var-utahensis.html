Juniperus californica var. utahensis

Juniperus californica var. utahensis, commonly known as the Utah juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves and small, blue-gray berries that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating remarkable drought tolerance. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in rocky or sandy habitats.
