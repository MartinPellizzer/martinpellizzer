Juniperus davurica var. parsonsii

Juniperus davurica var. parsonsii, commonly known as the Parson's juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that is typically dark green and slightly needle-like. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.
