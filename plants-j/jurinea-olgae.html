Jurinea olgae

Jurinea olgae, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and blue or purple flower heads. The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, making it well adapted to maritime environments. Its flowers are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Jurinea olgae is often used in ornamental gardening for its drought tolerance and visual appeal.
