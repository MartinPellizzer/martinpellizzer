Justicia exilissima

Justicia exilissima, commonly known as the narrowleaf false arum, is a tropical perennial plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, are pollinated by insects, particularly flies and beetles.

Justicia exilissima is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and unique floral structure.
