Jumellea lignosa

Jumellea lignosa, commonly known as the African sandthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible but have a sharp, acidic taste. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Jumellea lignosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
