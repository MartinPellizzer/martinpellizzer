Jasminum francii

Jasminum francii, commonly known as the Chinese jasmine or fragrant jasmine, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white to yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or climbing vine, with opposite, simple leaves that are often aromatic. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are highly valued for their sweet scent, making it popular in horticulture.

Jasminum francii is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
