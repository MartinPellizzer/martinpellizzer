Josephia formosa

Josephia formosa, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and along canyon walls. Its leaves are typically narrow and gray-green, adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry climates.

The species is known for its ability to survive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient and drought-tolerant plant.
