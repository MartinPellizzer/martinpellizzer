Juncus lomatophyllus var. typicus

Juncus lomatophyllus var. typicus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and pond edges. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
