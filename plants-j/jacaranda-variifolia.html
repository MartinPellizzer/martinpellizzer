Jacaranda variifolia

Jacaranda variifolia, commonly known as the variable jacaranda, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking purple-pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a variable shape, ranging from ovate to lanceolate. The tree produces long, cylindrical pods that contain flat, winged seeds.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
