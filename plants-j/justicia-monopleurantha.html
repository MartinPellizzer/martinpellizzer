Justicia monopleurantha

Justicia monopleurantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flame flower, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
