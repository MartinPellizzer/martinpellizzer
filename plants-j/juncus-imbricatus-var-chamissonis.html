Juncus imbricatus var. chamissonis

Juncus imbricatus var. chamissonis, commonly known as the Chamisson's rush, is a variant of the common rush found in wetland environments.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its cylindrical, grass-like stems that often grow in dense clusters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, three-lobed flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often in marshes, swamps, or along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various wildlife species.
