Justicia sisparensis

Justicia sisparensis, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of vibrant red or orange tubular flowers, which are highly attractive to hummingbirds and other pollinators. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its rapid growth rate makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both aesthetic and ecological benefits.
