Juncus exiguus

Juncus exiguus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 1 millimeter wide. It produces small, greenish flowers in dense spikes during the summer months.

Juncus exiguus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
