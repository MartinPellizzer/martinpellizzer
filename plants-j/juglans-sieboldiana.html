Juglans sieboldiana

Juglans sieboldiana, commonly known as the Japanese walnut, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the walnut family, Juglandaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive fruit. The tree produces edible nuts enclosed in a spiky husk, which are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.

The species is also cultivated for its hardy nature and ability to adapt to various soil conditions.
