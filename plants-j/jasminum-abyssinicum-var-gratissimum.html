Jasminum abyssinicum var. gratissimum

Jasminum abyssinicum var. gratissimum, commonly known as the Abyssinian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often releasing a sweet, heady scent. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and aromatic qualities.
