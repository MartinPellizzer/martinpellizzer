Justicia brasiliensis

Justicia brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian flame flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers. The plant typically grows as an herb or shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Justicia brasiliensis thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
