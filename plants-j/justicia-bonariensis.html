Justicia bonariensis

Justicia bonariensis, commonly known as the Brazilian flame flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking tubular red or orange flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a glossy appearance.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring full sun and well-drained soil.
