Jasminum octocuspe var. octocuspe

Jasminum octocuspe var. octocuspe, commonly known as the common jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a sweet, intoxicating scent. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sun for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and fragrance extraction.
