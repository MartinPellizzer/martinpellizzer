Juncus dolichanthus

Juncus dolichanthus, commonly known as the long-flowered rush, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features slender, grass-like stems that are often clustered together, forming dense stands. Its flowering spikes are tall and cylindrical, bearing small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Juncus dolichanthus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
