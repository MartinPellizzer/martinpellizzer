Juncus bulbosus var. eusupinus

Juncus bulbosus var. eusupinus, commonly known as the bulbous rush, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, pond edges, and damp meadows. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish or purplish in color. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers.

The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a slight sheen, contributing to its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
