Jacea oxylepis

Jacea oxylepis, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-draining soil and prefers indirect sunlight, making it a popular houseplant. Its botanical features include hairy stems and leaves with a waxy surface.

Jacea oxylepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
