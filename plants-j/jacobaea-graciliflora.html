Jacobaea graciliflora

Jacobaea graciliflora, commonly known as slender-flowered tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats.

Jacobaea graciliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
