Juniperus chinensis f. pendula

Juniperus chinensis f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping Chinese juniper, is a unique cultivar characterized by its cascading, drooping branches that create a distinctive, ornamental appearance.

This evergreen shrub or small tree is native to China and is prized for its attractive, feathery foliage that remains green throughout the year. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are typically not edible for humans but are used in various traditional contexts. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.

Its slow growth rate and compact form make it suitable for use as a specimen plant or in rock gardens.
