Jovellana albula

Jovellana albula, commonly known as the white-flowered ivy, is a climbing plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Jovellana albula is known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a popular choice for vertical gardens and wall coverings.

Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a preference for moist, well-drained soil.
