Jasminum lupinifolium

Jasminum lupinifolium, commonly known as the lupin-leaved jasmine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often found in coastal regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
