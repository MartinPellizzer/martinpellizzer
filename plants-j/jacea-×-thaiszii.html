Jacea × thaiszii

Jacea × thaiszii, commonly known as the hybrid jasmine vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a cross between Jacea indica and Jacea thailandica, resulting in a species with unique botanical characteristics. The plant is a climbing vine that produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a smooth texture.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
