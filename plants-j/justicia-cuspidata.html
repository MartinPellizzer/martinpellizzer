Justicia cuspidata

Justicia cuspidata, commonly known as the spiked flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves and produces showy bracts that enhance its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.

Justicia cuspidata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and butterflies.
