Juncus bufonius subsp. ranarius

Juncus bufonius subsp.

ranarius, commonly known as the marsh rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America. It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of greenish-yellow flowers. It is adapted to tolerate periodic flooding and has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil.

This species plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
