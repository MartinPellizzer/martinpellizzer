Justicia oblongifolia

Justicia oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf flamebeard, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
