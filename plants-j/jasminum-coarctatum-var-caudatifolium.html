Jasminum coarctatum var. caudatifolium

Jasminum coarctatum var. caudatifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

Due to its delicate nature, it requires careful attention to soil moisture and light conditions for optimal growth.
