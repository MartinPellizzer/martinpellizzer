Justicia ascendens

Justicia ascendens, commonly known as the skyflower or blue mist vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant produces showy, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Justicia ascendens is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of cultivation.
