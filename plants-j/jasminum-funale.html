Jasminum funale

Jasminum funale, commonly known as the funeral jasmine, is a flowering plant belonging to the Oleaceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a sweet, delicate scent. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture.

Jasminum funale is typically cultivated for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
