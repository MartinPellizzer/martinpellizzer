Juncus gracilis

Juncus gracilis, commonly known as slender rush, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and tall, slender flowering spikes. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense colonies that stabilize shorelines. Its narrow leaves are usually dark green and have a smooth, waxy surface.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in a spike that rises above the foliage.
