Jasminum insularum

Jasminum insularum, commonly known as the island jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a sweet scent that attracts pollinators. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and ovate, with a glossy green surface.

Jasminum insularum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
