Jussiaea berteroana

Jussiaea berteroana, commonly known as the Brazilian waterleaf, is a perennial aquatic plant native to South America.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often floating or partially submerged. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in greenish-white or yellowish hues.

Jussiaea berteroana plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
