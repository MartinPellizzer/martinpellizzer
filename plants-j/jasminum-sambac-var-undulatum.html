Jasminum sambac var. undulatum

Jasminum sambac var. undulatum, commonly known as the undulating jasmine, is a fragrant flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white, tubular flowers that emit a strong, sweet scent. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and aromatic value.
