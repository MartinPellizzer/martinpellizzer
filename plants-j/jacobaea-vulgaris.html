Jacobaea vulgaris

Jacobaea vulgaris, commonly known as common tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady areas and has a strong, often unpleasant odor. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

Jacobaea vulgaris is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
