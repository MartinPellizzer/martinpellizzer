Jonesiopsis luteola

Jonesiopsis luteola, commonly known as the yellow Jonesiopsis, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

It is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Asia, particularly found in countries like India and Malaysia. The plant features slender, hairy stems and pinnately compound leaves with a distinct, fern-like appearance. Its yellow flowers bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
