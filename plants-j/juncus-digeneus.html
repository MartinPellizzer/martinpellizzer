Juncus digeneus

Juncus digeneus, commonly known as the slender rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland and marsh environments in North America.

It typically grows in dense clusters, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often reddish or purplish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, which are arranged in spike-like inflorescences. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form extensive colonies in moist soils.

Juncus digeneus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
