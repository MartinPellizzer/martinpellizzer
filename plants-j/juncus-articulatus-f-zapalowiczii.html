Juncus articulatus f. zapalowiczii

Juncus articulatus f. zapalowiczii, commonly known as the Zapalowiczii rush, is a rare botanical variant of the common rush species.

It is characterized by its distinctive morphology, including elongated, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This subspecies is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along the edges of ponds. Its flowering stems produce small, clustered spikelets, which are typical of the Juncaceae family.

Due to its limited distribution and unique features, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
