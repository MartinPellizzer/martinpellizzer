Jasione montana subsp. paivae

Jasione montana subsp.

paivae, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It typically grows in rocky or limestone-rich soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments.

The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are bell-shaped, usually blue or violet, and are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
