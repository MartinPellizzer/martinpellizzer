Justicia lazarus

Justicia lazarus, commonly known as the Lazarus plant, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue or purple tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and butterflies. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Justicia lazarus thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
