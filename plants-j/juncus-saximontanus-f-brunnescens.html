Juncus saximontanus f. brunnescens

Juncus saximontanus f. brunnescens, commonly known as the brown-stemmed rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-brown flowers in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along stream banks. Its stems are reddish-brown in color, distinguishing it from other rush species.

This subspecies is native to western North America and is adapted to a variety of wetland habitats.
