Jasminum simplicifolium subsp. sootepense

Jasminum simplicifolium subsp.

sootepense, commonly known as the Sootep jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its simple, lance-shaped leaves are arranged oppositely along the stems.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.
