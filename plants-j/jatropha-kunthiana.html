Jatropha kunthiana

Jatropha kunthiana, commonly known as the silverleaf jatropha, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its silver-gray foliage. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by capsules containing toxic seeds. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, with serrated margins.

Jatropha kunthiana is often found in dry, rocky areas and is adapted to arid environments.
