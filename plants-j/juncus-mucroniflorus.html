Juncus mucroniflorus

Juncus mucroniflorus, commonly known as the sharp-flowered rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland and marsh environments in North America.

It features grass-like, cylindrical stems that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered at the ends of the stems, which are often surrounded by bracts. Its root system helps stabilize soil and improve water filtration in wetland ecosystems.

Juncus mucroniflorus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and environmental benefits.
