Jaliscoa pringlei

Jaliscoa pringlei, commonly known as the Mexican fireplant, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Jalisco, Mexico, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of vibrant red tubular flowers that bloom in summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

Jaliscoa pringlei is prized for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bold, fiery appearance.
