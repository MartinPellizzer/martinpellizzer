Jasminum transvaalense

Jasminum transvaalense, commonly known as the Transvaal jasmine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces fragrant, white flowers during the summer months. The plant has small, elliptical leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

The flowers are used in perfumery and traditional medicine due to their aromatic properties.
