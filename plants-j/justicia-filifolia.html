Justicia filifolia

Justicia filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf flameball, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its narrow, thread-like leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in clusters, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom in the summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers are pollinated by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
