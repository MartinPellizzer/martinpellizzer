Juncus punctorius var. mauritanicus

Juncus punctorius var. mauritanicus, commonly known as the Mauritania rush, is a perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems that grow close to the ground, often forming dense clusters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with poor soil conditions.

Its morphology and growth habits make it an important component of desert and steppe ecosystems.
