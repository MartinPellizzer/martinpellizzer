Jatropha gossypiifolia subf. flaviflora

Jatropha gossypiifolia subf.

flaviflora, commonly known as the yellow-flowered jatropha, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Asia. It features opposite, elliptic to ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly toothed margin.

The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that are typically bisexual and showy. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hard seeds.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.
