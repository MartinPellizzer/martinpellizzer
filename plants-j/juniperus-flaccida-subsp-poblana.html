Juniperus flaccida subsp. poblana

Juniperus flaccida subsp.

poblana, commonly known as the Mexican juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico. It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which consists of scale-like leaves arranged in dense clusters.

The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

The subspecies is notable for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
