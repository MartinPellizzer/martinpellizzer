Justicia triticea

Justicia triticea, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in moist, well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Justicia triticea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
