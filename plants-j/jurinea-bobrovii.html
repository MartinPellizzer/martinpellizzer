Jurinea bobrovii

Jurinea bobrovii, commonly known as the Bobrovii's sea bladder, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a halophyte, meaning it thrives in saline environments such as coastal areas and salt marshes. The plant is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, aiding in survival in arid conditions. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically greenish-white in color.

Jurinea bobrovii is often found in sandy or gravelly soils along the coasts of the Mediterranean and parts of Asia.
