Juncus lampocarpus f. parviflorus

Juncus lampocarpus f. parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are usually greenish or brownish, and it produces small, dry fruits.

This subspecies is often found in coastal regions and is adapted to tolerate saline conditions.
