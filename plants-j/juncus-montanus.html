Juncus montanus

Juncus montanus, commonly known as the mountain rush, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. The plant has grass-like, cylindrical leaves that are usually green to bluish-green in color. It produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are typically white or pale pink.

Juncus montanus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
