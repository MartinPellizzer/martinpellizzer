Jussiaea linearis

Jussiaea linearis, commonly known as the slender water-plantain, is a flowering plant native to wetland and aquatic environments in the Americas.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are typically submerged or floating, adapted for life in water. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Jussiaea linearis thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, often forming dense mats on the water surface.

Its morphology and growth habits make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
