Jasminum lang

Jasminum lang, commonly known as the long-flowered jasmine, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually hairy leaves, which are elliptical in shape. Its flowering period typically occurs during the warmer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
