Juncus orthophyllus f. congestus

Juncus orthophyllus f. congestus, commonly known as the dense rush, is a compact, herbaceous plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a low-growing mat. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, wetlands, and along the edges of ponds.

This subspecies is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support wetland biodiversity.
