Justicia comapuensis

Justicia comapuensis, commonly known as Mexican firecracker, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
