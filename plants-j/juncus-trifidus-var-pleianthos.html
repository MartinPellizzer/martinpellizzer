Juncus trifidus var. pleianthos

Juncus trifidus var. pleianthos, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in moist soils. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
