Jasminum virgatum

Jasminum virgatum, commonly known as the Japanese jasmine or winter jasmine, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring, often before the leaves appear. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a smooth margin.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to temperate climates.
