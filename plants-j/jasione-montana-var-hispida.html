Jasione montana var. hispida

Jasione montana var. hispida, commonly known as the hairy rock jasmine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features upright stems covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
