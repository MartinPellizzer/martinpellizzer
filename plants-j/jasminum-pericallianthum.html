Jasminum pericallianthum

Jasminum pericallianthum, commonly known as the golden jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

The flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils due to their aromatic properties.
