Juniperus virginiana var. hermannii

Juniperus virginiana var. hermannii, commonly known as the Hermann juniper, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features needle-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, providing a distinctive visual appeal. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.

This variety is particularly valued for its ability to withstand drought and poor soil conditions.
