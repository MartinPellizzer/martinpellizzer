Jonquilla viridiflora

Jonquilla viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered daffodil, is a species native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, elongated, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a bulbous root system typical of many Amaryllidaceae species. It blooms in spring and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered plant.
