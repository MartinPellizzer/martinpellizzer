Juncus grisebachii

Juncus grisebachii, commonly known as the gray rush, is a perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas, rocky slopes, and desert margins. Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil, making it useful for erosion control in dry environments.

Juncus grisebachii is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, showcasing its resilience in harsh climates.
