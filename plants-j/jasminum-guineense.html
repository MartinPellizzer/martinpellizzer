Jasminum guineense

Jasminum guineense, commonly known as African jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Jasminum guineense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
