Jossinia pasacaensis

Jossinia pasacaensis, commonly known as the Pasaca sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate habit and succulent leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
