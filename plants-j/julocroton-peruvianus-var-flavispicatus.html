Julocroton peruvianus var. flavispicatus

Julocroton peruvianus var. flavispicatus, commonly known as the golden-spined julocroton, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, woody structure and vibrant foliage. The plant features striking, elongated leaves with a glossy, dark green surface. Its most distinctive feature is the bright yellow spines that adorn the stems, giving it its common name.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
