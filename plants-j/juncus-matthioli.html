Juncus matthioli

Juncus matthioli, commonly known as the pink rush, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and dense clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and damp meadows. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense vegetation, which can help stabilize soil in wetland areas.

The flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and flies, contributing to its ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
