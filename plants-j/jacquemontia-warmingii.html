Jacquemontia warmingii

Jacquemontia warmingii, commonly known as the yellow balsam, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, erect stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically alternate along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Jacquemontia warmingii is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist habitats.
