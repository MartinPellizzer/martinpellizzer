Jacobaea kosteleckii

Jacobaea kosteleckii, commonly known as the Kostelecky's ragwort, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and woolly, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in alpine or mountainous regions. It produces small, elongated seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a noxious weed in some areas.
