Juglans regia f. monophylla

Juglans regia f. monophylla, commonly known as the Persian walnut, is a cultivated variant of the common walnut.

It is characterized by its large, edible seeds and heart-shaped leaves. The plant is native to the mountains of Central Asia and is widely grown for its nut production. It prefers well-drained soil and a temperate climate.

This form is distinguished by its monomorphic leaves, which are typically larger and more uniform in shape compared to the typical variety.
