Juncus acuminatus f. obtusatus

Juncus acuminatus f. obtusatus, commonly known as the obtuse spike rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in summer, which are arranged in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its foliage is typically narrow and somewhat fleshy, adapted to retain water in its native habitat.
