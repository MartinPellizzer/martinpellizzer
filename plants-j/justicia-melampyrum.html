Justicia melampyrum

Justicia melampyrum, commonly known as flame flower or red flame, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an annual or short-lived perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, while its stems are erect and branching.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
