Juniperus communis var. pendula

Juniperus communis var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping juniper, is a variant of the common juniper that exhibits a distinctive cascading growth habit.

It features slender, drooping branches that create a soft, flowing appearance, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant produces small, scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and whitish underneath, providing a textured foliage effect. It bears blue-black berries that are typically harvested in late summer, though the plant is primarily cultivated for its unique visual appeal rather than its fruit.

This variety thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, adapting well to a variety of garden settings.
