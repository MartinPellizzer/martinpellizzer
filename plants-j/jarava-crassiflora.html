Jarava crassiflora

Jarava crassiflora, commonly known as the narrowleaf jarava, is a perennial grass native to South Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and coastal areas, where it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, spike-like inflorescence produces numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Jarava crassiflora is adapted to dry conditions and plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
