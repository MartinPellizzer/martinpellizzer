Johnsonia acaulis var. drummondii

Johnsonia acaulis var. drummondii, commonly known as the Drummond's rockrose, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form with thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting to its harsh environment. The plant produces small, vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. Its leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps minimize water loss through evaporation.

This variety is valued for its resilience and ornamental appeal in xeriscaping and rock gardens.
