Jamesonia wurdackii

Jamesonia wurdackii, commonly known as the Wurdack's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features a fibrous root system and lanceolate, hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded woodland environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native gardens and conservation efforts.
