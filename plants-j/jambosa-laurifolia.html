Jambosa laurifolia

Jambosa laurifolia, commonly known as the African starfruit or Natal starfruit, is a tropical shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are similar in appearance to starfruit, with a rough, yellowish exterior and a sweet, aromatic pulp. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

Jambosa laurifolia is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
