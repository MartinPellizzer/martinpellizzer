Jacobaea echaeta

Jacobaea echaeta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Jacobaea echaeta has been used historically for its purported medicinal benefits, though its use is now less common.
