Jasminum bahiense var. fluminense

Jasminum bahiense var. fluminense, commonly known as the Brazilian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, simple, and ovate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the summer months. Its flowers are borne on peduncles that emerge from the leaf axils, and they are known for their strong, sweet scent.

The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and aromatic properties.
