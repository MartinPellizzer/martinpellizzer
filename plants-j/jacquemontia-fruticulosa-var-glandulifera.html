Jacquemontia fruticulosa var. glandulifera

Jacquemontia fruticulosa var. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular jacquemontia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its woody stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories.
