Juniperus thurifera subsp. africana

Juniperus thurifera subsp.

africana, commonly known as the African juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and small, blue-gray berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its evergreen foliage provides year-round structure in its native environment.

The species is valued for its ecological role and has limited commercial use.
