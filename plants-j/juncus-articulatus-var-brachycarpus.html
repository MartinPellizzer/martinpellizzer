Juncus articulatus var. brachycarpus

Juncus articulatus var. brachycarpus, commonly known as the short-carped rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and bear clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in aquatic environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically growing in dense clumps.

This variety is distinguished by its shorter, more compact inflorescences compared to other juncus species.
