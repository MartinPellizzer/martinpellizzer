Justicia colorifera

Justicia colorifera, commonly known as the flame flower or red flame plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its rapid growth habit makes it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
