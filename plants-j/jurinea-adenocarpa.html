Jurinea adenocarpa

Jurinea adenocarpa, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Eurasia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with a succulent, cushion-like structure that helps it conserve water. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a dense inflorescence, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are fleshy and covered in fine hairs, which reduce water loss and protect against harsh environmental conditions.

Jurinea adenocarpa is well adapted to sandy soils and can survive in extreme temperatures, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
