Juncus bolanderi var. riparius

Juncus bolanderi var. riparius, commonly known as the riparian rush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and wetlands. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers in late spring to early summer.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
