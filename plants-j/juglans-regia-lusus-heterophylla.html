Juglans regia lusus heterophylla

Juglans regia lusus heterophylla, commonly known as the Persian walnut with heterophyllous variation, is a variant of the Persian walnut tree characterized by its unique leaf morphology.

This plant exhibits heterophylly, a phenomenon where leaves differ in shape and structure depending on their position on the plant. The leaves are typically larger and broader on the upper parts of the plant compared to the smaller, more elongated leaves on the lower branches. It is native to regions of Central Asia and is cultivated for its ornamental and economic value.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
