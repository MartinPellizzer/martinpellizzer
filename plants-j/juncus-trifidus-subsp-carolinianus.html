Juncus trifidus subsp. carolinianus

Juncus trifidus subsp.

carolinianus, commonly known as the Carolina rush, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems that grow in dense clusters, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are linear and typically have three distinct lobes, giving it the species name "trifidus.".

This subspecies thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds and streams.
