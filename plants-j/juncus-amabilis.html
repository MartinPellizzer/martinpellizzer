Juncus amabilis

Juncus amabilis, commonly known as the pink rush, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and spikes of pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its slender, cylindrical stems are often used in floral arrangements due to their elegant appearance.

Juncus amabilis plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
