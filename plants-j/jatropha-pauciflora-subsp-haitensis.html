Jatropha pauciflora subsp. haitensis

Jatropha pauciflora subsp.

haitensis, commonly known as Haitian cupplant, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive appearance. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
