Justicia iochila

Justicia iochila, commonly known as the Mexican flameflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
