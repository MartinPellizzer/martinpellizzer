Jurinea humilis var. bocconei

Jurinea humilis var. bocconei, commonly known as the Boccone's sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in coastal and saline environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically silver-gray, covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to harsh maritime conditions and is often used in coastal landscaping.
