Juncus gerardi var. cristoflei

Juncus gerardi var. cristoflei, commonly known as the cristoflei rush, is a perennial plant native to the wetlands of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage that forms compact clumps, with narrow, cylindrical leaves that are often reddish at the base. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear greenish-white flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, pond edges, and along stream banks.

Due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal, it is a popular choice for wetland restoration and ornamental gardening.
