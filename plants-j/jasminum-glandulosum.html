Jasminum glandulosum

Jasminum glandulosum, commonly known as the hairy jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has thin, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to high levels of sunlight.

Jasminum glandulosum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
