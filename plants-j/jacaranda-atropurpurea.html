Jacaranda atropurpurea

Jacaranda atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple jacaranda, is a deciduous tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces long, cylindrical fruit pods. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental tree in warmer climates.

The tree is also valued for its ability to provide shade and its aesthetic appeal in landscaping.
