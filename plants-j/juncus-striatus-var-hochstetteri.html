Juncus striatus var. hochstetteri

Juncus striatus var. hochstetteri, commonly known as Hochstetter's rush, is a perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist or wet environments, such as streambanks and marshes. Its flowering stems produce small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

Hochstetter's rush is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
