Jatropha aconitifolia var. papaya

Jatropha aconitifolia var. papaya, commonly known as the papaya jatropha, is a variant of the jatropha genus native to tropical regions.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright orange or red berries containing toxic seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for medicinal use, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and research settings.
