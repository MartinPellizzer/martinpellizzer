Juncus greenei var. vaseyi

Juncus greenei var. vaseyi, commonly known as the Greene's rush, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage composed of narrow, cylindrical leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, riparian zones, and wet meadows. Its flowers are small, clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and are usually greenish or brownish in color.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
