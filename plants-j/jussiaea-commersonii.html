Jussiaea commersonii

Jussiaea commersonii, commonly known as the Commerson's bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its floating leaves and small, bladder-like structures that trap tiny aquatic organisms. The plant is typically found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats. It has a delicate appearance with finely divided leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Jussiaea commersonii plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for microorganisms.
