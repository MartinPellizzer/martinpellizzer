Juncus nodulosus var. uniceps

Juncus nodulosus var. uniceps, commonly known as the clustered rush, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and bear small, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of clusters of greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variant is distinguished by its dense, clustered growth habit and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
