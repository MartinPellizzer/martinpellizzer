Juncus compressus subsp. gerardi

Juncus compressus subsp.

gerardi, commonly known as Gerardi's rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Juncaceae family. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often found in temperate regions.

The plant has cylindrical, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
