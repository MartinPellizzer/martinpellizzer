Juncus pohlii

Juncus pohlii, commonly known as the Pohli's rush, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of eastern North America.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and tall, slender flowering spikes. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds and streams. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a delicate, feathery appearance that aids in wind pollination.

Juncus pohlii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
