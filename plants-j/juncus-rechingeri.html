Juncus rechingeri

Juncus rechingeri, commonly known as the Rechinger's rush, is a perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of brownish flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Juncus rechingeri plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment and is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to high-altitude conditions.
