Justicia lobelioides

Justicia lobelioides, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

The genus Justicia is characterized by its showy inflorescences, which are often arranged in spikes or clusters.
