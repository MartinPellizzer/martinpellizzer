Jatropha ribifolia

Jatropha ribifolia, commonly known as the red-leaf jatropha, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, often reddish bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing toxic seeds. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, with 5 to 9 leaflets that are ovate to elliptic in shape.

Jatropha ribifolia is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
