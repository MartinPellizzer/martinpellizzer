Jurinea heteromalla

Jurinea heteromalla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sea-bluemink, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue to violet flowers and narrow, succulent leaves. The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found in dunes and coastal grasslands. It produces small, dry fruits that contain single seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its specialized habitat, Jurinea heteromalla is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
