Juncus uliginosus

Juncus uliginosus, commonly known as the bog rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It typically grows in marshy areas, bogs, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and bear small, spike-like clusters of greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and they are adapted to thrive in waterlogged soils.

Juncus uliginosus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
