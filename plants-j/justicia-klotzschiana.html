Justicia klotzschiana

Justicia klotzschiana, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of blue to purple tubular flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It thrives in partial to full sun and is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
