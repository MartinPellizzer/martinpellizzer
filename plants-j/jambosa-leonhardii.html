Jambosa leonhardii

Jambosa leonhardii, commonly known as the African blackwood tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its dark, aromatic bark and dense, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by small, round, black berries. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and used in traditional medicine.
