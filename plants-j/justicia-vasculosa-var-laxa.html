Justicia vasculosa var. laxa

Justicia vasculosa var. laxa, commonly known as the looseleaf justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink or red, which bloom in summer. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and showy blooms.
