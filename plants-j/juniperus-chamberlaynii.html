Juniperus chamberlaynii

Juniperus chamberlaynii, commonly known as the Sierra Madre juniper, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its scale-like leaves and small, blue-gray berries that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid climates. It is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.

Juniperus chamberlaynii plays an important role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
