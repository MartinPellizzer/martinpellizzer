Juniperus chinensis var. pfitzeriana

Juniperus chinensis var. pfitzeriana, commonly known as the Chinese juniper or Pfitzer juniper, is a compact, evergreen shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It features scale-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, providing a distinctive visual contrast. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are blue or black in color, containing the seeds. It is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

This variety is noted for its slow growth and ability to thrive in both full sun and partial shade.
