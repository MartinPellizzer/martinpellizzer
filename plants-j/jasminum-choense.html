Jasminum choense

Jasminum choense, commonly known as the Cape jasmine, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture, while the flowers emit a sweet scent, especially in the evening.

Jasminum choense is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and aromatic properties.
