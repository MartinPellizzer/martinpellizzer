Jodina rhombifolia

Jodina rhombifolia, commonly known as the rhombic jodina, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rhombic-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories. Its botanical name reflects the distinctive shape of its foliage, which is a key identifier in the field.

Jodina rhombifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
