Juncus subtilis

Juncus subtilis, commonly known as the slender rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and damp woodlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

Juncus subtilis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
