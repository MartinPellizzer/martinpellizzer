Juncus bulbosus var. compressus

Juncus bulbosus var. compressus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and damp woodlands. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often slightly compressed, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters during the summer months.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
