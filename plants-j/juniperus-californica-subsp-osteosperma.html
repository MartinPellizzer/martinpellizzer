Juniperus californica subsp. osteosperma

Juniperus californica subsp.

osteosperma, commonly known as the California juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States. It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which consists of scale-like leaves arranged in flat sprays.

The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal sage scrub habitats.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to withstand drought conditions.
