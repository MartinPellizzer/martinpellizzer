Juncus punctorius

Juncus punctorius, commonly known as the dotted rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish or purplish in color, and it grows in dense clusters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown. It thrives in moist to waterlogged soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Juncus punctorius plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
