Jarandersonia paludosa

Jarandersonia paludosa, commonly known as the bog gentian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils found in bogs and swampy areas. The plant features showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Jarandersonia paludosa plays an important ecological role in maintaining the biodiversity of its wetland habitat.
