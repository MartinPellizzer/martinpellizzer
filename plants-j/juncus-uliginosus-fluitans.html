Juncus uliginosus fluitans

Juncus uliginosus fluitans, commonly known as the floating rush, is a wetland plant characterized by its erect, grass-like stems that often grow in shallow water or moist soil.

It produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers that are typically clustered at the tips of the stems. The plant has fibrous roots that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion in aquatic environments. It is often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes.

Its ability to thrive in waterlogged conditions makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
