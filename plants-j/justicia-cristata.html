Justicia cristata

Justicia cristata, commonly known as the Christmas star or flame of the forest, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange inflorescences that resemble stars, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
