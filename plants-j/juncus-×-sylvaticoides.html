Juncus × sylvaticoides

Juncus × sylvaticoides, commonly known as the marsh rush, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Juncus effusus and Juncus roemerianus.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and coastal areas. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in short, stiff leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it produces wind-pollinated seeds.

Juncus × sylvaticoides plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
