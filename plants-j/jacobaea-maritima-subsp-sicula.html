Jacobaea maritima subsp. sicula

Jacobaea maritima subsp.

sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian tansy, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its aromatic, finely divided leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas and rocky soils, adapting to saline and well-drained conditions. Its foliage contains volatile oils, contributing to its distinctive scent and potential medicinal properties.

It is often found in dry, sunny habitats along the coasts of Sicily and southern Italy.
