Jasione laevis subsp. orbiculata

Jasione laevis subsp.

orbiculata, commonly known as the smooth rock-jasmine, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

The flowers are typically blue to purple in color, with five petals and a distinct central stamen. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
