Jussiaea peruviana var. elegans

Jussiaea peruviana var. elegans, commonly known as the Peruvian water willow, is a semi-aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that often root at the nodes and grow in shallow water or moist soil. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in its wetland habitat.

Jussiaea peruviana var. elegans is often used in aquatic gardens and wetland restoration projects due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
