Jungia longifolia

Jungia longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved jungle plant, is a herbaceous species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are elongated and narrow, often with a slightly serrated edge.

Jungia longifolia is valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
