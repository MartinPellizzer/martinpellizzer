Juncus triformis var. uniflorus

Juncus triformis var. uniflorus, commonly known as the single-flowered rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like, cylindrical stems that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are usually clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found along the edges of ponds, streams, and wetlands.

This variety is notable for its limited floral display, making it a subtle yet ecologically important component of wetland ecosystems.
