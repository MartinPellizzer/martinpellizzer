Joinvillea bryanii

Joinvillea bryanii, commonly known as the Bryan's joinvillea, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of vibrant, tubular flowers that range from red to orange in color. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens. It is known for its hardiness and ability to withstand salty sea air, which makes it well-suited for coastal landscapes.

The flowers attract pollinators such as birds and insects, contributing to its ecological value.
