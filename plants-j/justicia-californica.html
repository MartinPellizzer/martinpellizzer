Justicia californica

Justicia californica, commonly known as the California flame flower, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
