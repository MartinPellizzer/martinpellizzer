Juniperus pachyphlaea

Juniperus pachyphlaea, commonly known as the thickleaf juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are typically reddish or purple in color, which are important for wildlife. It is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and can thrive in full sun conditions.

Juniperus pachyphlaea is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
