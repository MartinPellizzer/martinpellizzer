Justicia cuneata subsp. hoerleiniana

Justicia cuneata subsp.

hoerleiniana, commonly known as the Mexican flamebush, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America. It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant features opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds, enhancing its ecological value.
