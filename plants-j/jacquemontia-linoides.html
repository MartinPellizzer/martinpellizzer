Jacquemontia linoides, commonly known as the creeping yellow vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.
