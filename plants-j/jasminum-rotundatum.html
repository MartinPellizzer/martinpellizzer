Jasminum rotundatum

Jasminum rotundatum, commonly known as the Arabian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the night. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and a woody stem structure. It thrives in warm, arid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Jasminum rotundatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
