Jessenia bataua subsp. oligocarpa

Jessenia bataua subsp.

oligocarpa, commonly known as the red cedar, is a member of the Moraceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in lowland and hill forest habitats.

The plant is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and lanceolate, glossy dark green leaves. Its small, clustered flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The fruit is a small, woody drupe that contains a single seed, and the plant is valued for its durable timber and ecological importance in its native range.
