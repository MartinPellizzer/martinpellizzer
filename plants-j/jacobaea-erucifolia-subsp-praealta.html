Jacobaea erucifolia subsp. praealta

Jacobaea erucifolia subsp.

praealta, commonly known as the tall tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features a rosette of basal leaves that are aromatic and covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers in a single terminal head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or grassy habitats.

Its leaves contain compounds that have been historically used for their medicinal and pesticidal properties.
