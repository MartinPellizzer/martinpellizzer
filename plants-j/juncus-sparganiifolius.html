Juncus sparganiifolius

Juncus sparganiifolius, commonly known as the spike-leaved rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in marshy areas, along the edges of ponds, and in other moist habitats. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a dense, spike-like cluster at the top of its stem. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually appearing in late summer, and it produces distinctive, spiky seed heads.

Juncus sparganiifolius plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
