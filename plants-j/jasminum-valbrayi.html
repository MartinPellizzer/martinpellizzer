Jasminum valbrayi

Jasminum valbrayi, commonly known as the Madagascar jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. Its flowers are borne on pedicels and have a sweet, intoxicating aroma, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The plant thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade for optimal growth.
