Juno tubergeniana

Juno tubergeniana, commonly known as the White Mountain Juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves and small, cone-like fruits. The plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its foliage is often blue-green in color, and it produces aromatic oils that contribute to its resilience.

Juno tubergeniana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for wildlife.
