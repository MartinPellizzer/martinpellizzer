Jasminum angustifolium var. angustifolium

Jasminum angustifolium var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, and thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight. Its ornamental value is largely due to its pleasant scent and delicate floral display.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
