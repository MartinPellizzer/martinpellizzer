Justicia afromontana

Justicia afromontana, commonly known as the African flame or mountain flame, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of East Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces striking clusters of red, tubular flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in tropical and subtropical climates.

The genus Justicia is known for its showy inflorescences, and J. afromontana is no exception, showcasing vibrant colors and ornamental value.
