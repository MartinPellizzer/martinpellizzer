Juniperus davurica subsp. maritima

Juniperus davurica subsp.

maritima, commonly known as coastal juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to coastal regions of eastern Asia. It is characterized by its needle-like leaves and small, blue-gray berries that are used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand salt spray and coastal winds. It plays a vital role in stabilizing dunes and preventing erosion in coastal ecosystems.

Its resilience makes it an important species for ecological restoration projects in maritime environments.
