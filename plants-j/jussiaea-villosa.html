Jussiaea villosa

Jussiaea villosa, commonly known as the hairy willow or silky willow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and wetlands.

Jussiaea villosa is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate appearance and adaptability to wet conditions.
