Juglans olanchana

Juglans olanchana, commonly known as the Persian walnut, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the walnut family, Juglandaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and male and female flowers on separate trees. The tree produces edible nuts enclosed in a hard shell, similar to other walnut species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and timber.

The plant has historical significance in traditional medicine and horticulture.
