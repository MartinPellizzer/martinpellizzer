Justicia citrina

Justicia citrina, commonly known as the yellow flame plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual appeal to gardens. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Justicia citrina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
