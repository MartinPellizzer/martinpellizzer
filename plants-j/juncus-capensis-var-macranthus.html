Juncus capensis var. macranthus

Juncus capensis var. macranthus, commonly known as the Cape rush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical spikes of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as riverbanks and wetlands. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often reddish at the base.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
