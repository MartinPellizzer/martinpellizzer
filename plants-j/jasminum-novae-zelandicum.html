Jasminum novae-zelandicum

Jasminum novae-zelandicum, commonly known as the New Zealand jasmine, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces fragrant white flowers during the spring and summer months. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

The flowers are often used in perfumery and traditional medicine due to their aromatic properties.
