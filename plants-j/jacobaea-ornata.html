Jacobaea ornata

Jacobaea ornata, commonly known as the showy goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features bright yellow ray florets surrounding a central disc of tubular flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Jacobaea ornata typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
