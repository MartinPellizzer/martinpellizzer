Juncus decipiens

Juncus decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often grow in dense clusters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing greenish-white flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and colonize moist soils effectively.

Juncus decipiens is often found in marshes, pond edges, and other water-saturated habitats.
