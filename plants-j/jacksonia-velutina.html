Jacksonia velutina

Jacksonia velutina, commonly known as the velvet chalice, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its distinctive, showy, cup-shaped flowers. The plant features soft, hairy leaves that give it a velvety texture, hence its common name. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Jacksonia velutina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
