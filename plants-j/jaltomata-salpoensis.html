Jaltomata salpoensis

Jaltomata salpoensis, commonly known as the Salpoen Mountain morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshade plants, and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed habitats, thriving in high-altitude environments. Its flowers are usually blue or violet in color, opening in the morning and closing by midday.

Jaltomata salpoensis is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its striking floral display.
