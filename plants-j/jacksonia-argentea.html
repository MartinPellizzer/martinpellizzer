Jacksonia argentea

Jacksonia argentea, commonly known as the silver daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or gravelly soils. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Jacksonia argentea thrives in full sun and is relatively easy to cultivate in temperate climates.
