Jatropha transiens

Jatropha transiens, commonly known as the wandering jatropha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red berries containing toxic seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

Jatropha transiens is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
