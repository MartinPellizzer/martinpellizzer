Juncus articulatus f. alpicola

Juncus articulatus f. alpicola, commonly known as the alpine rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Juncaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and spike-like inflorescences. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in alpine and subalpine regions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, greenish flowers in summer.

It is adapted to harsh mountain environments and is often found in meadows and rocky areas at high elevations.
