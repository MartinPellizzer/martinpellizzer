Justicia marrubiastrum

Justicia marrubiastrum, commonly known as the Mexican verbena, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and branched.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
