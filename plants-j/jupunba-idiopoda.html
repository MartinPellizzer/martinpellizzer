Jupunba idiopoda

Jupunba idiopoda, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture.

It is native to regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Its roots are fibrous and can spread widely, making it a persistent weed in agricultural settings.

The plant's rapid growth and ability to regrow from fragments make it challenging to control.
