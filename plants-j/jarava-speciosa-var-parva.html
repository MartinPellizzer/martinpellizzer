Jarava speciosa var. parva

Jarava speciosa var. parva, commonly known as the dwarf bottlebrush grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive bottlebrush-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowering stems are dense and brush-like, giving it a unique appearance.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
