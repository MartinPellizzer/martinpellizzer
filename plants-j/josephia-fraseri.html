Josephia fraseri

Josephia fraseri, commonly known as the Fraser's jojoba, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces a unique, waxy substance known as jojoba oil, which is harvested from its seeds and used in various cosmetic and industrial applications. Its botanical features include narrow, leathery leaves and a deep root system that allows it to thrive in harsh, dry environments.

Josephia fraseri is valued for its adaptability and ecological importance in desert ecosystems.
