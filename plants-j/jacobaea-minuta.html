Jacobaea minuta

Jacobaea minuta, commonly known as the dwarf ragwort, is a small, flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and waste areas. The plant features basal leaves that are lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters, blooming from June to August.

Jacobaea minuta is part of the Asteraceae family and contains pyrrolizidine alkaloids, which can be toxic to livestock.
