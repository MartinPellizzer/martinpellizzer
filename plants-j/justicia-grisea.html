Justicia grisea

Justicia grisea, commonly known as the gray mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy spikes of blue or purple flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, grayish pubescence, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Justicia grisea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
