Jacobaea aegyptia

Jacobaea aegyptia, commonly known as the African pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to Africa, particularly South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its spread across various habitats. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it adaptable to a range of environments.

Jacobaea aegyptia is also noted for its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural pesticides.
