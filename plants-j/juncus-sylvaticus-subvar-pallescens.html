Juncus sylvaticus subvar. pallescens

Juncus sylvaticus subvar. pallescens, commonly known as the pale rush, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems that are typically pale green to grayish in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets of flowers that are usually brown or reddish in hue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting wildlife and stabilizing soil.
