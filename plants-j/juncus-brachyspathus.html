Juncus brachyspathus

Juncus brachyspathus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rush, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish-brown in color and bear small, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are inconspicuous and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, which appear at the tips of the stems.

Juncus brachyspathus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
