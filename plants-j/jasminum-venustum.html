Jasminum venustum

Jasminum venustum, commonly known as the Persian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a glossy green appearance.

The flowers are often used in perfumery and traditional medicine due to their aromatic properties.
