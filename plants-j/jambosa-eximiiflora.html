Jambosa eximiiflora

Jambosa eximiiflora, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has a distinctive, spreading canopy with thick, waxy leaves that provide shade. Its rapid growth rate makes it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

The fruit, which is edible, contains a single large seed surrounded by a fleshy pulp.
