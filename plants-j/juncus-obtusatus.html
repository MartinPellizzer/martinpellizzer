Juncus obtusatus

Juncus obtusatus, commonly known as bluntleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually light to dark green in color. Its flowering spikes consist of small, pale green to brown flowers that appear in late spring to early summer.

Juncus obtusatus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
