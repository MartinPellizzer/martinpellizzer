Juniperus occidentalis var. conjungens

Juniperus occidentalis var. conjungens, commonly known as the coastal juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the western coast of North America.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves and small, blue-black berries that are typically borne in clusters. The plant is adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils. It has a slow growth rate and can live for several decades.

Its foliage provides habitat for various wildlife species, and it is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
