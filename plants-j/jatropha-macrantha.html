Jatropha macrantha

Jatropha macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered physic nut, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and woody stems, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces toxic seeds that contain compounds such as ricin, making them harmful to humans and animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Jatropha macrantha is also valued for its potential in biofuel production due to its oil-rich seeds.
