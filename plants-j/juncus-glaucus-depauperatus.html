Juncus glaucus depauperatus

Juncus glaucus depauperatus, commonly known as the slender marsh rush, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often tinged with a bluish hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and sensitive indicator species.
