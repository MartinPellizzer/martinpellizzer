Justicia misera

Justicia misera, commonly known as the weeping justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant produces clusters of tubular, violet-blue flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its showy blooms.
