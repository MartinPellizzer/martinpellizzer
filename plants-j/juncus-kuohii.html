Juncus kuohii

Juncus kuohii, commonly known as the Kauai rush, is a perennial plant native to the island of Kauai in Hawaii.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy areas and is adapted to the humid, tropical climate of the Hawaiian Islands. Juncus kuohii is often found in coastal and lowland habitats, where it thrives in poorly drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
