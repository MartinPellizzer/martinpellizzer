Jasminum scandens

Jasminum scandens, commonly known as the climbing jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit, with slender stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

Jasminum scandens is often used in ornamental gardening for its pleasant aroma and aesthetic appeal.
