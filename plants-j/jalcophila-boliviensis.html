Jalcophila boliviensis

Jalcophila boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of Bolivia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and high humidity. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the rocky substrates where it grows.

Jalcophila boliviensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
