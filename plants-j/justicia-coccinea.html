Justicia coccinea

Justicia coccinea, commonly known as the red flame plant or scarlet justicia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking red bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with opposite leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its showy inflorescences consist of clusters of small white flowers surrounded by vibrant red bracts, making it a popular ornamental plant.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden cultivation in warm climates.
