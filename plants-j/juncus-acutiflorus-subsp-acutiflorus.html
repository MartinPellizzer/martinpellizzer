Juncus acutiflorus subsp. acutiflorus

Juncus acutiflorus subsp.

acutiflorus, commonly known as the sharpflower rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments in North America. It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that often reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, typically bearing greenish or reddish flowers. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
