Juno persica var. mardinensis

Juno persica var. mardinensis, commonly known as the Mardin peach, is a rare and ancient variety of peach native to the region of Mardin in Turkey.

It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange fruits with a distinctive, fuzzy skin. The plant is a deciduous tree that thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its flowers are white and fragrant, typically blooming in the spring.

Due to its historical significance and limited cultivation, it is considered a valuable species for botanical and agricultural study.
