Juncus nitidiflorus

Juncus nitidiflorus, commonly known as the smoothflower rush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of grass-like, cylindrical stems. The plant produces small, white to pale green flowers that bloom in late summer. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows.

Juncus nitidiflorus is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
