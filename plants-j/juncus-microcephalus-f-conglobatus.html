Juncus microcephalus f. conglobatus

Juncus microcephalus f. conglobatus, commonly known as the compact rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond margins, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for microorganisms.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for use in wetland restoration projects and ornamental landscaping.
