Jasminum divaricatum

Jasminum divaricatum, commonly known as the climbing jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters along its vine-like stems. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Jasminum divaricatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
