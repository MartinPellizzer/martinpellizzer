Jasminum bignoniaceum

Jasminum bignoniaceum, commonly known as the yellow jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and oval-shaped with a glossy surface.

The flowers are tubular at the base and open into five petals, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
