Joniris humilis

Joniris humilis, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. It has a compact growth habit, with flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale pink.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Joniris humilis is considered a rare and endangered species.
