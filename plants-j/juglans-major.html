Juglans major

Juglans major, commonly known as the Persian walnut, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Juglandaceae family and is characterized by its large, compound leaves and distinctive, elongated fruits. The tree produces edible nuts enclosed in a thick, husky outer layer. Its bark is dark and deeply furrowed, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Juglans major is valued for its timber and nuts, which are used in both culinary and industrial applications.
