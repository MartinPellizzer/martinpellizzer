Justicia amplifolia

Justicia amplifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, broad leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Justicia amplifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
