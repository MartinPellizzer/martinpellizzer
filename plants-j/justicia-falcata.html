Justicia falcata

Justicia falcata, commonly known as the sickleleaf false arum, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often deeply veined and have a waxy texture. The plant produces striking clusters of red or orange flowers that are surrounded by colorful bracts, making it highly ornamental. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its vibrant appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
