Jurinea ramosissima

Jurinea ramosissima, commonly known as the silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which helps reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its stems are typically branched and covered in fine, hairy structures that provide additional protection from desiccation.

Jurinea ramosissima is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor, well-drained habitats.
