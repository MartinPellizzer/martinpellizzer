Jatropha tuberosa

Jatropha tuberosa, commonly known as the tuberous jatropha, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features woody stems and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into spiny capsules containing toxic seeds. The plant is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are sometimes used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
