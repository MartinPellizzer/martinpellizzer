Jatropha gossypiifolia var. gossypiifolia

Jatropha gossypiifolia var. gossypiifolia, commonly known as the Crown of Thorns, is a succulent shrub native to Africa, Australia, and tropical Asia.

It features fleshy, spiny stems and leaves that are typically oval or lance-shaped. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into orange or red berries containing toxic seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy, drought-tolerant species.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
