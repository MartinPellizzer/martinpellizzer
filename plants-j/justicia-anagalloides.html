Justicia anagalloides

Justicia anagalloides, commonly known as the common justicia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
