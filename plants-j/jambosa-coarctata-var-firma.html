Jambosa coarctata var. firma

Jambosa coarctata var. firma, commonly known as the Brazilian plum or araçá-do-brejo, is a tropical shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It produces small, yellow to orange fruits that are typically consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine. The plant has simple, alternate leaves with a rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in yellow or green hues.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
