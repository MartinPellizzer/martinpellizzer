Jaegeria gracilis

Jaegeria gracilis, commonly known as the slender jaegeria, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features delicate, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and linear, growing in dense clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in spikes, usually in shades of white or pale green. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes make it both a useful ground cover and a potential weed in certain habitats.
