Juglans microcarpa var. stewartii

Juglans microcarpa var. stewartii, commonly known as Stewart's black walnut, is a subspecies of the black walnut tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its relatively small, dark-hued nuts and robust, deeply furrowed bark. The leaves are compound, typically with 15 to 23 lance-shaped leaflets, and the plant thrives in well-drained soils. It produces edible nuts that are valued for their flavor and use in cooking.

This variety is also notable for its ecological importance, providing food for wildlife and supporting local biodiversity.
