Jurinea transsylvanica

Jurinea transsylvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian sandwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Montiaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe, it thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, often found in alpine or steppe habitats.

Its medicinal properties have been historically noted, though its use is less common today.
