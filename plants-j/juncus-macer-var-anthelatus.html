Juncus macer var. anthelatus

Juncus macer var. anthelatus, commonly known as the purple rush, is a perennial wetland plant characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish-purple in color, especially during the growing season. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clustered spikes, typically in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils, making it a common sight in marshes, pond edges, and stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland environments.
