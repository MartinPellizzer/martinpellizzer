Juncus mustangensis

Juncus mustangensis, commonly known as the Mustang rush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and jointed stems. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and in wetlands. Its slender, cylindrical leaves are usually green, and it produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers in clusters.

Juncus mustangensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
