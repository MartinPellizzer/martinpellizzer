Jagera madagascariensis

Jagera madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry forest or savanna habitats.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
