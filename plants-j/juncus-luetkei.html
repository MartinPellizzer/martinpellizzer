Juncus luetkei

Juncus luetkei, commonly known as the Luetke's rush, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often used in floral arrangements. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters at the top of the stems. Juncus luetkei thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds, where it can tolerate waterlogged soil.

Its fibrous roots help stabilize the soil, making it ecologically valuable in wetland ecosystems.
