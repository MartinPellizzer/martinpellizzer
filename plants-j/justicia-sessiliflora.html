Justicia sessiliflora

Justicia sessiliflora, commonly known as Mexican verbena, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
