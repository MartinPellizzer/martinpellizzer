Jacksonia tarinensis

Jacksonia tarinensis, commonly known as the Tarin's knapweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white to pale pink petals. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or gravelly habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It has narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, bract-like structures around its flowerheads.

Jacksonia tarinensis is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
