Juncus lesueurii var. tracyi

Juncus lesueurii var. tracyi, commonly known as Tracy's rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and its relatively short stature.

Tracy's rush plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
