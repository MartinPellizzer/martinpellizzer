Jacobaea cannabifolia

Jacobaea cannabifolia, commonly known as the hemp-leaved ragwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has serrated, lance-shaped leaves that resemble those of hemp, giving it its common name.

Jacobaea cannabifolia produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.
