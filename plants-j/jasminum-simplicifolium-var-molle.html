Jasminum simplicifolium var. molle

Jasminum simplicifolium var. molle, commonly known as the soft-leaf jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The leaves are typically ovate and have a soft, velvety texture, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

This variety is a popular choice for gardens and as a houseplant due to its beauty and pleasant scent.
