Jasminum angustifolium var. hirsutum

Jasminum angustifolium var. hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy narrow-leaf jasmine, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the summer months. It is typically found in woodland areas and moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
