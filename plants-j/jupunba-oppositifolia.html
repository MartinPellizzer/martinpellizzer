Jupunba oppositifolia

Jupunba oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaf jupunba, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its opposite leaf arrangement, which is a key botanical feature. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Its unique morphology makes it an interesting subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
