Justicia bakeri

Justicia bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's flameflower, is a striking perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Justicia bakeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a container plant.
