Juncus ensifolius var. montanus

Juncus ensifolius var. montanus, commonly known as the mountain rush, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and rocky slopes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
