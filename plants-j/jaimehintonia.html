Jaimehintonia

Jaimehintonia, commonly known as the Hinton's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the humid rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, translucent petals and unique, almost ethereal appearance. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient absorption. Its flowers, which bloom only briefly, are pollinated by specialized insects, making it a fascinating subject for botanical study.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Jaimehintonia is critically endangered and is protected in many regions.
