Juncus ranarius

Juncus ranarius, commonly known as the rush plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves that form a compact, upright growth habit. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often found in shallow water or along the edges of ponds and streams. Its flowers are small, greenish-brown, and clustered in spikes, which are usually hidden within the leafy foliage.

Juncus ranarius plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
