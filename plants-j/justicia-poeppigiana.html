Justicia poeppigiana

Justicia poeppigiana, commonly known as the Mexican wheelcactus, is a succulent plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It features a short, thick stem with large, fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and vibrant flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
