Jasminum cardiophyllum

Jasminum cardiophyllum, commonly known as the heartleaf jasmine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and ability to twine around supports. It thrives in humid environments with partial shade and well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
