Juniperus communis var. pannonica

Juniperus communis var. pannonica, commonly known as the Pannonian juniper, is a subspecies of the common juniper native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with needle-like leaves that are often blue-gray in color. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
