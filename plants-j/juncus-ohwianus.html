Juncus ohwianus

Juncus ohwianus, commonly known as the California rush, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which can help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Juncus ohwianus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various insects and birds.
