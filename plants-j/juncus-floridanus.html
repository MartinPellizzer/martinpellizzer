Juncus floridanus

Juncus floridanus, commonly known as the Florida rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, usually appearing in late spring to early summer.

Juncus floridanus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
