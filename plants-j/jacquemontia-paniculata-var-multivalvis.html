Jacquemontia paniculata var. multivalvis

Jacquemontia paniculata var. multivalvis, commonly known as the multivalved jacquemontia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features hairy, alternate leaves that are often heart-shaped or ovate. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a distinctive, multivalved capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight.
