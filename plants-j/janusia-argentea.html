Janusia argentea

Janusia argentea, commonly known as silverleaf saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs that reflect light and help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in late spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
