Jambosa alba

Jambosa alba, commonly known as the white jambosa, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces edible, white fruits that are similar in appearance to lychees but have a unique, sweet flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Jambosa alba is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its fruit, which is enjoyed in local cuisines.
