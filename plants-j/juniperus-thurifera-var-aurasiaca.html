Juniperus thurifera var. aurasiaca

Juniperus thurifera var. aurasiaca, commonly known as the Asian juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and produces small, blue-black berries. The plant is evergreen, with a dense, compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
