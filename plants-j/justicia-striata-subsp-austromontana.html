Justicia striata subsp. austromontana

Justicia striata subsp.

austromontana, commonly known as the mountain justicia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
