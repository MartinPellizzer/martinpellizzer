Jasminum grandiflorum

Jasminum grandiflorum, commonly known as the common jasmine, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China, India, and the Middle East.

It is a shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring opposite, simple, and often fragrant leaves. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, emitting a strong, sweet fragrance. It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
