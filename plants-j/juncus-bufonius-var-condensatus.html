Juncus bufonius var. condensatus

Juncus bufonius var. condensatus, commonly known as the dense marsh rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in dense clusters, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with cylindrical, hollow stems that are often reddish or greenish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, which are usually greenish or brownish in hue. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form extensive mats in shallow water or moist soil.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
