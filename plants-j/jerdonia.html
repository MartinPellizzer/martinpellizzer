Jerdonia

Jerdonia, commonly known as the "bluebell of the desert," is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet bell-shaped flowers. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves that are adapted to conserve water in dry environments. Jerdonia typically blooms in spring, producing clusters of fragrant flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its resilience to drought makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping in hot, dry climates.
