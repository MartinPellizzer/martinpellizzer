Jasminum longipetalum

Jasminum longipetalum, commonly known as the broad-leaved jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant has oval, glossy leaves that are opposite in arrangement, giving it a lush, dense appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

Jasminum longipetalum is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic uses.
