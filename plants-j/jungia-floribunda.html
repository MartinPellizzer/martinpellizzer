Jungia floribunda

Jungia floribunda, commonly known as the purple beauty, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a velvety texture with a deep green color.

Jungia floribunda thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
