Jambolifera acidula

Jambolifera acidula, commonly known as the sour date tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. The tree bears fruit that is typically harvested when it is still unripe, giving it a tart flavor. The fruit is rich in nutrients and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptability to various soil types and its role in local ecosystems.
