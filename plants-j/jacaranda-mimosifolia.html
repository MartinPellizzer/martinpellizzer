Jacaranda mimosifolia

Jacaranda mimosifolia, commonly known as the jacaranda tree, is a deciduous legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in spring. The tree has feathery, bipinnate leaves and produces long, bean-like pods that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
