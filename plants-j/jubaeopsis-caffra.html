Jubaeopsis caffra

Jubaeopsis caffra, commonly known as the African blackwood, is a small, evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood, which is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Jubaeopsis caffra is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional African medicine.
