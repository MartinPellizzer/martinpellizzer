Juncus haussknechtii

Juncus haussknechtii, commonly known as the Mexican rush, is a flowering plant species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spikes. It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.

Juncus haussknechtii is also valued for its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
