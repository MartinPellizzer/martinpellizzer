Jaborosa laciniata

Jaborosa laciniata, commonly known as the jagged leaf jaborosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, serrated leaves and produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
