Juncus anceps f. coarctatus

Juncus anceps f. coarctatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in spikes, and are wind-pollinated.
