Juncus compressus condensatus

Juncus compressus condensatus, commonly known as the dense rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It features dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, cylindrical leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes during the summer months, which are clustered closely together. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in areas with standing water or high humidity.

Its compact growth habit makes it an effective stabilizer of shoreline ecosystems.
