Juniperus scopulorum var. viridifolia

Juniperus scopulorum var. viridifolia, commonly known as the Colorado spruce, is a variant of the Rocky Mountain juniper.

It is characterized by its dense, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The foliage is often a striking blue-green color, which gives it its common name. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is native to the western United States.

This evergreen shrub or small tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh environments.
