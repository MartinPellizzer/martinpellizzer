Juncus tiehmii

Juncus tiehmii, commonly known as Tiehm's rush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and stream banks.

Juncus tiehmii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
