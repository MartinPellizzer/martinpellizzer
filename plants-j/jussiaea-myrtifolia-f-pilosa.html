Jussiaea myrtifolia f. pilosa

Jussiaea myrtifolia f. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy myrtle, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny white flowers, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
