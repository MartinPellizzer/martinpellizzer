Jacaranda macrocarpa

Jacaranda macrocarpa, commonly known as the blue jacaranda, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in spring. The tree has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets and produces large, woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark is smooth and light gray, becoming fissured with age.
