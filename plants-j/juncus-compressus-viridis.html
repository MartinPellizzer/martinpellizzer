Juncus compressus viridis

Juncus compressus viridis, commonly known as the green rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, greenish flowers. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense mats, contributing to soil stabilization in wetland habitats.
