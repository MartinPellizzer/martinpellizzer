Jacksonia aculeata

Jacksonia aculeata, commonly known as the Christmas bush, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a shrub-like structure with woody stems. It produces small, spiky seed heads that are adapted for wind dispersal.
