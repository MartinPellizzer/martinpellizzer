Juglans frutescens

Juglans frutescens, commonly known as the red chestnut or Chinese walnut, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are either male or female, with the female flowers being clustered in catkins. The tree's leaves are alternate, compound, and have 5 to 9 leaflets with serrated edges. Its fruit is a spiny husk enclosing a single edible nut, which is rich in oils and nutrients.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its edible seeds.
