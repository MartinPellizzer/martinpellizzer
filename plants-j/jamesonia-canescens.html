Jamesonia canescens

Jamesonia canescens, commonly known as the white-flowered Jamesonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its leaves are elliptical and covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence, adding a distinctive visual texture to the plant.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
