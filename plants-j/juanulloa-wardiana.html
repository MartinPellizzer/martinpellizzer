Juanulloa wardiana

Juanulloa wardiana, commonly known as the Brazilian palm, is a striking tropical plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a medium-sized palm characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which develop into dark purple, berry-like fruits. Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ornamental contexts.

Juanulloa wardiana is also valued for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
