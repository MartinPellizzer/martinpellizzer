Juncus warakensis

Juncus warakensis, commonly known as the Warakensis rush, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Namibia.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Due to its limited distribution, Juncus warakensis is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
