Jarilla heterophylla

Jarilla heterophylla, commonly known as Mexican tea or desert tea, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and distinctive, often variegated foliage. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies, and it is known for its aromatic properties.

Jarilla heterophylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal in drought-tolerant landscaping.
