Juliania

Juliania, commonly known as the marsh hibiscus, is a genus of flowering plants in the Malvaceae family.

These plants are native to the southeastern United States and parts of Central and South America. They are characterized by their showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. Juliania species typically grow in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

The leaves are often heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to their distinctive appearance.
