Jasione montana var. heldreichii

Jasione montana var. heldreichii, commonly known as the Balkan bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is adapted to mountainous environments. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
