Juncoides

Juncoides, commonly known as the spike-rush, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Juncaginaceae.

These plants are typically found in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond edges, where they thrive in moist to water-saturated soils. Juncoides species are characterized by their grass-like appearance, with cylindrical stems and spike-like clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers. The leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and the plants often form dense mats that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

They play an important role in aquatic and wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
