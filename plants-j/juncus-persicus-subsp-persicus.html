Juncus persicus subsp. persicus

Juncus persicus subsp.

persicus, commonly known as Persian rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows.

The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, clustered spikelets of greenish or brownish flowers. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and usually have a slight waxy coating.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
