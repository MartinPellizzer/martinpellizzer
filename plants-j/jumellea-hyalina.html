Jumellea hyalina

Jumellea hyalina, commonly known as the glassy jumellea, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or pale yellow, adapted for pollination by wind or insects. Its growth form is typically shrub-like, with multiple stems arising from the base.

Jumellea hyalina is well-suited to extreme conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
