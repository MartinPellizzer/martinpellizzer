Justicia oncodes

Justicia oncodes, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees, making it a valuable addition to gardens.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, thriving in warm, humid climates.
