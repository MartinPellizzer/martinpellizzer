Justicia pseudobetonica

Justicia pseudobetonica, commonly known as the Mexican flameflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees, contributing to its ecological value.
