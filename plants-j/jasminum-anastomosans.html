Jasminum anastomosans

Jasminum anastomosans, commonly known as the African jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its stems are woody and can grow as either a shrub or a climbing vine, depending on the growing conditions.
