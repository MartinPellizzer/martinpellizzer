Jambosa cuspidata

Jambosa cuspidata, commonly known as the African pear, is a tropical plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, featuring a straight trunk and a broad, rounded crown. The leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate with a pointed tip, while the flowers are small, white, and clustered in panicles. The fruit is a yellow or orange, smooth-skinned drupe that contains a single large seed.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.
