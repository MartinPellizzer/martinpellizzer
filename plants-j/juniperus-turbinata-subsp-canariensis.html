Juniperus turbinata subsp. canariensis

Juniperus turbinata subsp.

canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Island juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and conical or cylindrical shape.

The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, and it produces small, berry-like cones that are edible. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the volcanic terrain of the Canary Islands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
