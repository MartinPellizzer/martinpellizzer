Jambosa baladensis

Jambosa baladensis, commonly known as the Baladensis jambosa, is a rare flowering plant native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often near streams or in dense forest understories. Its fruit, which is a small, round drupe, contains a single seed and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Due to habitat loss, Jambosa baladensis is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
