Juncus tokubuchii

Juncus tokubuchii, commonly known as the Tokubuchii rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Japan.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, preferring moist soil conditions. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that are often hollow, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowering spikes consist of small, greenish-white flowers that appear in late summer.

Juncus tokubuchii is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its upright growth habit and ability to thrive in waterlogged areas.
