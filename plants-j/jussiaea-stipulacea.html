Jussiaea stipulacea

Jussiaea stipulacea, commonly known as the slender water-plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often submerged or floating, adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and helps stabilize soil in marshy areas, contributing to wetland ecosystem health.

Jussiaea stipulacea is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater bodies, such as ponds and marshes.
