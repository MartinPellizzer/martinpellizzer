Juncus xiphioides var. macranthus

Juncus xiphioides var. macranthus, commonly known as the giant rush, is a tall, grass-like plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features long, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height, often forming dense clumps. The plant produces showy, clustered spikelets of white or pinkish flowers, which are arranged in panicle-like structures. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and typically have a bluish-green hue.

This variety is adapted to moist soils and is often found in marshes, pond edges, and along streams.
