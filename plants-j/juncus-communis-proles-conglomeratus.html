Juncus communis proles conglomeratus

Juncus communis proles conglomeratus, commonly known as the clustered rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves that form tight, compact growths. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikes, which are often hidden among the foliage. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, pond edges, and stream banks.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense stands, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
