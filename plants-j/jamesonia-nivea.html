Jamesonia nivea

Jamesonia nivea, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Jamesonia nivea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in woodland gardens.
