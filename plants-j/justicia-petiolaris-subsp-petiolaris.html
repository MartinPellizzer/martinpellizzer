Justicia petiolaris subsp. petiolaris

Justicia petiolaris subsp.

petiolaris, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America. It is characterized by its striking red-orange bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, typically blooming during the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its foliage is broad and glossy, adding to its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.
