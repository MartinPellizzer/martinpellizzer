Justicia leptocarpa

Justicia leptocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

The flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
