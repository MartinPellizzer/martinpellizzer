Jambosa reticulata

Jambosa reticulata, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical plant native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant produces a fruit with a rough, green exterior and a sweet, white, sponge-like pulp that contains numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is often consumed fresh or used in culinary and medicinal applications.
