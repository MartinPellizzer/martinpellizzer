Justicia kanal

Justicia kanal, commonly known as the flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular, red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in tropical and subtropical regions.
