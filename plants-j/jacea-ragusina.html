Jacea ragusina

Jacea ragusina, commonly known as the Ragusa bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its stems are upright and branched.

Jacea ragusina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
