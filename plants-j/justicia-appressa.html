Justicia appressa

Justicia appressa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf false heather, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, or white. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, Justicia appressa is often cultivated in warm climates.
