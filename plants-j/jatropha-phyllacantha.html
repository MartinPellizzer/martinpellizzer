Jatropha phyllacantha

Jatropha phyllacantha, commonly known as the African Crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy inflorescences that resemble a crown, composed of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with hairy stems and alternate leaves. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

The plant is also known for its toxic properties, with all parts containing latex that can cause skin irritation.
