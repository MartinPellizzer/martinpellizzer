Juncus geniculatus subsp. arthrophyllus

Juncus geniculatus subsp.

arthrophyllus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow leaves. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or reddish in color, which contain the flowers. Its root system is fibrous and helps stabilize soil in wetland habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
