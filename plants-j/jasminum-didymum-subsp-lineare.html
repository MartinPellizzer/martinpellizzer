Jasminum didymum subsp. lineare

Jasminum didymum subsp.

lineare, commonly known as the linear jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, often found in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers emit a sweet, intoxicating aroma, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated leaf shape and slightly different floral structure compared to other jasminum species.
