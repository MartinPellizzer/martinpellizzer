Jungia altissima

Jungia altissima, commonly known as the tall jungle grass, is a tall, perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows to heights of up to 4 meters, featuring long, narrow leaves and dense, fibrous roots. The plant is characterized by its robust growth habit and ability to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it can become invasive in certain ecosystems.
