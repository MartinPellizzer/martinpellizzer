Juglans australis

Juglans australis, commonly known as the Australian black walnut, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the walnut family, Juglandaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and edible nuts. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. Its fruits are encased in a thick, spiky husk and contain one or two edible kernels.

The plant is valued for its hard, durable wood and its role in local ecosystems.
