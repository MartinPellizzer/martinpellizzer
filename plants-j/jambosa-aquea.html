Jambosa aquea

Jambosa aquea, commonly known as the water jambosa, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are similar in appearance to lychees but have a unique, mildly sweet flavor. It thrives in moist, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Jambosa aquea is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its fruit, which is used in local cuisines.
