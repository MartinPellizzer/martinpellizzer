Jacaranda caucana subsp. glabrata

Jacaranda caucana subsp.

glabrata, commonly known as the smooth jacaranda, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern South America. It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are typically smooth and glossy.

The tree produces striking clusters of violet-blue flowers during the summer months, which are followed by flat, woody pods containing seeds. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often planted in gardens and urban landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
