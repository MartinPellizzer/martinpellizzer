Justicia graphophylla

Justicia graphophylla, commonly known as the red-flowered spiderling, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It prefers well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Justicia graphophylla is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
