Jasminum obtusifolium

Jasminum obtusifolium, commonly known as the obtuse-leaf jasmine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy on the underside. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Jasminum obtusifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
