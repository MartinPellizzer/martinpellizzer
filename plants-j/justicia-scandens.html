Justicia scandens

Justicia scandens, commonly known as the climbing justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange in color, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Justicia scandens is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant blooms and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
