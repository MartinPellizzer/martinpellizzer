Justicia strigilis

Justicia strigilis, commonly known as the velvetleaf justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces striking clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in suitable climates.
