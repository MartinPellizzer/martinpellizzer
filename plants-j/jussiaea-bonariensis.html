Jussiaea bonariensis

Jussiaea bonariensis, commonly known as the Patagonian marsh plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and along the edges of lakes and rivers. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of hair.

Jussiaea bonariensis is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
