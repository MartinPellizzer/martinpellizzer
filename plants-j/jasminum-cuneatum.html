Jasminum cuneatum

Jasminum cuneatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a glossy green surface.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
