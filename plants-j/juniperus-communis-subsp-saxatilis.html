Juniperus communis subsp. saxatilis

Juniperus communis subsp.

saxatilis, commonly known as the stone juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves and small, berry-like cones that are typically blue or black in color.

This subspecies thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous or coastal areas. It is evergreen, providing year-round foliage and is commonly used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its aromatic wood and historical use in traditional medicine.
