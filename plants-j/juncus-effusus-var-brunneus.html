Juncus effusus var. brunneus

Juncus effusus var. brunneus, commonly known as the brown rush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that are typically reddish-brown in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of greenish-yellow flowers in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions, often found in marshes, pond edges, and along stream banks.

Its dense growth helps stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
