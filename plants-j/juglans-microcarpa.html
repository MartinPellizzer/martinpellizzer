Juglans microcarpa

Juglans microcarpa, commonly known as the little walnut or Mexican black walnut, is a deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Juglandaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive walnut-like fruits. The tree produces small, hard-shelled nuts that are encased in a thick, spiny husk. Its bark is typically dark and fissured, with a rough texture.

This species is valued for its timber and has also been used in traditional medicine.
