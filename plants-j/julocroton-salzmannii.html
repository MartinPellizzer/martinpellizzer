Julocroton salzmannii

Julocroton salzmannii, commonly known as the Zanzibar glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has palmate leaves with lobed segments, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found growing near the sea.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for tropical gardens and landscapes.
