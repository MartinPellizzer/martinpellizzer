Juniperus communis var. aurea

Juniperus communis var. aurea, commonly known as the golden juniper, is a variant of the common juniper that exhibits striking golden foliage.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in rocky or sandy soils. The leaves are needle-like, dense, and emit a fragrant aroma, typical of the Juniperus genus. It produces small blue-black berries that are utilized in culinary and medicinal applications.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
