Juncus aemulans

Juncus aemulans, commonly known as the mimic rush, is a flowering plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense stands that stabilize shorelines. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Juncus aemulans plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
