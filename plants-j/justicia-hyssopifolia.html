Justicia hyssopifolia

Justicia hyssopifolia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its botanical characteristics include opposite leaf arrangement and showy inflorescences that give it a mist-like appearance.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscape design for its ornamental value.
