Jatropha palmata

Jatropha palmata, commonly known as the red torch tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red bracts that resemble torches, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into capsule fruits. Jatropha palmata thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or near water sources.

It is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
