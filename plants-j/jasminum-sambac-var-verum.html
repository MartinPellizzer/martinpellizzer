Jasminum sambac var. verum

Jasminum sambac var. verum, commonly known as the Arabian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often releasing a strong, sweet scent at night. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves and is typically shrub-like in growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

This variety is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and in perfumery due to its aromatic properties.
