Justicia pectinata

Justicia pectinata, commonly known as the flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble flames, composed of numerous small, tubular red or orange flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

Justicia pectinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
