Justicia ligustrina

Justicia ligustrina, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, blue-violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Justicia ligustrina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
