Juncus phaeocephalus var. phaeocephalus

Juncus phaeocephalus var. phaeocephalus, commonly known as the black-headed rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that are typically dark green to reddish in color, often with a dark brown or black tip. The plant produces dense clusters of small, pale green to yellowish flowers at the top of the stems. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This species is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
