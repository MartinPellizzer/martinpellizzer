Justicia cuneata subsp. cuneata

Justicia cuneata subsp.

cuneata, commonly known as the narrowleaf flame flower, is a perennial shrub native to the southern United States and Mexico. It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange.

The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy soils. It is often used in ornamental horticulture for its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds, contributing to ecological diversity.
