Jarilla heterophylla subsp. heterophylla

Jarilla heterophylla subsp.

heterophylla, commonly known as desert milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and compound leaves that are often lobed or divided.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale purple to white in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Its fibrous root system allows it to survive in harsh, drought-prone conditions.
