Jatropha rufescens

Jatropha rufescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed physic nut, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing toxic seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

Jatropha rufescens is known for its ornamental value and has been studied for potential uses in traditional medicine.
