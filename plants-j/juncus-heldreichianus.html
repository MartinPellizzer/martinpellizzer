Juncus heldreichianus

Juncus heldreichianus, commonly known as the Balkan rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along stream banks, wet meadows, and other wetland habitats. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish-brown in color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear greenish-yellow flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is adapted to thrive in environments with high humidity and good drainage.

Juncus heldreichianus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
