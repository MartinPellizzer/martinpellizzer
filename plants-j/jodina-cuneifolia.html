Jodina cuneifolia

Jodina cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf jodina, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that bloom in early spring, often in shades of white or pale pink. Jodina cuneifolia thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
