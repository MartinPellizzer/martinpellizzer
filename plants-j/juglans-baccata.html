Juglans baccata

Juglans baccata, commonly known as the Persian walnut, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with 15 to 23 leaflets and its distinctive, edible nuts enclosed in a rough, spiky husk. The tree produces flowers in early spring, with male and female flowers occurring on separate trees. Its wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for furniture and construction.

The plant also has historical significance in traditional medicine and as a food source.
