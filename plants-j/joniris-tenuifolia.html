Joniris tenuifolia

Joniris tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender-leafed fern, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves that are often arranged in a graceful, cascading pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
