Justicia canaliculata

Justicia canaliculata, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, cobalt-blue bracts that resemble mist. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, surrounded by showy foliage. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Justicia canaliculata is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant color and drought tolerance once established.
