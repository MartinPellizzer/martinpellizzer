Jacksonia juncea

Jacksonia juncea, commonly known as the New Zealand daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, yellow central disk surrounded by white to pale blue ray florets. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is well adapted to the temperate climate of its native habitat. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Jacksonia juncea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant and delicate appearance.
