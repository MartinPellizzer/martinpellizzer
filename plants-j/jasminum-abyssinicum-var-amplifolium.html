Jasminum abyssinicum var. amplifolium

Jasminum abyssinicum var. amplifolium, commonly known as the Abyssinian jasmine, is a shrub native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a strong, sweet aroma. The plant has simple, opposite, and lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

This variety is distinct from other jasmine species due to its larger foliage and unique floral structure.
