Justicia diminuta

Justicia diminuta, commonly known as the dwarf flame flower, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 30 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers, which bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow, are adapted to attract hummingbirds and other pollinators.

Justicia diminuta thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
