Jacobaea cernua

Jacobaea cernua, commonly known as the creeping tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial with a mat-forming habit, featuring yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and is often found in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides and meadows. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic, sometimes pungent scent.

Jacobaea cernua is also recognized for its historical use in herbal medicine and as a natural pest repellent.
