Jamesonia stuebelii

Jamesonia stuebelii, commonly known as the Stuebel's lily, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features rhizomatous growth and produces lance-shaped, dark green leaves. Its flowers are typically pink to red, with six tepals and a distinctive, spurred structure.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
