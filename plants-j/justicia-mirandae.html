Justicia mirandae

Justicia mirandae, commonly known as the Miranda's glorybower, is a striking flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in terminal clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

Due to its ornamental value, Justicia mirandae is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its striking appearance and ecological significance.
