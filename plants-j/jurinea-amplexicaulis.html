Jurinea amplexicaulis

Jurinea amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping jurinea, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant features a basal rosette of fleshy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in its harsh environment. Its small, yellowish flowers appear in early summer, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise rugged appearance.

Due to its adaptations, it is well-suited for xerophytic gardens and ecological restoration projects.
