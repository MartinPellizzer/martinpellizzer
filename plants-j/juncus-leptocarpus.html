Juncus leptocarpus

Juncus leptocarpus, commonly known as the slender-spike rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in the northern hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist soils, often in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which can help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Juncus leptocarpus plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
