Justicia breedlovei

Justicia breedlovei, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a strikingly ornamental shrub native to Mexico.

It features showy, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in warm climates.
