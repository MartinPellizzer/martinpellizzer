Jasminum longisepalum

Jasminum longisepalum, commonly known as the long-sepal jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its sepals are notably elongated, giving it its scientific name.
