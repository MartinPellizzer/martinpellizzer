Jambolifera arborea

Jambolifera arborea, commonly known as the Java apple tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or green when unripe and turn red when mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a dark green upper surface.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
