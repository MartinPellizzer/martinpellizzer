Juncus articulatus var. pallescens

Juncus articulatus var. pallescens, commonly known as the pale rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features cylindrical, grass-like leaves that are typically pale green to bluish-gray in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale yellow to greenish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, pond edges, and along streams.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
