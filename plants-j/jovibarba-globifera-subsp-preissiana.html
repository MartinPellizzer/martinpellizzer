Jovibarba globifera subsp. preissiana

Jovibarba globifera subsp.

preissiana, commonly known as the Preiss's rockrose, is a perennial succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves that are adapted to store water.

The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping in dry climates.
