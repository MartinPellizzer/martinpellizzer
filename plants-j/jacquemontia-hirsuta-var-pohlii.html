Jacquemontia hirsuta var. pohlii

Jacquemontia hirsuta var. pohlii, commonly known as the Pohli's hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with distinctive hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and ecological studies.
