Justicia linaria

Justicia linaria, commonly known as the narrowleaf false dragonhead, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, tubular flowers. The plant produces showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular plant in native plant gardens.
