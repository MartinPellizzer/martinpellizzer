Justicia membranacea

Justicia membranacea, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Mexican verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Justicia membranacea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
