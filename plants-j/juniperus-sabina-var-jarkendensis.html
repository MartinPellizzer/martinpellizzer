Juniperus sabina var. jarkendensis

Juniperus sabina var. jarkendensis, commonly known as the Jarkend Desert juniper, is a rare and endemic species native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

This shrub or small tree is characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage and small, berry-like cones that mature to a blue-black color. It thrives in harsh, dry environments, demonstrating remarkable adaptability to extreme temperatures and limited water availability. The plant's woody structure and aromatic resin contribute to its resilience against pests and environmental stress.

Its ecological significance lies in providing habitat and food for local wildlife in its native desert ecosystems.
