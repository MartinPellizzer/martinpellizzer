Jasminum primulinum

Jasminum primulinum, commonly known as the golden jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually hairy leaves that are oval to lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires plenty of sunlight to flourish.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
