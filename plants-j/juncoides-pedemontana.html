Juncoides pedemontana

Juncoides pedemontana, commonly known as the Alpine rush, is a perennial plant native to high-altitude regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine meadows and mountainous areas. It produces small, greenish flowers in dense spikes, which are usually arranged in clusters at the top of the stem.

Juncoides pedemontana is adapted to cold climates and is often used in ecological restoration projects in alpine environments.
