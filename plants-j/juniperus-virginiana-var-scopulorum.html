Juniperus virginiana var. scopulorum

Juniperus virginiana var. scopulorum, commonly known as the rock juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are often dark blue when mature, containing toxic seeds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in providing habitat and food for wildlife.
