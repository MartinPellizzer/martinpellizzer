Juncus castaneus f. pallidus

Juncus castaneus f. pallidus, commonly known as the pale rush, is a variant of the common rush species characterized by its lighter-colored foliage.

It is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia, typically found in wet or marshy habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often pale green or yellowish in color. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, which are clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

This form is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist environments.
