Japonicalia delphiniifolia

Japonicalia delphiniifolia, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features attractive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
