Jurinea ancyrensis

Jurinea ancyrensis, commonly known as the Armenian sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It is a low-growing, perennial plant that thrives in sandy and arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically linear and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Jurinea ancyrensis is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
