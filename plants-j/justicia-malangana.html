Justicia malangana

Justicia malangana, commonly known as Malayan flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has lance-shaped, opposite leaves that are often hairy beneath, and it thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
