Jarava arundinacea

Jarava arundinacea, commonly known as bamboo grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and fine, linear leaves that give it a grass-like appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in restoration projects to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
