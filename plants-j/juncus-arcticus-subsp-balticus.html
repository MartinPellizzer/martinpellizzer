Juncus arcticus subsp. balticus

Juncus arcticus subsp.

balticus, commonly known as the Baltic rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe and parts of Russia. It typically grows in wet, marshy environments and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and tall, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered at the tips of its stems. It is well adapted to cold climates and can thrive in poorly drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of Arctic and subarctic ecosystems.
