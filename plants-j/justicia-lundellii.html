Justicia lundellii

Justicia lundellii, commonly known as the Lundell's flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking tubular red or orange flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.

The showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds, contributing to its ecological significance.
