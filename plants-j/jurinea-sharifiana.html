Jurinea sharifiana

Jurinea sharifiana, commonly known as the Afghan sand flower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and succulent leaves. The plant is well adapted to harsh, dry environments, with deep roots that help it survive in sandy soils. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom during the summer months.

Jurinea sharifiana is often found in desert and semi-desert habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing sand dunes.
