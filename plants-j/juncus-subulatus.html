Juncus subulatus

Juncus subulatus, commonly known as the slender rush, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and tall, spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist or wet environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually arranged in dense clusters at the tip of the stem.

Juncus subulatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
