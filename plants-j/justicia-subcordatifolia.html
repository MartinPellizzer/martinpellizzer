Justicia subcordatifolia

Justicia subcordatifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf false arum, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and deep green, with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by colorful bracts, typically in shades of red or purple. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
