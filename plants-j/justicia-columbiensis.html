Justicia columbiensis

Justicia columbiensis, commonly known as the Columbia flameflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
