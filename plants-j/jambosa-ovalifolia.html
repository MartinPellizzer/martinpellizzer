Jambosa ovalifolia

Jambosa ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf jambosa, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its lanceolate, glossy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, fleshy fruits resembling small berries. Its botanical features make it adaptable to various soil types and climates, though it prefers well-drained, moist environments.

Jambosa ovalifolia is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its potential use in traditional medicine.
