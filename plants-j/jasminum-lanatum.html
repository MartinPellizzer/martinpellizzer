Jasminum lanatum

Jasminum lanatum, commonly known as the hairy jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive texture. The plant produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

Jasminum lanatum is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic benefits.
