Jurinea filicifolia

Jurinea filicifolia, commonly known as the fern-leaved jurinea, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to drought conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
