Juncus biflorus f. adinus

Juncus biflorus f. adinus, commonly known as the Adinean rush, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, cylindrical leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water gardens and wetland restoration projects.
