Juncus phaeocarpus

Juncus phaeocarpus, commonly known as the black fruit rush, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish or purplish in color. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are clustered at the tips of its stems. It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams.

Juncus phaeocarpus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
