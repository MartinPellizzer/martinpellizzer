Jasminum reticulatum

Jasminum reticulatum, commonly known as the Persian jasmine, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Jasminum reticulatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in gardens and as a hedge plant.
