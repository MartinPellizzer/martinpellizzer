Jacaranda longiflora

Jacaranda longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered jacaranda, is a deciduous tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its ability to provide shade and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
