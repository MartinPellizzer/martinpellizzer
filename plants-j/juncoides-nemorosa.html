Juncoides nemorosa

Juncoides nemorosa, commonly known as the wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands.

Botanically, it belongs to the Juncaceae family, which includes other rush-like plants.
