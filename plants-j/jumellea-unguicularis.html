Jumellea unguicularis

Jumellea unguicularis, commonly known as the red-flowered jumellea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring woody stems and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces striking clusters of bright red flowers, which are tubular in shape and attract pollinators such as birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Jumellea unguicularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
