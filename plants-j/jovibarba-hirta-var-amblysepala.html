Jovibarba hirta var. amblysepala

Jovibarba hirta var. amblysepala, commonly known as the woolly rock crasswort, is a perennial succulent native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, woolly leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to store water and reduce transpiration. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
