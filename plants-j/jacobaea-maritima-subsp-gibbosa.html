Jacobaea maritima subsp. gibbosa

Jacobaea maritima subsp.

gibbosa, commonly known as the sea ragwort, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and hairy, lobed leaves adapted to salty, windy environments.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along shorelines and is often found in maritime habitats. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.

However, it can be toxic to livestock, making it a concern in grazing areas.
