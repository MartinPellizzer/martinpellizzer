Justicia callistachya

Justicia callistachya, commonly known as the flame flower or red flame plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a slight pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warm climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which can last from spring through summer.
