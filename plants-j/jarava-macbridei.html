Jarava macbridei

Jarava macbridei, commonly known as the MacBride's jarava, is a perennial grass native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It typically grows in coastal and dry forest habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Jarava macbridei plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native invertebrates.
