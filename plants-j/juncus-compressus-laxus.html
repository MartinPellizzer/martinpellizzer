Juncus compressus laxus

Juncus compressus laxus, commonly known as the slender rush, is a species of flowering plant in the family Juncaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is often found in temperate climates.

The plant's structure and growth habits make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
