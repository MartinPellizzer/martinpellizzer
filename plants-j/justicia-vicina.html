Justicia vicina

Justicia vicina, commonly known as the small flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular, red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an herb or shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds and bees, and it thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sun exposure.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
