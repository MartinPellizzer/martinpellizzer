Jacquemontia caerulea

Jacquemontia caerulea, commonly known as blue jacquemontia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, trumpet-shaped blue to violet flowers. The plant has hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its flowers bloom in the spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
