Juncus debilis

Juncus debilis, commonly known as the slender rush, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and typically grows in shallow water or moist soil. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish flowers in spikes during the summer months. It plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Its adaptability to waterlogged conditions makes it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds.
