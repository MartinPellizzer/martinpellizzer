Juniperus oxycedrus f. densa

Juniperus oxycedrus f. densa, commonly known as the dense juniper, is a subspecies of the common juniper, characterized by its compact, dense growth habit.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, scale-like leaves that are dark green and closely arranged, giving it a dense appearance. Its cones, which are typically blue or black, contain the aromatic seeds used in various traditional and commercial applications.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
