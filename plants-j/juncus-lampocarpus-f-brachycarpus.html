Juncus lampocarpus f. brachycarpus

Juncus lampocarpus f. brachycarpus, commonly known as the short-carpelled rush, is a botanical variant of the broader Juncus lampocarpus species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. Native to specific regions of California, it thrives in wetland or moist soil environments. The plant's most distinguishing feature is its shortened, clustered fruiting structures, which set it apart from other forms of the species.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and small invertebrates.
