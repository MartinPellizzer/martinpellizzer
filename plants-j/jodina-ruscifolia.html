Jodina ruscifolia

Jodina ruscifolia, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant features upright, branching stems covered with small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually green with a waxy coating. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in umbels, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Jodina ruscifolia is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh, arid environments.
