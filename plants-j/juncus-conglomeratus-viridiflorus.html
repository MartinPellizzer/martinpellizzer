Juncus conglomeratus viridiflorus

Juncus conglomeratus viridiflorus, commonly known as the greenflower rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Juncaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and colonize new areas.

The species is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife.
