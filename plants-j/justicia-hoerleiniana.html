Justicia hoerleiniana

Justicia hoerleiniana, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The flowers attract hummingbirds and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
