Juniperus arizonica

Juniperus arizonica, commonly known as Arizona juniper, is a evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves and small, berry-like cones that mature to a dark blue color. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in dry conditions. It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

The wood of Arizona juniper is also valued for its durability and is used in traditional crafts and construction.
