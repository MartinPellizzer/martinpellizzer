Juncus supinus f. confervaceus

Juncus supinus f. confervaceus, commonly known as the mossy rush, is a variant of the common rush that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a perennial plant belonging to the Juncaceae family, typically found in wet or marshy environments. The plant features dense, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a fine, moss-like coating, giving it its distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

This form is notable for its ability to thrive in moist conditions and its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
