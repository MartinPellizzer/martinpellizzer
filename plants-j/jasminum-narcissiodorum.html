Jasminum narcissiodorum

Jasminum narcissiodorum, commonly known as the narcissus jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with opposite, simple, and ovate leaves. Its flowers emit a strong, sweet fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
