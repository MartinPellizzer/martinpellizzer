Juncus husnotii

Juncus husnotii, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands, stream banks, and marshes. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 1 inch in width. It produces small, greenish flower clusters that bloom in late summer.

Juncus husnotii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
