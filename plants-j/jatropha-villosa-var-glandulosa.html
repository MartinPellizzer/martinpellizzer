Jatropha villosa var. glandulosa

Jatropha villosa var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular jatropha, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which are characteristic of its varietal form. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers followed by toxic, orange-red berries. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in semi-arid conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for medicinal use, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and research settings.
