Juncus compressus f. laxus

Juncus compressus f. laxus, commonly known as the looseleaf rush, is a variant of the common rush species.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically more loosely arranged compared to the typical form. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.
