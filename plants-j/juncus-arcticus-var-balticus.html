Juncus arcticus var. balticus

Juncus arcticus var. balticus, commonly known as the Baltic rush, is a perennial plant native to arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered spikes of white or greenish flowers. This plant thrives in wet, boggy environments and is often found in tundra and coastal areas. Its fibrous stems are used in basket-making and traditional crafts by indigenous peoples.

The species is adapted to cold climates and has a low-growing, mat-forming habit.
