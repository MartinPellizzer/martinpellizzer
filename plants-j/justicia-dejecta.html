Justicia dejecta

Justicia dejecta, commonly known as the weeping justicia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically blue or violet in color and are arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
