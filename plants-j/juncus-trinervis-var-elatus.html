Juncus trinervis var. elatus

Juncus trinervis var. elatus, commonly known as the tall rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically growing in dense clusters at the base.

This variety is adapted to moist soils and is often found in marshes, pond edges, and other aquatic habitats.
