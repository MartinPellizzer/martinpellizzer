Juncus diemii

Juncus diemii, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 1 millimeter wide. It produces small, greenish flowers in dense spikes, which are typically found at the tips of the stems.

Juncus diemii plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
