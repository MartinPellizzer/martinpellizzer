Jurinea linearifolia

Jurinea linearifolia, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to saline and windy environments. Its flowering period typically occurs from June to August, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Jurinea linearifolia is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
