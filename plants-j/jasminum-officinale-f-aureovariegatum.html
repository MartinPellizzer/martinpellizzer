Jasminum officinale f. aureovariegatum

Jasminum officinale f. aureovariegatum, commonly known as the golden variegated jasmine, is a cultivar of the common jasmine plant.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves that display shades of yellow and white, contrasting with the typical green foliage. The plant produces fragrant white flowers similar to its parent species, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. It is often grown as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.

This variety thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners and landscapers.
