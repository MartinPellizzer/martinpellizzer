Jacquemontia solanifolia

Jacquemontia solanifolia, commonly known as the yellow vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes and potatoes, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate and hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Jacquemontia solanifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant floral display.
