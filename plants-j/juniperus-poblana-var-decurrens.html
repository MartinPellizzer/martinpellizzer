Juniperus poblana var. decurrens

Juniperus poblana var. decurrens, commonly known as the Mexican juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that is typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, cone-like fruits that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in local ecosystems and horticultural practices.
