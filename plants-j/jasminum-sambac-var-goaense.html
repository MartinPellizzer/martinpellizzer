Jasminum sambac var. goaense

Jasminum sambac var. goaense, commonly known as Goa jasmine, is a fragrant flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its small, white, star-shaped flowers that emit a sweet, intoxicating aroma. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, with opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is commonly cultivated for ornamental and aromatic purposes.

Its flowers are widely used in perfumery, traditional medicine, and as a decorative element in gardens.
