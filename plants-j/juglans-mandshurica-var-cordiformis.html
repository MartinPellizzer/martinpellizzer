Juglans mandshurica var. cordiformis

Juglans mandshurica var. cordiformis, commonly known as the Manchurian walnut, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Juglandaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, edible nuts. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it produces yellowish-green flowers in spring.

This variety is valued for its hard wood and is also cultivated for its nut production.
