Justicia paranaensis

Justicia paranaensis, commonly known as the parana rose or parana beauty, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of showy, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically pink or red, with a distinctive hooded shape, and are arranged in spikes at the ends of the branches. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds.
