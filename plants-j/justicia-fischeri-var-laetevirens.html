Justicia fischeri var. laetevirens

Justicia fischeri var. laetevirens, commonly known as the golden flame plant, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are dark green with a velvety texture. Its most distinctive characteristic is the vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters at the ends of the stems.

The flowers are tubular with a bilabiate (two-lipped) structure, typical of the Acanthaceae family.
