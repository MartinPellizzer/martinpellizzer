Juncus arcticus var. alaskanus

Juncus arcticus var. alaskanus, commonly known as Alaska rush, is a perennial plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tundra, wetlands, and along the edges of lakes and streams. It plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.

Its hardy nature makes it an important component of Arctic ecosystems.
