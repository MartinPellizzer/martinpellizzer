Juglans regia f. pendula

Juglans regia f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping walnut, is a cultivar of the Persian walnut tree characterized by its distinctive pendulous branches.

This ornamental tree features broad, serrated leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its flowers are catkins, with male and female flowers appearing on separate trees. The fruit is a large, edible nut enclosed in a thick, green husk.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and nut production.
