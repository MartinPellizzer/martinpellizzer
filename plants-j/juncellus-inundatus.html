Juncellus inundatus

Juncellus inundatus, commonly known as the water rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It typically grows in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are usually green or reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense clusters at the base.

Juncellus inundatus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
