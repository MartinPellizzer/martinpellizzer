Jasione montana var. boreaui

Jasione montana var. boreaui, commonly known as the northern rock-jasmine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish-white flowers in early summer. Its flowering period is brief, and it is often found in open, sun-exposed areas.

The species is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a characteristic component of mountainous flora.
