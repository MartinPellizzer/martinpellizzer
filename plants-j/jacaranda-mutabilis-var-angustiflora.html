Jacaranda mutabilis var. angustiflora

Jacaranda mutabilis var. angustiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved jacaranda, is a deciduous tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of violet or lilac flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental tree in warm climates.

The tree is known for its rapid growth and ability to provide shade in urban landscapes.
