Jacaratia conica

Jacaratia conica, commonly known as the coneflower or conical jacaranda, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, conical inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Jacaratia conica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types.
