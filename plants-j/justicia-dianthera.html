Justicia dianthera

Justicia dianthera, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in warm, humid environments. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by birds, especially hummingbirds, which are attracted to its nectar.
