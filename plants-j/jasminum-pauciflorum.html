Jasminum pauciflorum

Jasminum pauciflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, with opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate fragrance and compact growth habit.
