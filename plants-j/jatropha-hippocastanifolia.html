Jatropha hippocastanifolia

Jatropha hippocastanifolia, commonly known as the Indian physic tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically five to seven lobes, and its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that contain toxic seeds, making it important to handle with care.
