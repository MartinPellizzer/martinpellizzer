Justicia potamophila

Justicia potamophila, commonly known as the river jewelweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to red, often with contrasting purple markings. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas near rivers and streams, thriving in humid environments. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often trailing or prostrate.

Justicia potamophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
