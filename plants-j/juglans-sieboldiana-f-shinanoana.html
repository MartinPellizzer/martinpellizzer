Juglans sieboldiana f. shinanoana

Juglans sieboldiana f. shinanoana, commonly known as the Shinano walnut, is a variant of the Japanese walnut.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its large, compound leaves and distinctive, edible nuts. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is native to Japan, particularly in the regions of Shikoku and Kyushu. Its botanical features include a robust root system and a slow growth rate, making it a long-lived species.

The nuts are encased in a thick, woody husk and are valued for their flavor and nutritional content.
