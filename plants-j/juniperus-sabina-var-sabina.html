Juniperus sabina var. sabina

Juniperus sabina var. sabina, commonly known as the common juniper, is a coniferous evergreen shrub or small tree native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, scale-like leaves and small, blue-black berries that are often used in gin production. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to temperate climates. It has a slow growth rate and can live for several decades, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

The foliage is dense and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
