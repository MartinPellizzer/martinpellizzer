Jasione tomentosa

Jasione tomentosa, commonly known as woolly jasione, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or coastal regions. The plant is perennial, with a compact growth habit and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Its scientific name reflects its woolly appearance, with "tomentosa" meaning covered in fine hairs.
