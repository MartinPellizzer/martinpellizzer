Jatropha confusa

Jatropha confusa, commonly known as the Brazilian euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are clustered in umbels and are usually yellow with a red or purple calyx.

Jatropha confusa is known for its milky sap, which can be toxic and is a characteristic feature of the Euphorbiaceae family.
