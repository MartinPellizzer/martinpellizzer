Juncus jinpingensis

Juncus jinpingensis, commonly known as the Jinping rush, is a rare and endemic plant species native to China.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender, grass-like stems that often grow in wet or marshy environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in humid climates and is typically found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Juncus jinpingensis is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
