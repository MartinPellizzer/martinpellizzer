Jacobaea schwetzowii

Jacobaea schwetzowii, commonly known as the Schwetzow's ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. It features upright stems with finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer.

Jacobaea schwetzowii is noted for its toxicity, containing pyrrolizidine alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock and humans if ingested.
