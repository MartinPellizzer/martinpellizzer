Jessenia oligocarpa

Jessenia oligocarpa, commonly known as the Guatemalan Jessenia, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits that are edible.

Jessenia oligocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and ecological significance.
