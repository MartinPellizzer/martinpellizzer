Jambosa owariensis

Jambosa owariensis, commonly known as the African white sapote, is a tropical fruit-bearing tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, yellow-orange, sweet and creamy fruit with a smooth, leathery skin. The tree can grow up to 10 meters tall and produces flowers that are typically white or pale green. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a smooth margin, while the fruit develops from an inferior ovary.

The plant is valued not only for its edible fruit but also for its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses.
