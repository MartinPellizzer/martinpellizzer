Jatropha diversifolia var. pandurifolia

Jatropha diversifolia var. pandurifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf jatropha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often pubescent and have a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing toxic seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for medicinal use, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
