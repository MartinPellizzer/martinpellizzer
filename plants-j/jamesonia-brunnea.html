Jamesonia brunnea

Jamesonia brunnea, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a branching stem that supports its clustered inflorescence.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance in native ecosystems.
