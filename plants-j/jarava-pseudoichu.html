Jarava pseudoichu

Jarava pseudoichu, commonly known as the desert needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of hair. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in low-nutrient soils and extreme temperatures.

Jarava pseudoichu plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
