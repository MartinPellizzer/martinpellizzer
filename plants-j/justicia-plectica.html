Justicia plectica

Justicia plectica, commonly known as the whorled flameflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Justicia plectica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in gardens and as a houseplant.
