Jambosa inophylla

Jambosa inophylla, commonly known as the African starfruit or yellow star apple, is a tropical plant native to Africa.

It is a member of the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and yellow, star-shaped fruits. The fruit has a soft, edible pulp with a sweet, tangy flavor, often compared to a mix of mango and pineapple. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for both its fruit and aesthetic qualities.
