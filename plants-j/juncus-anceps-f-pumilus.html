Juncus anceps f. pumilus

Juncus anceps f. pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf rush, is a compact, herbaceous plant characterized by its dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish or brownish and appear in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
