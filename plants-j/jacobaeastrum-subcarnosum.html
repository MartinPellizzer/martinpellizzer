Jacobaeastrum subcarnosum

Jacobaeastrum subcarnosum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, narrow leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators.

The species is often found in sagebrush steppe and other arid environments.
