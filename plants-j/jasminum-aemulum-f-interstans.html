Jasminum aemulum f. interstans

Jasminum aemulum f. interstans, commonly known as the fragrant jasmine or sweet scented jasmine, is a flowering plant belonging to the Oleaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often releasing a sweet, intoxicating aroma. The plant typically has slender, woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy in appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

This variety is a popular choice for gardens and as a decorative plant due to its pleasant fragrance and visual appeal.
