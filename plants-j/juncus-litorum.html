Juncus litorum

Juncus litorum, commonly known as saltmarsh rush, is a perennial plant native to coastal regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It thrives in brackish or saline wetlands, often found in marshes and tidal flats. The plant has slender, grass-like stems that are typically green or reddish in color. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spike-like clusters and are pollinated by wind.

Juncus litorum plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
