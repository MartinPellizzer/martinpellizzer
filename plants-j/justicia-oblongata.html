Justicia oblongata

Justicia oblongata, commonly known as the narrowleaf flamebeard, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Justicia oblongata is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
