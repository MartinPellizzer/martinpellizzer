Juglans alba acuminata

Juglans alba acuminata, commonly known as the white walnut, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the walnut family, Juglandaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated leaflets. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by spherical, green husked fruits. Its seeds, encased in a hard shell, are edible when properly prepared.

The wood of Juglans alba acuminata is highly valued for its strength and fine grain, making it a prized material in carpentry and furniture-making.
