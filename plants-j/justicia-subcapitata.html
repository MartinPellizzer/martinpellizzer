Justicia subcapitata

Justicia subcapitata, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

The flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
