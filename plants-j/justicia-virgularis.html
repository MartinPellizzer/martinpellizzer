Justicia virgularis

Justicia virgularis, commonly known as the silverleaf flameflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped, silver-green leaves that are glossy and often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.

Justicia virgularis is also known for its fast growth and ability to produce abundant blooms throughout the growing season.
