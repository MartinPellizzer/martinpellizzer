Justicia tenella

Justicia tenella, commonly known as the silverleaf justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage and clusters of tubular, violet-blue flowers. The plant is a perennial shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, powdery coating that gives them their distinctive appearance.

Justicia tenella is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
