Jacobinia magnifica var. carnea

Jacobinia magnifica var. carnea, commonly known as the pink jacaranda, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, adding vibrant color to the landscape. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces long, bean-like pods that contain seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for warm climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators.
