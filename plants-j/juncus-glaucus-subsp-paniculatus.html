Juncus glaucus subsp. paniculatus

Juncus glaucus subsp.

paniculatus, commonly known as the blue rush, is a flowering plant in the Juncaceae family. It is a perennial herb characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and slender, cylindrical stems.

The plant produces small, pale blue to greenish flowers in spike-like clusters, which are typically borne on tall, branched panicles. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its fibrous roots and rhizomes make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
