Jussiaea inclinata var. amazonica

Jussiaea inclinata var. amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian water willow, is a semi-aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It typically grows in shallow water or along the edges of rivers and lakes, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils. The plant has slender, branching stems with small, oval-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating. Its flowers are tiny and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, producing nectar that attracts insects.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
