Jacobinia pohliana f. velutina

Jacobinia pohliana f. velutina, commonly known as the velvet jacaranda, is a unique variant of the jacaranda tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage, which gives it a distinct appearance compared to other jacaranda species. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a dense, hairy texture that contributes to its common name. This plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in regions with a warm climate.
