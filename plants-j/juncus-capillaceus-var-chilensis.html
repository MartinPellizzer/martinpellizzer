Juncus capillaceus var. chilensis

Juncus capillaceus var. chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean feather sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and dense clusters of small, thread-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. It produces tiny, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
