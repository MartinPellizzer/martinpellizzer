Juncus trifidus var. uniflorus

Juncus trifidus var. uniflorus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, streambanks, and roadside ditches. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish-brown in color and are covered with small, scale-like leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, usually appearing in late spring to early summer.

This species is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
