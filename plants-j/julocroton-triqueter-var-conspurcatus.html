Julocroton triqueter var. conspurcatus

Julocroton triqueter var. conspurcatus, commonly known as the spotted croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that often display a striking combination of green, yellow, and red hues. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
