Justicia hyssopus

Justicia hyssopus, commonly known as redleaf justicia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking, velvety leaves that range in color from deep red to bronze, providing ornamental value in gardens. The plant produces clusters of tubular, brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of pink or red, which bloom from summer to fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its foliage and flowers make it a popular choice for attracting pollinators like butterflies and bees.
