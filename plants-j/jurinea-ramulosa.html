Jurinea ramulosa

Jurinea ramulosa, commonly known as the ramose jurinea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its branched stems and small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Jurinea ramulosa is often found in steppe and desert environments, playing a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
