Justicia boliviana subsp. subintegrifolia

Justicia boliviana subsp.

subintegrifolia, commonly known as the Bolivian flame flower, is a tropical perennial shrub native to South America. It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that consist of bright red or orange bracts with small white flowers.

The plant has ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often slightly serrated along the margins. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in warm climates.
