Jatropha curcas var. rufa

Jatropha curcas var. rufa, commonly known as the red-veined physic nut, is a variant of the physic nut plant native to tropical regions.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves with prominent red veins. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by orange to red, fleshy capsules containing toxic seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

The species is also noted for its potential in biofuel production due to its high oil content in the seeds.
