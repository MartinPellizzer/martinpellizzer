Jumellea imerinensis

Jumellea imerinensis, commonly known as the desert daisy, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, succulent growth habit and produces showy, bright yellow flowers. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in harsh environments. It blooms during the rainy season, typically in winter, and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low precipitation.

Jumellea imerinensis plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing nectar for pollinators and shelter for small animals.
