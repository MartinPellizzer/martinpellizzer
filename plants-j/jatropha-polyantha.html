Jatropha polyantha

Jatropha polyantha, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces bright purple to blue berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans and livestock. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its rapid growth and ability to attract wildlife make it a valuable addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
