Jacea subcordata

Jacea subcordata, commonly known as the heart-shaped leaf plant, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden areas or indoor cultivation.

Jacea subcordata is often valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
