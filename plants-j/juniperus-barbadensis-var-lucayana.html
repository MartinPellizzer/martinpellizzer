Juniperus barbadensis var. lucayana

Juniperus barbadensis var. lucayana, commonly known as the Lucayan juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage and small, blue-gray berries that are typically retained on the plant for extended periods. The plant is adapted to coastal and dry environments, showing resistance to salt spray and drought. Its aromatic wood has been historically used for construction and fuel.

The species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
