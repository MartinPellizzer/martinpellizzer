Jamesonia mayoris

Jamesonia mayoris, commonly known as the mountain laurel, is a deciduous shrub native to the Appalachian Mountains in the eastern United States.

It features showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, blooming in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and partial to full shade.

Jamesonia mayoris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
