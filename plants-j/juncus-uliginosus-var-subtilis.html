Juncus uliginosus var. subtilis

Juncus uliginosus var. subtilis, commonly known as the slender swamp rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to reddish in color, growing in dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, which are often surrounded by bracts. It thrives in moist to waterlogged soils, making it a common species in marshes and bogs.

Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil, contributing to the ecological health of wetland habitats.
