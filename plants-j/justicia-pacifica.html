Justicia pacifica

Justicia pacifica, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower clusters that resemble mist, hence its common name. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
