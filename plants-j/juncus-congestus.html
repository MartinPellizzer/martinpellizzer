Juncus congestus

Juncus congestus, commonly known as the clustered rush, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves that grow from a rhizomatous base. The plant typically thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in spike-like clusters, and it produces dark brown, three-lobed seeds.

Juncus congestus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
