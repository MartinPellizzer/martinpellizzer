Jacosta acicularis

Jacosta acicularis, commonly known as the needleleaf jacosta, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are often pink or purple in color, which bloom in summer. It is native to regions of South America, particularly in Brazil and Peru.

Jacosta acicularis is adapted to grow in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal areas.
