Josephia cirsioides

Josephia cirsioides, commonly known as the white coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy white ray flowers surrounding a yellow disk, giving it a coneflower appearance. It has hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

Josephia cirsioides thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
