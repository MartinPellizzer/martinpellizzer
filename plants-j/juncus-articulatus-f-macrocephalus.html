Juncus articulatus f. macrocephalus

Juncus articulatus f. macrocephalus, commonly known as the broad-headed rush, is a variant of the common rush that exhibits distinctive botanical features.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color. The inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, three-lobed flowers, which are typically greenish or reddish in hue. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and arranged in two rows along the stem.

This plant thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, pond edges, and damp woodlands, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
