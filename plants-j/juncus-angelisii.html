Juncus angelisii

Juncus angelisii, commonly known as the angel's grass, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of North America.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and tall, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its narrow, cylindrical leaves are usually green to bluish-green, and it produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers in early summer.

Juncus angelisii plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
