Juncus acutiflorus wulfenii

Juncus acutiflorus wulfenii, commonly known as the Wulfen's rush, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with cylindrical, hollow stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically found at the tips of the stems. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows.

Juncus acutiflorus wulfenii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
