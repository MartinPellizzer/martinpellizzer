Juncus conglomeratus var. altissimus

Juncus conglomeratus var. altissimus, commonly known as the tall rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows to heights of up to 2 meters, making it one of the tallest members of the Juncaceae family. Its leaves are narrow and hollow, adapted for water retention in saturated soils.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
