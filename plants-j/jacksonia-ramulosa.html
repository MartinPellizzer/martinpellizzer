Jacksonia ramulosa

Jacksonia ramulosa, commonly known as the pink everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple daisy-like flowers. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are easily dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation. Jacksonia ramulosa thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in various parts of the world.
