Justicia seclusa

Justicia seclusa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spiderflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features striking, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a versatile addition to many garden landscapes.
