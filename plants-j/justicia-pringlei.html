Justicia pringlei

Justicia pringlei, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in clusters, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which attract hummingbirds and other pollinators. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

The genus Justicia is part of the Acanthaceae family, known for its ornamental value and diverse floral structures.
