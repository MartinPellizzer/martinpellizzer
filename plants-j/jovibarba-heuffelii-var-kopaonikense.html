Jovibarba heuffelii var. kopaonikense

Jovibarba heuffelii var. kopaonikense, commonly known as the Kopaonik stonecress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pungent scent. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing a rosette of thick, waxy leaves that help retain moisture. Its flowers, which bloom in spring, are usually white or pale purple, attracting pollinators such as bees.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
