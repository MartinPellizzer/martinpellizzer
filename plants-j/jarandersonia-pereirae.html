Jarandersonia pereirae

Jarandersonia pereirae, commonly known as the Peruvian flame tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its environment. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species in botanical studies.
