Jacquinia umbellata

Jacquinia umbellata, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Jacquinia umbellata is valued in gardens for its delicate appearance and ecological benefits to pollinators.
