Justicia oellgaardii

Justicia oellgaardii, commonly known as the red flame plant, is a flowering shrub native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in dense clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in arid regions.
