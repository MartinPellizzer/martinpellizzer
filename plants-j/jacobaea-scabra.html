Jacobaea scabra

Jacobaea scabra, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy, rough-textured stems and leaves. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. Jacobaea scabra typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in many regions.
