Juncus acutiflorus coarctatus

Juncus acutiflorus coarctatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a member of the Juncaceae family.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. Native to wetlands and moist soils in North America, it thrives in environments with standing water or saturated ground. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered at the tips of the stems.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
