Juniperus chinensis var. horizontalis

Juniperus chinensis var. horizontalis, commonly known as creeping juniper, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features dense, scale-like leaves that form a mat-like structure, often growing close to the ground. The plant produces small, blue-gray berries that are typically borne on the tips of the branches. It is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of climates.
