Juncus leptocladus

Juncus leptocladus, commonly known as the slender club rush, is a flowering plant native to wetlands and moist environments in North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and slender, cylindrical stems that often grow in clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spike-like clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in shallow water or damp soil, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Juncus leptocladus plays a role in water filtration and provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.
