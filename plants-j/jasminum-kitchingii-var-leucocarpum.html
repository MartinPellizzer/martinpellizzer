Jasminum kitchingii var. leucocarpum

Jasminum kitchingii var. leucocarpum, commonly known as white-fruited jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant produces small, white, berry-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and as a hedge plant.
