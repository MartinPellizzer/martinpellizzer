Justicia foliolosa

Justicia foliolosa, commonly known as the silverleaf false heather, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Justicia foliolosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
