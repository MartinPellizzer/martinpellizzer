Jatropha induta

Jatropha induta, commonly known as the velvet leaf jatropha, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red berries containing toxic seeds. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often poisonous species.

Jatropha induta is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage.
