Jambosa clavata

Jambosa clavata, commonly known as the African wild date, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by fleshy, red berries that are edible and have a sweet, dates-like flavor. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions and rocky outcrops.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for its medicinal properties.
