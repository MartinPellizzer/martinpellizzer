Justicia jujuyensis

Justicia jujuyensis, commonly known as the Jujuy flame or Jujuy torch, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of Argentina.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, tubular red flowers arranged in dense spikes. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy blooms. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting flowers that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
