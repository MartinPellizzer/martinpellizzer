Justicia rendlei

Justicia rendlei, commonly known as the red mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, featuring dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, red, tubular flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
