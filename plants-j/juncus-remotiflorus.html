Juncus remotiflorus

Juncus remotiflorus, commonly known as the distant-flower rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and small, clustered spikelets of flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces both male and female flowers on the same plant.

Juncus remotiflorus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
