Juncus mertensianus var. columbianus

Juncus mertensianus var. columbianus, commonly known as the Columbia rush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like clusters of narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny white or greenish flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is adapted to a variety of wetland habitats and plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
