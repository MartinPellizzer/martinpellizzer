Jacaranda copaia var. spectabilis

Jacaranda copaia var. spectabilis, commonly known as the Brazilian jacaranda, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with long, thin leaflets and produces small, woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture.
