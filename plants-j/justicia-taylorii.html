Justicia taylorii

Justicia taylorii, commonly known as Taylor's flamebeard, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to the arid regions of Texas and New Mexico.

It is a shrub-like perennial that features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers at the base of the plant. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
