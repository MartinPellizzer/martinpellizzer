Juncus melanocephalus

Juncus melanocephalus, commonly known as black-headed rush, is a perennial plant native to parts of eastern North America.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish or brownish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered brownish flowers at the top of the stems.

This species is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
