Jungia pyramidalis

Jungia pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramid bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of hair, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Jungia pyramidalis is valued in gardens for its delicate blooms and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
