Juncus dichotomus var. platyphyllus

Juncus dichotomus var. platyphyllus, commonly known as the broadleaf rush, is a variant of the common rush found in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves that are typically arranged in two opposite rows, distinguishing it from other rush species. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in spikes, which are clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, ponds, and along stream banks.

This plant plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
