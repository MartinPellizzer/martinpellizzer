Justicia mollissima

Justicia mollissima, commonly known as velvetleaf justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that give it its common name, and it produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Justicia mollissima is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
