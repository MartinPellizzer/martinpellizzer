Juno carterorum

Juno carterorum, commonly known as the Carter's juniper, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves and small, blue-gray berries that ripen in the fall. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments. Juno carterorum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
