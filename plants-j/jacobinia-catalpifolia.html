Jacobinia catalpifolia

Jacobinia catalpifolia, commonly known as the catalpa tree, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, orange-and-yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The tree produces long, cylindrical fruit pods that split open to release its seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and can reach heights of up to 30 meters.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
