Jasione crispa var. tazzekana

Jasione crispa var. tazzekana, commonly known as the Tazzekan bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is often found in rocky or dry soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.
