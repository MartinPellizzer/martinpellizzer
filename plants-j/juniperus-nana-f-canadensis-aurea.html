Juniperus nana f. canadensis-aurea

Juniperus nana f. canadensis-aurea, commonly known as the golden dwarf juniper, is a compact, slow-growing shrub native to North America.

It features dense, upright growth with bright golden-yellow foliage that remains vibrant throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, blue-black berries in the fall, which are attractive to birds. It is highly drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a foundation plant.

Its ornamental value comes from its vivid color and hardy nature, making it a popular choice in landscaping.
