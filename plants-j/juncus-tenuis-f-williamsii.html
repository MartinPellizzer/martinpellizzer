Juncus tenuis f. williamsii

Juncus tenuis f. williamsii, commonly known as the slender rush, is a flowering plant species belonging to the family Juncaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in dense spikes. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and damp woodlands. It is native to parts of North America and is often found in areas with poor drainage.

The plant's rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
