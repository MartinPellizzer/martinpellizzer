Juniperus phoenicea var. sclerocarpa

Juniperus phoenicea var. sclerocarpa, commonly known as the Mediterranean juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

Its aromatic wood and resilience to drought make it a notable species in Mediterranean ecosystems.
