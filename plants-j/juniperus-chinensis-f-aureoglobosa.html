Juniperus chinensis f. aureoglobosa

Juniperus chinensis f. aureoglobosa, commonly known as the golden globe juniper, is a compact, spherical shrub with dense, scale-like leaves.

Its most distinctive feature is the golden-yellow foliage, which remains vibrant throughout the growing season. This ornamental plant is highly valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a topiary. It is relatively low maintenance, tolerating a variety of soil types and climates.

The plant produces small blue-black berries that are attractive to birds but not typically consumed by humans.
