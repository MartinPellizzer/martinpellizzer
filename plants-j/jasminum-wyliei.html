Jasminum wyliei

Jasminum wyliei, commonly known as the Wylie's jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Jasminum wyliei is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic benefits.
