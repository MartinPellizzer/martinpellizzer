Juncus balticus var. montanus

Juncus balticus var. montanus, commonly known as the mountain rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or rocky habitats. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of white or pale green flowers. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude environments.

Botanically, it is classified within the Juncaceae family and is known for its rhizomatous growth habit.
