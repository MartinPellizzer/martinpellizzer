Jasminum pulvinatum

Jasminum pulvinatum, commonly known as the African jasmine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It features fragrant, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a subtle scent. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. Its stems are woody and can be used for ornamental purposes in gardens.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
