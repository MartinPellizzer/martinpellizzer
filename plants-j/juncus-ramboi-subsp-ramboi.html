Juncus ramboi subsp. ramboi

Juncus ramboi subsp.

ramboi, commonly known as the ramboi rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as swamps and along riverbanks.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually green and cylindrical in shape. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
