Justicia verticillata

Justicia verticillata, commonly known as the whorled flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in whorls around the stems. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
