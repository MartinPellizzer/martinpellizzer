Jasminum syriacum

Jasminum syriacum, commonly known as the Syrian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer. The plant has slender, woody stems and oval-shaped leaves with a smooth texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate fragrance and elegant appearance.
