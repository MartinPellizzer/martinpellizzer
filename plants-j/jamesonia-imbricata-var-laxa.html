Jamesonia imbricata var. laxa

Jamesonia imbricata var. laxa, commonly known as the looseleaf jamesonia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or shaded areas. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and a low-growing habit.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
