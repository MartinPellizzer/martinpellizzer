Justicia subintegrifolia

Justicia subintegrifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf false arum, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers that are often surrounded by showy bracts. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy areas or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for tropical gardens and greenhouses.
