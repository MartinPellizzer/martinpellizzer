Juncus glaucus var. acutissimus

Juncus glaucus var. acutissimus, commonly known as the sharp-pointed rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that are often bluish-green in color, with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown, depending on the season. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil, it plays an important role in wetland ecosystems.
