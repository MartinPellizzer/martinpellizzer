Jasminum rigidum var. tetraphis

Jasminum rigidum var. tetraphis, commonly known as the four-leaf jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often releasing a sweet aroma at night. The plant has woody stems and glossy, dark green leaves that are typically opposite in arrangement. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and as a houseplant.
