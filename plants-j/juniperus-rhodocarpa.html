Juniperus rhodocarpa

Juniperus rhodocarpa, commonly known as the red juniper, is a species of coniferous evergreen shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for various birds and mammals. Juniperus rhodocarpa is adapted to harsh, arid environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Its resilience and hardy nature make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
