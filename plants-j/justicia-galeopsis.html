Justicia galeopsis

Justicia galeopsis, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, sky-blue bracts that resemble mist. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces small, tubular flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Justicia galeopsis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
