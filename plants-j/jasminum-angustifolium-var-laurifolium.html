Jasminum angustifolium var. laurifolium

Jasminum angustifolium var. laurifolium, commonly known as the evergreen jasmine, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California and northern Baja California.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are evergreen, providing year-round foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and fragrance, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in mild climates.
