Justicia neoglandulosa

Justicia neoglandulosa, commonly known as the silverleaf false heather, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
