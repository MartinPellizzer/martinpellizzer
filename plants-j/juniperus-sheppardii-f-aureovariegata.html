Juniperus sheppardii f. aureovariegata

Juniperus sheppardii f. aureovariegata, commonly known as the golden variegated sheppard juniper, is a unique variant of the sheppard juniper.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, which features a combination of yellow and green variegation on its needle-like leaves. This form is typically found in arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States. The plant is a slow-growing evergreen shrub or small tree with a compact, rounded form.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its distinctive coloration and drought tolerance, making it suitable for xeriscaping in suitable climates.
