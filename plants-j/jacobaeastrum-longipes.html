Jacobaeastrum longipes

Jacobaeastrum longipes, commonly known as the longipes thistle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its structure includes a tall, branching stem with a basal rosette of leaves.

Jacobaeastrum longipes is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
