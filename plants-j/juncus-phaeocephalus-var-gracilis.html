Juncus phaeocephalus var. gracilis

Juncus phaeocephalus var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender dark-headed rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. The plant has tall, slender stems that are often reddish-brown in color, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. It produces small, dark purple to black flowers in dense clusters at the top of the stems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist soil conditions.
