Jacquemontia fruticulosa f. angustifolia

Jacquemontia fruticulosa f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed jacquemontia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five ovate leaflets, often with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and native plant gardening.
