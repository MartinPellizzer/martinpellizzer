Juniperus phoenicea var. pyramidalis

Juniperus phoenicea var. pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramid juniper, is a compact, slow-growing evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a narrow, conical shape with dense, scale-like leaves that are dark green and often have a bluish tint. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are typically blue or black when mature, containing toxic seeds. It is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
