Justicia bruneelii

Justicia bruneelii, commonly known as the red-flowered justicia, is a tropical plant belonging to the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant is characterized by its striking red bracts that surround small, tubular flowers, which are often mistaken for petals. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy underside.

Justicia bruneelii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant color and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
