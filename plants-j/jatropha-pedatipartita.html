Jatropha pedatipartita

Jatropha pedatipartita, commonly known as the broadleaf jatropha, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries containing toxic seeds.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
