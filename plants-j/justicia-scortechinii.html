Justicia scortechinii

Justicia scortechinii, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a perennial shrub native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, red or orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It prefers well-drained soil and is drought-tolerant once established.

Justicia scortechinii is often used in landscaping for its vibrant color and ornamental value.
