Juncus conglomeratus subuliflorus

Juncus conglomeratus subuliflorus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and bear small, spike-like clusters of greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and covered in a fine, powdery coating.

This species is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
