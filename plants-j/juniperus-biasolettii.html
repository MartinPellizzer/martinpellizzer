Juniperus biasolettii

Juniperus biasolettii, commonly known as the Mexican redcedar, is a species of juniper native to Mexico.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has scale-like leaves that are dense and closely packed, giving it a compact appearance. Its cones are small and berry-like, containing aromatic seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The plant is well adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing its resilience in harsh conditions.
