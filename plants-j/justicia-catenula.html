Justicia catenula

Justicia catenula, commonly known as the red mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, red bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its clusters of small white or pink flowers are surrounded by brightly colored bracts, making it a popular ornamental plant.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.
