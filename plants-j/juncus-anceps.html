Juncus anceps

Juncus anceps, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and lack leaves, with small, spike-like inflorescences at the tips. Its flowers are usually pale green to brown and are wind-pollinated.

Juncus anceps plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
