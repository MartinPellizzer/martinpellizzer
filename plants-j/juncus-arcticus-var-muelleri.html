Juncus arcticus var. muelleri

Juncus arcticus var. muelleri, commonly known as the Mueller's rush, is a perennial plant native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

This variety is an important component of tundra ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
