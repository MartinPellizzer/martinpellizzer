Juncus campestris var. tenuis

Juncus campestris var. tenuis, commonly known as narrowleaf rush, is a flowering plant species belonging to the family Juncaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. This plant is often found in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, ditches, and along the edges of ponds. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread vegetatively and form dense stands.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
