Justicia betonica var. villosa

Justicia betonica var. villosa, commonly known as hairy justicia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces spikes of bright blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
