Justicia matinsoniana

Justicia matinsoniana, commonly known as the Matinson's glorybower, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant produces showy bracts that often resemble petals, enhancing its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.
