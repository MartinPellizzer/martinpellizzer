Jarilla heterophylla subsp. pilosa

Jarilla heterophylla subsp.

pilosa, commonly known as hairy jarilla, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its variable leaf morphology, with both basal and stem leaves often being hairy and sometimes lobed.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually alternate and may vary in shape depending on the growth conditions.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and dry habitats, showcasing its resilience in arid environments.
