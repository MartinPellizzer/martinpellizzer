Justicia betonica

Justicia betonica, commonly known as redroot false nettle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a reddish stem, which gives it its common name. Its showy, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
