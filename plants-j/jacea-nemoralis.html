Jacea nemoralis

Jacea nemoralis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. The plant features slender stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are bell-shaped, usually blue or violet, and bloom in summer.

Jacea nemoralis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
