Jasminum trichotomum

Jasminum trichotomum, commonly known as the Chinese jasmine or three-part jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has slender, woody stems and opposite, simple leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

Jasminum trichotomum is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
