Jasminum albicalyx

Jasminum albicalyx, commonly known as the white jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often releasing a sweet scent during the evening. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with glossy, dark green leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
