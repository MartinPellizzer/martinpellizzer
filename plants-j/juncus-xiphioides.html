Juncus xiphioides

Juncus xiphioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of white or pale green flowers. It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in water gardens and bog landscapes.
