Jacquemontia eriocephala var. maynensis

Jacquemontia eriocephala var. maynensis, commonly known as the Maynensis false morning glory, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features hairy stems and heart-shaped, palmate leaves that are typically light green. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The species is characterized by its distinctive seed pods, which are cylindrical and covered in soft, cotton-like hairs.
