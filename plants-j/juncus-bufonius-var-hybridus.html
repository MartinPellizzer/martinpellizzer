Juncus bufonius var. hybridus

Juncus bufonius var. hybridus, commonly known as the hybrid rush, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and damp meadows. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often green or reddish in color, and it produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The hybrid rush is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
