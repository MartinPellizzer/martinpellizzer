Juncus supinus var. nodosus

Juncus supinus var. nodosus, commonly known as the nodding rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, pond edges, and stream banks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense stands that stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.

The nodding appearance of its flowers is a distinguishing feature, giving it a delicate, grass-like appearance.
