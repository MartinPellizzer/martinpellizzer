Jarava speciosa var. atuelensis

Jarava speciosa var. atuelensis, commonly known as the Atuel jarava, is a perennial grass species native to the arid regions of Argentina.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and covered with fine hairs, adapting to the dry, windy conditions of its habitat.

This species plays a role in stabilizing soils in semi-arid environments and is part of the Poaceae family.
