Juncus bufonius f. divaricatus

Juncus bufonius f. divaricatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a variant of the broad-leaved rush species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 1 mm wide. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is often found in marshy areas and is adapted to moist, nutrient-poor soils.
