Joniris triflora

Joniris triflora, commonly known as the three-flower orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters of three. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Joniris triflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique floral structure.
