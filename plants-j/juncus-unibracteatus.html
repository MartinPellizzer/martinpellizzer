Juncus unibracteatus

Juncus unibracteatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae and is characterized by its cylindrical, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, often emerging from the base of the plant.

Juncus unibracteatus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
