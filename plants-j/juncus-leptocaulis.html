Juncus leptocaulis

Juncus leptocaulis, commonly known as slender rush, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikes of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into hard, three-angled berries.

Juncus leptocaulis plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
