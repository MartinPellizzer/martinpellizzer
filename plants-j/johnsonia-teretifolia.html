Johnsonia teretifolia

Johnsonia teretifolia, commonly known as the slender-leafed Johnsonia, is a rare and unique plant native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate touch to its foliage. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, moist soil of its natural habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Johnsonia teretifolia is considered a species of conservation concern.
