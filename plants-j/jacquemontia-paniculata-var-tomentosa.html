Jacquemontia paniculata var. tomentosa

Jacquemontia paniculata var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and disturbed soils. The plant features hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it its "tomentosa" variety name, meaning "woolly.". Its showy yellow flowers are arranged in paniculate inflorescences, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is characterized by its distinct morphology and ecological adaptability, making it a notable component of its native flora.
