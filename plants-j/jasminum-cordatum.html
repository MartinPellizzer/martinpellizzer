Jasminum cordatum

Jasminum cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its flowers emit a sweet, intoxicating scent, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Jasminum cordatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
