Jasione crispa subsp. centralis

Jasione crispa subsp.

centralis, commonly known as the central crisped bellflower, is a perennial herb native to central Europe. It typically grows in grassy areas and open woodlands, reaching a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple, blooming from June to August. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its stems are erect and branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
