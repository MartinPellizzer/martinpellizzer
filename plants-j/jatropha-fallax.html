Jatropha fallax

Jatropha fallax, commonly known as the Mexican cupped spurge, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright orange, cup-shaped fruits. Its leaves and all parts of the plant are toxic, containing latex with irritant properties.

Jatropha fallax is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and striking fruit display.
