Justicia acanthoides

Justicia acanthoides, commonly known as flame flower or red flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Justicia acanthoides is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
