Jarava patagonica

Jarava patagonica, commonly known as Patagonian needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and grasslands. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often wind-pollinated.

Jarava patagonica plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native habitat.
