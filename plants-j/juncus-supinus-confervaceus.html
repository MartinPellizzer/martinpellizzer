Juncus supinus confervaceus

Juncus supinus confervaceus, commonly known as the silky rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers that are clustered in spikelets at the tips of the stems. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in coastal areas and freshwater habitats.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
