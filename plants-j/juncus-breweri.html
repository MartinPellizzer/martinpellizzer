Juncus breweri

Juncus breweri, commonly known as Brewer's rush, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of white or pale green flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and disturbed areas. It is a member of the Juncaceae family, which includes other rush species.

Brewer's rush is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
