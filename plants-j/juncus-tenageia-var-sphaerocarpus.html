Juncus tenageia var. sphaerocarpus

Juncus tenageia var. sphaerocarpus, commonly known as the spherical fruit rush, is a variant of the rush plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves and produces spherical, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its botanical structure includes hollow, fibrous stems and small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variant is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
