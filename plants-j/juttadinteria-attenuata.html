Juttadinteria attenuata

Juttadinteria attenuata, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with a deep taproot system that allows it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, hairy fruits that are dispersed by the wind. Its leaves are typically narrow and fleshy, adapted to reduce water loss.

Juttadinteria attenuata is often found in sandy soils and is well-suited to xerophytic conditions.
