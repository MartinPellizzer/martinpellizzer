Juncus articulatus var. hylandri

Juncus articulatus var. hylandri, commonly known as the Hyland's rush, is a perennial plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that are segmented and often reddish in color. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense spikes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and the edges of ponds.

Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil, making it ecologically important in wetland habitats.
