Jasminum streptopus var. transvaalense

Jasminum streptopus var. transvaalense, commonly known as the Transvaal jasmine, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It features fragrant, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant has wiry, evergreen stems that can be trained to grow along trellises or other supports. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly serrated margin.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
