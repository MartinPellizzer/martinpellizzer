Jasminum paniculatum

Jasminum paniculatum, commonly known as the common jasmine or winter jasmine, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the winter season. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually ovate or elliptic leaves that are glossy and dark green. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its aromatic blooms make it a valuable plant in both ornamental and perfumery applications.
