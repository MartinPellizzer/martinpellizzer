Justicia flavescens

Justicia flavescens, commonly known as the yellow flame plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. The flowers are tubular with two lips, and they attract a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
