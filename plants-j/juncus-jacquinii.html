Juncus jacquinii

Juncus jacquinii, commonly known as the slender rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish-brown in color and lack leaves, instead having small, scale-like structures at the base. Its flowering spikes appear in late spring to early summer, bearing tiny, greenish-white flowers.

Juncus jacquinii plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
