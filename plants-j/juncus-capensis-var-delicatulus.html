Juncus capensis var. delicatulus

Juncus capensis var. delicatulus, commonly known as the Cape rush, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems that grow in dense clusters, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color, arranged in spikes along the stems. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, making it a common sight in swampy areas and along riverbanks.

Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil, contributing to its ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
