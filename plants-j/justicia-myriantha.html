Justicia myriantha

Justicia myriantha, commonly known as the velvetleaf false arum, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. Its flowers are clustered in spikes and are usually a combination of white, purple, and green hues.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and unique floral display.
