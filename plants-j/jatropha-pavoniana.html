Jatropha pavoniana

Jatropha pavoniana, commonly known as the purple leaf jatropha, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking purple foliage and yellow-orange flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant produces capsule fruits containing toxic seeds, which are a key feature of the genus Jatropha. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice in landscaping.
