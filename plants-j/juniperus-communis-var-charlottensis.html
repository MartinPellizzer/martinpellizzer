Juniperus communis var. charlottensis

Juniperus communis var. charlottensis, commonly known as the Charleston juniper, is a subspecies of the common juniper native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in sandy soils along coastal areas. The plant features scale-like leaves that are tightly packed, giving it a dense, compact appearance. Its cones, which are small and berry-like, contain the aromatic seeds used in various traditional and medicinal applications.

This variety is well adapted to maritime climates and is often found in dune environments.
