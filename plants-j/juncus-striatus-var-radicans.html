Juncus striatus var. radicans

Juncus striatus var. radicans, commonly known as the creeping rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and have a smooth texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, pond edges, and along streams.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, contributing to erosion control in aquatic habitats.
