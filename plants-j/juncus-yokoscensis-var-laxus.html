Juncus yokoscensis var. laxus

Juncus yokoscensis var. laxus, commonly known as the loose spike rush, is a perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems that often grow in dense clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Its foliage is usually dark green and can be used in ornamental gardening or as a habitat for aquatic insects.
