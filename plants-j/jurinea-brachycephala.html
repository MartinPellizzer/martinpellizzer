Jurinea brachycephala

Jurinea brachycephala, commonly known as the narrow-headed sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, succulent leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert or steppe environments.

Due to its specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
