Juncus maritimus ponticus

Juncus maritimus ponticus, commonly known as the Pontic rush, is a perennial plant native to the coastal regions of the Black Sea and the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and cylindrical in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in spike-like clusters, typical of the Juncaceae family. It thrives in moist, saline environments, often found in salt marshes and tidal flats.

Juncus maritimus ponticus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
