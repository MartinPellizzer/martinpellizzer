Juncus leiospermus var. ahartii

Juncus leiospermus var. ahartii, commonly known as the Ahart's rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to specific regions of North America.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that grow in dense clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight sheen, and it thrives in moist to wet environments such as wetlands and streambanks.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
