Jatropha kamerunica

Jatropha kamerunica, commonly known as the Cameroon physic nut, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that contain toxic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and swampy areas.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
