Juncus pauciflorus var. cheesemanii

Juncus pauciflorus var. cheesemanii, commonly known as the Cheeseman's rush, is a perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and slender, cylindrical stems. The plant typically grows in wet or damp environments, such as marshes and streambanks, and prefers nutrient-rich soils. It produces small, spike-like clusters of white or pale green flowers, which are arranged in a compact inflorescence.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in stabilizing wetland habitats.
