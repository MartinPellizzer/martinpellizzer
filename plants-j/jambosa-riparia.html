Jambosa riparia

Jambosa riparia, commonly known as the river jambosa, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows along riverbanks and moist soils, adapting well to aquatic environments. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible berries that are a food source for various wildlife. Its leaves are simple and elliptical, often with a glossy surface, and it has a woody stem that provides structural support.

Jambosa riparia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks and supporting local biodiversity.
