Jasminum seguinii var. latilobum

Jasminum seguinii var. latilobum, commonly known as the broad-lobed jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves with a slightly rough texture. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun exposure.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
