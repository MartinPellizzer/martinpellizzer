Justicia enarthrocoma

Justicia enarthrocoma, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring attractive blue-violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
