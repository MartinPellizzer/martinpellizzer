Jussiaea japonica

Jussiaea japonica, commonly known as Japanese water plantain, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to East Asia.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds, streams, and marshes. The plant has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are often submerged or floating, with small white flowers that appear in late summer. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize the sediment in its habitat.

Jussiaea japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in water ecosystems.
