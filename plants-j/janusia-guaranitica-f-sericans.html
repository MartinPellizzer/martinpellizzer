Janusia guaranitica f. sericans

Janusia guaranitica f. sericans, commonly known as the sericant form of the Guarani grass, is a bromeliad native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette, adding a subtle ornamental appeal. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

This form is notable for its unique leaf structure and is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological and morphological traits.
