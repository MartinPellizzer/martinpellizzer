Jambosa saligna

Jambosa saligna, commonly known as the silky mango, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a silky texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit, which resembles a small mango, is edible and has a sweet, fibrous pulp.

Jambosa saligna is often grown for its ornamental value as well as for its edible fruit.
