Jatropha cuneifolia

Jatropha cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf jatropha, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries containing toxic seeds.

Jatropha cuneifolia is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.
