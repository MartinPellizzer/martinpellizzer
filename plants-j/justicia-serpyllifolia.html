Justicia serpyllifolia

Justicia serpyllifolia, commonly known as carpet grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with slender stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, which contributes to its common name.

It is often found in lawns and disturbed soils, thriving in warm, sunny conditions.
