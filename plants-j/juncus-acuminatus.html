Juncus acuminatus

Juncus acuminatus, commonly known as the slender rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and grow in dense clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, typically blooming in late summer. Juncus acuminatus is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soil and can thrive in a variety of moist habitats.

Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in wetland areas.
