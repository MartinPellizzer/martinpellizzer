Juncus alpinoarticulatus

Juncus alpinoarticulatus, commonly known as the alpine rush, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in dense, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny white or greenish flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates with short growing seasons.

Juncus alpinoarticulatus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms in its harsh environment.
