Johnia salacioides

Johnia salacioides, commonly known as the coastal rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, with yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It is well adapted to salt spray and strong coastal winds, making it a hardy species.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing dune vegetation.
