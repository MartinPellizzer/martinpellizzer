Justicia littoralis

Justicia littoralis, commonly known as the coastal jewelweed, is a herbaceous plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting throat. The plant typically grows in sandy or muddy soils near the coast, where it thrives in saline and tidal environments. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Justicia littoralis is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed coastal areas, making it an important species in coastal ecology.
