Juncus septangulus

Juncus septangulus, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has slender, grass-like stems that are often clustered and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets and are usually greenish or brownish in color.

Juncus septangulus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
