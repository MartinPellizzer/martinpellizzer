Jacquemontia evolvuloides var. parviflora

Jacquemontia evolvuloides var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered jacquemontia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes morning glories, and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
