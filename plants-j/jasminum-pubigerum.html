Jasminum pubigerum

Jasminum pubigerum, commonly known as the hairy jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant white flowers and hairy stems, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
