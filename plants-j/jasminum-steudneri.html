Jasminum steudneri

Jasminum steudneri, commonly known as the Mexican jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its flowers are highly aromatic, often used in perfumes and traditional medicine.

Jasminum steudneri is valued for its ornamental qualities and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
