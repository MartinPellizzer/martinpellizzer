Juncus dregeanus

Juncus dregeanus, commonly known as the dwarf rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wetlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form dense clumps, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and colonize areas effectively.

Juncus dregeanus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
