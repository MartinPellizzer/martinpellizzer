Juncus alpinus var. fuscoater

Juncus alpinus var. fuscoater, commonly known as the alpine rush, is a perennial plant native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves that are typically dark green to reddish-brown in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in spike-like inflorescences, typical of the Juncaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas.

Its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions makes it an important component of mountain ecosystems.
