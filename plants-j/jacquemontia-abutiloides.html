Jacquemontia abutiloides

Jacquemontia abutiloides, commonly known as the silky morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 2 meters tall, characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the morning. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.

Jacquemontia abutiloides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
