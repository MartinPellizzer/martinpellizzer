Jacksonia carduacea

Jacksonia carduacea, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features striking, fiery red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a robust, upright stem with rough, hairy foliage. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
