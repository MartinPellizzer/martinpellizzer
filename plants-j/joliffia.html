Joliffia

Joliffia, commonly known as the silverleaf plant, is a flowering shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, silvery-green leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Joliffia is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
