Jamesonia accrescens

Jamesonia accrescens, commonly known as the giant goldenroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in late summer, which are followed by bright orange berries.

It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in woodland areas, making it a valuable species for ecological restoration projects.
