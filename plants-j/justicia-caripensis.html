Justicia caripensis

Justicia caripensis, commonly known as the red flame plant or flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are pollinated by birds, particularly hummingbirds, due to their shape and color.

Justicia caripensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
