Jamesonia imbricata var. glutinosa

Jamesonia imbricata var. glutinosa, commonly known as the stickyleaf waxplant, is a variant of the waxplant species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that often have a sticky surface, which is a distinguishing botanical feature. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its unique foliage and adaptability make it a notable addition to native plant collections.
