Juglans fruticosa

Juglans fruticosa, commonly known as the creeping walnut, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the walnut family, Juglandaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and male and female flowers on separate plants. The plant produces small, round nuts enclosed in a spiky husk, which are typically consumed by wildlife. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
