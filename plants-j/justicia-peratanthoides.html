Justicia peratanthoides

Justicia peratanthoides, commonly known as the flame violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to purple. The plant produces showy bracts that resemble petals, enhancing its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Justicia peratanthoides is often cultivated as a garden plant due to its vibrant blooms and relatively low maintenance requirements.
