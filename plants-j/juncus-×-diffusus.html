Juncus × diffusus

Juncus × diffusus, commonly known as the common rush, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves that grow from a rhizomatous base. The plant typically thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wetlands. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in spikes, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

Juncus × diffusus plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
