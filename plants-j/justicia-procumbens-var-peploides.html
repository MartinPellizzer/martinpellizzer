Justicia procumbens var. peploides

Justicia procumbens var. peploides, commonly known as silverleaf flameflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, narrow, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant, heat-resistant plants.
