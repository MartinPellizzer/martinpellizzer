Justicia simonisia

Justicia simonisia, commonly known as the yellow flame plant, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular, orange-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its showy bracts and clustered inflorescences make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
