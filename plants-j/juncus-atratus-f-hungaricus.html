Juncus atratus f. hungaricus

Juncus atratus f. hungaricus, commonly known as the Hungarian dark rush, is a variant of the dark rush species native to Central Europe.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, slender stems that often grow in dense clumps. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear dark purple to black flowers. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, ditches, and along waterways.

Its botanical significance lies in its ecological role and its use in wetland restoration projects.
