Jacquemontia revoluta

Jacquemontia revoluta, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Jacquemontia revoluta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
