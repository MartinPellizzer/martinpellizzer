Jacquemontia tomentella

Jacquemontia tomentella, commonly known as the woolly groundcherry, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features hairy, upright stems and produces showy yellow flowers with five petals. The plant's leaves are oval-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its fruit is a small, round capsule that turns yellow when mature, containing several seeds.

Jacquemontia tomentella is often found in moist, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges.
