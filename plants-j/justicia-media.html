Justicia media

Justicia media, commonly known as redstem false nettle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of tubular, red or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
