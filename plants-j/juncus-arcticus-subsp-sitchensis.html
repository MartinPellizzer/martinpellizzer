Juncus arcticus subsp. sitchensis

Juncus arcticus subsp.

sitchensis, commonly known as the Sitka rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions. It typically grows in wet, coastal environments such as tundra and moist meadows.

The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine, grass-like leaves. Its flowering spikes are usually short and bear small, inconspicuous flowers.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various arctic wildlife.
