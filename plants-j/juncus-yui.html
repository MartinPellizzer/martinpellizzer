Juncus yui

Juncus yui, commonly known as the Japanese rush, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Juncaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, cylindrical leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Juncus yui is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
