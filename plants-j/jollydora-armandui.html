Jollydora armandui

Jollydora armandui, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, which bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its timber, which is prized for its durability and fine grain.
