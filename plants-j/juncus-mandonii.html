Juncus mandonii

Juncus mandonii, commonly known as the Mandon's rush, is a flowering plant in the family Juncaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often found in temperate regions. The plant has slender, grass-like stems that are usually green or reddish in color. Its small, spike-like inflorescence contains numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Juncus mandonii is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
