Jasminum diversifolium var. tomentosum

Jasminum diversifolium var. tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
