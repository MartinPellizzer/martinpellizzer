Juncus mertensianus subsp. gracilis

Juncus mertensianus subsp.

gracilis, commonly known as slender rush, is a grass-like plant native to wetland environments in North America. It features cylindrical, grass-like stems that are typically green to reddish in color, and it grows in dense clusters.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
