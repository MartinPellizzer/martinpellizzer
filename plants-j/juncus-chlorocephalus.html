Juncus chlorocephalus

Juncus chlorocephalus, commonly known as the yellow rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish or yellowish in color, which give it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in late spring to early summer.

Juncus chlorocephalus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
