Justicia alsophila

Justicia alsophila, commonly known as the silverleaf justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage and clusters of tubular, violet-blue flowers. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds, making it a valuable addition to gardens.
