Jasminum eminii

Jasminum eminii, commonly known as the Armenian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and Turkey.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

Jasminum eminii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aromatic blossoms.
