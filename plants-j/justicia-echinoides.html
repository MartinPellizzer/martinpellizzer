Justicia echinoides

Justicia echinoides, commonly known as the spiked false heather, is a perennial shrub native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It features slender, upright stems covered in small, glandular hairs. The plant produces clusters of bright orange or red tubular flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant for its vibrant floral display and drought tolerance.
