Justicia prolixa

Justicia prolixa, commonly known as the showy justicia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of tubular, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom in the summer months. Its inflorescence is a raceme, and the flowers are often visited by hummingbirds and bees.

The plant is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
