Juncus istriacus

Juncus istriacus, commonly known as the Istrian rush, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of greenish or brownish flowers. It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
