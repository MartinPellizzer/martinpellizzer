Justicia ackermannii

Justicia ackermannii, commonly known as the flame flower or red flame plant, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds and bees, making it a valuable addition to pollinator gardens.

The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid regions.
