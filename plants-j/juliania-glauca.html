Juliania glauca

Juliania glauca, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in soft, gray-green foliage. Its most distinctive characteristic is its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

Juliania glauca is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
