Juniperus virginiana var. keteleeri

Juniperus virginiana var. keteleeri, commonly known as the Keteleeri juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, blue-gray needles that are tightly clustered around the stems. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are typically dark blue to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
