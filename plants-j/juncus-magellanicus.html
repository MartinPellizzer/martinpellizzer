Juncus magellanicus

Juncus magellanicus, commonly known as the Magellan rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to South America.

It thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of freshwater bodies. The plant has cylindrical, grass-like stems that are typically green or reddish in color. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in spike-like clusters, and it reproduces through seeds and rhizomes.

Juncus magellanicus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
