Jasminum laurifolium var. brachylobum

Jasminum laurifolium var. brachylobum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has dark green, elliptical leaves that are typically narrower than those of the typical variety. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This variant is distinguished by its compact growth habit and unique leaf morphology.
