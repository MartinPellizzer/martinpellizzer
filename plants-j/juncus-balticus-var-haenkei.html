Juncus balticus var. haenkei

Juncus balticus var. haenkei, commonly known as the Baltic rush, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, which are usually greenish or brownish in hue. It thrives in wetland environments such as marshes, ponds, and along the edges of water bodies.

This variant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
