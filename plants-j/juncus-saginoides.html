Juncus saginoides

Juncus saginoides, commonly known as the marsh rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It has tall, cylindrical, grass-like stems that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters near the base of the stems, which are often surrounded by bracts. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Juncus saginoides plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
