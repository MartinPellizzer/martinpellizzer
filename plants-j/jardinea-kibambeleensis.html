Jardinea kibambeleensis

Jardinea kibambeleensis, commonly known as the Kibambele sandveld grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in sandy or loamy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in open grasslands and is relatively drought-tolerant.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock and wildlife.
