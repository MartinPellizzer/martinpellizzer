Justicia adhatoda

Justicia adhatoda, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a perennial shrub native to tropical Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tubular, blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported respiratory and anti-inflammatory properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal significance.
