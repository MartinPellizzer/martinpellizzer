Jaffrea xerocarpa

Jaffrea xerocarpa, commonly known as the desert fig, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy, edible fruits that are typically consumed raw or cooked. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring.

Jaffrea xerocarpa is well adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils and can thrive with minimal water.
